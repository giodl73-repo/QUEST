## Verdict: PASS (barely — treasure axis is this campaign's structural gap)

## Strongest axes

**Premise (8/10):** Velantha's story is exactly the kind of lore that should generate treasure. She used a forbidden ritual. She carried that ritual from somewhere. She paid with her life. Every one of those moments is a potential object: the ritual scroll or vessel, the object she carried the plague-cure in, the thing she left behind for the congregation. The premise is rich. The treasure design hasn't started.

**Lore (7/10):** High Sorcery as the source of Velantha's ritual is canonically coherent and dramatically loaded. The Orders classified the ritual as too dangerous after the Cataclysm — which means there's a document trail, a named prohibition, a possible Order faction still interested in suppressing it. Good lore hooks.

**Agency (7/10):** Route D — giving the cathedral to the congregation — is the kind of ending where the party's *restraint* is the treasure. That's the Schick ideal: the most valuable thing you can take away is sometimes the thing you didn't take.

## Weakest axes

**Treasure (3/10):** This campaign has no signature item. C1 had emotion-objects. C2 had compact clauses. C3 has — what? The spec doesn't say. Seven adventures. Zero treasury guidance. The congregation presumably *has* things — items Velantha gave them, objects from their lives, pieces of the ritual. These are the signature items. But the spec doesn't name or shape them.

The most glaring gap: Velantha's ritual components should be the campaign's treasure spine. Each section of the cathedral should contain one piece of what she used — and recovering those pieces tells her story while also giving the party something to hold, weigh, and decide about. The spec has the story. It doesn't have the objects that carry it.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — No campaign treasure spine**
The spec needs a "Treasure Spine" section before campaign-planner runs. Minimum: name the category of significant object in each adventure. My suggestion: Velantha's ritual left physical traces — components, records, personal effects carried by her faithful. Each adventure's signature item is one piece of her. The finale choice is then partly about what to do with those pieces. **Fix:** Add a section: "Campaign Treasure Structure — each adventure contains one artifact from Velantha's life or ritual. These seven objects together constitute the physical evidence of what happened. The party's choice of what to do with them at the finale mirrors the choice about the cathedral."

## Suggestions

1. The Sacristy (0019) contains the Orders' man who provided the ritual. He should have a physical copy or fragment of the original ritual document. That's the campaign's most important piece of treasure — the proof. Schick says: the room's architecture should express the object. The Sacristy's design must make it clear this is where a secret was kept.
2. The Crypt (0020) — Velantha's section — should contain something she left *intentionally* for whoever came looking. Not her ghost. An object. Her last deliberate act. What did she make or set aside in the thirty years since she died, waiting to be found?
