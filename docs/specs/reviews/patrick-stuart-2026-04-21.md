## Verdict: PASS

The premise has genuine strangeness in it — undead as congregation, a miracle that isn't, a building half-finished for thirty years. That's enough for me to pass it. But the spec is all architecture and no texture. I know the shape of this campaign. I don't know what it smells like.

## Strongest axes

**Premise (9/10):** "The haunting is not a haunting" is the best sentence in the spec. The congregation has been building their own version of the cathedral from the inside — shrines made of arranged remains, rites performed in the unfinished nave. That image is genuinely strange. I want to see a room where the undead have laid out construction tools in a pattern that almost makes sense. That's the texture I'm looking for. Counter-evidence: it's a sentence in a design doc, not a room. The texture has to survive into dungeon-smith.

**Lore (8/10):** High Sorcery as the source of Velantha's ritual is the right call. The Orders classified it as too dangerous after the Cataclysm — meaning there's a wound in the Orders' own history here. The Orders' man in the Sacristy (0019) is the most interesting NPC in the campaign: a man who gave someone a weapon they shouldn't have had, watched them die with it, and then *also died and stayed*. Why did he stay? What does his grief look like after thirty years? That's an NPC with genuine interiority. The spec hints at it. It needs to be explicit.

## Weakest axes

**Curse (6/10):** The foundation secret is conceptually strong but atmospherically thin. The spec says "a silence that has the quality of something waiting" — one good line — and then moves immediately to plot mechanics. What are the sensory symptoms of a half-consecrated space filled with thirty years of unauthorized worship? What does the stone feel like? What do the congregation's shrines smell like? What sound do they make when they pray? The curse in C1 had specific symptoms distributed across rooms. This campaign needs the equivalent — atmospheric signatures that the party notices before they understand.

**Table (5/10):** Not the spec's job to have read-alouds. But: the spec gives dungeon-smith nothing to generate texture from. There's no "atmosphere note" per section — what distinguishes the Gatehouse from the Nave from the Bell Tower beyond who's inside? Give each section one sensory anchor and dungeon-smith will generate rooms worth reading.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — No atmosphere specification per section**
The spec names seven sections but gives each only occupants and a truth fragment. There's no sensory or atmospheric guidance for dungeon-smith. The result will be seven mechanically distinct sections that feel the same. **Fix:** Add a one-line "atmosphere note" per section row in the adventure structure table. Examples: Gatehouse — "smells of fresh earth; the congregation's shrines are still being assembled"; Bell Tower — "the rope has been replaced with something braided from hair and cloth; the bell does not ring when struck."

**P0.2 — The Orders' man in the Sacristy has no interiority in the spec**
He's listed as "he regrets it differently than she does" — which is a promissory note, not a character. For Stuart, this NPC is the campaign's moral center: the person who made Velantha possible and has had thirty years to think about it. The spec needs to commit to *what he regrets* so that npc-architect can design his arc-completion properly. **Fix:** Add one sentence to the Sacristy entry: "The Orders' man regrets [specific thing] — not that he gave her the ritual, but that [specific failure]. His arc-completion fires when the party [specific condition]."

## Suggestions

1. The congregation has been building their own version of the cathedral for thirty years. What have they actually built? There should be one impossible thing in there — something that required skills or materials the congregation shouldn't have. That's the image that will haunt the players.
2. Velantha's section (the Crypt, 0020) should have one thing that surprises even the players who've pieced everything together. She had thirty years in that crypt. She didn't spend it waiting. What did she do?
