## Verdict: FAIL

The dungeon axis carries triple weight for me, and this spec gives dungeon-smith nothing to work with regarding topology. Seven sections listed in a table is a linear railroad until proven otherwise. I don't care that cathedrals have natural topology — the spec must commit to the non-linearity or dungeon-smith will default to a corridor.

## Strongest axes

**Premise (8/10):** A single location with seven distinct factions occupying it simultaneously — this is the kind of dungeon that can be alive between visits. If the congregation is dynamic (if killing members in 0017 changes 0018; if negotiating in 0016 opens a shortcut in 0019), then this location has everything I want. The potential is here. The spec just doesn't commit to it.

**Agency (7/10):** The three routes are genuinely differentiated. Route D requiring surrender of ownership is the most interesting finale I've seen from this workshop. But — see P0.

## Weakest axes

**Dungeon (3/10):** Seven sections. No topology. No connections specified. No loops. No shortcuts. The spec lists them in escalating order (0015 → 0021) which *implies* linearity even if not intended. A curious party entering through the Bell Tower at night — can they? A party that befriends the Nave congregation — do they learn about a back passage to the Sacristy? This needs topology guidance before dungeon-smith runs.

**Treasure (4/10):** Same concern as Gygax. Factions in a dungeon need things — objects that can be taken, traded, or used as keys. The congregation's personal items could function as Jaquayan faction-tokens: take an item from one group and another group treats you differently. The spec mentions none of this.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — No topology mandate**
The spec must state: (a) the cathedral's sections are accessible in non-linear order; (b) the congregation's presence/absence changes connection availability (e.g., the Nave congregation guards a stairway that leads to the Sacristy — befriend them and the stairway is open, fight them and it isn't). Without this mandate, dungeon-smith will generate a linear walk. **Fix:** Add a "Topology mandate" paragraph: "The seven sections must form a non-linear map. At minimum: two ways into the Nave, one shortcut unlocked by befriending a congregation faction, and vertical connections between the crypt and at least two upper sections."

**P0.2 — Congregation behavior is static in spec**
The spec describes who is *inside* each section but not how they *behave between* sections. Jaquays-alive dungeons have factions that react to what the party did elsewhere. If the party kills members in the Bell Tower, do the Nave congregation know? Do they redeploy? Does Velantha's behavior in the Crypt change? The spec needs a one-line behavior matrix per faction. **Fix:** Add a "Cross-section reactions" note — even a sketch: "Bell Tower deaths are known to the Nave by morning. The Transept Witness will not speak if the party has been violent."

## Suggestions

1. The Orders' man in the Sacristy (0019) is the most interesting faction-play NPC in the spec. He *provided the ritual*. His presence creates a natural triangle: the party, the congregation, and the Orders' complicity. Design his knowledge as something that can be traded between the other factions — not just handed to the party.
2. Consider whether any congregation members wander between sections. A wanderer from the Nave who shows up in the Bell Tower is a living faction signal — and a potential shortcut to the truth if the party befriends them early.
