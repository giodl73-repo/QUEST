## Verdict: PASS

## Strongest axes

**Premise (8/10):** The "monsters are the congregation" setup forces honest decisions. Route A — weaponizing truth to clear a building — is exactly the kind of morally ugly but mechanically valid choice I want on the table. Players cannot just cut their way through. There are real costs to every option. The premise earns this score.

**Curse / Foundation Secret (7/10):** The consequence structure is sound. Velantha's unauthorized miracle has a real cost that compounds: the longer it stays buried, the larger the congregation grows, the harder the finale becomes. That's attrition by design. I approve.

## Weakest axes

**Treasure (4/10):** Seven adventures, zero guidance on what the party finds. In C1, the emotion-objects were the treasure spine. In C2, the clauses were. Here, nothing. The spec defers all treasure design to treasure-forger with no campaign-level guidance. Treasure-forger will invent in a vacuum. Give it a spine: what is the equivalent of the emotion-object structure for this campaign?

**Encounter (4/10):** "Lots of monsters" is not encounter design. The spec gives dungeon-smith no CR targets, no encounter-count floor, no wandering-pressure guidance for the whole campaign. At minimum the spec should commit to: (a) the congregation has non-combatant and combatant tiers — specify the threshold; (b) each section has a minimum encounter pressure target; (c) the dangerous ones in the Bell Tower need a CR anchor.

**Agency (6/10):** The three routes are defined but the party's *active agency within each adventure* is not. Route A/C/D describe the finale choice, not whether the party has real choices in adventures 0015–0018. The spec doesn't say: can the party skip sections? Can they negotiate safe passage? What happens if they kill congregation members — does that close Route D? These mid-campaign agency questions are unanswered.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — No treasure spine**
The campaign has no equivalent to the emotion-objects or compact clauses. Treasure-forger has nothing to anchor to. **Fix:** Add a "campaign treasure structure" section to the spec. Minimum: name the category of findable object in each adventure (congregation members' personal items? Velantha's ritual components? Construction records?). This can be loose — but treasure-forger needs a direction.

**P0.2 — Route D closes if congregation is killed**
The spec does not address what happens if the party kills congregation members during adventures 0015–0017. If Route D requires the congregation's consent and goodwill, then lethal playthroughs should close Route D. This needs to be stated explicitly so dungeon-smith can write the gate into each adventure's GM Notes.

## Suggestions

1. Add a one-paragraph "attrition budget" to the spec: what percentage of party resources should each section drain? The congregation-heavy middle (0017–0018) should hit harder than the surface layer.
2. The Bell Tower (0017 — dangerous ones) is doing a lot of structural work for the campaign. It's where Route D starts to cost something. Flag this explicitly so dungeon-smith prioritizes it.
