# Design Spec: The Convergence (Campaign 7)

**Adventures:** 0043–0049  
**Party:** the-strangers  
**Date:** 2026-04-22

---

## Research Rationale

This is the hardest test the workshop has run. Every prior campaign gave PCs designed personal stakes — grief paragraphs, loyalty objects, professional codes, Unit Bonds. The Convergence strips all of that out. The four PCs share nothing except a road and a problem they cannot solve alone.

The research question is whether the 7-session spine achieves emotional completion when the spine has no designed emotional foundation. If it does, the mechanism is pure situational pressure + player style. If it doesn't, the prior campaigns' success was load-bearing on the designed personal stakes — a different and equally useful finding.

---

## The Stranger PC Innovation

PC sheets in this campaign carry:

- **Appearance** — physical description only
- **Background** — where they're from, why they're on this road (minimal; no backstory depth required)
- **Motivation** — what they want to get to; narrow and specific; no arc designed into it
- **Stat block + class features**
- **Personality** — 5e style (Trait / Ideal / Bond / Flaw)
- **Playstyle Heuristics** — built from personality, not from grief or code
- **Secret** — one thing they haven't told the others; this is the H1–H3 seed

What PC sheets do NOT carry: grief paragraph, loyalty object, professional code. These are not accidentally omitted. Their absence is the test condition. Any session where a player-style fires despite this absence is a positive RV9 result.

---

## Reciprocal Acts System

No token economy. No formal trust tracker. Instead: **Reciprocal Acts**.

Each session, a PC does something for another PC that they did not have to do. The session log notes it. The DM does not prompt, reward, or acknowledge it in-fiction — it is logged as an observation. In the finale (0049), the DM reads the full log and assesses: genuine mutual reliance, or operational cooperation only?

This is the test of RV10. Bilateral arc-completion between PCs requires a relationship to complete. The question is whether a relationship forms from acts alone, with no designed backstory connection.

The absence of a formal system is intentional. A token mechanic would make the reciprocal acts visible to the players and create a feedback loop. The point is to see whether acts accumulate into something real without that feedback loop. YAGNI: no trust mechanic is needed until there is evidence that the absence of one is preventing something. There is no such evidence.

---

## RV9–RV12 Measurement Protocol

**RV9** (player styles without personal stakes):
- Positive: ≥3 player-style instances with "situational" trigger (road event, not backstory) across 4+ sessions
- Negative: player styles fire only after session 4 when relationships exist — still valid data, different result

**RV10** (bilateral arc-completion without designed relationship):
- Positive: two PCs complete a bilateral arc before session 6; the completion is documented in log as reliance-evidence, not just cooperation
- Negative: no bilateral arc fires — characters stay operationally cooperative only

**RV11** (emotional completion without grief/duty anchor):
- Positive: session 7 produces a Route E outcome; DM log documents that the choice felt earned based on acts alone
- Negative: Route A or B — still useful; documents that the spine requires designed personal stakes for Route E

**RV12** (NPC arc-completion without backstory connection):
- Positive: at least one NPC completes their arc independent of any PC backstory
- Negative: NPC arc-completions only fire when PCs have developed situational investment (session 3+)

After each session, the session log tags player-style instances with trigger type (situational / character-internal / hybrid). Aggregate at campaign end.

---

## YAGNI Guardrails

- No trust mechanic until evidence it is needed (reciprocal acts log is sufficient)
- No formal relationship tracker; session logs are the record
- No grief paragraph introduced retroactively unless a player requests it for their PC
- No backstory connections designed between PCs; any connections that emerge in play are emergent, not designed
- No loyalty objects unless a PC acquires one in play; no pre-loaded loyalty objects on any sheet
- PC sheets are intentionally thin; do not fatten them with optional depth before session 1
