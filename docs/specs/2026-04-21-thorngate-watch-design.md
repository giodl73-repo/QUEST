# The Thorngate Watch — Design Spec

## What this campaign is for

Campaigns 1–3 established the workshop's narrative pipeline. All three were low-combat: C1 averaged ~35 dice per session; C2 had sessions with zero combat dice; C3's first party HP damage came in session 3. The rubric's Mechanical Fairness dimension (v1.4) has the infrastructure to score resource-exhaustion-as-narrative, but no campaign has stressed it.

Campaign 4 stress-tests that infrastructure. The design question: **can the workshop produce Route E outcomes under sustained resource pressure without losing the character-driven dimensions that have scored 7–10 in every prior campaign?**

## Central question

*How much of yourself do you spend on people who don't trust you yet?*

This question operates on two levels simultaneously:
- **Mechanically:** HP, spell slots, hit dice, Supply Die — spent on a garrison that does not acknowledge the party's authority.
- **Narratively:** trust, disclosure, restraint — spent on garrison members who give nothing back until the moment they do.

## Setting

**Thorngate Keep** at **The Needle**, a mountain pass in the Kharolis foothills. The party arrives as Rose Council reinforcements four days into a siege by **the Reconstitution** — a Solamnic sub-order that believes the Order of the Rose has been compromised since the Cataclysm. The garrison did not ask for help. The Warden has not acknowledged the party's authority.

The siege is not what it appears. **Commander Saren Coldforge** (Reconstitution military commander) trained at Thorngate under Warden Bren Asholt twenty years ago. She left over a disagreement about who the Oath is owed to. The garrison has been sheltering 47 non-human and half-Solamnic refugees in its lower level for three years. Saren knows. This is the siege's real center.

## Resource exhaustion mechanic

### Between-session HP carry-over

The party does not receive a full long rest between adventures. Each session begins at:

> **[end-of-last-session HP] + [1 hit die of recovery]**

This persists until the siege breaks (finale end) or the party abandons the walls. Abandoning the walls restores full rest but permanently removes Asholt's token (locks out Route E).

**Design intent:** Make resource depletion visible across sessions. A Cleric who burns all spell slots in S2 is genuinely weaker in S3. The party must plan across adventures, not just within them.

### Supply Die

Tracks the garrison's healing materials. Degrades after each session in which a combat encounter fires.

| Die | Garrison healing available |
|---|---|
| d12 | Full kit; 1d4+1 potions per session |
| d10 | Full kit; 1d4+1 potions per session |
| d8 | Reduced; 1 potion per session |
| d6 | Reduced; 1 potion per session |
| d4 | Emergency only; DM's one-item choice |
| Exhausted | No garrison healing. Party is on its own. |

Opens at d12 (S1). Design target: exhausted by start of S7. The finale is fought entirely on party resources. The Supply Die is the campaign's attrition clock.

**Session-runner note:** Roll the Supply Die at the end of each session that had combat. The result is the number of healing items available *next session*, not the current one. A d4 result of 1 means one healing item; a result of 4 means four.

## The Watch-Token system

Every garrison member carries a carved wooden disc (unit identifier). Giving a token to an outsider means: *"I will answer for you."* This is the garrison's trust-language.

The party accumulates tokens by meeting character-logic conditions — not by persuasion rolls, not by heroics. By being witnesses rather than commanders.

**Token count gates the finale:**
- 3+ tokens → Route A available
- 4+ tokens (including Dene's) → Route B available
- 7 tokens (6 + Asholt's full) → Route E available

Tokens cannot be lost once earned (the garrison does not rescind trust), but they can be made unavailable by the wrong action earlier in the campaign (e.g., reporting Luca to Asholt locks out Luca's token permanently).

## The three end conditions

### Route A — The Table
Political resolution. 3+ tokens. Formal terms: Reconstitution withdraws; garrison records open; refugees formally recognized. Works. The personal resolution between Saren and Asholt does not happen.

### Route B — The Revelation
Surfaces the refugees publicly to both Saren and Asholt simultaneously. Forces the twenty-year conversation. Saren withdraws without formal terms. Less stable than A; more honest.

### Route E — The Accounting *(hardest / most complete)*
The party earns every token. One PC delivers a spoken accounting at the parley table — naming what both sides spent, what Saren was right about, what Asholt was right about, what the refugees cost. Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table. She takes it. She withdraws. The party receives nothing from either side.

**What makes Route E different from prior campaigns' Route D:** Prior Route D outcomes involved a physical act (forging, speaking a compact, setting a keystone). Route E is entirely relational — its evidence is that Saren and Asholt are at the same table, that seven tokens were earned, and that one PC spoke without asking for anything. The session-runner logs the accounting speech verbatim; that speech is the campaign's artifact.

## The 4 hints

The hint structure follows prior campaigns: four pieces of information, planted across S1–S6, all needed for Route E. Each has a primary delivery and a recovery path.

| Hint | Content | Needed for |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | The grievance is personal, not strategic | Route A+ |
| H2 | The refugees exist and are the siege's real center | Route A+ |
| H3 | Saren trained at Thorngate; left over the refugees; has known she was wrong for twenty years | Route B+ |
| H4 | The pass is no longer strategic; Saren is acting alone without authorization; she needs a way out | Route E |

H4 is the key Route E unlock. Without it, the party cannot offer Saren what she needs (a way to withdraw without losing face). With it, the accounting speech has a target.

## PC-authored finale deliverable

Following the C2 precedent (Thessaly's compact opening sentence), Campaign 4 has one PC composing the accounting speech across sessions S5–S7.

The speech is not scripted by the module. The module provides:
- S5 (0026): the Wizard/outsider PC names the institutional/personal split — gives the speech its framework.
- S6 (0027): the full shape of Saren's situation lands — gives the speech its content.
- S7 (0028): one PC synthesizes and speaks. Logged verbatim.

Track in handoff from S6 onward: *"[PC] is composing the accounting. Current shape: [accumulated lines]."*

## Rubric implications

**Mechanical Fairness (v1.4):** The Supply Die and between-session carry-over should push this dimension toward 10 regularly. Resource depletion will shape decisions narratively (e.g., a Cleric refusing to cast Cure Wounds in S4 because they need the slot for S6). The cross-character compound anchor (Bless stacking on a contested roll, Portent-as-benediction equivalent) should fire under pressure when slots are scarce.

**Character Agency:** Token conditions are character-logic conditions, not roll conditions. The party's choices about reporting Hessa, covering for Luca, and how to respond to the refugees shape which routes are available. This is the strongest Agency design in any campaign to date — consequences are permanent and accumulate.

**Module Fidelity:** The Supply Die being exhausted at S7 is a fidelity target, not a guarantee. If the Supply Die degrades faster (party takes more damage than designed), the finale runs with less. This is acceptable and in-theme. If it degrades slower (party is unusually resource-efficient), the finale may feel less pressured — the DM should note this in the session log.

**Atmospheric Landing:** The Needle pass and Thorngate Keep are designed for sensory texture: cold stone, arrow-walk wind, the smell of too many people in a closed space too long. Each session brief should include at least one sensory anchor. The garrison's silence (they do not thank the party; they do not acknowledge the party) is itself an atmospheric condition that should be maintained until tokens are earned.

## YAGNI guardrails

- No between-session resource tracking spreadsheet until S3 proves it's needed. Session logs note HP at end of scene; session-runner carries that forward.
- No new rubric amendments proposed until S3. The v1.8 rubric handles resource exhaustion; no changes presumed necessary.
- Supply Die mechanic is tracked in session log only — no external file until S4+ if tracking becomes complex.
