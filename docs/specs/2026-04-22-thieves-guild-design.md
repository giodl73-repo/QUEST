# Design Spec — Campaign 5: The Thieves Guild

## Why This Campaign

Campaigns 1–4 demonstrated that player styles (sheet-deep-reader, craft-witness, act-without-announcement) emerge reliably from grief-paragraph PC sheets. The GLYPH paper requires a contrasting data set: **do these styles emerge from professional-motivation PC sheets with no grief paragraphs?**

Campaign 5 is the validation campaign. Seven adventures, same rubric, same measurement protocol — but the PCs are professionals, not mourners. Their loyalty objects are tools and contracts, not memories of the dead. Their codes are operational, not ethical in the grief-adjacent sense. If the player styles still emerge, the GLYPH finding generalizes. If they don't, that's also a result.

Secondary validation targets: NPC arc-completion in morally grey contexts (RV2) and PC-authored action as finale deliverable (RV3).

## The Silken Ledger — Guild Structure

**Guild Master:** Orren Pale. Information broker, political survivor, currently selling member identities to the Solamnic Watch.

**Tiers:**
- **Fences** — information brokers and receivers. Tessaly Ink operates here.
- **Runners** — field operatives: locksmiths, extractors, shadows. Darro Velt operates here.
- **Faces** — social operatives: grifters, contacts, plants. Mira Calloway operates here.
- **Muscle** — enforcement and protection. Cael Ironback operates here.

**Operating territory:** Havenmere's chandler district. The chandlery provides cover: wax, rope, and lamp-oil move constantly; deliveries and visitors are unremarkable.

**Guild currency:** Markers — written tokens of favor issued by Tessaly. One marker = one obligation honored. The party's cohesion runs on Tessaly's markers.

## The Professional Code PC Sheet Innovation

Prior campaigns used grief-paragraph sheets: each PC had a named loss, and playstyle heuristics were built around that loss. Campaign 5 replaces the grief paragraph with two new fields:

**Professional Code:** What the PC will and will not do. Not ethical axioms — operational rules derived from experience. "Never sell a name" is a professional code, not a moral claim. The code exists because selling names gets you killed or gets your contacts killed, and this PC has seen it happen.

**Loyalty Object:** One specific thing the PC protects by choice. Not a person (persons complicate the job). A ledger, a set of picks, a contract, a set of papers. The loyalty object is what tells us who the PC is when everything else is negotiable.

Playstyle heuristics are built around the code and the object, not around loss.

## Guild Markers Trust System

Each adventure issues and cancels markers. Markers replace the Watch-Token system from prior campaigns.

- A marker issued = an obligation created
- A marker cancelled = an obligation honored or burned
- Route A: 3+ markers cancelled through official exposure
- Route B: 5+ markers cancelled through Pale's own contacts
- Route E: all markers + PC-authored job plan executed

The finale's deliverable is a job plan, not a speech. This tests RV3.

## Research Validation Targets

- **RV1:** Do sheet-deep-reader, craft-witness, and act-without-announcement emerge from professional-code PC sheets with no grief paragraphs?
- **RV2:** Does NPC arc-completion fire when the NPC is a fence, a guild lieutenant, or a corrupted guild master rather than a grieving craftsperson?
- **RV3:** Can the finale's central PC deliverable be an action (executing a plan) rather than a verbal articulation?
- **RV4:** Does act-without-announcement fire from professional-code PCs — specifically from Darro's pick-maintenance and Mira's persona-shifts — without grief as the trigger?

## YAGNI Guardrails

- No new reference material until a session needs it.
- No guild org-chart document until two sessions have established canon members organically.
- No Havenmere geography document until a room file or session log specifies a location that needs cross-referencing.
- Measurement protocol is append-only at session handoff; no separate metrics database until 3+ sessions complete.
