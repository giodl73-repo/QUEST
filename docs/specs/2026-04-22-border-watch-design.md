# Border Watch — Campaign 6 Design Spec

## Research rationale

Campaigns 1–3 (Moon-Silver Cycle, Conclave Compact, Halted Spire) established that grief-keyed PCs + ritual-ending arcs + Route D produce consistent high-manifest play. Campaign 4 (Thorngate Watch) introduced the HP carry-over mechanic and tested trust-accumulation as a resource. Campaign 5 (Thieves Guild) tested an obligation/loyalty central question with a non-grief party archetype.

Campaign 6 tests four open questions that prior campaigns have not answered:

- **RV5:** Resource exhaustion on OTHER PEOPLE (unit cohesion) vs. party resources. Does tracking "how many soldiers are left" amplify character agency the same way tracking "how many HP do I have" does? C4's Supply Die tracked consumables; this tracks persons.
- **RV6:** Can documentary-witness emerge from a field journal without grief as the underlying design? C1–C3 used grief paragraphs to anchor reception mechanics. The field journal is duty-anchored. Does the accumulation still fire?
- **RV7:** Duty/obedience as a central question. Prior central questions have been about grief (C1, C3), covenant (C2), trust (C4), and loyalty-under-pressure (C5). "When do orders become wrong, and who do you tell?" is a new vector — specifically Solamnic, specifically military.
- **RV8:** Field journal as PC-authored finale deliverable. C2's compact-speaking and C3's Velantha account were speech acts. C4's finale was a speech (Davan). Can a physical document work as the finale delivery? Does writing it accumulate meaning the same way speech does?

## Unit Cohesion Die — full spec

**Opening value:** d12 (12-soldier unit at full strength)

**Degradation triggers (each causes one die-size step down: d12 → d10 → d8 → d6 → d4 → d2):**
- A unit member is killed or permanently lost in combat
- The PC party openly defies a direct order from command (not requests or challenges — open defiance)

**Die result:** At the start of each session, roll the current Cohesion Die. The result = number of unit soldiers the party can call on as battlefield support during that session. A result of 1 on a d4 means one soldier; a result of 1 on a d12 means one soldier. The range narrows as the die degrades.

**Interaction with HP carry-over:** Party HP carry-over (C4 mechanic, retained) tracks the PCs' personal attrition. The Cohesion Die tracks the unit's attrition. These are independent. A party at full HP can have a d4 Cohesion Die. A party at minimum HP can have a d12 Cohesion Die. The two pressures do not offset each other.

**Recovery:** No mechanical recovery. A degraded die stays degraded. The party cannot "earn back" soldiers who are dead or trust that was broken by defiance. This is intentional — the die models irreversibility, the same way the Silver-Ingot Confession modeled the permanence of grief.

**Finale interaction:** End conditions (Routes A, B, E) require specific loyalty thresholds. The Cohesion Die at finale-session start signals what is still achievable. If the die has degraded to d4 and loyalty count is below Route B's threshold, Route E may still be reachable if Varet's arc has fired.

## Duty Object — PC sheet innovation

Prior campaigns used grief paragraphs (a specific loss the PC carries and cannot set down). Campaign 6 replaces grief paragraphs with two sheet-deep-reader anchors:

**Duty Object:** A physical item the PC maintains as part of their professional practice. Not a memento. Not a grief-anchor. A working document, tool, or record that accumulates the session's events. The field journal (Varet), medical log (Lenne), patrol map (Taris), unit contract (Dorn) are each Duty Objects.

Design hypothesis: if the Duty Object is updated in-fiction after each session beat (new entry, new marking, new annotation), it will accumulate documentary-witness content the same way Kessa's commonplace book did — without grief as the fuel.

**Unit Bond:** A specific named person in the unit (not a PC) that the PC has a professional obligation to. Not friendship. Not love. A duty-relationship that creates a decision-cost when that person is put at risk. The Unit Bond is the Cohesion Die's character-level analogue — what the die-roll means in personal terms.

## RV5–RV8 measurement protocol

After each session, session-gate scores as usual. In addition, record:

- **RV5 check:** Did the Cohesion Die result produce a decision the party would not have made at d12? (Y/N + quote)
- **RV6 check:** Did the field journal receive an entry that accumulated meaning across the session without being prompted by a grief-beat? (Y/N + entry text)
- **RV7 check:** Did the central question — "when do orders become wrong" — generate at least one party-splitting position? (Y/N + positions stated)
- **RV8 check:** Is the field journal legible as a standalone document that a reader could use to reconstruct the campaign's ethical spine? (Y/N after S7)

## YAGNI guardrails

- No new NPC architecture until Arren Vole, the cache-owner, and the blackmailer are each specified at module-time for their individual adventures.
- No Irenvale Crossing geography beyond what each patrol-action requires.
- The Cohesion Die tracks twelve soldiers; they do not need names until they become relevant to a Cohesion event. Name on loss, not on roster.
- No additional end conditions. Routes A, B, E are sufficient for a 7-adventure arc. The rubric's Route D framework applies; Route E is this campaign's equivalent.
- No cross-campaign seeding until C6 S3+ establishes what is worth seeding forward.
