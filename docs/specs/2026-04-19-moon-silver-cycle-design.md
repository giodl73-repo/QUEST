# The Moon-Silver Cycle — Campaign Arc Design

A seven-adventure campaign for the Varduin Muster, set in late Age of Might (PC 20 → PC ~15 in-fiction). Each adventure centers on **one emotion** transacted by **one moon-silver artifact**. The campaign's moral question: *what is an emotion worth trading?*

## 1. Context & Anchored Decisions

### What already exists

- Adventures **#0001** (Silver Rose · Fear) and **#0002** (Varran's Reliquary · Grief) played through full 7-stage pipeline. S01 + S02 complete; rubric v1.2 adopted.
- **#0003** (Aelwen's Mirror · Love) designed but not yet retrofitted for spine-awareness.
- Workshop has 11 skills built, 2 player-styles promoted (`sheet-deep-reader`, `craft-witness`), campaign continuity locked in `CLAUDE.md`.

### What this spec adds

A **campaign spine** layered over session emergence. Each adventure still goes through brainstorm → spec → design → review → play → harvest via existing skills. The spine gives adventures *direction* and *seed-placement guidance* without reducing organic surprise.

### Anchored decisions (from this brainstorm)

| Dimension | Decision |
|---|---|
| Framing | **Emotion-objects.** Each artifact operates in an emotional domain — it *transacts* in that emotion. |

## 3. Artifact Designs (brief)

### #0004 — The Wrath-Coin (Rage)

Moon-silver coin, hammered to deform its original naming-engraving. Closed in a fist with a named enemy in mind, concentrates rage. **Rare · attunement required · prerequisite: name a living person who has wronged the bearer.** Benefit: advantage on attack rolls vs. named person. Curse: bearer cannot mentally forgive named person until coin shatters or person dies. Every moment of waking includes that person. Ending: Reorx-shattering rite OR named person dies.

**Plot:** Duren's disturbed grave holds a burnt confession — the mine-collapse was **Thane Krun Ironhaft**'s order to silence Duren's safety investigation. Krun is now respectable in Palanthas. The Coin was commissioned by Krun years ago to bear Duren's name against any investigator; Duren's colleague stole it and hid it at the grave, unnamed.

**Hint 2 lands here.** Grom's Reorx-rite at Duren's grave has enough cosmic weight to unlock Kessa's warded elven margin-note. Vethrenn's hypothesis: *"We made the rose to hold. We did not try to release. I think release is possible. I do not know how."*

### #0005 — The Pride-Circlet (Pride)

Moon-silver brow-band. Cipher reveals only when worn. **Rare · attunement · benefits:** advantage on Persuasion/Deception/Performance vs. a named admirer. **Curse:** bearer cannot experience surprise about themselves — no self-discovery, no growth. **Ending:** forge-rite OR named admirer stops admiring (usually via their death).

**Plot:** Wena Laen has been recruited by **Lady Tiran of the Purple Rose** — a dying Istaran noble — to serve as a Solamnic-political vessel via the Circlet. Tiran wears her own; is dying from over-use. Wena does not want to leave her. The party must free Wena against her own preference.

**Aelric's secondary spotlight:** a Knight of the Crown confronting Istaran pride.

### #0006 — The Thread-That-Frays (Longing)

Single moon-silver thread, 2 ft, frayed ends. Worn at the wrist. Shows brief glimpses of a future where a named absent person has returned. **Rare · attunement · benefits:** advantage on Wisdom saves vs. despair while named person alive. **Curse:** bearer's present becomes less real; chronic waiting. **Ending:** absent person arrives and stays; OR bearer burns thread in their presence as reciprocal commitment; OR absent person dies.

**Plot:** Thera's father **Nenn Hillfoot is alive** — waystation-keeper at the North Market 23 years. The letter she has carried six years was **Adda's letter to Nenn** (Adda's hand; pre-death; asking him to stay and help). Nenn wears a Thread — commissioned from a **non-Vaenshold moon-silver smith**, longing for Adda. He has worn it 23 years. He cannot meet Thera as Thera; only as a ghost of Adda's imagined future.

**Important plant:** this adventure reveals the vein has multiple smith-families. This matters at the finale — the inversion must account for all smith-lineages, not just Aelwen's.

### #0007 — The Silver-Ingot Confession (Shame · FINALE)

**Not an artifact to attune. An artifact to *read*.** Aelwen's last work — set aside, unforged, at the Vaenshold vein. Inscribed:

> *"I made six. I tried to unmake the rose. I could not — it would have required seven back-takes I could not teach myself. Seven. The seventh is the one who reads this. I leave this unforged because if I forged it I would have made it shameful, and I am already ashamed."*

**Hint 4 lands here** via reading the inscription. If Hints 1 + 2 were caught, party has 3/3 and can attempt **D (inversion)**.

## 4. Finale Branches (#0007 decision)

Party reaches the vein chamber, reads the Confession, faces the decision.

### D — Inversion (optimal; requires ≥ 2 hints caught)

Seven back-takes — one per artifact. Kessa channels (most likely; inverter role). Cost:

- **The inverter loses capacity to perform moon-silver magic forever.** The vein rejects her henceforth.
- **Prior bearers** — Aelwen, Baron Edric, Ilendra, the anonymous dead held in the shelf-reliquaries — have their transactions *reversed*. Aelwen's daughter's face is returned to Aelwen's memory (posthumously held by a descendant); Ilendra's list of forgotten names is restored (also posthumously); Fern of the Sixth House's last minute returns to her family.
- **Current party's transactions are NOT automatically restored.** Aelric's Varran-memories remain gone. The inversion restores *others*; the party carries what they chose to carry.

Aftermath: vein sealed. Future moon-silver work impossible from this vein. The second smith-family (from #0006) still exists but makes fewer artifacts without the Vaenshold source.

### A — Charter (fallback; hints irrelevant; requires shelf-reliquary intact + Laen alive + regulation-argued)

Kessa (or Aelric) proposes Rose Council regulation. Council adopts a charter. Artifacts catalogued; vein sealed; future use sanctioned-only. Cost: party becomes Council agents. Future adventures Council-commissioned.

### C — Destruction (default; no conditions)

Grom's Reorx-rite at scale. Vein consumed; seam blackens permanently. Existing artifacts may or may not survive (DM's per-artifact call). Clean break.

## 5. Seed System

### Seed types (four)

- **Character** — named NPCs whose role activates later.
- **Location** — places named but not visited.
- **Fact** — things the party learns that set up future adventures.
- **Hint** — the 3 D-hints specifically; tracked separately because they condition the finale.

### Seed tracker (new file: `campaign/seed-tracker.md`)

Append-only. Each seed has: slug, type, planted-in, status, target-adventure.

Updated by `session-handoff` post-every-session. Audited by `arc-audit` pre-every-adventure-design and pre-finale.

### Finale decision logic

```
At Adventure #0007:

hint_count = number of D-hints the party can cite in-fiction
  - Hint 1 counted if any PC remembers fresco-heal (memory is in S01 log; recoverable)
  - Hint 2 counted if Kessa unlocked warded note in #0004 and still has it
  - Hint 4 counted if party reads the Confession inscription at the finale

IF hint_count ≥ 2:
  D (inversion) offered. Party chooses D, A, or C.
ELIF hint_count == 1:
  D not cleanly available. Kessa may propose partial inversion (Arcana DC 20;
    Portent can substitute). Success: some prior bearers restored.
ELSE (hint_count == 0):
  Only A or C available.

A requires:
  - ≥1 shelf-reliquary intact (not destroyed earlier by Grom)
  - Brother Laen alive and reachable
  - A PC actively argues for regulation

C requires:
  - Nothing extra. Default.
```

## 6. New Skills (3)

### `campaign-planner`

**Input:** campaign intent (setting, party, central question, N adventures, emotions/themes).
**Output:** `docs/campaign/<slug>-arc.md` — the spine doc with adventure slots, PC spotlights, artifacts, seeds, finale branches.
**Cadence:** run once per campaign; revised sparingly when direction shifts.

### `seed-tracker`

**Input:** a seed description + source adventure + target adventure + type.
**Output:** append to `campaign/seed-tracker.md`.
**Cadence:** every session-handoff updates. Orphan-detection at arc-audit time.

### `arc-audit`

**Input:** campaign spine + TRACKER + workshop-canon + played sessions.
**Output:** report flagging orphan seeds, missing foreshadowing, hint-drift, campaign-continuity violations.
**Cadence:** run pre-every-adventure-design; run pre-finale for the full check.

## 7. Updates to Existing Skills

Small additions, surgical:

- **`dungeon-smith`** premise step: read `docs/campaign/<slug>-arc.md` for this adventure's slot; retrieve relevant seeds into `premise.md`.
- **`session-handoff`** step 7: update `campaign/seed-tracker.md` with this session's retrieved + planted seeds; update hint-status.
- **`adventure-lint`** new gate: at least one seed retrieved (if the spine has any scheduled); at least one seed planted (unless finale); all scheduled hints present.

## 9. YAGNI Guardrails

- **No campaign-orchestrator skill.** The spine doc is read by humans and existing skills.
- **No formal seed-type taxonomy beyond the four.** Character / Location / Fact / Hint.
- **Seed-tracker is markdown.** No YAML.
- **No retroactive seed-planting across played sessions.** If future adventures need new seeds, plant them in current-adventure revisions, not in retcons of played sessions.
- **Campaign spec is a living doc.** Session-learnings may change which seed activates which adventure. Re-edit the spec; changelog each edit.
- **Stop at 7 adventures.** The campaign concludes. Post-campaign adventures (Pella at 14, etc.) are a *new campaign* with its own spec.

## 11. Moral Question

The campaign asks: **what is an emotion worth trading?**

Seven answers offered (one per artifact). The finale asks for the party's aggregate answer:
- **C (destroy)** — none. All trades are theft.
- **A (charter)** — some, with institutional oversight.
- **D (invert)** — all *prior* trades are reversible; *current* trades are the cost of restoring others.

There is no right answer. The campaign rewards choice with weight, not with verdict.
