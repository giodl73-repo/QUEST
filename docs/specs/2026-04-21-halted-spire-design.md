# Campaign 3 — The Halted Spire

## Core Premise

A regional cathedral has been under construction for eleven years. Three months ago, the workers stopped coming. No one will say exactly why — just that the site has turned wrong. The foreman went mad. The architect drowned in the dry foundation crypt. The last night watchman walked into the hills and didn't come back. The local authorities have exhausted every option. The party arrives to find a cathedral frozen mid-reach: scaffolding, rough-cut stone, half-laid floors, and a silence that has the quality of something waiting.

The party has been sent for because no one else will come.

## The Foundation Secret

The cathedral is being built to honor **Sister Velantha** — a beloved local healer who died on this spot thirty years ago in circumstances her community calls a miracle. She reportedly called down Mishakal's healing light to cure a plague at the cost of her own life. The town built its identity around this story. It is why they chose this site. It is why they're building a cathedral.

Velantha was real. She was genuinely devoted. She did die here. But what happened was not a divine miracle — it was a High Sorcery working she performed without the Orders' sanction, using a ritual she had no right to possess. The plague was cured. People lived. She died. The source was forbidden magic, not divine grace.

**The haunting is not a haunting.** The congregation filling the cathedral are gully dwarves — the creature every faction in Dragonlance agrees is worthless, incapable of faith, beneath notice. Velantha encountered a gully dwarf community suffering from the same plague. She healed them too. She died doing it. For thirty years they have tended her memory: building shrines from construction offcuts, performing rites in the unfinished nave, arranging the tools she left behind. The workers who came to build encountered them in the dark and fled. A few were hurt in the confusion when the congregation defended themselves. Nobody stopped to find out what was actually happening.

The "monsters" filling the cathedral are gully dwarves who have kept faith for thirty years.

The Orders' prohibition on Velantha's ritual was not only that it was "too dangerous" — it was sanctioned for humanoid use only. Using it on gully dwarves was the specific violation. The Orders' man in the Sacristy knew this when he gave it to her.

## Central Question

*Who gets to decide who is worth saving — and who gets to decide what counts as faith?*

Secondary: *Can something be made sacred when its foundation is a lie — or does the lie need to become part of what it stands for?*

## The Three Routes

Every adventure supports all three routes. The finale (0021) is where the choice is made explicit.

| Route | Action | What is lost | What is gained |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Expose** | Tell Velantha's faithful the truth: she used forbidden magic, not divine grace. Their faith shatters. They leave. The cathedral is finished as planned. | The truth was used as a weapon to clear a building. Velantha's faithful lose the only sacred space they have. | The town gets the cathedral they commissioned. Clean, complete, official. |
| **C — Share** | Negotiate a coexistence. Velantha's faithful and the official congregation both use the space. | Permanent instability. Someone will always be unhappy. The cathedral means different things to different people at the same time. | Neither community is erased. The tension is real and ongoing. |
| **D — Give** | Accept that this place belongs to Velantha's faithful now. The town commissions a second cathedral on clean ground — or accepts the transformation and lets the original become something unexpected. | The town doesn't get the cathedral they planned on this site. | The original cathedral becomes what it actually is: a cathedral to an unauthorized miracle, built by the people it saved. The new build starts with honest foundations. |

Route D inverts the premise: the town hired the party to reclaim a building, and the answer turns out to be letting it go.

## Adventure Structure

Seven adventures. The cathedral has seven sections. Each section holds a group of Velantha's faithful with their own story. Meeting them is how the party pieces together who Velantha really was and what she actually did.

### Topology mandate

The cathedral's sections must be accessible non-linearly. Dungeon-smith is required to implement:
- **Two entry paths into the Nave** (through the Gatehouse, and via a collapsed external wall the congregation uses).
- **One shortcut unlocked by faction trust:** the Nave congregation guards a stairway to the Sacristy — befriend them and it opens; be violent in the Bell Tower and they bar it.
- **Vertical connections:** the Crypt connects via a shaft to the Bell Tower and via stairs to the Transept. The congregation uses these routes daily.
- **Cross-section reactions:** Bell Tower deaths are known to the Nave congregation by morning. The Transept elder will not speak to a party that has been violent. The Orders' man in the Sacristy will hide if the party has antagonised the congregation.

The congregation is alive between sessions. If the party leaves and returns, sections may be differently occupied.

### Escalation shape

- Adventures 0015–0016: Surface layer. The party learns the congregation exists, begins understanding what they want.
- Adventures 0017–0018: Middle layer. Not all of Velantha's faithful are peaceful. The Orders have a stake. The truth becomes complicated.
- Adventures 0019–0020: Deep layer. The full account surfaces. The party meets Velantha.
- Adventure 0021: The choice. All gathered factions. All three routes executable.

## Campaign Treasure Structure

Each adventure contains one of **Velantha's healing tools** — objects she carried into the gully dwarf camp the night she performed the ritual. The congregation has kept them as relics for thirty years. Through acts of faith and daily veneration, the objects have gained minor magical properties they did not have when Velantha used them.

The seven objects together constitute the physical evidence of what happened. The party recovers them through the adventures; what they do with them at the finale mirrors the choice about the cathedral.

| Adventure | Object | Magical property (from congregation's faith) |
|---|---|---|
| 0015 | Field satchel | Herbs inside stay fresh; the satchel is always slightly warm |
| 0016 | Measuring cup | Liquid poured in is always the correct dose for the person holding it |
| 0017 | Promise-cord | An open knot tied around a kept promise tightens on its own |
| 0018 | Ritual focus | Faintly warm; hums near High Sorcery workings |
| 0019 | Dispensation letter | Cannot be destroyed — fire goes out, ink will not dissolve |
| 0020 | Healer's journal | The last entry rewrites itself when read by someone who faced the same choice Velantha faced |
| 0021 | Keystone | Unhewn; gains its property only if Velantha's name is carved before it is set (Route D) |

**Treasure-forger guidance:** Each object is Tier 1, uncommon rarity. The magical properties are minor and non-combat. The objects are not powerful — they are *meaningful*. Their significance is narrative, not mechanical.

## Design Principles Carried Forward from C2

1. Give the archivist (Bard) something they cannot document.
2. Design a PC-authored finale deliverable — one PC produces something during the campaign that becomes part of the consecration rite or the choice.
3. Design bilateral arc-completions prospectively: write both the Warlock's and Fighter's naming conditions before play.
4. At least one adventure features an NPC who holds information rather than an object.
5. The Monk needs a structural element to "read" in every session — design the cathedral's features so each section offers one.

## Dragonlance Anchors

- **Setting:** Post-War of the Lance era. The town is in a period of spiritual reconstruction — the gods have returned but institutional faith is still fragile.
- **Velantha's ritual:** Sourced from the Orders of High Sorcery. The specific ritual is a pre-Cataclysm healing working that the Orders classified as too dangerous for unsanctioned use after the Cataclysm.
- **Mishakal:** The god the cathedral is being built for. Goddess of healing and restoration. The irony: her cathedral is built on an unauthorized healing act.
- All new named entities (Velantha, the Orders' man in the Sacristy, the cathedral's location) to be registered in `reference/dragonlance/workshop-canon.md` when the campaign spine is written.
