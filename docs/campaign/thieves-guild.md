# Campaign 5 — The Thieves Guild

**Central question:** *When the guild you serve becomes the thing you're stealing from, who do you still work for?*

**Adventures:** 0029–0035 (7 adventures)
**Theme:** 7 jobs, each exposing a layer of the same corrupted institution
**Setting:** Port city of **Havenmere** (invented, Abanasinian coast, workshop-canon)

## The Silken Ledger

A guild of information brokers and thieves operating out of Havenmere's chandler district. The guild's business is discretion: knowing what others want hidden, moving what others can't move openly, and keeping accounts that no one else is allowed to read.

**The corruption:** Guild Master **Orren Pale** has been selling member identities to the Solamnic Watch in exchange for protection — using his own people as currency.

## End Conditions

- **Route A** (3+ markers cancelled): Expose Pale through official channels. The guild fractures. The party walks.
- **Route B** (5+ markers): Turn Pale over to his own Watch contacts. Dirty. The guild survives under new leadership.
- **Route E** (all markers + PC-authored job executed): The party steals Orren Pale's entire dossier on them and disappears. Professional. No winners.

## Hint Structure

- **H1:** Pale sells identities (S1–S2 pre-plant)
- **H2:** The party's last 3 jobs were setups (S3)
- **H3:** Pale's Watch contact is someone the party has worked around (S4–S5)
- **H4:** Pale is already negotiating their sale (S5–S6)

## Research Validation Targets

This campaign is a GLYPH paper validation campaign.

- **RV1:** Do player styles (sheet-deep-reader, craft-witness, act-without-announcement) emerge from professional-motivation PC sheets with NO grief paragraphs?
- **RV2:** Does NPC arc-completion fire in morally grey contexts (fences, guild bosses)?
- **RV3:** Can a PC-authored finale deliverable be an ACTION (the final job plan) rather than a speech?
- **RV4:** Does act-without-announcement fire from professional-code PCs, not just grief-adjacent ones?

## Measurement Protocol

- At session handoff, score each player-style instance: "grief-triggered" vs. "professionally-triggered" vs. "ambiguous"
- If ≥3 player-style instances are scored "professionally-triggered" across 3+ sessions: RV1 positive result
- If any NPC arc-completion fires from a morally grey NPC (fence, guild lieutenant): RV2 positive result
