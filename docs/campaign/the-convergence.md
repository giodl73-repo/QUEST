# The Convergence

**Central question:** *When people with nothing in common have to act as one, what do they actually share?*

**Adventures:** 0043–0049 (7 adventures)

**Theme:** 7 encounters in which four strangers who happened to be in the same place are forced to cooperate — and discover whether that's enough.

**Setting:** Post-War-of-the-Lance Ansalon, on a trade road through Abanasinia. The PCs did not know each other before the first session. They have no designed backstory connections. They have different home settlements, different allegiances, different reasons to be on this road.

**The situation:** A series of events on the road — none of which any single person could resolve alone — forces the four strangers to keep working together. By session 7, they have either formed something real or they haven't.

## Trust Mechanic: Reciprocal Acts

**No token system.** Instead: **Reciprocal Acts** — each session, a PC does something for another PC that they did not have to do. These are not tracked formally; the session log notes them. In the finale, the DM assesses whether the four characters have built genuine mutual reliance or merely operational cooperation. Route E requires genuine reliance (evidenced in logs), not just acts.

---

## End Conditions

- **Route A:** The situation is resolved. They go their separate ways. Professional. Correct. No claim on each other.
- **Route B:** The situation is resolved. One pair stays together by choice. The other two go separate ways.
- **Route E:** The situation is resolved. All four make an active choice to travel together — not because they have to, but because the road is safer with people who have seen you at your worst and didn't leave.

---

## Hints (4)

The hints in this campaign are about the PCs, not the external situation:

- **H1:** One PC knows something about the road situation they haven't said (S1–S2)
- **H2:** One PC has met someone involved before (S3)
- **H3:** The external situation was caused by something one PC did (S4–S5)
- **H4:** The resolution requires admitting H3 to the other three (S5–S6)

## Research Validation Targets

- **RV9:** Do player styles emerge without ANY designed personal-stakes content (no grief, no professional code, no loyalty object — just strangers on a road)?
- **RV10:** Does bilateral arc-completion fire between PCs with no designed relationship?
- **RV11:** Does the 7-session spine achieve emotional completion without a central question anchored in grief or duty?
- **RV12:** Does NPC arc-completion fire with NPCs who have no designed connection to PC backstory?

### Measurement Protocol

- Track player-style instances by session; note whether trigger was situational (road event) vs. character-internal (backstory/grief).
- If RV9 positive: ≥3 player-style instances with "situational" trigger across 4+ sessions.
- If RV9 negative: player styles only fire after PCs develop relationships (session 4+) — still interesting, different timing result.

## Campaign Results (FINAL)

**Route E achieved.** All four PCs chose the south road from Stonehaven's south gate on Day 8.

**RV9:** POSITIVE. Player styles fired situationally S1-S3 (7 instances before any relationship existed); then shifted to relationship-driven triggers S4-S7. Same player styles, trigger-type change tracks session number.

**RV10:** POSITIVE. Tam S6: "I suppose we're traveling the same road tomorrow." Vess did not deny. Route E S7: four simultaneous relational choices.

**RV11:** POSITIVE. Route E by genuine character logic, four PCs with no grief paragraphs, no loyalty objects, no professional codes.

**RV12:** POSITIVE. Maret Sunn (S2), Elder Daven Sorr (S3), Warden Cassel (S4) — all three NPC arc-completions before S6, all from situational acts or smart questions.

**Reciprocal Acts:** 25 total across 7 sessions. Trigger type arc: situational (S1-S3) → relationship-driven (S4-S7).

**Gate scores:** 69 / 71 / 72 / 74 / 75 / 76 / 77. Mean: 73.4.
