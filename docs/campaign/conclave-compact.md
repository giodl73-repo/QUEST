# The Conclave Compact

## Central question

*When a covenant breaks, who bears the cost of mending it — the one who broke it, or the one who must live with the break?*

## Campaign premise

Before the Cataclysm, the three Orders of High Sorcery — White Robes, Red Robes, Black Robes — maintained their compact with the three moons of Krynn through a single physical object: the Conclave Compact, an obsidian tablet inscribed with seven clauses, each sworn by a named wizard. The tablet was housed in the Tower of High Sorcery at Istar.

When the Cataclysm destroyed Istar, the tablet shattered. The seven shards were scattered. The wizards who swore the clauses are dead. The compact's terms — already disputed before the Cataclysm — are now unknown. What remains is: each shard still carries magical weight; the oaths inscribed on them have not been released; and someone has noticed.

The compact-wardens are tasked by a fragmented remnant of the Conclave to recover the shards before a rising faction of renegade wizards reassembles the tablet to claim sole authority over the Orders. The renegades' goal is not restoration — it is control.

**The party does not know, at first, what the compact actually said.**

## Three-route outcomes (finale, #0014)

**Route A (Charter):** The party assembles all seven shards and speaks the original clauses verbatim. The old compact rebinds. The Orders are reconstituted under their original terms — including the clauses the defiant wizard objected to. Valid ending; not the optimal one.

**Route C (Dissolution):** The party shatters the shards or allows the renegades to take the compact. The covenant ends. The moons are freed from it — and the Orders lose their moon-alignment entirely. A real cost, a real consequence.

**Route D (Inversion):** The party writes a new compact, spoken aloud by a mortal, witnessed by representatives of all three Orders including the renegade faction. The new compact incorporates the defiant wizard's amendment. The party is the conclave now. Requires: all 4 hints caught + Religion DC 16 by the party's designated speaker.

## Adventure briefs

### #0008 — The First Clause *(designing now)*

**Setting:** Korrath-by-the-Sea (a port city with an active, if small, Red Robe presence). The Conclave remnant makes contact here.
**Hook:** A letter from Archivist Tessamine (a White Robe elder, non-combatant, dying) reaches Thessaly through a Red Robe courier. Tessamine has found the Clause of Witness — the first shard — in a private collection in Korrath. She cannot retrieve it herself. The collection's owner doesn't know what he has.
**Artifact:** Clause of Witness — the shard inscribed "Let no mortal act without witness." A fragment of obsidian the size of a palm, warm to the touch, that dims slightly when a lie is spoken within 20 feet.
**Spotlight:** Thessaly (White Robe; her grief about dimming her sister's ability intersects with "let no mortal act without witness").
**Emotion:** Suspicion — the party doesn't know if the compact can or should be restored. They're doing a job. The job's larger implications haven't landed yet.
**Seeds to plant:**
- Archivist Tessamine (NPC with arc-completion: she cannot speak the compact's purpose aloud until she knows the party is ready to hear it)
- The renegade faction's courier (seen in the port; not yet identified as hostile)
- The Witness Clause's dimming behavior (Hint 1 vehicle: Thessaly reads the fragment and the inscription glows)
**Seeds to retrieve:** none (first adventure of campaign 2)
**Hint 1 plant:** Thessaly's Arcana (passive 12) on the shard inscription: she reads "Let no mortal act without witness" and understands it as a covenant clause, not a curse. The compact needed witnesses, not oaths.
**Open questions:** How many rooms? Is the collection owner hostile or just uninformed? What does the renegade faction look like?

### #0009 — The Ambitious Mage's Vault

**Setting:** A Red Robe mage's tower in the Kharolis foothills. The mage is dead; his apprentice is not.
**Hook:** The Clause of Ambition was purchased at auction by the dead mage ten years ago. His apprentice (now independent) has been studying it, not knowing its origin.
**Artifact:** Clause of Ambition — "Let no Order claim more than its share of the sky." Vibrates faintly when held by someone ambitious.
**Spotlight:** Orik (his grief about failing his hall connects to ambition's cost).
**Emotion:** Ambition — the apprentice wants the shard for power; the party must navigate her ambition without destroying her.
**Hint 2 plant:** Moon-calendar on the tower wall shows a triple-moon convergence in ~3 months. Red Robe alignment.

### #0010 — The Grief Inscription

**Setting:** A ruined chapel to the departed gods outside Qualimosti territory, tended by a single Kagonesti elder.
**Hook:** The elder found the shard in the chapel's foundations while making repairs. He knows it is magical. He does not know it is dangerous. He does not want to give it away.
**Artifact:** Clause of Grief — "Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks." The shard makes anyone who holds it for more than a minute remember their worst loss as if it just happened.
**Spotlight:** Sera (her grief about the settlement she lost track of is her most unexamined wound).
**Emotion:** Grief — the party must receive the shard's memory before they can hold it safely.

### #0011 — The Defiant Word

**Setting:** An underground library maintained by a sect of scholars who left the Orders before the Cataclysm.
**Hook:** The Clause of Defiance was given to this sect voluntarily by the defiant wizard before she died. She left instructions: give this to whoever comes asking with a White Robe, a Red Robe, and a Black Robe present. The party has one. They need three.
**Artifact:** Clause of Defiance — "Let any mortal amend what any mortal has sworn." The shard has a second inscription in different handwriting on the reverse.
**Spotlight:** Calder (his grief about the plague; the defiant wizard's objection was about a clause that allowed the Orders to withhold healing magic during a Cataclysm-precursor event).
**Hint 3 plant:** The second inscription on the shard, revealed by Religion DC 15: "As I wrote above, what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true. The compact did not make it so; it merely acknowledged it."
**Emotion:** Defiance — the party learns the compact was already contested by its own signatories.

### #0012 — The Reckoning Stone

**Setting:** The renegade faction's regional base (a repurposed Order safehouse in Palanthas).
**Hook:** The renegades have the Clause of Reckoning. They don't intend to give it up. This is the first direct confrontation.
**Artifact:** Clause of Reckoning — "Let the Orders answer to those they serve." The renegades believe this clause justifies their claim to reconstitute the Conclave under new leadership.
**Spotlight:** Thessaly (her personal stakes are highest; she's White Robe and the renegades were Red Robe before they broke away).
**Hint 4 plant:** The renegade leader's research notes (accessible if the party searches her study): she has reconstructed the compact's intent. "The compact was never about the Orders. It was about protecting mortals from the Orders' wars." Auto-delivered passive 15 to Calder.
**Emotion:** Reckoning — the party faces what has been done in the compact's name; the renegades have a point.

### #0013 — The Forgiveness Key

**Setting:** Wherever the sixth shard has ended up; the NPC who holds it is someone the party (or Thessaly specifically) has wronged or failed.
**Hook:** Determined at design time when the campaign reaches this point. The shard requires an act of forgiveness to be received — from the party, not toward them.
**Artifact:** Clause of Forgiveness — "Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."
**Spotlight:** All four (this is a party arc-completion adventure; every PC's grief-paragraph gets a moment).
**Emotion:** Forgiveness.

### #0014 — The New Covenant *(FINALE)*

**Setting:** The ruins of the Tower of High Sorcery at Istar, accessible only during a triple-moon convergence (Hint 2 delivers the timing).
**Hook:** The party has six shards. The seventh is in the renegade leader's hands. She is also at the ruins for the same reason.
**Finale scene:** The compact must be spoken. Route D requires a mortal to speak a new compact, witnessed by all three Orders (represented by whoever is present — including the renegade faction). The speech is a dm-letter scene.
**Emotion:** Covenant.

## NPC arc-completions to design

Per npc-architect skill, these NPCs need full arc-completion sections before their adventure is designed:
- **Archivist Tessamine** (#0008): cannot speak the compact's purpose until she knows the party is ready — her arc-completion is the moment she tells them what the compact was actually for
- **Kagonesti Elder** (#0010): cannot release the grief shard until someone names their own grief first — his arc-completion is a reciprocal naming
- **Renegade Leader** (#0012): will not release the reckoning shard until the party acknowledges she is right about something — her arc-completion is being heard
