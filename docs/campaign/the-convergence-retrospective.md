# The Convergence — Campaign Retrospective

**Party:** the-strangers (Brek Ashfall, Sera Windholt, Tam Overfield, Vess Ironmark)
**Adventures:** 0043-0049
**Sessions:** 7 (C7-S1 through C7-S7)
**Route:** E (all four PCs chose the south road, Day 8, Stonehaven south gate)
**Gate scores:** 69 / 71 / 72 / 74 / 75 / 76 / 77. Mean: 73.4.

---

## The Central Finding

Player styles emerge from situational pressure alone, before any relationship exists.

In Session 1, before any of the four travelers knew each other's names, seven player-style instances fired from pure situational triggers: Vess repositioned away from a fall line without announcing it; Sera documented a person's action before she documented the structure; Brek heard "Stonehaven" and held his expression still. No backstory. No grief. No loyalty object. The situation produced the behavior.

This is the positive answer to RV9. The workshop has run this test six times with designed personal stakes (grief paragraphs, professional codes, loyalty objects). This campaign ran it without. The result is the same. Player styles emerge from personality plus situation. Designed personal stakes are one way to anchor personality. They are not the only way.

---

## RV9 — Player Styles Without Personal Stakes

POSITIVE.

S1-S3: 7 instances all coded "situational" — Sera's documentation blank, Vess's double camp-position adjustment, Brek's Stonehaven restraint, Vess's fall-line warning, and others. All fired before any relationship existed.

S4-S7: trigger types shift to "relationship-driven" — Brek vouches first; all three follow Brek down wrong fork; Brek occupies chair; Vess within sight of fire.

The trigger-type arc is the finding. The same player styles fire throughout the campaign. What changes is the trigger: situational (S1-S3) then relational (S4-S7). The transition tracks session number and relationship development. This is the clearest evidence the workshop has produced that player styles are a function of personality plus context, not of designed emotional stakes.

## RV10 — Bilateral Arc-Completion Without Designed Relationship

POSITIVE.

Tam's line in Session 6: "I suppose we're traveling the same road tomorrow."

Vess did not deny it. Brek did not respond. Sera wrote something. Nobody objected. The bilateral is not a declaration — it is an acknowledgment of a fact. That is what a bilateral looks like when the relationship was built from road-pressure rather than designed backstory.

Session 7 delivered four simultaneous bilateral completions: each PC at the south gate chose the south road for a distinct reason that traces to character, not module.

---

## RV11 — Emotional Completion Without Grief/Duty Anchor

POSITIVE.

Route E achieved. The session log documents each PC's mechanism:

Vess: "Millhaven delivery contact is south. I'm going south." Found a reason; the finding was the choice.

Brek: "Stonehaven's done." Destination completed; kept going south.

Tam: "Circuit continues south anyway." Arranged the circuit to coincide; the arranging was the decision.

Sera: (nothing; already through the gate) — walking was the commitment.

The four mechanisms are different. None of them is grief. None of them is duty. All of them are genuine.

---

## RV12 — NPC Arc-Completion Without Backstory Connection

POSITIVE.

Three NPC arc-completions, all before Session 6:

1. Maret Sunn (S2): Named her son who helped build the inn. Trigger: Brek went into the burning building and came out carrying her. No backstory connection.

2. Elder Daven Sorr (S3): Named her garden (forty years, three seed packets kept, the rest covered for whoever comes next). Trigger: Tam asked the human question, not the tactical one. No backstory connection.

3. Warden Cassel (S4): Admitted the package was never meant for Vess. Trigger: Tam asked the right question. No backstory connection.

All three arcs fired from smart questions or situational acts in Sessions 2-4. NPC arcs fire from the quality of PC engagement, not from backstory investment.

## New Findings

### Session-Relationship-Triggered Arc-Completion

Proposed new trigger type: arcs fire because a PC built genuine attentiveness within a session through consistent behavioral stance, not from backstory or external event. Sorr's arc (S3) fired because Tam spent 30 minutes walking beside her without asking tactical questions. See I-C7-S3-01 in playtest-innovations.md.

### Documentation-Blank-as-Social-Signal

Sera wrote a "travelers encountered" category in Session 1 with a blank for the name she did not have. The absence in the documentation is itself a finding. See I-C7-S1-02 in playtest-innovations.md.

### The Trigger-Type Transition

The most significant campaign-level finding: player-style trigger types shift predictably as sessions accumulate. S1-S3 situational; S4-S7 relationship-driven. The transition is clean and tracks session number. Situational familiarity becomes relational investment over time, and player styles are the evidence of both.

## What the Campaign Got Wrong

No combat in S4 or S7. Three of seven sessions had no meaningful combat. The Tactician lens consistently rated these sessions lower. A DM running this campaign cold needs to accept that the research sessions are intentionally combat-light.

Dragonlance texture was thin through S3. The road authority (Wardens, toll stations) is consistent with Ansalonian canon but not explicitly labeled. Tam's Gilean priesthood provides the clearest setting signal.

---

## Reciprocal Acts (Complete)

25 acts across 7 sessions. The campaign began with Vess assisting Brek with a slab she was not asked to move and ended with Tam bringing Brek bread without explanation. The first act and the last are structurally similar: one person did something small for another without announcement because it was useful and available.

## The Final Image

Vess outside the firelight. Within sight of it. Eight sessions of sitting outside the firelight and this is where she ends up: the same position, smaller distance. She did not announce the change. She just sat where she sat.

The campaign is over when the fire goes through the night and in the morning the road is south.
