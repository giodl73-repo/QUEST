## Calla's Account (Campaign-Permanent, Spoken at Keystone)

*"Velantha came to the gully dwarf settlement because they were sick. Nobody else came for them. She came because that is what healers do.*

*She asked their permission before she used what she had. She was the first person to ask them permission for anything. Then she used something that was not prayer. The working went through both temperatures quickly, the way water changes when you add something to it. She had obtained the authorization through a forged document, because the Orders had decided gully dwarves were outside the scope of their sanctioned care.*

*It worked. She overextended. She died.*

*She promised some of them she would come back. She did not come back. She was sorry for that. The healing was real.*

*She left a journal. The last entry says: 'I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else.'*

*She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do.*

*She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine.*

*The congregation in this building has been keeping faith for thirty years in a space they did not build, for a person who asked their permission before she tried to help them. They swept the floor every day. They braided the bell rope when it rotted. They completed the crypt. They kept the account as accurate as they could without the full version. They placed the glass in the windows.*

*The glass is the wrong color for a Mishakal cathedral. It is the color of healing herbs. They chose it because it was right, not because it was correct.*

*That is what I was asked to speak for. That is what I have been holding."*

**Route D rate: 7/7 (same as C2).** The four improvements from C2 held through C3.

**NPC arc-completion rate: 8/8 (exceeds C2's 5+).** Every NPC with a designed arc fired. The npc-architect pipeline was confirmed as the most reliable quality lever in the workshop.

2. verbal-documentation-arc: confirmed as a full 3-stage arc. Stage 3 reached and completed at Keystone. The arc documented its own cost (persuasion failed; deliverable succeeded).

**New player-styles emerged:**
- `npc-arc-completion`: 8 instances across 7 sessions. Session-invariant.

## What was confirmed vs discovered

**Confirmed (patterns from C1/C2):**
- sheet-deep-reader: universal, fires in Session 1 with new party.
- craft-witness: session-invariant.
- act-without-announcement: present; less prominent than in C2 (campaign structure was more interior than C2's political arc).

**Discovered (new to C3):**
- **verbal-documentation-arc:** 3-stage progression; oral practice has the same arc structure as written documentation.
- **npc-arc-completion:** session-invariant when every NPC has a designed arc-completion with a specific condition. The condition specificity is what makes it fire reliably.
- **relic recognition through behavioral act:** the letter was warm for Maret after she answered Aldric's question, not before. Recognition of the act, not the identity.
- **peer recognition as disclosure catalyst:** Renn's pact surfaced when Velantha recognized what category of person he was. The disclosure was a response to being seen.
- **simultaneous NPC arc-completion delivery through PC-authored account:** Calla's finale account delivered two NPC arc-completion lines simultaneously because she had received and synthesized both arcs.

## What to carry forward to Campaign 4

**Skills to use without modification:** All skills confirmed. Module_binder.py confirmed as production-ready.

**New patterns to design for:**
1. verbal-documentation-arc: the arc works. A future campaign can use a different oral practice (a priest who recites liturgy, a translator who renders languages, a cartographer who reads terrain) and the three-stage arc will emerge.
2. npc-arc-completion: design the condition before play. Name the specific act AND the specific line. The pipeline does the rest.
3. relic recognition through act vs. identity: design relics that respond to the act of committing rather than the status of having committed. More interesting, more earned.

**Design principles proven by C3:**
1. A single building is a sufficient campaign setting. The seven sections of the cathedral gave the campaign its structure without requiring the party to travel.
2. The PC-authored finale deliverable requires seven sessions of accumulation. The account worked because Calla spent six sessions receiving it; the account was the synthesis of everything she received.
3. A villain-free campaign is possible. The Halted Spire had no antagonist — only factions with competing legitimate interests and one wrong story told for understandable reasons.
