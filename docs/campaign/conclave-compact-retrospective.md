# Campaign 2 Retrospective — The Conclave Compact

## Campaign at a glance

**Central question:** *When a covenant breaks, who bears the cost of mending it?*
**Answer delivered:** Both. The one who broke it names what they broke. The one who lived with the break names what they carried. The compact is spoken by mortals, for mortals, with Sylaren's amendment incorporated and Lenne's accountability language in the Reckoning clause.

**Duration:** 7 adventures (0008–0014), 7 sessions, played 2026-04-20 through 2026-04-21.
**Party:** compact-wardens (Thessaly Nightmantle, Orik Flintback, Sera Ashfall, Calder of the Third Road; Lenne Stormwatch joined C2-S02).
**Outcome:** Route D in every session. 100% manifest delivery in every session. The new compact spoken whole.

## What the compact says

Thessaly's opening sentence (composed across S04–S06, spoken S07):

> *"We speak this not to bind the Orders to each other but to bind each Order to the people who live beneath the consequences of what the Orders do, and to bind the right of those people to hold the Orders accountable for those consequences."*

**The seven clauses (as spoken):**

1. *"Let no mortal act without witness."* — Selindra's clause.
2. *"Let no Order claim more than its share of the sky."* — Toven's clause.
3. *"Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks."* — Davan's clause.
4. *"Let any mortal amend what any mortal has sworn. And as Sylaren wrote, twelve years after she swore it: what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true."* — Sylaren's clause with her amendment incorporated.
5. *"Let the Orders answer to those they serve. The Orders demonstrate accountability through: publicly naming the costs of their decisions to the communities affected; accepting amendment requests from any mortal affected by an Order's action; and providing account to a council of non-Order witnesses within forty days of any significant Order action. This is not courtesy. This is the compact's requirement."* — Toven's clause with Lenne and Vorn's accountability language.
6. *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."* — Orien's clause, spoken by all five together.

**The witnesses:** Mira Coldsteel, Deva, Vorn Haleth, the coordinator (whose name the compact-wardens never learned), Sevven's letter, Morreth's chapel record. Plus the five speakers.

## What worked better than Campaign 1

**Route D rate: 7/7 vs 1/7.** The four improvements from campaign 1 produced this:
1. **npc-architect** (prospective arc-completion design) — every significant NPC had their condition written before play. All fired.
2. **dm-letter** (hold-space guidance) — the naming scenes had structural support. None stalled.
3. **P0 self-review checklist** — no decision-point without a fallback; no critical roll without a failure state.
4. **Three-route naming (A/C/D)** — the party always knew which route they were on.

**Hint delivery rate: 4/4 at scheduled adventures vs late delivery in Campaign 1.** All four hints delivered in their designed sessions (or earlier — Hint 4 was on the wall of Vorn's research room and auto-delivered in Scene 1 of the session it was scheduled for).

**NPC-arc-completion prospective design beat retrospective discovery.** Every NPC whose arc-completion was designed in advance (via npc-architect) fired cleanly. The bilateral pattern (Sera → Morreth, Thessaly → Vorn) was designed into the NPC conditions and emerged naturally.

## What was confirmed vs. discovered

**Confirmed (patterns held from Campaign 1):**
- sheet-deep-reader: universal, fires in Session 1 with a new party
- grief-paragraph-dialogue: 3-session cross-campaign confirmation
- act-without-announcement: cross-campaign, cross-PC universal

**Discovered (new to Campaign 2):**
- craft-witness: session-invariant (not periodic)
- bilateral-arc-completion: campaign structure, repeatable
- documentation-arc: 3-session character pattern for archivist PCs
- PC-authored finale deliverable: the most effective finale element was not in the module
- NPC-as-information-holder: distinct from NPC-as-artifact-holder
- Party-level operational posture: combined decision-orders produce consistent campaign behavior

## What to carry forward to Campaign 3

**Skills to use without modification:** All current skills have been validated across two campaigns. No structural gaps remain.

**New skills to build before Campaign 3:**
- None required. The deferred skills (persona-review, encounter-balancer, lore-weaver, rule-lookup) were all built during Campaign 2 and functioned correctly.

**Design principles proven:**
1. Give the archivist something they cannot document.
2. Design a PC-authored finale deliverable in the campaign spine.
3. Design bilateral arc-completions: write the NPC's naming as a response to a PC's naming.
4. Design NPCs who hold information rather than objects for at least 1-2 adventures per campaign.
5. craft-witness PCs need a structural element to read in every session — design for it.

**What a Campaign 3 party should test:**
- Does craft-witness remain session-invariant without a hall-guard? (Test with a PC whose practice is non-physical — a prayer, a breath-work, a reading ritual.)
- Does documentation-arc emerge from a PC whose documentation practice is verbal rather than written?
- Does bilateral-arc-completion fire in the first half of a campaign (it fired in S03/S05 here) or can it be the finale structure?

## The compact-wardens: where they are now

The compact has been spoken. The witnesses were present. The accountability language is in the record.

Mira Coldsteel will return to Palanthas and work with Vorn on the Conclave's reconstitution under the new compact's terms. She has twelve years of operational knowledge and the compact now gives her a framework that isn't broken.

Vorn Haleth will publish her twelve-year research as the compact's historical foundation. The Reckoning Archive will become the compact's official scholarly record.

The coordinator — who kept her word to come and hear the compact — has not been named in any of the records. She came. She heard it. She left. That is enough.

Thessaly has not decided what to do with the attuned Witness shard. The curse ("Who witnessed this?" once per day after unseen actions) is active. She notices it less than she expected. She has been acting without unwitnessed acts for a while.

Orik is looking for a new hall to guard. He has not said this aloud.

Sera is looking at maps. She knows the road to Thornmere; she knows the road to Korrath. She is calculating whether she could check on the settlement. She probably could. She has not decided whether she will.

Calder is writing a letter to the village in the Abanasinian river-country. Not the forty-one; their families. He is telling them what happened. He does not know if they will receive it. He is writing it anyway.

Lenne is at Volenn's tower. She went back. She is reading her copy of his moon-calendar — not the convergence entry, which has been filled, but the ones before it. Forty years of observations. She wants to understand what he was tracking before the one he never reached.
