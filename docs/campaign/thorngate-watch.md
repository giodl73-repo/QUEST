# The Thorngate Watch — Campaign 4

## Central question

*How much of yourself do you spend on people who don't trust you yet?*

## Setting anchor

**The Needle** — a mountain pass in the Kharolis foothills, one day's ride south of Thorbardin territory. Too narrow for an army to charge; too strategic to abandon. The pass controls supply lines between three Solamnic territories.

**Thorngate Keep** — the garrison fortress at The Needle's narrowest point. Built 200 years ago. Warden Bren Asholt has held it for 22 years. The walls are thick, the supplies are finite, and the garrison has not asked for help in living memory.

**The party's position:** Sent by a Solamnic Rose Council authority to reinforce Thorngate against the Reconstitution siege. They arrived 4 days before the siege tightened. The garrison did not request them. The Warden has not acknowledged their authority. His exact words on arrival: *"You are welcome to help. You are not welcome to command."*

**The Reconstitution** — a Solamnic sub-order that believes the Order of the Rose has been structurally compromised since the Cataclysm. They are not the enemy of Solamnia; they believe they *are* Solamnia, and that Thorngate's refusal to recognize their authority is the problem, not their siege.

**Commander Saren Coldforge** — the Reconstitution's military commander at The Needle. Trained at Thorngate Keep under Warden Asholt twenty years ago. Left under a cloud. She knows every weakness of these walls. She is not here because it is strategic. She is here because something was never finished between her and Asholt — a disagreement about who deserves shelter, and who the Oath is owed to.

**The Watch-Tokens** — every garrison member of Thorngate carries a carved wooden disc (their unit identifier, worn on a cord). Handing a token to an outsider is the garrison's act of full trust: *"I will answer for you."* The party accumulates tokens across sessions. Token count at the finale determines which end condition is achievable.

**The resource pressure:** The Needle cannot be resupplied under siege. The garrison's food, arrows, and healing materials degrade each adventure. The party carries over their HP between sessions — no full long rest until the siege breaks. Every spell slot spent in S2 is a spell slot not available in S6.

## Adventure briefs

### #0022 — The Arrival at the Needle

**Setting:** Thorngate Keep, day four of the siege. The Reconstitution has taken the outer ridge. The gate has held. The garrison watches the party with open suspicion.

**Hook:** The party arrives under Rose Council orders. The Warden acknowledges them once and assigns them to the east wall. The Reconstitution attacks that night — before the party has spoken to anyone, before they've been given anything.

**Garrison figure:** Sergeant Tomek Irr — the Warden's senior NCO. Pragmatist. Has seen reinforcements come and go. If the party holds the east wall without being told to, without credit-seeking, he notices. He does not say so.

**Resource note:** First session. Party at full HP. Supply Die opens at d12. This is the last fully-resourced session.

**Seeds to plant:**
- Warden Asholt has a locked room two floors below the keep's hall. When asked, he says: "Maintenance." The garrison doesn't react. (→ 0025)
- Archer Luca Bent (youngest garrison member) watches the Reconstitution standard at dawn with something that is not hatred. (→ 0026)
- One Reconstitution soldier stopped mid-assault when he recognized the garrison insignia. Retreated before ordered. His sergeant called after him. He didn't answer. (→ Hint 1 pre-plant)

**Hints:** Hint 1 pre-plant only. The recognition-anomaly exists; no explanation yet.

**Route notes:** All three end conditions open. Route E begins here if the party acts without orders and earns Tomek's notice without claiming it.

**Token condition:** Tomek gives his token at session end if the party held the east wall without orders AND did not approach Asholt afterward to report it. Hard condition; achievable on a strong first session.

**Open questions:** Party composition (to be decided by party-builder). The Knight's specific Oath-tension — what aspect of divided Solamnic loyalty is most alive for them.

### #0023 — The Supply Run

**Setting:** Day 8. The last supply cart is overdue. The keep has 12 days of food at current ration, 6 days of healing materials. A patrol must go out through the secondary pass.

**Hook:** Healer Maret Stave tells the party privately before the morning briefing: if the next assault comes before the cart, she will have nothing left for the wounded. She has not told the Warden. She is asking the party to go — not because they are soldiers, but because no garrison member can be spared.

**Garrison figure:** Healer Maret Stave — the keep's only healer. Running out of supplies and rationing silently. Her trust is earned by demonstrating the party will treat their own wounds as tactical problems, not automatic calls on her resources.

**Resource note:** Supply Die d12 → d10. Party starts at end-of-S1 HP + 1 hit die of recovery. First explicit Supply Die roll.

**Seeds to plant:**
- The supply cart was ambushed — not by the Reconstitution. Tracks suggest a third party, or someone who knew the route. (→ 0024, Hessa investigation)
- Maret has been giving double rations to someone in the lower level of the keep. Not a garrison member's name on the ledger. She deflects if asked. (→ 0025 refugee reveal)

**Hints:** Hint 1 partial delivery — a captured Reconstitution soldier, when shown the Thorngate garrison insignia, says: *"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."* Then stops. Won't elaborate. Resists every follow-up.

**Token condition:** Maret gives her token if the party returns with supplies AND has used zero of her healing materials across S1+S2 (automatic); one use (DC 14 Persuasion, her framing: "You're careful with what isn't yours"); two or more uses (not available this session — try again in S3).

### #0024 — The Saboteur

**Setting:** Day 14. Half the west wall's arrow stock has been tampered with — shaft-rot; they will fail on draw. It was not Reconstitution work. Someone inside the keep is working against the garrison.

**Hook:** Gate-Captain Hessa Var asks the party to investigate before the Warden finds out. She has her own reasons for wanting this handled without official inquiry — she has been quietly passing messages through the gate at night, believing them harmless. The investigation will prove her wrong.

**Garrison figure:** Gate-Captain Hessa Var — gate commander; controls who moves in and out. She made a private arrangement she told herself was harmless. It was not. Her trust is earned if the party gives her the chance to correct it herself before the Warden is told.

**Spotlight:** Scout — investigation, tracking, evidence recovery inside the keep. The saboteur is a garrison member who was coerced; they fight rather than be taken.

**Resource note:** Supply Die d10 → d8. Party starts at end-of-S2 HP + 1 hit die. Combat encounter: the saboteur, at bay. Designed to cost HP; no garrison healing available unless Maret's token was earned.

**Seeds to plant:**
- Hessa's message carrier is connected to the Reconstitution's intelligence network — but not Saren's command. A different channel, one Saren may not know about. (→ Hint 3 pre-plant: the siege is not unified)
- The coercion lever: someone on the Reconstitution side holds family members of garrison personnel. The saboteur gives one name before dying or surrendering. (→ Hint 3)

**Hints:** Hint 2 pre-plant — Hessa, when pressed about the lower keep during the investigation, says: *"The lower levels are Dene's responsibility. Ask Dene."* Too quickly.

**Token condition:** Hessa gives her token if the party gives her 4 hours to address her message-carrier situation before reporting to the Warden. No roll required; pure character-logic condition. If the party reports immediately, Hessa's token is not available this campaign.

### #0025 — The Lower Keep

**Setting:** Day 19. A night assault nearly breaks the inner gate. In the chaos, a door the party has not seen opens — and a face looks out. A child's face. Not a garrison member's child.

**Hook:** There are refugees in the lower keep. Not a few — forty-seven people, non-human and half-Solamnic, some with Kharolis border-family ties, sheltered here for three years. Quartermaster Dene Flor has been feeding them from the supply ledger. The Warden does not know. This is why the Reconstitution is really here.

**Garrison figure:** Quartermaster Dene Flor — has been managing this deception for three years. Completely loyal to the refugees. Does not yet know if the party can be trusted. His trust fires when the party's first response is human, not tactical.

**Spotlight:** Knight — the Solamnic Oath requires honesty with the command structure. The refugees require protection. The Knight cannot do both. This session names that conflict directly. It does not resolve it.

**Resource note:** Supply Die d8 → d6. The assault costs significant HP; no full rest before session end. First session where the Supply Die result can directly affect outcomes (a d6 roll for remaining healing materials).

**Seeds retrieved:** Maret's double rations (0023). Hessa's deflection about the lower keep (0024).

**Seeds to plant:**
- Dene tells the party: the Reconstitution knows the refugees are here. Saren Coldforge knows. It is one of her stated justifications for the siege — the garrison has been harboring non-Solamnic civilians in a military installation. (→ Hint 3, Route B trigger)
- One refugee adult was at Thorngate Keep twenty years ago, during Saren's time. They know why she left. (→ Hint 3 delivery in 0026)

**Hints:** Hint 2 delivered — the refugees exist. The garrison has been protecting them. The Reconstitution's strategic justification (control of the pass) is at least partially a cover for removing this population.

**Token condition:** Dene gives his token immediately if the party's first response to the opened door is protective, not tactical. Character-logic; no roll. If the party's first action is to report to the Warden, Dene's token is not available this campaign.

### #0026 — The Defector

**Setting:** Day 24. Archer Luca Bent is missing from the pre-dawn watch. He has not deserted. He has gone over the wall to the Reconstitution camp. His older brother serves there. He comes back before dawn.

**Hook:** The party must decide: go after Luca (risking exposure outside the walls), hold and trust he returns, or cover his absence until he does. The Warden cannot know Luca left — desertion carries a severe penalty and Luca is not a deserter.

**Garrison figure:** Archer Luca Bent — nineteen years old. His brother is a Reconstitution archer. He crossed the wall to confirm his brother was alive. He comes back because Thorngate is his post. Both things are true simultaneously. His trust is earned when the party handles his return without making him choose between his brother and his garrison.

**Spotlight:** Wizard (or non-Solamnic PC) — the institutional outsider. They understand, in a way the Solamnic Knight may not, that personal loyalty and institutional loyalty are different obligations. Luca needs this framing from someone who has lived it.

**Resource note:** Supply Die d6 → d4. The party exits the walls for the first time since S1. Reconstitution patrols are more dangerous. The secondary pass combat is designed to cost spell slots specifically.

**Seeds to plant:**
- Luca's brother told him: Saren has not requested final assault authorization from the Reconstitution's high command. She has been stalling. Her command is growing impatient. (→ Hint 4 pre-plant)
- The Reconstitution camp is exhausted. Units rotating out. Some soldiers don't know why they're still here. (→ Hint 4 confirmation)

**Hints:** Hint 3 delivered — the refugee who was at Thorngate twenty years ago tells the party what happened between Saren and Asholt. Not a betrayal. A disagreement: Asholt sheltered non-Solamnic civilians inside a military installation. Saren believed it violated the Oath's letter. She left in protest. She was wrong about the people. She has known for twenty years. She cannot say so. This is why she is here — not to win, but to find a way to acknowledge it.

**Token condition:** Luca gives his token when he returns and the party does not report him. Automatic if the party covered his absence. If reported, no token; Luca is confined and the party has lost a garrison voice for the finale.

### #0027 — The Standard

**Setting:** Day 31. At first light, Saren Coldforge raises her personal standard at the siege line — the Thorngate Watch's own insignia, inverted. A challenge. The Warden goes to the wall and looks at it for a long time without speaking.

**Hook:** The party confronts Asholt: they know about Saren, the refugees, the twenty-year grievance, and the fact that Saren is stalling. His response: *"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her."* He pauses. *"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her. But I will not be the one to ask first."*

**Garrison figure:** Warden Bren Asholt — partial trust only. He gives half his token. He will not give the full token until Saren sits at the table. This session ends with the offer, not the resolution.

**Spotlight:** Ensemble — all four PCs have a moment here. The full picture has landed. This session is built around that weight, not around action.

**Resource note:** Supply Die d4. A major assault at midnight — the hardest combat encounter of the campaign. At session end, the Supply Die is exhausted. No further garrison healing materials exist. The party carries only what they have left.

**Seeds retrieved:** Hint 3 (Saren's history). Hint 4 pre-plant (Saren stalling without authorization).

**Seeds to plant:**
- A Reconstitution deserter comes over the wall at dawn — tells the party Saren has been refusing final assault orders from her own command for two weeks. She is acting alone now. (→ Hint 4 delivery)

**Hints:** Hint 4 delivered via the deserter: the pass is no longer strategically significant. A new supply route was opened three months ago. The Reconstitution's command has been ordering Saren to withdraw. She is here on her own authority. She cannot leave without something to show. She does not know what that something is.

**Token condition:** Asholt gives half his token at session end. Condition: the party does not give him advice, orders, or a plan in this session. They ask him one question and listen to the full answer. The garrison's trust-language is witness, not direction.

### #0028 — The Parley

**Setting:** Day 36. The Supply Die is gone. The Reconstitution's command has formally recalled Saren. She has 48 hours before she is in open defiance of her own order. The party holds Asholt's half-token, the garrison's accumulated trust, and the full truth.

**Hook:** The party must bring Saren to the table — not to surrender, not to be defeated, but to be witnessed. She needs to be able to name what she came here to say without losing everything. Asholt needs to say what he should have said twenty years ago. The party is the only condition under which both of those things can happen.

**Garrison figure:** Warden Bren Asholt — full token, given across the table when Saren sits down.

**Resource note:** Party at minimum resources. Supply Die exhausted. Combat is available (Reconstitution hard-liners may try to prevent the parley) but resource exhaustion is already the session's shape — the party fights with what remains. No heroic reserves. This is what was left.

**Seeds retrieved:** All four hints. All tokens earned on Route E.

**PC-authored finale deliverable:** One PC speaks to Saren directly — naming what both sides have spent, what the refugees need, what Saren was right about and what she was wrong about. Not a negotiation; an accounting. The session-runner logs it verbatim.

**Finale branches:** See below.

## Finale branches

### Route A — The Table
**Condition:** 3+ tokens. Party brings Saren to formal negotiation.
**What happens:** Terms agreed: the Reconstitution withdraws; Thorngate opens its garrison records for independent review (exposing the refugee shelter in the process); the refugees receive formal recognition under Rose Council authority.
**What is lost:** The personal resolution between Saren and Asholt does not happen — formal terms substitute for it. The party receives a professional acknowledgment. No full token from Asholt.
**What is gained:** The siege ends. The garrison survives. It works.

### Route B — The Revelation
**Condition:** 4+ tokens, including Dene's. Party surfaces the refugees to both Saren and Asholt simultaneously as the true center of the conflict.
**What happens:** Saren sees the refugees. Asholt names them to her directly — for the first time. The thing she left over is resolved: she was right about the Oath's letter; he was right about its spirit. Saren withdraws without formal terms. The Reconstitution loses its justification.
**What is lost:** Some garrison members did not consent to the refugees being revealed. The terms are informal; Saren's personal authority is now strained with her own command.
**What is gained:** The actual grievance was addressed. Saren and Asholt have spoken. The refugees are no longer secret.

### Route E — The Accounting *(hardest / most complete)*
**Condition:** 6 tokens + Asholt's full token (7 total).
**What happens:** One PC delivers the accounting — naming what both sides have spent, what Saren was right about, what Asholt was right about, what the refugees cost everyone, and what it would mean to stop. Saren listens. Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table — the garrison's trust, offered to the woman who left it. She takes it. She withdraws with her command intact. The pass holds. The refugees are placed under Rose Council protection.
**What is lost:** The party receives nothing. The garrison acknowledges them in the garrison's language only — by treating them as garrison members for the duration. The Rose Council expects a tactical report. The actual resolution is not in the report.
**What is gained:** The central question answered in full: *this much. Exactly this much. Every token of it.*
**PC-authored deliverable speaks:** The accounting speech is logged verbatim by session-runner.

## Resource exhaustion mechanic

**Between-session carry-over:** Party starts each session at [end-of-last-session HP + 1 hit die of recovery]. No full long rest until the siege breaks (at finale end) or the party abandons the walls (which removes Asholt's token permanently and locks out Route E).

**Supply Die:** Begins at d12. Degrades by one die size each session in which a combat encounter occurs.

| Die | Garrison healing available |
|---|---|
| d12/d10 | Full healing kit; 1d4+1 potions per session |
| d8/d6 | Reduced kit; 1 potion per session |
| d4 | Emergency supplies only; DM's one-item choice |
| Exhausted | No garrison healing materials. Party is on its own. |

The Supply Die being exhausted at the start of S7 (the parley) is the design target — the finale is fought with what remains. Every slot spent in S2 is visible at S7.

**Rubric note (Mechanical Fairness):** The v1.4 cross-character mechanical compound anchor should fire reliably under resource pressure — Cleric Bless stacking on a contested roll, shared healing decisions framed in-voice as character choices. Resource exhaustion makes mechanical compounds visible because every resource expenditure is felt.

## PC-authored finale deliverable

**PC:** The Knight (primary) or Wizard (secondary) — whichever PC's arc most directly concerns the cost of unreciprocated sacrifice.
**Deliverable:** The accounting speech at the parley table (0028). Not a negotiation; a naming of what was spent and by whom.
**Build across sessions:**
- 0025: The Knight names the Oath collision for the first time — what loyalty costs when it points two ways.
- 0026: The Wizard names the institutional/personal split — what it means to serve something that doesn't serve you back.
- 0027: The ensemble session gives the full shape of what Saren is carrying. The PC begins composing — noted in handoff as in-progress.
- 0028: One PC synthesizes. Logged verbatim by session-runner.

**Track in handoff:** *"[PC] is composing the accounting. Current shape: [whatever has accumulated]."* Note this from 0027 onward.

### What held

**The resource-depletion mechanic worked.** The Supply Die degrading from d12 to exhaustion across 7 sessions created genuine scarcity. Sava managed slots across the full campaign as a unified resource, not session-by-session. Caelith's Portent [6] carried for 4 sessions and was spent at exactly the right moment (cross-character foreknowledge to Davan, S4). The Supply Die exhausting at S5 changed how S6 and S7 played: no safety net, no recovery, every decision mattered.

**The token system sustained itself.** Seven distinct token conditions across seven garrison figures, each grounded in a specific character act. No two conditions were the same type: hold the wall without orders; conserve resources; give time to self-correct; first response human not tactical; don't report the departure; ask one question and listen; bring her to the table. The conditions were escalating from behavioral-pattern to character-decision to relational act. Route E required all seven.

**The accounting speech accumulated correctly.** Four layers across four sessions, each from a different character-register moment: S4 (gap in the Oath); S5 (institutional question as architecture); S6 (cost of correctness); S7 (Orel named, both sides' expenditures named). The speech at the parley table was not scripted; it emerged from the structural invitation built across six sessions.

**Simultaneous arc-convergence fired at the finale.** Davan's speech created the table. Asholt and Saren's arc-completions fired from the same moment. The pattern (v1.6) holds across three campaigns: a precise naming act in the presence of the right witnesses triggers all outstanding arc-completions simultaneously.

**The cliff-gap seed paid off three sessions later.** Talis's passive 16 detection in S1, used in S5, is a clean example of the three-session payoff that the workshop is designed to produce.

**Caelith's grey-blue annotations became a player-style.** Six sessions of documentary annotation converged in one sentence and ended with a failed Insight. The documentary-witness pattern is promoted to a player-style.

### What to carry forward to Campaign 5

- **v1.10 is the production rubric.** v1.11 amendment (Portent-as-directed-foreknowledge) pending; apply if a second campaign instance validates.
- **Documentary-witness player-style:** proposed from C4. Should be formally written if a C5 party includes an analytical/documentation-oriented PC.
- **Grief-paragraph-as-deployable-resource:** I-C4-S7-02 is a new sub-type not yet in the rubric. If a C5 PC deliberately deploys their grief-paragraph rather than being triggered, propose a v1.11 anchor.
- **The resource-depletion mechanic is production-validated.** Supply Die + no-long-rest creates genuine scarcity without punishing the party fatally. The mechanic is reusable for any siege or sustained-pressure campaign.
- **Token systems as trust-accumulation:** seven discrete trust-conditions across seven garrison figures is the right scale for a 7-adventure campaign. Conditions should escalate from behavioral to relational. Route E should require the hardest condition (bring the antagonist to the table).
- **Saren's arc:** she knew the day she left. She spent twenty years knowing. The resolution required a table she did not have to be the one to ask for. The party made the table. That is what the campaign was about.

### The accounting speech (verbatim, campaign-permanent)

*"We are here because the Rose Council sent us to reinforce. That is the order. I am here because I was assigned and I followed my orders. That is the truth. But I have been carrying something else for the past thirty-six days that I think you need to hear.*

*The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. I have followed the Oath for twelve years. I believed it would be clear, every time — that the Measure would point to the right thing and I would do it. It does not always tell you. It told you about the letter. It told Warden Asholt about the people. You were both right. You were both following the Oath. You were following different parts of it.*

*I know what it costs to be right about the letter. Five years ago, I testified at a tribunal. My older brother — his name is Orel, he was in the Order of the Sword, he was a good knight — had sheltered a deserter for two days before turning him over. He believed the deserter had a reason. The tribunal disagreed. He asked me to testify honestly. I did. My honest testimony was what closed the case against him. He was cashiered. He left Solamnia. I have not heard from him since.*

*I was right about the letter. The tribunal's finding was correct. I gave the truth and the truth had the consequence it was supposed to have. And I have been counting the cost of that truth for five years.*

*You spent twenty years knowing you were wrong about the people and not knowing how to come back. Warden Asholt spent twenty years knowing that, and not knowing how to tell you. The garrison has spent thirty-six days on this wall. The forty-seven people in the lower keep have spent three years in that basement because one woman falsified ration entries to keep them alive.*

*I am not here to tell you the siege was wrong. I am not here to tell you the Oath is wrong. I am here because I have spent six weeks watching what happens to people when the Oath is used as a letter, and I cannot give you a verdict. What I can give you is this:*

*The Warden says he will speak to you. He has been waiting for a table to sit across. That is what I came to tell you."*

— Davan Fostermark, Knight of the Sword (Seconded), Day 36, Thorngate Keep parley ground
