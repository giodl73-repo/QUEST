## RV1 — Sheet-Deep-Reader Without Grief Hooks: CONFIRMED

Sheet-deep-reader fired from professional code across all 7 sessions, 4 PCs, ~40 instances. Zero grief-triggered instances. The hypothesis — that player styles designed around professional codes would fire without grief hooks — was validated definitively.

Key evidence: Tessaly's ledger notation fired as documentary-witness every session before speaking, from professional code, never from emotional response. Cael's contract-touch fired at every commitment point. The S4 primary test (Tessaly showing Gorren her own ledger) produced sheet-deep-reader via professional accountability, not grief over the dead member she barely knew. No instance required grief as an alternative explanation.

**Implication for GLYPH paper B2:** Professional code is sufficient to trigger player styles. Grief is not a necessary condition.

---

## RV2 — Morally-Grey NPC Arc-Completion by Character Logic: CONFIRMED

Two instances: Aldras Bent (S2) and Pell Dorvast (S5).

Aldras: professional debt paid in truth on the street, without being asked, after extraction. No grief connection to the party. No persuasion roll.

Pell: voluntary admission note after completing his test report to Pale's subsidiary. A professional courtesy extended because the refusal was clean. Not a defection. Not coerced.

**Implication:** Morally-grey NPC arc-completion is achievable from character logic alone. Grief connection and persuasion are both unnecessary.

---

## RV3 — PC-Authored Deliverable as Action: CONFIRMED

Mira built the false dossier framework across six sessions from professional necessity, without disclosure. Six components accumulated from S3-S6. Announced in S7: "I have it." Produced and logged verbatim. Quality determined by real dice (partial success — passes passive 14, flaw at DC 16). Route B selected partly because dossier quality required it.

**Implication:** PC-authored deliverables are most effective when accumulated from professional necessity across sessions. Single-scene production cannot replicate the authenticity.

---

## RV4 — Act-Without-Announcement Professionally Triggered: CONFIRMED

Three primary instances: Mira's six-session framework (cross-session form), Cael's code-stop S5 (intra-session form), Cael's Route B decision S7 (intra-session form). All professionally-triggered, no grief. S5 produced the first confirmed simultaneous v1.5+v1.12 compound in the workshop.

**Implication:** Act-without-announcement is not grief-dependent. Professional code is sufficient.

## What Held

- Loyalty-object rituals established party identity from session 1. Darro's picks and Tessaly's ledger fired every session, professionally-triggered every time.
- All five NPC arc-completions fired from character logic without persuasion: Bessa, Aldras, Nenne, Gorren, Pell.
- The false dossier was genuinely PC-authored across six sessions. The deliverable was earned.
- Dice drove the route (partial-success forgery made Route B necessary).
- Zero grief-triggered instances. Most complete RV1 validation in workshop history.

---

## What Didn't Work

- Cael's Battle Master kit never deployed in combat. The campaign had no combat encounters. Future campaigns with martial PCs should include at least one scene requiring the combat kit under pressure.
- Mira's post-job persona-calibration behavior seeded but did not fully land as visible consequence. Subtle rather than observable. Development opportunity for a future campaign.

## Closing Image

The campaign ended with Darro performing his pick maintenance the morning after the final job. He did not know this was the end. He did it because this is what he does every morning. The picks were fine.

The campaign's central question was answered by the party's behavior over seven sessions: they worked for the professional code. When the guild became the problem, the code resolved it. The answer was never stated. It was recorded.
