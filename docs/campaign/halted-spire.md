# The Halted Spire — Campaign 3

## Central question

*Who gets to decide who is worth saving — and who gets to decide what counts as faith?*

## Setting anchor

**Town: Mireth-on-the-Hill** — a mid-sized post-War of the Lance town in Abanasinia, in a period of spiritual reconstruction. The cathedral has dominated the hill above the town for eleven years: half-built, scaffolded, unmistakably unfinished. The town's identity is bound to finishing it.

**Patron: Magistra Doran of Mireth** — the town's senior civil authority. She hired the party because no one else will come. She wants Route A: clear the site, finish the cathedral, restore the town's story. Discovering the truth will challenge her personally. Her arc-completion: she commissions the second cathedral herself, in writing, having accepted what the first one is.

## Adventure briefs

### #0015 — The Gatehouse Watch

**Setting:** The cathedral's outer gatehouse; newly arrived gully dwarf congregation members, confused and not yet integrated into the congregation's rites.
**Hook:** The party enters the cathedral grounds for the first time. They expected an empty building. They find gully dwarves.
**Artifact:** Velantha's field satchel — warm to the touch; one compartment holds herbs that should have dried thirty years ago.
**Spotlight:** Fighter — their practical stake is immediately complicated. These are not monsters attacking. The Fighter's "crack the site will find" is activated here: what they lost to something they couldn't fix meets a community that was fixed.
**Seeds retrieved from earlier adventures:** None (campaign opens here).
**Seeds to plant:**
- The congregation has an elder who knows everything — he lives deeper in the building (→ 0018).
- The field satchel herbs are impossibly fresh — something kept them (→ 0018/0019).
- Someone organized this congregation — the gatehouse group refers to "the ones inside" with reverence (→ 0016/0017).
**Hints:** Hint 1 delivered — "she healed gully dwarves." The party's first contact with the congregation makes this undeniable.
**Route notes:** All three routes open. Route D begins here if the party does not treat the gully dwarves as enemies.
**Open questions:** Fighter's specific grief — to be determined by party-builder; must connect to something lost that couldn't be fixed by conventional means.

### #0016 — The Nave Congregation

**Setting:** The cathedral nave; the largest congregation group; organized; have been here longest; hold the oral traditions.
**Hook:** The party must navigate the largest and most organized group of the congregation to learn anything about Velantha. The nave congregation are not hostile but they are protective of their rites.
**Artifact:** Velantha's measuring cup — cracked, sealed with tallow, always holds exactly the right dose for whoever is holding it.
**Spotlight:** Monk — the congregation's daily prayer practice mirrors and complicates the Monk's own. *craft-witness test fires here for the first time:* the Monk's breath-work ritual at the start of session finds its structural element in the congregation's morning rite. The question the session surfaces: is the Monk's practice different in kind from what the congregation does, or only in object?
**Seeds retrieved:** "Someone organized this congregation" (0015) — the nave congregation confirms: there is an elder. There is also an Orders' man. Both are further inside.
**Seeds to plant:**
- The Orders' man is among the congregation — the nave group speak of him obliquely, with complicated respect (→ 0019).
- Not all of the congregation agrees — there are voices of resentment; the nave congregation knows about "the ones in the tower" and their tone shifts (→ 0017).
- The congregation's oral tradition names a specific night thirty years ago — they don't know the details but the elder does (→ 0018).
**Hints:** No new hint. Hint 1 deepens — the congregation's faith is visible, daily, organized.
**Route notes:** Route D requires the party to have not antagonized the nave congregation. If the party is violent here, the faction-gated stairway to the Sacristy closes permanently.
**Open questions:** What is the congregation's morning rite? Dungeon-smith to design; must be simple, repeated, physically readable by the Monk.

### #0017 — The Bell Tower

**Setting:** The bell tower; the aggrieved ones — gully dwarves Velantha healed whose lives after healing remained hard. Resentment has curdled. Some are dangerous.
**Hook:** The party learns the congregation is not unified. These members kept faith for thirty years but they are angry at what faith cost them. They are the campaign's primary combat adventure.
**Artifact:** Velantha's promise-cord — knotted tracking string; some knots cut, some open. An open knot tied around a kept promise tightens on its own.
**Spotlight:** Warlock primary; Fighter bilateral arc-completion fires this session.
**Bilateral arc-completion (design mandate):** The Warlock's arc and the Fighter's arc are designed to complete in response to each other in this session. The specific naming conditions must be written by party-builder (into the PC sheets) before play begins. Structural requirement: the bell tower group's resentment — "she saved us but she could not save us from everything" — must surface the specific thing both the Warlock and Fighter have been carrying. The Warlock names first; the Fighter responds. Or reverse. The exact order is the DM's to find; the content is in the PC sheets.
**Seeds retrieved:** "Not all of the congregation agrees" (0016) — confirmed and dangerous. "What she promised some of them" — the bell tower group know Velantha made specific commitments she could not keep.
**Seeds to plant:**
- What Velantha promised she couldn't deliver (→ 0020 — she addresses this in the Crypt).
- The ritual focus is among the aggrieved group's sacred objects — they have it but don't know what it is (→ 0018).
- Route D begins closing if the party is violent here without attempt at understanding — the nav congregation will know by morning.
**Hints:** Hint 2 delivered — through the aggrieved group's anger, the party learns the healing was not divine. "She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal." Recovery path: the ritual focus (0018) confirms this mechanically if the session's dialogue doesn't.
**Route notes:** The most dangerous adventure. Route C requires surviving without destroying the bell tower group. Route D requires the party to have understood — not necessarily forgiven — their resentment.

### #0018 — The Transept Witness

**Setting:** The transept; partially finished by the congregation — coloured glass in the window frames; the light through them is wrong. The congregation's elder is here: the one gully dwarf who was present and lucid during the ritual itself.
**Hook:** The elder has the full account. He has been waiting thirty years for someone who would actually listen to it.
**Artifact:** Velantha's ritual focus — faintly warm; hums near High Sorcery workings; the Orders would recognise it immediately.
**Spotlight:** Bard — *verbal documentation-arc activates here.* The elder's account is the most complete version of Velantha's story anyone will ever speak aloud. The Bard hears it. The test: can they hold it? What does it cost to carry a story this size in your head rather than writing it down? What changes in the telling?
**Seeds retrieved:** "The congregation has an elder" (0015). "The congregation's oral tradition names a specific night" (0016). "The ritual focus is among the aggrieved group's objects" (0017) — the party may have recovered it; the elder can identify it.
**Seeds to plant:**
- The ritual focus identifies the specific Orders chapter — "the mark on the base is Aldric's. He was stationed at Mireth thirty years ago. He is still here." (→ 0019).
- The full account names the dispensation letter — "she had a paper. Forged. He wrote it for her. She burned it before she began the rite. Or tried to." (→ 0019).
**Hints:** No new hint. Hint 2 confirmed by the elder's account with specifics. Hint 3 pre-planted: "There is a man in the sacristy. He has been copying my account for twenty years. He was the one who gave her the working."
**NPC arc-completion (elder):** The elder's arc-completion fires when the party listens to the full account without interrupting. He has told it to the congregation many times. He has never told it to someone who would take it outside these walls. His arc completes when he says: "She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do." Write this line into his npc-architect file as the specific arc-completion condition and the specific line.
**Open questions:** The elder's name — workshop-canon to decide; Dragonlance gully dwarf naming conventions should inform this.

### #0019 — The Sacristy

**Setting:** The sacristy; smells of old ink and cold stone. Aldric Voss — Red Robe wizard, thirty years dead and returned — has been here since. The walls are covered with his handwritten copies of the congregation's oral accounts.
**Hook:** The party meets the Orders' man. He knows what the party will ask. He has been preparing his answer for thirty years.
**Artifact:** Velantha's dispensation letter — the forged document Aldric wrote authorizing her access to the ritual. He has tried to burn it for thirty years. It will not burn. It will not dissolve. It cannot be destroyed.
**Spotlight:** Monk — the Orders' complicity forces the campaign's institutional-vs-personal faith question into direct confrontation. The Monk's second spotlight: their practice has been holding against the site's faith-destabilization since 0016. Here, the Orders' man asks the Monk directly: "What do you practice, and who gave you permission?" The Monk's craft-witness test deepens — does their practice require institutional sanction to be valid?
**Seeds retrieved:** "The Orders' man is among the congregation" (0016). "Aldric identified by the ritual focus" (0018). "The dispensation letter exists but was supposed to be burned" (0018).
**Seeds to plant:**
- The dispensation letter cannot be destroyed → 0021. It will exist at the finale. It is evidence. What the party does with it is part of the choice.
- Aldric's thirty years of written copies → 0020. He gives them a copy. Velantha's own account in the Crypt will confirm or contradict it.
- "She knew the Orders would eventually come looking. She left something in the crypt." (→ 0020).
**Hints:** Hint 3 delivered — "I gave her the ritual. I knew the prohibition. I gave it to her anyway because she was right about the gully dwarves and I was a coward about the Orders." His specific regret: not that he gave it, but that he never told the Orders and let the false miracle narrative take root unchallenged.
**NPC arc-completion (Aldric Voss):** Aldric's arc-completion fires when the party asks him what he regrets. He says: "I regret that I let them call it a miracle. She would have hated that. She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine." His arc does not complete if the party only asks about the ritual — they must ask about the story, not the act.
**Route notes:** If the party antagonized the nave congregation, the faction-gated stairway was barred and they must have found the alternative route. Aldric's tone toward a violent party is closed rather than confessional — he will confirm facts but will not give the full arc-completion line.

### #0020 — The Crypt

**Setting:** The Crypt — the only finished room in the cathedral. The congregation completed it over thirty years: clean, lit, correctly laid out as a Mishakal crypt. Velantha is here.
**Hook:** The party has the full factual account. Now they meet Velantha. She has her own account — not of what happened, but of what she intended, what she couldn't deliver, and what she hopes.
**Artifact:** Velantha's healer's journal — final volume. The last entry rewrites itself when read by someone who has faced the same choice Velantha faced (not any choice; this specific one: knowing the cost, acting anyway, without permission, for the sake of someone the world has decided is not worth it).
**Spotlight:** Bard — *verbal documentation-arc critical session.* The thing the Bard cannot document: Velantha speaks to them directly. What she says is true but it changes every time it is repeated. The Bard will try to hold it exactly. They will find they can't. This is the session that names what the verbal documentation-arc costs. What cannot be kept in the voice exactly as it was given.
**PC-authored finale deliverable:** This session is the structural invitation. Velantha does not tell the Bard what to say at the finale. She gives them the journal. She says: "Someone should speak for what I actually did. Not what they've built up there. What I did." The Bard begins composing. They do not speak it yet. It is noted in the handoff as in-progress.
**Seeds retrieved:** "What Velantha promised she couldn't deliver" (0017) — she addresses it directly here. "She left something in the crypt" (0019) — the journal. "Aldric's copies" (0019) — she confirms what he got right and what he softened.
**Seeds to plant:**
- "The journal's last entry rewrites for the right reader" → 0021. The party carries it. At the keystone, someone may read it again. It will rewrite once more.
- "Velantha's answer to what she couldn't deliver" → 0021. Her answer is not an apology. It is "I knew I couldn't. I did it anyway. That is what healing means."
- The Bard is composing (→ 0021 finale deliverable, noted in handoff).
**Hints:** Hint 4 delivered — auto-delivery from the journal if passive 15 is met by any PC reader. "I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else."
**NPC arc-completion (Velantha):** Velantha's arc-completion fires when the party asks her what she wants — not what happened, not why she did it, but what she wants *now*. She says: "I want the gully dwarves to have somewhere that is theirs. They kept faith for thirty years in a building they didn't build. They deserve the building." Her arc is complete. She does not ask the party to agree. She asks them to choose.

### #0021 — The Keystone

**Setting:** The unfinished keystone arch — the cathedral cannot be consecrated without it. The stone is cut. It has been waiting thirty years. All factions are present: the congregation, Aldric Voss, Velantha (if the party invites her), Magistra Doran and the town's representatives, the party.
**Hook:** The party has the full truth. The factions are assembled. The choice must be made.
**Artifact:** The keystone — unhewn face, blank. Route D: the congregation chisel Velantha's name into it before it is set.
**Spotlight:** Ensemble — every PC has a moment.
**PC-authored finale deliverable:** The Bard speaks Velantha's true story aloud — in full, to all assembled factions — before the choice is made. This is what they have been building since 0018. They have the elder's account, Aldric's copies, Velantha's own words, and thirty years of congregation faith. They speak it. What they say is their own synthesis. The session-runner notes the first time the Bard speaks it, verbatim.
**Seeds retrieved:** All campaign seeds. Full hint delivery confirmed. "The dispensation letter cannot be destroyed" (0019) — it is present; what happens to it is part of the choice. "The journal rewrites for the right reader" (0020) — if read again here, it changes once more.
**Finale branches:** See "Finale branches" section below.
**Open questions:** Magistra Doran's arc-completion — what does she say when she commissions the second cathedral? This is designed by npc-architect before 0021 runs.

## Finale branches

### Route A — Expose
**Condition:** Party tells the congregation the truth: Velantha used forbidden magic, not divine grace. Her faith was authorized sorcery, not Mishakal's grace. The congregation's faith shatters — it was built on a miracle that wasn't miraculous.
**What happens:** The congregation leave. The cathedral is cleared. The keystone is set with no inscription. The town finishes the build. The dispensation letter is presented to the Orders; Aldric is formally named as the source; his case is closed thirty years late.
**What is lost:** The congregation's thirty years of faith is delegitimized. The truth was used as a clearance tool.
**Narrator note:** Route A is available. It is not the optimal route. The DM does not signal this; the PCs feel it.

### Route C — Share
**Condition:** Party brokers a formal coexistence agreement between the town and the congregation. Two services. Shared space. Written terms.
**What happens:** The keystone is set with no inscription. The cathedral is consecrated — ambiguously, under two different rites simultaneously. Neither community is fully satisfied. Magistra Doran accepts because she has no better option. The congregation accepts because it is better than nothing.
**What is lost:** The question is never answered. The tension is permanent. The cathedral means different things to different people who use it at the same time.
**What is gained:** Neither community is erased. The truth is on record. Nobody was weaponized.

### Route D — Give
**Condition:** Party persuades Magistra Doran to commission a second cathedral on clean ground and formally cede this one to the congregation. The congregation chisel Velantha's name into the keystone face before it is set.
**What happens:** The Bard speaks Velantha's true story at the setting of the keystone. The congregation hear their own history named correctly for the first time by someone outside it. Magistra Doran signs the commission for the second cathedral in the same moment. The keystone is set. Velantha's name is in the arch.
**What is gained:** The cathedral becomes what it actually is. The new build starts with honest foundations. Magistra Doran's arc-completion fires.
**What is lost:** The town does not get the cathedral they built their story around. They get a different one — new, clean, correctly founded. Some of them will never forgive this. The party should feel the cost.
**PC-authored deliverable speaks:** The Bard's account is spoken here. The session-runner logs it verbatim.

## PC-authored finale deliverable

**PC:** The Bard.
**Deliverable:** The Bard speaks Velantha's true story aloud to all assembled factions at the keystone setting (0021). Not a transcription — a living account built from the elder's oral history (0018), Aldric's written copies (0019), and Velantha's own words (0020).
**Build across sessions:**
- 0018: Bard receives the elder's full account. Verbal documentation-arc activates. The Bard begins holding the story.
- 0019: Bard receives Aldric's written copies. First written version of the story. They can check it against the oral account. They find differences.
- 0020: Velantha says "someone should speak for what I actually did." The Bard begins composing their version. Noted in handoff as in-progress.
- 0021: The Bard speaks. Logged verbatim by session-runner.
**What to track in handoff:** "The Bard is composing Velantha's true account" — note this each session from 0018 onward, with any lines the Bard has assembled so far.
