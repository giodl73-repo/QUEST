## Setting & Background

Resolving the full situation — the network, the package, the debt, the documentation — requires something real from each PC, and they do it without being asked.

**Stonehaven, Mile 96** (workshop-canon, south Abanasinia). Stonehaven is a substantial mining settlement and regional market. The Overseer — the authority figure at the top of the network Cassel is investigating, who also authorized the fraudulent mill contract — is here. Vess's delivery address is here. Sera's person of interest (logged in Maret's ledger as "Torvens Aldric" — but it is actually a different person, **Aldric Halven**, a scholar who knew Sera's missing person) is here. All four destinations converge in Stonehaven.

The four travelers have arrived. Each of them has something to do here. The session is what each one does — and what they do for each other.

1. **Arrival** — Stonehaven. Each PC has their destination. For the first time since the roadblock, they will separate. Except they don't, immediately.
2. **Vess's delivery** — the package. The address is a merchant house connected to Doss's network. Vess must deliver it, or refuse. If she delivers it, she is paid. If she refuses, she forfeits the contract. The document from Torven, if she has it, changes what she knows about what she's delivering. Her decision is her own.
3. **Brek's completion** — Brek has the money (if he took it in S5). He does not need to be in Stonehaven anymore. His reason for being on this road is resolved. He is still here.
4. **Sera's person** — Aldric Halven, the scholar. He is in Stonehaven and he has information about the person Sera has been looking for. The conversation happens. She finds what she needs — and it is harder than finding the documentation.
5. **Tam's wayshrine** — the Gilean wayshrine at the Abanasinian crossroads is in Stonehaven's market district. He reaches it before the equinox, barely. The rite is performed. It is a small thing, done correctly.

- Brek stays in Stonehaven without a reason. He does not explain why.
- Tam delays his wayshrine to accompany Vess to the merchant house. He does not say it is to protect her.
- Vess tells Sera where the scholar is before being asked. She saw him at the market and put it together.
- Sera shares what the scholar told her — she does not have to, and she does not know if it is useful to anyone else. She tells Brek.

S6 ensemble. Peak Reciprocal Acts session. Do PCs act for each other without announcement? Do the acts come from five sessions of operational familiarity, or from something more? RV10 bilateral attempt: does anyone name what the group has become?

## NPCs

# Aldric Halven

**Role:** Scholar. The person Sera has been looking for on this road — specifically, the person who knew the person Sera was actually looking for (Torvens Mikela).

## Appearance

Sixties, white-haired, with the organized clutter of a working researcher. His rented room above the tanner's shop is covered in documents. He smells like tanner's oil that he has stopped noticing.

## Emotional position

Aldric has been in Stonehaven for six weeks trying to establish what happened to Torvens Mikela. He has not succeeded. He has found the documentation trail she was following — the same one Cassel is following from the other end — but Mikela herself disappeared three months ago. He believes she was taken into custody by the Stonehaven authority under Overseer Crel. He believes she is alive and in holding. He has no evidence for either belief.

He is still here because leaving feels like abandoning a colleague he was not close enough to that leaving would be obvious. He does not know exactly what his obligation to her is. He is trying to figure it out by staying.

## Arc-completion

He does not have a designed arc-completion. He is a delivery NPC for Sera's H2 seed content. His emotional position is relevant only insofar as it mirrors Sera's situation — she has been looking for someone the same way Aldric has been staying for someone.

If Sera notices this parallel: log as RV9 situational resonance instance (Sera reads Aldric's situation through her own parallel without announcement).

## What he tells Sera

*"Mikela was following the mill contracts. She found the fraudulent deeds going back four years. She was going to the circuit magistrate at Millhaven with documentation. She left here three months ago. She hasn't arrived at Millhaven — I checked. The most likely explanation is that she was stopped somewhere between here and Millhaven, at the same toll operation you've been navigating."*

He does not know which toll station. He does not know who specifically. He gives Sera his copies of the documentation trail.

## What he asks

*"If you're going to Millhaven — will you find out if she reached it?"* He does not ask her to rescue Mikela. He asks her to find out. He has been here six weeks without finding out; he thinks someone moving south might do it.

## Voice

Precise, quiet. He does not dramatize. He reports what he knows and flags clearly what he does not know.

### Merchant House Confrontation

# Merchant House Confrontation

## Setup

2 guards (Soldier stat blocks) at door. If Vess attempts redirecting the package to investigation: the house's merchant representative may escalate.

## CR / XP Budget (if combat)

- 2 × Guard (CR 1/8, 25 XP each) = 50 XP. Trivial for L3 party.
- If merchant representative involved: add 1 × Spy (CR 1, 200 XP). Total adjusted: ~200 XP. Easy encounter.
- **Assessment:** Combat is not the point. If it occurs, it is brief and the party has a clear advantage.

## Preferred resolution

Vess presents her decision (deliver / refuse / redirect) to the clerk. The clerk accepts delivery or notes the refusal. If Vess attempts to redirect (get the package to Cassel's investigation), she needs to communicate with the circuit magistrate network — Tam has access via Gilean courier system. Persuasion DC 10 to arrange the redirect; Tam provides a Gilean seal.

## Research coding

Brek's positioning (left of Vess, interposes) at the door: [Brek → Vess → interposes without announcement]. Trigger type: relationship-driven.
Tam's accompaniment: [Tam → Vess → accompanied without being asked]. Trigger type: relationship-driven.

## Downstream effects of Vess's choice

- **Deliver:** 50 gp paid. Network intact. Clean end to contract. Package is drugs connected to the mining settlement's labor problem.
- **Refuse:** 0 gp. Contract forfeited. Package returned. Network loses one shipment. Vess's courier reputation takes a formal "refused delivery" note.
- **Redirect:** 0 gp. Package becomes evidence in Cassel's investigation (combined with Torven's documents — devastating to the network). Vess's license is formally flagged but the circuit magistrate can clear it given the circumstances.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
