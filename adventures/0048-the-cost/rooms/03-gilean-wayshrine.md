# Room 03 — Gilean Wayshrine

## Read-aloud

> The wayshrine is in the market district's quiet corner — a small stone structure, older than the town around it, with the open-book symbol of Gilean worn almost smooth on the lintel. The autumn equinox is tomorrow. Tam arrived in time.

## DM notes

- **The rite:** Twenty minutes. Tam performs it correctly. No roll required — he has done this circuit for five years. The rite is the acknowledgment that the shrine is still here, still maintained, still visited. That is all Gilean requires.
- **What the rite means for Tam:** He was sent on this specific road by his superior. He still does not know why. The rite is done. The mission (whatever it was) may be complete — or it may not be something the wayshrine can reveal.
- **After the rite:** Tam leaves the shrine and returns to the market. The others are either still dispersed or regrouping. He finds them by going where the weight is — wherever the hardest scene is happening, Tam gravitates toward it without being called.
  - If Sera is at Room 02 with bad news: Tam arrives at the tanner's shop just as Sera is coming down. He reads her expression. He does not ask what happened. He says: *"There's an inn. Good fire. Come on."*
  - Log: [Tam → Sera → presence and direction without asking]. Trigger type: relationship-driven.
- **The wayshrine beat as research data:** Tam's rite is a private moment. He does not perform it for the group. He does not report completing it. He completes it and returns. This is the intra-session restraint instance: he received what he came for (the rite is done) and expresses it through return to the group, not announcement.
