## Scene 0 — Arrival

Stonehaven is larger than expected. Walled market town, mining settlement up the eastern hill. The entry gate is busy. The four arrive together — naturally; they've been traveling together for seven sessions. There is no ceremony at the gate.

Each of them has somewhere to go.

**Vess — first Reciprocal Act of S6:**

At the market gate, before the group splits, she says to Sera: *"The man you're looking for — scholar, traveling with a research pack? Room above the tanner, north quarter. I saw him when we came in."*

She does not explain how she knows Sera is looking for someone. She noted Sera looking at the ledger repeatedly. She noted the quality of attention. She noted the scholar at the gate when they arrived and cross-referenced.

*Research coding: [Vess → Sera → scholar's location, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven (she observed Sera's attention over multiple days; she connected the observation to what she could do for it). Session 6. S6 first Reciprocal Act.*

**Sera:** She stops. She has been looking for this person for six days. The information arrived without her asking.

## Scene 1 — Merchant House (Room 01)

Vess goes to the merchant house. Brek comes. He does not explain why. He is already moving toward the east quarter when Vess turns that direction.

*Research coding: [Brek → Vess → accompanied without being asked]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 6.*

Tam sees them moving and delays his wayshrine. He joins.

*Research coding: [Tam → Vess → accompanied without being asked]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 6.*

At the door: the house name. **Havel-Aldric Trading.**

**Vess:** Her passive Perception 16 reads it instantly. The merchant from the roadblock. Havel. He scouted them on Day 1. He knew what was in her pack because he was there when she arrived at the blockage. He selected her.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-driven -->
*"He was at the roadblock."*

*Research coding: SURPRISE. The Session 1 merchant who gave them road information was the network's courier-scout. He was there specifically. Havel knew Vess was the carrier. He waited at the roadblock to confirm the package was in transit.*

**Brek:** He looks at the door. He looks at Vess. He steps slightly left of her — the interpositioning, automatic.

*Research coding: [Brek → Vess → interposed at door, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 6.*

**Vess's decision:**

She has Torven's documents (Brek gave them to the group after the ford; they are in Sera's pack now). She has Cassel's business card (Tam kept it, being Tam). She has the package. She has a choice.

She does not deliver the package. She has already decided this — she decided it at the ford when she looked at the river and said *"I don't open the packages."* She said it to remind herself she had the choice. She does not deliver this one.

She uses Tam's Gilean seal to send the package to the circuit magistrate route toward Cassel's investigation, combined with Torven's documents. The redirect takes twenty minutes and a 15-gp filing fee. Tam covers 8 gp of it without being asked.

*Research coding: [Tam → group → covered filing fee, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 6.*

The clerk inside the merchant house: he looks at the redirect documents and does not argue. He does not want the scrutiny that comes with arguing.

**Vess forfeits her delivery fee (50 gp).** The contract is void. She is 50 gp short and her license has a "refused delivery" notation. She absorbs this.

## Scene 2 — Scholar's Lodgings (Room 02)

Sera goes to the scholar's lodgings. Brek comes — he left the merchant house satisfied that Vess's situation was resolved, and he tracked toward wherever the next scene was.

*Research coding: [Brek → Sera → followed to scholar's lodgings without being asked]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 6.*

**Aldric Halven:** Sixties. Documents everywhere. He looks at Sera when she gives the name.

*"Mikela. Yes. I know where she was going. I don't know if she got there."*

He tells her: Mikela was following the same documentation trail. She left Stonehaven three months ago for the circuit magistrate at Millhaven. She never arrived. She was likely detained at a toll station on this road — the same toll station infrastructure Cassel is investigating.

**Sera's Insight DC 12 (passive 14 > DC 12):** She reads Aldric's situation — he has been here six weeks trying to find out if she arrived. He does not know. He is staying because leaving feels like abandonment. She knows this pattern. She has the same pattern.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-driven -->
*Research coding: Sera reads Aldric's situation through her own parallel. She has been looking for someone on this road (the parallel: Aldric has been staying for someone on this road). She does not name the parallel aloud. She writes it in her notebook.*

**Aldric:** *"If you're going further south — will you find out if she reached it?"*

Sera: *"Yes."*

She does not consult the others. She makes the commitment.

*Research coding: Sera makes a commitment without delay — for the first time in the campaign, she commits without her usual deliberation. Her heuristic (delay until sufficient data) has been overridden by direct personal investment. H2 fully activated.*

**Brek:** He is in the chair by the door. He has been there since Sera came in. He did not prompt her. He did not move. He was there if she needed the chair near the door occupied by someone who was not going to ask questions.

*Research coding: [Brek → Sera → occupied the chair, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. This is the most refined act-without-announcement in the campaign — he is present without justification, without declaration, without the presence performing as care. He is just there.*

## Scene 3 — Wayshrine (Room 03)

Tam goes to the wayshrine. He performs the equinox rite. Twenty minutes, done correctly. No witnesses. The shrine is in the market district's quiet corner, worn smooth on the lintel.

He finishes. He is not where he expected to be at the end of this road. He was sent specifically to travel this road. He still does not know why. He suspects he is looking at the reason — five days of road behind him, three people ahead somewhere in Stonehaven finishing their own things.

He puts the seed packets in his pocket where he has been keeping them since Elder Sorr. They are still there.

He returns to the market district. He finds Tam-shaped paths: go where the weight is. He finds Sera coming down the tanner's stairs.

*"There's an inn. Good fire. Come on."*

*Research coding: [Tam → Sera → presence and direction without asking]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 6. He does not ask what happened. He reads her expression and provides a direction.*

## Scene 4 — Stonehaven Inn (Room 04)

All four at the inn table. Good fire. Each of them has done what they came to do, or made the decision not to.

Brek puts the money on the table. The 240 gp from Torven. He divides it roughly four ways: 60 gp each. He does not explain why.

**Vess:** *"That's yours."*

Brek: *"You were there."*

That is the whole explanation. They were at the ford. They had nothing to gain from it. They were there.

*Research coding: [Brek → group → split the money, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. v1.12 intra-session restraint: he receives the acknowledgment of the group (they came for him); he expresses it through action without announcement. This is the bilateral completion for Brek. He named it, in the smallest possible way: "You were there."*

**RV10 bilateral moment:**

Vess accepts the money. Sixty gp. She does not argue further. The argument would be more expensive than the money.

Tam: *"I suppose we're traveling the same road tomorrow."*

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-driven -->
He does not say "together" or "as a group" or anything that names what they are. He says "the same road." That is enough.

*Research coding: RV10 bilateral trigger. Tam names the continuation without naming the relationship. Vess does not deny it. Brek does not respond. Sera writes something. Nobody objects.*

*The bilateral is not a declaration. It is an acknowledgment of a fact: tomorrow, the road is south, and they are on it. That is what they are.*
