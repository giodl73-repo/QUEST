# Nenn Hillfoot, 58, Gate-Tender at Stonfold; Thera's Father

## Appearance

Fifty-eight, human, Varduin-born. Shorter than average for a Varduin hill-crafter; wide across the shoulders from twenty years of gate-work and odd-jobs. Sun-wheat hair gone silver at the temples — the same color Thera's would be in thirty years. A small scar on his left thumb from a gate-latch accident that he has had since before Thera was born. He wears the same kind of soft leathers that hill-country workers wear; he has always worn them. He does not look like someone who has been running. He looks like someone who stopped running and does not know if he should start again.

He has a silver finger-ring on his left thumb. Too large for the thumb; he spins it when thinking. The same ring Thera has, oversized on her thumb. Neither of them has ever said where the design came from. It was Adda's pattern.

## Background

Born in Varduin-by-the-Pines. Married Adda Hillfoot at twenty-four. Had Thera at twenty-seven. When Adda died of winter-sickness at thirty — *Thera was eight* — he held on at Varduin for three more years. Then he left. He has been leaving ever since: hill-roads, Nerakan border towns, Crystalmir-adjacent farmholds, and finally, eight months ago, Stonfold.

He did not stop at Stonfold because it was his destination. He stopped because Tova Stonfold offered him steady work and the gate-post was away from the main forge and he did not have to talk to people unless he wanted to. That is how he has chosen most of his accommodations.

**He wrote a letter** — the one Thera has carried for six years. It was addressed to himself at the Varduin way-house as a return address, with Thera's name on the inside flap. He meant to send it to her but could never choose a permanent address to send it from. He left it at the way-house when he left Varduin for the first time, trusting the way-house keeper to know what to do with it. Thera got it somehow. He does not know this.

**He does not know Thera is coming.** He does not know Thera has the letter. He does not know the party is connected to his daughter until the moment Thera walks through the Stonfold gate.

**He longs for Adda.** This is why Tova will not let him near the Thread. The vibration near the spool when Nenn passes the archive is the loudest Tova has felt in forty years.

## Voice & manner

- Very quiet. Few words. Not because he is taciturn — because he has been living in places where silence was easier than explanation.
- When he sees Thera: his first word is her name. Just: *"Thera."* And then nothing, because there is nothing to say first that does not require saying everything.
- He will not apologize before Thera asks him to. He left; she did not. He is aware of what that cost. He will say so if asked, specifically. He will not volunteer it.
- He knows what the letter says. He will not tell Thera what it says before she opens it. If she has not opened it in his presence and asks: *"You haven't read it."* Not a question.

## What he wants

- **Primary:** to see Thera without destroying what remains of anything good between them. He does not know if there is anything good remaining. He suspects there is but has been afraid to test the hypothesis for twenty-three years.
- **Secondary:** to know how Adda is remembered. He left; he was not there for whatever rituals Varduin kept. He knows Thera was raised by the way-house keepers. He does not know what she remembers.
- **Tertiary:** to not pull a thread on the spool. He has been told what it costs. He is afraid that if he sees Adda once, he will keep pulling until she is gone.

## Stat block (Commoner; non-combatant; will not fight his daughter's enemies)

- **AC** 12 · **HP** 16 (3d8+3) · **Speed** 30 ft
- Str 12, Dex 12, Con 12, Int 11, Wis 14, Cha 11.
- **Skills:** Perception +4, Survival +4 (hill-roads; decades of practice).
- **Senses:** passive Perception 14.
- **Languages:** Common, hill-Halfling (conversational from years of Verduin-halfling neighbors including, once, Thera's mother's friends).

## Secrets

- **The letter says:** *"Thera. I left because the forge-hall where your mother used to buy herbs still smelled like her. I could not go past it without losing the whole day. I should have stayed for you. I knew that when I left and I still left. I am sorry. If you want to find me, I am moving east. I don't have an address yet. I will try to have one someday. — Nenn."* It was written when Thera was thirteen. She has carried it since she was twenty-five.
- **He also longs for Thera.** The Thread vibrates for him from two directions — Adda, and the daughter he left. He cannot tell them apart. He thinks this is what longing feels like when you have earned it twice.
- **He has been saving** — carefully, over eight months — to buy a piece of Stonfold moon-silver to send to Thera at Varduin. He does not know she is no longer at Varduin. He was going to send it with a letter that said: *"I am at Stonfold. I am not moving again."*

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 2 (arrival):** He is at the gate. He sees Thera walk up. Says: *"Thera."* Does not move toward her. Does not move away. Waits.
- **Scene 3 (Nenn-Thera confrontation):** the session's emotional fulcrum. This is the scene the whole adventure is built toward. See Scene 03 room file.
- **Scene 5 (Nenn and the Thread):** if Thera takes Nenn to see the Thread in the archive, the spool vibrates when he enters the room. He says: *"I can feel it."* He does not explain. He does not touch the case.
- **Scene 6 (the Thread's moment):** Nenn is present if Thera wants him there. He will not tell her what to do. He will watch.
- **Scene 7 (aftermath):** if Thera has opened the letter (any time): Nenn waits for her to finish reading. Then: *"I wrote it when you were thirteen. I did not send it because I did not have an address."* If she has not opened it: he will not ask. If the Thread was destroyed via reciprocal naming: Nenn performed his naming (to Adda, aloud, in front of Thera and Grom). He has named the longing. He does not know what happens next. He asks Thera: *"Are you coming back through Stonfold?"*

## GM notes

- **Do not rush this.** Scene 03 (Nenn-Thera confrontation) needs to breathe. The party should watch. Only Grom will know what to say, and what Grom says is probably nothing.
- **The letter is Thera's choice.** She has it. She has carried it for six years. Whether she opens it before or after talking to Nenn or not at all is her decision. The DM should not prompt this decision. If Thera's player doesn't bring it up, let it sit. It may be the thing she carries home.
- **He spins the ring.** The same ring Thera spins. They do not comment on this.
- **If Nenn attunes the Thread:** Option C must fire for Thera (not Nenn). Thera watching her father pull a thread to see Adda is a different kind of loss. This is the dark-ending branch.
