# Tova Stonfold, 62, Matriarch of the Stonfold Smith-Family

## Appearance

Sixty-two, hill dwarf, compact and wide-shouldered. Her hair is iron-grey, unplaited, kept back with a leather cord — not the Thorbardin formal style; the Stonfold working style. Burns on both forearms from two hundred years of family forge-work (not her two hundred years; the family's). Her hands are the widest Grom has ever seen on a dwarf her height. She does not greet people; she assesses them and then decides if the greeting is worth the breath.

She is Seralen's cousin — Mira's mother's people. She took Mira in because she owed Seralen something she never specified. She has not regretted it, mostly.

## Background

The Stonfold smith-family has worked the Stonfold vein for two hundred years — older than the Vaenshold family's Abanasinian operation, started by a different ancestor in different circumstances. The two families have been competitors and occasional allies since Aelwen's time. **Tova knows about the Vaenshold forgings.** Her grandmother's grandmother traded moon-silver ingots with Aelwen directly; the trade-records are in the Stonfold archive. She knows that Aelwen made six objects. She knows that the Thread was one of them.

**She inherited the Thread from her mother**, who inherited it from her mother's mother, etc. — four generations of Stonfold women who refused to pull a thread. *"My grandmother said: if you pull a thread, you will not stop pulling. She was correct. She tried once. She stopped at four."* The Thread has been in a locked case in the Stonfold forge-archive for forty years, since Tova's mother's generation. Tova has never opened the case.

**She contacted Brother Laen's Rose Council** three months ago, through Mira, because Mira told her about the Varduin Muster. Tova wants the Thread disposed of responsibly before she dies. She is not dying imminently. But she is sixty-two and the Thread has been in the family long enough.

## Voice & manner

- Direct. No preamble. She has met too many people in her life to spend breath on courtesy she does not mean.
- Addresses Grom specifically as *"forge-priest"* within three sentences of meeting him. She has correctly identified who in the party she needs to talk to.
- Calls the Thread *"the spool"* consistently. Never its full name. She has not given it that much importance in forty years and does not intend to start.
- Will not be manipulated. She is sixty-two and has run a forge-family for thirty years. Every manipulation tactic is one she has used herself.

## What she wants

- **Primary:** the spool gone. Destroyed. She does not care how. She prefers the Reorx-shattering rite because she respects Reorx-priesthood discipline and trusts it more than Solamnic archive.
- **Secondary:** Mira settled and functional at Stonfold. Three months in, Mira is still adjusting. Tova wants the party to see that it is going well.
- **Tertiary:** to know what happened to Seralen's name. Mira told her about the Council file-note; Tova wants to hear it from Grom's mouth.

## Secrets

- **She knows about Nenn Hillfoot.** He arrived eight months ago; she gave him the gate-tender job because he was quiet and capable and clearly needed a place to be that was not where he had been. She did not know his connection to Thera until Mira arrived three months ago and mentioned the party might come. She has said nothing to Nenn.
- **The Stonfold archive has records of a third moon-silver vein.** In Aelwen's letters to Tova's grandmother's grandmother: Aelwen mentioned a third vein she had accessed briefly, in the deep Nerakan border-country, which produced silver with a different resonance — *"the cold kind, not the grief-kind."* Aelwen used it for the sixth artifact. This is a seed for #0007's finale: the Silver-Ingot Confession requires three-vein accounting, not two.
- **She pulled two threads once, forty years ago.** She saw her wife Hevara — who had died the year before — and then her daughter as a child. She put the spool back in its case. She has not thought about it without pain since. She will not say this unless directly asked, and then only once.

## Stat block (CR 2, 450 XP — master smith and forge-hold leader)

- **AC** 16 (forge-master's work-apron + natural toughness; she is harder than she looks) · **HP** 55 (7d8+21) · **Speed** 25 ft
- **Str** 16 · **Dex** 10 · **Con** 16 · **Int** 14 · **Wis** 17 · **Cha** 14
- **Saves:** Con +5, Wis +5
- **Skills:** Athletics +5, History +4, Insight +7, Perception +5
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft; passive Perception 15; **Stonecunning:** History (stonework) +4 = effectively +8
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish
- **Dwarven resistances:** advantage vs. poison; poison resistance
- **Forge-master's assessment (free, once per creature):** when Tova meets a new creature, she makes a passive Insight check (15). If it succeeds, she knows whether the creature is currently longing for someone or something (the Thread's passive "vibration sense" is also hers, from years of proximity).
- **Hammer (if threatened):** +5, 1d8+3 bludgeoning; this is her work hammer; she would rather not.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 2 (arrival):** opens the gate herself (Nenn is on the gate; she is behind him). Assesses the party in under ten seconds. Says to Grom: *"Forge-priest. The spool will be yours to deal with. Come inside."* Does not wait for the party to recover from Nenn.
- **Scene 4 (the archive):** shows the party the Thread in its case. Explains its history in three minutes. Opens the case herself. Does not touch the spool.
- **Scene 5 (Tova's disclosure):** if the party asks about the third vein, she will show them the archive records. This is the #0007 seed. She will not offer it; she will show it if asked directly.
- **Scene 6 (the Thread's moment):** present but not central. She is watching Grom's rite preparation or watching Thera. She does not intervene in Thera's decision. She does not comment on what she sees unless it is over.
- **Scene 7 (aftermath):** *"It is done."* That is all she says about the Thread. Then: to Grom, *"My grandmother's grandmother sent Aelwen a quarter-pound of Stonfold silver in PC 178. One year before she hanged herself. I do not know if it helped. I have always wondered."*

## GM notes

- **Play Tova as the session's moral adult.** She is the only character who has known what the Thread costs and chosen not to pay it. She is not afraid of longing; she is afraid of running out of it.
- **Her Forge-master's assessment fires automatically on Thera.** She will feel Thera longing. She will not say this aloud unless Thera asks her directly. If asked: *"Yes. For a long time, from the feel of it."*
- **The third-vein seed is important.** If the party discovers it in Scene 5, flag it for the session log. Kessa will cross-reference with Tiran's research and the Hint 4 synthesis. The ingot that Aelwen should have forged was made from cold-vein silver — the third vein. Not grief-coiled; not warm. That is why it was the right material for the un-forged confession.
