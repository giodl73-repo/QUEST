# Scene 05 — The Spool

**Location:** Stonfold archive, front of the unlabeled case. **Time:** Day 3 evening, after briefing.

**Read-aloud:**

> Tova opens the case. Inside: a spool of very fine moon-silver thread, cool-toned against the dark velvet lining. It vibrates when Thera steps close. It vibrates when Nenn enters the room. When both of them are present, the vibration is not louder — just more certain, like two tuning forks in the same key.

**Features:**

- **The spool.** Smaller than a fist. Stonfold silver: cooler than Vaenshold work, slightly blue undertone. Grom's passive: *this is not warm silver. This is the longing-kind, not the grief-kind.* (Connecting to his Hint 3 understanding: grief-coiled forgings feel warm; this one is cooler — the second smith-family's vein has a different forging register.)
- **The vibration.** Passive Perception 10 for anyone in the room. It is not sound; it is felt through the floor and the nearby case-shelf. It changes when different people are near it.
- **10 threads visible.** Investigation DC 10: the thread appears to have exactly ten discrete visible strands at the spool's outer edge, each a slightly different length.
- **The small mirror on the archive wall** (not Aelwen's work). Tova put it there years ago for the same reason Tiran put the mirror in her vault. Neither of them has used it.

**The decision:**

Three options branch here:

**Option A — Grom's Reorx-rite (destroy the spool).** Requires moving to the Stonfold forge (main forge-hall; temperature reaches 1,800°C per Tova's confirmation). Religion DC 15. If Thera or Nenn want to watch, they may.

**Option B — Thera pulls a thread.** **Option C spawn mandatory.** The Thread will show Thera either Adda or Nenn depending on which longing is deeper. See the Option C subagent prompt (inline at Scene 06).

**Option C — Leave the spool with Tova pending disposition.** Tova agrees to wait until the party communicates through Laen. The spool stays in its case. The decision is deferred.

**If Nenn is in the room and the party watches:** the spool vibrates more clearly when Nenn approaches within 5 ft. He says: *"I can feel it."* Thera can now see what the spool does to her father when he is near it. This may influence her decision.

**Encounter:** None.

**GM Notes:**

- **This is not the same as the vault in Scene 06.** Scene 05 is the first encounter with the spool; Scene 06 is the resolution. Give the party Scene 05 to look, ask questions, understand the mechanics. Then Scene 06 for the decision.
- **Grom's passive.** Let him feel the difference between this silver and Vaenshold silver. The forge-priest reads materials; this is a craft-witness moment. The Thread is cooler. Different forging register. Connects to the third-vein seed without explicitly naming it.
- **The spool vibrating for both Thera and Nenn.** This is the scene's most loaded moment. Neither has pulled a thread. Both are within the spool's range. The vibration for both of them in the same register — the same longing-frequency — is what tells Thera they are both longing for Adda.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The thread is Stonfold-family silver, forged from a vein that runs through the east face of a hill that has not been named in two hundred years of Stonfold use. The vein has no name because the Stonfold family considers naming a vein to be bad luck — naming a thing gives it the idea that it could run out. The thread knows this about its source. When someone longs in its presence, it says nothing. It just vibrates at the right frequency. It is very good at this.*
