# Scene 01 — The Road to Stonfold (conditional encounter)

**Location:** Kharolis foothills, approach road from Crystalmir direction. **Time:** Day 3 of travel.

**Read-aloud:**

> The road to Stonfold climbs into country the maps call "hill-dwarf hold territory" and the hill-dwarves call "ours." Slate-grey stone, short pine, and the occasional sight-line to a snow-capped ridge that was there before Istar was a city. The road is real — worn smooth by two hundred years of Stonfold-family ore-wagons — but it is not welcoming. It accepts you because you are on it, not because it invited you.

**Features:**

- **The ore-road.** 15 ft wide; smooth stone; Stonfold family freight has used this road for two centuries. Grom Stonecunning passive: the road's construction method is different from Thorbardin outer-hold work; older style, pre-Cataclysm technique.
- **The Nerakan artifact-collector.** A small party — three humans and a cart — is camped at the side of the road, blocking one lane. Their cart has a locked iron chest. They are polite. They are going to Stonfold. They have heard the family has an Aelwen artifact and have made an offer Tova has not yet declined.

**Encounter — The Collector's Party:**

This is the session's **required combat encounter** (per S05 Tactician panel feedback; S06 must reintroduce dice-weight).

> **Artifact Collector (CR 1, 200 XP):** Istaran-hired factor; academic dress; carries a shortsword he rarely uses. AC 13, HP 32. **Persuasion +6; Intimidation +4; Investigation +5.** If the party identifies as representatives of the Rose Council (Aelric's Council letter; formal connection): the collector stands down immediately — he knows the Council's authority over Aelwen artifacts post-S03 Korrath settlement. **No combat if Council authority is invoked.**

> **Collector's guards × 2 (CR ½, 100 XP each):** Road-hired. AC 14 (leather + shield), HP 22. Shortsword +4, 1d6+2. Will flee at HP ≤ 8.

**Encounter XP:** 200 + 200 = 400 base × 2 (3 creatures) = **800 XP. Hard.** But skippable if Council authority invoked (Aelric's letters; DC 10 Persuasion after showing them; likely auto-pass).

**Tactics:**
- Collector opens with negotiation: he has a legitimate buyer (unnamed; Kessa Investigation 15: it is Felor of Second House — Tiran's factotum, still working).
- If party rejects: guards advance; collector uses Ledger-Tongue-adjacent persuasion (Intimidation DC 12 to make a guard hesitate per round).
- Collector withdraws at HP ≤ 10. He is commercial; not zealous.

**Intel (if collector captured/negotiated with):**
- His buyer: Felor of Second House (Persuasion DC 14 or Kann's finger-bone leverage → auto).
- Felor has a new principal: not Tiran (she told Felor the Circlet disposition is final); this is a different Second House faction.
- The collector has a manifest of known Aelwen artifacts. **It lists six.** Including one entry: *"sixth artifact: believed to have been forged from a third vein; cold silver; shame-related properties; location unknown."* This is Tiran's research independently confirmed. Kessa takes this.

**Treasure:**
- Collector's kit: various; 30 gp coin; the six-artifact manifest (KEY; auto-grab if collector surrenders).
- Guards' kits: 5 gp each.

**Connections:** north → [2] Stonfold holding gate.

**GM Notes:**

- **This encounter is optional** if the party invokes the Council letter. That is the correct diplomatic solution. Combat is available if the party is aggressive or if the guards escalate.
- **The six-artifact manifest** is the most important loot. If the party bypasses combat, Kessa can still get the manifest via Persuasion DC 16 (the collector will share academic notes with a Wizard of High Sorcery if treated as a peer).
- **Felor's new principal** plants a seed for #0007 aftermath — the Second House is still shopping.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The collector's iron chest holds a copy of Aelwen's commission-letter to the Stonfold family — the one requesting quarter-pound of Stonfold silver in PC 178. He bought it from an estate sale in Istar three years ago. He does not know it is the same family whose gate he is camped outside. He was going to use it as a provenance document for the Thread. He will now never use it. He will carry it home and eventually lose it in a library archive. Someone will find it in two hundred years and wonder what it proves.*
