# Scene 06 — The Resolution

**Location:** Stonfold main forge-hall (if Reorx-rite) OR Stonfold archive courtyard (if Thera pulls a thread) OR archive itself. **Time:** Day 3 late evening.

**Read-aloud:**

> The Stonfold forge-hall is larger than any the party has seen outside Thorbardin. The forge fires have been damped to half for the night; the heat is still substantial. The spool sits in a cloth-wrapped bundle on the forge master's anvil — the one Tova uses for ceremony, not work. Nenn Hillfoot is at the door. He was not invited. He is here.

**Three resolution branches:**

---

### Branch A — Grom performs the Reorx-rite

**Religion DC 15 at dawn** (advantage if Grom has performed hearth-greetings at Stonfold forges throughout Day 3). **Tova confirms the forge reaches ≥1,800°C at dawn** — no check needed to verify temperature; she has run this forge for thirty years. If the party asks: *"It is hot enough. I have burned silver here before."*

**What happens:** the thread un-spools into the forge-fire. Not dramatically — the silver melts cleanly; the thread dissolves; a small bright vapor rises. **The vapor smells like something the party cannot name.** Grom's craft-witness: *"It smells like what you miss before you know you've started missing it."*

The spool is gone. The threads that remained in it have been released without being consumed. **Anyone who was near the spool during the rite receives a single moment of clarity** — not a vision, not a hallucination. Just: *"I knew, for a second, what I was reaching for."* It fades immediately. They will remember that they knew but not what they knew.

If Thera was watching: she will remember, for one second, something about Adda she had almost stopped remembering. It will not come back. She will be glad it came once.

If Nenn was watching: same.

**Outcome: Spool destroyed. Threads released. Campaign-permanent.**

---

### Branch B — Thera pulls a thread

**This is the Option C moment.**

```
You are Thera Hillfoot of the Varduin Muster. Your full PC sheet is at
personas/parties/varduin-muster/thera-hillfoot.md. READ THAT SHEET FIRST.

Context: You are in the Stonfold archive, the Thread-That-Frays spool in
front of you. Nenn Hillfoot is in the room. Your father. You haven't spoken
to him properly yet (or you have — whatever happened in Scene 03).

If you pull a thread, you will see what you long for most — not an
idealized version, a true version. The first thread completes attunement.

Your most-longed-for is probably your mother Adda — who died when you were
eight, who cut bread the wrong way (long-ways, not across), who said
something to you that you remember exactly. The vision would show you Adda.
You cannot speak to her. You cannot touch her. The ending-condition for the
curse is naming the longing to the object while the object is present and
listening. Adda is dead. If you pull threads until they run out, the longing
for Adda is permanently gone from memory.

Alternatively: your most-longed-for might be Nenn. The father who left.
Who is standing in this room. If the Thread shows you Nenn, the ending-
condition is immediately possible: name the longing to him, here, now, and
the thread re-spools. The curse ends. But naming it requires saying it.

Your heuristic: Don't die > Curiosity > Profit > Loyalty. Your doubt-die
at critical moments: 1-3 = profit (is there value?), 4-6 = story (what
does this tell me?).

What do you do? Do you pull a thread? If yes: does it show Adda or Nenn?
You decide which longing is deeper. Name who you see and what the vision
shows. If you name a longing to Nenn (if the Thread shows Nenn): say aloud
what you say to him.

Stay in Thera's voice. Respond in a single paragraph. It is binding.
```

**If Agent unavailable:** ask Thera's player: *"Do you pull a thread? If yes: who does it show — Adda or Nenn?"* Their answer is binding.

---

### Branch C — Reciprocal Naming (golden path, if Nenn + Thera name it)

This branch is available if:
- The Thread shows Nenn (Branch B) AND Thera names her longing to him
- OR if the party facilitates Nenn and Thera doing the naming simultaneously

**The naming:**

Thera (or both): *"I longed for you. [specific name]. I am naming it. I am not asking anything."*

**The thread re-spools.** Attunement ends. The spool is full again. The spool is then offered to the Reorx-rite (Branch A) or surrendered to Tova.

**What Nenn says after the naming:**

He is quiet for a long time. Then, to the spool: *"I longed for Adda. And I longed for you. I am naming both."* He is not addressing the spool's conditions; he is addressing the accuracy. He is getting the accounting right.

---

**Encounter:** None in Branch A (ceremony only). Branch B may have a saving throw.

**Connections:** all branches → [7] aftermath.

**GM Notes:**

- **All three branches are valid.** The golden path is Branch C (reciprocal naming), but Branch A (Reorx-rite) is equally legitimate. Branch B (thread-pulled) is the hardest but the most emotionally honest.
- **Nenn's presence (P0 fix — active moment).** He is at the door. He was not invited. He stays unless the party asks him to leave. **If the party moves to leave with the Thread before any resolution path has begun** — before Thera has spoken her choice — Nenn steps into the doorway. Not threatening. He says: *"She should decide first."* That is all. He does not move. He does not explain. Grom's Insight DC 10: *"He is not stopping them out of obstruction. He is stopping them out of accuracy — this is not done yet."* If the party acknowledges him, he waits. If Thera has already decided, Nenn does not need to intervene; he is witness.
- **The smell in Branch A.** *"What you miss before you know you've started missing it"* is a craft-witness description Grom generates from the vapor. Let it land. It is not mechanical; it is the art in the engine.
- **POST-SESSION MANDATORY:** Write Spool disposition (destroyed/attuned/full+surrendered) + Thera's attunement state (if any) + Nenn's naming (if any) to CLAUDE.md.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
