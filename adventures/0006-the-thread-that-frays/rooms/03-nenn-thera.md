# Scene 03 — Nenn and Thera (Courtyard or Gate-Side)

**Location:** Stonfold courtyard or gate-side, wherever the party leaves them space. **Time:** Day 3, late afternoon, immediately after Scene 02.

**Read-aloud:**

> Tova has taken the others inside. Mira has stayed at her forge but is not looking away. The courtyard is not quiet — the forges have their own noise — but the space between Thera and Nenn is as quiet as anywhere.

**The scene:**

This is the session's moral center. The DM does not script this. The DM plays Nenn; Thera's player plays Thera. The party is mostly inside; Grom may have stayed, or Kessa — neither should interrupt unless invited.

**Nenn's opening (after Scene 02's pause):**

*"You're thirty-one."* Not a question. He has been counting. *"Last I saw you, you fit in one arm."*

He does not say: I'm sorry. He does not say: I had to leave. He has said both of those things in his head for twenty-three years. They are not the first things.

**What Nenn will say if asked directly:**

- *"Why did you leave?"* — *"Because the forge-hall where your mother used to buy her herbs still smelled like her. I could not get past it. I knew what that was doing and I left anyway. That is the answer. I do not have a better one."*
- *"Did you know I had the letter?"* — *"No. I left it at the way-house. I did not know you had it until now."* He looks at her hands when he says this, to see if she has it.
- *"Why didn't you come back?"* — *"I kept moving. Then I stopped moving here. I was going to write again. With an address this time."*
- *"Do you remember her?"* — *"Every day. That has not changed."*

**The letter (if Thera opens it in this scene):**

If Thera takes the letter out and opens it in front of Nenn:

He goes very still. When she finishes reading, he says: *"I wrote that when you were thirteen. I did not send it because I did not have an address."* And then, because he is not going to waste the moment: *"I have one now. If you want it."*

**Option C prompt for Thera (if the moment reaches it):**

If Thera's player reaches a decision point — specifically, whether Thera gives Nenn the address back, whether she says anything about Adda, whether she names the longing she has been carrying — spawn Thera as a subagent:

```
You are Thera Hillfoot of the Varduin Muster. Your full PC sheet is at
personas/parties/varduin-muster/thera-hillfoot.md. READ THAT SHEET FIRST.

Context: You are in the Stonfold courtyard with your father, Nenn Hillfoot.
You have not seen him since you were eight. He recognized you at the gate.
He said your name. It has now been twenty minutes.

You have in your pocket a letter he wrote when you were thirteen that says:
"I should have stayed for you. I am sorry."

Your mother Adda is dead. She died when you were eight. You remember the
bread she cut long-ways, wrong, and what she said about it. You have not
cut bread long-ways since.

Your heuristic says: Don't die > Curiosity > Profit > Loyalty (growing).
Your voice tag says: goes quieter, not louder, when a thing becomes serious.

He has just told you he has an address now, if you want it.

What do you say? What do you do? Stay in Thera's voice.
Do not produce a tearful scene if Thera would not produce one.
Do not produce a cold scene if the warmth is real.
Respond in a single paragraph. Your answer is binding.
```

**If Agent tool unavailable:** ask the player in-character: *"Thera. He has an address. What do you say?"* Let them answer. It is binding.

**Encounter:** None. This is the session's soul.

**GM Notes:**

- **Do not make Nenn the villain.** He left; he was wrong to; he knows it; he does not excuse it. Those four things can all be true. Do not simplify him into absence-as-malice.
- **Grom's presence.** If Grom stayed — and he might have, because his heuristic says "stand between" and he would want to be near — he does not speak. He touches the gate-hinge instead. The Stonfold gate-hinge is well-made. He notes this. He says nothing.
- **The letter.** If Thera never opens it in this scene, it stays in her pocket. Nenn will not ask. The letter is hers.
- **The ring.** Thera's ring is on her left thumb, too large; she spins it. Nenn's ring is on his left thumb, too large; he spins it. They do not mention this. If Thera stops spinning and looks at his: he stops spinning and looks at hers. Neither speaks. This is a Memory Fragment delivered in real time.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Adda Hillfoot wore the ring on her right index finger, which is where it fit. She left it to Nenn when she died. He put it on his thumb because that is where he could feel it. He gave a copy to the way-house keeper for Thera the day he left Varduin. He did not know if Thera would want it. He knew she would keep it.*
