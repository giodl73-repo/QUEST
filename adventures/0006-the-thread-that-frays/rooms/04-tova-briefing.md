# Scene 04 — Tova's Briefing (Stonfold Archive)

**Location:** Stonfold archive building — small; functional; three generations of smith-family records. **Time:** Day 3 evening.

**Read-aloud:**

> The archive is smaller than Tiran's. It is also better organized. Every shelf has a label; every record bundle has a date cord. There is a locked case in the center of the back wall, on its own shelf, with no label at all. That is where the spool is. Tova stands in front of it with her arms folded and says: "Ask your questions first. Then I will open it."

**Features:**

- **The unlabeled case.** The Thread's case. Investigation DC 10: the case has been handled more than the others — fingerprints of different sizes at different heights; multiple generations of Stonfold women have touched the latch.
- **The archive records.** Investigation DC 10: Stonfold family forge-records going back 200 years. DC 12: a series of trade-invoices with the Vaenshold family, including one dated PC 178 from Aelwen herself — a quarter-pound of Stonfold silver, paid in full, for a forging she notes as *"for my own accounting."* DC 14: Tova's grandmother's notes in the margin: *"This is the thread-spool material. Do not sell the spool. Keep the receipts."*
- **The third-vein reference.** Investigation DC 15 OR auto-delivery if Kessa has the collector's manifest: Aelwen's invoice notes a meeting with a Nerakan prospector in PC 177 who told her about a cold-vein deposit in the deep Kharolis border-country. The invoice has a map sketch. **This is the third-vein seed for #0007.**

**Tova's briefing:**

*"The spool has been in the family since Aelwen's granddaughter sold it to my great-grandmother in exchange for fifty years of ingot supply. We have known what it does since my grandmother tried it. She pulled four threads. She stopped. She said: it shows you real things, and real things cost something. She put it in the case. She told her daughter. Her daughter told me. I am telling you."*

On what she wants: *"Destroyed. Clean. If the forge-priest has the rite, I want to watch it."*

On Nenn: *"He has been here eight months. He is good at his job. He does not talk about what he longs for. The spool tells me anyway."* A pause. *"Your girl with the short hair. The rogue. She is the one it is for, isn't she."* Not a question.

**Kessa's cross-reference opportunity:** if Kessa has the collector's manifest AND Tiran's research AND the Hint 4 synthesis she articulated in S05: she can connect the third vein. *"Cold silver. Not grief-coiled. Not longing-keyed. The third vein is for something else. Shame, maybe. Aelwen had three veins to work from and she chose shame for the last one. The one she couldn't finish."* **This is the deepest synthesis of the campaign so far.** <!-- SURPRISE candidate. -->

**Encounter:** None.

**Connections:** archive → [5] the spool (after briefing); archive → [6] Nenn and Thera's decision.

**GM Notes:**

- **Tova is the scene's emotional intelligence.** She has known about Nenn and Thera for two months. She has watched Nenn not-talk about his longing for eight months. She sees Thera's longing the moment Thera walks in. She is not surprised by any of this. She is waiting for the party to catch up.
- **The third-vein connection** is the session's biggest intellectual payoff for Kessa. Let her have it.
- **The archive's organization** is a craft-witness moment for Grom. Everything here is honest work. He will note that Tova's records are better than his own shrine's.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Tova's grandmother, PC 75 (fifty-five years ago): she pulled four threads at midnight in this same room. The first showed her wife Hevara at the moment Tova's grandmother loved her most — not the day they married but a specific Tuesday afternoon when Hevara was reading aloud to the family forge-records and mispronounced "ingot" as "in-got" and laughed at herself before anyone else did. The second showed Hevara sick, before the sickness was named. The third showed Hevara gone. The fourth showed the grandmother herself, in this room, pulling threads alone. She put the spool back in its case. She understood that the spool was telling her something. She decided she did not want to know the rest.*
