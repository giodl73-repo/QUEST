# Premise — The Thread-That-Frays

**Campaign position:** #0006 of 7 in the **Moon-Silver Cycle**. Emotion: **Longing**. PC spotlight: **Thera Hillfoot** (Nenn retrieves).

**Logline:** Thera Hillfoot has carried a letter addressed to her father for six years without knowing if she was meant to open it or deliver it. She finds Nenn Hillfoot at Stonfold — where Mira Vaenshold has been living for three months — and the Stonfold matriarch Tova has the Thread-That-Frays. The thread shows you what you longed for most as if it were still within reach. Thera's most-longed-for is her mother, who died when Thera was eight. But her father is standing right there.

## Setting

**Place:** **Stonfold** — a mountain-country smith-family compound eighty miles east of Crystalmir-by-the-Fells, in the Kharolis foothills. Not a city; a working family holding — forge-halls, stone longhouses, a moon-silver vein in the hillside behind the main forge. The Stonfold family has worked this vein for two hundred years. **Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith and Harel arrived here three months ago** (post-S03). **Nenn Hillfoot arrived eight months ago**, working as a gate-tender and odd-job man.

**Time:** Late Age of Might, **PC 20** (~4 weeks after S05). The party has ridden from Korrath.

## Hook

Thera has carried two things for years without knowing what to do with them:

1. **The letter she's had for six years.** It's addressed to *"Nenn Hillfoot at an unknown address"* — she found it in Varduin's way-house records when she was 25, after years of not asking where her father went. She doesn't know if Nenn wrote it to her (using his own name as a return address) or if she was meant to deliver it to him. She has never opened it.

2. **The knowledge that her father left Varduin when she was eight.** After Adda died. She has not seen him since.

**The party arrives at Stonfold** because the seed-tracker points here: Tova Stonfold was named by Mira in S03 as the Stonfold matriarch who would take her in. Kann's Stonfold finger-bone (Kessa carries it) is made from Stonfold-family moon-silver and links to both #0006 and the #0007 finale. The party has reasons beyond Thera's private history.

But when they arrive: **Nenn Hillfoot is at the gate.** Doing exactly what he's always done — keeping a gate, watching who comes and goes.

He sees Thera. He recognizes her. She is thirty-one now. She was eight the last time he saw her. He says nothing. She says nothing. The party is watching.

**Brother Laen's follow-up courier** (arriving simultaneously from the opposite direction) brings word: *"Tova Stonfold has contacted the Council about an inherited Aelwen artifact she wishes to dispose of responsibly. She asked for a referral. I am referring you. — L."* The Council has learned from S03 and S04; they now route Aelwen-artifacts to the party instead of trying to handle them directly.

## Central Artifact

**The Thread-That-Frays** — a small spool of moon-silver thread, Stonfold-vein work (not Vaenshold). Aelwen's fifth secret forging, PC 179. The thread is very fine; the spool is smaller than a fist. When a thread is pulled, it shows you what you longed for most — as if it were still within reach.

**Effect on pulling a thread:** the holder sees a vision of what they longed for most. Not a hallucination; a clarity — the way you see something you have been trying not to see clearly for years. The vision is true but framed at its most bearable. First-time holders pull a thread without understanding the cost.

**Presence / Desire:** the spool vibrates faintly when held by someone who is longing for something. Everyone longs for something. The vibration is always present.

**Rarity:** Very Rare · attunement required (first pulled thread completes attunement) · no kin prerequisite.

**Benefits (attuned):**
- Once per short rest: the bearer may pull a thread and see a vision of what they long for most. The vision provides advantage on Wisdom saves for 1 hour (they have been reminded of what they are holding onto).
- Passive: the bearer knows when a creature within 60 ft is longing for something. Not what — just that they are longing. Like sensing body heat.

**Curse: Thread-loss.**
Each vision consumes a thread. When all threads in the spool are gone (10 threads at attunement), the specific longing that anchored the bearer's emotional life is permanently forgotten — not repressed, not suppressed. Gone. The bearer retains the emotional tone (a general ache, a persistent sense that something is missing) but cannot name what they were longing for or who it was for. Permanent. Greater Restoration cannot restore a consumed longing.

**The Blank-Thread Trap:** if the bearer's most-longed-for is someone who has renounced all connection to the bearer — not death, renunciation — the thread shows a blank. A blank means: the bearer pulls ten threads in succession, consuming all of them at once, in a single session of fraying. Instant complete-loss.

**Ending the curse:**
1. **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** forge ≥1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Destroys the spool. Threads consumed so far are gone permanently; remaining threads' longing returns to the bearer as a single named clarity. They will not forget again.
2. **Reciprocal naming:** the bearer names their longing aloud — specifically, by name, not as a request — to the object of that longing, while the object is present and listening. *"I longed for you. I am naming it. I am not asking anything."* The thread re-spools; attunement ends; the longing returns, un-frayed, permanently.

## Themes

- Longing is not the same as wanting. Wanting can be satisfied; longing can only be named.
- The people you longed for most are usually the ones who are gone. Occasionally one of them is standing right there.
- Nenn Hillfoot did not leave because he stopped loving Thera. He left because he could not be in the same town as the forge-hall where Adda used to buy herbs. There are worse reasons; there are no better ones.
- Stonfold's moon-silver vein is older than Vaenshold's. The Thread was made from it. The two families have been competing and occasionally trading for two hundred years. The finale requires both.

## The Real Decisions

Four overlapping:

1. **Does Thera open the letter?** The Nenn-letter she has carried for six years. She can open it before speaking to Nenn. She can open it after. She can never open it. Whatever it says — it is real.
2. **Does Thera attune the Thread?** Her longing is Adda (dead). If the Thread shows her Adda, the ending-condition (name the longing to the object while they are present) is impossible. She will lose threads. **Option C mandatory if she pulls a thread.**
3. **What does Nenn want?** He also longs for Adda. He has been at Stonfold for eight months. He has not pulled the thread — Tova would not let him. But the vibration when he is near the spool tells Tova he longs for someone. He does not know Thera is coming.
4. **Where does the Thread end up?** Destroyed (Grom's rite), surrendered to Council/Tova, attuned to a PC, or left with Nenn for his own ending-attempt.
