# The Thread-That-Frays

*A small spool of moon-silver thread, Stonfold-vein — not Vaenshold. It vibrates when someone nearby is longing for something. Everyone longs for something. It is always vibrating.*

## Appearance

The spool is smaller than a fist — three inches across, two inches tall. Stonfold-family moon-silver: cooler in color than Vaenshold silver, with a slight blue undertone rather than the warm gold of Vaenshold work. The thread on the spool is very fine; it catches light like a spider's web in morning frost. There is no visible seam on the spool; Aelwen forged it as a single piece. The thread appears to be wound around a core, but the core is also thread — it is threads all the way down; there is no central rod.

**How it vibrates:** not audible; not violent. It is the vibration of a tuning fork held against a wall — the kind you feel through your palm before you hear it. Someone who holds the spool while longing for something will feel it vibrate in resonance with them. First-time holders often think it is their own pulse. It is not their pulse.

## Provenance

Aelwen forged the Thread in **PC 179** — the fifth of her six secret forgings, after the Rose, Reliquary, Mirror, Wrath-Coin, and Pride-Circlet. The Thread is the only artifact Aelwen made from Stonfold-vein silver rather than Vaenshold-vein. She requested a quarter-pound of Stonfold raw material from Tova Stonfold's grandmother's grandmother in PC 178, paying fair market price and noting in the invoice: *"For a forging that will not serve me."*

Aelwen completed the Thread in late PC 179 and placed it with the other secret forgings in her workshop vault. Heva Aelwensdaughter recovered it, along with the others, when she entered the vault in PC 179. Unlike the Mirror and Wrath-Coin, which Heva refused to touch repeatedly, the Thread passed to a Stonfold-family trader within a generation: Heva sold it to Tova's family's ancestor in exchange for twenty years of Stonfold-vein ingots to keep the Vaenshold silversmith line running. **The Thread has been in Stonfold hands ever since — eighty years now.**

## Properties (mechanical)

### Attunement

**Fast-attune: first pulled thread completes attunement.** No short rest. The thread is pulled; the vision begins; the attunement completes during the vision. The holder can drop the spool before pulling (no attunement), but cannot stop the attunement once a thread begins to unwind.

**What the vision shows:** the bearer's most-longed-for person or thing, as it existed at the peak of the bearer's longing — not an idealized version; a *recalled* version, made clear and close. For a dead mother: her hands on dough. For a father who left: his footsteps going away. For a city never visited: the exact smell the bearer imagined it would have. The visions are specific, not symbolic.

**Duration:** visions last 1 round (6 seconds) per thread pulled. They are not real. The bearer cannot speak to or touch what they see.

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Thread-vision (1/short rest):** pull one thread; see one vision. The vision grants advantage on Wisdom saving throws for 1 hour (the bearer has been reminded of what anchors them).
- **Longing-sense (passive):** the bearer knows when a creature within 60 ft is longing for something — not what, but that it is present. Like warmth from a fire through a wall.
- **Steadfast (1/long rest):** when the bearer would fail a save against Charmed or Frightened, they may pull one thread as a free action. If the vision is of the person they most longed for, they may reroll the save with advantage.

### Curse: Thread-loss

**Each vision consumes one thread.** The spool begins with 10 threads at attunement. Each thread pulled removes one; when all 10 are gone, the specific longing that anchored the bearer's emotional life is **permanently forgotten** — the object of the longing is gone from memory. The bearer retains an emotional residue (a general ache, a sense of loss, a persistent incompleteness) but cannot identify what they are aching for or who was at its center. Greater Restoration cannot restore it.

**Tracking:** the DM tracks how many threads have been pulled. The session log must note this.

**When 5 threads remain:** the bearer begins to notice the visions are shorter. The DM may note this.

**When 2 threads remain:** the vision-duration drops to 3 seconds per thread. The bearer feels the loss approaching.

**When 0 threads remain:** the bearer's most-longed-for person or thing is gone from memory. It is not painful in the moment — it is quiet. The absence is noticed only by contrast: someone mentions a name the bearer can no longer place, or a smell triggers nothing where once it triggered everything.

### The Blank-Thread Trap

If the bearer's most-longed-for has **renounced all connection to the bearer** — not death (dead people can be longed for); active renunciation, disavowal, cutting of bonds — the first pulled thread shows blank. A blank-thread forces the spool to **unwind all remaining threads simultaneously**, one vision per round until they are gone. The bearer cannot let go of the spool during a blank-thread event (Strength DC 14 per round to release; advantage if the bearer has a friend physically restraining their hand).

**This is not a realistic risk for the Varduin Muster.** Nenn Hillfoot did not renounce Thera; he left. That is different. Adda is dead; not renounced. The Blank-Thread Trap is theoretical for this session.

### Ending the curse

1. **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** forge ≥1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Destroys the spool. Threads consumed so far are **gone permanently** — those longing-memories do not return. Remaining threads' longing returns to the bearer as a single named clarity: *"This is what I longed for. I know its name now."* The bearer will not forget again. This is the cleanest ending but does not un-consume what was already pulled.
2. **Reciprocal naming:** the bearer names their longing aloud — specifically, by name, as an acknowledgment not a request — while the object of that longing is present and listening. *"I longed for you. I am naming it. I am not asking you for anything."* The thread re-spools; attunement ends; the longing returns, un-frayed, permanent. **This requires the object of the longing to be alive, present, and willing to hear.** For Thera: Nenn is alive and present. She could name her longing for him (not Adda — Nenn). He would hear it. This is the golden-path ending.

## Hooks (PC-specific)

- **Thera + Adda (dead) or Nenn (present).** Thera's most-longed-for: almost certainly Adda — her mother, dead since Thera was 8. If Thera attunes and sees Adda, the reciprocal-naming ending requires naming it to Adda *in her presence*, which is impossible. She would need to consume threads or use the Reorx-rite. **BUT:** if Thera's most-longed-for is actually Nenn — the father who was absent but alive — the Thread will show her Nenn. And Nenn is standing right there. The reciprocal-naming ending is immediately possible. **Option C mandatory.** The Thread itself is the test of which longing is deeper.
- **Grom + Duren.** Grom's most-longed-for is almost certainly Duren. The Thread would show him Duren — specifically the moment Duren said *"inside-curve is the honest one"*, because that is when Grom was most paying attention. Grom would not pull a thread unless asked or unless something went wrong. His heuristic says: mend, not consume.
- **Kessa + Vethrenn.** Vethrenn never met Kessa; the Mirror and Circlet both suggested Vethrenn's love/admiration was transmission more than personal feeling. The Thread would show Kessa... Vethrenn's workshop, probably. Not Vethrenn herself. The vision would be an absence with a name. Appropriate.
- **Aelric + Varran.** Aelric remembers Varran only as dying. The Thread's vision of Varran would be the sixty-second Minute — the only Varran-memory Aelric has. He would see Varran dying. This is the worst possible vision. He should not attune the Thread. He knows this.
