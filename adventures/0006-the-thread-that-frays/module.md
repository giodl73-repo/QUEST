# The Thread-That-Frays — DM Module

*Thera Hillfoot has carried a letter addressed to her father for six years without knowing whether she was meant to open it or deliver it. She finds Nenn Hillfoot at Stonfold — where Mira Vaenshold has been living for three months — and the Stonfold matriarch Tova has the Thread-That-Frays. The thread shows you what you longed for most as if it were still within reach. Thera's most-longed-for is her mother, who died when Thera was eight. But her father is standing right there.*

## Summary

A single-session adventure built around one person finding another person who does not know she is coming. The party rides from Korrath to Stonfold — a working smith-family compound eighty miles east of Crystalmir — on Council business: Tova Stonfold, the family matriarch, has an Aelwen artifact she wishes disposed of responsibly. When they arrive, Nenn Hillfoot is at the gate. Thera's father. He has been here eight months. He has not pulled a single thread on the Thread-That-Frays, because Tova would not let him and because he is afraid of what he would see.

Four overlapping decisions drive the session: Does Thera open the letter she has carried for six years? Does she attune the Thread? Does Nenn get to say what he has been holding for twenty-three years? Where does the Thread end up — destroyed, attuned, or named into silence? The session's required combat encounter (per S05 Tactician panel) arrives on the road before any of this: a Nerakan artifact-collector who has been trying to buy the Thread from Tova and has not yet accepted her silence as a no.

The sixth moon-silver artifact is the only one Aelwen forged from Stonfold-family silver rather than Vaenshold. That distinction — cooler, longing-keyed, a different vein entirely — connects to the third-vein synthesis that Kessa will carry into the #0007 finale.

## Hook

Two things arrive at the party's encampment within a day of each other:

**Brother Laen's referral courier:** *"Tova Stonfold has contacted the Council about an inherited Aelwen artifact she wishes to dispose of responsibly. She asked for a referral. I am referring you. — L."* The Council has learned from S03 and S04; they now route Aelwen-artifacts to the party instead of attempting to handle them directly.

**Thera's private knowledge:** She has carried a letter for six years — addressed to *"Nenn Hillfoot at an unknown address"* — that she found in Varduin's way-house records. She does not know if Nenn wrote it to her (using his own name as a return address) or if she was supposed to deliver it. She has never opened it.

The party has seed-tracker reasons to go to Stonfold beyond Thera's history: Tova Stonfold was named by Mira in S03 as the matriarch who would take her in; Kann's Stonfold finger-bone (Kessa carries it) is made from Stonfold-family moon-silver and links to both #0006 and the #0007 finale. The party's reasons converge. The road points east.

## Setting & Background

**Stonfold** is not a city. It is a working family holding — forge-halls, stone longhouses, a moon-silver vein in the hillside behind the main forge. The Stonfold family has worked this vein for two hundred years; it is older than the Vaenshold family's Abanasinian operation, started by a different ancestor in different circumstances. The two families have been competitors and occasional allies since Aelwen's time. The Stonfold vein produces silver that is cooler in color than Vaenshold work — a slight blue undertone rather than warm gold. Grom will notice this on first approach.

**Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith and Harel** arrived here three months ago (campaign-permanent, post-S03). Mira is Tova's cousin's daughter; Tova took her in because she owed Seralen something she never specified. Harel is now sixteen. She is carrying forge-tools. She is fine.

**Nenn Hillfoot** arrived eight months ago. He is not hiding. He is working the gate-tender job because the post is away from the main forge and he does not have to talk to people unless he wants to. He has repaired fourteen gates, three well-pulleys, and six storage-hall doors in eight months. No one asked him to do most of them.

**The Thread-That-Frays** has been in Stonfold family custody for eighty years, since Heva Aelwensdaughter sold it to Tova's family's ancestor in exchange for twenty years of Stonfold-vein ingots to keep the Vaenshold silversmith line running. Four generations of Stonfold women have refused to pull a thread. Tova pulled two, forty years ago. She saw her wife Hevara and her daughter as a child. She put the spool back in its case. She has not thought about it without pain since.

### Scene 1 — The Road to Stonfold (conditional encounter)

**Location:** Kharolis foothills, approach road from Crystalmir direction. **Time:** Day 3 of travel.

**Read-aloud:**

> The road to Stonfold climbs into country the maps call "hill-dwarf hold territory" and the hill-dwarves call "ours." Slate-grey stone, short pine, and the occasional sight-line to a snow-capped ridge that was there before Istar was a city. The road is real — worn smooth by two hundred years of Stonfold-family ore-wagons — but it is not welcoming. It accepts you because you are on it, not because it invited you.

**Features:**

- **The ore-road.** 15 ft wide; smooth stone; two centuries of Stonfold freight. Grom Stonecunning passive: the road's construction is different from Thorbardin outer-hold work — older style, pre-Cataclysm technique. (Note for new readers: the Cataclysm was a catastrophic event that reshaped Krynn's surface roughly three hundred years ago; pre-Cataclysm work is ancient by any standard.)
- **The Nerakan artifact-collector.** A small party — three humans and a cart — is camped at the road's side, blocking one lane. Their cart has a locked iron chest. They are polite. They are going to Stonfold. They have heard the family has an Aelwen artifact and have made an offer Tova has not yet declined.

### Scene 2 — Stonfold Holding, Arrival

**Location:** Stonfold gate and courtyard. **Time:** late afternoon Day 3.

**Read-aloud:**

> Stonfold is not a town. It is a holding — a walled compound of stone longhouses and forge-halls built into the slope of a hill that has a moon-silver vein running through its eastern face. He is watching the road. When the party comes into view, he stops spinning the ring. He looks at Thera. He says one word. He says: *"Thera."*

Come inside."* Does not wait. **As she leads the party through the gate**, the hinge swings true — no squeal, no resistance. Grom passive 13 / Stonecunning: recently re-fitted; good inside-curve work. Tova, unprompted: *"The gate-tender repaired that eight months ago. Nenn. Quiet man. Good with his hands."* **[P0 FIX: Nenn planted before Scene 03.]** If the party asks about the Thread: *"I will show it to you. But I will not hand it to you until the girl has spoken to her father. That is the condition."* [P0 FIX: safety net.] If asked who longs for someone here: *"You know him by the gate."*

### Scene 3 — Nenn and Thera (Courtyard or Gate-Side)

**Location:** Stonfold courtyard or gate-side, wherever the party leaves them space. **Time:** Day 3, late afternoon, immediately after Scene 2.

**Read-aloud:**

> Tova has taken the others inside. Mira has stayed at her forge but is not looking away. The courtyard is not quiet — the forges have their own noise — but the space between Thera and Nenn is as quiet as anywhere.

**The scene:**

This is the session's moral center. The DM does not script this. The DM plays Nenn; Thera's player plays Thera. The party is mostly inside; Grom may have stayed, or Kessa — neither should interrupt unless invited.

**Nenn's opening (after Scene 2's pause):**

*"You're thirty-one."* Not a question. He has been counting. *"Last I saw you, you fit in one arm."*

He does not say: I'm sorry. He does not say: I had to leave. He has said both of those things in his head for twenty-three years. They are not the first things.

**What Nenn will say if asked directly:**

- *"Why did you leave?"* — *"Because the forge-hall where your mother used to buy her herbs still smelled like her. I could not get past it. I knew what that was doing and I left anyway. That is the answer. I do not have a better one."*
- *"Did you know I had the letter?"* — *"No. I left it at the way-house. I did not know you had it until now."* He looks at her hands when he says this, to see if she has it.
- *"Why didn't you come back?"* — *"I kept moving. Then I stopped moving here. I was going to write again. With an address this time."*
- *"Do you remember her?"* — *"Every day. That has not changed."*

**The letter (if Thera opens it in this scene):**

If Thera takes the letter out and opens it in front of Nenn, he goes very still. When she finishes reading, he says: *"I wrote that when you were thirteen. I did not send it because I did not have an address."* And then: *"I have one now. If you want it."*

**Tova's briefing:**

The one she couldn't finish."* **This is the deepest synthesis of the campaign so far.** Flag as SURPRISE candidate.

### Scene 5 — The Spool

**Location:** Stonfold archive, front of the unlabeled case. **Time:** Day 3 evening, after briefing.

## Treasures

### The Thread-That-Frays (central artifact)

*A small spool of moon-silver thread, Stonfold-vein — not Vaenshold. It vibrates when someone nearby is longing for something. Everyone longs for something. It is always vibrating.*

**Appearance (player-facing).** The spool is smaller than a fist — three inches across, two inches tall. Stonfold-family moon-silver: cooler in color than Vaenshold silver, with a slight blue undertone rather than the warm gold of Vaenshold work. The thread on the spool is very fine; it catches light like a spider's web in morning frost. There is no visible seam on the spool; Aelwen forged it as a single piece. The thread appears to be wound around a core, but the core is also thread — it is threads all the way down; there is no central rod. First-time holders often think the vibration is their own pulse. It is not their pulse.

**Rarity:** Very Rare · attunement required (first pulled thread completes attunement) · no kin prerequisite.

**Provenance.** Aelwen forged the Thread in **PC 179** — the fifth of her six secret forgings. The Thread is the only artifact Aelwen made from Stonfold-vein silver rather than Vaenshold-vein. She requested a quarter-pound of Stonfold raw material from Tova's grandmother's grandmother in PC 178, paying fair market price and noting in the invoice: *"For a forging that will not serve me."* Aelwen completed the Thread in late PC 179 and placed it with the other secret forgings. Heva Aelwensdaughter recovered it; it passed to Stonfold-family hands within a generation — sold to Tova's family's ancestor in exchange for twenty years of Stonfold-vein ingots to keep the Vaenshold silversmith line running. **The Thread has been in Stonfold hands for eighty years.**

**Attunement.** Fast-attune: the first pulled thread completes attunement. No short rest required. The thread is pulled; the vision begins; attunement completes during the vision. The holder can drop the spool before pulling (no attunement), but cannot stop attunement once a thread begins to unwind.

**What the vision shows:** the bearer's most-longed-for person or thing, as it existed at the peak of the bearer's longing — not idealized; *recalled*, made clear and close. For a dead mother: her hands on dough. For a father who left: his footsteps going away. Visions are specific, not symbolic. Duration: 1 round (6 seconds) per thread pulled. The bearer cannot speak to or touch what they see.

**Benefits (while attuned):**
- **Thread-vision (1/short rest):** pull one thread; see one vision. The vision grants advantage on Wisdom saving throws for 1 hour (the bearer has been reminded of what anchors them).
- **Longing-sense (passive):** the bearer knows when a creature within 60 ft is longing for something — not what, but that it is present. Like warmth from a fire through a wall.
- **Steadfast (1/long rest):** when the bearer would fail a save against Charmed or Frightened, they may pull one thread as a free action. If the vision is of their most-longed-for person, they may reroll the save with advantage.

**Curse: Thread-loss.** Each vision consumes one thread. The spool begins with 10 threads at attunement. When all 10 are gone, the specific longing that anchored the bearer's emotional life is **permanently forgotten** — not repressed; gone. The bearer retains an emotional residue (a general ache, a sense of loss, a persistent incompleteness) but cannot identify what they are aching for or who was at its center. **Greater Restoration cannot restore it.**

*Tracking:* the DM tracks how many threads have been pulled and notes this in the session log. When 5 threads remain: visions grow shorter; the DM may note this to the attuner. When 2 threads remain: vision-duration drops to 3 seconds per thread. When 0 threads remain: the absence is noticed only by contrast — someone mentions a name the bearer can no longer place, or a smell triggers nothing where once it triggered everything.

**The Blank-Thread Trap.** If the bearer's most-longed-for has **renounced all connection to the bearer** (not death — dead people can be longed for; active renunciation), the first pulled thread shows blank. A blank-thread forces the spool to unwind all remaining threads simultaneously, one vision per round until gone. The bearer cannot release the spool (Strength DC 14 per round to release; advantage if a friend physically restrains their hand). **This is not a realistic risk for the Varduin Muster this session.** Nenn left; he did not renounce. Adda is dead; not renounced. The Blank-Thread Trap is theoretical.

**Ending the curse:**

1. **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** forge ≥1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Destroys the spool. Threads consumed so far are gone permanently — those longing-memories do not return. Remaining threads' longing returns to all present as a single moment of clarity: *"I knew, for a second, what I was reaching for."* The bearer will not forget what they know in this moment.
2. **Reciprocal naming:** the bearer names their longing aloud — specifically, by name, as an acknowledgment not a request — while the object of that longing is present and listening. *"I longed for you. I am naming it. I am not asking you for anything."* The thread re-spools; attunement ends; the longing returns, un-frayed, permanent. **This requires the object of the longing to be alive, present, and willing to hear.** For Thera: Nenn is alive and present. She could name her longing for him. He would hear it. This is the golden-path ending.

**Hooks (PC-specific):**
- **Thera + Adda (dead) or Nenn (present).** If Thera attunes and sees Adda, the reciprocal-naming ending is impossible (Adda is dead). She would need to consume threads or use the Reorx-rite. But if Thera's most-longed-for is actually Nenn — the father who was absent but is alive — the Thread shows her Nenn, and Nenn is standing right there. The reciprocal-naming ending is immediately possible. Option C mandatory. The Thread itself is the test of which longing is deeper.
- **Grom + Duren.** Grom's most-longed-for is almost certainly Duren. The Thread would show him Duren — specifically the moment Duren said *"inside-curve is the honest one."* Grom would not pull a thread unless something went wrong. His heuristic says: mend, not consume.
- **Kessa + Vethrenn.** Vethrenn never met Kessa personally. The Thread would show Kessa Vethrenn's workshop, probably — an absence with a name. Appropriate.
- **Aelric + Varran.** Aelric remembers Varran only as dying (campaign-permanent: the 60-second Minute). The Thread's vision of Varran would be Varran dying. This is the worst possible vision. He should not attune the Thread. He knows this.

**Campaign cross-status.** This is the **fifth moon-silver artifact** encountered in the campaign (the sixth is the unnamed shame-keyed un-forged ingot — subject of Kessa's Hint 4 synthesis). The Thread is the only artifact forged from Stonfold silver. Tova's archive records indicate Aelwen researched a third vein ("the cold kind, not the grief-kind") when she forged the sixth. The Silver-Ingot Confession at #0007 requires three-vein accounting: Vaenshold (warm; grief-coiled), Stonfold (cool; longing-keyed), and the third vein (cold; shame-keyed; un-forged). Kessa's cross-referencing here may unlock that synthesis in-session.

### Nenn Hillfoot, 58 — Gate-Tender at Stonfold; Thera's Father

**Appearance.** Fifty-eight, human, Varduin-born. Shorter than average for a Varduin hill-crafter; wide across the shoulders from twenty years of gate-work and odd-jobs. Sun-wheat hair gone silver at the temples — the same color Thera's would be in thirty years. A small scar on his left thumb from a gate-latch accident. He wears the same soft leathers that hill-country workers wear. He does not look like someone who has been running. He looks like someone who stopped running and does not know if he should start again.

He has a silver finger-ring on his left thumb. Too large for the thumb; he spins it when thinking. The same ring Thera has, oversized on her thumb. Neither of them has ever said where the design came from. It was Adda's pattern.

**Background.** Born in Varduin-by-the-Pines. Married Adda Hillfoot at twenty-four. When Adda died of winter-sickness — Thera was eight — he held on at Varduin for three more years. Then he left. He has been leaving ever since: hill-roads, Nerakan border towns, Crystalmir-adjacent farmholds, and finally, eight months ago, Stonfold. He stopped because Tova offered steady work and the gate-post was away from the main forge and he did not have to talk to people unless he wanted to.

**He wrote the letter** that Thera has carried for six years — addressed to himself at the Varduin way-house as a return address, with Thera's name on the inside flap. He meant to send it to her but could never choose a permanent address to send it from. He left it at the way-house when he left Varduin. Thera got it somehow. He does not know this. He does not know Thera is coming.

**He longs for Adda.** This is why Tova will not let him near the Thread. The vibration near the spool when Nenn passes the archive is the loudest Tova has felt in forty years.

**Voice.** Very quiet. Few words — not because he is taciturn, but because he has been living in places where silence was easier than explanation. When he sees Thera: his first word is her name. Just: *"Thera."* And then nothing, because there is nothing to say first that does not require saying everything. He will not apologize before Thera asks him to. He left; she did not. He is aware of what that cost. He will say so if asked specifically. He will not volunteer it.

**What he wants.** Primary: to see Thera without destroying what remains of anything good between them. Secondary: to know how Adda is remembered. He was not there for whatever rituals Varduin kept. Tertiary: to not pull a thread. He has been told what it costs. He is afraid that if he sees Adda once, he will keep pulling until she is gone.

**Secrets.**
- **The letter says:** *"Thera. I left because the forge-hall where your mother used to buy herbs still smelled like her. I could not go past it without losing the whole day. I should have stayed for you. I knew that when I left and I still left. I am sorry. If you want to find me, I am moving east. I don't have an address yet. I will try to have one someday. — Nenn."* Written when Thera was thirteen. She has carried it since she was twenty-five.
- He also longs for Thera. The Thread vibrates for him from two directions — Adda, and the daughter he left. He cannot tell them apart. He thinks this is what longing feels like when you have earned it twice.
- He has been saving, carefully, over eight months, to buy a piece of Stonfold moon-silver to send to Thera at Varduin. He does not know she is no longer at Varduin. He was going to send it with a letter that said: *"I am at Stonfold. I am not moving again."*

**Stat block — Commoner; non-combatant; will not fight Thera's enemies:**

> **AC** 12 · **HP** 16 (3d8+3) · **Speed** 30 ft
> Str 12, Dex 12, Con 12, Int 11, Wis 14, Cha 11
> **Skills:** Perception +4, Survival +4 (hill-roads; decades of practice)
> **Senses:** passive Perception 14
> **Languages:** Common, hill-Halfling (conversational)

**Scene-by-scene:**
- Scene 2: at the gate. Sees Thera. Says: *"Thera."* Does not move. Waits.
- Scene 3: the session's emotional fulcrum. DM plays Nenn; Thera's player plays Thera. Grom may have stayed; should not interrupt.
- Scene 5: if Thera takes Nenn to see the Thread, the spool vibrates when he enters. He says: *"I can feel it."* He does not explain. He does not touch the case.
- Scene 6: present if Thera wants him there. He will not tell her what to do. He will watch.
- Scene 7: if the letter has been opened — *"I wrote it when you were thirteen. I did not send it because I did not have an address."* If the Thread was destroyed via reciprocal naming — Nenn has named the longing to Adda, aloud, in front of Thera and Grom. He does not know what happens next. He asks Thera: *"Are you coming back through Stonfold?"*

**GM notes.** Do not rush Scene 3. The party should watch. Only Grom will know what to say — and what Grom says is probably nothing. The letter is Thera's choice. She has it. Whether she opens it before or after talking to Nenn or not at all is her decision. The DM should not prompt this. He spins the ring. Thera spins the ring. They do not comment. If Thera stops spinning and looks at his: he stops spinning and looks at hers. Neither speaks.

### Attunement Rules (inline — SRD + Thread-specific)

- Standard attunement: short rest (1 hour), 3 items maximum, curse revealed on attunement.
- **Thread-That-Frays: fast-attune (first pulled thread; no short rest; cannot abort once thread begins unwinding).**
- **Curse is Thread-loss** — specific longing-memory, not emotional capacity. The bearer can still love. They cannot name what they are missing.
- **Blank-Thread Trap:** only triggers on active renunciation by the bearer's most-longed-for. Not applicable this session (Nenn left; did not renounce; Adda is dead, not renounced).
- Ending options: Reorx-rite (forge ≥1,800°C, Religion DC 15) OR Reciprocal naming (object of longing present, alive, and listening).

### Conditions Used (inline — SRD)

- **Charmed:** a charmed creature can't attack the charmer or target it with harmful abilities or spells. The charmer has advantage on social ability checks against the charmed creature.
- **Frightened:** a frightened creature has disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of its fear is within line of sight. The creature can't willingly move closer to the source.
- **Thread-loss (curse, not a standard condition):** permanent memory-loss of specific longing-object. Does not affect combat stats. Affects roleplay at DM discretion when the lost object is referenced.
