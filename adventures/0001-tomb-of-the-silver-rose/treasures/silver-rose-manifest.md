# Curse Symptoms Manifest — The Silver Rose of Vaenor

The dungeon housing the Silver Rose of Vaenor MUST express the memory-erosion curse in at least 3 of the following ways, distributed across at least 2 rooms. `dungeon-smith` reads this file and honors it.

## Required (≥3, distributed across ≥2 rooms)

- [x] **Visual symptom (rose-petal scoring).** Carvings defaced from inside the tomb, strike-marks shaped like rose petals. Placed: Room 03 (pillars + mosaic face), Room 06 (frescoes scored on 7 of 8 panels).
- [x] **Textual symptom (attribution drift).** A quote attributed to Aelwen in one room and to no one in another. Placed: **Room 04 (attributes the "shape of ourselves" passage to Aelwen's margin-hand) paired with Room 06 (same passage inscribed in a fresco caption, uncredited).**
- [x] **Textual symptom (orthographic drift).** The name Vaenor spelled *Vanor* on exactly one inscription where no one notices. Placed: **Room 02 (west-archway lintel, faint chisel)** — the tomb is forgetting the name of the woman it holds.
- [x] **Auditory symptom (repeated phrase).** A single sentence the DM reads in one room, then again — verbatim — in a later room, without drawing attention to it. **"And the winter was colder than any I have known."** First heard in Room 02 on a failed Wisdom save; repeated, without context, at the moment the Rose is taken in Room 06.
- [x] **Sensory symptom (dryness).** The tomb is dry everywhere except Room 02, and even the Room 02 water "falls at the pace of tears, not of groundwater." The cairn holds in its cold, does not weep except where grief was specifically installed.
- [x] **Reaction moment (fresco heals).** When the Rose is removed from the sarcophagus, **Panel 6 of the Room 06 frescoes** (Ilendra at her parents' funeral, faces previously scored away) briefly restores — faces visible — then re-scores before the party can record them. Exactly one fresco, exactly one heartbeat. The tomb is returning a memory it had stolen.

## Required (always)

- [x] **Architectural expression of the mechanism.** Memory-erosion → walls whose carvings are being edited. Placed in Room 03 (pillars) and Room 06 (frescoes).
- [x] **Earlier-room foreshadowing of the reveal.** Aelwen's Letter in Room 04 (Book III) names the memory-erosion mechanism explicitly. A party that reads it knows what the rose does before Room 06.

## Suggested (optional)

- [x] **Per-room memory fragments.** Added to the GM Notes of every room: a one-sentence beat from a prior bearer's perspective, read-only-if-party-lingers. Cast:
  - Room 01 — Aelwen's perspective (forger's walk)
  - Room 02 — Aelwen (forging nights)
  - Room 03 — Baron Edric (the gift)
  - Room 04 — Baron Edric (what he understood too late)
  - Room 05 — Vesk the Bone-Collector (his daughter)
  - Room 06 — Sir Ilendra (the moment of attunement)
- [x] **NPC in starting settlement who met a prior bearer.** Brother Laen's mother in Varduin-by-the-Pines met a Rose Knight in her youth; Laen has heard her story. (Documented in `npcs/brother-laen.md`.)
- [x] **Secondary object the curse has touched.** The silver rose-petal pressed into the cold ashes of Room 05's brazier — a piece that a prior bearer broke off as a "safer alternative" that wasn't.
