# The Silver Rose of Vaenor

*A rose of moon-silver, petals hinged sharp as razors. It is cold even through a glove.*

## Appearance

A single rose, about four inches across, sculpted from moon-silver (an alloy associated with Solinari's light — but this was not forged by a High Sorcery wizard, and the silver is tarnished in an unnatural way, darker along the petal-edges where it has touched unwilling hands). Seven petals, each hinged at the base so the flower can fold shut like a fist. The stem is barbed. Weighs about four ounces. It is always a little colder than the air around it, even in sunlight, even after a week carried against a body.

## Presence / Desire

The rose wants to be held, and it wants to be held *by the right hand.* It is drawn to the grief-carrier in a party — the character who has lost the most, whether they know it or not. In that hand it warms by a quarter-degree, and the bearer finds it fits — too well — the cup of their palm. In the hand of the ungrieving, the rose stays cold, and the barbed stem seems to angle inward. Set down, it does not tarnish; left too long untouched, it grows perceptibly colder, and the air around it dries, as if it is calling to be picked up.

The rose is silent. It does not speak. But it has preferences, and the DM should improvise those preferences freely: it fits in some pockets and not others; it chimes faintly — once, softly, in its lifetime — when the bearer passes near something it remembers from a prior bearer's life; it will not be carried in a bag with food; it prefers to be held. A bearer who ignores the rose's preferences is a bearer the rose is patient with. It has time.

## Provenance

- **Aelwen the Silversmith of Vaenshold — Forger (PC 180).** A hedge-talent working in the Baron's household. She accepted the Baron's commission to forge a relic of remembrance for his grieving daughter. Midway through the work, Aelwen began to forget her own daughter's face. She finished the rose under compulsion, burned her tools and her wax mold after delivering it, and wrote to the Ergothian abbot begging him to keep the Baron from giving the rose to Ilendra. The letter was never sent. Aelwen hanged herself a year later (PC 179). Her own daughter, **Heva Aelwensdaughter** (then ~18), told her mother's friends only: *"She looked at me like she was trying to place me."* Heva survived her mother, apprenticed to a different smith, and continued the Vaenshold silversmith line. Heva died at about 78 (~ PC 120). Her great-great-granddaughter, **Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith**, lives in Crystalmir-by-the-Fells at session time (PC 20) and knows family stories about "the Baron's rose" but nothing precise. Mira may become relevant in a future adventure.

- **Baron Edric of Vaenshold — Bereaved patron (PC 145, given; PC 143, died).** Commissioned the rose after his son Caen's death, for his daughter Ilendra. He gave it to her on the anniversary of her brother's funeral. He held it once, on the ride home from Aelwen's shop, and that night he woke weeping for a man he could not name. He died two winters later — no illness, no wound. The family chronicler wrote only: *"The Baron's hearth went cold."*

- **Sir Ilendra Vaenor of the Order of the Rose — Last wielder (received PC 145, buried PC 100).** Ilendra attuned at age twenty-six and did not age past fifty. Her fellow Rose Knights noticed she did not remember the children of her own sponsor, though she had stood godmother at their naming. She served with distinction in the Third Dragon War — unnaturally composed in battle, as if the rose had taken grief's edge out of her. By the last years, she kept a written list of the living, a list she added to when a new face became unfamiliar. She asked to be interred holding the rose, in a cairn that no Knight of the Rose would visit for penance. The Order agreed. It was the last request of a great knight who had forgotten what the Order was for.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
**Rare (effectively — see note).** Attunement **required.** No prerequisite of class or alignment, but see Curse.

*Note on rarity:* By casual appearance (`detect magic` without deeper inspection, or a merchant's appraisal), the rose reads as **uncommon** — a pretty silver trinket with a faint protective aura. The rare-tier effects are not detectable until attunement.

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Steady Heart.** Advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed. Grief cuts the edge off other emotions.
- **Relic of the Lost.** Once per long rest, the bearer may ask the rose a single question about a named deceased person — any named person, not only someone the bearer knew. The rose answers by causing the bearer to remember a single true fact about that person, not from any of the bearer's experience. (This is memory the rose has harvested from prior bearers and the tomb's slow accumulation.) The fact is always accurate, but may not be relevant.
- **Silent Step (limited).** While holding the rose in one hand, the bearer is treated as having proficiency in Stealth. (If already proficient, no effect.)

### Curse (while attuned)

**Memory Erosion.**

At the end of each long rest, the bearer must succeed on a **DC 11 Wisdom save** or lose one memory of a person they love. There is no warning in the moment — the player chooses, or the DM prompts them to choose, one specific memory (a particular day, a particular face, a particular quiet moment). That memory is *replaced* with a fragment of Sir Ilendra's grief for her brother Caen. The fragment is vivid, detailed, painful, and not the bearer's.

**First reveal:** the curse is not detectable until the **third** long rest the bearer holds the rose. On the third morning, the player is asked to describe the memory they lost in the prior two rests — and realizes they cannot. (On rests 1 and 2, the DM quietly notes which memories were taken; on rest 3, the DM prompts.)

**The DC increases by 1** per long rest failed, to a maximum of 18.

**Symptoms in fiction (before the reveal):**
- NPCs the bearer once knew feel slightly unfamiliar.
- The bearer increasingly uses the phrase "my brother" in conversation without noticing.
- The rose warms slightly, for the first and only time, after a lost memory.

### Ending the Curse

Required ritual:

1. The Rose must be returned to its **original cairn** (Room 06 of this adventure), under the **first light of Paladine's star** (any dawn on a day when the White Moon Solinari is waxing).
2. The bearer must offer, voluntarily and spoken aloud, **the memory of the person they love most in the world** — not a memory of a lost person, but of a living one (or a dead one most recent / most dear). This memory is *surrendered permanently*, not merely forgotten — the rose consumes it and folds its petals shut forever.
3. A witness who speaks the name of **Caen Vaenor** aloud at the moment of surrender closes the rite.

Lesser effects (*remove curse*, *greater restoration*) **suspend** the memory loss for the duration specified by the spell but do not end the curse. The next long rest resumes it.

If the rose is destroyed (it can be destroyed by being shattered on the altar of a High Sorcery wizard who willingly sacrifices their Test-bond — effectively, their attunement to their own magic): the curse ends for all living bearers, but any memories *already* lost are not restored. The silversmith Aelwen's daughter remained a stranger to her mother to the end.

## Hooks

- **The Bone-Collectors of Neraka.** A necromantic cult that has been tracking the rose since Ilendra's interment. They want it for their own purposes (the rose is, for them, a tool for reliable grief-harvesting at scale). The desiccated corpse in Room 05 was one of their scouts. Seeds adventure #0002.
- **Brother Laen.** The archivist will be deeply uncomfortable if the party reveals they know of the curse. He will offer them *more* coin to surrender it (up to 400 gp — double the usual rate for the Order's "discreet retrieval" contracts). Their refusal will escalate to Solamnic retrieval teams in future adventures.
- **The Vaenshold Line.** The Baron's line is extinct — but his cousin's line continues, and the current head of House Vaenor-Meryn is a woman who has heard family rumors and would pay a great deal to *see* the rose and then have it buried properly. A roleplay hook. She knows more of the lore than the Order admits.
- **Ilendra's unfinished list.** If the party finds Ilendra's journals (cross-reference to adventure #0003 — the Vaenshold archive, as yet unwritten), they will find a list of names of people Ilendra was trying to remember. Returning the rose's stolen memories to those names' families is a campaign-long quest.

## GM Notes

- The rose should feel like a **gift** in the first session and a **problem** in the second. Do not make the first rest a curse-trigger in live play — even though mechanically it is. Let the player discover what they've lost.
- The memory the player chooses to have erased is collaborative. Ask them for a specific memory of a specific loved one. Write it down in your DM notes. Describe it back to them next session — in Ilendra's voice — as the fragment they now carry.
- The ritual of ending is designed to hurt. A player who enters the ritual should be asked whether they are certain. The cure is a cost, not a reset.
- If the party simply returns the rose to Brother Laen and walks away, they never trigger the curse. That is a valid outcome and should not be punished. The curse requires attunement — the rose cannot compel unwilling attunement. (Though the rose DOES subtly invite attunement from anyone holding it for more than an hour: "it just fits in the hand," "you can almost hear it," etc.)
