# S01 LOG — The Tomb of the Silver Rose × The Varduin Muster

## Session Summary

The Varduin Muster descended into the Rose Cairn on the third day of the spring thaw, hired by Brother Laen of the Solamnic Rose Council's clerkwork to retrieve the Silver Rose of Vaenor. Over approximately two hours in-fiction, the party took the Scriptorium route (west from the Antechamber), read Aelwen's Letter (recognizing the artifact as unsanctioned grief-magic), descended through the shaft to Room 05, discovered Vesk the Bone-Collector's desiccated corpse and his daughter Pella's crayon drawing, and entered the Sarcophagus Chamber.

Finding a sword-key slot in Ilendra Vaenor's carved stone fist but lacking a key, the party elected to backtrack — but an atmospheric collapse of the east archway in Room 02 forced a longer ascent via Room 05's stair — and fought four skeletal attendants in the Pillar Hall. Thera recovered the Notched Longsword in the combat's opening strike. The party returned to Room 06, used the sword as key (Option B), and retrieved the Silver Rose without triggering Ilendra's Animated Armor.

At the moment of retrieval, the tomb expressed three of its curse symptoms simultaneously — the repeated phrase ("*and the winter was colder than any I have known*") returned verbatim for the first time since Grom's failed save in Room 02; Panel 6's frescoes healed for a single heartbeat, and Grom (critical Perception) saw Ilendra's parents' faces clearly; Panel 3's caption briefly reacquired Aelwen's attribution, which Kessa alone witnessed. Ilendra's Memory Echo appeared and spoke her one line; Kessa spoke Caen's name and the Echo wept silver light for a heartbeat; Grom showed her Pella's drawing and received the reward-line, *"Then someone is still remembering."*

The party exited to a suddenly warm wind. They returned to Varduin and negotiated with Brother Laen: they surrendered the rose and Aelwen's Letter for 300 gp plus four letters of Solamnic service, retaining Pella's drawing, the silver rose-petal, the Notched Longsword, and — critically — **Kessa's full transcription notes**, which Laen conceded but asked her not to publish. **No PC attuned to the rose.** The session closes with the rose in the Rose Council's vault by morning, the party divided about whether the arrangement will hold, and a Bone-Collector signet in the party's shared purse — seeding adventure #0002.

## Scene 1 — The Way-house at Varduin (hook)

### Setup

The common room of the Varduin way-house, mid-morning, third day of the thaw. The fire was down to red embers; Thera had let it go on purpose because the way-house steward was slow to refill the cordwood and she was making a small quiet point about it. Kessa sat at the corner table with a book face-down; Grom was at the hearth examining the andirons for wear. Aelric stood near the door because Aelric always stood near a door.

Brother Laen came in from the stable-yard with his folio under his arm and the particular posture of a man who had rehearsed his sentences on the walk from the rectory.

### Beats

**Brother Laen** — formal, diffident, addressed Aelric first because Aelric was his junior within the Order. *"I have an errand that is properly mine, and I would prefer to pass it to your care. The Rose Council wishes to retrieve a sigil of the fallen Vaenor line from the Rose Cairn on the Dergoth bluff. Eight miles south-southwest of here. The sigil is to be returned unopened to me. Eighty gold for the retrieval, and a letter of Solamnic service to each of you who wants one."*

**Aelric:** formal acceptance. *"We would be obliged, Brother. May I ask the sigil's form?"*

**Brother Laen:** *"A rose of silver. Moon-silver, I am told, though my expertise in metals is poor. It will be set in the carved breastplate of the sarcophagus within."*

**Kessa** — quiet until this point; put the book face-up. *"May I ask who made it?"*

**Brother Laen**, quieter: *"A provincial silversmith. The maker is not the Council's concern."*

**🎲 Kessa — Arcana (discerning the sigil's magical character from Laen's description)** — `1d20+5` → rolls=[20] mod=+5 **total=25 CRIT**

> Kessa went still. She had spent the winter waiting for a signal. She understood, in the five seconds between Laen's sentence and her own reply, that this was the signal — that "a rose of moon-silver made by a provincial silversmith" was the exact phrasing Vethrenn's commonplace book had used on a line Kessa had read four hundred times. The margin-note was overwritten; the line above it was not. *"A rose of moon-silver, made provincially, carries the kind of resonance the Test does not name."* She had read it at fourteen and never understood it. She was understanding it now. <!-- SURPRISE — Kessa recognizes the adventure from Vethrenn's book before entering the tomb. Changes her playstyle priority from "record" to "rescue-a-memory." -->

**Kessa**, very level: *"We accept."* (She spoke before Aelric. Aelric blinked; did not object.)

**Thera** — bored, pretending to be bored. *"Aye, we'll go."* Then, after a beat: *"Brother Laen, did you eat before you came here?"* (One of her signature one-odd-questions.)

**🎲 Thera — Insight (trying to pierce Laen's dissembling)** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[8] mod=+3 **total=11** (Laen's DC 16; **fail**)

> Laen hesitated at the question — the wrong-topic opening had thrown him for a second — but he answered smoothly. *"Tea, nothing more."* Thera nodded, took the answer, and did not press. She could not read him. He was better at this than he looked.

**Grom** — offered Laen a seat by the hearth for the tail of the conversation. *"Come down from the door, Brother. You've walked three miles already today."* Laen sat, uneasily. Grom gave him a cup of cold tea. *"You'll have the rose by sundown tomorrow, Reorx willing."*

### Resolution

Contract accepted. 80 gp on return; letters of service promised. Laen did not volunteer the curse; Thera did not extract it; Kessa did not tell the others what she had just understood. They left the way-house with packs at noon; weather clear, cold, the wind dropped.

**Scene 1 outcome:** Party has the errand. Kessa carries a private recognition none of the others share. No resource delta.

### Wrapping the rose

Thera produced a spare silk handkerchief from her pocket (halflings carry handkerchiefs; it is known). She did not touch the rose with a bare hand — Grom had warned her, and she had listened. She wrapped it, folded it twice, and tucked it into an inner pocket of her leather vest.

No one attuned. No one asked. The rose would travel in Thera's pocket until the walk home. Nobody was volunteering to wear it.

Aelric retrieved the Notched Longsword from Ilendra's stone fist (the slot released it cleanly, like a lock returning a key), and tucked it under his own arm for the walk back. He took nothing else from the sarcophagus — he did not disturb the lid. Ilendra's pendant, her sword, her armor-carved on the sarcophagus-lid: undisturbed. This was Option B. The party had come for the rose and taken only the rose.

Grom placed Pella's drawing back in Thera's breast pocket. He did not keep it. *"The child's. Not ours."*

## Scene 9 — Egress and Return to Varduin

### Beats — the walk out

The ascent was quiet. Through R05 up the shaft to R04 (silk rope; all climbed cleanly, Climb DC 10 auto with rope). Through R02 (Kessa paused at the VANOR lintel; did not comment; wrote its exact glyph-shape in her notebook one more time, with a tiny new mark next to it — a question mark she had not used before). Up the R01 stair. Through the capstone.

The wind **was warm.** They felt it at the instant they cleared the stone. Aelric said: *"It was not this warm when we went down."* Grom said: *"It was not."* Thera, pragmatically: *"The cairn stopped being the cairn."*

Exhaustion faded as they stepped onto the bluff-grass (per `wandering-pressure.md` outcome 3 — exhaustion fades outside the tomb). All four recovered to L0 within a hundred paces.

The walk to Varduin took until dusk. Nobody spoke much. Thera did not take the rose out of her pocket. Kessa wrote continuously in her notebook while walking (she had practiced this during her Qualinesti tower years). Grom hummed a Reorx-hymn under his breath and did not notice he was humming. Aelric walked rear-guard with the Notched Longsword under his arm, watching the plains; nothing moved out there.

### Beats — Brother Laen at the way-house

The common room was emptier than the morning. Brother Laen had been waiting near the hearth with a cup of cold tea, identical to the one Grom had poured him twelve hours earlier. He stood when they entered. His hand went to the folio.

Aelric spoke first. *"Brother Laen. The errand is complete."*

Thera produced the rose — still in its silk-wrapping — and laid it on the table between them without unwrapping it. She did not open it for him.

**Laen's eyes did not leave the silk.** He did not unwrap it either. He simply counted to three internally — Aelric could see the count in his throat — and then he opened the folio.

**Grom placed Aelwen's Letter on the table beside the rose.** Laen's hand stopped.

### The negotiation

Per Brother Laen's behavior matrix:

> *"Presents Aelwen's Letter" → Goes very quiet. Offers **300 gp** instead of 80. Takes the letter as 'archive material.'"*

**Brother Laen:** *"...Where did you find this?"*

**Grom:** *"Room adjacent to the burial chamber. Stone lectern. Three books. This one was sealed into a bound copy. We broke no seal that was not already broken."*

Laen picked up the letter. Read it. Read it again. Laid it down with deliberate care, as if it might leave a mark on the wood.

**Brother Laen:** *"I will be plain with you. The Council has known this letter existed. It has been missing for ninety years. We assumed it was destroyed. The Council will pay **three hundred gold** for the rose, the letter, and your silence on both. I would be grateful if you accepted this offer. I would be grateful if you did not."*

He looked up.

*"Sir Aelric. You will want to know whether this satisfies the Measure. It does not. The Measure requires truth. The Council's interest requires concealment. I am asking you to prefer the Council's interest. I cannot defend the ask. I can only make it."*

Silence in the way-house. The fire had gone low.

**Aelric closed his eyes for a breath.** His hand did not move toward the rose. His heuristic said *Oath first; Order's honor second.* The Oath required truth; the Order was asking for silence. These were in conflict. Per the Measure, when in conflict — *per the spirit, not the letter* — the higher Order-level interest took precedence, because the individual knight's oath existed to *serve* the Order, not to overrule it.

**🎲 Aelric — Charisma (Insight) on Laen's sincerity (DC — contested by Laen's resolve)** — `1d20+4` → rolls=[9] mod=+4 **total=13**

(Inconclusive. Aelric felt Laen was being *honest about the difficulty of the ask*, which is not the same as being *trustworthy in the long run*.)

**Aelric opened his eyes.** *"Three hundred. And the letters of service for each of us. And — I will say this once, Brother — if the Council brings this errand back to me in a different form in a different year, I will decline. I will not be asked to cover a thing I have uncovered."*

**Brother Laen, quietly:** *"Agreed."*

**Thera shrugged.** *"Three hundred's fine. I'll take mine in Palanthian silver; the Varduin coin's getting debased."*

**Kessa spoke last.** *"I keep the transcription of my notes. I will not publish them. But I will not destroy them."*

**Brother Laen:** *"...Keep your notes. Do not publish."*

**Grom did not speak.** He looked at Laen, and Laen looked at Grom, and something small and true passed between them — the recognition of a man who has compromised meeting a man who is not yet ready to — and the money was paid, and the rose was taken, and the letter was taken, and the errand was closed.
