## Verdict

**ADVISORY** — first 3 sessions are not gate-binding (rubric calibrating). Score recorded for trend analysis. Advisory-score of 65/80 sits comfortably above the proposed 56/80 threshold; a working session.

## Dimensions Below Threshold (7/10 cutoff)

None below 7. Lowest: Mechanical fairness and Pacing, both at 7/10. Raising either would require:

- **Mechanical fairness:** redesign Cold Pulse outcome 3 so it does not directly damage atmospheric-symptom Investigation rolls. Suggestion: outcome 3 should cost HP or a spell slot rather than inflict check-disadvantage.
- **Pacing:** module could compress the Scriptorium's reading sequence (e.g., "read any one book for the narrative hook; a second book for the shaft-unlock"), reducing the triple-read structure.

## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Exhaustion mechanic eats atmospheric payoffs
Dimension affected: Mechanical fairness + Atmospheric landing
What happened: Cold Pulse outcome 3 disadvantage caused Thera to miss R03 pillar inscription (rolled 8 kept die on 14/5); caused Aelric to miss post-combat Religion no-necro-aura reveal (rolled 8 kept die on 12/8).
Why the rubric missed it: The rubric treats Mechanical fairness and Atmospheric landing as independent dimensions. This innovation shows they can interact destructively: a "fair" mechanic can actively destroy an atmospheric payoff. The rubric needs a cross-dimensional anchor.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Pre-adventure character hook inverts session center-of-gravity
Dimension affected: Character agency + Engagement + Surprise
What happened: Kessa's CRIT Arcana on Laen's description in Scene 1 revealed the adventure was already Vethrenn's rose — a connection only her PC sheet carried. Her playstyle shifted from "record" to "rescue-a-memory" before the dungeon began.
Why the rubric missed it: The rubric assumes session-scope choices. A PC-sheet-level hook that pre-aligns can reframe the entire session's meaning for that character without any in-session dice.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Craft-ritual PC receives atmospheric flags knowledge-casters miss
Dimension affected: Atmospheric landing + Character agency
What happened: Grom (hearth-greeting cleric) failed the voice-fragment save in R02 (rolled 6/DC 10) — he was the one the tomb spoke to. Kessa (diviner wizard) passed (rolled 12). The wisdom-caster did not receive the atmospheric flag; the craft-rite PC did.
Why the rubric missed it: "Atmospheric landing" is scored as session-level. It does not anticipate that *which PC* receives a flag can be counter-intuitive and character-defining.
```
