## Critique

### Overall

This was a character-forward session. Four PCs, four voices, four distinct paradigms. The DM did not rotate spotlight mechanically — moments landed because a particular PC's playstyle heuristics made them the right fit for that moment. That's the hardest kind of roleplaying session to run well, and this one ran it.

### What worked

- **Grom showing Ilendra's Echo the Pella drawing.** *"I brought it because I thought you should know: someone is still remembering."* (Scene 8.) This is the session's strongest character work. Grom's playstyle heuristic "mend first, keep the party whole second, honor Reorx third" routed him to this action. The module scaffolded it (Pella's drawing unlocks the Echo's second line); the session *earned* it (Grom had to have picked up the drawing in R05, which required Thera's Investigation 22 and Grom's attention to the brazier's inside-curve — and then he had to offer it, which required his forge-priest's impulse to *witness* the dead). Every one of those links was character-driven.
- **Aelric's condition on Brother Laen: "I will not be asked to cover a thing I have uncovered."** (Scene 9.) That sentence is pure Solamnic Measure. It's the Oath working *through* Aelric rather than being recited by him. His character sheet's Ideal is *"The Measure is a mirror. If I cannot stand to look, I must change what looks back."* Scene 9's negotiation made him look, and he changed what looked back. This is Aelric growing.
- **Kessa's private investigation as parallel arc.** (Scenes 1, 4, 6, 8.) She carries a prior-adventure hook — Vethrenn's margin-note — that only her character sheet provides. The session let her prosecute it quietly, writing in her notebook, re-crediting Aelwen's line silently in R06, never telling the party. That's a Roleplayer's dream session-thread: a character pursuing their own arc *inside* the shared arc, and the shared arc not being diminished by it.
- **Thera's grief-chain-reaction to Pella's drawing.** (Scene 8.) No save, no check — the emotional density of another PC's moment triggered Thera's memory of her mother's last loaf of bread. That's the kind of interior beat the module didn't scaffold and the DM honored.

### What needs work

- **Aelric's role in Scenes 2-4 was observational, not agentic.** He had no meaningful choice until Scene 6's "go get the key" vote and Scene 9's Laen negotiation. The Paladin defaulted. That's in-character (he defers on non-Oath questions), but four scenes of deferral is thin.
- **Voice tags are inconsistent.** Grom's forge-metaphor vocabulary is consistent throughout ("ash and cinder," "by the Black Anvil," "needs hammering out"). Aelric's Solamnic formal register holds in dialogue but the narration isn't always matched. Thera's Abanasinian hill-vernacular is present but could be thicker. Kessa's "too many words under stress" is well-represented in dialogue but undercited in narration.
- **Nobody attuned to the rose, but no PC argued *for* attunement.** This feels like a missed character beat. Kessa's "understand it" priority would plausibly push her toward attunement despite Test-violation; the session let the non-attunement default happen rather than foregrounding the argument.

### The question I'd push on

**If four PCs with four distinct griefs all kept their griefs private through this entire session, is the adventure's curse actually operative as a character threat, or only as an object?** The Rose erases memory of loved ones. Not one PC spoke, aloud, to another PC, about the loved one the rose would target. The grief paragraphs are real on the character sheets. They did not travel to the table. The session's deepest character work (Grom + Pella) touched *someone else's* grief, not the PC's own. I want a session where one of these four PCs says their lost person's name aloud to another PC. That will be S02's roleplay gate.
