**Panel verdict:** 65.0 / 80 weighted mean. Above the advisory threshold of 56/80. Spread of 3 points across lenses — tight consensus, which is expected for a session that hit most of its designed beats. The lowest-scoring lens (Fresh Face) and highest (Roleplayer) diverge on accessibility-vs-density — the most durable philosophical tension in the roster.

## Strongest Consensus

**Character agency (9/10 unweighted).** All five lenses praised real decisions by real PCs: Kessa's "west first," the "go get the key" vote, Grom's Pella-drawing offering, Aelric's Laen condition. The session's final state depended on specific PC choices. This is what the workshop designed for; this session proves the design pattern works.

**Atmospheric landing (9/10 unweighted).** The fresco heal, the Memory Echo interactions, the repeated phrase symmetry, the warm wind on egress — multiple, memorable, named. Lorekeeper and Roleplayer lenses each singled out the moments as sessionworthy; even Fresh Face (lowest weight on this dimension) granted it.

**Surprise (9/10 unweighted).** 12 `<!-- SURPRISE -->` tags. Improviser specifically noted this count is healthy. Tactician treats surprise as secondary but conceded the tags are substantive.

## Most Dissent

**Mechanical fairness (7/10).** Tactician weighted this at 1.6 (highest weight across the roster) and identified the Cold Pulse outcome 3 as a cross-dimensional collision (mechanical fairness destroying atmospheric landing). Fresh Face weighted it at 1.1 and wanted the mechanic made more visible to new readers. Roleplayer weighted it at 0.5 and barely cared. The dimension holds different stakes for different lenses; this is the rubric's design doing its job.

**Pacing (7/10).** Every lens flagged Scene 4's Scriptorium sequence as slightly over-long. Nobody flagged the climax or combat as mistimed. A consistent note across all 5 lenses makes this a real convergent suggestion.

## Convergent Suggestions (multiple lenses agree)

1. **Cold Pulse outcome 3 needs redesign.** Tactician and Fresh Face both flagged that disadvantage-on-ability-checks actively destroys atmospheric payoffs. Gate scorecard's Innovation Flag #1 documents the same pattern. **Unified fix:** change outcome 3 from "Con save DC 10 or 1 exhaustion" to "1d4 psychic damage, no save" or "expend 1 hit die" — costs resource without damaging atmospheric rolls.

2. **Scriptorium reading sequence could be compressed.** Every lens flagged it. **Unified fix:** modify R04 so reading *any one* book unlocks a hint and *any two* unlocks the shaft. This reduces the triple-read structure to a double-read minimum while preserving the attribution-drift seed.

3. **New-reader accessibility needs a convention.** Fresh Face and Lorekeeper both flagged: "Solamnic," "Portent," "Test of High Sorcery," Oath translations are used without in-fiction glossing. **Unified fix:** declare a workshop policy on accessibility. If sessions are for experienced Dragonlance readers, say so in the blueprint. If not, add a pass of in-fiction glosses to module.md.

4. **Grief-paragraphs should travel to the table.** Roleplayer noted that no PC said their lost loved one's name aloud to another PC. Improviser noted the session's deepest grief-work was about Vesk's daughter, not the PCs' own dead. **Unified fix:** S02's hook should include an NPC who asks a question about a PC's specific named dead. Force the grief-paragraph into dialogue.

5. **Workshop-canon vs. Dragonlance-canon needs a declaration.** Lorekeeper flagged invented locations (Varduin, Crownhold, Vaenshold) without a canon-policy. **Unified fix:** create `reference/dragonlance/workshop-canon.md` declaring what's ours vs. Dragonlance's.

## Divergent Suggestions (single-lens; keep or ignore)

- **Tactician:** Wandering outcome 4 collapse should be d3-randomized among specific corridors, not DM-choice. *Good procedural discipline; low cost to implement.*
- **Roleplayer:** S02's roleplay gate — require at least one PC to speak a lost loved one's name aloud to another PC. *Strong character-forward push; could be prescriptive.*
- **Roleplayer:** Aelric needs more agency in mid-scenes; the Paladin defaulted for four scenes before Scene 6's vote. *Module-specific; could be addressed by adding a Paladin-specific hook to R01 or R02.*
- **Lorekeeper:** Aelwen's daughter needs a name if she's going to be a thread. *Small fix; high leverage for continuity.*
- **Lorekeeper:** `reference/dragonlance/key-events.md` needs to exist to cite Third Dragon War dates. *Deferred item from original Phase 1.*
- **Improviser:** The "try any sword in the slot" beat was skipped. *Minor module addition: add a DC 10 Investigation option to recognize the wrong sword won't fully turn the mechanism.*
- **Fresh Face:** Cold Pulse / Wandering rolling schedule should be visible to a new player. *DM-facing vs. player-facing tension; solution may be a sidebar in module.md.*

## Questions Raised About the Module or the System

1. **Cold Pulse outcome 3 is a cross-dimensional problem.** Flagged by Gate and reinforced by Tactician + Fresh Face. Belongs to `playtest-innovation` as a dimension-2+ candidate (Mechanical fairness + Atmospheric landing simultaneously).

2. **Pre-adventure PC hooks can reframe sessions.** Flagged by Gate's Innovation Flag #2 (Kessa recognizing the rose from Vethrenn's book). A pattern worth watching for clustering.

3. **Atmospheric flags landing on counter-intuitive PCs.** Flagged by Gate's Innovation Flag #3 (Grom hearing the voice-fragment, not Kessa). A pattern worth watching.

4. **The Pella interaction in Scene 8 is the single most-praised moment across all five lenses.** The multi-step setup (R05 attention → Grom's offering → Echo's reward) is what the workshop is FOR. A pattern to preserve and to reverse-engineer for S02's module design.

5. **Grief as private-to-PC-but-not-at-table.** Roleplayer's challenge. Not yet an innovation candidate; becomes one if S02 produces the same pattern.

## Panel Verdict (unified)

S01 is a working first session at 65.0 / 80 weighted. It is advisory-only; the rubric is calibrating. Two of the five convergent suggestions (Cold Pulse redesign; canon declaration) are priority-1 for workshop-level changes. Three (Scriptorium compression, accessibility convention, grief-at-table) are priority-2 for S02 planning.
