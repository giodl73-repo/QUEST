## Critique

### Overall

The Age of Might held. Specifically, it held in the details that a post-Cataclysm gazetteer wouldn't give you: Brother Laen as Knight of the Crown doing clerkwork for the Rose Council (correct pre-Cataclysm hierarchy); Varduin as a Solamnic-maintained way-house (correct for a minor hill-village in that era); the Plains of Dergoth wind (specifically-spring-wind, not generic fantasy-wind); the Oath inscription over the R05 door in a context that made Aelric recognize his own vow. Someone read `reference/dragonlance/` closely, or is holding it well. These are small things. They are the right things.

### What worked

- **The Order hierarchy.** (Scene 1 and Scene 9.) Brother Laen is explicitly a Knight of the Crown, the entry-order, doing clerkwork for the Rose Council. The Crown/Sword/Rose hierarchy is used correctly. The Measure vs. the Oath distinction surfaces organically in Aelric's Scene 9 decision.
- **Aelwen as hedge-wizard, not Wizard of High Sorcery.** (Scene 1 Kessa Arcana CRIT.) This is the correct Dragonlance lore move: the Rose is *unsanctioned* grief-magic, not Test-passed magic. The session names this cleanly.
- **Kessa's background-lore surfaces.** (Scenes 1, 4, 6, 8.) Her connection to Vethrenn Silverglass and the commonplace book came from her PC sheet and the session let it matter. Vethrenn's silence ~PC 100 (Ilendra's era) is specifically documented in `reference/dragonlance/factions.md` territory, and the session respected the timeline.
- **VANOR as orthographic drift.** (Scene 3.) Thera's Investigation 20 landed on a detail that only someone who knew Solamnic family names would recognize as wrong. The module planted it; the session's PC composition landed it because Thera grew up in a Solamnic way-house.
- **The Cataclysm is future, not past.** The session is set in the Age of Might. The DM never let Cataclysm-era idiom leak in. When a post-Cataclysm reader might have expected a reference to "the ruined Istar," the session held that line. Good.

### What needs work

- **Varduin-by-the-Pines is our invention.** (Named in `premise.md` and reinforced through Scene 1 and Scene 9.) It is Dragonlance-plausible for Abanasinia but not canon. Future sessions should flag this as workshop-canon rather than Dragonlance-canon. The same applies to Crownhold (Aelric's family seat) and Vaenshold (Aelwen's and Baron Edric's location). These names need to be audited for conflict with established Dragonlance locations before any public sharing.
- **Aelwen's daughter is unnamed.** (Referenced in Aelwen's Letter, in Baron Edric's journal quote from Scene 4's Memory Fragment, and as a thread in the Open Threads.) If she's going to be a future-adventure thread, she needs a name now. *Right now* she is a lore-hole.
- **The Third Dragon War is referenced but not sourced.** (In the silver-rose.md provenance and Aelric's background.) `reference/dragonlance/key-events.md` is still deferred. The Third Dragon War reference needs that file to exist, or we need to pick a dating convention and document it. This is a `reference/dragonlance/` gap identified.

### The question I'd push on

**Is the workshop building Dragonlance, or building a Dragonlance-adjacent fiction that tolerates canon but does not require fidelity?** Session S01 could go either way. The Order-hierarchy discipline says "yes, Dragonlance." The invented locations (Varduin, Crownhold, Vaenshold) say "Dragonlance-adjacent." Pick one. If the answer is "Dragonlance," then invented locations should be marked in a `workshop-canon.md` file and future sessions should prefer canon locations. If the answer is "Dragonlance-adjacent," the workshop should be open about that when publishing.
