## Critique

### Overall

Clean dice. Every roll traced to seed `S01-20260418`. Attack rolls, saves, skill checks, wandering-table rolls — all logged with context and outcome. That is the first and most important Tactician test, and this session passed it cleanly. Combat in Scene 7 was brief (2 rounds) but tactically correct. Thera's Cunning Action → Hide → sneak-attack on the sword-bearer was textbook Thief play.

### What worked

- **Scene 7, Round 1, Thera's opener.** Cunning Action bonus to Hide behind a pillar produced advantage. `1d20+5 adv` → rolls=[14, 12] kept 14, +Bless `1d4` → 3, = 22 vs AC 13 hit. Sneak Attack +2d6 on advantage: 9+8=17 damage. Skel D (13 HP) destroyed in one turn. The party took out the target with the Notched Longsword on Round 1, Turn 1. That's a well-designed Thief target-priority move, and the module earned it by placing the key in the hands of a specific skeleton.
- **Scene 7, Round 2, Aelric's Smite.** `3d8` radiant added to `1d8+3` bludgeoning (×2 vulnerability on Skel C) totaled 33 damage. Skeleton's 6 HP → obliterated. The Paladin's slot-spend was calibrated: he saved smite for the finisher, not Round 1. That is tempo discipline.
- **Cold Pulse integration.** Outcome 3 (DC 10 Con save or exhaustion) produced real resource pressure. Aelric and Thera picked up L1 exhaustion; it carried into combat. That's the *kind* of attrition old-school DMs demand.

### What needs work

- **Scene 8, fresco-heal Perception rolls.** Aelric rolled 18/1 with disadvantage from exhaustion → kept 1 → fumble. Aelric missed the fresco-heal. This is the Mechanical-Fairness-vs-Atmospheric-Landing collision. A tactician doesn't mind that Aelric fumbled; a tactician minds that the *exhaustion mechanic is doing two jobs* — resource attrition AND atmospheric-payoff gating. **Recommend: separate the functions.** Cold Pulse outcome 3 should cost HP or a slot, not inflict check-disadvantage.
- **Scene 7, post-combat Religion check.** Aelric tried to identify absence-of-necromancy; rolled `1d20+2 dis` → rolls=[12, 8] kept 8, +2 = 10, failed DC 15. Another atmospheric payoff cost by exhaustion. Same failure mode. Two misses from one mechanic.
- **Wandering-table outcome 4 (collapse) at 30 min was adjudicated as "R02 east archway."** The module says "random; DM choice." Fine. But the DM's choice here happened to specifically reinforce the party's forced route (via R05 stair). This collapsed a fallback path — which is good drama, but a tactician notes this was DM-selected rather than randomized. A d3 among three possible corridors would be cleaner.

### The question I'd push on

**If exhaustion (disadvantage on ability checks) is the Cold Pulse's mechanical cost, how do you prevent it from consistently destroying atmospheric payoffs that depend on skill checks?** Every atmospheric-landing payoff in this module is gated by an Investigation or Perception check. Exhaustion affects those checks. Therefore Cold Pulse → atmospheric payoff missed, in every session where exhaustion lands. This is not a bug; it's the mechanic doing exactly what it was designed to do. The question is whether the design intended this side-effect.
