# Room 01 — The Cairn-Cap

## Read-aloud
> A shoulder of stone-and-turf crowns the bluff. Black pines lean from the wind. At the cairn's center, a flat capstone — carved with a rose so worn you could miss it. Cold air rises from around its edge: the stone is not flush. Something is beneath it.

## Features

- **The capstone.** A single slab, heavy. **Strength (Athletics) DC 13** to lever aside. Two characters cooperating: automatic success. Beneath it: a stone stair descending into the earth, ~15 ft.
- **The rose carving.** Weathered to near-illegibility. **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 13** reveals the rose has been **re-carved** over an earlier carving — someone in a previous generation tried to deface a sigil, and a later hand restored it. (Foreshadows the Order's ambivalence about Ilendra; clue for Patrick Stuart's lens.)
- **The wind.** Cold even for spring. **Wisdom (Survival) DC 10** notes the wind carries no insects, no bird-call — Nature finds this hill quieter than it should be.
- **The pines.** Small, crooked. No nests.

## Encounter
None. The first room should let the party arrive.

## Treasure
None in this room. The older re-carved line beneath the rose reads, if Investigation exceeds DC 15: *"Sister of Caen."* (Her brother's name was Caen.)

## Connections
- **Down (stair, ~15 ft):** Room 02 — Antechamber of Tears (`02-antechamber-of-tears.md`).

## GM Notes
- Do not narrate that the place is "haunted." Let the wind and the quiet do the work.
- If a player asks whether the area around the cairn is wet, say no — oddly dry given the spring weather.
- Brother Laen does not accompany the party into the cairn. He waits at the village way-house.

### Memory Fragment (optional — read only if the party lingers)
> *Aelwen the silversmith walked this path once, on a spring morning not unlike this one, to deliver a rose to a Baron. She remembered the walk. Later, she could not remember her daughter's name.*
