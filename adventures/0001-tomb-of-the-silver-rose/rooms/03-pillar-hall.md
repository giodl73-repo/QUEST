# Room 03 — The Pillar Hall

## Read-aloud
> Eight pillars, two rows of four, support a low vaulted ceiling. Each pillar is carved with a rose — some defaced, slashed across. The mosaic floor shows a woman in Solamnic plate accepting a silver rose from a figure whose face has been chiseled away. A stair descends through the far wall.

## Features

- **The pillars (eight, in two rows).** Each carved with the Solamnic rose. Three of the eight are **scored** — the carvings struck with a sharp tool sometime after burial. DC 13 Investigation identifies the strike-marks as *from inside the tomb* (i.e., after the sealing). Something in here has been taking issue with the sigil.
- **The mosaic floor.** A woman in Knight of the Rose plate (Ilendra) receives the silver rose from a second figure. The second figure's face has been **scoured away to bare stone** — again, from inside. DC 13 History recognizes the Solamnic rose armor. DC 15 Investigation guesses the scouring post-dates the laying.
- **The stair (east wall).** Descends ten feet to Room 05.
- **The silence.** Normal, until the party crosses the centerline between pillar-rows. Then: nothing. The silence *tightens.*

## Encounter
**See `encounters/03-pillar-hall-attendants.md`.**

**Trigger:** When any character crosses the centerline between pillar-rows, **four skeletons** rise from the floor — former attendants interred with Ilendra to wait on her in the next world. They animate not from undead-necromancy but from the rose's residue; a DC 15 Religion check after combat notices their bones bear no necromantic aura.

They fight silently. One carries a **notched longsword** (see Treasure below).

## Treasure

- **The Notched Longsword.** Carried by one skeleton. Masterwork quality but deeply notched, a chunk missing from the blade. DC 12 History: Solamnic knight's arming sword, missing a piece — the same piece that would fit a slot in Ilendra's sarcophagus lid (Room 06). Worth 15 gp as scrap, *priceless* as a key.
- **Attendants' gravegoods.** 25 gp in old Ergothian mint coinage (bagged in each attendant's ribcage), tarnished but intact. Mentioned in `treasures/minor-loot.md`.
- **Pillar sigil fragments.** A character who carefully takes a rubbing of one scored pillar (DC 10 Investigation, 10 min) may realize later (Room 06) that the striations match the rose's petal pattern.

## Connections
- **West (open archway):** Room 02 — The Antechamber of Tears.
- **East (stair down):** Room 05 — The Rose Stair.

## GM Notes
- The skeletons are tactical: they use the pillars for cover and try to flank. They do not pursue across the centerline if the party retreats.
- If the party backs out of the hall without attacking, the skeletons freeze mid-step and collapse to bones. They re-rise if anyone crosses the centerline again.
- This is the party's first mechanical hint that the rose actively damages what it sees. The scoured faces and slashed pillars are its work, slow, over decades.
- **Curse symptom — attribution drift.** The easternmost pillar bears a short inscription near its base: *"We do not carry the dead within us. We carry the shape of ourselves against their absence. — Aelwen."* A character who carefully notes it (Investigation DC 12) will later see the same sentence in Room 06, carved into a fresco caption, uncredited. The rose has taken Aelwen's name from one of the two inscriptions. Do not signpost which is the earlier truth.

### Memory Fragment (optional — read only if the party lingers)
> *The Baron held the rose once, on the ride home from Aelwen's shop, and that night he woke weeping for a man he could not name. The man's name was his son's. He never told his daughter he had held the gift first.*
