# Room 02 — The Antechamber of Tears

## Read-aloud
> The stair ends in a round chamber, twenty feet across. Water weeps from one wall in a slow silver line, collecting in a stone bowl sunk into the floor. The bowl is half full. The air is cold and tastes of iron. Two archways leave the chamber, east and west.

## Features

- **The weeping wall (north).** Water seeps from a joint in the stone in a thread too slow to erode anything. Looking closely (Investigation, no DC): the water is falling at exactly the pace of tears, not of groundwater. Sample taken: clean, drinkable.
- **The stone bowl (floor, center).** Half full. DC 12 Arcana: the bowl is warded — it will never overflow, no matter how much water falls.
- **The air.** Anyone who rolls a Wisdom save DC 10 against the room's atmosphere hears nothing. Those who **fail** the save catch, at the edge of hearing, a woman's voice — mid-sentence — saying something like *"... and the winter was colder than any I have known ..."* and then gone. No mechanical effect. Ask the player to write the fragment down.
- **The east archway.** Open, unlocked. A faint *click-scrape* carries from somewhere beyond — the sound of stone settling against stone, at irregular intervals. Leads to Room 03.
- **The west archway.** Open, unlocked. A dry smell carries from it — old vellum, parchment dust, ink. Above the lintel, chiseled deeper than the natural wear accounts for: **VANOR.** A close look (Investigation DC 13) reveals the carver tried to spell *Vaenor*, left out the *ae*, and did not correct the slip. Leads to Room 04.

## Encounter
None. This is the Stuart room. Resist the urge to put a fight here.

## Treasure
None.

## Connections
- **Up (stair, ~15 ft):** Room 01 — The Cairn-Cap.
- **East (open archway):** Room 03 — The Pillar Hall.
- **West (open archway):** Room 04 — The Scriptorium.

## GM Notes
- The voice-fragment is the rose beginning to touch them. Players who keep the fragment and later write it into a "memory they lost" during the curse phase will feel the weight. Do not explain this.
- **The VANOR inscription is a curse symptom** — the tomb is forgetting the name of the woman it holds. Do not explain. If a player remarks on the misspelling, shrug. If a player later, in another room, sees *Vaenor* spelled correctly and asks about the disagreement, shrug. The tomb is sick; the world is not.
- If a character tries to **drink from the bowl**: no mechanical effect. The water tastes like rainwater. Ask what memory they were thinking of when they drank.
- If a character tries to **fill a flask with the water**: fine. They may later discover the water does not evaporate or spoil for months.
- Keep this room quiet. A party that breezes through will miss it, and that is a feature — this is an *invitation* to slow down, not a gate.
- **The repeated phrase.** The voice-fragment here — *"and the winter was colder than any I have known"* — will repeat, verbatim, in Room 06 at the moment the Rose is taken. Write it in your notes. Say it the same way both times.
