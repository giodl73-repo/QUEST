# Room 04 — The Scriptorium

## Read-aloud
> Three stone lecterns stand in a triangle; on each, a small bound book. The far wall is painted — a funeral. Bearers carry a young man's body down a hillside. A woman in Solamnic plate stands apart, watching. In the floor before you, a round iron shaft-cover set with a silver rose.

## Features

- **The three lecterns, each with a book.**
  - **Book I — A Knight's Elegy.** An Ergothian verse-book with Ilendra's annotations in the margins. Reading (5 min): Ilendra's handwriting turns grief-driven halfway through; she is writing *to* her brother Caen, not *about* him.
  - **Book II — On Memory.** A philosopher's treatise on the nature of remembering. Ilendra has underlined a single passage: *"We do not carry the dead within us. We carry the shape of ourselves against their absence."* Below, in her hand: *"And if that shape should be filled?"*
  - **Book III — A Silversmith's Letter.** A sealed letter copied fair. Signed *Aelwen.* Reads: *"My Lord Baron, I have finished the rose and I beg you: do not give it to your daughter. When I held it, I began to forget my own daughter's face. I have burned the mold. I will not make another. — Aelwen, silversmith of Vaenshold."* (This is the letter from the first prior owner.)

- **The funeral fresco (north wall).** A young man in Solamnic cadet attire carried downhill by his companions. A woman — unmistakably the same as on the mosaic in Room 03 — stands apart. Her face here is *not* defaced. DC 13 History: the fresco predates Ilendra's own burial by at least 80 years. Someone was painting her grief a lifetime before she died.

- **The shaft in the floor (center).** A circular iron plate, ~3 ft across, set into the stone. A silver rose is inlaid in the plate. Seven hinge-lines radiate from the rose (petals). **Sealed.**

### Unlocking the shaft *(rev 3: compressed per S01 panel feedback)*

**Reading ONE book gives a hint. Reading TWO books unlocks the shaft.**

- **After any one book is read (5 min, no check):** the silver rose on the shaft-plate *shivers* — a hinge moves. It has not opened. The DM narrates the shiver as a hint: "something more is needed."
- **After a second book is read:** the rose on the shaft-plate *folds* — petals hinge open like an iris, revealing a 10-ft drop.

**Alternative non-narrative solutions (any ONE also works, independent of book-reading):**

1. **Arcana:** Intelligence (Arcana) DC 17 to understand the seal-ward.
2. **Religion:** Wisdom (Religion) DC 15 (a paladin, cleric, or anyone proficient in Religion can offer a prayer to Paladine).
3. **Brute force:** Strength (Athletics) DC 20 to pry the plate. Success: the plate cracks, opens, *and* silver-rose fragments cut the breaker for 1d4 slashing damage.

*(Rev 3 change: was previously "read any two of the three books" as a single narrative step, which produced a triple-read structure at the table. The new "one book = hint; two books = shaft" creates a progress signal on the first read, compressing the feeling of the sequence without changing the essential book-count.)*

## Encounter
None. This room is solved, not fought through.

## Treasure
- **Aelwen's Letter.** Book III is the provenance document — if recovered, the party has written evidence of the curse's first victim. Worth 0 gp; invaluable as evidence or as a roleplay trigger for Brother Laen.
- **Ilendra's annotations.** Book I, detached pages of annotations. 0 gp; a key clue for any character who wants to understand what happened to Ilendra.

## Connections
- **East (open archway):** Room 02 — The Antechamber of Tears.
- **Down (shaft, ~10 ft, once unlocked):** Room 05 — The Rose Stair.

## GM Notes
- **Do not push players toward the books.** If they don't open the books, they don't unlock the shaft, and they take the Pillar Hall route. The Scriptorium is a reward for curiosity.
- If the party reads **all three** books, offer one of them (player's choice) a flash of insight: they understand that the rose is a device of grief, not a device of power, and that it has claimed three owners already.
- When the shaft opens: a waft of cold, dry air from below, and a sound like a single sharp intake of breath. No enemy is causing it. The tomb is reacting.
- Players who enter via the shaft skip Room 03's encounter entirely. That's correct. Do not penalize it; a Jaquaysed dungeon rewards thoroughness.
- **Curse symptom — attribution drift (setup).** Book II's margin-hand (Ilendra's) attributes the quoted passage — *"We do not carry the dead within us. We carry the shape of ourselves against their absence."* — to *Aelwen*, writing *"as Aelwen said to my father."* Room 06's frescoes (Panel 3's caption) will bear the same sentence uncredited. The rose has erased Aelwen from one of the two. Only a party that reads Book II carefully and then examines the Room 06 frescoes will notice.

### Memory Fragment (optional — read only if the party lingers)
> *Baron Edric wrote in a journal he later burned: "I understood at the end what Aelwen had begged me to hear. I could not remember whether I had understood it for a day, or a year, or whether I had been understanding it in the wrong tense the whole time. I forgot her daughter's name also. Her daughter was named for my wife."*
