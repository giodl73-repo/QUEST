# Room 05 — The Rose Stair

## Read-aloud
> A small vestibule, square and low. To your right the stair you descended; above, a circular shaft — cold air sighs through it. A dead brazier stands by a single oaken door. Above the lintel, chiseled deep: *Est Sularus oth Mithas.*

## Features

- **The stair (south).** Up to Room 03 (Pillar Hall).
- **The shaft (ceiling).** Up to Room 04 (Scriptorium), once opened. Climb DC 10 without a rope.
- **The brazier.** Dead coals, cold for a century. A DC 13 Investigation reveals:
  - A small silver rose-petal pressed into the cold ash — matching the full silver rose in Room 06. Someone left an offering here and left without lighting it.
  - Beneath the petal, scratched into the ash with what looks like a fingernail — or a bone — a name: **VESK**. Beside it, smaller, almost buried: the name **PELLA**.
- **The door (north).** Oak, iron-banded. Unlocked. The inscription is the Solamnic Oath. A paladin, cleric, or Solamnic-trained character who speaks the words aloud causes the door to open silently (roleplay beat; no mechanical effect).
- **The air.** Dry. Not musty — dry as a sealed crypt should not be after a century.

## Encounter

**Conditional.** If the party was LOUD in Rooms 03 or 04 (combat triggered in 03, or brute-force solution used in 04), **Vesk the ghoul** emerges from behind the brazier — a Bone-Collector scout of Neraka who died in this room three weeks ago and has not yet been let go by the rose.

- If triggered: **Vesk** (1 × Ghoul, SRD, CR 1, 200 XP).
- Tactics: ambushes from behind the brazier; leads with a claw attack (paralysis is the real threat); retreats if reduced below 5 HP, trying to reach the door he came in through.
- If NOT triggered: Vesk's **desiccated corpse** lies behind the brazier, long still. A DC 12 Medicine check notes he gnawed through most of his own left arm before dying; the wound is not a self-feeding wound but a *writing-tool wound* — he used his bone and his own blood to scratch the names in the ash.

### Who Vesk was

**Vesk of the Bone-Collectors, born in Neraka.** A tomb-robber and scout for a small cell of necromantic cultists tracking the Silver Rose of Vaenor. He was forty-six and had a daughter, **Pella**, aged nine, living in a waystation north of Neraka with his sister. Vesk had not seen Pella in three years.

Vesk entered the cairn three weeks ago, descended this far, was turned back at the Oath-inscribed door (he could not speak it; the door would not open for him), and was overtaken by the rose's residue in the stair. He felt his memory of his daughter's face beginning to go — he could still see her at the moment he noticed. He understood, late, what the rose was doing.

He chose, before he died, to remember Pella's name at all costs. He scratched it in the ash with his own arm-bone, beside his own, so someone would know the two together.

**A character who discovers the names and makes the connection (Investigation DC 13) may read Vesk's rag-pocket:** a folded scrap of parchment with a child's crayon-drawing of a waystation, signed *Pella, for papa*. 5 gp sentimental; invaluable if the party chooses to deliver it.

## Treasure

- **The silver rose-petal in the ashes.** A single petal, matching the full rose. Cold to the touch. Worth 25 gp as silver; more as a clue. Anyone who attunes to the full rose later is unsettled to find this petal in their belongings.
- **Vesk's Bone-Collector signet** (on Vesk, whether ghoul or corpse). 10 gp to a fence; seeds adventure #0002.
- **Pella's drawing** (Vesk's rag-pocket, if searched). 5 gp sentimental; a roleplay hook the length of a campaign.

## Connections
- **Up (stair):** Room 03 — The Pillar Hall.
- **Up (shaft):** Room 04 — The Scriptorium (climb DC 10).
- **North (oak door, unlocked):** Room 06 — The Sarcophagus Chamber.

## GM Notes
- If the party triggered the ghoul encounter, let the fight be short but *tactically pressured* — paralysis is real for a level-3 PC.
- The silver petal is a callback to the rose's thefts over time. Describe it cold to touch even through cloth.
- The inscription door is a free roleplay beat. A player who speaks the Oath earns acknowledgment — maybe a small advantage on a check in Room 06, DM's discretion.
- **Vesk's names in the ash are the moral weight of this room.** He is not the villain. He is the second-to-most-recent victim, whose last act was to remember. The players who notice and care are the players who will have an answer when the rose asks them, in Room 06, what they will give up.

### Memory Fragment (optional — read only if the party lingers)
> *Vesk, on the third day: "I will not look at the rose again. I will think of her face. I will think of her face. I will think of her —" And he forgot the word he was reaching for, and picked up the bone, and began to scratch her name in the ash.*
