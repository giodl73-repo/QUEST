# Encounter — The Pillar Hall Attendants

**Location:** Room 03 — The Pillar Hall.

**Trigger:** Any character crosses the centerline between the two rows of pillars.

**Adversaries:** 4 × Skeleton (SRD; CR ¼ each, 50 XP each; 200 XP total).

## Party Math (4 PCs, level 3)

| Threshold (per party) | XP |
|---|---|
| Easy | 300 |
| Medium | 600 |
| Hard | 900 |
| Deadly | 1,600 |

**This encounter:** 200 XP base × 2 (3-6 monsters multiplier) = **400 adjusted XP**. Easy-to-medium for a 4 × L3 party. Appropriate opener.

## Statblock (Skeleton, SRD 5.1)

- **AC** 13 (armor scraps)  ·  **HP** 13 (2d8+4)  ·  **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Str** 10 · **Dex** 14 · **Con** 15 · **Int** 6 · **Wis** 8 · **Cha** 5
- **Damage vulnerabilities:** bludgeoning.
- **Damage immunities:** poison.
- **Condition immunities:** exhaustion, poisoned.
- **Senses:** darkvision 60 ft.; Passive Perception 9.
- **Languages:** understands the languages it knew in life; cannot speak.
- **Challenge:** ¼ (50 XP).

**Actions:**
- **Shortsword.** *Melee.* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6+2) piercing.
- **Shortbow.** *Ranged.* +4 to hit, range 80/320 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6+2) piercing.

**This encounter's skeletons carry:** three with shortswords; one carries the **Notched Longsword** (still treat as shortsword for mechanics — 1d6+2 piercing — the notch makes it useless as a proper longsword, but mark the skeleton visually).

## Tactics

- **Silent attack.** The skeletons make no sound beyond the scrape of bone on stone. They do not speak, do not rattle. This is unusual — player cue that they are not ordinary undead.
- **Use pillars for cover.** Each skeleton takes half-cover behind a pillar on round 1. AC 13 → effective 15 while behind a pillar.
- **Flank priority.** If multiple skeletons are adjacent to different PCs, they shift to get flanking with each other rather than ganging on one PC.
- **Do not cross the centerline backward.** If a PC retreats past the centerline, the skeletons will **not** pursue across it — they stop at the line, sag, and wait. This is the rose's limit: the attendants are bound to the Hall.
- **Give up when reduced.** At 0 HP, each skeleton collapses to a loose pile of bone and does not rise again.
- **Re-trigger.** If the party leaves Room 03 without finishing combat and returns, the skeletons do **not** re-animate the same bodies — but a fresh crossing of the centerline spawns the encounter anew from new bones rising from the floor (up to the original 4 total, minus any whose skulls were specifically destroyed).

## Variants

- **Weaker party (3 PCs or level 2):** Reduce to 3 skeletons.
- **Stronger party (level 4):** Replace the sword-wielder carrying the Notched Longsword with a **skeleton captain** (as skeleton but 22 HP, AC 14, one extra attack per turn). Give the Notched Longsword to the captain.

## Read-aloud (when triggered)

> The moment you cross into the hall's center, the silence *tightens.* Four of the floor-flagstones shudder. Bones rise — unburied, unasked — and stand upright in armor-scraps. Swords find hands. Eyes do not light. The attendants of Ilendra Vaenor have waited a long time.

## Notes for the DM

- This is the first fight and should feel *formal* — not raucous undead, but attendants performing a duty. Describe their movements as a ritual.
- **Do not** describe the skeletons as reanimated by necromancy. They stand because the rose wanted them to. This should feel wrong to any cleric in the party.
- After combat: a DC 15 Religion check reveals these bones bear no necromantic aura. They are not *undead* in the spell-sense. They are *held.*
