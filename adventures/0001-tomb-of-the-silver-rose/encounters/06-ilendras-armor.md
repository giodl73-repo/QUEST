# Encounter — Ilendra's Armor

**Location:** Room 06 — The Sarcophagus Chamber.

**Trigger:** The sarcophagus is disturbed (by Options A, B, or D from the room file). **Option C (the Notched Longsword key) bypasses this encounter entirely** — the armor does not animate.

**Adversaries:** 1 × Animated Armor (SRD; CR 1, 200 XP).

## Party Math (4 PCs, level 3)

- Medium threshold: 600 XP.
- This encounter: 200 XP base × 1 = **200 XP.** Easy for a 4 × L3 party in isolation.
- However: this is the **climax** of the adventure, after some attrition. Expect the party to have spent at least 1-2 hit dice and some spells before they arrive. The encounter's real weight is **atmospheric,** not mechanical.

## Statblock (Animated Armor, SRD 5.1)

- **AC** 18 (natural armor)  ·  **HP** 33 (6d8+6)  ·  **Speed** 25 ft.
- **Str** 14 · **Dex** 11 · **Con** 13 · **Int** 1 · **Wis** 3 · **Cha** 1
- **Damage immunities:** poison, psychic.
- **Condition immunities:** blinded, charmed, deafened, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, stunned.
- **Senses:** blindsight 60 ft. (blind beyond this); Passive Perception 6.
- **Languages:** —
- **Challenge:** 1 (200 XP).
- **Antimagic Susceptibility:** The armor is incapacitated while in the area of an *antimagic field.* If targeted by *dispel magic*, the armor must succeed on a Con save against the caster's spell save DC or fall unconscious for 1 minute.
- **False Appearance:** While motionless, indistinguishable from a normal suit of armor.

**Actions:**
- **Multiattack.** Two slam attacks.
- **Slam.** *Melee.* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6+2) bludgeoning.

## Tactics

- **Priority: whoever is closest to the Rose.** The armor is animated by the rose's residue and protects the artifact first. If the rose has been removed from the breastplate already, the armor attacks whoever is *carrying* it.
- **Does not pursue out of the room.** If the rose-bearer leaves Room 06, the armor stops at the threshold. (But if the party leaves *without* the rose, the armor powers down and the rose sits in the sarcophagus, waiting.)
- **Cold deliberation.** The armor fights with an unnatural composure — it does not miss twice in a row against the same target (DM discretion; narratively, the armor *learns* a PC's defenses within a round). This is flavor; mechanics are per the stat block. Describe, don't multiply.
- **Ilendra's gauntlets tremble when landing a hit.** As if the armor itself is reluctant.
- **At 0 HP,** the armor does not break apart. It sags, slowly, to a seated position against the east wall, and ceases. Someone who reassembles it will find it is ordinary plate, not enchanted, not cursed. It no longer fits any living person.

## The Memory Echo (during or after combat)

When the Rose is taken from its setting — whether before the fight (Option B, Option C), during (mid-combat looting), or after — a translucent figure of Sir Ilendra at age fifty appears by the sarcophagus. She is not an adversary. She is not a ghost in the mechanical sense (no stat block, no HP, no aggression).

She speaks one sentence, softly, clearly. The room hushes for her:

> *"Tell my brother I did not forget."*

Then she fades. The armor, if still fighting, pauses a beat — as if listening — and then resumes. A character who succeeds on a DC 12 Wisdom (Insight) check realizes: Ilendra does not remember that her brother is dead.

## Read-aloud (when the armor animates)

> The suit of armor by the east wall — ceremonial, polished a century ago for a funeral — takes a half-step forward. Pauldron rolls. Greaves scrape. A cold silver light burns for a moment in the eyeslits and is gone. The armor turns toward the rose.

## Variants

- **Weaker party:** remove one slam attack (Multiattack → single Slam). HP 24.
- **Stronger party (level 4):** add a second Animated Armor, identical, from the west wall. Memory Echo still appears, only once. 400 XP total — a climactic, hard encounter.

## Notes for the DM

- This is the last fight of the session. Make it count atmospherically, but do not kill the party — a TPK here is not the story this adventure is telling.
- If the party used **Option C (Notched Longsword key)**, the armor does not animate at all. They take the rose, see the Memory Echo, and leave. That is the *good* outcome and a Jaquays-style reward for thoroughness.
- If the party takes the rose and immediately tries to return it to the sarcophagus mid-fight, the armor pauses — give them one round to decide. If they commit the rose to its setting, the armor sags down and the fight ends. The party has chosen not to keep it. This is a valid and interesting outcome.
