# Temporal Pressure — Wandering Encounters & Cold Pulse

Two overlapping pressure mechanics. Revised in rev 3 after S01's Cold Pulse outcome 3 damaged atmospheric payoffs (see `personas/playtest-innovations.md` entries I-S01-08 and I-S01-09). The new design costs resources cleanly (HP / hit dice) without inflicting ability-check disadvantage.

## Use

- Roll the **Cold Pulse** d6 every **10 minutes of in-fiction time** the party spends within the tomb (Rooms 02-06). Atmospheric erosion that costs concrete resources, not abstract check-disadvantage.
- Separately, roll the **Wandering Encounter** d6 every **30 minutes** — outside intruders reaching the tomb or deeper disturbances. Mechanical pressure.
- Both tables stop at Room 06. Once the party enters the Sarcophagus Chamber, no further Cold Pulse or Wandering rolls — the climax plays without interruption.

## Cold Pulse Table (roll d6 every 10 min in tomb) — rev 3

| d6 | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Nothing.** The air holds. |
| 2 | **A hush.** For three heartbeats, no sound carries — even voices. Then normal. |
| 3 | **The cold bites.** Each character in less-than-heavy armor takes **1d4 cold damage** (no save). Heavy-armor characters take nothing. *(Rev 3 change: was previously "Con save DC 10 or 1 exhaustion" — that inflicted ability-check disadvantage which damaged atmospheric-landing rolls. Now it costs clean HP, per rubric v1.1's cross-dimensional-harmlessness anchor.)* |
| 4 | **A forgotten word.** One random character is asked by the DM to name an NPC from the party's home settlement. They cannot remember the name for one minute (roleplay; no mechanical penalty). The name returns on its own. |
| 5 | **A distant weeping.** Barely audible, somewhere in the tomb. Active Perception check (not gated — each PC may try) localizes it — it always comes from Room 02 (the weeping wall) regardless of the party's current location. Add one thread to the silver water in the bowl. |
| 6 | **The rose stirs.** Whoever is carrying the Silver Rose (if it has been retrieved) feels it warm in their hand for a full minute — the only time it is ever warm. If they have not yet retrieved the rose, all characters hear a single petal-chime from the direction of Room 06, once, gently. |

## Wandering Encounter Table (roll d6 every 30 min in tomb) — rev 3

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Nothing.** The tomb keeps its own. |
| 3 | **A Bone-Collector scout arrives at the cairn-cap.** They climb down into Room 01 and attempt to follow the party. A single **Bandit** (SRD, CR ⅛) moving quietly; Perception DC 13 to notice if the party is engaged. Speaks only Nerakan; carries a bone-signet. If killed: the signet matches the one in Room 05's ghoul encounter — they are searching together. |
| 4 | **Collapse** *(rev 3: now d3-randomized).* Roll `1d3`: 1 = the R01→R02 stair shifts (characters passing must DC 12 Acrobatics or 1d4 bludgeoning + prone); 2 = the R02 east archway collapses (seals east archway; R03 only reachable via R05 stair); 3 = the R05 shaft partially collapses (climb DC changes from 10 to 15). The collapse never fully seals the path. *(Rev 3 change: was previously "DM choice"; now d3 among three specific outcomes for procedural honesty per Tactician lens S01 feedback.)* |
| 5 | **A second ghoul** (SRD, CR 1) arrives from a fresh crack in Room 05's wall. It has been starving in the rubble and goes for the weakest party member. If the Room 05 ghoul has already fought, this replaces it; if the corpse-version was found, this ghoul emerges from *behind the brazier*. |
| 6 | **The tomb speaks.** The DM whispers one sentence that a random party member hears only for them — one of the memory fragments from the GM Notes of any room the party has visited. No save. No effect beyond the roleplay beat. Write the sentence down; if the player later repeats it, ask them whose memory it was. |

## Pacing Guidance

- An attentive party will stay in the tomb about **60-90 minutes** of in-fiction time if they don't dally. That's 1-3 Cold Pulse rolls and 1-2 Wandering rolls.
- A slow, thorough party may stay **2-3 hours.** That's 6+ Cold Pulse rolls and 3-4 Wandering rolls. Build the pressure.
- **Do not force-trigger** either table. Let the dice do the work.

## Design Notes (rev 3)

- **Cold Pulse outcome 3 now costs HP,** not check-disadvantage. Rubric v1.1's Mechanical fairness cross-dimensional-harmlessness anchor requires this: a fair mechanic should not regularly destroy atmospheric-landing or module-fidelity payoffs as a side-effect of its design. S01's Cold Pulse (outcome 3 as DC 10 Con save or exhaustion) produced exactly that failure mode.
- The HP-cost is light (1d4 per trigger, max 4) but real. Parties in heavy armor are immune — fitting the in-fiction logic that plate keeps out the bone-cold. Light-armor and unarmored casters bleed HP; clerics and paladins can heal it cleanly.
- **Wandering outcome 4 collapse** is now d3-randomized for procedural integrity. The DM still picks among three specific authored outcomes, but not which one fires.

## Rubric compliance

- ✓ v1.1 cross-dimensional-harmlessness: Cold Pulse outcome 3 costs HP, not ability-check disadvantage. Score-cap risk removed.
- ✓ Atmospheric payoffs (Investigation / Perception / History rolls elsewhere in the module) are no longer gated by temporal-pressure disadvantage.
