# Brother Laen of the Rose Council's Clerkwork

A Knight of the Crown in his mid-thirties who does the Rose Council's paperwork because his hands are steadier than his nerves. Patron of this adventure.

## Appearance & Manner

A narrow man with ink-stained fingers and the kind of posture that suggests he has been told to stand straighter for a long time. Closely-cropped beard, Solamnic knight's tabard worn one size too large (inherited; he has not yet grown into the Order). Soft-spoken, deliberate, avoids eye contact during the hard parts of conversations. Carries a small leather folio everywhere; it is almost always empty, but holding it calms him.

Speaks Common and passable Solamnic formal register. Does not speak Krynnish vulgar.

## Stat Block

Treat as **Knight** (SRD reskin) if combat arises. He will not initiate it.

- **AC** 18 (plate + shield) · **HP** 35 (stat block approximation — not a cinematic figure) · **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Str** 14 · **Dex** 10 · **Con** 13 · **Int** 12 · **Wis** 12 · **Cha** 13
- **Saves:** Con +3, Wis +3
- **Skills:** History +3, Insight +3, Persuasion +3
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 11
- **Languages:** Common, Solamnic
- **Brave.** Advantage on saves against being frightened.
- **Actions:** **Longsword** +4 to hit, 1d8+2 slashing. Will draw only if imminently in danger. Will not pursue.

Brother Laen is not a fighter. If the party becomes hostile, he will run. If cornered, he will surrender. He is not equipped to die well in a fight; he is a clerk in ceremonial armor.

## The Public Story (what he tells the party)

The Silver Rose of Vaenor is a "sigil of the fallen Vaenor line," an item of sentimental and historical value to the Order. It was interred with Sir Ilendra Vaenor, a Knight of the Rose, in the Age of Might. The Council wishes to retire it with honors to the Order's vaults. Payment: **80 gp** upon return, plus a **letter of Solamnic service** to any party member who wants one.

## The Private Story (what he knows but will not say)

The Rose Council has kept records of the Silver Rose for a century. The Council knows:
- The rose was not sanctioned by the Wizards of High Sorcery.
- It was made by a hedge-wizard (Aelwen) whose death shortly after was noted and filed.
- It erodes memory in those who attune.
- Sir Ilendra Vaenor's list of the living — a document kept under lock in the Rose Council's archive — shows the names of people *the Order knows she forgot.*
- If the rose were publicly acknowledged, the Order would have to explain why a Knight of the Rose was permitted to carry it. No explanation exists that does not embarrass the Order.

**Brother Laen has read the Council's file.** He understands the stakes. He is not evil; he is a loyal junior clerk asked to clean up an institutional embarrassment a century in the making.

## Private Motive

Brother Laen has a younger sister, Wena, who is a novice with the Revered Daughters of Paladine. If the rose is ever publicly linked to the Order, the scandal would reach her cloister. He believes — rightly or wrongly — that his sister's position is the leverage the Council has already quietly used to ensure his discretion.

He will never speak of her.

**A character who succeeds on a DC 18 Insight during a long conversation** notices he is carrying two things in his folio: a blank page, and a small letter in a woman's hand that he touches when he thinks no one is watching.

**Note for the DM:** Brother Laen is not a villain. He is an agent of institutional self-protection whose compromises are costly to him. If he is killed, the adventure becomes about the Order rather than the rose; that is probably not the story this module wants.

## Notes on the name

"Laen" is a variant of *Lain*, a common Abanasinian hill-village name. He came to the Order through local Solamnic patronage, not through a knight's pedigree. His full name in the Order's records is **Laen of Varduin-by-the-Pines**. Varduin is the hill-village the party visits at the start of the adventure — eight miles from the cairn.

If the party ever travels to Varduin itself, they may meet Laen's elderly mother, who will recognize the party's faces because Laen has written to her about them. She is not discreet.
