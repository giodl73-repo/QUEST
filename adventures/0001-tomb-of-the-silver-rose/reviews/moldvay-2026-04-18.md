## Critique

A DM can run this cold. That is the first thing I want to say, and it is the most important thing I will say, because most published modules cannot pass that test. I opened `module.md` and imagined myself behind a screen at a table of four players at levels 3, and I could see how to run the first two rooms without flipping a page. That is correct.

Specifically: the read-aloud text is under sixty words in every room. I counted. The DCs are inline with the hazards that demand them. The stat blocks are reproduced in each room where the monster appears, *and* aggregated in an appendix, *and* summarized in the cheatsheet — which is the right amount of redundancy for a DM running under table pressure. When a player says *"I search the bowl"* in Room 02 you do not have to flip past the Pillar Hall to find the Arcana DC 12. It's on the Room 02 page.

The DM cheatsheet at the end is particularly well done. Key DCs sorted by room; scene triggers for predictable player questions; and at the bottom, the most valuable paragraph in the whole module — the "If the party avoids Room 04 entirely" line. That's a sentence many modules would *hide.* Acknowledging that players might skip your cleverest room is one of the surest signs of a mature design.

My objections are small ones, in the tradition of an editor rather than a critic.

**The premise file is doing too much.** It contains the hook, the setting, the artifact seed, the themes, the constraints, the prior owners, and the NPC notes. A DM preparing the session needs to know: where is this, who hires the party, what do they want, what can go wrong. The current `premise.md` is three pages. Cut it to one. Push prior-owner detail to `treasures/silver-rose.md` where it belongs. Push NPC notes to `npcs/brother-laen.md` (which does not yet exist and should). The module-binder should pull only what it needs.

**Room 06 has four bulleted options for retrieving the Rose.** That is one too many. Option B (Investigation DC 15 to find the hidden catch) and Option C (the Notched Longsword) are both the "clever" solution — they differ only in whether the Armor triggers. Consolidate: the hidden catch either *is* the notched-sword slot, or it requires the notched sword to operate. Fewer bullets, same choices.

**The Memory Echo has no stat block even though it is a named entity the players may try to interact with.** If a character tries to *stop* Ilendra from disappearing — grab her, speak to her, pull her close — what happens? You have left this to DM discretion. Discretion is fine, but a one-line note would be better: *"Ilendra is an echo. She cannot be touched, but she will meet a paladin's eyes. If a character speaks the name Caen to her, she weeps silver light for a moment; no further effect."* That is one sentence. It saves a DM from inventing under pressure.

**The map is readable, but the ASCII might be clearer with slightly simpler line art.** The current layout mixes `[N]` boxes with stair symbols (`/ \`) and walls (`||`) in a way that almost works but occasionally reads as clutter. I would strip to a single symbol per connection — `→ 2 stair` beats `/ \ stair direction`. This is a stylistic preference, not a structural problem.

**Attunement rules are mentioned on the cheatsheet but the cheatsheet points to `reference/srd/attunement.md`.** That is correct for a workshop reference; for a table-ready module, inline the three sentences of attunement rules that actually matter (3-item limit, short rest to attune, revealed on attunement). A DM at the table should not have to open a second file.

These are all polish points. The structural work is sound. The adventure is runnable as written.

## Specific suggestions

1. **Cut `premise.md` to one page.** Move provenance to `treasures/silver-rose.md`; NPC to `npcs/brother-laen.md` (create file); keep premise to hook, setting, central artifact one-line, scope, and themes.
2. **Consolidate Room 06 retrieval options.** Three is enough: Brute (A), Clever+Key (B using the Notched Longsword — always), and Smash (D). Collapse the stone-fist version into the sword-key version.
3. **Inline the three critical attunement facts on the cheatsheet.** "Attunement: requires a short rest (1 hour) focused on the item. Limit 3 per character. Curse revealed on attunement (or, here, on third long rest)." Saves a file lookup.
4. **Add one sentence for the Memory Echo's non-violent interaction** — what happens if a player tries to reach for her.

## Final note

This is a clean first dungeon. The prose is under control, the mechanics are legible, the DM has everything they need on the page where they need it. A few polish edits will take this from "runnable cold" to "runnable cold *and* fast."
