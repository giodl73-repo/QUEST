## Critique

Six rooms. A modest outing, and the better for being modest — overmuch scale is the ruin of many a dungeon. I have seen promising designs sink themselves in forty empty corridors.

But six rooms with only two proper encounters? I say this is *thin.* A dungeon is meant to apply pressure. Where is the wandering monster table? What do I roll when the party stops to read three books for fifteen minutes at those lecterns? The Plains of Dergoth above are not empty; the cairn itself has been visited at least once by Neraka's scouts — where are the signs of further intrusions? A proper Age of Might cairn, untouched for a century, ought to have grown its own small ecology of vermin, scavengers, and perhaps one or two encroaching hazards. As written, the players can linger in any room as long as they please, at no cost. A dungeon that does not bleed the party of resources is not a dungeon; it is a museum.

I will grant you this — the cursed rose itself is a **splendid** treasure. A creeping cost rather than a cliff-drop; a ritual end that hurts the players to undertake; and a provenance chain I can use in future adventures without invention. This is treasure-as-reward done properly. A player who attunes is paying in something valuable (memory) for something valuable (benefits). The bargain is real. *Good.* And that you give the players four ways to retrieve it, and four ways to dispose of it — real choices, with real consequences — this is the heart of the game. I commend it.

The map is honest. A loop, a vertical shaft, a shortcut that unlocks through play — this is more than most published modules give me. But I note that Room 04 is a puzzle-room the party may skip entirely, and this is a design flaw: if half the party's options are behind a door only curious parties open, you have two different adventures depending on party temperament. The Pillar Hall party and the Scriptorium party will not play the same session. I prefer that *all* my core mechanics be reachable from the main path, with *optional* depth behind the secret door.

The skeletons stopping at the centerline is a fine touch — it rewards caution — but I would have had them *slowly close the distance* over three rounds, with the party able to retreat into the Antechamber if they are outmatched. The current design ends combat the moment the party backs up; this is too kind.

Brother Laen is an adequate patron. I would have preferred the party not know whether he was an ally until the end of the adventure — a Solamnic archivist who is secretly in league with the Bone-Collectors, say. As written he is merely a bureaucrat, and bureaucrats do not fill the role of an adventure's antagonist well.

Read-aloud text: brief and serviceable. I counted; none exceed sixty words. *Well done.* Stat blocks inline in the rooms section: *well done.* The DM cheatsheet at the end: *well done.* A DM can run this cold.

My three strongest objections:

1. **No wandering monster table.** A dungeon with no time pressure is a dungeon where the players never feel haste. I would install a d6 table rolling every ten minutes: 1-2 nothing, 3 a second ghoul from the vents, 4 a patrol of 2 bandits (Bone-Collectors), 5 a collapse in Room 03 that shifts a pillar, 6 the cold air in the tomb briefly intensifies (Con save DC 10 or 1 point of exhaustion).
2. **Only two proper fights, and one conditional.** Three to four is the minimum for a tier-1 session.
3. **The puzzle-branch makes for two different adventures.** I would expose some hint of Room 04's existence in Room 02, so the mere curious party — not only the thorough one — finds the shortcut.

Verdict: this is a promising piece of work. It will play well for a clever party. It will play **thin** for an aggressive party. Thicken the center, sir or madam — give it more teeth.

## Specific suggestions

1. Add a **wandering-encounter table** (d6 per 10 min of in-tomb time). Seed with Bone-Collectors from outside and one tomb-hazard.
2. Add a **third proper encounter** somewhere between Rooms 02 and 06 — perhaps a cold-damage hazard triggered in Room 02 or an additional skeleton ambush in Room 05 unconditionally.
3. **Signpost Room 04 from Room 02** — a carved rose over the west archway, matching the lecterns within. This removes the "two-different-adventures" problem without railroading.
