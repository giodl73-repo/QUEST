# Review Summary — *Tomb of the Silver Rose*

Aggregated persona reviews for the first dungeon in this workshop. Five designers, five lenses, productive disagreement.

**Panel verdict:** 66.6/80 weighted average — solid draft, strong core. Per `personas/rubric.md`, a score in the 65-80 band is "shippable; run it as-is." We're barely into that band, with a strong spread. Every persona scored it above 60 (the "strong draft" threshold), which means no one thinks the bones are wrong.

## Strongest Consensus

**Treasure-as-story (9.4)** — Every persona scored this 9 or 10. Schick and Stuart gave it a perfect 10. The central artifact and its curse are doing the work the adventure asks of them. This is the strongest axis and validates the workshop's "treasure IS the story" north star.

**Curse/consequence (9.0)** — Same two 10s from Schick and Stuart. The creeping cost, the three-rest reveal, the ritual-of-ending that costs a living memory — unanimous respect for the mechanic.

**Premise clarity (8.6)** — Logline pitches cleanly; the real decision (return / keep / destroy / sell) is legible.

## Most Dissent

**Dungeon integrity** — Gygax scored 6 (wants more rooms, more attrition, wandering monsters). Jaquays and Moldvay scored 8 (approve of the loop, shortcut, verticality at this scale). **Root disagreement:** is a 6-room dungeon too small to apply real pressure? Old-school says yes; non-linear-design says the topology does its job at the chosen scale.

**Encounter craft** — Gygax scored 6 (two proper fights is thin). Schick scored 8 (likes the Armor + Memory Echo signature scene). **Root disagreement:** is encounter craft about *quantity under pressure* (Gygax) or *quality of the signature moments* (Schick)?

**Player agency** — Gygax and Jaquays scored 9 (four retrieval options + four post-retrieval choices = real agency). Schick scored only 7 (WPM-style: "the dungeon is what it is, the players navigate it"). **Root disagreement:** philosophical — is agency about how-you-take-the-loot or what-you-do-with-it?

## Convergent Suggestions (multiple personas agree)

1. **Address the Room 04 discovery problem.** Gygax ("the puzzle-branch makes for two different adventures") and Jaquays ("Room 02 offers no cue distinguishing the west archway") both flag that a non-curious party will miss the Scriptorium entirely. **Unified fix:** add a sensory cue to Room 02 that distinguishes the west archway — a draft carrying vellum-smell, a fragment of a hymn on the lintel, a subtly different sound from the air.
2. **Add a third encounter / temporal pressure.** Gygax wants a wandering monster table; Moldvay wants no more retrieval options to choose from; Stuart wants the tomb itself to be "eroding" in real time. **Unified fix:** one of two moves — (a) a Bone-Collector scout arrives mid-session (wandering encounter, resolves Gygax + seeds adventure #0002), or (b) an atmospheric "cold pulse" triggers periodically in the tomb and forces minor attrition (resolves Stuart's erosion and Gygax's attrition without new combat).
3. **Develop Brother Laen into his own file.** Gygax wants him ambivalent-possibly-villainous; Moldvay wants `npcs/brother-laen.md` for organizational cleanliness; Stuart wants him carrying *institutional shame* visibly. **Unified fix:** create the NPC file with (a) stat block, (b) dissemble-or-not behavior matrix, (c) a single private motive that makes him *not a villain but not an ally either.*
4. **Sensory erosion of the tomb, not just visual.** Stuart's specific suggestions (rename *Vaenor* as *Vanor* on one inscription; repeat a phrase across rooms; attribute a passage to Aelwen in Room 04 and anonymously in Room 06) add atmosphere without new mechanics. Schick's suggestion that the rose warm or cool in the bearer's hand does similar work. **Unified fix:** commit to 3-4 such "tomb-forgets-itself" details and distribute across rooms.
5. **Consolidate Room 06 retrieval options (3, not 4).** Moldvay specifically; Schick implicitly praises Option C and would not miss B being merged. **Fix:** collapse Option B (stone-fist catch) into Option C (the catch IS the Notched Longsword slot).

## Divergent Suggestions (keep or ignore; persona-specific)

- **Gygax:** wandering monster table (d6 per 10 min). *Adds mechanical pressure; may conflict with session length.*
- **Jaquays:** make the attendants patrol, not static. *Adds complexity; significant rewrite of Room 03 encounter.*
- **Jaquays:** glimpse of Room 05's shaft from Room 02 for premap. *Nice; may erode surprise.*
- **Schick:** give the rose a *desire* in prose — "drawn to the hand of whoever has lost the most." *Low-cost atmospheric upgrade.*
- **Schick:** let a fresco panel *heal* when the rose is taken, restoring one stolen image. *Powerful scene; single line to add.*
- **Stuart:** give the Bone-Collector corpse a name and a daughter he remembered. *Emotional weight; ~2 paragraphs.*
- **Stuart:** per-room "memory fragment from a prior bearer" line (optional DM reads). *Atmospheric; optional, so low-cost.*
- **Moldvay:** shorten `premise.md` to one page, spin out `npcs/brother-laen.md`. *Organizational hygiene.*
- **Moldvay:** inline three sentences of attunement rules on the cheatsheet. *Small fix, high DM-ergonomic payoff.*

## Retrospective Notes — What the personas revealed about the skills

For when we build `persona-review` as a skill in Phase 6:

1. **The 5-persona panel produces productively diverse scores.** The spread (62-70.5 weighted) validates that distinct philosophies are being represented; they're not all saying the same thing in different voices.
2. **Rubric axes 3-4 (dungeon integrity, encounter craft) got the widest spread** — these are the axes where designers most disagree philosophically. The rubric is doing its job of surfacing disagreement, not enforcing consensus.
3. **Treasure-as-story is the workshop's strongest axis.** Across all 5 personas, 9+. This confirms the "treasure IS the story" principle was correctly implemented, not just stated.
4. **Table-readiness got a 7 from Stuart and 9 from Moldvay — the widest-voiced-philosophical-disagreement.** This is *expected* (Stuart weights it 0.4, Moldvay 1.5). The weighting system works: different designers weight differently, so Stuart's 7 and Moldvay's 9 can both be right relative to what they care about.
5. **Gygax is our lowest scorer across adventures we've written (n=1).** This suggests the workshop's default style (atmospheric, curse-as-story, smaller-scale) doesn't maximize for the Gygax lens. If future adventures want to score higher on Gygax's weights, we need to push encounter count and attrition pressure. If we don't care about that lens, his score being lower is fine.

## Next Steps

- Create `npcs/brother-laen.md`.

**After revision:** re-score with the same panel to see score movement.

**Long-term:**

- `encounter-balancer` skill should include a lens-check: does this encounter satisfy the Gygax weight (attrition), the Schick weight (signature moment), or both?
- `lore-weaver` skill should include Stuart-style atmospheric symptoms as a required output — not just "add a lore section," but "add a sensory symptom the dungeon is having."
