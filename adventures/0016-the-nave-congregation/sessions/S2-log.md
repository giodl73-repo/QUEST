# S2 LOG — The Nave Congregation

## Session Summary

The Witnesses entered the nave and found it exactly as the congregation had been keeping it for thirty years: swept floor, candles everywhere, a daily practice that has been performed without a keeper's formal instruction since Velantha died. Veth met them at the threshold and asked what they were trying to understand. A rat swarm followed them from the gatehouse — the congregation stood on the pews while the party handled it. Afterward the morning rite proceeded, Maret's craft-witness fired for the third time, and Calla recognized the imperfect rite in the Apse as something she understands — not what it is, but what it has lost. Gorret answered Dren honestly and let them pass. Veth answered Calla honestly and told them the elder has the real account. The cup was shown to Maret. The Transept is the next destination.

### The Morning Rite

Veth leads seven steps. The party observes.

Step 1: water carried from the cistern.
Step 2: sharing — the cup passed along the rows. **Relic sign landed:** Veth pours water into the cup. The amount settles without measurement. **DC 11 Perception** — Maret (passive 17) auto-detects: the amount is never wrong. Veth does not measure.

**🎲 Maret Perception (rite structure — craft-witness)** — `1d20+5` → rolls=[7] mod=+5 **total=12** · DC 11 — makes it. She sees the sequence: seven steps, the same order, repeated without variation. This is the craft-witness resonance beat.

Step 3 (spoken verse): *"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it."*

**🎲 Calla Religion (oral verse recognition)** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[9] mod=+3 **total=12** · DC 13 — misses by 1. She hears the verse and knows it has been said this way for thirty years. She cannot formally identify it as a fragment of something longer. The recognition lives one step below articulation.

Steps 4-7 complete: floor-sweep, candle placement, second water offering to the altar stone in R4, closing silence.

*Two minutes of silence. The congregation does not move. The candles hold steady.*

**Craft-witness third activation logged:** Maret observes the rite's complete sequential structure (seven steps, devotional without formal tradition, repeated daily for thirty years). After the silence ends, she performs her three breaths in the same space. The congregation member nearest her — not the same unnamed member from S1, but the general practice — continues their own arrangement. The structural parallel is present and fully visible: Maret's practice and the congregation's rite are the same kind of act.

<!-- SURPRISE --> *The craft-witness fires during the closing silence — not during the rite itself but in the two-minute gap afterward, when Maret performs her practice in the space the rite just occupied. **Cold Pulse 10min-3** — `1d6` → rolls=[6] **total=6** · A second congregation member in R3 speaks the verse alone, immediately after the closing silence ends, as if in response: *"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it."* Three utterances of the verse in two hours: the closing silence of the rite, the spoken verse during the rite, and the individual verse in the aisle. The verse is living and circulating.

### Resolution

### Beats

**Cold Pulse 10min-4** — `1d6` → rolls=[2] **total=2** · Gorret moves. He was at the south wall of R2; he has repositioned to the R2/R5 junction, where he can see both the nave and the stairway approach. He is watching the party's movement without appearing to watch.

**Calla's documentation-suspension deepens.** Standing in R3, she has now encountered the congregation's practice in three distinct forms: (1) the gatehouse shrines in S1, (2) the organized morning rite in R2, (3) the individual aisle shrines here. The progression is visible. The practice has thirty years of development visible in the difference between the oldest shrines (R3, east end) and the newest (gatehouse). She holds the developmental arc. She still cannot name the tradition. But she now knows it has one.

### Resolution
R3 explored. Child's drawing found and replaced. Calla's arc evolves from "cannot categorize" to "can see the arc but not name it." Party proceeds east toward R4.

## Scene 2.4 — Apse (R4): The Imperfect Rite

### Beats

**🎲 Renn Insight (imperfect-rite practitioners)** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[17] mod=+3 **total=20** · He reads them completely: they are sincere. They are not performing. They genuinely do not know if they are doing it right.

*Calla Fosse stands at the edge of the Apse and looks at three people who have been practicing something they were taught imperfectly by someone who was taught imperfectly by someone who was there. *She says: "The verse comes third. The sharing comes second."*

*The practitioners look at each other. The one who asked nods and moves the verse to step 3. They continue.*

**Relic sign landed: reaction moment fired.** Maret holds the cup with intent. The correct amount settles. She holds it for five seconds and sets it back.
