## Critique

### Overall assessment

The Roleplayer gives this session its highest score of the campaign so far. All four PCs said or did something their character sheets promised: Calla inverted her second heuristic and gave information away; Maret held the cup and felt something she did not explain; Renn said "thirty years" at the gallery railing; Dren named Magistra Doran in his honest answer. Four PCs, four distinct character moments, none of them interchangeable.

### What worked

- **Veth's arc-completion (Scene 2.7):** *"I don't know if I am doing it right. I do it anyway."* This is the best NPC line of the campaign so far. It says something that is also true of Calla (who doesn't know if she's performing Thornfield's tradition correctly), also true of Maret (who doesn't know what her practice is for), and also true of the party's investigation itself (they don't know if they're approaching this correctly — they do it anyway). The arc-completion rippled across the party without requiring mechanics. The parallel is present in the scene without being narrated.

- **Calla's correction of the imperfect rite (Scene 2.4):** She said *"The verse comes third. The sharing comes second."* The session log describes the practitioners adjusting. There is no other moment in the campaign so far where a PC gave something away rather than holding it. Calla's documentation-arc is about preservation; here, preservation required transmission. The arc is moving.

### What needs work

- **Dren's dialogue in the honest answer (Scene 2.5):** *"We need to understand the full building before we can tell our employer anything useful."* This is correct and honest. But it is also functional — it sounds like a report, not a person. Dren's voice-tags (short sentences, finishes them; says "noted" when filing something; the pause before "healer") are present elsewhere. In his highest-stakes moment of the session, his voice became generic. The Roleplayer wants: one Dren-specific word in that sentence. Not more words — one word that couldn't have come from anyone else. He has Aldis in his pocket. He doesn't have to say the name. But the sentence should contain his weight.

- **Maret after the cup (Scene 2.4):** She holds the cup for five seconds and sets it back. The session log describes the practice-recognition beat — the cup and the three-breath practice are the same kind of knowledge. Then: nothing. The log does not record what Maret does next. The Roleplayer wants: Maret does one specific thing after setting the cup down. Not a declaration. Not an explanation. One action that carries the weight. Does she straighten the cup on the shelf? Does she look at her hands? One gesture.

### The question I'd push on

**When does Calla say Tarrin's name aloud?** She has now corrected an imperfect rite, recognized oral transmission without a keeper, and held three documentation-suspension beats across two sessions. She is accumulating the vocabulary for what she experienced when Thornfield burned and Tarrin died. The arc has been building toward a moment when she names what she is doing and why. That moment is not yet. But it needs a designed entry-point — a scene in a future adventure where the conditions are right for the naming. Design Calla's naming beat into 0018 or 0020 (when she hears the full account of Velantha's practice, which parallels Thornfield's). Put it in the campaign spine's PC-authored deliverable section.
