## Critique

### Overall assessment

Two sessions. Zero party damage. I understand this is a social campaign. I accept that the encounters are social. But a level-3 party with a fighter, a monk, and a warlock has not been tested mechanically in two consecutive sessions, and the Bell Tower is next. Design something with teeth for 0017 or the party will arrive at the final confrontation not knowing what their resources feel like under pressure.

### What worked

- **Scene 2.2 (Rat Swarm, initiative order):** Dren went first (19) because of his stat investment. He dealt 11 damage (with GWF reroll). Renn followed and killed it. The initiative order rewarded the party's construction — the high-initiative fighter went first and did the heavy work. This is how combat should feel at the table: the right tool, in the right slot, at the right moment.

- **Gorret's confrontation (Scene 2.5):** Not a combat encounter but a tactical decision-point. Dren's doubt-die resolved to "honest answer." The honest answer contained Magistra Doran explicitly — *"before we can tell our employer anything useful"* — which is the most tactically precise thing Dren has said in two sessions. He named the constraint he is operating under. That is how soldiers talk.

### What needs work

- **Zero party damage in two sessions.** The Tactician's standard: a session should include at least one moment where mechanics told the players something they didn't want to hear. Both sessions have ended with the party at full HP. The rat swarm died before it could bite Dren. The Ghoul died before it could paralyze Maret. The party's defenses have not been tested. The Bell Tower (0017) must have an encounter where the party uses resources. Design it to drain at least one spell slot and one hit-dice worth of HP before the session ends.

- **GWF reroll via unplanned die.** Scene 2.2: Dren rolled a 2 on his damage die (2d6), triggering GWF reroll. The session log used the Calla RP beat roll (total 14, using 4 as the reroll value). This is correct in outcome but the reroll should be a formally tracked die in the sequence. For S3, ensure the DiceEngine pre-rolls include at least 2 damage reroll slots for GWF.

### The question I'd push on

**0017 must have a combatant that hits.** The Bell Tower's aggrieved congregation is described as "dangerous — resentment has curdled." That means they fight if the party does not handle them socially. Design at least one combatant in 0017 with +5 or better to-hit against AC 14-16 and multiple attacks or a dangerous ability (not just damage — a condition). The party needs to learn what being hit feels like before the campaign's midpoint.
