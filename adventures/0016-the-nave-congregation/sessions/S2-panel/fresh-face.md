## Critique

### Overall assessment

The session is followable. The morning rite is the clearest structured event the workshop has produced — seven steps narrated in sequence, each comprehensible. The imperfect rite in the Apse makes the seven-step structure visible by contrast. A new reader could follow what the rite is, what the cup does, and why both matter. My concern: "gully dwarves" still unglosed for the third time (S1 summary, S2 opening, S2 narrative). And Gorret's confrontation uses "gully dwarf" without ever explaining what makes their thirty-year practice extraordinary.

### What worked

- **The morning rite narration (Scene 2.2):** Seven steps, each named and distinct. *"Step 1: water carried from the cistern. Step 2: sharing — the cup passed along the rows."* A new player reads this and understands: the congregation has a structured practice; the cup is central to it; the amount is always right without measurement. No jargon. No assumed knowledge. The rite is described in its actions, not its meaning.

- **Gorret's question and answer (Scene 2.5):** *"Why do you need to go up?"* and *"We need to understand the full building before we can tell our employer anything useful."* A new reader understands both: the gatekeeper is asking whether the party belongs here; the party is admitting they're hired and uncertain. The exchange is clear even without knowing the full context.

### What needs work

- **"Gully dwarves" unglosed for the third time.** The S1 panel's convergent suggestion has not been applied in two consecutive sessions. A new reader opening S2 encounters "gully dwarves" in the session summary as if they should already know what makes their 30-year devotion remarkable. They don't. One sentence. Three sessions now. Apply it to the S3 log's first paragraph.

- **"Awakened Mind" and "Eldritch Blast" appear without translation.** In Scene 2.1: "Renn (via Awakened Mind)." In Scene 2.2: "Renn: Eldritch Blast." These are class feature names. The session log should translate them: "Renn (speaking directly into Veth's mind without words)" and "a bolt of dark force from Renn's outstretched hand." The S1 log translated Awakened Mind well; this session slipped back to mechanic-naming.

### The question I'd push on

**What does a new player understand about Velantha after two sessions?** S1: a healer died here. S2: she used a cup and left a satchel and did something the congregation is grateful for. The fresh face doesn't yet know what she did or what the connection to High Sorcery is. This is correct pacing — the mystery is unfolding. But by the end of S3 (adventure 0017), the fresh face should have enough to formulate a theory. Design one piece of explicit information that a non-Dragonlance reader can grasp in 0017. The fresh face earns full credit when they can say: "Velantha used forbidden magic to heal the gully dwarves because the official channels wouldn't." That sentence needs to be available by 0017.
