## Critique

### Overall assessment

Two off-book moments that both had consequences: Calla correcting the imperfect rite (not listed in any route condition; had real faction effect), and Veth's arc-completion generating information beyond its designed scope. The DM said yes to both. The Improviser is satisfied. My one critique: the debris clearance failure (Maret Athletics 11 vs DC 13) should have had a consolation beat — she doesn't clear the debris but she *learns something* from the attempt. The no-consequence failure is the weakest kind.

### What worked

- **Calla's rite correction (Scene 2.4):** Off-book in the most complete sense — not listed as an option, not a route condition, not prompted by any NPC. The DM adjudicated it as a real action with real faction effect (Nave congregation regard improves; Gorret "notes it" from R6). The yes was earned and it had consequences. The Improviser gives this the highest marks of the session.

- **Veth's arc-completion unlocking the elder disclosure (Scene 2.7):** The arc-completion fired as designed. Then Veth voluntarily added: *"The elder in the Transept was there the night she performed the ritual."* This was in his information readiness conditions but not in his arc text. The DM let the information flow after the arc fired, rather than pausing it for a separate social check. This is correct: an NPC who has just said something vulnerable is more likely to keep talking, not less. The DM read this correctly.

### What needs work

- **Maret's debris failure (Scene 2.6, Athletics 11 vs DC 13):** She fails to clear the debris. The session log records: *"She notes the passage mentally."* That is accurate but flat. A failed check in a significant location should produce information or a sensory beat even on failure. What does the debris smell like? What is it made of — is it construction material, or did the congregation block this passage intentionally? The failure tells the player "not now" but should also tell them something about "why." The Improviser would have had the debris shift enough to reveal a draft — the Bell Tower is breathing through this passage. One line changes the failure from closed to interesting.

- **Gorret's quiet rite at the cup (Wandering Encounter, Scene 2.4):** This happened and was correctly narrated. The congregation member said: *"He does that sometimes."* Correct and understated. But Gorret has not yet had his confrontation at R5 at this point in the session. His private rite fires before he asks his question. The Improviser wonders: does this beat change Dren's honest answer? If Dren sees Gorret perform the verse privately, before the confrontation, the honest answer might be different (more direct, more personal). The module should note: if the party witnesses Gorret's private rite before the R5 confrontation, Dren's answer will be informed by it.

### The question I'd push on

**What is in the debris blocking the R6 east passage?** Maret failed to clear it. But the DM should know what she would have found if she'd succeeded. Construction material? A deliberate block by the congregation for reasons they haven't explained? Something from the Bell Tower side? The Improviser needs the DM to have an answer before 0017, because the party will eventually clear this passage and the answer shapes the encounter.
