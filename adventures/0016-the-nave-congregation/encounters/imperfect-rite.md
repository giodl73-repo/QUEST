# Encounter — Imperfect Rite

## Type

Social/atmospheric encounter. Non-combatant. The session's thematic statement.

## Setup

Three recently-arrived congregation members are in R4 practicing the morning rite. They know four of the seven steps. They have the spoken verse. They are performing the steps out of sequence — the verse comes in step 2 (should be step 3), the water-offering comes last (should be step 6), the floor-sweep is missing entirely. They do not know they are wrong. They were taught by another recently-arrived member who also did not have the full sequence.

They stop when the party enters. They look uncertain — not afraid, but unsure if the interruption means they are doing something wrong.

## The Question They Ask

One of the three says, in broken but clear Common: *"Is that right?"*

They are asking about the rite. They genuinely want to know.

## The Party's Options

1. **Explain what they observed of the correct rite in R2.** The three practitioners listen carefully. One of them corrects the others on the verse timing. They restart the rite, this time with the verse in the right place. They still miss the floor-sweep but they are closer. **Faction effect:** the Nave congregation's regard for the party increases; Gorret, if he observes from R6, notes it.

2. **Say they don't know the correct rite either.** Honest answer. The three practitioners nod — they were not certain either. They continue their version. **Faction effect:** neutral; no change.

3. **Say nothing.** The three practitioners wait for a moment, then continue their version. **Faction effect:** neutral.

4. **Correct them dismissively or mockingly.** The three practitioners go still. One of them says: *"We are learning."* They continue. **Faction effect:** mild negative; Gorret, if watching, stands up from his position in R6.

## What This Encounter Is About

The imperfect rite is what happens to oral tradition without a keeper. The full sequence is with the Transept elder (0018). What is happening here is three generations of re-teaching without access to the source. The party, having just witnessed the full rite performed correctly by Veth in R2, is now seeing what the rite looks like when it has been transmitted imperfectly.

**Calla's documentation-suspension:** This is the third activation of Calla's arc. She has now witnessed: (1) the shrine arrangements she cannot categorize (0015, 0016), (2) the morning rite performed correctly by Veth, (3) the morning rite performed imperfectly by practitioners who don't know it is imperfect. She holds all three observations. She cannot file any of them under a tradition she knows. But she is beginning to understand what the tradition IS — it is imperfect transmission of something that happened once, by someone who didn't survive to pass it on directly. This is also what Thornfield's oral tradition is. Calla has been performing an imperfect rite for ten years.

The DM should not narrate this connection for Calla. Let it sit.

## Route Implications

- All routes: this encounter plays out the same regardless of route. The imperfect rite is atmospheric — it doesn't gate any path. Its function is thematic, not mechanical.
