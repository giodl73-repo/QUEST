# Wandering Pressure — The Nave Congregation

*Roll Cold Pulse (d6) every 10 minutes of in-game exploration. Roll Wandering Encounter (d6) every 30 minutes.*

*Justification for temporal pressure: the Nave is an active, inhabited space with an ongoing daily schedule. The congregation's rite occurs at specific times; arriving between rite time and rest time produces different social conditions. Temporal pressure here is social and atmospheric, not structural.*

---

## Cold Pulse Table (d6, every 10 min — atmospheric)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | The morning rite begins or ends (depending on time). The congregation assembles or disperses. Seven steps, twenty minutes. If the party is in R2, they may observe or participate. |
| 2 | Gorret moves. If he has already stepped aside (arc-completion fired), he is in R6 watching. If he has not: he repositions, choosing a spot with better sight lines to wherever the party currently is. |
| 3 | A congregation member approaches Veth with a quiet question about the rite. Veth answers. The party can hear: *"...does the verse come before or after the water?"* The transmission problem is audible. |
| 4 | The herb smell intensifies briefly — one of the practitioners in R4 has added dried herbs to a candle-nail holder as a personal offering. The smell, for a moment, is identical to the field satchel. |
| 5 | Wind through the gallery gaps above (R6). It moves the candle-nail flames in R2 simultaneously — hundreds of small flames flickering left at once, then settling. The congregation does not look up. This happens every day. |
| 6 | A congregation member in R3 (north aisle) begins the spoken verse quietly, alone: *"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it."* Once. Then silence. The fragment has been spoken in solitude for thirty years. |

## Wandering Encounter Table (d6, every 30 min — social/mechanical)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | Mig finds the party. She has been in the nave since she came through the inner door in 0015. She has placed her cup at the shrine in R4 (the cup she was carrying, not the measuring cup). She does not say anything to the party. She looks at them. She has already accomplished what she came to do. |
| 2 | A congregation member approaches the party with a small offering — a smooth stone, selected specifically. They hold it out. This is the congregation's version of an invitation: *you are being counted among us, if you want to be.* The party can accept (no mechanical effect; faction relationship improves) or decline (no negative consequence; the member understands). |
| 3 | Gorret descends from R6 (if he is there) and walks through the nave without looking at the party. He performs his own brief version of the rite — just the spoken verse, once, at the cup position. He returns to R6. He does not explain this. |
| 4 | A congregation member approaches Calla specifically — they have noticed she is watching without writing or recording. They say: *"You are trying to remember it."* Not a question. They sit beside her for a moment and say nothing else. Documentation-suspension beat for Calla: the congregation knows what she is doing. They are also doing it. |
| 5 | Three recently-arrived congregation members from the gatehouse (not from R4's imperfect-rite group) enter the nave for the first time. They stop at the threshold. They have never been in this room. They look at the full nave — the pews, the candles, the congregation, the cup. One of them sits down on the floor immediately. The others stand. This is their first time seeing what they came to. |
| 6 | The measuring cup is moved briefly during the morning rite (if rite is in progress) — Veth carries it through the congregation rows. The correct amount is visible in action: each person receives the right amount, and no one measures anything. If the party has not yet understood what the cup does, DC 11 Perception to notice; if they have, this is confirmation. |
