# Gorret, the Oldest

## Appearance

Male gully dwarf, 60+ years — the oldest member of the Nave congregation. Thin in the way of old age, not illness. He sits rather than stands when he can; standing requires effort that he conserves. His eyes are sharp. He has been watching people walk through this nave for thirty-two years and he has developed very good judgment about what they intend to do.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 6, AC 10, Speed 15 ft. He does not fight. If directly attacked, the congregation (20+ members in R2) will respond as a group.

## Public Knowledge

- He has been here for thirty-two years — longer than any other living congregation member. He arrived the year after Velantha died.
- He was healed. He does not say what of.
- He knows the rite. He was taught by Dova herself. His version is the most accurate; he does not correct the others because that is Veth's role, and Veth does not ask him to.
- He is skeptical of the party. He is skeptical of most outsiders. He was right to be skeptical, twice before: two previous attempts by construction workers to forcibly clear the nave, both of which ended with the congregation temporarily displaced before returning. He remembers both.

## Private Knowledge

- He was not healed of the plague. He was healed of something else — a bone-break that would have killed him slowly over years. Velantha set it correctly. He has been walking on that leg for thirty-two years. He does not know if she knew what it would cost her.
- He believes the party is ultimately going to clear the building. He is staying anyway. He always stays.

## Information Readiness Condition

Gorret holds no special information. He is a gatekeeper, not an information holder. His function is the question: *"Why do you need to go up?"*

He does not respond to clever arguments, persuasion rolls, or bribes. The only thing that moves him is a direct, honest statement of purpose.

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Gorret's response |
|---|---|
| Party approaches R5 | He looks at them. He says: "Why do you need to go up?" |
| Honest answer (any form) | He is quiet. Then: "At least you say what you are." He stands, steps aside, ascends to R6 to watch from above. |
| Evasive answer | He stands. He places himself between party and stairway. He says: "Come back when you know why you're here." Not hostile — conclusive. |
| Party tries to walk past | He does not physically stop them. He says loudly: "Veth." He is calling for Veth. The congregation's attention turns to R5. |
| Party removes shoes before ascending | He looks at their feet. He looks at their face. He asks the question anyway, but his tone is different: less confrontational, more patient. He has seen this gesture. |
| Party has already participated in the rite | He looks at Veth first. If Veth nods: Gorret says nothing and moves aside without asking the question. |

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** The party gives an honest answer to his question.

**The act:** *"At least you say what you are."*

He says this once. He stands. He moves aside. He does not look at the party as they ascend. He climbs to R6 after them and watches from above without speaking to them again. The arc is not reconciliation; it is acknowledgment. He has been asking this question for thirty-two years. The party is the first group to answer it honestly. That is enough.

**Why this is sufficient:** Gorret is not asking the party to agree with him, to earn his trust, or to explain their employer. He is asking whether they know what they are doing in this building. An honest answer — whatever it contains — is the only answer he has ever wanted. The arc fires from truth-telling, not from any specific content of the truth.
