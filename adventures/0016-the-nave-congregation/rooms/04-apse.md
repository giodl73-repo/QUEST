# Room 04 — Apse

## Read-aloud

> The east end of the nave. An unfinished stone table — the altar that was never decorated. On the stone shelf behind it: a cracked clay cup, the crack sealed with tallow. A ring worn into the shelf's surface beneath the cup. Three gully dwarves are here, performing a version of the morning rite that is almost right — four of the seven steps, in the wrong order, with the spoken verse coming earlier than it should.

## Features

- **The measuring cup** (stone shelf, centered). A cracked clay cup. The crack is visible — tallow-sealed, decades old. The ring worn into the shelf beneath it: DC 10 Perception from up close. The cup is the session's central relic. See `treasures/measuring-cup.md`. **Two retrieval paths:** (1) participate in the full morning rite in R2 with Veth; after the rite, Veth brings the party here and holds the cup for them. (2) Ask Veth what the cup was *for* (the specific question about Velantha's practice, not the cup's current use) — Veth will bring them here directly, bypassing the rite.

- **The three imperfect-rite practitioners** (non-combatants). They are recently-arrived congregation members who have been taught the rite secondhand — they know four of the seven steps and have the spoken verse memorized, but they are performing it out of sequence. They do not realize they are doing it wrong. They are practicing, not performing. **DC 13 Religion** to recognize the sequence is wrong. **DC 10 Insight** to recognize these are newer members who have been taught by other newer members — the third-generation of transmission.

- **The unfinished altar stone** (center). Stone table, no carving, no cloth, no decoration. The congregation uses it as a surface for the rite's water-offering step. The stone is stained with thirty years of water offerings. No other mark. **DC 14 History** to understand: a cathedral altar would have specific carvings and consecrations. This one was never given them. It is a stone table that has been used as an altar for thirty years by people who were not told how to consecrate it.

- **The back corridor** (west wall, connecting to R3). Narrow passage. On the R4 side: DC 8 Perception to notice. Always passable.

- **Comparison moment** (if party has the field satchel or has held it): the measuring cup and the field satchel share the same magical signature — warmth from the satchel, correct-dose from the cup, faith-generated, same source. **DC 13 Arcana or Medicine** (advantage if the party member has held both objects): the two objects were made magical by the same mechanism. The congregation's thirty years of veneration generated the same quality of faith in both cases. The cup is the second object in the set.

## Encounter

**The imperfect rite** (social/atmospheric). See `encounters/imperfect-rite.md`. The three practitioners are not hostile — they are focused. If interrupted, they stop (uncertain whether the interruption is a sign they are doing it wrong). If the party watches without interrupting, the practitioners finish their version and look to the party for confirmation: *"Is that right?"* They are genuinely asking. This is the moment the party can help (by explaining what they observed of the correct rite in R2) or not. Helping deepens the congregation's trust; not helping has no negative consequence. Either way, the scene is the session's clearest statement of imperfect oral transmission.

## GM Notes

This is the session's climax room — the equivalent of R6 (Inner Door) in S1.

**The imperfect rite as the session's thematic statement.** The three practitioners are getting it wrong because the oral transmission has degraded over three generations of re-teaching. The full account is with the Transept elder (0018). What is happening in this room is what happens to every oral tradition without a keeper: it drifts, it loses sequence, it preserves the verse but not the order. Calla should feel this most keenly — she is an oral historian whose tradition is also imperfect since Thornfield burned.

**Maret's spotlight moment here.** When Maret holds the cup (if she holds it), the cup holds exactly the dose her practice needed to be the practice it is. The DM plays this as a physical sensation — the cup settles in her hand the way her breath settles in the third breath of the morning pattern. She does not need to understand it. The DM does not narrate what it means. She holds it and the practice is recognized. Log this as the craft-witness third activation: the relic confirms what the practice has been doing all along.

**Gorret's arc-completion fires at R5, not here.** This room is for the cup and the imperfect rite. The stairway confrontation is in R5. Do not conflate the two.

**Seeds planted here:**
- *oral-tradition-names-specific-night:* the spoken verse ("she came to us in the dark") is heard again in this room from the imperfect practitioners. The party now has two sources for the same fragment. The full account is elsewhere.
