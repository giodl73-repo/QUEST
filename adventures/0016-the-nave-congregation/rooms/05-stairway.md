# Room 05 — The Stairway

## Read-aloud

> Stone stairs ascending. At the foot of the stairway, on the south side of the nave, an older gully dwarf is sitting on the floor. He is not blocking the stairs. He is simply there. He looks at you when you approach. He says: "Why do you need to go up?"

*(51 words)*

## Features

- **Gorret** (sitting at the foot of the stairs). See `npcs/gorret.md`. He is not physically blocking — he is seated. He could be stepped over or walked past. The stairs are not locked. But his presence is a social gate: the congregation will interpret any party member who ascends without addressing Gorret as someone who has not understood what they are in. That changes the faction relationship.

- **The worn stone at the stair base** (DC 10 Perception). A smooth depression in the stone just before the first step — not structural erosion but deliberate: this is where congregation members leave their shoes before ascending the upper gallery. A tradition from Dova: the upper level is reserved for those in the congregation who need solitude. Bare feet on the upper stone is how you signal you understand this is private space. **Incidental purpose:** roleplay trigger — a party member who removes shoes before ascending will be noticed by Gorret, who will look at them differently. He will still ask his question. But he will ask it differently.

- **Sound from above** (DC 11 Perception while standing at the stair base). Voices — a small number, quiet, from R6. The congregation members who use the gallery as personal space. Not rite-sounds; conversation or silence. Evidence of people above.

- **The faction gate.** The stairway ascent requires one of: (a) Gorret's step-aside (arc-completion fires when the party answers his question honestly); (b) Nave congregation's trust demonstrated through rite participation and Veth's endorsement; (c) DC 15 Stealth to ascend without Gorret noticing (requires no congregation members in line of sight to the stairway — difficult during rite time). Option (c) closes Route D if used before Gorret's arc-completion.

## Encounter

**Gorret confrontation.** See `encounters/congregation-confrontation.md`. The single question: *"Why do you need to go up?"*

**Correct answers** (any honest statement): "To understand what's up there." / "To see the full cathedral." / "We are trying to understand what happened here." / "Velantha." The answer does not need to be eloquent. It needs to be true.

**Incorrect answers** (evasions or lies): "Our employer wants to know." / "We were hired to clear the site." / "It's part of our assessment." Any answer that frames the party's purpose in Doran's terms — clearing, assessing, restoring to construction — will make Gorret stand up. Not attack; stand up and place himself between the party and the stairs. He says: *"Then you should leave and come back when you know why you're here."*

**Gorret's arc-completion:** when the party gives an honest answer, he looks at them for a moment and says: *"At least you say what you are."* He stands, moves aside, and does not speak again. He climbs to R6 after the party ascends and watches from above.

**Fallback path (if Gorret blocks):** Veth will come to R5 if the party is stuck. He will ask Gorret to let them pass if he judges they have demonstrated understanding through their behavior in the nave (rite observation, cup interaction, R3 engagement). Gorret will comply but will say to Veth: *"You're vouching for them."* Veth: *"Yes."*

## Treasure

None. The worn stone is not portable and has no mechanical function.

- **Up (stairs):** R6 (Upper Gallery) — physically clear, socially gated

## GM Notes

This room's entire job is Gorret's question. One question. It sounds simple. It is not.

**Why the question matters.** The party has been sent here by Magistra Doran to assess and clear the site. They have not yet decided what to tell her. Gorret's question forces them to articulate their purpose — to themselves as much as to him. A party that has been playing Route D throughout the session will answer naturally. A party that has been hedging toward Route A will find the question uncomfortable.

**Fallback path exists for every answer.** No answer permanently closes the stairway. Even "we were hired to clear the site" leads to Gorret saying come back when you know why you're here — not never come back. The session continues. The party can return to the nave, engage more deeply, and try again.

**Do not make Gorret hostile.** He is not the Bell Tower's aggrieved group. He has thirty-two years of practice keeping faith. He is skeptical of the party's purpose, not of the party's presence. He is the congregation's honest voice for its own suspicion. Treat him as someone who has been asking difficult questions for thirty-two years and has developed patience with people who can't answer them immediately.
