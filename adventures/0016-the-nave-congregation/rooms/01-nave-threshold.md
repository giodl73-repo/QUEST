# Room 01 — Nave Threshold

## Read-aloud

> The nave: open sky where the roof should be, candles in holders bent from construction nails. The floor is swept. An older gully dwarf stands at the nave's edge watching you. The congregation moves behind him. The smell of herbs is distributed evenly — not strongest anywhere, simply present.

*(49 words)*

## Features

- **Veth** (threshold, waiting). See `npcs/veth.md`. He is not blocking; he is present. He looks at the party the way someone looks at people they have been expecting. He says: *"You came through. Good. Come in."* And steps aside.

- **Scale of the nave** (first impression). The party can see the full length from here: the pews in rows, the congregation in motion, the far east end where something sits on a stone shelf. **DC 10 Perception** to identify the object at the far end as a clay cup on a stone surface. **DC 12 Perception** to notice the worn ring in the stone beneath it from here (the ring is visible at closer range).

- **Candle-nail holders** (everywhere). Made from bent construction nails, each holder slightly different from the last — different bends, different thicknesses. Hundreds of them. If counted, there are more than could have been made by one person in a season. The congregation has been making them for thirty years, and each person's version is their own. **DC 13 Investigation** to notice: some of the oldest ones have a different style of bend — the original design, different from how the newer makers do it. Someone taught the congregation this. The teaching was imperfect transmission; each generation's version drifted slightly from the last.

- **Quiet north arch** (north wall, obvious). A stone arch to the left leads to the side aisle. From here, the sound of the congregation in R2 is clearly audible. Through the arch, it muffles. **Sensory cue at branch:** stepping through the arch, the congregation sounds recede. The side aisle is quiet.

## Encounter

**Veth introduction.** See `encounters/veth-introduction.md`. This is the session's first social encounter: the party meets the Nave congregation's keeper. Veth will ask one question before showing them further: *"What are you trying to understand?"* Not "what do you want" — what are you trying to understand. The distinction matters to him.

## GM Notes

This room is orientation and introduction. Its job is three things: (1) the party sees the full scope of the nave for the first time; (2) Veth's first question sets the session's framing ("what are you trying to understand?" is the question the whole adventure is asking); (3) the candle-nail detail establishes the congregation's thirty-year history of imperfect transmission, which will matter when the party reaches R4 and the practitioners who are getting the rite wrong.

**Veth's question.** Do not make the wrong answer punishing. If the party says "we were hired to clear this site," Veth nods slowly and says: "That's what you were told to do. I asked what you're trying to understand." He is patient. He has twenty-two years of practice being patient.

**Maret's first moment here:** She sees the candle-nail holders. She does not comment. She notices the variation — each slightly different, each serving the same purpose. This is the setup for her craft-witness deepening in R2 when she sees the rite itself.

**Seeds retrieved this room:** None directly. The congregation-was-organized seed is confirmed by Veth's presence and manner — he was expecting them, the congregation defers to him, this is organized.
