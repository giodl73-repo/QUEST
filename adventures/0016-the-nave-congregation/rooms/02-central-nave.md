# Room 02 — Central Nave

## Read-aloud

> Unfinished pews in rows, swept floor, the congregation moving between tasks or seated in the morning quiet. Candles everywhere. At the nave's south side, a broad stairway rises toward the upper level. At the far east end, the stone shelf with the clay cup. The congregation does not stop when you enter. They watch without turning fully toward you.

*(59 words)*

## Features

- **The morning rite** (if party is present at rite time — morning, approximately 1 hour after first light). Veth leads seven sequential steps: the carrying of water from a stone cistern near R3; the sharing (cup passed along the rows, always holding exactly the right amount); the spoken verse (*"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it"*); the floor-sweep; the candle placement; the second cup-use (water poured to the unfinished altar stone in R4, as an offering); the closing silence (two minutes, no movement). **DC 11 Perception** to notice the cup is never measured — Veth pours and the amount settles without calibration. **DC 13 Religion** to recognize the verse as the congregation's oral-tradition fragment — not a formal prayer but something that has been spoken this way so many times it has become one.

- **Congregation members** (approximately 12 present). Non-combatants. They follow Veth's lead. If the party behaves respectfully: neutral and watchful; they will answer direct questions in broken Common + gesture. If the party behaves disrespectfully (sits in the rows without asking, touches the pews without permission, handles objects without invitation): the congregation's neutral posture shifts to close attention, and Gorret moves from R5 to R2.

- **The north side arch** (midpoint of north wall). Connects to R3 (North Side Aisle). **Sensory cue:** through the arch, the congregation sounds muffle; the incense smell is different (personal, individual shrines rather than the collective practice).

- **The south stairway base** (south wall, ~30 ft from the east end). Stone steps ascending. Gorret is positioned here — not at the base of the stairs, but on the south side of the nave, approximately 15 ft from the stairway entrance. He watches the party's movement. He does not approach unless the party moves toward R5.

- **Oral verse audible during rite** (if party attends rite). The spoken verse (*"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it"*) is the oral-tradition seed. **DC 13 Insight** during the rite: this verse is a fragment of something longer; the congregation speaks it the way you speak something that used to be part of a larger thing. There is more. The elder (in the Transept) has the more.

- **The floor** (everywhere). Swept so many times it is almost polished. No debris. No construction dust. The polish is achieved through repetition, not tools. **DC 10 History** to recognize that this kind of polish takes decades — this floor has been swept daily for a very long time.

## Encounter

**Primary: Veth leads the morning rite** (social/atmospheric). See `encounters/veth-introduction.md`. If the party is present during rite time, Veth will invite them to observe — not participate, not yet. Observation is the first step.

**Craft-witness (Maret Sorn, spotlight):** During the morning rite, Maret sees the congregation's seven sequential steps. She has been performing her own practice (three breaths, sequential, devotional without formal tradition) for six years. The rite is longer and more collective but the structure is the same. This is the designed craft-witness resonance. The DM should note Maret's posture shifting when the rite's structure becomes clear. She does not comment. She watches. After the rite ends, she performs her three breaths. **Log this as the craft-witness second activation.** The parallel is named structure (sequential devotional practice without formal religion) rather than just parallel practice.

**Calla (documentation-suspension):** The spoken verse is the third time Calla has encountered something she cannot categorize. The verse is not poetry, not prayer, not liturgy. It is a fact, stated in the rhythm of a fact that has been stated a very long time. She holds the observation. The DM should note: she does not write this down. She does not have her lute. She is simply holding it.

**Secondary: Gorret watches from south wall** (conditional). Gorret's presence in R2 escalates if the party moves toward R5 without having demonstrated understanding through the rite or conversation with Veth. See `npcs/gorret.md` and `encounters/congregation-confrontation.md`.

**Optional: rat swarm remnant** (d6 at session start, 1-2 = present). A small remnant of the R2/R3 rat population from the gatehouse, following the party's scent. Half-HP swarm (12 HP, AC 10). Triggers if party moves quickly through R2 without pausing. The congregation retreats to the pews (not the bench frames — actual pews this time). Gorret watches.

## Treasure

None in this room. The cup is visible from here (eastern end, R4) but not accessible without moving to R4.

**Incidental:** The water cistern near R3 is stone-carved, construction-era, functional. It was built to supply the pilgrims who were supposed to come to this cathedral. The congregation uses it for the daily rite. It is one of the few construction-era features the cathedral was completed. Inside: a construction tool that fell in thirty years ago and has been sitting at the bottom since. **DC 15 Athletics or Investigation** to retrieve: a carpenter's square in reasonable condition. The congregation doesn't retrieve it because they don't know it is there.

## GM Notes

This is the session's main social arena — the equivalent of R2 (Guardroom) in 0015, but larger and more organized.

**Seeds planted here:**
- *aggrieved-faction-exists:* Veth, if asked about the congregation's full composition, says: *"Some of us came here angry. Some are still angry. They are in the tower."* He says it without apology and without further elaboration. This is the tell.
- *oral-tradition-names-specific-night:* The spoken verse during the rite ("she came to us in the dark") is the seed. The party hears the fragment. Insight 13 tells them it is a fragment. The elder has the full account.

**The rite schedule:** The morning rite occurs approximately 1 hour after first light. If the party arrives later in the day, they can ask Veth about it and he will describe it or demonstrate. He will offer to perform it again the following morning if the party stays.

**Gorret's behavior here:** He is not aggressive in R2. He watches. His intervention happens at R5 (the stairway). In R2, his presence is only pressure — the congregation knows he is watching; the party will feel it.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers near the pews)*
*The first time the pews were arranged in rows, there were only seven of them who knew how a cathedral worked. They had seen one once, in a valley two days' walk from where they grew up, before it was destroyed. They remembered the rows. They set the frames in rows. It felt right. It still feels right, thirty years later, even though the frames never got their planks.*
