# Room 06 — Upper Gallery

## Read-aloud

> Gallery above the nave: stone gaps where floor should be, open sky through the roof frame. Below — the full nave, congregation in rows, the cup on the shelf. Four congregation members settled in corners with their own arrangements. An arch east leads toward the Bell Tower.

*(47 words)*

## Features

- **View down into the nave** (the rail, always visible). From the gallery, the party can see the full nave below — the congregation from above, the rite if it is in progress. The cup on the shelf in R4 is visible from above. **DC 10 Perception** from the gallery: the worn ring under the cup is clearer from above than from the floor, because the angle of the light reveals it. *Relic sign confirmed from a different position.*

- **The east arch** (connecting to 0017 Bell Tower approach). A stone arch, unfinished, leading east. The passage is blocked by debris — construction materials the congregation has not cleared (they do not go this direction). **DC 13 Athletics or Investigation** to clear enough debris to pass. The passage leads to the Bell Tower approach (adventure 0017). Beyond the debris: a draft, and the distant sound of something that might be a bell that doesn't ring anymore.

- **Four congregation members** (settled in corners, DC 11 Perception to count). These are congregation members who use the gallery as private space — not the oldest members (those are in R3 aisle) but the ones who need height, light, sky. They are startled when the party ascends if the ascent was unexpected (Gorret didn't announce them). **Social beat:** the four members look up when the party arrives; they look at each other; they look at the party. One of them says, in clear Common: *"Gorret let you up."* Not a question. An observation.

- **Gaps in the stone floor** (two sections, clearly visible). The gallery was never finished; there are sections where the stone was never laid. **DC 10 Perception** to see them before stepping; no save required since they are visible. Each gap is approximately 3 ft wide — jumpable with a DC 10 Athletics check; the 20 ft drop to the nave floor below requires a DC 12 Dex save to avoid 2d6 bludgeoning on a fall. The congregation members navigate the gaps without thinking — they know where they are.

- **Wind** (from the roof gaps, constant). The gallery is exposed. Weather reaches it. The congregation members here have their arrangements weighted with small stones. The candle-nail holders here are designed differently — deeper cups to hold the wax in the wind. Someone adapted the design for the gallery conditions.

- **The orders-man seed** (planted here if not already planted in R2). One of the four gallery members, if the party asks about the full congregation composition, will mention: *"There is one who never comes up here. He stays in the sacristy and writes."* **DC 11 Insight** on their body language: they are not afraid of him; they are simply describing a member whose habits differ from the community's. He is not a threat to them. He is just the one who stays inside and writes.

## Encounter

**Four congregation members** (social, brief). Non-combatants. They accepted Gorret's judgment by remaining here after the party ascended. They are not hostile. They will answer questions if asked. The session's most important thing available in this room: the mention of "the one who writes" (the Sacristy seed).

## Treasure

None.

## Connections

- **Down (stairs):** R5 (Stairway) — always passable
- **East (arch, debris):** 0017 Bell Tower approach — DC 13 Athletics or Investigation to clear debris; campaign lock otherwise

## GM Notes

The gallery is reward and horizon simultaneously. From here the party sees the full nave — the thing they have been navigating — from above. The thirty years of work are visible: the swept floor, the arranged pews, the candles, the cup on the shelf. This is the session's horizon-broadening moment.

**The east arch toward 0017:** The debris is clearable but the session is designed to end here, not at the Bell Tower. If the party clears the debris and tries to proceed to 0017 in the same session: they can go, but 0017 is not yet designed and should be treated as a continuation of 0016 rather than a separate session. The DM should note the debris cleared in the handoff.

**Seeds planted here:**
- *orders-man-among-congregation:* "the one who stays in the sacristy and writes." Planted by one of the gallery congregation members. This is the session's most oblique plant — the party hears it in passing, from someone who doesn't find it interesting.

**Gorret relocates here** after his arc-completion fires. He climbs to the gallery and watches from above while the party explores. He does not descend. He is done with the confrontation at R5. From the gallery, he can see everything the party does in the nave below. This is how he has been watching for thirty-two years: from above, from a distance, from a place where he can see the full thing at once.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the gallery railing looking down)*
*The first time she looked down from here, she saw the Nave empty. She thought: someone will fill this. Not in her lifetime — she did not expect to live — but after. Someone will find this empty cathedral and they will fill it, the way things that matter get filled. She was right. She did not live to see it. The filling happened anyway.*
