# Room 03 — North Side Aisle

## Read-aloud

> The side aisle is quieter. Six gully dwarves are spaced along it, each at their own arrangement of stones on the floor. None of them are doing the same thing. The arrangements are more elaborate than the gatehouse shrines — more stones, more cloth, some with small objects placed carefully in the center. One has what looks like a child's drawing, protected under a flat stone. The R2 rite sounds distant here.

## Features

- **Individual shrine arrangements** (spaced along the north wall, 6 of them). These are the congregation's private devotional spaces — each maintained by a specific member, each different from the others. Unlike the gatehouse shrines (assembled from construction offcuts, still being worked out), these are finished: the stones are chosen, the cloth is specific, the arrangement is personal. **DC 12 Insight** to recognize: each arrangement represents approximately 1-5 years of personal refinement; the oldest ones (near the east end) are the most developed. **DC 14 Religion** to understand: these are not a formal tradition but they are a *practiced* tradition — each person has their own version of the same impulse, worked out over years.

- **The child's drawing** (protected under a flat stone, fourth arrangement from west). A piece of cloth-fragment with a drawing made in charcoal: a figure carrying a bag, a smaller figure beside them, a building behind. The drawing style suggests a child made it. **DC 12 Investigation** to assess: the charcoal has not faded under the stone protection; this is not ancient. Someone brought this drawing to the nave recently — possibly from the gatehouse (where child-sized tallow-handprints were found in S1). **Incidental item purpose:** evidence that the congregation includes children; evidence that a gatehouse child has an adult connection in the Nave. Roleplay trigger — if the party asks Veth about the drawing, he says: *"She made that for her grandmother. Her grandmother is in the Nave."*

- **Narrow back corridor** (east end of aisle, nearly hidden). The aisle connects to R4 (the Apse) via a narrow passage that runs behind the unfinished altar area. **DC 12 Perception from R3** to notice the passage — it blends into the stonework. **DC 8 Perception from R4** looking west into the aisle. The passage is always accessible; it is not guarded.

- **Incense smell** (throughout). Different from R2 — more varied, personal. The individual shrine practitioners have each developed their own sensory component. Six slightly different smells, all of them rooted in the same herbs. One shrine (third from east) smells almost identical to the field satchel from S1. **DC 11 Nature or Medicine** to recognize the herb match: the congregation in the aisle and in the nave use the same herbs, sourced from the same place. They are all, still, connected to Velantha's satchel.

- **The congregation members here** (6, non-combatants). They do not approach the party. They are in their practice. If addressed: they will respond briefly in broken Common, gesture toward their shrines, and return to practice. They are not unfriendly — they are simply busy. **DC 13 Persuasion** to get one of them to leave their practice and speak at length: the conversation, if achieved, reveals that each person here has a specific reason for being in the aisle rather than the main nave — some prefer solitude, some are newer arrivals who haven't integrated into the collective rite, some are the congregation's elders whose practice predates the organized morning rite.

## Encounter

None. This is the Stuart room — purely atmospheric. No creature will attack the party here unless the party has made the whole congregation hostile. **Documentation-suspension (Calla Fosse):** She enters the aisle and attempts, again, to categorize what she sees. She has now encountered the shrines in three locations (gatehouse R2, gatehouse R5, and here). The progression is visible: the gatehouse shrines are the newest and least developed; the nave aisle shrines are the oldest and most refined. The practice has been evolving for thirty years. She can describe the evolution. She still cannot name the tradition. The vocabulary continues to fail. But the failure is now *specific*: she knows it is a practice without a name, not a practice without a form.

## GM Notes

R3 is the aisle of personal practice — the congregation's version of what Maret does. The difference between R2 (collective rite) and R3 (individual practice) maps directly to the two modes of Maret's craft-witness: the structured collective morning rite (R2) and the individual breath-work (R3). This is the room that deepens the parallel from "their practice is like mine" to "their practice and mine come from the same place."

**Calla's arc:** The specific failure of her vocabulary shifts here. In S1, she couldn't categorize the shrines at all. In this session, she recognizes a developmental arc in the practice — from simple to complex, from uncertain to refined. She still cannot name it. But the failure is more articulate. This is the arc's second phase: the vocabulary hasn't arrived, but the shape of what she's missing has become clearer.

**The child's drawing:** If the party retrieved the gatehouse's tallow handprints (they were noted but not portable), the drawing here is the same child's work — same scale, same charcoal-on-cloth. The child who pressed their hand into the tallow in the gatehouse drew this picture of Velantha-with-the-bag for their grandmother in the Nave. Connecting these is a reward for attention.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the oldest shrine near the east end)*
*Dova's arrangement was there before anyone else's. She never explained it to the others. After she died, two members added to it — not in imitation, just in proximity. Over years, others added shrines near the east end. The aisle developed from the east backward, which is the opposite of how cathedrals are usually built.*
