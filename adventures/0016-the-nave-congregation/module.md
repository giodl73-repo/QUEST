## Setting & Background

**Place:** The nave of the Halted Spire — the cathedral's main body, the largest unfinished space in the building. The construction stopped eleven years ago, but the congregation has been finishing it in their own way: the nave floor swept clean daily for thirty years, the unfinished pew frames arranged in rows, candles burning in holders made from bent construction nails. Where the roof should be, open sky. Where the stained glass should be, empty stone frames. The congregation has placed smooth stones and cloth offerings in the sills.

The party enters from the inner door (R6 of adventure 0015) and steps into the first fully occupied section of the Halted Spire's interior.

**Atmosphere:** The nave floor has been swept so many times it is almost polished. The pews are unfinished wood but arranged with care. The candle-nail holders are everywhere — hundreds of them, each one made by a different hand at a different time, each one the same in purpose. The smell of herbs, which was strongest at R6's threshold, is here distributed evenly through the space. It comes from the measuring cup kept at the head of the nave. The cup has been at the head of the nave for thirty years.

The party enters. The Nave congregation has already been told the door is open — sound carries in a half-built cathedral. They were not afraid when Mig came through. They were not afraid of the party before they arrived.

**The Nave congregation's ambassador** — the older gully dwarf who appeared at the threshold in 0015 — waits at the nave's entrance and introduces them to the congregation. His name is **Veth**. He is not the elder (the elder is in the Transept, deeper in). He is the congregation's daily keeper: he sweeps the floor, he tends the candles, he ensures the rite happens every morning whether five congregation members are present or fifty. He has done this for twenty-two years. He does not find it remarkable. It is simply what he does.

The Nave congregation numbers approximately twenty-five. They have been here the longest. They are organized. They have roles: sweepers, candle-tenders, rite-leaders, three who hold the oral fragments between them (not the full account — that is the Transept elder — but the daily verses). They know what Velantha used. They were told by Dova, the First-Keeper, who was told by those who were there. They do not care. She healed them. The means are not the point.

**They are not hostile.** They will engage with the party if the party engages respectfully. They will share their daily rite if asked to observe it. They will share what they know about the congregation's history — up to the point where the full account lives (the Transept elder, further in). They will not give up the measuring cup to someone who has not demonstrated they understand what it is.

**Veth's information readiness condition:** Veth will share the seeds-to-be-planted if the party demonstrates they are trying to understand the congregation, not clear it. Specifically: if the party asks about the congregation's practice (not its history — its *practice*) Veth will explain how the rite works and mention that there is someone further in who holds the full account. He will also mention, obliquely, that the congregation is not entirely unified: *"Some of us came here angry. Some are still angry."* This is the seed for 0017 (the Bell Tower).

**The Orders' man:** Veth knows there is an Orders' man in the Sacristy. He will not say so unless the party asks directly about the congregation's full composition. He does not mention him casually. If asked: *"He came later than most. He does not join the rite. He writes."* This is the seed for 0019.

The cup is at the head of the nave. The congregation will not give it to the party on demand. Two retrieval paths:

1. **Participate in the morning rite** — observe and participate (not just watch) in the congregation's daily practice. Veth will offer the cup to the party member who participated most genuinely as a gesture of recognition. Not a transfer of ownership — a showing.

2. **Ask about Velantha** — if the party asks the congregation what the cup was *for* (the specific question about Velantha's practice, not just "what is that cup"), Veth will take them to the head of the nave and show it to them. He will hold it in front of whoever asks. The person who asked will be able to feel whether the dose is correct for them. This is the relic's property activating: whoever holds it feels the dose settle.

Neither path involves stealing or combat. Route D requires not taking the cup by force.

**Primary encounter — Congregation member with resentment:**
One of the Nave congregation members — **Gorret**, male, the congregation's oldest member, who came thirty-two years ago before the gatehouse group, before almost anyone — was the first to arrive after Velantha's death. He kept faith for thirty-two years. He is also the congregation member who argues most openly that the party's presence is a threat. He is not wrong to be suspicious. He is not hostile. He is simply the congregation's most honest voice for the suspicion the others hold privately.

Gorret will challenge the party's presence if they move toward the Sacristy stairway. This is the encounter: not combat but a confrontation that requires the party to explain why they should go deeper. If they explain honestly (they want to understand, not clear), Gorret will step back — grudgingly. If they lie or evade, Gorret will call for Veth, who will ask the party to leave the nave and reconsider.

CR: 0 (non-combatant; Gorret does not fight unless directly attacked). The encounter's "failure state" is the party being asked to leave and return when they have a clearer answer.

**Secondary encounter — Rat swarm remnant (from 0015 crawlspace):**
After the Ghoul was destroyed in 0015, the rat swarm may have expanded into the nave area through cracks in the floor. Roll for this at session start: d6, 1-2 = a small rat swarm (half HP: 12 HP) has followed the party's scent from the gatehouse. This encounter is optional and can be skipped if the session needs time elsewhere.

Cold Pulse (d6 per 10 min): the nave is more active than the gatehouse — the congregation performs throughout the day.
See `encounters/wandering-pressure.md` for tables.

- **congregation-was-organized** (planted 0015): confirmed and exceeded — the Nave congregation is the organizing center; the gatehouse group defers to them correctly.

- **orders-man-among-congregation** (→ 0019): Veth mentions "the one who writes" in the Sacristy — obliquely, if asked about congregation composition.
- **aggrieved-faction-exists** (→ 0017): Gorret's confrontation introduces the tension; Veth's *"some of us came here angry"* names it directly.
- **oral-tradition-names-specific-night** (→ 0018): the congregation's daily rite includes a verse that refers to "the night she came." The party hears it. The elder (deeper in) has the full account.

No new hint this session. Hint 1 deepens: the congregation's knowledge of Velantha's method (they know she used something other than prayer; they simply do not weigh it the way the institutions do) reinforces what the party learned in 0015. This is not new information — it is confirmation from a larger, more organized source.

Maret's second spotlight. The designed craft-witness resonance: the congregation performs their morning rite in the nave (assembled, sequential, seven steps, three people leading). Maret observes. Her breath-work practice and the congregation's rite are structurally parallel — devotional without formal tradition. This is the session that confirms whether the craft-witness resonance holds with a named NPC source (Veth) versus the unnamed member from S1.

**Additional Maret beat:** The measuring cup. Maret is not the party's healer — Calla has Healing Word; she has medicine checks. But Maret has spent years practicing a discipline without knowing what it is *for*. The cup's relic property (it holds the correct dose for whoever is holding it) resonates with her practice: it is also a form of knowing without being able to explain what you know. If Maret holds the cup, it holds exactly the dose that her practice needed to be the practice her practice is. The DM should play this beat if Maret is the first to hold the cup.

### Scene 1 — Nave Threshold

> The nave: open sky where the roof should be, candles in holders bent from construction nails. The floor is swept. An older gully dwarf stands at the nave's edge watching you. The congregation moves behind him. The smell of herbs is distributed evenly — not strongest anywhere, simply present.

#### Features

- **Veth** (threshold, waiting). See `npcs/veth.md`. He is not blocking; he is present. He looks at the party the way someone looks at people they have been expecting. He says: *"You came through. Good. Come in."* And steps aside.

- **Scale of the nave** (first impression). The party can see the full length from here: the pews in rows, the congregation in motion, the far east end where something sits on a stone shelf. **DC 10 Perception** to identify the object at the far end as a clay cup on a stone surface. **DC 12 Perception** to notice the worn ring in the stone beneath it from here (the ring is visible at closer range).

- **Candle-nail holders** (everywhere). Made from bent construction nails, each holder slightly different from the last — different bends, different thicknesses. Hundreds of them. If counted, there are more than could have been made by one person in a season. The congregation has been making them for thirty years, and each person's version is their own. **DC 13 Investigation** to notice: some of the oldest ones have a different style of bend — the original design, different from how the newer makers do it. Someone taught the congregation this. The teaching was imperfect transmission; each generation's version drifted slightly from the last.

- **Quiet north arch** (north wall, obvious). A stone arch to the left leads to the side aisle. From here, the sound of the congregation in R2 is clearly audible. Through the arch, it muffles. **Sensory cue at branch:** stepping through the arch, the congregation sounds recede. The side aisle is quiet.

#### Encounter

**Veth introduction.** **Encounter — Veth Introduction**

**Type**

Social encounter. The party's first meeting with the Nave congregation's daily keeper.

**Setup**

Veth has been expecting the party since Mig came through the inner door and the Nave congregation heard the door open. He is standing at the threshold when the party arrives — not blocking, simply present. He does not look alarmed. He looks like someone who expected this.. This is the session's first social encounter: the party meets the Nave congregation's keeper. Veth will ask one question before showing them further: *"What are you trying to understand?"* Not "what do you want" — what are you trying to understand. The distinction matters to him.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- **West:** 0015 inner door (back connection; always passable)
- **East:** R2 (Central Nave) — open main aisle
- **North:** R3 (North Side Aisle) — quiet arch, always accessible

#### GM Notes

This room is orientation and introduction. Its job is three things: (1) the party sees the full scope of the nave for the first time; (2) Veth's first question sets the session's framing ("what are you trying to understand?" is the question the whole adventure is asking); (3) the candle-nail detail establishes the congregation's thirty-year history of imperfect transmission, which will matter when the party reaches R4 and the practitioners who are getting the rite wrong.

**Veth's question.** Do not make the wrong answer punishing. If the party says "we were hired to clear this site," Veth nods slowly and says: "That's what you were told to do. I asked what you're trying to understand." He is patient. He has twenty-two years of practice being patient.

**Maret's first moment here:** She sees the candle-nail holders. She does not comment. She notices the variation — each slightly different, each serving the same purpose. This is the setup for her craft-witness deepening in R2 when she sees the rite itself.

**Seeds retrieved this room:** None directly. The congregation-was-organized seed is confirmed by Veth's presence and manner — he was expecting them, the congregation defers to him, this is organized.

She stood at this threshold for a moment, holding the satchel, looking at the space she had intended to consecrate. Then she walked in. She did not come back.*

### Scene 2 — Central Nave

> Unfinished pews in rows, swept floor, the congregation moving between tasks or seated in the morning quiet. Candles everywhere. At the nave's south side, a broad stairway rises toward the upper level. At the far east end, the stone shelf with the clay cup. The congregation does not stop when you enter. They watch without turning fully toward you.

#### Features

- **The morning rite** (if party is present at rite time — morning, approximately 1 hour after first light). Veth leads seven sequential steps: the carrying of water from a stone cistern near R3; the sharing (cup passed along the rows, always holding exactly the right amount); the spoken verse (*"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it"*); the floor-sweep; the candle placement; the second cup-use (water poured to the unfinished altar stone in R4, as an offering); the closing silence (two minutes, no movement). **DC 11 Perception** to notice the cup is never measured — Veth pours and the amount settles without calibration. **DC 13 Religion** to recognize the verse as the congregation's oral-tradition fragment — not a formal prayer but something that has been spoken this way so many times it has become one.

- **Congregation members** (approximately 12 present). Non-combatants. They follow Veth's lead. If the party behaves respectfully: neutral and watchful; they will answer direct questions in broken Common + gesture. If the party behaves disrespectfully (sits in the rows without asking, touches the pews without permission, handles objects without invitation): the congregation's neutral posture shifts to close attention, and Gorret moves from R5 to R2.

- **The north side arch** (midpoint of north wall). Connects to R3 (North Side Aisle). **Sensory cue:** through the arch, the congregation sounds muffle; the incense smell is different (personal, individual shrines rather than the collective practice).

- **The south stairway base** (south wall, ~30 ft from the east end). Stone steps ascending. Gorret is positioned here — not at the base of the stairs, but on the south side of the nave, approximately 15 ft from the stairway entrance. He watches the party's movement. He does not approach unless the party moves toward R5.

- **Oral verse audible during rite** (if party attends rite). The spoken verse (*"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it"*) is the oral-tradition seed. **DC 13 Insight** during the rite: this verse is a fragment of something longer; the congregation speaks it the way you speak something that used to be part of a larger thing. There is more. The elder (in the Transept) has the more.

- **The floor** (everywhere). Swept so many times it is almost polished. No debris. No construction dust. The polish is achieved through repetition, not tools. **DC 10 History** to recognize that this kind of polish takes decades — this floor has been swept daily for a very long time.

### Scene 3 — North Side Aisle

> The side aisle is quiet. Six congregation members, each at their own stone arrangement on the floor — more elaborate than the gatehouse shrines, more personal. None of them are doing the same thing. One has what looks like a child's drawing under a flat stone. The nave's rite sounds muffled from here.

#### Features

- **Individual shrine arrangements** (spaced along the north wall, 6 of them). These are the congregation's private devotional spaces — each maintained by a specific member, each different from the others. Unlike the gatehouse shrines (assembled from construction offcuts, still being worked out), these are finished: the stones are chosen, the cloth is specific, the arrangement is personal. **DC 12 Insight** to recognize: each arrangement represents approximately 1-5 years of personal refinement; the oldest ones (near the east end) are the most developed. **DC 14 Religion** to understand: these are not a formal tradition but they are a *practiced* tradition — each person has their own version of the same impulse, worked out over years.

- **The child's drawing** (protected under a flat stone, fourth arrangement from west). A piece of cloth-fragment with a drawing made in charcoal: a figure carrying a bag, a smaller figure beside them, a building behind. The drawing style suggests a child made it. **DC 12 Investigation** to assess: the charcoal has not faded under the stone protection; this is not ancient. Someone brought this drawing to the nave recently — possibly from the gatehouse (where child-sized tallow-handprints were found in S1). **Incidental item purpose:** evidence that the congregation includes children; evidence that a gatehouse child has an adult connection in the Nave. Roleplay trigger — if the party asks Veth about the drawing, he says: *"She made that for her grandmother. Her grandmother is in the Nave."*

- **Narrow back corridor** (east end of aisle, nearly hidden). The aisle connects to R4 (the Apse) via a narrow passage that runs behind the unfinished altar area. **DC 12 Perception from R3** to notice the passage — it blends into the stonework. **DC 8 Perception from R4** looking west into the aisle. The passage is always accessible; it is not guarded.

- **Incense smell** (throughout). Different from R2 — more varied, personal. The individual shrine practitioners have each developed their own sensory component. Six slightly different smells, all of them rooted in the same herbs. One shrine (third from east) smells almost identical to the field satchel from S1. **DC 11 Nature or Medicine** to recognize the herb match: the congregation in the aisle and in the nave use the same herbs, sourced from the same place. They are all, still, connected to Velantha's satchel.

- **The congregation members here** (6, non-combatants). They do not approach the party. They are in their practice. If addressed: they will respond briefly in broken Common, gesture toward their shrines, and return to practice. They are not unfriendly — they are simply busy. **DC 13 Persuasion** to get one of them to leave their practice and speak at length: the conversation, if achieved, reveals that each person here has a specific reason for being in the aisle rather than the main nave — some prefer solitude, some are newer arrivals who haven't integrated into the collective rite, some are the congregation's elders whose practice predates the organized morning rite.

#### Encounter

None. This is the Stuart room — purely atmospheric. No creature will attack the party here unless the party has made the whole congregation hostile. **Documentation-suspension (Calla Fosse):** She enters the aisle and attempts, again, to categorize what she sees. She has now encountered the shrines in three locations (gatehouse R2, gatehouse R5, and here). The progression is visible: the gatehouse shrines are the newest and least developed; the nave aisle shrines are the oldest and most refined. The practice has been evolving for thirty years. She can describe the evolution. She still cannot name the tradition. The vocabulary continues to fail. But the failure is now *specific*: she knows it is a practice without a name, not a practice without a form.

R3 is the aisle of personal practice — the congregation's version of what Maret does. The difference between R2 (collective rite) and R3 (individual practice) maps directly to the two modes of Maret's craft-witness: the structured collective morning rite (R2) and the individual breath-work (R3). This is the room that deepens the parallel from "their practice is like mine" to "their practice and mine come from the same place."

**Calla's arc:** The specific failure of her vocabulary shifts here. In S1, she couldn't categorize the shrines at all. In this session, she recognizes a developmental arc in the practice — from simple to complex, from uncertain to refined. She still cannot name it. But the failure is more articulate. This is the arc's second phase: the vocabulary hasn't arrived, but the shape of what she's missing has become clearer.

**The child's drawing:** If the party retrieved the gatehouse's tallow handprints (they were noted but not portable), the drawing here is the same child's work — same scale, same charcoal-on-cloth. The child who pressed their hand into the tallow in the gatehouse drew this picture of Velantha-with-the-bag for their grandmother in the Nave. Connecting these is a reward for attention.

### Scene 4 — Apse

> The altar end of the nave: unfinished stone table, no decoration. On the shelf behind it: a cracked clay cup, tallow-sealed crack, a ring worn into the stone beneath it. Three congregation members are attempting the morning rite — four of seven steps, wrong order, spoken verse too early.

# Veth, the Keeper

## Appearance

Older male gully dwarf, approximately 55 years (old for his kind). Compact. His hands are calloused from the morning sweep — a specific callous pattern on the right palm from holding the same broom handle the same way for twenty-two years. He moves through the nave without looking where he places his feet. He knows every stone.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 8, AC 10, Speed 20 ft. He does not fight. If directly threatened, he will place himself in front of the measuring cup and not move. The congregation will defend him.

## Public Knowledge

- He has been the daily keeper of the nave for twenty-two years.
- The morning rite has seven steps. He knows them in sequence; he learned them from the previous keeper, who learned them from Dova.
- The measuring cup has been at the head of the nave for thirty years. He has used it every morning for twenty-two. It always holds the right amount.
- Velantha came here thirty years ago. He was not alive. He knows the story through the rite and the oral transmission.
- There are congregation members who are not peaceful. They are in the tower. He says this without judgment.
- There is a man in the sacristy who writes. He has been there longer than most. He does not join the rite.

## Private Knowledge

- He suspects the measuring cup and the field satchel are connected — he noticed the satchel from the gatehouse when it came through the inner door with Mig, and felt something similar to what he feels holding the cup. He has not shared this with anyone. He does not have the vocabulary for it.
- He knows the rite's seven steps are being imperfectly transmitted. He knows the practitioners in R4 are getting it wrong. He has not corrected them because he is not certain his version is entirely right either — three generations of oral transmission. He holds this doubt privately.

## Information Readiness Condition

Veth holds two key seeds: the orders-man seed and the aggrieved-faction seed. He will give them when:

- **Orders-man seed:** The party asks about the congregation's full composition — who is here, where does everyone live in the building. He answers: *"Most of us are in the nave. Some are in the tower. One stays in the sacristy. He came later than most. He writes."*
- **Aggrieved-faction seed:** The party asks why the congregation is divided or whether everyone is content here. He answers: *"Some of us came here angry. Some are still angry. They are in the tower."*

The condition for both: the party must ask directly. Veth does not volunteer this information. He is not concealing it — he simply does not offer what is not asked.

### Congregation Confrontation

# Encounter — Congregation Confrontation

## Type

Social encounter. Non-combatant. Gorret's single question.

## Setup

Gorret is seated at the south side of R2, near the stairway base (R5). He is there whenever the party is in the nave — watching from a distance, tracking their movement. When they approach R5 (the stairway), he rises.

He is not blocking the stairs physically. He is socially blocking them. The congregation knows this. If the party tries to walk past without addressing him, a congregation member near the stairway will quietly redirect: "Gorret wants to speak with you."

## The Question

*"Why do you need to go up?"*

One question. He waits.

## Honest answers (stairway opens)

Any of these suffice:
- "To understand what else is in the building."
- "To see where the Bell Tower is."
- "We are trying to understand what happened here thirty years ago."
- "Velantha."
- "We don't know yet." (Gorret is especially patient with this one — it is honest.)
- Any statement that contains genuine purpose rather than institutional framing.

**Gorret's response to an honest answer:** *"At least you say what you are."* He stands. Steps aside. Climbs to R6.

## Evasive answers (stairway stays closed)

- "We were hired to assess this building."
- "Our employer wants to know."
- "It's part of our commission."
- Any institutional framing of the purpose.

**Gorret's response to an evasive answer:** He stands — not moving toward them, just standing. He says: *"Come back when you know why you're here."* He is conclusive, not hostile. The party can leave R5, engage further with the nave, and return.

## Fallback path (Veth's endorsement)

If the party is stuck and Veth judges they have demonstrated genuine engagement with the nave (participated in rite observation, held the cup, engaged with the aisle congregation), Veth will come to R5 and say: *"Let them pass."* Gorret will look at Veth for a moment and say: *"You're vouching for them."* Veth: *"Yes."* Gorret steps aside. He does not say his arc-completion line in this version — the arc requires the party's honest answer, not Veth's endorsement.

## Route Implications

- Route A: party answers institutionally, cannot ascend this session; stairway route remains available in future sessions if they return.
- Route C: party attempts to force past; congregation (20+ in R2) moves toward R5; non-violent collective pressure; Veth asks the party to leave; Route D closes.

### Imperfect Rite

# Encounter — Imperfect Rite

## Type

Social/atmospheric encounter. Non-combatant. The session's thematic statement.

## Setup

Three recently-arrived congregation members are in R4 practicing the morning rite. They know four of the seven steps. They have the spoken verse. They are performing the steps out of sequence — the verse comes in step 2 (should be step 3), the water-offering comes last (should be step 6), the floor-sweep is missing entirely. They do not know they are wrong. They were taught by another recently-arrived member who also did not have the full sequence.

They stop when the party enters. They look uncertain — not afraid, but unsure if the interruption means they are doing something wrong.

## The Question They Ask

One of the three says, in broken but clear Common: *"Is that right?"*

They are asking about the rite. They genuinely want to know.

## The Party's Options

1. **Explain what they observed of the correct rite in R2.** The three practitioners listen carefully. One of them corrects the others on the verse timing. They restart the rite, this time with the verse in the right place. They still miss the floor-sweep but they are closer. **Faction effect:** the Nave congregation's regard for the party increases; Gorret, if he observes from R6, notes it.

2. **Say they don't know the correct rite either.** Honest answer. The three practitioners nod — they were not certain either. They continue their version. **Faction effect:** neutral; no change.

3. **Say nothing.** The three practitioners wait for a moment, then continue their version. **Faction effect:** neutral.

4. **Correct them dismissively or mockingly.** The three practitioners go still. One of them says: *"We are learning."* They continue. **Faction effect:** mild negative; Gorret, if watching, stands up from his position in R6.

## What This Encounter Is About

The imperfect rite is what happens to oral tradition without a keeper. The full sequence is with the Transept elder (0018). What is happening here is three generations of re-teaching without access to the source. The party, having just witnessed the full rite performed correctly by Veth in R2, is now seeing what the rite looks like when it has been transmitted imperfectly.

**Calla's documentation-suspension:** This is the third activation of Calla's arc. She has now witnessed: (1) the shrine arrangements she cannot categorize (0015, 0016), (2) the morning rite performed correctly by Veth, (3) the morning rite performed imperfectly by practitioners who don't know it is imperfect. She holds all three observations. She cannot file any of them under a tradition she knows. But she is beginning to understand what the tradition IS — it is imperfect transmission of something that happened once, by someone who didn't survive to pass it on directly. This is also what Thornfield's oral tradition is. Calla has been performing an imperfect rite for ten years.

The DM should not narrate this connection for Calla. Let it sit.

## Route Implications

- All routes: this encounter plays out the same regardless of route. The imperfect rite is atmospheric — it doesn't gate any path. Its function is thematic, not mechanical.

### Veth Introduction

# Encounter — Veth Introduction

## Type

Social encounter. The party's first meeting with the Nave congregation's daily keeper.

## Setup

Veth has been expecting the party since Mig came through the inner door and the Nave congregation heard the door open. He is standing at the threshold when the party arrives — not blocking, simply present. He does not look alarmed. He looks like someone who expected this.

His first words: *"You came through. Good. Come in."*

## The Question

Before showing the party further, Veth asks one question: *"What are you trying to understand?"*

Not "who are you," not "what do you want" — *what are you trying to understand.* The distinction is his and it is deliberate. He has had too many people arrive wanting something. He wants to know if they are trying to learn.

**Answers that open the nave:**
Any answer that contains genuine curiosity or honest acknowledgment: "We don't know yet." / "We want to understand why no one will work here." / "We want to know who you are." / "Velantha." These all open the conversation.

**Answers that don't open it:**
Evasions or institutional framings: "We were hired to assess the building." / "Our employer wants the construction to resume." These prompt Veth to say: "That is what you were told to do. I asked what you're trying to understand." He is patient. He will give the party as many tries as they need.

## Social Resolution

The encounter resolves when the party answers honestly. Veth then:
1. Invites them to observe the morning rite (if rite time is near).
2. Offers to explain what the congregation does here (if they ask).
3. Shows them the nave's extent — the pews, the candles, the cup visible at the east end.

## Route Implications

- Route A: party answers institutionally; Veth is patient; session continues with limited congregation engagement.
- Route C: not triggered at this encounter.
- Route D: party answers honestly; Veth's full information becomes available; morning rite observation possible; cup interaction possible.
