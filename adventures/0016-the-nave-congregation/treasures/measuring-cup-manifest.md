# Relic Signs Manifest — Velantha's Measuring Cup

The dungeon housing this relic MUST express the presence of thirty years of congregational use in at least **3 of the following ways**, distributed across at least **2 rooms**.

These signs communicate: *something has been used here, with precision, for a very long time.*

## Required (≥3, distributed across ≥2 rooms)

- [ ] **Worn-smooth sign:** The cup's position at the head of the nave has worn a faint ring into the stone surface beneath it. Not deep — thirty years of a clay cup resting in the same place, moved and replaced daily. A character who looks closely at where the cup sits sees the ring. It is evidence of repetition without any other explanation.

- [ ] **Daily rite sign:** The congregation's morning rite includes the cup. The rite is observable: Veth lifts the cup, pours water (or medicine preparation), passes it to those who need it. The amount is always right — never short, never excess. At least one room must stage this rite as something the party can witness: the cup being used, the congregation receiving the exact amount, nobody measuring.

- [ ] **Congregation knowledge sign:** At least one congregation member, if asked about the cup, responds in a way that reveals they know what it does without finding it remarkable. Not "it's magical" — more like "it holds what needs to be held." The knowledge is matter-of-fact. The congregation has thirty years of daily evidence.

- [ ] **Comparison sign:** The party has already encountered the field satchel (0015). At least one scene in this adventure should create the opportunity to notice that two objects have the same magical signature — warmth, calibration, faith-generated. The measuring cup is the second object in a set. If the party holds both (the satchel in memory, the cup in hand), a character with Medicine or Arcana DC 13 recognizes the connection.

- [ ] **Crack-and-tallow sign:** The crack sealed with tallow is visible on the cup. DC 10 Perception to notice it from a normal viewing distance. The tallow is decades old — yellow, settled, still holding. This is the detail that tells the party the cup was nearly discarded and was kept anyway. Velantha used a cracked cup because it was what she had.

## Required (always)

- [ ] **The cup is present and observable before the party interacts with it.** Players should see the cup at the head of the nave before any scene requires them to engage with it. It should read as ordinary on first viewing. The relic properties emerge through the morning rite, not through the party picking it up and triggering an effect.

- [ ] **One room's organization expresses the cup's purpose.** The nave's layout — swept floor, arranged pews, candles at intervals — is organized around the morning rite in which the cup is used. The room is shaped by daily practice, which is shaped by the cup. This is the architecture expressing the artifact.

## Suggested (optional)

- Gorret (the resentful congregation member) holds the cup differently than Veth does — tighter, less settled. He has been coming to the rite for thirty-two years and has never found the correct amount reassuring. The cup gives him what he needs. He does not feel that what he needs is enough.
- One congregation member is visibly unwell and has been unwell since before the party arrived. During the rite, the cup holds more for her than for anyone else. The party may notice this if they watch carefully.

## Reaction moment (when the cup is first held by a non-congregation member with intent to help)

When a party member holds the cup *intending to help someone* — not just examining it, but with purpose — the cup holds exactly the right amount. The DM describes: *"The cup is heavier than it looked. The amount in it is right. You didn't measure anything."*

Do not explain the property here. Let the player ask. The question leads naturally to Veth and the congregation's thirty years of evidence.
