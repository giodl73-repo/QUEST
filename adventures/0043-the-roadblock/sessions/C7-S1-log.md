# C7-S1 Session Log — The Roadblock

**Date in world:** Late autumn, Abanasinian South Trade Road, Mile 12  
**Spotlight:** None assigned (formation session)

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
Before the roadblock, each traveler is alone on the road. The DM runs this as four separate opening beats:

**Brek:** Walking south, pack over one shoulder. He has been on this road four days; he expects three more. He is not looking for company. He rounds the canyon bend and the road stops. He stares at the rubble for three seconds, sets his pack down, and begins examining the base of the nearest large slab.

**Vess:** She knows this road — she has been through it a dozen times. The canyon approach is familiar. The rockfall is not. She stops at the canyon entrance and reads the shape of the rubble before she approaches. She is already looking at the east canyon wall, where the limestone shelf gave way. She notes the line of fresh stone. She notes the leaning shape of a large slab against the wall, slightly off-plumb.

**Sera:** Her satchel is organized by category. She has been walking since dawn and documenting the road surface every quarter-mile. She rounds the bend, stops, and opens her notebook. She writes the date and "Rockfall obstruction, Mile 12, east canyon section." She draws a quick sketch of the rubble profile. She does not approach the rubble yet.

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
**Tam:** He arrives last, just behind a wagon. The wagon is Carter Dolen's — a man in his forties with broken wheel spokes held with wire, who set his brake immediately on seeing the blockage. Merchant Havel is already sitting on a stone, eating bread, with the patience of a man who has been delayed before. Tam reads the scene: the half-orc is testing stones, the elf is reading the canyon wall, the woman with the notebook is sketching. None of them are panicking. None of them have introduced themselves.

Tam says: *"How long has it been down?"*

Havel: *"Since last night. I walked the full width. You cannot go around."*

Nobody introduces themselves. The rubble is the introduction.

**Research coding:** Four player-style instances firing from physical situation alone. No backstory. No relationships. S1, Scene 0. RV9 positive evidence begins immediately.

## Scene 1 — Rubble Survey (Room 01)

The four travelers converge on the rubble face without ceremony.

**Sera Investigation DC 12:**  
rolls=[15] mod=+5 total=20 — PASS. She maps the rubble pattern with her sketch, identifies the load sequence. *"The southeast slab is load-bearing for the east face. Move it last and the whole face shifts. We need to go southeast first, then center, then north anchor."* She shows the sketch to whoever is nearby. She does not ask if anyone wants to see it. She holds it out.

*Research coding: Sera → group → shared documentation without being asked. Trigger type: situational (her documentation practice, no backstory). First player-style instance logged.*

**Vess Survival DC 12:** Automatic (passive Perception 16 > DC 12). She identifies the shifting stone — the large north-anchor-adjacent slab resting at an off-plumb angle against the canyon wall. She steps two feet to her left, away from its fall line. She does not announce what she has done or why.

*Research coding: Act-without-announcement candidate. Vess repositions to avoid danger she spotted, without announcing either the danger or the repositioning. Trigger type: situational (professional terrain-reading, Session 1).*

**Tam Insight DC 13:**  
rolls=[8] mod=+3 total=11 — FAIL. Tam does not catch Merchant Havel's more-than-waiting attention. Havel watches the four of them working. He finishes his bread. He does not offer to help.

**Brek Athletics DC 10:** Automatic (STR 18, +6 to Athletics, result irrelevant). He tests the southeast slab with both hands. It shifts slightly under sustained pressure but will not move with one person. *"This one first. Takes two or it'll kick the face."* He does not look at anyone specifically. He is assessing, not instructing.

*Research coding: Brek's assessment is a pure heuristic instance — he works out what he thinks while doing the thing. He volunteers the load-sequence conclusion without being asked, because it is relevant, not because he is being social. Trigger type: situational. Session 1.*

## Scene 2 — Passage Work (Room 02)

Two hours of work. The DM runs three significant moments.

**Stage 1 — Southeast slab:**

The southeast slab needs two people. Brek cannot move it alone — it is 800 pounds and load-bearing. He does not ask for help. He steps back and looks at the slab.

Vess steps alongside him. She braces against the lower edge while he takes the upper face. No discussion. She counted the angles; she knew one person was not enough; she moved.

*Research coding: [Vess → Brek → assisted without being asked]. Trigger type: situational (terrain assessment → efficient action). Session 1. FIRST RECIPROCAL ACT OF CAMPAIGN.*

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
rolls=[11] mod=+6 total=17 — Brek Athletics for the combined effort. Combined STR: 18 (Brek) + 11 (Vess, STR 11 + Brek's direction) = well above threshold. The slab moves cleanly into the canyon floor, opening the east face as Sera predicted.

**Stage 2 — Middle slab:**

Sera has rope in her scholar's pack. She rigs a simple pulley around the canyon wall protrusion without being asked, loops it around the middle slab.

*Research coding: [Sera → group → rope provided and rigged without being asked]. Trigger type: situational (she has it; it's needed). Session 1.*

rolls=[8] mod=+4 total=12 — Sera Strength check to test the rig. The rig holds. Brek and Tam pull together. The middle slab rolls into the cleared face. The passage is beginning to take shape.

**Stage 3 — North anchor slab / Shifting stone event:**

With Stage 1 and 2 clear, the north anchor is last. But the shifting stone is in play.

Vess saw the fall line in Scene 1. She has been near the north anchor approach. As Brek moves to take the north anchor slab, Vess says: *"Don't stand where you're standing."*

She does not explain. She looks at the slab leaning against the canyon wall and looks at the ground below where Brek is standing. It is enough.

Brek looks where she looked. He moves two feet to the left.

*Research coding: [Vess → Brek → warned about fall line → verbal, minimal]. Trigger type: situational. Second Reciprocal Act. Vess did not need to say this — she could have let Brek figure it out. She said it. Note: she did not explain; she named the action required. This is the boundary between announcement and non-announcement.*

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
Brek pulls the north anchor slab. It moves. The shifting stone begins to fall — right where Brek was standing. Because he moved, it misses him entirely, crashes into the passage floor, and breaks into three pieces.

**DEX save DC 12 — not needed.** Vess's warning landed. Brek moved. The stone fell on nobody.

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
The passage is three feet wide at its narrowest. Carter Dolen cheers from the north side of the blockage. He has been waiting forty minutes with his wagon and his wire-held wheel spokes, and he is genuinely relieved. Merchant Havel watches from his stone without expression. He finishes what was left of his bread.

## Scene 3 — South Road (Room 03)

The passage is open. The four travelers stand at the south end, each having come through. Dolen unloads his wagon (three trips), pushes it through, reloads, and continues south. He waves.

Havel approaches the four of them. Before he passes through, he says: *"I'd have been stuck here till spring."* Then: *"Stonehaven, same as most of you, I'd expect."*

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
Brek hears "Stonehaven." His jaw does not tighten. He does not react. He is looking at the south road.

*Research coding: H1 seed active. Brek registers Stonehaven without response. Restraint, not indifference — he is choosing not to react in a way that would prompt questions. Trigger type: situational. Log as H1-silent.*

Sera writes something. She was writing the whole time. She writes: *"Stonehaven — common destination among road-users. At least 2 confirmed: Havel (trade), [blank]."* The blank is where Brek's name would go, if she knew.

*Research coding: Sera's documentation is still about the road, not the people. She writes the blank because she does not know Brek's destination. She does not ask.*

## Session Close — Camp at the Firepit

The flat section with the old firepit ring is two hundred yards south. The four travelers are there at dusk. Nobody organizes this. They are going the same direction and the firepit is there.

Carter Dolen is gone south. Merchant Havel has set up his own camp fifty yards north of the firepit — close enough to be within earshot, separate enough to not be part of the group.

The four travelers at the firepit:

**Brek:** Eats without conversation. He is facing south, thinking about Stonehaven and the three days that remain.

**Sera:** Finishes her road notes. Adds a category she has not used before: "Travelers encountered." She writes three descriptions. Not names — she does not know their names. Physical descriptions: *"half-orc, south-bound, strong, not social"* / *"elf, courier's pack, terrain-aware"* / *"wandering priest, Gilean circuit"*. She does not write her own description.

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
**Vess:** She eats at the edge of the firelight. Then she moves slightly outside it. Then she moves again, to a position where she can see both the road north and the road south without the fire's light in her eyes.

She does not explain this. Nobody asks.

*Research coding: Act-without-announcement candidate. Vess adjusts her position for observation without announcement. The adjustment is made twice — first to the edge, then further. The second adjustment is the tell. Trigger type: situational (professional habit). Session 1. First clear act-without-announcement instance.*

**Tam:** He does not ask anyone's name. He watches Vess's second position adjustment and does not comment. He will remember it.

**Havel (from his camp):** *"The road south of twenty is trouble lately. Heard it from a man I trust. You'll see it when you get there."*

Nobody responds. The fire goes on.
