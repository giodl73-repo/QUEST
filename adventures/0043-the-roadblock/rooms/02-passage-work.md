# Room 02 — Passage Work

## Read-aloud

> Two hours of work. The limestone does not cooperate. It shifts in ways it should not; it holds in ways it should not. There is a rhythm to it eventually — one person moving stone, one steadying the face, one watching the east wall for movement, one clearing the scree behind.

## DM notes

- This is group labor. Approximately 2 hours in-world. DM narrates three rounds of significant work:
  - **Round 1 — The southeast slab:** Requires STR 14+ or two people (combined STR 18+). Brek can move it alone (STR 18). Other PCs may assist — if they do, note it as Reciprocal Act candidate.
  - **Round 2 — The middle slab:** Requires rope management. If Sera has rope in her gear (she does — scholar's pack includes rope), she rigs a pulley that makes it manageable. If not, Athletics DC 15.
  - **Round 3 — The shifting stone:** This is the dangerous moment. The north anchor slab must be moved last. When it's pulled, the shifting stone (spotted by Vess in Room 01) begins to fall toward whoever is in the passage.

- **The shifting stone event:**
  - Whoever is in the passage must DEX save DC 12 or take 2d6 bludgeoning damage.
  - If Vess warned anyone about the fall line (she spotted it), they have advantage on the save.
  - **First Reciprocal Act opportunity:** If Vess warned someone, log: *Vess → [PC name] → warned about fall line → unprompted.*
  - **Second opportunity:** Brek can use his action to anchor the stone instead of getting clear — he takes 1d6 damage (auto-hit) but prevents the stone from falling on whoever else is in the passage. If he does this for a stranger, log: *Brek → [PC name] → anchored stone → unprompted.*

- Carter Dolen cheers from the north side when the passage opens. He does not help.
- Merchant Havel watches without expression.

## What this room establishes

Physical cooperation under real (minor) danger. First Reciprocal Act candidates. Brek's act-first heuristic in non-combat context.

## Exits

North: Rubble Survey (Room 01).
South: South Road (Room 03) — opens when passage is cleared.
