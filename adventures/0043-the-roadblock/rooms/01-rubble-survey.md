# Room 01 — Rubble Survey

## Read-aloud

> Limestone slabs fist-sized to chest-high, loose scree below. The east canyon wall shows the fresh scar — pale stone against grey. Forty feet wide, five feet deep at the narrowest. A passage is possible: one person wide, currently blocked by three large load-bearing slabs.

## DM notes

- **Survey options by heuristic:**
  - Brek (Athletics/physical): tests the largest stones by pushing. Discovers the load-bearing arrangement. *"These three have to move first, in order. Move the wrong one and the whole east face shifts."*
  - Sera (Investigation/documentation): sketches the rubble pattern, identifies the passage line. May note: *"If we remove the southeast slab first, the load transfers to the canyon wall. Then the middle slab. Then the north anchor."*
  - Vess (Survival/terrain): reads the canyon walls, identifies the shifting stone's position and fall line. Does not announce this — she positions herself away from it.
  - Tam (Perception/insight): notices Merchant Havel watching with more attention than a waiting traveler should have. Does not act on this yet.
- **Key skill checks:**
  - Brek: Athletics DC 10 to determine load sequence. Automatic — he's strong enough to test without risk.
  - Sera: Investigation DC 12 to map passage correctly. Success: she identifies the safest clearing route.
  - Vess: Survival DC 12 to spot the shifting stone's fall line. Passive 16 — she spots it automatically.
  - Tam: Insight DC 13 to read Havel's interest. If passed: Havel recognizes this passage and has used it before.

## What this room establishes

The problem requires coordinated effort. Nobody can do it alone. The survey is the first functional cooperation.

## Exits

West: Road Approach (Room 00).
East: Passage area (Room 02) — only accessible once survey is complete.
