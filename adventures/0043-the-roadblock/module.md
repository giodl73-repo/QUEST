## Setting & Background

A rockfall has blocked the only viable road south; four travelers who do not know each other discover they cannot clear it alone and must cooperate without explanation, introduction, or trust.

**The Abanasinian South Trade Road, Mile 12** (workshop-canon, southern Abanasinia, late autumn). A limestone shelf has collapsed across the road, filling a short canyon section with rubble. The blockage is forty feet wide and six feet deep at the highest point. It cannot be climbed safely without rope management; it cannot be moved without multiple people working in coordination. The day is overcast and cold. There are no other travelers behind the party yet. There is no inn close enough to make retreat a reasonable option.

Four strangers are on the same road for different reasons. The road is blocked. Neither can move it alone. The roadblock is not dramatic — it is merely inconvenient in a way that requires cooperation to solve. No backstory is required. The situation is the whole story.

1. **The road ends** — each traveler arrives at the blockage independently, within fifteen minutes of each other. First meeting has no ceremony. It's a logistical problem.
2. **The survey** — assessing the blockage. Sera documents. Vess maps the terrain. Brek tests the largest stones. Tam talks to the other travelers stuck behind (a carter and an elderly merchant).
3. **The work** — clearing a passage requires strength, rope management, and coordination. The passage takes about two hours. Complications: one stone shifts dangerously during clearing; someone is almost pinned.
4. **The passage opens** — the road is clear. The four travelers are standing at the same point. There is no reason to stay together except that they're going the same direction.
5. **Nightfall decision** — where to camp. The four are not a party yet; they are people who solved the same problem.

- No seeds yet. Pure formation session. Research baseline.

- **Carter Dolen** — a civilian traveler stuck on the south side; gratitude but no arc. One line: "I'd have been stuck here till spring."
- **Merchant Havel** — an elderly trade-road regular; watches the PCs work without offering to help. May know useful road information if asked.

S1 baseline session. Formation by necessity. The test: does anyone help someone else beyond the minimum required to clear the road? Do any player-style instances fire before any relationship exists? First Reciprocal Act opportunity: whoever secures the shifting stone for someone else during Scene 3.

## NPCs

# Carter Dolen

**Role:** Civilian traveler; gratitude NPC; no arc.

## Appearance

Forties, broad, with a carter's build — thick forearms and a permanent squint from years of reading weather. His wagon has two broken wheel spokes held with wire, which is doing fine actually.

## Emotional position

Grateful and straightforward. He can't clear the blockage himself. If the PCs clear it, he says: *"I'd have been stuck here till spring."* Then he unloads his wagon (three trips), pushes the wagon through, reloads, and continues south. He waves when he leaves.

## What he knows

Nothing useful. He's carrying timber fittings from a mill north to a construction project somewhere he doesn't name. He does not know what's happening in Stonehaven. He does not care about the road condition ahead. He is going to deliver his goods and return.

## Arc-completion conditions

No arc. His function is to demonstrate that the blocked road affects real people, and to produce a small moment of unceremonious gratitude when the passage opens.

## Voice

Direct and undemonstrative. *"I'd have been stuck here till spring."* That's his line. He does not elaborate.

# Merchant Havel

**Role:** Road-regular; observer; low-information NPC with incidental Stonehaven knowledge.

## Appearance

Sixties, grey-bearded, sitting on a stone with a piece of bread and the patience of a man who has been delayed before. He wears the practical clothes of a long-road trader — wool, patched, functional. His mule is picketed behind him.

## Emotional position

Havel has been doing this road for twenty years. He reads travelers the way he reads weather — quickly, professionally, without investment. He is not unfriendly. He is not warm. He will provide information if asked because information shared tends to return with interest.

## What he knows

- Stonehaven is 8 days south at wagon pace (5 days on foot, quick). He goes there twice a year.
- The road south of Mile 20 has had "trouble" for the last two weeks — he doesn't know exactly what, only that a courier he trusts told him to take the long road if he had a choice. He took the short road because he doesn't have a choice.
- He knows a man in Stonehaven named Torven who runs the weighing station. Torven is honest but under pressure from someone upstream. He will not elaborate on this unless asked twice.

## Arc-completion conditions

No arc for Havel — he is an informational NPC, not an arc-completion NPC. His role is to deposit Stonehaven context early and move on. He will not appear in later sessions.

## Voice

Flat, measured. He does not ask personal questions. If asked where he's going: *"Stonehaven."* If asked what's there: *"Work."* If the PCs mention Stonehaven themselves, he watches Brek for a moment before continuing.

## Session use

- Mention Stonehaven unprompted (triggered when PCs discuss destinations, which happens naturally).
- If Brek hears "Stonehaven" and does not react visibly: log as H1 restraint instance.
- If Brek asks follow-up questions about Stonehaven: H1 is active earlier than designed. Adjust S4 accordingly.

### Roadblock Clearing

# Roadblock Clearing (Group Skill Challenge)

## Setup

Not a combat encounter. Group skill challenge requiring coordination over 3 sequential stages.

## Stages

**Stage 1 — Southeast Slab (load-bearing)**
- STR 14+ to move alone, OR two characters with combined STR 18+
- Brek (STR 18) can solo it. If another PC assists, note cooperation.
- Athletics DC 12 to move safely without shifting the face.

**Stage 2 — Middle Slab (rope management)**
- Requires rope OR Athletics DC 15 brute-force.
- Sera has rope. If she provides it to the clearing effort: [Sera → group → rope shared → unprompted].
- With rope: Athletics DC 10 to rig, no damage risk.

**Stage 3 — North Anchor Slab + Shifting Stone**
- The dangerous stage. See shifting-stone.md for full mechanics.
- Must be done last per load-sequence (identified in survey).

## Time

Approximately 2 hours in-world. DM narrates highlights, not every minute.

## Failure conditions

- Moving Stage 1 before survey: face shifts, 1d4 bludgeoning to one random PC, work continues but with 30-minute delay.
- Moving Stage 3 before Stage 2: cascade failure — rubble refills the passage. Start over. No damage but full 2-hour reset.

## Assessment

Easy challenge. The party has sufficient combined STR and skills to succeed. The scenario is not about success/failure — it is about who does what for whom, and whether anyone acts beyond their minimum requirement.

### Shifting Stone

# Shifting Stone

## Setup

Environmental hazard, not combat. One large limestone slab (approximately 400 lbs, 6 ft × 4 ft) is resting at an unstable angle against the canyon wall. When the north anchor slab is removed, it falls toward whoever is in the passage.

## Mechanics

- **Fall trigger:** When the north anchor slab is moved, the shifting stone begins to fall.
- **Warning:** If Vess successfully spotted the fall line (Survival DC 12, passive 16), she may warn others. Warning grants advantage on the DEX save.
- **DEX save DC 12:** Failure = 2d6 bludgeoning damage (falling stone). Success = half damage (dodge partially).
- **Brek intervention option:** Brek can use his action (before the stone falls) to brace it with his body: takes 1d6 automatic bludgeoning damage, stone does not fall on the person in the passage. This is a voluntary act — he does it for no mechanical benefit; it is purely for someone else.

## XP

No combat XP. Environmental encounter — experience delivered as narrative resolution.

## Research coding

If Brek braces the stone: log as [Brek → (target PC) → braced shifting stone → unprompted]. Trigger type: situational (no backstory).

If Vess warns beforehand: log as [Vess → (target PC) → warned about fall line → unprompted]. Trigger type: situational (professional habit).

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
