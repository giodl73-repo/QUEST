# C5-S2 Session Log — The Informant

**Date in world:** PC 188, three days after C5-S1. Late night.
**Spotlight:** Darro Velt
**Seed:** C5S2-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro laid out his pick roll on the boarding house floor before Tessaly had finished her brief. The others were still settling. He went through the set in order — tension wrench, hook, short hook, diamond, the four he would need tonight — while Tessaly described the Watch House layout. He did not look up. When she reached the cell mechanism (key-and-bar, key on the duty rack), he re-rolled the short hook and set the roll on his left hip.

*"Key rack behind the duty desk,"* he said. *"I heard you."*

Tessaly noted it in the ledger margin: *S2 / Mercer Lane Watch House / South Station.*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira pressed her jacket, located the paper case, and turned her attention to the floor plan. She had spent the afternoon as "Heila Voss" doing a legitimate delivery two streets over from Mercer Lane — same Watch district, different errand. She came back knowing the constable's circuit ran south before north. Tessaly had that already. Mira did not mention she had confirmed it. She had confirmed it. That was enough.

## Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane: The Brief

Tessaly's notes on the South Station came from Rue Ashen, supplemented by two previous guild jobs involving Watch-adjacent operations. The map was accurate. Three cells, duty desk, sergeant's office.

She had not told the party why she trusted Aldras. She said it was a job.

*"The interrogating officer arrived from the main gaol this afternoon,"* Tessaly said. *"She is not Watch — she is from a unit that handles guild intelligence specifically. Rue confirmed this an hour ago. Aldras's second interrogation is at dawn. We have from now until first light."*

*"What does he know that she wants?"* Cael asked.

*"His cover name is already known to them. The question is whether he knows anything about our operation."*

Pause. Nobody asked whether he did.

*"Get him out,"* Tessaly said. *"That is the job."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael's left hand went flat against his chest. The contract. Present. He had checked without thinking about it. The job parameters matched his terms. He stood.

---

## Scene 2 — South Station Approach

After 3 bells. Mercer Lane, two blocks from the Wharfside. The Watch House had its night lamp at the duty desk entrance; one window dark (cells corridor), one half-lit (sergeant's office — the officer from the main gaol had not entirely extinguished hers).

Darro went to the service door via the alley. Stealth approach: **d20+8 → rolls=[6] mod=+8 total=14**. The constable was on exterior rounds — he had gone north thirty seconds before Darro arrived. Fourteen was enough for the alley, not enough for the interior.

The service door: standard ward, DC 13 (Darro at DC 9 with expertise). Thieves' Tools: **d20+8 → rolls=[12] mod=+8 total=20**. Clean. Door opened without sound.

The cells corridor was dark. The duty sergeant was at his desk — Passive Perception 8, asleep for the third hour. The officer's door was half-open.

Darro crossed toward the key rack. Interior Stealth: **d20+8 → rolls=[1] mod=+8 total=9 FUMBLE**. His left boot caught the leg of the duty chair — a small scrape, two inches on stone floor. Specific sound in a quiet building.

The officer's door opened wider.

*"Hello?"*

Alert voice. She had been half-asleep; the sound finished waking her. She could not see the corridor — her lamp was behind her, shadow forward.

Mira was in the corridor. She had come in behind Darro, positioned for this. Mira's Insight on the officer (read through the door crack): **d20+5 → rolls=[16] mod=+5 total=21**. The officer's boots were up on the cot frame — an aversion to floor contact when sleeping in unfamiliar buildings. Mira filed this in approximately two seconds.

Mira used Words of Terror. One minute of murmured conversation through the door as "a constable on rounds, heard something outside, please stay back." She named what was in the drain. Officer's WIS save: DC 13. **d20+1 → rolls=[16] mod=+1 total=17**. Fail.

Mira had said: *"Something moved in the drain. Could be rats. Big ones."* Calm, specific, not alarmed — which is more alarming than alarm. The officer stayed in the doorway. She was frightened of a thing she could not see and would not be able to say why when she thought about it later.

Darro had the key rack in twenty seconds. He did not run.

---

## Scene 3 — Cell Two

Aldras Bent was sitting up on his bench. Not asleep. He had the particular exhaustion of someone cycling between fear and fatigue without reaching either end. He looked at Darro through the bars.

*"Tessaly sent you."* Not a question.

Darro inserted the key. Cell bar mechanism: **d20+8 → rolls=[16] mod=+8 total=24**. Bar lifted cleanly, without sound. The cell door opened inward.

Aldras stood. Dock work clothes — arrested at the end of a shift. He did not ask about the other detainees. He did not ask about the officer. He followed Darro because Tessaly had sent him and that was the calculus. He walked exactly as fast as Darro walked. Not faster. He understood he was a professional problem and the best way to be one was to behave like one.

---

## Scene 4 — The Street

Two blocks from Cordwainer Lane. Constable's circuit had gone south and not yet returned north. Mira had rejoined from the alley. Cael had held the street entrance position.

Aldras stopped walking.

No signal, no preamble, no consultation. He simply stopped.

*"I need to tell her something. You can tell her, or I can. But it needs to be said tonight."*

Tessaly Insight on sincerity: **d20+4 → rolls=[2] mod=+4 total=6**. She could not read him — the flat exhaustion on his face had erased everything she might have calibrated from. Professional calculation: he had just been extracted at personal risk. If he wanted to buy goodwill with false information, this was not the moment. She nodded.

*"Tell me."*

*"The officer knew things only Pale could have told her. The date of a meeting in Pale's counting house — three months ago. She described the room. She had a list of informants by their cover names. I was fourth. The first three are quiet now."*

He was looking at the middle distance of Mercer Lane.

*"I'm not guessing. He sold the list. He sold it to someone in that unit — probably the handler, not a Watch officer directly, because the Watch does not know that unit operates in Havenmere. But it went through Pale."*

He stopped. Then:

*"I don't know why he would burn his own network. I have been trying to understand it for six hours and I cannot. But he did."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
**Arc-completion fired.** Aldras named Pale without being asked, from his own decision, when the extraction was complete and he had something to repay. He owed Tessaly a debt he could only settle with the truth. He settled it. This was not grief. He had no grief connection to Pale or to the party. He had a professional obligation and a professional debt and the debt required truth. He gave truth.

This is RV2: a morally-grey NPC completing his arc by character logic, no persuasion, no grief, no prompted disclosure.

---

## Scene 5 — Cordwainer Lane

Tessaly sat with Aldras at the table. Three clarifying questions: the date of the meeting (Aldras named it), the officer's name (he had heard it through the door during his first interrogation — a Revenue Intelligence roster name Tessaly recognized from Rue's files), and the method of contact (Pale's counting house, not a Watch station — voluntary sale, not coerced).

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
She wrote while he spoke. Date, source, content summary — the three cover names Aldras knew, the officer's roster name, Pale's counting house location. She wrote Pale's name last. The entry was complete before she said anything.

*"Thank you."*

Aldras reached into his dock coat and produced a smooth bone coin — worn through years of handling. A dockhand's marker.

*"I'll answer for you if you need it on the docks."* He set the coin on the table. *"I mean it."*

He left at dawn.

<!-- SURPRISE ambiguous -->
After he left, Mira was quiet for a moment. She checked the paper case — not the pre-approach check, something else. She held it for three seconds before withdrawing her hand. Nobody noticed. She had added a note. She did not say this. She had been sitting with Aldras's account and something in the account had started a line of thinking she wanted to preserve. She closed the jacket. She did not say what she had written.
