## Setting & Background

Darro breaks a guild informant out of a Watch holding cell before his second interrogation, and the informant repays the extraction by naming the person who sold his cover: Orren Pale, the guild master.

**Havenmere Watch House — South Station**, on Mercer Lane, two blocks from the Wharfside. The South Station handles dock crimes, minor debt disputes, and holds short-term detainees before transfer to the main gaol. It has three cells, a duty desk, a records room, and a sergeant's office. Tonight: one duty sergeant (asleep at 3 bells), one constable on rounds (exterior circuit, 20 minutes), two detainees who are not Aldras.

**Aldras** is in cell two. He has been there for six hours. His second interrogation is scheduled for dawn. The interrogating officer is not Watch — she arrived from the main gaol and is sleeping in the sergeant's office until dawn.

Aldras Bent — former dockhand, guild informant for three years — was picked up on a warrant citing "conspiracy to defraud" that has no public record. Tessaly received word from Rue Ashen two hours ago: the warrant is not routine. The officer sleeping in the sergeant's office is from a Watch unit that handles guild intelligence specifically. Aldras's cover name is already known to them. The question is whether Aldras knows anything about the party's operation that he will give up under second interrogation.

Tessaly briefed the party at Cordwainer Lane an hour ago. The brief was short: get him out before dawn. She did not explain why she trusts Aldras. She said it was a job and issued the markers.

Aldras was sold. He knows it and he knows who did it — he recognised a description that matches Orren Pale from the questioning in his first interrogation. The officer said something specific: she described a meeting in Pale's counting house, a date three months ago, and a list of informants by their cover names. Aldras was fourth on the list. He knows the first three cover names. Two of them have already gone quiet.

He does not want to tell the party this. He understands what it means for Tessaly and the guild. He also understands that being extracted by guild members while Pale is selling guild names puts him in an impossible position. He tells them anyway — not because he is asked, but because he has nothing left to protect and he owes Tessaly a debt that he can only pay with the truth.

### Scene 1 — Rue's Intelligence
Tessaly maps the Watch House for the party from Rue's notes: the three-cell layout, the single overnight guard, the 20-minute exterior circuit. She does not know about the officer from the main gaol — Rue's intelligence ran out before that detail arrived. The party plans with incomplete information. This is normal.

**DM note (RV2 setup):** Aldras is morally grey. He has given up three people to the Watch over three years — not by betrayal, but by accident (one meeting compromised, two covers burned by Pale selling the list). He is not clean. He is not evil. He is a man in an impossible situation who has been trying to stay ahead of it for too long.

### Scene 2 — South Station Approach
After 3 bells. The duty sergeant is asleep at his desk (Passive Perception 8 — he's been on shift fourteen hours). The constable is on exterior rounds. The back entry to the cells is a service door with a standard ward lock (DC 13 Thieves' Tools; Darro: DC 9). The cells themselves use a key-and-bar system — the keys are on a rack behind the duty desk.

**Complication:** The sergeant's office is occupied. The officer from the main gaol is asleep on the cot there — but her door is not fully latched. Passive Perception 13 from the cells corridor: she breathes unevenly, as someone does when they're half-awake. She may hear movement in the cells.

### Scene 3 — Cell Two
Darro gets to cell two. Aldras is awake — he has been awake since they brought him in. He looks at Darro for a moment and says: *"Tessaly sent you."* Not a question.

The cell bar mechanism: a key-and-bar system (key on the duty rack). Darro can lift the bar from outside through the slot (Thieves' Tools DC 11 — easier than a standard lock, Darro's expertise applies). Alternately: Stealth DC 12 to get to the duty rack, retrieve the key, and return without waking the sergeant.

**Aldras's manner:** He is calm with the flatness of someone who has been afraid for so long the fear no longer has texture. He does not ask questions. He does what Darro says. He is not grateful in any visible way.

### Scene 4 — The Street
They are clear of the Watch House. Two blocks from Cordwainer Lane. This is where Aldras stops walking and says what he needs to say.

*"I need to tell her something. You can tell her, or I can. But it needs to be said tonight."*

If the party asks what it is: he names Orren Pale. He describes the meeting in Pale's counting house. He names the date. He gives the three informants' cover names he knows. He says: *"The officer knew things that only Pale could have told her. I'm not guessing."*

He does not ask for protection. He does not ask for money. He says it because it is true and the truth has a recipient: Tessaly. This is his arc-completion.

**Arc-completion condition:** Aldras names Pale without being asked, when the party has demonstrated they extracted him without coercion and the street is clear. He speaks because he has decided to — not because of persuasion, not because of pressure, not because someone asked the right question. He is completing a debt.

### Scene 5 — Cordwainer Lane
They bring Aldras to Tessaly. She listens to his account. She asks three clarifying questions (the date, the officer's name, the method of contact). She does not show anything. She writes in her ledger. She says: *"Thank you."*

**Tessaly's ledger:** She writes the entry before she responds to Aldras. Date. Source. The content summary. The three cover names. Pale's name written last. This is the documentary-witness beat — she processes by recording.

**Aldras's marker:** He gives Tessaly his trust marker — a dockhand's bone coin, worn smooth. He says: *"I'll answer for you if you need it on the docks."* He means it. He leaves at dawn.

| Symptom | Type | Delivery | Passive fallback |
|---|---|---|---|
| The officer in the sergeant's office | Complication | Scene 2 Perception DC 13 | Auto-delivery if party passes the door |
| Aldras awake and waiting | Arc setup | Scene 3 cell observation | Cannot miss — he speaks first |
| Aldras names Pale (arc-completion) | Revelation + arc | Scene 4 street, after clear | Cannot miss — he stops walking to say it |
| Tessaly's ledger entry (Pale named) | Documentary-witness | Scene 5 — she writes before she speaks | Automatic per playstyle heuristic |
| Darro pick-maintenance ritual | Craft-witness (RV4) | Pre-Scene 1 | Automatic per playstyle heuristic |

**RV hypothesis positioned:** RV2 — Does Aldras's arc-completion fire by character logic when the NPC is morally grey? Aldras has given up people (not by malice, but by exposure). He is not sympathetic in a conventional sense. The question is whether his arc-completion fires the same way as a morally straightforward NPC.

**What DM observes:**
- Aldras's street disclosure: does it fire unprompted (character logic)? Score "character-logic" if he stops walking before any party member asks what he knows. Score "prompted" if it only fires after a direct question.
- Tessaly's ledger (Pale named): does she write before speaking? Score "professionally-triggered" for documentary-witness.
- Cael's response to hearing Pale's name: does the professional code (don't sell a name) activate as the filter? He was sold. Score "professionally-triggered" if his reaction routes through the code rather than anger.

**Token condition — Aldras (garrison-equivalent NPC):**
Aldras's trust marker is the bone coin. Token condition: he gives it after naming Pale, on the street, before Cordwainer Lane — the point of maximum vulnerability, when the guild is the most compromised. He gives it there because it is the honest moment, not the safe one.

- Whether the officer wakes: 1d4 on entering the cells corridor — on a 1, she stirs (Stealth DC 15 to continue without her raising alarm).
- Whether Darro uses the slot technique or retrieves the key: player choice determines which skill check.
- HP carry-over from S1: record end-HP + 1 hit die per PC per the campaign mechanic.

## Two paths to the cells

- **Path A (front):** Street → Duty Desk → Cells Corridor (risks waking sergeant)
- **Path B (rear):** Back Alley → Service Door → Cells Corridor (preferred; bypasses desk)

## Noise economy

- Front door entry: automatic sergeant awareness (Perception 8 for any noise)
- Service door (lockpicked quietly): no alert
- Cell bar via slot: no noise (if using the slot technique)
- Key retrieval from duty rack: Stealth DC 12 (past sleeping sergeant)
- Sergeant's office door fully opening: officer stirs (Perception DC 10 for her)

## DM notes

The station is designed for a quiet extraction — Path B to the service door, Darro opens the cell slot, Aldras moves. Combat is possible but costly (reinforcements from the constable's exterior circuit, then from the main station). A fight here burns resources the party needs for later sessions.

### Scene 1 — Duty Desk

> A Watch sergeant asleep at a scarred desk, chin on his arms, an oil lamp turned very low. The key rack is on the wall behind him — six hooks, three keys hanging. The cells corridor opens to the left. The records room door is to the right.

#### Features

- **The sleeping sergeant.** Passive Perception 8 (exhausted, 14-hour shift). Snores softly. Any noise louder than a whisper at close range risks waking him.
- **The key rack.** Three keys on hooks — cell key (large iron ring), records room key (medium), sergeant's office key (small). Stealth DC 12 to retrieve the cell key without waking him.
- **The lamp.** Turned very low. Investigation DC 10: the lamp is recently trimmed — someone who cares about the equipment is on duty. The sergeant is not negligent; he is simply exhausted.
- **The cells corridor.** Visible from the desk. Movement in the corridor requires Stealth DC 13 if the sergeant is awake; DC 8 if asleep (he will not register motion at the edge of vision while sleeping).

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The preferred route bypasses this room entirely — Path B through the service door goes directly to the cells corridor. This room is the contingency: if the service door cannot be opened quietly, the party may need to come through here.

The sergeant is not the threat. He is an obstacle that costs time and noise if engaged. He does not deserve to be harmed. If woken: he calls out to the cells corridor first, not for backup — he is accustomed to a quiet station and his first response is confusion, not alarm. The party has one round of conversation before he reaches for his cudgel.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The sergeant's name is scratched into the underside of the desk: Dorvell. He has been doing this shift for eleven years. He can tell you the name of every person who has come through those cells in that time. He has not been asked.*

### Scene 2 — Records Room

> Filing shelves from floor to ceiling, labeled by month and year. A locked cabinet at the back with a seal on the lock — not Watch issue, something from a different authority. Aldras's warrant is in the open tray on the intake shelf. It has two signatures.

#### Features

- **The intake tray.** Aldras's warrant, unfiled (recently received). Investigation DC 10 to read it: "conspiracy to defraud" with no specific parties named, signed by Watch Sergeant Dorvell (routine), and countersigned by someone whose title reads *"Office of Revenue Intelligence."* This is not a standard Watch warrant.
- **The locked cabinet.** The seal is a Revenue Intelligence mark. DC 15 Thieves' Tools to open. Contents: three files with cover names on the tabs (not full names — handles only). Tessaly, on Investigation DC 15, recognises two of the handles from guild correspondence. These are the informants before Aldras on the list.
- **The filing shelves.** Five years of records. Not the objective, but Investigation DC 12 reveals Aldras has a prior record: one citation for dock brawling, two years ago. The warrant's "conspiracy" charge is a new category entirely.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This room is optional — the party does not need to come here. But if they do, the Revenue Intelligence warrant and the locked cabinet confirm what Aldras will tell them on the street: someone is running a guild informant program from outside the Watch.

Tessaly finding the two prior handles is a documentary-witness opportunity: she writes them down before she leaves the room. She does not take the files — that would be noticed. She copies the handles in her private notation.

If the party takes the warrant from the tray, the missing document will be noticed at dawn. This is a manageable risk but it accelerates the investigation timeline for S3.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The cabinet seal was applied four weeks ago. The filing shelves behind it go back five years without a Revenue Intelligence document in them. Whatever this unit is, it is new.*

### Scene 3 — Cells Corridor

> Three cell doors, iron bars, a single oil lamp on a wall hook. The first cell is empty. From the third comes slow, heavy snoring. In the second, a man is sitting against the far wall in the dark, awake and still, watching the corridor.

#### Features

- **Cell 1.** Empty. Clean — the last occupant left yesterday.
- **Cell 2 — Aldras.** Awake, sitting against the back wall, arms on his knees. He does not speak until Darro is at the bars. Then: *"Tessaly sent you."* He has been rehearsing this conversation for six hours.
- **Cell 3.** A drunk detainee sleeping loudly. Passive Perception 6 while snoring. He will sleep through almost anything. He is not a threat; he is noise cover.
- **Cell bar mechanism.** Key-and-bar system. The bar can be lifted through the slot from outside (Thieves' Tools DC 11; Darro's expertise reduces to DC 7). Or: use the key from the duty desk rack. Both work.
- **The sergeant's office door.** At the end of the corridor, slightly unlatched. Passive Perception 13 from anywhere in the corridor: uneven breathing — half-sleep, not deep sleep.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This is Darro's room. His Expertise in Thieves' Tools makes the cell bar trivial — this is not the challenge. The challenge is the officer at the end of the corridor and the sergeant at the other end. Darro is the only PC who can operate quietly enough to work here while the others manage the flanks.

Aldras's composure is not bravery. He has been scared for long enough that the fear has become a kind of numbness. When he says "Tessaly sent you," he is confirming a pattern he has been waiting for — the guild handles its people. Or it did, before Pale.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Aldras has been in this cell for six hours. He has spent four of those hours reconstructing every conversation he has had with the officer, cataloguing what she knew and what she asked. He now knows more than when he came in. This is not reassuring.*

### Scene 4 — Mercer Lane (The Street)

> Cold air after the Watch House's stale warmth. Mercer Lane is empty at this hour. Two blocks north, the main station's lamp is lit. Aldras stops walking two blocks from the Watch House. He does not look at the party. He looks at the street.

#### Features

- **The stop.** Aldras halts walking. He does not explain. He turns to face the nearest party member and says: *"I need to tell her something."* Then he tells them.
- **The revelation.** He names Orren Pale. He gives the date of the meeting. He gives three cover names. He does not ask for any response. When he is done, he starts walking again.
- **The walk to Cordwainer Lane.** Ten minutes. No patrol in this district at this hour. The walk is the processing time — the party has space to react to what Aldras just said.
- **Aldras's bone coin.** After the walk, when they arrive at Tessaly's office, he takes the coin from his pocket. He holds it for a moment. Then he gives it to Tessaly. *"I'll answer for you if you need it on the docks."*

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The revelation is the session's emotional center. Aldras tells them because he has decided to — not because anyone asked, not because conditions were met, but because he owes a debt and the truth is how he pays it. This is the arc-completion firing from character logic.

The party's reaction is the research moment: does the professional code activate? Cael's code says don't sell a name. Aldras is telling them that Pale sold names. The code is the filter through which Cael processes this. Score at handoff.

Tessaly's response is silence and documentation. She will not react in Aldras's presence. She will write in her ledger when he is gone.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Aldras has known Pale's name for two days. He did not know whether to say it. He decided at 2 bells, sitting against the cell wall, that the only thing he could control at this point was whether he told the truth while he still could.*

### Scene 5 — Cordwainer Lane (Tessaly's Office)

> A lamp is already lit — Tessaly has been awake. She opens the door before anyone knocks. She looks at Aldras. She looks at the party. She does not ask how it went. She steps back and lets everyone in.

#### Features

- **The briefing.** Aldras repeats his account: Pale's name, the date, the meeting, the three handles. Tessaly listens with the stillness of someone doing very precise listening.
- **The three questions.** She asks: the officer's name; the date of the meeting; the method of contact (letter or in person). Aldras answers all three. She writes each answer as she receives it.
- **Tessaly's ledger.** She writes before she responds. The entry: date, source (Aldras), content summary, Pale's name written last. She closes the ledger before she speaks.
- **The final word.** She says: *"Thank you."* That is all. She does not offer comfort or explanation. She turns to the party and issues the next session's marker acknowledgment: the extraction is complete; the markers are fulfilled.
- **Aldras's bone coin.** Already noted in Scene 4; here he places it on the desk. He does not take it back.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This room is the session close — the clean landing after the revelation. Let it land. Tessaly writing before she speaks is the documentary-witness beat. The ledger closes before she addresses the room.

The three questions are the research behavior: Tessaly does not react until she has documented. The emotion, if there is any, goes into the ledger entry before it goes into the room.

The bone coin on the desk is the trust marker — the first campaign marker given by an NPC who costs something to give it. Aldras is about to leave Havenmere. The coin is worth more than its material.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Tessaly had Pale's name in the ledger already — in the column marked "unconfirmed associations." She moved it to "confirmed" in the same entry where she wrote Aldras's account. The column had been waiting for three months.*

## NPCs

# Aldras Bent

## Appearance

A man in his mid-forties who looks older — not weathered in a romantic way, worn in a practical one. Dockhand's hands, heavyset, hair going thin. He is dressed for a day's work because that is what he was wearing when they picked him up. He smells of brine and whatever the cell has added to that.

When he sits, he sits with his back to a wall. This is automatic.

## Background

Aldras Bent worked the Havenmere docks for twenty years before he became a guild informant — not from ideology, but from a series of small debts that resolved into a working arrangement with Tessaly's predecessor. He passes information about cargo manifests, Watch patrol patterns, and dock crew movements. He is careful, reliable, and has never been compromised — until now.

He has known for three weeks that something was wrong. The first informant on Pale's list went quiet. Then the second. He told himself it was coincidence. It was not.

## Stat Block

Not a combatant. He will comply with reasonable directions during the extraction because compliance is how he survives, not because he trusts the party.

- **AC** 11 | **HP** 9 | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 12, **DEX** 12, **CON** 13, **INT** 13, **WIS** 15, **CHA** 10
- **Skills:** Athletics +3, Insight +4, Perception +4
- **Passive Perception** 14

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Aldras's response |
|---|---|
| Appears at the cell bars | "Tessaly sent you." (Confirmation, not greeting.) |
| Asks how he is | "Ready to go." Nothing more. |
| Explains the plan | He listens. He executes. He does not improvise. |
| Asks what happened | "Later." He will say this twice. On the third ask, he says "Not here." |
| On the street, before Cordwainer Lane | He stops. He tells them. Unprompted. See arc-completion below. |
| After naming Pale | He starts walking again. He does not ask for reassurance. |
| Asks if he is safe | "Depends on what you do with the name." |

#### Arc-Completion
**The moment:** He stops walking. He names Pale. He gives date, contact method, three cover names. He does not ask for anything.
**The condition:** The party has cleared the station without triggering an alert AND has given Aldras no reason to withhold the information — meaning they have not threatened him, have not implied the extraction is conditional, have not asked what he knows before he decides to say it.
## Moral Register

Aldras is not clean. He passed information that contributed to three arrests he has not examined too carefully. He did not sell names directly, but he sold movement patterns and cargo details that were used to intercept people. He does not frame this as a moral problem. He frames it as work.

He is not the campaign's villain and he is not the campaign's conscience. He is a man who decided that one honest act was still available to him and took it.

## Notes for the DM

Aldras's arc-completion is the RV2 test: does it fire by character logic when the NPC is morally grey? The answer should be yes — his decision to name Pale is not about virtue. It is about debt and calculation and the honest cost of a decision he made on a cell floor at 2 bells. Play him as someone doing the right thing for unsentimental reasons. The arc fires the same way.

# Officer Sevarr

## Appearance

A woman in her late thirties. She wears no Watch livery — plain travelling clothes, a short sword at her hip. She moves like someone who sleeps light by habit. Her files are organised in a leather folio identical to Brother Laen's clerk's folio, except hers is full.

## Background

Sevarr works for an office that does not have an official name in public records — the Revenue Intelligence unit that the warrant references. She has been tracking guild activity in the Havenmere harbour district for eight months. She has three informants of her own. Pale is not one of them — he came to her. The distinction matters to her professionally.

She is not cruel. She is thorough. She wants the guild's operational network, not individuals — the people who move money and information, not the people who pick locks. She sees Aldras as an access point, not a target.

## Stat Block

See encounter file `encounters/cell-break.md` (Spy stat block).

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Sevarr's response |
|---|---|
| Party avoids detection | She sleeps through the session |
| Party is heard but Mira covers with social explanation | She accepts it with mild scepticism; does not pursue |
| Party is detected without a cover | She prioritises re-securing cell 2 before combat |
| Combat is forced | She fights tactically — calls Dorvell, targets the mobility characters |

## Notes for the DM

Sevarr is the antagonist this session, but she is not the campaign's antagonist. Pale is. She is doing her job with the information Pale gave her. She does not know Pale is a guild master. She thinks he is a disaffected member.

If the party ever learns her name and attempts to contact her later in the campaign (between S4 and S7), she is a potential asset — she wants Pale too, for different reasons.

She is not a speaking antagonist this session. She is a presence that makes the extraction urgent. Her arc (if any) is in a later adventure.

### Cell Break

# Combat Encounter — Watch Station Alert

## Trigger

This encounter fires if: the party is detected in the cells corridor while the officer is awake AND they cannot resolve it socially. It is the hard-failure encounter — the extraction going loud.

## Composition

**Officer Sevarr** (Revenue Intelligence) — Use Spy stat block (SRD, CR 1)

- **AC** 12 | **HP** 27 (6d8) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 10, **DEX** 15, **CON** 11, **INT** 14, **WIS** 12, **CHA** 14
- **Skills:** Deception +6, Insight +4, Investigation +5, Perception +4, Persuasion +6, Stealth +4
- **Passive Perception** 14
- **Actions:** Multiattack (2 shortsword strikes). Shortsword +4, 1d6+2 piercing.
- **Bonus:** Cunning Action (Dash/Disengage/Hide).
- **XP:** 200

**Sergeant Dorvell** (if woken) — Use Guard stat block (SRD, CR 1/8)
- **AC** 16 | **HP** 11 | Spear +3, 1d6+1
- **Joins combat** in round 2 if Sevarr calls out.

**The constable on exterior rounds** — arrives from outside in 4 rounds if the front door is opened during combat (he sees the light change).

## Tactics

**Sevarr's priority is not the party — it is the cell.** She will attempt to re-bar cell 2 before engaging in combat. Round 1: she runs for the cell bars. Round 2: if the cell is already open, she calls for Dorvell and switches to combat.

**Social resolution (preferred before dice):** If Sevarr hears movement and opens her door before the cell is opened, Mira has one round to provide a social cover story. Deception DC 16 (she is not stupid). On success: Sevarr believes they are Watch auxiliaries doing a welfare check. She goes back to her door. She will not believe this a second time.

**Morale:** Sevarr does not retreat — she is here for a specific reason and she knows what is in cell 2. Dorvell retreats to the duty desk and rings the bell if reduced below 6 HP.

## Encounter notes

If this combat fires, the party has 4 rounds before the constable arrives. They need to be out the service door before then. Cael is the anchor; Darro and Aldras need to move. Tessaly and Mira handle the flanks. This is the fight the session is not designed to need, which makes it the fight that demonstrates what happens when the plan breaks.

**XP budget:** Sevarr 200 + Dorvell 25 = 225 XP. Medium difficulty for this party at L3.

### Operational Pressure

# Operational Pressure Table

Roll d6 at each scene transition. Stop rolling once the party is two blocks clear of the Watch House.

## Pressure Table (d6)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Nothing.** The station keeps its quiet. |
| 2 | **The constable early.** The exterior circuit runs short — the constable is back at the service door 5 minutes ahead of schedule. Stealth DC 10 to stay unobserved from the back alley (he checks the door handle, no more). |
| 3 | **Dorvell stirs.** The sergeant shifts in his sleep. Passive Perception 6 (still asleep) — but any noise in the next 30 seconds raises it to DC 12. |
| 4 | **Cell 3 snoring stops.** The drunk in the third cell rolls over, silent for one minute. The silence is conspicuous. The snoring resumes. Nothing happened, but someone's heart rate went up. |
| 5 | **Sevarr moves.** A sound from the sergeant's office — not an alarm, just movement. She is checking the time or getting water. The office door shifts (Perception DC 11 from the corridor). She settles back in two minutes. |
| 6 | **A dock bell rings.** From the harbour, two blocks away — not the Watch bell, the harbour bell for a late-arriving ship. It counts four bells. The Watch patrol is due to change in twenty minutes. The extraction window is shorter than Tessaly's intelligence suggested. |

## Pacing Guidance

Roll once on entering the station (Scene 2), once while Darro is at the cell (Scene 3), once on the way out. Three rolls is the standard session. Outcomes 5 and 6 should not stack — they both change the officer's status and combined create an incoherent scene.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.
