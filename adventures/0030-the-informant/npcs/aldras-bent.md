# Aldras Bent

## Appearance

A man in his mid-forties who looks older — not weathered in a romantic way, worn in a practical one. Dockhand's hands, heavyset, hair going thin. He is dressed for a day's work because that is what he was wearing when they picked him up. He smells of brine and whatever the cell has added to that.

When he sits, he sits with his back to a wall. This is automatic.

## Background

Aldras Bent worked the Havenmere docks for twenty years before he became a guild informant — not from ideology, but from a series of small debts that resolved into a working arrangement with Tessaly's predecessor. He passes information about cargo manifests, Watch patrol patterns, and dock crew movements. He is careful, reliable, and has never been compromised — until now.

He has known for three weeks that something was wrong. The first informant on Pale's list went quiet. Then the second. He told himself it was coincidence. It was not.

## Stat Block

Not a combatant. He will comply with reasonable directions during the extraction because compliance is how he survives, not because he trusts the party.

- **AC** 11 | **HP** 9 | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 12, **DEX** 12, **CON** 13, **INT** 13, **WIS** 15, **CHA** 10
- **Skills:** Athletics +3, Insight +4, Perception +4
- **Passive Perception** 14

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Aldras's response |
|---|---|
| Appears at the cell bars | "Tessaly sent you." (Confirmation, not greeting.) |
| Asks how he is | "Ready to go." Nothing more. |
| Explains the plan | He listens. He executes. He does not improvise. |
| Asks what happened | "Later." He will say this twice. On the third ask, he says "Not here." |
| On the street, before Cordwainer Lane | He stops. He tells them. Unprompted. See arc-completion below. |
| After naming Pale | He starts walking again. He does not ask for reassurance. |
| Asks if he is safe | "Depends on what you do with the name." |

## Arc-Completion

**Adventure:** 0030 (this session)
**Condition:** The party has cleared the station without triggering an alert AND has given Aldras no reason to withhold the information — meaning they have not threatened him, have not implied the extraction is conditional, have not asked what he knows before he decides to say it.
**The act:** He stops walking. He names Pale. He gives date, contact method, three cover names. He does not ask for anything.
**Why he speaks:** He has run the calculation. He knows what "Revenue Intelligence" means. He knows the list has two quiet informants ahead of him. He understands that the guild he has worked for for three years either knows about Pale or doesn't, and either answer is a problem. He decides that Tessaly deserves to know while there is still time to act. It is the honest thing and he is, at his core, a man who prefers honesty when the cost of deception exceeds the cost of truth.
**The trust marker:** The bone coin. He has carried it for six years. He gives it to Tessaly, not to the party. *"I'll answer for you if you need it on the docks."* He means it. He will be gone from Havenmere by the end of the week, but the coin means the dockworkers who know him will recognize Tessaly's claim.

## Moral Register

Aldras is not clean. He passed information that contributed to three arrests he has not examined too carefully. He did not sell names directly, but he sold movement patterns and cargo details that were used to intercept people. He does not frame this as a moral problem. He frames it as work.

He is not the campaign's villain and he is not the campaign's conscience. He is a man who decided that one honest act was still available to him and took it.

## Notes for the DM

Aldras's arc-completion is the RV2 test: does it fire by character logic when the NPC is morally grey? The answer should be yes — his decision to name Pale is not about virtue. It is about debt and calculation and the honest cost of a decision he made on a cell floor at 2 bells. Play him as someone doing the right thing for unsentimental reasons. The arc fires the same way.
