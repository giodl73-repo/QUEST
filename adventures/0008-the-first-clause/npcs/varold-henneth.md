# Varold Henneth

*Merchant, 58. Korrath harbor-district. Collector of pre-Cataclysm objects. Good man.*

## Appearance

Varold is prosperous and comfortable — the kind of man who has done well enough to surround himself with beautiful things and has not yet had a reason to question whether he deserves them. He is heavyset, well-dressed without being flashy, with the careful manners of someone who learned etiquette late in life and applied it conscientiously ever since. His study is his pride: six shelves of pre-Cataclysm fragments and decorative objects, each with a small handwritten card describing provenance and purchase price.

## Stat block

**Non-combatant.** Will call for staff (2 household guards, use Bandit stat blocks) if threatened.

## What he knows (public)

- He has a display collection. He will show it if asked nicely.
- He purchased the Clause fragment at a Palanthas estate auction three years ago.
- He cannot read High Istaran.
- He paid 15 gold for it as part of a lot.

## What he knows (private)

- His business partner Sevel has been skimming from their joint shipping contracts for eight months. Varold knows and has not yet decided what to do about it.
- He is more sentimental about his collection than he admits. He will not sell. He might donate.

## Emotional position

**What he wants:** For his collection to be appreciated by people who understand what they're looking at.

**What he can't say:** He is frightened of Sevel and does not know what to do about the skimming. He needs someone to tell him that the right thing to do is also the possible thing to do.

**The condition for the shard:** If the party explains what the shard is — genuinely explains it, not as a con — and treats him as someone capable of making a good decision, he gives it to them. He does not need payment. He needs to feel that handing it over is the right thing to do, not that he has been tricked.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Henneth's response |
|---|---|
| Deception attempt to buy/steal the shard | Becomes suspicious; calls for staff if they persist. The shard dims when the lie lands; he doesn't notice, but the party can. |
| Explains what the shard is (Conclave Compact fragment, magical) | Listens carefully. Does not immediately believe, but doesn't dismiss. "It's valuable then?" — he's thinking about his collection's reputation, not the money. |
| Explains the compact's larger context | Is genuinely interested. He is an educated man. He reads. He knows about the Cataclysm. |
| Asks if he would donate it to a Conclave recovery effort | Considers seriously. "How do I know this 'Conclave remnant' is legitimate?" Fair question. Tessamine's letter/seal answers it. |
| Tessamine's letter presented | Gives the shard. He knows Tessamine's name; she is known in scholarly circles. "Take good care of it. It's the only thing in my collection that's never collected dust." |

## Arc-Completion

Henneth does not have a deep arc-completion. His arc is small and practical: he gives the shard to people he trusts, because trusting people is what he does when the choice is clear. His arc-completion is simply: when presented with Tessamine's letter and a genuine explanation, he opens the case, lifts the shard, and holds it out. He says: *"I always thought it was warmer than it should be."* Then he gives it over.

**What it produces:** The shard is recovered cleanly. No conflict. The campaign proceeds.

**If the condition is never met (party deceives him):** He gives a copy of the shard — a decorated obsidian fragment from another part of the collection — while the real one is hidden in his safe. The party discovers this when they bring it to Tessamine. Recovery: return to Henneth and apologize.

## GM Notes

- Henneth is not an obstacle. He is a decent person who has something the party needs. Treat him accordingly.
- His business partner Sevel will visit during the party's time in the study (see Room 03 GM Notes). This is when the shard dims visibly for the first time.
- The shard dimming during Sevel's lie is the adventure's key atmospheric seed. The party notices before they know what it means.
