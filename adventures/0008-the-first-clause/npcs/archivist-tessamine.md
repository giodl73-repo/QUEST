# Archivist Tessamine

*White Robe wizard, 74. Dying. She has six weeks left and she knows it. She has been arranging things.*

## Appearance

Tessamine is small and diminished — the illness has taken weight and color from her, and she looks like a letter that has been folded too many times. She wears White Robe robes too large for her current frame, belted close. Her hands still move with precision when she gestures; her eyes are sharp and unhurried. She smells of paper and cedar, which is the smell of her archive.

## Stat block

**Non-combatant.** She has not cast a combat spell in forty years and does not intend to start. AC 10, HP 8. She would be killed in one hit by anyone who tried.

## What she knows (public)

- The location of the Clause of Witness (Henneth's townhouse, harbor district).
- The Conclave Compact existed and the shards are worth recovering.
- The renegade faction exists and has begun moving.
- She taught Thessaly for six years before the Test.

## What she knows (private)

- What the Conclave Compact actually said. She has reconstructed it from fragments and secondary sources over the past decade. She cannot speak this yet — she needs to know the party is ready to receive it, and that requires them to have demonstrated they understand what the shards are for, not just what they're worth.
- What happened to Mira (Thessaly's sister). She knows Thessaly's Test; she knows the spell; she has never told Thessaly she knows. She will not tell her unless Thessaly names it herself first.
- The triple-moon convergence timing. She has an astronomer's chart in her archive that shows the date (Hint 2 vehicle — but she will only share it if asked, and only if the party is committed to the D-route outcome).

## Emotional position

**What she wants:** For the compact to be properly understood before it is reassembled. Not just recovered — *understood.* She has spent ten years reconstructing what it said because she believes the renegade faction will use the compact the wrong way even if they have the right shards. She wants the party to understand what they're carrying.

**What she can't say:** She cannot speak the compact's purpose until she knows the party is ready. This is not caginess — it is a professional judgment about how much weight a person can receive at once. She will not unload everything in the first meeting.

**The condition:** She speaks the compact's purpose when a PC demonstrates genuine understanding of what the shards are for — not the mechanics, the purpose. Thessaly naming what the inscription means to her (if Hint 1 delivers) is the condition. Or any PC asking "what was the compact actually for?" and waiting for the real answer.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Tessamine's response |
|---|---|
| Asks what the shard is | Gives the technical answer: "A fragment of the Conclave Compact, pre-Cataclysm, seven clauses, seven wizards' oaths." Does not yet give the purpose. |
| Asks why the compact matters | Gives the political answer: "Because the renegades will use it to legitimize reconstituting the Conclave under their own authority." Still not the purpose. |
| Thessaly names what the inscription means to her | Tessamine goes quiet for a moment. Then: "Yes. That is exactly what it is for." Then she speaks the compact's purpose. Arc-completion. |
| Any PC asks "what was the compact *actually* for?" and waits | If they wait — if they do not fill the silence — Tessamine looks at them carefully and then speaks. The waiting is the condition. |
| Party asks about Thessaly's sister | "I taught Thessaly for six years. I know what she carries. That is not mine to say." She will not elaborate. |
| Party presses about the renegades | Gives what she knows: names, approximate locations, known resources. She is not protective of operational information — she is dying and information hoarded dies with her. |
| Party seems to be treating the compact as purely political | She corrects them, gently. "The compact was not about the Orders. It was about protecting mortals from the Orders' wars." She says this only once. |

## Arc-Completion

**The moment:** When Thessaly (or any PC who has heard Hint 1's delivery) names what the Clause of Witness is for — not the political reason, the human reason — Tessamine sets down whatever she is holding and says: *"The compact was not written to bind the Orders. It was written to bind the Orders to the people who live under the sky they argue about. Every clause is a reckoning the Orders agreed to face. What you're carrying is an agreement to be responsible. That is what the renegades want to control."*

She then goes to her archive shelf, takes out a folded piece of paper, and gives it to whoever she trusts most in the room. The paper has her reconstruction of all seven clauses — incomplete, but close. It is the closest thing to a map of what the compact said.

**What it produces:** The party now has Tessamine's reconstruction. They know what they're looking for. The Hint 1 delivery is complete. This is Route D prerequisite number one.

**What happens if the condition is never met:** Tessamine gives the party the shard delivery note (confirming receipt), the operational information about the renegades, and a brief summary of the compact's political importance. The paper stays in her archive. She dies without passing it on. The party can recover the information in a later adventure (from the shard inscriptions themselves, as they accumulate), but they start the campaign without the map.

## GM Notes

- Do not rush to Tessamine's arc-completion. Let the party ask questions. Let Thessaly read the shard first if at all possible. The arc-completion lands harder after the party has encountered the shard.
- Her archive has the moon-calendar (Hint 2 vehicle) if the party examines it. She will not mention it; she will not hide it.
- DM-letter: this is a scene that requires witnessing, not directing. If Thessaly is about to name the inscription's personal meaning, the DM should stop narrating and wait. See `dm-letter-scene-01.md`.
