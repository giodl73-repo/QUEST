# S01 LOG — The First Clause

## Session Summary

Archivist Tessamine's letter brought the compact-wardens to Korrath-by-the-Sea. The Clause of Witness was in Merchant Varold Henneth's display case — three years in a display case, with a handwritten card that said "High Istaran script (unread)" — and it had been waiting.

Thessaly read it through the glass before opening the case, lips moving slightly, and the inscription brightened. She said, quietly, to no one in particular: *"This is the first clause."* Hint 1 delivered in the first five minutes of the study scene, unprompted. Henneth's business partner Sevel arrived and lied three times about cargo manifest numbers; the shard dimmed three times. Henneth, who had been noticing the dimming for three years without understanding it, asked when they finally explained the shard: *"Is that why it dims when Sevel lies?"* He gave the shard over cleanly. *"Take good care of it. It's the only piece in my collection that I never had to dust."*

At Tessamine's archive on the return, Tessamine asked Thessaly what the inscription meant — to her, not politically. Thessaly named it: a spell at her Test, cast without being seen, that cost someone something she cannot give back. The shard glowed because it knew. Tessamine received this, set down her pen, and spoke the compact's purpose for the first time to someone who was ready to hear it. The reconstruction document was given. Route D prerequisite 1 of 4 confirmed.

The session used 0 dice. The clock ran 26 minutes of the 90-minute townhouse timer. No combat. No deception. The party walked in with credentials, told the truth, and the adventure opened accordingly.

Thessaly went to the display case the moment she entered. She read the inscription through the glass, lips moving slightly (*"Let no mortal act without witness"*), and the inscription brightened.

She said, quietly: *"This is the first clause."*

Orik saw the glow from the window. Calder watched Thessaly. Neither said anything yet.

### Scene 06 — Tessamine's Archive (Return)

Tessamine looked at their faces before she looked at the shard. She held it, wrote the receipt, filed it (40 seconds; no rushing). Then: *"Did you read it?"*

Thessaly said the clause.

Tessamine asked what it meant — to her. Not politically. Actually.

The room was quiet. Sera looked at the harbor. Orik had his back to the wall. Calder did not fill the silence.

**Thessaly named her wound:**
*"I cast a spell at my Test that I was not supposed to cast. It cost someone something I can't give back. No one saw me do it. I thought that meant it was contained."* Pause. *"The shard glowed when I read it. I think it knew."*

**Tessamine's arc-completion:**
She set down her pen. She said: *"The compact was not written to bind the Orders. It was written to bind the Orders to the people who live under the sky they argue about. Every clause is a reckoning the Orders agreed to face. What you're carrying is an agreement to be responsible. That is what the renegades want to control."*

She gave Thessaly the reconstruction document (7 clauses, some incomplete). She has been holding it for ten years. She has found someone who can carry it.

Calder asked her timeline a second time. She deflected again. He did not push.

**Route D prerequisite 1 confirmed:** Hint 1 delivered + Thessaly named her wound + Tessamine spoke the compact's purpose + reconstruction document given.
