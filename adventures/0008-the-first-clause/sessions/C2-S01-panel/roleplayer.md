## Critique

### Overall assessment

This is a near-perfect opening session for character work. All four PCs had at least one scene-defining moment that could only have come from their specific sheets, and Thessaly's wound-naming in Scene 06 is one of the cleanest first-session grief-paragraph activations in the workshop's history. The Roleplayer's one reservation is structural: with four PCs at a table, the session's spotlight distribution is slightly uneven — Thessaly carries Scenes 01, 03, and 06; Orik and Sera and Calder each get one strong moment, but the session's emotional weight lives with Thessaly. This is acceptable for a session that explicitly sets up the campaign's central question. It should not persist into S02.

### What worked

- **Scene 06 — Thessaly names her wound:** *"I cast a spell at my Test that I was not supposed to cast. It cost someone something I can't give back. No one saw me do it. I thought that meant it was contained."* This is the grief-paragraph spoken verbatim, in voice, to the exact NPC whose arc-completion was waiting for it. The line is specific — it names a Test, a cost, a witness gap — and each word is doing work. No other PC at this table could have said this. This is sheet-deep-reader at its highest.

- **Sera's harbor-look (Scene 06):** She gave Thessaly space by looking at the harbor window. She did not explain this. She did not announce it after. The Roleplayer wants to quote this action to every DM who ever asked "how do I show a character's care without having them say 'I care'?" This is the answer: act-without-announcement. Sera's sheet says *"shows care through attention, not words"* and this is exactly that.

### What needs work

- **Calder's voice is thin this session.** He asked Tessamine's timeline twice and didn't push the second time — good decision-order visible there. But the *texture* of Calder's specific voice (Life cleric who administered the wrong aid and lived with the accounting) does not emerge. His grief paragraph involves the forty-one people. That grief is never pulled on in Session 1. The module's social-first approach doesn't create a moment that asks Calder something only he can answer.

- **Orik's exit-counting is signature behavior but not yet character work.** He counted exits in three rooms. The Tactician loves this; the Roleplayer wants to see it connected to his grief. The hall — the oil — the fire — the seven apprentices. The counting is a compulsion, not a choice. That distinction should be visible by S02.

### The question I'd push on

Tessamine's arc-completion fires when Thessaly names her wound. The arc is Thessaly → Tessamine. But Calder *watched Thessaly* in Scene 06 and "knows that look." What does Calder know? The session acknowledges his observation but doesn't use it. The Roleplayer wants S02 to give Calder something that requires him to speak from that knowing.
