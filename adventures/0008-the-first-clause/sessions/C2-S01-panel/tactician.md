## Critique

### Overall assessment

Zero dice in a session opener is a red flag even when the session lands artistically. The absence of any mechanical resolution means the Tactician cannot audit fairness, dice discipline, or action economy — the three pillars this lens cares about most. The shard's passive properties (dimming, warmth, glow) fired correctly and produced genuine narrative drama, but "narrative drama" is not the same as "mechanics that told the party something they didn't want to hear."

The Table Readiness score of 5 is the Tactician's honest assessment: if a new DM runs this at a real table and the players decide to pickpocket the shard instead of negotiating, what happens? The module's social-first routing is correct for this party, but the zero-dice precedent sets a troubling campaign baseline.

### What worked

- **Scene 03 — Sevel's Lies:** The shard dimming three times on three lies is a mechanic even if no dice were rolled. It gave the party observable, repeatable, falsifiable information (the shard is a lie detector). Orik and Calder both noted it. This is good systematic observation under Tactician criteria — the party gathered intelligence by watching a passive artifact property. The module's design is sound here.

- **Orik's exit-counting (Scenes 01, 02, 03):** This is tactical behavior — automatic threat assessment in every room, every time. The hall-guard discipline is exactly what the Tactician wants to see. It didn't produce dice, but it produced the right mindset. The module rewarded it with Orik calling the move at 26 minutes, which is a genuine decision under timing pressure.

### What needs work

- **Zero dice_log.jsonl:** This is a structural audit failure. The Tactician cannot verify a session where there are no dice entries. The narrative says "no roll required" because Thessaly's passive Arcana = 18. Fine. But the session's only mechanical events — the shard's passive firings — are not logged in a format that can be reviewed or replicated. If a future DM runs this session cold, they have no mechanical scaffolding.

- **Sevel's deception: no counter-roll offered.** Sevel lied three times. The shard caught it via passive observation. But the party never had to make a Deception vs. Insight contest to catch the lie themselves — the artifact did the work entirely. This is a missed opportunity: a DC 12 Insight to notice the shard's reaction before Sevel leaves would have given a player a meaningful roll. Instead the module delivers the information automatically. Automatic delivery is the enemy of tension.

### The question I'd push on

If the next session also uses zero dice, is this a campaign structural choice or a design accident? The Tactician is willing to grant one zero-dice session. Two in a row becomes a pattern that disadvantages every player who came to the table to see their character sheet's combat stats matter.
