## Critique

### Overall assessment

The setting is legible and internally consistent. Korrath-by-the-Sea feels like a port city in the Age of Might — the harbor guard who waves Tessamine's people through without explanation implies layers of prior institutional relationship; the archive's organization (proper receipt, 40-second filing) implies a functioning scholarly culture. The Lorekeeper's concern is that the Conclave Compact itself is introduced primarily through Tessamine's explanation rather than through visible in-world evidence. We are told about the compact; we see only one clause. The session establishes the premise strongly but relies heavily on one NPC's exposition to carry the lore load.

### What worked

- **Henneth's display case:** *"High Istaran script (unread)"* on the handwritten card is an excellent piece of in-world archaeology. A merchant had this artifact for three years and couldn't read it — because High Istaran is a dead scholarly language, because he's a merchant not an archivist, because the cultural knowledge to decode it has been lost. All of this is embedded in that one card. The session doesn't call attention to it; it's just correct. The Lorekeeper values this density.

- **Tessamine's ten-year stewardship of the reconstruction document:** She has been holding it for ten years. She found someone who can carry it. This is exactly the right scale for a pre-Cataclysm compact whose recovery has taken generations — not a dramatic rediscovery, but a careful handing-off by someone who recognized the right hands when she finally saw them. The setting's sense of time is present.

### What needs work

- **The compact's purpose arrives through one NPC's speech, not through the world.** Tessamine delivers the compact's purpose in a single speech: *"not written to bind the Orders to each other but to bind each Order to the people who live under the sky they argue about."* This is a good speech. But the Lorekeeper wants to see this reflected in the shard itself — in the inscription, in the reaction moment — not just in an archivist's explanation. The inscription is glossed (*"Let no mortal act without witness"*) but its political significance relies entirely on Tessamine's frame. If she were absent, a party arriving cold would have a shard with a moral principle and no context.

- **The Unbound Conclave is named but not shown.** Sera spots a courier. The log notes agents will appear in S02. But the party has no worldly sense of what the Conclave *is* yet — why it opposes the compact, what its grievance looks like, how it operates. This is an appropriate mystery for S01, but the Lorekeeper wants S02 to give it texture from the world, not just from combat.

### The question I'd push on

The compact was written pre-Cataclysm. The Cataclysm destroyed the institutional order it was meant to govern. Why was the compact not destroyed with everything else? Where has it been for three hundred years? Tessamine's reconstruction document implies someone preserved it deliberately. The Lorekeeper wants to know who — and whether the world shows evidence of that preservation effort anywhere outside the archive.
