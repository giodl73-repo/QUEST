# Premise — The First Clause

## Hook

Archivist Tessamine's letter reaches Thessaly through a Red Robe courier in Korrath-by-the-Sea: *"The Clause of Witness is in Merchant Varold Henneth's private collection. He does not know what he has. He purchased it at a Palanthas estate sale three years ago, catalogued as 'decorated obsidian fragment, pre-Cataclysm.' He lives at the Henneth townhouse on the Korrath harbor front. I am dying. I cannot go. You can. — T."*

The compact-wardens arrive in Korrath with three days before Tessamine's health fails enough that she will need them back at her archive.

## Setting

Korrath-by-the-Sea. A mid-sized port city on Ansalon's eastern coast; pre-Cataclysm wealth, post-Cataclysm maintenance debt. The harbor district holds the Henneth townhouse (4 floors, private dock, a small staff of servants). The Red Robe chapter-house is three streets inland — small, functional, Tessamine's operational base.

## Artifact

**The Clause of Witness** — a shard of the Conclave Compact, obsidian, palm-sized, warm to the touch. Inscribed: *"Let no mortal act without witness."* Dims slightly when a lie is spoken within 20 feet (the shard knows). Currently mounted in a display case in Henneth's private study alongside other "decorative fragments."

See `treasures/clause-of-witness.md` for full artifact details.

## Themes

**Suspicion** — the party is doing a job. The job's larger implications haven't landed. They will start to land in this adventure, specifically when Thessaly reads the inscription.

**Witness** — the Clause of Witness is Thessaly's grief in compact form. She has acted without witness. She has acted knowing the cost. The shard glowing when she reads it (Hint 1 delivery) is the moment the campaign puts a mirror in front of her.

## Three-route outcomes (A/C/D)

**Route A (Charter/Fallback):** The party negotiates with or deceives Henneth to recover the shard. No violence; the shard is retrieved; no deeper complications. Tessamine gets the shard; the campaign advances. Thessaly does not yet understand the Hint 1 implication.

**Route C (Destruction/Default):** The renegade faction's courier (present in Korrath, not yet identified as hostile) gets wind of the party's mission and creates a complication — the party must recover the shard quickly and under pressure, without time for the quiet reading that delivers Hint 1. The shard is recovered but Hint 1 is not delivered this session; recovery path: Tessamine explains it later.

**Route D (Inversion/Optimal):** Thessaly reads the Clause inscription with genuine attention (passive Arcana 12 or voluntary investigation). The shard glows. She understands "Let no mortal act without witness" as a wound she has been carrying. She tells the party what the shard means — not the compact's meaning, but her meaning. This is the first time in the campaign a PC names something. Hint 1 delivered. Full inversion requires this.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5 party. One combat encounter expected (renegade-faction ambush, optional — see encounters/). Social encounters: Tessamine (NPC arc-completion), Henneth (negotiation/deception), courier (recognition/confrontation if observed).

## Constraints

- Room count: 5-6 keyed locations (Tessamine's archive, Henneth's townhouse entry, study, servant quarters, private dock; optional: the harbor market where the courier is seen)
- This is a social-first adventure. The dungeon is a house. The topology is rooms in a building, not a classic dungeon.
- Jaquays gate: non-linearity in a house is about *options* (front door / servant entrance / dock access) not about loops. Apply the spirit of the gate, not the letter.
- One cursed-artifact manifest symptom must fire in the townhouse: the shard dims when Henneth's business partner lies to him in the study, before the party knows the shard is cursed.
