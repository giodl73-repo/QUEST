# Scene 02 — Henneth Townhouse, Entry Hall

**Location:** Henneth's townhouse, harbor district. Ground floor, front entry. **Time:** Day 1, late morning.

**Read-aloud:**

> The Henneth townhouse: four floors of pre-Cataclysm stone, well-kept. A housekeeper takes your letter and asks you to wait. Two chairs, dried harbor-flowers, a portrait above the fireplace — Henneth's wife, from the bone structure. The room smells of salt and beeswax.

**Features:**

- **The housekeeper (Pella, 45; non-combatant).** Pella takes the introduction letter and returns with Henneth in three minutes. She is watchful without being hostile. If the party examines anything while waiting, she notices (Insight DC 10 reveals she is noting what they look at).
- **The portrait.** Henneth's wife, Dana Henneth, died eight years ago. He has never remarried. The portrait is the only personal item in an otherwise professional-looking entry. Investigation DC 12 or Perception DC 10: the portrait has a small Conclave-order mark in the lower corner — a decorative flourish that might be a White Robe sigil, or might just be the painter's style. (It is the painter's style. It is not significant. But it will make Thessaly look twice.)
- **Henneth arrives.** He enters from the Study stairs, already curious — Tessamine's name on the introduction letter has preceded the party's appearance. He greets them warmly; he has been hoping someone from the scholarly community would visit his collection.

**Encounter:** None. This is the social approach.

**GM Notes:**

- **The routes to the study from here.** Henneth invites the party to his study (if the letter of introduction is presented = shortcut to Scene 03 without going through Scene 04). If the party does not present the letter, Pella leads them to wait in the entry and Henneth must be sought out (Scene 04 → 03 path).
- **The portrait misread.** Thessaly will look at the sigil mark. Let her. She does not find what she's looking for; she finds something that makes her think. This is not significant mechanically — it is sheet-deep-reader bait. The White Robe sigil mark on the Clause of Witness (Selindra's personal mark) is similar but not identical to this painter's flourish.
- **Orik and the room.** Orik will check the exits before he sits (signature move). There are two: the front door (entry); the door to the stairs (Study/upper floors). He will note that the servant entrance is accessible from the side alley.

**Connections:** Scene 03 (Study, invited; direct path with letter), Scene 04 (Servant Quarters, unescorted; if letter not presented or party explores independently).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Pella has worked for Henneth for twelve years. She watched him buy the obsidian fragment at the Palanthas auction — she thought it was ugly. She has watched it sit in the case for three years and never collect dust. She has not mentioned this to Henneth. She does not know why she hasn't. It seems like the kind of thing she would have already mentioned if she were going to.*
