# DM Letter — Scene 06 (Tessamine's Archive: The Return)

*This letter is for you, the DM. Read it before the session.*

---

## What this scene is

The party has returned with the shard. Tessamine is in the same chair. She has been waiting.

She has spent ten years reconstructing what the compact said. She has been waiting for someone who was ready to hear why it matters — not politically, but actually. If the party has demonstrated that readiness (Thessaly named the inscription's meaning, or a PC asked the right question and waited), Tessamine will speak the compact's purpose. This is not a speech. It is a sentence or two from a dying woman who has finally found someone to receive what she has been holding.

Your job: receive it with her. Witness the giving.

---

## What to do before it starts

- Check: did Thessaly read the inscription in Scene 03? Did any PC name what the Clause means, or ask what the compact was for and then wait?
- If yes: the arc-completion is available. Tessamine will attempt it.
- If no: she gives the operational brief (renegade movements, shard locations) and asks the party to return in the morning. The arc-completion is available in the morning meeting. She has not given up.

---

## What to do during

**When the party enters:** Tessamine looks at their faces before she looks at the shard. Read-aloud has this. Let it breathe for two seconds of silence before you continue.

**The delivery of the shard:** She holds it briefly. She writes the receipt note. She files it. She has been doing archival work for fifty years; this is reflex. The reflex is deliberate — it grounds her before the more important thing.

**The arc-completion condition:** The condition is that Thessaly (or another PC who has heard Hint 1) speaks first about what the inscription means — even briefly, even quietly. If that hasn't happened yet, Tessamine will invite it: she will look at Thessaly and say: *"Did you read it?"* Then she waits. That is the condition. She is asking Thessaly to name it, not asking for a mechanical answer.

**If Thessaly (or any PC) names what the inscription means:** Let them speak. Do not fill the silence between their words and Tessamine's response. When Tessamine responds, she will set down whatever she is holding. Her arc-completion text is in `npcs/archivist-tessamine.md` — the exact words to use. After she speaks, she goes to her archive and returns with the folded paper (her reconstruction of the compact's clauses). She gives it to whoever she trusts most in the room. She does not rush.

**If no PC speaks:** Tessamine tries once more: *"What was written on it?"* If still nothing: she gives the operational brief. She does not press further. Tomorrow.

**The paper:** A folded document, handwritten, in Tessamine's precise script. Incomplete; some clauses are labeled [?]. Seven clauses total. The party now knows what they are looking for. This is Route D prerequisite number one — and it has just been given.

---

## What ends the scene

The scene ends when:
- Tessamine has given (or declined to give, if conditions not met) her reconstruction document.
- The operational brief for the next shard has been delivered.
- The party has left the archive.

After that: end of adventure 0008. Handoff notes to follow.

---

## What happens if the arc-completion never fires

The paper stays in Tessamine's archive. She dies with it. The party recovers the compact's clauses the slower way — through the shards themselves, as they accumulate. Route D is still possible; it is just later and harder.

That is an acceptable outcome. It is not a failure. It is what happens when the party isn't ready yet.
