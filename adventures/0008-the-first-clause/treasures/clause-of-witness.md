# The Clause of Witness

*The first shard of the Conclave Compact. Palm-sized. Warm.*

## Appearance

A fragment of polished obsidian approximately ten centimeters long, irregular at the break-edges where it separated from the full tablet, smooth where the original craftwork survived. One face is inscribed in a precise archaic script that predates the Cataclysm by at least two centuries. The inscription reads, in High Istaran: *"Let no mortal act without witness."* Below the clause: a wizard's personal sigil — the mark of Archmage Selindra of the White Robes (PC 180; died PC 219, one year before the Cataclysm). The sigil is worn but legible.

The shard is always slightly warm — the warmth of a palm that has been holding it for an hour, whether or not anyone is holding it.

## Presence / Desire

The Clause of Witness wants to be read. More precisely: it wants the person reading it to understand what they are reading. It has no patience for catalog entries, for *"decorated obsidian fragment, pre-Cataclysm."* It has been in a display case for three years and it has spent that time being warm and waiting.

Its behavior: dims — not goes dark, dims — whenever a lie is spoken within 20 feet. The dimming is subtle enough that Henneth has never noticed. The party may notice. Thessaly will notice.

## Provenance

1. **Selindra of the White Robes (PC 180–219):** Swore the Clause at the original Conclave Compact signing in the Tower of High Sorcery at Istar. Died one year before the Cataclysm, from natural causes. Never knew it was coming. The shard passed to her Order's archive.
2. **The Tower of High Sorcery at Istar (PC 219–PC 220):** The Cataclysm destroyed the Tower. The shard survived the destruction and was scattered with the rubble.
3. **The Coral Salvage Company (PC 220–unknown):** A pre-Cataclysm-salvage operation working the Istar ruins. Found the shard in the rubble, catalogued it as "decorative obsidian fragment, possibly pre-Cataclysm artifact," valued for decorative sale.
4. **Palanthas estate auction (5 years ago):** The shard passed through several hands and ended in a Palanthas estate auction, where it was purchased by a collector for 15 gold as part of a lot.
5. **Varold Henneth (3 years ago – present):** Purchased the shard at the Palanthas auction. It is currently mounted in his study alongside similar fragments — he has a taste for pre-Cataclysm decorative objects. He has not read the inscription carefully. He does not know High Istaran.

## Properties

**Passive:** The shard is warm to the touch at all times.
**Passive:** The shard dims when a lie is spoken within 20 feet. The dimming lasts for 3 seconds; if the party is watching, Perception DC 12 to notice it. Creatures with Arcana proficiency notice automatically.
**Passive:** A creature with Arcana proficiency who reads the inscription (voluntarily or via passive 12) understands it is a magical oath-clause from the Conclave Compact.
**Hint 1 delivery:** Thessaly reading the inscription triggers a specific response — the inscription brightens briefly, as if it recognizes that someone who understands what it means is reading it. Thessaly's passive Arcana (18) triggers this automatically.

## Curse

**The Clause's weight:** A creature who attunes to the shard is compelled toward transparency — not honesty, exactly, but a deep discomfort with acting without witnesses. The attuned creature has disadvantage on Deception checks while within 60 feet of the shard. Additionally, once per day, the shard asks: *"Who witnessed this?"* — this is an inward question, not an outward effect; the attuned creature hears it in their mind when they have just taken an action no one else saw. There is no save. It is just the question.

**Ending:** The shard's clause is released when the Conclave Compact is spoken whole — either restored (Route A), spoken new (Route D), or dissolved (Route C). At that point, the shard becomes inert obsidian, warm for one more day, then cool.

## GM Notes

- The shard's dimming behavior is the adventure's primary supernatural tell. Plant it early (Scene 01 in Tessamine's archive: Tessamine lies about how much time she has left; the shard dims). The party may not understand what they saw. They will remember it.
- Thessaly and the inscription: do not force it. If Thessaly's player reads it, Hint 1 delivers. If not, Tessamine can describe it later — recovery path.
- Henneth is not an enemy. He is a collector who bought something without knowing what it was. The most elegant resolution is one where he gives it up willingly, or where the party explains what it is and he donates it to the Conclave cause.
