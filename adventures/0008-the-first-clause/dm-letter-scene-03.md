# DM Letter — Scene 03 (The Study: Thessaly Reads the Inscription)

*This letter is for you, the DM. It does not go in the module. It is not read aloud. Read it before the session so you know how to be present for what's about to happen.*

---

## What this scene is

Thessaly is a White Robe wizard who once cast a spell that permanently dimmed her sister's magical ability. Her sister does not know. Thessaly has been carrying this for three years without naming it.

The Clause of Witness is inscribed: *"Let no mortal act without witness."*

She has been acting without a witness. That is what she did, in the Test chamber, four steps past the threshold.

When she reads the inscription, she is reading her own wound. The shard will recognize her. She will feel it.

Your job is not to tell her what it means. Your job is to be the room she reads it in.

---

## What to do before it starts

- Read Thessaly's grief paragraph in `personas/parties/compact-wardens/thessaly-nightmantle.md`. Specifically: "The sound of her own boots on the Test chamber's stone floor, and the moment — four steps past the threshold — when she realized the spell had drawn from somewhere it should not have."
- The shard is in the display case. The inscription faces up. She reaches for it, or she reads it from the case, or she asks what it says. Any of these is sufficient.
- The shard brightens when she reads it. This is automatic — her Arcana passive is 18. You do not need a roll. You just need her to look.

---

## What to do during

**If Thessaly reads the inscription aloud (the most common path):** Let her read it. When she finishes — *"Let no mortal act without witness"* — narrate the brightening: *"The inscription glows for a moment, briefly, and warm. Not alarming. Just: recognized."* Then stop. Wait.

**If she asks what it means:** Do not answer. This is her question, not yours. If the player seems to want a prompt, offer the short scaffold: *"The clause is a covenant promise — every mortal action witnessed. You know what it is for."* Then stop. Wait.

**If she names what it means to her personally:** This is the moment. Let her speak. Do not add narration over her words. Do not rush the outcome. If she names her sister, if she names what she did at the Test, if she names the weight she has been carrying — that is the arc-completion seed for Scene 06. Note it. When she finishes speaking: *"The shard is warm in her hands. It is still warm. It has been waiting for someone who could read it."*

**If Orik or Sera or Calder is watching:** They are watching. They do not understand what they are seeing. That is correct. Let them witness it without comprehending it. The party will understand later.

**If she says nothing — just holds the shard:** That is also a valid response. She read it. She knows. She puts it down or keeps it. Either way: Hint 1 delivered. The campaign has its first seed.

---

## What ends the scene

The scene ends when:
- Thessaly has read the inscription (the shard glows; Hint 1 delivered).
- Sevel has come and gone (the dimming behavior has fired).
- Henneth has given the party the shard (or the ambush has triggered, and they leave with it another way).

After that: Scene 05 or 06. Normal session narration resumes.

---

## What happens if Thessaly doesn't read it

Hint 1 has a fallback: Tessamine reads the inscription aloud in Scene 06. The delay is acceptable. The seed is still planted — just by a different hand.

Do not engineer a moment if the player doesn't reach for one. If Thessaly looks at the shard, puts it in her pack, and moves on — that is her choice. The campaign continues. The arc is patient.

---

*The inscription has been waiting three years in a display case. It waited in the ruins for two hundred years before that. It can wait through one more scene.*
