# Encounter — Street Stop (Between Archive and Townhouse)

**Triggers:** Once, on the party's first passage from Tessamine's archive to Henneth's townhouse. A Korrath harbor guard stops the party. This is non-optional.

**Context:** Korrath is a working port city with a regular harbor patrol. A White Robe wizard (Thessaly) and a Dwarven fighter (Orik) moving purposefully through the harbor district are unusual. The guard's question is routine, not hostile.

## Stat block

**Korrath Harbor Guard** (×1; 1 guard approaches; 3 more visible at the patrol station 40 ft. away)

Non-combatant unless violence is initiated. **AC** 16 (Chain Shirt + Shield) | **HP** 22.
Skills: Insight +3, Perception +3.

**Goal:** Confirm the party has legitimate business in Korrath. He has a description of an "unusual party" from a Conclave contact (Tessamine arranged this months ago; the guard is on notice to let them through but needs to confirm identity).

## How it resolves

**Option A — Tessamine's letter:** The party presents the letter. The guard reads the White Robe seal and says: *"Archivist Tessamine's people. Right. Carry on. Keep your documents visible."* 20 seconds; no roll.

**Option B — Persuasion/Deception:** If the letter is not presented, the guard needs to be satisfied. Persuasion DC 13 (the party seems legitimate) or Deception DC 12 (they are scholars, merchants, or sailors). Failure: the guard escorts them to the harbor marshal's office — 30-minute delay; the marshal confirms Tessamine's legitimacy; the party is released. The ambush timer advances by 20 minutes (the delay counts).

**Option C — Violence:** The guard calls his three companions immediately. This is Tier 2 party combat at Easy-Medium difficulty (4 guards, CR 1/4, roughly 200 adjusted XP). The party can win; the cost is: harbor reputation soured for the rest of the session (checks in Korrath made at disadvantage), and the ambush timer advances by 40 minutes.

## GM Notes

- This encounter is brief and social. It gates the party's timeline without being hostile.
- Its narrative purpose: introduce that Korrath notices unusual people, establish that Tessamine has prepared for the party's arrival (even if they don't know it yet), and give the party a moment to decide how they want to operate in this city.
- Orik's read of the patrol: he counts four guards at the station (signature move). He will note: *"We're not in trouble yet."*
- Sera's read: the guard is not looking at Thessaly specifically — he is looking at Orik. A Dwarf is unusual in Korrath's human-majority port. She notes this without saying it.
