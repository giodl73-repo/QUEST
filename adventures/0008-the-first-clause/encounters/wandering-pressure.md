# Wandering Pressure — The First Clause

This adventure takes place in a living city (Korrath-by-the-Sea), not a sealed dungeon. "Wandering pressure" here means social and urban complications — events that develop if the party lingers or does not move with purpose.

## Cold Pulse Table (d6, every 30 minutes in the city)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | A harbor worker points out the party to a colleague and they whisper. The party is being noticed — but not necessarily in a bad way. Korrath is not used to visitors with a White Robe letter. |
| 2 | The light shifts — a cloud passing, or the harbor mist thickening. Something that was clear becomes obscured. This is an omen; the DM decides if it's meaningful. |
| 3 | A smell of pine resin from a dockside chandler's stall. Orik's hands go still. This resolves on its own. |
| 4 | A courier — not the Unbound Conclave courier; a city courier — asks if any of the party is "the scholar from the archive." Tessamine sent a message: she found something in her papers they should see. (The moon calendar; Hint 2 carrier available for pickup.) |
| 5 | Rain begins. Nothing stops; the city simply gets wet. The study window was open when the party arrived at the townhouse; if they're in the study, Henneth quietly closes it and apologizes for the delay. |
| 6 | The harbor bells ring the hour. Three hours until the tide changes. The sloop at Henneth's dock will be difficult to sail out-harbor after the tide turns. This is only relevant if the party plans a water exit. |

## Route C timer (non-optional after 90 minutes)

The courier is always watching. The party has 90 minutes in the townhouse before the ambush activates. This is a hard clock, not a die roll.

**Timer mechanics:**
- **0–30 min:** The party works. No pressure.
- **30–60 min:** Sera (passive Perception 16) or Investigation DC 12 from the dock can spot the courier in the harbor market. She is watching. This is a warning.
- **60–90 min:** The party knows the clock is running. Every scene that stalls costs time.
- **90 min:** The ambush activates. Two Unbound Conclave agents enter via the private dock. No die roll; this always happens at 90 minutes if the party is still in the townhouse with the shard unrecovered.

**If the shard is recovered before 90 minutes:** The ambush does not trigger at Henneth's townhouse. Instead, the courier sends a runner; the party may be followed on the street back to Tessamine's archive. Route A: they make it back clean. Route D: they made it back with time to spare and had the quiet reading moment in Scene 03.

**Note:** The 90-minute clock makes the adventure fair. The ambush is the consequence of moving slowly or without purpose; it is not arbitrary pressure. A party that is direct (letter → study → honest explanation → shard) finishes in under 30 minutes. A party that is cautious (stealth, investigation, back stairs) finishes in under 60. Only a party that is stalled (deception attempts, exploration for its own sake, socializing without progress) reaches 90 minutes.

This table replaces a traditional dungeon wandering-monster check. The pressure is temporal and social, not combative by default.
