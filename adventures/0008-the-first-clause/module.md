*A dying archivist. A merchant who doesn't know what he owns. A shard of obsidian that dims when you lie. The campaign's first piece.*

## Summary

The compact-wardens are sent by Archivist Tessamine (dying; 6 weeks) to recover the Clause of Witness — the first shard of the Conclave Compact — from Merchant Varold Henneth's private collection in Korrath-by-the-Sea. Henneth doesn't know what he has. He purchased it at a Palanthas estate auction three years ago.

The Unbound Conclave (renegade faction) has a courier in Korrath watching for exactly this kind of recovery operation. The party has 90 minutes in Henneth's townhouse before she activates her agents.

---

## Hook

Archivist Tessamine's letter, delivered to Thessaly via Red Robe courier:

> *"The Clause of Witness is in Merchant Varold Henneth's private collection at his townhouse on the Korrath harbor front. He does not know what he has. He catalogued it as 'decorated obsidian fragment, pre-Cataclysm.' I am dying. I cannot go. You can. Bring it to my archive. — T."*

## Setting & Background

**Korrath-by-the-Sea** — A mid-sized Ansalon port city (eastern coast). Pre-Cataclysm wealth; post-Cataclysm maintenance debt. Harbor district includes Henneth's townhouse and Tessamine's Red Robe chapter-house (three streets inland). The city runs on maritime trade; an unusual party is noticed.

**The Conclave Compact** — Before the Cataclysm, the three Orders of High Sorcery (White Robes, Red Robes, Black Robes — the three guilds of Krynn's arcane practitioners) maintained a binding agreement with the three moons of the world through a physical tablet inscribed with seven clauses. Each clause was sworn by a named wizard. The tablet was in the Tower of High Sorcery at Istar. The Cataclysm — the gods' punishment that destroyed Istar and remade the continent's coastlines three centuries ago — shattered the tower and the tablet both. Seven shards. Seven oaths that have not been released.

**The Unbound Conclave** — A renegade faction of former Red Robe and Black Robe wizards who believe the Conclave Compact can legitimize their claim to reconstitute the Orders under their own authority. They want the shards for control, not restoration.

## Scenes

### Scene 01 — Tessamine's Archive

**Read-aloud:**

> Three walls of shelves, one wall of window. Harbor light through old glass. Archivist Tessamine sits at the table — she didn't rise to greet you. Your folded letter is already between you. She's read it first.

**Features:**
- **Tessamine.** She briefs the party: Henneth's address (harbor district, 3 streets away), letter of introduction (White Robe seal), operational brief on the Unbound Conclave (active courier in harbor market, grey robes, not yet hostile but watching). She says she has 6 weeks. She has 4. The dimming shard-reference on her desk dims when she says this — Perception DC 10 / Arcana proficiency automatic.
- **Moon-calendar (Hint 2 carrier, optional).** Investigation DC 12 or Calder passive Insight 14: a moon-calendar with a date circled three months from now. Tessamine does not flag this; it is in her working papers.

**GM Notes:**
- **The dimming.** Tessamine's lie (6 weeks; she has 4) dims the shard-reference on her desk for 3 seconds. Note it quietly if noticed; do not explain it. *"The document on her desk dims slightly when she says 'six weeks.' You aren't sure what you saw."*
- **DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-06.md` before this session. If Thessaly is likely to engage deeply with the shard, also read `dm-letter-scene-03.md`.

### Scene 02 — Henneth Townhouse, Entry Hall

**Street Stop (before entry — non-optional, once):**

A Korrath harbor guard stops the party between the archive and the townhouse.

**Read-aloud (street stop):**

> A harbor guard steps from the patrol station — one of four; the others stay visible. "Hold a moment. Your business in the harbor district?"

**Resolution:** Tessamine's letter (20 seconds; no roll) | Persuasion DC 13 or Deception DC 12 (failure = 30-minute escort delay; ambush timer +20 min) | Violence (Easy combat, 4 guards CR 1/4; ambush timer +40 min).

**Read-aloud (entry hall):**

> The Henneth townhouse: four floors of pre-Cataclysm stone, well-kept. A housekeeper takes your letter and asks you to wait. Two chairs, dried harbor-flowers, a portrait above the fireplace — Henneth's wife, from the bone structure. The room smells of salt and beeswax.

**Features:**
- **Pella (housekeeper).** Takes the introduction letter; returns with Henneth in 3 minutes. Notes what the party examines (Insight DC 10).
- **Portrait of Dana Henneth.** Small Conclave-order mark in lower corner — painter's flourish, not significant, but Thessaly will look twice. Investigation DC 12 to see it clearly.
- **Henneth arrives.** From the Study stairs, already curious (Tessamine's name on the letter). Greets the party warmly; offers to show his collection.

**Connections:** Study (invited, shortcut); Servant Quarters (unescorted).

### Scene 03 — The Study

**Read-aloud:**

> Six shelves of pre-Cataclysm fragments, each with a handwritten card. The display case along the east wall holds six pieces. The third from the left is different from the others: the others have a fine dust-film. This one is clean.

**Features:**
- **The display case.** Third shard from left: clean, warm (Symptom 5 and 2 of manifest). Henneth's card: *"Obsidian fragment, pre-Cataclysm. Palanthas estate auction. High Istaran script (unread). 15 gp."*
- **The inscription.** Thessaly reads it automatically (Arcana passive 18). Others: Arcana DC 12 or History DC 14. Hint 1 delivery: inscription brightens briefly when Thessaly reads it. **DM-letter scene-03.md applies here.**
- **Sevel's visit.** Henneth's business partner arrives unannounced mid-scene, lies to Henneth twice (shipping contract numbers). The shard dims visibly each time Sevel lies (Symptom 1, primary occurrence). Perception DC 10 / Arcana automatic. Sevel leaves after 8 minutes. Henneth doesn't address the discrepancy; he knows and hasn't decided what to do.
- **Reaction moment (artifact removal).** When the shard is removed from the case, the case is suddenly empty — the velvet beneath the other fragments seems darker. Henneth looks at the empty space: *"I always thought it was warmer than it should be."*
- **Henneth's decision.** If the party explains what the shard is (genuine explanation), Henneth gives it to them. See NPCs section for behavior matrix and arc-completion.

**Encounter:** None at baseline. Household guards (2, Bandit stats) if theft is attempted. Unbound Conclave ambush (Route C — see Encounters Appendix) if 90-minute timer expires.

**Treasure:** Clause of Witness (display case). See Treasures section.

**Connections:** Entry Hall; Private Dock (internal stairs, Perception DC 14 or Henneth points it out).

**GM Notes:**
- **Thessaly and the inscription.** She reads it quietly to herself (signature move). When she finishes, wait. Do not explain. If she names its meaning to the party, that is the arc-completion seed for Scene 06. Note it for Tessamine's return.
- **Sevel and the dimming.** The party has seen the shard dim at the archive (Tessamine's lie) and now again at Sevel's lies. They don't know why yet. That's correct.

### Scene 05 — Private Dock

**Read-aloud:**

> A small dock, one sloop, harbor sounds. Henneth uses it for weather observation. The water is three feet below. From here, the harbor market stalls are visible — and one grey-robed figure who doesn't match the market crowd.

**Features:**
- **Harbor view.** The Unbound Conclave courier visible in harbor market. Investigation DC 12 or Perception DC 14 — a figure in grey robes who is watching the townhouse, not shopping.
- **Water exit.** The sloop is seaworthy. The party can leave by water.

**Encounter:** Unbound Conclave Ambush (two agents; see Encounters Appendix) — activates if Route C timer expires and party is still here.

**GM Notes:**
- **Orik and the pine resin.** The dock smells of pine resin (rope-maintenance oil). Orik's hands go still for a moment (grief trigger; signature move). Note it briefly; do not narrate heavily.
- **The courier.** She is not yet hostile. She is watching. If the party approaches, she denies being anything other than a merchant (Insight DC 14 to read the denial).

### Scene 06 — Tessamine's Archive (Return)

**Read-aloud:**

> Tessamine is still in the same chair. She's added a shawl. The light is later now — orange through old glass. She watches you cross the room to her desk, and she doesn't look at the shard first. She looks at your faces.

**Features:**
- **The delivery.** Tessamine holds the shard, confirms its identity by the warmth, writes the receipt note, files it.
- **Tessamine's arc-completion.** Available if Thessaly named the inscription's meaning (Scene 03) or if any PC asks "What was the compact actually for?" and waits. See NPCs section.
- **Operational brief for adventure 0009.** Two shard locations known; two suspected; renegade faction movements updated.
- **Hint 1 fallback.** If inscription was not read in Scene 03, Tessamine reads it aloud now. Passive Arcana 10 for any party member to understand it.

**GM Notes:**
- **DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-06.md`. If the arc-completion fires, stop and witness. Do not narrate over it.
- **"She looks at your faces."** Two seconds of silence before you continue. She is assessing.

## Treasures

### The Clause of Witness

*Palm-sized obsidian shard. Warm to the touch at all times. Inscribed in High Istaran: "Let no mortal act without witness."*

**Appearance:** Fragment of polished obsidian, 10 cm, irregular at break-edges, smooth where original craftwork survived. The inscription is precise and pre-Cataclysm. Below it: the personal sigil of Archmage Selindra of the White Robes (died PC 219, one year before the Cataclysm).

**Presence / Desire:** The shard wants to be read. It has been in a display case for three years. It is warm and waiting.

**Passive properties:**
- Always warm (warmth of a held palm).
- Dims for 3 seconds when a lie is spoken within 20 feet (Perception DC 12 / Arcana proficiency automatic).
- Arcana-proficient creature who reads the inscription understands it as a Conclave Compact clause.
- **Hint 1:** Thessaly reading triggers a brief brightening (inscription recognizes her). Automatic.

**Curse — The Clause's Weight:**
A creature who attunes to the shard has disadvantage on Deception while within 60 feet of the shard. Once per day after taking an unseen action, the attuned creature hears internally: *"Who witnessed this?"* No save. Just the question.

**Provenance:**
1. Archmage Selindra of the White Robes (PC 180–219) — swore the clause at the Conclave Compact signing
2. Tower of High Sorcery at Istar (PC 220) — destroyed by the Cataclysm; shard scattered
3. Coral Salvage Company — found in ruins; catalogued "decorative obsidian fragment"
4. Palanthas estate auction (5 years ago) — sold as part of a lot
5. Varold Henneth (3 years ago – present) — display case; never read the inscription

**Ending:** The shard becomes inert when the Conclave Compact is spoken whole (Route A, C, or D at adventure 0014).

## NPCs

### Archivist Tessamine

*White Robe wizard, 74. Dying (4 weeks, not 6 — she lied). She has been waiting for someone who could receive what she knows.*

**Non-combatant.** AC 10, HP 8.

**Emotional position:**
- **Wants:** For the compact to be understood before it is reassembled.
- **Can't say:** The compact's purpose — until she knows the party is ready to receive it.
- **Condition:** Thessaly names the inscription's meaning, or any PC asks "What was it actually for?" and waits.

**Arc-completion:** When the condition is met, Tessamine sets down what she's holding and says: *"The compact was not written to bind the Orders. It was written to bind the Orders to the people who live under the sky they argue about. Every clause is a reckoning the Orders agreed to face. What you're carrying is an agreement to be responsible. That is what the renegades want to control."* She then gives Thessaly (or the most trusted PC) her reconstruction document — all seven clauses, incomplete, but close.

**Behavior matrix:**

| Party action | Response |
|---|---|
| Asks what the shard is | Technical answer: "A fragment of the Conclave Compact, seven clauses, seven oaths." No purpose yet. |
| Asks why the compact matters | Political answer: renegades will use it for authority. Still not the purpose. |
| Thessaly names inscription's meaning | "Yes. That is exactly what it is for." → arc-completion. |
| Any PC asks "what was it *actually* for?" and waits | If they wait: she looks carefully, then speaks. |
| Party asks about Tessamine's sister (Mira) | "I taught Thessaly for six years. I know what she carries. That is not mine to say." |

---

### Varold Henneth

*Merchant, 58. Collector. Good man. Doesn't know what he has.*

**Non-combatant.** Will call 2 household guards (Bandit stats: AC 12, HP 11, Shortsword +4, 1d6+2) if threatened.

**Arc-completion:** When presented with Tessamine's letter and a genuine explanation of what the shard is, Henneth opens the case, lifts the shard, holds it out: *"I always thought it was warmer than it should be."* Then gives it over.

**Behavior matrix:**

| Party action | Response |
|---|---|
| Deception attempt | Suspicious; calls guards if pressed. (Shard dims when the lie lands.) |
| Genuine explanation of what the shard is | Listens carefully. "It's valuable then?" (Thinking about the collection's reputation.) |
| Tessamine's letter presented | Gives the shard immediately. He knows her name; she's credible. |
| Asks if he would donate it | Considers seriously — his collection is his legacy, not his revenue. |

## Encounters Appendix

### Street Stop (non-optional, Scene 02 bridge)

One Korrath harbor guard; 3 more visible at patrol station.

**Guard:** AC 16 (Chain Shirt + Shield), HP 22. Non-combatant unless attacked.

**Resolution:** Tessamine's letter = immediate pass | Persuasion DC 13 or Deception DC 12 = pass | Failure = 30-minute escort delay (ambush timer +20 min) | Violence = Easy combat (4 guards, CR 1/4 each, ~200 XP adjusted).

---

### Unbound Conclave Ambush (Route C — activates at 90-minute mark)

Two Unbound Conclave Agents (Thug reskin): **AC** 11 | **HP** 32 | Multiattack: 2 mace attacks (+4, 1d6+2) | Pack Tactics.

**Agent 1 (Osvin):** Carries a short staff — once per day, casts Thunderwave (1st level, save DC 11, 2d8 thunder, pushed 10 ft. on fail).

**Agent 2 (quiet one):** Carries grappling hook and rope; attempts grapple on shard-carrier (Athletics +4).

**Tactics:** Round 1: Announce terms ("Give us the fragment — no one is hurt"). Round 2 (if refused): Thunderwave toward cluster; grapple attempt on shard-carrier. Round 3+: If below half HP or clearly outmatched, Osvin calls retreat. They disengage and flee via dock. They do not kill; they want the shard, not a war.

**XP:** 300 adjusted (Easy for L5 party). Intentionally manageable — Route C is the pressure route, not a death check.

---

### Household Guards (if theft triggered)

2 × Bandit stat block (SRD): **AC** 12 | **HP** 11 | Shortsword +4 (1d6+2) | Crossbow +4 (1d8+2, 80/320 ft.)

**Tactics:** Call for Henneth (round 1). Protect the study. Do not pursue outside the townhouse.

## Wandering Pressure

Urban pressure replaces dungeon wandering encounters. The party has 90 minutes in the townhouse. This is a hard clock.

| Time | Pressure |
|---|---|
| 0–30 min | No pressure. Party works. |
| 30–60 min | Courier visible from dock (Perception DC 14 or Investigation DC 12 from Scene 05). Warning available. |
| 60–90 min | Party knows the clock. |
| 90 min | Unbound Conclave ambush activates. Two agents enter via dock. Non-optional. |

**Cold Pulse (d6, every 30 minutes in city):**

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | Party is noticed (harbor workers pointing; no consequence yet) |
| 2 | Light shifts — cloud or harbor mist. The DM decides if it's meaningful. |
| 3 | Pine resin smell from chandler's stall. Orik goes still for a moment. |
| 4 | City courier with a message from Tessamine — moon-calendar pickup available (Hint 2 carrier) |
| 5 | Rain begins. Nothing stops; everything gets wet. |
| 6 | Harbor bells ring the hour. 3 hours to tide change. Sloop exit difficult after that. |
