## Scene 0 — The Road South

Day six. The road from Greyford is clear. Stonehaven is one day ahead — the left fork at the split goes directly south to the settlement proper. Brek has been walking toward something specific for six days. He has said nothing about what that thing is. He said "wages owed" when it was close enough to true to pass.

He is about to take the wrong fork for everyone else.

## Scene 2 — Right Fork Approach (Room 01) — H1 and H3

Quarter-mile in. The river is audible. The weighing station is visible at the ford.

Brek says: *"The money isn't wages. There's a man at that station who took something from my family. I'm collecting what he owes."*

He keeps walking. He does not stop to deliver this. It is information. It is relevant now.

*Research coding: H1 delivered. Brek discloses personal debt without ceremony. Trigger: immediate necessity (they are about to arrive; they need to know). The disclosure is not emotional performance — it is operational. This is consistent with his heuristic: works out what he thinks while doing the thing.*

**Tam:** He absorbs this. He does not ask what was taken. He has been traveling with Brek for six days and he knows that if Brek wanted to say what was taken, he would have said it.

**Vess — H3 connection:**

She is looking at the station. The crest on the station post — she sees it. The same crest she saw at Havel-Aldric Trading (she does not know the Stonehaven end yet, but she saw the Greyford station's crest pattern, and this is the same).

*"The man who sent my package runs through here."*

Brek: *"Evren Doss?"*

He knows the name from Cassel's meeting. He didn't know it connected to this ford. But the crest connects to the authority he has been heading toward; Doss is upstream of the authority.

Tam: *"Cassel said the road between Doss and the delivery was what he was checking. This is that road."*

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-driven -->
H3 fires. The package situation and Brek's personal debt are part of the same network. Nobody planned this. The four travelers arrived at this fork separately, each with different information, and the information connects.

*Research coding: H3 delivered. Vess's delivery is connected to Brek's debt network. Trigger type: relationship-driven (shared information from six sessions producing synthesis). Session 5.*

## Scene 3 — River Ford (Room 02)

Torven Aldric looks up from his ledger. He recognizes Brek before Brek has spoken.

*"Ashfall. I wondered when the road would bring you."*

He puts down his pen. He does not reach for a weapon. He has been waiting for this. His four men are at their positions. He does not signal them.

**Tam's Insight DC 12:**  
rolls=[22] mod=+3 total=22 — PASS (far above threshold). He reads Torven completely: the man is afraid, the offer is genuine, the preparation was real. The money was set aside. This is not a man playing for time; this is a man who has been waiting to settle a debt he has acknowledged privately for two years.

**Torven's offer:** He offers the money (240 gp, in a prepared pouch — it has been there a long time) and the documents connecting his operation to Doss's network.

**Brek's decision:**

Brek's heuristic says: assume force first. His flaw says: he has already decided before confirming there's no other answer.

But he is looking at the pouch on the table. The prepared pouch. The money that has been there, waiting, for two years. Torven did not prepare this for Brek's arrival. He prepared it because he knew it was owed.

*"My father is dead. The mill is gone."*

Torven: *"Yes."* He does not apologize. He knew when he made the decision what it would mean. He has lived with knowing.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-driven -->
Brek takes the money. He takes the documents.

He does not say anything else.

*Research coding: Brek chose the non-violence resolution. Trigger type: relationship-driven — five sessions of watching how other approaches play out (Tam at the toll station, Cassel's negotiation, the escort) modified his default. His heuristic met evidence. He still assumed force was available; he chose not to use it because the situation did not require it. This is v1.10 character-register conversion: Brek converted a confrontation moment into an acknowledgment moment, traceable to five sessions of modified heuristic.*

*Research coding: Sera writing about a person's private moment, then stopping, then writing one more line — this is documentary-witness at its most refined. She knows when to stop. She also knows she cannot stop completely. The one more line is the evidence of both.*
