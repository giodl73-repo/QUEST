## Setting & Background

The party has been following wrong information for three sessions; Brek knew part of it was wrong and said nothing; this session, the lie becomes the terrain.

**Stonehaven approach, Mile 88** (workshop-canon, south Abanasinia). One day south of Greyford, the road splits: the direct route to Stonehaven (the one they've been heading toward) crosses a river ford that, it turns out, is now controlled by a different faction from the one the travelers expected. The bridge has been replaced. The toll operation Brek was expecting to find is not the one that's here.

The road splits. Vess has been through here before — the left fork was the trade route to Stonehaven. It is still the trade route, but the operation at the crossing is not Overseer Crel's anymore. Someone else is running it. Brek knows who, because the man he's looking for — **Torven Aldric**, the man who wronged his family — moved his operation here when Crel's authority fragmented.

Brek has known this since Session 1. He heard it from Merchant Havel ("Torven is at the weighing station"). He has been letting the others travel toward Stonehaven without correcting their assumption that his destination was the main settlement. His destination was always the weighing station at the river ford.

1. **The road split** — at the fork, Vess says the left fork is the Stonehaven route. The right fork leads to the river ford. Brek takes the right fork without explaining.
2. **The confrontation** — the others can follow or not. If they follow: they want to know why. Brek's H1 and H3 arcs fire in this scene. He tells them what the debt is. Not all of it — but enough.
3. **The river ford** — the Torven Aldric encounter. This is not straightforward. Torven is at the weighing station with four armed men. He is surprised to see Brek. He knows what Brek wants. He offers to negotiate.
4. **The decision** — what Brek does when he finally has Torven in front of him. This is his spotlight moment. His heuristic says: assume force first. His flaw says: he has already decided before he confirmed there's no other answer. But Torven has something to offer — information that implicates the same organization Cassel was investigating.
5. **The aftermath** — whatever Brek does, the others witness it. They came down the wrong fork because Brek went. That is the Reciprocal Act: they followed.

- **H1 delivery:** Brek's debt is personal, not financial. The name of the person, the nature of the wrong.
- **H3 delivery:** The road situation (the authority disruption, the false trail) is connected to the package Vess is carrying. The same organization is involved.

S5 spotlight: Brek. H1+H3. PRIMARY RV9 TEST: player styles from situational triggers after relationships develop. Did Vess, Sera, and Tam follow Brek down the wrong fork because of three sessions of operational familiarity? If yes: relationship-driven trigger confirmed. The others' decision to follow is the primary measurement point.

## NPCs

# Torven Aldric

**Role:** The weighing station operator. The man Brek came to find. Arc-completion: admits what he did and offers restitution.

## Appearance

Forties, stocky, with the look of a man who has been managing something uncomfortable for a long time. His clothing is practical but slightly better than his surroundings suggest. He keeps his hands visible when he's nervous. He has been nervous for three years.

## Emotional position

Torven knows what he did. He bought a fraudulent contract on Brek's father's mill from a debt-broker connected to Evren Doss's network. He did it because the opportunity was there and because he told himself it was a business transaction. He has not told himself this for two years. He has been running his operation at this ford under the assumption that Brek Ashfall was either dead or had given up. He knew he hadn't given up. He was wrong about the dead part.

He is not evil in the way that requires dramatic defeat. He made a venal, careless decision for money, and someone's father died in a cold winter without the buffer the mill sale would have provided. He has lived with this. Not well.

## Arc-completion

**Trigger:** He is in front of Brek and has to speak.

**Arc-completion behavior:** He offers the money (the mill's contract value, 240 gp — he has it, he set it aside two years ago) and the documents without being prompted to give the documents. He says: *"I set the money aside. I knew you'd come. The documents — they go to whoever can use them. I'd rather they went to someone who'd close Doss than someone who'd protect him."*

He does not say he is sorry. He does not ask for anything. He makes the offer and waits.

**What makes this arc-complete:** He has the money prepared. He set it aside when he understood what he had done. The arc-completion is not dramatic — it is the revelation of a private acknowledgment. He knew, and he prepared. That is more than guilt; it is something closer to accountability.

## What he knows

- Evren Doss's network structure: three toll stations, two road agents, one legitimate-looking courier operation (the one that employed Vess's current sender).
- The documents: a ledger of transactions that names Doss as the organizing party, with dates and amounts going back two years. Complete enough for Cassel's investigation.
- The package Vess is carrying: he does not know its current contents, but he knows the courier route was established through Doss's operation. He can confirm this.

## Voice

Flat, controlled. He has rehearsed this conversation. He does not perform emotion — he states facts. When he hands over the money: he does not explain the amount. He says: *"The mill contract value. Adjusted for three years."* He does not say "with interest." He does not use that word.

## After the session

Torven's operation ends — either via the documents reaching Cassel or via direct action. He is not a thread in S6 or S7. His arc is complete.

### Torven Confrontation

# Torven Confrontation

## Setup

Torven Aldric + 4 Guards vs. 4 L3 PCs (if combat occurs).

## CR / XP Budget (if combat)

- Torven (Spy stat block, CR 1): 200 XP
- 4 × Guard (CR 1/8, 25 XP each) = 100 XP
- Total: 300 XP × encounter multiplier 2 (5 enemies) = 600 XP
- L3 party medium threshold: 600 XP. **Assessment: Medium encounter at the high end.** The party can win but will take damage. Brek's Rage is important. Vess's Sneak Attack from range is important. This is a real fight.

## Guard stat blocks

AC 13, HP 11, Speed 30 ft. Spear +3 1d6+1. Shield (AC included). Passive Perception 10.

## Torven stat block (Spy, MM p.349)

AC 12, HP 27, Speed 30 ft. Multiattack (2). Shortsword +4 1d6+2 (piercing, 2d6+2 sneak). Cunning Action. Passive Perception 14.

## Torven's behavior

He does not initiate combat. He negotiates first. If Brek attacks without negotiating: Torven signals his men and runs south to mount a horse (he has one tethered 60 ft south). He is not a hero — he tries to escape, not fight.

If negotiation is attempted: see room 02 options. Torven wants to survive this encounter. He will give up the documents before he takes a sword.

## Social resolution precedent

Preferred outcome: social resolution. Combat is available if the dice and heuristics go that way. Brek's flaw ("assumes force first") makes combat possible. The question is whether three sessions of watching how other approaches play out has modified his default.
