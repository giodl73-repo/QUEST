# Torven Aldric

**Role:** The weighing station operator. The man Brek came to find. Arc-completion: admits what he did and offers restitution.

## Appearance

Forties, stocky, with the look of a man who has been managing something uncomfortable for a long time. His clothing is practical but slightly better than his surroundings suggest. He keeps his hands visible when he's nervous. He has been nervous for three years.

## Emotional position

Torven knows what he did. He bought a fraudulent contract on Brek's father's mill from a debt-broker connected to Evren Doss's network. He did it because the opportunity was there and because he told himself it was a business transaction. He has not told himself this for two years. He has been running his operation at this ford under the assumption that Brek Ashfall was either dead or had given up. He knew he hadn't given up. He was wrong about the dead part.

He is not evil in the way that requires dramatic defeat. He made a venal, careless decision for money, and someone's father died in a cold winter without the buffer the mill sale would have provided. He has lived with this. Not well.

## Arc-completion

**Trigger:** He is in front of Brek and has to speak.

**Arc-completion behavior:** He offers the money (the mill's contract value, 240 gp — he has it, he set it aside two years ago) and the documents without being prompted to give the documents. He says: *"I set the money aside. I knew you'd come. The documents — they go to whoever can use them. I'd rather they went to someone who'd close Doss than someone who'd protect him."*

He does not say he is sorry. He does not ask for anything. He makes the offer and waits.

**What makes this arc-complete:** He has the money prepared. He set it aside when he understood what he had done. The arc-completion is not dramatic — it is the revelation of a private acknowledgment. He knew, and he prepared. That is more than guilt; it is something closer to accountability.

## What he knows

- Evren Doss's network structure: three toll stations, two road agents, one legitimate-looking courier operation (the one that employed Vess's current sender).
- The documents: a ledger of transactions that names Doss as the organizing party, with dates and amounts going back two years. Complete enough for Cassel's investigation.
- The package Vess is carrying: he does not know its current contents, but he knows the courier route was established through Doss's operation. He can confirm this.

## Voice

Flat, controlled. He has rehearsed this conversation. He does not perform emotion — he states facts. When he hands over the money: he does not explain the amount. He says: *"The mill contract value. Adjusted for three years."* He does not say "with interest." He does not use that word.

## After the session

Torven's operation ends — either via the documents reaching Cassel or via direct action. He is not a thread in S6 or S7. His arc is complete.
