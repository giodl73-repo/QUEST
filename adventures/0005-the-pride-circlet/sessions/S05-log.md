# S05 LOG — The Pride-Circlet

## Session Summary

The Varduin Muster rode eleven days south and east to Korrath-by-the-Sea at the invitation of Wena Laen — Brother Laen's twenty-eight-year-old sister, placed at a Revered Daughters chapter-house under Lady Tiran's patronage and asking, in the precise language of someone who has been waiting four months, for the party to come. The session was purely diplomatic — no combat, no dice rolls, no HP lost. The engine ran all seven scenes through the marathon-runner CLI; every mechanical beat was Python-resolved or narrated by the DM.

Kessa caught Vaenshold-vein silver in the market before the party spoke a word. Grom greeted a gate-hinge. Thera spotted the empty case in Tiran's reception hall. Three ratified player-styles fired on arrival before anyone sat down.

The session's pivot came at Scene 04 (the joint meeting): Wena offered to witness Tiran without the Circlet — to sit with her and hear what Edric saw, without the attunement. Tiran was silent for a long moment and then named, for the first time, that she did not want to see herself as Edric saw her; she wanted someone to know that he had seen her that way. Those are different things. She had not named the difference until Wena offered the alternative.

Aelric picked up the Circlet in the vault and felt it warm and set it down. His Oath made the choice before his grief could. He did not say Varran's name.

### Scene 1.0 — Korrath-by-the-Sea, Arrival

Korrath is what Second House money looks like after sixty years: immaculate harbor, purple-rose flags on four ships, market stalls running from the gate to the chapter-house district. The air smells of salt, rendered tallow, and wealth.

Kessa stops at a silver stall before the party says a word. The pendants are shop-grade; none have the weight of an artifact. But the smith-mark on two of them is Vaenshold-vein — the same lineage as Aelwen's forgings, selling for 15 gp. She writes the stall's location and the date. Does not explain.

Grom passes the chapter-house gate and touches the iron hinge. *"Thou'rt seen,"* in Dwarvish. The senior sister at the gate looks at him for three seconds and decides he is acceptable. **Craft-witness fires off-camera before Scene 2.**

Thera clocks the harbor exits: two service alleys, one storm-drain wide enough for a halfling, the inn's back door. She buys cheese she does not need.

Aelric stands at the market's center and is assessed by every Second House retainer in it simultaneously. He makes his assessments back. Then the party moves.

### Scene 2.0 — Lady Tiran's Estate, First Meeting

The reception hall is exactly what money kept immaculate for sixty years looks like. Marble floors. A painting of Korrath harbor circa PC -30 — the fishing-town version. A locked ebony case on a low table near the window, lined in purple velvet.

Felor pours tea without asking. Lady Tiran sits in a high-backed chair facing the case. She does not stand. She gestures to the chairs.

Her opening: *"Sir Aelric of the Crown. I have read your family history. Minor line, western Abanasinia. Your brother was a Knight of the Sword — an honor your line does not often achieve. You wear his armor."* Then: *"I have a commission. You may have come to prevent it. I am prepared to discuss both possibilities over tea."*

**Thera's Investigation DC 10 (reflex):** the interior velvet of the ebony case is flat-cut, not crown-shaped. The case is empty. It is a staging prop. She does not say this aloud. She files it. **Silent reception — empty case is Tiran's tell: the Circlet is elsewhere.**

Kessa: the harbor painting is pre-Second-House Korrath. A smaller harbor, a fishing town. Tiran has framed what she replaced. Whether this is honest or self-indulgent is undecided.

Grom: when Tiran breathes, the room knows. The lung ailment is audible. He grips his tongs without thinking.

Tiran on the Circlet, when asked: *"I intend to see how Edric saw me. Once. Before I die."* She does not apologize. She is 68 years old.

### Scene 3.0 — Wena's Briefing

Then: *"You came. Good. I was starting to think I would have to write a third letter."*

She gives the party Tiran's research notes freely. Five artifacts named. Sixth listed as: *"One unnamed; suspected properties related to shame or self-concealment; provenance unknown."*

### Scene 4.0 — The Joint Meeting

Tiran and Wena in the same room. The empty case is gone from the table.

Tiran: *"You have brought the party here to witness your refusal. I want to understand: is your refusal theological or personal?"*

Wena: *"Both."*

The exchange: Paladine vs. patronage vs. sixty years of earned gratitude. Neither woman is wrong about the thing she is defending. Neither is winning.

Grom Insight DC 23 on Tiran during the exchange: she is surprised by what comes next.

Wena, quietly: *"I can witness you. I cannot witness an attunement I believe will harm you. But I can be present when you tell someone who loves you what you are most proud of. That is not the Circlet. That is just talking."*

Tiran: *"Edric is dead."*

Wena: *"Then tell me. I am here."*

**The room is very quiet.** Felor is absolutely still.

**Grom Insight 23:** she is deciding. This is the moment.

Kessa does not take notes. Aelric's jaw tightens once and then relaxes. The Measure has a word for what Wena just offered. He could not have found the word first. He is grateful she did not need him to. **Silent reception — sheet-deep-reader: the Measure's word for "bearing witness to the dying" lives in Aelric's sheet.**

Tiran: *"That is correct. I do not want to see myself as Edric saw me. I want someone to know that he saw me that way. Those are different things. I did not — I had not named the difference until now."*

### Scene 5.0 — Tiran's Archive

Tiran gave them access herself. The archive: three walls of shelves, a reading table with a refilled scholar's lamp, a faint metallic trace Kessa catches immediately.

The shaped case on its dedicated shelf. Empty. The Circlet is through the vault door.

Kessa searches. Thera finds Tiran's pencil annotation in the manifest margin: *"Would E. have wanted this for me?"* Written six months ago. Not answered.

Kessa finds Aelwen's water-reflection fragment pinned to the inside back cover of the manifest — same handwriting as the journal page and the apron-scrap. She reads it aloud:

> *"If someone wears the Circlet and looks into water — not a mirror, but moving water — they see what they look like to everyone who has ever admired them, simultaneously. I tried this once. I will not describe what I saw. I have closed the workshop and will not return. — A."*

Three Aelwen fragments. Doubt (journal page). Resolve (apron-scrap). **Horror** (water-reflection). The diptych became a triplet.

Aelric is standing very still.

### Scene 6.0 — The Vault

Tiran opens the vault. The Circlet is on its stand, uncovered — a band of moon-silver, eleven half-bloomed roses in laurel arrangement. It sits as if it expects to be placed.

Grom stands near the door. He does not touch the Circlet. He can feel it wanting from here. Not hunger — something smaller. An attention. He thinks: *it is practicing at being worn again.* He touches the vault's stone wall with his thumb instead. The stone is cold and does not want anything. **Craft-witness: the Circlet's desire vs. the stone's indifference.**

Aelric steps forward. He picks up the Circlet.

The first touch is cool — two seconds — then warm. Eleven roses. He turns it in his hands. He does not hold it to his head. He does not look at the water bowl on the floor. He looks at it for perhaps twenty seconds.

The Measure says: *a knight must hold their honor in both directions — toward others, and toward themselves.* A knight who cannot see their own failures cannot hold honor. This object would give him the first without the second.

He sets it back on the stand.

*"I understand why someone would want it,"* he says. He does not say whose face the Circlet might have shown him. He does not say Varran's name. **No Option C spawn required — Aelric's decision order resolved cleanly without the grief-paragraph needing adjudication. The Oath was sufficient.**

Tiran, watching: *"I will not use it tonight. I will tell Wena what Edric saw."* Then, very quietly: *"I do not know if that is the same thing. I will find out."*

Felor blinks twice — the most visible surprise he has shown all session.

### Scene 7.0 — Aftermath at Dawn

Wena Laen is at the chapter-house courtyard gate in her work clothes. She has a bag. She is not leaving. She has a hinge to install.

She gives the party Tiran's complete six-forging research — the original. *"You will need this more than she will."*

Kessa takes it with careful hands. She has been carrying fragments long enough to know that one more is weight that matters.

Tiran: in the chapter-house chapel with Wena. Wena says — to no one in particular, still looking at the gate — *"She told me what Edric saw. I will not repeat it. It is hers."* A pause. *"It was sufficient."*

Brother Laen's courier delivers 350 gp and a formal letter: *"Whatever you have done — I hope it holds. Return when you can. — L."* A Second House formal-transfer document from Felor: *"Professional services rendered. Circlet in care of the Varduin Muster pending disposition."*

Aelric reads Laen's letter twice. Places it in his breast-pouch beside Wena's note. The pouch now holds three things: the note, the letter, and Varran's blue-leather saddle-lashing.

Grom, last: he does not say goodbye to Wena. He touches the chapter-house gate-hinge — the one she installed — and runs his thumb along the inside curve. It is true. He nods once and goes.
