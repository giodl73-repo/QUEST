# Encounter — Felor's Intervention (Scene 5, conditional)

**Location:** Tiran's archive or vault, Scene 5 or 6.
**Trigger:** party enters without Tiran's permission AND Felor notices (1d6+2 rounds; DM rolled privately).

## Adversaries

> **Felor of Second House (CR 1, 200 XP)** — see `npcs/felor-of-second-house.md`. AC 14, HP 38. Prefers negotiation. Will fight to protect Tiran's person; not the Circlet (he cares about the Circlet commercially, not personally).

> **Household guard × 1d4 (CR ⅛, 25 XP each).** AC 13 (scale mail), HP 11. Spear +3 (1d6+1); shortsword +3 (1d6+1). Loyal to Second House paycheck; not zealous. Morale breaks at 2 guards down.

## Encounter XP
- Felor solo: 200 × 1 = **200 XP.**
- Felor + 2 guards: 200 + 50 = 250 base × 1.5 (3 creatures) = **375 XP. Medium (L4 party).**
- Felor + 4 guards: 200 + 100 = 300 × 2 = **600 XP. Hard.**

## Tactics

Felor's opening: *"This is a Second House estate. You are trespassing. State your business or leave. I am being patient because Lady Tiran asked me to be. She did not specify for how long."*

**Negotiation (preferred):**
- **Persuasion DC 14:** party explains their intention is to assess the Circlet, not steal it. Felor stands down; reports to Tiran; schedules a formal vault visit.
- **Intimidation DC 16:** Felor is not easily intimidated; he calculates consequences, not reactions.
- **Leverage (Kann finger-bone connection):** if the party names Kann + Felor's commissioned acquisition explicitly, Felor's Persuasion DC drops to 10 and Intimidation DC drops to 12. He knows his exposure. He will negotiate cleanly.

**Combat (if unavoidable):**
- Felor uses **Ledger-Tongue** on the physically closest enemy (action-nerf for 1 round).
- Guards protect the corridor, not advance aggressively.
- Felor will retreat at HP ≤ 15 to fetch more guards.

## GM Notes

- **The goal is to not have this encounter.** If the party has been diplomatic, Tiran will have granted archive access by Scene 5 and this encounter never fires.
- **The Kann-leverage conversation is the encounter's ending.** If the party names Kann and Felor's involvement, the encounter becomes a legal discussion, not a fight.
- **Tier 2 note:** at L4, this encounter is manageable (Medium at worst), but the political fallout of killing Felor or guards in a Second House estate is significant — Brother Laen's letter confirms the Council is watching.
