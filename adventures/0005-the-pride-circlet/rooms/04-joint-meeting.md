# Scene 04 — The Joint Meeting (Tiran's Estate, Reception Hall — Second Visit)

**Location:** Tiran's reception hall. **Time:** Day 2 morning. **Setup:** Party has briefed Tiran that they want a meeting with both her and Wena present. Wena has agreed to come.

**Read-aloud:**

> Lady Tiran and Wena Laen in the same room have the particular texture of two people who have been carefully polite to each other for three months and are no longer pretending it is easy. Tiran sits in her chair. Wena stands near the window. Felor is at the door. The empty case is gone from the table.

**Features:**

- **The empty case is absent.** Tiran removed it before this meeting. If the party noticed it was empty in Scene 2, they understand why.
- **The dynamic.** Tiran and Wena are not enemies. They are two women with incompatible positions: Tiran wants a Paladine witness; Wena refuses to be one. The party's job is to understand what both of them need that the attunement-witness cannot provide.
- **The leverage point.** If Kessa (or any PC) has done the cross-reference from Scene 3's research notes: the Mirror is listed as still-with-Mira. Tiran does not know it was destroyed. This is information the party can use — not as blackmail, but as a reality-check. *"Your network is outdated. The Mirror is gone. The Wrath-Coin is gone. The Pride-Circlet is the last artifact you can acquire. Is that the reason you are in a hurry?"*

**Key scene beats:**

**Tiran addresses Wena directly:**
*"You have brought the party here to witness your refusal. I want to understand: is your refusal theological or personal?"*

Wena: *"Both. I am a novice. I cannot be a Paladine-representative for something I think Paladine would refuse to sanction."*

Tiran: *"Paladine sanctioned sixty years of my donations to your Order."*

Wena: *"That is not the same thing."*

**The real question under the meeting:** Tiran does not want to see her finest self alone. She wants a witness. Someone who will remember, after she is gone, that she was seen as she deserved to be seen. This is not a theological question; it is a grief question. She is dying. She is afraid of dying without anyone knowing what she looked like at her best.

**If the party names this explicitly:** Tiran will be silent for a long moment. Then: *"That is correct."* Then nothing else.

**Wena's offer (if party navigates well):** *"I can witness you. I cannot witness an attunement I believe will harm you. But I can be present when you tell someone who loves you what you are most proud of. That is not the Circlet. That is just talking."*

Tiran, quiet: *"Edric is dead."*

Wena: *"Then tell me. I am here."*

**This is the golden-path pivot.** If Tiran accepts Wena's offer — to be heard without the Circlet — the attunement is no longer necessary. This does not resolve the Circlet question, but it shifts the session from *artifact-retrieval* to *what Lady Tiran actually needs*.

**Investigation beats:**

- Insight DC 13 on Tiran listening to Wena: she is surprised by Wena's offer. She did not anticipate it. She is measuring it.
- Insight DC 15 on Felor: he is calculating whether Wena's offer is legally advantageous. He will support it if it means the Circlet stays in estate archive unattuned (clean for Second House).

**Encounter:** None. This is the session's moral fulcrum.

**GM Notes:**

- **This is the session's most important scene.** If the party navigates it well, the Circlet question becomes downstream. If they miss the real question (Tiran does not want the Circlet; she wants to be witnessed), they will have a harder Scene 5.
- **Wena's offer is character-consistent.** She is a novice in a healing order; she knows what it is to sit with the dying. She has been Tiran's guest for three months. She has been *witnessing* Tiran this whole time without the Circlet.
- **Aelric's role.** He is the one with institutional standing in this room (Solamnic knight; Laen's representative). His job is not to argue but to create the space for Tiran and Wena to say what they need to say. If he is patient, the scene resolves. If he leads with the Measure, it complicates.

She does not often think of that morning. She thinks of it now, with Wena Laen at the window in his place.*
