# Scene 05 — Tiran's Archive (Estate Third Floor)

**Location:** Tiran's private archive. **Time:** Day 2 afternoon (after or during the joint meeting, per party approach). **Access:** either Tiran grants access, or Thera and the party approach on their own (Investigation DC 13 to find unlocked window route; Thera Stealth DC 14 to enter without Felor noticing).

**Read-aloud:**

> The archive is the size of a small library. Three walls of shelves. A reading table with a scholar's lamp. And a smell: old paper, candlewax, and — Kessa will notice — a faint metallic trace that Vaenshold-lineage silver leaves in a room where it has been stored for decades. The Circlet has been here. Its case is here: a silver-chased ebony box, properly shaped, on a dedicated shelf. The case is empty. The Circlet is in the vault through the door to the left.

**Features:**

- **The archive's collection.** Forty years of Second House correspondence; maps of Korrath's harbor expansion; land title documents; and Tiran's personal research collection. **Aelwen's six-forging manifest** is here (the original; the copy Wena has is transcribed from this). Investigation DC 10 finds it in under a minute — it is on the reading table. It was the last thing Tiran read before the party arrived.
- **The Circlet's case.** Silver-chased ebony; interior purple velvet; **shaped precisely to a narrow band.** The case is empty. The Circlet is through the vault door. **Kessa Arcana DC 10:** the case's silver chasing is Vaenshold-vein work, same as the Korrath market pendants. Tiran commissioned the case from the same stock. She has been careful.
- **The vault door.** Locked. Investigation DC 13 to assess: high-grade Istaran lock; unique key; not pickable under 10 minutes (Thera's Thieves' Tools DC 16 for faster access). Tiran can unlock it. Felor has a copy of the key.
- **Aelwen's note** (in the original manifest, pinned to the inside back cover): Tiran's researcher found a fragment — different from the apron-scrap and the journal page. This one reads:
  > *"If someone wears the Circlet and looks into water — not a mirror, but moving water — they see what they look like to everyone who has ever admired them, simultaneously. I tried this once. I will not describe what I saw. I have closed the workshop and will not return. — A."*
  
  **Investigation DC 12** to find the note; **auto-delivery** if Kessa is searching (she knows what Aelwen's handwriting looks like from the prior fragments).

**Investigation beats:**

- The six-forging manifest (DC 10, on the table).
- Aelwen's water-reflection fragment (DC 12 or auto-Kessa).
- The vault door assessment (DC 13).
- Insight DC 12 on the archive's organization: Tiran organized this herself; the scholar's lamp has been refilled recently; she has been working in this room. This is where she has been spending her dying months.

**Encounter (conditional):** If the party entered *without Tiran's permission* (Thera stealth-approach), Felor finds them at the 1d6+2 round mark (rolled by DM; Felor is on 3-minute patrol). Encounter: Felor + 1d4 household guards (CR ⅛). Felor will negotiate before fighting (Persuasion DC 14 to stand down; Intimidation DC 16). If party has leverage (Kann finger-bone; Tiran's outdated Mirror intel), DC drops by 2.

**Connections:** through vault door → [6] the Vault.

**GM Notes:**

- **The water-reflection fragment is Aelwen's third surviving piece.** The journal page (#0003), the apron-scrap (#0004), and now this. Three fragments; three different registers (doubt, resolve, horror). Let Kessa read this one aloud.
- **Tiran's archive-hours.** The refilled scholar's lamp is the tell: she has been spending hours in this room, with the empty case, reading her own research. This is a woman who could have attuned any time in the last fifty years and chose not to, until now. That choice is worth understanding.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Tiran has read the six-forging manifest forty-three times in the last six months. She has made small pencil annotations in the margins. Most of the annotations are questions she cannot answer. One of them, next to the Circlet entry, reads: "Would E. have wanted this for me?" She wrote it six months ago. She has not answered it.*
