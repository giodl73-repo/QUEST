# Scene 02 — Lady Tiran's Estate, First Meeting

**Location:** Harbor-facing townhouse; reception hall. **Time:** mid-afternoon Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The reception hall is exactly what money kept immaculate for sixty years looks like. Marble floors from the Kharolis interior. A painting of Korrath harbor circa PC -30 on the west wall — the fishing-town version, before the Second House money arrived. On a low table near the window: a locked ebony case, fifteen inches across, lined in purple velvet. The case is closed. Lady Tiran sits in a high-backed chair facing it. She does not stand when the party enters. She gestures to the chairs across from her.

**Features:**

- **The ebony case (the Circlet's case).** Locked. Investigation DC 13 to assess the lock — Felor-quality; high-grade Istaran work. The case is not the Circlet. The Circlet is in the vault (Scene 6). *This case is empty.* Tiran uses it for the meeting as a visual anchor; the real Circlet is in the vault. **This is her tell:** she doesn't trust the party with the Circlet in the same room yet. Insight DC 14: she positioned the empty case deliberately.
- **The harbor-painting.** Kessa History DC 12: the painting dates from approximately PC -28 — before the current Second House era. It shows a *smaller* harbor than today's. Tiran has framed what she built.
- **Felor** stands near the door. He offers tea. He pours it regardless of answer.
- **Tiran's presence.** She is well-dressed, breathing carefully, precise. She will open the conversation.

**Tiran's opening:**

*"Sir Aelric of the Crown. I have read your family history. Minor line, western Abanasinia. Your brother was a Knight of the Sword — an honor your line does not often achieve. You wear his armor."*

She waits. This is both a statement and a question: *does Aelric confirm this?*

Then: *"I have a commission. You may have come to prevent it. I am prepared to discuss both possibilities over tea."*

**Dialogue beats:**

- **On the Circlet:** she will name it, describe it accurately (she knows its properties from Tiran's own research), and state her intention: *"I intend to see how Edric saw me. Once. Before I die."* She does not apologize for this. She is 68 years old and has earned her directness.
- **On Felor/Kann connection:** if the party mentions Kann's finger-bone cord and Felor's name, Felor goes still. Tiran: *"Felor's methods are his own. I ask for results. I do not ask how."* This is technically true and morally cowardly. Aelric's Insight DC 13: she knows, at some level, what commissioning Kann meant.
- **On Wena:** *"Wena Laen is my witness. I have asked her to be present for the attunement. She has not agreed. I am patient."*

**Investigation beats:**

- DC 10 on the empty case: the interior velvet is not shaped to a crown; it's flat-cut. The case is a placeholder.
- DC 13 on Felor's reactions: he becomes 15% more alert when Kann is mentioned.
- Insight DC 14 on Tiran: she is afraid of the Blank-Circlet-Trap, though she hasn't named it. She knows Edric admired her. She is not certain he admired her *most*. The uncertainty is the wound.

**Encounter:** None. This is a dialogue scene.

**Connections:** back to [1] market; ahead to [3] chapter-house (party should go find Wena before the next Tiran meeting); or directly to [5] archive if Tiran allows.

**GM Notes:**

- **Do not rush this scene.** Tiran is the session's moral weight. Let her speak. She has earned the time.
- **The empty case is the scene's puzzle.** The party should notice it is empty or not; either way is fine. If they don't notice, Wena will confirm the Circlet is in the vault at Scene 3.
- **Aelric's Solamnic register.** He will address Tiran formally; she will address him with precision. Two people who know exactly how much their titles mean and how much they don't.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The painting of the fishing-town Korrath was commissioned by Tiran at thirty, when she had just rebuilt the harbor. She intended it as a record of what she had improved. She has since come to think of it as a record of what she replaced. She has not removed it. She is not sure whether keeping it is honest or self-indulgent. She has not asked anyone. The question feels too personal.*
