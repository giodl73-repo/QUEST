## Summary

Korrath-by-the-Sea is an Istaran port with Second House money at every corner. Lady Tiran of the Purple Rose — 68, dying — has spent three months trying to recruit Wena Laen (Brother Laen's sister, a Revered Daughters novice) as a witness to her planned attunement of Aelwen's fifth moon-silver forging: the Pride-Circlet, a narrow headband that shows the wearer their finest self as seen through the eyes of their greatest admirer. Wena has refused. She sent a note to Aelric instead.

Four overlapping decisions drive the session: Does Aelric attune the Circlet? Is Lady Tiran an antagonist or a dying woman with the wrong tool? Does Wena leave the convent? Where does the Circlet end up?

The session's moral center is Scene 4: the joint meeting where the party discovers that Tiran does not want the Circlet — she wants to be *witnessed*. Her greatest admirer was her husband Edric, dead eight years. She does not know whether the Circlet can still reach him. That uncertainty is the wound. The Pride-Circlet is the wrong cure for a grief that is not yet done.

Aelric's specific danger: Varran — likely his most-admirer — is dead, and his memories of Varran were consumed by the Reliquary in S02. If Aelric attunes and the Circlet finds no living anchor, the Blank-Circlet-Trap fires. Wena knows this. That is why she wrote.

## Hook

**Wena Laen's note** arrived at Varduin via Brother Laen's courier at S04 Scene 7. Sealed, addressed to Aelric:

> *"Thank you for keeping my brother. He keeps writing to me as if I were still eleven. Tell him I am twenty-eight. I know what a Pride-Circlet is. — W."*

Kessa cross-referenced the moon-silver artifact register: the Pride-Circlet is Aelwen's fifth forging (inferred from the journal-page + apron-scrap diptych; three named + two inferred = five). Wena knows. Someone told her. That someone is most likely her order's patron — Lady Tiran.

**Brother Laen's follow-up letter** (two days later): Wena has been placed at the Korrath chapter-house partly through Tiran's patronage influence. *"I did not know this when I arranged the placement. I am asking you to go. If Wena is in danger, the Council cannot extract her without a diplomatic incident. But the Varduin Muster is not the Council."*

**Aelric's reason to go:** His Oath. Wena is 28, a novice, placed in a political arrangement she may not have consented to. The Solamnic Measure does not tolerate instrumentalizing the Revered Daughters. And privately: Aelric knows Wena's note was a signal, not a report. She named the artifact deliberately. She wants the party to come.

## Setting & Background

**Korrath-by-the-Sea** is a mid-sized Istaran port city, 11 days' ride south and east from Varduin. Lady Tiran's money built much of it — she endowed the harbor wall, the inn, the chapel, and the Revered Daughters chapter-house over forty years. The city holds Second House flagged ships in its harbor, luxury goods in its market, and harbor-facing estate of Lady Tiran herself.

**Glosses for new readers:**
- *The Revered Daughters of Paladine: an Order of Solamnic-adjacent clergy-women who serve Paladine through temple-construction and ritual care for the dying. They are not Solamnic knights; they are under no Measure. They have their own hierarchy.*
- *The Second House is one of Istar's old-money noble factions. They fund temples, buy influence, and maintain ships. Lady Tiran is their most visible Korrath representative.*
- *Istar's social hierarchy imposes station-rankings on all inhabitants. The Circlet enforces these rankings on the attuned bearer — including on a Solamnic knight.*

**Time:** Late Age of Might, PC 20. The Cataclysm is 20 years away; the Kingpriest is consolidating moral authority; the Second House is wealthy enough to buy silence.

### DM Notes

- **Korrath is a functioning Istaran port.** Second House wealth is visible at every corner. The inn charges three times Varduin rates. This is not a dungeon — it is a political city.
- **Tier 2 context.** Korrath has Istaran guards (CR ½; 10 on duty near Tiran's estate); Felor's household guards (1d4 + Felor himself); and Second House political leverage (arrest is possible if the party creates an incident). Combat is available; it is never the preferred path.
- **The chapter-house is neutral ground.** The Revered Daughters are apolitical in Istaran terms; Tiran's influence is financial, not legal, inside the convent walls.
- **v1.3 positional redundancy:** the Pride-Circlet appears in Tiran's reception (Scene 2, its case), is described by Wena (Scene 3), researched in the archive (Scene 5), and physically accessible in the vault (Scene 6). Multiple positions; multiple approach paths.

### Scene 2 — Lady Tiran's Estate, First Meeting

**Location:** Harbor-facing townhouse; reception hall. **Time:** mid-afternoon Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The reception hall is exactly what money kept immaculate for sixty years looks like. Marble floors from the Kharolis interior. A painting of Korrath harbor circa PC -30 on the west wall — the fishing-town version, before the Second House money arrived. On a low table near the window: a locked ebony case, fifteen inches across, lined in purple velvet. The case is closed. Lady Tiran sits in a high-backed chair facing it. She does not stand when the party enters. She gestures to the chairs across from her.

**Features:**

- **The ebony case (the Circlet's case).** Locked. **🎲 Investigation** — `d20+mod` → DC 13 to assess the lock: Felor-quality Istaran work, not casual. *This case is empty.* Tiran uses it as a visual anchor; the real Circlet is in the vault. **Insight DC 14:** she positioned the empty case deliberately — she does not trust the party with the Circlet in the room yet.
- **The harbor-painting.** **🎲 Kessa History** — `d20+mod` → DC 12: dates from approximately PC -28 — before the current Second House era. It shows a smaller harbor than today's. Tiran has framed what she built.
- **Felor** stands near the door. He offers tea. He pours it regardless of answer.
- **Empty case Investigation (DC 10):** the interior velvet is flat-cut, not shaped to a crown. The case is a placeholder. **DC 13 on Felor's reactions:** he becomes noticeably more alert when Kann is mentioned.

**Tiran's opening:**

*"Sir Aelric of the Crown. I have read your family history. Minor line, western Abanasinia. Your brother was a Knight of the Sword — an honor your line does not often achieve. You wear his armor."*

She waits. This is both a statement and a question: does Aelric confirm this?

Then: *"I have a commission. You may have come to prevent it. I am prepared to discuss both possibilities over tea."*

**Dialogue beats:**

- **On the Circlet:** she will name it, describe it accurately, and state her intention: *"I intend to see how Edric saw me. Once. Before I die."* She does not apologize for this.
- **On Felor/Kann connection:** if the party mentions Kann's finger-bone cord and Felor's name, Felor goes still. Tiran: *"Felor's methods are his own. I ask for results. I do not ask how."* **🎲 Aelric Insight** — `d20+mod` → DC 13: she knows, at some level, what commissioning Kann meant.
- **On Wena:** *"Wena Laen is my witness. I have asked her to be present for the attunement. She has not agreed. I am patient."*
- **Insight DC 14 on Tiran:** she is afraid of the Blank-Circlet-Trap, though she hasn't named it. She knows Edric admired her. She is not certain he admired her *most*. The uncertainty is the wound.

**Encounter:** None. Dialogue scene.

**Connections:** back to [1] market; ahead to [4] chapter-house (find Wena); or directly to [5] archive if Tiran allows.

**GM Notes:**
- Do not rush this scene. Tiran is the session's moral weight.
- The empty case is the scene's puzzle. If the party doesn't notice, Wena will confirm the Circlet is in the vault at Scene 3.
- Aelric's Solamnic register: two people who know exactly how much their titles mean and how much they don't.

**Memory Fragment:**
> *The painting of the fishing-town Korrath was commissioned by Tiran at thirty, when she had just rebuilt the harbor. She intended it as a record of what she had improved. She has since come to think of it as a record of what she replaced. She has not removed it. She is not sure whether keeping it is honest or self-indulgent. She has not asked anyone. The question feels too personal.*

**Manifest symptoms landed:**

### Scene 4 — The Joint Meeting (Tiran's Estate, Reception Hall)

**Location:** Tiran's reception hall. **Time:** Day 2 morning. **Setup:** party has requested a meeting with both Tiran and Wena present. Wena has agreed to come.

**Read-aloud:**

> Lady Tiran and Wena Laen in the same room have the particular texture of two people who have been carefully polite to each other for three months and are no longer pretending it is easy. Tiran sits in her chair. Wena stands near the window. Felor is at the door. The empty case is gone from the table.

**Features:**

- **The empty case is absent.** Tiran removed it before this meeting. If the party noticed it was empty in Scene 2, they understand why.
- **The dynamic.** Tiran and Wena are not enemies. They are two women with incompatible positions: Tiran wants a Paladine witness; Wena refuses to be one.
- **The leverage point.** If Kessa has the Mirror cross-reference from Scene 3: Tiran does not know the Mirror was destroyed. Not blackmail — a reality-check. *"Your network is outdated. The Mirror is gone. The Wrath-Coin is gone. The Pride-Circlet is the last artifact you can acquire. Is that why you are in a hurry?"*

**Key scene beats:**

**Tiran addresses Wena directly:**
*"You have brought the party here to witness your refusal. I want to understand: is your refusal theological or personal?"*

Wena: *"Both. I am a novice. I cannot be a Paladine-representative for something I think Paladine would refuse to sanction."*

Tiran: *"Paladine sanctioned sixty years of my donations to your Order."*

Wena: *"That is not the same thing."*

**The real question under the meeting:** Tiran does not want to see her finest self alone. She wants a witness. Someone who will remember, after she is gone, that she was seen as she deserved to be seen. This is not a theological question. It is a grief question.

**If the party names this explicitly:** Tiran will be silent for a long moment. Then: *"That is correct."* Then nothing else.

**Wena's offer (if party navigates well):** *"I can witness you. I cannot witness an attunement I believe will harm you. But I can be present when you tell someone who loves you what you are most proud of. That is not the Circlet. That is just talking."*

Tiran, quiet: *"Edric is dead."*

Wena: *"Then tell me. I am here."*

**This is the golden-path pivot.** If Tiran accepts Wena's offer, the attunement is no longer necessary. The session shifts from artifact-retrieval to what Lady Tiran actually needs.

**Investigation beats:**
- **🎲 Insight DC 13** on Tiran listening to Wena: she is surprised. She did not anticipate Wena's offer.
- **🎲 Insight DC 15** on Felor: he is calculating whether Wena's offer is legally advantageous for Second House (it keeps the Circlet unattuned; clean for the archive).

**Encounter:** None. The session's moral fulcrum.

**GM Notes:**
- This is the session's most important scene. If the party navigates it well, the Circlet question becomes downstream. If they miss the real question — Tiran does not want the Circlet; she wants to be witnessed — Scene 5 will be harder.
- Aelric's role: create space for Tiran and Wena to say what they need to say. If he leads with the Measure, it complicates. If he is patient, the scene resolves.
- Wena's offer is character-consistent: she is a novice in a healing order; she has been witnessing Tiran for three months without the Circlet.

**Memory Fragment:**
> *The window in Tiran's reception hall faces the harbor. She has looked at it for forty years. She has seen it once at dawn with Edric beside her, the first winter they lived here, when neither of them had built enough to know whether it would hold. The harbor held. She does not often think of that morning. She thinks of it now, with Wena Laen at the window in his place.*

- Wena's offer as moral pivot (anchor-level narrative moment; all PCs witness)
- The empty case's absence confirming the Circlet is elsewhere

### Scene 5 — Tiran's Archive (Estate Third Floor)

**Location:** Tiran's private archive. **Time:** Day 2 afternoon. **Access:** Tiran grants access after Scene 4, or Thera leads a stealth approach (Investigation DC 13 to find unlocked window route; Stealth DC 14 to enter without Felor noticing).

**Read-aloud:**

> The archive is the size of a small library. Three walls of shelves. A reading table with a scholar's lamp. And a smell: old paper, candlewax, and — Kessa will notice — a faint metallic trace that Vaenshold-lineage silver leaves in a room where it has been stored for decades. The Circlet has been here. Its case is here: a silver-chased ebony box, properly shaped, on a dedicated shelf. The case is empty. The Circlet is in the vault through the door to the left.

**Features:**

- **The archive's collection.** Forty years of Second House correspondence, maps, land titles, and Tiran's personal research collection. **🎲 Investigation DC 10** finds the six-forging manifest in under a minute — it is on the reading table, the last thing Tiran read before the party arrived.
- **The Circlet's case.** Silver-chased ebony; interior purple velvet; **shaped precisely to a narrow band.** The case is empty. **🎲 Kessa Arcana DC 10:** the case's silver chasing is Vaenshold-vein work, same as the Korrath market pendants.
- **The vault door.** Locked. **🎲 Investigation DC 13** to assess: high-grade Istaran lock; unique key; not pickable under 10 minutes. Thera's Thieves' Tools DC 16 for faster access. Tiran can unlock it; Felor has a copy.
- **Insight DC 12 on the archive's organization:** Tiran organized this herself; the scholar's lamp has been refilled recently. She has been spending her dying months in this room.
- **Tiran's pencil annotation** on the six-forging manifest, next to the Circlet entry: *"Would E. have wanted this for me?"* Written six months ago. Unanswered.

## Treasures

### The Pride-Circlet

*A narrow band of moon-silver, engraved with the Vaenshold half-bloomed rose repeated as a crown-of-laurel. Lighter than it looks. Heavier each time you wear it.*

**Appearance (player-facing):** Two inches wide; moon-silver band; circumference fits most adult heads — Aelwen sized this one to herself, made it last. The engraving is of the half-bloomed-rose-opening pattern from the Mirror's handle, but here repeated eleven times around the band in a laurel-crown arrangement. The interior face is polished to full convergence. The exterior face is left rough — Aelwen's tool-marks visible. She did not finish the outside. She did not think the outside mattered.

When placed on bare skin (head): cool for two or three seconds, then warms to body temperature. On second wearing it warms immediately. On third wearing it has always been warm.

**Rarity:** Very Rare. Attunement required. No kin prerequisite. **Fast-attune: one wearing** — placing the band on the head and looking into any reflective surface. The attunement completes at the moment of reflection; it does not require intention. First-time bearers often do not realize they are attuned until the curse's first effect appears.

**Provenance:** Aelwen forged the Circlet in PC 179, after the Mirror and before the unnamed sixth forging. She made it for herself, then could not use it, then gave it to Heva Aelwensdaughter with the instruction: *"Empty it. Break it. Do not let them hold what is not theirs."* Heva could not empty or break it. Sold PC 175 to an Istaran scholar; changed hands seven times in forty years; worn once by Tiran at a market in PC -18 — for thirty seconds, then put back. Tiran has held it for fifty years without wearing it again.

**Presence / Desire:** It wants to be worn again. Not compulsion — desire. The mirror interior pulls attention the way a window pulls attention in a dark room. Aelwen's workshop notes: *"The Coin wanted to be held. The Mirror wanted to be lifted. This one wants to be placed. I think it reaches for the place behind the eyes where we keep our best version of ourselves."*

---

#### Properties (mechanical)

**What the bearer sees on first wearing:** their own face as the person who *admires them most* sees it — not love (that is the Mirror's domain), but admiration: the respect that comes from seeing competence and honor. The admiration-face is the bearer's finest version — curated, not idealized. The reflection lasts as long as the bearer can hold the wearing; most first-time bearers hold 5-10 seconds before the image overwhelms.

**Benefits (attuned):**
- **Social standing within shared institutions.** Advantage on Persuasion and Intimidation checks against creatures who share the bearer's institutional allegiance (Solamnic Order, Revered Daughters, same merchant guild, same noble house).
- **Self-projection (1/long rest).** Name a specific person who admires the bearer and see themselves as that person's current view. Dead persons' views are frozen at moment of death.
- **Steadfastness.** Immune to the Frightened condition in social contexts (courts, temples, public markets). Physical combat is not a social context.

---

#### Curse (attuned): Pride-Lock

**Self-correction failure.** When the bearer would acknowledge a mistake or personal limitation *aloud*, make a DC 13 Wisdom save. On fail: they rationalize instead (*"That was a choice, not an error"*). This is not charm — the bearer is not controlled; they are genuinely failing to perceive the failure for what it is.

**Social hierarchy imposition.** The bearer cannot easily accept correction, advice, or leadership from persons of lower social station by Istaran reckoning (which the Circlet imposes regardless of location). In the Varduin Muster: Thera and Kessa are lower-station; Grom is ambiguous (dwarf-priest; clergy-ranked but dwarf). Aelric would override Thera's tactical suggestions and dismiss Kessa's research until corrected by someone of equal or higher station.

**The curse accumulates:**
- Week 1: self-correction save + station imposition (above)
- Week 2: disadvantage on Insight checks (cannot read social failures in others)
- Week 3: any public failure triggers the Charmed-by-reflection condition (see below) for 1d4 hours

---

#### The Blank-Circlet Trap (anchor-level)

If the bearer's most-admirer is dead AND the admiration has not been replaced (the Circlet judges current emotional landscape, not the calendar): the reflection shows the finest-self endlessly. The bearer cannot look away for **10 rounds** (Wisdom save DC 15 per round after the first, or remain transfixed). If they do not break concentration by Round 10: **Charmed by their own reflection** for 1d4 hours — incapacitated, effectively catatonic, unreachable except by physical contact.

**This is Aelric's specific trap.** Varran's memories were consumed by the Reliquary in S02. Varran was likely Aelric's most-admirer (his older brother; the Knight of the Sword who trained him; the one whose armor he wears). Without Varran's memory, the Circlet may find no anchor — or it may find Varran's pre-death admiration still present in the emotional record, show it, and then Aelric will see a face his admirer could no longer identify.

**For Lady Tiran:** Edric died eight years ago. His admiration was present at the moment of death. The Circlet will show his view — the finest version of Tiran as Edric last saw her. This is not blank. This is the hardest gift possible.

---

#### PC-specific hooks

- **Aelric + Varran.** If Aelric attunes: Varran's memories consumed by the Reliquary. Whether Varran's admiration-trace still exists in Aelric's emotional ledger is uncertain. The Circlet will know; Aelric will discover the answer in the reflection. **Blank-Circlet-Trap candidate.** Option C mandatory.
- **Kessa + Vethrenn.** Admiration is a different question from love. Did Vethrenn admire the person Kessa would become? If so, the Circlet shows a face that makes sense only if Vethrenn believed in her. If not: blank.
- **Thera + Adda.** Adda's admiration was for eight-year-old Thera. The Circlet shows Thera how Adda's most-admired version of her daughter — which is the child's version, not the adult. Heavy; not blank.
- **Grom + Duren.** Duren's admiration deepened into fatherhood (S04 confirmed). The Circlet would show Grom the forge-priest Duren saw in him. Grom can look at this without danger. He might even laugh.

---

#### Ending the curse

1. **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** forge ≥ 1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Circlet destroyed; attunement ends; self-image returns to baseline. The admiration-memory of the reflection remains — the bearer still remembers seeing themselves as admired. That is permanent.
2. **Reciprocal Recognition:** the bearer looks into a reflective surface while wearing the Circlet; the person who most admires them stands present and names one specific failure of the bearer aloud — specifically, accurately, without anger. The bearer must hear it without the save (they may waive the save voluntarily; the waiver must be sincere). On success: attunement ends; Pride-Lock releases. On failure: nothing; try again later.

The gap becomes invisible. That is not the same as closing it. I am sorry. — A."*

### Felor of the Second House of Istar, Lady Tiran's Factotum

**Appearance:** Fifty-one, human, compact and efficient. Hair cut practical-short; Second House signet on left index finger (not thumb; not titled). Always carries a writing kit; pen behind one ear. When he enters a room, he scans for exits before he scans for people.

**Background:** Twenty-two years in Second House service; Lady Tiran's factotum for six. He commissioned Kann via the finger-bone cord to acquire any moon-silver artifact before the cell went quiet. He did not know Kann's expedition would end in failure and death. He has good information networks and suspects the timing puts the Varduin Muster at Kann's death — but bears no personal animus. Kann was a vendor, not an ally.

**Voice and manner:** Clerical precision. Numbers, timelines, sourcing. Addresses Aelric as *"Knight"* — exactly that, no name. Will not be manipulated via social pressure; he calculates the cost of every concession in advance.

**What he wants:** (1) Tiran to get what she wants, within budget and without legal exposure. (2) Party to leave quickly and quietly. (3) Not to be implicated in anything that could affect his post-Tiran employment.

**Secrets:**
- He has already contacted three other buyers for the Pride-Circlet in case Tiran dies before using it.
- He did not tell Tiran about the Mirror's destruction. He cannot predict whether knowing would make her more or less reasonable.
- He is afraid of the Circlet. He held it once; put it down before the reflection resolved. He has not told anyone.

**Stat block (CR 1, 200 XP):**

> **AC** 14 (studded leather under formal wear) · **HP** 38 (7d8+7) · **Speed** 30 ft

### Attunement Rules (inline — SRD + Pride-Circlet-specific)

**Standard SRD:** short rest (1 hr) to attune; max 3 attuned items; curse is revealed on attunement.

**Pride-Circlet fast-attune:** one wearing + reflection = complete. Does not require intention. First wearers often do not realize they are attuned until the curse fires.

**Pride-Lock (curse):** DC 13 Wis save whenever bearer would acknowledge mistake aloud. Fail = rationalization, not admission. Station-imposition on lower-Istaran-station creatures (Thera, Kessa = lower; Grom = ambiguous). Accumulates: Week 2 adds Insight disadvantage; Week 3 adds public-failure → Charmed-by-reflection.

**Blank-Circlet-Trap:** most-admirer dead + admiration not replaced → 10 rounds forced concentration (Wis DC 15 per round after first) → Charmed by own reflection 1d4 hours if Round 10 fails. **Aelric is the primary candidate.**

**Ending attunement:**
1. Reorx-shattering rite (Grom): forge ≥ 1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Circlet destroyed.
2. Reciprocal Recognition: bearer wears Circlet + most-admirer present + names one specific failure of bearer aloud + bearer accepts without the save (voluntary waiver). Attunement ends; curse releases.

---

### Conditions Used (inline — SRD)

- **Frightened:** disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is visible; can't willingly move closer to the source.
- **Charmed:** can't attack the charmer; charmer has advantage on social interaction ability checks against the charmed creature. For Blank-Circlet: the "charmer" is the bearer's own reflection — bearer is incapacitated, unreachable by anything except physical contact.
- **Overwhelmed (water-bowl variant, non-SRD):** disadvantage on all ability checks for 1 minute. Triggered by Wis save DC 12 failure after using water bowl with Circlet.
