# Lady Tiran of the Purple Rose, 68, of the Second House of Istar

## Appearance

Sixty-eight years old, Istaran-human, once tall — now slightly diminished by the lung ailment she will not name. Silver hair kept in the elaborate Istaran court-braid that went out of fashion fifteen years ago; she wears it anyway. Always in purple — the deep, dye-costly purple of Second House formal wear. A signet on her right thumb: the purple rose of her house, moon-silver-set (the setting is Vaenshold work; she commissioned it thirty years ago from a trader who got it from a Kharolis market). Her breathing is audible in quiet rooms. She does not apologize for it.

## Background

Born PC -48 to a merchant family that bought its way into Second House standing when Tiran was twelve. She has spent fifty-six years making that purchase irreversible — through political marriages, through Council philanthropy, through the Revered Daughters patronage she endowed at twenty-five, and through the careful cultivation of a reputation as *the woman who kept her word.*

She has kept her word. Every political alliance she made, she honored. Every donation she pledged, she delivered. Her word is the only currency she has left, and she knows the lung ailment will end her account within a year. She is not afraid of death. She is afraid of being forgotten.

**She discovered Aelwen's artifacts via Keloth's cell.** Three years before S01, Keloth's Bone-Collector network was operating as a semi-legal antiquities brokerage for Istaran faction-buyers. Tiran commissioned a manifest of all available moon-silver objects. Keloth's manifest named five: the Silver Rose (unavailable; Solamnic archive), the Reliquary (Nerakan; considered too dangerous), the Mirror (with Mira Vaenshold; price negotiations ongoing), the Wrath-Coin (with a rogue Thorbardin Thane; unavailable), and the Pride-Circlet (location unknown; suspected Istaran-market provenance). She paid for the manifest. She never got the Mirror or the Wrath-Coin. The Pride-Circlet was the only one her people found.

**Felor of Second House** is her factotum, not her relative. He handles acquisitions; she handles decisions. He is the name on the finger-bone cord.

**She does not know Keloth is dead.** She knows the cell went quiet. She attributed it to Solamnic interference (she is not wrong).

## Voice & manner

- Formal Istaran register; titles always; never a first name unless offered.
- Speaks in complete sentences, never fragments. Precision is her armor.
- Will not be hurried. The lung makes her pace measured; she has turned this into authority.
- Addresses Aelric specifically as *"Sir Aelric of the Crown"* after the first meeting — a studied recognition of exact rank that is simultaneously polite and diminishing (she knows his is a junior line; she has read his family history).
- On the Pride-Circlet: calls it *"the appropriate instrument."* Not a weapon. Not a curse. *An instrument.*

## What she wants

- **Primary:** attune the Pride-Circlet before she dies. Hear the Circlet's reflection of her finest self. Know, for certain, that someone saw her that way.
- **Secondary:** have the party leave Wena alone and carry news back to Laen that Wena is safe and well-placed.
- **Tertiary:** have the Circlet formally transferred to Second House archive upon her death, so it can be *her artifact* in the historical record.
- **Not wanted:** to be corrected, pitied, or offered alternative consolations. She has heard them.

## Stat block (CR 2, 450 XP — Istaran noble with political resources; non-combatant preferred)

- **AC** 12 (court robes) · **HP** 45 (6d8+12) · **Speed** 30 ft (25 in later scenes as ailment worsens)
- **Str** 8 · **Dex** 12 · **Con** 14 · **Int** 18 · **Wis** 15 · **Cha** 18
- **Saves (proficient):** Intelligence +7, Charisma +7
- **Skills:** Deception +9, History +7, Insight +5, Persuasion +9
- **Senses:** passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Istaran, Solamnic (reads only)

**Actions:**
- **Voice of the Second House (action, 1/short rest):** Tiran speaks a formal accusation or command. Target creature Wis save DC 15 or is Frightened of Tiran for 1 minute (social-combat context only; does not apply in physical combat). **Solamnic knights** have advantage on this save (their Measure protects against social coercion).
- **Summon Felor (bonus action):** Felor arrives within 1d4 rounds if within the estate. He brings 1d4 household guards (CR ⅛).

**No combat statistics beyond the above.** If threatened physically, Tiran will not fight; she will negotiate or retreat.

## Secrets

- **The Circlet was with her for six years before she understood what it was.** She wore it once at twenty-two, at a ball, before she knew it was Aelwen's work. She saw her finest self for thirty seconds and put it back in its case and has not touched it since. She knew, from the first wearing, that the second wearing would be permanent.
- **She has already named who will see her finest self when she looks.** Her late husband Edric, who died of the same ailment eight years ago. She does not know whether his admiration can still be *received* by the Circlet if he is dead. She has not asked anyone this question. She is afraid of the answer.
- **She has drafted a letter to Wena** — not a command, a request. She planned to give it to Wena directly. She has not delivered it. The letter asks Wena to witness the attunement. As a representative of Paladine. Tiran wants a priest present.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 2 (Tiran's estate, first meeting):** formal; she receives the party as guests; offers tea; does not pretend to be surprised they have come. She is direct about the Circlet: she has it, she wants to use it, she sees no reason to hide this. She will answer questions for a price: their discretion.
- **Scene 4 (Tiran and Wena's joint meeting):** Tiran's plan is exposed (Wena is not playing along; she had reservations she did not express to Tiran). This scene is where the alliance between party and Wena begins.
- **Scene 5 (confrontation or negotiation):** per party's approach. If combat: Felor intervenes. If negotiation: Tiran's price is the Circlet's use, witnessed, with the party ensuring it is recorded in Second House archive. She will not forgo the attunement.
- **Scene 6 (the Circlet's moment):** if Tiran attunes, she hears Edric's view of her — his last-known view. He died still admiring her. The Circlet shows her the finest version. She weeps for thirty seconds. Then the curse begins — she cannot hear correction from anyone.
- **Scene 7 (aftermath):** per outcome. Tiran in her final configuration (attuned / unattuned / Circlet destroyed).

## Post-session outcomes

- **Tiran attuned:** curse active; she will spend her final months in Pride-Lock; she will die admired, in her own estimation. Campaign-permanent. Not a happy ending but not a failure.
- **Tiran unattuned (negotiated):** she did not hear Edric's admiration; she will not, in this life. This is the hardest truth the adventure can give her.
- **Circlet destroyed:** Tiran watches it burn. Says: *"I suppose that means I must be remembered the way people choose to remember me."* Pauses. *"That will have to do."*
- **Circlet surrendered to Second House archive (unattuned):** political resolution; Tiran names it her bequest; it enters the archive as an Aelwen artifact with provenance. The campaign must account for it in #0007 finale.

## GM Notes

- **Do not play Tiran as villainous.** She is dying and afraid of being forgotten. This is a human condition. The party's job is to navigate her choices, not defeat her.
- **The Solamnic register.** Aelric addressing Tiran formally (*"My Lady Tiran"*) while holding the Measure against her ambition is the session's political wire. He must respect her station while refusing her request. Both can be true.
- **Wena is not Tiran's captive.** She is Tiran's chosen witness. This distinction matters.
