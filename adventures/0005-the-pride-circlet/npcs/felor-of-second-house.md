# Felor of the Second House of Istar, Lady Tiran's Factotum

## Appearance

Fifty-one, human, Istaran-born. A compact, efficient man — not small, but the kind of person who takes up exactly the space he intends. Hair cut practical-short; an Istaran Second House signet on his left index finger (not thumb; he is not titled). Carries a writing kit. Always has a pen behind one ear. When he enters a room, he scans for exits before he scans for people.

## Background

Felor has served the Second House for twenty-two years, initially as a clerk, then as a factor, then as Lady Tiran's factotum when her previous one died six years ago. He is not her relative; he is her instrument. He does not love her or resent her; he respects her precision and is aware that his standing dies with her commission.

**The finger-bone cord** — Kann carried Felor's name on the cord of his Stonfold finger-bone (identified by Kessa via Identify ritual after S03 Scene 5). Felor commissioned Kann to acquire *any* moon-silver artifact from Keloth's network before the cell went quiet. He did not know Kann's expedition to Crystalmir would end in failure and Kann's death. He has been fielding Tiran's questions about the Mirror for eighteen months and has nothing to show for it.

**He knows the party killed Kann.** He does not know this for certain, but he has good information networks and the timing fits. He bears no personal animus — Kann was a vendor, not an ally. But he notes it.

## Voice & manner

- Clerical precision. Numbers, timelines, sourcing. He speaks the way a well-kept ledger reads.
- Addresses Aelric as *"Knight"* — exactly that, no name.
- Will not be manipulated via social pressure; he calculates the cost of every concession in advance.
- Calm in dangerous situations. He has been in dangerous situations before.

## What he wants

- **Primary:** Lady Tiran to get what she wants, within budget and without legal exposure.
- **Secondary:** the party to leave Korrath-by-the-Sea quickly and quietly.
- **Tertiary:** to not be implicated in anything that could affect his post-Tiran employment prospects.

## Stat block (CR 1, 200 XP — factotum; can fight but prefers not to)

- **AC** 14 (studded leather under formal wear) · **HP** 38 (7d8+7) · **Speed** 30 ft
- **Str** 11 · **Dex** 15 · **Con** 12 · **Int** 16 · **Wis** 14 · **Cha** 13
- **Skills:** Deception +5, History +5, Insight +4, Investigation +5, Persuasion +5
- **Senses:** passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Istaran
- **Multiattack:** 2 shortsword attacks
- **Shortsword:** +4, 1d6+2 piercing. Carries a knife; does not advertise it.
- **Ledger-Tongue (action, 3/day):** Felor quotes a specific legal precedent or financial consequence. Target makes Int save DC 14 or becomes Uncertain (can't take reactions and has disadvantage on attack rolls for 1 round) as they process the implication.

## Secrets

- **He has already contacted three other buyers** for the Pride-Circlet in case Tiran dies before she uses it. He will not let the artifact disappear into a Solamnic archive.
- **He did not tell Tiran about the Mirror's destruction.** She does not know Aelwen's Mirror is gone. If she knew, she would understand the Circlet is *the last chance* — and would be either more or less reasonable; Felor cannot predict which.
- **He is afraid of the Circlet.** He has held it once. He is not a vain man; he put it down before the reflection resolved. He has not told anyone he touched it.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 2:** greets the party at the estate gate; polite, correct, slightly ahead of them in every exchange. Informs them of the meeting time with Lady Tiran.
- **Scene 5:** intercedes if combat breaks out; his household guards (1d4 CR ⅛ veterans) appear on his signal.
- **Scene 7:** if Circlet is destroyed, Felor will assess whether legal action is possible. If Tiran is attuned, he will ensure the archive transfer goes smoothly. If Circlet is taken by party: he will file a formal grievance through Second House channels and contact Brother Laen's superior.

## GM Notes

- **Felor is not the villain.** He is the bureaucrat of someone else's desire. He is very good at his job.
- **The finger-bone cord connection** (from S03/S04) is the party's leverage. Kessa knows his name is on the cord; he commissioned Kann. This puts Felor in legal jeopardy if the party names Kann's mission publicly. Play him aware of this risk without naming it first.
