# Wena Laen, 28, Novice of the Revered Daughters of Paladine

## Appearance

Twenty-eight years old, human, Laen-family dark hair kept in the simple novice's braid. Taller than Brother Laen by two inches; broader through the shoulders. The novice's white robe fits her well enough except at the arms — she is a better carpenter than she is a seamstress, and she has made minor repairs with the wrong thread twice. Her hands are capable hands. Her expression when she first sees Aelric is: *relieved, and slightly annoyed that it took this long.*

## Background

Brother Laen's younger sister by twelve years. Grew up in Varduin-by-the-Pines watching her brother negotiate the politics of Crown-knight clerkwork with increasing distaste. Joined the Revered Daughters at eighteen — not because she was pious, but because the Order of Paladine was one of the few institutions in Abanasinia that would train a woman in both theology and practical construction (the Daughters build the temples they serve in). She took her novice vows at twenty-two and has been taking the long path to full sisterhood ever since.

**She was placed at Korrath-by-the-Sea** through a combination of Brother Laen's Council connections and Lady Tiran's patronage. Laen wanted her closer to the Istaran chapter-house for reasons he has not shared. Tiran wanted a novice with Solamnic-adjacent family connections who could serve as a witness with legal standing.

**She has known about the Pride-Circlet for four months.** Tiran showed it to her — not as a secret, but as an object she was asking Wena to witness the use of. Wena said she needed to think about it. She has been thinking about it for four months. Her note to Aelric is the conclusion of that thinking: *she wants the party to come so she doesn't have to make this decision alone.*

## Voice & manner

- Blunt where her brother is careful. She did not inherit the diplomatic gene.
- Addresses Aelric by name immediately — *"Aelric"*, not *"Sir Aelric"* — because the note was addressed to him and she has been waiting for him specifically.
- Does not pretend to defer to the Sisters in front of the party. She will defer in the convent; she will not perform deference in conversation.
- Uses the Varduin hill-vernacular on occasion (*"right then"*, *"fair enough"*), which she picked up from the town before she left it. She does not notice herself doing this.

## What she wants

- **Primary:** the party to handle the Circlet question without her having to be the one to refuse Tiran. She asked them to come; she is asking them to decide. She will not tell them what to do, but she will be grateful when they do it.
- **Secondary:** to go back to Varduin eventually. She does not say this. She has not said this to anyone. The Daughters are a good Order. She misses home.
- **Tertiary:** Tiran to be treated with respect. Wena has been her guest for three months. Tiran is not cruel. She is dying and wrong and those two things should be acknowledged separately.

## Stat block (Novice; non-combatant)

- **AC** 11 · **HP** 18 (4d6+4) · **Speed** 30 ft
- **Str** 12 · **Dex** 11 · **Con** 12 · **Int** 14 · **Wis** 15 · **Cha** 13
- **Skills:** History +4, Insight +4, Medicine +4, Persuasion +3, Religion +4 (plus carpentry proficiency — no stat block expression, narrative only)
- **Senses:** passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Solamnic
- **Novice's Blessing (1/day):** Wena may cast Bless as a 1st-level cleric (three targets, 1 min, concentration). She only uses this if the party is in genuine danger.

## Secrets

- **She has read everything Tiran has compiled on Aelwen's six forgings.** Tiran has a researcher's collection — better than anything the party has seen except the S03 apron-scrap diptych. Wena knows about the Mirror, the Reliquary, the Rose, the Wrath-Coin, and the Circlet. She does not know about the sixth — Tiran's notes say *"one unnamed, possibly shame-related."* This is the closest Hint 4 has come to articulation yet.
- **She wrote her note to Aelric specifically** because she read Tiran's file on the Varduin Muster. Tiran has a dossier. It is flattering to Aelric and accurate about Kessa and politely dismissive of Thera and Grom. Wena found this annoying.
- **She has already decided** she will not witness the attunement. She has not told Tiran this. She has been waiting for someone to arrive who would make the refusal less lonely.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 1 (Korrath arrival):** Wena is not at the convent gate; a senior sister says she is in the workshop. She is installing a door hinge in the chapter-house library. She stops when she sees Aelric, looks at him for three seconds, then says: *"You came. Good. I was starting to think I would have to write a third letter."*
- **Scene 3 (Wena debrief):** Wena tells the party what she knows. She gives them Tiran's compiled research (her gift; she was given a copy for reference). She explains the attunement-witness request. She explains her refusal. She asks: *"What do you intend to do?"*
- **Scene 4 (Tiran joint meeting):** Wena is present when the party meets Tiran formally. She is quiet during the meeting. She is watching Aelric.
- **Scene 6 (the Circlet's moment):** Wena is present whether invited or not. She did not come this far to leave the room.
- **Scene 7 (aftermath):** Wena's response per outcome. If Circlet destroyed: *"Good."* If Tiran attuned: she remains near Tiran for the remainder; doesn't leave until she knows the curse has settled. If Circlet unattuned: she touches Tiran's shoulder once and says nothing.

## GM Notes

- **Wena is the moral anchor, not the moral authority.** She knows what she thinks is right; she has been waiting for help doing it. Do not make her either helpless or in control.
- **Her knowledge of the six forgings** is the session's lore-reward. She knows more than any NPC the party has met. She will share it if asked directly. She does not volunteer it because she assumes the party already knows.
- **The Varduin connection.** She has never been to the party's Varduin home. But she grew up twelve years after Laen in the same village. She knows Brother Laen's mother's name. She knows things about the party's home that the party may not. Use this.
