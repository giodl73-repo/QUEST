# The Pride-Circlet

*A narrow band of moon-silver, engraved with the Vaenshold half-bloomed rose repeated as a crown-of-laurel. Lighter than it looks. Heavier each time you wear it.*

## Appearance

Two inches wide; moon-silver band; circumference fits most adult heads (Aelwen sized this one to herself — she made it last). The engraving is of the half-bloomed-rose-*opening* pattern Aelwen used on the Mirror's handle, but here repeated eleven times around the band in a laurel-crown arrangement. The interior face of the band is polished to full convergence, like the Wrath-Coin. The exterior face is left intentionally rough — Aelwen's tool-marks visible. She did not finish the outside. She did not think the outside mattered.

When placed on bare skin (head), it is cool for two or three seconds, then warms to body temperature. On second wearing it warms immediately. On third wearing it has always been warm.

## Presence / Desire

**It wants to be worn again.** This is not charm or compulsion — it is described as *desire*, not manipulation. The mirror face (interior) pulls attention the way a window pulls attention in a dark room. Aelwen's workshop notes (not yet recovered by the party, but Tiran's research hints at it): *"The Coin wanted to be held. The Mirror wanted to be lifted. This one wants to be placed. I do not know what it is reaching for. I think it reaches for the place behind the eyes where we keep our best version of ourselves."*

## Rarity

**Very Rare.** Attunement required. No kin prerequisite. Fast-attune: **one wearing** (placing the band on one's head and looking into any reflective surface while wearing it). The attunement completes at the moment of reflection — it does not require intention.

## Provenance

Aelwen forged the Circlet in **PC 179** — after the Mirror (PC 180) and before the unnamed sixth forging. Her notes on the Circlet are sparse; she did not commission it for a client; she made it for herself, then could not use it, then gave it to Heva Aelwensdaughter with the same instruction she gave everything: *"Empty it. Break it. Do not let them hold what is not theirs."*

Heva could not empty it (she did not know how) and could not break it (she tried; moon-silver refuses). Heva sold it in PC 175 to a traveling scholar from Istar for what was then a modest sum. The Circlet entered the Istaran antiquities market and stayed there, changing hands seven times in forty years, never worn by anyone who understood what it was until Tiran held it at a market in PC -18 and put it on once, briefly, and put it back.

**Tiran has held it for fifty years.** She has not worn it again.

## Properties (mechanical)

### Attunement

**One wearing + reflection = attunement complete.** The bearer does not need to know this; the Circlet does not announce it. First-time wearers often do not realize they are attuned until they notice the curse's first effect.

**What the bearer sees on first wearing:** their own face as the person who *admires them most* sees it. Not love (that is the Mirror's domain); admiration — the respect that comes from seeing competence and honor. The reflection lasts as long as the bearer can hold the wearing; most first-time bearers hold for 5-10 seconds before the image is too much.

The admiration-face is the bearer's finest version — not idealized beyond recognition, but *curated*: the version that exists in the specific mind of the specific person who admires them most. If that admirer has complex feelings, the Circlet shows the *peak* of those feelings, not the average.

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Social standing within shared institutions.** Advantage on Persuasion and Intimidation checks against creatures who share the bearer's institutional allegiance (Solamnic Order, Revered Daughters, same merchant guild, same noble house). The Circlet amplifies the bearer's claim to their own excellence within their own hierarchy.
- **Self-projection (1/long rest).** The bearer may name a specific person who admires them and see themselves as that person sees them — their current view, not necessarily positive. Dead persons' views are frozen at moment of death.
- **Steadfastness.** The bearer is immune to the Frightened condition in social contexts (courts, temples, public markets). Physical combat is not a social context.

### Curse (while attuned): Pride-Lock

**Self-correction failure.** When the bearer would acknowledge a mistake or personal limitation *aloud*, they must make a DC 13 Wisdom save. On fail: they rationalize instead (*"That was a choice, not an error"* or equivalent). This is not charm — the bearer is not controlled; they are genuinely failing to perceive the failure for what it is. The perception is altered, not the will.

**Social hierarchy imposition.** The bearer cannot easily accept correction, advice, or leadership from persons of lower social station (by Istaran reckoning, which the Circlet imposes regardless of where you are). This manifests as irritation, dismissal, or selective deafness — not violence, not compulsion. In the Varduin Muster: Thera and Kessa are technically lower-station; Grom is ambiguous (dwarf-priest; clergy-ranked, but dwarf). Aelric would find himself over-riding Thera's tactical suggestions and dismissing Kessa's research notes until corrected by someone of equal or higher station.

**The curse accumulates.** First week: the above. Second week: disadvantage on Insight checks (cannot read social failures in others). Third week: the bearer's social self-perception becomes load-bearing — any public failure triggers the Charmed-by-reflection condition (see below) for 1d4 hours.

### The Blank-Circlet Trap (anchor-level)

**If the bearer's most-admirer is dead AND the admiration has not been replaced** (the Circlet judges current emotional landscape, not the calendar): the reflection shows the finest-self endlessly. The bearer cannot look away for 10 rounds (Wisdom save DC 15 per round after the first, or remain transfixed). If they do not break concentration by Round 10, they become **Charmed by their own reflection** for 1d4 hours — incapacitated, effectively catatonic, unreachable except by physical contact.

**This is Aelric's specific trap.** Varran's memories were consumed by the Reliquary in S02. Varran was likely Aelric's most-admirer (his older brother; the Knight of the Sword who trained him; the one whose armor he wears). Without Varran's memory, the Circlet may find no one to anchor to — or worse, it may find Varran's pre-death admiration still present in the emotional record, show it, and then Aelric will see a face his admirer could no longer identify.

**For Lady Tiran:** Edric died eight years ago. His admiration was present at the moment of death. The Circlet will show his view — the finest version of Tiran as Edric last saw her. This is not blank. This is the hardest gift possible.

### Ending the curse

1. **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** forge ≥ 1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Circlet destroyed. Attunement ends. Self-perception returns to baseline. Admiration-memory of the reflection does not fade — the bearer still remembers seeing themselves as admired. That is permanent.
2. **Reciprocal Recognition:** the bearer looks into a reflective surface while wearing the Circlet; the person who most admires them stands present and names one specific failure of the bearer aloud — specifically, accurately, without anger. The bearer must hear it without the save (they can waive the save voluntarily; the waiver must be sincere). On success: attunement ends; the Pride-Lock releases. On failure (bearer cannot accept the correction): nothing; the Circlet remains. Try again later.

## Hooks (PC-specific)

- **Aelric + Varran.** If Aelric attunes: Varran's memories were consumed by the Reliquary. Whether Varran's admiration-trace still exists in Aelric's emotional ledger is uncertain. The Circlet will know; Aelric will discover the answer in the reflection. **Blank-Circlet-Trap candidate.** Option C mandatory if attunement attempted.
- **Kessa + Vethrenn.** Vethrenn never met Kessa; the nature of Vethrenn's relationship to Kessa is "transmission, not love" (per the Mirror's S03 near-attuning). *Admiration* is a different question — did Vethrenn admire the person Kessa would become? The Circlet might show Kessa a face that makes sense only if Vethrenn believed in her. If so, it is a gift. If not, blank.
- **Thera + Adda.** Adda saw eight-year-old Thera. Her admiration was for that child. The Circlet would show Thera how Adda's most-admired version of her daughter — which is the child's version, not the adult. Heavy; not blank.
- **Grom + Duren.** Duren's admiration for Grom deepened into fatherhood (S04 confirmed). Duren's admiration is not in question; the Circlet would show Grom the forge-priest Duren saw in him. Grom can look at this without danger. He might even laugh.

## Aelwen's note on the Circlet (not yet found; in Tiran's archive)

> *"I made this for the people who cannot see themselves whole. I do not mean modestly. I mean the people who live in the gap between what they know they are capable of and what they have been allowed to be. I made it to show them the gap is smaller than they think. I made it wrong. The gap becomes invisible. That is not the same as closing it. I am sorry. — A."*
