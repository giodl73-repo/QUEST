# Premise — The Pride-Circlet

**Campaign position:** #0005 of 7 in the **Moon-Silver Cycle**. Emotion: **Pride**. PC spotlight: **Aelric of Crownhold** (Oath meets political ambition).

**Logline:** A dying Istaran noblewoman is commissioning moon-silver artifacts to preserve her name past death — and the Revered Daughter novice she has chosen as her instrument is Wena Laen, Brother Laen's sister, who knows exactly what a Pride-Circlet is.

## Setting

**Place:** **Korrath-by-the-Sea** — a mid-sized Istaran port city, 11 days' ride south and east from Varduin. The city holds a chapter-house of the **Revered Daughters of Paladine** (novices and senior sisters both), a Palanthas-era merchant quarter, and the harbor estate of **Lady Tiran of the Purple Rose** — an aging Istaran noble of the Second House, dying of a lung ailment she refuses to name.

**Time:** Late Age of Might, **PC 20** (approximately three weeks after S04). The Cataclysm is 20 years away; Istar's Kingpriest is beginning to consolidate moral authority; the Second House is wealthy enough to buy silence.

## Hook

**Wena Laen's note** (received at Varduin via Brother Laen's courier at S04 Scene 7):

> *"Thank you for keeping my brother. He keeps writing to me as if I were still eleven. Tell him I am twenty-eight. I know what a Pride-Circlet is. — W."*

The note is sealed and addressed to Aelric. It arrived with Laen's courier along with the protection-payment for S03. Kessa cross-referenced the moon-silver artifact register and confirmed: the Pride-Circlet is Aelwen's fifth forging (inferred from the journal-page + apron-scrap diptych; three named + two inferred = five). **Wena knows.** That means someone told her. That someone is most likely her order's patron — Lady Tiran.

**Brother Laen's follow-up letter** (delivered two days later) explains: Wena has been placed at the Korrath-by-the-Sea chapter-house at Council arrangement. Lady Tiran is a patron of that chapter-house. Wena's placement was brokered partly through Tiran's influence. *"I did not know this when I arranged the placement. I am asking you to go. If Wena is in danger, the Council cannot extract her without a diplomatic incident. But the Varduin Muster is not the Council."*

**Aelric's reason to go:** His Oath. Wena is 28, a novice, placed in a political arrangement she may not have consented to. The Solamnic Measure does not tolerate instrumentalizing the Revered Daughters. And — privately — Aelric knows that Wena's note was a signal, not a report. She named the artifact deliberately. She wants the party to come.

## Central Artifact

**The Pride-Circlet** — a narrow moon-silver headband, engraved with a laurel of the Vaenshold half-bloomed rose pattern. Aelwen's fifth forging, made in PC 179 after the Mirror and before the unnamed sixth. She did not name it in the journals; she called it *"the one I made for those who cannot see themselves whole."*

**Effect on wearing:** the wearer sees their own face as the person who *admires* them most sees it. Not love (that is the Mirror); admiration. The face they see is their finest self — the version that would make their order, their family, their name, proud. First-time wearers stay in the reflection for longer than Mirror-attuners: 5-10 seconds before the image overwhelms.

**Presence / Desire:** the Circlet is lightweight on first touch and becomes heavier with each wearing. Aelwen's workshop notes: *"It wants to be worn again. It is the only one that wants."*

**Rarity:** Very Rare · attunement required · no kin prerequisite.

**Benefits (attuned):**
- Advantage on Persuasion and Intimidation checks against creatures who share the bearer's institutional allegiance (Order, faction, family line).
- Once per long rest: the bearer may name a specific person who admires them and see themselves as that person sees them. For persons who have revoked their admiration or died in disappointment, the mirror shows *only the finest version* — it cannot show a loss.
- **Passive:** the bearer is immune to Frightened condition while in public-facing contexts (courts, temples, markets).

**Curse (attuned): Pride-Lock.**
The bearer cannot recognize their own failures while attuned. All self-doubt is muted; all self-criticism is rejected as error. When a PC would acknowledge a mistake or limitation aloud, they must make a DC 13 Wisdom save. On fail, they instead rationalize: *"That was not error — that was a choice."* This is not charm; it is self-image rewriting.

Additionally: **the bearer cannot accept any correction from someone of lower social station** (per Istaran reckoning, which the Circlet imposes). Thera and Kessa are lower-station by Istaran standards; Aelric would stop deferring to them on decisions. Grom is a dwarf-priest; he is lower-station but spiritually-ranked; the Circlet is uncertain about him.

**The Blank-Circlet Trap:** if the bearer's most-admirer has died or has publicly revoked their admiration, the Circlet shows the idealized self endlessly — the bearer cannot look away for 1 minute (concentration, 10 rounds). If they do not break concentration (Wisdom save DC 15 per round after the first), they become Charmed by their own reflection for 1d4 hours. Non-restorable without Reorx-shattering or reciprocal-recognition.

**Ending:**
1. **Reorx-shattering rite** (Grom) at forge ≥ 1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Circlet destroyed; curse ends; self-image returns.
2. **Reciprocal Recognition:** the bearer looks in the Circlet's reflection while the person who most admires them looks at them simultaneously and names one specific failure of the bearer aloud. The admission is accepted. Circlet un-attunes; curse ends. This requires an admirer present and willing to name a fault.

## Themes

- Pride is not the same as confidence. Confidence can be corrected; Pride cannot hear the correction.
- The Solamnic Measure requires the knight to hold their honor in both directions: toward others AND toward themselves. The Pride-Circlet gives a knight the first without the second.
- Lady Tiran has spent sixty years building her name and cannot imagine leaving without it intact. She wants to *die admired*. That is not shameful. The Circlet is the wrong tool for it.
- Wena Laen is 28 and knows what she was placed in. She is not helpless; she is waiting.

## The Real Decisions

Four overlapping:

1. **Does Aelric attune the Pride-Circlet?** Spotlight-PC at highest attunement-risk: his grief-paragraph names *the Order's honor* as his second priority; the Circlet amplifies exactly that. The Blank-Circlet-Trap for Aelric: his most-admirer is probably Varran — **who died, whose memories Aelric no longer has.** If Aelric's most-admirer is now undefined (the Varran-memories are gone; Doriel never met him; no one else is established), the Circlet goes **blank.** Option C strongly recommended.
2. **Is Lady Tiran an antagonist or a dying woman who needs a different tool?** The party may choose to prevent her attunement by force, or negotiate, or — the hardest path — help her find a way to be remembered that doesn't require the Circlet.
3. **Does Wena leave the convent?** She is not imprisoned; she is placed. But her placement is politically entangled. If the party extracts her, Brother Laen's Council relationship with the Second House deteriorates.
4. **Where does the Circlet end up?** Attuned (Aelric or Lady Tiran), destroyed (Grom's rite), surrendered to the Revered Daughters, or left with Wena.

## Spotlight Notes — Aelric

Aelric's S04 shadow: he invoked Solamnic kin-witness protocol to help convict Krun. He knelt when he fumbled initiative and it worked better than a swing would have. He carries Wena's note in his breast-pouch. He has not read it a third time.

**The Blank-Circlet-Trap is tailored to Aelric specifically:**
- His most-admirer is Varran (dead; memories gone).
- If the Circlet reads "no one you remember admires you most," it goes blank.
- He would see his finest self for 10 rounds of forced concentration.
- This is the adventure's most dangerous attunement — not because of the curse, but because of what the blank would confirm: that the Varran-attuning in S02 cost him not just memories but also the Circlet's ability to see him clearly.

**If Aelric does NOT attune:** his choice to decline, and why he declines, is the session's character arc. The Measure says: *"hold your honor in both directions."* A knight who cannot look clearly at himself cannot hold honor. The Pride-Circlet is not the Measure's tool.

## Expected outcome paths

- **Golden:** Aelric declines; Lady Tiran agrees to be memorialized through the Sisters' oral tradition (Wena's suggestion); Circlet destroyed or surrendered unattuned; Wena stays but on her own terms.
- **Typical:** Aelric declines; Tiran resists; negotiated resolution; Circlet in party inventory unattuned.
- **Dark:** Aelric attunes; Blank-Circlet fires (Varran is dead/forgotten); Aelric is Charmed by own reflection for 1d4 hours during the session. Option C spawn; binding.
