# Serrak

**Role:** Mountain farmer. Injured bystander. Not an informant — just a man who knows what he saw.

## Stat block

Commoner (MM p.345): AC 10, HP 4 (wounded). No combat capability.

## Emotional position

Serrak has lived in these mountains for thirty years. He has watched border patrols come and go. He pays his taxes. He moves his sheep. He does not involve himself. He is sitting against a rock with a military crossbow bolt in his side and he is deciding whether involving himself is unavoidable now.

He trusts Lenne before he trusts anyone else in the unit because she approaches first and she asks about the wound before she asks anything else. That order matters.

## Arc-completion conditions

Serrak is not a major arc character. His arc-completion is small: he tells Lenne one true thing without being asked a second time. When he does, he says: *"I don't know what they were doing. I know they weren't patrol. Patrol has never come from the east in thirty years."*

That is the thing. East is the wrong direction for any legitimate military operation on this route.

## What he knows

- The men came from the east, at night.
- They had military crossbows.
- They were arguing — two voices. He heard the word "Veth" but did not know what it meant.
- He did not see their faces.

## Voice

Short sentences. Suspicious pauses. He speaks like a man who has learned that saying more than necessary is how trouble finds you.
