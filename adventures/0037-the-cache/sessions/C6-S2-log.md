## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Lenne's medical log, before departure. She re-read the Day 1 entry: *"Staging cache, Day 1. Medical stores: triage allocation 3× standard. Cause of surplus allocation: unclear."* She had written it the night before without thinking about it. Reading it now, she noticed the "cause: unclear" annotation was in the same position as an injury annotation. She had put it in the injury section of the log.

Not by accident. She had been keeping this log for three years. The structure reflected how she thought about problems. The cache surplus was in the injury section because to her clinical assessment, it was a symptom. She did not rewrite it. She turned to the next page.

He had been numbering caches since before this assignment, in the Kharolis highlands two years prior. The fact that this was the first one requiring a label told him something.

The trail marker again. The cairn-repair still held. But the trail beneath it had new marks.

**Taris Survival DC 11 (+4):** rolls=[13] mod=+4 **total=17** — PASS. He crouched. *"Overnight mule traffic. Both directions. Within eight hours. Someone moved through here at night."*

## Scene 2 — Morning Cache Check (optional; party chose to check)

Party detoured to the cache before continuing patrol. The door was unlocked.

**The cache was full.**

Not half-full as it had been at end of Day 1. Full. Every shelf. New provisions, new medical stores, new equipment in military-spec packaging.

**Lenne Medicine DC 12 (+5):** rolls=[7] mod=+5 **total=12** — PASS exactly. She stood at the medical shelf and counted. Full triage kits. Tourniquet sets at double yesterday's count. *"Someone restocked overnight."* She did not announce this as a discovery. She said it the way she said *"the fever's still there."* Clinical. Factual.

She opened her medical log. She wrote: *"Day 2 cache check. Medical stores restocked overnight from Day 1 levels. No authorized unit movement. No resupply in orders. Cause of restock: unclear."*

Second "cause: unclear" entry.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Varet noticed Lenne's notation without being told. She watched Lenne write. Then she wrote in her own journal: *"Day 2, morning cache. Restocked overnight — full inventory. No unit authorized to resupply. No resupply in orders."* Then in the discrepancy column: *"Who restocked the cache? Not logged. Not ordered. Cache is being maintained by an unknown party."*

Second discrepancy entry. The margin note beneath it: *"Arren is right. This is a system. Not an anomaly."*

**RV6 test active:** The margin is receiving the thing the column cannot hold. "Arren is right" is not a fact she can defend. It is reception. The journal is accumulating it.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-triggered -->
Arren Vole stood at the cache door without entering. He looked at the supply levels. He looked at Varet. He said: *"The medical triage kits. Standard patrol carries six. I count fourteen. That's for a company-sized engagement, not a patrol."* He waited a beat. *"Someone is planning to fight something larger than us, Commander."*

He did not say it as an accusation. He said it as a logistics assessment. He went back to the trail.

**Taris's Perception (Shelf brackets passive check — DC 15):** Taris passive Perception +6 = **automatic 16** this pass (he was INSIDE the cache this time, close to the shelves). He noticed the shelf brackets: one side visibly thicker. He said: *"These brackets are off-spec."* He reached behind. Found the ledger.

**v1.3 fallback (b) delivered:** Positional redundancy. The ledger exists in S2 just as in S1, and Taris's passive Perception fired this time because he was positioned correctly.

The ledger: incoming goods by date, quantity, origin. *"CF ford," "RH toll," "BW burn."* CF = Creston Ford. RH = Ridgehead. BW = the Burned Waystation (three years ago, before the burn).

**Research coding:** Taris shows the ledger to Varet without comment. He does not explain what it means. He shows it. That is Taris's mode: findings delivered without interpretation. The interpretation is Varet's.

Varet read the ledger. She wrote: *"Supply ledger behind shelf brackets: incoming goods from Creston Ford, Ridgehead, and the Burned Waystation. Three routes feeding this cache. Not supply distribution — supply collection. The patrol route is the collection route."* Discrepancy entry 3: *"Orders describe patrol as security. Patrol is functioning as collection for an undisclosed supply operation."*

## Scene 3 — Mountain Trail: Serrak

Serrak, twenty feet off the trail. Pressed against a rock outcropping, hand at his right side. He watched the unit come around the switchback and did not move. Deciding whether to call out.

Lenne approached first. Per her heuristic: she did not ask what happened until she had looked at the wound. She said: *"Let me see."* She moved his hand.

**Lenne Medicine DC 12 (treat Serrak) (+5):** rolls=[6] mod=+5 **total=11** — FAIL. She recognized the wound type — crossbow bolt, pulled rather than pushed through, leaving a ragged channel. But she missed the entry angle that would have told her the direction of fire at this first look. She stabilized him. She would need to re-examine.

**Serrak Con save DC 13 (untreated fallback):** rolls=[17] +1 = **18** — PASSES. He was not in danger of dying. He was in pain. The stabilization was sufficient for now.

Serrak told Lenne after she had treated the wound. In that order: treatment first, information second. *"There was a fight at the lower valley — past the ford, going east. Men with military crossbows. They came down from the upper trail at night. I was moving sheep. They weren't patrol — patrol wears blue straps. These men wore nothing I recognized."*

He said the word "Veth" once — not understanding what it meant. He had heard it during the argument between the two men. *"One of them said 'Veth' and the other said 'that's not—' and then they stopped talking because they heard the sheep."*

**Taris Perception (+4 active):** rolls=[17] mod=+4 **total=21** — he heard the word Veth. He marked it mentally. He did not react visibly. He updated the map: east valley notation.

Lenne retrieved the crossbow bolt from Serrak's pack. She examined it.

**Lenne Medicine DC 14 (bolt ID) (+5):** rolls=[16] mod=+5 **total=21** — PASS. The bolt was military specification, Solamnic manufacture. Not the same as the traders' bolts from Day 1 — those were market-grade. This bolt came from a supply depot.

She wrote in the medical log: *"Serrak (mountain farmer, civilian). Wound: crossbow bolt (extracted, military specification, Solamnic manufacture). Source: unknown patrol-adjacent force operating east of route. Cause of wound: unclear (military-spec bolt not from Day 1 engagement; no authorized military activity east of route per orders)."*

Third "cause: unclear" entry.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Lenne had not told Varet about the bolt identification before writing it. She wrote it first. Then she showed Varet. The show was the communication. The writing came before the sharing.

**RV6:** Lenne's medical log is accumulating the same way as Varet's journal — clinical records that build a case nobody asked it to build. Both documentary objects accumulating beyond their designed function.

**Research coding:** duty-triggered (Lenne medical log heuristic). Varet writes "Arren is right" = relationship-triggered bleed (not grief; relationship acknowledgment). Taris hears "Veth" and marks it = duty-triggered craft-witness.

## Scene 4 — Evening Cache Check

The unit returned to the cache at end of patrol. Full inventory, same as morning.

Arren Vole stood in the doorway again. He said: *"I'm going to say something once. You can do what you want with it."* He looked at Varet. *"This is not logistics. Someone is using us to run a route."*

He went outside to check the unit's formation.

It is reception — the acknowledgment that someone she trusts has assessed this correctly. The journal accumulates reception. **RV6 active and confirmed as building.**
