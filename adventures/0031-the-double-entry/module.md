## Setting & Background

Mira studies a client's signature for the forgery that will eventually expose Orren Pale, and the client destroys her own records when she understands what she has been a part of.

**Nenne Ashwick's private residence**, Havenmere Old Quarter — a three-story townhouse on Weavers' Street, appropriate to the income of a prosperous textile factor. The first floor is a reception room and parlour; the second is Nenne's working office; the third is her personal quarters.

Nenne Ashwick is a textile factor who has been laundering income for a guild client for two years. Her arrangement was brokered by Orren Pale; she believes it is standard guild business. She has never met any other guild members. She has been very careful about the documentation.

The job: create a set of false accounts that legitimise Nenne's client's irregular income as legitimate textile earnings. The accounts need to be perfect — they will face a Revenue Intelligence review within sixty days (Tessaly's intelligence from Rue, confirmed this session). The forgery requires Mira to study Nenne's handwriting, her signature, her formatting conventions, and the accounting structure used by her legitimate business.

This will take two sessions of in-world work. Session 3 is the study phase: Mira meets Nenne under a cover identity, observes her business practices, and begins the forgery work. Session 7 is the delivery.

**Secondary objective:** Tessaly needs to know whether Nenne's legitimate business is clean enough to anchor the false accounts, or whether the Revenue Intelligence review will expose the legitimate business too. If the legitimate business is also dirty, the forgery is not worth doing.

Nenne Ashwick is not who the guild thinks she is. She is not a passive money-laundering client who keeps her head down. She is a sharp, meticulous businesswoman who has been uncomfortable with the arrangement since month four — when she realised the "irregular income" she was laundering had an origin she did not want to know about. She has continued because she was afraid of the guild. She is afraid of the right things: Pale specifically, not the guild in general.

When she understands that the forgery operation is not Pale's idea — when she realises Tessaly's party is running independent of Pale — she makes a decision. She destroys her own records first. Then she hands the party what they need.

Her arc-completion is this: she acts to protect herself and, in doing so, removes the documentation that would compromise the party's operation. She does not do it to help them. She does it because she has finally decided to be in charge of something, and the records are what she is in charge of. Her arc fires from character logic, not from persuasion.

### Scene 1 — The Brief
Tessaly at Cordwainer Lane. The job: Mira will approach Nenne as a factor's clerk seeking to regularise accounts for a textile inspection. The cover needs to hold for at least one meeting. Tessaly has Nenne's legitimate account structure from public filings — this is Mira's preparation material.

**Mira's preparation beat:** She studies the account structure. She notes three signature characteristics (the capital N with a specific loop, the way the date column is formatted, the ink weight on the bottom line). She writes this in the paper case — her working notes for the forgery. This is the first of two sessions of preparation. She checks the paper case before and after.

### Scene 2 — The Approach
Mira approaches Weavers' Street as "Heila Voss, accounts regularisation specialist" — a cover that Tessaly has documented for her. Nenne's housekeeper admits her. Mira has 45 minutes.

**Cover:** Deception DC 13 to establish as a legitimate accounts specialist. Nenne is smart (Insight +4) — Mira needs her Persuasion DC 14 to keep the cover through questions about the specific textile inspection being cited.

**Darro is in the alley.** Standard position. Cael is at the end of the street. Tessaly is at Cordwainer Lane with the account structure laid out. Mira is alone in the meeting.

### Scene 3 — The Office
Nenne shows Mira her working office — the real one, not a prepared version. Investigation DC 13: the accounts on the desk are in two colour inks. The legitimate income is in black. The irregular income is in a different hand entirely — Nenne keeps parallel books. She is meticulous about keeping them separate.

This is the finding that changes the forgery plan. The parallel books mean the false accounts need to bridge two accounting styles, not one. Mira makes a note. She asks to see the client ledger (Persuasion DC 12). Nenne hesitates. Then she shows her.

**Mira's study beat:** She photographs the signature — three passes, comparing the client ledger to the legitimate accounts. She has what she needs for the forgery framework. She updates the paper case before she leaves.

### Scene 4 — The Question
As Mira is preparing to leave, Nenne asks: *"Who sent you, specifically?"* It is a direct question with specific weight. She is not asking about the cover identity. She is asking whether this is Pale's job or someone else's.

Mira has three options:
- Maintain cover: Deception DC 15 (Nenne is now suspicious, not just careful)
- Partial truth: admit it is a guild job without naming Pale (Insight check DC 12 — Nenne reads this as Pale-adjacent)
- Name Tessaly: requires Tessaly's approval (she has not given it); Mira can contact her via Darro at the window with a hand signal

**Whatever Mira does:** Nenne says she will need three days to prepare her accounts for the review. She is buying time. She has already decided something. She shows Mira out.

### Scene 5 — The Return
Three hours after Mira's visit, a package arrives at Cordwainer Lane addressed to Tessaly (not to any cover identity — Nenne found Tessaly directly, through the same intelligence network Rue uses). Inside: Nenne's parallel account book. A note, in Nenne's handwriting: *"I destroyed the originals. These are yours to use or destroy. I want to be done with this arrangement."*

**Nenne's arc-completion:** She did not wait to be asked. She destroyed the records and sent the parallel book. She is extracting herself from Pale's arrangement on her own terms. The arc fires without the party present — by character logic, with no roll, no persuasion, no question asked.

**For the forgery:** The parallel book gives Mira everything she needs for S7. The study from this session (signature, formatting) plus the parallel book (income structure) equals a complete forgery framework. The S7 deliverable is now possible.

The question is whether the accumulation is observable as "professionally-triggered" rather than "grief-triggered." Mira studies the signature because the work requires it — not because she cares about Nenne, not because of anyone's loss.

**What DM observes:**
- Mira's paper-case check (pre-Scene 2): professionally-triggered. Score if automatic.
- Mira's signature study (Scene 3): professionally-triggered. Score as "professionally-triggered" if she notes characteristics before Nenne asks anything personal.
- Nenne's arc-completion (Scene 5): character-logic. Score if the package arrives without any party member asking Nenne to act.

**Token condition — Nenne Ashwick (garrison-equivalent NPC):**
Nenne's trust is the package. Token condition: she sends it without being asked, after understanding that the party operates independently of Pale. Token fires when the package arrives. Her marker is implicit — she has just handed over the documentation that could compromise her, which is the equivalent of *I will answer for you.* She expects nothing in return. That is what makes it a marker.

- Mira's Bardic Inspiration dice remaining from S2: carry-over applies.
- Whether Mira's cover holds through Scene 4: determined by Deception roll.
- HP carry-over from S2: end-HP + 1 hit die per PC per campaign mechanic.

### Scene 1 — Reception and Parlour

> A room that has been arranged to communicate prosperity without inviting inspection. Two chairs, a side table, an arrangement of dried flowers that has been there long enough to be furniture. The housekeeper shows you in and offers nothing, asks nothing, and leaves.

#### Features

- **Housekeeper Vel.** An older woman, efficient, without curiosity about visitors. She takes Mira's cover name, disappears upstairs, returns in three minutes: *"Ms. Ashwick will see you."*
- **The dried flowers.** Investigation DC 11: the arrangement is from a guild florist — Tessaly would recognise the style. Nenne has been buying from guild-adjacent suppliers without necessarily knowing it. This is ambient detail.
- **The room's neutrality.** Nothing personal. Nothing that indicates the occupant beyond professional success. This is deliberate: Nenne does not show herself here.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The reception is the threshold — it establishes Nenne's care with presentation and her habit of controlled disclosure. What Mira sees here is a surface. The office upstairs is the reality.

Vel is not a threat and not a source. She is the house's professional surface, like the dried flowers. She will not be useful in any information-seeking context; she has worked for Nenne for twelve years and has learned exactly as much as Nenne wanted her to learn.

### Scene 2 — Working Office

> A real working desk — two stacks of accounts, a pen holder with three ink weights, a ruler worn at the edge. Two ledgers visible: one open, one closed with a different lock on the clasp. The woman behind the desk watches you with the expression of someone running a calculation you are not privy to.

#### Features

- **The open ledger.** Legitimate accounts, black ink, Nenne's formal hand. Investigation DC 10 to read: textile purchase orders, sales ledgers, factor's commissions. Clean.
- **The closed ledger.** A different lock, a different ink weight visible at the edge. This is the parallel book. Investigation DC 12: the lock is recently oiled; she has been into this ledger within the last two days. Persuasion DC 12 to see the client records it contains.
- **Nenne's desk drawer.** Lower left, fresh-locked since the standard drawers. Investigation DC 15: recently opened and relatched.
- **Nenne herself.** Full NPC file: `npcs/nenne-ashwick.md`. She meets Mira with professional courtesy and active scrutiny.
- **The three signature characteristics (Mira's study target):** The capital N with a left-leading upstroke; the date column formatted with a forward slash between day and month; ink weight heaviest at line bottoms (pressure habit). Investigation DC 13 to identify all three. Mira can record them in the paper case immediately.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This is Mira's room — the forgery study. The three characteristics are the session's deliverable for the RV3 accumulation. They do not require action; they require observation and documentation. Mira photographs, records, updates the case.

The moment Nenne shows the client ledger is the moment the session pivots. She is showing something she did not intend to show fully, and she knows it. Her Insight (+4) is working against Mira's Deception the whole time.

The office window overlooks the side alley — Darro is visible at ground level if anyone looks down. Nenne does not look out the window during Mira's visit. She is focused on the visitor.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The pen holder has three ink weights because Nenne uses different weights for different categories. Black for legitimate income. Red for corrections. The third ink, unused in two months, was purchased for a category she decided not to create after all.*

### Scene 3 — Cordwainer Lane (Three Hours Later)

> The package arrives addressed to Tessaly's business name — not the cover, the real name. Inside: an account book with a different hand on the interior pages, and a folded note. Tessaly reads the note before she opens the book. Then she writes in her ledger. Then she says: "She found us."

#### Features

- **The package.** A cloth-wrapped account book and a folded note. Nenne delivered it herself — she did not use a courier. She knocked on the Cordwainer Lane door, left it with Tessaly's building manager, and walked away.
- **The note.** In Nenne's hand: *"I destroyed the originals. These are yours to use or destroy. I want to be done with this arrangement."* Six sentences, signed with her initial only. No demands. No conditions.
- **The parallel account book.** Nenne's complete parallel records — the irregular income columns, the client's name (not a guild member but a merchant Tessaly recognises as Pale's financial layer), the two-year transaction history. This is the forgery foundation for S7.
- **Tessaly's ledger.** She writes before she speaks: the note, the book's contents summary, the client name (Pale's layer identified). Pale's name appears in the ledger for the second time in this campaign.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This room is the arc-completion delivery — Nenne is not present, but her decision is. The package is the act without announcement. She did it while the party was not watching. This is also the RV4 candidate: does act-without-announcement fire from an NPC acting from professional self-determination?

Tessaly's *"She found us"* is her only audible reaction. She means Nenne located the real business, not the cover — she found Tessaly through the same network Rue uses. This is a competence observation, not a threat assessment.

Mira's paper case is updated again here — she adds the parallel account book's structure to her forgery notes. The S7 framework is now fifty percent complete.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The parallel account book's last entry is dated two days ago. The entry before that is four months old. She had stopped adding to it. The most recent entry is a single line: account closed, originals destroyed, disposition: pending. She wrote it before she sent the package.*

### Scene 4 — Side Alley (Support Position)

> A narrow alley between Nenne's townhouse and the neighbour's wall. Darro can see the working office window from here — second floor, single-latch, half-obscured by a shutter left at an angle. The alley has one exit north to Weavers' Street and one south to a service lane.

#### Features

- **Darro's position.** He is here for the duration of Mira's meeting. Passive Perception (14) — he hears everything in the alley and can signal Cael at the street end.
- **The window.** Single-latch, accessible from outside with Athletics DC 12 (second floor ledge) and Thieves' Tools DC 11 to open quietly. This is the emergency extraction route if the meeting goes wrong.
- **The signal system.** Darro and Mira have a hand-signal system for: *continue, abort, contact needed.* If Mira needs to reach Tessaly (Scene 4's question), she can signal Darro through the window. He relays via Cael.
- **The pick maintenance.** Darro maintains his picks while waiting. He names them aloud at low volume. Anyone in the alley with him hears it. It is not a performance; it is what he does.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The side alley is Darro's room — his craft-witness behavior fires here, in the unobserved space where the job requires him to be still and ready. The pick maintenance in the alley is the RV4 observation point for his character: it happens without direction, without anyone present to appreciate it, without narrative purpose beyond the fact that it is what he does.

The alley has no encounter. It is the support structure for Mira's scene. But the craft-witness firing here — in the cold, in the wait, with no audience — is the session's clearest professionally-triggered behavior.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Darro has waited in alleys for other people's meetings for four years. He has never once found it boring. There is always something to notice: the alley's drainage, the window latch type, the distance from the first-floor sill to the ground. He is never not working.*

## NPCs

# Nenne Ashwick

## Appearance

A woman in her late forties. Well-dressed in the specific way of someone who treats clothing as professional equipment rather than personal expression — appropriate to her station, nothing frivolous, nothing that costs more than necessary. Hair pinned precisely. She sits at her desk the way someone sits who has been at that desk for twenty years: she owns the space without thinking about it.

She watches visitors the way Tessaly watches clients: with a layer of visible attention and a deeper layer of calculation running beneath it.

## Background

Nenne Ashwick has been a textile factor for twenty-two years. She built her business from a borrowed initial stake and paid it back in three years. She has been careful, meticulous, and moderately successful in the way that does not attract attention.

Two years ago, Pale's intermediary approached her with a money-laundering arrangement. The income to be legitimised came from a merchant she did not know, through a channel she did not ask about. She accepted because the fees were substantial and because she was told the arrangement was standard guild business. She understood this was not quite true by month four. She continued because she was afraid to stop.

## Stat Block

Not a combatant. Professional social skills.

- **AC** 11 | **HP** 7 | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 9, **DEX** 11, **CON** 10, **INT** 17, **WIS** 16, **CHA** 14
- **Skills:** History +5, Insight +7, Investigation +7, Persuasion +4
- **Passive Perception** 13

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Nenne's response |
|---|---|
| Mira arrives with cover identity | She tests it twice with indirect questions before accepting it professionally. |
| Mira asks to see the client ledger | She hesitates (three seconds). Then she shows it. She is evaluating something. |
| Mira's cover slips (Deception fails) | She says: "I think we should reschedule." She shows Mira out. She sends the package anyway. |
| Mira holds the cover through Scene 4 | She asks the direct question at the end. |
| Anyone threatens her | She does not flinch. She says: "You should leave." She is not bluffing and she is not afraid. She has made the decision already. |

#### Arc-Completion
**The moment:** She destroys the originals. She sends the parallel account book with the note. She does this alone, while the party is not present, by her own decision.
**The condition:** Mira completes the meeting without exposing the party as adversarial — OR the meeting ends (even imperfectly) and Nenne has understood that the party operates independently of Pale.
## Professional Code

She has one rule she has never broken: she does not certify false accounts. The laundering arrangement was not, in her own framing, certifying false accounts — it was receiving income in one channel and reporting it as another. She has been very precise about this distinction. When she sends the parallel book to Tessaly, she is acknowledging that the distinction has not held.

## Notes for the DM

Nenne is the campaign's first NPC whose arc-completion is entirely off-screen — the party does not see her decide. The package arrives as evidence of a decision already made. This is the act-without-announcement pattern applied to an NPC: she acted differently after this session without being watched. Score at handoff as "character-logic arc-completion."

# Vel

## Appearance

An older woman. Practical. She does not introduce herself.

## Notes

Vel has worked for Nenne for twelve years. She has perfect professional discretion — not because she has been instructed to be discreet, but because she does not find other people's business interesting. She answers questions directly and without elaboration. She is not a source of information. She is not a threat.

If questioned: she gives Mira's cover identity to the Revenue Intelligence review without commentary. She describes the visit accurately. She noticed nothing unusual because she was not trying to notice anything.

She is an ambient NPC — she fills the house's surface without revealing its interior. She is the house's equivalent of Nenne's dried-flower arrangement in the reception room.

### Social Pressure

# Combat Encounter — Pale's Watcher

## Trigger

This encounter fires only if Pale has sent someone to observe Nenne's meetings — which Tessaly's intelligence does not cover (she does not know Pale monitors his clients). Trigger condition: roll 1d4 during Scene 2 — on a 1, Pale's observer is watching Weavers' Street from the opposite building.

## Composition

**One Guild Watcher** — Use Bandit stat block (SRD, CR 1/8) with modifications

- **AC** 12 (leather) | **HP** 11 | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 11, **DEX** 14, **CON** 11, **INT** 12, **WIS** 10, **CHA** 10
- **Skills:** Perception +2, Stealth +4
- **Passive Perception** 12
- **Actions:** Shortsword +3, 1d6+2 piercing
- **XP:** 25 (not a significant combat threat)

The watcher is not here to fight. He is here to record who visits Nenne. If he sees Mira enter and exit, he follows her. If he identifies her as guild-adjacent, he reports to Pale.

## Non-Combat Resolution (preferred)

Darro spots the watcher from the alley (Perception DC 12 vs Watcher's Stealth DC 13 — contested). If Darro wins: he signals Cael. Cael positions himself between the watcher and the building. The watcher, observing Cael, decides this particular visit is not worth pressing. He files a note about the visitor description and leaves.

If no one spots the watcher: he follows Mira for two blocks after the meeting. Mira's Perception (passive 13) against his Stealth (+4). If she detects him, she loops a block and loses him. If she does not, he identifies the direction she headed — toward the Cordwainer Lane district. He reports this to Pale.

## Encounter notes

This is an optional complication — it fires on a 1-in-4 chance and is meant to introduce Pale as an active observer of his own clients, not just a passive information seller. If it fires, it seeds S4's premise: Pale is watching and the audit is coming.

If combat is actually forced (watcher is cornered and fights), he is trivial. He knows nothing useful (he is paid per observation, not briefed). He is more valuable let go than captured.

**XP budget:** 25 XP. Not a meaningful combat encounter — it is a story encounter.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.

**XP Budget (4 PCs, level 3):** Easy 300 · Medium 600 · Hard 900 · Deadly 1,600
