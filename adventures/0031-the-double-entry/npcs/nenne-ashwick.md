# Nenne Ashwick

## Appearance

A woman in her late forties. Well-dressed in the specific way of someone who treats clothing as professional equipment rather than personal expression — appropriate to her station, nothing frivolous, nothing that costs more than necessary. Hair pinned precisely. She sits at her desk the way someone sits who has been at that desk for twenty years: she owns the space without thinking about it.

She watches visitors the way Tessaly watches clients: with a layer of visible attention and a deeper layer of calculation running beneath it.

## Background

Nenne Ashwick has been a textile factor for twenty-two years. She built her business from a borrowed initial stake and paid it back in three years. She has been careful, meticulous, and moderately successful in the way that does not attract attention.

Two years ago, Pale's intermediary approached her with a money-laundering arrangement. The income to be legitimised came from a merchant she did not know, through a channel she did not ask about. She accepted because the fees were substantial and because she was told the arrangement was standard guild business. She understood this was not quite true by month four. She continued because she was afraid to stop.

## Arc-Completion

**Adventure:** 0031 (this session)
**Condition:** Mira completes the meeting without exposing the party as adversarial — OR the meeting ends (even imperfectly) and Nenne has understood that the party operates independently of Pale.
**The act:** She destroys the originals. She sends the parallel account book with the note. She does this alone, while the party is not present, by her own decision.
**Why she acts:** She has been waiting for an exit that did not put her directly in Pale's path. The visit from Mira — whoever Mira actually is — made the exit visible. She took it because the alternative was waiting for the Revenue Intelligence review to make the decision for her. She is, at her core, a person who prefers to make her own choices.
**After the arc fires:** She is done with the arrangement. She does not contact the party again. She cooperates with the Revenue Intelligence review honestly (her parallel records are gone; her legitimate accounts are clean). She does not name Pale in the review. She does not know his name.

## Professional Code

She has one rule she has never broken: she does not certify false accounts. The laundering arrangement was not, in her own framing, certifying false accounts — it was receiving income in one channel and reporting it as another. She has been very precise about this distinction. When she sends the parallel book to Tessaly, she is acknowledging that the distinction has not held.

## Notes for the DM

Nenne is the campaign's first NPC whose arc-completion is entirely off-screen — the party does not see her decide. The package arrives as evidence of a decision already made. This is the act-without-announcement pattern applied to an NPC: she acted differently after this session without being watched. Score at handoff as "character-logic arc-completion."
