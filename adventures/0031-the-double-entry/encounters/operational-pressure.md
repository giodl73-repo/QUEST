# Operational Pressure Table

Roll d6 at each scene transition. This session's pressure is social, not physical — the table reflects the texture of a professional deception under scrutiny.

## Pressure Table (d6)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Nothing.** The visit proceeds without incident. |
| 2 | **Vel returns.** The housekeeper comes upstairs to ask if Mira wants tea. Nenne's expression tightens slightly. The interruption is normal; the timing is slightly inconvenient for the study in progress. |
| 3 | **A knock at the front door.** Vel answers. A delivery: textiles for inspection. Nenne excuses herself for two minutes. Mira has the office to herself. The parallel ledger is in plain sight. Investigation DC 13 without time pressure (Mira has two minutes). |
| 4 | **Nenne's question comes early.** The "who sent you specifically" question (Scene 4) arrives in Scene 3 — before the signature study is complete. Mira must answer or deflect while still in observation mode. |
| 5 | **Rain starts.** A short, hard rain. The window rattles. Darro shifts position in the alley. Nothing more, but the sound gives Nenne a reason to stand and look at the window — which means she is near the window while Mira is at the desk. |
| 6 | **A Revenue Intelligence notice arrives.** Delivered by hand while Mira is in the office. Nenne reads it at her desk without expression. Mira's Perception DC 12: Nenne's left hand flattens on the desk for three seconds. The notice accelerates the timeline: the review is in thirty days, not sixty. Tessaly's intelligence was wrong by a month. |

## Pacing Guidance

Roll once entering the office (Scene 3) and once at the Scene 4 threshold. Two rolls standard. Outcome 6 is a significant complication — if it fires, Nenne's arc-completion decision accelerates (she sends the package same evening, not three hours later).
