# C5-S3 Session Log — The Double Entry

**Date in world:** PC 188, five days after C5-S2. Afternoon into evening.
**Spotlight:** Mira Calloway
**Seed:** C5S3-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro's picks before the brief. Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond. The same order, the same naming, the same three minutes. He rolled them back into the sleeve and set the sleeve on his left hip. Tessaly had the ledger open before the others arrived. She wrote the entry header — *S3 / Weavers' Street / Nenne Ashwick / forgery study* — before she looked up to confirm the party was assembled.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira checked her jacket. The paper case. Present. Then she opened it — which she had not done in front of the party before — and took out the note she had added at the end of S2. She read it without reading it aloud. Then she added two lines to it in her own quick shorthand. She put it back. She closed the jacket.

Tessaly watched this. She did not ask. Mira did not explain.

## Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane: The Brief

Tessaly spread Nenne Ashwick's public filing accounts on the table alongside the floor plan Mira had reconstructed from memory after last week's reconnaissance. The cover: "Heila Voss, accounts regularisation specialist" — documented, back-storied, consistent with the Revenue Intelligence inspection timeline.

*"Forty-five minutes,"* Tessaly said. *"You need: one — her signature, three samples at minimum; two — her formatting conventions, particularly the date column and bottom-line weight; three — confirmation that her legitimate accounts are clean enough to anchor the false records."*

Mira studied the public filings for three minutes. Then she wrote three things in the paper case: the specific characteristics of Nenne's hand from the public filings.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
The paper case addition was silent. She was building the framework. She did not announce that this was the beginning of the forgery. She was doing the preparation that the job required. That was all.

*"Darro — alley position. Cael — end of Weavers' Street. I go alone."*

---

## Scene 2 — The Approach

Weavers' Street, Old Quarter. Nenne's townhouse: three stories, narrow front, a prosperous factor's building. The housekeeper admitted Mira without comment — Heila Voss had been expected, the timing matched.

Nenne Ashwick was in the parlour. She was in her late forties, precisely dressed, with the particular focus of someone who has been conducting business interviews for twenty years and can read the room before the visitor sits down.

Mira's Deception to establish cover DC 13: **d20+7 → rolls=[3] mod=+7 total=10**. The cover wobbled. Nenne's passive Insight: 14. Mira was projecting something more careful than "Heila Voss" would be — too much attention to the room, too much interest in the furniture and layout as data. Nenne noticed.

She did not call it. She showed Mira to the parlour and offered tea and talked about the Revenue Intelligence inspection timeline with the specific unhurriedness of someone who is buying time to decide something.

Mira held the persona. Her professional code: the persona is the work; dropping it is failing the work. She adjusted — softer posture, more deferential register, the specific deference that accounts clerks show to principals. Not a new persona. A tuned one.

---

## Scene 3 — The Office

Nenne showed Mira the working office — the real one. Not a prepared version. She did not explain why she chose the real office.

Investigation DC 13 to notice the parallel books: **d20+3 → rolls=[3] mod=+3 total=6**. The parallel books were not initially visible — they were filed by colour-coded spine, a system that looked like alphabetical order unless you knew what to look for. The difference in ink would become clear later.

Mira's Persuasion to see the client ledger DC 12: **d20+7 → rolls=[20] mod=+7 total=27 CRIT**. This was not persuasion in the conventional sense. Mira asked a specific question about how the Revenue Intelligence inspection would handle irregular income that had been properly routed but not categorised in the standard format. The question demonstrated knowledge of the inspection's specific scope — knowledge that "Heila Voss" should not have unless she was briefed by someone inside the inspection process.

Nenne stared at her for three seconds. Then she opened the client ledger and set it on the table.

The parallel books were immediately visible from this position. Black ink: legitimate income. Different hand: the irregular routed income. Meticulous separation. Nenne had been keeping parallel books for two years.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira photographed it. Three passes: signature study, date-column format, ink weight on the bottom line. She noted all three in the paper case before she looked up. The framework was building.

---

## Scene 4 — Nenne's Question

Mira was preparing to leave. Nenne was at the office door.

*"Who sent you, specifically?"*

The weight of the question was different from the question it appeared to be. She was not asking about the cover identity. She was asking whether this was Pale's job or something else.

Mira's Deception DC 15 (Nenne now suspicious, not just careful): **d20+7 → rolls=[17] mod=+7 total=24**. Cover held. Heila Voss was a regularisation specialist from Marten Factors with no guild affiliation. The answer was clean and consistent and did not give Nenne anything to fix on.

Nenne looked at her for a long moment.

*"I will need three days to prepare my accounts for the review,"* she said. *"I appreciate your time."*

She showed Mira out.

Tessaly's Insight on Nenne's intent from Cordwainer Lane (she had been watching the building from a position across the street): **d20+4 → rolls=[16] mod=+4 total=20**. Tessaly read her correctly through three hours of window observation. Nenne was deciding something. Not afraid. Deciding.

*"She's going to act independently,"* Tessaly told the party. *"What she does will depend on what she decides the situation is."*

---

## Scene 5 — The Package

Three hours later. A package arrived at Cordwainer Lane addressed to Tessaly — her actual name, at her actual address. Not a cover identity. Nenne had found her through the same network Rue uses.

Inside: Nenne's parallel account book. A note in Nenne's handwriting:

*"I destroyed the originals. These are yours to use or destroy. I want to be done with this arrangement."*

**Arc-completion fired.** Nenne had decided. She acted not to help the party — she acted to be in charge of something, after two years of being afraid of a guild she had never met except through Pale's instructions. She had identified that Tessaly's party was not Pale's party — the woman asking about the inspection had knowledge that Pale's people would not have been given, which meant she was independent — and she had made a decision about what being independent meant. She destroyed the originals and sent the book.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly wrote the entry before she read the note to the party: *source: Ashwick / account book / originals destroyed / sender identified party as independent of Pale.* She wrote it in fifteen seconds. Then she read the note aloud.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira opened the paper case. She added to the framework notes. The Nenne account structure: how the parallel books bridged two accounting styles; the specific Ashwick formatting conventions that would need to match the Pale dossier's operational file format; the third signature characteristic she had not had room to note in Scene 3. She worked in her quiet shorthand for four minutes.

She closed the jacket. The framework had three components now.

She did not say that she was building toward the final job. She was doing preparation. That was all.
