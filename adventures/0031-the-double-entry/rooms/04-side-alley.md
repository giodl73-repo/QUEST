# Room 04 — Side Alley (Support Position)

## Read-aloud
> A narrow alley between Nenne's townhouse and the neighbour's wall. Darro can see the working office window from here — second floor, single-latch, half-obscured by a shutter left at an angle. The alley has one exit north to Weavers' Street and one south to a service lane.

## Features

- **Darro's position.** He is here for the duration of Mira's meeting. Passive Perception (14) — he hears everything in the alley and can signal Cael at the street end.
- **The window.** Single-latch, accessible from outside with Athletics DC 12 (second floor ledge) and Thieves' Tools DC 11 to open quietly. This is the emergency extraction route if the meeting goes wrong.
- **The signal system.** Darro and Mira have a hand-signal system for: *continue, abort, contact needed.* If Mira needs to reach Tessaly (Scene 4's question), she can signal Darro through the window. He relays via Cael.
- **The pick maintenance.** Darro maintains his picks while waiting. He names them aloud at low volume. Anyone in the alley with him hears it. It is not a performance; it is what he does.

## GM Notes

The side alley is Darro's room — his craft-witness behavior fires here, in the unobserved space where the job requires him to be still and ready. The pick maintenance in the alley is the RV4 observation point for his character: it happens without direction, without anyone present to appreciate it, without narrative purpose beyond the fact that it is what he does.

The alley has no encounter. It is the support structure for Mira's scene. But the craft-witness firing here — in the cold, in the wait, with no audience — is the session's clearest professionally-triggered behavior.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Darro has waited in alleys for other people's meetings for four years. He has never once found it boring. There is always something to notice: the alley's drainage, the window latch type, the distance from the first-floor sill to the ground. He is never not working.*
