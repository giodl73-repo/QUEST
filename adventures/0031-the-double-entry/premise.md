# 0031 — The Double Entry

## The adventure in one sentence

Mira studies a client's signature for the forgery that will eventually expose Orren Pale, and the client destroys her own records when she understands what she has been a part of.

## Setting

**Nenne Ashwick's private residence**, Havenmere Old Quarter — a three-story townhouse on Weavers' Street, appropriate to the income of a prosperous textile factor. The first floor is a reception room and parlour; the second is Nenne's working office; the third is her personal quarters.

Nenne Ashwick is a textile factor who has been laundering income for a guild client for two years. Her arrangement was brokered by Orren Pale; she believes it is standard guild business. She has never met any other guild members. She has been very careful about the documentation.

## Hook

The job: create a set of false accounts that legitimise Nenne's client's irregular income as legitimate textile earnings. The accounts need to be perfect — they will face a Revenue Intelligence review within sixty days (Tessaly's intelligence from Rue, confirmed this session). The forgery requires Mira to study Nenne's handwriting, her signature, her formatting conventions, and the accounting structure used by her legitimate business.

This will take two sessions of in-world work. Session 3 is the study phase: Mira meets Nenne under a cover identity, observes her business practices, and begins the forgery work. Session 7 is the delivery.

**Secondary objective:** Tessaly needs to know whether Nenne's legitimate business is clean enough to anchor the false accounts, or whether the Revenue Intelligence review will expose the legitimate business too. If the legitimate business is also dirty, the forgery is not worth doing.

## The central tension

Nenne Ashwick is not who the guild thinks she is. She is not a passive money-laundering client who keeps her head down. She is a sharp, meticulous businesswoman who has been uncomfortable with the arrangement since month four — when she realised the "irregular income" she was laundering had an origin she did not want to know about. She has continued because she was afraid of the guild. She is afraid of the right things: Pale specifically, not the guild in general.

When she understands that the forgery operation is not Pale's idea — when she realises Tessaly's party is running independent of Pale — she makes a decision. She destroys her own records first. Then she hands the party what they need.

Her arc-completion is this: she acts to protect herself and, in doing so, removes the documentation that would compromise the party's operation. She does not do it to help them. She does it because she has finally decided to be in charge of something, and the records are what she is in charge of. Her arc fires from character logic, not from persuasion.

## The five scenes

### Scene 1 — The Brief
Tessaly at Cordwainer Lane. The job: Mira will approach Nenne as a factor's clerk seeking to regularise accounts for a textile inspection. The cover needs to hold for at least one meeting. Tessaly has Nenne's legitimate account structure from public filings — this is Mira's preparation material.

**Mira's preparation beat:** She studies the account structure. She notes three signature characteristics (the capital N with a specific loop, the way the date column is formatted, the ink weight on the bottom line). She writes this in the paper case — her working notes for the forgery. This is the first of two sessions of preparation. She checks the paper case before and after.

### Scene 2 — The Approach
Mira approaches Weavers' Street as "Heila Voss, accounts regularisation specialist" — a cover that Tessaly has documented for her. Nenne's housekeeper admits her. Mira has 45 minutes.

**Cover:** Deception DC 13 to establish as a legitimate accounts specialist. Nenne is smart (Insight +4) — Mira needs her Persuasion DC 14 to keep the cover through questions about the specific textile inspection being cited.

**Darro is in the alley.** Standard position. Cael is at the end of the street. Tessaly is at Cordwainer Lane with the account structure laid out. Mira is alone in the meeting.

### Scene 3 — The Office
Nenne shows Mira her working office — the real one, not a prepared version. Investigation DC 13: the accounts on the desk are in two colour inks. The legitimate income is in black. The irregular income is in a different hand entirely — Nenne keeps parallel books. She is meticulous about keeping them separate.

This is the finding that changes the forgery plan. The parallel books mean the false accounts need to bridge two accounting styles, not one. Mira makes a note. She asks to see the client ledger (Persuasion DC 12). Nenne hesitates. Then she shows her.

**Mira's study beat:** She photographs the signature — three passes, comparing the client ledger to the legitimate accounts. She has what she needs for the forgery framework. She updates the paper case before she leaves.

### Scene 4 — The Question
As Mira is preparing to leave, Nenne asks: *"Who sent you, specifically?"* It is a direct question with specific weight. She is not asking about the cover identity. She is asking whether this is Pale's job or someone else's.

Mira has three options:
- Maintain cover: Deception DC 15 (Nenne is now suspicious, not just careful)
- Partial truth: admit it is a guild job without naming Pale (Insight check DC 12 — Nenne reads this as Pale-adjacent)
- Name Tessaly: requires Tessaly's approval (she has not given it); Mira can contact her via Darro at the window with a hand signal

**Whatever Mira does:** Nenne says she will need three days to prepare her accounts for the review. She is buying time. She has already decided something. She shows Mira out.

### Scene 5 — The Return
Three hours after Mira's visit, a package arrives at Cordwainer Lane addressed to Tessaly (not to any cover identity — Nenne found Tessaly directly, through the same intelligence network Rue uses). Inside: Nenne's parallel account book. A note, in Nenne's handwriting: *"I destroyed the originals. These are yours to use or destroy. I want to be done with this arrangement."*

**Nenne's arc-completion:** She did not wait to be asked. She destroyed the records and sent the parallel book. She is extracting herself from Pale's arrangement on her own terms. The arc fires without the party present — by character logic, with no roll, no persuasion, no question asked.

**For the forgery:** The parallel book gives Mira everything she needs for S7. The study from this session (signature, formatting) plus the parallel book (income structure) equals a complete forgery framework. The S7 deliverable is now possible.

The question is whether the accumulation is observable as "professionally-triggered" rather than "grief-triggered." Mira studies the signature because the work requires it — not because she cares about Nenne, not because of anyone's loss.

**What DM observes:**

- Nenne's arc-completion (Scene 5): character-logic. Score if the package arrives without any party member asking Nenne to act.
- Tessaly's ledger (Scene 5 — she receives the package): professionally-triggered. She writes the entry before she opens the account book.

**Token condition — Nenne Ashwick (garrison-equivalent NPC):**
Nenne's trust is the package. Token condition: she sends it without being asked, after understanding that the party operates independently of Pale. Token fires when the package arrives. Her marker is implicit — she has just handed over the documentation that could compromise her, which is the equivalent of *I will answer for you.* She expects nothing in return. That is what makes it a marker.
