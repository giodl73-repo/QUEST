# Scene 03 — Reading Room (Optional — Social Path)

**Location:** White Quill underground library, reading alcove off the main stacks. **Time:** Day 2.

**Read-aloud:**

> A side room off the main stacks — four reading desks, lamps, and a long wall of reference material. One desk has a cup of cold tea and a volume open to a page that was being read before Sevven came to meet you. He picks up the tea, remembers it's cold, puts it back. He says: "Sit. There are things you should know before you see the shard."

**Features:**

- **Sevven's cold tea.** He forgets drinks while reading. Always has.
- **The pre-Cataclysm events wall.** A reference panel on the east wall — a timeline of the Orders' actions PC 195-220, annotated. This is Sevven's context-setting for visitors (he has had three visitors in fifty-eight years; this is his fourth). The timeline includes: the Dalimvar healing withdrawal (PC 203); Sylaren's departure (PC 203); the Defiance clause swearing (PC 198, five years earlier); the compact's signing (PC 198); the Cataclysm (PC 220).
- **What Sevven tells them.** He explains the Dalimvar withdrawal — the Orders voted not to provide healing assistance during a regional civil conflict because the Council ruled it a *"local matter outside the Conclave's mandate."* Seven hundred people died in the following year. Sylaren was the only dissenting vote. She left nine years after swearing the clause — nine years of watching what the compact allowed, then leaving.
- **Calder and the timeline.** If Calder looks at the timeline: the Dalimvar withdrawal (PC 203) — seven hundred deaths, Orders absent — is structurally identical to the plague village. He will recognize this before Sevven finishes explaining. He will say nothing. He will read the rest of the timeline.

**Encounter:** None.

**P0 note:** This scene is optional — if the party has already assembled three Orders (and uses the shortcut), they go straight to the Deep Archive. The Reading Room is for parties that need context before they're ready to receive the shard.

**Connections:** Main Stacks (02).

**GM Notes:**
- **Sevven's explanation style.** He does not dramatize. He presents facts in order. The drama is in the facts themselves. He says: *"Seven hundred in the following year. She was the only one who voted no. She left nine years later, not immediately — nine years of staying and documenting what the compact allowed. Then she left."*
- **Calder and what he doesn't say.** He will read the Dalimvar entry and the numbers and the date and he will be quiet for a longer time than usual. Thessaly and Lenne will notice. Orik will count the room exits. Sera will look at the timeline without knowing what she's looking for yet — she is tracking the shape of a decision, not its content.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The reading desks were built by the library's first archivist, three hundred years ago, from wood she carried underground because there were no trees below. She built them to the proportions of a desk she remembered from her own teacher. She has been dead for two hundred and seventy years. The desks have been resurfaced twice. They are the right height for reading.*
