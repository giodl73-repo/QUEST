# Scene 05 — Sevven's Study (Optional)

**Location:** White Quill, small room off the deep archive. **Time:** Day 2.

**Read-aloud:**

> Sevven's study is the smallest room in the library and the most lived-in. A desk, a bed, a bookcase that holds three personal volumes (not the library's). Behind the bookcase: an air-shaft that is barely large enough for one person at a time. A small card above it says *"Emergency exit. Single file."* He has had this card for fifty-eight years.

**Features:**

- **The three personal volumes.** Investigation DC 10: Sylaren's collected correspondence (a personal copy, different from the library's); a journal (Sevven's; dated from 58 years ago to the present; he writes one sentence per day); a small volume of Kagonesti prayers (he is not Kagonesti; he has kept it because it was given to him by a traveler 40 years ago and he has not found an occasion to pass it on).
- **Sylaren's personal message.** Sevven will show this to Calder if Calder named the plague village in Scene 04. It is in the personal correspondence volume, loose inside the cover. One line: *"If whoever reads this was also the delayed response — it was the protocol, not you. The protocol was the thing that needed amending. You were doing what you could within what you had been given."* She wrote this in PC 213, a year after she added the second inscription to the shard. She knew someone would eventually be what she had been: the healer who arrived to find the numbers already wrong.
- **The air-shaft exit.** Behind the bookcase. 3 feet wide; Sevven maintains it annually; it exits on the hillside 100 feet from the entrance. Emergency only. If the splinter cell compromised the entrance: this is the exit.

**Encounter:** None.

**Connections:** Deep Archive (04).

**GM Notes:**
- **Calder and Sylaren's note.** This is available only if Route D fired in Scene 04. Sevven does not offer it otherwise — it would be intrusive. If Calder named the plague village, Sevven will say: *"She wrote something for whoever it would be. I don't know if it's still true after three hundred years. You can read it or not."*
- **Orik's assessment.** He will look at the air-shaft and mentally note it as the emergency exit. He will not say this. He will position himself so he can see it.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Sevven has written one sentence per day for fifty-eight years. In all that time, the sentence has always been about what happened in the library that day. He has never written about what he is feeling. He is not sure this is a problem. He is an archivist. He records what happened.*
