# Scene 02 — Main Stacks

**Location:** White Quill underground library, main level. **Time:** Day 2.

**Read-aloud:**

> The main stacks are floor-to-ceiling shelving on three walls, with rolling ladders on iron tracks. The collection is organized by date, not subject — chronological, so that everything that happened in a given year is together. The earliest section (pre-Cataclysm; PC 150-220) is marked with a different color of binding. There is a lot of it. An elderly woman is at the far end of the room, reshelving something. She does not look up immediately.

**Features:**

- **Deva.** She is reshelving a volume that was requested once in the last decade and is now back. She will look up when addressed. She is precise and does not waste words.
- **The chronological organization.** Investigation DC 10: the pre-Cataclysm section (PC 150-220) is the most heavily annotated — someone has read these extensively and added dated marginalia. The marginalia is in Sevven's handwriting, across fifty-eight years.
- **The oldest volume visible.** Perception DC 12: one volume on the lowest shelf of the pre-Cataclysm section has a different binding material — older than the others. It is labeled *"Sylaren: Collected Correspondence, PC 198-221."* Sevven kept her letters. All of them.
- **The Black Robe question.** If the party needs a Black Robe: Deva is the obvious candidate. She does not announce this. The party must ask. See `npcs/deva.md` for her arc-completion.

**Encounter:** None. If the splinter cell somehow bypassed the entrance: they would come through here. Deva knows where the emergency exit is.

**Connections:** Entry/Anteroom (01); Reading Room (03); Deep Archive (04).

**GM Notes:**
- **Lenne and the shelving.** Lenne will read the organizational system immediately (objects before people). *"Chronological, not subject. She's cataloguing what happened, not what it was about."* This is Sevven's philosophy of history: you cannot know what matters until everything is laid out in order.
- **Calder and Sylaren's correspondence.** If he finds the volume: Sevven will not stop him from taking it down. He can read one letter — Sylaren's letter to the White Quill the day she arrived, explaining why she left the Orders. *"I could not stay in an institution that decided seven hundred lives were a local matter. I have no more to say about this. I would like to begin cataloguing."* She went straight to work.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The rolling ladders were installed by the library's second archivist, 280 years ago. They have been serviced every generation. The iron tracks have been replaced twice. The ladders themselves are original; the wood is old-growth oak that does not exist anymore. Deva oils the bearings annually. She does not think about this. It is on the maintenance schedule.*
