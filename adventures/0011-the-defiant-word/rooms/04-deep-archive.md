# Scene 04 — Deep Archive (Shard Location + Hint 3 + Arc-Completion)

**Location:** White Quill underground library, restricted archive. **Time:** Day 2.

**Read-aloud:**

> The deep archive is cooler and quieter than the main stacks. The collection here is smaller — perhaps two hundred volumes, plus a locked display case against the far wall that holds objects rather than books. Sevven takes a key from his collar and opens the case. He removes a cloth-wrapped object about the size of a large fist. He says: "Read both sides."

**Features:**

- **The display case.** Behind glass: several objects of compact-related significance. The cloth-wrapped shard. Two letters in protective sleeves — Sylaren's correspondence. A small carved figure (not the carved figure from Morreth's chapel; a different one; Sylaren carved it in the ruins of Istar, two years after the Cataclysm, before she died).
- **The shard.** Room temperature. Two inscriptions. Sevven hands it to whoever is standing at the front of the group (likely Thessaly or Calder). He says: *"Read both sides."*
- **Hint 3 delivery (front inscription read-aloud → Calder reads reverse):**
  - Front: *"Let any mortal amend what any mortal has sworn."*
  - Reverse (Calder reads; Sevven points him to it; passive Insight 14 auto): *"As I wrote above: what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true. The compact did not make it so; it merely acknowledged it. — Sylaren, PC 212."*
  - Calder Arcana DC 12 or Thessaly/Lenne auto: the second inscription means the new compact they speak can be different from the broken one. It can include Sylaren's amendment. It can include the Defiance clause's second inscription as part of its own text.

**Arc-completion — Calder (Route D):**

After reading the second inscription, Calder is quiet. Then:

He says — to Sevven, to the three Orders assembled in the room, to the shard: *"In the forty-second year after the Cataclysm, there was a village in the Abanasinian river-country that took sick. I was the only healer in range. I arrived three days after the outbreak. I spent eight days there. Forty-one people died. Seventeen survived. The reason I was the only healer in range is that the Orders — the pre-Cataclysm Orders — had adopted the Conclave's healing-distribution protocol that classified Abanasinian river-villages as Tier 3. Tier 3 settlements received delayed response. I was the delayed response. I don't know if the Orders knew the protocol was still operating. I know it was operating."*

He is not angry. He is precise. He is accounting for something.

Sevven: *"The Dalimvar protocol. She argued against its adoption in PC 199. Four years before she left."* He pauses. *"The protocol was still operating in the forty-second year after the Cataclysm. Nearly three hundred years."*

The second inscription warms briefly — the same quality as Hint 1. Sylaren's amendment demonstrating itself. What any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. The forty-one dead are not unamended. But the clause that killed them can be.

**P0 fallback:** If Calder does not name the plague village, Hint 3 still delivers via the inscription reading. The warmth does not occur; the text does. Route A: the party takes the shard; Sevven gives the explanation (his arc-completion) afterward. Still a complete session.

**Connections:** Main Stacks (02); Sevven's Study (05).

**GM Notes:**
- **DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-04.md`. Calder naming the plague village is a hold-space moment. The accounting is precise; do not add emotion to it in narration. Let the precision be the emotion.
- **Lenne and the shard.** She will want to hold it immediately after the inscription is read. She will not, this time. She looks at Orik. He shakes his head slightly. She doesn't hold it.
