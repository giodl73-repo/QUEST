# Premise — The Defiant Word

## Hook

Tessamine's reconstruction document describes the Clause of Defiance as given voluntarily to a sect of pre-Cataclysm scholars who left the Orders before the disaster. Her note: *"The defiant wizard Sylaren gave this shard to the library-keepers of the White Quill — scholars who departed the Conclave over a point of conscience. Her instruction: 'Give this to whoever comes asking with a White Robe, a Red Robe, and a Black Robe present. All three must be willing.'"*

The party has White (Thessaly) and Red (Lenne). They need a Black Robe.

Mira Coldsteel is traveling with them now — or rather, she is a day behind them, making her own way northeast. She is not yet party-adjacent. But she is Red Robe trained and could identify a Black Robe contact. One message and two days' wait.

The White Quill's underground library is in the Qualimosti eastern foothills, two days' travel from the ruined chapel. They have been there for three hundred years, maintaining the records of what the Orders were and what they failed to be.

## Setting

The White Quill — an underground library maintained by scholars who left the Conclave before the Cataclysm. Three hundred years of continuous occupation. They are not hostile to the Orders; they are disappointed in them. The Archivist, Sevven, is 80 years old and has been keeping these records since he was 22. He has been waiting for someone to ask with all three Orders represented.

## Artifact

**The Clause of Defiance** — a compact shard. Inscribed: *"Let any mortal amend what any mortal has sworn."* The defiant wizard Sylaren added a second inscription on the reverse in different handwriting after the original swearing: *"As I wrote above: what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true. The compact did not make it so; it merely acknowledged it."*

**Hint 3 delivery:** The second inscription is accessible via Religion DC 15, or Calder's passive Insight 14 triggers Sevven to point it out directly. Hint 3: the compact was already being amended by its own signatories before the Cataclysm. The defiant wizard was right. The moral: a covenant that forbids amendment is not a covenant — it is a commandment. The new compact can be spoken differently.

See `treasures/clause-of-defiance.md`.

## Themes

**Defiance** — but defiance in the spirit of conscience, not rebellion. Sylaren left the Orders because a specific clause — one that allowed the Orders to withhold access to healing during a crisis that preceded the Cataclysm — required her to choose between her oath and people dying. She chose the people. She is dead. The clause she objected to is the one Calder has been thinking about since the Grief Inscription.

**Calder's spotlight** — his grief is the forty-one people who died in the plague village. The Orders were not there. This clause — the one Sylaren defied — was written to allow exactly that absence. When Calder reads Sylaren's second inscription (*"what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay"*), he understands that the defiant wizard was arguing for exactly what the new compact requires: the right of mortals to amend what was sworn when the swearing costs lives.

**The Black Robe requirement** — the puzzle. The party needs a third Order represented. Mira is available but choosing to ask her is a decision with weight: it means acknowledging her as a genuine Conclave-adjacent participant, not just an observer. Or: the White Quill may have a Black Robe associate — Sevven's assistant, who trained with the Black Robes before leaving the Orders and joining the library sixty years ago.

## Three-route outcomes (A/C/D)

**Route A (Charter/Fallback):** The party locates a Black Robe (via Mira's contact or Sevven's assistant). The three-Order requirement is met. Sevven gives them the shard. Calder reads the second inscription. Hint 3 delivers via the inscription itself.

**Route C (Default):** They cannot assemble the three Orders in time (or refuse to ask Mira). Sevven gives them a copy of the second inscription — not the shard — and sends them away. They retrieve the shard in a later session when the requirement can be met. The campaign continues but Hint 3 is delayed.

**Route D (Inversion/Optimal):** Calder names the plague village — the forty-one deaths, the Orders' absence, the clause that allowed it — in the presence of all three Orders. He names it as the thing that needs amending. When he does, the shard's second inscription glows (same mechanism as Hint 1). Sylaren's voice is present in the room — not literally; through the inscription's warmth, the way Vethrenn's margin note warmed in Kessa's hand. The compact's self-amendment is demonstrated, not just described.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5 (party of 5 + possible Black Robe NPC). One combat encounter: a remnant Unbound Conclave splinter cell (who did not receive Mira's stand-down order) attacking the White Quill's hidden entrance. This establishes that Mira's stand-down was not universal — there are factions within the renegades.

## Constraints

- Room count: 5 (library entrance, main stacks, reading room, deep archive where shard is held, Sevven's study)
- The Black Robe requirement is a decision, not a puzzle to solve cleverly. The party either asks Mira (weight: acknowledging her as a true participant) or uses Sevven's assistant Deva (weight: trusting a stranger's Order credentials).
- Hint 3 is mandatory delivery for this session. It must land regardless of route.
- P0 self-review: every scene needs a fallback if the party refuses to produce a Black Robe.
