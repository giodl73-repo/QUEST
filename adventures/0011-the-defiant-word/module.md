## Summary

The Clause of Defiance is at the White Quill — an underground library maintained for three hundred years by scholars who left the Orders before the Cataclysm. Archivist Sevven, 80, has been waiting fifty-eight years for someone to come asking with all three Orders present.

The party has White (Thessaly) and Red (Lenne). They need a Black Robe. Sevven's assistant Deva trained with the Black Robes for four years before leaving the Orders sixty years ago. The question is whether she still counts.

Two splinter-cell agents are at the entrance when the party arrives. Easy encounter; they didn't get Mira's stand-down. Their presence establishes a new antagonist thread: the splinter faction that operates outside Mira's authority.

Inside: Sevven presents the shard. It has two inscriptions — the original clause and Sylaren's amendment added twelve years later. The second inscription is Hint 3. When Calder reads it, he will understand that the compact's self-amendment is not an innovation; it was always possible. Sylaren said so.

## Setting & Background

**The White Quill** — underground library, Qualimosti eastern foothills. Founded pre-Cataclysm by scholars who left the Orders over a point of conscience. Three hundred years of continuous occupation. Chronological organization: everything that happened in a given year is filed together. The pre-Cataclysm section (PC 150-220) has fifty-eight years of Sevven's annotations.

**The Dalimvar withdrawal (PC 203)** — The Orders voted to withhold healing access from the Dalimvar region during a pre-Cataclysm civil conflict, classifying it as a "local matter outside the Conclave's mandate." Seven hundred died in the following year. Sylaren was the only dissenting vote. She left nine years later. The Healing Tier protocol she opposed was still operating three hundred years later when Calder arrived too late to a plague village.

**The Black Robe requirement** — Sylaren's condition: all three Orders present when the shard is given. The party has White (Thessaly) and Red (Lenne). Deva (Sevven's assistant, former Black Robe trainee) is the available option. She will agree if asked with respect for her former standing.

## Scenes

### Entrance Encounter (non-optional)

**Read-aloud:**

> The entrance is a flat stone set into the hillside — too level, the kind of levelness that takes maintenance. Two grey-robed people stand in front of it. One has a hand on the stone. They are trying to find the latch. They have not heard you yet.

Sera saw them from the hillside (signature move; perimeter check). Party has surprise.

**Social resolution (preferred):** Persuasion DC 12 or "Mira Coldsteel stood your organization down" → they confirm they're a splinter faction, leave without fighting. Free intel: three splinter cells; tasked with White Quill shard; reporting to a Palanthas contact.

**Combat:** 2× Thug reskin (see Encounters Appendix). Easy for 5 PCs. 300 XP adjusted. 60 XP per PC.

### Scene 01 — Entry Anteroom

**Read-aloud (after encounter):**

> The tunnel descends twenty feet. At the bottom: underground quiet, the specific silence of a maintained space that is rarely visited. A lamp burns — Continual Flame, pre-Cataclysm. A card on a low table: *"Please announce yourselves. The archivist will come."*

Sevven appears within two minutes.

---

### Scene 02 — Main Stacks

**Read-aloud:**

> Floor-to-ceiling shelving on three walls with rolling ladders. Organized chronologically by year — everything that happened in a given year filed together. The pre-Cataclysm section (PC 150-220) is a different color binding. An elderly woman is at the far end, reshelving something. She does not look up yet.

**Features:** Deva (potential Black Robe; see NPCs). The pre-Cataclysm section, heavily annotated (Sevven's 58 years of marginalia). Sylaren's correspondence volume on the bottom shelf (Investigation DC 10).

**The Black Robe question:** The party needs to ask Deva. She will agree if respected for her former standing (see NPCs). Lenne recognizing her as a practitioner is the most natural trigger.

---

### Scene 03 — Reading Room (Optional — Social Path)

**Read-aloud:**

> A side alcove off the main stacks — four desks, lamps, a timeline wall. Sevven picks up a cup of cold tea, remembers it's cold, puts it back. "Sit. There are things you should know before you see the shard."

**Features:** The PC 195-220 timeline (Dalimvar withdrawal marked; Sylaren's departure; compact signing). Sevven explains the Dalimvar numbers: seven hundred deaths; the only dissenting vote; nine years of watching before she left.

**Calder and the timeline:** He will recognize the Dalimvar withdrawal as structurally identical to the plague village. He will not say this in this scene.

---

### Scene 04 — Deep Archive

**Read-aloud:**

> Cooler, quieter than the main stacks. A smaller collection — two hundred volumes and a locked glass case. Sevven takes a key from his collar. He removes a cloth-wrapped object. He says: *"Read both sides."*

**Features:** The display case (shard, Sylaren's correspondence, Sylaren's carved figure). The Clause of Defiance — room temperature; two inscriptions.

**Three-Order requirement:** When Thessaly (White), Lenne (Red), and Deva (Black) are all present and willing, Sevven retrieves the shard.

**Hint 3 delivery:**
- Front inscription: *"Let any mortal amend what any mortal has sworn."*
- Reverse (Calder reads; Sevven points to him; Insight passive 14 auto): *"As I wrote above: what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true. The compact did not make it so; it merely acknowledged it. — Sylaren, PC 212."*
- Implication (Arcana DC 12; Thessaly/Lenne auto): the new compact they speak can incorporate Sylaren's amendment. It does not have to match the broken original.

**Route D (Calder names the plague village):** If Calder names the forty-one deaths, the protocol, the delayed arrival — in the presence of all three Orders — the second inscription warms briefly. Same quality as Hint 1's glow. Sylaren's amendment demonstrating itself.

**Sevven's arc-completion (after shard given):** *"The Dalimvar protocol. She argued against its adoption in PC 199. Four years before she left. The protocol was still operating in the forty-second year after the Cataclysm. Nearly three hundred years."*

**P0 fallback:** If Calder doesn't name the village, Hint 3 still delivers via the inscription reading. The warmth does not occur. Route A valid.

**DM-letter:** `dm-letter-scene-04.md`. Calder's accounting requires hold-space. The precision is the emotion. Read before the session.

## Treasures

### The Clause of Defiance

*Fourth shard. Room temperature. Two inscriptions in two different hands.*

**Front:** *"Let any mortal amend what any mortal has sworn."* Archmage Sylaren of the White Robes (PC 198–221). Sigil: small stylized quill.

**Reverse:** *"As I wrote above: what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true. The compact did not make it so; it merely acknowledged it. — Sylaren, PC 212."* Added 12 years after the original.

**Passive:** Room temperature. No active effects when held briefly.

**Hint 3 delivery:** Religion DC 15 or Calder passive Insight 14 (Sevven points him to it) → reads second inscription → Arcana DC 12 (auto for Thessaly/Lenne): the new compact they speak can incorporate Sylaren's amendment; it need not restore the original verbatim.

**Route D:** When Calder names the plague village in the presence of all three Orders, the second inscription warms briefly.

**Curse — The right to amend:** Attuned creature becomes aware once/day of any unjust commitment they are maintaining. Inward recognition only; no compulsion; the shard names it.
