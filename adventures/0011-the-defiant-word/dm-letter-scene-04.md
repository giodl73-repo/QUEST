# DM Letter — Scene 04 (The Deep Archive: Calder's Accounting)

*This letter is for you, the DM. Read it before the session.*

---

## What this scene is

Calder administered last rites for forty-one people in eight days. He saved seventeen. He has been carrying the number 41 since then. He is also a Life Domain cleric — he was literally built to heal. The reason he arrived three days late to a four-day outbreak is that the protocol classified the region as Tier 3. The protocol was the compact's operating arm. The compact did not prevent this. The clause Sylaren swore — and then amended — was written specifically to prevent this.

Calder is not angry. He has done the accounting. He knows where the number comes from. When he reads Sylaren's second inscription, he will recognize it as the answer to a question he didn't know he was asking.

Your job: hold the space for him to name the accounting. Not the grief — the accounting. Calder's mode is precision. Let the precision be the emotion.

---

## What to do before it starts

- Read Calder's decision-order: *"Is anyone dying? What is the weight of this? Who is being ignored? What does Mishakal require?"*
- The weight he has been calculating since the Grief Inscription: seven hundred in the Dalimvar withdrawal; forty-one in the plague village. Same structure. Different scales. He has been sitting with the comparison.
- The second inscription: *"What any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay. This was always true."* When he reads this, he will understand that the compact's self-amendment is not an innovation — it was always present. Sylaren said so. Twelve years after swearing it.

---

## What to do during

**When Sevven hands over the shard:** He says "Read both sides." Hand it to Calder, or to whoever is at the front, and let it come to Calder naturally. Thessaly may read the front inscription; Calder will turn it over.

**When Calder reads the reverse:** He will read it quietly. He may read it twice. Sevven will say: *"She added that one twelve years after the first. Nine years before she left."* Then: *"That clause she swore — the one on the front — she wrote the amendment because she had watched the Orders use the original clause in ways she had not anticipated when she swore it."*

**If Calder speaks after reading the second inscription:** Stop. Do not add narration. Let him speak. His accounting will be precise — dates, numbers, the specific protocol name. This precision is not coldness; it is the specific way Calder holds weight. If he names the forty-one: the second inscription warms. Narrate it simply: *"The second inscription warms briefly, the same quality as the first shard's glow — recognition, not approval."*

**If Calder says nothing:** The Hint 3 still delivers via the inscription. Route A is fine. Sevven will explain the Dalimvar context from the Reading Room. The accounting Calder is holding may surface later — in Sevven's study, in the quiet after, or not at all this session.

---

## What ends the scene

The scene ends when the shard is in party hands and Sevven has confirmed the three-Order requirement is met. After that: Sevven's arc-completion (he names the Dalimvar numbers). The party has the shard.

---

## What happens if Calder doesn't speak

The inscription delivers Hint 3 regardless. The second inscription can be read; its meaning is available. The warmth is Route D only. Route A gives the party everything they need to proceed.

Calder's accounting may surface in 0012 (The Reckoning Stone) when the renegade leader reveals what the compact was built to enable. The grief has weight; it does not expire.

---

*The Dalimvar withdrawal (PC 203) and the plague village (forty-second year post-Cataclysm) are separated by approximately 260 years. The protocol was still operating. Sylaren was right. Calder was the delayed response. He knows this. He is accounting for it now.*
