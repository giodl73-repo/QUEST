# Sevven

*White Quill Archivist, 80. He has been maintaining this library for fifty-eight years. He knows where everything is, including the things that should not be found by the wrong people.*

## Appearance

Sevven is ancient in the way that archivists get ancient — lean, precise, unhurried. He moves through the stacks without looking at his feet; he has been doing this for decades. His hands are ink-stained to the wrist. He wears spectacles with one cracked lens that he has not replaced because the crack does not interfere with reading. He smells of old paper and cedar oil.

He has been waiting fifty-eight years for someone to come asking with all three Orders represented. He is not impatient. He is an archivist; he understands that some documents wait.

## Stat block

**Non-combatant.** AC 10, HP 8. He will not fight. If the library is threatened, he will attempt to evacuate the most irreplaceable records; he knows exactly which ones they are.

## What he knows (public)

- The White Quill has had the Clause of Defiance since PC 212, when Sylaren gave it to them personally.
- Sylaren's instruction: give it to whoever comes asking with all three Orders represented. All three must be willing.
- The library has been here for three hundred years.
- His assistant Deva trained with the Black Robes for four years before leaving the Orders sixty years ago. She is not technically a Black Robe anymore. Whether she counts is a question Sevven has never had to answer.

## What he knows (private)

- He has a theory about what the compact was for. He has had fifty-eight years to develop it. He will share it if asked directly; he does not volunteer it.
- The Unbound Conclave splinter cell that attacked the library entrance last week was looking for this shard specifically. They knew about it. Someone in the Unbound Conclave has better records than the party does.
- Sylaren's second inscription was not written in anger. He has read it thousands of times. It was written with relief — the relief of someone who had worked out a proof and needed to record it before she died.

## Emotional position

**What he wants:** For the right people to receive the shard and understand what it means. Not just to have it — to understand it. The second inscription is the part that matters.

**What he can't say:** He is not sure the compact should be restored at all. He has read fifty-eight years of documentation about what the Orders did with the first compact. His reservations are substantial. He will not say this until after the shard is given — it would taint the giving.

**The condition:** He gives the shard when all three Orders are present. Period. He has waited fifty-eight years; he can wait for the party to assemble the third.

## Arc-Completion

**The moment:** After the shard is given and the Hint 3 delivery has happened — after Calder has read the second inscription and, if Route D fires, named the plague village — Sevven says: *"She left the Orders in PC 203 because the council voted to withhold healing from the Dalimvar region during the pre-Cataclysm civil conflict. Seven hundred people died in that region over the following year. The Orders said it was a local matter. She said it was not. She was the only dissenting vote."* He returns to his shelving. He does not look at anyone. He has said the thing he was not going to say until after.

**What it produces:** The party now knows the specific event that drove the Defiance clause. They know the compact was contested even before the Cataclysm. They know Calder's plague village was not the first.

**What happens if never met:** The party has the shard and the inscription. The specific event remains in the library's records, undiscovered. It can be found on a return visit.

## GM Notes

- **Do not rush Sevven.** He has been waiting fifty-eight years. A few more minutes means nothing to him. Let him set the pace.
- **The shard's temperature.** When he retrieves it: *"It's room temperature. Always has been. Sylaren said that was appropriate — a defiance that requires no warmth to be believed."*
- **His cracked spectacles.** He notices if anyone notices them. He says: *"The crack doesn't interfere with reading. It interferes with peripheral vision. Fifty-eight years underground, I don't need peripheral vision."*
