# Premise — The Ambitious Mage's Vault

## Hook

Tessamine's reconstruction document names the second shard's last-known holder: Magister Volenn Ashford, Red Robe, Kharolis foothills tower. Volenn died eight years ago. His apprentice, Lenne Stormwatch, inherited the tower and has been working there independently ever since — studying the objects Volenn left behind, including one she has catalogued as "resonant obsidian fragment, High Istaran provenance, responds to intent."

She doesn't know what it is. She thinks it is a power source. She is wrong, but she is not stupid.

The Unbound Conclave knows where Lenne's tower is. They got the same information the party did; they had it first. Two agents — Osvin and the quiet one, last seen in Korrath — are two days ahead of the party on the road.

## Setting

Volenn's Tower, Kharolis foothills. A working mage's tower, five floors, maintained by one person. It smells of burnt parchment and preserved herbs. The Kharolis borderlands are not wilderness — there are mining roads, cart-tracks, small holds. The tower is not remote; it is simply not on the main road. Lenne knows the area well. The party does not.

## Artifact

**The Clause of Ambition** — a shard of the Conclave Compact, slightly larger than the Clause of Witness. Inscribed in High Istaran: *"Let no Order claim more than its share of the sky."* When held by a creature with genuine ambition (the shard reads intent, not rank), it vibrates faintly — like a caught breath, a half-second. Lenne has been holding it for eight years.

See `treasures/clause-of-ambition.md`.

## Themes

**Ambition** — the emotion of this adventure. Lenne's ambition is real and legitimate. The shard is not a corruption; it is a mirror. The question is not whether Lenne should want things — she should — but whether what she's working toward costs what she thinks it costs.

**What Orik sees** — Lenne works alone in a tower, drives herself past safe limits, and does not stop when she should because stopping feels like failing. Orik has seen this. He watched seven apprentices do exactly this. He was not there when the lamp overturned.

## Three-route outcomes (A/C/D)

**Route A (Charter/Fallback):** The party explains the compact; Lenne gives the shard in exchange for recognition of her research and a genuine connection to a network that can support her work. The shard changes hands professionally. Lenne continues working, better resourced.

**Route C (Default):** The Unbound Conclave agents arrive at the tower before or during the party's time there. The three-way confrontation forces the shard to change hands under pressure. Lenne may end up with neither party; agents take it; the party recovers it from the agents (see encounter). Tense, costly, no arc-completion.

**Route D (Inversion/Optimal):** Orik tells Lenne what ambition-as-fear costs. Not a lecture — a specific story: the hall, the oil, the pine resin, the fire. She receives it. She gives the shard freely and changes something specific about how she works. Arc-completion fires. Hint 2 is retrieved. The shard is given, not taken.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5. Two combat encounters expected: road ambush (Unbound Conclave agents on approach) and optional tower confrontation (if Route C triggers). Social encounters: Lenne (NPC arc-completion, Orik spotlight), tower interior investigation.
