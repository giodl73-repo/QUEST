# Lenne Stormwatch

*Red Robe wizard, 29. She inherited Volenn's tower eight years ago and has been here alone since. She is brilliant, overworked, and does not know it.*

## Appearance

Lenne is slight and precise — she moves with the economy of someone who has arranged a workspace exactly to her needs and resents having to navigate around anyone else. Her Red Robe is practical rather than ceremonial; she has added pockets. Her hair is pulled back in a way that was probably tidy three days ago. She is not unkempt — she is busy. There is a difference, and she knows it, and she is not sure it's a meaningful one anymore.

Her hands are ink-stained on the right, from writing. On the left, the palm is slightly calloused — from holding the shard repeatedly, day after day, testing its response to different intents.

## Stat block

**Combatant (reluctant).** AC 12 (Mage Armor 15) | **HP** 38 | **Speed** 30 ft.
**INT** 18 (+4) | **WIS** 13 (+1) | **CON** 12

**Spells (Red Robe L7 equivalent):** Counterspell, Fireball, Hold Person, Misty Step, Shield, Detect Magic.
**Combat style:** Defensive first. She will counterspell before attacking. She does not want to fight; she wants the intrusion to end.

She will not fight to keep the shard — she will fight to keep people from taking things without her understanding why.

## What she knows (public)

- She has a "resonant obsidian fragment" she has been studying for eight years.
- Volenn bought it from a Palanthas dealer; she inherited it.
- It vibrates when she holds it. She has documented this response across 300+ trials.
- She has not been able to replicate the effect with any known magical medium.
- She does not know it is a Conclave Compact shard.

## What she knows (private)

- She is exhausted. She will not say this.
- She has been working alone for eight years because asking for help feels like admitting the work is beyond her.
- Volenn taught her that self-sufficiency was a virtue. She has taken this further than he meant.
- She has a specific fear: that if she stops working as hard as she does, someone will notice the work wasn't worth it. She has not named this fear.

## Emotional position

**What she wants:** To understand what the shard is and what it does. She has been working on this for eight years; she is not giving it up without understanding what she spent the eight years on.

**What she can't say:** She is afraid the work has been a waste. She has been holding a thing that vibrates for eight years and she doesn't know why, and stopping now means she spent eight years not knowing. She cannot say this.

**The condition:** She gives the shard when someone (Orik, specifically, or any PC who reads her situation correctly) tells her that what she spent the eight years learning — how to read an object's intent, how to document a magical response systematically, how to work alone with rigor — was real, was good, was not a waste. She needs to hear that the work was worth doing even if the object is not what she thought. She cannot ask for this. She will respond to it if it is offered without being asked for.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Lenne's response |
|---|---|
| Announces they're here for the shard | "No." Simple, flat. She is not hostile yet, but this conversation is over if that's how they open. |
| Asks to see her research | Considers. Possibly yes — she is proud of the work, and researchers like an audience. |
| Explains what the shard actually is | Listens very carefully. Asks two clarifying questions. Does not immediately give the shard. |
| Tries to take the shard | Counterspells the attempt. Then: "You should leave." |
| Orik names what ambition-as-fear costs | Goes still. Does not answer immediately. This is the arc-completion condition. |
| Thessaly offers to share the reconstruction document | Interested — this is information she has been trying to reconstruct herself. Goodwill gain. |
| Unbound Conclave agents arrive | Immediately hostile to the agents. Will work with the party against them — the agents are not here for understanding; they want the shard for power. She knows the difference. |

## Arc-Completion

**The moment:** Orik says something specific — not a lecture about ambition, but a specific true thing about what it costs to work too hard and not know why. The closest version: *"I know what it looks like when someone can't stop working because stopping means asking whether it was worth it. I know because I watched seven people do it in a hall I was paid to guard, and I left, and when I came back the hall was gone."* He does not say this to teach her. He says it because he recognizes her, and recognition costs him something.

When this happens: Lenne is quiet for longer than feels comfortable. Then she picks up the shard. It vibrates. She says: *"Eight years. I thought it was a power source."* She hands it to Orik. When she releases it, the vibration stops before it leaves her palm.

**What it produces:** The shard is given freely. Hint 2 is available — Lenne points them to Volenn's old study, where the moon-calendar is. *"Volenn was obsessed with moon-alignment. If the shard connects to that, his old study is the place to look."* The vibration stopping mid-handoff is Symptom 4.

**What happens if the condition is never met:** Lenne holds the shard. She will not give it without understanding. Route A (Charter): she trades it for the reconstruction document and connection to the Conclave remnant's network. Route C: agents arrive; chaos; shard changes hands under pressure.

## GM Notes

- **The eight-year weight.** Do not play Lenne as a villain or an obstacle. She is a person who has been alone in a tower with an object she cannot understand, working too hard to admit she cannot understand it. Orik recognizes this immediately (decision-order: "What did I miss last time?"). Give him room to act on it.
- **DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-03.md`. Orik's arc-completion moment (naming the hall) requires witnessing, not directing.
- **Lenne after the session.** If the shard is given via Route D: Lenne has just been seen, clearly, by someone who didn't know her. This changes something. She is not instantly better; she is aware that she has been working in a way she cannot sustain. The Conclave connection (via the party) gives her a reason to change it.
