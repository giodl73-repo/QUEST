# Scene 03 — Research Floor (Floor 2)

**Location:** Volenn's Tower, second floor. **Time:** Day 2 afternoon.

**Read-aloud:**

> The research floor is organized the way a mind is organized when it has been working alone for a long time — perfectly logical to one person, impenetrable to anyone else. Notes cover the east wall floor to ceiling. A standing desk faces north. On the desk, in a padded cradle: the shard. It is slightly larger than the one in your pack. When Lenne picks it up to show you, her hand trembles — half a second, then still.

**Features:**

- **The shard.** On a padded cradle at the standing desk. Lenne keeps it in reach; she has been working with it daily. She picks it up when she talks about it — this is habit, not performance. **Symptom 1 fires here:** the vibration is visible; her hand trembles for half a second. She does not apologize for this. She explains: *"It responds to intent. I have documented 327 trials."* She slides a bound journal across the desk — the trial records. The journal is thorough. She has been doing real work.
- **The notes wall.** Three years of documentation, east wall. Investigation DC 12: she was trying to isolate the vibration response to specific intents (ambition, desire, fear). She has categorized ambition correctly. She has not identified the origin of the fragment or the inscription's significance — she cannot read High Istaran fluently.
- **The inscription.** Lenne shows them the inscription when they ask. Thessaly reads it automatically: *"Let no Order claim more than its share of the sky."* She says what it is. Lenne is quiet for a moment. Then: *"'Share of the sky.' That's lunar. Volenn had a moon-calendar. Third floor."*
- **Orik and the research floor.** He reads the room as a workspace. He sees: one standing desk (she never sits while working); two failed experiments on the shelf (burned-out foci, shattered crystal — she kept the failures, not just the successes); a bedroll in the corner (she sleeps up here sometimes rather than taking the stairs). He does not say anything yet.

**Encounter:** None unless the Unbound Conclave Route C pressure fires — if agents survived the road encounter and regrouped, they arrive at the tower during Scene 03 via the utility ladder (exterior bypass). Sera will hear them on the ladder (Perception passive 16). See `encounters/tower-intrusion.md`.

**Treasure:** Clause of Ambition (on the desk). See `treasures/clause-of-ambition.md`.

**P0 fallback — if Lenne refuses to give the shard:**
She will not give it if she does not understand what she is giving. Route A: she trades it for the reconstruction document + genuine network connection. Route C: agents arrive; shard changes hands under pressure. Route D: Orik's arc-completion fires.

**P0 failure state for Route C:** The agents take the shard; the party recovers it from them in the tower-intrusion encounter (see encounters/). The session ends with the shard in party hands regardless of route.

**Connections:** Scene 02 (stair down); Scene 04 (stair up); utility ladder (exterior, via window-hatch).

**GM Notes:**
- **Lenne's journal.** If the party examines it: real work, real data. She has been doing science on a magical object for eight years with no external validation. Calder will recognize this — it is similar to documenting plague symptoms alone in a village without a healer's college backing. Orik will recognize it differently.
- **DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-03.md`. Orik's arc-completion moment (naming the hall) requires the DM to stop and witness. The moment Lenne goes still is the moment to hold.
- **The reconstruction document as goodwill.** If Thessaly offers to share the reconstruction document here: Lenne reads it, compares it to her trial data, and says: *"Three of my documented vibration-patterns match these clauses. I've been measuring the compact this whole time without knowing it."* Goodwill gain. She is more likely to give the shard via Route A.
