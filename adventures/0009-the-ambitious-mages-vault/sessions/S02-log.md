# S02 LOG — The Ambitious Mage's Vault

## Session Summary

Two Unbound Conclave agents were on the road. The party fought them in the Kharolis foothills, one mile from the tower — a Medium encounter that lasted 1.5 rounds before Osvin, frightened and at 8 HP from Orik's Menacing Attack, called retreat. The hired crossbowman surrendered when the principals fled. No PC took damage. Calder used his first spell slot of the campaign (Bless).

Lenne Stormwatch had been watching from the tower's narrow window. She came down when the party knocked. She said: *"You're not Unbound Conclave."* She was right. They weren't.

Thessaly showed her the reconstruction document. Lenne matched three of her vibration-trial patterns to the compact clauses immediately — eight years of real work suddenly contextualized. She took them upstairs.

On the research floor, Orik read the room. He read the storage crates (four-month gap), the standing desk (she never sits), the bedroll in the corner (three years old), the failed experiments in order on the shelf. He read a person who has been alone too long, working too hard, because stopping feels like asking whether it was worth it. He had seen this before. He named it: the hall, the oil, the fire, the seven apprentices. *"The shard vibrates for me because I'm still trying to prove it was worth going."* He put the shard down. Lenne went still. Then she picked it up, said *"Eight years. I thought it was a power source,"* and handed it to him. The vibration stopped before it left her palm.

In Volenn's old study: the moon-calendar. Thessaly read Volenn's annotation — *"Triple convergence. All three moons full simultaneously. First occurrence since the Cataclysm. If the clauses were written to align with this event — the compact can only be spoken whole when the sky is right."* She said: *"Three months."* She copied the annotation verbatim. Hint 2 delivered.

On the roof: Lenne calculated that Volenn died seven years and nine months before the convergence. He was supposed to be here for it. He is not. She asked to come with the party. Thessaly said they'd talk about it. Orik watched the road.

### Scene 03 — Research Floor

The shard trembled in Lenne's hand — half a second. She showed them 327 trial entries. She showed them the inscription. Thessaly read it automatically: *"'Share of the sky.' That's lunar."*

Lenne: *"Volenn had a moon-calendar. Third floor."*

Then Orik spoke.

*"I know what it looks like when someone can't stop working because stopping means asking whether it was worth it. I watched seven people do it in a hall I was paid to guard. The shard vibrates for me because I'm still trying to prove it was worth going."*
