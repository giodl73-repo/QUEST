## Critique

### Overall assessment

The road ambush is the Tactician's first real data point in this campaign, and it is clean. Eleven rolls, two rounds, no fudges. Initiative order mattered: Sera's CRIT (24) gave her Round 1 double-action via Commander's Strike; Calder's slow init (5) meant Bless went in after the enemy's first actions, requiring Orik and Sera to manage a round without the bonus. The frightened condition produced the retreat — not HP attrition. This is mechanically well-designed. The session earns its 9 in Mechanical Fairness.

The one Tactician complaint is that the arc-completion scene (Research Floor) ran entirely on heuristics, not dice. A moment this significant — Orik naming his grief to an NPC who holds the shard — should have had at least one optional roll. The module chose not to include one. The choice is defensible but the Tactician notes it.

### What worked

- **Round 1, Orik Menacing Attack:** Menacing Attack vs. Osvin (+7) → [14] = 21 HIT; damage 15 → Osvin 23→8 HP; Wis save DC 15 → [4] FAIL → Osvin frightened. This is exactly right. The Battle Master used his superiority die at the correct moment (when the target was already below half HP, making the frightened condition the knockout blow). The retreat fired from mechanics, not DM fiat.

- **Commander's Strike compound:** Orik's Commander's Strike (bonus action) gave Sera a reaction attack on the highwayman. Sera: Longbow → [11] = 18 HIT; damage 7 → Highwayman 11→4 HP. Two-character mechanical compound in Round 1. Cross-character action economy used correctly. This is the Tactician's greenlight: initiative order produced a specific party-coordination moment that would not have been possible if Calder had gone first.

### What needs work

- **The arc-completion scene has no optional roll.** Orik's naming of the hall, the oil, the fire — the central emotional beat of the session — happened without a dice line. This is a design choice; the module treats grief-paragraph surfacing as automatic if the player reads the room correctly. The Tactician is not opposed to this in principle, but the absence of even an optional Investigation or Insight roll means the scene has no mechanical texture. What if Orik had read the room wrong? The module doesn't say.

- **Lenne joining creates unresolved party-composition mechanics.** She's a fifth party member with unspecified combat abilities. The module ends the session without establishing her mechanical role. The Tactician wants to know: if there's a Hard encounter in S03, what does Lenne do? Table Readiness gains 1 point back from the S01 baseline, but the Lenne gap partially offsets this.

### The question I'd push on

The road ambush was a Medium encounter that ended in 1.5 rounds with no PC damage. This is a functional warm-up encounter, not a meaningful tactical test. Was it supposed to challenge the party or just establish that enemies exist? If the campaign's first real tactical encounter is intentionally soft, the Tactician wants to know when the first Hard encounter is designed.
