## Critique

### Overall assessment

The Roleplayer's highest score in the campaign so far. Orik's arc-completion in Scene 03 is the single best piece of character work in the workshop's history for a second session. The grief-paragraph content surfaced not as self-disclosure but as *explanation* — Orik named his grief in order to explain the artifact's mechanism to Lenne. The naming was in service of understanding, not performance. This is the rarest kind of character moment: the PC reveals themselves by trying to help someone else understand something. The scene earned every line.

### What worked

- **Scene 03 — Orik names the hall:** *"I know what it looks like when someone can't stop working because stopping means asking whether it was worth it. I watched seven people do it in a hall I was paid to guard. They worked late without supervision because they wanted to prove themselves. I left to buy something and when I came back the hall was gone. The shard vibrates for me because I'm still trying to prove it was worth going."* This is not a monologue. It is a room-reading out loud. Every sentence is Orik doing what he does — observing, cataloguing, constructing a picture — and the picture he constructs is himself. No other PC at this table could have given this speech. Sheet-deep-reader confirmed.

- **Calder reading Volenn's note as a dying man's to-do:** Scene 04. *"He knew. He was planning to address it."* He did not finish the thought. The party saw him go quiet. The Roleplayer wants to cite this as an instance of the character's grief paragraph activating silently — Calder sat with forty-one people's deaths; he knows how to read the last plans of someone who ran out of time. He said two sentences and the rest was visible on his face. This is voice consistency at its best.

### What needs work

- **Sera's hold-space pattern is confirmed but underexplored.** Three consecutive instances of Sera giving someone space. This is act-without-announcement firing consistently, which is wonderful for player-style documentation. But the Roleplayer wants to see Sera given a moment where *her* silence is the subject — where someone else gives her space. She has been the space-giver for two full sessions. What is she carrying that she hasn't named?

- **Lenne joins the party at the end of the session and her voice is not yet established.** She says *"I want to come with you"* and Thessaly says *"We'll talk about it."* Fair staging. But Lenne's addition to the party means the Roleplayer will need four or five character voices to track in S03. If the DM doesn't establish her voice quickly, she will default to "the smart mage who provides exposition."

### The question I'd push on

The vibration stopped before the shard left Lenne's palm. Both felt it. Neither spoke. The Roleplayer is satisfied with the silence in the moment — but wants to know whether this will ever be spoken aloud. The compact's design has Orik carrying the Ambition shard all the way to the finale. Does he know, in his body, what the vibration stopping means about his grief? Or does he carry it as a data point he has not yet processed?
