## Critique

### Overall assessment

The session is dramatically more accessible than S01 because it has eleven dice rolls — and they are narrated clearly. A new player reading this log understands initiative order, attack rolls, damage, saves, and the frightened condition all from the combat narrative alone. The road ambush is a clean tutorial: *"CRIT initiative" → "Menacing Attack" → "frightened" → "retreat."* The arc-completion scene that follows does not require mechanical knowledge to follow. This is the correct structure: mechanical grounding in combat, emotional depth in social scenes.

### What worked

- **The combat is narrated with full roll transparency.** Every hit shows the to-hit total; every damage shows the dice and result. *"Orik Menacing Attack vs. Osvin (+7) → [14] = 21 HIT | Damage 1d8+4+1d8 → [6,5] = 15."* A new player reading this can check the math, can see what "+7" means as a modifier, can understand why Orik "goes for the target with the most damage already on them." This is table readiness at its best — mechanical transparency without stopping the fiction.

- **Orik's grief-paragraph explanation is accessible to a newcomer.** *"I know what it looks like when someone can't stop working because stopping means asking whether it was worth it."* This requires no Dragonlance knowledge. The Fresh Face can follow it entirely on its own terms: a person who survived a fire, who has been counting exits ever since, recognizes another person counting in a different way. The setting-specific content (the shard, the compact, High Sorcery) is context; the human core is universal.

### What needs work

- **"Red Robe" is used without glossing.** Lenne identifies Mira as "Red Robe trained" and Mira confirms this by recognizing "Volenn's style." A new player has no context for what robe colors mean in this world. The session needed one sentence from a PC — or from Mira herself — that translates this for a newcomer. Compare: "Red Robe trained — she uses the balanced school, neither the White Robe's restrictive practice nor the Black Robe's expansive one" would have taken eight words and cost nothing.

- **Calder's Mishakal symbols are mentioned but not explained.** Lenne reads the party on arrival and notes Calder's Mishakal symbols. A new player doesn't know that Mishakal is Krynn's goddess of healing, or that her worship was suppressed in the pre-Cataclysm period, or why a Life cleric's symbols would be a meaningful identity marker in this world. The detail is atmospheric; it could be accessible.

### The question I'd push on

The convergence is 88 days away and the party now knows this. A new player reading this log has just been told their characters have less than three months to find five more compact clauses. The Fresh Face wants to know: does the session communicate the *stakes* of failure clearly enough? What happens if the compact is not spoken at the convergence? The session mentions "the next opportunity is three centuries" but doesn't say what that means for the people living under the Orders now.
