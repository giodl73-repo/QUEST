## Critique

### Overall assessment

This is a strong Improviser session because the two most significant events — Orik's arc-completion and Lenne asking to join the party — were not scripted as outcomes. The module designed the arc-completion *condition* (Orik reads the room, names his grief-form ambition); the *moment* of it was heuristic-emergent. The arc-completion-as-opening (Lenne joining) was not in the module at all. These are genuine off-book wins. The Improviser also notes that the combat — while scripted as an encounter — was resolved faster and cleaner than designed, which demonstrates the party's tactical competence rather than DM soft-pedaling.

### What worked

- **Orik initiated arc-completion without prompting.** The module designed a Scene 03 where the arc-completion *could* fire if Orik read the room and named his grief. It did not script Orik to initiate. Orik read the bedroll, the crates, the standing desk, and spoke. This is the Improviser's highest praise: the player tried something the module couldn't guarantee — speaking from his own grief-paragraph as an explanation — and the DM (the marathon-runner) honored it fully. The vibration stopping mid-handoff (Symptom 4) was the yes-and: the artifact confirmed the naming was real.

- **Lenne asks to join the party.** This was not in the module. She made the offer from situation logic after her arc-completion fired. *"He was the one who was supposed to — How many shards do you have?"* The DM correctly responded with Thessaly's *"We'll talk about it"* — a yes that defers commitment without closing the door. This is fair adjudication of an off-book NPC request: neither forced yes nor arbitrary no.

### What needs work

- **The road ambush was somewhat scripted.** Osvin's retreat at 8 HP (frightened, unable to approach Orik) was the designed outcome. The combat played exactly as designed. The Improviser is not opposed to designed combat — encounters should have predictable outcomes for CR-calibration reasons — but this means the session's only combat encounter was not a site for improvisation. The party fought the encounter that was put in front of them in the way the module expected.

- **Thessaly showed the reconstruction document without giving it.** This is a good off-book judgment call (the player decided to create goodwill without surrendering leverage). But the DM didn't need to adjudicate this — Lenne responded to the showing appropriately without any friction. The Improviser wants to know: if Thessaly had given the document, would the outcome have been different? The module should make that choice matter.

### The question I'd push on

Lenne is now a party member. She was designed as an NPC with an arc-completion. The Improviser wants to know how the marathon-runner handles a new full PC who arrived by improvisation rather than by design. If the party-builder hasn't defined Lenne's heuristics, what does S03 look like when she has to make a decision under pressure?
