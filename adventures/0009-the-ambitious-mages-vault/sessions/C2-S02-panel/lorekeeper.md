## Critique

### Overall assessment

The session's lore density increases significantly from S01. Volenn's tower is a lived-in research site — the standing desk (she never sits), the bedroll in the corner (three years old), the failed experiments in order on the shelf. Each detail implies a specific scholarly temperament over a specific duration. The Lorekeeper values this kind of archaeology: the room tells you who Lenne is before Lenne speaks. The moon-calendar annotation — *"First occurrence since the Cataclysm"* — is the best single setting detail in the session, because it anchors the campaign's deadline to the world's most significant historical event without requiring a lecture.

### What worked

- **Volenn's annotation: "First occurrence since the Cataclysm."** This is exactly how Dragonlance lore should be embedded — as a date reference that implies the full weight of history without requiring explanation. A Dragonlance reader knows what the Cataclysm means; the Fresh Face can infer a major historical rupture from the phrase's tone. The 88-day deadline is now simultaneously a personal urgency (the convergence window) and a historical reckoning (three hundred years since the last attempt). Good.

- **Orik's room-reading of Lenne's research site:** The four-month delivery gap, the three-year-old bedroll, the failed experiments in sequence. This is the Lorekeeper's greenlight: a PC's background expertise (hall-guard; reading spaces; noting what people have and haven't done) deployed to produce setting-specific interpretation. Orik is doing what an eight-year hall-guard would do. The tower reveals itself through his professional lens.

### What needs work

- **The Unbound Conclave's motivation is still thin.** Osvin and the Quiet One are in the Kharolis foothills to steal a compact clause. Why does the Unbound Conclave want the clauses? What is their stated reason — not the party's interpretation, but the Conclave's own justification? Osvin's opening line ("Last chance — give us the shard and this ends clean") gives us a combatant, not a faction with coherent goals. The Lorekeeper wants to know what the Conclave believes before it becomes a combat problem.

- **"Red Robe" and the three-robe system are introduced without glossing.** Lenne: *"Red Robe trained. Or was."* Mira, recognizing the read: *"Volenn's style."* A new reader has no context for what "Red Robe" means in this setting — the Towers of High Sorcery, the Test, the robe colors as alignment indicators. The session misses the Accessibility Policy's first-use requirement here.

### The question I'd push on

Volenn "was supposed to be here" for the convergence. He has been dead for seven years and nine months. Who knew he was working on the compact? The Lorekeeper wants to understand whether Volenn's work was secret, institutional, or lonely — and whether anyone in the current Orders' structure knows a systematic reconstruction effort has been underway in an old mage's tower in the Kharolis foothills.
