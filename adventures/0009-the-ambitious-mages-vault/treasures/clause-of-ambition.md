# The Clause of Ambition

*The second shard. Slightly larger than the first. When it vibrates, you feel it before you hear it.*

## Appearance

A fragment of the same obsidian tablet as the Clause of Witness — the break-pattern is consistent. Roughly 12 cm. The inscription is in the same archaic High Istaran hand: *"Let no Order claim more than its share of the sky."* Below: the personal sigil of Archmage Toven of the Red Robes (PC 190; died PC 218, two years before the Cataclysm). The sigil is a small stylized flame — distinctive, memorable.

The shard is slightly warmer than the Clause of Witness. Not by much. The warmth feels different: more active, less patient.

## Presence / Desire

The Clause of Ambition wants to be *used*. It has been in Lenne's possession for eight years, and it has been vibrating the whole time — a faint half-second tremor when she picks it up, every time. She thinks this is a power response to intent. She is not wrong; she is reading the symptom correctly but misidentifying the source.

The shard does not reward ambition. It measures it. The vibration is recognition, not approval.

## Provenance

1. **Archmage Toven of the Red Robes (PC 190–218):** Swore the Clause at the original Conclave Compact signing. Died two years before the Cataclysm. A pragmatist; he wrote this clause because he had watched the White Robes accumulate more tower access than they needed and the Black Robes take political influence that was not theirs. He believed in the compact because he believed in limits.
2. **Tower of High Sorcery at Istar (PC 218–220):** Destroyed by the Cataclysm. Shard scattered.
3. **Unnamed salvage operation (PC 220–unknown):** Found in the ruins; sold as "resonant obsidian, Red Robe provenance."
4. **Three intermediate holders (unknown, ~200 years):** The shard passed through several mage's hands; each kept it for 10–30 years. No record survives.
5. **Magister Volenn Ashford, Red Robe (30 years ago – 8 years ago):** Purchased at a Palanthas specialty auction. Catalogued it accurately — he knew High Istaran and identified the clause — but chose to study it rather than return it to the Conclave. He died before deciding what to do with it.
6. **Lenne Stormwatch (8 years ago – present):** Inherited the tower and everything in it. She has been holding the shard longer than anyone in recorded provenance.

## Properties

**Passive:** Vibrates faintly (half-second tremor, felt before heard) when held by a creature with genuine ambition. This is not a concentration effect; the shard is reading intent continuously. Creature must be genuinely ambitious, not merely motivated — the shard does not vibrate for duty or fear alone.

**Passive:** Arcana DC 12 to identify as a magical fragment of compact origin. Thessaly: automatic (passive 18).

**Passive:** A creature who holds it for more than 1 minute and succeeds on Insight DC 14 understands that the vibration is recognition, not power — the shard is measuring them, not fueling them.

**Hint 2 connection:** The Clause of Ambition's inscription (*"Let no Order claim more than its share of the sky"*) refers specifically to moon-alignment. A Red Robe who reads it (or any PC with Arcana DC 14) understands that "share of the sky" means the lunar cycle — each Order's power peaks at different moon phases. This connects directly to the moon-calendar in Volenn's old study.

## Curse

**The Measure:** A creature who attunes to the shard becomes acutely aware, once per day, of any moment when they are claiming more than their share — of resources, attention, credit, power. This is not a mechanical penalty. It is a specific awareness, delivered as an inward recognition: *"This is more than your share."* The creature may ignore this recognition; the shard does not enforce it. It simply notes it.

Additionally: the attuned creature has disadvantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks when arguing for more than a fair share of something. The shard makes overreach visible, and others sense it.

**Ending:** As with all compact shards, the curse releases when the compact is spoken whole at adventure 0014.

## GM Notes

- **Lenne's eight years of holding it.** She has been ambitious — genuinely ambitious, legitimately ambitious — for eight years with this shard in her hands. She has also been alone. The shard has been measuring her ambition and she has had no one to compare it against. This is the core of her situation.
- **Orik and the vibration.** If Orik holds the shard, it vibrates. He is ambitious — he wants to not have failed; he wants to have been there; he wants to have saved the seven apprentices. That is ambition in its grief-form. The shard recognizes it. Let Orik know the shard vibrates when he holds it.
- **The inscription and Hint 2.** The phrase "share of the sky" should prompt Thessaly to ask what Volenn's moon-calendar shows. This is the delivery path for Hint 2.
