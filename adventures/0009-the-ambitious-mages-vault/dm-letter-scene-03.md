# DM Letter — Scene 03 (The Research Floor: Orik's Arc-Completion)

*This letter is for you, the DM. Read it before the session.*

---

## What this scene is

Orik watched seven people die in a fire he could have prevented. He was buying pine resin oil. He came back and the hall was gone.

Lenne has been working alone in a tower for eight years, driving herself past safe limits, not stopping when she should, because stopping feels like admitting the work wasn't worth it.

Orik recognizes her. He doesn't have to know her story to recognize the pattern — he knows what it looks like when someone can't stop working because stopping means asking whether it was worth it. He watched seven people do it. He was paid to watch and he left for two hours.

He is not going to save her. He is going to tell her something true about what her pattern costs. That is all.

---

## What to do before it starts

- Read Orik's grief paragraph: "the hall, the oil, the pine resin, the fire. Seven apprentices. He has been buying pine resin whenever he doesn't need it, to make the smell ordinary. It hasn't worked."
- Read Lenne's emotional position: she can't say she's afraid the work has been a waste. She will respond when someone tells her the work was worth doing even if the object is not what she thought.
- These two things are not the same thing. Orik's true thing and Lenne's needed thing are adjacent, not identical. When he speaks, she will receive it the way she can receive it, not the way he delivers it.

---

## What to do during

**Orik will decide when to speak.** His decision-order is: "What did I miss last time?" — the heuristic that has been active since he walked in and read the storage crate dates and saw the bedroll in the corner. He has been building toward this since Scene 02. Let him choose the moment.

**The moment Orik speaks:** Stop. Do not add narration before he finishes. Do not prompt him further. If the marathon-runner is generating this: the scene_narration beat for this moment should contain only Orik's words and Lenne's response — no ambient atmosphere, no "the room is quiet," nothing. Just the words.

**Lenne's response:** She goes still. Longer than feels comfortable. This is correct. She is receiving something. Do not fill the pause. After the pause: she picks up the shard. It vibrates. She says: *"Eight years. I thought it was a power source."* She hands it to Orik. The vibration stops before it leaves her palm. That is Symptom 4.

**If Orik doesn't speak in Scene 03:** He may speak on the roof (Scene 05). Lenne mentions she hasn't been to the market in four months; this is the opening. If he takes it, the arc-completion fires there.

**If Orik never speaks:** Route A (she trades for the reconstruction document + network connection) or Route C (agents arrive). The campaign continues. The arc is patient.

---

## What ends the scene

The scene ends when:
- The shard changes hands (Route A: traded; Route D: given freely; Route C: taken under pressure).
- Lenne points them to Volenn's old study (Scene 04) for the moon-calendar.
- The party has what they came for.

---

## What happens if the condition is not met

Lenne is not destroyed if the arc-completion doesn't fire. She is still a Red Robe wizard working alone in a tower. The party can return. She is not a one-session NPC.

If Route C fires and agents intrude: she fights alongside the party. After, she is shaken enough to consider Route A. The arc-completion can fire in the aftermath — Orik speaks in the quiet after the combat, when Lenne is more open.

---

*The hall burned eight years before Lenne arrived in her tower. Neither of them knows the other's timing. The pattern between them is the same. Orik will know it when he sees it. Let him speak when he's ready.*
