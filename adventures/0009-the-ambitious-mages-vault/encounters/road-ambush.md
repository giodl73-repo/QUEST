# Encounter — Road Ambush (Non-optional)

**Location:** Kharolis foothills mining road, 1 mile before the tower.
**Trigger:** Always fires. Osvin and the quiet one have been waiting here for two days.

**Context:** They recognized the party in Korrath. They got to the tower first, confirmed the shard is there, couldn't get it (Volenn's ward made the shard inert for them), and fell back to the road to intercept. They are better prepared than last time — they know the party's formation (Orik front, Sera flanking, Calder rear, Thessaly center-rear), and they have the cart as a bottleneck.

## Encounter balance

**Osvin and the Quiet One:** Same stats as Korrath (Thug reskin). **BUT:**
- Osvin has expended his Thunderwave (he used it at the tower trying to shake the shard loose; it failed because of the ward). He has a Dagger of Warning instead (stolen from a fallen Conclave agent three months ago): advantage on initiative for him and all allies within 30 ft.
- The Quiet One has upgraded his rope to a Bola (Dex save DC 13 or restrained for 1 round, no concentration).
- **Third element:** A hired Kharolis highwayman (Bandit stat block: AC 12, HP 11, Shortsword +4, Light Crossbow +4) is positioned in the treeline with a crossbow. He is not Unbound Conclave; he is paid. He surrenders if either principal is downed.

**Adjusted XP:** 2 Thugs (100 XP each) + 1 Bandit (25 XP) = 225 base. ×2 multiplier (3 enemies) = 450 adjusted.
**Party budget (Medium, L5, 4 PCs):** ~900 XP. **This is a Medium encounter.** Harder than the Korrath non-encounter.

**Encounter-balancer check:**
- Instakill threat: Osvin's advantage on initiative could give him first action; his mace (+4, 1d6+2 = avg 5) is not instakill. Bola restrain (Quiet One) could lock a PC for one round — not instakill but creates action economy cost. **Orange flag:** The Bola + Osvin's first attack = one PC restrained while Osvin attacks them (Pack Tactics on the restrained target). Manageable with L5 HP pools. Not blocking.
- Action economy: Party 4 actions vs. enemies 3 actions. Party advantage.
- Tactical choices: (1) Priority target = Osvin (leader; Dagger of Warning initiative bonus disrupts party rhythm if he acts first). (2) Environmental: cart across road = half-cover for enemies behind it; Sera can use it as a platform if she moves to it. (3) Non-combat resolution: Osvin will call retreat if reduced to 12 HP or fewer — he will shout "Stand down! We'll find another way." (4) Hired highwayman: surrenders immediately if principals downed; he will talk.

**Tactics:**
Round 1: Osvin calls out from behind the cart: *"Last chance — give us the shard and this ends clean."* He is not bluffing (he would prefer not to fight; his orders are "retrieve the shard," not "kill the Conclave wardens"). If the party refuses or attacks, combat begins.
Round 2+: Quiet One targets whoever holds the Clause of Witness (Thessaly, if she has it in her pack). Osvin attacks whichever PC is most forward. Highwayman fires from treeline.
**Retreat trigger:** Osvin at ≤12 HP or Quiet One downed. *"We'll find another way."* They disengage and flee into the treeline. The highwayman throws down his crossbow immediately.

**Capture option:** If the party subdues Osvin: he will confirm — the Unbound Conclave knew about the party's mission from Korrath (the courier sent a report); they have been tasked with collecting all seven shards; he does not know where the other five are. He knows Lenne Stormwatch's name and that she has "the second shard."

**XP award:** 225 XP × 2 multiplier = 450 adjusted. Divided by 4 = 112 XP per PC.
