# Encounter — Night Assault

## Overview

The Reconstitution assault force has targeted the inner gate on night watch, when garrison alertness is lowest. Six soldiers with a battering frame, two crossbow-equipped shield-bearers, and a sergeant directing from behind. This is coordinated — the siege commander has adjusted after earlier assaults failed. The Reconstitution's goal is not to hold the gate; it is to break it so the morning assault can exploit the damage.

**Design intent:** This combat costs 35–50% of party HP (targeting 50–65% HP at session end). The 1-garrison-potion limit is the session's mechanical pressure point — Sava must decide when to use it. The Supply Die degrades to d4. The assault is larger and better-coordinated than previous engagements. A Hard-to-Deadly encounter for an L3 party of 4.

**XP budget:** 6 × Reconstitution Soldier (CR 1/2, 100 XP each) + 1 × Reconstitution Sergeant (CR 1, 200 XP) = 800 XP × 1.5 (adjusted for group size/type) = **1,200 adjusted XP.** At 4 PCs L3, Hard threshold is 900 / Deadly is 1,600. This lands at the Hard-to-Deadly boundary. **Intentional.**

## Tactics

### Round 1

- Frame-carriers advance to gate arch (Room 01). Two shield-crossbow soldiers hold at 40 feet, firing suppression on the party. Sergeant directs from 50 feet, calls targets.
- Frame does not reach the gate bar on Round 1 — they need Round 2.
- Party opportunity: disrupt frame-carriers before they reach the gate. If two frame-carriers are incapacitated, the frame drops and the other two must act to pick it up (next round).

### Round 2

- Frame reaches gate arch if not disrupted. Ram attempt (2d6+4 to gate bar). Gate bar holds on DC 14 Str check (built to Asholt's specifications — it holds, but the DC starts at 14 and drops by 2 each successful hit).
- Sergeant pushes forward to 30 feet, inside the corridor. He uses Tactical Command to redeploy if a frame-carrier is down.
- Shield-crossbow soldiers advance to 30 feet (half cover from arch walls; +2 AC).

### Round 3+

- If frame is still intact: second ram attempt. Gate DC is now 12 (or 10 if the party let two hits land). A third ram attempt at DC 8 succeeds — the bar cracks.
- If frame is disrupted: soldiers go to melee. The assault becomes a corridor fight in Room 01 and potentially Room 02 if soldiers push through.
- Party's choice: hold the corridor (choke point, outnumbered 6-to-4 but with position advantage) or break the crossbow suppression first.

## Portent Condition (Optional — Caelith's Last Die)

**Caelith's Portent [6] may fire in this encounter.** The die has been carried for four sessions.

**Best designed trigger:** The sergeant's Tactical Command maneuvering a frame-carrier past Talis's position. If Talis is in position to intercept (DC 14 Athletics check to maintain blocking position), Caelith can replace this with his Portent [6]. A 6 succeeds for Talis.

**Alternative trigger:** The gate bar's DC check. The party may ask Caelith to apply his die to the bar's resistance roll (treated as a Str check by the gate itself — conceptually, Caelith sees the gate hold before it rolls). A 6 on a DC 12 or lower succeeds; marginal on DC 14.

**If Portent is not used:** The die carries to S5 — but S5 is the last designed opportunity before the campaign finale. DM note: the die has been carried 4 sessions as of S4. If not used in S4 or S5, it expires with the next long rest (which follows the finale).
