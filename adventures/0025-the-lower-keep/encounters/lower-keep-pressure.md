# Lower Keep Pressure Table

*After the assault breaks and the door opens, the keep is not still. Garrison members are awake, wounded, reporting. Asholt will hear about this. The question is when and from whom. Roll once per scene transition after Room 03 opens (d6).*

## Pressure Table (d6)

| Roll | Event |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Contained.** The garrison soldiers who were in Room 02 are tending to their own wounded and have not yet reported to anyone senior. The party has a scene transition with full privacy in Rooms 04–05 before anyone asks questions. |
| 3 | **Sergeant Irr comes looking.** Tomek Irr (from S1) appears at the edge of Room 02 after the combat ends. He does not enter the east passage. He looks at the party, looks at the open door, and waits. He is not hostile — he is giving the party a chance to say something before he decides what to say to Asholt. **DC 12 Persuasion** to ask him to wait an hour; on a success, he agrees and stands post at Room 02's south entrance. On a failure or if not addressed: he goes to Asholt in the next 20 minutes. |
| 4 | **A garrison archer saw the door open.** They are not speaking yet — they are confused and slightly frightened. But they have a name: they can say "the east maintenance door opened and people came out" to Asholt if asked directly. The party has 30 minutes before the morning report. |
| 5 | **Dene hears boots on the north passage.** Asholt or his adjutant is doing a post-assault circuit — standard procedure. They will reach the north corridor (Room 05's outer door) within 10 minutes. Dene looks at the party and says nothing. He is waiting to see if they cover the door or open it themselves. |
| 6 | **Asholt is in Room 02.** He came to assess the gate damage himself. He is standing in the hall. He can see the open east-passage door. He has not walked down it yet. He is looking at whoever is nearest. **This is the window:** the party can speak to him here, in the hall, before he goes to the east passage himself. Whatever they say in the next exchange sets the frame for the S5 confrontation. |

## Notes

Roll this table at each of the following scene transitions: (1) Room 03 opens → party enters Room 04, (2) Room 04 → Room 05, (3) Room 05 → any report to garrison command. Three rolls maximum. A result of 5 or 6 on the final roll collapses the timeline — the party must decide whether to get ahead of Asholt or let him find it.

**The Asholt encounter (result 6):** This is not a combat trigger. Asholt is not angry — he is the Warden; anger is not his first tool. He is waiting for someone to explain what he is looking at. The party's response here is the S4 closing beat. If Davan speaks, this is where the Oath-collision line lands in a direct frame rather than a private one. Log verbatim.

**If Asholt finds the refugees himself (no party intervention):** He goes silent. He walks into Room 04 and stands at the entrance for a long moment. He does not speak to the party first. He speaks to Dene: *"How long."* Not a question — a demand. Dene answers: *"Three years."* Asholt turns and leaves. He does not speak to the party about it until S5. This is an acceptable outcome — but the party loses the initiative on the framing.
