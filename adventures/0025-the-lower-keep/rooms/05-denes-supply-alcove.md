# Room 05 — Dene's Supply Alcove

## Read-aloud

> A tight space off the north corridor — shelves floor to ceiling, a standing desk, a lamp. The shelves hold garrison stores. On the desk: two ledgers. One in the garrison's standard hand. One in a different hand — smaller, denser, cross-referenced.

## Features

- **The official ledger:** Standard garrison supply log — ration counts, arrow stock, medical materials, fuel. Written in the garrison quartermaster's formal hand. Accurate for items that go to garrison members.

- **The second ledger (Dene's private record):** Same format, different hand, different content. It lists 47 names by initials and family glyphs. Daily ration entries going back three years, cross-referenced with the official ledger by page number. The entries show exactly how Dene has been hiding the discrepancy: small overstatements in the official record across dozens of line items, individually undetectable, collectively substantial. **DC 14 Investigation to understand the full method.** DC 12 to see that it has been done deliberately and systematically.

- **The method:** Dene has been reporting slightly higher garrison figures to the supply chain — 52 soldiers where there are 48, 19 horses where there are 16 — and diverting the surplus. He used the supply cart route to bring in additional stores before the siege. After the siege began, he has been rationing the hidden supply more aggressively. **The supply shortfall the party is experiencing is partly because Dene is feeding 47 extra people from a fixed supply.** This is not a judgment — it is a fact about where the supplies are going.

- **Maret's entries:** The medical notes in the second ledger are in Maret's handwriting. Anyone who interacted with Maret in S2 can recognize her writing with DC 12 History (or auto if they worked closely with her). The notes are clinical — injury treatment, fever records, childbirth (one entry, two years ago), a death (six months ago, an elderly man, named only as "G.F."). Maret has been treating these people as patients.

- **Dene's personal note (at the back of the second ledger):** A single page, not an entry. Written in a more personal hand. *"If this is found: I did not tell the Warden because I did not trust him to make the right decision. I still do not know if I was right about that. I fed them because they were hungry. That was the decision. The rest is accounting."*

- **Supply implication:** DC 14 Investigation: the current supply shortfall can be calculated. The keep has approximately 8 days of supplies at current consumption — but only 4 days if it tries to sustain the additional 47 people at full rations. This is the pressure the party now holds. Informing Asholt means he must decide: continue feeding the refugees (4-day supply window) or not. **This is not a problem with a clean answer.**

## GM Notes

**The second ledger is the session's primary discovery document.** It gives the party the full picture: how long, how many, how it was hidden, and at what cost to the keep's overall supply. They now hold information that Asholt does not.

**The supply implication:** Do not over-explain this. Place it in the features, let the party find it if they investigate. If they ask Dene about the supply numbers, he answers honestly: *"I know. I have four days if nothing changes and I keep feeding everyone."* He does not ask the party to cover it. He does not ask them to report it. He is done asking for things. He gave the accounting.

**Davan in this room:** If Davan reads Dene's personal note, let it land. The note says: "I did not trust him to make the right decision." Davan has been following orders and trusting authority his entire career. He has paid for it once with Orel. This is a man who made the opposite choice. No comment from the DM. Just the note.

**Maret's presence in the ledger:** If the party realizes Maret knew, their relationship to her in S5+ is shifted. She kept this from them in S2 when they were working together. She did not lie — she simply did not say. Whether that is the same thing is a question the party will carry.

## Memory Fragment
