# Room 01 — Inner Gatehouse

## Read-aloud

> Three hammer blows on iron, then shouting. The passage ahead frames torchlight and running garrison shapes. The outer gate is holding — barely. Through the arch, you can see the battering frame: eight shoulders, moving fast, angled for the gate's left post.

## Features

- **The portcullis mechanism:** An iron-chain winch on the north wall of the gatehouse corridor. Dropping the portcullis requires an action and DC 14 Athletics. It stops the battering frame advance but also closes the party inside the outer approach — they would need to climb over or find another exit. **If dropped before the assault troops are dealt with:** the troops regroup at the portcullis (can't reach the party but can fire arrows through the gaps). The siege sergeant will call a retreat within 3 rounds if he cannot reach the gate.

- **Garrison positioning:** Two garrison archers are on the gatehouse roof slots (cannot be reached from the ground approach; they are firing down). Three garrison soldiers are at the gate arch trying to hold the bar. Davan's positioning instinct triggers here automatically: he moves to the arch to hold it.

- **The assault approach:** Eight Reconstitution soldiers in formation — six carrying the battering frame, two acting as shields with crossbows. Their sergeant is directing from 30 feet back. See `encounters/night-assault.md` for stat blocks and tactics.

- **Torchlight:** Two wall torches in the corridor. The outer face is lit by the assault party's held torches. Vision is adequate for combat; no darkness penalties inside.

## GM Notes

**This is the entry into combat.** The party arrives here when the alarm sounds. They do not have a chance to prepare — Sava cannot cast Mage Armor before the first round unless she held a slot open overnight (design assumes she did not). Caelith is without Mage Armor for the first round unless his Portent [6] changes circumstances.

**Davan's auto-positioning:** Per his heuristics, he moves to the arch without being told. He does not announce this. If the DM is tracking his signatures, note it in the log.

**The garrison soldiers at the gate arch:** They are not party members — they hold the bar but cannot fight effectively while doing so. The party is the mobile combat force. If any garrison member drops below half HP in this room, Davan's protect-trigger fires.

**Portcullis option:** The portcullis is a real choice with real consequences. Dropping it ends the immediate battering frame threat but changes the tactics (arrows through gaps). If Sava or Caelith is in danger and the party needs a breathing room, the portcullis buys it. Note in session log: portcullis used (yes/no).
