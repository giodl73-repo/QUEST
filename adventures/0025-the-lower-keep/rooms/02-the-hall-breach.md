# Room 02 — The Hall Breach

## Read-aloud

> The hall is chaos — garrison soldiers sprinting for positions, someone shouting counts. Two pillars down the center. An east passage you have never seen open. And noise from the gate behind you: the frame has not hit yet, but it will.

## Features

- **Two stone pillars:** Center of the hall, north-south axis. Provide half cover (+2 AC) to any creature adjacent to them. The Reconstitution sergeant will push troops toward these if he gets inside. The party can use them too — Talis has natural affinity for this cover.

- **The garrison scramble:** Four garrison soldiers arrive during Round 1–2 of combat, coming from the Great Hall (south). They are not organized — they are responding to the alarm. They take defensive positions (near door arches, south wall) rather than engaging at the gate. They are not an asset to combat; they are a presence that will witness what happens next.

- **The east passage (30 feet, ends at Room 03):** Visible from the south end of the hall during combat. The plank door at the far end is labeled "maintenance" in faded chalk. **It is closed at combat start.** It opens on Round 3 or later when the assault reaches maximum noise — the people inside can no longer stay quiet and the sound covers movement. The party may notice it open mid-combat: **passive 14 Perception** to see it happen; active attention required to see the face.

- **Combat flow into Room 02:** If the portcullis was not dropped and the outer gate bar fails, 2–3 Reconstitution soldiers push into the hall before the party stops them. Combat then continues here with the pillars as cover terrain. If the portcullis was dropped, combat stays at the arch and the hall breach scenario does not fully develop.

## GM Notes

**The east passage visibility:** The door is there to be noticed if anyone looks east during a lull. Do not call attention to it before Round 3. If Talis is active and her passive Perception applies, she notices the plank door is newer than the stone around it — it was not original construction. She can note this without knowing what it means.

**The garrison soldiers:** They are witnesses. When the door opens and the face appears, they will see it too — or some of them will. This is intentional: the refugee reveal is not just between the party and Dene. The garrison is going to know. What Davan says in this moment is said in front of garrison members, not in private.

**Pillars as tactical terrain:** Encourage the party to use them. Davan's position-first signature suggests he will take the north pillar as an anchor point for the gate defense. Talis behind the south pillar is natural. This sets up a clear field of fire for Caelith.

**Round 3 door signal:** When you open Room 03, do it in the middle of a round — during a moment of narrative lull (the battering frame has been pushed back, the assault is breaking, the garrison is catching its breath). The girl's face appears in the quiet between the noise. It lands harder that way.
