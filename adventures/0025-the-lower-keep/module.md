## Setting & Background

On day 19 of the siege, a major night assault drives the party and garrison to the edge of the inner gate, a door labeled "maintenance" opens in the chaos, and a child's face looks out — revealing that Quartermaster Dene Flor has been sheltering 47 refugees for three years without Warden Asholt's knowledge.

**Thorngate Keep, Night of Day 19.** The siege has been grinding for over two weeks. The Reconstitution has learned from its earlier assaults — this attack comes at the second watch, when exhaustion sits heaviest, and it concentrates on the inner gate's eastern approach rather than the walls. The garrison scrambles. The party holds the breach. In the chaos, a door that has never opened on any garrison tour swings wide.

**Hint 2 delivery:** The refugees exist. 47 people in the lower keep's eastern chambers — families, two elderly, six children, eleven men and women who cannot fight — have been fed by Dene Flor on falsified ration entries for three years. Warden Asholt does not know. His gate-captain Hessa Var did not know. Sergeant Tomek Irr did not know. **One person knew: Dene.** And one other: Maret Stave, who noted in her healer's log (S2) that she was dispensing medicines to patients not listed on the garrison rolls.

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S3 + 1 hit die of recovery (by DM calculation). Davan likely at 22–28 HP, others at similar partial recovery.
- **Supply Die:** d6 entering. **1 garrison potion available.** After combat: d6 → d4.
- **Sava spell slots:** Carry-over from S3. Design assumes approximately 2 L1, 1 L2 available.
- **Caelith Portent:** [6] carried from S3 (the last die; used [7] on Wil Fen). This die has been carried 4 sessions. **If used in S4, it resets only on long rest — still not happening.**

**Design intent:** The assault should cost real HP. Party ends at 50–65% of max HP. The 1-potion limit bites. Sava's slot use matters. The Supply Die degradation to d4 signals that the keep's resources are genuinely critical.

Second watch. The party is asleep or near-rest when the assault alarm rings — not the standard wall-walk bells but the inner gate's own signal: three sharp blows on iron. Something is wrong at the gate itself, not the walls.

By the time the party reaches Room 01, they can see the situation: the gate approach is already crowded with Reconstitution assault troops, a battering frame being carried forward under arrow cover.

The assault is a Fighter scene — positioning, holding a breach, protecting the gate while the garrison gets into position. Davan's Battle Master maneuvers (Precision Attack, Rally, Disarming Attack) have natural application here. But the spotlight is not the combat.

**The spotlight is what happens after Room 02 clears and Room 03 opens.**

Davan is standing in the cleared hall, bloodied, with the garrison around him, when the door opens. He sees the child's face. His first response — not tactical, not procedural — is the condition for Dene's token.

**Davan's Oath collision (named aloud):** In Room 04 or 05, with refugees behind the open door and the garrison still unsettled, Davan says the thing he has been not saying. The specific line: something about how the Oath was supposed to tell him who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell him this. This is the first layer of the accounting speech.

The Reconstitution assault force — six soldiers, Hard to Deadly for an L3 party — concentrates on the inner gate approach using a battering frame and arrow suppression. They have not been here before, but their sergeant knows the gate's geometry. The party must hold the outer side of the inner gate while the garrison bolts the inner locks.

See `encounters/night-assault.md`.

Room 03 — labeled "maintenance" on every garrison inspection — opens when the assault noise is loudest and its occupants can no longer stay hidden. The face at the door is a girl, maybe eight years old, who does not look frightened. She looks like someone who has been told what to do if this happened and is doing it.

**Character-logic condition (no roll):** If the party's FIRST RESPONSE to the face is human — speaking to the girl, moving toward her without a weapon raised, asking her name — Dene's token fires. If the first response is tactical (barricade the door, secure the area, report to Asholt), the window closes. It re-opens in S5 only if Davan's accounting speech lands.

**Dene's line (campaign-permanent):** *"Three years. The Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret."*

Spoken to the PC who responded to the child with humanity, not tactics. After the party is inside Room 04 and the assault has been broken. Before anyone has spoken to Asholt.

Among the 47 refugees is **Helin**, a woman in her mid-fifties. She watches Davan specifically during the first entry into Room 04. She says nothing this session except to the girl (her grandchild). But she watches Davan the way someone watches a person they recognize without being recognized back.

She knows why Saren Coldforge left Thorngate. She was here — not in this keep, but at the pass — twenty years ago, when the first group of non-Solamnic travelers came through in winter and Asholt sheltered them. She left before the incident with Saren. She knows what was said.

**She does not speak this in S4.** It is a seed. The party will encounter her again in S5 through Luca's egress route.

End-of-session handoff note: *"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: 'the Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this.' First line of the speech, spoken aloud, S4."*

## DM notes

**Combat terrain (Rooms 01–02):** The gatehouse approach is the assault choke point. Pillars in the Hall Breach provide partial cover. The portcullis mechanism in Room 01 is a tactical option — dropping it costs an action and requires DC 14 Athletics. The party's goal is to break the assault before the battering frame reaches the outer gate face.

**The door geometry:** Room 03's door faces the Hall Breach from the east passage. It is visible from the south end of Room 02 during combat — the party will see it if they look east during the fight. It opens while the fight is ongoing (Round 3 or later, when the assault noise is loudest). The girl's face appears in the doorframe during a lull or break in combat action.

**Refugee chamber access:** Room 04 connects to Room 05 via Dene's passage. The party can find the ledger in Room 05 without going through Room 04, but finding it through Dene (Room 04 → 05 with him) is the designed path.

### Scene 1 — Inner Gatehouse

> Three hammer blows on iron, then shouting. The passage ahead frames torchlight and running garrison shapes. The outer gate is holding — barely. Through the arch, you can see the battering frame: eight shoulders, moving fast, angled for the gate's left post.

#### Features

- **The portcullis mechanism:** An iron-chain winch on the north wall of the gatehouse corridor. Dropping the portcullis requires an action and DC 14 Athletics. It stops the battering frame advance but also closes the party inside the outer approach — they would need to climb over or find another exit. **If dropped before the assault troops are dealt with:** the troops regroup at the portcullis (can't reach the party but can fire arrows through the gaps). The siege sergeant will call a retreat within 3 rounds if he cannot reach the gate.

- **Garrison positioning:** Two garrison archers are on the gatehouse roof slots (cannot be reached from the ground approach; they are firing down). Three garrison soldiers are at the gate arch trying to hold the bar. Davan's positioning instinct triggers here automatically: he moves to the arch to hold it.

- **The assault approach:** Eight Reconstitution soldiers in formation — six carrying the battering frame, two acting as shields with crossbows. Their sergeant is directing from 30 feet back. See `encounters/night-assault.md` for stat blocks and tactics.

- **Torchlight:** Two wall torches in the corridor. The outer face is lit by the assault party's held torches. Vision is adequate for combat; no darkness penalties inside.

#### Encounter

**Encounter — Night Assault**

**Overview**

The Reconstitution assault force has targeted the inner gate on night watch, when garrison alertness is lowest. Six soldiers with a battering frame, two crossbow-equipped shield-bearers, and a sergeant directing from behind. This is coordinated — the siege commander has adjusted after earlier assaults failed. The Reconstitution's goal is not to hold the gate; it is to break it so the morning assault can exploit the damage.

**Design intent:** This combat costs 35–50% of party HP (targeting 50–65% HP at session end). The 1-garrison-potion limit is the session's mechanical pressure point — Sava must decide when to use it. The Supply Die degrades to d4. The assault is larger and better-coordinated than previous engagements. A Hard-to-Deadly encounter for an L3 party of 4.

**XP budget:** 6 × Reconstitution Soldier (CR 1/2, 100 XP each) + 1 × Reconstitution Sergeant (CR 1, 200 XP) = 800 XP × 1.5 (adjusted for group size/type) = **1,200 adjusted XP.** At 4 PCs L3, Hard threshold is 900 / Deadly is 1,600. This lands at the Hard-to-Deadly boundary. **Intentional.**.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- North: outer gate (outside walls; assault approach)
- South: Room 02 Hall Breach (20-ft corridor)
- Portcullis: can be dropped (closes north exit; see Features)

#### GM Notes

**This is the entry into combat.** The party arrives here when the alarm sounds. They do not have a chance to prepare — Sava cannot cast Mage Armor before the first round unless she held a slot open overnight (design assumes she did not). Caelith is without Mage Armor for the first round unless his Portent [6] changes circumstances.

**Davan's auto-positioning:** Per his heuristics, he moves to the arch without being told. He does not announce this. If the DM is tracking his signatures, note it in the log.

**The garrison soldiers at the gate arch:** They are not party members — they hold the bar but cannot fight effectively while doing so. The party is the mobile combat force. If any garrison member drops below half HP in this room, Davan's protect-trigger fires.

**Portcullis option:** The portcullis is a real choice with real consequences. Dropping it ends the immediate battering frame threat but changes the tactics (arrows through gaps). If Sava or Caelith is in danger and the party needs a breathing room, the portcullis buys it. Note in session log: portcullis used (yes/no).

### Scene 2 — The Hall Breach

> The hall is chaos — garrison soldiers sprinting for positions, someone shouting counts. Two pillars down the center. An east passage you have never seen open. And noise from the gate behind you: the frame has not hit yet, but it will.

#### Features

- **Two stone pillars:** Center of the hall, north-south axis. Provide half cover (+2 AC) to any creature adjacent to them. The Reconstitution sergeant will push troops toward these if he gets inside. The party can use them too — Talis has natural affinity for this cover.

- **The garrison scramble:** Four garrison soldiers arrive during Round 1–2 of combat, coming from the Great Hall (south). They are not organized — they are responding to the alarm. They take defensive positions (near door arches, south wall) rather than engaging at the gate. They are not an asset to combat; they are a presence that will witness what happens next.

- **The east passage (30 feet, ends at Room 03):** Visible from the south end of the hall during combat. The plank door at the far end is labeled "maintenance" in faded chalk. **It is closed at combat start.** It opens on Round 3 or later when the assault reaches maximum noise — the people inside can no longer stay quiet and the sound covers movement. The party may notice it open mid-combat: **passive 14 Perception** to see it happen; active attention required to see the face.

- **Combat flow into Room 02:** If the portcullis was not dropped and the outer gate bar fails, 2–3 Reconstitution soldiers push into the hall before the party stops them. Combat then continues here with the pillars as cover terrain. If the portcullis was dropped, combat stays at the arch and the hall breach scenario does not fully develop.

### Scene 3 — The Lower Keep Door

> The east passage ends at a plank door. It stands open. A girl, maybe eight, holds the frame. She does not look frightened. She looks like someone who was told what to do and is doing it.

#### Features

- **The door itself:** Plank wood, iron-banded on the hinge side. Normally locked (Dene's key; also DC 14 Dexterity with thieves' tools to open). It has been opened from inside. The lock mechanism is intact — it was not forced. The chalk lettering above reads "MAINTENANCE" in an older hand than the door itself. The door is visibly newer than the stone arch it sits in.

- **The girl:** Named Sera by Dene (the party may learn this). Eight years old. She has been here since she was five — she does not fully remember living anywhere else. She was told: if the noise gets very loud and the soldiers come to the east passage, open the door and stand in it. She is doing that. She does not speak to the party unless spoken to.

- **Character-logic condition (no roll):** The party's FIRST response to her determines Dene's token.
  - **Human (token fires):** Speak to her. Move toward her without a weapon raised. Ask her name. Crouch to her level. Any of these.
  - **Tactical (token window closes):** Barricade the door. Secure the area. Report to Asholt or Sergeant Irr immediately. Draw a weapon.
  - **Ambiguous:** DM judgment. If the first action is to check whether she is a threat (DC 12 Insight) but the framing is gentle and the movement is non-threatening, count it as human. If the first framing is "secure the door," count it as tactical.

- **What the girl does if the party responds humanly:** She steps back into the passage. She says: *"Dene said you might come."* She leads them to Room 04.

- **What the girl does if the party responds tactically:** She steps back inside and closes the door. Dene opens it from inside within one round — he has been listening. But the moment is gone.

- **The passage (10 feet, 6 steps down):** Beyond the door, stone steps descend to Room 04. The air here is different — warmer, smelling of smoke and bodies and stored grain. This is not maintenance space. This is lived space.

#### Encounter

None. Combat is over before this door opens, or ending as it opens. There is no fight here.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- West: Room 02 Hall Breach (30-ft passage, east face)
- East/Down: Room 04 First Chamber (6 steps down; the opened door)

#### GM Notes

**The door opens mid-session, not after.** This is the design intent: the assault is breaking, the garrison is catching its breath, and the door opens. The party does not have a clean transition moment. They are still in post-combat state — weapons out, HP down, garrison around them. The girl's face appears in that condition. What they do in the next five seconds is the test.

**Dene is audible:** If anyone has passive Perception 13+, they can hear quiet movement behind the door before it opens — shuffling, someone shushing, then the door moving. This is not a surprise.

**The garrison witnesses:** At least one garrison soldier is in Room 02's line of sight to this passage. When the door opens, they see it. They do not react violently — they are as stunned as the party. But they will remember it. Asholt will hear about this whether the party reports it or not.

**Dene's position:** Dene is in Room 04, not at the door. He sent Sera to the door because he knew a uniformed soldier at the threshold might escalate. He is listening. He will come forward when he hears how the party responds.

### Scene 4 — The First Chamber

> Low ceiling, two hearths banked to coals. Pallets cover the floor — dozens of them. People are awake. They are watching the door. Nobody moves. A man steps forward from the back wall, carrying a ledger.

#### Features

- **47 people:** Adults, elderly, children. Faces from across the Kharolis region — not exclusively Solamnic, which is the first thing the party will notice if they look. They are not garrison. They are not prisoners — no restraints, no guards. They are people who have learned to be very quiet. **DC 10 Perception:** At least three distinct languages being whispered. At least two faces the party does not recognize as Solamnic types.

- **Two hearths:** Banked low, well-managed. The smoke is being vented through a passage that connects (via Dene's work) to the keep's main chimney stack. This is not improvised — it was planned. Someone with access to the keep's construction records knew where to route it.

- **The pallets:** Straw, with rough blankets. Not comfortable, but not inadequate. Children have the wall-side pallets. The elderly are near the hearths. This is organized care, not emergency refuge.

- **Dene's ledger:** He carries it forward. It contains: names (in a code using initials and family glyphs only), daily ration entries (cross-referenced to the falsified supply records), medical notes (in Maret's handwriting, which the party will recognize from S2). **If a PC asks to look at it:** Dene hands it over without hesitation.

- **Helin (Hint 3 seed):** She is in the back-left corner, sitting on a pallet, her grandchild (the girl Sera) beside her. She watches Davan specifically when the party enters. **Passive 12 Perception:** The party notices she is watching a specific person, not the group. She does not speak to the party this session. Her body language is not hostile — it is assessing. She is deciding something.

- **Davan's Oath collision (this room):** With 47 people behind him and the garrison outside the door and the assault still echoing, Davan says the thing he has been not saying. The designed moment: the party is standing in Room 04 with Dene, and the shape of the situation is clear — these people exist, they have been here for three years, the Warden does not know, and someone has to decide what happens next.

  **Davan's line:** *"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this."* He does not say it to anyone in particular. He says it to the room, or to himself, or to the stone. This is the first layer of the accounting speech. Log verbatim.

#### Encounter

None. This is not a combat space. If combat comes here, something has gone deeply wrong.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- West/Up: Room 03 Maintenance Door (6 steps up)
- North: Room 05 Dene's Supply Alcove (connecting passage; Dene opens this)

#### GM Notes

**The room should feel dense.** 47 people in a 40×20 space is approximately two people per 10×10 square. It is warm, close, and human in a way the rest of the keep is not. The party has spent 19 days in garrison space — uniform, functional, military. This room is the opposite of that. Let the smell and the heat land.

**Dene's entrance:** He comes forward with the ledger before anyone speaks. He is not defending. He is accounting. He has been waiting for this moment for three years — not because he wanted to be caught, but because he has known it could not be hidden forever and he has been deciding what he would say.

**Maret's handwriting in the ledger:** The party met Maret in S2. If anyone recognizes her handwriting (DC 13 History or auto if Sava or Talis worked with her closely), it connects the S2 seed to this reveal. Maret knew. She said nothing. She treated their wounds. This is the full picture.

**Helin's watch:** She is seeding S5. Do not force the party to interact with her — let her be a detail. The passive 12 Perception note is the designed delivery. In S5, when Talis goes through the cliff-gap, Helin's watching-Davan moment will have been planted.

**Davan's Oath collision:** Let him say it. If the player (or DM in solo play) does not arrive at this line naturally, offer it through the heuristic: Davan's decision-order places "oath" third — "when the Oath clearly applies (clear wrong being done)." But this is not a clear wrong. It is a gray space. That's the collision. The Oath gives him no guidance here, and he knows it. The line is him naming the silence.

### Scene 5 — Dene's Supply Alcove

> A tight space off the north corridor — shelves floor to ceiling, a standing desk, a lamp. The shelves hold garrison stores. On the desk: two ledgers. One in the garrison's standard hand. One in a different hand — smaller, denser, cross-referenced.

#### Features

- **The official ledger:** Standard garrison supply log — ration counts, arrow stock, medical materials, fuel. Written in the garrison quartermaster's formal hand. Accurate for items that go to garrison members.

- **The second ledger (Dene's private record):** Same format, different hand, different content. It lists 47 names by initials and family glyphs. Daily ration entries going back three years, cross-referenced with the official ledger by page number. The entries show exactly how Dene has been hiding the discrepancy: small overstatements in the official record across dozens of line items, individually undetectable, collectively substantial. **DC 14 Investigation to understand the full method.** DC 12 to see that it has been done deliberately and systematically.

- **The method:** Dene has been reporting slightly higher garrison figures to the supply chain — 52 soldiers where there are 48, 19 horses where there are 16 — and diverting the surplus. He used the supply cart route to bring in additional stores before the siege. After the siege began, he has been rationing the hidden supply more aggressively. **The supply shortfall the party is experiencing is partly because Dene is feeding 47 extra people from a fixed supply.** This is not a judgment — it is a fact about where the supplies are going.

- **Maret's entries:** The medical notes in the second ledger are in Maret's handwriting. Anyone who interacted with Maret in S2 can recognize her writing with DC 12 History (or auto if they worked closely with her). The notes are clinical — injury treatment, fever records, childbirth (one entry, two years ago), a death (six months ago, an elderly man, named only as "G.F."). Maret has been treating these people as patients.

- **Dene's personal note (at the back of the second ledger):** A single page, not an entry. Written in a more personal hand. *"If this is found: I did not tell the Warden because I did not trust him to make the right decision. I still do not know if I was right about that. The keep has approximately 8 days of supplies at current consumption — but only 4 days if it tries to sustain the additional 47 people at full rations. This is the pressure the party now holds. Informing Asholt means he must decide: continue feeding the refugees (4-day supply window) or not. **This is not a problem with a clean answer.**
