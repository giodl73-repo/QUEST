# C4-S4 SESSION LOG — The Lower Keep

*Day 19 of the siege. A night assault nearly breaks the inner gate, a maintenance door opens in the chaos, and a child's face looks out — revealing that Quartermaster Dene Flor has been sheltering 47 refugees for three years.*

---

## Scene 01 — Second Watch, Inner Gate (Room 01: Inner Gatehouse)

Three sharp blows on iron. Not the wall-walk bells — the inner gate's own alarm, a sound the garrison has drilled for and hoped never to hear.

Davan is awake before the third blow. He has slept in his armor for nineteen days; the habit of doing so had stopped feeling like a habit. He is on his feet and moving before Talis has cleared her bedroll, before Sava has her satchel, before Caelith has closed his spellbook.

The inner gatehouse corridor is lit by two torches, which is not enough. Sergeant Irr is already at the east passage, shield up, giving orders in the flat voice of a man who has stopped conserving his calm for anything. He meets Davan's eyes: *"East approach. Frame. Arrow suppression. They found the geometry."*

The party reaches Room 01 — the inner gatehouse's firing platform — just as the first volley comes over the outer wall. Three arrows. One buries in the beam above Talis's shoulder. She does not flinch. She reads the angle before the shaft stops vibrating.

**Talis reads the ground:**

**🎲 Talis Perception (initial threat read)** — `1d20+6` → rolls=[13] mod=+6 **total=19 (success)**

She counts four approach lanes in the dark below the gate. Two torches. A silhouette carrying weight — the battering frame, two-handled, six feet long. Eight soldiers visible, one sergeant at the rear coordinating arrows. She tells the party in cardinal bearings: *"Frame team: seven north, forty feet. Suppression archers: six in cover, northeast quadrant. Sergeant: rear of frame, approximately eighty feet."*

Davan positions himself at the gatehouse's east breach-point — the place where an attacker who makes it past the outer gate would have to come through. He does not report that he is doing this. He is simply there when Irr looks for where to reinforce.

Initiative is called.

**🎲 Initiative — Davan** — `1d20+1` → rolls=[15] mod=+1 **total=16**
**🎲 Initiative — Sava** — `1d20+1` → rolls=[19] mod=+1 **total=20**
**🎲 Initiative — Talis** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[2] mod=+3 **total=5**
**🎲 Initiative — Caelith** — `1d20+2` → rolls=[10] mod=+2 **total=12**
**🎲 Initiative — Assault Squad** — `1d20+1` → rolls=[2] mod=+1 **total=3**

**Order:** Sava (20) → Davan (16) → Caelith (12) → Talis (5) → Assault Squad (3)

The assault squad is coordinated but slow — this is their first attempt at this gate geometry and they are feeling their way. The garrison has nineteen days of practice. The first round goes to the defenders.

**ROUND 2**

The garrison is arriving in force now — Irr has pulled the reserve from the west side, four soldiers moving through the corridor. They will not reach the breach for two more rounds. The party is alone for this round and the next.

Sava reads the positions. Davan is down seven potential HP if the sergeant gets through; Talis is already hit. She makes the decision fast: Healing Word on Davan first, then position for the corridor threat.

**🎲 Sava Healing Word on Davan** — `1d4+3` → rolls=[4] mod=+3 **total=7**

Seven HP restored. **Davan: back to full 28/28.**

She then calls another Sacred Flame on Soldier A — the one still half-HP from Round 1.

The sergeant has moved forward. His longsword is long and he knows how to use it in a corridor. He attacks Davan.

**🎲 Enemy Sergeant attack on Davan (d20+4)** — `1d20+4` → rolls=[9] mod=+4 **total=13 (miss, AC 18)**

The sergeant's blade hits Davan's shield square. The shield holds. Davan does not move.

Soldier D, coming around the sergeant's right, finds Sava exposed.

**🎲 Enemy Soldier D attack on Sava** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[15] mod=+3 **total=18 (hit, AC 14)**

**🎲 Soldier D damage** — `1d6+2` → rolls=[3] mod=+2 **total=5**

Five damage. Sava takes a short-sword across the ribs. She does not stop. **Sava: 19/24 HP.**

Davan's eyes move to Sava for a fraction of a second. His decision order fires: protect any PC below half HP — she is not below half, she is functional. He turns back to the sergeant and attacks.

**🎲 Davan Precision Attack (expend superiority die d8)** — `1d8` → rolls=[3] mod=+0 **total=3**

**🎲 Davan attack (with +3 Precision)** — `1d20+5+3` → rolls=[15] mod=+8 **total=23 (hit)**

**🎲 Davan damage** — `1d8+3` → rolls=[3] mod=+3 **total=6**

Caelith — watching the sergeant, watching the position — identifies the moment. The sergeant is going to try to rally and pull back rather than die here; his Dex will kick in. Caelith has been carrying the Portent [6] for four sessions. He reads the calculus of the moment, holds his notebook under his left arm, and does the thing he has been not-doing:

*"Davan. I see what he does next."*

He says it to Davan, not to the room. His voice has the tone of someone reporting a fact, not asking a question.

Caelith has no Portent dice remaining until long rest.**

Soldier E — the most committed, the one who came forward rather than fell back when the sergeant dropped — presses through to Talis.

**🎲 Talis shortbow attack (close range)** — `1d20+5` → rolls=[12] mod=+5 **total=17 (hit)**

## Scene 03 — The Door Opens (Room 03: The Lower Keep Door)

The breach is quiet. Davan stands in it, longsword in hand, while the garrison secures the approaches. His breathing is controlled in the way that means it is costing him something to keep it controlled. Sava moves through the fallen, checking for survivors. Talis — still at seventeen HP, not asking for help, re-quoting her quiver count — sweeps the east approach with her eyes, speaking in distances: *"Sixty feet. Eighty. Clear."*

Caelith closes his spellbook over the Portent notation. The [6] die has a line through it now. He does not say anything about it.

The garrison potion: Sava makes the decision without asking. She pulls the cork and hands it to Davan.

**Garrison potion used by Sava on Davan** — `2d4+2` → rolls=[1,3] mod=+2 **total=6**

Six HP. It is not much. **Davan: 28/28** (was at 28 after Healing Word; potion was preemptive) — it goes to Sava, who is at 19. **Sava: 24/24.**

*Wait — the module's intent: 1 garrison potion, supply die d6 → d4. The potion is consumed. Supply Die degrades.*

**SUPPLY DIE: d6 → d4.** The keep's last tier-one healing resource is spent.

It is in this silence — the silence after an assault, when you can hear your own blood — that Room 03's door opens.

The door labeled "maintenance" on every garrison inspection. The door Warden Asholt showed the party on their first tour without opening. The door that has been locked since before Sergeant Irr was promoted.

It opens three inches. A face looks out.

A girl, maybe eight years old. Dark eyes. She does not look frightened. She looks like someone who has been told what to do if this happened, and she is doing it.

She says nothing. She is waiting to see what the people with swords do next.

<!-- SURPRISE: The child's face. The door that was always locked. The garrison goes entirely still. -->

Davan's decision order fires. It is not about protect, not about orders, not about the Oath. He takes two steps forward — toward the girl, not toward the door handle, toward her — and says, quietly:

*"Hey. It's over. You're all right."*

He crouches to her eye level. He does not lower his sword to the floor; he turns the blade away. He does not announce that he has done this.

**Dene's token condition fires: first response to the child is human, not tactical.**

<!-- SURPRISE: Davan's movement — toward the child, not toward the door. The garrison watches. Irr watches. Nobody speaks. -->

The girl's eyes move from Davan to Sava, who has moved to stand beside him. Sava reaches into her satchel — old habit, the same movement she uses when she checks a patient — and holds out a small piece of bread. The garrison's ration. She has been carrying it since morning. She does not explain why she held it out; it is simply what her hands did.

The door opens fully.

Quartermaster Dene Flor is standing behind the girl, one hand on the girl's shoulder. Forty-seven years old. She has been on the garrison rolls as quartermaster for nine years. She has been running the lower keep's eastern chambers as a shelter for three.

She looks at Davan. She says:

*"Three years. The Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret."*

That is all. She does not explain. She does not apologize. She does not ask for mercy. She is telling the person whose first response was human what he needs to know.

**Dene's arc-completion line delivered. Token earned.**

## Scene 04 — The First Chamber (Room 04: The First Chamber)

The party enters the lower keep chambers. What they find:

Forty-seven people. Families — a father with two sons, a grandmother who has made a chair of crates, a young couple who cannot be more than twenty with a child who was born here, in these rooms, in the dark, at the beginning of the second year. Six children total. Eleven adults who can work but cannot fight. Eleven more who are elderly, injured, or ill. Twelve men and women in their prime who look at the party with the specific expression of people who have practiced not looking like they need anything.

Davan stands in the doorway and counts.

His count takes forty seconds. The people in the room watch him count. When he finishes, he says, to no one, in the voice he uses for things that are not questions: *"Forty-seven."*

Talis is behind him, a step back. She is doing her own scan — exits, floor plan, air quality, the tell-signs of long occupation. But she stops on a girl, maybe twelve, sitting against the far wall with a smaller child on her lap. The smaller child has its face turned into the older girl's shoulder. The older girl is watching the door with the expression of someone who has been someone's last line for a long time and is used to it.

**Talis's grief-paragraph fires silently.**

<!-- SURPRISE: Talis sees herself — the specific shape of being responsible for something smaller than you, of being the last thing between a threat and someone who cannot protect themselves. Asha's cord is in her equipment pouch. She does not touch it. She reads the room. -->

*Finder: Talis. Receiver: Talis (silently). The pattern is exact — her grief-paragraph contains this configuration exactly. She does not speak it.*

Helin is in the room. Mid-fifties, a deliberate stillness that reads as someone who has learned to take up less space than she needs. She watches Davan from across the room. Not a threat-read — a recognition. She has the look of someone who knows why they are watching without being able to explain it to someone who hasn't been where she was twenty years ago.

**H3 seed planted: Helin's attention on Davan. Passive 12 Perception (Caelith) catches it:** *she is watching him specifically*.

**🎲 Caelith Perception (noting Helin's attention)** — `1d20+2` → rolls=[13] mod=+2 **total=15 (success, beats passive 12)**

Caelith notes it. He does not say anything. He writes something in his notebook.

Sava moves through the chamber with her healer's satchel. Two of the elderly — an older man with a chest cough that has been untreated for months, a woman whose ankle is badly wrapped — are her immediate concerns. She works without being asked.

Caelith finds the far corner and does what he always does: counts, observes, annotates. He notes the rationing marks on the wall — tally scratches at regular intervals, the quarters of the year, for three years. He notes the crates labeled with garrison supply marks. He opens his notebook. The contractor drop point notation is three pages back. He does not open to it. He is thinking about something else.

## Scene 05 — Davan's Oath Collision (Room 05: Dene's Supply Alcove)

Dene leads Davan to the supply alcove while the others settle the refugees. Caelith is with them — his institutional-lens fires: he wants to understand the logistics of what Dene has done, how she falsified the ration entries, what the supply chain looks like from the inside.

Dene shows him the ledger. The falsified entries are meticulous. She has been pulling rations from three separate allocation streams for three years. The margin of error is exactly one person per allocation cycle — barely above detection threshold.

*"Maret knew,"* Dene says. *"She wouldn't report it without telling me first. She never told me. So I never had to tell her I knew she knew."*

Caelith reads the ledger with his full attention. He reads the contractor notation in his own book. He thinks about what these two things mean next to each other. He does not speak.

Davan stands at the alcove entrance. He has the burned letter in his breast pocket — the evidence of what Wil Fen was used for, the correct evidence for the right action. He has Tomek's token in his pocket. He is standing in a room where forty-seven people have been kept alive for three years on the basis of one person making a decision she was not authorized to make, and he is trying to find where his Oath touches this.

He says it to Dene, not to Caelith:

*"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. I've been following it for twelve years. It doesn't tell me this."*

Not an accusation. Not a question. He says it the way he says things that are not yet fully formed, that have more shape than he can name.

**Davan's Oath collision: named aloud. First layer of the accounting speech established.**

Dene looks at him. She says, simply: *"No. It doesn't."*

She is not relieved that he understands. She is not asking him to agree. She is acknowledging that he has seen the gap correctly.

In the silence that follows, Davan takes out his notebook — not the party logbook, the small one from his inside pocket — and writes one line. He caps it. He does not show it to anyone.

**PC-authored accounting speech current shape:** *"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this."*

## Scene 06 — Dawn (Aftermath)

First light finds the lower keep's eastern chambers with forty-seven people who have been in them since the beginning of the siege and thirty-seven more who are only now learning they exist.

Sergeant Irr takes the news the way he takes all news: with his jaw set and his eyes on the problem. He does not report to Asholt yet. He looks at Davan: *"That's your call, Fostermark."* Which is not how the garrison chain of command works, but it is how this garrison has started to work over nineteen days.

Davan says: *"Warden Asholt needs to know. I'll tell him."*

It is not bravery. It is obligation. He takes no pleasure in it.

*The session ends on the threshold between what happened in the night and what has to happen in the morning. Forty-seven people, an accounting Warden Asholt has not asked for, and a line in Davan's notebook that he has not yet spoken aloud to anyone who needs to hear it.*
