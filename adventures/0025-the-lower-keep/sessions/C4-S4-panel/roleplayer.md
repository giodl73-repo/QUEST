# Roleplayer Lens — C4-S4

**Lens question:** Did PCs speak and act from character? Did NPCs breathe?

## What worked

Davan's approach to the child is the session's defining moment. The behavioral sequence — move toward her, not toward the door; crouch to eye level; turn the blade away; say "It's over" — is entirely from his character sheet. His voice tag says "brief answers; does not elaborate unless asked twice." The line "Hey. It's over. You're all right." is exactly that. Three words of reassurance from a man who counts things and keeps his words lean.

Dene Flor's two lines — "Three years. The Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret." — are campaign-permanent and excellent. She does not explain, does not apologize, does not ask for mercy. This is the voice of someone who made a choice and has been living with it for three years without flinching. The NPC voice is exactly right.

Davan's Oath collision: "The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. I've been following it for twelve years. It doesn't tell me this." This is the first time in the campaign that Davan has named the gap between the Oath as a list of rules and the Oath as a description of what a person should be. His character sheet says: "The Oath is not a list of rules. It is a description of what a person should be. Rules can be gamed. You cannot game what you are." He is beginning to understand that the thing he has been following as a list is not what it was written to be.

Sava's bread: a pure act-without-announcement beat. She reaches into her satchel and holds out bread. No explanation. Her grief-paragraph (Elara's death from insufficient early care) means she keeps food on hand because she has learned that people in distress need small things first.

## Concerns

Caelith's institutional voice was strong in Scene 05 (reading Dene's ledger) but his decision about the contractor drop point was deferred again. This is the third session that intelligence sits in his notebook unshared. The character sheet says his institutional-analysis heuristic drives his decisions; withholding intelligence that the party needs is not consistent with the heuristic — it would be *holding* intelligence until the right moment, which is different. S5 must create the pressure that makes sharing necessary.

Talis's silent grief-paragraph recognition was strong but she gets no dialogue this session. She speaks directions in combat (cardinal bearings) and nothing else. Her voice tag — "does not explain conclusions, just states them" — is honored, but she is beginning to feel like the session's spotter, not its participant.

## Score: 76/80

Davan and Dene are excellent; Sava's unannounced beat is strong; Caelith's deferred notebook starting to feel like delay rather than design; Talis needs a line in S5.
