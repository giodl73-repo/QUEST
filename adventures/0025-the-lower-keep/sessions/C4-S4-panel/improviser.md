# Improviser Lens — C4-S4

**Lens question:** Did the session leave room for play? Did unexpected things lead somewhere?

## What worked

The double fumble in Round 1 — Davan's attack and the enemy soldier's attack both fumbling in the same round — is exactly the kind of emergent moment that improv thrives on. The log uses it: "the gate is held by chaos as much as by skill." The DM did not fudge either roll and let the symmetry speak. This is good.

The sergeant's capture (rather than death) opens a possibility the session did not use. A conscious enemy sergeant is a source of intelligence, a potential exchange, and a person with a belief. The improviser loves this: he is present, he is alive, the session did not foreclose his arc.

Helin watching Davan is a beautifully planted seed. She does not speak. Her attention is a gap in the session's conversation that the next session will fill. The improviser notes: the module gave her the line she will eventually speak, but let the watching be its own thing first.

Sava's bread: genuinely unscripted behavior that shaped the scene. The girl looked at Dene and nodded after Sava held out the bread. This is emergence — the module did not script the connection between Sava's gesture and Dene's decision to open the door fully.

## Concerns

The session is mechanically very tight — each scene follows the module's design closely. The child's-face moment fires exactly as designed; Davan's response fires exactly as his character sheet predicts; Dene's line fires exactly as written. This is good design executing cleanly. But the improviser notices there is no moment where the DM had to pivot — no element that broke from the plan and became something else.

The exception is the garrison potion target confusion — the DM had to make a mid-session call that the module didn't specify. Small, but it is the session's only genuine improvisation moment.

## Score: 75/80

Strong emergence in combat (fumble symmetry; sergeant capture); seed-planting is elegant; session is executing cleanly but not adventurously.
