# Lorekeeper Lens — C4-S4

**Lens question:** Does the world feel real, consistent, and knowable? Does lore serve the story?

## What worked

The lower keep's material detail is good: tally scratches at regular intervals marking the quarters of the year for three years; crates labeled with garrison supply marks; the margin of error in Dene's ration falsification is exactly one person per allocation cycle. This is the texture of a person who has been doing something for a long time and is very good at it. The worldbuilding serves the character.

Hint 2 delivery — 47 refugees — lands with specificity. The census Davan does (forty seconds, seven groups, a count) is exactly right: a Battle Master accounts for things. The number 47 has weight because it was counted, not guessed.

Davan's Oath reference works correctly within Solamnic lore: the Measure is the Measure, but the Measure is also supposed to describe what a knight *is*, not just what a knight *does*. The gap Davan names is a real lore-grounded tension in the Order of the Sword's tradition.

## Concerns

**Reconstitution doctrinal texture — third flag.** This obligation has been carried from S3. The unconscious sergeant is available for one line or one document. The session log does not give him anything. He is stabilized and unnamed. This is the third consecutive session without a Reconstitution-believer's voice. The Lorekeeper is now flagging this as a hard obligation for S5 — not optional, not "if time."

The garrison's eighteen other members are named collectively (Irr's reserve, four soldiers) but not individually. This is consistent with prior sessions but creates a sense that the party is fighting with abstractions rather than people. If the garrison is going to matter in the parley, they need at least one named face beyond Irr, Hessa, Maret, and Dene. A small thing. Addressable in S5.

## Score: 72/80

Strong material texture; Reconstitution doctrinal texture now overdue; garrison depth still thin.
