# Fresh Face Lens — C4-S4

**Lens question:** Could someone arriving at this session without prior context follow what was happening and care?

## What worked

The child's face at the door is universal. You do not need to know anything about the Solamnic Order, the Reconstitution, or the Needle Pass to understand what it means that a door that was always locked opened during a night assault and a child looked out. The image carries its own weight.

Davan's line — "The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this" — is accessible to a fresh reader. You understand: there is a rule he has followed, and the rule has a gap, and the gap is standing in front of him. This is a human problem. You do not need to know what the Oath is.

Dene's two lines land clearly. "Three years" does the work. The specificity of "the Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret" is a fresh reader's briefing in one sentence: there is a hierarchy, someone in the hierarchy doesn't know, one person chose not to tell.

The glossary of terms: "Solamnic Order," "the Measure," "the Oath" — all received in-text context. No assumption of prior knowledge.

## Concerns

The tally marks on the wall (three years, quarterly) — this is evocative but a fresh reader might not immediately clock that Dene has been running this *for the entire time she has been at the garrison*. The log says the detail clearly, but it might need a line from a PC naming it: "She's been here nine years. The tallies go back three." Sava or Caelith could say this.

Caelith's Portent use: "I see what he does next" — a fresh reader understands the *effect* (the sergeant fails his save) but might not understand *why* this is significant. The log notes it is his last Portent die and he has been carrying it for four sessions, but a fresh reader doesn't have that context. Consider adding a brief beat where Caelith notes the die has no replacement — something that lands even without session continuity.

## Score: 74/80

Child's face and Oath collision are universal and accessible; minor glossing gaps (tally context; Portent cost) that a line of dialogue could fix.
