# Tactician Lens — C4-S4

**Lens question:** Were tactics interesting, consequential, and mechanically honest?

## What worked

The three-round combat structure was tight. Initiative order put Sava first, which is the correct call for a Life Cleric in a breach fight — Sacred Flame at range before melee locks in. Davan's Precision Attack expenditure on Round 2 was the right call: sergeant was at low HP, needed a guaranteed hit, and the +3 to the attack roll turned a probable hit into certain hit at 23. The Portent [6] as a replacement for the sergeant's Dex save was exactly the designed use case — below AC for most targets but perfect for a DC-12 check.

Talis's decision to absorb Soldier E's hit to get inside his guard is the best tactical moment in the session. The Colossus Slayer fires on targets she has already damaged; by being in range and taking the hit, she set up a 14-damage round that ended the assault. Taking damage to access a better attack line is exactly the Hunter Ranger's intended play.

Combat ended in 3 rounds with 4 enemies down, 3 fled — appropriate for a Hard encounter with a depleted garrison.

## Concerns

The garrison potion was used on Sava rather than Davan, which required a mid-session correction. This is a small module gap: the module says "Sava must spend the garrison potion" but does not specify the target. When Davan is healed to full by Healing Word in Round 2 and then faces no hits in Round 2-3, the potion becomes redundant if directed at him. The DM correctly redirected it to Sava. Module should specify "on the most-damaged PC."

The enemy sergeant being knocked to 0 HP (unconscious) rather than dead creates a resource question: who stabilizes him? Sava doesn't use a slot (Spare the Dying is a cantrip). This was handled correctly. But the sergeant's fate is a dangling thread — prisoner? Released? Exchanged? S5 should resolve this briefly.

## Score: 73/80

Strong combat mechanics; Portent-as-designed is satisfying; minor module gap on potion target.
