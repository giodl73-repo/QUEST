# 0025 — The Lower Keep

**Logline:** A night assault nearly breaks the inner gate, a maintenance door opens in the chaos — and there are 47 people behind it who were never supposed to be here.

## The adventure in one sentence

On day 19 of the siege, a major night assault drives the party and garrison to the edge of the inner gate, a door labeled "maintenance" opens in the chaos, and a child's face looks out — revealing that Quartermaster Dene Flor has been sheltering 47 refugees for three years without Warden Asholt's knowledge.

## Setting

**Thorngate Keep, Night of Day 19.** The siege has been grinding for over two weeks. The Reconstitution has learned from its earlier assaults — this attack comes at the second watch, when exhaustion sits heaviest, and it concentrates on the inner gate's eastern approach rather than the walls. The garrison scrambles. The party holds the breach. In the chaos, a door that has never opened on any garrison tour swings wide.

**Hint 2 delivery:** The refugees exist. 47 people in the lower keep's eastern chambers — families, two elderly, six children, eleven men and women who cannot fight — have been fed by Dene Flor on falsified ration entries for three years. Warden Asholt does not know. His gate-captain Hessa Var did not know. Sergeant Tomek Irr did not know. **One person knew: Dene.** And one other: Maret Stave, who noted in her healer's log (S2) that she was dispensing medicines to patients not listed on the garrison rolls.

## Resource state entering S4

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S3 + 1 hit die of recovery (by DM calculation). Davan likely at 22–28 HP, others at similar partial recovery.
- **Supply Die:** d6 entering. **1 garrison potion available.** After combat: d6 → d4.
- **Sava spell slots:** Carry-over from S3. Design assumes approximately 2 L1, 1 L2 available.
- **Caelith Portent:** [6] carried from S3 (the last die; used [7] on Wil Fen). This die has been carried 4 sessions. **If used in S4, it resets only on long rest — still not happening.**

**Design intent:** The assault should cost real HP. Party ends at 50–65% of max HP. The 1-potion limit bites. Sava's slot use matters. The Supply Die degradation to d4 signals that the keep's resources are genuinely critical.

## Hook

Second watch. The party is asleep or near-rest when the assault alarm rings — not the standard wall-walk bells but the inner gate's own signal: three sharp blows on iron. Something is wrong at the gate itself, not the walls.

By the time the party reaches Room 01, they can see the situation: the gate approach is already crowded with Reconstitution assault troops, a battering frame being carried forward under arrow cover.

## Spotlight: Davan

The assault is a Fighter scene — positioning, holding a breach, protecting the gate while the garrison gets into position. Davan's Battle Master maneuvers (Precision Attack, Rally, Disarming Attack) have natural application here. But the spotlight is not the combat.

**The spotlight is what happens after Room 02 clears and Room 03 opens.**

Davan is standing in the cleared hall, bloodied, with the garrison around him, when the door opens. He sees the child's face. His first response — not tactical, not procedural — is the condition for Dene's token.

**Davan's Oath collision (named aloud):** In Room 04 or 05, with refugees behind the open door and the garrison still unsettled, Davan says the thing he has been not saying. The specific line: something about how the Oath was supposed to tell him who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell him this. This is the first layer of the accounting speech.

## The assault

The Reconstitution assault force — six soldiers, Hard to Deadly for an L3 party — concentrates on the inner gate approach using a battering frame and arrow suppression. They have not been here before, but their sergeant knows the gate's geometry. The party must hold the outer side of the inner gate while the garrison bolts the inner locks.

See `encounters/night-assault.md`.

## The door

Room 03 — labeled "maintenance" on every garrison inspection — opens when the assault noise is loudest and its occupants can no longer stay hidden. The face at the door is a girl, maybe eight years old, who does not look frightened. She looks like someone who has been told what to do if this happened and is doing it.

**Character-logic condition (no roll):** If the party's FIRST RESPONSE to the face is human — speaking to the girl, moving toward her without a weapon raised, asking her name — Dene's token fires. If the first response is tactical (barricade the door, secure the area, report to Asholt), the window closes. It re-opens in S5 only if Davan's accounting speech lands.

## Dene Flor's arc-completion

**Dene's line (campaign-permanent):** *"Three years. The Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret."*

Spoken to the PC who responded to the child with humanity, not tactics. After the party is inside Room 04 and the assault has been broken. Before anyone has spoken to Asholt.

## Hint 3 seed (plants for S5)

Among the 47 refugees is **Helin**, a woman in her mid-fifties. She watches Davan specifically during the first entry into Room 04. She says nothing this session except to the girl (her grandchild). But she watches Davan the way someone watches a person they recognize without being recognized back.

She knows why Saren Coldforge left Thorngate. She was here — not in this keep, but at the pass — twenty years ago, when the first group of non-Solamnic travelers came through in winter and Asholt sheltered them. She left before the incident with Saren. She knows what was said.

**She does not speak this in S4.** It is a seed. The party will encounter her again in S5 through Luca's egress route.
