# Dene Flor (Quartermaster)

## Appearance

Late fifties. Heavy through the middle; the build of someone who has spent decades lifting crates and managing physical inventory rather than fighting. Thinning grey hair, close-kept. He carries the second ledger the way other men carry a weapon — it is the first thing he reaches for. His hands are ink-stained on the right side from three years of double-entry accounting. He is not frightened. He stopped being frightened about this some time ago and moved into a kind of quiet resolution.

## Role in this session

Dene gives the party the full accounting of what he has done and why. He does not defend it strategically — he simply explains it. He knows the Warden will find out. He has known this was coming for a long time and he has made peace with the consequences.

## Public knowledge

- Dene Flor has been Thorngate's quartermaster for eleven years.
- He manages supply inventory, ration accounting, and the logistics chain.
- He is competent, thorough, and has never had an official complaint against his records.
- He is not a soldier. He has no weapon training and no inclination toward it.

## Private knowledge

- For three years, Dene has been sheltering 47 people in the keep's eastern lower chambers.
- He diverted supplies by reporting slightly inflated garrison figures to the supply chain and routing the surplus.
- He connected the lower chambers' hearths to the main chimney stack using a passage he found in the keep's original construction records. This took him four months.
- He told one person: Healer Maret Stave. He needed medical care for the refugees and she was the only person in the keep he trusted. She agreed to help without asking him why.
- He chose not to tell Asholt because he did not trust the Warden to decide correctly. He says this plainly if asked.

## Behavior matrix

### When the party enters Room 04

He comes forward. He does not wait for accusations. He holds the ledger open to the first page and says: *"Three years. The Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret."* He then waits.

He is not confessing under pressure. He is giving an accounting because someone has finally arrived who can receive it.

### If asked why he didn't tell Asholt

*"Because I knew what he would decide. He would decide the Oath didn't cover them. And he might be right about the Oath. But I knew what they needed."*

He does not elaborate unless asked. He does not ask the party to agree with his reasoning. He is not looking for validation.

### If asked about the supply numbers

He is direct: *"I have four days if nothing changes. Less if I try to keep everyone at full rations. More if I cut portions for the garrison."* He does not suggest a solution. He presents the arithmetic. He has been living inside it.

### If asked about Maret

*"She came to me after she treated their first fever. She didn't ask me to stop. She just started keeping records."* He does not speculate about her motivations. He trusts her and has never needed to understand why.

### After arc-completion (token given)

He is not relieved. He is still the same man, still holding the ledger, still running the numbers. But something has shifted in his posture — a quality of waiting that has been present for three years is gone. He is present now, not braced.

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** The party's first response to Sera (the girl in Room 03) is human, not tactical.

**The act:** Dene speaks his arc-completion line directly to the PC who responded to Sera — the one who spoke to her, or moved toward her without a weapon raised.

**Arc-completion line (campaign-permanent):**

*"Three years. The Warden doesn't know. Nobody does except Maret."*

Spoken in Room 04, when Dene steps forward with the ledger.

**What closes the arc:** He has been carrying this alone for three years. When someone in the party responds to Sera as a child rather than a security threat, it confirms the judgment he made three years ago — that there were people here worth this cost. The line is not an apology. It is the accounting he has been holding in reserve. It is spoken to someone who has just demonstrated they can hold it.

**Token delivery:** He gives the party his quartermaster's token — a flat iron disc stamped with the supply cross (the garrison's logistics device) — after his arc-completion line, or at the start of S5 if the party's response was borderline and Dene needs to observe them further before committing.

## Information-holder

Dene holds information the party needs if they want to understand the full supply situation:
- The current 4-day window (if feeding everyone at full rations)
- The exact method of his falsification (available from the ledger with DC 14 Investigation, or directly from Dene without a check)
- Maret's involvement (he will confirm it if the party finds her handwriting; he does not volunteer it unless asked)

He will not speculate about what Asholt will do when he finds out. *"I don't know what he'll decide. I made my decision three years ago. His is still ahead of him."*

## Notes for future sessions

- His arc is complete. Future appearances are texture, not new arc material.
