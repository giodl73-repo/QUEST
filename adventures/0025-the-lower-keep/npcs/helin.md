# Helin (Refugee Elder)

## Appearance

Mid-fifties. She has the compact, weathered quality of someone who has spent decades outdoors in Kharolis — not a farmer, but someone whose work took her across the foothills. Grey-streaked dark hair, worn back. Capable hands. She does not look like a refugee; she looks like someone who has simply been in one place for a while longer than she expected. Her granddaughter Sera is the girl in Room 03.

## Role in this session (S4)

Helin watches. She does not speak to the party. She watches Davan specifically — not in a hostile way, but with the focused attention of someone recognizing a type. She knows the Solamnic device, the posture, the equipment. She has seen it before. She is deciding whether this particular knight is the same kind of knight she saw twenty years ago at the pass, or a different kind.

**This session she gives nothing.** She is a seed, planted for S5.

## Private knowledge

- Helin was at the Needle Pass twenty years ago — not as a garrison member, but as part of the first group of non-Solamnic travelers who came through in winter and were sheltered by Warden Asholt.
- She left before the incident that ended Saren Coldforge's time at Thorngate. She did not see it happen. But she knows what was said before it: she heard the argument between Asholt and a young knight-in-training about whether the Oath's hospitality provisions applied to non-Solamnic travelers.
- She knows that the young knight-in-training said: *"The Oath is for those who can swear it."* And she knows that Asholt said: *"Then I will shelter them on my authority, not the Oath's."*
- She knows the knight who objected was Saren Coldforge. She knows Saren left shortly after.
- She believes Saren's objection was wrong about the people — the non-Solamnic travelers were in genuine need — but she has spent twenty years wondering whether Saren was right about the Oath's letter. She does not think these are the same question.
- She returned to the Needle — not for the pass, but to find Asholt. She wanted to tell him she had thought about what he did for twenty years and had decided it was right. The siege was already underway when she arrived. She has been here for four months.

## Behavior matrix

### S4 — The first chamber

She watches Davan. Passive 12 Perception: the party notices she is watching a specific person, not the group. She does not acknowledge the party's attention if they look back. She is not frightened and she is not performing — she is simply looking.

If a PC speaks to her directly: she answers briefly. *"My granddaughter's name is Sera. She is well."* She does not volunteer anything else.

**Do not force the party toward her.** She is texture this session. The seed is in her watching.

### S5 — Outside the walls (Hint 3 delivery)

When Talis uses the cliff-gap egress and encounters the refugees who have been moved to an outdoor position (Dene's emergency protocol after the S4 assault), Helin is there. This is where she speaks.

**Hint 3 delivery (S5, campaign-permanent):**

*"She said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people. She never came. This is her coming back."*

The pronoun is Saren Coldforge. The party will understand this in context. If they do not, Helin elaborates — but only enough: *"The young knight who argued with Asholt. She left. I have been waiting twenty years to say what I am going to say to Asholt when this is over. She is waiting to say the same thing."*

## Arc-Completion

**Arc-completion adventure:** 0026-the-defector

**Condition:** Helin is given the opportunity to speak about Saren without pressure — the party is outside the walls, in a moment of relative quiet, and someone (not Davan) has asked her about the past.

**The act:** She delivers the Hint 3 line above.

**What closes the arc:** She has been carrying two things for twenty years: gratitude toward Asholt (who sheltered her) and curiosity about Saren (who objected). The party's presence at the siege has made both of these things present and active again. Delivering the Hint 3 line is the moment she names both at once — in a form that gives the party the information they need about Saren, and that names her own position without turning it into a speech.

- After S5, she remains in the keep (or the emergency outdoor position) with the other refugees.
