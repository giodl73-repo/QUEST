**Date in world:** PC 188, Day 4, Irenvale patrol
**Spotlight:** Dorn (contract reading — arc-completion fires). PRIMARY RV5 test.
**Seed:** C6S4-2026-04-22

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Dorn read the contract again. Before the briefing. In his bunk. Clause 4 in particular. *No military resources shall be transferred to civilian private custody without a standing tribunal order or the express written authorization of a Commander of the Sword or above.* He had read this clause two hundred times. He knew it by the shape of the letters as much as by the words.

He was not expecting to need it today. He folded the contract and put it in the oilskin sleeve. He put the sleeve in his pack beside the rations.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Varet's journal entry before the briefing. She re-read the four discrepancy entries from Days 1-3. She wrote at the top of the Day 4 page: *"Pattern observed: 4 entries. All point toward supply system operating through patrol route. No orders explain the supply system. No orders acknowledge it exists."* Then she closed the journal. Then she went to the briefing.

## Scene 0 — Briefing

Veth handed Varet a new order without preamble. *"Transport detail. Cache to new coordinates. One day. Mules from the stable."* The order packet included a sealed cargo manifest: everything in the staging cache. The destination coordinates were in a second sealed insert.

Varet opened the insert at the briefing table. Taris was beside her when she read the coordinates aloud.

**Taris Investigation DC 11 (+1):** rolls=[11] mod=+1 **total=12** — PASS. He checked his map. *"These coordinates are 8 miles west. That's the Sorn factor-house. Lord Herath Sorn. Private estate."* He showed Varet the map. His finger on the mark he had made two days ago from the ledger entries.

Varet asked Veth to clarify. Veth: *"Orders are orders, Commander. Logistics isn't your purview."* He ended the briefing.

**Varet Insight DC 12 on Veth (+4):** rolls=[4] mod=+4 **total=8** — FAIL. Veth read the same way he always read. She could not tell if he knew what the order meant or was passing it without knowledge. She wrote: *"Veth: unreadable again. He may not know what this order is for."*

**Taris marked his map.** The Sorn factor-house coordinates joined the cache site, the east valley holdout, and the patrol route marks. He did not annotate it. He marked it. The map was getting dense.

## Scene 1 — Cache Loading

The staging cache, by morning light. Unit soldiers loading mules with military-grade supplies.

Lenne stood at the medical shelf before transferring the supplies. She counted what she was moving. Then she wrote in the medical log: *"Medical stores transferred to unknown civilian facility. Coordinates: Sorn factor-house. Cause of transfer: unclear. Note: these medical stores have been sourced from a supply depot (military spec); their delivery to a private civilian estate is not a medical determination but the relocation represents a disruption to forward medical capacity that cannot be explained by any order I have received."*

This was her longest log entry. It was still clinical. It just had more facts to record.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Lenne's entry was longer than usual because there was more to say. She did not register that it was longer. She recorded what she observed. The length was a function of the observation, not of her emotional state.

**Dorn picked up a crate.** Paused. Set it down. Reached into his pack. Took out the contract in the oilskin sleeve. Read Clause 4. Silently. Put the contract back. Picked up the crate again.

The half-second. The party had learned to watch for it. This was the longest half-second of the campaign.

**Arren Vole:** *"I don't suppose there's a tribunal order attached to those orders?"* He asked it while checking the mule harnesses. He did not look at Varet. He was asking the harness.

Varet checked. There was no tribunal order.

---

## Scene 2 — On the Road: Intercept

Four miles out. A mounted officer appeared on the road ahead. Plate mail, Solamnic insignia, riding hard. He pulled up and read the formation: *"Commander Senn. Lieutenant Cael Ossren. I'm supervising this transfer."*

He did not offer a written authorization.

**Varet Insight on Ossren (+4):** rolls=[4] mod=+4 **total=8** — FAIL. She couldn't read him either. He looked like a man following orders who was fairly certain he was in the right position.

Ossren to Dorn (the largest, most visibly uncomfortable unit member): *"This unit was assigned to this route for a reason, soldier. You should think about what that reason is."* He meant it as a warning. He did not know it was the truth.

**Taris passive Perception DC14 (+4 = 14 total, passive):** rolls=[8] mod=+4 **total=12** — misses the aside (12 vs 14 passive). Ossren's quiet comment to his aide (*"...the arrangement should hold; Veth said the unit would comply..."*) went unheard. v1.3 fallback-a active: Ossren's arc-completion line will confirm the arrangement exists.

## Scene 3 — The Contract Reading

**Dorn stopped walking.**

The mule-column behind him stopped with him, by the logic of columns — when the man at the front stops, the line stops.

He reached into his pack. Unfolded the oilskin sleeve. Unfolded the document inside it. He read:

*"No military resources shall be transferred to civilian private custody without a standing tribunal order or the express written authorization of a Commander of the Sword or above."*

He folded the document. Put it back in the sleeve. Put the sleeve in his pack. He looked at Lieutenant Ossren. He did not say anything else.

**This is Dorn's arc-completion.**

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
He had read Clause 4 two hundred times. He had never read it to a superior officer before. The motion was the same as it had been in the bunk this morning. Same fold. Same sleeve. Same pack position. The context had changed; the contract had not.

**Ossren's response:**
Initial: *"I have orders from above Captain Veth, soldier. Your contract doesn't override—"*

Dorn: *"I didn't ask a question, Lieutenant."*

Arren Vole (from beside Dorn, flat): *"That's the contract, Lieutenant."*

Varet stepped forward. *"Lieutenant, what written authorization does your order carry?"*

Ossren went still. His hand went to the order packet. He unfolded it. He read it. He folded it.

*"I was told it was logistics."*

**Ossren arc-completion.** The realization happened while he held the order. He had been a tool. He was honest about it.

**Varet Persuasion DC 15 (+5):** rolls=[2] mod=+5 **total=7** — FAIL. She had started to say something more — to ask Ossren for his cooperation, to frame the formal objection process. The words came out wrong. She sounded like she was managing up. Ossren's expression closed slightly.

She stopped. She did not try again.

Instead: *"Lieutenant. I'm filing a formal objection to this transfer order. I need a witness."*

Ossren looked at the order in his hand. At the four PCs. At the unit. At the contract in Dorn's pack.

*"I'll witness it,"* he said.

This was not Persuasion. This was the social dynamic changed by the contract reading. Ossren was honest. He witnessed.

**Varet History DC 10 to draft formal objection (+3):** rolls=[18] mod=+3 **total=21** — PASS with margin. The formal objection was legally defensible, cites the tribunal order requirement from the military code (Dorn named the specific clause), and includes Ossren's witness signature.

Transfer halted. Mules unloaded. Unit returned to Thorngate with the cache inventory intact.
