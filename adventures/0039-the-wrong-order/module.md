# 0039 — The Wrong Order
## Module Compiled

---

## DM Cheatsheet

**Unit Cohesion Die:** d10 (degraded from S3). Roll d10 for available soldiers.
**Party HP:** Carry-over + 1 hit die.
**Dorn's contract (Clause 4):** "No military resources shall be transferred to civilian private custody without a standing tribunal order or the express written authorization of a Commander of the Sword or above." Dorn has this memorized. He reads it flat. No elaboration.
**No combat required this session.** All conflict is procedural/social.

---

## Scene 0 — Briefing

**Read-aloud:**
> Veth hands Varet the order without preamble. "Transport detail. Cache to new coordinates. One day. Mules from the stable." The order packet includes a sealed cargo manifest: everything currently in the staging cache. The destination coordinates are in a second sealed insert.
>
> Varet opens the second insert at the briefing table. Taris is beside her when she reads the coordinates aloud. He goes still.

**Beats:**
- Taris checks coordinates against his map. Immediately. "These coordinates are 8 miles west. That's the Sorn factor-house. Lord Herath Sorn. Private estate." He shows Varet the map.
- Varet: asks Veth to clarify. Veth says: "Orders are orders, Commander. Logistics isn't your purview." He ends the briefing.
- **Taris marks his map.** The destination is now on his personal map. He does not annotate the map with commentary. He marks it. The mark is legible.

---

## Scene 1 — Cache Loading

**Read-aloud:**
> The staging cache, by morning light. Twelve soldiers loading mules with military-grade supplies. Lenne stands at the medical shelf for a moment before she begins transferring supplies. She counts. She writes in her log: "Medical stores transferred to unknown civilian facility. Cause of transfer: unclear."

**Beats:**
- Lenne's log entry: second "cause: unclear" — now a pattern. She writes it without comment.
- Dorn picks up a crate. Puts it down. Picks up his unit contract. Reads Clause 4 silently. Puts the contract away. Picks up the crate again. This is the half-second pause the party has learned to watch for.
- Arren Vole (to Varet): "I don't suppose there's a tribunal order attached to those orders?"

---

## Scene 2 — On the Road (intercept)

**Read-aloud:**
> Four miles out. A mounted officer appears on the road ahead — plate mail, Solamnic insignia, riding hard. He pulls up twenty feet from the unit, reads the formation, and says: "Commander Senn. Lieutenant Cael Ossren. I'm supervising this transfer." He does not offer a written authorization.

**Beats:**
- Ossren to Dorn (the largest, most visibly uncomfortable unit member): *"This unit was assigned to this route for a reason, soldier. You should think about what that reason is."* He means it as a warning. He doesn't know it's the truth.
- **Taris (passive Perception 14):** Ossren, to the aide beside him (quietly, thinking he's not heard): *"...the arrangement should hold; Veth said the unit would comply..."* Taris hears "the arrangement." He marks it mentally. He does not react.
- H2 confirmation: "the arrangement" named aloud. Taris is the finder. He makes no show of it.

**Anchor-level symptom (v1.3 fallbacks):**
- (a) Taris passive Perception 14.
- (b) Ossren's aide is questioned later — he knows nothing beyond "Ossren's assignment."
- (c) Ossren himself confirms "the arrangement" in arc-completion if asked.

---

## Scene 3 — The Contract Reading

**Read-aloud:**
> Dorn stops walking. The mule-column behind him stops with him. He reaches into his pack, unfolds a document in an oilskin sleeve, and reads.
>
> *"No military resources shall be transferred to civilian private custody without a standing tribunal order or the express written authorization of a Commander of the Sword or above."*
>
> He folds the document and puts it back. He looks at Lieutenant Ossren. He does not say anything else.

**This is Dorn's arc-completion.**

**Ossren's response (social encounter):**
- Initial: "I have orders from above Captain Veth, soldier. Your contract doesn't override—"
- Dorn: "I didn't ask a question, Lieutenant."
- Arren Vole (from beside Dorn, flat): "That's the contract, Lieutenant."
- Varet: Steps forward. "Lieutenant, what written authorization does your order carry?"
- Ossren: Goes still. His hand goes to the order packet. He unfolds it. He reads it. He folds it. *"I was told it was logistics."* (Ossren arc-completion.)

**Social resolution options:**

1. **Party files formal objection, halts transfer:** Varet logs the objection in writing, hands Ossren a copy. Transfer suspended pending authorization. Cohesion holds (d10). This is the middle path.
2. **Party defies outright:** Transfer refused, Ossren withdraws. Cohesion d10→d8 (order defied). Ossren's superior will respond in S5.
3. **Party complies:** Ossren produces a verbal assurance (not written). Transfer proceeds. Cohesion holds (d10). H3 still plants (Ossren's line fires regardless).

**Best outcome for Route E:** Option 1 (formal objection). Varet's journal has the discrepancy; the transfer is halted legally; Ossren cooperates.
