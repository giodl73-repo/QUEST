# Lieutenant Cael Ossren

**Role:** Supervising officer for the transfer. Unknowing instrument of the scheme.

## Stat block

Knight (MM p.347): AC 18 (plate), HP 52, Speed 30 ft. Multiattack (2). Longsword +5, 1d8+3. Parry reaction.

## Emotional position

Ossren is 28. He received the transfer order this morning and rode hard to meet the unit. He has orders from above Veth — he thinks this is a logistics matter. He does not know about the estate, the toll system, or the arrangement. He knows he has orders and a unit that is being inconvenient.

When Dorn reads the contract, Ossren's first reaction is to contest it. His second reaction — the one that comes about three seconds later — is that Dorn is right and someone sent Ossren here to do something that is specifically illegal.

## Arc-completion conditions

Ossren's arc fires when he says: *"I didn't know what the order was for. I was told it was logistics."* He does not say this to defend himself. He says it because Varet asks him — directly, after Dorn has read the contract — "Lieutenant, what were you told this order was for?" And he answers honestly.

His arc-completion is not dramatic. It is a man realizing he has been a tool and being honest about it in front of the people he was being used against.

## Voice

Clipped. Professional. Increasingly uncertain. He starts the scene like he has authority. He ends it like someone trying to work out what just happened.

## H3 seed (planted without knowing)

He says to Dorn, before the contract reading: *"This unit was assigned to this route for a reason. You should think about what that reason is."* He means it as a warning — "follow orders." He does not know it is also the truth — the unit WAS assigned for a reason, and the reason is not what Ossren thinks.
