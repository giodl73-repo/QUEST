## Setting & Background

Forty-eight hours before Saren defies her own command's final recall, the party arranges the parley, Davan speaks what he has been composing for six sessions, Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table, and the siege ends with the thing that was never said finally said.

**Thorngate Keep and the no-man's-land between the walls, Day 36 of the Siege.** Saren has 48 hours before she violates a direct recall order from Reconstitution command. The new supply route confirmation (Mik's Hint 4) is in the party's possession. Asholt's half-token is in the party's possession. The party is at whatever HP they carried out of S6.

**The pressure:** Saren is not going to be here in 48 hours if nothing changes. Her command will pull her out physically if she refuses again. The window is real and it is closing.

**The keep's state:** Supply Die exhausted. No garrison healing. The midnight assault cost the party significant HP. They enter S7 carrying that cost. The combat in this session is brief — 2–3 hard-liner soldiers — but it is fought with no resources. Every HP spent is real.

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S6 + 1 hit die of recovery (capped at max).
- **Supply Die:** Exhausted (unchanged). No garrison healing. No potions from garrison stock.
- **Asholt's half-token:** In party custody. Physical: a split wooden disc, cord tied off.
- **All other tokens collected:** Note total token count from handoff. Route determination depends on this number.

The parley can fire three ways, depending on the party's token count. The route determines what the parley achieves — not whether it happens. All three routes assume the party successfully brings Saren to the table.

### Route A (3–4 tokens)
Political resolution. Formal terms negotiated. Reconstitution withdraws under Reconstitution command's recall plus a face-saving formal document acknowledging the Oath question was legitimate (not Saren wrong, but the question unresolved). Garrison records opened. Refugees formally recognized as sheltered under Solamnic garrison provision. Asholt and Saren exchange formal words. They do not have the personal conversation.

**What fires:** The accounting speech (Davan) goes into the record. Formal terms. Refugee recognition. The siege ends.
**What does not fire:** Asholt's full token. The Oath conversation. Saren's arc-completion line.

### Route B (4+ tokens + Dene's token)
Refugee surfacing. In addition to Route A's political resolution, the refugees are named at the table and both Asholt and Saren hear it simultaneously. Saren confronts the fact that Asholt sheltered people she would have turned away, and that they are alive. She withdraws without formal terms — she walks away from the table and does not come back. Her line as she leaves: *"You should have told me twenty years ago."* She is not wrong.

**What fires:** Refugee recognition (direct, named). Saren hears the people she called wrong to shelter. Her withdrawal is personal, not tactical.
**Route E fires additionally** if token count is 7.

### Route E (7 tokens — 6 party tokens + Asholt's full)
The accounting is spoken. Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table. She takes it.

**Requirements:** All 6 garrison tokens earned + Asholt's half-token in party custody. The party must have his half-token to present; Asholt brings his half; the disc is rejoined at the table before he places it.

**What fires:** Davan's accounting speech (logged verbatim). Asholt speaks the arc-completion line to Saren directly. Saren takes the token. Her arc-completion line fires. The siege ends with something restored that was broken twenty years ago.

The party must accomplish three things before the parley can happen:

1. **Reach Saren** — cross the no-man's-land under a flag of parley (Solamnic tradition: white cloth tied to a garrison standard). The first attempt will be observed and potentially intercepted by hard-liner soldiers. See Room 02 and combat encounter.

2. **Present the case to Saren** — tell her what the party knows. She knows about the new supply route. She does not know that the party knows. The revelation that the party has Mik's information shifts the dynamic: she can no longer maintain the strategic fiction. She must engage personally.

3. **Get Asholt to the table** — the party has his word (*"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her."*). He comes when the party returns and says Saren is willing. He walks across the gap himself. He is not accompanied by garrison soldiers. He comes alone.

This is the session's central deliverable. The DM does not script it. The player speaks it.

The structural invitation, accumulated across six sessions:
- S3: Davan encountered "correct evidence / wrong person" — he had the right understanding about the Oath but applied it to the wrong situation.
- S4: Davan named "the Oath doesn't tell me this" — the first time he acknowledged the Oath's limits.
- S5: Davan witnessed the institutional/personal split — Helin's account of why Saren left named the Oath and the people as two separate things.
- S6: Davan heard Asholt's lines in the corridor — "she was right about the Oath, wrong about the people" — and heard Mik's information. He now has the full picture.

**The shape (from the S6 handoff, campaign-permanent):** *"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times."*

The speech synthesizes what both sides spent. It names what Saren was right about (the Oath's letter). It names what Asholt was right about (the people). It names what the refugees cost everyone, and what it would mean to stop. The DM logs it verbatim. The session's artifact is Davan's words.

**See DM instructions in Room 04 for the exact invitation structure.**

She has been waiting for 31 days for something to arrive at the table. She does not know what it is. When the party presents it — when Davan speaks it — she recognizes it. Her arc-completion fires at the table when she takes Asholt's full token. Her line: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

She is not a villain. She is a person who was right about a letter and wrong about a use, who has spent twenty years holding both halves without a way to put them down. The token in her hands is the way to put them down.

**See NPC file:** `npcs/saren-coldforge.md`

**Saren's withdrawal:** She stands her soldiers down. She orders the camp struck. The process takes the day. By nightfall of Day 36, the Reconstitution camp is withdrawn from The Needle pass.

**The garrison:** The siege is over. 36 days. Sergeant Irr tells the party: *"We held."* He does not say more. Maret Stave opens the supply ledger column again. The blank pages are the record.

**The refugees:** Whatever route fired, the refugees are acknowledged — formally (Route A/B) or in the context of the personal conversation (Route E). Dene Flor comes out of the lower keep with the children first.

**The party's position:** They were sent to reinforce. They did something different. Whether the Rose Council considers what they did to be within their orders is a question for S8 or beyond. Asholt tells them, before they leave: *"You are welcome to help. I did not ask for command. You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."* This is the closest he has come to acknowledging their role.

## Note on Rooms 03 and 04

Rooms 03 and 04 are the same physical location — the parley ground at the camp's edge. They are separated by narrative function: Room 03 is the arrival and setup (who is here, what the conditions are, hard-liner combat if not already resolved in Room 02). Room 04 is the accounting scene itself (Davan speaks, Asholt gives token, Saren responds). The DM may run them as one extended scene; the separation is for clarity of beats.

## Scale

One square ≈ 10 feet. No-man's-land: 250 feet of open ground (25 rounds of movement at speed 30, or 5 dashes). The parley ground at the camp edge is a 30-foot circle marked by Saren's soldiers with flags. Asholt's walk from the east postern to the parley ground: approximately 3 minutes at walking pace. He does not rush.

## DM notes

**The postern crossing is the point of commitment.** Once the party is outside the wall, they cannot return quickly — Hessa bars the gate behind them (security protocol). The return requires the gate to be opened from inside. This is not a trap; it is the scene's architecture. The party is outside, in the open, carrying a parley flag. This is the most exposed they have been in the campaign.

**Asholt's walk:** When the party returns from Room 03 to fetch Asholt (or sends a message back), he crosses alone. No garrison escort. No weapons drawn. He carries the other half of his token. The party watches a 58-year-old man in garrison officer's uniform walk across 250 feet of killing ground toward the camp of the person who has been besieging him for 36 days. This is the visual of his arc.

**Hard-liner position:** The combat in Room 02 or 03 is brief — 2–3 soldiers who do not have Saren's authorization to interfere with a Solamnic parley flag. They act anyway. They are Reconstitution hard-liners who believe the siege should end in a military outcome. Saren, when she sees the flag approach, sends her own soldier to call them off if they intercept at the camp edge (her authority on her own ground). If the combat happens in the no-man's-land (Room 02), she has no jurisdiction. The party fights with whatever HP they have.

### Scene 1 — The Postern

> The east postern gate. Hessa Var has the bar in her hands. Outside: the killing ground, 250 feet of open country, and the Reconstitution camp at the tree line. Saren's standard is still flying. The party is about to leave the walls one last time.

#### Features

- **The parley flag (party initiative):** The party must declare they are crossing under a parley flag. The Solamnic tradition of parley under white cloth is known to both sides — **DC 10 History, or any Solamnic-background PC knows without a check:** a white cloth tied to a garrison standard pole signals formal parley request. Under Solamnic convention, the requesting party may cross no-man's-land; the receiving party may not fire on them without violating the convention. Saren will honor it. The hard-liners may not. This is the parley's tactical risk.

  If the party asks Asholt for authorization to use the parley flag: he says yes. He does not accompany them at this stage — he has given his word that he will come when Saren is at the table. He is not going first.

- **Hessa Var's role:** Gate-Captain Hessa Var opens the east postern for the party and bars it behind them. She will open it again when the party returns — a party member at the wall base can signal her (one sharp knock on the gate base, audible to the guard on the other side). She stays at the gate herself for the duration. **If the party asks her something:** *"I'll be here when you get back."* Nothing more. She has earned that certainty.

- **What the party can see from the postern threshold:** The Reconstitution camp is visible at the tree line, 250 feet away. Movement around the outer perimeter: standard rotation, fewer soldiers than a week ago. The inverted standard is still flying. **DC 12 Perception:** One figure standing outside the command tent, not moving — she is watching the postern. She has been watching it since dawn. She knows the party has Mik's information. She is waiting.

- **The garrison at the walls:** The wall-watchers on the east parapet see the party exit. They do not cheer. Some of them have tokens in the party's possession — Irr's, Maret's, Hessa's, Dene's, Luca's. They hold their posts. They watch. The session log should note this: the garrison witnesses the party's exit.

- **Asholt's position:** He is not at the postern. He is in the corridor (Scene 05 of the previous session was his commitment: he will come when she is at the table). The party crosses without him. This is the condition he named. He meant it.

#### Encounter

None in this room. The combat is in Room 02 or 03.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- Interior (gate, barred): Great Hall and east wall (accessible via Hessa Var's signal)
- Exterior (east, open): The Approach (Room 02), no-man's-land crossing

#### GM Notes

**Let the party choose the timing.** They may cross at dawn, midday, or dusk. The timing does not change the parley's availability — Saren is watching the gate regardless — but it changes the scene's atmosphere. Dawn crossing: cold, early light, the standard visible against the sky. Midday: full sun, exposed, no shadows. Dusk: the deadline is 48 hours and counting; the urgency is real.

**The 48-hour window (established in premise):** Saren has 48 hours before her own command's recall becomes impossible to refuse. The party knows this from Mik (implied in "she's been refusing withdrawal orders for two weeks"). The session runner should note the time at the postern crossing and track it — not punishing the party, but keeping the clock present.

**Hessa's line ("I'll be here when you get back") is earned.** She gave her token in Session 3 after holding herself accountable. She has been at this gate for 31 days. She is not performing steadiness — she has it. Let the line land without commentary.

**The garrison watching:** This detail should be mentioned when the party steps through the gate. Not as theater — as fact. The people the party has been working with for six sessions are watching them walk toward the camp that has been besieging them. They do not need words. The moment is the moment.

### Scene 2 — The Approach

> Open ground. The killing ground of 36 days, walked in daylight with a white cloth on a pole. The Reconstitution camp is ahead. Three soldiers break from the tree line — not under orders. They are coming anyway.

#### Features

- **The no-man's-land crossing:** 250 feet of open ground between the east wall and the tree line. The party crosses with a parley flag (white cloth on garrison standard pole). The crossing takes approximately 2 minutes of in-game time at walking pace. **DC 12 Perception (Talis, auto):** The soldiers break from the tree line before the party has crossed halfway. They are moving fast. One of them is calling back toward the camp — not a warning call, an argument. Someone is telling them to stop. They are not stopping.

- **The hard-liners:** Two or three Reconstitution soldiers who do not have Saren's authorization to intercept a parley flag. They know they do not have her authorization. They are acting anyway — they believe the siege should end in a military outcome, not a conversation, and the party's approach with a flag is the thing they have been dreading. Their exact composition depends on the token count and Session 6's end state:

  - **If the session has high tension (wall breached in S6):** Three hard-liners; one is a sergeant. The sergeant knows he is wrong and comes anyway because he cannot make himself stop.
  - **Standard encounter:** Two hard-liners. They fight. They do not yield until below 5 HP.

  See `encounters/hard-liner-intercept.md`

- **Saren's response (from the camp edge):** On a DC 12 Perception: a figure at the camp edge is shouting in the direction of the hard-liners. She is ordering them to stand down. She does not have a weapon drawn — she is ordering, not fighting. If the combat takes more than 2 rounds in the no-man's-land: one of her soldiers (a sergeant loyal to her, not a hard-liner) runs forward and physically intervenes, restraining the closest hard-liner. This ends the combat — the hard-liners yield to her sergeant. The party's flag is still valid.

- **After the combat:** The hard-liners are restrained or incapacitated. The path to the camp edge is clear. Saren's sergeant waves the party forward. Saren herself has stepped outside the camp threshold onto the open parley ground. She is waiting.

- **Party HP after the combat:** Record. This is what they carry into the parley scene. There is no healing available. Every HP spent here is spent for the rest of the session.

#### Encounter

**Encounter — Hard-Liner Intercept**

**Overview**

Two or three Reconstitution soldiers who break from the tree line to intercept the party before they reach Saren. They do not have Saren's authorization. They are violating Solamnic parley convention. They know this. They believe the siege should end in a military outcome and they cannot make themselves stand down when they see the flag.

**Design intent:** Brief, expensive encounter. The party fights with whatever HP they carry from S6. No garrison healing. No potions. 2–3 soldiers (fewer than the midnight assault, but the party's HP pool is smaller). XP budget: 2 × 100 XP = 200 XP (or 3 × 100 = 300 XP for high-tension variant). **Medium.** Intentionally moderate — the session's weight is the parley, not this combat. The combat's function is cost and a last physical obstacle.

**Trigger:** Fires in Room 02 (the approach, no-man's-land) if the party's crossing is spotted by the hard-liners before Saren can send a stand-down order. Alternatively fires in Room 03 if one hard-liner breaks from camp after the main intercept group has been handled.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- South: East wall postern (Room 01, return route)
- North: Parley position at camp edge (Room 03)

#### GM Notes

**This combat is brief by design.** 2–3 soldiers. The party fights with whatever HP they have from S6. The encounter's difficulty is situational — the party is in the open, with a parley flag, without garrison backup, on no sleep. The soldiers are not tactical geniuses. They break at the 5-HP threshold or when Saren's sergeant intervenes.

**The parley flag does not provide mechanical protection.** It provides moral and narrative weight — Saren will not fire on a parley flag. The hard-liners are violating the convention and they know it. If they kill a party member here, the parley is over and Saren will be furious. The DM should communicate this pressure without guaranteeing the party's safety.

**Saren's intervention is automatic after Round 2.** Her sergeant arrives in Round 3 if the combat is still happening. The party does not need to last more than 2–3 rounds. The combat's purpose is cost, not survival test.

**The shout from the camp edge:** If a PC is paying attention during the combat (DC 12 Perception mid-combat, or passive check), they can hear that Saren is calling her soldiers off. This is information: she honored the flag. She is in control of her camp, not the hard-liners. This matters for how the parley opens.

### Scene 3 — The Parley Position

> Saren Coldforge is waiting at the camp edge. No escort. No armor display — she is in field uniform, not dress kit. She watches the party cross the last twenty feet. She does not speak first.

#### Features

- **Saren's appearance and bearing:** Late forties. Solamnic bearing worn at the edges — not eroded, modified. She trained here. The walls of Thorngate are visible behind the party. She is looking at the walls as much as at the party. Her Thorngate insignia — the same device as the standard — is on her collar, not inverted. She has it right-side up, on her body, here, at the parley. **DC 12 Insight:** She came to this parley as a Thorngate-trained officer, not as a Reconstitution commander. The device on her collar is not an accident.

- **The opening of the parley:**

  She speaks after the party stops moving.

  *"You have thirty-six days of what I cost you in that keep. Say it."*

  She is not inviting small talk. She is not asking for accusations — she is asking for a real accounting. She has been waiting for someone to be willing to name what the siege cost. She is the one naming it as something the party is owed. This is the invitation for Davan's accounting speech. See Room 04.

- **What Saren knows before the parley (acknowledged if asked):**
  - She knows the party has Mik's information. Mik crossed with everything she was holding as a private position. She is not angry — he did what he had to. She says: *"The pass hasn't mattered for three months. I knew you'd find out."*
  - She knows her command's recall is two days away. She says: *"I have until the third courier. After that it's not my choice anymore."*
  - She does not know if Asholt is willing to speak to her. This is the piece the party carries.

- **The party's task in this scene:**

  1. Present what they know — her situation, Mik's information, the recall window. (She already knows all of it; presenting it is the signal that the party came with the full picture, not a partial one.)
  2. Tell her Asholt will come if she is willing to be at the same table. His exact words from the corridor: *"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her."*
  3. Wait for her answer.

  **Her answer:** *"Tell him to come."*

  She does not say more. The party returns to the keep, tells Asholt, and he crosses. His walk across the no-man's-land is witnessed by everyone on both sides.

- **If the party has fewer than 3 tokens:** The parley still happens. The route narrows (Route A becomes the ceiling) but the parley is not prevented. Saren came to the table because the party arrived with a flag and the full picture. Token count determines what fires at the table, not whether she sits down.

- **Asholt's arrival:** When Asholt appears at the east wall postern and begins crossing, the parley ground goes quiet. Saren's soldiers watch. The garrison watches from the wall. Asholt walks alone. He carries the other half of his token. He does not look at anything except Saren. He arrives without announcement. He sits down. He says: *"I'm here."*

#### GM Notes

**Saren's opening line is an invitation, not an attack.** She is asking for the accounting. She has spent 36 days holding a siege that she knows, privately, is not going anywhere. She needs someone to name what it cost. The party has been accumulating that cost across six sessions. Davan has been composing the answer. This is where he gives it.

**"Tell him to come" is the hinge of the session.** Everything that follows depends on this moment: Saren is willing, Asholt is willing, and the party is the bridge. The bridge has been the party's function since the campaign's first session. Asholt said on arrival: *"You are welcome to help."* This is what the help was for.

**Asholt's walk:** Give this moment its weight. He is 58 years old, and he is walking across 250 feet of killing ground toward the person who has been besieging him because the party asked him to and he gave his word. No speech. No escort. Just a man and a half-token and the other half still in the party's possession. If the party is watching from the parley ground: describe him walking. That is the scene.

**Route E signal:** When Asholt sits down, the party shows him Saren is present, and he shows them his half of the token — the other half of the disc they've been carrying since Scene 05 of the previous session. The physical reunion of the token halves happens in Room 04, at the moment of placement. This scene (Room 03) is the setup. The act is in Room 04.

**The three-route door:** Once Saren and Asholt are both at the parley ground, the route fires based on token count. The DM should know the token count before this scene. Routes A and B fire through the accounting speech alone. Route E fires through the accounting speech AND Asholt's token. The DM's job is to create space for Davan to speak and to hold what follows.

### Scene 4 — The Accounting Scene

> The table. Three chairs. Saren across from Asholt. The party between them — not literally, but functionally. Davan has been composing this for six sessions. This is the room where he says it.

#### Features

- **The DM's instruction for Davan's accounting speech:**

  The DM creates space and then stops. The exact structure:

  > *Saren asked for the accounting. She said: "Say it." The table is here. Asholt is here. She is here. Everything Davan has learned since Session 3 about the Oath, the people, the letter vs. the use — the shape from the S6 handoff is the floor, not the ceiling. Davan, what do you say?*

  The DM does not script the speech. The DM does not prompt individual lines. The DM creates the opening and holds silence. The speech is Davan's. Whatever Davan's player says is logged verbatim by the session runner as the session's artifact.

  **The structural shape (campaign-permanent from S6 handoff, provided as a floor):**

  *"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times."*

  The actual speech will be longer, more specific, more personal. The floor is there so the DM can confirm the essential elements are present — not to constrain the player's voice.

- **Saren's response to the accounting:**

  She listens. She does not interrupt. When Davan finishes: a pause. Then: *"That's the first time someone put it that way."* If she is in Route B or E territory, she adds: *"I knew it, the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* (Route B fires this line on the refugee surfacing; Route E fires it on Asholt's token.)

- **Route A fires here (3–4 tokens, political resolution):**
  The accounting speech is logged. Saren offers formal terms — she will draft a withdrawal document acknowledging the Oath question as unresolved and the garrison's right to shelter in ambiguous cases. She does not ask for Asholt's forgiveness. She asks for the garrison records to be opened — the refugees formally recognized. Asholt agrees. He does not give his full token. He says: *"The records will be opened."* That is the terms.

- **Route B fires here (4+ tokens + Dene's):**
  The accounting speech is logged. Saren's acceptance of Route A terms is interrupted — Davan (or another PC) names the refugees at the table. Not abstractly — *"the people you would have turned away are alive in Thorngate's lower keep"*. Saren sits with this. She looks at Asholt. He says: *"I couldn't tell you before. Now you know."* She stands. She says: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* She walks away from the table. The siege is over. No formal terms — she gives the order, and her sergeant begins the withdrawal.

- **Route E fires here (7 tokens — all six + Asholt's full):**
  The accounting speech is logged. The route includes Route B's refugee naming. And then:

  Asholt reaches into his collar and takes out his half of the token. The party presents their half. He takes both halves and holds them together — the disc reunited, the cord's two cut ends touching — and then he places it on the table. Not toward Saren. Not toward the party. **Between them.**

  He says: *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

  A long pause. Saren looks at the token on the table. She reaches across and picks it up. She says: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

  She stands. The token is in her hand. She gives the withdrawal order.

  **The party receives nothing from either side. No terms, no formal acknowledgment, no document. What they receive is that this happened.**

- **What the session runner logs verbatim:**
  - Davan's full accounting speech
  - The route that fired (A, B, or E)
  - Asholt's exact line (Route E: see above; Routes A/B: as delivered)
  - Saren's arc-completion line: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*
  - Token state at close: what Saren is holding; what Asholt holds (nothing, Route E; his token, Routes A/B)

#### Encounter

None in this room. Any remaining hard-liner combat resolves in Room 02 or 03.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- Prior: The Parley Position (Room 03) — same physical space, scene continuation
- Following: The Withdrawal (Room 05) — automatic on parley resolution

#### GM Notes

**Hold the space.** When the DM opens the accounting invitation, they should not speak until Davan speaks. No "take your time." No nodding. No half-sentence prompts. Open space. Wait. If the player seems frozen: *"Saren is waiting. She asked for it. Davan has been carrying this since Session 3."* Then silence again.

**The speech does not need to be perfect.** It needs to be real. Davan does not need to synthesize all four hints into one polished address. He needs to name what both sides spent, what Saren was right about, and what it would mean to stop. If he does that, the speech is complete. Log whatever he says.

**Asholt's Route E placement:** When he places the token between them — not toward her, between them — the spatial detail matters. The token is not a gift and it is not a demand. It is an acknowledgment that both of them are on either side of it, and that neither of them can reach it alone. The party put it on the table by creating the parley. He is completing what they made possible.

**Saren picking up the token:** She does not hold it to her chest. She does not examine it. She holds it like she is deciding something, and then her decision is visible in the way she stands up. The token is not a prop — it is the physical form of the twenty-year thing being put down.

**After Saren leaves:** Asholt sits at the table for a moment. He does not speak. He is not performing grief — he is settling. The party is with him. This is the last time Davan or any PC needs to ask him a question. They have already asked the one question that mattered.

### Scene 5 — The Withdrawal

> Saren's order reaches her soldiers before the party crosses back. Tents come down. Packs go up. The standard — the inverted one — is the last thing struck. She takes it down herself. She does not invert it back. She rolls it and puts it under her arm.

#### Features

- **The withdrawal in motion:** Saren's soldiers strike camp with the efficiency of a unit that has been waiting for this order. Some of them move too fast — they have been ready to leave. The soldiers who remain (her people, not the rotated-out units) do the work without complaint. By nightfall of Day 36, the Reconstitution camp is gone from The Needle pass. **DC 10 Perception (automatic):** The standard — the Thorngate Watch device, inverted — comes down last, by Saren's hand. She rolls it without reinverting it. She is not performing anything. She is taking it with her.

- **The party's return across the killing ground:** This time, with no flag, no combat, no purpose except return. The postern gate opens when they reach it — Hessa Var has been watching. She does not say anything when they come through. She bars the gate behind them.

The children come out first.

An hour later, he appears in the great hall. He says to the party — not to the garrison, to the party: *"You are welcome to help. I did not ask for command. You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."*

  This is not formal thanks. It is not gratitude-performance. It is acknowledgment. He is naming what the party did without dressing it up.

- **The refugees (Route-dependent):**
  - **Routes A/B/E:** Dene Flor opens the lower keep with explicit authorization — Asholt gives it, or Saren's terms included it, or the table scene named them. The forty-seven people in the lower keep come out into the keep's light for the first time in 31 days.

Asholt reads the relevant line aloud to Dene: *"Solamnic garrison provision includes shelter of non-combatant persons seeking refuge from conflict."* It is the first time that sentence has been written down in his keep's records.

- **The party's position at close:** They were sent to reinforce a garrison under siege. They reinforced it for 36 days. The siege ended not because the walls held — they did hold, and that mattered — but because someone was willing to say the thing that ended it. The party made that possible. **This is not announced. It does not need to be.**

# Saren Coldforge (Reconstitution Commander)

## Appearance

Late forties. Solamnic bearing worn at the edges — not eroded, modified. She has been a military officer for 25 years, a Reconstitution commander for the last 20 of them. She is in field uniform at the parley, not dress kit. She looks like someone who has been sleeping four hours a night for five weeks and is not performing her tiredness. Her Thorngate Watch insignia is on her collar — right-side up, the training-house device, worn where she has worn it since she left. She never stopped wearing it. She never reinverted it except on the challenge standard. That distinction is legible to anyone looking.

## Background (for DM knowledge)

Twenty years ago, Saren Coldforge was a senior trainee officer at Thorngate Keep under Warden Bren Asholt. She was 27 years old. She was excellent — Asholt's best officer candidate in a decade. She had a strict Solamnic interpretation: the Oath (*Est Sularus oth Mithas* — "my honor is my life") defined the scope of garrison obligation. The garrison protected Solamnia. Non-Solamnic, non-aligned persons seeking shelter were not the garrison's obligation.

Asholt disagreed. When a group of non-Solamnic travelers arrived at the keep seeking shelter during a border conflict, he gave it. Saren objected formally — she wrote a protest to the Rose Council citing the garrison's oath-bound scope. The Council did not act on it. She left Thorngate rather than serve under a Warden she believed was in violation of the Oath's covenant.

She was right about the Oath's letter. She was wrong about what the Oath is for.

She has spent twenty years in the Reconstitution, building a case for a strict-interpretation Solamnic order, winning her commander's rank, and returning to The Needle — not for the strategic value of the pass (the new supply route eliminated that three months ago) — but because she needed to say something to Asholt before her professional position made it impossible. She did not know what the something was, exactly. She raised the inverted standard as a challenge because she could not find another way to name it.

## Public knowledge (what can be observed or inferred)

- Saren Coldforge commanded the Reconstitution force besieging Thorngate for 36 days.
- She trained at Thorngate Keep under Warden Asholt twenty years ago.
- She left under a disagreement. The details were not publicly recorded.
- She has been refusing withdrawal orders for two weeks (Mik's information; Hint 4).
- She raised the Thorngate Watch insignia inverted on Day 31 — a formal personal challenge.
- The pass has not been strategically significant since a new supply route opened three months ago.
- She acted without Reconstitution command authorization for the last two weeks of the siege.

## Private knowledge (what she knows and will share under the right conditions)

- She knows she was right about the Oath's letter. She has always known this.
- She has known for approximately 15 years that she was wrong about Asholt's decision. She has not been able to figure out what to do with this knowledge.
- She raised the standard because she could not find another way to signal that she wanted to finish the argument properly — not to win it, to finish it.
- She does not know if Asholt is willing to speak to her. (The party tells her he is.)

## Behavior matrix

### Before the parley (visible from Scene 01, Day 31)

She is at the camp, watching the garrison. She is waiting. She does not know what she is waiting for. The inverted standard is the signal she sent. She has been waiting for an answer that did not come through military channels.

### At the parley ground (Room 03, initial meeting)

She waits for the party. She does not speak first. When the party stops moving, she says: *"You have thirty-six days of what I cost you in that keep. Say it."*

This is an invitation. She is asking for the accounting. She is not posturing — she is giving the party the space to say what she owes them to hear.

**If the party presents Mik's information:** She acknowledges it without defensiveness. *"The pass hasn't mattered for three months. I knew you'd find out."* She is not embarrassed. She is past embarrassed.

**If the party presents the recall window:** *"I have until the third courier. After that it's not my choice anymore."* She is naming her own constraint, not managing theirs.

**When the party tells her Asholt will speak to her:** She is still for a moment. Then: *"Tell him to come."* Nothing more. She does not prepare a speech. She waits.

### At the table (Room 04)

She listens to Davan's accounting without interrupting. She does not look away. When he finishes: *"That's the first time someone put it that way."*

**Route A (political resolution):** She agrees to formal terms. She does not offer Asholt forgiveness — she does not have the right. She asks for the refugee recognition. She says: *"Put it in writing. Not for me. For the record."* She does not give Asholt more than formal acknowledgment. She leaves the table without her arc-completion line — Route A is what it is.

**Route B (refugees named at table):** The party or Davan names the refugees in the lower keep directly. She sits with it. She looks at Asholt. He says: *"I couldn't tell you before. Now you know."* She stands. She says: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* She leaves. The arc is complete.

**Route E (full token, Asholt's direct line):** She hears Davan's accounting. She hears Asholt place the token on the table. She hears: *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

She looks at the token on the table — between them, face-up. She reaches across and picks it up.

Her line (arc-completion, campaign-permanent, must be delivered verbatim):

*"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

She stands. She is holding the token. She gives the withdrawal order to her sergeant, who has been waiting at the edge of the parley ground.

She does not give the token back. She carries it when she leaves.

#### Arc-Completion
**The moment:** *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*
**The condition:** Asholt places his full token on the table (Route E), or the refugees are named at the table (Route B).
