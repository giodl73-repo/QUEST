# Parley Pressure Table

*Day 36. The 48-hour window is real and visible. Roll once when the party transitions from Room 01 to Room 02, and once more if the party delays in Room 03 (more than 10 minutes of scene-time before presenting Asholt's condition to Saren). Roll d6.*

## Pressure Table (d6)

| Roll | Event |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Undisturbed.** The parley ground is quiet. The camp is striking itself without incident. Saren's authority holds. The party has the full window. |
| 3 | **Courier arrives at the Reconstitution camp.** A single rider, carrying the Reconstitution seal, enters the camp from the north road. The party can see this from the parley ground. **DC 12 Insight or DC 14 History:** This is the third courier. After this one, there are no more withdrawal requests — only a compelled recall. Saren watches the courier arrive from the parley position. Her expression does not change. She says: *"That's the last one."* The window just narrowed. |
| 4 | **Sergeant Harven (Reconstitution) approaches the parley ground.** Saren's most senior NCO — not a hard-liner, but not a supporter of the parley either. He asks Saren, in the party's hearing: *"Commander, when do you want us ready?"* She says: *"I'll tell you."* He leaves. This is not a threat; it is a signal that her camp's patience is finite and her soldiers are waiting for a decision. |
| 5 | **A message arrives for Asholt from the Rose Council.** Sergeant Irr intercepts it at the garrison gate and brings it to Asholt just before he crosses. Asholt reads it. He says nothing to Irr. He crosses anyway. At the table, if asked, he says: *"The Council has opinions about how this should go. I have done what I think is right."* He does not elaborate. The message's contents are not shared. This is campaign-permanent texture: Asholt made his choice against explicit Council pressure. |
| 6 | **Saren's personal standard comes down.** While the party is in Room 03 or 04, her soldiers strike the inverted standard early — before the session's final scene. The pole is still there. The standard is gone. Saren sees the party notice it. She says: *"I decided."* She does not say when. The standard is rolled, under her arm, before the accounting speech is delivered. The scene at the table proceeds with the knowledge that she has already started ending this — the party's speech and Asholt's token are the completion of a decision that was already in motion. |

## Notes

Roll this table at: (1) Room 01 → Room 02 transition (after the party exits the postern), and (2) during Room 03 if the parley setup takes longer than expected. Two rolls maximum.

**Result 6 note:** If Saren's standard comes down early, her arc-completion line — *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* — is delivered not at the token moment but at the moment the party notices the empty pole. She says it once. She does not repeat it. If Route E fires at the table, Asholt's line stands on its own; her line has already been spoken. This is not a failure state — it is an alternative delivery of the same arc-completion. Log it verbatim.

**Result 3 note (the third courier):** This is the timeline made physical. The party can see the courier. Saren can see the courier. The window is not abstract anymore. If the party has been moving slowly, this accelerates everything — they have approximately one in-game hour before the courier gives Saren a direct order she cannot refuse. Use this result to close a session that has been lingering in exposition.
