# Encounter — Hard-Liner Intercept

## Overview

Two or three Reconstitution soldiers who break from the tree line to intercept the party before they reach Saren. They do not have Saren's authorization. They are violating Solamnic parley convention. They know this. They believe the siege should end in a military outcome and they cannot make themselves stand down when they see the flag.

**Design intent:** Brief, expensive encounter. The party fights with whatever HP they carry from S6. No garrison healing. No potions. 2–3 soldiers (fewer than the midnight assault, but the party's HP pool is smaller). XP budget: 2 × 100 XP = 200 XP (or 3 × 100 = 300 XP for high-tension variant). **Medium.** Intentionally moderate — the session's weight is the parley, not this combat. The combat's function is cost and a last physical obstacle.

**Trigger:** Fires in Room 02 (the approach, no-man's-land) if the party's crossing is spotted by the hard-liners before Saren can send a stand-down order. Alternatively fires in Room 03 if one hard-liner breaks from camp after the main intercept group has been handled.

---

## Reconstitution Hard-Liner Soldier (CR 1/2, 100 XP)

*End-of-siege, no authorization, acting on personal conviction. Not reckless — deliberate. They know they're wrong and they're doing it anyway.*

**AC** 14 (chain shirt + shield) · **HP** 16 (3d8+3) · **Speed** 30 ft.

| Str | Dex | Con | Int | Wis | Cha |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 12 (+1) | 11 (+0) | 10 (+0) | 9 (−1) |

**Saves:** Str +4, Con +3
**Skills:** Athletics +4, Perception +2
**Senses:** passive Perception 12

**Longsword.** +4 to hit, 5 ft., 1d8+2 slashing.
**Javelin.** +4 to hit, range 30/120 ft., 1d6+2 piercing (2 per soldier; used on approach if party is still crossing when combat opens).

**Morale break:** When a hard-liner drops to 5 HP or fewer, they make a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, they disengage and fall back toward the camp. On a success, they fight on (to 0 HP). The sergeant variant fights to 0 HP regardless.

**Convention awareness:** These soldiers know they are violating parley convention. This manifests as hesitation — they have disadvantage on the first attack roll they make against a PC who is visibly carrying the parley flag. After Round 1, this hesitation is gone.

## High-Tension Variant (wall breached in S6)

If the east wall was breached during the midnight assault:

Add a third soldier — a sergeant. The sergeant has:
- **AC** 15 · **HP** 22 (4d8+4)
- **Rallying Shout (bonus action, once):** Grants one adjacent ally advantage on their next attack roll.
- **No morale break:** The sergeant fights to 0 HP regardless of the Wisdom save.
- **Motivated:** He is doing this because one of his soldiers died on that east wall last night. He is not wrong to be angry. He is wrong about what to do with it.

## Tactics

### Round 1
- Hard-liners approach from the tree line or camp edge at speed. If the party is still crossing the no-man's-land, they throw javelins first (Round 1 ranged attack), then close to melee.
- If the party is already at the camp edge (Room 03), the hard-liners close from camp interior. No javelin range; direct melee.
- Parley flag disadvantage applies on Round 1 attacks only.

### Round 2+
- Standard melee. They do not retreat except on morale break (DC 12 Wisdom).
- They do not communicate with Saren or each other in terms that give tactical information. They are not tacticians — they are soldiers acting on feeling.

### Saren's intervention (automatic Round 3 in Room 02, Round 2 in Room 03)
Her sergeant arrives and physically restrains the closest hard-liner. This ends the encounter — the hard-liners yield to her authority, not the party's. The party has not "won" this fight; the fight was stopped. This is a meaningful distinction.

If the party has already incapacitated all hard-liners before the intervention arrives: the sergeant still comes, looks at the scene, and says nothing. He waves the party forward.

---

## End States

**Saren's sergeant intervenes (standard):** Hard-liners are restrained. Party proceeds. Note HP at end of combat.

**Party incapacitates all hard-liners before intervention:** Same outcome; sergeant waves party forward. Note in session log that the party resolved it before Saren's intervention — this is a marginal signal of force (not negative, just factual).

**A hard-liner is killed (party choice):** Saren notes it. At the parley table, she says nothing about it directly. But at the close of the session (Room 05), when the camp is being struck, she pauses at the table and looks at the party. She does not accuse. She is noting the cost. Log this in the session record.

**Hard-liner escapes (morale break, both soldiers flee):** They return to camp. Saren's sergeant collects them. The parley proceeds. The escaped soldiers are a Reconstitution internal problem.
