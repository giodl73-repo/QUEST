# Saren Coldforge (Reconstitution Commander)

## Appearance

Late forties. Solamnic bearing worn at the edges — not eroded, modified. She has been a military officer for 25 years, a Reconstitution commander for the last 20 of them. She is in field uniform at the parley, not dress kit. She looks like someone who has been sleeping four hours a night for five weeks and is not performing her tiredness. Her Thorngate Watch insignia is on her collar — right-side up, the training-house device, worn where she has worn it since she left. She never stopped wearing it. She never reinverted it except on the challenge standard. That distinction is legible to anyone looking.

## Background (for DM knowledge)

Twenty years ago, Saren Coldforge was a senior trainee officer at Thorngate Keep under Warden Bren Asholt. She was 27 years old. She was excellent — Asholt's best officer candidate in a decade. She had a strict Solamnic interpretation: the Oath (*Est Sularus oth Mithas* — "my honor is my life") defined the scope of garrison obligation. The garrison protected Solamnia. Non-Solamnic, non-aligned persons seeking shelter were not the garrison's obligation.

Asholt disagreed. When a group of non-Solamnic travelers arrived at the keep seeking shelter during a border conflict, he gave it. Saren objected formally — she wrote a protest to the Rose Council citing the garrison's oath-bound scope. The Council did not act on it. She left Thorngate rather than serve under a Warden she believed was in violation of the Oath's covenant.

She was right about the Oath's letter. She was wrong about what the Oath is for.

She has spent twenty years in the Reconstitution, building a case for a strict-interpretation Solamnic order, winning her commander's rank, and returning to The Needle — not for the strategic value of the pass (the new supply route eliminated that three months ago) — but because she needed to say something to Asholt before her professional position made it impossible. She did not know what the something was, exactly. She raised the inverted standard as a challenge because she could not find another way to name it.

## Public knowledge (what can be observed or inferred)

- Saren Coldforge commanded the Reconstitution force besieging Thorngate for 36 days.
- She trained at Thorngate Keep under Warden Asholt twenty years ago.
- She left under a disagreement. The details were not publicly recorded.
- She has been refusing withdrawal orders for two weeks (Mik's information; Hint 4).
- She raised the Thorngate Watch insignia inverted on Day 31 — a formal personal challenge.
- The pass has not been strategically significant since a new supply route opened three months ago.
- She acted without Reconstitution command authorization for the last two weeks of the siege.

## Private knowledge (what she knows and will share under the right conditions)

- She knows she was right about the Oath's letter. She has always known this.
- She has known for approximately 15 years that she was wrong about Asholt's decision. She has not been able to figure out what to do with this knowledge.
- She raised the standard because she could not find another way to signal that she wanted to finish the argument properly — not to win it, to finish it.
- She does not know if Asholt is willing to speak to her. (The party tells her he is.)

## Behavior matrix

### Before the parley (visible from Scene 01, Day 31)

She is at the camp, watching the garrison. She is waiting. She does not know what she is waiting for. The inverted standard is the signal she sent. She has been waiting for an answer that did not come through military channels.

### At the parley ground (Room 03, initial meeting)

She waits for the party. She does not speak first. When the party stops moving, she says: *"You have thirty-six days of what I cost you in that keep. Say it."*

This is an invitation. She is asking for the accounting. She is not posturing — she is giving the party the space to say what she owes them to hear.

**If the party presents Mik's information:** She acknowledges it without defensiveness. *"The pass hasn't mattered for three months. I knew you'd find out."* She is not embarrassed. She is past embarrassed.

**If the party presents the recall window:** *"I have until the third courier. After that it's not my choice anymore."* She is naming her own constraint, not managing theirs.

**When the party tells her Asholt will speak to her:** She is still for a moment. Then: *"Tell him to come."* Nothing more. She does not prepare a speech. She waits.

### At the table (Room 04)

She listens to Davan's accounting without interrupting. She does not look away. When he finishes: *"That's the first time someone put it that way."*

**Route A (political resolution):** She agrees to formal terms. She does not offer Asholt forgiveness — she does not have the right. She asks for the refugee recognition. She says: *"Put it in writing. Not for me. For the record."* She does not give Asholt more than formal acknowledgment. She leaves the table without her arc-completion line — Route A is what it is.

**Route B (refugees named at table):** The party or Davan names the refugees in the lower keep directly. She sits with it. She looks at Asholt. He says: *"I couldn't tell you before. Now you know."* She stands. She says: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* She leaves. The arc is complete.

**Route E (full token, Asholt's direct line):** She hears Davan's accounting. She hears Asholt place the token on the table. She hears: *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

She looks at the token on the table — between them, face-up. She reaches across and picks it up.

Her line (arc-completion, campaign-permanent, must be delivered verbatim):

*"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

She stands. She is holding the token. She gives the withdrawal order to her sergeant, who has been waiting at the edge of the parley ground.

She does not give the token back. She carries it when she leaves.

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** Asholt places his full token on the table (Route E), or the refugees are named at the table (Route B).

**The act:**

*"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

**What closes the arc:** She left Thorngate because she was right about the letter. She has been right, in a narrow technical sense, for twenty years. What she did not know — what she was waiting to have someone say — is that being right about the letter is different from being right about the people. Asholt's line (*"I was right about the people"*) is the thing she has been waiting to hear someone say, not as a victory for her, but as an acknowledgment that the two of them were looking at different parts of the same truth. She could not come back until someone put those two parts in the same sentence.

The token in her hand is the physical form of putting it down. She was holding the challenge — the inverted standard, the siege, the twenty years — and now she is holding the house token instead. That is the arc.

**Token delivery:** She does not give a token to the party. She takes Asholt's. She carries it south.

**Route E only:** Her arc-completion fires in full only if Route E fires. Route B delivers her arc-completion line but without the token — the incomplete version of the same moment. Route A does not fire her arc-completion.

## Appearance details (for table play)

- Thorngate Watch insignia on collar: right-side up at all times except on the challenge standard.
- No armor display at the parley: she is in field uniform. She came to talk, not to impress.
- She does not make eye contact with the party except when she is speaking to them. When Asholt arrives, she looks at him immediately and does not look away.
- She is not cold. She is contained. The difference is perceptible.

## Notes for future sessions

**Saren is not a recurring figure after this campaign.** Her arc is complete at the parley table. She withdraws south. She will return to Reconstitution command and give an account of her actions — she defied orders for two weeks. That reckoning is hers to handle. It is not a campaign thread.

The token she carries: the Thorngate Watch disc, reunited, face-up. It was Asholt's for 22 years. She has it now. This is not tracked as a campaign artifact — it is simply the object that travels with the truth of what was said at the table.

**If the DM is asked about Saren in future campaigns:** She handled the recall, accepted whatever censure came with the unauthorized two weeks, and continued serving. The Oath question — what it is for, not just what it says — remains in the record of the parley, not in any official Reconstitution document. She does not forget it.
