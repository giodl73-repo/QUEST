# Warden Bren Asholt (Full Token — Finale)

## Role in this session

Asholt's arc completes here. In S6 he gave the party his half-token and told them the thing he had never said aloud. In S7 he walks across the killing ground alone, sits at the table with Saren, and says it to her directly. His arc-completion line is different from S6's: the S6 version names Saren's truth and his own separately; the S7 version claims his own truth in the first person.

**The shift from S6 to S7:**

S6 (to the party): *"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people."*  
S7 (to Saren): *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

The change: "she was wrong about the people" → "I was right about the people." He is not correcting her anymore. He is owning his half of what was true.

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** Route E fires (7 tokens at the table). Asholt places his full token on the table between himself and Saren.

**The act:**

He takes both halves of the token — his half from his collar, the party's half — and holds them together. He places the reunited disc on the table. Face-up. Between them, not toward her.

He says:

*"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

(Campaign-permanent. Log verbatim.)

**What closes the arc:** He has had the thing to say for twenty years. He did not have a way to say it. The party created the table. He walked across the killing ground to reach it. He says it. The arc is the gap between knowing what to say and having the conditions to say it — and the act of walking across the gap when the conditions finally exist.

**What he receives in return:** Nothing material. Saren takes the token. She says her line. She leaves. He sits at the table for a moment. Then he goes back. The token is gone. He has been carrying the house device in his collar for 22 years; he does not have it anymore. He does not replace it.

## Post-Arc Behavior

After Saren leaves: Asholt sits at the table. The party is with him. He does not ask for anything. He does not speak. This is the last time the party needs to ask him a question.

When he returns to the keep and comes to the great hall: *"You are welcome to help. I did not ask for command. You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."*

This is his acknowledgment of the party. It is not gratitude-performance. It is accurate accounting.

**He does not discuss the parley after this.** If asked, in a future session, what happened at the table: *"We finished something."* That is all.

## Notes on route dependency

**Route E only:** His arc-completion fires only if 7 tokens are present. If the party has 6 tokens (missing Asholt's full token), the parley fires at Route A or B — Asholt is present at the table but does not give the full token speech. He gives Route A or B versions (formal terms, refugee acknowledgment), which are politically significant but are not his arc-completion. His arc remains incomplete; it is not a failure — it is what the campaign's accumulated choices produced.

**The token's reunion:** The physical act of joining the two halves is the session runner's to describe. The cord's two ends do not re-tie easily — they are frayed where they were cut. Asholt holds them together with one hand while he places the disc with the other. The disc is not perfectly seamless when placed. The join is visible. That is correct. The seam is part of what happened.
