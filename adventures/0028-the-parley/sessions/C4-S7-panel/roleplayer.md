# Roleplayer — C4-S7

Davan names Orel Fostermark at the parley table. He has been carrying his brother's name for seven sessions. He spent it at the table so that Saren and Asholt could understand he is not outside their situation. "My honest testimony was what closed the case against him. I was right about the letter. I have been counting the cost of that truth for five years." This is the line the character sheet was building toward. It is spent at the right moment.

Asholt's line — "She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago" — is campaign-permanent and exactly right. "I should have told you that." Not sorry. Not wrong. The specific failure: he didn't tell her.

Saren's line — "Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back" — is the mirror. She knew. The problem was the path back.

Saren stopping the fight with one word: she uses her authority. One word. She came to meet the flag.

Score: 80/80 — the campaign's finest roleplaying session. All three arc lines are earned.
