# C4-S7 SESSION LOG — The Parley (FINALE)

*Day 36 of the siege. The party brings Saren Coldforge to the table. One PC speaks the accounting. The disc is rejoined. The thing that could not be said for twenty years is said.*

---

## Scene 01 — The Postern (Room 01): The Flag of Parley

Morning. Day 36. Forty-eight hours before Saren's command issues a forced recall.

Davan goes to Asholt before anyone else is awake. He finds him in the gatehouse, standing at the narrow window that overlooks the east approach.

Davan says: *"We want to go under a flag of parley."*

Asholt looks at him. A pause. *"You'll take a flag from the garrison standard-room. White cloth on the garrison pole. She'll know what it means."*

*"Will she honor it?"*

*"She will."* He does not explain how he knows.

He adds: *"If you can get her to the table, I will come. I told you that."*

Davan nods. He goes to find the others.

Gate-Captain Hessa Var opens the postern for them. She does not speak. She waits until they are through and bars it behind them.

## Scene 03 — The Parley Position (Room 03): Saren

The party approaches the camp threshold. Saren stands at the parley ground — a thirty-foot circle of open space, flagged at the corners, no weapons in anyone's hands. She is fifty-seven years old, lean, grey-haired, with the specific posture of someone who has been standing on siege lines for five weeks and has not changed how she stands because of it.

She looks at the party. Then she looks at the half-disc in Davan's hand — the split wooden token.

**🎲 Saren Insight on the party** — `1d20+4` → rolls=[17] mod=+4 **total=21**

She reads them immediately. She sees the half-disc. She sees Davan holding the flag and the token at the same time — not threatening; carrying. She sees Sava's satchel. She sees Talis reading the camp perimeter even now, even here.

She says: *"You held the wall."*

Not a question. An acknowledgment.

Davan says: *"The Warden sends his word. If you sit at this table, he will come."*

A pause. Saren looks at the half-disc again.

Then: *"Where is he?"*

*"At the keep. He will cross when I come back and tell him you're willing."*

Another pause. The longest one. Then she sits. She does not invite anyone. She simply sits at the parley ground's center stone and waits.

**🎲 Caelith Insight on Saren (what is she feeling)** — `1d20+2` → rolls=[2] mod=+2 **total=4 (fail)**

Caelith cannot read her. She is not closed — she is simply too composed for a 4 to penetrate. She is waiting, and the waiting has no clear emotional signature. He looks at her for a long moment and writes nothing.

The party sits across from her.

## Scene 04 — The Accounting Scene (Room 04): Davan's Speech

Before Asholt is summoned — before the party tells him she is willing — Saren says one thing:

*"Tell me why you're here. Not why the garrison needed you. Why you."*

She is asking the party, not Davan specifically. But she is looking at Davan.

**🎲 Davan Persuasion (implicit)** — `1d20+0` → rolls=[7] mod=+0 **total=7**

The dice do not matter here. This is not a persuasion roll. Saren is not asking to be convinced. She is asking to be told the truth. A 7 and a 20 produce the same outcome because the outcome is Davan choosing to speak.

He speaks. This is Davan Fostermark's accounting speech, spoken aloud at the parley table on Day 36 of the Siege of Thorngate Keep. It is logged verbatim. It is campaign-permanent.

---

**DAVAN FOSTERMARK'S ACCOUNTING SPEECH:**
*(spoken at the parley table, Day 36, before Asholt's arrival — verbatim, campaign-permanent)*

*"We are here because the Rose Council sent us to reinforce. That is the order. I am here because I was assigned and I followed my orders. That is the truth. But I have been carrying something else for the past thirty-six days that I think you need to hear.*

*"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. I have followed the Oath for twelve years. I believed it would be clear, every time — that the Measure would point to the right thing and I would do it. It does not always tell you. It told you about the letter. It told Warden Asholt about the people. You were both right. You were both following the Oath. You were following different parts of it.*

*"I know what it costs to be right about the letter. Five years ago, I testified at a tribunal. My older brother — his name is Orel, he was in the Order of the Sword, he was a good knight — had sheltered a deserter for two days before turning him over. He believed the deserter had a reason. The tribunal disagreed. He asked me to testify honestly. I did. My honest testimony was what closed the case against him. He was cashiered. He left Solamnia. I have not heard from him since.*

*"I was right about the letter. The tribunal's finding was correct. I gave the truth and the truth had the consequence it was supposed to have. And I have been counting the cost of that truth for five years.*

*"You spent twenty years knowing you were wrong about the people and not knowing how to come back. Warden Asholt spent twenty years knowing that, and not knowing how to tell you. The garrison has spent thirty-six days on this wall. The forty-seven people in the lower keep have spent three years in that basement because one woman falsified ration entries to keep them alive.*

*"I am not here to tell you the siege was wrong. I am not here to tell you the Oath is wrong. I am here because I have spent six weeks watching what happens to people when the Oath is used as a letter, and I cannot give you a verdict. What I can give you is this:*

*"The Warden says he will speak to you. He has been waiting for a table to sit across. That is what I came to tell you."*

---

Saren is quiet for a long time.

She does not say Davan is right. She does not say he is wrong. She looks at the half-disc in his hand.

She says: *"Bring him."*

Talis goes. Not the fastest runner but the one who knows the terrain. She is back across the gap and at the postern before the silence becomes too long.

---

Asholt crosses the gap alone. No garrison escort. He walks 250 feet of open ground with his duty cloak and his belt knife and nothing else. He sits across from Saren.

For a moment they just look at each other. Twenty years. A parley ground between them.

He reaches into his coat. He takes out the other half of the disc. The party's half is in Davan's hand. He holds his half across the table — not to the party, not to Davan — to Saren.

He says:

**Asholt's arc-completion line (campaign-permanent):**

*"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

He holds the disc out.

Saren looks at it for a long time. She reaches out and takes the full disc — Davan has placed the party's half on the table; Asholt puts his half against it; they are held together in her hand.

**Saren's arc-completion line (campaign-permanent):**

*"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

She holds the disc in both hands. The cord is still cut. She does not have a way to repair it right now. She holds it as two halves that belong together.

**Simultaneous arc-convergence fires:** Asholt's arc-completion and Saren's arc-completion fire simultaneously because Davan's speech created the conditions for both. The table is the space where neither of them has to be the one to ask first. The party made the table; the table made the moment; the moment let both of them speak.

## Scene 05 — The Withdrawal (Room 05)

Saren stands her soldiers down. She does it herself — walks from the parley ground back to the command tent, convenes her lieutenants, issues the order. The camp begins striking. The process takes the rest of the day. By nightfall of Day 36, the Reconstitution camp has withdrawn from The Needle pass.

The garrison watches from the wall. Sergeant Irr is on the east wall when the last Reconstitution standard comes down. He watches until the tree line is empty. He says nothing. He goes down the east stairs to check on his garrison.

**The refugees:** Dene Flor brings the children out first. The lower keep's eastern chambers open. The forty-seven people who have been in those rooms for three years walk out into the afternoon light of the courtyard.

Maret Stave opens her medical log to a fresh page.

**The garrison:** They held. Thirty-six days. Two major night assaults, one saboteur, one desertion, one defection. Zero deaths in the garrison (two wounded from S6 assault who are recovering). The party held the east wall at every point where it mattered.

**What the party receives from either side:** Nothing formally. Saren withdraws without terms. Asholt does not commission a formal report. He stands at the postern when they prepare to leave and says:

*"You are welcome to help. I did not ask for command. You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."*

That is all. It is enough.
