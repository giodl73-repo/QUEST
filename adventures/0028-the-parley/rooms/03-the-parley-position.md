# Room 03 — The Parley Position

## Read-aloud

> Saren Coldforge is waiting at the camp edge. No escort. No armor display — she is in field uniform, not dress kit. She watches the party cross the last twenty feet. She does not speak first.

## Features

- **Saren's appearance and bearing:** Late forties. Solamnic bearing worn at the edges — not eroded, modified. She trained here. The walls of Thorngate are visible behind the party. She is looking at the walls as much as at the party. Her Thorngate insignia — the same device as the standard — is on her collar, not inverted. She has it right-side up, on her body, here, at the parley. **DC 12 Insight:** She came to this parley as a Thorngate-trained officer, not as a Reconstitution commander. The device on her collar is not an accident.

- **The opening of the parley:**

  She speaks after the party stops moving.

  *"You have thirty-six days of what I cost you in that keep. Say it."*

  She is not inviting small talk. She is not asking for accusations — she is asking for a real accounting. She has been waiting for someone to be willing to name what the siege cost. She is the one naming it as something the party is owed. This is the invitation for Davan's accounting speech. See Room 04.

- **What Saren knows before the parley (acknowledged if asked):**
  - She knows the party has Mik's information. Mik crossed with everything she was holding as a private position. She is not angry — he did what he had to. She says: *"The pass hasn't mattered for three months. I knew you'd find out."*
  - She knows her command's recall is two days away. She says: *"I have until the third courier. After that it's not my choice anymore."*
  - She does not know if Asholt is willing to speak to her. This is the piece the party carries.

- **The party's task in this scene:**

  1. Present what they know — her situation, Mik's information, the recall window. (She already knows all of it; presenting it is the signal that the party came with the full picture, not a partial one.)
  2. Tell her Asholt will come if she is willing to be at the same table. His exact words from the corridor: *"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her."*
  3. Wait for her answer.

  **Her answer:** *"Tell him to come."*

  She does not say more. The party returns to the keep, tells Asholt, and he crosses. His walk across the no-man's-land is witnessed by everyone on both sides.

- **If the party has fewer than 3 tokens:** The parley still happens. The route narrows (Route A becomes the ceiling) but the parley is not prevented. Saren came to the table because the party arrived with a flag and the full picture. Token count determines what fires at the table, not whether she sits down.

- **Asholt's arrival:** When Asholt appears at the east wall postern and begins crossing, the parley ground goes quiet. Saren's soldiers watch. The garrison watches from the wall. Asholt walks alone. He carries the other half of his token. He does not look at anything except Saren. He arrives without announcement. He sits down. He says: *"I'm here."*

## Encounter

None in this room. Combat, if not already resolved in Room 02, fires here — a hard-liner who broke from camp before Saren's sergeant could stop them. See `encounters/hard-liner-intercept.md`

## GM Notes

**Saren's opening line is an invitation, not an attack.** She is asking for the accounting. She has spent 36 days holding a siege that she knows, privately, is not going anywhere. She needs someone to name what it cost. The party has been accumulating that cost across six sessions. Davan has been composing the answer. This is where he gives it.

**"Tell him to come" is the hinge of the session.** Everything that follows depends on this moment: Saren is willing, Asholt is willing, and the party is the bridge. The bridge has been the party's function since the campaign's first session. Asholt said on arrival: *"You are welcome to help."* This is what the help was for.

**Asholt's walk:** Give this moment its weight. He is 58 years old, and he is walking across 250 feet of killing ground toward the person who has been besieging him because the party asked him to and he gave his word. No speech. No escort. Just a man and a half-token and the other half still in the party's possession. If the party is watching from the parley ground: describe him walking. That is the scene.

**Route E signal:** When Asholt sits down, the party shows him Saren is present, and he shows them his half of the token — the other half of the disc they've been carrying since Scene 05 of the previous session. The physical reunion of the token halves happens in Room 04, at the moment of placement. This scene (Room 03) is the setup. The act is in Room 04.

**The three-route door:** Once Saren and Asholt are both at the parley ground, the route fires based on token count. The DM should know the token count before this scene. Routes A and B fire through the accounting speech alone. Route E fires through the accounting speech AND Asholt's token. The DM's job is to create space for Davan to speak and to hold what follows.

## Memory Fragment

> The parley ground at the camp edge has been unused for 36 days. Reconstitution soldiers have passed through it, used it as a transit zone, eaten meals at its edge. Now there is a table — Saren's soldiers put it up when the parley flag appeared on the killing ground. Two chairs. A lamp, unlit. Saren did not ask for three chairs. She assumed. The party brought Asholt. She was right to assume.
