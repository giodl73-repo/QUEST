# Room 05 — The Withdrawal

## Read-aloud

> Saren's order reaches her soldiers before the party crosses back. Tents come down. Packs go up. The standard — the inverted one — is the last thing struck. She takes it down herself. She does not invert it back. She rolls it and puts it under her arm.

## Features

- **The withdrawal in motion:** Saren's soldiers strike camp with the efficiency of a unit that has been waiting for this order. Some of them move too fast — they have been ready to leave. The soldiers who remain (her people, not the rotated-out units) do the work without complaint. By nightfall of Day 36, the Reconstitution camp is gone from The Needle pass. **DC 10 Perception (automatic):** The standard — the Thorngate Watch device, inverted — comes down last, by Saren's hand. She rolls it without reinverting it. She is not performing anything. She is taking it with her.

- **The party's return across the killing ground:** This time, with no flag, no combat, no purpose except return. The postern gate opens when they reach it — Hessa Var has been watching. She does not say anything when they come through. She bars the gate behind them.

- **The garrison's response:** They do not cheer. They watch from the walls as the camp comes down. Sergeant Irr counts the tents. Healer Maret Stave opens the supply ledger column that has been blank for nine days. She writes: *"Day 36. Siege lifted. Opening column."* Dene Flor opens the lower-keep door. The children come out first.

- **Asholt's return:** He walks back from the parley ground after Saren has left. He comes through the postern last. He does not ask to be acknowledged. He goes to his office. An hour later, he appears in the great hall. He says to the party — not to the garrison, to the party: *"You are welcome to help. I did not ask for command. You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."*

  This is not formal thanks. It is not gratitude-performance. It is acknowledgment. He is naming what the party did without dressing it up.

- **The refugees (Route-dependent):**
  - **Routes A/B/E:** Dene Flor opens the lower keep with explicit authorization — Asholt gives it, or Saren's terms included it, or the table scene named them. The forty-seven people in the lower keep come out into the keep's light for the first time in 31 days.
  - **Route A (political resolution):** The refugees are formally recognized in the withdrawal document. Asholt reads the relevant line aloud to Dene: *"Solamnic garrison provision includes shelter of non-combatant persons seeking refuge from conflict."* It is the first time that sentence has been written down in his keep's records.
  - **Route E:** The refugee recognition is not formal — it is Saren's private knowing, already carried from the table. Dene opens the door because Asholt tells him to. The children come out. There is no document. There is just the door open.

- **The party's position at close:** They were sent to reinforce a garrison under siege. They reinforced it for 36 days. The siege ended not because the walls held — they did hold, and that mattered — but because someone was willing to say the thing that ended it. The party made that possible. **This is not announced. It does not need to be.**

## GM Notes

**The standard's handling is the session's closing visual image.** Saren takes it down herself. She does not reinvert it. She rolls it and carries it. She trained here. She has carried the Thorngate device for twenty years. She is taking it with her now not as a challenge and not as a trophy. She is taking it because it is hers.

**The garrison's silence is intentional.** No cheering. These people have been holding a wall for 36 days. Some of them have wounds Maret could not treat. The end of the siege is not a victory they perform — it is a weight being put down. Let the silence land.

**Dene Flor opens the door.** The children come out first. The party has seen these people only in the lower keep — in the cramped space below the hall, with Dene's rations and his careful ledger. They come into the light. The session runner should describe this simply. The forty-seven people who have been here for three years come out of the lower keep. That is all that needs to be said.

**Asholt's line to the party:** *"You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."* This is the campaign's summary from his perspective. He did not ask for them. They came anyway. They stayed. They did not tell him what to do — they asked him one question and listened. They created the table. He said what needed to be said. This is what help looks like, in his accounting.

## Memory Fragment

> When the last Reconstitution tent came down, the pass was empty for the first time in 36 days. The mountains did not change. The pass was what it had always been: a gap between two cliff faces, one day's ride from Thorbardin, controlling three supply routes. It would control them next week and the week after. The standard that had been above the camp for 36 days was under someone's arm on the road south. The pass did not know the difference. Some of the garrison soldiers did.
