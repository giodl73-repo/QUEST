# Room 04 — The Accounting Scene

## Read-aloud

> The table. Three chairs. Saren across from Asholt. The party between them — not literally, but functionally. Davan has been composing this for six sessions. This is the room where he says it.

## Features

- **The DM's instruction for Davan's accounting speech:**

  The DM creates space and then stops. The exact structure:

  > *Saren asked for the accounting. She said: "Say it." The table is here. Asholt is here. She is here. Everything Davan has learned since Session 3 about the Oath, the people, the letter vs. the use — the shape from the S6 handoff is the floor, not the ceiling. Davan, what do you say?*

  The DM does not script the speech. The DM does not prompt individual lines. The DM creates the opening and holds silence. The speech is Davan's. Whatever Davan's player says is logged verbatim by the session runner as the session's artifact.

  **The structural shape (campaign-permanent from S6 handoff, provided as a floor):**

  *"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times."*

  The actual speech will be longer, more specific, more personal. The floor is there so the DM can confirm the essential elements are present — not to constrain the player's voice.

- **Saren's response to the accounting:**

  She listens. She does not interrupt. When Davan finishes: a pause. Then: *"That's the first time someone put it that way."* If she is in Route B or E territory, she adds: *"I knew it, the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* (Route B fires this line on the refugee surfacing; Route E fires it on Asholt's token.)

- **Route A fires here (3–4 tokens, political resolution):**
  The accounting speech is logged. Saren offers formal terms — she will draft a withdrawal document acknowledging the Oath question as unresolved and the garrison's right to shelter in ambiguous cases. She does not ask for Asholt's forgiveness. She asks for the garrison records to be opened — the refugees formally recognized. Asholt agrees. He does not give his full token. He says: *"The records will be opened."* That is the terms.

- **Route B fires here (4+ tokens + Dene's):**
  The accounting speech is logged. Saren's acceptance of Route A terms is interrupted — Davan (or another PC) names the refugees at the table. Not abstractly — *"the people you would have turned away are alive in Thorngate's lower keep"*. Saren sits with this. She looks at Asholt. He says: *"I couldn't tell you before. Now you know."* She stands. She says: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."* She walks away from the table. The siege is over. No formal terms — she gives the order, and her sergeant begins the withdrawal.

- **Route E fires here (7 tokens — all six + Asholt's full):**
  The accounting speech is logged. The route includes Route B's refugee naming. And then:

  Asholt reaches into his collar and takes out his half of the token. The party presents their half. He takes both halves and holds them together — the disc reunited, the cord's two cut ends touching — and then he places it on the table. Not toward Saren. Not toward the party. **Between them.**

  He says: *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

  A long pause. Saren looks at the token on the table. She reaches across and picks it up. She says: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

  She stands. The token is in her hand. She gives the withdrawal order.

  **The party receives nothing from either side. No terms, no formal acknowledgment, no document. What they receive is that this happened.**

- **What the session runner logs verbatim:**
  - Davan's full accounting speech
  - The route that fired (A, B, or E)
  - Asholt's exact line (Route E: see above; Routes A/B: as delivered)
  - Saren's arc-completion line: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*
  - Token state at close: what Saren is holding; what Asholt holds (nothing, Route E; his token, Routes A/B)

## GM Notes

**Hold the space.** When the DM opens the accounting invitation, they should not speak until Davan speaks. No "take your time." No nodding. No half-sentence prompts. Open space. Wait. If the player seems frozen: *"Saren is waiting. She asked for it. Davan has been carrying this since Session 3."* Then silence again.

**The speech does not need to be perfect.** It needs to be real. Davan does not need to synthesize all four hints into one polished address. He needs to name what both sides spent, what Saren was right about, and what it would mean to stop. If he does that, the speech is complete. Log whatever he says.

**Asholt's Route E placement:** When he places the token between them — not toward her, between them — the spatial detail matters. The token is not a gift and it is not a demand. It is an acknowledgment that both of them are on either side of it, and that neither of them can reach it alone. The party put it on the table by creating the parley. He is completing what they made possible.

**Saren picking up the token:** She does not hold it to her chest. She does not examine it. She holds it like she is deciding something, and then her decision is visible in the way she stands up. The token is not a prop — it is the physical form of the twenty-year thing being put down.

**After Saren leaves:** Asholt sits at the table for a moment. He does not speak. He is not performing grief — he is settling. The party is with him. This is the last time Davan or any PC needs to ask him a question. They have already asked the one question that mattered.

## Memory Fragment

> In Solamnic tradition, when two officers of the same order reach a formal understanding that was not possible through the chain of command, the gesture of recognition is to place the house token face-down on the ground between them. Face-down means: not on display, not claimed, available. Asholt places his token face-up. He is not hiding it. He never was.
