# Room 02 — The Approach

## Read-aloud

> Open ground. The killing ground of 36 days, walked in daylight with a white cloth on a pole. The Reconstitution camp is ahead. Three soldiers break from the tree line — not under orders. They are coming anyway.

## Features

- **The no-man's-land crossing:** 250 feet of open ground between the east wall and the tree line. The party crosses with a parley flag (white cloth on garrison standard pole). The crossing takes approximately 2 minutes of in-game time at walking pace. **DC 12 Perception (Talis, auto):** The soldiers break from the tree line before the party has crossed halfway. They are moving fast. One of them is calling back toward the camp — not a warning call, an argument. Someone is telling them to stop. They are not stopping.

- **The hard-liners:** Two or three Reconstitution soldiers who do not have Saren's authorization to intercept a parley flag. They know they do not have her authorization. They are acting anyway — they believe the siege should end in a military outcome, not a conversation, and the party's approach with a flag is the thing they have been dreading. Their exact composition depends on the token count and Session 6's end state:

  - **If the session has high tension (wall breached in S6):** Three hard-liners; one is a sergeant. The sergeant knows he is wrong and comes anyway because he cannot make himself stop.
  - **Standard encounter:** Two hard-liners. They fight. They do not yield until below 5 HP.

  See `encounters/hard-liner-intercept.md`

- **Saren's response (from the camp edge):** On a DC 12 Perception: a figure at the camp edge is shouting in the direction of the hard-liners. She is ordering them to stand down. She does not have a weapon drawn — she is ordering, not fighting. If the combat takes more than 2 rounds in the no-man's-land: one of her soldiers (a sergeant loyal to her, not a hard-liner) runs forward and physically intervenes, restraining the closest hard-liner. This ends the combat — the hard-liners yield to her sergeant. The party's flag is still valid.

- **After the combat:** The hard-liners are restrained or incapacitated. The path to the camp edge is clear. Saren's sergeant waves the party forward. Saren herself has stepped outside the camp threshold onto the open parley ground. She is waiting.

- **Party HP after the combat:** Record. This is what they carry into the parley scene. There is no healing available. Every HP spent here is spent for the rest of the session.

## GM Notes

**This combat is brief by design.** 2–3 soldiers. The party fights with whatever HP they have from S6. The encounter's difficulty is situational — the party is in the open, with a parley flag, without garrison backup, on no sleep. The soldiers are not tactical geniuses. They break at the 5-HP threshold or when Saren's sergeant intervenes.

**The parley flag does not provide mechanical protection.** It provides moral and narrative weight — Saren will not fire on a parley flag. The hard-liners are violating the convention and they know it. If they kill a party member here, the parley is over and Saren will be furious. The DM should communicate this pressure without guaranteeing the party's safety.

**Saren's intervention is automatic after Round 2.** Her sergeant arrives in Round 3 if the combat is still happening. The party does not need to last more than 2–3 rounds. The combat's purpose is cost, not survival test.

**The shout from the camp edge:** If a PC is paying attention during the combat (DC 12 Perception mid-combat, or passive check), they can hear that Saren is calling her soldiers off. This is information: she honored the flag. She is in control of her camp, not the hard-liners. This matters for how the parley opens.
