# Room 01 — The Postern

## Read-aloud

> The east postern gate. Hessa Var has the bar in her hands. Outside: the killing ground, 250 feet of open country, and the Reconstitution camp at the tree line. Saren's standard is still flying. The party is about to leave the walls one last time.

## Features

- **The parley flag (party initiative):** The party must declare they are crossing under a parley flag. The Solamnic tradition of parley under white cloth is known to both sides — **DC 10 History, or any Solamnic-background PC knows without a check:** a white cloth tied to a garrison standard pole signals formal parley request. Under Solamnic convention, the requesting party may cross no-man's-land; the receiving party may not fire on them without violating the convention. Saren will honor it. The hard-liners may not. This is the parley's tactical risk.

  If the party asks Asholt for authorization to use the parley flag: he says yes. He does not accompany them at this stage — he has given his word that he will come when Saren is at the table. He is not going first.

- **Hessa Var's role:** Gate-Captain Hessa Var opens the east postern for the party and bars it behind them. She will open it again when the party returns — a party member at the wall base can signal her (one sharp knock on the gate base, audible to the guard on the other side). She stays at the gate herself for the duration. **If the party asks her something:** *"I'll be here when you get back."* Nothing more. She has earned that certainty.

- **What the party can see from the postern threshold:** The Reconstitution camp is visible at the tree line, 250 feet away. Movement around the outer perimeter: standard rotation, fewer soldiers than a week ago. The inverted standard is still flying. **DC 12 Perception:** One figure standing outside the command tent, not moving — she is watching the postern. She has been watching it since dawn. She knows the party has Mik's information. She is waiting.

- **The garrison at the walls:** The wall-watchers on the east parapet see the party exit. They do not cheer. Some of them have tokens in the party's possession — Irr's, Maret's, Hessa's, Dene's, Luca's. They hold their posts. They watch. The session log should note this: the garrison witnesses the party's exit.

- **Asholt's position:** He is not at the postern. He is in the corridor (Scene 05 of the previous session was his commitment: he will come when she is at the table). The party crosses without him. This is the condition he named. He meant it.

## Encounter

None in this room. The combat is in Room 02 or 03.

## Connections

- Interior (gate, barred): Great Hall and east wall (accessible via Hessa Var's signal)
- Exterior (east, open): The Approach (Room 02), no-man's-land crossing

## GM Notes

**Let the party choose the timing.** They may cross at dawn, midday, or dusk. The timing does not change the parley's availability — Saren is watching the gate regardless — but it changes the scene's atmosphere. Dawn crossing: cold, early light, the standard visible against the sky. Midday: full sun, exposed, no shadows. Dusk: the deadline is 48 hours and counting; the urgency is real.

**The 48-hour window (established in premise):** Saren has 48 hours before her own command's recall becomes impossible to refuse. The party knows this from Mik (implied in "she's been refusing withdrawal orders for two weeks"). The session runner should note the time at the postern crossing and track it — not punishing the party, but keeping the clock present.

**Hessa's line ("I'll be here when you get back") is earned.** She gave her token in Session 3 after holding herself accountable. She has been at this gate for 31 days. She is not performing steadiness — she has it. Let the line land without commentary.

**The garrison watching:** This detail should be mentioned when the party steps through the gate. Not as theater — as fact. The people the party has been working with for six sessions are watching them walk toward the camp that has been besieging them. They do not need words. The moment is the moment.

## Memory Fragment

> The east postern gate was last opened in anger on day three, when Asholt sent a formal message to the Reconstitution demanding they identify their command authority. The message went through this gate. It came back three days later, unsigned. Hessa was the one who received it. She knew then that the siege was not going to end through correspondence.
