# 0028 — The Parley

**Logline:** On day 36 of the siege, the party brings Saren Coldforge to the table, one PC speaks the accounting, and Warden Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table — after twenty years, the thing that could not be said is said.

## The adventure in one sentence

Forty-eight hours before Saren defies her own command's final recall, the party arranges the parley, Davan speaks what he has been composing for six sessions, Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table, and the siege ends with the thing that was never said finally said.

## Setting

**Thorngate Keep and the no-man's-land between the walls, Day 36 of the Siege.** Saren has 48 hours before she violates a direct recall order from Reconstitution command. The new supply route confirmation (Mik's Hint 4) is in the party's possession. Asholt's half-token is in the party's possession. The party is at whatever HP they carried out of S6.

**The pressure:** Saren is not going to be here in 48 hours if nothing changes. Her command will pull her out physically if she refuses again. The window is real and it is closing.

**The keep's state:** Supply Die exhausted. No garrison healing. The midnight assault cost the party significant HP. They enter S7 carrying that cost. The combat in this session is brief — 2–3 hard-liner soldiers — but it is fought with no resources. Every HP spent is real.

## Resource state entering S7

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S6 + 1 hit die of recovery (capped at max).
- **Supply Die:** Exhausted (unchanged). No garrison healing. No potions from garrison stock.
- **Asholt's half-token:** In party custody. Physical: a split wooden disc, cord tied off.
- **All other tokens collected:** Note total token count from handoff. Route determination depends on this number.

## The three routes

The parley can fire three ways, depending on the party's token count. The route determines what the parley achieves — not whether it happens. All three routes assume the party successfully brings Saren to the table.

### Route A (3–4 tokens)
Political resolution. Formal terms negotiated. Reconstitution withdraws under Reconstitution command's recall plus a face-saving formal document acknowledging the Oath question was legitimate (not Saren wrong, but the question unresolved). Garrison records opened. Refugees formally recognized as sheltered under Solamnic garrison provision. Asholt and Saren exchange formal words. They do not have the personal conversation.

**What fires:** The accounting speech (Davan) goes into the record. Formal terms. Refugee recognition. The siege ends.
**What does not fire:** Asholt's full token. The Oath conversation. Saren's arc-completion line.

### Route B (4+ tokens + Dene's token)
Refugee surfacing. In addition to Route A's political resolution, the refugees are named at the table and both Asholt and Saren hear it simultaneously. Saren confronts the fact that Asholt sheltered people she would have turned away, and that they are alive. She withdraws without formal terms — she walks away from the table and does not come back. Her line as she leaves: *"You should have told me twenty years ago."* She is not wrong.

**What fires:** Refugee recognition (direct, named). Saren hears the people she called wrong to shelter. Her withdrawal is personal, not tactical.
**Route E fires additionally** if token count is 7.

### Route E (7 tokens — 6 party tokens + Asholt's full)
The accounting is spoken. Asholt gives his full token to Saren across the table. She takes it.

**Requirements:** All 6 garrison tokens earned + Asholt's half-token in party custody. The party must have his half-token to present; Asholt brings his half; the disc is rejoined at the table before he places it.

**What fires:** Davan's accounting speech (logged verbatim). Asholt speaks the arc-completion line to Saren directly. Saren takes the token. Her arc-completion line fires. The siege ends with something restored that was broken twenty years ago.

## The parley arrangement

The party must accomplish three things before the parley can happen:

1. **Reach Saren** — cross the no-man's-land under a flag of parley (Solamnic tradition: white cloth tied to a garrison standard). The first attempt will be observed and potentially intercepted by hard-liner soldiers. See Room 02 and combat encounter.

2. **Present the case to Saren** — tell her what the party knows. She knows about the new supply route. She does not know that the party knows. The revelation that the party has Mik's information shifts the dynamic: she can no longer maintain the strategic fiction. She must engage personally.

3. **Get Asholt to the table** — the party has his word (*"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her."*). He comes when the party returns and says Saren is willing. He walks across the gap himself. He is not accompanied by garrison soldiers. He comes alone.

## Davan's accounting speech

This is the session's central deliverable. The DM does not script it. The player speaks it.

The structural invitation, accumulated across six sessions:
- S3: Davan encountered "correct evidence / wrong person" — he had the right understanding about the Oath but applied it to the wrong situation.
- S4: Davan named "the Oath doesn't tell me this" — the first time he acknowledged the Oath's limits.
- S5: Davan witnessed the institutional/personal split — Helin's account of why Saren left named the Oath and the people as two separate things.
- S6: Davan heard Asholt's lines in the corridor — "she was right about the Oath, wrong about the people" — and heard Mik's information. He now has the full picture.

**The shape (from the S6 handoff, campaign-permanent):** *"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times."*

The speech synthesizes what both sides spent. It names what Saren was right about (the Oath's letter). It names what Asholt was right about (the people). It names what the refugees cost everyone, and what it would mean to stop. The DM logs it verbatim. The session's artifact is Davan's words.

**See DM instructions in Room 04 for the exact invitation structure.**

## Saren Coldforge

She has been waiting for 31 days for something to arrive at the table. She does not know what it is. When the party presents it — when Davan speaks it — she recognizes it. Her arc-completion fires at the table when she takes Asholt's full token. Her line: *"Twenty years. I knew it the day I left. I didn't know how to come back."*

She is not a villain. She is a person who was right about a letter and wrong about a use, who has spent twenty years holding both halves without a way to put them down. The token in her hands is the way to put them down.

**See NPC file:** `npcs/saren-coldforge.md`

## Post-parley

**Saren's withdrawal:** She stands her soldiers down. She orders the camp struck. The process takes the day. By nightfall of Day 36, the Reconstitution camp is withdrawn from The Needle pass.

**The garrison:** The siege is over. 36 days. Sergeant Irr tells the party: *"We held."* He does not say more. Maret Stave opens the supply ledger column again. The blank pages are the record.

**The refugees:** Whatever route fired, the refugees are acknowledged — formally (Route A/B) or in the context of the personal conversation (Route E). Dene Flor comes out of the lower keep with the children first.

**The party's position:** They were sent to reinforce. They did something different. Whether the Rose Council considers what they did to be within their orders is a question for S8 or beyond. Asholt tells them, before they leave: *"You are welcome to help. I did not ask for command. You gave it to me anyway, when I needed it."* This is the closest he has come to acknowledging their role.
