# 0041 — The Last Patrol
## Module Compiled

---

## DM Cheatsheet

**Unit Cohesion Die:** d10 or d8 → **degrades to d6 (or d4 if already d6) this session.**
**Party HP:** Carry-over + 1 hit die.
**Hardest combat.** Veterans flanking Round 2. Lenne is a priority target. Dorn's Action Surge is for this session.
**Key note:** Unit soldiers act on Varet's direction (bonus action). With d8 ≈ 4-8 soldiers, party has meaningful support but Veterans are a real threat.

---

## Scene 0 — Departure

**Read-aloud:**
> Morning. The unit forms up. Nobody talks much. Arren Vole checks his sword twice. He does not comment on it. Lenne counts her supply kit; she has been counting more carefully since the cache sessions. Taris has his map out before anyone else is ready. Dorn stands in formation and looks at Carra, then Fest, then Oln. He does not speak.
>
> Varet reads the orders. Standard patrol. She does not read the discrepancy column aloud. She folds the journal and leads the unit out.

## Scene 1 — Ridgehead Marker

**Read-aloud:**
> The marker. Taris stops. He crouches for a moment over the trail. He stands. "Eight sets of tracks. Moving toward the ford. Within the last hour." He looks at Varet. He does not say what it means. He has already updated the map.

**Beats:**
- Taris: Survival DC 10 (Favored Terrain: auto-advantage, effectively auto-pass). Eight sets of tracks, fresh.
- Varet: prepares the unit. Unit soldiers fan out (reduce column to line, cover flanks).
- Dorn: positions Carra, Fest, Oln — left flank, right flank, rear. Announces their positions aloud. Heuristic firing.

## Scene 2 — Creston Ford Ambush

**Read-aloud:**
> The ford opens up. And the men are already there — four in the water, two at the south bank behind the mule-post cover, crossbows up. You hear movement behind you a half-second before you see the two flankers emerge from the tree-line, closing fast.

**ROLL INITIATIVE.**

**Combat: Creston Ford Ambush**

Enemy positions:
- 4 Thugs in ford (HP 32 each, difficult terrain)
- 2 Bandit archers south bank (half-cover; targeting Lenne)
- 2 Veteran flankers north bank (arrive Round 2 from tree-line concealment — NOT visible Round 1)

**Unit soldiers (d8/d6 result, directed by Varet):**
- Varet directs as bonus action. "North — take the flankers when they show." Soldiers move to intercept flankers on Round 2.

**Key PC moments:**

**Taris:**
- Hunter's Mark (bonus action, Round 1): mark lead Thug in ford.
- Colossus Slayer fires on first Thug below max HP.
- Favored Terrain: mountain. No difficult terrain penalty in ford.
- Initiative +5 (Dex +3, proficiency relevant in Favored Terrain).

**Varet:**
- Sacred Weapon: +Cha (+3) to attacks for 1 minute (bonus action).
- Turn the Tide (reaction): when a unit soldier or PC drops to 0 HP, grant 1d6+3 temp HP as reaction. This fires in Round 2 when Carra drops.
- Lay on Hands: 15 HP pool. She uses it on Carra at the first opportunity (not herself).

**Lenne:**
- **Lenne calls Fel's position aloud, Round 2:** "Fel — left flank — go wide." She knows where he is. Her heuristic fires. It is a tactical command. It is also the heuristic made audible.
- Spiritual Weapon (bonus action, L2): spectral weapon in ford, attacks Thugs.
- Preserve Life (Channel Divinity, if needed): distribute up to 30 HP among creatures within 30 ft, up to half their max.
- Healing Word as needed (bonus action).

**Dorn:**
- Round 1: Menacing Attack on nearest Thug (Superiority Die d8, add to damage, DC 14 Wis save or frightened).
- Round 2: **BRACE (reaction)** when Veteran closes on Carra — fires before Veteran's attack if within reach. Attack: +5, 1d8+3.
- If Carra still drops: **Action Surge** fires on Veteran that took her down.
  - This is the contractual firing condition: *"Action Surge is reserved for scenes where the contract would otherwise be violated."* Carra going down = Dorn's professional obligation to the people he has served with before.

**Carra's incapacitation (mechanically designed):**
- Round 2: one Veteran reaches north bank. Targets Carra (HP 11 soldier — automatic 0 HP from Veteran's longsword +5, 1d8+3 ≈ 8 avg, but can hit 11+ on higher rolls). Dorn's Brace reaction fires.
- Carra at 0 HP: Lenne's Spare the Dying stabilizes her (cantrip, no action cost).
- Dorn: announces Carra's position. *"Carra's down — left flank."* Not an emotion. A report.

**Wandering table:** Not applicable — active combat encounter.

**Resolution:**
- Veterans are the deciding factor. Once they fall or flee, Thugs break (morale).
- Bandit archers: if Lenne takes significant damage (>12 HP), they shift to second PC.
- Combat ends in 4-5 rounds.

## Scene 3 — Post-Combat: Captured Fighter

**Read-aloud:**
> Arren Vole has one of the Sorn fighters face-down on the bank, hands held. The man is breathing. He is scared.
>
> Arren looks at Varet. He doesn't ask permission. He says: "He's ready to talk, Commander."

**Interrogation (social, no roll required — fighter is willing):**
- Varet asks one question: "Who sent you?"
- Fighter: *"Drev Ossian. He's the one who pays Sorn. I don't know more than that. I was hired three weeks ago. We were told to get the journal. We weren't told to kill anyone. I think—"* He stops. He said too much. "I think the orders changed."

**H4 full delivery:** Drev Ossian (Istaran trade factor) → Sorn → Veth → unit. The chain is named. The origin is not a military officer. It is a trade network.

**v1.3 fallbacks (H4):**
- (a) Fighter names Ossian explicitly.
- (b) Ossian's name appears in the Thorngate record-room document (Scene 4).
- (c) Arren Vole confirms Ossian's trade-factor status from patrol records in S7 if missed.

## Scene 4 — The Record-Room

**Read-aloud:**
> Thorngate Keep's record-room. A clerk who has seen too much and chosen to see nothing. Varet presents the formal objection document from S4, which gives her standing to request patrol-related correspondence. The clerk checks the standing. He checks it twice. He gives her access to a file labeled "Irenvale Crossing — logistics correspondence."
>
> The file has fourteen documents. Thirteen are standard. One is a letter on Istaran trade-house stationery signed by a factor named Drev Ossian. It references "the unit assigned to the crossing" as "a temporary variable to be managed." It is dated six months ago.

**Investigation DC 11:** the letter is the document. It exists. It is findable.
**Varet: takes the document.** This is evidence. It goes in the journal sleeve (the journal has a leather document-sleeve sewn inside the back cover — she designed it for this purpose without knowing what she would carry).

**Anchor-level symptom:** The Ossian letter. v1.3 fallbacks already noted in Scene 3.
