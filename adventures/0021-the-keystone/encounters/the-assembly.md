# Encounter — The Assembly

## Type

Social encounter (extended). Three stages: faction positions, Calla's account, the choice.

## Stage 1 — Faction Positions

**The congregation's position (Veth speaks for them):**
*"We have been in this building for thirty years. We are not leaving. We would like to stay, but we understand that is not ours alone to decide."*

**The town's position (Doran speaks):**
*"This building was commissioned for the town of Mireth-on-the-Hill and the faith of Mishakal. I need it completed and functional. I need an answer today."*

**The party's position:** The party delivers the truth: Velantha used unauthorized High Sorcery; the miracle was not divine grace; the congregation is real; the faith is real; the authorization was forged.

Doran hears this. She asks: *"Is there proof?"* She is shown the dispensation letter (warm, indestructible, marked with Aldric's notation). She asks Aldric to confirm. He does.

## Stage 2 — Calla's Account

Calla Fosse stands in the center of the nave.

She has three sources: Morren's oral account (in her memory), Aldric's written copy (in her hands), Velantha's journal (in her hands). She has been composing since the Crypt.

**She speaks.**

The session-runner logs the account verbatim. Whatever Calla says — that is what was spoken. The DM plays Calla from her character sheet's heuristics: she listens before speaking; when she speaks, she gives what she holds. She speaks the synthesis of seven sessions of accumulation.

**The journal's once-per-day exact transmission:** She may use this once to speak one sentence exactly as it was given to her. She will know which sentence. The session log notes which sentence she chose to speak exactly.

**The account includes, in some form:**
- She came because they were sick
- She used something that wasn't prayer
- She asked their permission
- She promised some she would come back and died instead
- She never asked for a cathedral; she asked for medicine
- She called it the right thing to do

The account does not have to be exactly these words. It is Calla's synthesis. But these are the things she holds.

**When Calla finishes:** The room is quiet. Then Doran says: *"She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine."* She does not ask this as a question. She is repeating the line from the account. She is understanding it.

## Stage 3 — The Choice

**After Calla speaks:** The party presents the route options.

### Route A
Party exposes the truth as a clearance tool. Congregation's faith shatters — the miracle was a High Sorcery working, not divine grace. The congregation leaves. The keystone is set blank. The building becomes the town's cathedral.

### Route C
Party brokers coexistence. Two services, shared space, written terms. The keystone is set blank. The tension is permanent. Neither community is fully satisfied.

### Route D
Party asks Doran to commission a second cathedral and cede this one. She goes to R5 (if she wants privacy) or decides here. The congregation chisels Velantha's name. The keystone is set. Doran signs the commission. Her arc-completion fires.

## Dren's Second Arc Beat

During or after the assembly, someone asks Dren what he is going to do. (Candidate: Veth, or Renn, or Doran, or no one — but someone will ask.) He answers: he names what he is going to do about Aldis's family. He names the insignia. He names what it has meant to carry it. He says what he is going to do next.

This is the second arc beat. It fires in this room, during or after the assembly, when someone asks the right question. The DM should create the space for it — not force it, but not let the scene end without the space being present.

## Maret's Practice

Before Calla speaks: Maret performs three breaths in the center of the nave. The congregation recognizes the practice. No one comments. Maret steps aside. Calla takes her position.

This is the craft-witness's final expression: the practice, performed at the campaign's climax, in the room where the campaign's question will be answered.
