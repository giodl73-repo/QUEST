## Features

This room has no features to examine. It is what comes after.

**Campaign-permanent facts (post-S7, Route D):**
- The Halted Spire is formally ceded to the congregation. Velantha's name is in the keystone. The building is consecrated as the Cathedral of Velantha of Mireth (or a name the congregation will choose).
- Magistra Doran has commissioned a second cathedral on new ground. Construction will begin in spring. The commission is signed and witnessed.
- The dispensation letter: wherever the party left it. If they gave it to Doran, she has the evidence for the Orders. If they kept it, they carry permanent evidence. If they showed it to the congregation: the congregation now knows what Velantha used and that she didn't ask for a cathedral — she asked for medicine. (They already knew; now it is confirmed by the evidence.)
- The healer's journal is with Calla. If she held it when the keystone was set, it rewrites once more.

**What the party carries away (Route D):**
- The promise-cord: Dren attunes it or returns it to the congregation. Either is valid.
- The ritual focus: Renn keeps it. The mark on it connects him to Aldric, who is staying in the Sacristy, which is now part of a cathedral that belongs to gully dwarves. Renn's pact and Velantha's working and Aldric's copying are all under the same roof.
- The dispensation letter: indestructible, warm, permanent.
- The journal: rewrites for the right reader. Will continue to do so.

## GM Notes

The campaign is complete.

What Calla spoke: the session-runner logged it verbatim. It is campaign-permanent. Whatever synthesis she produced from Morren's oral account, Aldric's written copy, and Velantha's journal: that is what was spoken at the Keystone. That is what the session log records. The PC-authored deliverable is complete.

What the party does next is not the campaign's question. The campaign's question was "Who gets to decide who is worth saving — and who gets to decide what counts as faith?" The answer, in Route D, is: the people who kept faith for thirty years. The building decides nothing. The people do.
