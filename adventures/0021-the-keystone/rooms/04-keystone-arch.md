# Room 04 — The Keystone Arch

## Read-aloud

> The arch. The keystone above in its cradle, held by the construction rig that has been here for eleven years. The blank face visible — smooth limestone, uncut. The stone that will complete the building and allow consecration. It has been waiting.

*(44 words)*

## Features

- **The keystone** (in cradle). Cut. Blank face. Route D: the congregation chisels Velantha's name into it. Twenty minutes of work. They will do it correctly.

- **The construction rig** (eleven years old, congregation-maintained). The congregation oiled the rig joints periodically for eleven years. It still works. They have been ready to set the stone; they needed the right conditions.

- **The setting mechanism** (visible). The keystone can be set by turning the rig's crank and guiding the stone into position. DC 10 Strength, one action, with help. Dren (Strength 17) can do this alone.

## Encounter

**The Setting (Route D):**
1. The congregation chisels Velantha's name (20 minutes).
2. Calla speaks the account in the nave (while chiseling proceeds).
3. Doran signs the commission (during or after the account).
4. Dren sets the keystone.
5. The arch is complete. The building can be consecrated.

**If Calla holds the journal when the keystone is set:** The journal's final entry rewrites. *"They set the stone with my name in it. That is what I would have asked for, if anyone had thought to ask. I am glad."*

## Connections

- **West arch:** R3 (The Nave — assembly)

## GM Notes

The keystone setting is one action from Dren and twenty minutes from the congregation. The campaign's physical completion is simple. The choice that preceded it was not.

**Dren's moment:** He turns the crank. The stone drops into position. The arch completes. The building holds. Dren's hands are on the crank when the building becomes what it is. He has been securing positions since Session 1. He secured this one last.
