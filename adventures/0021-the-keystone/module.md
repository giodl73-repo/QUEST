## Setting & Background

**Place:** The Halted Spire — all of it. The finale takes place in and around the cathedral: the exterior court (where Doran waited), the nave (where the factions assemble), and the keystone arch position (where the choice is made and enacted). The building has been the campaign's location and is now its stage.

**The keystone:** A cut stone, unhewn face blank. The arch above the nave's main threshold — the final stone that would complete the building's structural integrity and allow consecration. It has been cut and waiting for eleven years. The congregation has been here for thirty. The keystone waited for the party.

**All factions present:**
- **The congregation**: Mig, Veth, Sharr, Morren, Gorret, and the others from all sections of the building. They have come together for the first time.
- **Aldric Voss**: here from the Sacristy. He has his new ceramic holder and the letter he cannot destroy.
- **Velantha**: present, if the party invited her. She came for the same reason she came thirty years ago.
- **Magistra Doran of Mireth** and her representatives: she came herself. She is here with the town's authorization to act.
- **The Witnesses**: Maret Sorn, Calla Fosse, Renn Ashkeld, Dren Holt.

When the party returns from the Crypt, Doran is at the exterior. She came because she said she would. The party has one conversation with her before the assembly convenes inside.

Then: the assembly. Then: Calla speaks. Then: the choice.

**The keystone** — the final stone of the arch. Unhewn face, blank. Route D: the congregation chisels Velantha's name into it before it is set.

Full artifact file: `treasures/keystone.md`

**Route A — Expose:**
The party tells the congregation the truth as a clearance tool. The congregation's faith shatters. They leave. The keystone is set blank. The cathedral becomes the town's Mishakal cathedral. Aldric's case is referred to the Orders. The dispensation letter is presented as evidence.

**Route C — Share:**
The party brokers a coexistence agreement. Two services. Shared space. The keystone is set blank. Neither community is fully satisfied. The tension is permanent but both communities remain.

**Route D — Give:**
The party persuades Doran to commission a second cathedral on clean ground and formally cede this one to the congregation. The congregation chisels Velantha's name into the keystone's face. The Bard speaks Velantha's true story to all assembled factions. Doran signs the commission. The keystone is set. Velantha's name is in the arch.

**Calla speaks Velantha's true story.** She has been composing since the Crypt. She speaks it before the choice is made, to all assembled factions. What she says is her own synthesis of Morren's oral account, Aldric's written copy, and Velantha's journal. The session-runner logs it verbatim.

The structural moment: Calla stands in the nave with the keystone behind her, all factions present, and speaks. She has held this for seven sessions. She speaks it now.

The journal's once-per-day exact transmission: she may use this once to speak one sentence exactly as it was given to her. The sentence she chooses to speak exactly: the party will know when she speaks it.

**Condition (Route D):** Doran commissions the second cathedral herself, in writing, having accepted what the first one is.

**Her line:** *"The town I know will not like this. I am doing it anyway."* She signs the commission document.

Doran's arc completes when she acts against the expectation of the people she serves because she has understood something they haven't yet. She hired the party to clear a building. She is signing a commission to build a different one. She will not be thanked for this. She does it anyway.

Someone asks Dren during the assembly or afterward: "What are you going to do about it?" — referring to Aldis, to the insignia, to the question the inscription answered ("She came when we asked"). 

His answer: he has been carrying the insignia for four years. He names what he is going to do: find Aldis's family. Tell them. Return the insignia to them, or keep it as something else — something he carried for both of them. This is the second arc beat. The first was naming Aldis. The second is what he does with the name.

The party knows about his pact. In the assembly, with everyone present, he stands in the room where three unauthorized acts that cannot be undone are represented: the letter (Aldric's), the working (Velantha's), the pact (his). He does not need to speak about this. His presence in the room is the completion.

Her craft-witness practice has been confirmed session-invariant across the campaign. In the assembly, she performs her three breaths before Calla speaks. No one knows why. It is the practice. It is enough.

All campaign seeds are present at the finale. The dispensation letter is here. The journal is here. The keystone is here. The party decides what to do with all three.

# The Keystone

*A cut stone, blank face, waiting thirty years to be set.*

## Appearance

Grey limestone, cut to the keystone shape for the nave's main arch. The face that will be visible when set — the outer face of the arch — is unhewn: smooth from the cutting but not dressed, not inscribed. Eleven years of exposure to the hill's weather. Thirty years of a congregation that has walked past it daily without touching it, because it was not theirs to set.

It is larger than it looks from a distance. When you stand next to it, you understand: this is the last structural element. Without it, the arch is incomplete. With it, the building holds.

## Properties

### Route D Property (acquired when Velantha's name is carved before setting)

**Consecration true-foundation:** When the keystone is set with Velantha's name carved into its face, the arch is completed and the building can be consecrated — not as a Mishakal cathedral under the town's original commission, but as what it actually is: a cathedral to an unauthorized miracle, built by the people it saved. The consecration is honest. The foundation is true.

This is not a mechanical effect in the D&D sense. It is the building's purpose, declared at the moment of its completion. The consecration is the party's to perform, in the words Calla speaks, in the choice Doran makes, in the act of setting the stone.

### Route A/C Property (set blank)

If set without inscription: the arch is complete. The building can be consecrated as originally planned — a Mishakal cathedral for the town of Mireth-on-the-Hill. The congregation's thirty years are not written into the foundation. The truth is not embedded in the stone.

### The Journal's Rewrite (if held at setting)

If the healer's journal is held by a qualifying reader when the keystone is set — specifically Maret (passive 17) or Calla (who has been composing from it) — the final entry rewrites once more:

*"They set the stone with my name in it. That is what I would have asked for, if anyone had thought to ask. I am glad."*

This is the campaign's last relic sign. The journal's response to the finale.

## GM Notes

The keystone is not a magical item in the traditional sense. It gains its significance from what is carved into it and what that carving means. The congregation chiseling Velantha's name is twenty minutes of work by people who have been maintaining this building for thirty years. They are good at this. They will get the letters right.

Let them.

### Doran Negotiation

# Encounter — Doran's Negotiation

## Type

Social encounter. Two stages: initial confrontation (R1) and, for Route D, private consideration (R5).

## Stage 1 — Initial Confrontation (R1, Exterior)

Doran: *"I've been waiting two hours. I need an answer today."*

The party can:
- Tell her they cleared the site (Route A — she accepts; they proceed)
- Tell her they found something unexpected (Routes B/C/D — she needs more information)
- Ask her to come inside (all routes — she will)

If the party says they found something unexpected: *"Then I need to see it."* She follows them inside.

## Stage 2 — After Calla's Account (Route D)

Doran has heard the account. She has held the dispensation letter. She has seen it not burn in her hands.

In private or in public, she says:

*"You're asking me to sign a commission for a building that doesn't exist yet, to replace a building that thirty years of work has already made into something else, and to tell the town of Mireth that the story they built their identity around was not a miracle — it was a healer using methods the Orders had forbidden, for the sake of creatures the theology excludes."*

Pause.

*"Is that what you're asking?"*

If the party says yes: she is quiet for a long time.

*"The town I know will not like this."*

Longer pause.

*"I am doing it anyway."*

**Doran's arc-completion fires.** She signs the commission.

## What Changed Her

The account contained the line: "She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine."

Doran has been building this town for thirty years. She has been maintaining the Velantha-miracle story for thirty years. She built her town's identity around a woman who came when the town wouldn't, and she built that identity on the version of the story that served the town's needs.

She is now signing a commission to build a different building because the correct version of the story — the one that is true — requires a different foundation.

She is a good administrator. She is making the correct decision for the foundation, not the convenient one.

**Doran's commission document:** She signs it. The document commits the town of Mireth-on-the-Hill to commission a second cathedral on new ground, in Mishakal's name, with correct authorization. The first cathedral is formally ceded to the congregation of Velantha of Mireth.

She does not say she believes this is the right thing to do. She does not say she believes in the congregation's faith. She says she is doing it. That is her arc.

### The Assembly

# Encounter — The Assembly

## Type

Social encounter (extended). Three stages: faction positions, Calla's account, the choice.

## Stage 1 — Faction Positions

**The congregation's position (Veth speaks for them):**
*"We have been in this building for thirty years. We are not leaving. We would like to stay, but we understand that is not ours alone to decide."*

**The town's position (Doran speaks):**
*"This building was commissioned for the town of Mireth-on-the-Hill and the faith of Mishakal. I need it completed and functional. I need an answer today."*

**The party's position:** The party delivers the truth: Velantha used unauthorized High Sorcery; the miracle was not divine grace; the congregation is real; the faith is real; the authorization was forged.

Doran hears this. She asks: *"Is there proof?"* She is shown the dispensation letter (warm, indestructible, marked with Aldric's notation). She asks Aldric to confirm. He does.

## Stage 2 — Calla's Account

Calla Fosse stands in the center of the nave.

She has three sources: Morren's oral account (in her memory), Aldric's written copy (in her hands), Velantha's journal (in her hands). She has been composing since the Crypt.

**She speaks.**

The session-runner logs the account verbatim. Whatever Calla says — that is what was spoken. The DM plays Calla from her character sheet's heuristics: she listens before speaking; when she speaks, she gives what she holds. She speaks the synthesis of seven sessions of accumulation.

**The journal's once-per-day exact transmission:** She may use this once to speak one sentence exactly as it was given to her. She will know which sentence. The session log notes which sentence she chose to speak exactly.

**The account includes, in some form:**
- She came because they were sick
- She used something that wasn't prayer
- She asked their permission
- She promised some she would come back and died instead
- She never asked for a cathedral; she asked for medicine
- She called it the right thing to do

The account does not have to be exactly these words. It is Calla's synthesis. But these are the things she holds.

**When Calla finishes:** The room is quiet. Then Doran says: *"She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine."* She does not ask this as a question. She is repeating the line from the account. She is understanding it.

## Stage 3 — The Choice

**After Calla speaks:** The party presents the route options.

### Route A
Party exposes the truth as a clearance tool. Congregation's faith shatters — the miracle was a High Sorcery working, not divine grace. The congregation leaves. The keystone is set blank. The building becomes the town's cathedral.

### Route C
Party brokers coexistence. Two services, shared space, written terms. The keystone is set blank. The tension is permanent. Neither community is fully satisfied.

### Route D
Party asks Doran to commission a second cathedral and cede this one. She goes to R5 (if she wants privacy) or decides here. The congregation chisels Velantha's name. The keystone is set. Doran signs the commission. Her arc-completion fires.

## Dren's Second Arc Beat

During or after the assembly, someone asks Dren what he is going to do. (Candidate: Veth, or Renn, or Doran, or no one — but someone will ask.) He answers: he names what he is going to do about Aldis's family. He names the insignia. He names what it has meant to carry it. He says what he is going to do next.

This is the second arc beat. It fires in this room, during or after the assembly, when someone asks the right question. The DM should create the space for it — not force it, but not let the scene end without the space being present.

## Maret's Practice

Before Calla speaks: Maret performs three breaths in the center of the nave. The congregation recognizes the practice. No one comments. Maret steps aside. Calla takes her position.

This is the craft-witness's final expression: the practice, performed at the campaign's climax, in the room where the campaign's question will be answered.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
