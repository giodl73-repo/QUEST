# The Wrath-Coin — DM Module

*A forge-priest returns to his master's grave for the three-year rite — and discovers the mine-collapse was no collapse. The hammer that should be buried is not. What Duren was secretly forging — Aelwen's fourth moon-silver object, a coin that holds the rage of everyone who has wronged the bearer — is in a Thorbardin elder's vault, and he intends to use it this week.*

## Summary

A three-act adventure spanning the Kharolis road, Gavrek's Anvil (Grom's ordination shrine), the Lower Ore-Shaft (where Duren died), and Kruntharrak (Thane Krun Ironhaft's mine-hold). Four overlapping decisions drive the session:

1. **Does Grom forgive, kill, or Reorx-exile Thane Krun?** Krun ordered Duren's murder and is Duren's half-brother by their shared mother.
2. **Does a PC attune the Wrath-Coin?** 1-round fast-attune with a burn-warn window.
3. **Is the coin destroyed, attuned, Reciprocally-Forgiveness-emptied, or surrendered?**
4. **Is Krun's household attacked or negotiated with?**

**Two hint deliveries at Scene 3's Reorx-rite:**
- **Hint 2 — Vethrenn's warded margin UNLOCKS.** *"We made the rose to hold. We did not try to release. I think release is possible. I do not know how."*
- **Hint 3 — Reorx's Judgment PLANTS.** *"Grief-coiled, not forged-with-intent. A coil can be uncoiled."* Delivered to Grom via craft-witness reception.

## Hook

Two letters arrive at Varduin three days apart:

1. **Sigga Coalbeard** — Duren's widow — invites Grom to Duren's three-year rite at Gavrek's Anvil. Says she has *"something I need you to see."*
2. **Brother Laen** identifies **Thane Krun Ironhaft** (Thorbardin Palanthan elder) as the unclaimed buyer of an Aelwen moon-silver commission — discovered via Mira's sales-ledger transcription from #0003.

The two letters arriving the same week is not coincidence Laen is willing to sleep on. The party rides southwest.

## Setting & Background

**Gavrek's Anvil** is a minor Thorbardin outer-hold shrine — Reorx-priesthood; a forge-hall; a nave; a small graveyard on the east slope. Duren Coalbeard was its sixth forge-master. He was Grom's ordination-master. He died PC -23 (three years ago) in the Lower Ore-Shaft, officially by mine-collapse.

**Kruntharrak** is a functioning Thorbardin mine-hold three hours east of Gavrek's Anvil. Owned by **Thane Krun Ironhaft**, 71, Palanthan elder. Krun's father was murdered when Krun was 14; Krun has held the grievance for fifty years and has spent his wealth acquiring the tool he believes will identify the killer.

**Aelwen forged the Wrath-Coin in PC 179** — her final year — on Krun's commission, as a tool to identify his father's killer. She hanged herself before delivering. Heva Aelwensdaughter buried it. The coin passed through Vaenshold silversmiths refused; sold by Seralen (Mira's mother) in financial need; passed through Kharolis markets; **intercepted by Duren in PC -23 to destroy it**; **Krun ordered Duren killed** (via Jarek Stoneforge, sabotage-by-mining-charge disguised as collapse) to recover the coin. Krun has held it three years; his self-imposed cool-off ends this week.

**Grom was sent for water** 15 minutes before the charge fired. He stopped to greet a hearth at the outer shrine on the way. The hearth-greeting made him late. *The hearth-greeting saved his life.* Duren knew the charge was likely already set; sending Grom for water was Duren's last teaching.

**Krun and Duren shared a mother — Hilda Stoneforge.** Krun's father died PC -35; Hilda remarried hill-dwarf Coalbeard PC -32; Duren born PC -30. They were twenty-two years of half-brotherhood before Hilda died PC -22. Thorbardin law does not excuse kin-slaying — but the half-kinship is **not public**. Sigga knows. Grom does not yet.

### Scene 1 — Kharolis Road Ambush (conditional)

**Location:** Kharolis road, switchback halfway to Gavrek's Anvil. **Time:** late morning Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The road climbs toward Gavrek's Anvil through winter-bare pines. Low sun; long shadows; the silence of a road that has not carried caravans in weeks. Halfway up a switchback, a wagon sits sideways across the track — axle broken, cargo tarped, no driver visible. The nearest cover is the pines themselves, twenty yards off.

**Features:** Staged wagon (broken axle clean chisel-cut Investigation DC 12); tarped rocks as fake cargo; pines hiding **Jarek Stoneforge + 3 hired hill-dwarf mercs**.

**Trigger:** Passing the wagon without diverting (**passive Perc 12** catches Jarek's plait behind the axle).

**Encounter (Partial-Conditional):**

> **Jarek Stoneforge — CR ½, 100 XP.** AC 15, HP 30, Speed 25 ft. Str 15, Dex 12, Con 16. Skills: Athletics +4. Darkvision 60 ft. Dwarf poison-resistance.
> **Multiattack:** warhammer + miner's pick (bonus).
> **Warhammer:** +4, 1d8+2 bludgeoning. **Miner's pick:** +4, 1d6+2 piercing; crit embeds (Str DC 13 to free).
> **Mining charge (1/rest, thrown 30 ft):** 10-ft Dex DC 13; 2d6 thunder + prone on fail; dwarves immune to surprise.
> **Blood-call intercept (1/short rest):** not usable Scene 1 (Krun absent).

> **Hired hill-dwarf merc × 3 — CR ⅛, 25 XP each.** AC 13, HP 11. **Warhammer:** +3, 1d8+1. Darkvision 60 ft.

**Encounter XP:** 175 base × 2 (4 creatures) = **350 XP. Medium for 4×L3.**

**Tactics:**
- **Round 1:** Jarek declares *"We mean no death. Turn back."* Mercs move from cover; don't attack first unless PCs do.
- **Rounds 2-3:** Jarek uses mining charge if party clusters ≥3 in 10 ft. Mercs melee. Jarek melee warhammer + pick.
- **Jarek withdraws at HP ≤ 15.** Stealth +5 cover-rich terrain.

**Intel (if Jarek captured / mercs interrogated):**

Merc kits × 3 (hammer + leather + shield + 3 gp each). **Wagon hidden compartment (Investigation DC 15):** 40 gp Thorbardin coin + **"J. from K." letter** ("Delay them. Not bodies. **Connections:** South → Varduin. North → [2] Gavrek's Anvil (~6 hrs).

### Scene 2 — Gavrek's Anvil, Arrival

**Location:** Shrine courtyard + nave + forge-hall. **Time:** late afternoon Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The shrine sits in a hollow below a low cliff, roofed in slate that the forge's smoke has blackened into one long char. Stone walls. A single iron-hinged gate. A well at the courtyard's center, water-bright. The forge's hearth is live — you can hear it breathing. A grey-braided dwarf woman in a leather apron stands at the shrine-door, watching the party ride in. She does not wave. She does not smile. She waits until Grom dismounts. Then she says, in Dwarvish: *"Forge-priest. You came."*

**Features:**
- **Courtyard well** (Gavrek's founding well; cold aquifer water; pilgrim-drink).
- **Forge-hall** (hearth live; **Vell** — young dwarf, 22, newly ordained — tending; Grom passive-notice catches the forge's weakened inside-curve maintenance).
- **Shrine-nave** (small stone chapel, 12 pews, anvil on raised stone at altar — Gavrek's original, never struck).
- **Sigga's private quarters** (not entered Scene 2).
- **Two pilgrim dwarves** sharing a flask (background; leave by dusk).

**Sigga's greeting:** offers bread + cold well-water. *"The three-year rite is at dawn tomorrow. You are early. I am glad. Rest tonight."* **Does not yet mention hammer or murder — measuring.**

**Grom passive Perc 13** at the forge catches: **the forge has been tended by someone who does not forge often.** Vell's inside-curve maintenance is incomplete. Duren's craft-pulse is missing. **Craft-witness reception: silent.**

**Krun's visit (mid-afternoon):** Thane Krun arrives on horseback, flanked by Jarek (if alive). Does not dismount.

> **[DM note — kinship-seed]:** Krun is about to name Grom *"cousin"* without explanation. The kinship is not public. **The reveal comes at Scene 4** (Sigga discloses the Hilda Stoneforge half-brotherhood). **Do not telegraph surprise.** Read the line flat. Party's confusion is intended reception; Sigga will resolve it Scene 4. Insight DC 12 on the "cousin" word: *"Not normal Thorbardin etiquette"* — no more.

*"Cousin. You have come for the rite. Go with Reorx. But I would speak with you before you return south — at my hold, at dusk tomorrow. There is business between our lines that has waited three years. Come alone if you come."*

**"Cousin"** — **KINSHIP SEED.** Krun names publicly without explaining. Grom's confusion. Sigga watches — she will explain Scene 4.

**Investigation beats:**
- **Insight DC 12 Krun's "cousin":** *"Not normal Thorbardin etiquette."*

- **History DC 13 (Kessa) on Gavrek's Anvil lineage:** seven forge-masters since Gavrek; Duren sixth; seventh unnamed.
- **Religion DC 10 (Grom) on forge's inside-curve:** Duren's maker-mark on two hanging-tools. **Grom's Second-Chance warhammer is re-forged from a broken hammer of Duren's** — same maker-mark. Recognition.

### Scene 3 — Duren's Grave / Reorx-rite (DAWN Day 2)

**Location:** East slope grave. **Time:** pre-dawn Day 2.

**Read-aloud:**

> The east slope is still grey when the party and Sigga walk out from the shrine. The grave is unmarked stone, as Reorx-priest tradition demands — a forge-master's tomb is a forge his family can kindle if they need light. The stone is large enough to stand on. A coiled forge-chain lies across it. The chain is the shrine's second-holiest object: Gavrek's original; each link hand-forged in PC -135. Behind you the shrine's hearth begins its morning fire. Ahead of you the grey turns toward pink.

**Features:**
- **The grave-stone.** 4×4 ft basalt. Unmarked per tradition. **Investigation DC 14 (Stonecunning +2 → Grom effective 11):** shifted slightly in the last three years. Someone has been to the grave. Not natural.
- **The forge-chain.** Coiled on the stone. Ritual tool. Will be wrapped around the stone and struck 7× with a silver-headed mallet.
- **Duren's hammer is NOT in the grave.** Grom doesn't yet know. Sigga knows.

**The Rite (Religion DC 15; Grom +3; advantage if prior hearth-greetings):**

1. Grom speaks in Dwarvish: *"Reorx, father-smith, your son Duren Coalbeard comes to you cold. Three years in the forge of the ground. Three years of our memory's fire. We come to re-light what we cannot finish. Accept what we offer. Accept what we are."*
2. Seven strikes on the coiled chain; each ring held three breaths before the next.
3. Grom kneels. Thumb on grave-stone's inside-curve. *"Thou'rt seen. Forge-master."*
4. Rite complete.

**Post-rite deliveries (simultaneous):**

#### ⭐ Hint 2 — Vethrenn's warded margin UNLOCKS

As the 7th strike rings, **Kessa feels Vethrenn's commonplace book warm in her pack.** The overwritten margin-note surfaces:

> *"We made the rose to hold. We did not try to release. I think release is possible. I do not know how. — V., the night I went."*

Kessa reads aloud — the words come before she plans. She writes into her active transcription-log. *"She knew. She knew there might be a release. She did not find it."*

#### ⭐ Hint 3 — Reorx's Judgment PLANTS

At the 7th strike, **Grom receives insight (non-verbal craft-register)** via craft-witness reception:

> *"The forger came to me in PC 179. I accepted her soul. I did not accept her forgings. They were grief-coiled, not forged-with-intent. A tool made with grief is not a tool; it is a holding. A holding can be un-held. A coil can be uncoiled."*

Grom knows this the way he knows inside-curve is honest. He cannot quote it; he can act on it. **Religion DC 10 self-check to articulate aloud.** On **nat-20**: he speaks it to the party — *"Aelwen's forgings — they are coils, not tools. Reorx did not accept them. They can be uncoiled."* If so, **Kessa immediately cross-references with Vethrenn's margin — "release is possible" — and the two hints lock together in-session.** Massive synthesis moment.

> **[DM note — silent reception is reception]:** Grom understands this the way he knows stone or fire — without words, with absolute certainty. The knowing lands in his hands. **This is not "nothing happened."** Craft-witness reception is the ratified player-style; silent reception counts per rubric v1.2. If the player asks *"what did I just get,"* answer: *"Something changed. You cannot name it yet. It will act through you."* Do not paraphrase the insight in-character unless Grom rolls the self-check or articulates the understanding in Scene 5, 6, or 7.

**The missing hammer:** after the rite, Sigga says quietly (to Grom, alone if possible):

*"Forge-priest. Here is the thing. His hammer is not in the grave. I hid it the night before he died. He asked me to. I have kept it three years. It is in the shrine's east cellar. Come tonight — I will give it to you."*

**Insight DC 13:** *"She has told you this because she has decided it. The decision took three years. She will not retreat."*

**Investigation beats:**
- Grave-stone shifted (DC 14 / Stonecunning 11): someone visited.
- Religion DC 12 forge-chain: **7th link newly-forged within the last 3 years** (replaced; original at Krun's hall).

**Encounter:** None. **Jarek Stoneforge watches from 200 ft** (passive 13 spots him). He does not engage.

**Treasure: Duren's hammer (delivered Scene 2 night, post-rite):** Masterwork warhammer; Duren's maker-mark; non-magical; 30 gp market; **priceless to Grom**. Grom may dual-wield (+0 off-hand TWF, 1d8 bludgeoning) or keep as relic.

**Connections:** Up-slope → [2] shrine. Down-slope → [5] shaft. Cellar → Scene 2 night (shrine east door).

**GM Notes:**
- **Session's moral foundation.** Play quiet, with weight.
- **Two ratified player-styles fire simultaneously:** sheet-deep-reader (Kessa's book-paragraph activates) + craft-witness (Grom's rite delivers Reorx's Judgment). First cross-receiver moment in campaign.

- **Jarek watching:** if engaged, synthesis miscarries.

**Memory Fragment:**

Grenn died three months later. Duren carried the sentence forty-one years. He said it to Grom once. Grom carries it now.*

### Scene 5 — The Lower Ore-Shaft

**Location:** 120 ft below Gavrek's Anvil's east slope. **Time:** Day 2 mid-morning OR Day 1 night.

**Read-aloud:**

> The shaft-head is a square stone hole in the slope's base, timber-framed, cold air breathing out. A wooden door bolted shut; the bolt is new. Jarek's monthly maintenance. A sign hanging: "CLOSED — unsafe — by order of Thane Krun." Crude lettering. The bolt is the third one installed in three years. Dwarf-work replaces dwarf-work fast.

### Attunement Rules (inline — SRD + Wrath-Coin-specific)

- Standard: short rest (1 hr) to attune; 3 items max; curse revealed on attunement.
- **Wrath-Coin: fast-attune (1 round; burn-warn window; drop to reject).**
- **Curse is re-keying of anger** — not permanent self-image replacement like Mirror.
- Ending: Reorx-rite OR reciprocal forgiveness (6-named).

### Conditions Used (inline — SRD)

- **Prone:** attacker has advantage melee; target disadvantage own attacks; half-move to stand.
- **Surprised:** can't move/act first turn; no reaction that turn.
- **Restrained:** speed 0; attacks against have advantage; target's attacks disadvantage.
- **Exhaustion (used by Wrath-Coin only):** 1 = disadvantage ability checks; 2 = half speed; 3 = disadvantage attacks + saves; 4 = HP max halved; 5 = speed 0; 6 = death.
