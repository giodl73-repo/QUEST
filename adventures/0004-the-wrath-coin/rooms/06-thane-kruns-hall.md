# Scene 06 — Thane Krun's Hall (Dusk)

## Read-aloud

> Kruntharrak at dusk: the gate-wardens recognize Grom by the Coalbeard profile and let the party through without a word. The outer hall is warm, long-shadowed, stone-floored. At the hall's end, a curtained doorway. Inside: a round chamber lit by a single lantern. Thane Krun Ironhaft sits at a low stone table. On the table, a black velvet cloth. On the cloth, face-up with the rage-glyph showing: a coin of polished moon-silver, the size of a palm. Krun has not yet touched it. He is looking at it. He looks up when you enter.

## Features

- **Krun's hall.** Round; 20 ft diameter; stone floor; low stone ceiling with carved flame-patterns. A single lantern. The hall is *ready for a ritual* — Krun has cleared it.
- **The stone table.** Central. 4 ft diameter. On it: the black velvet cloth; the Wrath-Coin; a small silver-bladed knife (for the blood-commitment Krun intended to make during attunement); a prepared waxed parchment with a single name on it (Krun's father's killer's suspected identity).
- **Jarek Stoneforge** (if alive + not surrendered): stands at the curtained doorway as the hall's guard. CR ½ stats. Has Blood-call reaction available.
- **Krun** (at the table): CR 1 stats. Does not rise when the party enters. *"Cousin. You have come sooner than tomorrow."*

## The confrontation

Krun controls the opening of the scene. The party arrives and is seen. Krun speaks first; plays politics. The confrontation has branches based on what the party knows:

### Branch A — Party has NO kinship-reveal (Sigga not consulted)

Krun operates from "Voice of the Thane" strength. He cites Thorbardin law; Grom is a priest without hold-standing; Aelric is a Solamnic knight with no jurisdiction here. Krun will offer: *"I will pay you 500 gp each to ride away tomorrow. I will pay Sigga's widow-pension for ten years. I will give Gavrek's Anvil a new forge-beam. The coin is a family matter."* Persuasion DC 16 from the party may shift him; otherwise combat begins when the party rejects (which they will, if they have the murder evidence). Combat XP: Krun (200) + Jarek (100; if present) + 2 hall-guards (50 each) = 400 base × 2 (4 creatures) = **800 XP. Hard for 4×L3.**

### Branch B — Party HAS kinship-reveal (Sigga's Scene 4 confirmed)

Grom (or Aelric) names the half-kinship aloud: *"Duren was your half-brother. You ordered the death of your mother's son."* Krun's political armor cracks; his "Voice of the Thane" still fires but at disadvantage because his own household guard hesitates. **If Aelric invokes Solamnic kin-witness protocol** (a specific Order-of-the-Crown ceremonial clause): Krun must answer as kin, not as Thane. His Persuasion advantage is removed. Jarek — if present — will hesitate (Insight DC 12 catches his flinch); he is the witness.

### Branch C — Party has Jarek's confession AND kinship-reveal

Krun is effectively cornered. He will attempt the attunement *desperately* — reach for the coin. This triggers an **attunement race** (see below). If attunement is prevented, Krun surrenders to diplomatic process. If attunement completes, Krun's Wis-save-cycle begins; his first grievance-targeted action will be to name *Jarek* as the killer (of Duren, which is factually wrong — Jarek executed Krun's order) and attempt to kill Jarek with the silver knife. Grom will have to intervene.

## The Attunement Race (if Krun reaches for the coin)

**Initiative.** If the party has declared intent to stop Krun, they roll initiative against Krun (+0 Init). Krun is 5 ft from the coin. The coin takes 1 round to attune (Wrath-Coin's fast-attune).

### Attunement-Race flowchart (DM reference)

```
ROUND 1 — Krun's turn (Init +0)
  ├─ Free object-interact: grab coin (if 5 ft reach; auto-succeeds)
  ├─ Coin in hand → ATTUNEMENT BEGINS (burn-warm 1 round)
  └─ End of turn: nothing yet happens; coin is warm

ROUND 1 — PC turns (intervene before Krun's NEXT turn)
  ├─ [A] Aelric Shove: Athletics contest (+5 vs Krun +0) → coin flies to floor
  ├─ [B] Thera Disarm: Cunning Action + Thieves' Tools DC 15 (Expertise +7) → pluck from hand
  ├─ [C] Kessa Magic Missile at Krun's wrist: 3d4+3 auto-hit → Krun Con save DC 10 or drops (DM-adjudicated Sleight of Hand; not RAW)
  ├─ [D] Grom Guiding Bolt at the coin (AC 15; moon-silver resists magic → half damage): coin HP 10; 2-3 hits destroy it. Krun drops damaged coin.
  └─ [E] Thera hand crossbow at Krun's hand (attack at disadvantage for called shot): hit → Krun Con DC 10 or drops

ROUND 2 — Krun's turn
  ├─ If Krun STILL holds coin at start of turn: ATTUNEMENT COMPLETES end-of-turn
  │    └─ Wis DC 12 re-keying save (mandatory)
  │         ├─ Fail (likely): re-keys to Sigga's rage → Krun stabs self (1d4+2)
  │         └─ Succeed: Krun numbly attuned; father-killer dead; no target
  └─ If coin has been dropped/stolen: Krun attempts to recover (Athletics / Acrobatics / initiative re-positioning)
```

**Key timing rule:** attunement begins Round 1 (Krun's grab) and completes **end of Krun's Round 2 turn** — giving the party one full round of intervention. If the coin leaves Krun's hand before Round 2 ends, attunement does NOT complete.

**Intervention summary:**
- **[A] Shove** (Athletics +5 vs +0): coin on floor; Krun spends next turn retrieving.
- **[B] Disarm** (Thieves' Tools DC 15; Expertise +7): Thera holds the coin. Does NOT attune her (she has not turned a face toward skin).
- **[C] Magic Missile + drop** (force damage + Sleight DC 10): *DM discretion on the drop-adjudication; Kessa must narrate the wrist-target.*
- **[D] Guiding Bolt at coin** (cleanest path; coin AC 15, HP 10, half-damage from magic → ~5 damage/hit): destroys coin in 2-3 hits.
- **[E] Ranged interdict** (called shot disadvantage; on hit Con DC 10).

**One Option C per scene** (per `session-runner` 2.3). Pick the decision that best reflects the scene's pivot. Grom's Krun-fate remains the default; others are alternatives if Grom's decision is Option-A-tractable (e.g., Grom clearly acts per heuristic without deep sheet-consultation).

## If Krun attunes the coin

**Krun's first Wis save (DC 12) against the coin's re-keying:** roll. Any result triggers *"your anger is not your own"* mechanically. If fail: Krun's nominal target (his father's killer, a dead man) cannot be reached; the coin re-keys to the strongest *current* grievance — which may be **Sigga's un-spoken rage at Krun himself**. Krun's first action would then be to stab *himself*. (Mechanical-mood adjudication: Krun stabs own hand with the silver knife, drops the knife, howls as the coin's first inheritance lands.)

This is *horrific* in a way that serves the narrative. The coin turns against Krun immediately because his own household-adjacent rage (Sigga, Jarek-the-unsleeping-killer, Duren's distant ghost-rage if counted) are directed at *him*. **Krun's fifty-year-planned attunement fails in its first round.**

## If Krun is prevented from attuning

The coin sits on the cloth, un-held. **Decisions now:**

1. **Destroy the coin (Reorx-rite).** Grom carries it to Kruntharrak's forge OR back to Gavrek's Anvil. Religion DC 15. **P0 destruction-path readability (from #0003 panel):** pre-write this narration. *"The obsidian back shatters along Aelwen's convergent polish — the fully-polished face refuses the fire's asymmetry; the moon-silver un-forges; a small bright vapor rises; the coin's weight drops to exactly one ounce before it dissipates."*
2. **Attempt Reciprocal Forgiveness (Aelwen's intent).** The party identifies the six primary grievances; names them; offers amends. **This requires knowing all six.** Scene 4's Sigga-disclosure + Scene 5's shaft-evidence + Scene 6's Krun-confrontation can uncover 4-5 of the 6 (Krun's father's killer; Sigga; Jarek; Duren-post-mortem-rage-at-Krun). The 5th (Heva's grief-rage) is obscure — Kessa History DC 15 may surface it. The 6th (Aelwen's own un-spoken rage) is only accessible if Kessa has cross-referenced the apron-scrap with the journal page — which she will have, per Scene 4. **This is the hard path but the Aelwen-intent ending.**
3. **Take the coin unattuned.** Carry it; decide at #0005 or later. Risky.
4. **Surrender the coin to Thorbardin authority.** Brother Laen can arrange; the Council of Thanes would hold it. Politically clean.
5. **Leave it on the table.** Walk away. Krun is arrested; the coin is impounded with Kruntharrak. Thorbardin will destroy it at their own forge. This is possible but requires Krun's arrest — see Krun's Fate below.

## Krun's Fate

**If attunement happened and Krun stabbed himself:** he is unconscious / wounded. Party chooses: stabilize (hand him to Thorbardin Council) or let him die. Grom's decision — this is the **Option C moment** for this adventure. Spawn Grom as subagent.

**If attunement was prevented + Krun is captured:** Grom's decision is between (a) kill him (revenge for Duren), (b) surrender to Thorbardin law (diplomatic-legal route), (c) offer a Reorx-priest judgment (Grom as Reorx's instrument: allow Krun to live with a ritual-bound-confession and exile from Thorbardin — the Reorx-priest has standing to adjudicate kin-crimes in outer holds). **Option (c) is the golden-ending path** — unlocked by Hint 3 (Reorx's Judgment), which Grom received in Scene 3. The hint tells Grom that grief-made-tools can be un-held; the same principle applies to Krun's grief-made-life. He can be un-held from Thorbardin by formal exile.

## GM Notes

- **This is the session's climax. Play it slow.** Krun's opening speech should take at least two minutes of real-time. The party's kinship-reveal (if they have it) is the pivot. The attunement-race, if it happens, is tight.
- **Krun must not feel un-sympathetic.** He is a 71-year-old man who has held a grievance for fifty years. His fault is specific — the fratricide — but his *grief* is legitimate. Grom's judgment must account for both.
- **The Reorx-exile option is Grom's unique path.** It requires Hint 3 (received Scene 3). If Grom has articulated Hint 3 aloud to the party, Kessa can coach him on the ritual-form. Otherwise Grom knows it but cannot explain it; he acts.
- **Jarek's role:** if alive, he is Grom's mirror — the instrument of Duren's death. Grom may offer him the same Reorx-exile mercy. Or not. Also Option-C-adjacent; DM may choose to fold Jarek's fate into Grom's subagent prompt.
