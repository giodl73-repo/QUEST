# Scene 05 — The Lower Ore-Shaft (Mid-Morning OR Night before Scene 6)

## Read-aloud

> The shaft-head is a square stone hole in the slope's base, timber-framed, cold air breathing out. A wooden door bolted shut; the bolt is new. Jarek's monthly maintenance. A sign hanging: "CLOSED — unsafe — by order of Thane Krun." Crude lettering. The bolt is the third one installed in three years. Dwarf-work replaces dwarf-work fast.

## Features

- **The shaft-head door.** Bolted. Investigation DC 10 shows the bolt is new (installed within 6 months). Picked easily with Thera's tools (Thieves' Tools DC 10 — auto for her with +7).
- **Vertical descent: 120 ft.** Stone spiral-stair; no lift. Standard hill-dwarf shaft — one person abreast. No light source; darkvision or torches required.
- **Shaft-bottom: the Lower Ore-Shaft itself.** 40x40 ft worked chamber. Ore-veins (now dormant). Collapse-rubble on the west wall — the "natural collapse" that killed Duren. Timber support-beams on the east wall, heavy iron-cleated, which were unaffected by the alleged collapse.
- **The support-beam shaft-west wall collapsed from.** Investigation DC 13 (Stonecunning gives +2 → effective 11 for Grom): this beam did not break under weight. It was **cut deliberately** with a miner's pick along a specific fracture-line. Normal collapse tears beams; this beam was scored.
- **The mining-charge residue.** Investigation DC 15 OR Grom Stonecunning DC 12: trace of thunder-stone powder on the fracture line. Used as a *slow-acting charge* — the beam was set to fail under its own weight hours after Duren descended, giving Jarek time to be elsewhere and the explosion-signature time to dissipate.
- **Duren's tools.** At the shaft's far wall, a small pile of Duren's own tools, buried under lesser rubble. Investigation DC 12 (or Grom passive touch) finds: his **chisel with his maker-mark**, a pair of tongs, a small sack with **3 ounces of moon-silver ingot** (the raw material Duren was going to use to overlay the Wrath-Coin in an un-making polish). **The moon-silver matches the Wrath-Coin's vein** (Kessa Arcana DC 10 confirms). Duren was *in-process* of starting the destruction when the shaft fell.
- **An oil-cloth bundle.** Investigation DC 13: contains Duren's hand-written destruction-plan for the coin. Step-by-step. Reorx-rite with specific words. Forge temperature. The destruction was *meant to succeed*; Duren had it documented. **This is the third Aelwen/Vaenshold-related document the party has recovered this session** (after Krun's letters + the apron-scrap).

## Encounter

**Conditional.** If Jarek Stoneforge survived Scene 1 AND has NOT surrendered yet AND the party descends during daylight when Jarek might be present: **Jarek intercepts the party at shaft-bottom** to prevent evidence discovery.

Jarek's forces here: Jarek alone (he does not bring mercs to the shaft — too conspicuous).

**Tactics:** Jarek lets the party descend (he is already there, hiding in a side-shaft). Ambushes at shaft-bottom. Uses a mining-charge (1/rest remaining if he used one Scene 1; otherwise 2) to collapse the entry — sealing the party in temporarily. Then combat.

**Encounter XP:** 100 (Jarek solo, CR ½). Easy for 4×L3, but sealing the party in adds tension.

**🎲 Jarek roll** at ambush: Stealth +5 → party Perception vs DC check.

**If the shaft is sealed:** party must use 3d6 rounds of digging (Athletics DC 15 × 3 successes) OR blast out with Kessa's Magic Missile+Shield+improvisation. Two-hour delay if they dig; costly time-wise.

**If the party descends at night:** Jarek is asleep at Kruntharrak. Shaft is unattended. Full evidence recovery uncontested.

### Jarek's surrender option

At HP ≤ 15 OR if directly asked: *"Did you sabotage this shaft?"* with **Persuasion DC 16** (or Intimidation DC 14 if party has the commission-letters), Jarek surrenders and **confesses the sabotage and the murder-order**. This is the Thorbardin-admissible witness. His surrender here makes Scene 6 a diplomatic arrest rather than a combat confrontation.

## Investigation — the full murder-evidence bundle

Combined with **Krun's commission-letter-bundle** from Scene 4 and **Jarek's confession** (if obtained): Thorbardin tribunal would convict Krun. Grom has the option to pursue legal justice.

## GM Notes

- **This is the scene that makes the murder undeniable.** A party that skips this scene (if Jarek surrendered earlier) can still proceed to Scene 6, but without the physical evidence they must rely on Jarek's testimony alone. Thorbardin courts prefer physical evidence.
- **Grom's reception:** he will be quieter after this scene than after any previous. He has just read his own past — the "mine-collapse" he escaped by chance was *not* a mine-collapse. He was meant to die too. Jarek's charge-timing would have hit when Grom returned with water; Grom was late because he stopped to greet a hearth at the outer shrine. **The hearth-greeting saved Grom's life.** Let him realize this. Craft-witness in its most painful form.
- **Rubric v1.3 positional-redundancy:** the murder evidence is intentionally placed in *three* forms (beam, residue, destruction-plan) across this single scene, plus Jarek's possible confession. Anchor-level symptom has ≥ 2 of 3 fallbacks. Single-roll fragility avoided.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The mining-charge Jarek set, PC -23: Thorbardin outer-hold standard, 2-lb thunder-stone compound in a baked-clay shell. Delayed fuse. Jarek's own recipe-modification let the fuse degrade slowly, producing the look of natural collapse. He set the charge that morning; Duren descended after lunch; the charge fired at 3 p.m.; Duren's body was never recovered in full because the shaft-wall fell on the shaft-floor. Grom was sent for water at 2:15. He did not know why, three years later, he remembered the exact minute.*
