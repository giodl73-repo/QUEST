# Scene 04 — The Forge Records (post-rite, shrine's east cellar)

## Read-aloud

> The shrine's east cellar is low-ceilinged, lamp-lit, stone-walled. Three oak chests, iron-banded. Sigga's key turns in the oldest. She lifts the lid and steps back. Inside: bundled scrolls — three centuries of forge-master's records. Commissions, receipts, apprentice certificates, dispute-judgments. The top bundle is Duren's — the last twenty years of his life. She has not opened it since he died.

## Features

- **The three oak chests.**
  - Chest 1: Duren's twenty years of records (and Krun's commission-letters).
  - Chest 2: Duren's master Grenn's records (PC -20 to PC -10).
  - Chest 3: earlier forge-masters (historical; not plot-relevant).
- **The hidden stone.** Under the floor-flag by the east wall: **Duren's hidden hammer**, wrapped in oiled cloth, hidden here by Sigga the night before Duren died. Sigga will point to it after the party has looked at the chests.
- **The shrine-ledger** (on a pedestal; not in a chest): the shrine's running ledger, maintained since Gavrek's time. Contains records of every ordination, marriage, and funeral at Gavrek's Anvil.

## Sigga's explanations

Once the party is reading, Sigga speaks:

*"Krun and Duren shared a mother. Their mother was Hilda Stoneforge — a miner's daughter from the eastern holds. She married Krun's father first and bore Krun in PC -49. She was widowed in PC -35 when Krun's father was killed. She remarried a hill-dwarf forger named Coalbeard in PC -32. She bore Duren in PC -30. Krun was seventeen. Duren was newborn. Hilda died of fever in PC -22. Krun and Duren shared twenty-two years of half-brotherhood before their mother died, though Krun was already in Thorbardin Palanthan politics by then. They did not grow up together. But they were half-brothers in Thorbardin law, and Thorbardin law is not casual about kinship."*

She pauses. Then:

*"Krun did not kill Duren over coin. Krun killed Duren over the coin."*

She nods to the chest.

## What the party finds (Investigation DC 13 to dig through)

1. **Krun's commission-letter to Duren, PC -29** (Duren was 1 year old; Krun was 18): *"Brother. If you are ever a forger I will commission from you. That is what I ask. That is what I offer. — K."* Boyhood oath; unfulfilled by Krun until later.

2. **Krun's commission-letter to Duren, PC -3** (Duren was 20, apprenticed to Grenn; Krun was 46, a Thane): *"Brother. I have a commission that is not public. The matter is inherited. I will pay 400 gp for a single object that holds the rage of those who have been wronged by a named person. The commission is for me to name my father's killer. The forging is subtle; Aelwen of Vaenshold refused. I come to you because you are kin and trusted. Reply. — K."*

3. **Duren's reply to Krun, PC -3:** *"Brother. I will not forge this. Aelwen's refusal is the correct refusal. You will be a worse man if you forge this, and I will be a worse forger. I am sorry. — D."*

4. **Krun's reply to Duren, PC -2:** *"Cousin,"* — (not "brother," a public-formal usage deliberate demotion) — *"I do not require your hand in the forging. I require only that you not intercept the coin should it come to the outer markets. I ask this as a courtesy. — K."*

5. **Duren's reply to Krun, PC -2:** *"I will intercept it if I can. I told you once. I tell you again. — D."*

6. **Duren's final letter-draft, PC -23** (never sent; folded inside the same bundle): *"Brother. I have it. It was in a Kharolis merchant's cart, sold for 40 gp by a fool who did not know. I will break it at my forge tonight. If you require formal-justice for my interception, petition Thorbardin and I will answer. — D."*

**This is the murder-motive documented.** Duren intercepted the coin; Duren told Krun; Krun ordered Duren's death before Duren could destroy the coin.

7. **Aelwen's second fragment** (the apron-scrap Duren obtained via a Kharolis merchant): pinned to the back of the commission-bundle. **Investigation DC 12** OR auto-delivery if any PC has Aelwen's journal page from #0003 (Kessa does). The fragment reads:

> *"The Coin I will forge with full polish. This will be my good work. It is the one I mean. Do not sell it. Empty it. Break it. Do not let them hold what is not theirs. — A., the night before."*

"The night before" — the night before Aelwen hanged. **The coin was meant to be emptied and destroyed, not attuned.** Aelwen's final written word on her own work. **Kessa will cross-reference with the #0003 journal page immediately** — same hand, same ink-type, same "night before" register. The two fragments are complementary: the journal page says "I should have forged an ingot instead"; the apron-scrap says "The Coin I will forge with full polish. This will be my good work." Aelwen considered the Wrath-Coin *redemption-work* — not coil-work, despite it being moon-silver and commission-driven. It was the one she finished cleanly.

## Investigation beats

- **Kessa History DC 12:** Hilda Stoneforge is named in the shrine-ledger as Duren's mother; the ledger confirms Sigga's account.
- **Kessa Investigation DC 13** on any chest: the PC 20 entry (three years ago) for "Duren's inventory" is incomplete. The missing entries match the Wrath-Coin's likely weight and moon-silver composition. Duren concealed its presence in the ledger.
- **Grom Insight DC 10 on Sigga:** she knew about the coin. She did not know about the half-brothers' dispute-letters. She has not read them until today.

## GM Notes

- **This is the scene where the half-kinship is revealed by name.** If the party attempts Scene 6 without this reveal, Scene 6 is politically-legible only (Krun's "Voice of the Thane" works at full strength). With this reveal, Scene 6 becomes morally-legible (Krun is a fratricide and his household guard will hesitate).
- **The document-reading can be paced by the DM.** Not all seven letters need to be narrated in full. Summarize Krun's rejection + Duren's refusal-intercept + Krun's threat + Duren's final draft. The apron-scrap is the centerpiece. Let Kessa cross-reference it with the journal page aloud.
- **Hint 4 reinforcement:** the apron-scrap + journal page together form a **diptych** — two Aelwen-written fragments, each recovered from a different adventure, each naming an Aelwen-intent that the finale's Silver-Ingot Confession will resolve.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Aelwen's apron-scrap, the night before: she was wearing the apron when she wrote on it — ink onto cloth does not erase. She wrote with the silver-ink pen that Heva would later inherit. The scrap has survived 180 years because Heva cut it from the apron the night of the pyre and kept it sewn into her own underclothes for the next seventy-eight years, and because Heva's daughter Rillina knew to preserve it, and because Rillina's apprentice's grandson sold it to Duren via three merchants in PC -5 because he needed money and did not know what he was selling. Each hand that carried it was a hand that did not read it. Duren read it. He is the first.*
