# Scene 02 — Gavrek's Anvil, Arrival

## Read-aloud

> The shrine sits in a hollow below a low cliff, roofed in slate that the forge's smoke has blackened into one long char. Stone walls. A single iron-hinged gate. A well at the courtyard's center, water-bright. The forge's hearth is live — you can hear it breathing. A grey-braided dwarf woman in a leather apron stands at the shrine-door, watching the party ride in. She does not wave. She does not smile. She waits until Grom dismounts. Then she says, in Dwarvish: *"Forge-priest. You came."*

## Features

- **The courtyard well.** Drinkable. Gavrek's Anvil's water runs cold from a deep aquifer; the well is named for Gavrek himself, the shrine's founder. Pilgrims typically drink before entering the shrine.
- **The forge-hall.** Door open; hearth breathing; **Vell** (young dwarf, 22, newly ordained) tending. Vell nods silently at Grom; he was ordained two winters ago and has heard Grom's name as "Duren's last apprentice." Vell stays in the forge-hall during the session; present for atmosphere, not action.
- **The shrine-nave.** Small stone chapel; 12 pews; an anvil on a raised stone at the altar (Gavrek's original anvil, never struck). Reorx's hammer-and-spark sigil in carved stone above the altar.
- **Sigga's private quarters.** Attached to the shrine; not entered this scene.
- **Two traveling pilgrim dwarves** in the courtyard sharing a flask. Non-combat background. Will leave by dusk.

## Sigga's greeting

Sigga (see `npcs/sigga-coalbeard.md`) leads the party inside. She offers bread and cold shrine-well water. She says, once they are seated:

*"The three-year rite is at dawn tomorrow. You are early. I am glad. Rest tonight. We will walk to the grave in the morning together."*

She does **not** yet mention the hammer or the murder.

**Grom Perception passive 13** at the shrine's forge catches: **the forge has been tended by someone who does not forge often.** The inside-curve on the hanging-tools has not been maintained. Sigga's husband would have kept it; Vell is learning, not mastering. The forge's *craft-pulse* is wrong — not dead, but weaker than Duren's work would produce. **Craft-witness reception: silent.** The forge tells Grom something is off before any NPC does.

## Krun's visit

**Mid-afternoon, before the rite:** Thane Krun Ironhaft arrives at the shrine-gate on horseback, flanked by Jarek Stoneforge (unless Jarek is dead/captured from Scene 1 — in which case Krun arrives alone with two other household-guards). He does not dismount.

Krun's words to Grom:

*"Cousin. You have come for the rite. Go with Reorx. But I would speak with you before you return south — at my hold, at dusk tomorrow. There is business between our lines that has waited three years. Come alone if you come."*

Then he wheels his horse and rides east. Does not wait for a response.

**"Cousin"** — **THIS IS THE KINSHIP SEED.** Krun names it publicly to make Grom feel claimed; he does not yet reveal WHY they are cousins. Grom's reaction: confusion. Sigga watches. Sigga will explain at Scene 4.

## Investigation beats

- **Insight DC 12 on Krun's visit:** *"He called you cousin. He did not explain. This is not normal Thorbardin etiquette."*
- **Insight DC 15 on Sigga:** *"She is measuring you. She has been measuring you since you dismounted. She has information she has not shared."*
- **History DC 13 (Kessa) on Gavrek's Anvil lineage:** the shrine has had seven forge-masters since Gavrek. Duren was the sixth. The seventh has not been named.
- **Religion DC 10 (Grom) on the forge's inside-curve:** Duren's hammer-mark is on two hanging-tools (Grom passive touch catches it). **Grom is carrying Second-Chance, re-forged from a broken hammer of Duren's** — this detail is on his PC sheet. The maker-mark match between Second-Chance and these hanging-tools is immediate recognition.

## GM Notes

- **Play the shrine as *quiet*, not reverent.** It is a working craft-place; it has not been a destination of pilgrimage for decades. The solemnity is private, not public.
- **Sigga does not give the hammer yet.** She is measuring. Grom must pass a test she will not name. The test is: does he greet the forge correctly.
- **Grom's hearth-greeting:** if Grom performs his *"thou'rt seen"* greeting at the forge-hall's hearth, Sigga witnesses and makes her decision. This is **craft-witness reception** (silent; Sigga receives Grom's signature move).
- **The Krun "cousin" claim is narrative-gravity, not mechanical.** Let it land with confusion; Sigga resolves it at Scene 4.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Sigga's wedding-day at Gavrek's Anvil, PC 32. She was thirty-two. Duren was thirty-seven. The shrine-priest who married them said, in his benediction: "You will have children of stone and iron; not of flesh. Your work will outlive your names." Duren heard this as his craft. Sigga heard it as her body's incapacity. They never discussed which of them the priest meant. The benediction proved true for both.*
