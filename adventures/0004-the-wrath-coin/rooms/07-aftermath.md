# Scene 07 — Aftermath at Gavrek's Anvil (Late Night / Dawn)

## Read-aloud

> The ride back from Kruntharrak is three hours in the dark. The shrine is lit — Sigga has been up all night. She is waiting at the gate. She does not ask. She does not need to. She looks at Grom and reads the answer in how he stands.

## Features

- **The shrine-nave, lit.** Sigga has prepared the altar: the forge-chain uncoiled; the silver mallet set; the second anvil (Duren's own) placed at the nave's center. A quiet re-interment is possible.
- **Vell** (the young priest) is awake. He has been reading Duren's archive-records by lamp-light. He looks up when the party returns.
- **The east slope** is pre-dawn grey. Duren's grave awaits.

## The scene

### Sigga's confession

If Krun is surrendered / exiled / dead, Sigga speaks — quiet, not dramatic:

*"Duren knew he might die for the coin. He told me the night before. He said: 'If I do not come back from the shaft, it will be because of the coin. Do not give it to Krun. Do not give it to the shrine. Keep it until someone comes who should have it.' I have kept the hammer three years. I have also kept this."*

She hands Grom **a second sealed letter** — Duren's, written the night before, addressed to *"Grom, if he survives,"* and kept by Sigga unopened. The letter reads:

> *"Grom.
> I am writing this because I may die tomorrow. Krun wants the coin; I will not give it to him. If I die and you come to the shrine for the rite, Sigga will give you this letter. Here is what you should know:
>
> The coin is Aelwen's fourth forging. She made it to be emptied and broken. She meant well by it. Krun means ill. I intercepted the coin to destroy it. If I fail, perhaps you will do what I could not.
>
> The inside-curve is the honest one. You learned this from me. I learned it from Grenn. Grenn learned it from Duren-the-elder of Deep-Thorbardin. It is older than all of us. Carry it.
>
> I am sorry you had to learn the mine-collapse was not one. I am sorry Sigga carried it three years. I am sorry about the hammer I hid from you. I did not want you dead beside me; I sent you for water because I suspected the charge was already set and you were too young to stop it. This was a gift. Receive it as a gift.
>
> May Reorx keep you.
> — Duren, PC -23, the night before."*

Grom reads the letter. The party watches. He does not speak. He folds it, places it inside his apron, then — for the first time in the session — he performs his **hearth-greeting** at the shrine's forge-hearth. *"Thou'rt seen, forge-master. I hear you."* Sigga's hand finds his shoulder.

### The Reorx-rite completion

Duren's hammer is placed in the grave. Grom re-performs the Reorx-rite — this time with the *complete* offering (the hammer that should have been there three years ago). The rite's second performance cleans the debt. Grom's burn-scar on his left hand *stops aching* — a detail on his PC sheet that has been present since he put the scar on himself at Duren's wake three years ago. It has ached at every dawn. It does not ache anymore.

**Campaign-permanent fact:** Grom's burn-scar has stopped aching. This is the three-year-debt's resolution.

### The party's choices — what to do with the coin

Four sub-branches, per Scene 6 outcome:

**A. Coin attuned (Krun or a PC).** If Krun: Krun is alive (stabilized) or dead. If he lives, he is attuned; exhaustion begins; Thorbardin will handle long-term. If a PC (unusual — only Grom at high likelihood): the PC carries the coin; campaign-permanent; Reciprocal Forgiveness path now active.

**B. Coin destroyed via Reorx-rite** (Scene 6 branch, or Scene 7 via Grom at Gavrek's forge). Religion DC 15; Grom +3; Bless from Aelric (L1 slot) = advantage. Likely success. Narration: see Scene 6 treasure-path. The coin is gone.

**C. Coin Reciprocal Forgiveness (partial or complete).** If the party has named grievances at Scene 6 or 7, the coin's weight has dropped per 1/6 per named release. This is long-form work; likely not complete in-session. The coin is *emptying*; the party carries it partially-lighter.

**D. Coin surrendered to Thorbardin.** Brother Laen's courier can arrange. Political-legal chain; Council of Thanes will handle. Coin disappears from the party's responsibility.

### Krun's disposition

- **Reorx-exile (Grom's Option-C option):** Krun lives, exiled from Thorbardin by Grom's Reorx-priest authority. The exile-ritual binds him; he cannot return. He must make his way in outer-hold society alone. This is the golden ending.
- **Thorbardin surrender:** Krun faces Thorbardin law. Execution by court likely.
- **Killed by Grom:** Grom resolved the debt with blood. Thorbardin investigates; Brother Laen handles.
- **Escape:** Krun flees to a deeper Thorbardin hold. Campaign antagonist for later adventures.

### Jarek's disposition

- **Surrendered (Scene 1 / 5 / 6):** testifies to Thorbardin tribunal alongside the party's evidence.
- **Killed:** body returned to his family.
- **Escaped:** minor future-adventure threat.

### Brother Laen's courier (dawn)

A Solamnic rider arrives at dawn from Varduin. Carries:

1. **A letter from Brother Laen:** *"Grom. If you have word by now, the Council will read it. I am sending this rider to fetch your news. If Krun lives, I can arrange Thorbardin-Council communication. If he does not, Varduin is waiting. — L."*
2. **250 gp** — payment for the protection commission (the second Mira-letter formalized as a quasi-commission).
3. **Brother Laen's sister Wena update:** *"Wena has been approached by a Pride-Circlet-seeker in Istar. The Council has arranged her placement in a southern Revered-Daughters convent. She is safe for now. She sent you a note — it is in the parcel."*

Wena's note — brief — reads: *"Thank you for keeping my brother. He keeps writing to me as if I were still eleven. Tell him I am twenty-eight. I know what a Pride-Circlet is. — W."*

This is a **seed for #0005** (Pride — Lady Tiran — Wena's placement in the Revered Daughters; Aelric's Solamnic-order connection).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Sigga, the night before Duren died, asked him: "Do you love me." He did not answer in words. He took her hand and pressed her palm against the inside-curve of the forge-iron they were shaping together. The curve was warm from the fire. He said: "This is the answer. The inside-curve is where you find out if you meant it. I meant it. I mean it." She did not answer; she did not need to. The inside-curve was warm for another half-hour. Then he slept. Then the next day, he descended. She has been carrying the warmth of that iron for three years.*
