# Scene 03 — Duren's Grave / The Reorx-rite (DAWN)

## Read-aloud

> The east slope is still grey when the party and Sigga walk out from the shrine. The grave is unmarked stone, as Reorx-priest tradition demands — a forge-master's tomb is a forge his family can kindle if they need light. The stone is large enough to stand on. A coiled forge-chain lies across it. The chain is the shrine's second-holiest object: Gavrek's original; each link hand-forged in PC -135. Behind you the shrine's hearth begins its morning fire. Ahead of you the grey turns toward pink.

## Features

- **The grave-stone.** A 4x4 ft basalt slab. Unmarked — as tradition. Dwarven Stonecunning Perception reveals it has been *slightly shifted* in the last three years (Investigation DC 14 catches) — someone has been to the grave between interments. Not natural erosion.
- **The forge-chain.** Coiled on the stone. Ritual tool. For the three-year rite, it is wrapped around the stone and struck with a silver-headed mallet seven times. Grom will perform this.
- **The hammer-that-should-be-buried.** Per Reorx-rite tradition, a forge-master's hammer is buried with them. Duren's hammer is NOT in the grave. Grom does not yet know this; Sigga knows.

## The Rite

Grom conducts. The party watches (Kessa with Vethrenn's book in her pack — she has been carrying it since S01). Sigga stands at 10 ft distance, hands folded.

**The rite's structure** (Grom's PC sheet specifies he has never performed this before; he knows the words from Duren's teaching):

1. Grom speaks the Invocation in Dwarvish: *"Reorx, father-smith, your son Duren Coalbeard comes to you cold. Three years in the forge of the ground. Three years of our memory's fire. We come to re-light what we cannot finish. Accept what we offer. Accept what we are."*

2. Grom strikes the coiled chain seven times with the silver mallet. Each strike rings; each ring is held three breaths before the next.

3. Grom kneels. Touches the inside-curve of the grave-stone with his thumb (the **hearth-greeting** pattern, adapted for stone). Says, in Dwarvish: *"Thou'rt seen. Forge-master."*

4. The rite is complete. Religion DC 15 to perform cleanly. **If Grom has been treated as craft-witness throughout — if he has performed hearth-greetings in prior scenes — grant advantage on this roll (craft-attention accumulates).** With or without advantage, Grom passes DC 15 on any +3 roll of ≥12.

**🎲 DC check:** Religion +3. With advantage from prior hearth-greetings, the rite lands cleanly with high probability.

## Post-rite deliveries

**Hint 2 — Vethrenn's warded margin UNLOCKS.**

As the seventh strike rings, Kessa feels **Vethrenn's commonplace book warm in her pack.** She opens it. The warded margin-note, overwritten in ink since her inheritance, *surfaces*:

> *"We made the rose to hold. We did not try to release. I think release is possible. I do not know how. — V., the night I went."*

Kessa reads the note aloud. She did not plan to; the words come. Vethrenn wrote this the night she went silent (PC 100); her pen must have left a ward that would break only at a Reorx-consecrated moment. Kessa does not yet know how it happened. She writes the note into her active transcription-log (verbatim, silver-ink). She says, to no one in particular: *"She knew. She knew there might be a release. She did not find it."*

**Hint 3 — Reorx's Judgment PLANTS.**

Grom, at the moment of the seventh strike, receives an **insight** — non-verbal, craft-register. The rite's cosmic weight delivers what a priest of Reorx might call *judgment*:

> *"The forger came to me in PC 179. I accepted her soul. I did not accept her forgings. They were grief-coiled, not forged-with-intent. A tool made with grief is not a tool; it is a holding. A holding can be un-held. A coil can be uncoiled."*

Grom does not hear this as a voice. He *knows* it, the way he knows the inside-curve is honest. He will act on it; he cannot yet quote it. (The party will learn what Grom knows by watching him act — Scenes 4, 5, 6 will show his instinct re-tuned.)

**Grom Religion DC 10 self-check** to articulate the insight to himself: passes on any roll. On a nat-20, Grom can articulate it aloud to the party as: *"Aelwen's forgings — they are coils, not tools. Reorx did not accept them. They can be uncoiled."* **If articulated aloud, Kessa will immediately cross-reference with Vethrenn's margin-note** — *"release is possible"* — and the two hints lock together in-session. This is a **massive synthesis moment**.

## The missing hammer

After the rite completes, Sigga steps forward. She says, quietly:

*"Forge-priest. Here is the thing. His hammer is not in the grave. I hid it the night before he died. He asked me to. I have kept it three years. It is in the shrine's east cellar. Come tonight — I will give it to you."*

Grom's response is character-specific. Play him per heuristic and decision-order. The most likely response: he says nothing. He nods once (Duren's benediction). He returns to the shrine.

**Insight DC 13 on Sigga** (after she speaks): *"She has told you this because she has decided it. The decision took three years. She will not retreat from it."*

## Investigation beats

- **Investigation DC 14 on the grave-stone:** shifted in the last three years. Someone has been here.
- **Religion DC 12 on the coiled forge-chain:** the chain's seventh link is *newly-forged* — replaced within the past three years. Gavrek's original chain had a flaw in that link; someone re-forged it. Who? (Possible lead: Krun's Scene 6 hall has the original seventh link in his display-case — stolen for superstition; this is a subtle DM-discretion detail.)
