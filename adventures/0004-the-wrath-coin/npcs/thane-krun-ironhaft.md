# Thane Krun Ironhaft, Palanthan Elder of Thorbardin

## Appearance

Seventy-one years old, hill dwarf (mountain dwarf mix in his mother's line, which matters in Thorbardin politics and which he never mentions). Short even for a dwarf — four-foot-four; thick-shouldered; beard iron-gray but unplaited in the elder style (a minor flouting of Palanthan custom). Wears a heavy moon-silver signet on his left thumb, visibly, at all hours. Carries himself like a man who learned to stand in meeting-rooms the way others learned to stand in forges. Limps slightly from an old mine-injury he does not discuss; the limp is worse in cold weather, which is most weather in Thorbardin.

## Background

Born Kruntharrak in PC -49 to a minor Palanthan line that owned a single declining mine in the Kharolis foothills. His father was murdered by a rival hold when Krun was fourteen — a grievance Krun has never set down. He rebuilt the mine, acquired two more by strategic marriage, acquired four more by strategic patience, and was named **Thane of Kruntharrak** in PC 5, making him one of the younger Thanes in Thorbardin's outer-hold hierarchy. He has held the seat fifteen years.

**Krun commissioned the Wrath-Coin from Aelwen in PC 179** — the year of Aelwen's hanging. Krun was thirty. The commission was his attempt to *resolve* his father's death by ritual: he intended to use the coin to feel his father's killer's rage, identify the killer definitively (his father's death had three suspected agents), and exact justice. Aelwen forged the coin; Aelwen hanged herself before delivering it; Heva Aelwensdaughter buried the coin in Aelwen's workshop among the other five artifacts; **Krun never received it**. He has been waiting fifty years.

**Duren Coalbeard** was Krun's **half-brother by their shared mother**. Krun never publicly acknowledged the kinship (his mother's earlier marriage was below his political line); Duren knew; Duren kept the silence out of respect for Krun's Thane-seat. When the Vaenshold silversmith line fragmented and Aelwen's workshop-goods began circulating in the outer markets, Duren — through his half-brother connection — intercepted the Wrath-Coin in **PC 22** (the year Duren died). Duren was going to quietly destroy it at his forge. Krun discovered the interception. **Krun ordered Duren's death** (via his enforcer Jarek Stoneforge) and recovered the coin.

Duren has been buried at Gavrek's Anvil for three years. The coin has been in Krun's vault for three years. **Krun intends to attune it this week** — at the three-year anniversary of Duren's death, which is also the three-year anniversary of the coin's final seizure, which is also the three-year cooling-off period Krun's personal discipline required. He will use the coin to identify his father's killer. He has waited fifty years. He will not be stopped by a party of four who arrived by Sigga Coalbeard's letter.

## Voice & manner

- Formal elder-register. Uses the Thorbardin imperative tense when speaking to the party ("you will not pursue this"; "this matter is closed"). Does not ask; announces.
- Calls Grom *"cousin"* on first meeting. It is not friendly. It is a claim. Grom's half-kinship to Duren through Krun's mother makes Krun and Grom technically distant kin by Thorbardin reckoning. Krun uses the title to put Grom at a disadvantage in any argument about justice.
- Does not say Duren's name until forced. When he does: flat, without inflection.
- Wears his grief for his father like a craft. He will discuss his father at length if asked. He will not discuss Duren at all.

## What he wants this session

- **Primary:** attune the Wrath-Coin in three days' time. Identify his father's killer. Exact whatever justice the coin directs.
- **Secondary:** the party leaves Gavrek's Anvil without discovering Duren's death was murder.
- **Tertiary:** if discovery happens, the party is silenced — preferably by legal process (Thorbardin law is friendly to Thanes), otherwise by force.
- **Not wanted:** direct combat with a Solamnic paladin. Krun is a political animal; he would prefer a legal entanglement or a bribe.

## Stat block (homebrew CR 1, 200 XP — older, political, not a frontline combatant)

- **AC** 15 (breastplate; rarely in armor; assume +2 Dex only in non-combat) · **HP** 40 (5d8+15) · **Speed** 25 ft (limp)
- **Str** 10 · **Dex** 11 · **Con** 16 · **Int** 14 · **Wis** 14 · **Cha** 15
- **Saves (proficient):** Con +5, Wis +4, Cha +4
- **Skills:** Deception +6, History +4, Insight +4, Intimidation +6, Persuasion +6
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft; passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish
- **Dwarven racial:** advantage saves vs. poison; resistance to poison damage; stonecunning.

**Actions (if forced to combat):**
- **Warhammer:** +2 to hit, 1d8 bludgeoning (unenthusiastic; he hasn't trained in years).
- **Voice of the Thane (action, 1/short rest):** Krun declares a creature's actions illegal by Thorbardin law. Target makes a Wis save DC 14 or loses their action and gains disadvantage on their next attack. (Mechanically: a charm-adjacent political move. Does not work on creatures who do not recognize Thorbardin law — e.g., Kessa as a Qualinesti elf gets advantage on the save.)

**Reactions:**
- **Blood-call (1/combat):** when an attack would reduce Krun to 0 HP, he calls for his enforcer Jarek Stoneforge and guards. If Jarek is within 60 ft and audible, Jarek uses his reaction to intercept and take the hit. Krun's HP stays at 1.

## Secrets

- **Krun and Duren shared a mother.** This is not publicly known. If the party learns this, it changes the political texture of Duren's murder — it becomes fratricide, not a Thane ordering a subordinate's death. Thorbardin law treats fratricide more harshly.
- **Krun's father's killer is now dead.** Krun does not know this. The killer died of plague in PC -2 (47 years after the murder). If Krun attunes the coin and discovers this, the coin will find no living target — and Krun's fifty-year grievance will be without an outlet. He will have to feel the *absence* of the object of his rage. This is the most humane outcome for Krun, and he will refuse it if offered in advance.
- **Sigga Coalbeard has Duren's missing hammer.** She hid it the night before Duren died (Duren asked her to). It contains Duren's maker-mark and could be used to identify Duren's forge-signature on the partially-finished Wrath-Coin destruction-work. Krun has searched for it three years and not found it.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 1 (Road Ambush):** Krun is not present. Jarek Stoneforge commands the ambush.
- **Scene 2 (Gavrek's Anvil Arrival):** Krun visits the shrine briefly — for political reasons (he is a patron; his mine-hold is three hours east). He will greet the party coldly and warn them against "disturbing old graves." He does not stay.
- **Scene 3 (Duren's Grave / Reorx-rite):** Krun is absent; Jarek watches from a distance.
- **Scene 4 (Forge Records):** Krun is not present, but his commission-letters are in the shrine's records (Duren's forge-archive). The letters name him directly.
- **Scene 5 (Lower Ore-Shaft):** Krun is not present. Jarek may be (if he survived Scene 1).
- **Scene 6 (Thane Krun's Hall — Confrontation):** **Primary scene.** The party arrives at Kruntharrak. Krun is in his hall with the Wrath-Coin on a velvet cloth before him; he was about to attune. The party's arrival forces a decision: bargain, assault, arrest, or withdraw.
- **Scene 7 (Aftermath):** Krun's fate per Scene 6 outcome — surrendered to Thorbardin law, killed, or (rarely) escaped to deeper Thorbardin holds.

## Post-session outcomes

- **Krun surrendered:** Thorbardin fratricide trial. Execution likely; exile possible. Krun's mine-hold confiscated. **Golden ending.**
- **Krun killed by Grom:** Thorbardin will investigate; a Solamnic-involved-dwarven-killing becomes a minor political incident; Brother Laen may have to intervene. Diplomatic cost.
- **Krun killed by another PC:** same political cost; Grom specifically did not resolve his grief.
- **Krun escapes:** he retreats to a deeper Thorbardin hold; becomes a long-term campaign antagonist. Not preferred per campaign arc.
- **Krun bargained with:** he surrenders the coin and confesses the fratricide in exchange for legal-process rather than combat-death. Possible with Persuasion DC 18 or a PC who names the half-kinship (which requires discovering it first, Scene 2 or 4).

## GM notes

- **Play Krun as a *grievance-holder*.** His father's death at fourteen shaped him. He is not evil in the Solamnic sense; he is *unresolved*. The Wrath-Coin is his attempt at a tool for what he cannot process. The rubric v1.3 "rage of others directed at bearer" is a clinical description of what Krun has been *wishing for* for fifty years.
- **Krun's Thane-formality is weaponized.** When Aelric speaks, Krun will counter with Solamnic-Thorbardin protocol ("the Knights of Solamnia have no jurisdiction in outer-hold matters"). When Grom speaks, Krun will counter with dwarven-family-protocol ("cousin, this is not Crown business"). When Kessa speaks, Krun will dismiss her as "a Qualinesti guest" who "cannot weigh a Thane's matter." Thera he will ignore until she forces him to engage.
- **If the party discovers the half-kinship** (Scene 2 via Sigga, or Scene 4 via genealogical records in the shrine), Krun's authority collapses. Fratricide is a Thorbardin capital matter; even his own guards will hesitate to defend him. Use this as Scene 6's pivot: the kinship-reveal, if played, turns the confrontation from a political argument into a legal one, and Krun's "Voice of the Thane" no longer commands his own household.
- **The half-kinship reveal should land to Grom specifically.** He shares a mother-line with Krun (distantly; through Duren). The moment Grom learns Krun and Duren were half-brothers, his Reorx-priest instinct will read the murder as *kin-slaughter*. This is the moral fulcrum of Scene 6.
