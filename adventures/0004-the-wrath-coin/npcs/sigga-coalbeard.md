# Sigga Coalbeard, Widow of Duren; Shrine-Keeper's Wife at Gavrek's Anvil

## Appearance

Fifty-eight, hill dwarf, shorter than Grom by two inches, grey-braided in the four-plait elder style (which she wears correctly, unlike Krun). Strong hands — she has done the shrine's forge-cleaning for twenty-six years and can spot a re-forged joint by thumb alone. A small hammer-mark burn on her right forearm, matching Grom's left hand — she was present when Duren ordained Grom at Gavrek's Anvil in PC 18 and she took the same wake-mark three years later on her husband's death. Black widow-cord at her left wrist. Eyes grey-green; Grom's eyes are cooled-steel; they are not the same but they pair.

## Background

Born in a mining-craft family at the eastern Kharolis holds. Married Duren Coalbeard at 32 (young for hill dwarves); moved to Gavrek's Anvil when Duren inherited the forge-master seat from his own master. They had no children — a grief they carried together and never named. She is the shrine-keeper's wife; she keeps the shrine's ledger, the forge-fire, and the traveling-priest hospitality.

**She knew Krun and Duren shared a mother.** Duren told her on their third anniversary. She never told anyone. She still has not told Grom.

**The night before Duren died (PC 23), Duren handed her his hammer** — the one his master forged, the one that should be buried with him — and said: *"Hide this. If I do not come back from the shaft, it will be because of the coin. Do not give it to Krun. Do not give it to the shrine. Keep it until someone comes who should have it."* She hid the hammer under a stone in the shrine's east cellar. It is still there. She has not touched it in three years.

**She has come to believe Grom is the someone who should have it.** She does not know why. She wrote him a letter. She did not mention the hammer.

## Voice & manner

- Terse, the Thorbardin way. Does not fill silences.
- Addresses Grom as *"forge-priest"* or *"young priest"* — not by name. It is affectionate, in the way only Thorbardin dwarves can make professional titles sound close.
- When she decides to tell something hard, she uses an old hill-dwarf phrase: *"Here is the thing."* Then she tells it. She has used this phrase perhaps ten times in her life.
- Will cry without apology. Does not cry often.

## What she wants this session

- **Primary:** the hammer to go where Duren meant. If Grom is the one who should have it — as she has come to suspect — then Grom takes it.
- **Secondary:** Duren's name cleared. She has known for three years that the mine-collapse was not a collapse. She has not known what to do with the knowledge.
- **Tertiary:** herself safe. She is not naive; she knows that telling Grom what she knows puts her in Krun's reach. She is willing; she is not eager.

- **AC** 10 · **HP** 8 · **Speed** 25 ft
- Str 11, Dex 10, Con 12, Int 13, Wis 15, Cha 12.
- **Skills:** Insight +4, Religion +3, Perception +4, History +3 (stonework +5 with Stonecunning).
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft; passive Perception 14.
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish.
- **Hearth-priest's pebble (personal item):** a small Gavrek's Anvil river-stone Duren gave her. No mechanical effect. She carries it in her apron.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 2 (Gavrek's Anvil Arrival):** she greets the party formally at the shrine-gate. Offers water and bread. Tells Grom she is glad he came for the rite. Does **not** yet tell him about the hammer or the murder; she is measuring him first.
- **Scene 3 (Duren's Grave / Reorx-rite):** she attends the rite. When the rite completes, she says quietly to Grom (alone, if possible): *"Here is the thing. His hammer is not in the grave. I hid it the night before he died. He asked me to. I will give it to you."*
- **Scene 4 (Forge Records):** she unlocks the forge-archive for the party. Shows them Krun's commission-letters. Says: *"Krun and Duren shared a mother. This is not public. I tell you now because if you are going to Kruntharrak, you should know."* This is the **half-kinship reveal** — it reframes Scene 6 entirely.
- **Scene 5 (Lower Ore-Shaft):** she is not present; she stays at the shrine. She will see the party off with a touch to Grom's left hand (where his hammer-mark burn is) and say nothing.
- **Scene 6 (Kruntharrak):** she is not present.
- **Scene 7 (Aftermath):** she participates in Duren's full rite-of-re-interment. If Krun is surrendered to justice, she watches the party ride toward Thorbardin-proper with Krun in custody and does not speak. If Krun is killed by Grom, she will say — the first time in three years she has said it aloud — *"I did not ask you to. I did not ask you not to. I will sit with the weight."*

## Secrets

- **She knows Duren was working to destroy the Wrath-Coin**, not deliver it. She does not know what the coin does; she knows Duren did not want it delivered.
- **She has suspected Grom's non-falling-family-story.** She has never asked. She will not, even now. But she will look at him for a moment when he greets the shrine's forge for the first time, and if he uses the inside-curve thumb-check on the forge-iron, she will know Grom is real.
- **Duren asked her to name their unborn child (had there been one) *Anvil*.** She has not told this to anyone. It is not session-material; it is character-gravity.

## GM notes

- **Play Sigga as restrained gravity.** She has carried three years of knowledge; she is not going to spill it in one conversation. Scene 2 is measuring. Scene 3 is the *hammer* offering. Scene 4 is the *kinship* offering. Scene 7 is the *confession*. Each is a separate weight.
- **The hammer is treasure-grade weight.** When Grom takes it, play the moment with silence. The hammer is a non-magical masterwork warhammer with Duren's maker-mark; its significance is narrative, not mechanical. If Grom dual-wields with Second-Chance, that is fine.
- **Sigga is the party's primary ally in this adventure.** She is not a quest-giver; she is a *widow with information*. Treat her accordingly.
