# Jarek Stoneforge, Thane Krun's Enforcer

## Appearance

Forty-five, hill dwarf, broad-shouldered, red-gold beard in a single plait (younger style; he has not taken an elder's four-plait despite his seniority in Krun's household — a sign of his self-image as a *worker*, not a *lord*). Wears the boiled-leather of a mine-overseer, not the chain of a household guard. Carries a miner's pick in addition to his warhammer; uses the pick more than the hammer.

## Background

Jarek's father was Krun's father's foreman. The loyalty is generational; when Krun was fourteen and lost his father, Jarek (then eight) was the son-of-the-foreman who stood beside him at the Thorbardin rite. They grew up in parallel. Jarek has served Krun's hold since he was twenty.

**Jarek was the instrument of Duren's death.** Krun ordered; Jarek planned; Jarek executed. Specifically: Jarek sabotaged the Lower Ore-Shaft's support-beam using a slow-acting mining charge that mimicked collapse-conditions. He was below ground with Duren for the sabotage; he escaped before the charge fired; Duren did not. Jarek carried the sabotage-rig back out in pieces in his tool-pouch so no evidence remained. **He has not slept well in three years.** He does not discuss this with anyone, including Krun.

## Voice & manner

- Short sentences. The mining register, where wasted words can cost ore or lives.
- Does not meet Grom's eyes for more than a beat. Grom does not know why; Grom will notice.
- When pressed on details of the mine-collapse, Jarek recites a prepared account. The account is verbatim the same each time. A skeptical listener will notice.
- Will not lie to a direct question *about himself*. He will lie about Krun's orders.

## What he wants this session

- **Primary:** protect Krun's life. Generational loyalty.
- **Secondary:** not have his role in Duren's death exposed. If exposed, Jarek will face Thorbardin law alongside Krun.
- **Tertiary:** survive. Jarek is not suicidal; if cornered, he will surrender. He may even turn on Krun under the right pressure (Persuasion DC 16 after exposure).

## Stat block (homebrew CR ½, 100 XP — mining-trained frontliner)

- **AC** 15 (studded leather + shield) · **HP** 30 (4d8+12) · **Speed** 25 ft
- **Str** 15 · **Dex** 12 · **Con** 16 · **Int** 10 · **Wis** 11 · **Cha** 10
- **Skills:** Athletics +4, Perception +2, Stonecunning on History +4
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft; passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish
- **Dwarven racial:** advantage vs. poison; poison resistance.

**Actions:**
- **Multiattack:** warhammer + miner's pick (bonus action with dual-wielding-style ruling).
- **Warhammer:** +4 to hit, 1d8+2 bludgeoning.
- **Miner's pick (bonus action):** +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing. **On crit:** the pick embeds; target has disadvantage on next attack while pick is being extracted (Str check DC 13).
- **Mining charge (1/rest; thrown, 30 ft):** Dex save DC 13 in 10-ft radius; 2d6 thunder + knocked prone on fail; half + no prone on save. **Dwarves are immune to being surprised by this charge.**

**Reactions:**
- **Blood-call intercept** (1/short rest): when Krun calls *"Stoneforge,"* Jarek takes a reaction-hit for Krun at any range up to 60 ft, provided he can reach. After intercept, Jarek is at disadvantage on his next turn.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 1 (Road Ambush):** Jarek **commands the ambush**. He has three hired-mercenary dwarves with him — Bandit-stat hill-dwarves, CR ⅛. The ambush is intended to *stall* the party (not kill), giving Krun time to prepare Kruntharrak's defenses. Jarek will withdraw at half-HP.
- **Scene 2 (Shrine):** he is present at a distance — watching Krun's visit. Party may spot him: Perception DC 13.
- **Scene 3 (Rite):** he watches from the east slope. He does not interfere.
- **Scene 5 (Lower Ore-Shaft):** **this is where his secret is.** He may be present in person or absent. If the party descends while he is absent, they find evidence of the sabotage (Investigation DC 13); if he is present, he attacks to prevent discovery (Scene 5 encounter).
- **Scene 6 (Kruntharrak):** commands the hall's guards. Uses his Blood-call reaction to protect Krun. If the half-kinship is revealed to Jarek as an unknown, he may hesitate (Insight DC 15 notices his hesitation; Persuasion DC 16 flips him to turn on Krun).
- **Scene 7 (Aftermath):** per Scene 6 outcome.

## Secrets

- **He sabotaged the Lower Ore-Shaft.** If the party discovers this, his surrender is the only alternative to dying for Krun.
- **He knew Duren was Krun's half-brother.** This knowledge has eroded him. He killed a kinsman of his lord. Thorbardin law makes no exception for "I was ordered." If the party confronts him with the kinship AND the sabotage, he will break — Wisdom save DC 13 to resist; on fail, he confesses fully.

## GM notes

- **Play Jarek as a man who has not slept.** Short sentences. Shadows under the eyes. Attention to Grom that he cannot explain. If Grom says anything forge-related, Jarek flinches at hearing Duren's professional register.
- **His fight is not a priority fight.** If he dies in the road ambush, the party may never learn his sabotage-role; evidence in Scene 5 becomes the alternative path. If he lives to Scene 6 and turns, he becomes the *witness* the Thorbardin law needs to convict Krun.
- **Insight reads on Jarek:**
  - **DC 12:** *"He is lying about something specific, not everything."*
  - **DC 15:** *"He hesitates when Grom speaks forge-words."*
  - **DC 18:** *"He has killed someone he should not have. He does not know how to sleep."*
