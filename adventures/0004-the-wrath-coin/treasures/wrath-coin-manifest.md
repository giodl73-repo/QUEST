# Curse Symptoms Manifest — The Wrath-Coin

Cross-skill contract: `dungeon-smith` must honor ≥ 3 symptoms across ≥ 2 rooms/scenes. This manifest delivers **8 symptoms across 6 scenes**, plus **2 hint events** (Hint 2 unlock + Hint 3 plant) at Scene 3. **All anchor-level symptoms have ≥ 2 of 3 fallback paths (v1.3)**.

## Required (≥ 3, distributed across ≥ 2 scenes)

- [x] **Sensory symptom (coin's subjective weight).** The coin weighs more than one ounce on hand. Primary: any PC who picks it up notices (passive, no roll). **Redundancy:** multiple PCs will handle the coin in Scenes 4, 5, 6 (path b — cross-scene redundancy). **Rubric v1.3 anchor-level: ≥2 fallback paths.**

- [x] **Sensory symptom (rage-glyph face cooler).** The engraved face is perceptibly cooler than the mirror-smooth face. Primary: Grom passive touch at Scene 4 or Scene 6 (path a — no roll, passive). **Redundancy:** Sigga will mention this detail in her Scene 4 disclosure as part of Duren's research notes (path c — NPC-delivered recall).

- [x] **Textual symptom (Aelwen's second fragment — "do not let them hold what is not theirs").** Found in Duren's forge-archive (Scene 4). Investigation DC 12 or DM-obligation to any PC who has Aelwen's journal page from #0003 in hand (they will see the handwriting match; auto-delivery). **Redundancy:** if not found in Scene 4, Sigga herself can quote the fragment in Scene 7 having memorized it (path c).

- [x] **Auditory symptom (the coin's "listening" when grievance is spoken nearby).** When a PC articulates a grievance in the coin's presence, the coin briefly warms (Scene 4 when the Jarek-sabotage evidence is discussed; Scene 6 when Krun names his father's killer). Perception DC 10; passive 12 also fires.

- [x] **Reaction moment (Krun's attunement-cool-off ending this week).** Scene 6's temporal pressure: Krun is at his *moment of attunement*. The party interrupts. The coin is on a velvet cloth, unattuned but *ready*. Scene-pacing reception — any PC present experiences it.

- [x] **Reaction moment (Voidmouth warning — optional symptom).** If any PC attempts to empty the coin via partial forgiveness and then hold it past emptying, the coin begins to *re-listen*. Active Arcana DC 14 or Kessa passive 15 detects. **Redundancy:** Sigga's letter (Scene 7) can warn of this if Sigga has seen Duren's notes (path c).

## Required (always)

  - (ground) Duren's forge at Gavrek's Anvil — where Duren intended to destroy it

  - (east) Kruntharrak mine-hold — where the coin has been held three years
  - (above all) the grave — where Duren's hammer should have been buried and is not
  The structure is *denied-destruction-protected-by-murder-kept-in-vault*. The coin's location tells its story.

Scene 3 (rite): Reorx judgment delivered to Grom (Hint 3 plant). Scene 4 (forge records): Krun's commission-letters. Scene 5 (shaft): sabotage evidence. Scene 6 (hall): the coin itself. The coin's reality accrues backward from Scene 6 through Scenes 4-1.

## Hint deliveries (Scene 3, at Duren's grave Reorx-rite)

### Hint 2 — Vethrenn's warded margin-note UNLOCKS

**Pre-conditions:** Kessa has Vethrenn's commonplace book present during the rite. The rite must complete successfully (Religion DC 15 or DM-obligation delivery). The elven ward on the margin-note requires *cosmic weight* to break; Grom's Reorx-rite at a consecrated grave provides this. Per #0001 + #0003 setup: the ward weakened at Aelwen's shop proximity (#0003 near-unlock); it **breaks fully** at the Reorx-rite's consecration moment.

**Delivery:** as Grom completes the rite, Kessa feels the book warm in her pack. She opens it (Free action; in-fiction narrated as "the silver-ink has surfaced again"). The margin-note now reads:

> *"We made the rose to hold. We did not try to release. I think release is possible. I do not know how. — V., the night I went."*
>
> *— Vethrenn Silverglass, PC 100, White Robe of the Fifth Rank*

**"The night I went"** = the night Vethrenn went silent. She wrote this margin-note before disappearing. She knew the Rose's mechanism; she *guessed* a release existed; she did not find it.

**This is Hint 2 caught cleanly.** Required for the finale's D-route.

### Hint 3 — Reorx's Judgment PLANTS

**Delivery:** during the rite's completion, **Grom receives a theological insight** via craft-witness reception (no dice; the ratified player-style delivers). Reorx, through the rite's attention, judges:

> *"The forger came to me in PC 179. I accepted her soul. I did not accept her forgings. They were grief-coiled, not forged-with-intent. A tool made with grief is not a tool; it is a holding. A holding can be un-held. A coil can be uncoiled."*

Grom hears this not as external voice but as *craft-understanding* — the same register in which Duren's "inside-curve is the honest one" reached him three years ago. He cannot quote it to Kessa; he can *act on it*. The party will have to piece together what Grom knows by watching him act.

**This is Hint 3 planted.** Required for the finale's D-route. Synthesis with Hint 4 (Aelwen's ingot reference) at #0007.

**Combined Scene 3 significance:** the Reorx-rite at Duren's grave delivers TWO hints in one moment — Hint 2 unlocked (Kessa), Hint 3 planted (Grom). This is a **craft-witness + sheet-deep-reader cross-receiver moment**; both ratified player-styles fire. **Per rubric v1.2 finder-vs-receiver: FINDER = Grom (the rite's celebrant); RECEIVERS = Kessa (Hint 2, via her own book) + Grom (Hint 3, via craft-understanding). Two simultaneous receptions.**
