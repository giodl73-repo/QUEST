# The Wrath-Coin

*A palm-sized moon-silver coin, engraved on one face with a fist inside a flame, polished to full convergence on the other. Heavier than it should be.*

## Appearance

One ounce exactly. Circular, 1.5 inches diameter, 2mm thick. Moon-silver through-and-through (no core, no plating). One face bears **Aelwen's rage-glyph** — a fist clenched inside a flame, Vaenshold-provincial engraving; the flame is stylized like a three-petaled half-bloomed rose turned upside down. The other face is **polished to full convergence** — the mirror-bright finish Aelwen's craft demanded. *This is the only one of Aelwen's six forgings that Aelwen completed to her own standard.* The Mirror was unfinished. The Reliquary was workshop-rushed. The Rose was a client commission. The Coin is Aelwen *as she meant to forge.*

When placed on an open palm, the coin **weighs more than an ounce.** Estimates vary — three ounces, four, once reported as a pound in a 16th-century Thorbardin metallurgist's journal. The weight is subjective; the scale reads one ounce. The weight is *felt*, not measured.

The rage-glyph face is always cooler than the mirror-smooth face. No one has measured the temperature difference. Grom would notice on passive touch.

## Presence / Desire

The coin is waiting to be held *against the pulse of a grievance*. Those who lift it with curiosity alone feel only the weight. Those who lift it with a grievance in mind — even an unconscious one — feel the coin warm against the rage-glyph face within two breaths.

Aelwen's workshop-notes call this *"the listening that is not listening."* Compare to the Mirror's *"waiting that is not waiting."* Both are attention-dependent.

**Rage-glyph face held to bare skin:** the holder feels the rage of every person who has ever been wronged *by* them, directed *at* them, as a chorus. First-time holders report the sensation as *"many people shouting inside my chest but no voices."* It is not ideation. It is *felt fact*: other people's fury, acknowledged by the silver.

**Mirror-smooth face held to bare skin:** the holder feels the coin's *weight* change. Lighter if they are currently un-wronged by anyone; heavier if many unresolved grievances exist against them. Functions as a rough barometer of *how much rage the holder has earned*.

## Rarity

**Very Rare.** Attunement required (1 round — faster than short rest, slower than instant; burns warm during the round, allowing the holder to drop the coin before attunement completes).

## Provenance

Aelwen forged the Wrath-Coin in **PC 179** — the final year of her life, after the Rose and Reliquary and Mirror, in secret. Commissioned by **Thane Krun Ironhaft of Kruntharrak** (then 30) as a tool to identify his father's killer. Aelwen accepted the commission; Aelwen forged the coin; Aelwen hanged herself before delivering it. Heva Aelwensdaughter buried the coin in Aelwen's workshop among the other five artifacts, PC 179.

Timeline of possession:

- **PC 179 — PC 178:** Heva hides the coin in the Vaenshold workshop vault.
- **PC 178 — PC 60:** the coin passes through the Vaenshold silversmith line (Heva → Rillina → Avel), each silversmith refusing it. Avel's ledger notes: *"I will not sell this. I will not destroy this. I will pass it. May it find a forge."*
- **PC 60 — PC 32:** Avel's daughter Seralen (Mira's mother) refuses the coin and sells it via a third party to a minor Kharolis-foothills merchant — the only Aelwen-forging ever voluntarily-sold by the Vaenshold line. Seralen needed the money; she did not ask the merchant's name. The coin entered the outer markets.
- **PC 32 — PC 23:** the coin passed through three minor holds in Thorbardin's outer hierarchy, never attuned. Each holder sensed the weight-that-was-wrong and sold it on.
- **PC 23 (Duren's final year):** **Duren Coalbeard purchased the coin** through his half-brother connection to Krun. He did not tell Krun. Duren was planning to destroy the coin at his forge at Gavrek's Anvil, quietly, before Krun's attunement-cool-off period ended. **Duren was murdered before he could begin the destruction.**
- **PC 23 — PC 20 (three years):** the coin has been in Krun's vault at Kruntharrak. Krun has not yet attuned. He set a three-year cool-off period (his personal discipline) to ensure he was certain. The cool-off ends **this week**. Scene 6 is Krun's attunement-day.

**Krun is the intended original attuner.** He has waited 50 years. The coin has been aimed at his hands since PC 179.

## Properties (mechanical)

### Attunement

**Required.** Takes **1 round** — the coin burns warm against bare skin during one round, completing attunement at the end of the round unless the holder drops it. **This is a fast-attune, slower than the Mirror.** The holder has one decision-window (the round) to reject attunement.

When the attunement completes, the holder feels the *primary grievances* currently held in the coin — as of this session, six are known:

1. **Krun's father's killer** (a rival-hold dwarf who died in PC -2 of plague; Krun does not know he is dead).
2. **Duren Coalbeard's widow Sigga** — her grievance against Krun for murdering her husband. She has not named this grievance to anyone; the coin heard it without her permission.
3. **Aelwen herself** — her grievance against the Vaenshold patriarchal order that named her a craftswoman and not a wizard. The coin carries her own un-spoken rage at her place. First time this has been articulated; even Aelwen did not write it.
4. **Heva Aelwensdaughter** — her grief-rage at Aelwen's suicide, unresolved for a century before her own death.
5. **An unnamed Kharolis foothills merchant** (the PC 32 purchaser) — his rage at the person who sold him a coin he could feel was cursed without telling him.
6. **Jarek Stoneforge** — his post-murder rage at himself. The coin caught this when it sat in Krun's vault three years, three feet from Jarek during household meetings.

Additional rages can accumulate during play — the coin *listens*.

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Grievance-Sense** (passive, free). The bearer can sense the direction and rough strength of rage directed at them within 60 ft, provided the anger is one of the primary grievances held in the coin OR a grievance the coin has added in play.
- **Intimidation Advantage.** The bearer has advantage on Intimidation checks against any creature whose grievance is held in the coin.
- **Rage-Damage Resistance.** Rage-driven attackers (Berserker frenzy, barbarian rage, creatures with Rampage traits) deal **−1 damage per hit** against the bearer. Cumulative with resistance from other sources.
- **Named Direction (1/long rest).** The bearer may speak a specific person's name and feel the direction + rough strength of that person's rage toward them, within 1 mile. Functions even for named-dead persons if their rage is held in the coin (result: *"Direction: none. They are dead. The rage remains."*)

### Curse (while attuned)

**Inherited Rage.**

Whenever the bearer experiences anger in any scene (DM's discretion for scene-intensity trigger), the bearer rolls a **DC 12 Wisdom save**. **On failure:** the bearer's anger is *re-keyed* to the strongest grievance currently in the coin. They act on *that* grievance, not their own. Their words, their target, their tactical choices — all re-tuned.

This is not charm. It is *amplification of a misaligned rage-current*. The bearer is themselves; their anger is not.

Example: a PC discovers a trapped door. Frustration rises. DC 12 Wis save: fail. Instead of acting on the immediate frustration, the PC acts on Krun-father-killer-rage (strongest grievance in coin) — spending their next turn breaking the door with *disproportionate* violence, insulting the trap's maker in the specific cadence Krun used about his father's killer, attempting to chase down whoever-set-the-trap for a rage-target that cannot be satisfied.

Additional curse: **the bearer gains 1 level of exhaustion per 30 days** that does not remove on long rest. Exhaustion persists until (a) attunement ends, (b) a specific-named act of forgiveness is performed toward one of the primary grievances, or (c) the bearer completes the Reciprocal Forgiveness ending (all 6 grievances named and released).

### The Voidmouth (anchor-level warning)

**If all 6 primary grievances are released and the coin is empty**, and the bearer continues to hold the coin: the coin begins to **listen for new grievances.** It will record grievances directed at the bearer from anyone within 1 mile, continuously. The bearer does not know this is happening. After 30 days, the coin is loaded with whatever casual grievances the bearer has generated — unspoken complaints from tavern-keepers, passing irritations from farmhands, family members' un-processed resentments. These become the new primary grievances.

An emptied Wrath-Coin, kept, **re-fills.**

This is the trap most attuners do not foresee.

### Ending the curse

1. **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** at dawn on a forge ≥ 1,800°C, **Religion DC 15** (same as Mirror). Destroys the coin. Attunement ends. Exhaustion persists until next long rest. **Un-made rages are released to silence** (no inheritors; the rages dissipate with the forging's destruction).
2. **Reciprocal Forgiveness (the Aelwen-intent ending):** the bearer identifies each of the primary grievances, names the wrong specifically, and offers amends (symbolic or material). The named person need not be alive or present, but the bearer must know their *name* and the *specific wrong*. When all 6 are named and released, the coin is empty. If the bearer **immediately destroys the empty coin** (any method; it is non-magical after emptying), they complete Aelwen's intended ending. **This was Aelwen's original design** — the coin was meant to be *emptied and destroyed*, not *held*.
3. **Partial forgiveness:** any named-and-released grievance drops the exhaustion-level by 1 and lightens the coin by 1/6th weight. Progress persists across long rests.

## Hooks (PC-specific)

- **Grom + Krun (half-kin).** If Grom attunes the coin (unlikely per heuristics, but possible after Scene 5's murder-evidence lands), he will feel — among the six rages — **Sigga's unspoken rage against Krun.** This is the widow of his forge-master's murderer's brother, channeled through the coin to Grom who *also* shares mother-line kinship with Krun. The rage will be unbearable. Blood-call into a rage Grom cannot resolve. **Blank-Mirror-analog risk:** Grom attuning the coin is the single worst PC-attunement in the campaign thus far.
- **Aelric + Kann's client Felor.** The coin does not carry Felor's rage (he has not been wronged by Aelric). But attuning the coin and using the Named Direction on Felor may reveal Felor's position relative to Aelric — useful intel for #0005.
- **Thera + Nenn.** Thera's father left her in PC 3. She has not unpacked this. If she attunes, the coin may add *her* father's rage (he has been wronged by her, in his own narrow reading) as a 7th grievance. Risk.
- **Kessa + Vethrenn.** Vethrenn went silent PC 100. She has no directed rage at Kessa (they never met). Coin would be blank for Kessa — but not Blank-Mirror-blank; a different blank. The coin would tell her Vethrenn's silence is *not* a grievance. That may be a revelation, not a curse. Consider Kessa-attuning as a subtle rubric-v1.2 finder-vs-receiver moment: she receives by *absence*, not by landing.

## Cross-campaign status

This is the **fourth moon-silver artifact** the party has encountered. Of Aelwen's six, two remain unseen (the Pride-Circlet #0005; the Thread-That-Frays #0006). The seventh-as-ingot (Aelwen's unforged confession) is the subject of the #0007 finale.

**Important campaign-continuity note:** This is the **first moon-silver artifact whose provenance includes a living antagonist (Krun) who commissioned it and intends to attune it.** The Silver Rose, Reliquary, Mirror, and any others we've seen have been *unclaimed* or *inherited.* Krun is the first would-be attuner with an explicit goal. This matters for #0005's Lady Tiran (who is also a would-be attuner of the Pride-Circlet).

## Aelwen's note (found at Scene 4 in Duren's forge-archive)

Duren discovered, in his research, a **second surviving Aelwen-fragment** — a scrap from her apron, preserved by a workshop-boy and passed through three generations until it reached Duren via a Kharolis merchant. The fragment is inked:

> *"The Coin I will forge with full polish. This will be my good work. It is the one I mean. Do not sell it. Empty it. Break it. Do not let them hold what is not theirs. — A., the night before."*

**"The night before"** — the night before Aelwen hanged herself. This note is Aelwen's final-forging intent. It is the second of her two surviving fragments (first = journal page from #0003). Feed into Hint 3 synthesis at Scene 3.
