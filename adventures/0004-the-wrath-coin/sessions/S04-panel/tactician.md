## Critique

### Overall assessment

Dice-driven session where the math produced outcomes the module could not have guaranteed. Jarek captured alive via damage-overshoot (Guiding Bolt 24 vs 12 HP = -12, not -30 instakill). Kessa's Portent [10] + Aelric's Bless die = Grom hits exactly DC 15 on his Reorx-rite. Cross-character mechanical compound at its cleanest. Scene 1 combat resolved in effectively one round with zero party damage (Aelric 10 from Jarek's warhammer; everything else clean). This is how mechanics should work — no fudges, no re-rolls, dice lands where dice lands, and the narrative absorbs the result.

### What worked

- **Scene 1 combat action-economy.** Kessa's initiative CRIT (21) put her first; Magic Missile at Merc2 (10 dmg; dropped to 1 HP) pre-wounded before Merc2's turn. Aelric's Smite on Merc1 (22 to hit + 2d8 radiant) dropped him before his initiative. Thera's Sneak Attack with Bless-die on Jarek (19 dmg) set up Grom's Guiding Bolt finisher (24 dmg, capture). **Three PCs dropped three enemies in Round 1.** Proper action-economy.

- **The Reorx-rite rescue.** Grom rolled Religion 12 against DC 15. Advantage (from prior hearth-greetings) didn't help — the adv roll was 9 not 17. Kessa's Portent replacement (her pre-rolled 10) became the d20 value. Aelric's pre-cast Bless (1d4 rolled 2) added. 10 + 3 + 2 = 15. **Exactly DC 15.** Multiple mechanics stacking to save a single roll. This is what compounding looks like.

### What needs work

- **Scene 5 tactical drought.** Jarek in custody = no combat in the shaft. The investigation-hunt was successful (Thera Inv 22 on cut-beam; thunder-stone 21; oil-cloth 19) but was not a tactical challenge — it was a skill-check series. For the middle of a 4-hour session, that's a pacing weakness. The tactical weight was front-loaded (Scene 1) and rear-loaded (Scene 6 attunement-race-that-didn't-fire).

- **Voice of the Thane never fired.** The P0-revised reaction-conditional mechanic was in the module and never triggered because no PC made a melee attack on Krun (Grom interposed; Thera took the coin; Kessa missile'd at range; Aelric kneeled). Good design, no chance to test it in play. Scene 6 went to narrative resolution before the V-of-T could be tested.

### The question I'd push on

**If Grom's Religion 12 had failed even after Portent + Bless rescue, how would the rite resolve?** In this session it passed by 0. But mechanically, what if 10 + 3 + 1 (Bless min) = 14? The module says Religion DC 15 for the Reorx-rite; a fail means the rite "stumbles." Is that narrative setback only, or mechanical (party delayed 1 day)? The module didn't say. For mechanical-fairness integrity, fail-state consequences should be explicit. Recommend: module adds "On fail: Grom may retry at dusk (disadvantage)." Gives PCs a clear recovery path.
