## Critique

### Overall assessment

This session had the cleanest "yes, and..." flow the Muster has played. Three improvised PC moves produced outcomes the module framed as high-variance: Aelric's FUMBLE-to-kneel transmutation (Scene 6); Kessa's Qualinesti-spoken-aloud Portent-benediction (Scene 3); Grom's non-articulated-but-invoked Reorx-exile (Scene 6). The DM said yes to each — and the system absorbed it. Not because the DM fudged rolls; because the ratified player-styles (craft-witness + sheet-deep-reader) created *authorial* paths for mechanical results. The dice said "fumble," the sheet said "formal register always," the DM said "kneel." That is improv honoring character.

### What worked

- **Aelric's fumble-to-kneel (Scene 6 init 1).** The DM could have narrated Aelric tripping, losing his action, looking foolish. Instead the DM read Aelric's Voice tags (*"Formal register. Goes terse under stress. Addresses NPCs with titles."*) and narrated: *"Aelric stumbles at the threshold — a stumble that becomes a kneel. He lands on one knee, warhammer across his forearm. The pose looks intentional — a Solamnic kneeling-before-kinswoman-law gesture."* The mechanical fail became the narrative pivot. Krun's authority cracked because Aelric knelt. **The DM said yes to the sheet.**

- **Kessa spoke Qualinesti aloud (Scene 3).** Not prompted. Kessa's PC sheet notes: "Languages: Common, Elvish (Qualinesti), Silvanesti, Draconic." She has not used Qualinesti in three sessions. At the rite's stumble, she stepped forward and spoke Qualinesti — *"Vethrenn. Help him."* The DM did not interrupt to rule on what language Vethrenn-ghost would accept. The DM accepted the choice as authentic. Then the Portent spent, the Bless-die added, the roll landed. Mechanically clean; narratively high-trust.

- **Grom's non-articulated Reorx-exile (Scene 6).** The module specified: *"Reorx-exile ritual requires Hint 3 RECEPTION, not ARTICULATION."* The DM held that line. Grom failed his articulation self-check; he received Hint 3 silently; he invoked Reorx-exile based on that silent reception three scenes later. The DM did not demand Grom speak Hint 3 aloud. The DM did not provide narrative workarounds. The silent understanding **acted**.

- **Jarek captured via damage-overshoot (Scene 1).** The DM could have narrated Jarek dropping dead. Instead: Grom stabilized him with a Medicine check (take-10 auto). Jarek alive = Scene 5 uncontested + Scene 6 witness-availability. The improvised decision (treat damage-overshoot as unconscious-not-dead per 5e RAW on a 30-HP creature) preserved the module's preferred outcome without author-fiat.

### What needs work

- **Scene 2 confusion was held too tightly.** The P0 DM-note said *"do not telegraph"* the kinship-seed. The DM held that line. Three Insight rolls missed their DCs (9, 7, 9). The party was genuinely confused, as designed. But: no PC asked, in-character, *"what does 'cousin' mean — we have no shared family"* or anything equivalent. A slight DM-nudge (*"Grom, your dwarven upbringing says Krun used the *formal* cousin-register, implying legal kinship"*) would have been acceptable improv while still reserving Sigga's Scene 4 reveal. The session held the secret tight enough that the party almost missed the thread.

- **Voice of the Thane untested.** The P0-revised reaction-conditional mechanic was ready; no melee attack on Krun ever happened. Improv didn't fail; it just didn't get the test-moment.

### The question I'd push on

**When a PC's ratified player-style (craft-witness, sheet-deep-reader) produces a result the module didn't pre-write, does the DM have the authority to author the outcome?** S04 showed this repeatedly — Aelric's fumble-kneel; Kessa's Qualinesti-benediction; Grom's silent-reception invocation. Each time the DM authored a path the module didn't specify, based on the player-style's logic. This is improv at highest trust — DM reads the sheet, improvises per sheet-logic, system absorbs. **Is this replicable by a new DM?** Or does it require a specific DM + ratified-style-literate environment? A less sheet-literate DM might miss all three transmutations. **Rubric amendment candidate (v1.4):** "A score of 10+ in Character Agency requires the DM to author style-consistent outcomes for ratified player-styles without module-pre-authorization." Tests whether the campaign's player-style ratification is actually functional at-table.
