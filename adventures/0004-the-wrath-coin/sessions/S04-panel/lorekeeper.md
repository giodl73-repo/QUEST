## Critique

### Overall assessment

The Moon-Silver Cycle is now fully loaded with lore that could only exist in this world. Aelwen's six forgings has become a documented arc: the Silver Rose (#0001), the Reliquary (#0002), the Mirror (#0003 — destroyed), the Wrath-Coin (#0004 — destroyed). The apron-scrap diptych with the #0003 journal page locked the setting's most ambitious cross-adventure thread. Hint 2 unlocked (Vethrenn's White-Robe hypothesis that *release is possible*). Hint 3 planted (Reorx's Judgment: grief-coils can be uncoiled). The campaign's theological spine is now explicit — Aelwen's forgings were made-wrong-and-meant-to-be-unmade, and the finale's inversion-ritual has both the Vethrenn-thesis and the Reorx-judgment. This is setting doing work.

### What worked

- **The Aelwen diptych.** Two fragments, two adventures, same hand, same ink, same "night before." Kessa's cross-reference in the cellar synthesized Aelwen's final two thoughts — despair (ingot-unforged) + redemption-resolve (the Coin as *"my good work"*). The character lived; the player found her by archaeology across sessions.

- **Thorbardin kinship-law mechanically enforceable.** Krun's Voice-of-the-Thane save-DC dropped from 14 to 10 when kinship was invoked. The DC-drop wasn't mere flavor — it was *law in effect*. Thorbardin culture matters at the dice level. This is the setting mattering beyond flavor.

- **Krun-Duren half-kinship via Hilda Stoneforge.** Sigga's disclosure at Scene 4 is the setting's most load-bearing NPC-delivery of this campaign. She names the lineage back to PC -49 (Krun's father's death), PC -32 (Hilda's remarriage), PC -30 (Duren's birth), PC -22 (Hilda's death). Genealogy as character. *"They did not grow up together. But they were half-brothers in Thorbardin law."*

- **The six rage-sparks dispersing east at the coin's destruction.** Setting-consistent imagery (east is the Nerakan border, where Krun's father's killer died of plague 22 years ago, where Jarek went guilty for 3 years). The sparks go toward the origins of their grievances. Small but correct.

- **Sigga's craft-chain final line.** *"I heard Mira Vaenshold went to Stonfold. A silversmith's lineage meeting a silversmith's lineage. Good."* Three adventures of women-smiths now connected — Mira (Vaenshold line) → Sigga (forge-master's widow) → Harel (Mira's apprentice, at Stonfold) → Tova Stonfold (non-Vaenshold moon-silver line). Kessa writes this in her book. **The female-craft-witness thread locks.**

### What needs work

- **Hint 3 articulation deferred to #0007.** Grom received the Reorx-Judgment at Scene 3; his self-check failed (Religion 8 vs DC 10). He invoked Reorx-exile at Scene 6 without articulating the judgment aloud. The party does not know, yet, what Grom received from Reorx. The synthesis moment (Kessa cross-referencing Hint 2's "release is possible" with Hint 3's "coil can be uncoiled") is preserved for the finale — correct by design — but the party has to carry a partial understanding forward. If a less sheet-deep PC had been Grom, the insight could have been lost. **The silent-reception chain worked because of the ratified player-styles. Without them, it would have been fragile.**

- **No Thorbardin political NPC with direct voice.** Krun is named-present Thane. Thora Stoneforge (second cousin, inherits Kruntharrak) is mentioned but not voiced. The Thorbardin Council of Thanes is referenced as judicial body but never appears. For lore-depth, the Council's voice would matter — especially for Jarek's separate tribunal. Seed for #0005 or #0006: a Thorbardin Council scene where Krun's exile and Jarek's sentencing are formally weighed.

### The question I'd push on

**Is Krun's Reorx-exile actually legal in Thorbardin?** Grom invoked it as "Reorx-priest authority on kin-crimes in outer holds." The module framed this as Grom's unique path unlocked by Hint 3. But Thorbardin has its own law (fratricide = execution by Council). A Reorx-priest invoking exile on a fratricide Thane is a *theological* override of a *legal* verdict. Is this canonical Reorx-priesthood authority, or is Grom claiming more than his ordination grants? The campaign should decide before Krun's exile is tested (he may be recaptured by Thorbardin enforcers who don't recognize Reorx-priest legal standing). Lore-clarification needed — possibly via `reference/dragonlance/religions.md`.
