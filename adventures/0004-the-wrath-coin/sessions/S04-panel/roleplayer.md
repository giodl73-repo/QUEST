## Critique

### Overall assessment

The best session the Muster has played. Again. S03 was already the best, and S04 exceeded it. Four PCs acting from their sheets, producing a collective outcome (Krun Reorx-exiled; Coin destroyed; Grom's burn-scar stopped) that none of them individually scripted. Kessa spoke Qualinesti aloud for the first time in three sessions — not because the DM prompted it; because her heuristic said *"address a dead wizard's spirit; this is the register that belongs."* That is character-made-choice at its deepest.

### What worked

- **Grom's Reorx-exile invocation (Scene 6).** Doubt-die rolled "stand between" (4 on d6). He did. Then — without the module's flagged Option-C spawn — he invoked Reorx-exile from his silent Hint-3 reception. He did not articulate what Reorx had told him at the grave; he simply *acted on it*. The player (or DM playing him) did not need the subagent to find this. Craft-witness in its fullest form: the sheet's ritual-practice delivered decision-authority.

- **Kessa's Portent framed as benediction.** Second time in two sessions. This time explicitly in-voice: *"Vethrenn. Help him. You wrote: release is possible. Let this be a releasing."* Qualinesti spoken aloud (she does not normally use the language). The mechanic (Portent replaces a d20) was pure Divination School; the delivery was spoken-prayer to a never-met mentor. Mechanical + sheet-hidden-content + craft-ritual — three ratified lenses firing at once.

- **Aelric's FUMBLE-to-kneel (Scene 6 init).** Nat-1 on initiative. The DM did not narrate stumbling-lose-turn. The DM narrated Solamnic kneeling-before-kinswoman-law. Aelric's Voice tags (formal register; "Est Sularus oth Mithas"; uses titles) guided interpretation. The fumble delivered heavier than a 20 would have — a knight kneeling to declare arrest lands harder than a knight striking first.

- **The Aelwen diptych reading aloud.** Kessa sets both fragments on the cellar floor. Reads the journal page (#0003: "I should have forged an ingot instead"). Reads the apron-scrap (#0004: "The Coin I will forge with full polish"). Says: *"These are the same woman, one hour apart. She despaired. Then she forged anyway. Then she went to the pine."* This is the full campaign arc synthesized by a PC, in her own register, with texts she recovered herself.

### What needs work

- **Scene 2 Insight blank-out.** Grom rolled 9 on Sigga (missed DC 15). Kessa rolled 7 on Gavrek's Anvil lineage (missed 13). Party Insight on Krun's "cousin" was 9 (missed 12). Three critical character-reads missed their DCs. The DM held the line (no telegraphing) per the P0 note, which is correct. But this meant Scene 2's atmospheric-reading-weight rested entirely on Grom's craft-witness passive (forge's weakened pulse) and Grom's Duren-maker-mark recognition. **Sigga's measuring — her three-year-weight — was NOT read by the party in Scene 2.** It landed at Scene 3 post-rite. Good arc, but a beat later than intended.

- **Thera's silence after Scene 4.** Thera held the coin cloth-wrapped at Scene 6 — a quick, competent move. But her grief-paragraph (Adda; the bread cut wrong-way) never surfaced in this adventure. She carries Seralen's "Do not look" note from S03; she did not read it aloud. Thera's internal life remained private. Not a session failure — she is a private PC by sheet — but two consecutive sessions of Thera-as-support suggest her spotlight is overdue.

### The question I'd push on

**What has Grom's silent Hint 3 reception cost him?** Three scenes carried an un-articulated understanding that drove a campaign-permanent decision (Reorx-exile). That is the highest form of craft-witness play. But: what does Grom now *know* that he has not *spoken*? If the party asks him directly in a later session, can he articulate it? Or has the silent reception become part of his decision-order itself, operating without his conscious awareness? This is a character-continuity question for Grom's sheet. The DM should note Grom's S04 Reorx-exile invocation as a sheet-update: *"Grom understands, non-verbally, that Aelwen's forgings are grief-coils and that coils can be uncoiled. He has not found words for this."*
