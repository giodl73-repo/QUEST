## Critique

### Overall assessment

A new player could follow this session. The P0 revisions applied pre-play (Scene 2 kinship-DM-note; Hint-3-silent-reception note; attunement-race flowchart; Voice-of-Thane reaction-conditional; non-Grom Option C; Scene 1 skip → Jarek-Scene-6 note; coin visual-appearance) all tested cleanly. The module ran cold. Every lookup was inline. Every DC was in two places. The Reorx-exile ritual-framework was explicit so Grom could invoke it without DM-improvisation.

**First session to test the binding threshold** (56+/80 S04 rubric v1.3). Scored 76/80 — **+20 above threshold.** The system has graduated from advisory to binding with margin to spare.

### What worked

- **Scene 2 kinship-DM-note.** Three Insight rolls missed their DCs (9, 7, 9). Without the DM-note, a cold-reading DM might have fudged or clued the party; with the DM-note, the DM held confidence. The misdirection landed as designed. Sigga's Scene 4 disclosure vindicated the patience.

- **Attunement-race flowchart.** Not triggered (no attunement). But having it in the module meant the DM had one less thing to improvise if it had fired. Peace of mind for a first-time DM running this module.

- **Hint 3 silent-reception DM-note.** When Grom's articulation failed (Religion 8 vs DC 10), the DM had explicit guidance: *"Silent reception counts. If the player asks 'what did I just get,' answer: 'Something changed. You cannot name it yet. It will act through you.'"* The DM delivered that phrasing, Grom's player accepted it, and the Reorx-exile invocation three scenes later landed on that acceptance. **Documentation enabled the player-style at-table.**

- **Reorx-exile ritual framework explicit.** Grom could invoke it because the module said *"Reorx-priest authority on kin-crimes in outer holds."* A cold-reading DM would not need to invent this; the module authorized it. Grom's Religion DC 12 check was in the text.

- **Voice-of-Thane reaction-conditional reform.** Not triggered (no melee on Krun), but reading cleaner than the original action-tax version. If a new DM had run the original, they would have had to parse a save-based action-tax mid-combat — cognitive load the reaction-conditional eliminates.

### What needs work

- **Mira/Sigga/Harel craft-chain reference (Scene 7).** Sigga's final line — *"I heard Mira Vaenshold went to Stonfold. A silversmith's lineage meeting a silversmith's lineage."* — requires S03 context to land fully. A fresh reader at #0004 would get it as atmosphere; they wouldn't feel the *weight* of three adventures of women-smiths converging. The accessibility policy (campaign-reward on anthology-floor) holds, but Sigga's line is edging toward campaign-required.

- **Aelwen diptych reading aloud (Scene 4).** Kessa reading both fragments — journal page (#0003) + apron-scrap (#0004) — is a campaign-scale moment. A fresh-face running the module cold would deliver it correctly (text is there), but wouldn't feel the *gravity* of the two fragments being from the same woman one hour apart across 180 years. This is the first session where **anthology-floor accessibility is tested against campaign-depth payoff** — and campaign-depth wins on emotional impact.

### The question I'd push on

**Where does the anthology-floor break?** The accessibility policy (ratified 2026-04-18, clarified 2026-04-19) says a fresh reader should be able to enter any single session without prior knowledge. S04 is the first session where that is beginning to strain. The Aelwen diptych *requires* #0003 context to feel earned. The Mira/Sigga/Harel craft-chain *requires* knowing Mira went to Stonfold. Hint 3 *requires* understanding what Reorx's Judgment refers to (Aelwen's forgings from earlier adventures). **A fresh reader at #0005 or #0006 will probably hit the accessibility wall.** The workshop should decide: (a) explicitly signal "campaign-readers-only from #0005 onward," or (b) invest more strongly in in-module glosses that recap prior-adventure context. Current session: still anthology-floor-safe, but barely.
