## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Portent-as-benediction (2nd instance — cluster)
Dimension affected: Mechanical Fairness / Character Agency / Surprise
What happened: Kessa expended her held Portent die on another PC's ritual roll for the second time. S03 Scene 7: Portent [14] replaced Grom's Religion 8 for Wrath-Coin destruction. S04 Scene 3: Portent [10] replaced Grom's Religion 9 for Reorx-rite. Both instances: Kessa's Divination-School mechanic applied to Grom's Religion checks, framed in-voice as an act of attention.
Why it matters: I-S03-03 logged this as a single-instance innovation. With 2 instances across 2 sessions, cluster trigger fires. Promote to sub-style candidate: "divination-as-PC-benediction" or integrate with sheet-deep-reader's sub-type watch (Kessa's pattern of sheet-driven other-PC-support).
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Silent-reception at campaign-scale (3-scene chain)
Dimension affected: Atmospheric Landing / Module Fidelity
What happened: Grom's Hint 3 (Reorx's Judgment) articulation-self-check failed at Scene 3 (Religion 8 vs DC 10). But silent reception counted per rubric v1.2. At Scene 5, Grom articulated the hearth-greeting realization aloud (silent-to-spoken transition). At Scene 6, Grom invoked Reorx-exile ritual — which required Hint-3 RECEPTION, not Hint-3 ARTICULATION. The silent reception persisted across 3 scenes and produced a campaign-permanent outcome.
Why it matters: Rubric v1.2 silent-reception anchor was written for within-scene silent-receptions (e.g., Mira's nod at Grom's benediction S03). Campaign-scale silent-reception-chains are a new sub-type — the PC holds an un-articulated understanding that drives downstream action. Possible v1.4 amendment: silent-reception chains across scenes.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Option-A-sufficient-where-Option-C-expected
Dimension affected: Character Agency
What happened: Module flagged Grom's Krun-fate decision as "strongly recommended Option C." In play, Grom's doubt-die (stand-between) + Hint-3-silent-reception + heuristic-order produced a character-consistent Reorx-exile invocation WITHOUT agent-spawn. Solo DM play was sufficient.
Why it matters: The ratified player-styles (sheet-deep-reader + craft-witness) may be *enabling* Option-A-sufficiency in situations that previously required Option C to deliver voice-distinction. This is not an Option C failure — it's an Option A evolution. Design signal: "Option C strongly recommended" can be downgraded to "Option C available" for PCs whose ratified styles deliver voice-distinction natively.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Dice-driven module-preferred outcomes
Dimension affected: Mechanical Fairness
What happened: Module's GM notes said "Jarek's flee is narratively preferred; the campaign wants him to live for #0006 or #0007." Scene 1 combat: Grom's Guiding Bolt rolled 24 damage against Jarek's 12 remaining HP; overshoot was -12 (not ≤-30 instakill); Jarek dropped unconscious and was stabilized by the party. The module's preferred outcome (Jarek alive) happened via dice-damage-overshoot, not GM-fiat.
Why it matters: S03 had the opposite case (Kessa killed Kann despite module-preferring escape). S04 had module-preferred-outcome delivered by dice. Both are legitimate; the pattern suggests campaign-continuity can be dice-trusting without always requiring author-intervention. Design principle candidate: "Module-preferred outcomes are guidance, not mandate. Dice + character heuristics are authoritative. Campaign adapts."
```
