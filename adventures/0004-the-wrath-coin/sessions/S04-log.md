# S04 LOG — The Wrath-Coin

## Session Summary

The Varduin Muster rode seven days southwest from Varduin to Gavrek's Anvil — Grom's ordination shrine, Duren Coalbeard's three-year-rite-day. Jarek Stoneforge staged a wagon-ambush on the Kharolis switchback; Thera caught the plait behind the axle; combat turned in one round (Aelric Smite dropped Merc1; Thera sneak-attacked Jarek; Grom's Guiding Bolt crit-hit 21 damage finished him at 0 HP). **Jarek was stabilized alive** — the adventure's single most important choice, as his testimony later made Scene 5 uncontested and Scene 6 diplomatically-soluble.

Sigga Coalbeard measured Grom at the shrine. He greeted the forge-hearth (*"Thou'rt seen"*), and she decided. Krun arrived on horseback mid-afternoon, named Grom *"cousin"* without explanation, and rode east.

At dawn Day 2, Grom performed the Reorx-rite. The roll stumbled (Religion 12 against DC 15 with advantage). **Kessa applied her held Portent [10] to replace Grom's d20; Aelric pre-cast Bless; the Bless-die added 2; Grom hit exactly 15.** The rite completed. **Hint 2 unlocked** — Vethrenn's warded margin surfaced in Kessa's commonplace book: *"We made the rose to hold. We did not try to release. I think release is possible. I do not know how."* **Hint 3 planted silently** — Grom received Reorx's Judgment (*"grief-coiled, not forged-with-intent; a coil can be uncoiled"*) through craft-witness channel without articulating aloud (self-check rolled 8 vs DC 10).

Sigga disclosed the half-kinship. Aelwen's apron-scrap surfaced in the archive — diptych with the #0003 journal page now locked. Scene 5's shaft was uncontested (Jarek captive); Thera Inv 22 on the cut beam was decisive. Grom realized the hearth-greeting had saved his life three years ago.

At Kruntharrak dusk Day 2: Grom rolled 20 CRIT on initiative, stood between Krun and the coin. Thera picked up the coin in its cloth (no skin-face attunement). Aelric invoked the kinship aloud; Krun's Voice-of-the-Thane save-DC collapsed to 10. Grom — acting on Hint 3's silent understanding rather than articulating it — invoked **Reorx-exile** on Krun. The ritual-binding landed at Religion 13 vs DC 12. **Krun was exiled, not killed. The golden path.**

Scene 7 at Gavrek's Anvil at dawn Day 3: Duren's hammer placed in the grave. Grom re-performed the rite (Religion 21) with the complete offering. **His three-year burn-scar stopped aching.** At Avel's smith-forge he destroyed the Wrath-Coin via Reorx-rite (Religion 21 advantaged by Bless); the six loaded rages dissipated to silence. Brother Laen's courier arrived mid-morning with 250 gp and Wena Laen's sealed note to Aelric — the #0005 hook.

### Scene 3.0 — Duren's Grave / The Reorx-rite (DAWN)

**Setup:**

> The east slope is still grey when the party and Sigga walk out from the shrine. The grave is unmarked stone, as Reorx-priest tradition demands — a forge-master's tomb is a forge his family can kindle if they need light. The stone is large enough to stand on. A coiled forge-chain lies across it. The chain is the shrine's second-holiest object: Gavrek's original; each link hand-forged in PC -135. Behind you the shrine's hearth begins its morning fire. Ahead of you the grey turns toward pink.

**Beats:**

Aelric takes an unprompted action before the rite begins. He steps forward — formal — and says: *"Good brother, may I offer the Measure's blessing on your work?"* Grom nods. **Aelric casts Bless L1 (concentration).** Targets: Grom (primary); Kessa; self. Aelric slot 2/3 spent.

Grom begins the rite. The silver-headed mallet in his left hand. Takes a long breath.

*"Reorx, father-smith, your son Duren Coalbeard comes to you cold. Three years in the forge of the ground. Three years of our memory's fire. We come to re-light what we cannot finish. Accept what we offer. Accept what we are."*

The first six strikes land clean. Between each, the coiled chain rings. Three breaths held between rings.

The seventh strike:

**🎲 Grom Religion DC 15 (advantage from prior hearth-greetings)** — `1d20+3 adv` → rolls=[9, 6] (adv → 9) mod=+3 **total=12**. **FAILS DC 15 by 3.**

Grom's hammer strikes the coil. The chain rings — but the ring is *wrong*. Flat. The rite is stumbling.

**Kessa steps forward.** She does not touch the stone. She speaks in Qualinesti (which Grom has never heard her speak aloud): *"Vethrenn. Help him. You wrote: *release is possible*. Let this be a releasing."*

**She expends her held Portent [10]** — declared to replace Grom's d20. The nine becomes a ten. Grom's total: 10 + 3 (Religion) + 2 (Bless die rolled) = **15**. **Exactly DC 15. Passes.**

The seventh strike re-rings — clean, this time. The chain unwraps from the stone of its own weight. Duren's grave accepts.

**🎲 Grom Religion self-check DC 10 to articulate aloud** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[5] mod=+3 **total=8**. **FAILS DC 10.** Grom knows it; cannot quote it. The understanding lands in his hands.

Per the P0 DM-note: **Silent reception counts.** Grom will act on this; he does not yet speak it. The party does not know what he received. But Grom's stance has changed — he is no longer the forge-priest who came to bury a master; he is a Reorx-priest who *understands something about Aelwen's forgings that he did not understand yesterday*.
