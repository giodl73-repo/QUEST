# Encounter — Thane Krun's Hall (Scene 6, climax)

**Location:** Kruntharrak mine-hold, round chamber in Krun's inner hall.
**Trigger:** Party arrives at Kruntharrak at dusk (Scene 6 begins).

## Adversaries (per Scene 6 branches)

### Full roster (Branch A, no kinship-reveal)

- **Thane Krun Ironhaft** (CR 1, 200 XP)
- **Jarek Stoneforge** (CR ½, 100 XP) — if alive + not surrendered
- **2 hall-guards** (CR ⅛, 25 XP each, hill dwarf Bandit variant)

### Reduced roster (Branch B, kinship-reveal active)

- Krun (political-armor degraded; "Voice of the Thane" at disadvantage)
- Jarek may hesitate to intercept on Blood-call (Insight DC 12 catches)
- Guards may hesitate (Intimidation or Persuasion DC 14 → stand down)

### Minimum roster (Branch C, full evidence + kinship + Jarek-confession)

- Krun alone (desperate)
- Possibly only the attunement-race scene (no combat required)

## Encounter XP

- **Branch A (full combat):** 200 + 100 + 50 = 350 base × 2 = **700 XP. Hard.**
- **Branch B (Krun + hesitant household, partial combat):** 200 + 100 = 300 base × 2 = **600 XP. Medium-Hard.**
- **Branch C (attunement race + diplomatic):** 200 XP narrative-resolution.

## Stats

### Thane Krun (CR 1, 200 XP)
- AC 15 (breastplate; rarely armored in hall-scene — assume AC 13 in robes), HP 40
- Str 10, Dex 11, Con 16, Int 14, Wis 14, Cha 15
- Saves: Con +5, Wis +4, Cha +4
- **Warhammer:** +2, 1d8 bludgeoning (unenthusiastic).
- **Voice of the Thane** (REACTION-conditional, 1/combat, per v1.3 persona-panel revision): when a creature within 5 ft makes a melee attack, Krun may use his reaction to declare the attack *"unlawful in Thorbardin hold."* Target Wis save DC 14 or the attack misses entirely. Kessa (Qualinesti, no Thorbardin jurisdiction-recognition) has advantage. **If kinship-reveal has been invoked:** save-DC drops to 10 (political authority collapsed). *Replaced earlier action-tax version per Gygax + Jaquays + Stuart panel.*
- **Blood-call (1/combat, reaction):** when reduced to 0 HP, summons Jarek or guards — next attack Krun would take is intercepted (at 1 HP instead).

### Jarek Stoneforge (if present)
- See `npcs/jarek-stoneforge.md`. AC 15, HP 30, multiattack.
- **Blood-call intercept for Krun:** reaction, once per combat.

### Hall-guards (CR ⅛, 25 XP)
- AC 13 (scale mail), HP 11. **Warhammer:** +3, 1d8+1. Darkvision 60 ft.

## The Attunement Race

If the party intends to stop Krun from reaching/holding the coin:

**Initiative roll:** all combatants + Krun.

- Krun's movement: 5 ft to the coin. Free action (5 ft within normal move).
- Grabbing the coin: Object Interaction (free) or action (DM discretion — 5e says object interactions are free per turn, so Krun can grab as a free action).
- Attunement: **1 round** — completes at the end of Krun's next turn.

**Party intervention options:**

1. **Disarm/Shove Krun (Aelric):** contested Athletics (Aelric +5 vs Krun +0) → coin flies; spends 1 round on floor.
2. **Thera pluck (Cunning Action + Thieves' Tools DC 15, Expertise +7):** plucks the coin from Krun's hand.
3. **Magic Missile at Krun's wrist/hand (Kessa):** 3d4+3 damage; not RAW-disarm, but DM may allow a disarm-effect on hit (Sleight of Hand DC 10).
4. **Guiding Bolt at the coin (Grom):** 4d6 radiant vs coin AC 15 (moon-silver is magic-resistant = half damage); the coin has 10 HP for mechanical purposes; ~2-3 hits would destroy it. Krun drops any damaged coin. **This is the cleanest path.**
5. **Ranged interdict (Thera hand crossbow +5):** target = Krun's hand (attack at disadvantage; −5 penalty for called shot); if hits, force Con save DC 10 or Krun drops.

## If attunement completes

Krun is attuned. Immediately rolls **Wis save DC 12** against the coin's re-keying:

- **Succeeds:** Krun's anger is keyed to his primary target (his father's killer, Kildan Heartstone, dead 22 years). The coin has no target; Krun is attuned but numbed. He sits. The coin holds him; he holds nothing.
- **Fails** (likely; Krun's mental state is fractured): the coin re-keys to the strongest currently-held grievance. Sigga's un-spoken rage at Krun himself is the strongest. **Krun's first action re-keys to Sigga → Krun stabs himself with the silver knife.** Narratively: he howls; the knife is in his own hand; he drops it. HP reduced by 1d4+2 (self-damage).

This triggers **Option C for Grom's decision** — see Scene 6.

## If attunement is prevented

Krun is subdued (either non-lethal, or surrendered). Options per Scene 6 — Reorx-exile (Grom's path), Thorbardin surrender, or kill.

## Guard behavior

- **Default (Branch A):** guards defend Krun. Engage melee PCs.
- **Branch B / C (kinship-reveal):** guards hesitate. Persuasion DC 14 → they stand down. Intimidation DC 14 → same.
- **If Jarek present AND confesses:** guards lose all will to defend; they withdraw.

## Loot

- **Krun's signet** (25 gp; Thorbardin political-signature).
- **Krun's waxed parchment** with Kildan Heartstone named (0 gp; Kessa's notes).
- **Hall coin-purse** (200 gp household treasury).
- **Silver trinket** (25 gp; Kessa's interest for scholarly).

## GM Notes

- **This encounter has extremely high branching.** Play the branch-match precisely — the party's path determines whether this is a Hard fight, Medium-Hard fight, or attunement-race + diplomatic.
- **Krun's Voice of the Thane is political, not magical.** If the party refuses to acknowledge Thorbardin authority (e.g., Aelric invokes Solamnic sovereign-state), the save is at disadvantage. Kessa (Qualinesti) has advantage on the save always.
- **Jarek's role as witness:** if Jarek confesses, the political dimension collapses entirely. Krun's power relies on household loyalty; Jarek's defection is the most potent move against it.
- **The Reorx-exile ritual (Grom):** requires Hint 3 received at Scene 3. The ritual is Grom-delivered; Kessa may coach; Aelric may witness. The binding is permanent.
- **Rubric v1.3:** The anchor-level moments (coin on cloth; attunement attempt; kinship invocation) have positional-redundancy — the coin is visible across multiple scenes of approach; the kinship is deliverable via Sigga (Scene 4) OR Grom-invokes (Scene 6); attunement outcome is documented across multiple fallback paths.
