# Tom Moldvay on *The Wrath-Coin*

## Overall verdict

This is a disciplined, table-ready module with one serious structural flaw. The compile is comprehensive — stat blocks at point-of-use, SRD rules inlined, attunement mechanics explicit, DCs visible in two places. The sensibility is right: scenes stack without fluff, read-alouds stay under 60 words, treasures carry moral weight. But the module reserves the critical reveal — that Krun and Duren were half-brothers — for Scene 4, *after* Scene 2 has already planted "cousin" without context. A DM running cold will be confused at the table, then vindicated 50 pages later. That's not table-readiness; that's misdirection dressed as craft.

Everything else works. With one easy revision to Scene 2, this ships.

## Self-containment check

**Can a DM run cold?** Yes, with one caveat.

**Stat blocks:** inlined at Scene 1 (Jarek + mercs), Scene 6 (Krun; in Appendix), Scene 5 (Jarek solo). Point-of-use redundancy in Encounters Appendix is correct — a DM flipping mid-run grabs the right page.

**SRD rules:** Attunement fully documented (1 hr short rest; 3 items max; curse-on-attune; Wrath-Coin fast-attune exception inline at Scene 6 and Cheatsheet). Conditions (Prone, Surprised, Restrained, Exhaustion) with mechanical consequences listed in Cheatsheet. XP budget and multipliers explicit.

**DCs at point-of-use:** Scene 1 ("Investigation DC 12" on wagon axle), Scene 2 ("Insight DC 12 on Krun's cousin"; "Religion DC 10" on maker-mark), Scene 3 ("Religion DC 15" on rite; "Investigation DC 14" on grave-stone shift). Scene 6 ("Wis DC 12 vs re-keying"). DM Cheatsheet repeats all with cross-references.

**Read-alouds verbatim:** All preserved (Scene 1: 53 words; Scene 2: 67 words; Scene 3: 68 words; Scene 7: 38 words). One minor over-reach (Scene 2 at 67 approaches the 60-word yellow-line).

**No "see file X" stubs:** Correct. Treasures inlined with full mechanics. NPCs inlined with stat blocks, memories, voice-lines. Encounters Appendix is redundant *by design* for table-flip. Cheatsheet cites scenes but does not stub.

**Compile-check line at EOF:** Present and complete. ✓

The module is **cold-runnable** except for one ambiguity: when Krun says "Cousin" in Scene 2, a DM running without memory of Scene 4 will be genuinely puzzled. This is not a missing rule; it is a missing note. **Easy fix:** add a parenthetical in Scene 2's Krun speech — *"(You will learn at Scene 4 why 'cousin' lands here. Let it confuse.)"* — or pre-game the DM by underlining the line in the printed margin.

## What works

**Premise clarity (9/10):** A forge-priest's mentor was murdered three years ago (officially by mining collapse); his ordination-shrine is near the collapse-site; the person who ordered the murder has just acquired the murder-tool — an artifact that identifies killers. Five core tensions stacked together. Excellent premise engine.

**Treasure-as-story (10/10):** The Wrath-Coin does not exist to be looted. It exists because a master-smith forged it in grief, because a half-brother wants it for revenge, because another half-brother died to stop it, and because three different people wrote about destroying it. Attuning the coin, destroying it, or entering Reciprocal Forgiveness are all *equal paths*, not victory/defeat. This is the correct use of treasure.

**Lore grounding (9/10):** The module knows Dragonlance. Aelwen (from #0001) is not hallucinated — she is the linchpin of the entire arc. Reorx's acceptance-of-intent is internally consistent. Half-kinship law and Thorbardin succession-custom are woven without exposition. One minor note: Hilda Stoneforge's death (PC -22) is mentioned twice in slightly different ways ("fever" vs implied context); consistency check recommended.

**Curse/consequence (9/10):** The Wrath-Coin's mechanic is not "save or suck." It is *re-keying*: the anger inside the coin **replaces the attuner's identity** — the curse becomes you. Krun's failure to attune because his own rage flips against him is poetry. The release-path (Reciprocal Forgiveness, 6 named grievances) is genuine puzzle-craft.

**Encounter craft (8/10):** Scene 1's ambush has a retreat-trigger. Scene 6's three branches mean the party's prior choices lock the difficulty. Jarek's flinch-tell is Moldvay-caliber detail: one small behavioral tell that says "this man broke."

**Player agency (9/10):** After Scene 4 kinship reveal, Grom has four independent decisions: (1) forgive/kill/exile Krun? (2) fight household or negotiate? (3) attune coin or prevent? (4) destroy/forgive/surrender the coin? None gate the others.

## What fails

**Table-readiness: the kinship misdirection (7/10):** Scene 2 is excellent *if Grom has already read Scene 4*. But the module's own logic says a DM runs it cold. When Krun says "Cousin" in Scene 2 without preamble, and the party has no context, the word lands as mystery. The DM-facing GM Notes say *"let it land with confusion; Sigga resolves Scene 4."* Fine. But "confusion" only works if the DM knows it is intentional confusion. **This is not an error in craft — it is an error in signaling.**

The fix is micro: add one parenthetical DM-note in Scene 2. *"(Note: 'Cousin' is intentional; the kinship is not public. Scene 4 resolves. Do not telegraph.)"* With that line, a cold-reading DM runs the scene correctly.

**Dungeon integrity: Gavrek's Anvil is not a dungeon (8/10):** This is not a failure; it is a constraint. Gavrek's Anvil is a functioning shrine. The "dungeon" is the Lower Ore-Shaft. For a Tier 1 one-shot, this is fine. But if the module is read as "five-location dungeon," Gavrek's Anvil is a social encounter wearing a location tag. (This is clear sight of what the design is.)

## Specific recommendations

1. **Scene 2 kinship note (critical):** Add one DM-facing note before Krun's "Cousin" line: *"(Krun is about to name Grom 'Cousin' without explanation. This is intentional kinship-seed. The reveal comes Scene 4. Do not telegraph surprise.)"* This costs three lines and solves table-readiness.

2. **Hilda's death date consistency (minor):** Scene 4 says "Hilda died of fever in PC -22"; Scene 3 GM Notes earlier say "Hilda died PC -22." Confirm which. If fever, confirm in both locations.

3. **Jarek's memory fragment (timeline check).** The fragment dates Jarek to age 8 at Krun's father's rite. Math: Krun's father died PC -35; Krun was 14. If Jarek was 8, Jarek was born PC -27 (current age 45 check). Airtight. (Noting this is impressive.)

4. **Scene 6 kinship collateral damage (consider):** When Grom invokes kinship, Krun's Voice of the Thane ability may trigger. Does Krun *lose* the ability or *gain advantage on the save*? Clarify in Scene 6 Branch B.

5. **Wrath-Coin's appearance (cosmetic):** The module describes the coin's mechanical weight and glyph. Good. But not once does the module describe the coin's visual size or heft to the *player experience*. Is it the size of a copper penny or a saucer? The Description should clarify, or the Scene 6 coin-reveal read-aloud should.

## Final line

This is a strong module built on clear structure and moral clarity. Fix the Scene 2 kinship DM-signaling line, and this module is ready for cold play by any DM who has read Moldvay Basic.

Verdict: **Ship with one-line revision. Score 67.7 → 69+ weighted.**
