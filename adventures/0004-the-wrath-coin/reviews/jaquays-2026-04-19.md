# Jennell Jaquays on *The Wrath-Coin*

## Overall verdict

You have built something most dungeons never achieve: a place that can be drawn, remembered, and navigated with player skill. The Wrath-Coin's three-zone topology — road spur, shrine cluster with vertical slope, and the 120-foot shaft descent — gives players geography they can exploit. The shrine itself rejects the dungeon frame entirely; it's a working religious space with NPCs who have priorities, which is exactly right. What impresses me most is the verticality with purpose: the shaft isn't window-dressing, it's evidentiary. Players descend to answer a specific question (how did Duren die), not to open the next door. That's craft.

Where this wavers is the shrine-spur-shaft-kruntharrak relationship. You've mapped it; you've given it multiple entrances and one genuine loop (shrine ↔ grave ↔ shaft). But the loop is mostly scenic — players can't use it tactically to bypass encounters or change the sequence without accepting narrative cost. The spur to Kruntharrak is mandatory, not optional. You're close to Jaquaysing this. You're not quite there yet.

## Topology analysis

The map can be drawn. Players can learn it.

**The three zones:**

1. **Kharolis road (Scene 1):** Staged wagon. Optional — Survival DC 12 alternate trail (half-day cost), Persuasion DC 18 at first contact, or dusk-arrival (Jarek won't fight in darkness). This is *good branching.* No false railroad.

2. **Gavrek's Anvil cluster (Scenes 2-4, 7):** shrine courtyard + nave + forge-hall + east slope with grave + eastern cellar archive. Five distinct spaces, three accessible from the courtyard. The shrine is not keyed like a dungeon — there's no forced sequence. Sacred space, not tactical space. **DM, do not treat this like a 5-room dungeon.**

3. **Lower Ore-Shaft (Scene 5):** 120 feet down. Spiral stair, single chamber at bottom. This is *linear*, but linear *in service of a question*. That's acceptable linearity.

4. **Kruntharrak (Scene 6):** Three rooms. Branching: stealth-bypass at night (DC 15 Stealth); frontal approach; parley at gate. Multiple approaches; same encounter. Access-variety, not outcome-variety.

**The loop:**

Shrine ↔ grave ↔ shaft. After Scene 3, players know grave is upslope from shaft. They *could* return from shaft to grave to shrine. But why? No encounter to avoid, no resource to recover, no treasure behind them. The loop is scenically present but tactically hollow.

**Verticality:** the 120-foot shaft is your strongest element. Descent via spiral stair, evidence-hunt at bottom, ascent back. The shaft has a *ceiling* (120 feet of time and resource cost). If Jarek seals it with mining charge, party faces Athletics DC 15 × 3 successes to dig out. The shaft creates consequence.

**Shortcuts:** none earned mid-session. Stealth-bypass at Kruntharrak saves gate-encounter only.

**Mappability:** I can draw this. Three zones connect via road and trail. No ambiguity. That's the bar you've met.

## What works

1. **The scene-order flexibility.** Archive (Scene 4) and Shaft (Scene 5) are swappable; party's priority determines sequence.

2. **Jarek as the hinge.** His flinch (Scene 1), his watching from the slope (Scene 3), his appearance or absence in Scene 5 — his presence or absence *cascades* through the module.

3. **Kinship as the hidden third rail.** Krun calls Grom "cousin" without explanation (Scene 2). Reveal happens Scene 4 or not at all. If revealed, Scene 6 Krun's Voice of the Thane at disadvantage; household guards hesitate; attunement-race becomes tactically possible. The party's *knowledge* changes the encounter.

4. **The attunement-race mechanic.** Krun 5 feet from coin, free object-interact, attunement completes end of next turn. Party has one round to intervene via five options. This is what attunement-races *should* feel like.

5. **Curse-over-attunement re-keying.** If Krun fails Wis DC 12 re-keying, coin re-keys to Sigga's rage, and Krun's first action is to stab himself. His fifty-year-planned victory turns into self-harm in round one. Magnificent.

6. **Table-readiness on DCs and stat blocks.** Every important save called out. Combat stat blocks complete. DM cheatsheet professional.

## What fails

1. **Dungeon integrity: the mandatory spur.** Kruntharrak is a three-room extension the party *must visit* to retrieve the coin and confront Krun. You can't bypass; can't shortcut; can't negotiate away the meeting. This is a veiled railroad. You have all the pieces for a true branch (burn the coin at the shrine; return to Varduin; surrender to Thorbardin council). But none of these alternatives avoid visiting Kruntharrak. They're "what do with coin" branches, not "do we go" branches.

2. **Player agency in Scene 6 framing.** Krun says: *"Come alone if you come."* In practice, party goes together. But the *framing* is an expectation of obedience. Better: Krun sends word *after the rite* via Jarek or messenger. Party decides whether to answer. That choice belongs to them.

3. **Encounter craft: Voice of the Thane ability.** Krun's once-per-short-rest action forces Wis DC 14 save or target loses action + disadvantage next attack. This is *save-based action tax*, not a tactical decision. Better: reaction-based ability — when a creature within 5 ft attacks, target Wis DC 14 or lose the attack. Make it *conditional* on enemy choice, not arbitrary on turn-order.

4. **Table-readiness: attunement-race under pressure.** Five intervention options all exist in text. At the table mid-combat, a DM will forget three and default to "initiative; Krun goes first." Pre-write as flowchart.

5. **Shrine cluster as "dungeon."** The module calls it "working religious space" — good instinct — then treats it like one. Scene 2-4 keyed and navigated as if the shrine were tactical. It's *social*. The distinction matters: in a dungeon you plan encounters in rooms; in a shrine you plan *conversations at focal points*.

## Specific recommendations

1. **Kruntharrak branching.** Add a *real alternate path* avoiding Krun's hall entirely. If party discovers Wrath-Coin's destruction-plan (Scene 5) and chooses to burn it at Gavrek's Anvil's forge, they never need to confront Krun. Courier: "The coin is destroyed. Your grievance has no tool." Scene 6 becomes optional.

2. **Reformat Scene 6 as flowchart.** Pre-write the attunement-race as a branching diagram:
   ```
   ATTUNEMENT RACE (Initiative)
   Krun acts first: Free interact (grab coin)
   → PC intervenes?
   → YES: [5 options listed with consequences]
   → NO: Krun attunement completes end-of-turn-2
           → Wis DC 12 re-keying [fail → self-stab / succeed → Krun attuned]
   ```

3. **Voice of the Thane revision.** Replace action-tax with reaction-conditional: *"When a creature within 5 feet attacks Krun with melee, he may use his reaction to force Wis DC 14 save or the attack misses entirely. Kessa has advantage."*

4. **Shrine focal-point reformat.** Instead of Scene 2 → 3 → 4 navigation, list the shrine's focal points (hearth, well, grave-stone, altar) and let Grom's reception and Sigga's openness determine what happens. Flatten the shrine into a *place with character* instead of a *building with rooms*.

5. **Add scene-flexibility note.** In GM Notes: "Scenes 2-5 can be reordered by party priority. Krun's invitation is for dusk Day 2, but party can visit shrine Day 1, return south, then approach Kruntharrak. Order flexibility increases player agency."

6. **Highlight Jarek's conditional presence.** Flag in every scene: "If Jarek surrendered Scene 1: [uncontested]. If alive: [optional encounter]. If dead: [full evidence unmarked]." Three different experiences, not just three statuses.

## Final line

You've built a module where the map teaches the story and the story justifies the map; remove the railroad into Scene 6, make Kruntharrak truly optional, and reformat the attunement-race for table-flip.
