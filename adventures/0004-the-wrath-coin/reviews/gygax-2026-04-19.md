# Gary Gygax on *The Wrath-Coin*

## Overall verdict

*The Wrath-Coin* is a stronger adventure than its predecessor because it remembers that dungeons are about choice and consequence. The spine — a murder, a magic coin, and a seventy-year grievance — is solid. The Lower Ore-Shaft (Scene 5) is a proper small dungeon: vertical, evidence-based, with a real boss encounter nested in it. The Wrath-Coin's curse (rage-re-keying) is mechanical and vicious, not cosmetic. And the adventure trusts its players: Scene 6's climax explicitly empowers one PC (Grom) to choose a third path (Reorx-exile) that the module-writer didn't pre-script, contingent on receiving a hint in Scene 3. That is the mark of a dungeon that respects player agency.

But it is less a dungeon and more a *quest with a dungeon inside it*. Gavrek's Anvil is a living shrine (well-keeper, young priest, pilgrims), not a trapped complex. Kruntharrak is a functioning mine-hold (20 retainers; guards). The shaft-descent is the only location where the topology and the pressure truly matter. The other scenes are rite, archive, negotiation. For Gygax weights, that is a liability: dungeon integrity is 1.3× everything else in my rubric. This adventure earns its score because *where it is a dungeon, it is a good one* — but it is not entirely a dungeon.

## What works

1. **Shaft combat with sealing mechanic (Scene 5).** Jarek's mining-charge seal (2d6 thunder + prone DC 13) forces a 15-ft Athletics check to dig out or Kessa's missile solution. This is the kind of encounter where the room shape matters more than HP attrition. Very good.

2. **Evidence triplication (Scene 5 sabotage).** Cut beam (DC 13 Stonecunning/Investigation), thunder-stone residue (DC 15), destruction-plan oil-cloth (DC 13), and optional Jarek confession create a murder-trial structure. No single roll fails the party. This is old-school redundancy done right.

3. **Wrath-Coin curse mechanics.** The re-keying (Wis DC 12; fail → anger shifts to strongest grievance in coin) means attunement is a trap *mechanically*, not thematically. If Krun attunes, he stabs himself (Sigga's un-spoken rage is stronger than his). That is elegant.

4. **Kinship as a conditional gate.** Scene 6 without kinship-reveal = Krun at full Voice-of-Thane strength (700 XP Hard). With reveal = Krun's political armor cracks; guards hesitate; ~600 XP Medium-Hard. The module explicitly branches on information the party earned in Scene 4. That respects player knowledge.

5. **Grom's three-path decision (Scene 6, Option C).** Kill Krun (blood-debt). Surrender him (Thorbardin execution). Or invoke Reorx-exile — a formal exile, ritual-sealed, unlocked only if Grom received Hint 3 (Scene 3 craft-witness delivery). This requires the module to trust both that the hint landed AND that the player will use it. It does.

6. **The hearth-greeting callback.** Grom was sent for water 15 min before Duren's charge fired. He stopped to greet a hearth at the shrine. The hearth-greeting saved his life. Scene 3 rite plants this; Scene 5 shaft-descent lets Grom realize it; Scene 7 re-interment lets him perform it and feel the 3-year burn-scar stop aching. That is craft-witness done at maximum precision.

7. **Shrine-pulse wandering table (d6/2 hrs).** Roll 3: *"Forge-hearth flickers. Grom (craft-witness) reads: 'something is listening in the hold east.'"* This is anchor-level ambient pressure; not a random encounter, but a sense. The table respects the shrine as a working sacred place.

## What fails

1. **Attunement-race feels bolted on.** The five intervention options (Aelric Shove, Thera Disarm, Kessa Missile, Grom Bolt, Thera hand-crossbow) are listed as a menu, not as emergent choices from the scene description. A player seeing those five options listed will feel they are reading a flowchart, not a dungeon. The race should emerge from the room (table's position, 5 ft to coin, Krun free to act). It does, but the module's presentation makes it feel like a mini-game with pre-set solutions.

2. **Gavrek's Anvil is not a dungeon; it is a location-hub.** The shrine is a working place (well-keeper, Vell, pilgrims). That is atmospherically right, but topologically it is a corridor: courtyard → forge / shrine → cellar → east slope → grave → shaft. There is no branching, no verticality, no loop that rewards backtracking. It is a quest-node, not a dungeon.

3. **Scene 6 negotiation frame.** Krun's opening offer (500 gp each, widow-pension, new forge-beam) is framed as a bribe-or-fight choice. But the module's own mechanics (Voice-of-Thane save; Kessa advantage; Krun at disadvantage if kinship invoked) suggest the encounter is meant to scale from a Persuasion problem to a combat problem to a negotiation-race. The module writer is trying to run a *social encounter* with *combat mechanics*. Mixing the two at the last-minute via Attunement-Race feels like the DM has lost control of tone.

4. **Two PCs get no spotlight.** Aelric has the kinship-invoke and Solamnic kin-witness protocol. Thera has the Disarm option in the Attunement-Race. Kessa has Vethrenn's margin unlock + journal-page diptych + Hint 2 lock. Grom is the entire character arc. If you are playing someone who is not Grom, Kessa, or Aelric, your only scene is Scene 1's road ambush (or Scene 6's combat). That is uneven spotlight.

5. **The "political negotiation" at Scene 6 misaligns with old-school values.** A 71-year-old thane in his own hall, with guards and a trained assassin present, should not be trying to *talk* his way out of a confrontation with a forge-priest who just proved he has murdered evidence. Thorbardin law says he executed. His only exits are kill the party, run, or fight until surrender. Offering a bribe is what a merchant does, not a thane in his own keep.

## Specific recommendations

1. **Collapse the shrine hub.** Gavrek's Anvil should be two scenes: Arrival (courtyard + Sigga's greeting) and Rite (grave + post-rite hammer-reveal). The archive (Scene 4) should be either in the shrine's cellar OR moved to Kruntharrak as the party's "evidence search" before confronting Krun. Current layout puts the party in the shrine for 12+ hours of in-fiction time. That is a lot of shrine.

2. **Rewrite Scene 6 opening as a clear combat setup.** Krun sits at his table, the coin in front of him. He has given the party no reason to expect negotiation. He ordered a man's murder. On the party's entrance, let Krun either (a) stand and draw his warhammer (combat-immediate) or (b) remain seated and make his opening move (coin-attune). The Attunement-Race becomes the center of conflict, not a side-effect of failed negotiation.

3. **Add a third-location scout opportunity pre-Scene 6.** Let the party approach Kruntharrak and see Krun's guards, the outer hall layout, the approach to his chamber. Let them make a choice: direct entrance, stealth, distraction. This gives the non-spotlight PCs a moment to shape the encounter before Grom's character-arc dominates.

4. **Clarify the wandering-pressure shutdown rule.** The module says: "Tables OFF during Scene 6." But Scene 6 is 20+ minutes of real-time play. Write it: "Wandering tables resume only if the party leaves Kruntharrak's hall to scout or retreat. If combat is joined or negotiation begins in the round-chamber, no wander-rolls until resolution."

5. **Expand Jarek's role in Scene 6 (if alive + not confessed).** The module says Jarek is present at the curtained doorway with Blood-call intercept ready. But if the party doesn't know Jarek is there, they won't target him in the Attunement-Race. Either place him visibly (seated at table with Krun) or let the party have a Perception check DC 13 to notice the shift in shadows at the curtain.

6. **Add a consequence to attunement by a non-Grom PC.** The module lists Grom's Option C as a subagent prompt. But what if Thera attunes first, to stop Krun? The Wrath-Coin's curse description says it re-keys to strongest grievance. Clarify: if a non-Krun PC attunes, coin still re-keys to strongest *of the six listed grievances* — it doesn't accept new ones until all six are released. Thera attuned would hear Sigga's rage directed at her. That is much worse for Thera than Krun.

## Final line

A solid adventure held back by indecision about whether it is a fight, a negotiation, or a heist; run the shaft descent cold and let the climax be what it wants to be — a test of the party's preparation, not a menu of pre-written outcomes.
