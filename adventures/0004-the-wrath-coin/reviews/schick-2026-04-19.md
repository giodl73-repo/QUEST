# Lawrence Schick on *The Wrath-Coin*

## Overall verdict

This is signature-room work married to signature-treasure work. The Wrath-Coin is the protagonist — not just plot-relevant, but genuinely a character with its own intention (Aelwen's redemption-act, carried forward), and the adventure knows this. The module doesn't drift into padding; every scene tilts the coin's story forward. Krun's hall at Scene 6 is where this crystallizes: a thane across from the object he commissioned fifty years ago, and the object is *listening* to his own rage. That's earned weirdness. The room exists because the treasure is a character and the room proves it.

Where it nearly stumbles is the distribution of authority. Too many scenes depend on Grom, and if Grom doesn't lean into craft-witness reception, the pacing splinters. But that's not a flaw — it's a bet on the player. Schick-era modules always bet on the player. This one asks: *do you know what inside-curve means?* If you don't, you're playing someone else's game. If you do, you're in.

**Shippable.** Minor refinements suggested, but nothing structural breaks.

## What works

**The treasure is a character.** This is where the module earns its voice. The Wrath-Coin doesn't just sit on a table waiting to be picked up. It *listens*. It knows Aelwen's intention — emptied and destroyed, not held. It has six grievances already loaded, one of them Aelwen's *own rage at her order's constraints*. That's personhood. The coin is more alive than Krun before he reaches for it.

**Aelwen's diptych (journal page + apron-scrap).** Seeing Aelwen's own writing twice — first her self-doubt (#0003: *"I should have forged an ingot"*), then her redemption-resolve (#0004: *"This will be my good work"*) — this is how you anchor a signature artifact across a campaign. The player doesn't just find an object; they *find her intention* in two fragments separated by a hundred pages of play.

**The Reorx-exile path.** Not every module gives the spotlight PC a *third* moral choice between blood-debt, state execution, and sacred exile. Hint 3 unlocks this: *"A coil can be uncoiled."* This is a Schick move — the solution is thematic, not mechanical. Grom doesn't roll to exile Krun; Grom *pronounces* it because he understands what Aelwen understood. Craft-witness as decision-hinge.

**Krun's attunement failing against himself.** If Krun reaches the coin, the curse re-keys his rage *from* his father's murder *to* Sigga's unspoken rage at him for Duren's death. He stabs himself. Fifty years of preparation, undone by the thing he was trying to use. That's not a death; that's a tragedy. It earns being in the book.

**The hearth-greeting saves Grom's life (and he doesn't know it for three years).** Duren sends him for water 15 minutes before the charge fires. Grom stops to greet a hearth. *This* is what keeps Grom alive — not a save-DC, not a dodge, but a moment of *faith in Reorx's order*. When Grom realizes this at Scene 5, it lands hard. Craft-witness in its most painful register.

**Sigga's three-year silence.** She hides Duren's hammer the night before he dies on his request. Then she *waits three years* to decide if Grom is who should have it. She's not a quest-giver; she's a widow testing a forge-priest.

## What fails

**Grom-centric Option C.** The spotlight PC gets the moral apex. That's WPM — Blackrazor's room is shaped around the fighter who wants to wield it. But this module is four PCs. Option C should spawn for any PC who makes a major decision in Scene 6, not Grom alone. If Thera disarms the coin or Kessa identifies the sixth grievance, their call should matter equivalently. Minor — the module flags this as flexible — but Grom carries 60% of the emotional weight.

**Craft-witness reception is silent (Hint 3).** When Grom receives Reorx's Judgment at the 7th strike of the chain, he *knows* the meaning but cannot articulate it. This is elegant design — it privileges the player's trust in their PC's knowledge. But at the table, a DM might read this as "nothing special happened here." One clarifying sentence — *"Grom understands this the way he understands stone or fire: wordlessly, with absolute certainty"* — would save confusion.

**Attunement-race intervention options vary in mechanical clarity.** Some (Shove, Disarm, Guiding Bolt) feel equally valid. Some (Magic Missile on wrist) require DM interpretation. A quick note — *"DM discretion on Sleight of Hand DC 10 to drop from closed hand"* — clarifies without closing options.

**Player agency at Scene 3 (Hint 3).** If Grom doesn't roll a nat-20, he gets Hint 3 *silently*. The other PCs don't know he received anything. This is intentional, but creates a knowledge-gap. Kessa can lock with Hint 2 aloud; Grom might not articulate Hint 3. If the DM doesn't prompt, the synthesis moment — the *campaign-scale* cross-receiver moment flagged in GM Notes — might misfire.

**Scene 1 road ambush is conditional-skippable, but no replacement scene.** If party takes SE trail and rolls 12+ on Survival, they bypass Jarek entirely. But Jarek's post-murder guilt is character-rich material. A brief line — *"If Scene 1 skipped, Jarek arrives Scene 6 unsummoned, guilt-driven"* — would tighten the narrative redundancy.

## Specific recommendations

1. **Add one sentence to Scene 3 after Hint 3 delivery:** *"Grom knows this the way he knows stone or fire — without words, with absolute certainty. The knowing lands in his hands."*

2. **Flag Attunement-Race as a DM-managed flow.** The five intervention options fire at different points in a 1-round-attune sequence. Add a mini-timeline to prevent the *"can we do this before he picks it up?"* loop.

3. **One-liner for Scene 1 skips:** *"If party bypasses Scene 1 via SE trail, Jarek still appears Scene 6 as Krun's backup (guilt-driven to re-engage). Scene 5 remains uncontested."*

4. **Clarify Hint 3 prompting for solo DMs.** Add: *"If the player does not articulate Hint 3 aloud, you may prompt at Scene 5 or Scene 6: 'Grom, what does Reorx's word to you mean for this moment?' Wait for their answer. It unlocks the Reorx-exile path."*

5. **Minor: Krun's stat block.** Voice of the Thane lists *"Kessa advantage (Qualinesti)."* A parenthetical — *(she has Solamnic kin-witness training)* — would clarify.

## Final line

*The Wrath-Coin* is what happens when you love a treasure enough to build the entire adventure around it, and you trust your players enough to make the moral choice a *third* option that requires thematic reading, not min-maxing. Ship it.
