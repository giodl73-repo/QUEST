# Premise — The Wrath-Coin

**Campaign position:** #0004 of 7 in the **Moon-Silver Cycle**. Emotion: **Rage**. PC spotlight: **Grom Ironhand** (Duren retrieves).

**Logline:** A forge-priest returns to his master's grave to perform the three-year rite — and discovers the mine-collapse that killed his master was no collapse. The hammer that should be buried with Duren is not. What Duren was secretly forging — Aelwen's fourth moon-silver object, a coin that holds the rage of everyone who has ever wronged the bearer — is in a Thorbardin elder's vault, and he intends to use it this week.

## Setting

**Place:** **Gavrek's Anvil**, a Reorx-shrine and minor outer hold of Thorbardin in the **Kharolis foothills** (~4 days' ride southwest of Crystalmir; ~7 days from Varduin). Duren Coalbeard's grave is on the shrine's east slope. Thane Krun Ironhaft's mine-hold — **Kruntharrak** — is three hours' walk further east, a proper Thorbardin outer-hold with gates, wardens, and a mine of its own.

**Time:** Late Age of Might, **PC 20** (approximately two weeks after S03 — Mira and Harel's departure-to-Stonfold happened; the party rode home to Varduin; Brother Laen's courier arrived with word of a summons to Gavrek's Anvil). The Cataclysm is 20 years away.

## Hook

Two pieces of correspondence arrive at Varduin within three days:

1. **Sigga Coalbeard** — Duren's widow, now 58, the shrine-keeper's wife at Gavrek's Anvil — writes to Grom via a traveling priest. The letter says: *"The three-year rite for Duren is at the next moon. Come if you can. I have something I need you to see. — Sigga."* The *something* is not named.

2. **Brother Laen** delivers a second letter at Varduin: a **receipt from Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith's sales ledger** — the one Kessa transcribed in #0003 — identifying **Thane Krun Ironhaft**, Thorbardin Palanthan elder and mine-magnate, as the buyer of an **unclaimed moon-silver commission** from Aelwen's workshop decades ago. Laen says: *"We do not know what Krun commissioned. The commission was never picked up. The artifact was never finished by Aelwen. But Krun is still alive, still a Thane, and Sigga Coalbeard's letter arriving in the same week is not a coincidence I am willing to sleep on."*

The party rides southwest.

**Grom has two reasons to accept.** The three-year rite is a personal obligation he has been unable to perform until now. And Sigga's letter — *"something I need you to see"* — means Duren's grave is not resting cleanly.

## Central Artifact

**The Wrath-Coin** — palm-sized, one ounce, moon-silver. One face bears Aelwen's rage-glyph: a fist inside a flame, Vaenshold-provincial engraving. The other face is polished to full convergence — the only one of Aelwen's six forgings with *fully* converged polish. She finished this one.

**Effect on holding:** when pressed to bare skin, the Wrath-Coin delivers to the holder the *rage of every person who has ever been wronged by the holder*. Not the holder's own rage. The rage of *others* directed at the holder. A chorus of grievance. First-time holders drop the coin after 1-2 seconds.

**Presence / Desire:** the coin is *heavier than an ounce should be*. Those who lift it expecting a coin are surprised by the weight. Those who lift it expecting an artifact are surprised by how *familiar* the weight feels — like something they should have been carrying already.

**Rarity:** Very Rare · attunement required (1 round) · no kin prerequisite.

**Benefits (attuned):**
- Advantage on Intimidation checks against any creature whose grievance is held in the coin.
- Rage-driven attackers (including Berserkers, frenzy effects) deal −1 damage per hit against the bearer.
- Once per long rest: the bearer may name a specific person and sense the direction + rough strength of that person's rage toward them, within 1 mile.

**Curse (attuned):** the bearer **cannot distinguish justified anger from inherited or projected rage**. When angry in any scene, the bearer rolls a DC 12 Wisdom save. On fail, they act on the *strongest grievance currently held in the coin* — which may be from a stranger or decades-dead wronged person, not the bearer's own situation. The PC's anger is no longer their own; it is *curated*.

Additionally: the bearer gains **1 level of exhaustion per 30 days** that persists until attunement ends OR an act of *specific, named forgiveness* is performed toward one of the people whose rage the coin holds.

**Ending:**
1. **Reorx-shattering rite** at forge ≥ 1,800°C, Religion DC 15 (same as Mirror). Destroys the coin; attuned curse ends; exhaustion persists until next long rest.
2. **Reciprocal forgiveness** — the bearer must identify a specific person whose rage is in the coin AND name the wrong that person suffered AND offer amends (symbolic or material). The named person need not be alive or present. If the named rage is truly-held in the coin, the coin's weight drops by 1/6th (it holds six primary rages at the time of this session; more can accumulate). When all rages are named and released, the coin is *empty* — a ordinary moon-silver ounce. Aelwen forged it for this, not for containment.

Full artifact file: `treasures/wrath-coin.md`. Manifest: `treasures/wrath-coin-manifest.md` (rubric v1.3 — positional-redundancy applied).

## Themes

- Rage is inherited when it is not named.
- What a forge-master was secretly working is a form of confession.
- Thorbardin's elders are not above the grudges of the outer holds.
- Duren's death was not what Grom was told. For three years, Grom has been grieving a fiction.

## The Real Decisions

Four overlapping:

1. **Does Grom forgive Thane Krun, kill him, or surrender him to Thorbardin justice?** Krun ordered Duren's murder (Scene 5 evidence confirms). Grom's PC-sheet decision order is "mend > whole > honor > own debts." Duren is his debt. This is a spotlight-PC moment — **Option C strongly recommended**.
2. **Does a PC attune the Wrath-Coin?** Attunement is 1 round with a "burning warm" signal — the holder can drop it. Each PC has a grievance the coin could target: Grom (Krun); Aelric (Kann's Istaran client Felor, indirectly for Varran's death-cell); Thera (Nenn her father, unresolved); Kessa (Vethrenn's silence, which is not a wrong but may read as one).
3. **Does the party shatter the Coin via Reorx-rite or attempt the reciprocal-forgiveness un-making?** Un-making requires naming six rages. The party knows one (Krun's grudge). The others are obscured.
4. **Is Thane Krun's household attacked or negotiated with?** Krun is a Thorbardin Thane with his own guard and holding. A full assault triggers a diplomatic incident. Negotiation requires offering something.
