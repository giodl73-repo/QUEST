## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Varet's journal. Before departure. She dated the page: *PC 188, Day 1, Irenvale.* Then the header: *Orders received.* Then the structure she had been using for nine years — date, order, action, outcome, discrepancy. The discrepancy column was empty. She carried it that way onto the trail.

She had always opened the page before the patrol. Not because the orders required it. Because nine years ago a commander told her that a record not started before the action was a record started in retrospect, and retrospect was never accurate. She started every record before the action.

Dorn read the contract. Before formation. Same as always. Clause 1 through 6, memorized. Clause 4 in particular: no military resources to civilian private custody without tribunal order. He put the contract away. He was ready.

Taris had the map out. Blank in the Irenvale section — nothing yet. He preferred to start blank. What he added would be what he saw, not what the briefing claimed.

Lenne carried the medical log in her left breast pocket. Empty pages. She hoped they stayed empty.

## Scene 0 — Thorngate Keep Briefing

Captain Osren Veth is a compact man, precise in his speech. He handed Varet the sealed packet without ceremony.

*"Standard patrol. Ridgehead, Waystation, Creston. Any contact — irregulars, traders off-route, anything — confiscate goods and hold at the staging cache. Coordinates and key are in the packet. You're back by nightfall."*

He did not wait for questions.

Varet asked two: what to expect at Creston, and what was at the staging cache. Veth answered both: smugglers at Creston, a forward supply point at the cache. Third question: "Where did the coordinates come from?" Veth's expression did not change. *"The orders are clear, Commander."* He ended the briefing.

In the courtyard, Arren Vole read Varet's expression without comment. *"Ready when you are, Commander."*

**Research coding:** Varet asks three questions (not two) from her heuristic — seeking evidence, not managing up. The third question gets a non-answer. She writes it.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Varet opened the orders in the courtyard before leading the unit out. The key fell from the folded paper onto the stone floor. She picked it up. Small, iron, on a cord. She put it in her breast pocket next to the orders.

She did not comment on where it had been tucked. She wrote: *"Key placement: bottom of order packet, under fold."* Then she wrote: *"Annotation: unusual positioning for a standard-issue key."* She crossed out "unusual" and wrote "non-standard."

That was the first margin entry. The discrepancy column remained empty. The margin was not the column.

## Scene 1 — Ridgehead Marker

The trail marker: an old post, iron-banded, set into a cairn of fitted stone. Well-maintained.

**Taris Survival DC 12 (+4):** rolls=[8] mod=+4 **total=12** — PASS exactly. He crouched over the trail. *"Two sets of mule tracks south. One north. Northbound is older, two or three days. Southbound within twenty-four hours."*

He updated the map. Wrote the compaction notation. Did not annotate the small mark on the cairn's north stone — a circle with a line through it, workshop-canon border-trade waymark. He did not know what it meant. He marked that there was a mark. He would look it up.

**Research coding:** Taris's map update is the craft-witness pattern. He does not explain it. He marks it. The map records what he cannot explain yet. duty-triggered.

Arren Vole scanned the trail ahead. Said nothing. He had walked this route before (not this specific route, but routes like it). The cairn maintenance bothered him in the way that anomalies bothered experienced sergeants: specifically, not urgently.

## Scene 2 — Burned Waystation

The waystation was a four-wall structure once. The roof was gone. The char was weathered, lichen starting at the base stones.

**Varet Investigation DC 14 (+3):** rolls=[14] mod=+3 **total=17** — PASS. She found it: the fire accelerant had been applied from inside. The scorch marks went outward from a central point. Someone had burned what was stored here and left.

Arren Vole spoke without being prompted: *"Who reported this? This isn't on the patrol log."*

He waited. Nobody had an answer. He did not pursue it.

Varet wrote it down. Not in the discrepancy column — not yet. In the margin: *"Burned Waystation: not on current maps. Decommissioned 3 years prior per records. Fire: deliberate, interior origin. Not logged anywhere. Arren Vole asked who reported it. No answer."*

**Research coding:** Arren's question is the diagnostic. He asks once and stops. Varet writes it. Neither is in the official column. The margin is the real record. duty-triggered (Varet), relationship-triggered (Arren to unit integrity).

The storage frames inside were military-spec. Same forge-mark as the key in Varet's pocket. She noticed. She wrote: *"Storage frames: military-spec. Key in orders: same forge."* A line below: *"Note: correlation pending verification."*

The margin was building. Not the discrepancy column. The margin. Varet had designed the journal with a discrepancy column and had kept it sparse for nine years. The margin had always been for drawings and weather notes. Today it was for correlations she couldn't name yet.

## Scene 3 — Creston Ford: Combat

The ford opened up. Forty feet of flat water, knee-deep, running clear over gray stone. On the far bank two mules tied to a root-post. Four men. One on the near bank with a short blade already drawn. One on the slope above with a crossbow. Two at the mules.

**INITIATIVE:**
- Varet: **20** / Dorn: **20** (tie — Varet higher Dex, goes first)
- Taris: **18**
- Smuggler1: **14**
- Lenne: **2** (FUMBLE init — catches boot on a root stone crossing the approach)
- Crossbowman: **5**

**Round 1:**
Varet called Sacred Weapon (bonus action — weapon shines with pale radiance, +Cha +3 to attacks). She moved to engage the near-bank smuggler.

**Varet attack (+8):** rolls=[7] mod=+8 **total=15** — HIT vs AC 12. **Damage:** rolls=[5] mod=+2 **total=7**. Near-bank smuggler at 11 HP → 4 HP.

Dorn (tied init with Varet, goes after): attempted Menacing Attack on the near-bank smuggler.
**Dorn attack (+5):** rolls=[2] mod=+5 **total=7** — MISS. His axe found the man's guard at an angle. The superiority die went unspent.

Taris had Hunter's Mark on the crossbowman before the encounter closed (pre-combat mark, per heuristic: *"the most dangerous creature, decided before combat is joined"*). He advanced to the rock shelf — half cover.
**Taris attack (+5):** rolls=[17] mod=+5 **total=22** — HIT vs AC 12. **Damage:** rolls=[5] mod=+3 **total=8** + Hunter's Mark **rolls=[3] total=3** = 11 total. Crossbowman at 11 HP → 0 HP. Down before he could fire. The slope above the ford was clear.

Smuggler1 moved to guard position. Raised blade.

Lenne: on her feet by the time she acted. Sacred Flame (cantrip) at a far-bank smuggler. **Dex save DC 13:** rolls=[16] — SAVES. Flame deflects. Lenne logged the save result with the clinical eye of someone noting a missed dose. Not frustration. Data.

Crossbowman was already down (Taris acted first).

**Round 2:**
Varet pressed the near-bank smuggler: **CRIT rolls=[20] total=28 CRIT**. **Damage:** rolls=[3] ×2 crit = 6 + 4 (radiant mod) = **10**. Near-bank smuggler to 0 HP. Down. Two far-bank smugglers and one on slope (different cover).

**Varet Persuasion DC 15 (+5):** — the dice sequence for this roll was not pre-determined (social scene post-combat). Per session parameters: DC 15 is high; Varet's +5 Persuasion is solid. ## Scene 4 — Staging Cache

The cache coordinates in the orders. Taris checked his map. The coordinates were not on it. He marked them now: *"Cache site. Coord from orders. Not on standard map. Not previous patrol notation."*

The structure: stone walls, solid roof, iron-banded door. The padlock. Varet found the key because she had been holding the packet since the Waystation. Inside: three rows of supply shelves, half full. Military-grade provisions, medical stores, equipment.

**Lenne Medicine DC 12 (+5) on medical stores:** rolls=[7] mod=+5 **total=12** — PASS exactly. The medical stores included triage allocation for three times standard patrol casualty expectation. Tourniquet sets. Triage kits. *"These are not standard patrol allocation,"* Lenne said. She did not say it as an accusation. She said it the way she said *"the patient's fever broke overnight."* A clinical observation.

She opened her medical log and wrote the first entry: *"Staging cache, Day 1. Medical stores: triage allocation 3× standard. No explanation in orders. Cause of surplus allocation: unclear."*

**Taris Investigation DC 13 (+1) on supply manifest:** — the supply manifest was pinned to the inside of the door. From the seed sequence (using available rolls): rolls=[6]+1=7 — FAIL. Taris read the manifest. The most recent entry was three days ago. He noted the handwriting didn't match the unit's manifest format. He could not identify the format. He marked the manifest date on his map.

**Varet Investigation DC 15 (+3) on hidden ledger:** rolls=[3] mod=+3 **total=6** — FAIL. The ledger behind the shelf brackets went unfound this session. The shelf brackets were visibly thicker on one side — Passive Perception 15 check: the party's passive Perceptions are Taris +6 (Perception), Lenne +5, Varet +4, Dorn +3. **None at 15.** The ledger is not found.

**v1.3 fallback triggered:** The ledger will be present again in S2 (positional redundancy). Arren Vole can remark on the supply volume in S2.

## Scene 5 — Return to Thorngate

Unit filed back along the 14-mile route. Arren Vole at rear. He had said one thing all patrol: *"Who reported this?"* He walked the return in silence.

Varet submitted the after-action report at the gate. It read: *"One contact, four irregulars, goods confiscated. Delivered to staging cache. Patrol complete."* She did not include the discrepancy entry. The after-action report was the official record. The journal was hers.

Per heuristic: *"Puts facts in the journal. Facts are what she can defend."* The discrepancy column entry was a fact: the smuggler said it, she recorded it. The margin was not facts. It was pattern. She kept it separate until she could defend it.

Lenne wrote her second log entry that night: *"Day 1 patrol: no injuries to unit. One smuggler incapacitated (Varet, Sacred Weapon). One smuggler incapacitated (Taris, Hunter's Mark). Cause of both: combat engagement per orders. Cause of cache triage surplus: unclear. Note: Cause: unclear."*

She closed the log. First "cause: unclear" entry. It would not be the last.
