# Fresh-Face Review — C6-S1

**Score: 70 / 80**

## Praise

This session is accessible to a reader arriving cold. The setting — a military unit on a mountain patrol — requires no prior reading. The mechanics (a combat at a river ford, a locked building, basic medical observation) are clear without explanation. The characters are legible from their first actions: the commander documents everything, the medic counts supplies, the ranger reads tracks, the veteran reads his contract before departure.

The toll system (smugglers paying into a supply cache through official-looking channels) is a concept the average reader will understand immediately. The session does not require knowledge of Solamnic political structure to understand that something wrong is happening.

## Friction

The Solamnic military terminology ("7th Expeditionary," "border irregularity corridor," "Commander of the Sword") is used without gloss. The fresh face will understand "military unit," "patrol," and "commanding officer" — but the rank structure above Varet (where is she in the hierarchy?) is unclear. Who is above Veth? What does "Commander of the Sword" mean? The Accessibility Policy says first-use terms should be glossed. These weren't.

## Recommendation

Add one line from Varet or Arren early in S2 that makes the hierarchy legible: "Veth reports to field command, and field command reports to the Sword." Or have Dorn refer to the contract's authority structure once in passing. A single gloss is sufficient — the campaign reader can assume it; the fresh face needs the line.
