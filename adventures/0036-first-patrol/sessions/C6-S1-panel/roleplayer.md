# Roleplayer Review — C6-S1

**Score: 72 / 80**

## Praise

Varet crossing out "unusual" and writing "non-standard" is the session's best character moment. The distinction is professional, not emotional — a precise woman choosing precise words for a record she will have to defend. It reveals nine years of field-journal practice in a single editorial action. The margin notes (not the column structure) as the reception layer is also exactly right for this character: she uses the column for what she can defend; she uses the margin for what she hasn't yet named. The gap between them is the character.

Arren's one question and his silence is strong. Twelve years of service and that is the diagnostic — a single question pointed at the absence of a record. He does not pursue it because he already knows the answer and is waiting to see if his commander does. That is a relationship expressed entirely through restraint.

## Friction

Lenne had the weakest character presence this session. The "cause: unclear" notation is correct and consistent with her heuristics, but the session gave her very little to do beyond heal and miss a Sacred Flame. The module design is appropriate (baseline, no injuries), but the Roleplayer lens notes that Lenne's arc is built on accumulation that requires something to accumulate FROM. One "cause: unclear" entry on a medical surplus (not an injury) is thin foundation for the arc that will fire in S5. Recommend Lenne have one additional observational beat in S2 that is NOT medical — something she notices and doesn't write down, which becomes the thing she wants to write down later.

## Recommendation

Taris's map-as-character is working: blank to first mark, no commentary. The FUMBLE (arrow wide) was the right kind of dice moment for him — he does not express frustration. He notes the mule shied. He adjusts his footing. He fires again. That behavioral consistency under failure is character. Continue building the map-update ritual as a recurring beat.
