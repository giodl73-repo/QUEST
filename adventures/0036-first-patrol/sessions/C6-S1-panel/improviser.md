# Improviser Review — C6-S1

**Score: 70 / 80**

## Praise

The Taris FUMBLE (arrow wide, mule shied) was the session's cleanest improvised moment. The DM didn't need to narrate a special consequence — the mule shying and the smugglers flinching was a natural physical effect, and the fact that the combat continued without drama was correct. A FUMBLE that produces a brief environmental effect and then moves on is better than a FUMBLE with a special penalizing consequence.

The Persuasion parley post-combat (Varet initiating) was a player-style improvisation: the module didn't require the party to try to get information from the surrendered smuggler. Varet tried it. It paid off. The result (toll revelation) was the module's intended discovery path, but the player choice to pursue it with Persuasion (rather than Investigation of the goods) was live. Good.

## Friction

The session has limited improvisation space by design — baseline sessions establish the world before surprises are introduced. However, the margin-note format (Varet crossing out "unusual" for "non-standard") could benefit from being a recurring space for the DM to offer small factual invitations. What if Varet had noticed something in the waystation that wasn't in the module? A half-burned document, a child's boot, something that adds texture without altering the lore? Baseline sessions with no anomalies can still have texture.

## Recommendation

Give Taris one observation per session that he makes BEFORE the rest of the party does. His passive Perception (+6) should fire proactively. In this session he noted the trail compaction and the waymark — but both came from prompted checks. One proactive Taris observation per session would establish the craft-witness pattern faster.
