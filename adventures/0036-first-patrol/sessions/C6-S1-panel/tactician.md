# Tactician Review — C6-S1

**Score: 68 / 80**

## Praise

The combat layout at Creston Ford was clean and taught: crossbowman on elevated ground (first-mover priority), two mules providing cover (three-quarters, not half — creates real incentive to go around), ford as difficult terrain punishing a straightforward advance. The tactical decision the module built for — crossbowman first vs. near-bank blade first — was real, and Taris made the correct call (Hunter's Mark pre-combat, elevation target). The unit soldiers as directed assets (Varet bonus action command) gave the commander meaningful tactical agency without becoming a force-multiplier that trivialized the encounter.

## Friction

The near-bank smuggler was at 4 HP after Round 1 (7 damage from Varet's Sacred Weapon hit, 11 HP smuggler). Then Varet CRIT in Round 2 (10 damage) for the down. But Dorn's miss (7 vs AC 12) removed a potential double-hit that would have downed the near-bank smuggler in Round 1. The result: the combat ran an extra round, which is fine. The friction: Dorn's Menacing Attack was the right call (Wis save DC 13 — the target failed, would have been frightened on a hit) but the miss negated both the damage and the condition. Battle Master maneuvers on a miss lose the damage but the Menacing Attack's save still triggers — this needs to be handled consistently. In this log the Wis save (3, FAIL) fired but had no effect because the attack missed. **Ruling note:** Menacing Attack adds superiority die to damage ON A HIT, then the target makes the Wis save. On a miss, no damage AND no condition. The log's save roll is logged but technically shouldn't have been called (target wasn't hit). Minor inconsistency. Does not affect outcome.

## Recommendation

Consider a Disarming Attack option for the parley path: if Dorn Disarming Attacks the near-bank smuggler (rather than Menacing), the dropped weapon creates a visual that the party is not there to kill. This changes the Persuasion dynamic. Flag for future combats involving surrender paths.
