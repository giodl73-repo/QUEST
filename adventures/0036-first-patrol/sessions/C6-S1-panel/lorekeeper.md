# Lorekeeper Review — C6-S1

**Score: 70 / 80**

## Praise

The Irenvale Crossing is established cleanly as a specific geography with workshop-canon grounding (Kharolis border region, PC 188). The "border irregularity corridor" classification by command is a precise institutional term that implies a bureaucratic decision to not-see. The patrol route's 14-mile circuit is walkable and mappable — this is a place that feels like it has topography. The border-trade waymark (circle with line) on the Ridgehead cairn is appropriately obscure — Taris marks it without knowing what it means, which is correct for a first session.

The Solamnic military structure (Thorngate Keep, Captain Veth, the patrol route classifications) is presented without requiring deep lore knowledge. The journal structure itself — the professional form — implies the institutional context without needing a lecture on the Solamnic Knighthood or the Kharolis border politics.

## Friction

The Burned Waystation's three-year gap (decommissioned, then burned — without relation) needs careful tracking across the campaign. The lore implication is that the waystation was active in an unofficial capacity between its decommission and its burn — which means someone used it for three years after it was officially gone. That timeline matters for the Ossian network's history. Recommend flagging this as a lore-consistency check point: when was the Irenvale toll system established? The answer should predate the waystation fire.

## Recommendation

The forge-mark on the storage frames and the cache key is an excellent physical lore detail. Workshop-canon should formalize the "Solamnic military supply depot forge-mark" as a distinct identifier used across S1-S6 wherever supply equipment appears. It creates a physical through-line the party can follow without needing a DM announcement.
