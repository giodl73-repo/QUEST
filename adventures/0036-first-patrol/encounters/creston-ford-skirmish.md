# Creston Ford Skirmish

## Setup

4 Border Smugglers (Bandit stat blocks) vs. party of 4 (L3) + up to 4 unit soldiers (directed by Varet).

## CR / XP Budget

- 4 × Bandit (CR 1/8, 25 XP each) = 100 XP base
- Adjusted for terrain (difficult terrain ford): ×1.25 = 125 XP
- Adjusted for ranged elevation (crossbowman): +15% tactical pressure
- **Total adjusted XP:** ~145 XP. Well within easy threshold for L3 party (525 XP easy, 1100 XP medium).
- **Assessment:** Easy encounter. This is intentional — session 1 baseline. The party should win cleanly. The research question is not whether they win but whether anyone tries to get information from the smuggler.

## Action economy

Party (4 L3 PCs) + 4 soldiers vs 4 Bandits. Party has massive numerical and action economy advantage. Recommend 2 soldiers only in ford — keeps unit coherent visually.

## Key tactical choices

1. **Crossbowman first or near-bank first?** Near-bank is closer; crossbowman threatens at range. Standard vs. high-value targeting.
2. **Subdue vs. kill?** Subdued smuggler answers the toll question.
3. **Engage ford?** Difficult terrain means PCs moving in take one round before they can attack. Soldiers directed here move faster (unit protocol, no hesitation).

## Wandering table

Not applicable — open terrain, daylight, brief encounter.

## Resolution

- Combat ends in 2-3 rounds under normal play.
- If all 4 smugglers downed: no toll information surfaces unless party investigates the mule goods (Investigation DC 11 — finds a toll receipt, workshop-canon format).
- If 1 subdued: toll information surfaces via RP.

## Post-combat

Mule goods: 4 canvas bundles. Contents: wax-wrapped salted fish, copper trade goods, one locked box (Investigation DC 12 — inside: a list of dates and quantities with CF, RH, BW notations matching the cache ledger). This is the positional-redundancy backup for the staging cache ledger.
