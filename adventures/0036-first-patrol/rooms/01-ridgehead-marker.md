# Room 01 — Ridgehead Marker

## Read-aloud

> The trail marker is an old post, iron-banded, set into a cairn of fitted stone. Someone has kept the cairn in good repair — no moss, the stones recently re-pointed. The trail here is compacted harder than mountain trails usually are. Multiple mules have come through within the last week, maybe less.

## DM notes

- Taris's Survival DC 12: identifies two sets of mule tracks heading south, one set heading north. The northbound set is 2-3 days older. The southbound sets are recent — 12-24 hours.
- Passive Perception 14: the cairn has a small mark scratched into the north-facing stone at knee height. Not a patrol mark. A symbol: a circle with a line through it. Workshop-canon: a supply-route waymark used by border traders.
- **Taris updates his map.** He marks the compaction. He does not annotate the waymark — he doesn't know what it means yet.

## What this room establishes

Trail traffic is heavier than command's briefing described. This is the first measurable discrepancy. Nobody raises it formally.

## Exits

North: Thorngate Keep (3 miles).
South: Burned Waystation (5 miles).
