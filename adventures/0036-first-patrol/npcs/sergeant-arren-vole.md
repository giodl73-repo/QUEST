# Sergeant Arren Vole

**Role:** The unit's senior NCO. Commander Varet's internal check.

## Stat block (if needed)

Veteran (MM p.350): AC 17, HP 58, Speed 30 ft. Multiattack (2). Longsword +5, 1d8+3. Parry reaction (+2 AC). Skills: Athletics +5, Perception +2. Alignment: Lawful Neutral.

## Emotional position

Arren has been a soldier for 12 years. He has served under four commanders and watched three of them make decisions they couldn't justify. He has not lost faith in the institution — he has lost faith in individuals within the institution. The distinction matters to him. He still performs every duty. He just watches now.

He trusts Varet. He does not yet know if that trust is justified. By session 3 he will know.

## Arc-completion conditions

Arren's arc fires when he says something to Varet that is not an order, not a report, and not a question — when he speaks as himself rather than as a sergeant. The trigger: Varet asks him directly what he thinks, not what he recommends. When she asks *that* question, he answers it.

His arc-completion line: *"I think you already know, Commander. I think you've known since the Waystation. The journal has it. You wrote it down. You just haven't read the discrepancy column yet."*

## What he knows

By session 1: He has noticed the Waystation fire. He has noticed the cache is overstocked. He has not reported either through formal channels. He is waiting.

By session 3: He knows a scout is missing and knows command's response was too slow.

By session 5: He understands the whole pattern. He will tell Varet if she asks. He will not tell her if she doesn't.

## Voice

Direct. Low. He does not use extra words. When something troubles him, he asks one question and stops. The question is usually the diagnosis.

## In-session behavior

- Appears in formation at session start. Moves to Varet's side when the patrol has a decision point.
- Asks the Waystation question in session 1: "Who reported this?" Waits. Does not pursue it.
- In sessions 2-5: one observation per session. Never an accusation. Always a question.
- In session 6-7: silent until Varet speaks to him directly.
