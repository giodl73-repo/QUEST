# Captain Osren Veth

**Role:** The unit's commanding officer at Thorngate Keep. Issues orders. Does not explain them.

## Stat block (if needed)

Noble (MM p.348) with Knight features: AC 18, HP 52, Speed 30 ft. Longsword +4, 1d8+2. Leadership reaction (until start of next turn, allies within 30 ft add 1d4 to attack rolls and saving throws). Skills: History +3, Insight +2, Persuasion +4.

## Emotional position

Veth is not corrupt in the way Dorn's contract covers. He is compliant. He receives orders from above and passes them down without questioning. He is not a bad man — he is a man who decided, some years ago, that questioning orders was above his pay grade. He has been paid, professionally and literally, for that decision.

He does not know the unit is meant to fail. He was told to assign them and he assigned them. He will be genuinely confused when the journal surfaces.

## Arc-completion conditions

Veth's arc fires at the tribunal in session 7 (if it occurs). He is asked if he issued the orders. He says yes. He is asked if he knew what the orders were for. He pauses — genuinely pauses — and says: *"I knew what they said. I didn't know what they meant."*

That distinction is the only honest thing he has said in three years.

## Voice

Clipped. Formal. Never hostile. He sounds like a man reading from a script he has memorized. The script is the institution.

## In-session behavior

- Appears in session 1 for briefing. Does not appear again until session 7 (tribunal).
- If the party sends a report through channels mid-campaign, he acknowledges it in writing. One line. "Report received." He does not act on it.
