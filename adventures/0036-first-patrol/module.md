## DM Cheatsheet

**Unit Cohesion Die:** d12 (12 soldiers). Result = soldiers available in combat this session.

**Party HP at start:** Varet 27, Lenne 24, Taris 26, Dorn 30. Full spell slots.

**Key mechanics:**
- Between-session HP carry-over: end HP + 1d8 (Varet, hit die d10+2 → use d8 avg), Lenne d8+2, Taris d10+1, Dorn d10+3.
- Unit soldiers: AC 13, HP 11, attack +3, 1d6+1 damage. They act on directed PC's command (bonus action to order, initiative 10).
- Difficult terrain (ford): costs 2 feet per foot. Creature at 0 HP in water: DC 10 Con save or begin drowning.

**Critical SRD rules:**
- Hunter's Mark (Taris): bonus action, 1 creature, Colossus Slayer adds 1d8 on damaged target (once/turn).
- Divine Smite (Varet): expend L1 slot on hit: 2d8 radiant (or 3d8 vs undead/fiend).
- Superiority Dice (Dorn): Menacing Attack (add d8 to damage, target DC 14 Wis save or frightened until end of next turn). Disarming Attack (add d8 to damage, target DC 14 Str save or drop item). Brace (reaction when creature enters reach: make attack).

## Scene 0 — Thorngate Keep Briefing

**Read-aloud:**
> Captain Osren Veth is a compact man, precise in his speech, with the look of someone who has read the same reports so many times that the words are furniture. He hands Varet a sealed packet without ceremony. "Standard patrol. Ridgehead, Waystation, Creston. Any contact — irregulars, traders off-route, anything — confiscate goods and hold at the staging cache. Coordinates and key are in the packet. You're back by nightfall." He does not wait for questions.

**Beats:**
- Varet may ask up to 3 questions (see Room 00 notes). Third question ends the briefing.
- Arren Vole in courtyard: "Ready when you are, Commander."
- Key falls out of folded orders. Small detail. First wrong note.

**Resolution:** Party departs south. Journal entry begins.

## Scene 1 — Ridgehead Marker

**Read-aloud:**
> The trail marker is an old post, iron-banded, set into a cairn of fitted stone. Someone has kept the cairn in good repair — no moss, the stones recently re-pointed. The trail here is compacted harder than mountain trails usually are. Multiple mules have come through within the last week, maybe less.

**Beats:**
- Taris: Survival DC 12 (two mule tracks south, one north, recent).
- Passive Perception 14: circle-with-line mark on north face of cairn. Supply-route waymark.
- Taris updates map. Announces it.

**Resolution:** No incident. Trail evidence noted. 5 miles to Waystation.

---

## Scene 2 — Burned Waystation

**Read-aloud:**
> The waystation was a four-wall structure once, stone base, timber walls and roof. The roof is gone. The walls stand to waist height where they haven't collapsed entirely. The fire was months ago — the char is weathered, lichen starting at the base stones. The floor inside is ash-black. There were supplies here once. The storage shelves are metal frames, still standing, empty.

**Beats:**
- Arren Vole (unprompted): *"Who reported this? This isn't on the patrol log."* Waits. Does not pursue.
- Investigation DC 14: fire set deliberately, from inside outward.
- History DC 12: waystation decommissioned 3 years ago. No fire incident recorded.
- Passive Perception 14: storage frames are military-spec. Forge-mark matches staging cache (anchor-level symptom).
  - Recovery path: Arren Vole mentions the frames again in Scene 4 if no PC noticed them here.

**Resolution:** Gap between fact and record established. Varet's journal: discrepancy column possibility.

## Scene 3 — Creston Ford

**Read-aloud:**
> The ford is wide here — forty feet of flat water, knee-deep, running clear over gray stone. On the far bank two mules are tied to a root-post, loaded with canvas-wrapped bundles. Four men. One on this bank with a short blade already drawn. One up on the slope behind him, crossbow visible. Two at the mules, hands moving. They see you at the same moment you see them.

**Encounter: Creston Ford Skirmish**
- 4 Bandits (near-bank blade, slope crossbow, 2 at mules).
- Up to 4 unit soldiers available (d12 result ≥ 4).
- Ford: difficult terrain. Rock shelf: half cover.
- Crossbowman priority target (ranged advantage).

**Bandit stat block (MM p.343):** AC 12, HP 11, Speed 30. Scimitar +3, 1d6+1. Light crossbow +3, 1d8+1. CR 1/8.

**Wandering table:** Not applicable (open daylight encounter).

**Smuggler parley (DC 15 Persuasion):**
> "We're not irregulars. We pay the toll. We always pay the toll. Ask at the staging cache."

**Mule goods (if investigated):** Locked box (DC 12) contains toll receipt with CF/RH/BW notations.

**Resolution:** Combat. Goods confiscated. Party proceeds to staging cache. Varet logs: "4 irregulars, goods confiscated."

---

## Scene 4 — Staging Cache

**Read-aloud:**
> The cache coordinates lead to a structure set back from the trail in a shallow hollow, screened by a stand of pine. Stone walls, solid roof, iron-banded door, and a padlock. The key is on a cord at the bottom of Varet's written orders — she finds it only because she looks. Inside: three rows of supply shelves, half full. Military-grade provisions, medical stores, equipment. More than this unit carried out of Thorngate. More than any single patrol would have brought.

**Beats:**
- Lenne (Medicine or Investigation DC 12): medical stores sized for 3× expected casualties.
- Taris (Investigation DC 13): manifest pinned inside door. Most recent entry: 3 days ago. Different hand.
- Investigation DC 15 (any PC): second ledger behind shelf brackets. Records: CF ford, RH toll, BW burn. **H1 confirmation.**
  - Passive Perception 15: shelf brackets visibly thicker. Active check or passive.
  - v1.3 fallbacks: (a) Passive 15; (b) ledger persists in S2; (c) Arren Vole mentions cache volume in S2.

**NPC note:** If no PC found the forge-mark in Scene 2, Arren Vole now says: *"Those frames in the Waystation — same forge-mark as these shelves."*

**Resolution:** Unit delivers confiscated goods. Returns to Thorngate. Session ends.

## Varet's Field Journal — Entry 1

After Scene 4, the DM (playing Varet) writes the journal entry aloud. Base version:
```
PC 188, Day 1. Route: Ridgehead–Waystation–Creston.
ORDER: Standard patrol, confiscate, deliver to cache.
ACTION: Route completed. 1 contact (4 irregulars). Goods confiscated and delivered.
OUTCOME: Clean.
DISCREPANCY: [see below]
```
**Discrepancy column trigger:** If the party found the second ledger OR heard the toll comment, Varet writes:
> *"Cache supply volume exceeds allocation. Waystation fire not in patrol record. Cache ledger shows prior entries from route markers. Confiscated goods appear to feed an established system, not a patrol reserve."*

**If not found:** Column stays blank. Arc is slower. Still recoverable via S2.
