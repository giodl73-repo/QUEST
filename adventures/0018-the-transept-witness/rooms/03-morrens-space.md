# Room 03 — Morren's Space

## Read-aloud

> An older gully dwarf in a chair near the north windows. Blue-green light across his hands. The floor near him is slightly shinier than the surrounding stone — worn smooth by a path walked many times. A practice stone near his chair, marked in a shorthand notation. He looks at you with the patience of someone who has been right for thirty years and hasn't been able to prove it. He says: "Sit down. I have been telling this story for thirty years. You are the first people who might be able to do something with it."

## Features

- **Morren** (in chair, waiting). See `npcs/morren.md`. He is ready. He has been ready.

- **Worn path in the floor** (passive Perception 15 to notice without rolling; **DC 13** if actively searching). Where he walks when he tells the account. Thirty years of repeated pacing has polished the stone slightly. *Relic sign: account sign — evidence the account is practiced, not improvised.*

- **Practice stone** (beside chair). A smooth flat stone with notation marks — a shorthand Morren developed to help him remember the account's sequence. **DC 12 Investigation** to notice the marks resemble the notation on the ritual focus's base (if the party has it). *Relic sign: notation mark sign — Morren and Aldric use similar notation systems because Morren learned from watching Aldric record the account twenty years ago.*

- **Wrong light** (strongest here, at north windows). The blue-green light is most concentrated in R3. In a certain position, standing near the north windows with the light on one's hands, the colour is the exact shade of fresh-bruised comfrey — a healing herb. No one in the party will necessarily know this. Calla, with her grandmother's herblore, might: **DC 13 Nature** to identify the specific colour match.

- **The ritual focus** (if party brought it). When held in R3's light, the focus is warmer than in R2. **No roll required.** The proximity to where the account is kept — near the person who witnessed the ritual — intensifies the residual warmth. *Relic sign: warmth sign — strongest in this room.*

## Encounter

**The Full Account** — See `encounters/the-full-account.md`. Seven stages. The party must listen without interrupting for the arc-completion to fire. Morren will answer interruptions; the arc simply does not fire.

**Hint 2 (auto-delivery, passive DC 11):** Delivered in Stage 3 of Morren's account — *"She held the stone. It changed temperature when she used it. Not cold. Not warm. It went through both, quickly, the way water changes when you add something to it. I had never felt anything like prayer do that."* All four party members have passive Perception ≥ 11. Hint 2 lands.

**Hint 3 (pre-planted):** After Stage 7 of the account, Morren says: *"There is a man in the sacristy. He has been copying my account for twenty years. He was the one who gave her the working. He regrets it differently than I do."*

**Ritual focus identification:** If the party shows Morren the focus before the account, he says: *"That is Aldric's mark. He was stationed at Mireth thirty years ago. He is still in this building."* He then begins the account. If after: he identifies it and adds: *"He gave her that too."*

## GM Notes

This is the session's only required scene. Everything else exists to get the party here and to give them space after.

**Calla's arc is the session's center.** She is the spotlight PC. The elder's account is the most complete version of Velantha's story. She receives it. The test is not whether she holds the words — she will hold the words. The test is whether she understands that when she repeats it, the directness will be gone. The account was spoken by the witness, in this room, after thirty years of waiting. She cannot recreate those conditions. She can only carry the account as her own synthesis. This is Stage 3's anteroom.

**Do not prompt Calla.** The DM does not say "Calla, what do you do?" She is listening. The DM plays Morren through the seven stages. At the end, Morren's arc-completion fires. Then the DM waits. Whatever Calla does or doesn't do — that is the session's final beat.

### Memory Fragment
*He was there. He has been there, in memory, every day since. The account has changed only slightly over thirty years — a word here, a clarification there. He is not performing it. He is trying to get it right.*
