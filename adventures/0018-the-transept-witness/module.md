## Setting & Background

**Place:** The transept of the Halted Spire — the cathedral's east-west cross-section, perpendicular to the nave. The transept is the only section the congregation has partially completed: they found coloured glass somewhere (a ruined building in the hills; a trade; no one asks) and set it into the window frames. The light through those windows is wrong — not wrong as in broken, wrong as in the colours don't match what a Mishakal cathedral would use. The congregation chose colours they associated with Velantha's practice: blues and greens, the colours of healing herbs. The light in the transept is the colour of things growing.

**Atmosphere:** The transept is quieter than the nave. The elder keeps it tidy. He has a chair near the north windows (the best light). He has been telling his account of the night Velantha performed the ritual to the congregation for thirty years. None of them fully receive it — they were not there. He has been waiting for someone who was not in the congregation: someone who would hear it as new.

The party enters via the connection from the nave's eastern archways. Veth (if the party maintained the Route D relationship) has told them: *"The elder in the Transept was there the night she performed the ritual. I know what was passed down. He knows what actually happened. They are not the same."*

The elder — **Morren** — is in his chair by the north windows. He sees the party enter and says nothing for a moment. Then: *"Sit down. I have been telling this story for thirty years. You are the first people who might be able to do something with it."*

He has been waiting for a long time. He is not patient about it — he is ready.

**The ritual focus.** The party either has it (if they retrieved it from the Bell Tower) or knows where it is. Morren identifies the mark on its base immediately when he sees it: *"That is Aldric's mark. He was stationed at Mireth thirty years ago. He is still in this building."* This is Hint 3's pre-plant and seeds the Sacristy.

**Morren** (workshop-canon name, to be registered) is approximately 65 years old. He was present and lucid during the ritual — the only congregation member who was both. Others were there; they were younger, smaller, more frightened. He was the one who watched what Velantha's hands did. He has the technical account.

He is not a storyteller by temperament. He is a witness. He gives the account the way you give evidence: sequentially, with corrections when he misremembers, with specific details that are not decorative but evidentiary.

His arc-completion fires when the party listens to the full account without interrupting. He says: *"She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do."* This is the line the congregation does not have. He has been waiting to say it to someone who would understand why the distinction matters.

**Velantha's ritual focus** — the carved stone object she channelled the High Sorcery working through. Already found in S3 (fell from a congregation member's hands, warm, marked on base). Morren identifies it. The Orders would recognize the mark immediately — it is Aldric Voss's notation mark, specific to Red Robe field stations.

Full artifact file: `treasures/ritual-focus.md`
Cross-skill manifest: `treasures/ritual-focus-manifest.md`

**Hint 2 (recovery path, auto-delivery):** When Morren describes the ritual, he includes what Velantha used — not abstractly but specifically: *"She held the stone. It changed temperature when she used it. Not cold. Not warm. It went through both, quickly, the way water changes when you add something to it. I had never felt anything like prayer do that. I had felt prayer. This was different."* 

**Delivery mechanism:** This is delivered auto in Morren's account (passive DC 11 to understand the significance — that he is distinguishing High Sorcery from divine grace). Does not require Insight roll; the information is in the account if the party listens. Per design mandate from panel: auto-delivery at passive DC = active DC + 2. Active check would be DC 9 (very easy); passive = DC 11. Any PC with passive Perception 11+ receives this. All four party members have passive Perception ≥ 11.

**Hint 3 (pre-planted):** After delivering the ritual account, Morren says: *"There is a man in the sacristy. He has been copying my account for twenty years. He was the one who gave her the working. He regrets it differently than I do."* This is Hint 3's plant: the party now knows Aldric gave Velantha the ritual.

The transept's coloured glass windows are set in partially-finished frames. One section of the transept floor — the northwest corner, where the original construction drainage trench was never filled — is unstable. **The unstable floor:** a 10×10 ft section near the northwest window, flagged by the congregation with a single rope tied across the entrance to that area. No sign; Morren knows to avoid it.

If a party member steps onto the unstable floor (they might if exploring without attention — DC 12 Perception to notice the rope barrier):
- **DC 13 Dex save** or the floor crumbles under them (5 ft drop, 1d6 bludgeoning; no serious injury at level 3 but resource-relevant: if it's Calla, who is already at 19/22 HP, this matters).
- If they fall: the section below is an empty construction cavity, not a hazard beyond the fall. They can climb out (DC 8 Athletics, 5 ft height).

This hazard: (a) costs HP if triggered; (b) tests the party's attention during a primarily social encounter; (c) is recoverable without drama.

**Verbal documentation-arc critical session.** The elder's account is the most complete version of Velantha's story that exists. Calla hears it. The test: can she hold it?

What she discovers: she can hold the structure (the seven stages Morren describes). She can hold the specific details (the stone, the temperature change, the moment Velantha's hands stopped). She cannot hold the **directness** — the account was spoken to her, by the person who witnessed it, in the room where he has been carrying it for thirty years. When she tries to repeat it, the directness will be gone. The transmission is the loss.

This is the verbal documentation-arc's Stage 2→3 transition point. She is not yet at Stage 3 (naming the thing). But she has received the source material. The gap she has been identifying now has a specific shape: the elder's account is the thing she has been trying to describe by its absence. She holds it. She will speak it in 0021.

**Documentation-suspension fourth activation of the second kind:** This is not a fumble (Stage 1). This is a success that reveals a new gap. She receives the account correctly; she understands immediately that repeating it will change it. The arc is in Stage 3's anteroom.

# Velantha's Ritual Focus

*A carved stone disc, palm-sized, warm to the touch in a way that doesn't fade — and marked on the base with a notation that belongs to an Order that no longer operates out of Mireth.*

## Appearance

Carved from dense grey stone, smoothed to a disc approximately three inches across and one inch thick. The face is plain — no inscription, no symbol. The edges have been worn smooth from handling. The base is different: a small, precise notation mark scratched in the stone with a stylus, deliberate and careful, in a shorthand used by Red Robe field stations to mark their tools and instruments. The mark is Aldric Voss's.

The disc is warm. Not the warmth of a held object — it does not cool when set down. It holds its temperature independent of environment. Morren (the Transept elder) describes: *"It went through both temperatures quickly, the way water changes when you add something to it."* Thirty years of congregation veneration has stabilized the warmth into its present steady state. The original warmth was the High Sorcery channelling moment; what remains is the faith's response to it.

## Presence / Desire

The focus does not want to be misidentified. It does not want to be called a miracle. It was a tool — specific, calibrated, used once for its intended purpose and used correctly. It has no ego about this. It simply hums, faintly, when held near any active or latent High Sorcery working. The hum is not a judgment. It is a recognition: *this is what I know how to do.*

In the hands of someone who does not know what High Sorcery is, the focus is warm and pleasant and unremarkable. In the hands of someone who does — Renn Ashkeld, whose pact with something ancient is a different kind of unsanctioned working — the focus hums distinctly. Once. Then settles. Recognition offered; no further comment.

## Provenance

**1. Aldric Voss, Red Robe field wizard, Mireth posting (~32 years before campaign).**
Aldric carried the focus as part of his field kit — standard-issue for a Red Robe handling sensitive non-standard workings. He marked it with his station notation. He gave it to Velantha along with the prohibited ritual, knowing she intended to use both on gully dwarves, which the Orders had classified as outside the permitted use scope. He did not ask for the focus back afterward. He did not expect her to survive.

**2. Sister Velantha (used once; died at the ritual site ~30 years before campaign).**
She used the focus to channel the High Sorcery working. Morren witnessed: her hands on the disc, the temperature change, the light that was not prayer. She set it down when the working was complete. She did not pick it up again. The congregation found it near where she stood.

**3. The Bell Tower congregation (kept ~30 years; most recently with the congregation member who dropped it in S3).**
They kept it because it was warm. They did not know what it was. They gave it a place in their devotional arrangements. The specific congregation member carrying it in S3 dropped it during the party's visit; the focus fell and the sound it made was a clear, clean note — the stone's response to being near the party, who carry the field satchel's resonance. Renn saw it and understood it was warm. The mark on the base: Aldric's notation. The elder identifies it.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a spellcasting creature, or any creature on whom a magical working has been performed (this includes: everyone healed by Velantha's ritual; every Order-sanctioned mage; every warlock or wizard in the workshop).

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **High Sorcery resonance.** The attuned creature is aware of active or latent High Sorcery workings within 60 ft. The awareness is faint: a warmth-increase in the disc rather than a specific signal. DC 10 Insight (not Arcana — this is felt, not analyzed) to understand the direction of a resonance.
- **Channelling stability.** When the attuned creature casts a spell requiring Concentration, they may roll a d4 when making a Concentration saving throw and add the result. Once per long rest.
- **Evidence.** Any Red Robe, White Robe, or Black Robe wizard who examines the focus (or its description) and succeeds on DC 10 Arcana identifies Aldric Voss's notation mark and the Orders chapter he served. This is the seed's delivery mechanism: the information is in the mark.

### Relic Test

The focus has no traditional relic test. It is a tool that became a relic. Using it for its original purpose (channelling a High Sorcery working) would require actual wizard levels and the appropriate spell; for a level 3 party, this is not accessible. If it were somehow misused as a weapon, the warmth fades permanently. The Orders would consider this desecration.

### Ending consequences

If attuned by a non-sanctioned magic user (Renn Ashkeld, for example), the focus hums once and settles. The Orders would classify this as a violation. The focus does not care about Orders classification.

## Hooks

1. **Aldric Voss is in the Sacristy.** The notation mark on the focus identifies him. He has been in the building for thirty years. He has been waiting for the focus to be recognized. When the party brings the focus to 0019, he will know immediately that someone has seen the mark.

2. **The Orders.** Any member of the Orders of High Sorcery who encounters this focus — or encounters a party member carrying it — would recognize both the mark and the prohibited working it was used for. This is Aldric's guilt, portable. The party is carrying the evidence of an unauthorized ritual.

3. **Renn's hum.** The focus hums distinctly once when Renn handles it. It does not hum for Calla (no active High Sorcery), Maret (no High Sorcery), or Dren (no magic). Only Renn. His pact is not High Sorcery — it is something older and stranger — but the focus recognizes it as a working of comparable character. This is not confirmation of anything. It is a recognition.

## GM Notes

The focus is the session's most significant piece of evidence. It identifies Aldric, which unlocks 0019. It also confirms Hint 2 mechanically (the temperature change Morren describes is a High Sorcery phenomenon, not a divine one).

When Morren identifies the mark in session, do not pause for explanation. He says: *"That is Aldric's mark. He was stationed at Mireth thirty years ago. He is still in this building."* He continues his account without elaboration. The party will ask about Aldric later; Morren will answer. The identification is a sentence, not a scene.

When Renn holds the focus: the hum is once, faint, then nothing. He does not need to roll to notice it. He notices. He does not need to comment. He may comment later — in 0019, with Aldric. Or never.

His hands are in his lap when he is not speaking; he uses them when he is. He is not physically imposing. He is precise. He has been telling this account for thirty years and he knows exactly where each word goes.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 8, AC 10. He does not fight; the congregation would defend him if attacked. In thirty-two years he has never been attacked. He does not expect to start now.

## Public Knowledge

- He was present the night Velantha performed the ritual. He was lucid. He watched.
- The account has seven stages. He knows all seven. The congregation knows fragments — most know Stages 1-3; few know Stages 5-7. Only Morren knows the full sequence.

Morren will deliver the full account when the party demonstrates they are listening, not collecting. The distinction he makes:

### The Full Account

# Encounter — The Full Account

## Type

Social encounter (extended). The session's primary encounter. Morren delivers Velantha's full account in seven stages. The party must listen without interrupting for the arc-completion to fire.

## The Seven Stages

Morren tells the account sequentially. He does not rush. He does not perform.

**Stage 1 — Why she came:**
*"She came because we were sick. The same fever that had reached the town. We had it first. Nobody came for us — the town's healers didn't come here; we were Aghar, we were not their concern. She came. She had heard there was a plague in the hills. She brought her bag."*

**Stage 2 — What she said:**
*"She said she could not promise it would work. She said she had something that might help. She said she needed our permission to try it — she asked. I had never been asked before. None of us had."*

**Stage 3 — What she used (Hint 2 delivery, passive DC 11):**
*"She held the stone. It changed temperature when she used it. Not cold. Not warm. It went through both, quickly, the way water changes when you add something to it. I had never felt anything like prayer do that. I had felt prayer — when the clerics came to the town and we stood outside and felt the edge of it. This was not that. This was different. It worked."*

**Stage 4 — What she promised:**
*"She promised some of us she would come back. I heard her say it. I believe she meant it. She believed it when she said it."*

**Stage 5 — What she said about the stone:**
*"Before she began, she showed me the stone. She showed me the mark on the base. She said: 'If anyone asks what this is, show them this mark. It belongs to someone who knows what it is.' She did not tell me his name."*

**Stage 6 — What happened at the end:**
*"She used the working. It worked. Everyone who was sick — we recovered. She had been well when she arrived. When it was over, she was not well. She had used something she could not replace. She set the stone down. She sat down where she had been standing. She said: 'I need to rest.' She did not get up."*

**Stage 7 — What she said at the end:**
*"The last thing she said to me — the last thing she said to anyone — was this: she had a paper, she said. A forged paper. Someone had written it for her. She had used it to get the stone. She tried to burn it before she began. She said she didn't know if it had burned. She said: 'If you find a man who marks his tools the way that stone is marked — tell him it worked. Tell him it was worth it. Tell him I'm sorry I can't come back.'* 

*"She didn't come back."*

---

**After Stage 7:** Morren pauses. If the party has listened without interrupting:

**Arc-completion:** *"She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do."*

He looks at his hands. He looks at Calla. He says: *"I hope that's useful to you."*

Then, without prompting: *"There is a man in the sacristy. He has been copying my account for twenty years. He was the one who gave her the working. He regrets it differently than I do."*

## Hint 2 Delivery (Stage 3)

**Auto-delivery mechanism:** Stage 3 is narrative. Morren describes the temperature change. Any party member with passive Perception ≥ 11 receives this as meaningful: this is how High Sorcery works — it is physical, not divine. All four party members have passive Perception ≥ 11.

**Confirmation if the party still isn't sure:** If any party member asks "was that prayer?" after Stage 3, Morren says: *"No. I know what prayer feels like. I said — this was different."*

Hint 2 delivered.

## Interruption Protocol

If the party asks a question during the account:
- Morren answers directly and continues.
- The arc-completion does **not** fire at the end.
- All information is still delivered (Hint 2, Hint 3, Aldric).
- The arc fires in a weaker form: Morren delivers Stage 7 without the arc-completion line. The party has the information; they do not have the line the congregation doesn't have.

## Route Implications

- **Route D:** Full account received without interruption; arc fires; full Hint 2 and Hint 3 delivered.
- **Route C:** Account received with some interruptions; information delivered; arc-completion line not spoken.
- **Route A:** Party asks only for specific information; receives it; leaves.

## Calla's Arc Note

Calla receives the account. She holds it. She immediately understands: when she repeats it, the directness will be gone. The account was spoken by the witness to her, in this room. When she speaks it to someone else, it will be secondhand. She will not be the witness; she will be the oral historian. The gap between those two positions is now specific and named.

This is Stage 3 of the verbal documentation-arc: she is in the anteroom of naming the thing. The account is the thing she has been trying to describe by its absence. She holds it. She will speak it at the Keystone (0021).
