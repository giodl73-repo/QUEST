## Critique

### Overall assessment

Three yes-and moments. The DM adjudicated all three correctly: (1) Calla's silence was let to breathe — no prompting, no forced dialogue; (2) Renn's independent parallel was let to develop through objects without the DM connecting the dots for him; (3) Dren's perimeter check before sitting was honored rather than rushed. This session had the highest respect for player behavior of any session in the campaign.

### What worked

**Renn arrived at the Sacristy before being told (Scene 4.5):** He smelled old ink (Perception 20). He said: *"Someone has been writing in there. For a long time."* The DM let him say it before Morren spoke. This is the Improviser's favorite moment of the campaign: the player found the next clue through their own engagement with the environment. Renn's pact-resonance (the focus's hum, the smell) led him to the same conclusion as Morren's account. Two paths to the same place. The DM said yes to the object-based path without making it feel like cheating.

**Wandering Encounter result 3 (Bell Tower member sees party with Morren):** The congregation member appeared, saw the party with the elder, and withdrew. The DM let this happen without interrupting Morren's account — Morren *paused for one breath*, then continued. This is the correct level of acknowledgment: the world continued around the account without consuming it.

### What needs work

**What did Calla do after Morren said the arc-completion line?** The Improviser agrees with the Roleplayer: one specific action was missing. The DM should have given Calla a beat of physical grounding. Not speech — an action. The improviser suggests: she stood up, which is not what anyone does at the end of a conversation in normal circumstances but is what she does when she has received something she needs to move with.

### The question I'd push on

**Does Aldric know the party is coming?** The Sacristy encounter in 0019 should begin with Aldric already knowing: the Bell Tower congregation member who saw the party with Morren will have told Sharr, who may send word toward the Sacristy; OR Aldric's written copies of Morren's account include the information that "someone who would take it outside these walls" would come eventually. Design 0019's opening with Aldric in mid-sentence — already composing his response to a party he expected.
