## Critique

### Overall assessment

Morren said the line Calla needed. Calla received it in silence. Renn arrived at his parallel without being led there. These three things constitute the campaign's best single session for character work. The Roleplayer's only concern: Calla's receipt of the account was documented through dice rolls and RP beats, but the session log does not record what she *did* immediately after Morren said the arc-completion line. One moment of specific behavior — not speech, just one action — would have closed the session cleanly.

### What worked

**Morren's arc-completion structure:** The line the congregation doesn't have — *"She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do."* — is the best single line of the campaign. It is specific. It distinguishes between how the congregation tells the story and how Velantha actually felt. It is only available to Calla because she listened without asking. The arc-completion conditioned on silence is the most unusual condition in the workshop's NPC design — and it fired correctly.

**Renn's object-based parallel (Scene 4.5):** He smelled the Sacristy before being told. The ritual focus hummed for him. He said *"Someone has been writing in there. For a long time."* He was standing next to Calla when Morren mentioned Aldric. Renn already knew. His pact and Velantha's working are the same category. He has not said this. But he knows. The Roleplayer gives this the highest character-work score of any Renn scene in the campaign.

### What needs work

**Calla's silence after the arc-completion.** The log ends Morren's arc with: *"He looks at Calla. He says: 'I hope that's useful to you.'"* Then Calla holds the account (RP roll 23). But the log doesn't record what she does next. Does she stand? Does she look at the practice stone? Does she perform her three breaths in this room? One specific action — not speech, just a motion — would have completed the scene. The verbal-documentation-arc practitioner receiving the source material deserves one beat of physical grounding.

### The question I'd push on

**When does Renn disclose the pact?** He has now connected his unauthorized transaction to Velantha's unauthorized transaction through the ritual focus and the Sacristy smell. He is standing at the threshold of 0019, where Aldric Voss will understand immediately what Renn is. The disclosure scene is in 0019. Design the exact line Renn might say — not a forced revelation, but the moment when Renn's silence about the pact becomes harder to maintain than speaking. *"She used something that wasn't prayer. I know what that costs."* That's the candidate line. Whether he says it to Aldric or to the party first: design it now, before 0019 runs.
