## Critique

### Overall assessment

The most accessible session of the campaign for a new reader. Morren's account is a self-contained story: a sick community, a healer who came when others wouldn't, something she used that wasn't prayer, a promise she couldn't keep, a line she said at the end. A reader who knew nothing about D&D or Dragonlance before this session now knows what happened and why it matters. The Fresh Face is satisfied.

### What worked

**Morren's account as a self-contained narrative.** Seven stages. Each one builds on the last. The Fresh Face test: can a new reader summarize it? Yes: *Velantha came to heal the gully dwarves when no one else would. She used a High Sorcery working — not prayer. She promised some she'd return. She couldn't. Her last words were instructions: find the person whose mark is on the stone and tell him it worked.*

This is a complete story. It has a beginning (she came), a middle (she used something that wasn't prayer), and an end (she didn't come back). The campaign's central mystery — what is Velantha's secret — has been answered for any reader paying attention since Stage 3.

**"She called it the right thing to do":** A new reader understands this without any Dragonlance context. It is an ethical claim, not a theological one. She did not defend herself with institutional authority. She said it was right. Any reader understands the distinction.

### What needs work

**Renn's pact still unexplained.** The session log includes: *"The focus hummed once for Renn... His pact with something ancient is a different kind of unsanctioned working."* A new reader who has not been tracking Renn's background has now seen the focus hum for him without explanation, seen him smell the Sacristy, and seen him connect to Velantha's working — but the reason (his unauthorized pact) has never been stated in the session summaries. The Fresh Face is 4 sessions in and does not yet know Renn is a warlock whose pact is unofficial. 0019's setup should include a single line about Renn's nature in the session summary.

### The question I'd push on

**What does a new reader know after 4 sessions?** They know: Velantha healed gully dwarves using High Sorcery; she died doing it; her congregation has been keeping faith for thirty years in an unfinished cathedral; some of them are angry; the party has been hired to clear the building and hasn't; there is a man in the Sacristy who gave Velantha the tool and has been copying the account for twenty years. This is enough. The Fresh Face says: this campaign is legible to a new reader at the halfway point. That's a first for the workshop.
