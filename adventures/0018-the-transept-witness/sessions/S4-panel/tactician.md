## Critique

### Overall assessment

Dren detected the floor (22 vs DC 12), reported it, and moved on. Correct. The hazard was real — if he had rolled average (12), he would have been standing at the rope barrier trying to decide whether to step over. The 22 means the hazard was neutralized by the highest-initiative PC doing his job. This is the Tactician giving credit for the system working.

### What worked

**Dren's perimeter check before sitting (Scene 4.3):** He didn't sit down with the rest of the party when Morren said "Sit down." He secured the perimeter first. He found the unstable floor. He reported it. No drama, no fanfare — just correct tactical behavior. The Tactician notes: this is the kind of play that keeps parties alive in complex environments. Dren's Heuristic 1 firing before the social encounter is exactly the discipline this workshop has been trying to demonstrate.

### What needs work

**Fourth consecutive session with minimal mechanical challenge.** S3 had Calla at 19/22 HP and 3/4 L1 slots at session start; this session began with a short rest restoring both. The party is entering the campaign's second half at full resources. 0019 and 0020 have no designed combat. For the finale (0021), the party needs to have experienced what it feels like to be depleted. **Design a non-combat resource cost into 0020 (Crypt / Velantha)** — the encounter with Velantha herself should cost something even if it doesn't injure. Suggest: a concentration check or Ki expenditure required to maintain presence during Velantha's account (the emotional weight is real and mechanical).

### The question I'd push on

**Does the promise-cord have a combat application?** Dren is carrying it unattuned. The cord's "once per long rest, know the count of unfulfilled promises" property is passive and non-combat. But the cord tightened over the Aldis insignia. When Dren attunes it — if he does — the tightening is ongoing, tied to a promise he hasn't yet fulfilled (finding Aldis's family). This is the Tactician's question: does Dren's unfulfilled promise generate any mechanical consequence before the campaign ends? Design that consequence into 0021 or earlier.
