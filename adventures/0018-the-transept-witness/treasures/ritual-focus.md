# Velantha's Ritual Focus

*A carved stone disc, palm-sized, warm to the touch in a way that doesn't fade — and marked on the base with a notation that belongs to an Order that no longer operates out of Mireth.*

## Appearance

Carved from dense grey stone, smoothed to a disc approximately three inches across and one inch thick. The face is plain — no inscription, no symbol. The edges have been worn smooth from handling. The base is different: a small, precise notation mark scratched in the stone with a stylus, deliberate and careful, in a shorthand used by Red Robe field stations to mark their tools and instruments. The mark is Aldric Voss's.

The disc is warm. Not the warmth of a held object — it does not cool when set down. It holds its temperature independent of environment. Morren (the Transept elder) describes: *"It went through both temperatures quickly, the way water changes when you add something to it."* Thirty years of congregation veneration has stabilized the warmth into its present steady state. The original warmth was the High Sorcery channelling moment; what remains is the faith's response to it.

## Presence / Desire

The focus does not want to be misidentified. It does not want to be called a miracle. It was a tool — specific, calibrated, used once for its intended purpose and used correctly. It has no ego about this. It simply hums, faintly, when held near any active or latent High Sorcery working. The hum is not a judgment. It is a recognition: *this is what I know how to do.*

In the hands of someone who does not know what High Sorcery is, the focus is warm and pleasant and unremarkable. In the hands of someone who does — Renn Ashkeld, whose pact with something ancient is a different kind of unsanctioned working — the focus hums distinctly. Once. Then settles. Recognition offered; no further comment.

## Provenance

**1. Aldric Voss, Red Robe field wizard, Mireth posting (~32 years before campaign).**
Aldric carried the focus as part of his field kit — standard-issue for a Red Robe handling sensitive non-standard workings. He marked it with his station notation. He gave it to Velantha along with the prohibited ritual, knowing she intended to use both on gully dwarves, which the Orders had classified as outside the permitted use scope. He did not ask for the focus back afterward. He did not expect her to survive.

**2. Sister Velantha (used once; died at the ritual site ~30 years before campaign).**
She used the focus to channel the High Sorcery working. Morren witnessed: her hands on the disc, the temperature change, the light that was not prayer. She set it down when the working was complete. She did not pick it up again. The congregation found it near where she stood.

**3. The Bell Tower congregation (kept ~30 years; most recently with the congregation member who dropped it in S3).**
They kept it because it was warm. They did not know what it was. They gave it a place in their devotional arrangements. The specific congregation member carrying it in S3 dropped it during the party's visit; the focus fell and the sound it made was a clear, clean note — the stone's response to being near the party, who carry the field satchel's resonance. Renn saw it and understood it was warm. The mark on the base: Aldric's notation. The elder identifies it.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a spellcasting creature, or any creature on whom a magical working has been performed (this includes: everyone healed by Velantha's ritual; every Order-sanctioned mage; every warlock or wizard in the workshop).

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **High Sorcery resonance.** The attuned creature is aware of active or latent High Sorcery workings within 60 ft. The awareness is faint: a warmth-increase in the disc rather than a specific signal. DC 10 Insight (not Arcana — this is felt, not analyzed) to understand the direction of a resonance.
- **Channelling stability.** When the attuned creature casts a spell requiring Concentration, they may roll a d4 when making a Concentration saving throw and add the result. Once per long rest.
- **Evidence.** Any Red Robe, White Robe, or Black Robe wizard who examines the focus (or its description) and succeeds on DC 10 Arcana identifies Aldric Voss's notation mark and the Orders chapter he served. This is the seed's delivery mechanism: the information is in the mark.

### Relic Test

The focus has no traditional relic test. It is a tool that became a relic. Using it for its original purpose (channelling a High Sorcery working) would require actual wizard levels and the appropriate spell; for a level 3 party, this is not accessible. If it were somehow misused as a weapon, the warmth fades permanently. The Orders would consider this desecration.

### Ending consequences

If attuned by a non-sanctioned magic user (Renn Ashkeld, for example), the focus hums once and settles. The Orders would classify this as a violation. The focus does not care about Orders classification.

## Hooks

1. **Aldric Voss is in the Sacristy.** The notation mark on the focus identifies him. He has been in the building for thirty years. He has been waiting for the focus to be recognized. When the party brings the focus to 0019, he will know immediately that someone has seen the mark.

2. **The Orders.** Any member of the Orders of High Sorcery who encounters this focus — or encounters a party member carrying it — would recognize both the mark and the prohibited working it was used for. This is Aldric's guilt, portable. The party is carrying the evidence of an unauthorized ritual.

3. **Renn's hum.** The focus hums distinctly once when Renn handles it. It does not hum for Calla (no active High Sorcery), Maret (no High Sorcery), or Dren (no magic). Only Renn. His pact is not High Sorcery — it is something older and stranger — but the focus recognizes it as a working of comparable character. This is not confirmation of anything. It is a recognition.

## GM Notes

The focus is the session's most significant piece of evidence. It identifies Aldric, which unlocks 0019. It also confirms Hint 2 mechanically (the temperature change Morren describes is a High Sorcery phenomenon, not a divine one).

When Morren identifies the mark in session, do not pause for explanation. He says: *"That is Aldric's mark. He was stationed at Mireth thirty years ago. He is still in this building."* He continues his account without elaboration. The party will ask about Aldric later; Morren will answer. The identification is a sentence, not a scene.

When Renn holds the focus: the hum is once, faint, then nothing. He does not need to roll to notice it. He notices. He does not need to comment. He may comment later — in 0019, with Aldric. Or never.
