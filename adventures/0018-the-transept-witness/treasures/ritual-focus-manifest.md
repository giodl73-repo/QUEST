# Relic Signs Manifest — Velantha's Ritual Focus

The dungeon housing this relic MUST express the following in at least **3 of the following ways**, distributed across at least **2 rooms**.

## Required (≥3, distributed across ≥2 rooms)

- [ ] **Wrong light sign:** The transept's coloured glass is blue-green, not Mishakal's white. The light through it is the colour of healing herbs, not of the goddess of healing. At least one room's read-aloud must note the colour of the light and its wrongness — not evil-wrong, just almost-right. This is the congregation choosing what they associated with Velantha's work rather than what the cathedral's goddess would expect.

- [ ] **Warmth sign:** The ritual focus is warm in a way that doesn't fade. In the rooms where it has been kept or where Morren is present (he has handled it for sessions), the air near him is slightly warmer than it should be. **DC 12 Perception.** This is the same warmth as the field satchel, the same warmth as the measuring cup — but more concentrated. The focus was at the ritual; it was warmer then than anything else has been since.

- [ ] **Notation mark sign:** The mark on the focus's base is Aldric Voss's Red Robe field notation. Morren identifies it: *"That is Aldric's mark."* At least one room must contain a reference to marks — either the mark on the focus itself, or other marks that the party has seen Morren make (he notes things down in a private shorthand that resembles the notation on the focus). **DC 12 Investigation** to notice the similarity between Morren's shorthand and the focus's mark.

- [ ] **Account sign:** Morren has been telling the account for thirty years. Fragments of it are present in the transept's material: a place where he rehearses, marks on a practice stone that record the ritual's sequence, or a worn spot on the floor where he walks while telling. These are evidence that the account is practiced, not improvised. The account the party receives is the same account he has been refining for thirty years.

- [ ] **Reaction moment:** When Renn Ashkeld first holds the focus, it hums once — distinct, brief. Not loud. Just present. The DM describes: *"The focus is warm. When Renn holds it, something in it notes him."* Then nothing further. No mechanical effect. No explanation. The recognition is the moment.

## Required (always)

- [ ] **The focus is present and visible before Morren identifies it.** The party should have opportunity to hold and examine it before Morren speaks. The identification is more powerful when the party has already held the warm, humming object and not understood what it is.

- [ ] **One room's architecture expresses the evidentiary nature of the focus.** The transept has been partially completed by people who did not have architectural training. The glass is in the windows because they wanted it there. The wrongness of the light is evidence of what the congregation valued, not what was planned. The focus's wrongness (warm, Orders-marked, used for an unauthorized working) is the same category of thing: right for its purpose, wrong for the institution.

## Suggested

- Morren's worn path in the transept floor — where he walks when he tells the account — is slightly shinier than the surrounding stone. A character with **passive Perception 15** notices it without rolling. Evidence of thirty years of a single repeated practice.
- One congregation member other than Morren is present during the account-giving. They have heard it before. They watch the party's faces more than Morren's words. They are evaluating whether the party is actually listening.
