# Morren, the Witness

## Appearance

Male gully dwarf, approximately 65 years. He sits in a wooden chair near the north windows — congregation-made, not construction-era. His hands are in his lap when he is not speaking; he uses them when he is. He is not physically imposing. He is precise. He has been telling this account for thirty years and he knows exactly where each word goes.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 8, AC 10. He does not fight; the congregation would defend him if attacked. In thirty-two years he has never been attacked. He does not expect to start now.

## Public Knowledge

- He was present the night Velantha performed the ritual. He was lucid. He watched.
- The account has seven stages. He knows all seven. The congregation knows fragments — most know Stages 1-3; few know Stages 5-7. Only Morren knows the full sequence.
- The mark on the ritual focus's base is Aldric Voss's notation. Aldric was stationed at Mireth. Aldric is still in the building.
- There is a man in the sacristy who has been copying his account for twenty years.
- She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do.

## Private Knowledge

- Morren knows Aldric regrets something, but not the same thing Morren regrets. Morren regrets that she died. Aldric regrets something about how the story was told afterward. Morren has never asked Aldric to explain.
- He has told the account to the congregation so many times that he can no longer tell whether they understand it or whether they have simply memorized it. He believes the party — being outside the congregation — might actually receive it rather than simply reproduce it.
- He knows that what he holds is perishable: when he dies, the account goes with him unless someone outside the congregation is carrying it. He is 65. This is urgent.

## Information Readiness Condition

Morren will deliver the full account when the party demonstrates they are listening, not collecting. The distinction he makes:
- **Listening:** facing him, not writing, not asking questions yet, not checking other parts of the room.
- **Collecting:** taking notes, asking for clarification of individual details before he is finished, treating his account as a list of facts.

If the party demonstrates they are listening (which is the default for this party's heuristics), he begins immediately. He will not withhold.

**Information sequence:**
- Ritual focus identification: *before or after the account* depending on when the party shows it.
- Account: seven stages, approximately 20 minutes in-fiction.
- Hint 2 delivery: embedded in Stage 3 (the temperature change).
- Hint 3 plant: after Stage 7 (*"There is a man in the sacristy."*).
- Aldric identification: from the ritual focus's mark.
- Dispensation letter: *"She had a paper. Forged. He wrote it for her. She burned it before she began the rite. Or tried to."*

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Morren's response |
|---|---|
| Party enters and sits | He nods. He begins. |
| Party shows the ritual focus first | He identifies it; says Aldric's name; continues to the account. |
| Party asks a question mid-account | He answers it. He continues. Arc does not fire. |
| Party listens without interrupting through all seven stages | He says: *"She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do."* Arc fires. |
| Party asks about Aldric after the account | He describes Aldric: *"He came later. He doesn't join the rite. He writes. He came here because he felt he owed it to be present."* |
| Party asks what she promised | *"She promised some of them she would come back. I heard her say it. She believed it when she said it."* |
| Party asks why he waited to tell this | *"Who was I going to tell? The congregation already knows. They needed to keep faith, not to understand the details. You are the first people who might take the account somewhere."* |

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** The party listens to the full account (all seven stages) without interrupting.

**The act:** After Stage 7, Morren says: *"She did not call it a miracle. She called it the right thing to do."*

He says this once. He looks at his hands. Then he looks at Calla — because she has been the most still during the account, and the most present. He says: *"I hope that's useful to you."*

He does not explain why he said it to Calla specifically. He knows how listening looks. He has been watching people fail to listen for thirty years.

**Why this is the completion:** Morren has told the account to the congregation hundreds of times. The congregation understands it as their foundation story. They do not understand it as evidence. Morren does. The account is evidence of what Velantha chose, how she chose it, and what she knew she was choosing. He has been waiting for someone who would treat it as evidence rather than as myth. When the party listens without interrupting, he knows they are treating it as evidence. The arc closes with the line the congregation does not have: *"She called it the right thing to do."* This is Velantha's own framing, reported by the only witness, delivered to the people who will carry it forward.
