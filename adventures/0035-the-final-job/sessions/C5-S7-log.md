# C5-S7 Session Log — The Final Job

**Date in world:** PC 188, nineteen days after C5-S6. Three days after the audit.
**Spotlight:** Ensemble finale
**Seed:** C5S7-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro's picks. Pre-session. Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond. He had been doing this for six years. He had done it every session of this campaign. The naming was the same. The order was the same. The three minutes were the same. He rolled them and set the roll on his hip.

The difference was that tonight he did it standing, not sitting. The session required all four picks.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly opened the ledger to a clean page. This was not the C4-2 page. This was the page after the last entry. She wrote the header: *0035 / PC 188 / the-guild / the-final-job.* Then she set the pen down. She was writing the entry before she knew what it would contain. This was how she always started.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira checked the paper case. Then she opened it. She took out six pages of framework notes — the accumulation of six sessions of silent preparation. She set them on the table.

This was the first time she had opened the paper case fully in front of the party.

She said: *"I have it."*

---

## Scene 1 — The Brief (Mira's Reveal)

Tessaly had been describing the vault — the service stair, the lock type, the overnight guard rotation — when Mira said it.

She put the six pages on the table beside the floor plan. She did not explain the accumulation. She explained what the false dossier would say.

*"The dossier will describe the party as a professional fencing operation. No intelligence-gathering component. Cover identities only — the three we have been using for the last three months. False client records: Nenne Ashwick's business structure used as the model for two false clients, modified enough that Nenne is not identifiable. Bessa Tor as a false reference (she will not contradict it). The date structure from the C4-2 era formatting convention — specifically the pre-Pale register, which Pale will recognize as pre-dating his access to Tessaly's records. It will look like a record of a low-risk operation from before he was watching."*

She answered questions for five minutes. She did not explain when she had built the framework.

**Act-without-announcement delivery (RV4):** She had been acting throughout six sessions. She announced at the moment of use. This is the RV4 completion: the act was the building; the announcement was the deployment.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Before she opened the paper case to work: she checked it was there. The check had become the thing before the work, the way Darro's naming was the thing before the lock. She had not noticed herself developing this sequence. It was professional now.

---

## Scene 2 — Final Forgery

Four hours. Mira worked.

First attempt, DC 16 (needs to pass Pale's active Investigation if he runs it): **d20+1 → rolls=[4] mod=+1 total=5**. The structural parallel was wrong — she had used the pre-Pale formatting convention correctly, but one of the false client records had an entry code that did not match the era. She caught it when she re-read the third page. Not subtle.

*"The client code is wrong,"* she said. She showed Tessaly the page. *"I used the current code structure on the pre-Pale era date. He will find this if he looks."*

She was not distressed. She was solving a problem.

Second attempt, DC 12 (partial success — passes passive 14, visible at active DC 16): **d20+1 → rolls=[17] mod=+1 total=18**. The correction fixed the client code. The dossier was complete but imperfect. It would pass Pale's passive review. If he ran an active check, he would find the inconsistency within a week.

*"Partial,"* Tessaly said.

*"Partial."* Mira looked at the document. *"It is the best I have."*

*"We need the RI correspondence,"* Cael said. He had been sitting at the table for three hours, doing nothing visible, which for him meant he had been calculating the route options. *"If Pale faces an investigation from Revenue Intelligence, he will not run an active check on a fencing dossier."*

He did not explain how he had arrived at this. It was the professional logic. Tessaly wrote it before she responded.

MARTEN-7 is a professional operation within normal guild fencing parameters. Independence risk: low. Revenue Intelligence value: none. Recommend: file inactive. No further monitoring required.

*End verbatim dossier. Mira Calloway, working as Calloway of the Silken Ledger. The dossier was placed in Orren Pale's operational vault in the same slot as the real dossier on the same night.*

## Scene 3 — The Approach

After midnight. The shipping concern, locked. Darro: alley door, DC 12. **d20+8 → rolls=[16] mod=+8 total=24**. Clean. Service stair, counting-room corridor. Guard rotation: 25 minutes. Tonight the window was 22 minutes: **d20+3 → rolls=[7] mod=+3 total=10** (Cael read the timing as 22 minutes, not 25; slightly tight but manageable).

Vault door, DC 15. **d20+8 → rolls=[11] mod=+8 total=19**. Clean.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael positioned himself between the party and the street exit. Left hand flat to chain mail. The contract. He was at the commitment point of the final job and the contract with Tessaly specified the terms. He knew the terms. He had checked.

---

## Scene 4 — The Vault

Darro opened the vault. Shelves of operational dossiers, alphabetical by operation code. The party's file: MARTEN-7.

Tessaly removed the real dossier. Mira placed the false dossier in its slot. Darro relatched the vault door.

**Route B — the RI correspondence:** The secondary lockbox. DC 14 to identify (Tessaly, Investigation +7, passive from the vault layout): **d20+7 → rolls=[12] mod=+7 total=19**. Found it. DC 13 to open: Darro, standard ward. Automatic with expertise.

The correspondence: three letters, dated over the last four months. Revenue Intelligence, addressed to Pale by his guild name, discussing the "MARTEN-7 network" — the party's cover identities — and Pale's terms for providing ongoing intelligence. The most recent letter: *"Pending cooperation contract — 90 days for execution. Non-compliance category: disposal."*

Eight additional minutes inside the vault. Guard circuit: 22 minutes. They had fourteen. They left with seven to spare.

---

## Scene 5 — Street

Out before the guard completed the circuit. Three blocks to the dispersion point. Party split by plan: Tessaly and Cael east, Darro and Mira west.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro, on the walk to the meeting point, counted the pick set. Aloud, quiet. *"Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond."* He had done this every session after a job that used all four. The picks were fine.

The job had used all four.

---

## Scene 6 — Route Resolution

Secondary location. Not Cordwainer Lane.

Tessaly read the real dossier before she showed it to the party — a single pass, professional. She wrote one entry in her ledger. Then she showed it.

Tessaly Investigation on real dossier: **d20+7 → rolls=[12] mod=+7 total=19**. She read it completely, including the "disposal" note. She read it the way she reads everything: for what is present, not for what she expected.

*"He was going to burn us."* She said it without intonation. Not anger — fact. *"Pending cooperation contract, 90 days. Non-compliance: disposal. The 90 days started three weeks ago. We have nine weeks."*

She closed the ledger. She handed the real dossier to Cael. Because the contract at his breast pocket was the measure against which Pale's "disposal" note should be read. He had a contract. Pale had offered a contract by different means. He took the dossier.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael read Pale's disposal note. He read it the way you read a document that contradicts the terms of an agreement you entered in good faith. He set the dossier back on the table. He said: *"Rue gets the correspondence tonight."*

Not a question. He touched the contract in his breast pocket — the check. The terms he had agreed to did not include Pale's disposal clause. He was adjusting the terms.

**Route B confirmed.** Rue Ashen received the Revenue Intelligence correspondence that night via the Night Market channel. Tessaly delivered it. Rue was at the lamp-oil stall. She read the letter without opening it to the light, the same way she had read the note in S1.

*"This will take ten days,"* Rue said. *"He will not be guild master by month's end."*

---

## Scene 7 — Campaign Close

Route B close. The Harbor Authority investigation opened within the week. Pale was removed from the guild master position before month's end, as Rue had said. The Silken Ledger continued under different leadership.

Tessaly was at a window in a coffeehouse on the Merchant Quarter when the announcement appeared in the harbor registry. She read it with the specific expression of someone who has documentation of exactly what happened. She had the ledger open to the final entry. She did not show it to anyone.

The final entry: *0035 / PC 188 / the-guild / the-final-job / route B / complete. The ledger is accurate. The record stands.*

She closed the ledger. She put it in the satchel. She walked.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro, the next morning, at the secondary location. Morning light. He laid out the pick roll. *"Tension wrench."* He lifted it, turned it once. *"Hook."* The second. *"Short hook. Diamond."* He went through all twelve. He named each one. He rolled them back into the sleeve. He set it on his left hip.

The campaign was over. The picks were fine.
