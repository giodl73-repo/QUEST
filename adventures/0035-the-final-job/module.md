## Setting & Background

The party steals Orren Pale's dossier on them and replaces it with Mira's false version — a forgery built from five sessions of accumulated professional work — and the session-runner logs the false dossier verbatim.

**Orren Pale's operations vault**, below the shipping concern where the audit occurred. The party did not know about the vault in S6. Rue Ashen confirmed its existence the day before the session, along with its lock type and the rotation of the overnight guard. The dossier on the party is in the vault. The party has three days before Pale decides what he saw on page seven.

Tessaly's brief, three days after S6: the vault is below the shipping concern, accessible via a service stair from the counting-room corridor. It contains one active dossier per ongoing operation. The party's dossier has everything Pale has collected: the Nenne job, the Aldras extraction, the burned-house investigation, the test-assignment refusal. It names all four party members.

If Pale delivers that dossier to Revenue Intelligence, the party's operations in Havenmere are finished. If the dossier is replaced with Mira's false version, Pale's Revenue Intelligence reports will be based on wrong information. The party exits the frame.

**The false dossier:** Mira has been building it since S3. It exists in the paper case as six pages of notes and frameworks: Tessaly's signature characteristics, Nenne's account structure (as model for false client records), the forgery methodology accumulated across four sessions. She produces the final document the morning of the session. The session-runner logs it verbatim — what the false dossier claims, in Mira's hand.

The false dossier is the campaign's primary PC deliverable. Its content is authored by the player running Mira. The session-runner records it verbatim in the session log. The false dossier must:
- Match Tessaly's formatting conventions (she knows Pale's files; the false dossier should look like a real Pale operational file)
- Describe the party as a professional fencing operation with no intelligence-gathering component
- Include false client names and false job records that will withstand Revenue Intelligence's preliminary review
- Not include any of the four party members' real names — only their cover identities for the last three months of operations

The session-runner does not edit or revise the false dossier. Whatever Mira's player writes is verbatim. The "specific claims" are the session log's canonical record.

**Route A (clean extraction):** The party replaces the dossier, exits clean, and Pale discovers the substitution within the week but cannot determine who did it. The party is no longer in the frame; his Revenue Intelligence reports are wrong. The guild continues without them.

**Route B (extraction + evidence):** The party replaces the dossier AND copies the Revenue Intelligence correspondence from Pale's vault (confirming the sell-out). They provide the copy to Rue Ashen, who has contacts at the legitimate Harbor Authority. Pale faces an investigation. He is removed from the guild master position. The Silken Ledger continues under different leadership.

**Route E (professional dissolution):** The party replaces the dossier, burns the vault's operational records completely (destroying Pale's leverage over all current network members including themselves), and leaves Havenmere. The guild dissolves without its operational backbone. The members who held markers scatter. The party is gone. No trail.

### Scene 1 — The Brief (Mira's Reveal)
Tessaly's briefing begins normally. She describes the vault, the lock type, the overnight guard rotation. Then she says: "The false dossier needs to be complete by 2 bells tonight."

Mira opens the paper case. She takes out six pages of framework notes. She says: *"I have it."*

This is the first time she has told the party about the accumulation. She presents the framework without explaining when she built it. She explains what it will say (the cover identities, the false client records, the formatting conventions). She answers questions. She does not explain the accumulation's history across sessions.

**DM note (act-without-announcement delivery):** Mira has been building this for four sessions without announcement. The reveal in Scene 1 is the first announcement. This is the act-without-announcement completing its arc: she acted throughout, announced at the moment of use.

**The paper case check:** She checks it before she opens it. Automatic, pre-announcement.

### Scene 2 — Final Forgery
Four hours. Mira produces the complete false dossier — the actual PC-authored document, based on her framework. This is the genuine skill moment: she is producing a document she has been building toward for the entire campaign.

Forgery Kit check (INT): DC 16 (Pale's Investigation is +7 active; this needs to pass his passive 14 and survive an active check of DC 17). At INT +1, she needs 15 on the die for DC 16. The die is real. The false dossier's quality is determined by the roll.

On success (DC 16): the false dossier is perfect — it will pass Pale's active investigation.
On partial success (DC 12-15): the dossier will pass passive 14 but has a flaw detectable at DC 16. Pale might find it.
On failure (below DC 12): the dossier has a visible flaw. Pale will find it on review. The party will need to act before he does.

**The session-runner logs the dossier verbatim** after the player produces it.

### Scene 3 — The Approach
After midnight. The shipping concern is locked. The service stair entrance is via an alley door (Thieves' Tools DC 12; Darro: DC 8). The counting-room corridor above the vault: overnight guard, single rotation every 25 minutes. One guard, armed.

**Darro's sequence:** Alley door, service stair, counting-room corridor while guard is in the far room, vault door (DC 15 — a proper security lock; Darro at DC 11 with expertise). Time per step: approximately 3 minutes. The 25-minute window is generous if nothing goes wrong.

**Cael's position:** He is between the party and the street exit. Standard. Left hand to chest — contract check — withdrawn. He is at the commitment point of the final job.

### Scene 4 — The Vault
Darro opens the vault door. Inside: shelves of operational dossiers, organized alphabetically by operation code. The party's file is under the code they have been using for three months (Tessaly knows it; she assigned it). It is exactly where it should be.

**The dossier exchange.** Tessaly removes the real dossier. Mira places the false dossier in its slot. The real dossier goes into Tessaly's satchel. Darro relatches the vault door.

**Route B opportunity:** Rue's intelligence mentioned that Revenue Intelligence correspondence is in a separate lockbox within the vault (DC 14 to identify, DC 13 to open). If the party goes for it: 8 additional minutes inside the vault. The guard returns in 15. Possible — tight.

**Route E opportunity:** A small incendiary exists for destroying vault contents (professional kit, standard guild inventory; Tessaly has it). Using it makes noise. It also makes the substitution undetectable because there is no surviving dossier to compare. The vault fire will be visible from the street within 4 minutes.

### Scene 5 — The Exit
They are out before the guard completes the circuit. Street. Three blocks to the nearest dispersion point. The party splits by plan (Tessaly and Cael, Darro and Mira; they do not walk to Cordwainer Lane together tonight).

**Darro's check.** He counts the pick set on the walk. "Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond." Aloud, quiet. Not for anyone's benefit — this is what he does after a job that used all four. The job used all four.

### Scene 6 — Route Resolution
The party convenes at a secondary location (not Cordwainer Lane — a neutral meeting point agreed in advance). Tessaly has the real dossier. She reads it before the party sees it — a single pass, quick, professional. She writes one entry in her ledger. Then she shows it.

**The real dossier's content:** Everything Pale has on them. All four names. The Nenne job. The Aldras extraction. The burned-house visit. The test-assignment refusal. Pale's assessment: *"Operational reliability: high. Independence risk: elevated. Revenue Intelligence value: contingent on continued cooperation."* The last line: *"Recommend for mandatory cooperation contract — 90 days. If refused, transfer to disposal."*

Tessaly closes the ledger. She says: *"He was going to burn us."* She hands the real dossier to Cael, because the contract at his breast pocket is the measure against which Pale's "disposal" note should be read.

### Scene 7 — Campaign Close
Route-dependent close. All routes are valid. The session-runner runs the applicable close scene.

**Route A:** The party splits. The guild continues without them. They are no longer in the frame. Tessaly's ledger has its final entry: the job number, the date, the word "complete." She closes the ledger for the last time. She puts it in the satchel. She walks.

**Route B:** Rue receives the Revenue Intelligence correspondence. The Harbor Authority investigation opens. Pale is removed within the month. Tessaly watches the announcement with the specific expression of someone who has documentation of exactly what happened. She does not show it to anyone.

**Route E:** The vault burned. The guild's operational records are gone. The members scatter. Tessaly's ledger is the most complete record of the Silken Ledger's operations in existence — including the four Pale entries, and now the vault burn. She has not decided what to do with it. She probably won't destroy it. She never destroys accurate records.

**Final image (all routes):** Darro maintains his picks at whatever secondary location they end up at. Morning. *"Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond."* The picks are fine. The job is done.

# Combat Encounter — Vault Guard (Early Return)

## Trigger

The guard's circuit is 25 minutes. The party has 20 minutes in the vault (generous margin). This encounter fires only if: the pressure table produces an early-return event AND the party has not yet exited the vault corridor when the guard completes the circuit early. Probability: low (requires two independent unfavorable events).

## Composition

**One Guild Guard** — Thug stat block (SRD, CR 1/2)

- **AC** 11 (leather) | **HP** 32 (5d8+10) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Skills:** Perception +2
- **Passive Perception** 12
- **Actions:** Club +4, 1d6+2 × 2 (multiattack). Heavy crossbow +2, 1d10.
- **XP:** 100

The guard is not expecting to find anyone. He has done this circuit for six months without incident. His first response to hearing something unexpected is to check the source, not to call for backup. He calls backup (rings a bell at the far-end office) only after he has confirmed there is actually something there — approximately 1 round of investigation before the call.

## Tactics

Round 1: He investigates the noise. This gives the party one round to hide or decide. Darro's Stealth (+8) makes hiding trivially possible; Cael's armor makes hiding difficult (Stealth +0 in chain mail). Tessaly's Deception is the social option if he sees them.

**Social resolution (preferred):** Mira's Deception DC 15 provides a cover story (they are reviewing operational records per Pale's direct request; she was just sent with the key). On success: he checks the story against a plausible alternative (did Pale tell him about a late-night review?). Insight DC 12 from him — he does not have a strong reason to disbelieve. He lets them continue. He will mention it to Pale.

**Combat:** If it comes to this — Cael holds the corridor, Darro and Mira exit via the drain (Athletics DC 12 to squeeze). Tessaly exits behind Cael. The guard is alone and not suicidal. Morale breaks if Cael drops him below 15 HP cleanly.

## Encounter notes

This encounter should not fire in normal play. The design intent is a clean extraction. The guard is here because the campaign has always had a combat encounter in every session and this one should too — but it is designed to be entirely avoidable with the planning the party has done.

**XP budget:** 100. Easy difficulty. Designed for a one-round resolution or a single Deception check.
