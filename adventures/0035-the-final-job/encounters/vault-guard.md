# Combat Encounter — Vault Guard (Early Return)

## Trigger

The guard's circuit is 25 minutes. The party has 20 minutes in the vault (generous margin). This encounter fires only if: the pressure table produces an early-return event AND the party has not yet exited the vault corridor when the guard completes the circuit early. Probability: low (requires two independent unfavorable events).

## Composition

**One Guild Guard** — Thug stat block (SRD, CR 1/2)

- **AC** 11 (leather) | **HP** 32 (5d8+10) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Skills:** Perception +2
- **Passive Perception** 12
- **Actions:** Club +4, 1d6+2 × 2 (multiattack). Heavy crossbow +2, 1d10.
- **XP:** 100

The guard is not expecting to find anyone. He has done this circuit for six months without incident. His first response to hearing something unexpected is to check the source, not to call for backup. He calls backup (rings a bell at the far-end office) only after he has confirmed there is actually something there — approximately 1 round of investigation before the call.

## Tactics

Round 1: He investigates the noise. This gives the party one round to hide or decide. Darro's Stealth (+8) makes hiding trivially possible; Cael's armor makes hiding difficult (Stealth +0 in chain mail). Tessaly's Deception is the social option if he sees them.

**Social resolution (preferred):** Mira's Deception DC 15 provides a cover story (they are reviewing operational records per Pale's direct request; she was just sent with the key). On success: he checks the story against a plausible alternative (did Pale tell him about a late-night review?). Insight DC 12 from him — he does not have a strong reason to disbelieve. He lets them continue. He will mention it to Pale.

**Combat:** If it comes to this — Cael holds the corridor, Darro and Mira exit via the drain (Athletics DC 12 to squeeze). Tessaly exits behind Cael. The guard is alone and not suicidal. Morale breaks if Cael drops him below 15 HP cleanly.

## Encounter notes

This encounter should not fire in normal play. The design intent is a clean extraction. The guard is here because the campaign has always had a combat encounter in every session and this one should too — but it is designed to be entirely avoidable with the planning the party has done.

**XP budget:** 100. Easy difficulty. Designed for a one-round resolution or a single Deception check.
