# Room 02 — Forgery Workspace (The Final Document)

## Read-aloud
> West window. The door locked. Mira spreads the framework on the left, blank paper on the right, the reference signatures arranged above. She does not look up when she begins.

## Features

- **The four-hour work.** This is the final and most demanding forgery of the campaign. DC 16 check (INT; she needs 15 on the die). The session-runner calls for the roll before the player produces the document — the roll determines quality, but the player produces the document regardless.
- **The player prompt.** Session-runner addresses the player directly: *"Mira has been building this for four sessions. What does the false dossier say?"* The player's answer is logged verbatim.
- **The document's required elements.** The false dossier must: (1) Use Tessaly's formatting conventions (the player should describe these based on what the character would know). (2) Name the party's cover identities only — no real names. (3) Include false client names and false job records. (4) Describe the party as a fencing operation, not an intelligence-gathering one.
- **Tessaly does not watch this time.** She is at the main desk preparing the operational brief. She trusts Mira to get it right. She does not supervise. This is the professional trust the campaign has been building.

## GM Notes

The final forgery is the campaign's culminating PC deliverable. The Forgery Kit check determines whether it will pass Pale's scrutiny. The player's authorship determines what it claims.

If the check succeeds (DC 16): the false dossier is perfect. Log it.
If partial success (DC 12-15): the dossier has one flaw. It will survive passive scrutiny (Pale's passive 14) but not active investigation (if Pale rolls Investigation with +7, he needs 10 for DC 17 — a real risk). Log it with a note about the flaw.
If failure (below DC 12): the dossier has a visible flaw detectable at DC 13. Pale will notice on review. The party must exit before he reads it. Log it and adjust the encounter.

**The verbatim log.** Whatever the player says is in the session log. The session-runner does not edit it. If the player gives the false dossier specific claims — fake names, fake jobs, fake clients, fake operational history — those claims become campaign-permanent. They are the false dossier. Pale believes them. Revenue Intelligence acts on them.
