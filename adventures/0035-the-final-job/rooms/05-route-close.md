# Room 05 — Campaign Close (Route-Dependent)

## Read-aloud (Route A)
> Secondary meeting point. The real dossier on the table. Tessaly reads it before she shows it. She writes one entry. She closes the ledger. She says: "He was going to burn us." She hands the dossier to Cael.

## Read-aloud (Route B)
> Rue Ashen opens the leather folder from the vault. She reads one letter. She looks at Tessaly. She says: "I can use this." She does not say thank you. She is already thinking about who to call.

## Read-aloud (Route E)
> The smoke from the shipping concern's sub-level reaches the street at 4 bells. The party is six blocks away. Tessaly's ledger is the only complete record of the Silken Ledger that now exists. She has not decided what to do with it. She probably won't destroy it. She never destroys accurate records.

## Features (all routes)

- **The real dossier.** Pale's assessment: the party members' real names, the four-month operation record, and the note: *"Recommend for mandatory cooperation contract — 90 days. If refused, transfer to disposal."* Tessaly reads this before showing the party. She writes the entry first. Then she shows it.
- **Cael receives the dossier.** Tessaly gives it to him specifically. He holds it. Left hand to chest — contract. He reads "disposal." He puts the dossier in his satchel. He says nothing. He will deal with it the way he deals with everything: not immediately, not dramatically, correctly.
- **Darro's pick maintenance.** Morning, wherever they end up. *"Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond."* The picks are fine. The job is done.
- **Tessaly's final ledger entry.** Date. Job number. The word "complete." She closes the ledger. She puts it in the satchel. She walks.
- **Mira's paper case.** She checks it before she moves. The false dossier framework is still in there — she left the working notes, not the final document. She could build another one. This is not something she announces. She puts the case away.

## GM Notes

The close is route-dependent in its consequences but consistent in its character beats. All four loyalty objects appear in their final form:
- Tessaly's ledger: final entry, closed, satchel.
- Darro's picks: morning maintenance, final naming.
- Mira's paper case: checked, replaced.
- Cael's contract: the disposal note is the contract's measuring moment. He holds the dossier because the contract and the disposal note belong in the same hands.

The "he was going to burn us" line is the campaign's emotional revelation — not grief, not loss, professional recognition: Pale was going to sell them out. The code they each hold independently (don't sell a name) is what Pale was preparing to violate. The finale is not about stopping him from burning the guild. It is about exiting the frame before he does.

The campaign closes on Darro maintaining his picks. Not because it is dramatic — because it is what he does. The campaign began with professional-code PCs. It ends with them being professional.
