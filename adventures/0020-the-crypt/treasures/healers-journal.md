# Velantha's Healer's Journal (Final Volume)

*A leather-bound volume, worn at the corners, with a final entry that says something different depending on who is reading it.*

## Appearance

Standard healer's field journal, similar to the kind any traveling practitioner would use. Leather cover, thread binding. The spine is worn; she opened it many times. Approximately two-thirds full. The entries are short — dates, patient names, what she administered, what helped. The final entry is dated the night before the ritual. It is brief. It is also, under certain conditions, different from what it was.

The journal is slightly warm. Not as warm as the field satchel — the satchel holds thirty years of congregation veneration. The journal holds thirty years of waiting in a crypt for someone to come and read it. That is a different kind of warmth.

## Presence / Desire

The journal wants to be read by someone who understands what it says. Not the medical entries — those are clear. The last entry. It has been waiting thirty years for a reader who has made the choice it describes. When such a reader holds it, the last entry changes. The journal does not do this to the reader. It does this because the last entry was written to be received, and it has been waiting for reception.

## Provenance

**1. Velantha (used ~20 years; placed in the crypt the night she died).**
She placed it on the shelf herself — the last thing she did before beginning the ritual. She had written the final entry. She knew she might not come back. She wanted the entry to reach whoever came looking.

**2. The congregation (left it on the shelf for 30 years; never read it).**
The congregation knows the journal is there. They do not read it. It is Velantha's. They completed the crypt around it. They maintained the crypt. They did not touch the journal. It has been on the shelf in the side alcove for thirty years.

**3. Whoever reads it in S6.** The journal has been waiting for this.

## Properties

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a creature that has made a choice knowing the cost — knowing they were acting without institutional sanction, for the sake of someone the world had decided was not worth the act.

Qualifying creatures in the party: Maret (practiced without permission), Renn (pact without Orders' sanction), Dren (named Aldis when he could have stayed silent), Calla (corrected the imperfect rite from her own knowledge).

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Memory-keeper.** The attuned creature has advantage on Intelligence checks to recall the content of any oral account they have personally received (Morren's account for Calla; Velantha's account for whoever receives it here).
- **Once per long rest:** The attuned creature may speak a sentence they have been holding — a held observation, a held name, a held truth — and it comes out exactly as they held it. The sentence does not change in the speaking. This is the journal's gift: one exact transmission per day.

### The Final Entry (H4 Delivery)

The final entry reads differently depending on the reader:

**Standard reading (passive Perception < 15):**
*"Night before. I have the working. I have the authorization. I have decided. I will begin at dawn."*

**H4 reading (passive Perception ≥ 15; or DC 15 Perception/Insight for lower passive):**
*"I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else."*

The H4 reading is the journal's last entry when read by someone capable of receiving it. Maret (passive 17) auto-detects the rewrite. The entry does not change permanently — it returns to the standard reading after the H4 reader sets the journal down. It will rewrite once more at the keystone (0021), if the right reader is present.

### Relic Test

No traditional relic test. The journal is not capable of being misused — it is a record. Using it as a weapon is possible only in the most absurd sense (it could be thrown). The journal's "test" is simply: does the reader receive what it says? There is no relic consequence for a reader who cannot receive the H4 entry; they simply read the standard text.

## Hooks

1. **Calla's deliverable.** Velantha gives Calla the journal and says: "Someone should speak for what I actually did." The journal is the source material for the PC-authored finale deliverable. Calla holds it, along with Morren's oral account and Aldric's written copy. Three sources. One synthesis. 0021.

2. **The journal rewrites at the keystone.** If the party is at the keystone (0021) and the right reader holds the journal, the H4 entry rewrites once more. The new version at the keystone will be slightly different — Velantha's account updated by thirty-one years of crypt time, slightly more certain. This is the finale's confirmation.

3. **Once-per-day exact transmission.** Calla can speak one sentence exactly as she received it — from Morren, from Aldric, from Velantha. Once per day. She has one chance per session to speak exactly what she was given. This is the verbal-documentation-arc's resolution mechanic: the arc completes when she speaks the synthesis, and the journal gives her one exact sentence as the seed.

## GM Notes

The journal is the most personal relic in the campaign. Handle it last — give the party time to explore the Crypt, hear Velantha, ask what she wants, before Velantha gives Calla the journal as a close.

When Maret reads the final entry and the H4 version appears: do not announce it. Let Maret's player (or the DM playing Maret) read it. The DM waits. Whatever Maret does or says: that is the session's final beat before the close.
