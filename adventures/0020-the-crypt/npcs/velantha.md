# Velantha

## Appearance

She appears as she was: approximately 45 years old, a healer of twenty years, someone who carried a field satchel on every house call and asked her patients' permission before administering anything. She is not theatrical. She does not glow or speak in echoes. She is simply present, in the finished crypt, in the sourceless light the congregation provided.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. Present. She cannot be harmed; she is already dead. She cannot be dismissed; she has been here for thirty years and she leaves when she chooses.

## Public Knowledge

- She died here the night she used the working.
- She knew it was unauthorized. She did it anyway.
- She is not angry about the false miracle narrative. She is disappointed. She never asked for a cathedral.
- The journal is on the shelf in the alcove. She put it there.
- She wants the gully dwarves to have somewhere that is theirs.

## Private Knowledge

- She knew Aldric would be too cowardly to correct the story. She forgave him for it. She has had thirty years.
- She is not certain the building is right for a Mishakal crypt — the congregation's wrong-light glass, the unauthorized rite, the gully dwarves the theology excludes. But she is certain the faith is real. She does not know what Mishakal thinks. She suspects Mishakal would approve of the ethics even if not the liturgy.
- She softened one thing in what she told Morren. She told him she was sorry about the promise she couldn't keep. She did not tell him she would make the same choice again. The journal's last entry says she would.

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Velantha's response |
|---|---|
| Party enters | *"You came far enough. I wasn't sure anyone would."* |
| Party asks what happened | She gives Morren's account, confirmed. No new information — confirms what they received. |
| Party asks about the promise | *"I promised some of them I would come back. I died instead. I am sorry for that. The healing was real."* |
| Party asks what she regrets | *"I regret the silence. I should have told them it was sorcery. I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong."* |
| Party asks what she wants | **Arc-completion fires.** |
| Renn says the pact line | She looks at him. *"Yes."* One word. Same as Aldric. She understands what he is. |
| Calla is present with all three accounts | Near the end: *"Someone should speak for what I actually did. Not what they've built up there. What I did."* She gives Calla the journal. |
| Party asks about Aldric | *"He was a coward about the Orders and a brave person about gully dwarves. Both things are true."* |

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** Party asks what Velantha wants — not what happened, not why she did it, but what she wants *now*, for the future.

**The act:** *"I want the gully dwarves to have somewhere that is theirs. They kept faith for thirty years in a building they didn't build. They deserve the building."*

She says this without drama. It is not a demand. It is an answer to a question. She does not ask the party to agree. She asks them to choose. Her arc completes because she has waited thirty years to say this to someone who might be in a position to act on it. The party is in that position. The arc is complete whether they agree with her or not.

**After the arc-completion:** She gives Calla the journal. She does not speak further unless the party has more questions. She is done. What happens at the keystone is not her session; it is theirs.
