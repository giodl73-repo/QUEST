# Room 04 — Journal Alcove

## Read-aloud

> A small side alcove. A stone shelf with a single leather-bound journal on it, spine out. The journal is warm to the touch. On the shelf beneath it: a dried herb — the last herb from the last thing she treated before she came to the gully dwarf settlement. It has been there for thirty years.

*(55 words)*

## Features

- **Velantha's healer's journal** (stone shelf). See `treasures/healers-journal.md`. Warm. Last entry rewrites for qualifying readers.

- **Dried herb** (under the journal). The last herb from her kit before the ritual. The field satchel holds the herbs she carried *to* the gully dwarf settlement; this one herb is from the last patient she treated *before* she went. She placed it here. **DC 13 Nature** to identify: it is a common cold remedy. She was treating someone for a winter illness the day before she went to the settlement. She treated a human patient's winter cold and then went to heal a gully dwarf community's plague. The herb makes the scale visible.

- **Journal warmth** (DC 10 Perception from within 5 ft; Maret passive 17 auto-detects from R3 entrance).

## Encounter

**Hint 4 delivery:** When a qualifying reader reads the final entry — specifically Maret (passive 17, qualifies for rewrite). The H4 text appears. The DM lets the reader see it and waits.

**What happens after H4:**

Maret reads: *"I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else."*

She holds the journal. She does not speak. She takes three breaths — her morning pattern. She hands the journal to Calla.

*She is now someone who has read all four hints. She has been carrying the campaign's factual foundation since S1 (warmth of the satchel). She received H2 in S4. She received H3 in S5. She reads H4 here. The complete picture has been received.*

## Connections

- **West:** R3 (The Crypt)

## GM Notes

The dried herb is the session's most understated object. It is the scale of what Velantha was doing in her daily life — common cold remedies, routine house calls — and the scale of what she chose to do outside it. She was a normal practitioner who made one extraordinary decision.

### Memory Fragment
*She left the herb there because she was going to pick it up on the way back. She believed she was coming back. The herb has been waiting for thirty years for the errand to be completed.*
