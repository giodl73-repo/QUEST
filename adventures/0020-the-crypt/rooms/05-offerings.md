# Room 05 — The Offering Wall

## Read-aloud

> A passage off the main crypt. One wall is covered in placed objects: smooth stones, cloth pieces, small carvings, a child's drawing, a knotted cord fragment, a dried flower. Thirty years of the congregation's offerings to the person they honored here. Each one placed deliberately. None of them labelled.

*(50 words)*

## Features

- **Congregation offerings** (wall, thirty years of them). Every significant object the congregation has placed here. The child's drawing from the nave aisle in S2 (R3 of 0016) has a twin here — the same child made two, one for the nave and one for the crypt. The knotted cord fragment: a piece of the bell rope, placed when a congregation member died. The dried flower: from the gully dwarf settlement's hills, placed by someone who walked back to get it.

- **Scale of the collection** (DC 10 Investigation to estimate how many objects). Approximately 150 objects over thirty years, from a congregation that never numbered more than 50. More than three objects per person, placed over time. This is not a one-time tribute. It is continuous practice.

## Encounter

None. Stuart room — atmospheric.

**Calla's documentation-suspension final activation:** She is standing in thirty years of the congregation's expression of something she cannot name. She has been building toward naming it since S1. She is not there yet — but she is in the room that contains all the evidence she needs. After she leaves here, she begins composing. The verbal-documentation-arc is entering Stage 3.
