## Setting & Background

**Place:** The Crypt of the Halted Spire — the only finished room in the building. The congregation completed it over thirty years: correct vaulting, correct proportions, correctly-laid stone. A Mishakal crypt as it should be. Clean, lit by a sourceless glow from a smooth stone the congregation placed at the center, and quiet in a way that the other sections of the building are not.

Velantha is here. Not a ghost — the congregation does not use that word. She is present. She has been present since she died here, in the space that the congregation built to honor her, and she has been waiting for thirty years for someone to come far enough to ask her what she wants.

**Atmosphere:** The crypt smells of clean stone and nothing else. No herbs, no tallow, no ink. After five sessions of navigating the building's smell-map, the absence of those smells is itself atmospheric. This room has been kept. The sourceless light is steady. The journal is on a stone shelf in a side alcove, spine out.

Aldric said: *"She left something in the crypt. I've never gone. I thought it wasn't my place."*

The party enters. Velantha is present. She says: *"You came far enough. I wasn't sure anyone would."*

She speaks. She answers. She has been waiting thirty years for this conversation.

**Velantha's healer's journal** — the final volume. The last entry is the night before the ritual. It rewrites itself when read by someone who has made the specific choice Velantha made: knowing the cost, acting without permission, for the sake of someone the world has decided is not worth it.

Full artifact file: `treasures/healers-journal.md`
Cross-skill manifest: `treasures/healers-journal-manifest.md`

Velantha is present as she was: approximately 45 years old at death, a healer of twenty years, someone who asked permission from her patients and did not ask it from the Orders. She is not performed or theatrical. She is direct.

She has been in this crypt for thirty years with nothing to do but think about what she did and what it cost. She is not angry. She is not grieving. She is past the acute phase of both. She is in the specific state of someone who has processed something enormous and arrived at clarity.

What she wants: the gully dwarves to have somewhere that is theirs. She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine. The cathedral happened. She has accepted that it happened. She would like the people she healed to have it.

Her arc-completion fires when the party asks her what she wants *now* — not what happened, not why she did it, but what she wants for the future. *"I want the gully dwarves to have somewhere that is theirs. They kept faith for thirty years in a building they didn't build. They deserve the building."* This is the Route D argument in her own words.

**Hint 4 (auto-delivery, passive 15):** When the journal is read, the last entry rewrites. The new text:

*"I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else."*

**Delivery mechanism:** Auto-delivery when a PC reads the final entry with passive Perception ≥ 15. Maret (passive 17) auto-detects. If no PC is present with passive ≥ 15: DC 15 Perception or Insight to notice the entry has changed. Recovery path in 0021: if unread here, the entry rewrites again at the keystone when the right reader is present.

When Velantha speaks about the cost of her choice — *"I knew what I was spending. I knew it was not sanctioned. I spent it anyway."* — Renn has the entry-point: *"She used something that wasn't prayer. I know what that costs."*

He said this to Aldric via Awakened Mind in S5. He can say it to the party here. The difference: saying it in the Crypt, in front of Velantha, in front of Calla who holds the account, is different from saying it silently to one person. If he says it here, the party knows. They understand Renn's pact. The session log records it.

Whether he says it or not: the doubt-die determines this. The scene is designed to create the conditions. The choice is Renn's.

Velantha speaks to Calla directly near the end of the session:
*"Someone should speak for what I actually did. Not what they've built up there. What I did."*

She gives Calla the journal.

*"You've been carrying both accounts. You know what they don't say. Speak for the one they're missing."*

This is the PC-authored finale deliverable's structural invitation. Calla begins composing in the handoff — she does not speak it here. It is noted: "Calla is composing."

- **velanthas-unkept-promise** (0017): Velantha addresses it — *"I promised some of them I would come back. I died instead. I am sorry for that. The healing was real."*
- **aldrics-written-copies** (0019): Velantha confirms Aldric's account is mostly right — *"He softened one thing. I wasn't sorry about the working. I was sorry about the silence."*

- **journal-rewrites-for-right-reader** (→ 0021): the journal will rewrite once more at the keystone
- **velathas-answer-to-unkept-promise** (→ 0021): *"I knew I couldn't. I did it anyway. That is what healing means."*
- **Calla is composing** (→ 0021): noted in handoff

The Crypt's cold: the congregation kept the crypt cool. It is noticeably colder than the rest of the building (DC 8 Con save on entry; **1d4 cold damage** on a failure — no serious harm, but the first physical cost of simply being here). This satisfies the design mandate for a resource cost in 0020.

# Velantha's Healer's Journal (Final Volume)

*A leather-bound volume, worn at the corners, with a final entry that says something different depending on who is reading it.*

## Appearance

Standard healer's field journal, similar to the kind any traveling practitioner would use. Leather cover, thread binding. The spine is worn; she opened it many times. Approximately two-thirds full. The entries are short — dates, patient names, what she administered, what helped. The final entry is dated the night before the ritual. It is brief. It is also, under certain conditions, different from what it was.

The journal is slightly warm. Not as warm as the field satchel — the satchel holds thirty years of congregation veneration. The journal holds thirty years of waiting in a crypt for someone to come and read it. That is a different kind of warmth.

## Presence / Desire

The journal wants to be read by someone who understands what it says. Not the medical entries — those are clear. The last entry. It has been waiting thirty years for a reader who has made the choice it describes. When such a reader holds it, the last entry changes. The journal does not do this to the reader. It does this because the last entry was written to be received, and it has been waiting for reception.

## Provenance

**1. Velantha (used ~20 years; placed in the crypt the night she died).**
She placed it on the shelf herself — the last thing she did before beginning the ritual. She had written the final entry. She knew she might not come back. She wanted the entry to reach whoever came looking.

**2. The congregation (left it on the shelf for 30 years; never read it).**
The congregation knows the journal is there. They do not read it. It is Velantha's. They completed the crypt around it. They maintained the crypt. They did not touch the journal. It has been on the shelf in the side alcove for thirty years.

**3. Whoever reads it in S6.** The journal has been waiting for this.

## Properties

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a creature that has made a choice knowing the cost — knowing they were acting without institutional sanction, for the sake of someone the world had decided was not worth the act.

Qualifying creatures in the party: Maret (practiced without permission), Renn (pact without Orders' sanction), Dren (named Aldis when he could have stayed silent), Calla (corrected the imperfect rite from her own knowledge).

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Memory-keeper.** The attuned creature has advantage on Intelligence checks to recall the content of any oral account they have personally received (Morren's account for Calla; Velantha's account for whoever receives it here).
- **Once per long rest:** The attuned creature may speak a sentence they have been holding — a held observation, a held name, a held truth — and it comes out exactly as they held it. The sentence does not change in the speaking. This is the journal's gift: one exact transmission per day.

### The Final Entry (H4 Delivery)

The final entry reads differently depending on the reader:

**Standard reading (passive Perception < 15):**
*"Night before. I have the working. I have the authorization. I have decided. I will begin at dawn."*

**H4 reading (passive Perception ≥ 15; or DC 15 Perception/Insight for lower passive):**
*"I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else."*

The H4 reading is the journal's last entry when read by someone capable of receiving it. Maret (passive 17) auto-detects the rewrite. The entry does not change permanently — it returns to the standard reading after the H4 reader sets the journal down. It will rewrite once more at the keystone (0021), if the right reader is present.

### Relic Test

No traditional relic test. The journal is not capable of being misused — it is a record. Using it as a weapon is possible only in the most absurd sense (it could be thrown). The journal's "test" is simply: does the reader receive what it says? There is no relic consequence for a reader who cannot receive the H4 entry; they simply read the standard text.

## Hooks

1. **Calla's deliverable.** Velantha gives Calla the journal and says: "Someone should speak for what I actually did." The journal is the source material for the PC-authored finale deliverable. Calla holds it, along with Morren's oral account and Aldric's written copy. Three sources. One synthesis. 0021.

2. **The journal rewrites at the keystone.** If the party is at the keystone (0021) and the right reader holds the journal, the H4 entry rewrites once more. The new version at the keystone will be slightly different — Velantha's account updated by thirty-one years of crypt time, slightly more certain. This is the finale's confirmation.

3. **Once-per-day exact transmission.** Calla can speak one sentence exactly as she received it — from Morren, from Aldric, from Velantha. Once per day. She has one chance per session to speak exactly what she was given. This is the verbal-documentation-arc's resolution mechanic: the arc completes when she speaks the synthesis, and the journal gives her one exact sentence as the seed.

## GM Notes

The journal is the most personal relic in the campaign. Handle it last — give the party time to explore the Crypt, hear Velantha, ask what she wants, before Velantha gives Calla the journal as a close.

When Maret reads the final entry and the H4 version appears: do not announce it. Let Maret's player (or the DM playing Maret) read it. The DM waits. Whatever Maret does or says: that is the session's final beat before the close.

# Velantha

## Appearance

She appears as she was: approximately 45 years old, a healer of twenty years, someone who carried a field satchel on every house call and asked her patients' permission before administering anything. She is not theatrical. She does not glow or speak in echoes. She is simply present, in the finished crypt, in the sourceless light the congregation provided.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. Present. She cannot be harmed; she is already dead. She cannot be dismissed; she has been here for thirty years and she leaves when she chooses.

## Public Knowledge

- She died here the night she used the working.
- She knew it was unauthorized. She did it anyway.
- She is not angry about the false miracle narrative. She is disappointed. She never asked for a cathedral.
- The journal is on the shelf in the alcove. She put it there.
- She wants the gully dwarves to have somewhere that is theirs.

- She knew Aldric would be too cowardly to correct the story. She forgave him for it. She has had thirty years.

But she is certain the faith is real. She does not know what Mishakal thinks. She suspects Mishakal would approve of the ethics even if not the liturgy.
- She softened one thing in what she told Morren. She told him she was sorry about the promise she couldn't keep. She did not tell him she would make the same choice again. The journal's last entry says she would.

### Velanthas Account

# Encounter — Velantha's Account

## Type

Social encounter (extended). Velantha speaks. The party responds. Hint 4 delivered via journal. Arc-completion on "what do you want." Calla receives the journal.

## Velantha's Opening

*"You came far enough. I wasn't sure anyone would."*

She waits. She lets the party speak first.

## Her Account (when asked what happened)

*"I came because they were sick. The same fever as the town. Nobody else came. I had a working that might help. I used it. It worked. I died — not from the working, exactly. From what the working cost. That's the distinction I want you to understand. The Orders have a word for it: an overextension. I reached past what my body could sustain. It was my choice. I made it with full information."*

## On the Promise

*"I promised some of them I would come back. I believed it when I said it. I died instead. I am sorry for that. The healing was real. I cannot apologize for the healing."*

## On the Silence

*"I should have told them it was sorcery. I should have told them gully dwarves are worth saving. I didn't, because I thought a miracle would be more useful than the truth. I was wrong about that. I was right about everything else."*

(This line appears in the journal. She says it here directly as well.)

## On Aldric

*"He was a coward about the Orders and a brave person about gully dwarves. Both things are true. He has been in that room for thirty years making copies of an account he partially understands. That is also a form of keeping faith."*

## Renn's Entry-Point

After her account of the cost:

*"I reached past what my body could sustain. It was a choice I made knowing what I was spending."*

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
