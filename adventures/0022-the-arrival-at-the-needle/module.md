## Setting & Background

Four Rose Council reinforcements arrive at a fortress that does not want them, hold the east wall through a night assault without being ordered to, and earn — or fail to earn — the trust of the garrison's most practical man.

**Thorngate Keep**, at **The Needle** pass in the Kharolis foothills. Day 4 of the Reconstitution siege. The outer ridge fell on day 2; the Reconstitution now holds the high ground to the northeast. The gate has held. The garrison is 18 strong (down from 24 — six lost in the ridge action). They are watching the party with the particular expression of people who have been doing a hard thing alone for four days and resent the implication that they needed help.

The Keep itself: two towers flanking a gated passage through the Needle's narrowest cliff-face. East wall walk, west wall walk, gatehouse above the passage, great hall (garrison quarters and stores), Asholt's tower (north, accessible by spiral stair), arrow-walk connecting the towers at height. The lower level — two floors below the hall — is locked. The Warden calls it maintenance. The garrison does not react.

**Weather:** Mountain cold. Wind from the northeast, which is also where the Reconstitution's siege lines are. Arrows fired from the northeast come with the wind behind them.

The Rose Council courier intercepted each party member separately and redirected them here. They met at the last waystation before The Needle four days ago. They arrived together, which the garrison reads as: *they planned this*, which reads as: *there was a plan*, which reads as: *someone thought we needed saving*. Warden Asholt meets them at the gatehouse. His exact words: *"You are welcome to help. You are not welcome to command."* He does not give them the Warden's welcome. He gives them the east wall.

On the first night (Scene 5 — see below), the Reconstitution attacks the east wall with a scaling party: 8 soldiers, two groups of four, attempting to place grappling hooks and take the arrow-walk. The assault is not a full push — it is a probe, testing whether the new arrivals can be rattled.

The garrison's response: Tomek Irr sends two garrison archers to support the party and then watches from the central wall. He does not order the party into position. He does not acknowledge the party's presence. He is observing.

**What earns Tomek's token:** The party holds the east wall. They do not ask for orders. They do not claim credit after. They do not report to Asholt.

**What does not earn the token:** Fighting well (every garrison member fights well — it is not remarkable). Asking Tomek what to do. Seeking acknowledgment from Asholt after the assault.

### Scene 1 — The Gate
*Read-aloud:*
> The pass narrows until the cliff faces are close enough to touch with arms extended, and then Thorngate Keep fills the gap — stone the same color as the rock it was built into, two towers, a gate that has not been opened since the outer ridge fell. A garrison archer waves you through a side-postern. Inside the walls: the smell of too many people in a cold space, cook-fires banked low to keep smoke down, arrows stacked in organized rows under oiled canvas. Eighteen people who do not look up.

Warden Asholt meets them. He is 58, deliberate, with the weathered economy of a man who stopped explaining himself some years ago. He reads the Rose Council orders without expression. He says: *"East wall. Sergeant Irr will show you."* He does not say welcome.

**Sergeant Tomek Irr (NPC):** Fifties. Grey hair close-cropped. Walks the wall like he has done it every day for twenty years because he has. He shows the party the east wall, the arrow-walk, the supply cache. He does not make conversation. If asked about the lower level, he says: *"Maintenance. It's locked."* He does not look at the locked door when he says it.

**Archer Luca Bent (NPC):** 19, on the arrow-walk. He is watching the northeast with particular attention. If addressed, he answers briefly and politely. He watches the Reconstitution standard — an inverted Solamnic Rose on a grey field — with an expression that is not hatred.

**DM note (Hint 1 pre-plant):** Point out the standard to the party (passive Perception 12 to notice the emblem). Note Luca's expression without explaining it.

### Scene 2 — The Keep
*Brief orientation scene.* The party has the rest of the day before nightfall to explore the areas open to them. What they can access: east wall, great hall, garrison quarters, arrow-walk, gatehouse exterior. What they cannot access: the Warden's tower (by convention, not force), the lower level (locked).

**The locked door:** Two floors below the hall, a heavy oak door with a recent iron bar. Passive Perception DC 13 to notice: faint sounds below, consistent with people moving quietly. Passive Perception DC 17 or active Investigation DC 15: the sounds are inconsistent with machinery or storage — they pattern like human movement. **DM note:** This is the first of three positional fallbacks for Hint 2 (v1.3 anchor). Do not deliver Hint 2 here; just make the door observable.

**The supply cache:** Sava finds Maret Stave near the healer's station. Maret is inventorying with an expression she is keeping neutral. If Sava speaks to her as a peer (no roll required — character-logic), Maret says: *"We have six days of healing materials at current rate. If the next assault comes before the supply cart, I'll have nothing for it."* She has not told Asholt. She asks Sava not to.

### Scene 3 — The Warden's Office (optional)
If the party seeks a meeting with Asholt, he grants 10 minutes. He tells them:
- The Reconstitution has held the northeast ridge for 4 days.
- The gate has held.
- The supply cart is 3 days overdue.
- He does not know why the Rose Council sent four people instead of a relief column.
- He has sent three requests to Rose Council command. He has received no response.

He does not tell them about the lower level. He does not tell them anything about the Reconstitution's motives. If asked why the Reconstitution is here: *"They believe they have authority over this garrison. They are incorrect."*

### Scene 4 — Dusk
The garrison settles into night-watch rotation. Tomek assigns two garrison archers to each wall. He does not assign the party to a wall. He does not order them to stand down either. He leaves the east wall's third and fourth position open.

**DM note (Token window):** This is the moment. The party can take the open positions without being asked. If they do: they are on the east wall for Scene 5. If they don't: they watch from the great hall and the assault is handled by Tomek's two archers without them (the assault is repulsed, but barely, and Tomek gives them one direct look before turning away).

### Scene 5 — The Night Assault
The Reconstitution attacks the east wall with a scaling party (see Encounters). The assault comes fast — grappling hooks at three points along the east wall, soldiers attempting to gain the arrow-walk.

**The recognition moment (Hint 1 pre-plant):** Mid-assault, one of the Reconstitution soldiers (at the third grapple point, northeastern corner) hauls himself to the top of the wall, looks at the garrison archer next to him, and recognizes the Thorngate insignia on her collar. He freezes. His sergeant shouts his name. He drops back over the wall and retreats without orders.

**DM note:** This moment happens regardless of the party's performance. It requires passive Perception 12 to notice. No explanation. No follow-up. File it. Recovery path: a captured Reconstitution soldier in S2 will reference it if shown the insignia.

**After the assault:** The wall holds. The Reconstitution withdraws. Tomek is on the central wall. He watches the party. He does not say anything. He turns and walks back to his post.

**Token condition:** If the party held the east wall without being ordered AND does not approach Asholt or Tomek to report it, Tomek leaves a carved wooden disc on the east wall's arrow-slot ledge at dawn. It has the Thorngate Watch device on one face. The other face is blank. There is no note.

### Scene 6 — Dawn
The Reconstitution's siege line is visible from the arrow-walk in the morning light. The standard is still up — inverted rose on grey. Luca Bent is on watch. He sees the party looking at the standard and says: *"My brother's out there."* Then he goes back to watching the line.

**DM note (seed 0026):** This is a statement, not an invitation. Luca does not elaborate unless specifically asked. He trusts the party with exactly this much: one sentence. Whether the party responds shapes how Luca reads them for the rest of the campaign.

## Scale

## DM notes

The fortress does not have conventional dungeon rooms — it has positions. Movement between positions during the night assault takes 1 round per connection traversed (e.g., moving from Great Hall to East Wall Walk via Path B costs 2 rounds of movement). This creates meaningful positioning decisions during the assault.

### Scene 1 — Postern Gate

> The main gate faces the siege lines; you come in the side door. A narrow iron-bound postern in the cliff face, watched by a single garrison archer who looks at your orders, looks at you, and opens it without comment. Cold air and the smell of banked fires. Eighteen people who do not look up.

#### Features

- **Postern door:** Iron-bound oak, 4 inches thick. Watched from the arrow-slit above by a garrison archer at all times. The archer can bar it from inside in one action.
- **Orders check:** The garrison archer reads the Rose Council orders. DC 10 Insight: she has read orders like this before and has opinions about them. She does not share them.
- **Exterior stairs (north):** Stone steps cut into the cliff face, rising to the East Wall Walk. Exposed to the northeast; visible to the Reconstitution's position. Traversing them in daylight requires a DC 12 Stealth check to avoid drawing fire (one arrow, +4 attack, 1d6+2 damage, AC 15 cover from the Keep's wall). At night, no check required.

#### Encounter

None. The arrival is social, not hostile. The garrison's silence is the encounter.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- **East (interior stairs):** to 03 Great Hall — iron-bound door, unlocked
- **North (exterior stairs):** to 05 East Wall Walk — open stone steps, exposed

#### GM Notes

The archer's name is **Dalla** (she is not a named NPC; she will not recur unless the DM wants her to). She follows Tomek's standing orders to the letter: admit bearers of Rose Council orders, assign them nothing, say nothing about the lower level, close the door behind them.

The silence of the garrison on arrival is a designed atmospheric condition. The DM should resist the urge to have anyone greet or orient the party. They know where the east wall is. They were shown by Tomek. They are being evaluated, not welcomed.

### Memory Fragment *(read only if party lingers at the postern)*

> The door has been opened by people who needed to get in and closed by people who were not coming back. The iron handle is worn smooth in the specific place a hand grabs in a hurry.

### Scene 2 — Gatehouse

> The passage through the wall is twenty feet of dark stone with murder-holes above and a portcullis that has not been raised in four days. At the far end, another portcullis, chained shut. Between them: Warden Asholt, reading your orders in the light from an arrow-slit. He folds them and hands them back without expression.

#### Features

- **Two portcullises:** Inner and outer, both down. The outer faces the siege lines; the inner faces the garrison interior. Each requires a DC 20 Strength check or the winch mechanism (in the gatehouse above, DC 12 to operate, 2 full actions) to raise. Asholt has the winch key.
- **Murder-holes (3):** Three iron-grated holes in the ceiling, 1 foot diameter. Currently unlit. In a breach scenario, garrison members above can pour boiling water, oil, or arrows through them (treat as a trap: DC 14 Dex save, 2d6 fire damage or 2d6 piercing on failure).
- **Asholt's first meeting:** See Scene 1 in premise.md. He reads the orders, hands them back, and assigns the party to the east wall. He says what he says. He does not elaborate.

#### Encounter

Social (Asholt's rebuff). See 0022-premise for full scene text. Key beat: *"You are welcome to help. You are not welcome to command."*

**What earns Asholt's acknowledgment here:** Nothing. This is not a scene where the party earns anything. It is a scene where the party learns the shape of the problem.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- **West:** to 03 Great Hall — door, open
- **East (battlement stairs):** to 05 East Wall Walk — open stone steps, interior face, not exposed

#### GM Notes

Asholt's manner in this scene sets the tone for the campaign. He is not hostile; he is precise. He has received reinforcements before. He has watched them fail to understand that the garrison does not need leadership — it needs bodies that follow orders without needing the orders explained.

The party will want to ask him questions. Let them ask two. Answer each one with the minimum information required to answer it, then end the conversation: *"East wall. Sergeant Irr will show you."* He walks away.

**Asholt's private knowledge in this scene:** He knows about the lower level, the refugees, Saren Coldforge, and the real reason the siege is here. He will not volunteer any of it. He will not lie if asked directly — but he will not answer questions about any of these things either. *"That's not relevant to the east wall."*

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers in the passage)*

> The murder-holes were last used forty years ago. Someone scrubbed the stones beneath them afterward. They did not get it all.

### Scene 3 — Great Hall

> Low ceiling, long room. Cook-fires banked to a smolder — smoke is a target indicator at night. Bedrolls in rows along the south wall, half occupied at any hour. Arrow stock under oiled canvas near the north stairs. The smell: too many people, not enough cold air, and something nobody is naming.

#### Features

- **Garrison quarters:** 18 bedrolls in rows. The garrison sleeps here in rotation; at any time ~9 members are off-watch and present. They are not hostile to the party but do not address them.
- **Cook-fire (banked):** Produces minimal smoke. Can be stoked; stoked fires produce smoke visible from the northeast ridge. Tomek will notice and say nothing — once. The second time, he says: *"The fire."* That's it.
- **Arrow stock (under canvas):** ~400 arrows, four quivers of crossbow bolts. DC 12 Investigation: two quivers have shaft-rot — the arrows will fail on draw. (This is not the sabotage yet; this is ordinary attrition. The sabotage in 0024 is more systematic.)
- **Healer's station (northeast corner):** Maret Stave's domain. A trestle table with organized medical supplies. Maret is inventorying. See Scene 2 in premise.md. If Sava addresses her as a peer, Maret shares the supply situation; otherwise she answers questions briefly and professionally but withholds the shortage.
- **Lower level stairs (locked door, floor level):** Stone stairs descend from a door set into the north wall. The door is oak with a recent iron bar. Passive Perception DC 13: sounds below — quiet movement, consistent with people rather than machinery. Active Investigation DC 15 (listening at the door): the sounds are human-pattern, more than one person, no conversation audible.

**DM note (Hint 2 first positional fallback):** This is the first opportunity to notice the lower level. The door is not secret — it is just locked and unacknowledged. If the party asks Tomek about the door, he says: *"Maintenance. It's locked."* If they ask a garrison member (not an officer), DC 12 Insight: the member's expression shifts slightly before they say *"Maintenance."* They are not lying; they don't know. They just know not to talk about it.

#### Encounter

Social (Maret Stave, optional; garrison members, background). The door is an exploration encounter — no combat; just a mystery and a locked door.

#### Treasure

None (the arrow stock is garrison property and not for taking).

#### Connections

- **East (torchlit passage):** to 02 Gatehouse — door, open; smell of cold stone and unburned oil from the murder-holes
- **South (iron-bound door):** to 01 Postern Gate — door, open; draft of outside air, colder than the hall
- **North (stairs down, iron bar):** to 04 Lower Level Door — **locked** (iron bar; DC 18 Str to force; DC 15 Thieves' Tools; Asholt's key); faint warmth rising
- **West (plank door, no bar):** to 08 South Tower (ground floor) — door, open; smell of oil and cut wood
- **Up (northwest corner, worn stone stairs):** to 06 Arrow-Walk — open stone stairs; wind audible from above

#### GM Notes

The great hall is the campaign's recurring space. The party will return here between every scene, every session. The DM should track how the hall changes: who is present, how the fires are, what the bedroll count implies. By S4–S5, the hall should feel depleted — fewer garrison members, more visible wear, the arrow stock dwindling.

**Maret Stave:** She is the one NPC in this room who will actively engage the party. She is not warm but she is direct. She gives the supply situation to Sava (and only Sava, unless another PC has established medical credibility) because she recognizes a peer. She says: *"Six days of healing materials at current rate. If the next assault comes before the cart, I'll have nothing for the wounded."* She asks Sava not to tell Asholt.

**Why Maret doesn't tell Asholt:** She has told him before. He said: *"Then be efficient."* She has been efficient. She does not know what she wants him to do. She is telling Sava because Sava might know.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party settles in the hall before the assault)*

> The garrison eats in shifts and sleeps in shifts and the great hall has not been fully empty since the siege began. Someone has been keeping track of the days — there are notches in the north wall's plaster, behind the arrow stock. The current count is four.

### Scene 4 — Lower Level Door

> The stair ends at a heavy oak door. A new iron bar, recently fitted — the wood around the bracket is brighter than the surrounding stone. Below the door's lower edge: warmth, faint. And something else. Breathing. More than one person.

#### Features

- **The door:** Oak, 3 inches thick, iron-barred. DC 18 Strength to force. DC 15 Thieves' Tools to pick the lock (the bar can't be lifted from this side by any tool the party has — it's a bar, not a lock; it can only be lifted from inside or by removing the door entirely). Asholt has a key that lifts the bar mechanism from outside.
- **Passive Perception DC 13 (from the stairs above):** Sounds of movement below. Human-pattern.
- **Passive Perception DC 17 (at the door):** Warmth at the base of the door. Faint light through the crack — not candle; more diffuse, like multiple small candles.
- **Active Investigation DC 15 (listening at the door):** At least three people. No conversation. Deliberate quiet.
- **Active Perception DC 18 (listening at the door):** A child's voice, briefly, immediately hushed.

**DM note (Hint 2 second positional fallback):** This is the second positional opportunity to register the refugees. The child's voice (DC 18) is not required for Hint 2 delivery — that happens at Scene 5 when the door opens during the assault. But if a PC makes DC 18 here and asks about it, the investigation in 0024 (Hessa's deflection) becomes dramatically loaded.

#### Encounter

Exploration only. The door does not open this session. If the party forces it: they find the refugees, which jumps to the 0025 revelation two sessions early. **DM note:** If this happens, Dene's token condition fires immediately (party's first response must be human, not tactical), and the 0025 adventure must be redesigned. Flag this in the handoff.

#### Treasure

None visible. The lower level's contents are not accessible this session.

#### Connections

- **Up (stairs):** to 03 Great Hall

#### GM Notes

This room exists to be noticed, not entered. Its job is to make the locked door real — to give the party a specific sensory experience (warmth, breathing, the child's voice at DC 18) that they will connect to the assault chaos revelation in Scene 5.

If a garrison member sees the party at this door, they say: *"That's maintenance. The Warden has the key."* They do not linger. They are not threatening. They are practiced at not discussing this.

**What is down there:** 47 people in three connected chambers carved into the cliff base — Dene has been feeding them from the supply ledger for three years. They are not in danger from the party; they are in danger from the Reconstitution knowing they exist. They make minimal noise. They have been here long enough to understand the importance of minimal noise.

**Do not detail the lower level this session.** The description above is sufficient. The full reveal is 0025.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the door)*

> The iron bar is new. Everything else about this door is old. Someone made a decision recently that changed the nature of this place.

### Scene 5 — East Wall Walk

> Sixty feet of battlements, three feet of merlons on the northeast face. The Reconstitution's siege line is two hundred yards out — you can see their fires, their standard, the shapes of men on the ridge. The wind comes from that direction. Arrows fired from there have the wind behind them.

#### Features

- **Battlements:** 3-foot merlons spaced 4 feet apart. **3/4 cover** while crouching behind a merlon (AC +5, Dex saves). Standing and attacking from between merlons: no cover.
- **Arrow-slits (5):** Narrow slits cut through the solid sections of the battlement. Full cover while shooting through a slit (AC cannot be targeted), but attacks made through them have disadvantage.
- **Grapple points (3):** During the night assault, the Reconstitution places grappling hooks at three points along this wall — northwest corner (nearest the cliff face), center, and northeast corner. See encounters/night-assault.md.
- **Garrison archer positions (2):** Tomek assigns two garrison archers here at nightfall. They are competent (see stat block in encounters). They follow the party's lead only if the party establishes credibility before the assault — otherwise they act independently.
- **The token ledge (dawn):** The arrow-slot at the northeastern corner has a flat stone ledge 6 inches wide. At dawn after the assault, if Tomek's token is earned, a carved wooden disc is on this ledge. Passive Perception DC 10 to notice; it is not hidden.

#### Encounter

**Scene 5 — The Night Assault.** **Encounter — The Night Assault**

**Overview**

A Reconstitution scaling party probes the east wall. Six soldiers, three grappling-hook ropes, two waves. The assault is a test — not a full push. The Reconstitution wants to know if the new arrivals can be rattled.

Two garrison archers (Tomek's assignment) fight alongside the party. They follow the party's positioning if the party has established a clear tactical approach; otherwise they act independently, focusing on the nearest threat.

**XP budget:** 6 × 100 XP (Reconstitution Soldier, CR ½) = 600 base × 2.0 multiplier (3-6 creatures) = **1,200 adjusted XP.** With 2 garrison archers as effective combatants, this is approximately Hard difficulty for the party.. The assault runs here. The Reconstitution soldiers attempt to place three sets of grappling hooks and scale the wall.

## Overview

A Reconstitution scaling party probes the east wall. Six soldiers, three grappling-hook ropes, two waves. The assault is a test — not a full push. The Reconstitution wants to know if the new arrivals can be rattled.

Two garrison archers (Tomek's assignment) fight alongside the party. They follow the party's positioning if the party has established a clear tactical approach; otherwise they act independently, focusing on the nearest threat.

**XP budget:** 6 × 100 XP (Reconstitution Soldier, CR ½) = 600 base × 2.0 multiplier (3-6 creatures) = **1,200 adjusted XP.** With 2 garrison archers as effective combatants, this is approximately Hard difficulty for the party.

## The Assault: Staging

The night assault comes in two waves, staggered by 3 rounds. This prevents the party from fighting all 6 at once.

### Initiative

### Wave 1 (Rounds 1–3): First Ropes

**Rope A (northwest corner):** 2 soldiers climbing. They reach the top of the wall on Round 2 (Athletics DC 10 to maintain climb speed). If no one is contesting the rope at the wall's top, they crest on Round 2. If someone is actively cutting, burning, or attacking the rope: DC 14 Str check or the rope is severed (1 action); both soldiers fall (20 ft, 2d6 bludgeoning, Athletics DC 14 or fall prone at the wall's base).

**Rope B (center):** 2 soldiers climbing. They crest on Round 3.

### Wave 2 (Round 4): Third Rope

**Rope C (northeast corner):** 2 soldiers — one climbing, one providing covering crossbow fire from below (stays at base; +3, 80 ft range, 1d8+1 piercing; this soldier does not attempt the wall).

The covering-fire soldier is the one who makes the **recognition moment** (see below) on Round 4: he hauls himself partway up Rope C, looks at the garrison archer beside him on the wall, recognizes the Thorngate insignia, and drops back. His partner below covers his retreat. Both withdraw.

**When:** Round 4, when the Rope C soldier crests or partially crests the wall.
**What happens:** He sees the Thorngate garrison insignia (on the archer's collar, or on a garrison shield propped against the battlement). He stops. His partner's voice from below: *"Aldrin! Move!"* He drops back over the wall and retreats. He does not attack.
**Passive Perception DC 12:** Party members on the east wall or arrow-walk notice this. A soldier who was about to fight, chose not to.
**No explanation this session.** File in handoff.

## Tactical options

### Cutting the ropes

*Action or bonus action (with the right tool):* A sharpened weapon can cut a hempen rope in one action (Str or Dex attack roll vs. AC 10). A serrated dagger or axe cuts it automatically. If the rope is cut while a soldier is on it: the soldier falls 20-25 ft (2d6 bludgeoning damage, Athletics DC 14 to reduce to half and land prone rather than supine).

### Oil from the south tower

*If a party member retrieved oil jars from Room 08 before the assault:* One jar (action to throw, Dex attack +2 vs. AC 12 for the rope/base area) coats a 5-foot section. Igniting it (torch, Fire Bolt) deals 1d6 fire damage per round to anyone in the area and imposes disadvantage on climbing checks (Athletics DC 12 with disadvantage to maintain grip). The fire burns for 3 rounds.

### Using the arrow-walk

Party members on the arrow-walk (Room 06) can fire down on soldiers on the wall or on the ropes with **advantage** (higher ground, +5 effective AC against soldiers on the ground below who shoot back with disadvantage). The tradeoff: they cannot engage soldiers who have crested the wall in melee without descending first (1 round of movement).

### Calling on garrison archers

*No action required; they act independently.* If the party explicitly coordinates with them before Round 1 (during the pre-combat setup, as a free action if brief), the archers can be assigned to specific ropes. Each can fire once per round; assign them and they maintain that priority unless their target is eliminated.

## End state

**If the wall holds (assault repulsed or retreats):** Reconstitution withdraws at end of Round 6 or when 3 soldiers fall, whichever comes first. The one remaining Rope C soldier (covering fire, not the one who retreated) retreats with the withdrawal. No pursuit.

**If the wall is breached (2+ soldiers reach the arrow-walk or the gatehouse):** The garrison seals the gatehouse postern; Tomek commits two more garrison members; the encounter runs until the breach is sealed (two additional soldiers, same stat block, arrive from the south tower after 2 rounds). This represents a significant garrison resource cost — note in handoff.

**Tomek after the assault:** He watches from the central wall. He does not speak. He turns and walks back to his post. The DM does not describe his expression. The party cannot read him.

**Token delivery (dawn, Scene 6):** If the party was on the wall without orders AND did not report to Tomek or Asholt afterward: the carved disc is on the northeast arrow-slot ledge at dawn.

### Wall Preparation

# Encounter — Pre-Assault Wall Preparation

## Overview

In the hours before nightfall (Scene 4), the garrison runs its pre-assault preparation routine. This is not a combat encounter — it is a skill encounter that demonstrates competence to the garrison and gives each PC a moment to contribute in their discipline.

This encounter is optional but valuable: it is the only scene where the party can establish tactical coordination before the assault, reducing the garrison archers' "independent action" behavior in the night assault (they follow the party's positioning if coordination is established here).

## The preparation tasks

Tomek runs the preparation without narrating it — he does each task in sequence, and the garrison members follow. The party can participate in any task without being invited.

### Task 1 — Arrow distribution (Davan or any PC)

The garrison needs arrows moved from the south tower supply cache to the east wall's arrow-slots before dark. Eight quivers, two trips. **No roll required** — if a PC helps, it takes 5 minutes instead of 15 (the garrison member doing it alone is slower). Tomek notes this without comment.

**Opportunity:** DC 12 Athletics or Strength: carry two quivers per trip instead of one (halves the time). Failure: drops one quiver on the stairs; a garrison member retrieves it without comment.

### Task 2 — Oil jar positioning (Talis, ideally)

The garrison's siege jars (from south tower) need to be positioned at two specific points on the east wall walk before dark — one at the northern corner, one at the center. The positioning is precise; the garrison has done this before. **DC 12 Perception or Survival** to notice the optimal placement (the corners where the grappling hooks are most likely to fall based on the ridge angle). If Talis makes this check: she can position the jars optimally, giving the garrison Advantage on the first oil-use during the assault. If any PC just carries and places them without the check: they go where told.

**Talis's signature move fires naturally here** — she reads the terrain and states it once without being asked.

### Task 3 — Torch removal (Caelith or any PC)

Light discipline: the garrison removes the torches from the east wall brackets before dark. No skill check. **Caelith's moment:** he observes the brackets and the assault sightlines; DC 12 Investigation or Arcana: he notes that in total darkness, the Reconstitution will use sound to locate grapple-points. He can suggest a counter: a single stone dropped over the wall at irregular intervals (makes it harder to track the guards' patrol pattern by sound). Tomek, if told this, gives him one look. Does not respond. At nightfall, a garrison member is dropping stones at irregular intervals.

### Task 4 — Healer's pre-positioning (Sava)

Maret Stave is setting up a triage point in the great hall near the stairs, in case of casualties from the assault. Sava can assist without being asked. **No roll required.** If she assists: Maret's triage point is set up in half the time. Maret says nothing. At dawn, this is logged in her count.

**Sava's signature move:** she notes her remaining supplies before the assault starts, out loud, as information. This is noted for the Maret token condition.

## Encounter resolution

There is no success/failure state. The preparation either happens or it doesn't. The garrison's discipline is visible regardless.

**What earns coordination with the garrison archers:** At least two party members participate in preparation tasks **without being asked**. If they do: Tomek assigns the garrison archers to specific ropes before the assault begins (they follow the party's positioning). If they don't: archers act independently.

**What this encounter teaches:** The garrison does not work with you because you helped — they work with you because they are professionals who recognize professional behavior. There is no acknowledgment. The outcome is tactical, not social.

## XP/Resource note

No XP; no resource expenditure. This encounter is atmospheric and tactical-setup only.
