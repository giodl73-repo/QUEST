# Warden Bren Asholt

## Appearance

Fifty-eight years old. Broad through the chest and neck, with the settled weight of a man who stopped growing decades ago and has been exactly this since. Grey hair, close-cropped. A face that has been weathered past the point where weather matters. He wears the Thorngate garrison device on his left breast — not the Rose Council device; the Keep's own, which predates his tenure by 150 years. He moves through the keep at the same pace regardless of urgency; the garrison reads his pace the way a crew reads the weather.

## Stat Block

*Non-combatant in S1 (does not fight this session).*

For S6 major assault: Warden Asholt fights as a **Veteran** equivalent (CR 3): AC 17 (splint), HP 58, Multiattack (2 longsword), Longsword +5 to hit (1d8+3), Parry reaction (+2 AC against one melee attack).

## Public knowledge

- He has commanded Thorngate Keep for 22 years.
- He received the Rose Council's reinforcement orders and did not request them.
- He has sent three requests to Rose Council command asking for relief-column instructions. No response.
- The Reconstitution does not have authority over this garrison.
- The lower level is "maintenance."

## Private knowledge

- The lower level houses 47 non-human and half-Solamnic refugees, sheltered for three years. Quartermaster Dene has been feeding them. Asholt authorized it. He has not told Rose Council command.
- Commander Saren Coldforge was trained at Thorngate under him twenty years ago. She left in protest over the first group of refugees he sheltered. He believes she was wrong about the people and right about the Oath's letter. He has not found a way to tell her.
- The siege's strategic justification (control of the pass) has been obsolete for three months. He does not know if Saren knows this.
- He has two letters from Saren in a locked chest in his tower — written over the past twenty years, never answered. He does not know why she wrote them. He does not know why he kept them.

## Arc-Completion

**Adventure:** 0028 (The Parley)
**Condition:** Saren Coldforge is sitting at the table, the party has 7 tokens, and one PC has just spoken the accounting.
**The act:** Asholt takes his token from the cord around his neck and places it on the table in front of Saren. Not toward her. On the table, between them. He says: *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*
**Why it cannot be scripted earlier:** He will not say this until he has evidence the party can hold what he says without using it. The tokens are that evidence. The accounting speech is the party proving they can name things correctly. He gives what he has.

**What does NOT fire the arc-completion:** A direct apology to the party. A speech about his reasoning. Any acknowledgment of the party's effort. His arc-completion is entirely addressed to Saren, not the party. The party witnesses it but is not the recipient.
