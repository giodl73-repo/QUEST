# Sergeant Tomek Irr

## Appearance

Fifty-three years old. Grey hair cropped to the scalp, a jaw that has absorbed some history, calloused hands with ink on two fingers (he does the garrison's administrative tallying). He walks the wall the way he has walked it for fifteen years: same pace, same checkpoints, same arc around the south tower. Garrison members orient off his pace the way sailors orient off the horizon. He does not look at new arrivals differently than he looks at garrison members who have been here for years. He looks at everything the same way: measuring.

## Stat Block

*Fights as a Garrison Veteran (simplified):* AC 16 (chain mail), HP 32 (5d8+10), Longsword +5 to hit (1d8+3), Shortbow +3 to hit, 80 ft (1d6+1). Morale: does not retreat unless Asholt orders it.

## Public knowledge

- Senior NCO, Thorngate garrison, fifteen years at the Keep.
- Runs the administrative records (arrow tallies, supply counts, garrison rotation).
- He assigns wall positions; he does not explain the assignments.
- He is the first point of contact for the party below Asholt.

## Private knowledge

- He knows about the refugees. He has known since year one of the three years they have been there. He has never commented on it to anyone.
- He has known about Saren Coldforge (by reputation and by Asholt's manner when her name is used) for twenty years.
- He has served under four officers in his military career. Asholt is the only one he has trusted entirely. His trust in Asholt is not based on respect for the man's judgment; it is based on fifteen years of watching the judgment be correct.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Tomek's response |
|---|---|
| Asks for orders | "Warden assigned you to the east wall." (Repeats this if pressed.) |
| Asks about the lower level | "Maintenance. It's locked." (Exactly what Asholt said.) |
| Reports after the assault | Listens. Nods. Returns to his post. |
| Holds wall without orders | Does not acknowledge in the moment. |
| Seeks credit after the assault | Registered. No response. No token. |
| Demonstrates competence without credit-seeking | Registered. Token at dawn. |
| Asks about the garrison's history | Gives factual answers only: names, dates, numbers. Nothing interpretive. |
| Addresses him by rank ("Sergeant Irr") before the token is earned | A barely visible calibration. Not a response; a registration. |

## Arc-Completion

**Adventure:** 0022 (this session)
**Condition:** The party holds the east wall through the night assault (Scene 5) without being ordered to, AND does not approach Asholt or Tomek to report it afterward.
**The act:** At dawn (Scene 6), Tomek places his carved Watch-Token on the arrow-slot ledge at the northeast corner of the east wall. He does not leave a note. He does not remain to see it found.
**This is the campaign's first moment of garrison trust.** It is given in the garrison's language: without explanation, without ceremony, without asking for acknowledgment. The party will not immediately know what it means. The meaning becomes clear across the campaign.

**What the token means (DM knowledge):** Tomek is not a man who explains trust. He either gives it or he doesn't. The disc on the ledge means: *I will answer for you.* He has given this to three people in fifteen years of service. The Warden is one of them. The other two are garrison members who died at the ridge action four days ago.

## Information-holder design

Tomek holds one piece of intelligence the party can use: he knows that the last supply cart ran into trouble on the secondary pass route (he has already sent a garrison scout; the scout has not returned). He gives this information **only if** the party demonstrates they understand the supply situation without being told — i.e., if they ask about the cart before Maret tells them to. Information readiness condition: the party asks Tomek about resupply before Scene 3 ends.
