# Healer Maret Stave

## Appearance

Forty-four years old. Compact, efficient. Brown hair going grey at the temples, worn in a single braid. Her hands are the most characteristic thing about her: she keeps them clean to a standard that the garrison has long since stopped questioning — she will not examine a wound with unclean hands, siege or no siege. Her healer's station is organized the way a soldier's kit is organized: everything in its place, everything counted, nothing redundant.

## Stat Block

*Non-combatant.* If forced into combat: AC 11 (no armor), HP 18 (4d8), Dagger +2 to hit (1d4 piercing). She does not fight unless directly attacked.

## Public knowledge

- The garrison's only healer. Has held the post for nine years.
- Maintains the healer's station in the northeast corner of the great hall.
- Does not involve herself in garrison command decisions.

## Private knowledge

- She has six days of healing materials at current rate. She has not told Asholt.
- She has told Asholt before (two years ago, when supplies were lower than this). He said: *"Then be efficient."* She has been efficient. She does not know what she wants him to do differently. She is not angry; she is tired of holding the information alone.
- She has been giving double rations to someone in the lower level. She knows who is down there. She does not know how the party will respond to that information. She has not told them.
- She trusts Sava Dawnmere on first professional contact — not personally, but collegially. She gives Sava the supply situation because she needs it to land somewhere useful, and Sava looks like someone who will use it correctly.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Maret's response |
|---|---|
| Sava addresses her as a peer (collegial, no hierarchy assumed) | Gives supply situation. Asks Sava not to tell Asholt. |
| Any other PC approaches first | Professional, brief. Does not give supply situation. |
| Party asks about double rations | "The lower level is Dene's responsibility. Ask Dene." (Too quickly.) |
| Party asks to use her supplies | "For what?" She requires justification before issuing. She is not obstructing; she is accountable. |
| Sava uses zero healing slots in S1 combat | Notices. Does not comment in S1. Gives token in S2. |
| Sava uses 1-2 healing slots | Professional acknowledgment. Token available with DC 14 Persuasion in S2. |
| Sava uses 3+ healing slots | Maret recounts her supplies at dawn. Not an accusation. Just a count. Token not available in S2. |

## Arc-Completion (0023)

**Condition:** The supply cart is found and returned (0023 objective). Sava has demonstrated resource conservation across S1 and S2.
**The act:** Maret gives her token to Sava directly, healer-to-healer. She says: *"You count before you spend. I've not seen that in someone from outside."* She hands the token over without ceremony — it comes from the cord she wears under her healer's apron, not from a pocket.
**What makes this specific:** The token condition is about discipline — Maret's trust is earned by being the kind of healer she is. Sava earns it not by heroism but by being correct about the same thing Maret is correct about. It is a peer acknowledgment, not a reward.

## Information-holder design

Maret holds one piece of intelligence for 0025: she knows about the people in the lower level (she has been feeding them). She will not give this information this session. Her information-readiness condition for 0025: the party's first response to discovering the refugees must be protective. If a party member asks her afterward *"How long have you known?"* she says: *"Three years."* Nothing else. She does not apologize. It was the right thing.
