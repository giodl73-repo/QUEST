# Archer Luca Bent

## Appearance

Nineteen years old. The youngest member of the garrison by six years. Brown hair that he cuts himself (imprecisely). Still growing into his shoulders. His garrison kit is correctly maintained but the lacing on his bracers is the wrong pattern — he was shown once and didn't quite catch it, and has been doing it his own way since; no one has corrected him because it works. He watches the northeast with a quality of attention that is different from tactical watchfulness: personal.

## Stat Block

*Garrison archer:* AC 13 (leather armor), HP 13 (3d6+3), Shortbow +5 to hit, 80/320 ft (1d6+3 piercing). Fights bravely; does not retreat unless ordered; will disengage and find cover rather than fight in melee.

## Public knowledge

- Youngest garrison member. Joined 11 months ago.
- Reliable on the arrow-walk; accurate at range.
- Has family in the area (the garrison knows this in general terms).

## Private knowledge

- His older brother **Aldren Bent** (23 years old) joined the Reconstitution eight months ago, four months before the siege of Thorngate began.
- Luca does not know if his brother is here by choice or by assignment. He does not know if his brother knows Luca is in this garrison.
- In Session 5 (0026), he goes over the wall to find out. He comes back. Both things are true simultaneously.

## Behavior matrix (S1)

| Party action | Luca's response |
|---|---|
| Addresses him normally | Answers briefly, politely. |
| Asks what he's looking at (the standard) | "I'm watching the line." (Not a lie; not the whole truth.) |
| Presses him on his expression | "It's a siege." Closes the conversation. |
| Does not press him | He notices. This is the right response. |
| Dawn Scene 6: Luca says "My brother's out there" | Party response noted. See below. |

## Response to the "brother" disclosure (Scene 6)

This moment is designed as an asymmetric disclosure — Luca says one sentence, unprompted, to the party. He is not asking for help. He is saying something that has been in his chest all night.

**Correct responses (earns no token yet, but builds toward 0026):**
- Acknowledging it and not pressing: *"I know,"* or silence, or a nod.
- Not reporting it to Asholt or Tomek.
- Not asking a tactical question about the brother.

**Incorrect responses (not punished, but noted):**
- Asking if the brother is an intelligence asset.
- Reporting it to Asholt.
- Telling Luca what to do about it.

The DM notes the response in the handoff. In 0026, Luca will behave toward the party based on how they responded here.

## Arc-Completion (0026)

**Condition:** Luca returns from the Reconstitution camp. The party did not report him. They may have gone after him, or they may have held and trusted him to return. Either is acceptable. The specific condition: Luca is back inside the walls, his absence was not reported to Asholt, and the party's first words to him on his return are not tactical.
**The act:** Luca gives his token to the PC who responds to his return without a tactical frame. He says: *"He's all right. He didn't know I was here."* He ties his token to his own wristlashing cord and then removes it and gives it to the party member. He does not say what it means. He goes back to his post.
**Why 0026:** His arc requires experiencing both sides — the garrison and the Reconstitution, his duty and his brother — and choosing to come back. That choice is the arc-completion.
