# Room 06 — Arrow-Walk

## Read-aloud

> Fifteen feet above the east wall, a narrow stone walkway between the two towers. Wind up here is constant and cold. From this height the siege line shows in full — fires, positions, the gap in the left flank where the cliff overhang cuts their arrow-coverage. Archer Luca Bent is here. Watching northeast.

## Features

- **Height advantage:** Attacks made from the arrow-walk against targets on the east wall walk below have advantage (higher ground). Attackers on the east wall firing up have disadvantage.
- **Tactical vantage (DC 12 Perception or Survival):** From this height, the gap in the Reconstitution's left flank is visible — a 20-foot section where the cliff overhang breaks their arrow-coverage. **This is the route Talis will use in 0026.** Note this in the handoff if discovered.
- **Luca Bent:** On watch. He acknowledges the party briefly. He is watching the northeast. The Reconstitution's standard is directly in his line of sight.
- **Wind:** Sustained 15 mph from the northeast. Ranged attacks made from the arrow-walk toward the northeast (toward the siege lines) have disadvantage. Ranged attacks from the northeast toward the arrow-walk have advantage. Ranged attacks made laterally (east wall walk to arrow-walk) are not affected.
- **Iron brackets (2):** Torch brackets at each end. Torches removed at night (light discipline). In darkness, the arrow-walk requires a DC 10 Dexterity check to traverse at speed; DC failure: 1d6 fall damage (5 feet to the east wall walk).

## Encounter

Social (Luca). No combat in this location during this session.

**Scene 6 (dawn) — Luca's disclosure:** After the night assault, Luca is here at dawn. He sees the party return. He says: *"My brother's out there."* He does not explain. He does not invite follow-up. This is a statement. The party's response is noted by the DM; it shapes Luca's willingness to share further in 0026.

**What response earns Luca's basic trust (not his token — that's in 0026):** Not pressing him. Not asking tactical questions about his brother. Not reporting the statement to anyone. Acknowledging it and moving on. *A nod, "I know,"* or silence are all correct responses.

## GM Notes

Jaquays shortcut note: The arrow-walk is the loop element — it connects both towers and both ends of the east wall. A party that uses the arrow-walk as a tactical position during the assault can cover both ends simultaneously with ranged attackers. A party that stays on the wall can only cover one end at a time with melee.

**The gap in the left flank** (DC 12 Perception): This is the future egress route for 0026 (Luca goes over the wall here; Talis follows). Note in handoff if discovered. Do not explain what it will be used for.

**The North Tower door:** It is not locked. It is closed. If the party opens it: they find a stone staircase, Asholt's personal quarters at the top, Asholt in them (not asleep — he doesn't sleep much during a siege). He looks at them without expression. *"Is there something that requires my attention?"* He is not angry. He is waiting. If there is nothing urgent, he closes the door. This is not a punishment; it is just his manner.
