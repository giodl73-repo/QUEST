# Room 07 — North Tower (Asholt's)

## Read-aloud

> *(Only if party enters — normally this door is closed.)* The staircase is narrow stone, worn in the center. At the top: a single room, desk, a cot that has not been slept in tonight, maps weighted down by stones at their corners. Warden Asholt, standing at the window. He does not turn immediately.

## Features

- **Asholt's desk:** Maps of the Kharolis foothills and The Needle approaches. DC 14 Investigation: one map has hand-notations in two different inks — older annotations in black, newer ones in grey-blue. The grey-blue annotations mark positions that match the Reconstitution's current siege lines — these positions were noted *before* the siege began. Asholt knew the approach routes. History DC 12: the grey-blue positions are tactically optimal and have been used for optimal approaches to this pass for 200 years.
- **The cot:** Not slept in for at least two days; the blanket is folded but the fold is mechanical. Passive Perception DC 14: the pillow is slightly off-center; someone has been sitting on it, not lying.
- **The window:** Faces northeast. Arrow-slit width. The best view of the Reconstitution's siege lines in the keep. From this window: DC 10 Perception to see individual soldiers; DC 14 to read the insignia on the command tent standard. The command tent standard bears a device Asholt does not look at directly.
- **A locked chest (under the cot):** DC 18 Thieves' Tools to open. **Do not open this session.** Contents become relevant in 0027. If forced: contains correspondence (old; pre-siege) in a hand the party doesn't recognize yet, and a garrison token identical to the Watch-Tokens but with both faces carved.

## Encounter

Social (Asholt, if party intrudes). He asks if there is something requiring his attention. He answers one direct question with the minimum necessary. He ends the conversation.

## Treasure

None this session (the locked chest is 0027 material).

## Connections

- **Down (stairs):** to 06 Arrow-Walk — tower door, currently closed by convention

## GM Notes

**This room is not accessible this session** except by party initiative (opening the closed-but-unlocked door from the arrow-walk). If they enter without cause, Asholt is here, working, and closes the conversation within two exchanges.

**The shortcut (unlocks through play):** After the full token is earned at the finale (0028 Route E), Asholt gives the party access to this tower formally. The tactical benefit: Advantage on Perception checks to read Reconstitution troop movements from the window's commanding view. This shortcut has no effect in 0022 but is designed here for completeness.

**The grey-blue map annotations** are a breadcrumb for 0027: Asholt pre-knew the siege approach routes because he trained the person who would use them. This is discoverable but not yet meaningful — the party doesn't know Saren exists. File it in the handoff if discovered.

**The locked chest:** Contains Saren Coldforge's letters to Asholt over 20 years — she has written him twice. Both letters were never answered. This material belongs in 0027; do not unlock this session.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers in the room)*

> The desk has been cleared and reorganized recently — the maps are fresh-weighted, not habitual. Someone has been preparing to have this room entered. Or perhaps preparing not to have it entered. The result looks the same.
