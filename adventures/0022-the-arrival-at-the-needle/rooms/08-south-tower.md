# Room 08 — South Tower

## Read-aloud

> The garrison stores occupy the tower's lower two floors. Organized to a military standard: arrow quivers in racks, oil jars on shelves with their quantities chalked on the wall above, rope in measured coils. The upper floor is visible through a trapdoor — more rope, climbing gear, a rack of crossbows. Everything is inventoried. Someone here is counting carefully.

## Features

- **Arrow stock (lower):** 200 arrows in quiver-racks. The chalk tally on the wall shows the count and the date it was last checked. DC 12 Investigation: the count is slightly off — either a garrison member has been borrowing arrows without tallying, or someone has been taking them. (This is mundane attrition; the systematic sabotage of the west wall stock in 0024 is different and more severe.)
- **Oil jars (lower):** 8 jars of lamp oil, 2 jars of rendered fat. The fat jars are labeled "wall use only." DC 14 History or Arcana: rendered fat poured from a height and ignited produces a sustained fire that clings to cloth and leather. These are siege jars, not cooking supplies.
- **Rope and climbing gear (upper floor):** 150 feet of hempen rope in coils, 2 grappling hooks (garrison issue), block-and-tackle rig. The grappling hooks are the garrison's — not the sabotage-relevant items. Talis recognizes them as standard Solamnic garrison issue (automatic, Ranger background or Survival DC 10).
- **Crossbow rack:** 4 heavy crossbows, 120 bolts. The crossbows are maintained to inspection standard. One has a cracked stock that has been repaired with wire — functional but flagged.
- **The chalk tally wall:** A section of the tower's interior wall serves as a running inventory. The most recent entry is today's date. The handwriting changes three times — three different garrison members have maintained this log. The oldest entries are in Asholt's hand.

## GM Notes

The south tower is the Jaquays room for this adventure — the place where multiple valid approaches intersect. A party that finds the oil jars and grappling hooks before the assault has two additional options during Scene 5: drop oil and ignite it against the base of the east wall (effectively suppressing one grapple point for 2 rounds; 2d6 fire damage, DC 14 Dex save, to any soldier within 5 feet of the base), or use the garrison's grappling hooks to drop back down to the lower east exterior if the assault pushes them off the wall.

**Talis specifically:** If Talis examines the climbing gear (she will — it is in her decision order), she notices the grappling hooks' dimensions. The Reconstitution's hooks, based on what she can see from the arrow-walk, are a different pattern — shorter arms, meant for speed over load. She can tell the DM: *"They're going to place fast. Three hooks, maybe thirty seconds between first and last."* This is useful tactical information. It earns nothing from the garrison (they already know), but it demonstrates the party's competence.

**The chalk tally:** If a PC reads through the older entries (DC 10 to find Asholt's handwriting): the oldest dated entry is 22 years ago — the day the siege is implied to have begun in the long sense, the day Asholt arrived. He has been maintaining this inventory personally for 22 years. He passed it to garrison members somewhere around year 10. The date is visible but not significant yet.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers in the south tower)*

> The inventory has never, in 22 years, shown a shortfall. The count has always been exactly what was used plus what was brought in minus what was issued. Someone here treats counting as a form of faithfulness.
