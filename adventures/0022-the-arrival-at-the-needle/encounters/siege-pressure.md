# Siege Pressure Table

*Adapted from wandering-pressure format. This adventure takes place in a military siege, not a sealed dungeon. Standard wandering-encounter logic (monsters appearing) is replaced with siege-logic: escalating pressure events tied to time of day and day count. The dungeon-smith temporal-pressure requirement is honored through the siege's inherent time cost — the garrison's supply and morale degrade with every passing hour, and the Reconstitution will eventually push.*

## When to roll

Roll on the Pressure Table every **scene transition** during daylight hours (approximately once per scene). At night, pressure is replaced by the Night Assault (Scene 5) — no table roll; the assault is the night pressure.

Roll **2d6** and consult the table. A result of 7+ escalates; a result of 6- is atmospheric.

---

## Daylight Pressure (2d6, once per scene transition)

| Roll | Event |
|---|---|
| 2 | **Arrow from the ridge.** A single Reconstitution archer tests the garrison's discipline — one arrow arcing over the outer wall, landing near whoever is most exposed on the east wall. +4 attack, 1d8+2 piercing. DC 12 Perception to notice the shooter's position (advantage on saving throws vs. the next arrow if noticed). |
| 3–4 | **The waiting.** Nothing. The Reconstitution's lines are unchanged. This is worse in its way. Each garrison member present makes a DC 10 Wisdom save or gains one level of anxiety (not a mechanic — an atmospheric note; DM reads one Memory Fragment from the current room). |
| 5–6 | **A garrison member's equipment fails.** Minor — a bowstring snaps, a shield strap tears. Requires 10 minutes and a DC 12 tool check to repair. Tomek notes this without comment. It is not important. It is the kind of thing that adds up. |
| 7 | **Reconstitution signal.** A horn from the northeast ridge — three short blasts, meaning unknown to the party (History DC 15: standard Solamnic assembly signal, but the pattern is non-standard). The garrison responds by going to their wall positions. No attack follows. The horn was either a test or a miscommunication. |
| 8 | **The supply cart question.** A garrison member — not Tomek, not an officer — asks the party quietly: *"Do you know when the cart is coming?"* They do not ask Tomek because they already know the answer Tomek gives. They are asking the party because the party came from outside. DC 10 Insight: this is not an accusation; it is fear that sounds like a question. |
| 9 | **Scout spotted.** A Reconstitution scout is observed on the northeast ridge, closer than the siege line. They appear to be observing the east wall specifically. Disappears before action can be taken. DC 14 Perception (with a minute of watching): the scout is noting the party's positions, not the garrison's. They have not been seen by a reinforcement party before. |
| 10 | **Maret's count.** Healer Maret Stave updates her chalk tally. The number is lower than yesterday. She does not explain; she recalculates silently. DC 12 Medicine or Insight (Sava): she has added a new patient — a garrison member with a stress injury, not a combat wound. She is counting injuries she hasn't reported. |
| 11 | **Luca on the wall.** Luca Bent has been at the arrow-walk for six hours. He has not come down for food. If the party says nothing, he stays. DC 10 Insight: he is not watching for tactical reasons; he is watching for one person. If addressed, he says: *"I'm fine."* He is not fine. He is not a problem yet. |
| 12 | **Asholt, alone.** The Warden is on the east wall, alone, at an odd hour. He is not inspecting — he is standing. He is looking at the inverted standard. He does not notice the party if they are quiet (Stealth DC 12). If noticed by him: *"Is there something that requires attention?"* He is asking genuinely. He is also giving them a chance to say nothing and leave him to it. |

## Night Pressure

Night pressure is not a table — it is the assault (Scene 5, `encounters/night-assault.md`). After the assault, the night is quiet. The Reconstitution does not attack twice in one night. The DM should not roll the pressure table during the post-assault period.

---

## Day-count escalation

After each full session (day-count advances by ~6-8 days of siege time per session), the DM should note:
- **Supply Die degradation:** After any session with a combat encounter, roll the current Supply Die and record the result.
- **Garrison count:** Note any garrison losses in the session log. Losses are permanent.
- **Morale note (narrative only):** By Session 3, garrison members speak less. By Session 5, some have stopped eating in the hall. The DM tracks this atmospherically, not mechanically.

---

## DM note on temporal pressure

The Reconstitution will not assault in force until Session 6 (The Standard). They are a siege, not a storm. Their pressure is time, supply, and Saren's private clock — her command's recall orders. The party should feel this: the Reconstitution is patient because patience is winning. Every dawn the garrison wakes up with slightly less than the day before.

The pressure table's job is to make this visible without requiring a combat every scene.
