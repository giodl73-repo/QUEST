## Critique

### Overall assessment

The night assault ran correctly and the resource mechanics held up. 0 party HP damage against a Hard encounter on Session 1 is credible given the setup — the party earned the garrison archer coordination, Talis had optimal oil placement (DC 12, rolled 18), and AC 18 did real work. Two issues: Caelith's Fire Bolt fumbled three times in a Hard encounter with no backup ranged contribution, which is a design flaw in the Divination-wizard kit; and the campaign's central resource mechanic (Supply Die + between-session carry-over) was introduced in documentation but didn't create any *in-session* pressure this session.

### What worked

- **Scene 1.4 / Scene 1.6:** The oil jar pre-positioning (Talis Survival 18, Scene 1.4) fed directly into the assault. This is the workshop's first clean pre-combat preparation → combat payoff in a non-artifact adventure. The garrison archers following party positioning priorities (earned by wall prep, not by social roll) is honest mechanical cause-and-effect. Quote: *"The garrison archer assigned to that section of the wall gives Talis one glance that is not hostile. The oil jars are in the right place."* Pre-combat setup actually mattered.

- **Initiative order discipline:** The FUMBLE on garrison archer initiative (total 2) was accepted and narrated — they arrived late to position, which is what a FUMBLE means for a defender. This is correct dice discipline. Round 1 with only the party defending the wall is meaningfully harder than a Round 1 where archers are already in position. The combat math tracked.

- **Combat economy:** 3-round Hard encounter, 6 enemies, 4 PCs + 2 archers, 0 party damage. The action economy is transparent: Davan's AC 18 made three enemy attacks miss cleanly; Talis's height advantage on the arrow-walk produced tactical positioning value; Caelith's Fire Bolt cantrip being wind-deflected three consecutive times created genuine pressure on party DPS. The garrison archers picking up the DPS slack (A2 finished by Archer 1, A1 damaged by Archer 2) is an honest tactical outcome.

### What needs work

- **Caelith's kit vs. the encounter:** Fire Bolt missed all three times (total 6, 13, 10 vs AC 16). A Divination wizard in a windy siege environment with disadvantage-level wind resistance should not rely exclusively on a ranged attack cantrip. The party's combat kit has a structural gap: if Talis is on the arrow-walk and Davan is in melee, Caelith is the only second ranged attacker — and his cantrip failed three times against a medium-difficulty target number. This is a *design* issue, not a bad-dice issue. The Portent dice ([6, 7]) were too low to salvage any of the Fire Bolt misses vs. AC 16. Recommendation: the module should note that Caelith's Toll the Dead (Wis save vs. AC-based attack) would be more reliable in the assault context. The kit fix is pre-session, not in-session.

- **Supply Die pressure didn't land in combat:** The Supply Die opened at d12, degraded to d10 post-session. But in-session, the Supply Die produced zero tension: nobody took damage, nobody needed healing, nobody had to make a decision about spending healing resources. The mechanic is designed to create pressure across sessions, and that's correct — but S1 gave the party no mechanical experience of the resource system being *real*. The first time the Supply Die actually matters (Sava has 1 potion instead of 5 and someone is at 2 HP) is when the mechanic becomes alive. That moment hasn't arrived. S2 should deliver it.

### The question I'd push on

Why is Caelith the only PC who missed in combat? He missed 3 times; Davan, Sava, and Talis all hit every attack. Is the Divination wizard's cantrip the right primary offense for a resource-pressure campaign, or should the session-runner have flagged the kit problem in PREP? The first session established a tactical imbalance (Caelith is the least reliable damage dealer by a wide margin) that the campaign will need to work around or address.
