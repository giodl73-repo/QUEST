## Critique

### Overall assessment

This session did something genuinely difficult: it established four distinct character voices in a single adventure without any of the conventional tools (no curse, no artifact, no major decision point). What made it work was that each PC's grief paragraph found its analogue in the situation. Sava's most alive moment this session was meeting Maret, not fighting the assault. Caelith's most alive moment was the optional Warden's meeting and the notebook he wrote in after combat. The session trusted its PC sheets.

### What worked

- **Scene 1.2 — Sava and Maret:** *"Sava sees Maret Stave before she is introduced. A healer's station, organized to clinical standard."* Sava addresses Maret without hierarchy (*"Can I see the count?"*) and immediately receives what Maret hasn't told Asholt. The resonance with Elara — a healer rationing supplies alone, not telling command — is the session's most textured character beat. The log doesn't announce the connection; it's present in the scene construction. The roleplay payoff was in the specificity of Sava's question, which is exactly what Elara would have asked.

- **Scene 1.8 — Luca's disclosure and the silence:** *"My brother's out there."* Four PCs, four different silences. The log documents: Davan nodded; Sava was quiet; Talis looked at the standard and looked away; Caelith's next written note was longer. This is four distinct character responses that happen to produce the same behavioral outcome (nobody reports it) for completely different reasons. That's voice-distinction without a fight. The log gives us each PC's reason without explaining it. That's the standard.

### What needs work

- **Davan's grief is dormant:** Orel Fostermark doesn't appear this session, which is correct — the connection to this situation isn't built yet. But Davan's inner life in this session is mostly tactical (positions himself, uses correct ranks, complies with orders). The log notes *"His decision order: comply with direct orders from Asholt. Asholt's order was 'east wall.' He takes position three."* That's mechanically correct but characterologically thin. For the Roleplayer, S1 is fine — it's a first session — but Davan's grief needs its first fictional contact point in S2 or S3 before the party is too far into the siege. Right now he is the party's most legible soldier and least legible person.

- **Caelith's voice is analysis, not presence:** *"Caelith fires at A1 — now climbing again, approaching the lip. The bolt lights the rope without connecting. A1's face is briefly visible — a man doing a job. Caelith notes his expression."* The observation is sharp. But Caelith's voice in this session is entirely in his notebook and his internal analysis. He never speaks a line to another party member that isn't transactional (*"In total darkness, they'll locate your positions by sound..."*). His grief (Magistra Vonn, the Test, the rules) is absent. For a session where he watched a Reconstitution soldier retreat at the sight of an insignia, Caelith's institutional-analysis lens should have produced *some* out-loud comment. The log gives him a notebook entry and silence. Correct for his voice-tags, but the Roleplayer wants the first crack in that silence earlier.

### The question I'd push on

When does Davan's grief first become visible to the other PCs? His arc is designed around Orel — he gave testimony that was accurate and ruinous — and the campaign is literally about loyalty and cost. The situation at Thorngate is his grief's exact mirror (the right thing and the wrong outcome). He hasn't yet made that connection out loud, or even internally in the log. The Roleplayer wants to know: is S2 the session where that crack opens, or is Davan being saved for S5 (the Knight spotlight)?
