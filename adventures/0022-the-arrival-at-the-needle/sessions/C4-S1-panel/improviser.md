## Critique

### Overall assessment

This session rewarded off-book behavior consistently. Caelith's stone-drop tactic wasn't in the module; it produced a real outcome (garrison member started dropping stones). Talis's oil positioning was a module option, but the DC was fair and she succeeded, and the fiction honored it. The one place the session felt funnel-y is the token condition: it's designed for one specific behavioral pattern, and anything outside that pattern (even excellent behavior) doesn't earn the token. That's a tension between "realistic garrison trust" and "player freedom."

### What worked

- **Scene 1.4 — Caelith's stone-drop tactic:** This is the purest off-book moment in the session. The module does not suggest this. Caelith's player (his decision-order: *information in unknown situation*) arrived at a genuine tactical insight from first principles, ran it past Tomek, and got a realistic response: silence, followed by a garrison member doing it. *"At nightfall, a garrison member is dropping stones at irregular intervals over the west face."* The DM didn't explain. The DM didn't reward with a token. The garrison just... adapted to useful input. That's the correct adjudication of an off-book contribution — it lands in the world without ceremony.

- **Scene 1.2 — Talis's south tower exploration:** Talis didn't wait to be shown the south tower. She went directly, assessed the stores, found the oil jars, made the tactical inference. She failed the flank-gap Survival check (total 11 vs DC 12 — one short) and the module honored the fail: *"The specific gap in the Reconstitution's northeast flank does not resolve into clarity from this angle."* The failure is specific rather than generic ("you don't find anything useful"). It points her toward the arrow-walk. Correct adjudication of a near-miss.

### What needs work

- **Token condition is a narrow gate:** The token requires: held east wall without orders AND did not report to Asholt or Tomek. Both conditions are met in this session. But the condition's narrowness is the Improviser's concern: what if a party member *had* gone to Tomek — not to claim credit, but because Tomek is their tactical chain of command and reporting seemed like the right thing to do? Would they be penalized for correct Solamnic behavior? The session didn't surface this tension because every PC's heuristic aligned with the token condition, but the question stands. A different Knight player — one who read the Oath's reporting obligation more strictly — might have earned the token and then lost it in Scene 1.7. The module should address this edge case.

- **The optional Warden's meeting is under-explored:** The module labels Scene 3 optional, and Caelith triggers it by decision-order. But the briefing produces only the facts Asholt is willing to give. *"He answers Caelith's questions with minimum-sufficient information."* The Improviser reads this as: the DM had a clear ceiling on what Asholt would share, and everything above that ceiling hit the wall. What if Caelith had asked about the grey-blue annotations directly — mid-meeting, before filing them silently? The scene doesn't address this. Asholt's behavior matrix says he answers direct questions, but not about the lower level. The annotations are neither. A direct question about the annotations in the Warden's presence would have been a significant off-book moment; the session avoided it by having Caelith file silently and leave. That's characterologically correct, but the Improviser would have wanted to see what happened if Caelith had pushed.

### The question I'd push on

What does the DM do when a PC acts correctly (reports to Tomek after the assault) and earns nothing? The token condition requires NOT reporting. But the Oath of a Solamnic Knight arguably requires reporting any tactical development to command. Davan's heuristic (comply with direct orders) resolved this cleanly: Asholt said "east wall," not "report to me after any assault." But another reading of the Oath would produce reporting. The campaign needs to address this tension explicitly before S2, or a future session will stall when a PC's correct Oath-behavior conflicts with a token condition.
