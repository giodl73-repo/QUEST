## Strongest Consensus

**Atmospheric Landing (9/10)** — all five lenses endorsed this score without raising a single counter-claim. The Maret/Sava peer scene, the collective silence at Luca's disclosure, and the token-on-the-ledge at dawn are specifically cited across four of five reviews. The Tactician (lowest atmospheric weight at 0.4) didn't contest it; they simply didn't foreground it.

**Table Readiness (9/10)** — Tactician, Fresh Face, and Lorekeeper all noted that the module ran cleanly from its own materials. The garrison NPC behavior matrices, token condition documentation, and Supply Die cheatsheet worked as designed.

## Most Dissent

**What the first session owes a reader** — the Lorekeeper and Fresh Face disagree with each other in direction: the Lorekeeper wants more setting depth (the Thorbardin proximity, the Solamnic history weight); the Fresh Face wants less assumed knowledge (the Reconstitution unexplained, the token mechanic implicit). These two critiques pull in opposite directions. The module serves both by choosing *in-fiction glossing* — the Fresh Face's fix (one line explaining the Reconstitution) is also the Lorekeeper's fix (ground it in setting knowledge delivered naturally). The divergence is actually convergent when implemented.

**Caelith's tactical value** — the Tactician raised a structural concern (Fire Bolt missed 3/3 in the assault; no reliable ranged DPS from the wizard) that no other lens flagged. The Roleplayer would accept three misses as honest and characterful; the Improviser appreciated that the DM didn't rig a hit; the Lorekeeper didn't notice. But the Tactician is right: in a resource-exhaustion campaign where every slot matters, a wizard who can't contribute offensively via cantrips is relying entirely on spell slots. This will become a design tension in sessions 3-5 when slots are genuinely scarce.

## Convergent Suggestions (2+ lenses)

1. **Gloss the Reconstitution in-fiction in S2** (Lorekeeper + Fresh Face): One line from a named NPC — a garrison soldier, a captured Reconstitution soldier, even Luca — that explains what the Reconstitution believes and why the garrison's refusal is meaningful. This doesn't need to be a lecture; *"Saren's lot think the Council's been wrong since the Cataclysm"* from a garrison member is sufficient.

2. **Give Davan's grief its first contact point** (Roleplayer + Improviser): Both lenses noted that Davan is the party's least characterologically revealed PC after S1. His Orel connection and its mirror in the campaign situation have not yet surfaced. S2 (spotlight: Sava/Cleric, but Davan is present throughout) should produce one beat where Davan's experience of the Oath-as-accurate-and-wrong surfaces — even internally in the log.

3. **Make the Watch-Token mechanic legible in-fiction** (Fresh Face + Improviser): The Improviser's concern (the token condition is a narrow gate) and the Fresh Face's concern (the token is unexplained) point to the same fix: one in-fiction moment where the token's meaning is demonstrated, not stated. Tomek's first conversation with a garrison member who HAS earned trust — where the token-exchange is visible in context — would accomplish this without meta-commentary.

## Divergent Suggestions (single-lens)

- **(Tactician):** Flag Caelith's Fire Bolt vulnerability to wind/siege conditions in the adventure notes, and consider whether Toll the Dead is a better default cantrip for this campaign environment. *Tactician-specific: the other lenses don't share this concern.*

- **(Lorekeeper):** Add one line of terrain acknowledgment from a party member — the foothills, the proximity to Thorbardin — to ground the party's spatial relationship to the setting's history. *Lorekeeper-specific: Fresh Face doesn't need more lore; they need less.*

- **(Improviser):** Address the edge case: what happens when a PC reports correctly (Solamnic chain of command) and that reporting conflicts with the token condition? Document Tomek's specific response to a party member who reports after the assault. *Improviser-specific: the session resolved this cleanly by aligning party heuristics with the token condition, but future sessions may not.*

- **(Roleplayer):** The Roleplayer wants Caelith's first crack in his silence earlier. His notebook entry after combat is correctly understated, but the Roleplayer suggests seeding a line of dialogue for S2 where Caelith's observation of A1's face (noted in the log) surfaces as a spoken thought — the first time his "institutions" analysis becomes interpersonal rather than analytical.

## Questions Raised About the Module or the System

1. **The Reconstitution gloss gap** is a straightforward module fix for S2. Add to Tomek's NPC file: one line he can say to the party explaining the Reconstitution in garrison-plain-speech.

2. **Caelith's cantrip kit** is a design tension the session log flagged. The advisory note in the session log ("is Fire Bolt the right cantrip for siege conditions?") should feed into the S2 prep — either Caelith finds cover and angle, or the module acknowledges the wind disadvantage as a recurring condition.

3. **The Watch-Token mechanic's accessibility** — three lenses noted this in different forms. The PREP for S2 should include one sentence explaining the token system to a potential reader who encounters the log without the campaign spine.

4. **No rubric amendment proposed this session.** The two innovation flags (grief-mapped resource conservation; independent-motivation convergence toward collective restraint) are strong candidates for player-style seeds in S3+ if the patterns repeat.
