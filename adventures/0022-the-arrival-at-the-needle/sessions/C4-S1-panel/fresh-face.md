## Critique

### Overall assessment

The session is followable. The situation — *four people arrived to help a fortress, the fortress doesn't want their help, there's a fight overnight* — is clear from the first scene. The watch-token mechanic is visible in retrospect (the disc appears at dawn; a reader unfamiliar with the campaign can understand "this is a trust object"). The things I couldn't follow were the factions: "the Reconstitution" is named repeatedly but never explained. I knew they were the bad guys because they were throwing grappling hooks over a wall, not because the session told me what they believed.

### What worked

- **Scene 1.1 — The physical arrival:** The read-aloud does genuine work for a new reader: *"The pass narrows until the cliff faces are close enough to touch with arms extended, and then Thorngate Keep fills the gap — stone the same color as the rock it was built into."* I know where I am. I know it's cramped. I know the garrison is cold to us: *"Eighteen people who do not look up."* The *"You are welcome to help. You are not welcome to command"* line is so clear it could go on the cover. A new reader knows the conflict in Scene 1.1. That's fast.

- **Scene 1.6 — The combat is readable:** Three rounds, clear action economy, each PC did something specific: Caelith fired from above (and missed, which is honest), Sava cast the radiant flame (and tied the save, which is interesting), Davan and Talis handled the wall. The recognition-retreat — *"He sees the garrison archer standing three feet away, and the archer's collar, and the garrison device on it. He stops."* — is visually clear. I don't know what it *means* yet, but I know something happened. Good.

### What needs work

- **"The Reconstitution" is never explained:** The Fresh Face test: at the end of the session, can I explain to someone else what the Reconstitution is? Answer: they are a Solamnic faction that thinks they have authority over this garrison. I got that from context. But I don't know what the Solamnic orders are, why their authority would even be contested, or what the Reconstitution *wants* (beyond "control of the pass," which the session implies is a cover story). The Lorekeeper flagged this too, but from a different angle: I'm flagging it as a comprehension problem, not a fidelity problem. The session assumes I know what a Solamnic Knight is and why their internal politics matter. I don't. One in-fiction gloss — even from a garrison member speaking to a recruit — would have fixed this.

- **The Watch-Token mechanic is implicit:** The carved disc appears at dawn. Davan picks it up. *"He does not know what it means yet."* Neither does the reader. The mechanic is explained in the module's DM cheatsheet (every garrison member carries a token; giving it means "I will answer for you"), but that explanation is DM-facing. The session log never says what the token *means* in the fiction. A new reader has to wait for later sessions to understand what Davan is holding. This is intentional — the garrison's trust-language is demonstrated, not explained — but the Fresh Face would appreciate at least one line of narrative context. Even: *"The token is the garrison's word for trust, and they do not give it to outsiders."*

### The question I'd push on

The Watch-Token system is the campaign's central mechanic and it's entirely implicit in Session 1. A new reader understands that the disc matters because Davan treats it as mattering, not because the session explains it. This will work if later sessions gradually clarify the mechanic. But if Session 2 opens and nobody mentions the disc, a new reader may lose the thread entirely. How explicitly will the campaign explain its own trust-token mechanics to readers who arrive at Session 2 cold?
