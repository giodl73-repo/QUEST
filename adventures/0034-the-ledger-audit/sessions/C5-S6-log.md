# C5-S6 Session Log — The Ledger Audit

**Date in world:** PC 188, sixteen days after C5-S5. Dawn.
**Spotlight:** Ensemble
**Seed:** C5S6-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro was at the table two hours before the brief. He had the pick roll open — not the maintenance sequence, a different work: he was practicing the specific motion of removing pages from a spine without tearing. He had a practice booklet that approximated the ledger's binding weight. He was failing on the fourth attempt when the others arrived.

*"The binding is tighter than a standard accounting ledger,"* he said. *"I have removed three pages cleanly and torn one."*

Tessaly looked at the practice page. *"Three clean is enough if we choose which pages."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly opened the real ledger and identified the Pale entries: four pages, each with enough neutral content on adjacent pages that their removal would leave gaps she could paper over. The adjacent pages were the ones Mira would need to reproduce. She showed Mira which sections.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira checked the paper case. Then she opened it and took out three pages of her working notes — framework materials from S3, S4, and S5. She set them beside the ledger's open pages. She was going to need to reproduce Tessaly's hand from the actual document, not from her earlier study of public filings. She had fifteen minutes to recalibrate before she started.

She said nothing. She began.

---

## Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane: Dawn Brief

*"Pale's summons arrived yesterday. Midday, his office. Bring your records — operational review."* Tessaly spoke without inflection. *"He has used this language twice before. Both times, the reviewed team's records were destroyed afterward."*

*"Are we being burned?"* Cael asked.

*"He tested us at the Anchor Bell. We passed. He is reviewing us before deciding. The records review is part of the deciding."*

She had a plan. Not a clean plan. Three parallel actions: Mira producing substitute pages (ten pages, four hours), Darro removing the Pale entries from the binding (DC 13 Sleight of Hand with care), Cael watching the approaches to Cordwainer Lane. The substitute had to look like Tessaly's hand, match her coding conventions, and survive Pale's scrutiny.

She did not mention what she expected would happen if it didn't.

---

## Scene 2 — The Forgery Work

Mira worked from the neutral section of the ledger. She had Tessaly's hand from months of observation. She had the format conventions documented in four components of the framework.

Mira Forgery Kit (INT +1), DC 15 for pages that survive Pale's scrutiny: **d20+1 → rolls=[5] mod=+1 total=6**. The ink weight was wrong — consistently slightly too light. She knew it was wrong before she finished the third page. She adjusted on the fourth page. Too late.

Second attempt on the compromised pages, DC 12: **d20+1 → rolls=[7] mod=+1 total=8**. Still below DC 12. The correction made the fourth page inconsistent with the third in a way that Tessaly confirmed with three seconds of looking: *"The third page is too light. The fourth overcorrects. An auditor will notice if he is looking specifically."*

Darro's page removal, DC 13 Sleight of Hand: **d20+8 → FUMBLE rolls=[1] mod=+8 total=9**. The first page came out clean. The second page tore — a small tear, two millimeters, at the upper margin where the binding thread passed through. The tear was not structural but it was visible under close examination.

Two failures. They had four hours. They had used two of them.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly looked at the pages. She did not show the expression she was suppressing. She said: *"We go with what we have. The water-damage cover. Several pages were damaged and rewritten from backup notes. It is the only cover that fits both the ink inconsistency and the tear."*

She wrote the water-damage notation into the margin of one of the undamaged pages — a small note in her own hand, dated three months ago, that could be read as a contemporaneous record of the damage event. She had just retroactively documented something that had not happened. This was a forgery of her own record.

She looked at it for a moment. Then she wrote the date.

---

## Scene 3 — Pale's Office

Midday. The shipping concern, second floor. Orren Pale was at a desk with two assistants. He was in his fifties, well-dressed in the understated way of someone who spent money on quality rather than visibility. He had the professional neutrality of someone who had read ledgers for forty years and found most of them unsurprising.

He took the substitute ledger without comment. He began reading.

Cael positioned himself between the party and the door. Contract check: left hand, flat to chest, withdrawn.

Pale read quickly. Three questions:

1. *"Where is the Nenne Ashwick job recorded?"* (Present — accurate, in the substitute.)
2. *"The Aldras Bent extraction — what was the fee structure?"* (Present — accurate.)
3. *"Pages eight through eleven — the ink weight varies from your previous entries in this book. And there is a small tear at the upper margin of page nine. How did this happen?"*

Tessaly's Insight on the three questions: **d20+4 → rolls=[16] mod=+4 total=20**. Questions one and two were genuine verification. Question three was not verification — he had already found the problem and he was asking how she would respond to being called on it.

*"Water damage,"* she said. *"Three months ago, a storage problem at Cordwainer Lane. Several pages were damaged and I recopied from backup notes. I flagged it at the time."* She gestured toward the retroactive margin notation.

Pale's active Investigation check (+4), DC 17 to find the full scope of the forgery: **d20+4 → rolls=[16] mod=+4 total=20**. His 20 meant he found inconsistency in two pages, not just one. He looked at the margin notation. He was silent for four seconds.

Then he closed the ledger. He said: *"The review is complete. Thank you for coming."*

He found it. He chose not to call it.

---

## Scene 4 — After

They were on the street two minutes later. None of them spoke for half a block.

Cael said: *"He knows."*

It was the same phrasing as Scene 3 of C5-S5. He said it the same way. He did not elaborate.

*"He knows,"* Tessaly confirmed. She had the real Pale entries in the waterproof sleeve at the secondary location. She had the substitute ledger back in the satchel — Pale had returned it. *"He reviewed the substitute and returned it. He did not call it. He chose not to call it."*

*"Why didn't he call it?"* Darro asked.

*"Because calling it would require him to explain why he was looking for a forgery rather than verifying operational records. It would expose the fact that he expected a forgery. He would have to admit he was looking specifically."*

She wrote the entry. Pale's name, for the fifth time. *Pale — reviewed substitute — found inconsistency — chose not to call — reason: exposure of intent.* She wrote the reason before she put the pen down.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira opened the paper case on the walk back to Cordwainer Lane. A brief opening — forty seconds while walking, while Tessaly and Cael were three paces ahead, while Darro watched the street. She added something. A single line in her framework notes. Then she closed the jacket. She had been watching Pale's questioning style — his method of testing while appearing to verify — and she had recorded one behavioral characteristic of how Pale's official documentation works.

She had not said she was doing this.

---

## Scene 5 — Cordwainer Lane Close

At Cordwainer Lane, Tessaly removed the waterproof sleeve from the secondary location. She spread the real Pale entries on the table — the four pages Darro had removed, the ones she had not shown Pale. She re-read them. She was verifying that the substitute had correctly omitted them and nothing else.

*"The substitute was clean except for the pages Mira produced. The Pale entries were not present. He reviewed for three minutes. He found the ink inconsistency. He did not call it."*

She put the entries back in the sleeve.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira's paper case had grown. Sixth component — Pale's auditor style, the three-question structure, the behavioral pattern of a man who tests while verifying. She had not said she was building toward the final job. She was doing preparation. She closed the jacket.

She had been doing preparation for six sessions.
