# Room 03 — Pale's Operations Office

## Read-aloud
> Second floor. A clean desk. Two young people at side tables with inkwells and blank sheets — transcriptionists, not bodyguards. Orren Pale is behind the desk: mid-fifties, heavy-set, with the look of a man for whom professional neutrality is not a posture but a habit of many years. He does not rise when the party enters.

## Features

- **Orren Pale.** Full NPC file: `npcs/orren-pale.md`. He takes the substitute ledger without comment. He reads at speed.
- **The three questions.** He asks them in the order Tessaly anticipated (she is good at this). All three have accurate answers in the substitute — because Tessaly replicated her real records accurately, omitting only the Pale entries.
- **Page seven.** He pauses. He reads it twice. Passive Perception DC 10 for anyone watching: he pauses (one full beat) before moving on. He does not ask a question about page seven.
- **"Satisfactory."** He closes the ledger and says one word. He hands it back. He says: "I'll have another review in thirty days." He does not look at Tessaly when he says it.
- **The transcriptionists.** They wrote nothing during the review. Their pages are blank. They were never going to transcribe anything unless Pale found something.

## GM Notes

Pale's review is the session's pressure peak. The party knows the substitute pages may not be perfect. They have to hold neutral expression for fifteen minutes while he reads.

The pause at page seven is Pale finding a flaw in one of Mira's DC 12 pages (the one she produced under time pressure). He does not act on it now. He is deciding whether the flaw means the ledger was replaced or simply that Tessaly had an off day. He is a careful man. He will decide later.

Pale's "thirty days" is not a standard review cycle — it is an acceleration. He reviews teams annually unless he suspects something. Thirty days means he suspects something. Tessaly knows this.

The transcriptionists' blank pages: this is the tell that the review was a fishing exercise. If Pale had found what he was looking for, they would have written. They wrote nothing. He found something adjacent to it. He is still fishing.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
