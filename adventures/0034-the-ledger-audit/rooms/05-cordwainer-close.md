# Room 05 — Cordwainer Lane (Session Close)

## Read-aloud
> Tessaly reintegrates the original pages from Darro's sleeve. She adds the audit entry to the real ledger before she speaks. She closes the ledger. Then: "We have one session to complete the job before he decides what he saw on page seven."

## Features

- **The reintegration.** Darro hands over the waterproof sleeve. The original Pale entries go back into the ledger spine. Tessaly handles this herself — it is her ledger; she closes it herself.
- **The audit entry.** Written before she speaks: the date, Pale's three questions, the pause at page seven, the thirty-day review cycle, the transcriptionists' blank pages. The factual record.
- **"One session."** The S7 operational timeline. She does not say what the final job is — not yet. She says "three days" for the brief.
- **What Mira does.** She checks the paper case before she leaves. This is the third act-without-announcement instance (if scored). She does not explain what she checked. She leaves.
- **The real ledger, closed.** Tessaly holds it for a moment after adding the entry. She does not put it in the satchel immediately. She holds it. Then she puts it away. This is the only moment this session where the ledger is something other than a professional tool — it is also what she has been protecting for eleven years. Then it goes in the satchel and she moves to the next task.

## GM Notes

The ledger holding moment is the sheet-deep-reader beat: the loyalty object in its full weight. Not grief — recognition. The ledger has been through an audit, survived, and will face one more. She holds it because it is complete and accurate and she has kept it that way for eleven years. Then she puts it away because the work is not done.

Mira's paper case check is the act-without-announcement close. Score it at handoff.

The session ends with the party knowing they have one session left before Pale decides what he saw. The S7 brief is three days away. The forgery framework is ready. The party does not yet know this. Mira does not say it.
