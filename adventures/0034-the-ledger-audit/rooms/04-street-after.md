# Room 04 — The Street After

## Read-aloud
> Two blocks. Nobody speaks. Then Tessaly says: "He paused at seven." She keeps walking.

## Features

- **The silence.** Two blocks of professional decompression. Nobody explains what happened — they all know. The audit passed. Pale paused at page seven. The session is not over.
- **Tessaly's statement.** She names the tell without elaborating. She is updating the party's shared intelligence: there is a flaw in page seven, Pale noticed it, and he did not act on it yet.
- **Cael's contract check.** Left hand, flat to chest, withdrawn. He processed the audit as a professional test. The code held: he did not sell a name to Pale (Pale asked no questions that required names — but the substitute ledger itself is a form of not selling the names in the real one). Score at handoff.
- **Mira's silence.** She says nothing for two blocks. She is thinking about page seven. She is thinking about what the S7 forgery would need to be if Pale's scrutiny is at that level. She updates the paper case later, in private. This is the second act-without-announcement instance.

## GM Notes

The street-after scene is a breath of normalcy between the audit's tension and Cordwainer Lane's close. Let the silence hold. Tessaly's statement is enough. The party processes on the walk.

Mira's internal activity (building the S7 framework) is the act-without-announcement research moment. It is not visible except through the paper case update (which happens at Cordwainer Lane before she leaves). If the session-runner can register this as separate from Mira's visible behavior, score it.
