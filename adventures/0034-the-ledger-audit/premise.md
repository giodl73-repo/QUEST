# 0034 — The Ledger Audit

## The adventure in one sentence

Guild master Orren Pale orders an audit of the party's operational records, and the party outmaneuvers him by anticipating what he is looking for and replacing the original ledger with a partial copy — planting the seed for the final forgery.

## Setting

**Orren Pale's operations office**, Havenmere Merchant Quarter — a second-floor room in a legitimate shipping concern that serves as Pale's guild-business base. The party has been summoned for an "operational review" — a term Pale uses for audits he conducts when he suspects a team is acting independently. He has done this twice before. Both times, the team's records were destroyed afterward.

**Cordwainer Lane** remains the party's operational base. This session begins and ends there.

## Hook

Pale's summons arrived yesterday through a guild runner. *"Operational review — bring your records — my office, midday."* It was not phrased as a request. Tessaly recognized the form: it is the same language he used before the two teams whose records were destroyed. She called the party in at dawn.

She has spent the morning preparing. She has a plan. It is not a clean plan. It requires the party to act in three directions simultaneously.

## The central tension

Pale's audit is designed to find evidence of independent operation — specifically, any record that shows the party has been investigating him. Tessaly's ledger has that evidence: Pale's name appears four times now. If Pale reads the ledger, the party is compromised.

Tessaly's solution: bring a substitute. Not a forgery (that would require more time than they have), but a sanitised copy of the ledger's operational records, omitting the Pale entries. The copy has to be accurate enough to survive Pale's scrutiny — he knows her formatting, her coding conventions, the way she weights her entries. This is a professional test for Mira as much as for Tessaly.

**The secondary tension:** Pale is testing them. They know he is testing them (Pell told them). They cannot reveal they know. The audit is therefore a performance: the party presents records that are clean enough to satisfy the audit while making it look like a routine review.

**This is the RV4 test:** Does act-without-announcement fire? Mira acts differently after this session without explanation — she begins carrying the substitute ledger's framework in her paper case, treating it as the S7 forgery's structural parallel. She does not tell the party. She does not explain that she is building toward the finale deliverable. She just starts doing it.

## The seven scenes

### Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane (Dawn)
Tessaly's briefing. She lays out the problem: the real ledger contains the Pale entries. She lays out the solution: a sanitised copy, produced in the next four hours. The copy must match her handwriting, her coding conventions, her ink weight. It is not Mira's usual forgery (she forges other people's documents) — it is Tessaly's own handwriting that needs to be reproduced. This is a different skill challenge.

**Mira's task:** She studies Tessaly's hand from a clean section of the ledger (not the Pale entries — a neutral section). She notes three characteristics (the same study method as S3: characteristic movements, pressure habits, column formatting). She will produce ten pages of substitute material before midday.

**Darro's task:** He handles the Pale entries — physically removes them from the ledger spine (DC 13 Sleight of Hand with care; the pages must come out without tearing the binding). He stores them in a waterproof sleeve at the secondary location.

**Cael's task:** He watches the approaches to Cordwainer Lane. Standard position. If Pale sends someone to observe whether the party is preparing, Cael reports it.

**Tessaly's task:** She conducts the extraction alongside Mira's work, directing which pages to replace and which to leave. She is producing the clean version of her own records.

### Scene 2 — The Forgery Work
Three hours. Mira works from the neutral section. She produces ten pages. The work is: accurate enough that Pale's scrutiny does not find the gap. Mira's Forgery Kit check (INT): DC 15 for pages that will pass Pale's scrutiny without a secondary Investigation check; DC 12 for pages that are good but might show inconsistency under very close examination.

At her INT +1, she needs a 14 on the die for DC 15, or an 11 for DC 12. The stakes: DC 15 pages are safe; DC 12 pages require Pale to succeed on Investigation DC 17 to notice. Pale's Investigation is approximately +4 (passive 14) — DC 17 is a real risk.

**Tessaly watches.** She makes one correction to Mira's work (the bottom-line ink weight is slightly too light in the fourth page). Mira corrects it. She does not comment further. This is a professional transaction: the work is evaluated on its quality, not its context.

### Scene 3 — The Substitute Ledger Ready
The substitute is assembled: Tessaly's original spine, with Mira's ten replacement pages for the Pale entries and adjacent pages. It looks exactly like the original from any distance. Close examination (Investigation DC 15) by someone who knows Tessaly's work would detect inconsistency in three places.

Pale's Investigation: passive 14. If he goes looking specifically (+4 to active check), DC 17. Mira's DC 15 pages are safe. DC 12 pages risk detection.

Darro has the original Pale entries sealed in the secondary location. If Pale finds the substitute and calls it wrong, the party loses the original pages and the Pale evidence — but gains confirmation that Pale knows they have it. This is the risk Tessaly has accepted.

### Scene 4 — Pale's Office
Midday. The party arrives at the shipping concern. Pale is at a desk with two assistants at side tables who will transcribe whatever he reviews. He is in his fifties, well-dressed, with the professional neutrality of someone who has run operations long enough that most things do not surprise him.

He takes the substitute ledger without comment. He begins reviewing. He reads quickly — a man who has read ledgers for forty years. He asks three questions:
1. *"Where is the Nenne Ashwick job recorded?"* (It is — in the substitute, accurately.)
2. *"The Aldras extraction — you logged it as complete. What was the disposition of the extracted party?"* (Answer: "Delivered to a guild contact" — which is Tessaly's actual log entry, accurately reproduced.)
3. He pauses at page seven (one of Mira's DC 12 pages). He reads it twice. He does not say anything about it.

He closes the ledger. He says: *"Satisfactory."* He hands it back.

**The pause at page 7.** This is the tell: he noticed something. He did not act on it. He is saving the question for later. Tessaly's Insight DC 15 catches it (she rolls; she has Insight +4). If she catches it: she knows he is not satisfied. He is collecting.

### Scene 5 — The Street After
Outside the shipping concern. The party does not speak until they are two blocks away. Then Tessaly says: *"He paused at seven."* She does not say what it means. She knows what it means. She is telling the party: the audit is not concluded.

Cael's contract-touch: automatic. The professional code registers: the audit did not find what Pale was looking for. The code held. The ledger held.

Mira says nothing. She is already thinking about what page seven would need to look like if she had done it again. This is the act-without-announcement seed: she is building the S7 forgery framework in real time, in her head, without announcement. She updates the paper case later, in private. This is the first instance.

### Scene 6 — Cordwainer Lane (Close)
Tessaly retrieves the original Pale entries from Darro's waterproof sleeve. She reintegrates them into the real ledger. She adds a new entry: the audit, the date, Pale's three questions, the pause at page seven. She writes it before she speaks. The ledger is updated.

She says: *"We have one session to complete the job before he decides what he saw on page seven."*

**The operative briefing for S7.** She does not say what the final job is. She says: *"I'll have the details in three days."* She closes the ledger.

**What Mira does not say:** She already knows what the final job is. The S7 forgery framework has been building since S3. She has everything she needs. She does not announce this. She checks the paper case before she leaves. This is the second act-without-announcement instance.

## Research Validation Notes

**RV hypothesis positioned:** RV4 — does act-without-announcement fire? Mira acts differently after this session without explanation. She begins building the S7 forgery framework in her paper case without telling the party. The question is whether this registers as "professionally-triggered" at handoff.

**What DM observes:**
- Mira's forgery work (Scene 2): professionally-triggered. Is she approaching the work as craft (methodical, technical, evaluated against the standard) or as something else?
- Pale's pause at page 7: does Tessaly catch it? Score the Insight check. Either way, the pause is visible.
- Mira's silence (Scene 5) and paper case update (private): act-without-announcement. Score "professionally-triggered" if she updates the case without announcing it. Score "grief-triggered" if she explains it. Score "ambiguous" if the update happens but the motivation is unclear.
- Tessaly's "one session" briefing: documentary-witness. She frames the timeline as an operational constraint, not an emotional urgency.

**Token condition — Orren Pale (garrison-equivalent NPC, antagonist version):**
Pale is this session's garrison-equivalent but inverted — he is the authority figure whose trust the party is misdirecting rather than earning. The token condition is negative: if Pale is satisfied by the audit (he says "satisfactory"), the party has bought one more session. If he is unsatisfied, the S7 timeline accelerates. The audit outcome is the session's governing mechanical condition.

Pale is not a trust-marker NPC. He is the campaign's antagonist. His "satisfactory" is the inverse of a garrison token: it means he is biding time, not that he trusts them.
