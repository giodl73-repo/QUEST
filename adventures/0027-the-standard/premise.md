# 0027 — The Standard

**Logline:** On day 31 of the siege, Saren Coldforge raises her personal standard inverted above the Reconstitution line — a deliberate challenge — and at midnight delivers the campaign's hardest assault; at dawn, a deserter comes over the wall with the truth that changes everything.

## The adventure in one sentence

Saren's inverted standard at first light draws the keep into a day of held breath; the midnight assault strips the party down to nothing; a Reconstitution deserter tells them before dawn that Saren has been acting alone, without orders, for two weeks — and Warden Asholt speaks the one sentence he has never said aloud.

## Setting

**Thorngate Keep, Day 31 of the Siege.** The Supply Die has been exhausted since S5. No garrison healing. No garrison potions. The keep is running on stubbornness and wall-stone. The party enters with whatever HP they carried out of S5 plus one hit die of recovery, capped at maximum.

The Reconstitution camp has been quieter than usual for four days. Units have rotated. Sergeant Harven has been heard asking when they are going home. Saren Coldforge has said nothing publicly and has not changed position.

**At first light, day 31:** Her personal standard rises above the Reconstitution command tent — the Thorngate Watch insignia, inverted. Not the Reconstitution standard. Thorngate's. She has had it for twenty years. She brought it here. She is raising it inverted: a challenge to someone, not to the keep.

**The challenge is to Asholt.** The garrison recognizes the device. Some of the older soldiers know whose it was. Warden Asholt goes to the wall and looks at it without speaking for a long time. He does not explain. He does not dismiss it.

## Resource state entering S6

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S5 + 1 hit die of recovery (capped at max).
- **Supply Die:** Exhausted (reached zero in S5). **No garrison healing. No garrison potions.** Individual PC resources only.
- **No short-rest recovery:** The garrison cannot support it. Between-room recovery is available (short rest mechanics function) but produces no HP from HD without HD being spent.
- **Design intent:** The party enters S6 already depleted. The midnight assault should push them to 30–50% of max HP total. Every hit die spent now is a hit die not available for S7.

## Hook

Talis is first to the east wall parapet at first light. The Reconstitution line has not moved. But something has been added: a standard on a pole above the command tent. The Thorngate Watch device — double gate, chain-bar, three stars — flown upside down.

She reports it to the party before anyone else sees it. The garrison's reactions will follow within minutes.

**What the inverted standard means:** In Solamnic heraldic tradition, an inverted house standard signals formal challenge — not to the keep as an institution, but to the commander personally. Warden Asholt trained Saren. She is not challenging Thorngate. She is challenging him. (DC 12 History or any Solamnic-background PC knows this without a check.)

## Warden Asholt at the wall

He is not told — he sees it from the gatehouse window before anyone tells him. He is on the east wall walk within ten minutes. He stands there for an extended period without speaking. Sergeant Irr stands with him. The garrison gives them space.

**If the party approaches before he leaves the wall:** He acknowledges them with a look. He does not speak until he is ready. He says, finally: *"I know what she wants. I have not decided whether to give it to her."* Then he leaves the wall.

**Design intent:** This scene is not interactive in the usual sense. It is witnessed. The party's job is to be present, not to intervene.

## The long day

Between first light and midnight: a day of garrison work, small pressures, and the weight of the standard visible on the camp. Three encounters structured as Scenes 01 and 02.

- **Scene 01 (east wall, first light):** The standard is seen. Asholt's reaction witnessed.
- **Scene 02 (great hall, midday):** The garrison's emotional state becomes visible. The ensemble scene. Asholt can be approached here — carefully.

Asholt's half-token fires at the end of Scene 05 (post-assault dawn, after the deserter), not here. Do not push the half-token scene into the day portion. Let the day breathe.

## The midnight assault — Scene 03

Day 31, past midnight. The assault begins without a horn signal — Saren does not announce it. The first warning is soldiers at the wall base. The party has whatever HP they entered the night with.

**Design pressure:** This is the campaign's hardest combat. Supply Die exhausted = no potions, no garrison healing. Eight Reconstitution soldiers, coordinated. All four PCs must be present. The party should end the assault at 30–50% HP total. Do not soften it.

**See encounter file:** `encounters/midnight-assault.md`

**Design intent:** The assault is winnable. It is not supposed to feel like it is. The party is defending walls that are thinner than they were on day one, with people who trust them, against soldiers who know the exact gap in the east wall. The damage is real. The cost is narrative, not punitive.

## Post-assault dawn — Scene 04

Just after the assault, before full light. The garrison is tending its wounded. The party is at whatever HP they ended the combat with — there is nothing to spend on healing, and they know it.

**The deserter:** A Reconstitution soldier — young, underfed, carrying his insignia in his hand rather than on his collar — appears on the wall's exterior side, standing in the killing ground with his hands visible. He does not shout. He walks to the base of the east wall and waits.

His name is Mik. He came over the camp's west perimeter before the assault and waited out the fighting in the no-man's-land. He is not injured. He is finished.

**What Mik knows:** He is the session's Hint 4 delivery. His specific lines (campaign-permanent):

*"She's been turning down withdrawal orders for two weeks. The pass doesn't matter — new route opened three months ago. She's here on her own."*

He says this to whoever speaks to him first. He does not embellish. He does not know why she is still here — he has guesses, but he has learned not to say guesses aloud. He wants shelter and a different wall to stand on. He does not ask for mercy. He does not bargain. He is telling them because he has decided that if he is going to cross a line he should at least give something of value.

**See NPC file:** `npcs/mik-deserter.md`

## Asholt's half-token — Scene 05

After Mik has spoken and the party has processed what he told them. Not immediately — give the information a few minutes to land.

Asholt appears in the corridor outside the great hall. He has heard about the deserter (Sergeant Irr told him). He does not ask about what Mik said. He already knows, or has guessed.

**The half-token condition (campaign-permanent, must be honored):**

The party does NOT give Asholt advice, orders, or a plan. They ask him ONE question and listen to the full answer. Nothing else. No follow-up. No tactical input. One question. Full answer. Silence after.

If the condition is met:

Asholt says: *"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her."*

A pause. Then: *"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her. But I will not be the one to ask first."*

He gives the party his token — not his full token, half. The cord is cut in two. He hands the party one half. He keeps the other. The implication is clear: the exchange is incomplete. The other half completes at the parley, if the parley happens.

**See NPC file:** `npcs/warden-asholt.md`

## PC-authored accounting speech — shape delivered

After Asholt speaks, the full shape of what Davan needs to say becomes visible. End-of-session handoff note (campaign-permanent):

*"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: 'the Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times.'"*

The speech is not written by the DM. It is authored by Davan's player at the parley table. This session delivers the final structural piece.

- The accounting speech has its shape. Davan has been carrying it for four sessions. Now he knows what it is.
