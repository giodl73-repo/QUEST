# C4-S6 SESSION LOG — The Standard

*Day 31. An inverted standard at first light. A day of held breath. A midnight assault with nothing in reserve. A deserter at dawn. And Warden Asholt speaks the one sentence he has never said aloud.*

---

## Scene 01 — First Light, East Wall (Room 01)

Talis is the first to the east wall parapet. This is not her assignment — first light watch belongs to Sergeant Irr's rotation — but she is awake before the watch change and she climbs the east stairs because she has been climbing them since Day 1 and old patterns do not break before they are ready.

She sees it before anyone else.

Above the Reconstitution command tent — 300 feet north, at the tree line — a standard has been raised on a pole. The device is the Thorngate Watch. Double gate, chain-bar, three stars. She knows it from every garrison inspection: it is carved above the keep's main gate, stitched into the Warden's dress-cloak, stamped on the supply ledger's cover.

It is flying upside down.

Talis stands on the parapet for a long moment. Then she goes to find Davan.

Davan's History check:

**🎲 Davan History (inverted standard meaning, DC 12)** — `1d20+2` → rolls=[7] mod=+2 **total=9 (fail, below DC 12)**

He does not know the formal meaning. But Sava is there, and Sava's background touches on Solamnic heraldry from her medical training in the Order's garrison hospitals. She explains it to Davan in a sentence: an inverted house standard signals formal personal challenge — not to the institution, but to the commander.

*"She is not challenging Thorngate,"* Sava says. *"She is challenging Asholt."*

Warden Asholt sees it from the gatehouse window before anyone tells him. He is on the east wall walk within ten minutes, in his duty cloak, without his sword. Sergeant Irr is with him. The garrison gives them space.

Asholt stands at the parapet and looks at the standard for a long time. The party is on the wall when he arrives. He acknowledges them with a look. He does not speak. He stands at the parapet and looks at it the way someone looks at a thing they expected and did not expect to feel.

<!-- SURPRISE: Asholt's face as he looks at the inverted standard — the garrison has never seen him look like that before. Not grief. Something more specific: recognition, and something unresolved that has been waiting to be resolved. -->

He says, finally, before he leaves the wall: *"I know what she wants. I have not decided whether to give it to her."*

He leaves. He does not explain.

**Asholt's beat: witnessed.** The party's job is to be present. They are.

## Scene 02 — Midday, The Great Hall (Room 02)

The garrison's emotional state has been building for thirty-one days. The great hall at midday — the meal hour, which is not a meal now because the garrison has been on half-rations since Day 25 — shows it.

**The ensemble scene:**

**Sergeant Irr** is in the hall's northwest corner, polishing his hauberk methodically. He has been doing this since before breakfast. The hauberk does not need polishing. He is doing something with his hands because his mind has nothing useful to hold onto.

*Davan's moment:* Davan sits across from Irr without being asked, picks up a piece of whetstone, and begins work on his belt knife. They do not speak for ten minutes. Irr puts down the cloth, looks at Davan, and says: *"You count things. How long do you think we have?"* Davan says: *"Long enough."* Irr does not ask what that means.

**Maret Stave** is in the supply annex, cross-referencing her medical log against the garrison's remaining supply. She has her healer's kit open on the table and she is not using it.

*Sava's moment:* Sava finds her there and sits beside her. Maret shows her the log without being asked — the column that stopped getting entries three weeks ago, when the supply lines were cut. The notation in the margin: *23 names. No remedies.*  Sava reads it. She does not say anything about Elara. She puts her hand on Maret's wrist, briefly, and they sit together with the empty supply column between them.

**Luca** is on the east wall parapet, watching the standard. He does not look away from it.

*Talis's moment:* Talis climbs to the wall and stands beside him. She does not tell him to come down. She stands there for five minutes. He says, without looking away from the standard: *"My brother has been under that flag for thirty-one days."* Talis says: *"So have we."* She goes back down.

**Caelith** is in the small room off the corridor where he has been keeping his notebooks. He has three notebooks open. He is annotating something.

*Caelith's moment:* The grey-blue ink appears in the margins of his notes about the contractor drop point. The annotations that have been running since the beginning of the campaign — the architectural observations, the structural notes — converge here, in this session. He is reading his own notes and seeing a pattern: everything he has documented about Thorngate's supply vulnerability, the contractor network, the siege's economics — it all points to a person who needed *time*, not victory. He writes, in the margin: *"She has not asked for authorization. She is not waiting to win. She is waiting to be heard."* He looks at it. He closes the book.

<!-- SURPRISE: Caelith's grey-blue margin note closes the annotation thread that has run since C4-S1. He has been documenting something for six sessions; now he names what it means. -->

**Four ensemble moments. All four PCs have a scene.**

## Scene 04 — Post-Assault Dawn (Room 04): Mik

Just after the assault. Before full light. The garrison tends its wounded (four garrison members hurt; none dead). The party stands in the cleared breach, exhausted, with nothing left in reserve.

A figure appears at the east wall exterior. A young man — underfed, collar insignia in his hand rather than on it — walking toward the wall base with his hands visible.

Talis sees him first from the parapet. She does not nock an arrow. She watches. He stops at the base of the wall and waits.

He does not shout. He waits.

Talis calls down: *"Who are you?"*

He says: *"Mik. I'm done."*

He is brought inside through the postern. Sava checks him — not injured, malnourished, tired. He is not a threat.

Davan asks him what he knows. Mik says:

**Hint 4 (full delivery — campaign-permanent):**

*"She's been turning down withdrawal orders for two weeks. The pass doesn't matter — new route opened three months ago. She's here on her own."*

That is all. He does not embellish. He does not know why she is still here — he has guesses, but he has learned not to say guesses aloud. He wants shelter and a different wall to stand on. He does not ask for mercy. He does not bargain.

**H4 fully delivered.** All four hints are now in the party's hands.

The garrison's reaction: Sergeant Irr takes the information with his jaw set. He has suspected something like this for a week — Saren's assault patterns have been inconsistent with a commander trying to win. He has suspected they were not designed to win. He says nothing and goes to check on his wounded.

## Scene 05 — Asholt's Corridor (Room 05)

Asholt appears in the corridor outside the great hall. He has heard about Mik (Irr told him). He does not ask what Mik said. He already knows, or he has guessed.

The party stands in the corridor. He stands across from them.

The module's condition is specific and the party meets it. They do not give Asholt advice. They do not offer a plan. They do not suggest what he should do next. They ask one question.

Sava asks it: *"Why did she leave?"*

Not "what do you know about Saren" — not a tactical question. A human one. The question that names the thing everyone has been circling for six sessions.

Asholt is quiet for a long moment. He says:

**Asholt's arc-completion-adjacent line (campaign-permanent):**

*"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her."*

A pause.

*"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her. But I will not be the one to ask first."*

He reaches into his coat and removes something: a wooden disc on a cord. He cuts the cord with his belt knife. One half in his hand. He gives the other half to the party — to Davan specifically. He says: *"The other half stays with me. When she sits at the table."*

**Asholt's half-token earned (6th total).** The disc is split. The other half completes the token at the parley, if Route E fires.

No one speaks after Asholt's line. The party holds the silence. He nods and walks back toward his quarters.

**Sustained behavioral pattern fires (v1.9 Character Agency anchor):** The entire interaction — one question, no advice, no plan, silence after — is a sustained behavioral pattern. Four PCs, two minutes, one question. The pattern IS the token condition. No calculation was required; the pattern was the choice.

## Scene 06 — Dawn (Accounting Speech Layer 3)

Davan is standing on the east wall at first light — alone, Sava and Talis in the great hall, Caelith writing — when the shape of the third layer arrives.

He takes out his notebook. He adds to the two lines he already has.

**PC-authored accounting speech current shape (three layers):**

*"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this.*
*The Oath tells you about the letter. It doesn't tell you about the people. Saren was right about one. Asholt was right about the other. So was I, at different times.*
*I know what it costs to be right about the letter. I have been counting the cost for twelve years."*

The third layer: naming what it costs. Not what Saren spent, not what Asholt spent — yet. He will name those at the table. For now: what he spent. What following the letter of something cost him, twelve years ago, in a corridor outside a hearing room, when Orel put his hand on his shoulder and said "tell them what happened."

He does not write Orel's name. But Orel is in the sentence.

**All four hints delivered. All four layers of the accounting speech in progress. Route E is achievable: 6 tokens + Asholt half = 6.5 tokens. One more full token needed. Asholt's full fires when Saren sits at the table.**
