# Roleplayer — C4-S6

Asholt's two lines are the session. "She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her." — this is twenty years of not-saying condensed into three sentences. The voice is exactly right: he is not explaining himself, not defending himself, not asking for sympathy. He is stating what he knows. The third sentence is the crucial one: "I have not found a way." Not "I did not tell her." Not "I chose not to." He has been looking for a way and has not found it.

Sava's question — "Why did she leave?" — is the roleplayer's MVP moment. Any tactical first response would have closed the corridor. She asked the human question. This is her character: the Life Cleric who watches, who listens, who asks the question that names the thing everyone is circling. It is consistent with every session she has been in.

Talis's "So have we" to Luca is five words and it does the work of a paragraph.

The ensemble scene worked because each PC's moment was true to their sheet. Davan counting time in silence with Irr. Sava reading the supply log beside Maret without speaking. Talis standing on the wall with Luca without telling him to come down. Caelith in his room annotating until the annotation answers itself.

Score: 78/80 — Asholt's lines are campaign-permanent and exactly right.
