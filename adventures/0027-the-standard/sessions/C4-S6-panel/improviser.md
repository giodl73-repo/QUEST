# Improviser — C4-S6

Talis drawing her shortsword after the fumble is the improviser's moment of the campaign. The fumble was real. The response — drawing a weapon she hasn't drawn in six sessions and getting into the breach beside Davan — was not scripted by the module or required by the dice. It is Talis responding to a failure by changing her operating mode. The improviser loves this: the fumble produced a better story than a hit would have.

Asholt cutting the cord with his belt knife and giving one half to the party is a physical staging detail that the module doesn't fully specify. The DM staged it as a physical action (the knife, the cord, the split disc) rather than a dialogue exchange. The physical staging makes it more powerful than a verbal token-giving would have been.

The enemy Wave 2 Leader surviving on one HP and retreating when the reinforcements arrived is good improvisation — the module doesn't specify the leader's exact HP, and the DM made the choice to let him escape rather than giving the party a clean kill. The unresolved leader is a seed: he was there, he saw what the garrison did, he can carry that knowledge back.

Score: 76/80 — strong physical staging; Talis's shortsword is the session's improvisational peak.
