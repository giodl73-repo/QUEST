5. **Davan's accounting speech.** The DM creates the space. The player speaks. The session logs it verbatim.

7. **Asholt's full token.** His half-disc is in his pocket. Her half-disc is in Davan's pocket (the party's half). At the table, Asholt takes both halves and holds the disc to Saren. She takes it.

10. **Withdrawal.** Saren stands her soldiers down. Siege over. Day 36.

**Accounting speech design note:** The speech is not scripted. The structural invitation across six sessions has produced:
- Layer 1: The Oath doesn't tell me this
- Layer 2: Saren right about letter; Asholt right about people; so was I, at different times
- Layer 3: I know what it costs to be right about the letter

**The 4th layer at the table names both sides' expenditures directly.** It synthesizes what the party has witnessed:
- What Saren spent: 20 years of knowing she was wrong about the people (no way to come back)
- What Asholt spent: 20 years of not saying it (no way to tell her)
- What the garrison spent: 36 days on a wall
- What the refugees spent: 3 years in a lower keep
- What the Oath costs when it is right about the letter and wrong about the person: "My brother's career. His trust that the truth would be enough. I gave the truth. I have been counting the cost of what the truth cost him."

This is the key revelation: Davan names Orel. At the parley table. In front of Saren and Asholt. He tells them what the Oath's letter cost him, so that they can both understand that he is not speaking from outside the situation — he is speaking from the exact same gap they are both in.
