*On day 31 of the siege, Saren Coldforge raises her personal standard inverted above the Reconstitution line — a deliberate challenge — and at midnight delivers the campaign's hardest assault; at dawn, a deserter comes over the wall with the truth that changes everything.*

## Setting & Background

Saren's inverted standard at first light draws the keep into a day of held breath; the midnight assault strips the party down to nothing; a Reconstitution deserter tells them before dawn that Saren has been acting alone, without orders, for two weeks — and Warden Asholt speaks the one sentence he has never said aloud.

**Thorngate Keep, Day 31 of the Siege.** The Supply Die has been exhausted since S5. No garrison healing. No garrison potions. The keep is running on stubbornness and wall-stone. The party enters with whatever HP they carried out of S5 plus one hit die of recovery, capped at maximum.

The Reconstitution camp has been quieter than usual for four days. Units have rotated. Sergeant Harven has been heard asking when they are going home. Saren Coldforge has said nothing publicly and has not changed position.

**At first light, day 31:** Her personal standard rises above the Reconstitution command tent — the Thorngate Watch insignia, inverted. Not the Reconstitution standard. Thorngate's. She has had it for twenty years. She brought it here. She is raising it inverted: a challenge to someone, not to the keep.

**The challenge is to Asholt.** The garrison recognizes the device. Some of the older soldiers know whose it was. Warden Asholt goes to the wall and looks at it without speaking for a long time. He does not explain. He does not dismiss it.

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S5 + 1 hit die of recovery (capped at max).
- **Supply Die:** Exhausted (reached zero in S5). **No garrison healing. No garrison potions.** Individual PC resources only.
- **No short-rest recovery:** The garrison cannot support it. Between-room recovery is available (short rest mechanics function) but produces no HP from HD without HD being spent.
- **Design intent:** The party enters S6 already depleted. The midnight assault should push them to 30–50% of max HP total. Every hit die spent now is a hit die not available for S7.

Talis is first to the east wall parapet at first light. The Reconstitution line has not moved. But something has been added: a standard on a pole above the command tent. The Thorngate Watch device — double gate, chain-bar, three stars — flown upside down.

She reports it to the party before anyone else sees it. The garrison's reactions will follow within minutes.

**What the inverted standard means:** In Solamnic heraldic tradition, an inverted house standard signals formal challenge — not to the keep as an institution, but to the commander personally. Warden Asholt trained Saren. She is not challenging Thorngate. She is challenging him. (DC 12 History or any Solamnic-background PC knows this without a check.)

He is not told — he sees it from the gatehouse window before anyone tells him. He is on the east wall walk within ten minutes. He stands there for an extended period without speaking. Sergeant Irr stands with him. The garrison gives them space.

**If the party approaches before he leaves the wall:** He acknowledges them with a look. He does not speak until he is ready. He says, finally: *"I know what she wants. I have not decided whether to give it to her."* Then he leaves the wall.

**Design intent:** This scene is not interactive in the usual sense. It is witnessed. The party's job is to be present, not to intervene.

Between first light and midnight: a day of garrison work, small pressures, and the weight of the standard visible on the camp. Three encounters structured as Scenes 01 and 02.

- **Scene 01 (east wall, first light):** The standard is seen. Asholt's reaction witnessed.
- **Scene 02 (great hall, midday):** The garrison's emotional state becomes visible. The ensemble scene. Asholt can be approached here — carefully.

Asholt's half-token fires at the end of Scene 05 (post-assault dawn, after the deserter), not here. Do not push the half-token scene into the day portion. Let the day breathe.

Day 31, past midnight. The assault begins without a horn signal — Saren does not announce it. The first warning is soldiers at the wall base. The party has whatever HP they entered the night with.

**Design pressure:** This is the campaign's hardest combat. Supply Die exhausted = no potions, no garrison healing. Eight Reconstitution soldiers, coordinated. All four PCs must be present. The party should end the assault at 30–50% HP total. Do not soften it.

**See encounter file:** `encounters/midnight-assault.md`

**Design intent:** The assault is winnable. It is not supposed to feel like it is. The party is defending walls that are thinner than they were on day one, with people who trust them, against soldiers who know the exact gap in the east wall. The damage is real. The cost is narrative, not punitive.

Just after the assault, before full light. The garrison is tending its wounded. The party is at whatever HP they ended the combat with — there is nothing to spend on healing, and they know it.

**The deserter:** A Reconstitution soldier — young, underfed, carrying his insignia in his hand rather than on his collar — appears on the wall's exterior side, standing in the killing ground with his hands visible. He does not shout. He walks to the base of the east wall and waits.

His name is Mik. He came over the camp's west perimeter before the assault and waited out the fighting in the no-man's-land. He is not injured. He is finished.

**What Mik knows:** He is the session's Hint 4 delivery. His specific lines (campaign-permanent):

*"She's been turning down withdrawal orders for two weeks. The pass doesn't matter — new route opened three months ago. She's here on her own."*

He says this to whoever speaks to him first. He does not embellish. He does not know why she is still here — he has guesses, but he has learned not to say guesses aloud. He wants shelter and a different wall to stand on. He does not ask for mercy. He does not bargain. He is telling them because he has decided that if he is going to cross a line he should at least give something of value.

**See NPC file:** `npcs/mik-deserter.md`

After Mik has spoken and the party has processed what he told them. Not immediately — give the information a few minutes to land.

Asholt appears in the corridor outside the great hall. He has heard about the deserter (Sergeant Irr told him). He does not ask about what Mik said. He already knows, or has guessed.

**The half-token condition (campaign-permanent, must be honored):**

The party does NOT give Asholt advice, orders, or a plan. They ask him ONE question and listen to the full answer. Nothing else. No follow-up. No tactical input. One question. Full answer. Silence after.

If the condition is met:

Asholt says: *"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her."*

A pause. Then: *"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her. But I will not be the one to ask first."*

He gives the party his token — not his full token, half. The cord is cut in two. He hands the party one half. He keeps the other. The implication is clear: the exchange is incomplete. The other half completes at the parley, if the parley happens.

**See NPC file:** `npcs/warden-asholt.md`

After Asholt speaks, the full shape of what Davan needs to say becomes visible. End-of-session handoff note (campaign-permanent):

*"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: 'the Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times.'"*

The speech is not written by the DM. It is authored by Davan's player at the parley table. This session delivers the final structural piece.

## Two scenes — same geography

Rooms 01 and 03 are the same physical location: the east wall parapet. They are separated by time (first light vs. midnight). The map represents them as distinct scenes but the DM should note this explicitly to players:

> *"You are back on the same wall. But it is midnight. Saren's standard is still there, invisible in the dark."*

## Loop

01 East Wall → 02 Great Hall → 05 Asholt's Corridor → 02 Great Hall → 03 East Wall → 04 Post-Assault Dawn → 05 Asholt's Corridor

The loop does not form a clean circuit because the assault interrupts normal transit. Post-assault, the party returns to the interior via the postern.

## Scale

One square ≈ 10 feet. East wall parapet: 60 feet long (six crenels, two drop-ladders). Great hall: 40 × 30 feet (garrison common; eight long tables, three fire-grates, supply ledger desk). Asholt's corridor: 25 feet long, 8 feet wide.

## DM notes

**Room 01 is a witnessed scene, not an interactive one.** The party sees Asholt at the wall. Their job is to be present. The scene is closed if they approach and push him to speak before he is ready — he gives the short answer and withdraws. If they hold, they get the longer look.

**Room 02 is the ensemble scene.** Every garrison NPC with a token in play is present or can be reached from here: Sergeant Irr (breakfast duty), Healer Maret Stave (wound-count from last week's pressure assault), Gate-Captain Hessa Var (supply ledger). Let the garrison breathe. The PCs can approach any of them. None of the NPCs have new arc beats to fire — this is texture, not token time.

**Room 03 (midnight assault) uses the same space as Room 01 but the tactical layout changes.** Daylight positioning (watching the standard) becomes night positioning (defending the crenels). The drop-ladders are relevant — the assault includes soldiers attempting to scale the wall, not just attack from below. See encounter file.

**Room 04 is exterior.** Mik stands on the ground below the east wall. The party must decide: drop a rope, open the postern, or communicate from height. He does not shout. He is calm. He is waiting for them to decide.

**Room 05 is private.** Asholt appears in the corridor, not the hall. The distinction is deliberate — he is not making a public announcement. He is speaking to the party and only to the party. If other garrison members are present (player initiative), they withdraw when Asholt appears.

### Scene 1 — East Wall at Dawn

> First light over the cliff face. There: above the Reconstitution command tent, on a pole that was not there yesterday — the Thorngate Watch device, double gate and three stars. Flown upside down. Someone in that camp has had that standard for twenty years.

#### Features

- **The standard (visible from the parapet):** The Thorngate Watch insignia — double-barred gate, three stars above — is unmistakable to any garrison member. It is flown inverted on a pole above what the garrison has identified as Saren Coldforge's command tent. **DC 12 History (automatic for any Solamnic-background PC, or any PC with Solamnic heraldic knowledge):** An inverted house standard is a formal challenge in Solamnic tradition — not to the keep as an institution, but to the commanding officer personally. Saren is not challenging Thorngate. She is challenging Asholt.

- **The garrison's reaction (automatic):** Within minutes of first light, soldiers are at the parapet pointing. The older soldiers (Sergeant Irr's cohort) go quiet. The newer ones ask each other what it means. **No check required:** The party reads the room by watching the garrison. The division between those who understand and those who don't is visible. Sergeant Irr sees the party seeing this, and he does not explain it.

- **Asholt arrives:** He is on the east wall walk within ten minutes of first light — arriving from the gatehouse, not from the garrison quarters. He has seen it from a window. He moves to the parapet without speaking to anyone. He looks at the standard. He does not look away for a long time. **DC 12 Insight:** He is not surprised. He is deciding something. He has been deciding it since she arrived. **DC 14 Insight:** He already knows what the standard means to her, and to him. The decision is not about what she is doing — it is about whether he is capable of answering it.

- **If the party approaches Asholt immediately:** He acknowledges them with a look. After a long pause: *"I know what she wants. I have not decided whether to give it to her."* He returns to looking at the standard. He does not continue the conversation. The right response is silence.

- **If the party stays at the wall without approaching:** Asholt eventually lowers his eyes from the standard and walks back toward the gatehouse without speaking to anyone. This is the correct read. His silence is the scene.

#### Encounter

None. This is a witnessed scene. No roll resolves what the party needs to understand; observation is the mechanic.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- East: Cliff face and no-man's-land (observable, not traversable in daylight)
- West (stairs down): Great Hall (Room 02)
- Northwest: Arrow-walk, north tower (standard Thorngate geography; not keyed this session)

#### GM Notes

**This is the session's opening image.** The party should see the standard before any NPC names it. Talis, with passive Perception 16, spots it at the moment of first light. Give her the visual first.

**Asholt's silence is not stonewalling.** He is a man who has had something sitting unresolved for twenty years looking at the person who has carried the other half of it for just as long. He is not being difficult. He is being honest — he does not yet know what he is going to do. The party's job is to witness this, not to intervene.

**The Read-aloud word count is 47 words.** Do not expand it at the table. The brevity is the point — first light, the image, the implication. The rest arrives through features.

**The inverted standard as timeline signal:** Saren has been here for 31 days. She has not raised this standard until now. The timing is connected to the assault tonight — she is telling Asholt what she wants before she sends the soldiers in. This is readable on a DC 16 History or Insight check, but do not prompt the roll. If a player asks "why now," that is the question worth answering.

### Scene 2 — The Great Hall

> Midday. Three fires going against the mountain cold. Soldiers at the long tables — some eating, some not. The supply ledger is open on the desk in the corner. Nobody is looking at it. The hall has the feel of people who have been waiting long enough that waiting has become its own kind of work.

#### Features

- **The garrison's emotional state (automatic):** The standard is still visible from the upper windows. The hall's atmosphere is specific: this is not fear, and it is not desperation. It is the exhaustion of people who have been right about something for a long time — right that the siege would grind, right that the help would not come fast enough — and who have stopped needing to say so. They are not broken. They are tired in the way that only matters when they are asked to do one more thing. **This is a free read:** the party observes this without a check. It is the room's answer to "how is the garrison doing?"

- **Sergeant Irr (present, breakfasting alone):** He has an empty cup and a half-finished portion in front of him. He is not eating. If approached: *"She'll come tonight. After a challenge like that, she comes tonight. That's how Asholt taught her."* He says this without bitterness and without advice. He is not asking the party to do anything. If pressed for context: *"They disagreed about who the Oath is owed to. She thought he was wrong. She left. She has been wrong about him for twenty years."* He does not explain further.

- **Healer Maret Stave (present, wound count):** She is writing in her supply ledger — not supplies, injuries. The column under "healing materials" is blank. She has been recording wounds without treatment for six days. If approached: *"I have twelve soldiers who need more than I can give them. I have been writing that down because it is the only thing I can do with it."* She does not ask for anything. She is telling the party because they asked, and because it is true.

- **Gate-Captain Hessa Var (present, east window):** She is watching the standard from the east-facing window at the hall's far end. If approached: *"She knows the east wall is the weak point. She's known it since her second year of training here. The standard is for Asholt. The assault will be for the east wall."* **DC 12 Insight:** She is not afraid of the assault. She is afraid the assault will work, and that Asholt will have been right and wrong simultaneously — right to hold, wrong to think holding was enough.

- **Approaching Asholt (available here, Scene 02):** Asholt is not in the great hall, but he can be summoned or intercepted in the adjoining corridor if the party seeks him. He will not give the half-token scene here — that is reserved for Scene 05. But if approached here: he answers specific tactical questions directly. If a PC asks a personal question (about Saren, about the standard), he says: *"Ask me that again tonight, after."* He is not deflecting. He means it.

- **The supply ledger (available, DC 10 Investigation):** The ledger Maret has closed is the garrison's resource record. The right-side column has been blank since day 27. The entry on day 27 reads: *"Supply Die: exhausted. No further garrison resources."* In Dene Flor's handwriting. It is the most honest sentence in the keep.

### Scene 3 — The East Wall (Midnight)

> No horn. No signal. The first warning is the sound of boots on the cliff face — soldiers climbing in the dark, using the handholds they know. The east wall. She taught them where to go.

#### Features

- **The assault's tactical structure:** Eight Reconstitution soldiers, no horn call, coordinated. The assault proceeds in waves: four soldiers at the base of the east wall using scaling ladders and drop-lines; four in reserve, waiting for a gap. The coordination is unusual — this is not a standard assault push. This has been rehearsed. **DC 12 Perception (auto for Talis, passive 16):** The soldiers on the scaling ladders are moving in pairs, leapfrogging each other. This is a trained maneuver, not improvised. Someone taught them this. **DC 14 History or Tactical:** This is the Keep's own training doctrine — assault by paired coverage. Saren did not just come here; she came here with Thorngate's own methods.

- **The terrain:**
  - **Six crenels on the east parapet:** Each crenel is 3 feet wide, 4 feet tall, spaced 8 feet apart. Partial cover (half cover) for defenders standing in a gap; full cover for defenders crouching.
  - **Two drop-ladders (exterior):** Built into the east wall face for maintenance access. The assault uses these first. **DC 14 Strength (any PC) to pull them up and deny access** — requires an action.
  - **Garrison crossbowmen (3):** Present and firing from flanking positions. They are garrison; they follow the party's positioning but cannot be commanded. They each have 6 bolts. Once bolts are spent, they switch to hand weapons (short swords). Each crossbowman: AC 13, HP 8.
  - **Arrow-loop access (east-facing):** Three arrow-loops on the interior wall of the east parapet. A PC in an arrow-loop has three-quarter cover (+5 AC). Limited field of fire — only the zone directly below the loop.

- **No potions. No garrison healing.** The party enters this combat with whatever HP they carried from the day. There is nothing to spend on healing. Every decision about positioning, cover, and action economy matters more than in any previous session. **Design reminder:** The assault is designed to push the party to 30–50% HP total. Do not pull punches.

## Wave Structure

### Wave 1 (Rounds 1–3): Four soldiers at the wall

### Wave 2 (Rounds 4–6): Four soldiers in reserve
These soldiers wait at the cliff base until a Wave 1 soldier establishes position on the parapet. If no Wave 1 soldier crests: Wave 2 holds and begins an arrow barrage from the cliff base (Range 80 ft., unobstructed line to east parapet; they fire from cover — half cover, +2 AC).

**Wave 2 arrow barrage:** Each of the four reserve soldiers fires per round if they cannot crest. They switch to advance if a gap appears in the party's line.

The assault breaks (Reconstitution soldiers withdraw) when **6 of 8 soldiers** are incapacitated or fleeing. The remaining 2 cover the retreat and withdraw. The party does not need to incapacitate all eight.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.
