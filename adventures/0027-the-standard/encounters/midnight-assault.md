# Encounter — Midnight Assault

## Overview

Eight Reconstitution soldiers attacking the east wall in coordinated pairs. No horn. No warning. Supply Die exhausted: no potions, no garrison healing. This is the campaign's hardest combat. The party should end it at 30–50% HP total. Design for real cost.

**XP budget:** 8 × 100 XP (CR 1/2 each) = 800 XP × 1.5 (wall-defense coordination multiplier) = **1,200 adjusted XP.** Hard-to-Deadly threshold for a L3 party of 4. Intentional.

**Design pressure:** The encounter is designed to push the party to their limit, not to kill them. The assault breaks when six soldiers are incapacitated or driven off (the wave loses coherence). The party's job is to hold the wall for enough rounds to reach that threshold.

---

## Wave Structure

### Wave 1 (Rounds 1–3): Four soldiers at the wall
Four soldiers approach the east wall using the two drop-ladders and two grapple-lines. They are in pairs — leapfrogging up (Thorngate's own assault training, per Scene 03 GM notes). First soldiers crest the parapet wall in Round 3 if the drop-ladders are not pulled up.

**Drop-ladder pull (DC 14 Strength, action):** If a PC spends Round 1 or 2 pulling the drop-ladders up, the Wave 1 soldiers cannot use them. The four soldiers must use grapple-lines only (slower; they crest in Round 5). This is worth the action.

### Wave 2 (Rounds 4–6): Four soldiers in reserve
These soldiers wait at the cliff base until a Wave 1 soldier establishes position on the parapet. If no Wave 1 soldier crests: Wave 2 holds and begins an arrow barrage from the cliff base (Range 80 ft., unobstructed line to east parapet; they fire from cover — half cover, +2 AC).

**Wave 2 arrow barrage:** Each of the four reserve soldiers fires per round if they cannot crest. They switch to advance if a gap appears in the party's line.

### Threshold
The assault breaks (Reconstitution soldiers withdraw) when **6 of 8 soldiers** are incapacitated or fleeing. The remaining 2 cover the retreat and withdraw. The party does not need to incapacitate all eight.

### Reconstitution Assault Soldier (CR 1/2, 100 XP)

*Coordinated attack formation, Thorngate-trained tactics, no morale breaks until threshold.*

**AC** 14 (chain shirt + shield) · **HP** 16 (3d8+3) · **Speed** 30 ft.

| Str | Dex | Con | Int | Wis | Cha |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 12 (+1) | 10 (+0) | 12 (+1) | 9 (−1) |

**Saves:** Str +4, Con +3
**Skills:** Athletics +4, Acrobatics +3
**Senses:** passive Perception 11

**Longsword.** +4 to hit, 5 ft., 1d8+2 slashing (melee).
**Shortbow.** +3 to hit, 80/320 ft., 1d6+1 piercing (ranged, Wave 2 barrage).

**Coordinated Pair (reaction, once per round):** When an ally within 5 feet takes damage, this soldier may make an immediate opportunity attack against the attacker. (Represents the leapfrog-cover training.)

**Wall Climber:** The soldier treats the drop-ladders and grapple-lines as climbing without requiring an Athletics check. If the ladders are pulled up, the soldier must make a DC 12 Athletics check to climb the wall via grapple-line each round of ascent.

## Garrison Support

**Three garrison crossbowmen** (not PC-controlled):
- AC 13, HP 8 each. Attack: +3 to hit, 1d8 piercing (heavy crossbow, 80/320 ft., 6 bolts each).
- They fire at Wave 2 soldiers during the barrage. They do not charge into melee.
- When bolts run out (Round 6+): they switch to short swords (+2 to hit, 1d6+1). Their melee is poor.
- If two crossbowmen are incapacitated: fire support effectively ends. The party is on their own.

**Sergeant Irr** (present, not combatant): Commands garrison flanks. He does not engage in primary combat — he is coordinating the wall defenders not controlled by the party. If targeted directly by a Reconstitution soldier: AC 15, HP 22, Longsword +4 to hit 1d8+2. He fights competently but he is not the combat's variable. He is the garrison's spine.

## Tactical Considerations

### Chokepoint control
The east parapet's two stairheads are the combat's key terrain. The party can hold the top of the stairs and force all climbing soldiers to crest at a chokepoint — advantage on attacks against soldiers climbing. If a soldier holds the stairhead against the party, it costs an action and a slot to dislodge them.

### Cover use
Three-quarter cover (arrow-loops): +5 AC, limited field of fire. Half cover (crenels): +2 AC. Characters not using cover are fully exposed to the Wave 2 barrage.

### The party's real constraint
No healing. No potions. Concentration spells broken by Wave 1 contact will not be re-established. Every caster must decide in Round 1 which slot is the last they are willing to spend.

### Spell economy notes
- **Sava's Healing Word:** Each use here is not available at the parley. Design for 1–2 uses maximum across the assault.
- **Caelith's spell slots:** Save highest-level slot for the parley's potential combat (S7). If he spends everything here, S7 opens with nothing.

## End States

**Party holds the wall (threshold reached):** Six soldiers incapacitated or fled. The remaining two cover retreat and withdraw. The assault is over. Record each PC's HP. This is what they carry to S7.

**Wall breached (one or more soldiers reach the stairhead interior):** The party falls back to the stairhead door. The garrison closes the upper door. The soldiers pound on it for one round and then withdraw — they have no support and are isolated inside a hostile garrison. No soldiers reach the hall. This is a partial breach: note it in the session log. It does not change the outcome (the assault fails) but it may have consequences at the parley (Saren knows the breach happened).

**A Reconstitution soldier is taken prisoner (party initiative):** The soldiers do not surrender except at the threshold. Before the threshold: they fight to incapacitation. After: the remaining two are willing to surrender if offered. Note any prisoners in the session log. Mik's information supersedes anything a prisoner might add, but a prisoner is evidence of the night's events.
