# Siege Pressure Table

*Day 31. The standard is up. The garrison is waiting. Roll once when the party transitions from Scene 01 to Scene 02, and once during Scene 02 if the party lingers (more than 10 minutes of scene-time). Roll d6.*

## Pressure Table (d6)

| Roll | Event |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Undisturbed.** The garrison manages its own tension. The party has space to work. Nothing intrudes. |
| 3 | **A garrison soldier approaches the party.** Not an officer — a wall-watcher, two years in, who has been at Thorngate since Asholt hired them. They say: *"You've been here a month. Is it going to be over?"* No tactical content. Just the question. The party can answer or not. Asholt is not present. Sergeant Irr, passing, gives the soldier a look but does not intervene. |
| 4 | **Sergeant Irr pulls the party aside briefly.** He has a piece of intelligence that missed the morning briefing: a Reconstitution courier left the camp yesterday afternoon and returned this morning with no answer. A withdrawal order that came back empty. He tells the party because they should know. He does not comment on what it means. He leaves them with it. |
| 5 | **The standard is changed.** Midday: a Reconstitution soldier climbs the pole at the command tent and adds something to Saren's standard — a Reconstitution pennant, smaller, below the inverted Thorngate device. **DC 14 History:** The pennant is a Reconstitution field signal meaning "engagement imminent." She is not waiting anymore. The assault is announced, not planned — it will come tonight. Any PC who recognizes this signal can share the information without a roll (they know it). This accelerates the party's preparation window. |
| 6 | **Asholt makes an announcement.** He stands in the great hall doorway and tells the garrison: *"We defend the east wall tonight. All stations, full rotation. No one sleeps until morning."* He does not explain the basis for this. He has read the situation from the standard, as the garrison expected him to. This removes the "surprise" element from the midnight assault (the garrison is prepared) but means the party is at full readiness — any preparatory actions they wanted to take in the pre-assault window (positioning, barrier placement, crossbow-assignment) are available. |

## Notes

Roll this table at: (1) end of Scene 01 (transition to Scene 02), and (2) during Scene 02 if the party spends more than 10 minutes in conversation before acting. Two rolls maximum.

**Result 5 note:** The pennant addition to the standard is a real Reconstitution signal. If the party correctly identifies and reports it to Asholt, he gives them a specific preparatory window before midnight: two hours to position. This reduces the combat's "no warning" element (the party gets to set up defensively) but does not reduce the encounter's difficulty (the soldiers still come, they still coordinate, there are still eight of them).

**Result 6 note:** Asholt making the announcement means the garrison is not surprised. The crossbowmen are at their posts when the assault begins. Their six bolts each have been pre-positioned. This is the "best-prepared" version of the assault — still hard, still intended to cost HP, but with the tactical advantage of knowing it is coming.
