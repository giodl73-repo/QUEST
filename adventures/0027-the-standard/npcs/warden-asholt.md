# Warden Bren Asholt (Half-Token Scene)

## Appearance

Late fifties. Garrison officer's bearing — not parade posture, working posture: the kind held in long halls and on wall-walks rather than in throne rooms. He has been at Thorngate for 22 years. It shows in the way he moves through the keep: he knows every floor-stone. His watch-token is worn on a cord under his collar; it has been there for 22 years and has never been given to anyone. He carries a lamp in the corridor. He is not pretending to be doing something else.

## Role in this session

Asholt's primary arc fires in S7 at the parley table. This session delivers the half-token — the mechanism of the split token — and his critical two sentences. He is not the session's central figure (that is the assault and the deserter). He is the session's closing beat: the thing the party carries into the finale.

## Public knowledge (what the garrison knows)

- Warden Bren Asholt has held Thorngate Keep for 22 years.
- He trained Saren Coldforge. She served under him for three years. She left after a disagreement about garrison policy.
- He has never discussed that disagreement publicly.
- He has not spoken about Saren since the siege began. He gives orders. He does his morning circuit. He does not discuss her.

## Private knowledge (available through the half-token scene)

Asholt knows the following and will say the following when the half-token condition is met:

*"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her."*

What this means:
- **"She was right about the Oath":** Saren's Solamnic interpretation — the Oath (*Est Sularus oth Mithas*, "my honor is my life") demands clarity about who is owed protection. She argued that giving shelter to non-Solamnic travelers without authorization violated the Oath's covenant structure. She was correct, technically. The Oath does not say protect everyone. It says protect Solamnia.
- **"She was wrong about the people":** The people Asholt sheltered were not a threat to Solamnia. They were people without a wall. The Oath did not tell him to turn them away; it did not tell him to shelter them either. He sheltered them because they had nowhere else to go. Saren called this a violation. She was right about the letter. She was wrong about what the Oath is for.
- **"I have not found a way to tell her":** He has known this for twenty years. He has not had the opportunity to say it. He is not asking the party to arrange an opportunity. He is telling them this because they asked — not because he expects them to act on it.

*"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her. But I will not be the one to ask first."*

What this means: He will not initiate. He has the thing to say. He cannot bring himself to walk across the killing ground to say it first. This is not pride — or not only pride. He does not know if she will hear it. He needs to know she is willing to be in the same space before he will speak. The parley must be the party's creation.

## Behavior matrix

### Scene 01 — The wall

He arrives. He looks at the standard. He does not speak until spoken to.

If the party speaks immediately: *"I know what she wants. I have not decided whether to give it to her."* He does not continue.

If the party holds silence: he looks at the standard for a long time, then leaves. That is the correct version of this scene.

### Scene 02 — The great hall

He is not present in the hall. He can be summoned or intercepted in the corridor. If approached:
- Tactical questions: answered directly, efficiently.
- Personal questions: *"Ask me that again tonight, after."*

He means it. After the assault.

### Scene 05 — The corridor (half-token)

**The condition:** The party asks ONE question and listens. No advice. No plan. No second question. Silence after.

**He knows if the condition is broken.** If the party offers advice: *"I did not ask for advice."* He leaves. The half-token is not available.

**If the condition is met:** He delivers the two lines verbatim. Then he splits his token cord and holds out one half. He keeps the other.

### The half-token (physical form)

A flat wooden disc, the Thorngate gate device (double-barred gate) on one face, blank on the other. The cord is cut and tied off. One half to the party. One half to Asholt. **Both halves must be present at the parley table for Route E to fire.** Asholt brings his half. The party brings theirs. The joining is the signal.

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** Route E fires at the parley table in S7. He places his full token (both halves rejoined) on the table between himself and Saren, and speaks to her directly.

**The act:** *"She was right about the Oath. I was right about the people. I should have told you that twenty years ago."*

(Note the inversion: at the corridor he says "she was right about the Oath, wrong about the people." At the parley he says "I was right about the people" — accepting his own half of the equation. The shift from third-person attribution to first-person ownership is the arc's resolution.)

**What closes the arc:** Twenty years of knowing the right thing to say and not having found a way to say it. The party creates the table. He brings his half of the token. He says it. That is the arc.

**Route E requirement:** 7 tokens total (6 party tokens + Asholt's full token). If the party has fewer than 7 tokens at the parley, Route E does not fire — Asholt cannot bring his full token to a table that does not have the conditions for it.
