# Mik (Reconstitution Deserter)

## Appearance

Twenty years old or thereabouts. Lean in the way of someone who has been eating campaign rations for two months. He carries his Reconstitution unit insignia in his left hand — not on his collar, not discarded, carried. He is not presenting it or hiding it. He has decided he is not wearing it anymore and he has not yet decided what to do with it. His uniform is standard Reconstitution field gray. He has mud on his knees from crossing the killing ground in the dark. He is not injured.

## Role in this session

Mik delivers Hint 4 — the session's central intelligence revelation — and then he does not do anything dramatic. He does not become a major character. He asks for shelter and a different wall to stand on. He delivers his information and then he stops performing. He is a young soldier who made a decision about which side of a line he wanted to be on. The decision is already made. He is past the moment of drama.

## Public knowledge (what the party can observe)

- He walked through the killing ground in the immediate aftermath of an assault. He was not in the assault — he waited it out in the no-man's-land.
- He is unarmed. He left his sword ten feet behind him.
- He is carrying his insignia, not wearing it.
- He waited for someone to speak to him rather than announcing himself.

## Private knowledge (what he tells the party)

### Hint 4 — delivered verbatim on first contact

*"She's been turning down withdrawal orders for two weeks. The pass doesn't matter — new route opened three months ago. She's here on her own."*

He does not elaborate unless asked. He does not explain why he is telling them this — he offers the information because he has decided that if he is going to cross a line he should give something of value. He does not say this aloud. He simply delivers the information.

### What he knows (available if the party asks)

- **The withdrawal orders:** He saw a courier leave the camp on three separate days. The first and second times the courier returned with orders he took to Saren's tent; she sent the courier back empty. The third courier came back with nothing. He does not know the contents — he is a soldier, not her staff. But he knows what couriers-returning-empty means.
- **The new supply route:** He knows it exists and that it opened approximately three months ago. He does not know its path. He knows it ends the strategic argument for holding the pass.
- **Camp morale:** Units from Solanthus have rotated out. Sergeant Harven has been asking when they are going home. The soldiers who remain are the ones who are Saren's, specifically — her people, not conscripted command rotation. They will stay as long as she stays.
- **Why he is here, not them:** *"They're loyal. I was assigned."* He was a general-pool soldier who was rotated into Saren's unit six weeks ago. He has no personal loyalty to her. He is not betraying her — he is leaving a siege that he was never part of for reasons he was never told.

### What he guesses (offered if directly asked "why is she still here?")

*"She's waiting for something to be said. I don't know what."*

He says this simply. He is twenty and has been watching her from fifty feet away for six weeks. He does not have the context (Helin's history, Hint 3, the relationship with Asholt) to name what needs to be said. He just knows the siege has a personal shape that he cannot resolve.

## Behavior matrix

### When the party first speaks to him

He delivers Hint 4 immediately. He does not perform it — he states it. He is not angling for anything. He is giving them what he has.

### If the party is hostile or tactical

He answers correctly and completely. He does not react to hostility with aggression. He has already made his decision; the party's reception of it is not his to control.

### If asked what he wants

*"A wall that isn't hers."*

He does not ask to be trusted. He does not negotiate. He asks to be somewhere that is not this siege. If the party advocates for him with the garrison, he accepts it without ceremony. If they do not, he waits.

### If asked about the assault that just ended

He says: *"I was in the no-man's-land before it started. I didn't cross to the keep side until it was over."* He did not participate. He is not responsible for the assault's casualties. He is not pretending this is uncomplicated — he was in a unit that just killed or injured garrison soldiers. He is aware of this. He does not apologize for it. He did not do it.

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** Mik delivers Hint 4 verbatim to the party. His arc is complete at delivery — this is not a slow burn. He came to do one thing and he did it. The arc does not require the party to do anything specific in return.

**The act:** Delivering the three sentences. That is the act.

**What closes the arc:** He was in a unit he did not choose, on a siege he was not explained, for a commander whose reasons he could not understand. He left. He told the people on the other wall what he knew. He asked for nothing except shelter. He was twenty years old. That is the whole of it.

**Token delivery:** Mik does not give a token. He is not a garrison figure. He is a deserter who crossed a killing ground and told the truth. What he gives is information, and the information is what changes everything.
