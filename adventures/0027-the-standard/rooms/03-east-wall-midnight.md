# Room 03 — The East Wall (Midnight)

## Read-aloud

> No horn. No signal. The first warning is the sound of boots on the cliff face — soldiers climbing in the dark, using the handholds they know. The east wall. She taught them where to go.

## Features

- **The assault's tactical structure:** Eight Reconstitution soldiers, no horn call, coordinated. The assault proceeds in waves: four soldiers at the base of the east wall using scaling ladders and drop-lines; four in reserve, waiting for a gap. The coordination is unusual — this is not a standard assault push. This has been rehearsed. **DC 12 Perception (auto for Talis, passive 16):** The soldiers on the scaling ladders are moving in pairs, leapfrogging each other. This is a trained maneuver, not improvised. Someone taught them this. **DC 14 History or Tactical:** This is the Keep's own training doctrine — assault by paired coverage. Saren did not just come here; she came here with Thorngate's own methods.

- **The terrain:**
  - **Six crenels on the east parapet:** Each crenel is 3 feet wide, 4 feet tall, spaced 8 feet apart. Partial cover (half cover) for defenders standing in a gap; full cover for defenders crouching.
  - **Two drop-ladders (exterior):** Built into the east wall face for maintenance access. The assault uses these first. **DC 14 Strength (any PC) to pull them up and deny access** — requires an action.
  - **Garrison crossbowmen (3):** Present and firing from flanking positions. They are garrison; they follow the party's positioning but cannot be commanded. They each have 6 bolts. Once bolts are spent, they switch to hand weapons (short swords). Each crossbowman: AC 13, HP 8.
  - **Arrow-loop access (east-facing):** Three arrow-loops on the interior wall of the east parapet. A PC in an arrow-loop has three-quarter cover (+5 AC). Limited field of fire — only the zone directly below the loop.

- **No potions. No garrison healing.** The party enters this combat with whatever HP they carried from the day. There is nothing to spend on healing. Every decision about positioning, cover, and action economy matters more than in any previous session. **Design reminder:** The assault is designed to push the party to 30–50% HP total. Do not pull punches.

- **Garrison support:** Sergeant Irr is present on the wall. He commands the crossbowmen and the garrison defenders (not the party). He coordinates on the east flank. He does not give the party orders — but if a PC creates an opening, he fills it. He fights with professional efficiency. He will not break morale. He will not call a retreat. His HP: 22 (he is not a major combatant; he is a witness to the party's choices).

- **Saren is not present in this assault.** She is at her command tent. The standard is still flying. **DC 14 Perception at any point during the assault:** A single figure stands outside the command tent, motionless, watching. Not intervening. Watching. This is her. She is witnessing what she sent. She does not call it off.

## GM Notes

**This is the hardest combat of the campaign.** Do not apologize for it. The encounter is designed to be won — but not easily, not without cost. The party's HP entering this combat is their real constraint. Every spell slot spent here is spent. Every hit die used here is gone.

**The drop-ladder pull:** This is an early opportunity to reduce the assault's action economy. If the party identifies the ladders in Round 1 and a PC spends an action pulling them up (DC 14 Strength), the soldiers on the ladders must spend movement to find another approach. This delays Wave 2 by 2 rounds. It costs an action. It is worth it. Do not telegraph it — let the party notice it or not.

**Garrison crossbowmen are support, not cavalry.** They will pick off wounded targets, pin movement, and cover gaps. They will not solo a wave. If two crossbowmen go down (likely in Round 4 when the second wave crests), the party loses fire support. Plan for this.

**Saren watching from the command tent:** This detail fires on a DC 14 Perception during the assault. If a PC makes it: the figure is still — not directing, not watching tactically. Just watching. This is information, not a tactical fact. Log it in the session record. It will mean something at the parley.

**The assault ends one of two ways:** (1) Eight soldiers incapacitated or fled — the assault breaks when the loss threshold hits six soldiers. (2) The party cannot hold a key position (stairhead, a drop-ladder gap) and must fall back inside — the garrison seals the upper gate; the assault stalls at the interior wall for one round before the soldiers withdraw. Either way: dawn comes. The assault is over.

**HP tracking is mandatory.** Record each PC's HP at the end of this scene. That number is what they carry into S7.

## Memory Fragment

The probe withdrew. He did not understand at the time why Saren would show him the east wall's vulnerability. He understands now: she was not attacking. She was reminding him she knew.
