# Room 05 — Asholt's Corridor

## Read-aloud

> Asholt is waiting in the east corridor, outside the great hall door. Not inside — outside. He has a lamp but he is not reading. He looks up when the party arrives. He knows about the deserter. He is not asking about him.

## Features

- **The half-token condition (campaign-permanent):** The condition for Asholt's half-token fires here and cannot fire anywhere else this session. The condition is:

  *The party asks him ONE question and listens to the full answer. No advice. No orders. No plan offered. One question. Full answer. Silence after.*

  If this condition is met, he speaks. If the party offers advice, shares a plan, or follows the question with another question before he has finished, the scene closes and the half-token is not available this session.

- **Asholt's response (campaign-permanent, must be delivered verbatim):**

  He answers whatever question the party asks with the same response — he has been preparing this. If asked about Saren, about the standard, about what to do, about why she is still here — any question in that territory — he says:

  *"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. I have not found a way to tell her."*

  A pause. He lets that land.

  *"If you can get her to the table, I will speak to her. But I will not be the one to ask first."*

  Then silence. He waits to see if the party holds. If they hold: he reaches into his collar and unstrings his watch-token from its cord. He splits the cord in two, ties off both ends, and holds out one half to the party. He keeps the other. The token — a flat wooden disc, the Thorngate gate device on one face, blank on the other — is now in two pieces. He does not explain the symbolism. If asked: *"You'll know when the other half belongs with it."*

- **The question the party should ask:** Any question about Saren, the standard, the siege, or what is unfinished. The quality of the question matters less than whether the party then stops and listens. The half-token is not a reward for cleverness. It is a reward for the discipline of asking and then shutting up.

- **The wrong approach:** Offering advice (*"Here's what we think you should do..."*), giving orders (*"You need to speak to her first..."*), sharing a plan (*"We're going to arrange a parley..."*). Any of these closes the scene. Asholt says: *"I did not ask for advice."* He leaves. The half-token is not available.

- **DC 14 Insight (optional, does not affect token outcome):** Asholt has been composing his answer to the party since he saw the standard this morning. He knew they would come to him eventually. He has been waiting for them to be ready to hear it rather than respond to it. The half-token condition is his test of readiness.

## Encounter

None. The half-token scene is the encounter. No combat; no roll for the token condition — the condition is behavioral, not mechanical.

## GM Notes

**The half-token fires here and only here.** If the party reaches the end of the session without entering this scene, Asholt approaches them — he comes to find them after an hour. The offer is available until the session ends. It does not require a specific room, but the corridor is the design location.

**The party will likely have Mik's information when they enter this scene.** They know Saren is acting alone. They know the pass is no longer strategic. They know she has been refusing withdrawal orders. This information, combined with Asholt's response, gives the party the full picture for S7. Do not summarize or narrate the synthesis for them — let them hold both pieces and connect them.

**Asholt's line is not an explanation.** *"She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people."* He is not defending himself. He is not condemning her. He is naming what is unresolved between them — the thing that has been sitting in the gap for twenty years — as plainly as he can. This is the most honest thing he has said in the campaign. It is available because the party asked and then stopped.

**Log Asholt's exact words verbatim.** Campaign-permanent. The S7 setup depends on this delivery.

**The half-token's physical form:** A split wooden disc — the cord cut in two. One half to the party, one half in Asholt's collar. The reunion of the halves at the parley table is the visual signal that Route E is possible. The party carries the split disc; Asholt carries the other half. They both know what the joining means.

**PC-authored accounting speech:** After this scene, the full shape of Davan's accounting is available. Do not name it aloud. Note it in the handoff: *"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: 'the Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter — and Saren was right about the letter, and you were right about the people, and I have been both of you at different times.'"* This is for S7.

## Memory Fragment

The garrison knows this about him: when he is in the corridor outside the hall, something is being decided. They give him the corridor. Tonight they know what is being decided.
