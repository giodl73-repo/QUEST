## Vertical connections

- East Wall (01 / 03) **down** to Great Hall (02) via internal stone stairs
- Great Hall (02) **down** to Lower Keep (0025 shared geography) via stone stairs
- East Wall to exterior (04) via east-wall postern gate (ground level, requires unbarring)

## Two scenes — same geography

Rooms 01 and 03 are the same physical location: the east wall parapet. They are separated by time (first light vs. midnight). The map represents them as distinct scenes but the DM should note this explicitly to players:

> *"You are back on the same wall. But it is midnight. Saren's standard is still there, invisible in the dark."*

## Loop

01 East Wall → 02 Great Hall → 05 Asholt's Corridor → 02 Great Hall → 03 East Wall → 04 Post-Assault Dawn → 05 Asholt's Corridor

The loop does not form a clean circuit because the assault interrupts normal transit. Post-assault, the party returns to the interior via the postern.

## Scale

One square ≈ 10 feet. East wall parapet: 60 feet long (six crenels, two drop-ladders). Great hall: 40 × 30 feet (garrison common; eight long tables, three fire-grates, supply ledger desk). Asholt's corridor: 25 feet long, 8 feet wide.

## DM notes

**Room 01 is a witnessed scene, not an interactive one.** The party sees Asholt at the wall. Their job is to be present. The scene is closed if they approach and push him to speak before he is ready — he gives the short answer and withdraws. If they hold, they get the longer look.

**Room 02 is the ensemble scene.** Every garrison NPC with a token in play is present or can be reached from here: Sergeant Irr (breakfast duty), Healer Maret Stave (wound-count from last week's pressure assault), Gate-Captain Hessa Var (supply ledger). Let the garrison breathe. The PCs can approach any of them. None of the NPCs have new arc beats to fire — this is texture, not token time.

**Room 03 (midnight assault) uses the same space as Room 01 but the tactical layout changes.** Daylight positioning (watching the standard) becomes night positioning (defending the crenels). The drop-ladders are relevant — the assault includes soldiers attempting to scale the wall, not just attack from below. See encounter file.

**Room 04 is exterior.** Mik stands on the ground below the east wall. The party must decide: drop a rope, open the postern, or communicate from height. He does not shout. He is calm. He is waiting for them to decide.

**Room 05 is private.** Asholt appears in the corridor, not the hall. The distinction is deliberate — he is not making a public announcement. He is speaking to the party and only to the party. If other garrison members are present (player initiative), they withdraw when Asholt appears.
