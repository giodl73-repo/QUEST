# Room 04 — Post-Assault Dawn

## Read-aloud

> The assault is over. The killing ground below the east wall is quiet. Grey light is coming in from the northeast. A figure stands at the wall's base — hands visible, arms out, not moving. He walked here. He is waiting.

## Features

- **Mik's position:** A Reconstitution soldier — young, perhaps twenty, carrying his unit insignia in his left hand rather than on his collar — stands approximately fifteen feet from the east wall base, in the middle of the killing ground. He is not armed (sword on the ground ten feet behind him, left there intentionally). He does not call out. He is not performing surrender — he is simply waiting for someone to decide what to do with him. **Automatic:** Any party member with line of sight sees this. He is not hiding.

- **Reaching Mik — the party's options:**
  - **Drop a rope from the parapet (easiest):** The east wall is 25 feet high. A rope dropped gives him access; he can climb it. He does not ask for help. If a rope appears, he uses it.
  - **Open the east postern (ground-level gate):** The gate is barred from inside. A garrison soldier must unbar it. Sergeant Irr will authorize this if the party asks — he has already assessed Mik as a genuine deserter, not a decoy. Irr says: *"He walked here through the aftermath of an assault. He's not a plant."* (Irr's tactical read.)
  - **Call down from the parapet:** He will answer from the ground. He speaks loudly enough to be heard — not shouting, just clearly. He is not ashamed of being heard.

- **What Mik says (Hint 4 — campaign-permanent, must be delivered verbatim):**

  When the party speaks to him — from the wall or in person — he speaks without being asked to explain himself:

  *"She's been turning down withdrawal orders for two weeks. The pass doesn't matter — new route opened three months ago. She's here on her own."*

  He does not elaborate unless asked. He is not performing revelation — he is stating facts. He has thought about what to say and these are the three facts that matter.

  **If the party asks why he is telling them this:**
  *"Because I walked through that killing ground and none of you shot me. That seemed like information worth having."*

  **If the party asks what he wants:**
  *"A wall that isn't hers."*

  **If the party asks about Saren:**
  *"She's not wrong about what she was right about. She just stopped having an order that matched it."*

- **What Mik does not know:**
  - Why Saren is still here (he has a guess: *"She's waiting for something to be said. I don't know what."*)
  - What the new supply route is (he knows it exists; he does not know the route)
  - What Saren's withdrawal orders say (he saw a courier leave twice; the third time the courier came back)

- **Disposition after Hint 4:** Mik does not ask to join the garrison. He asks to be housed somewhere that is not in the fight — the lower keep, the refugees' space, anywhere outside direct garrison command. He is not asking to be trusted. He is asking to stop. **If the party advocates for him:** Sergeant Irr grants it without argument. Dene Flor gives him a pallet in the lower corridor. **If the party doesn't:** He waits. He does not push. He will be where they put him.

## Encounter

None. The assault is over. This is a character scene. No combat; no roll required for Mik's Hint 4 delivery — it is automatic on first contact.

## GM Notes

**The Hint 4 delivery is automatic.** Mik came here specifically to say what he knows. He is not coy about it. The party does not need to ask the right question — they need only to speak to him. His three sentences (the campaign-permanent lines) are delivered in full on first contact.

**Log Mik's exact words verbatim in the session record.** They are campaign-permanent. The S7 handoff must confirm they were delivered.

**"She's here on her own":** This is the session's structural revelation. The party now knows that Saren does not have Reconstitution command behind her. The siege is not a military operation authorized by her superiors. It is one commander, acting alone, for reasons her own command does not endorse, on a timeline that has been slipping for two weeks. The party carries this into Scene 05 and into S7.

**Mik's guess about why she's still here:** His line — *"She's waiting for something to be said"* — is the party's first outside confirmation that the siege has a personal resolution. He does not know what needs to be said. He is twenty and has been in the Reconstitution for three years. He knows the siege is wrong; he does not know why it is happening. This gap is where the party's accumulated knowledge (Hint 3 from Helin: Saren left to say she was wrong about the people; Hint 4 from Mik: she has no orders left) becomes the full picture.

**After the Hint 4 scene:** The party should have enough to go to Asholt with a clear picture. Scene 05 is the next immediate beat. Do not let the session linger here — get the party moving toward the corridor.
