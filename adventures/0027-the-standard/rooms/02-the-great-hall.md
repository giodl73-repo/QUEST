# Room 02 — The Great Hall

## Read-aloud

> Midday. Three fires going against the mountain cold. Soldiers at the long tables — some eating, some not. The supply ledger is open on the desk in the corner. Nobody is looking at it. The hall has the feel of people who have been waiting long enough that waiting has become its own kind of work.

## Features

- **The garrison's emotional state (automatic):** The standard is still visible from the upper windows. The hall's atmosphere is specific: this is not fear, and it is not desperation. It is the exhaustion of people who have been right about something for a long time — right that the siege would grind, right that the help would not come fast enough — and who have stopped needing to say so. They are not broken. They are tired in the way that only matters when they are asked to do one more thing. **This is a free read:** the party observes this without a check. It is the room's answer to "how is the garrison doing?"

- **Sergeant Irr (present, breakfasting alone):** He has an empty cup and a half-finished portion in front of him. He is not eating. If approached: *"She'll come tonight. After a challenge like that, she comes tonight. That's how Asholt taught her."* He says this without bitterness and without advice. He is not asking the party to do anything. If pressed for context: *"They disagreed about who the Oath is owed to. She thought he was wrong. She left. She has been wrong about him for twenty years."* He does not explain further.

- **Healer Maret Stave (present, wound count):** She is writing in her supply ledger — not supplies, injuries. The column under "healing materials" is blank. She has been recording wounds without treatment for six days. If approached: *"I have twelve soldiers who need more than I can give them. I have been writing that down because it is the only thing I can do with it."* She does not ask for anything. She is telling the party because they asked, and because it is true.

- **Gate-Captain Hessa Var (present, east window):** She is watching the standard from the east-facing window at the hall's far end. If approached: *"She knows the east wall is the weak point. She's known it since her second year of training here. The standard is for Asholt. The assault will be for the east wall."* **DC 12 Insight:** She is not afraid of the assault. She is afraid the assault will work, and that Asholt will have been right and wrong simultaneously — right to hold, wrong to think holding was enough.

- **Approaching Asholt (available here, Scene 02):** Asholt is not in the great hall, but he can be summoned or intercepted in the adjoining corridor if the party seeks him. He will not give the half-token scene here — that is reserved for Scene 05. But if approached here: he answers specific tactical questions directly. If a PC asks a personal question (about Saren, about the standard), he says: *"Ask me that again tonight, after."* He is not deflecting. He means it.

- **The supply ledger (available, DC 10 Investigation):** The ledger Maret has closed is the garrison's resource record. The right-side column has been blank since day 27. The entry on day 27 reads: *"Supply Die: exhausted. No further garrison resources."* In Dene Flor's handwriting. It is the most honest sentence in the keep.

## GM Notes

**This is the ensemble scene.** Every garrison figure with a token in this campaign is present or reachable. The PCs can talk to any of them. None of them have new arc completions to fire — Irr's token fired in S1, Maret's in S2, Hessa's in S3. This scene is texture and confirmation: these people are real, they are still here, and they are not pretending that tonight will be easy.

**Sergeant Irr's line is crucial and should be delivered.** He names the frame: Saren is not here for the pass. She is here because she and Asholt never finished something. He says *"That's how Asholt taught her"* — which means the assault tonight is, in a backwards way, Asholt's own teaching being used against him. This is what the party needs to carry into Scene 03.

**Do not rush this scene.** It is the session's breathing room before the assault. Let the party spend time here. Let them talk to the garrison. The information is not tactical — it is emotional. They are filling in the picture they will need to hold through the midnight combat.

**Maret's wound count is a quiet devastation.** She is not accusing anyone. She is just recording. If a PC asks who those twelve soldiers are: *"Some of them you know. Some of them you don't yet."*

**Hessa's read on the east wall:** She is correct. Use this to prime the party for the assault's tactical structure — the Reconstitution knows the east wall. They will come there. The party's position on the east wall at midnight is not a coincidence.

## Memory Fragment

> There was a night, six years ago, when Asholt sat in this hall with a group of refugees who had nowhere else to go. He fed them from garrison stock. He did not record it in the ledger. He did not tell anyone except Dene Flor. He said: *"The Oath says I protect Solamnia. It does not say I get to decide what counts as Solamnia."* That was the argument he never got to finish with Saren.
