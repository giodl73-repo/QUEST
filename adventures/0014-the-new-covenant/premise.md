# Premise — The New Covenant

## Hook

Lenne has the logistics. The Tower of High Sorcery at Istar can be reached in seventy-two days by ship from Palanthas — leaving four days of margin. The ruins are underwater; there is a navigable access point during the convergence when the water level drops (a pre-Cataclysm design; the compact's signatories built the Tower knowing the convergence would come). The witnesses are assembled or will meet them there. The seventh clause — the compact spoken whole — is the act.

The compact-wardens sail from Palanthas in three days.

## Setting

The ruins of the Tower of High Sorcery at Istar. The Cataclysm destroyed the tower three hundred years ago; the coastline was redrawn; the ruins now stand in shallow water at low tide and are submerged at high. During the triple-moon convergence, the tide drops further than normal — a three-hour window when the tower's inner chamber is accessible. This is the window.

The inner chamber has been preserved by pre-Cataclysm ward magic — dry, stable, cold stone. The seventh vault-space, where the compact tablet was shattered, is in the center. Six indentations, one for each shard. The seventh, where the tablet's center was, is empty.

## The Compact

The compact-wardens have assembled:
- Six shards (Witness, Ambition, Grief, Defiance, Reckoning, Forgiveness)
- Tessamine's reconstruction document (incomplete but close)
- Sylaren's amendment (*"What any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay"*)
- Lenne and Vorn's accountability language
- Thessaly's opening sentence (still in the margin of the reconstruction document; not shared)
- The witnesses (Mira, Deva, Vorn, Sevven's letter, Morreth's chapel record, the coordinator — in person)

The new compact incorporates Sylaren's amendment. It does not restore the old compact verbatim. It names what the Orders owe, who holds them accountable, and how the amendment works. It is shorter than the original. It is more specific.

## Route D (the campaign's finale condition)

The compact is spoken by Thessaly, with Lenne's accountability language incorporated, in the presence of all witnesses, during the convergence window. The six shards are placed in their vault-spaces as each clause is spoken. The seventh — the empty center — fills with the spoken compact itself: a resonance that is not a shard but is something. The compact is not written; it is witnessed.

Routes A and C are available but wrong:
- **Route A:** The party speaks the original compact verbatim. The old clauses rebind. The accountability language is not incorporated. Sylaren's amendment is acknowledged but not enacted. The compact is restored but not improved.
- **Route C:** The party is interrupted before completing the speaking — something goes wrong in the ruins (structural failure; the convergence window closes early; a faction-neutral event). The compact is partial; the shards are placed but the speaking is incomplete. The campaign ends without full closure.

**Route D requires:**
1. All six shards placed in their vault-spaces (done as the clauses are spoken)
2. Thessaly speaks the opening sentence aloud — to the witnesses, to the empty center, to the tower — for the first time
3. Lenne's accountability language incorporated into the Reckoning clause
4. Sylaren's amendment spoken as part of the Defiance clause
5. The Forgiveness clause spoken last: *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation"*

## Structure

Three parts:

**Part 1 — The Approach (Hard combat encounter):** Factions who oppose the compact's speaking have learned about the convergence window. A last-faction attack at the ruins' waterline — not Unbound Conclave (they have stood down) but a different group: scholars who believe the compact's restoration would give any Order a legitimacy claim they dispute. Hard encounter; Lenne's Counterspell and Projected Ward are essential; Orik's Battle Master carries the front line. This is the session's combat load — it must be genuine.

**Part 2 — The Chamber (assembling the shards):** The inner chamber. The vault. The witnesses present. Each shard placed as its clause is spoken. Five of the six clauses are spoken by Thessaly, Calder, Orik, Sera, Lenne respectively (one per PC). The sixth (Forgiveness) is spoken by all of them together.

**Part 3 — The Speaking (Route D):** Thessaly speaks the opening sentence she has been composing for three sessions. The compact is witnessed. The convergence window closes. The campaign ends.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5 (party of 5; witnesses present for the speaking). Hard combat encounter in Part 1. Parts 2 and 3 are entirely narrative/social — the finale is a rite, not a dungeon.

## DM-letter

The Part 3 speaking is the most important DM-letter of the campaign. Thessaly has been composing the opening sentence since Session 4. She has not shared it. The witnesses do not know what she is going to say. The DM holds space for her to say it.
