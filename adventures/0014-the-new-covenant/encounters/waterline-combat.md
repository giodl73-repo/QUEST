# Encounter — Waterline Combat (The Opposition)

**Location:** The waterline at the Istar ruins, as the party approaches the entrance.
**Trigger:** Always fires. This is the session's combat load.

**Context:** A faction called the Conclave Neutralists — scholars and former mages who believe the compact's restoration would give any Order a legitimacy claim they dispute. They are not wrong about the risk; they are wrong that the solution is preventing the speaking. They have three combat-capable members and a theoretical position the party cannot spend time arguing with.

They are not aligned with the Unbound Conclave (who have stood down). They are a separate opposition, motivated by legitimate concern about Order overreach, using the wrong method.

## Stat blocks

**Conclave Neutralist Mage (×2):** CR 3, AC 12 (Mage Armor 15), HP 38.
**Spells:** Fireball (L3, 1/day), Counterspell (reaction), Hold Person (concentration), Web (L2, concentration).
**Combat style:** One opens with Web to split the party; the other holds Counterspell for Lenne or Thessaly.

**Conclave Neutralist Fighter (×1):** CR 3, AC 16 (Chain Mail + Shield), HP 52.
**Attacks:** 2× Longsword +6 (1d8+4). Action Surge (1/short rest).
**Combat style:** Targets whoever the mages have split off from the main group.

**Adjusted XP:** 2 Mages (700 XP each, CR 3 = 700 XP) + 1 Fighter (700 XP) = 2100 base. ×2.5 multiplier (3 enemies) = 5250 adjusted. Per PC (5): 1050 XP. **Hard encounter.** The most dangerous fight of the campaign.

**Encounter-balancer check:**
- Fireball (8d6, DC 14): average 28 damage. Sera (44 HP) is at risk. Lenne's Projected Ward essential.
- Counterspell duel: 2 mages with Counterspell vs. Lenne and Thessaly. Party should coordinate spell use carefully.
- Action economy: 3 enemy actions (2 mages + 1 fighter) vs. 5 party actions. Party advantage, but the mages' Counterspell reduces effective party spellcasting.

**Tactics:**
Round 1: Mage 1 casts Web on party cluster. Mage 2 holds Counterspell. Fighter advances.
Round 2: Mage 1 casts Fireball if Web fails or players cluster. Mage 2 Counterspells the first offensive spell from Thessaly or Lenne.
Round 3+: Fighter focuses on whoever is separated by Web. Mages maintain concentration.

**Retreat trigger:** Both mages below 15 HP. They disengage. The fighter retreats with them. They do not pursue into the ruins.

**Social resolution (pre-combat):** Persuasion DC 17 (they are committed and not easily swayed) or a specific factual counter to their position ("the accountability language we have built prevents the legitimacy claim you're worried about; come witness the speaking"). If they believe the party's position: they stand aside. This is very hard; their concern is legitimate and they have been preparing for this for months. Lenne presenting the accountability clause text directly gives advantage on the check.

**XP:** 1050 per PC if defeated. 700 per PC if stood down (partial, for a diplomacy approach).

**Why this encounter is load-bearing:** Six sessions of near-0-dice sessions. This is the finale's only combat. It must be genuinely dangerous; Lenne's Ward and Orik's Battle Master get their finale showcase. Design specifically so the party's unused capabilities get a moment.
