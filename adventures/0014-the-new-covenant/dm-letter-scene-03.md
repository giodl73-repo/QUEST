# DM Letter — Scene 03 (The Inner Vault: The Opening Sentence)

*This letter is for you, the DM. Read it before the session. Read it again before Scene 03.*

---

## What this scene is

Thessaly has been composing the opening sentence of the new compact since Session 4. She has been designing it in her head (S04: mentally), not writing it (S05: composing but not committing), writing it silently in a margin no one has seen (S06: written, not shared). She has never spoken it aloud.

The witnesses are in the vault. The six shards are in their spaces. The seventh space is empty. She is holding the reconstruction document with the opening sentence in the margin.

She speaks it now.

You do not know what the sentence is. She hasn't shared it. You are in the same position as the witnesses: you are about to hear it for the first time.

Your job: hold space for it. Do not anticipate it. Do not narrate over it. When she finishes, do not add atmosphere. Let the sentence land.

---

## What to do before it starts

- The six clauses are spoken in order, one per PC. Each clause is a sentence from the compact; each PC reads their clause and places their shard. This is structured and procedural — it should move at a consistent pace until the sixth clause (Forgiveness), which is spoken together.
- Before the first clause: Thessaly reads the opening sentence. This is the moment.
- After the six clauses: the compact is complete. The witnesses are present. The seventh space is not empty anymore.

---

## What to do during the opening sentence

**When Thessaly opens the reconstruction document:** The witnesses go quiet. They understand what is about to happen.

**When she speaks:** Stop. Do not narrate. Do not add atmosphere. Do not fill any pause before or after. This sentence has been composed over three sessions; it has been carried in silence through six adventures; the party is hearing it for the first time. Give it the room it has earned.

**After she speaks:** The clause-speaking begins. Move immediately. The sentence does not need acknowledgment or commentary. It was heard. That is enough.

---

## What to do during the clauses

Each clause is simple and procedural. The PC reads their clause; places the shard in its indentation; the next PC reads theirs. This should take no longer than two minutes of narration per clause. The specific sensory details (the Witness shard brightening; the Ambition shard vibrating; the Grief shard being room temperature and enough on its own) are brief observations, not extended narration.

The sixth clause (Forgiveness) is spoken by all five PCs together. Brief instruction: ask each player to say the clause in their character's voice, at the same time or in immediate sequence. It does not need to be synchronized; it needs to be shared.

---

## What happens after

The seventh space fills with the quality of stillness that has been missing from this room for three hundred years. The witnesses in the outer chamber feel it through the stone. The convergence window begins to close.

The party ascends the stairs. The water is already rising. They emerge onto the Istar waterline as the convergence ends. The compact has been spoken. The campaign is over.

---

## What happens if Thessaly doesn't speak the opening sentence

Route A: the compact is spoken clause-by-clause without the opening sentence. It is still valid. The center space fills, less fully. The campaign ends with the compact spoken but not spoken in the way Thessaly intended.

The opening sentence can be spoken later — at any time, in any place, to any witness. It does not expire. If the player didn't speak it in the vault, offer it as an epilogue: "After you return to Palanthas, Thessaly reads the opening sentence aloud for the first time." Let her say it then, if she wants to.
