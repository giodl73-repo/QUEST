# C6-S7 Session Log — The Reckoning

**Date in world:** PC 188, Day 7, Thorngate Keep
**Spotlight:** Varet (journal presentation — arc-completion). Arren Vole (speaks as himself). Lenne (log to tribunal). Brek (witness).
**Seed:** C6S7-2026-04-22
**Unit Cohesion Die:** d8 (from S6). Roll: 4. 4 soldiers in formal dress at tribunal.

**ROUTE E ACHIEVED.**

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Varet's journal. Before the tribunal. She read every entry once, start to finish. Not to prepare. She had written them — she already knew what was there. She read them to confirm that she would read them aloud. That was different from knowing. Reading them aloud was an act. Reading them privately was a habit. She needed to know the difference before she walked into the room.

She confirmed: she would read them aloud.

She opened the journal to a clean page and wrote the Day 7 header. Then she closed it. She did not write anything else yet. The entry would be written after.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
Arren Vole stood at parade rest outside the tribunal room. He had been there since dawn. Not on orders. The orders said the unit would present at the morning tribunal. They did not say he needed to arrive at dawn. He arrived at dawn.

He had served with three commanders before Varet. Two of them had done things he could not defend. One of them he had vouched for anyway, because the institution required it. He had thought about that commander every day since. He would not vouch for a fourth.

He would stand at the door. He would see what happened. He would act from what he saw.

## Scene 0 — Tribunal Assembly

Thorngate Keep's tribunal room. Three officers behind a long table. Colonel Derrath at center — compact, controlled. Major Fey to his left, attentive. Lieutenant Colonel Sorn (neutral, writing) to his right.

The unit entered in formal dress. **Cohesion die: d8 — roll: 4.** Four soldiers. The unit's full complement from six days of patrol. The tribunal saw the reduction — they could count.

Arren Vole brought up the rear. He took position at the door. He did not close it.

Derrath read from a report. The formal objection. The Ossren incident. The east-valley diversion. *"Commander Senn. This review follows a formal objection filed in this unit's assignment, a subsequent irregular engagement at east valley — unapproved search action — and a report from Lieutenant Ossren describing command resistance. You are here to answer for those actions."*

Varet stood at attention. She did not respond. She was waiting.

**Varet Insight on Derrath (+4):** rolls=[9] mod=+4 **total=13** — just below DC14. She could not read him fully. He was controlled. The control itself told her something: he had prepared for this. A man who had nothing to prepare for does not prepare this way.

Derrath: *"Do you have a response, Commander?"*

Varet: *"Yes, Colonel."* She opened the field journal.

Derrath: *"Commander, if you have documentation to submit, it goes to the clerk."*

Varet: *"Yes, Colonel."* She did not give it to the clerk. She began to read.

---

## Scene 1 — The Journal Presentation (ROUTE E — VERBATIM)

**This is the finale. Read aloud. Every entry.**

Varet began reading from the discrepancy column. Her voice was the same voice she used to issue orders. Flat. Professional. The precision of a nine-year field record.

> *"Irenvale Patrol Field Journal, Commander Varet Senn, 7th Expeditionary. Discrepancy column entries, in order.*
>
> *Day 1. Orders included coordinates for a staging cache not on any current patrol map. The key to the cache was at the bottom of the order packet, under the folding — non-standard placement. Location confirmed: permanent structure, stocked beyond patrol capacity. Second ledger inside cache: entries for 'CF ford,' 'RH toll,' 'BW burn.' CF: Creston Ford — our patrol confiscation point. RH: Ridgehead. BW: the Burned Waystation. Discrepancy: the unit's confiscations feed a pre-existing toll collection system. This was not in the orders.*
>
> *Day 2. Cache restocked overnight without authorized unit movement. No resupply in orders. Medical stores: triage allocation for three times standard patrol casualty expectation. Mountain farmer Serrak, civilian: crossbow wound from military-specification Solamnic bolt, east of patrol route. No authorized military activity east of route. Cause of Serrak's wound: unclear. My medic's notation. She writes 'cause: unclear' when the briefing she received cannot explain what she observed.*
>
> *Day 3. Scout Brek Aldren overdue from advance route. Captain Veth's order: continue patrol, log the absence. My assessment: the advance route takes Brek east — same direction as Serrak's attackers. I diverted search resources. We found Brek. He had heard the phrase 'the commander knows this route is flagged' from the guards holding him. He said this to me directly. Discrepancy: I was told I knew something I do not know. My name was used to indicate compliance. I have not complied with any flagging. I did not know the route was flagged. Someone told those guards I did.*

**Derrath (interrupting for the first time):** *"Commander, this is not the—"*

**Major Fey (sharply):** *"Colonel. Let her finish."*

Varet continued without pausing.

> *Day 4. Transfer order: everything in staging cache to civilian coordinates, Sorn factor-house, eight miles west. No tribunal authorization in the order packet. Veteran Dorn Ashfeld read Clause 4 of his enlistment contract aloud in the presence of Lieutenant Ossren. Clause 4: 'No military resources shall be transferred to civilian private custody without a standing tribunal order or the express written authorization of a Commander of the Sword or above.' Lieutenant Ossren's response: 'I was told it was logistics.' He did not have written authorization. Transfer halted via formal objection. Lieutenant Ossren witnessed the objection and signed it. Discrepancy: the order was not legal. No tribunal order existed. An officer sent to supervise had not been told what the order was for.*
>
> *Day 5. Captain Veth disclosed the following in private conference: Lord Herath Sorn holds payment documentation against him from seven years prior. Veth has been compliant since. This unit was assigned to Irenvale Crossing because my service record includes a complaint I filed three years ago, subsequently dismissed. The word used in the brief was 'manageable.' I was selected for this patrol because command expected I would not escalate. My own complaint was annotated after submission — not in my handwriting. I did not dismiss my own complaint. Someone did. Discrepancy: my record was modified. I have the original copy.*

**Derrath (interrupting for the second time):** *"This is a private disclosure from a superior officer. This cannot—"*

**Lieutenant Colonel Sorn (dryly):** *"The format is standard, Colonel. A commander reading her own field journal is standard procedure."*

Derrath was silent.

> *Day 6. Ambush at Creston Ford. Eight Sorn fighters, coordinated, briefed on our patrol schedule. They had specific orders to retrieve this journal. Soldier Carra: incapacitated in combat, stabilized, recovered. Captured fighter named Drev Ossian — Istaran trade factor — as the one who pays Sorn. I retrieved a document from Thorngate Keep's records: a letter on Istaran trade-house stationery signed by a factor named Drev Ossian. It references this unit as 'a temporary variable to be managed.' It is dated six months ago. The letter is in the document sleeve of this journal.*
>
> *Entry 5 addendum, written in this journal: 'Discrepancy count: six. Outcome count: two. Per my own standing rule — written in this journal's front matter three years ago — the arc fires when discrepancies exceed outcomes. The arc fires. I am the unit's commanding officer. I have the authority to act. I am going to act.'*
>
> *Discrepancy count: seven. Outcome count: three. Ratio: inverted.*
>
> *I am required to report accurately. This is my accurate report."*

Varet closed the journal.

She did not look at Derrath. She looked at Major Fey.

*"The Ossian letter is in the document sleeve."*

She slid the journal to the tribunal table.

---

**Silence in the room. Four seconds.**

Derrath stood. *"This proceeding is irregular. I am moving to—"*

**Arren Vole walked in through the door he had not closed.**

He took position inside the room. He did not draw a weapon. He stood in the doorway. Visible to the room. He did not speak.

Derrath looked at Arren.

Arren looked back.

**Derrath sat down.**

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-triggered -->
Twelve years. That was all the door held. A man who had served for twelve years standing in a doorway without a weapon, without a word, and a Colonel who understood exactly what twelve years of watching meant. No speech. No threat. Just the door.

## Scene 2 — Evidence Submission

**Taris shows the map (second time — first was to Varet in S5):**

Taris approached the witness position. He unfolded the map on the table beside the journal. *"Three weeks of patrol, marked as I observed it."* He pointed: the toll-route waymarks, the east valley site, the Sorn coordinates, the Ossian letter date matched to the patrol assignment date. He did not explain the map. The map was legible.

**Lenne's arc-completion:**

Lenne approached the tribunal clerk. She did not hand the log to Derrath. She handed it to Major Fey.

*"These are the ones I couldn't explain with the briefing I was given."*

She named three entries aloud: *"Serrak's wound — military specification bolt, east of route, cause unclear. Cache medical stores — three times standard allocation, cause unclear. Day four transfer — medical supplies to civilian estate, cause unclear."*

*"I don't write 'cause: unclear' unless I can't explain it. I couldn't explain any of these with what I was told."*

She stepped back. The log was in Major Fey's hands. Arc complete.

**Dorn reads the contract clause (third time total — once in bunk, once to Ossren, once here):**

Dorn stepped forward. He unfolded the contract. He read Clause 4. Flat. Complete. He folded it. He looked at Derrath. He did not say anything else.

**Varet Insight on Ossren (+4):** rolls=[11] mod=+4 **total=15** — PASS (DC12). Ossren was truthful. She had known he would be.

**Lieutenant Ossren (called into the corridor, enters):** *"I was told it was logistics. I did not know what the order was for. I was sent to supervise a transfer I had not been briefed on."* He did not defend himself. He reported.

**Brek Aldren (called as witness):**

**Brek Persuasion passive DC11 (+0):** rolls=[16] total=16 — He was credible.

*"They said the commander wouldn't look east. That the route was flagged. They said she knew."* A pause. *"I didn't think she knew. That's why I'm here."*

## Scene 3 — General Hadren

Major Fey spoke quietly to Lt. Col. Sorn. He excused himself. He returned three minutes later with General Sel Hadren.

**Hadren Investigation DC10 on Ossian letter (+1):** rolls=[17] mod=+1 **total=18** — He read the letter. He set it down. He said three things:

1. *"I need to verify the Ossian letter. That will take forty-eight hours."*

2. *"Pending verification, this unit is relieved of patrol duties — not punitively. Administrative stand-down. Full pay, quarters here."*

3. To Varet, looking at her directly: *"Nine years of clean record, Commander. This journal is that record. I'll read the discrepancy column myself."*

Derrath said nothing. He was calculating. His best move was now silence. The Ossian letter was public.

**Veth's response (called afterward):** He answered one question. *"I knew what the orders said. I didn't know what they meant."* He said it once. He did not elaborate.

---

## Scene 4 — Arren's Arc-Completion

Corridor. After the formal proceedings. The unit stood at ease.

Varet walked to Arren. She said: *"Sergeant. What do you think?"*

He was quiet for a moment. The first quiet in twelve years that wasn't tactical.

*"I think you already knew, Commander. I think you've known since the Waystation. The journal has it. You wrote it down. You just kept writing until the column was right."*

He paused.

*"Nine years. It was in the journal the whole time."*

**This is Arren Vole's arc-completion.** Not a question. Not a report. He spoke as himself — the first time in the campaign. She asked him what he *thought*. He answered.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-triggered -->
*"Nine years. It was in the journal the whole time."* He was not giving her credit. He was describing what he had watched. He had been watching for twelve years. What he saw was a woman who had been building the case without knowing she was building it, and he had been waiting to see if she would use it.

She had.

---

## Scene 5 — Closing Beats

**Varet:**

She did not speak a speech. She sat down on the corridor bench and opened the journal to the clean page she had set aside before the tribunal. She wrote:

*"Day 7. Tribunal. Journal presented verbatim. All evidence submitted. Discrepancy column: 7 entries. Outcome: investigation opened. The ratio has resolved."*

She held the journal for a moment. Then she wrote one more line, under the formal entry, in the margin:

*"Arren said it was in the journal the whole time. He was right. It was. I just had to write enough of it."*

She closed the journal.
