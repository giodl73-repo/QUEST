# 0042 — The Reckoning
## Module Compiled

---

## DM Cheatsheet

**Unit Cohesion Die:** d6 or d4 (from S6). Roll result = soldiers in formal dress at tribunal.
**Party HP:** Carry-over + 1 hit die. Combat possible but not primary.
**Session structure:** Tribunal room. Three acts. Journal read verbatim. Arc-completions: Varet, Arren Vole, Lenne (third).
**Route E requires:** Full unit loyalty + journal complete + arc fires (Varet reads journal aloud herself, in the tribunal room, without being given permission).
**No resources restored between S6 and S7.** Between-session HP carry-over applies.

---

## Scene 0 — Tribunal Assembly

**Read-aloud:**
> Thorngate Keep's tribunal room. Three officers behind a long table. Colonel Derrath at the center — compact, controlled, reading a report he already knows the contents of. Major Fey to his left: neutral, attentive. Lieutenant Colonel Sorn to his right: also neutral, older, writing something.
>
> The unit enters in formation. Cohesion die result visible: the soldiers are there, but the unit is reduced. Someone has been in those positions before. The positions are filled. They are not full.
>
> Arren Vole brings up the rear. He takes position at the door and does not close it.

**Beats:**
- Derrath reads from the report: *"Commander Senn. This review follows a formal objection filed in Session 4 and a subsequent incident report from Lieutenant Ossren. You are here to answer for those actions."*
- Varet: stands at attention. She does not respond. She is waiting.
- Derrath notes her silence and takes it as compliance. He continues. He is wrong.

---

## Scene 1 — Derrath's Framework

**Read-aloud:**
> Derrath lays out the framework: the unit filed an irregular objection to a transfer order. The objection delayed a logistics operation. The unit has been conducting unauthorized searches (east valley, S3). A report exists from Lieutenant Ossren describing "command resistance."
>
> "This review will determine whether the unit's conduct warrants reassignment or formal censure." He looks at Varet. "Do you have a response, Commander?"

**Beats:**
- Varet: "Yes, Colonel." She opens the field journal.
- Derrath: "Commander, this review follows standard procedure. If you have documentation to submit, it goes to the clerk."
- Varet: "Yes, Colonel." She does not give it to the clerk. She begins to read.

---

## Scene 2 — Journal Presentation (ROUTE E)

**This is the finale. The DM reads every entry aloud as Varet's voice. No abbreviation.**

**Read-aloud (Varet begins reading — verbatim):**

> *"Irenvale Patrol Field Journal, Commander Varet Senn, 7th Expeditionary. Discrepancy column entries, in order.*
>
> *Day 1. Orders included coordinates for a staging cache not on any current patrol map. The key to the cache was at the bottom of the order packet, under the folding. Location confirmed: permanent structure, stocked beyond patrol capacity. Second ledger inside: entries for 'CF ford,' 'RH toll,' 'BW burn.' CF: Creston Ford — our patrol confiscation point. Discrepancy: the unit's confiscations feed a pre-existing toll collection system. This was not in the orders.*
>
> *Day 2. Cache restocked overnight without record. No unit movement authorized. Medical stores: triage allocation for three times standard patrol casualty expectation. Mountain farmer Serrak: crossbow wound from military-specification bolt, east of patrol route. Discrepancy: military action east of route not in orders. Cause of Serrak's wound: unclear. This is Medic Orr's notation. She has begun writing 'cause: unclear' for wounds that do not match briefings.*
>
> *Day 3. Scout Brek Aldren overdue from advance route. Captain Veth's order: continue patrol, log the absence. My assessment: the advance route takes Brek east — same direction as Serrak's attackers. I diverted search resources. We found Brek. He had heard the phrase 'the commander knows this route is flagged' from the guards holding him. He said this to me directly. Discrepancy: I was told I knew something I do not know. My name was used to indicate compliance. I have not complied with any flagging. I did not know the route was flagged. Someone told those guards I did.*
>
> *Day 4. Transfer order: everything in staging cache to civilian coordinates, Sorn factor-house, 8 miles west. No tribunal authorization in the order packet. Veteran Ashfeld read Clause 4 of his enlistment contract aloud in the presence of Lieutenant Ossren. Clause 4: no military resources to civilian private custody without a tribunal order. Lieutenant Ossren's response: 'I was told it was logistics.' He did not have written authorization. Transfer halted via formal objection. Discrepancy: the order was not legal. No tribunal order existed. An officer sent to supervise had not been told what the order was for.*
>
> *Day 5. Captain Veth disclosed the following in private conference: Lord Herath Sorn holds payment documentation against him from seven years prior. Veth has been compliant under that documentation since. This unit was assigned to Irenvale Crossing because my service record includes a complaint I filed three years ago, subsequently dismissed. The word used in the brief was 'manageable.' I was selected for this patrol because command expected I would not escalate. Discrepancy: I am reporting this to the tribunal. The selection criterion was wrong. I have escalated.*
>
> *Day 6. Creston Ford ambush. Eight Sorn fighters, coordinated, briefed on our patrol schedule. They had specific orders to retrieve this journal. Soldier Carra: incapacitated, stabilized. Captured fighter named Drev Ossian — Istaran trade factor — as the one who pays Sorn. Ossian's letter is in the document sleeve of this journal. It references this unit as a 'temporary variable to be managed.' It is dated six months ago.*
>
> *Discrepancy count: seven. Outcome count: three. Per my own standing rule: when discrepancies exceed outcomes, the arc fires.*
>
> *I have filed seven discrepancy entries across six days of patrol. I am the unit's commanding officer. I am required to report accurately. This is my accurate report."*

**Derrath interrupts twice:**
- After Day 3 entry: "Commander, this is not the format for—"
  - Major Fey (neutral officer, sharply): "Colonel. Let her finish."
- After Day 5 entry: "This is a private disclosure from a superior officer. This cannot—"
  - Lt. Col. Sorn (dryly): "The format is standard, Colonel. A commander reading her own field journal is standard procedure."
  - Derrath is silent.

**After the final entry:** Varet closes the journal. She does not look at Derrath. She looks at Major Fey. She says: "The Ossian letter is in the document sleeve." She slides the journal to the tribunal table.

## Scene 3 — Evidence Submission

**Taris shows the map (second time — first was to the unit in S5):**
> Taris approaches the witness position. He unfolds the map on the table beside the journal. He says: "Three weeks of patrol, marked as I observed it." He points: the toll-route waymarks; the east valley holdout site; the Sorn coordinates; the track patterns from sessions 1-6. He does not explain it. The map is legible.

**Lenne's log transfer (arc-completion):**
> Lenne approaches the tribunal clerk. She does not hand the log to Derrath. She hands it to Major Fey. "These are the ones I couldn't explain with the briefing I was given." She names three entries aloud: Serrak's wound, the cache medical stores, the Session 4 transfer.
>
> Major Fey opens the log. He reads. He does not comment. He passes it to Lt. Col. Sorn.

**This is Lenne's arc-completion.** She names the entries aloud. The log is delivered to a neutral officer, not suppressed by Derrath.

**Dorn reads the contract clause (second time — third time total counting S4):**
> Dorn steps forward. He unfolds the contract. He reads Clause 4. He folds it. He looks at Derrath. He does not say anything else. He does not need to.

---

## Scene 4 — Arren Vole's Arc-Completion

**Read-aloud:**
> After Dorn's clause reading. Derrath stands. "This proceeding is irregular. I am moving to suspend—"
>
> Arren Vole walks in through the door he did not close. He takes position inside the room. He does not draw a weapon. He stands in the doorway. Visible to the room.
>
> Derrath looks at Arren. Arren looks back.
>
> Derrath sits down.

**After Derrath sits:** Major Fey speaks quietly to Lt. Col. Sorn. He excuses himself and leaves the room. He returns in three minutes with General Sel Hadren.

**Arren's arc-completion fires when Varet, after the tribunal, asks him directly what he thinks:**
> Varet: "Sergeant. What do you think?"
>
> Arren: "I think you already knew, Commander. I think you've known since the Waystation. The journal has it. You wrote it down. You just kept writing until the column was right." He pauses. "Nine years. It was in the journal the whole time."

---

## Scene 5 — Resolution

**General Hadren enters:**
> Hadren reads the Ossian letter. He reads the journal's discrepancy column again, quickly. He sets both on the table. He says three things:
>
> 1. "I need to verify the Ossian letter. That will take forty-eight hours."
> 2. "Pending verification, this unit is relieved of patrol duties — not punitively. You are on administrative stand-down. Full pay, quarters here."
> 3. To Varet, looking at her directly: "Nine years of clean record, Commander. This journal is that record. I'll read the discrepancy column myself."
>
> Derrath says nothing. He is calculating. His best move is now silence. The Ossian letter is public.

**Lieutenant Ossren (present in the corridor, called in):**
> He tells the truth. "I was told it was logistics. I did not know what the order was for. I was sent to supervise a transfer I had not been briefed on." He does not defend himself. He reports.

## Routes

**Route A:** 3+ unit members loyal. Journal submitted through official channels. Derrath attempts to suppress; Major Fey intercedes. Outcome: investigation opened, unit reassigned pending inquiry. Ossian letter eventually reaches General Hadren.

**Route B:** 5+ loyal. Derrath bypassed before he acts. Journal delivered directly to Hadren by Fey or Sorn who understood the implication. Outcome: investigation opened immediately. Ossian letter active same day.

**Route E:** Full unit loyalty + journal complete + Varet reads it herself without being given permission. Derrath's suppression attempt fails because the reading is already complete and public. All four evidence pieces (journal, map, medical log, contract) submitted simultaneously. Outcome: investigation opens. Veth cooperates (he called this in). Ossian network under pressure within the month.

**Route E special condition (Edric Senn):**
If Field Commander Edric Senn is invoked (he is Varet's commanding officer at the campaign level, above Veth): after Varet hands the journal to the tribunal table, Edric — present in the room or called for — picks it up. He presents it himself to Hadren. His line: "Nine years of field reporting under my command. Every entry accurate. I am presenting this journal on behalf of Commander Senn." Edric's arc: he chose loyalty to the institution over compliance with Ossian's network, because Varet showed him what the institution was supposed to look like.

## Campaign Closing Beats

After the formal proceedings, in the corridor:

**Varet:** She does not speak a speech. She opens the journal to a clean page. She writes:
> *Day 7. Tribunal. Journal presented. All evidence submitted. Discrepancy column: seven entries. Outcome: investigation opened. The ratio has resolved.*

She does not close the journal. She leaves it open for a moment. Then she closes it.

**Taris:** Folds the patrol map. He does not annotate it. He puts it in his pack. The map has served its purpose — it was legible when needed. He will update it tomorrow. Force of habit. The habit is him.

**Lenne:** Writes one last entry in the medical log: *"Cause of all preceding 'unclear' entries: systemic irregularity in command structure. Cause now explained. Log updated."* She closes the log. It is accurate.

**Dorn:** Takes his unit contract from the oilskin sleeve. Reads Clause 4. Puts it away. The clause held. The contract held. He held. That is sufficient.
