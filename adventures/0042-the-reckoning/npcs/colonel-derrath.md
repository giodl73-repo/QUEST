# Colonel Derrath

**Role:** Tribunal officer, aligned with Ossian's network. Present to manage the outcome.

## Stat block

Knight (MM p.347): AC 18, HP 52. Leadership reaction.

## Emotional position

Derrath was appointed to the tribunal because someone in Ossian's network placed him there. He does not know the unit has the Ossian letter. He thinks he is managing a routine review of a unit that filed an irregular formal objection. He is not prepared for the journal.

When Varet begins reading, he interrupts twice — standard protocol challenges. Both times, the neutral officers indicate she should continue. By the third entry he is silent. He is calculating.

He does not act violently. He is not that kind of threat. He is the kind of threat that moves paperwork — reassignments, suppressed findings, career deletions. When he realizes the journal includes the Ossian letter, he stands up. He says: "This proceeding is irregular." Arren Vole walks in the door.

## Arc-completion conditions

Derrath does not have an arc. He is the antagonist who makes the arc possible by being wrong. His role is structural: he is the reason the journal presentation needs to be aloud, in the room, in front of witnesses.

His exit line (after Hadren is summoned): he says nothing. He sits back down. The calculation has resolved: the Ossian letter is public. His best move is now silence.

## Voice

Formal, controlled, professionally unreadable until the Ossian letter surfaces.

# General Sel Hadren

**Role:** Senior officer, available via neutral officers. Has authority to act on the journal's evidence.

## Stat block

Veteran (MM p.350): AC 17, HP 58.

## Arc note

Hadren is not a surprise hero. He is an institutional mechanism — the part of the institution that functions. He receives the journal, the map, and the medical log. He says three things:

1. "I need to verify the Ossian letter."
2. "Pending verification, this unit is relieved of patrol duties — not punitively."
3. To Varet: "Nine years of clean record, Commander. This journal is that record. I'll read the discrepancy column myself."

He is the voice that the institution has when it works. He is not saved by him — they save themselves. He is the mechanism that receives what they built.
