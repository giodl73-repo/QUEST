# C7-S7 Session Log — The Road Again

**Date in world:** Day 8, Stonehaven south gate, morning  
**Spotlight:** None (finale)  
**Seed:** C7S7-2026-04-22

---

## Scene 0 — Morning

The inn at Stonehaven. Morning. The four travelers are at different points in the inn getting ready: Brek at the table with his pack already closed; Sera at the window with her notebook; Vess counting her remaining coin (less than yesterday); Tam at the bar having acquired bread from the kitchen without anyone knowing how.

Nobody says "good morning." There is no ceremony. There is packing and then there is the door.

---

## Scene 1 — South Gate (Room 00 — Primary Data Point)

The south gate. Morning. The road is open. The west road branches half a mile out. Vess has a delivery contact in Millhaven — south. Sera is going south to find Mikela. Tam's circuit continues south. Brek has nothing that requires him to go south.

**The DM does not direct. The DM narrates:**

*"You are at the gate. The south road to Millhaven is ahead. The west road branches off half a mile out. What happens?"*

**Verbatim — what each PC says at the gate:**

**Vess:** *(looks at the west fork sign; looks at the south road; looks at Sera's back)* "Millhaven delivery contact is south. I'm going south."

She has a reason. She chose to find a reason. Her west route is real; her Millhaven contact is also real. She chose the road where the others are going.

*Research coding: [Vess → south road]. Trigger type: relationship-driven (found a reason to go the same direction; the finding was the choice). Route E evidence.*

**Brek:** *(standing at the gate, pack on, looking south)* "Stonehaven's done."

He walks through the gate.

He does not say "I'll come with you" or "the road is south." He says what is true: Stonehaven is done. The road goes south. He goes south. The inference is available to anyone who wants to draw it.

*Research coding: [Brek → south road]. Trigger type: relationship-driven (his reason is gone; he is still here; he goes the direction the others are going because that is where the road is and they are on it). Route E evidence.*

**Tam:** *(to no one specifically)* "Circuit continues south anyway."

He says this as he steps through the gate. It is factually true. He arranged for it to be factually true after last night's inn, when he looked at his circuit map and confirmed the next wayshrine could be reached from the Millhaven road. He did the confirmation. The confirmation is the choice.

*Research coding: [Tam → south road]. Trigger type: relationship-driven (arranged his circuit to coincide with the group's route; the arranging was the decision). Route E evidence.*

**Sera:** *(already walking; does not stop at the gate; passes through while writing in her notebook)* Nothing.

She says nothing at the gate. She is already through.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-driven -->
*Research coding: [Sera → south road]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. She does not say anything because the choice requires no words. She made the choice by walking through the gate when the others walked through the gate. Six sessions of following people she did not plan to follow. The gate is just another moment of the same thing. She is already writing.*

**ROUTE E: all four on the south road. The campaign's Route E choice is complete.**

## Scene 2 — Millhaven Road (Room 01)

The first day south of Stonehaven. Flat country, cold air. The road is open in both directions. No one is following them.

They walk. The travel pattern reasserts itself: Vess slightly forward, Brek at the rear, Tam talking to people at waypoints when there are any, Sera writing.

The DM narrates three moments without directing:

**Morning:**

Vess spots a road marker she has used before — the workshop-canon border-trade waymark, a circle with a line through it, on a cairn stone. It means she has been through here. This is known territory. She notes it and continues.

She does not say anything about what it means that she is returning through known territory with people she did not plan to be traveling with.

**Midday:**

Tam eats his bread. He took enough for all four from the inn kitchen this morning. He hands bread to Brek without comment. Brek takes it without comment.

*Research coding: [Tam → Brek → bread, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. The most mundane Reciprocal Act of the campaign. That is correct for the last session.*

**Afternoon:**

Sera closes her notebook. She has been writing all day. She looks at the south road for a while without writing. Then she opens the notebook again.

She writes: *"Eight days. The road is south."*

That is the final documentation entry. She does not write what that means.

## Scene 3 — First Camp South (Room 02)

The first camp south of Stonehaven. The road's edge, a fire ring, old ash. They have used fire rings like this for seven sessions.

**The DM logs what each PC does at camp without direction:**

**Brek:** Sets the watch rotation without asking. *"You, midnight. You, three. I'll take first."* He does not assign himself to Vess or Tam or Sera specifically; he assigns the shifts in the order they are standing.

**Sera:** Opens her notebook at the camp light. She writes one more entry — the DM does not narrate this one. The camp-fire is orange; the notebook is visible; whatever she writes is private.

**Tam:** Tends the fire until it is the right size. Then stops. The fire is right. He does not adjust it again.

**Vess:** She eats outside the firelight. She has been outside the firelight since Session 1. This is not different. What is different: she can see the fire from where she sits. She sits within sight of it. That is the change.

*Research coding: Vess's position at camp is the campaign's final act-without-announcement. She is still outside the firelight — her heuristic has not changed. But she is close enough to see it. The distance has shortened. She did not announce this. The positioning is the evidence.*
