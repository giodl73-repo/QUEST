## Setting & Background

The road is clear; all four PCs can go their separate ways; they choose not to — Route E if all four choose to travel together by genuine character logic.

**Stonehaven south gate, morning** (workshop-canon, south Abanasinia). The morning after Session 6. The four travelers are standing at the gate with their packs. Each of them has a direction to go: Brek has no particular next destination; Sera should go to Millhaven; Tam's circuit continues south; Vess has another delivery route that goes west. The road is open. There is no more problem that requires all four of them.

The session is the goodbye. Route E requires each PC to choose, from genuine character logic, to stay with the group rather than go their separate way. The DM does not frame this as a choice — the characters arrive at the gate and decide what they're doing. If they choose the same road: that is Route E. If they choose different roads: that is Route A. Both are valid data.

1. **The gate — morning** — the four gather at the south gate without ceremony. The road is open. Vess's next delivery route goes west. Sera's scholar destination is Millhaven (south). Tam's circuit continues south. Brek has nothing to go toward.
2. **The decision moment** — the DM does not prompt. Each PC's heuristic and the six sessions preceding this one determine what they say. The DM logs verbatim what each PC says (or does not say).
3. **The road south (if Route E)** — they take the Millhaven road together. Not because all destinations converge — only Tam and Sera are going south. Brek and Vess are choosing the road rather than their previous direction.
4. **What is said on the road** — the session close is whatever conversation emerges. The DM does not direct it. The six sessions of accumulated Reciprocal Acts either produced something real or they didn't.
5. **The log entry** — session closes with the DM's research coding assessment: trigger types for the Route E choice, evidence for RV9–RV12 verdicts.

- Route E: all four choose the same road. Any reason (convenience, explicit naming of the group, implicit continuation) counts.
- Route A: fewer than four on the same road. Still valid data for research purposes.
- Route D: impossible in this design — there is no designed emotional spine to invoke.

S7. Gate 77 PASS target (high). The DM logs the Route E choice verbatim — what each PC says or does not say when the road clears. This is the primary data point of the entire campaign.

## NPCs

# The Road

The road is not a character. It is the container that forced four strangers to interact for six sessions and now, in the seventh session, makes no demands.

**Function in Session 7:** The road's function is to be open. It does not ask anything of the PCs. It does not require cooperation. It goes south because south is where several of them need to go, and that is all.

**The absence of road-pressure is the design.** Every prior session had a problem the road presented. Session 7 has no problem. The PCs travel because they choose to, not because the road requires them.

**DM note:** If you find yourself adding road complications in Session 7, you are providing a crutch that the research design intentionally removes. The session's data quality depends on the absence of external pressure. Let the road be open.

## Encounters Appendix

---

### No Encounter

# No Encounter

The finale session contains no designed combat encounter. The road is clear. This is intentional.

If the DM feels an encounter is needed: resist this feeling. The absence of encounter is the design. The session's content is the four PCs and what they say and don't say.

If a wandering encounter is rolled (DM may omit this table for this session): treat it as a trivial obstacle — travelers asking directions, a wagon with a wheel issue, something that takes five minutes and requires nothing. Do not let a combat encounter reframe the session's final movement.

## Research note

A 0-encounter final session is not a session-design failure. It is a measurement condition. If the campaign produced genuine mutual reliance in six sessions, the seventh session will be populated by what PCs do when there is nothing external requiring them to act. What they do then is the data.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
