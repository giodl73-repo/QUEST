# Fresh Face Lens — C5-S1

I came in knowing nothing about the Silken Ledger or the guild. By the end of Scene 1 I understood: this is a professional fencing operation with its own internal language, a guild master (Pale) who is somewhere in the background, and four people who trust each other's competence without trusting each other's motives. That is a lot of worldbuilding for a first scene.

**What worked:** The guild infrastructure is established through the party's behavior, not through exposition. I learned what a "marker" is from context (Tessaly issues them; Rue receives them; they represent trust that goes both ways). I learned what the ledger is from watching Tessaly use it. I did not need a glossary.

Bessa Tor was immediately legible as a person. She had been waiting. She asked one question. She accepted a partial answer. Everything about her situation was communicated through her posture and the key in her hand — I did not need backstory.

**Friction:** Orren Pale is mentioned three times (the arrested guild member, the audit concern, Rue's message) without being introduced. As a fresh reader I know he is important and I know something is wrong with him, but I don't have a face for the threat yet. This feels intentional — he's the season's Chekhov — but I wanted at least one concrete detail.

**Score:** 71/80 — fully legible for a new reader; Pale is appropriately mysterious.
