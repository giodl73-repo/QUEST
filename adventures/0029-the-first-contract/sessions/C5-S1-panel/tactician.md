# Tactician Lens — C5-S1

The tactical picture here is a clean two-path problem well executed. Two ingress options (window / Bessa), one time-pressure element (Watch patrol window), one noise economy. The party read it correctly and adapted when Bessa pre-resolved the bar problem.

**What landed:** The Watch patrol window as a real constraint. The noise economy — especially Darro's hard landing at total 8, which the DM let stand as a genuine consequence (30-second freeze). Cael's position between party and exit. These are tactically sound.

**Friction:** No combat, no tactical choice with real stakes beyond the patrol window. Cael's Battle Master dice never deployed. The tactical complexity ceiling was low by design (this is Session 1 of a professional heist campaign; appropriate). The hard landing was the only moment where tactics were genuinely forced by dice. From my chair this campaign will need to raise the tactical ceiling in sessions 3-5 or the enforcer's kit will never be used.

**Score:** 69/80 — clean but not complex. The session did exactly what Session 1 should do and no more.
