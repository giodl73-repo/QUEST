# Lorekeeper Lens — C5-S1

Havenmere as a setting is doing its work. The Wharfside at 1 bell — the smell of salt and cedar, the harbour carter still working south, the specific sound Mira produced for the store-room door — these details establish a port city at night with precision. The Age of Might framing is ambient but present.

**What landed:** The guild mechanics are legible from this session alone. Markers. Ledger entries. The coding convention (C4-2 → location code). A reader arriving cold at C5-S1 can infer the Silken Ledger is a professional operation with its own internal language. The infrastructure is established through behavior, not exposition.

Bessa Tor's situation was also fully established: four years, she knows what's in the ledger, she has been waiting. We understand what she has been carrying and why she gives the key without conditions. That is good worldbuilding through character.

**Friction:** Orren Pale is named in Rue's message (accounts surveillance moved north) but we do not yet have a picture of him. He is the campaign's antagonist and this session is appropriately light — but the lorekeeper in me wanted one more detail.

**Score:** 70/80 — setting and guild infrastructure established cleanly. Pale's picture is appropriately deferred.
