# Roleplayer Lens — C5-S1

The pre-session maintenance parallel is remarkable for a first session. Darro naming his tools aloud while Tessaly opens the ledger — neither looking at the other, neither performing for an audience — established the party dynamic in three minutes without any narrative scaffolding. That is the cleanest party-introduction this workshop has produced.

**What landed:** Tessaly's "That is not the same as yes" to Bessa. She did not smooth it. She could have — the professional answer would have been reassuring and technically defensible. She chose the accurate answer instead. This is character-deep in a way most first sessions don't reach.

Cael's drawer refusal. Not a dramatic speech. Not a consultation. One sentence: "This is not the job." Then he waited for Tessaly's confirmation and stood down. The economy of that moment is exactly right for a professional who trusts his code.

**Friction:** Mira's role was lighter than her sheet suggests. She handled the Minor Illusion and the Night Market delivery but we did not see the Calloway-pattern (persona calibration, different-after-the-job). Session 1 positions her as competent professional; the pattern will need to emerge from S2 onward.

**Score:** 72/80 — strong character voice in the opening session, with room for Mira's pattern to develop.
