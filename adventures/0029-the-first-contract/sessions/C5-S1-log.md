# C5-S1 Session Log — The First Contract

**Date in world:** PC 188, late summer, Havenmere. Three hours past midnight.
**Party:** Tessaly Ink (Rogue/Mastermind 3), Darro Velt (Rogue/Thief 3), Mira Calloway (Bard/Whispers 3), Cael Ironback (Fighter/Battle Master 3)
**Seed:** C5S1-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session: The Morning Before

The party has not eaten together before. They have worked adjacent to each other — Tessaly handled the intelligence, Darro the access, Mira the introductions, Cael the exits — but never the same table at the same time. Tessaly's summons arrived by runner the previous afternoon: *Cordwainer Lane. Dusk. Come alone.* Each one came.

**Darro** arrived first. He sat at the table near the window without being directed there. He set his pick roll on the table beside him and began.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
*"Tension wrench."* He lifted the smallest piece, turned it once, pressed his thumb against the shoulder. *"Hook."* The second. He worked through all twelve in order, naming each one aloud in a voice that was not aimed at anyone. It took him about three minutes. Then he rolled them back into the leather sleeve and set it on his left hip, and that was the end of it. He did not explain. The roll went back onto his hip where it had been before, and he sat with the expression of someone who had just confirmed that everything was as it should be.

Tessaly watched without comment. She had arrived while he was on the fourth pick — the Diamond. She recognised what she was looking at. She set her satchel on the table and opened the ledger to a clean page.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
She wrote at the top: *0001 / PC 188 / the-guild / 0029 / first contract.* The entry number, the date, the party name she had assigned them, the job code. She wrote it before she said a word to either of them. The pen moved with the particular pressure she uses for new entries — a little heavier than she will use for marginal notations, as if the beginning of a record deserves a different weight.

Mira arrived with Cael. They had come from opposite directions and met at the bottom of the street. Neither had planned this. They had the matched expressions of people who had not spoken but had arrived at the same conclusion: the other one was not a problem. Mira checked her jacket before she came through the door — left hand to the inside pocket, the paper case, present.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael's left hand went to his chest at the threshold of the room. Not the inside of the coat — the outside, flat to the chain mail, brief pressure, then withdrawn. The contract. He checked it the way a craftsman checks the seam of a joint before weight is applied. Then he came in.

## Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane: The Brief

Tessaly spread the floor plan on the table. It was Mira's work — obtained last week during a legitimate delivery, walking the building as Heila Voss of Marten Factors on a routine assessment. Four floors, two approaches.

*"Fenwick Gale runs import through Anchor Row,"* Tessaly said. *"His operational ledger names three guild members as silent investors in a trade arrangement that does not appear in any public record. One of those members was arrested yesterday on unrelated charges. The Watch is reviewing his finances. If they pull Gale's books as part of an asset inquiry, the three names are in the open."*

She tapped the floor plan. *"Ledger is in the counting room, fourth location. Barred from inside. High window to the alley, fifteen feet up, eight inches wide. Bessa Tor sleeps in the store-room adjacent to the ground floor. She has a key."*

*"She knows what's in the ledger?"* Mira asked.

*"She has been Gale's clerk for four years. She is thorough."*

*"Then she's a liability."*

*"Or a resource."* Tessaly did not expand on this. She returned to the floor plan. *"Watch patrol on Anchor Row runs at one bell, three bell, and five bell. Our window is from one to three. Darro — the front lock is a standard ward, DC 14."*

Darro said: *"DC 10 for me."*

*"I know. The alley window is DC 14 Athletics to get through the frame — it's designed to keep people out, not in."*

*"Advantage with the tension hook to widen the gap."*

*"Also noted. Two approaches: window or Bessa. Window bypasses the bar problem from inside. Bessa solves the bar problem directly."*

*"She may not open the door,"* Cael said.

*"She may."* Tessaly closed the ledger. *"We will find out."*

---

## Scene 2 — The Approach

One bell was cold. Anchor Row at this hour had the flat silence of a working district gone to bed, the sounds of water and rope from the harbour two blocks over. A cart horse stamped somewhere to the south. The Watch patrol had passed four minutes ago.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira checked her jacket at the corner. Her right hand pressed briefly to the outside of her coat, felt the slim case, withdrew. She had done it without looking down. She would do it again at the door.

Tessaly positioned them: Cael at the alley mouth, watching both approaches. Mira and Tessaly to the front door. Darro to the alley.

Cael's Perception to watch the approach: **d20+3 → rolls=[15] mod=+3 total=18**. The street was clear. He noted the harbour carter two blocks south working a late delivery, visible but not close. He did not mention it aloud. He filed it as not-a-problem and held the position.

Darro in the alley: Stealth approaching the building. **d20+8 → rolls=[18] mod=+8 total=26**. He moved along the alley wall with the particular patience of someone who had trained himself not to hurry in narrow spaces. No sound from the windows above. The candle in the store-room window was still lit — a pale strip under the shutter.

He examined the alley window: fifteen feet up, eight inches of clearance in the frame. The window faced east. The moon was behind the cloud. Good.

At the front door, Tessaly's Investigation: **d20+7 → CRITICAL (27)**. She read the lock at a glance before Mira had finished positioning herself — the ward pattern was a variant she had seen on three other Anchor Row buildings, all from the same hardware supplier. She communicated this to Mira with two fingers: *standard, no secondary ward.* Mira nodded.

The store-room window still had its light.

*"She's awake,"* Mira said quietly.

*"She has been awake for the last six hours, probably,"* Tessaly said. *"The ledger has been there. She knows someone is going to come for it eventually."*

They waited. Two minutes. Then Mira raised one finger — the Minor Illusion she had prepared.

---

## Scene 3 — The Entry Problem

The soft tap Mira produced was not a knock — it was the specific sound of a window shutter tapping in light wind, the kind of incidental noise that a sleeping person ignores and a waiting person registers. She placed it at the store-room door.

The light in the window moved.

Darro, at the alley window, had already set his tension hook in the frame gap and was calculating whether four minutes was enough time to widen it before the store-room door committed either direction. Athletics with advantage: **rolls=[19, 13] (adv → 19) mod=+4 total=23**. The window frame gave two additional inches — enough.

But the store-room door opened first.

Bessa Tor came out with the key already in her hand. She was in a working woman's clothes, not nightclothes — she had not been trying to sleep. She was in her late thirties, grey in her hair, with the steady expression of someone who had rehearsed this moment many times and found it finally arriving.

She looked at Tessaly and Mira for a moment. Then she said: *"Gale's ledger. You're here for it."*

Not a question.

---

## Scene 4 — Bessa's Question

She led them to the counting room without being asked. The bar lifted from inside — she had the mechanism memorised. The ledger was on the third shelf, behind two ledgers that were not the one they needed. Bessa went directly to it.

She held it for a moment. Then she handed it to Tessaly without conditions. No negotiation. No demands. She had decided something two years ago and she was following through on it.

*"Are they safe now?"* she said. *"The three of them."* She did not say their names. She did not need to.

Tessaly took the ledger. *"The arrest was on unrelated charges. If the ledger is gone before the asset inquiry reaches Gale, yes."*

*"That is not the same as yes."*

*"No,"* Tessaly said. *"It is not."*

Bessa nodded. She accepted the partial answer the way a person accepts a weather forecast — it was the honest available information and she had asked for it knowing that. She went back toward the store-room door. She stopped once.

*"There's a locked drawer in Gale's office desk. He keeps a second set of notes there — not a ledger, just notes. He doesn't know I know about them."*

She went to her cot and did not come out again.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly wrote the drawer note in the ledger's margin before she moved toward Gale's office. A single line, dated, coded: *secondary notes / Gale desk / locked / source: Tor.* She capped the pen and put it back. Then she nodded at Cael, who had come in silently from the alley entrance after the bar was lifted.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael checked the desk drawer: DC 13 Thieves' Tools, applied to a standard ward. He did not have Darro's expertise, but the drawer was lower-grade than the front lock. He did not open it. He looked at Tessaly. *"This is not the job,"* he said.

*"No,"* she said. *"Leave it."*

He left it.

---

## Scene 5 — The Exit

Darro re-latched the alley window from inside using the tension hook in reverse — the same tool, different application. Landing had been imperfect: **d20+4 → rolls=[4] mod=+4 total=8** — he came through the frame at speed and hit the counting room floor too hard, a sound like a bag of coins dropped on stone. He froze. Above them: Gale's bedroom, two floors up.

Silence for thirty seconds. No movement from upstairs. Gale slept through it.

Darro wiped his knee and said nothing.

The store-room was exactly as found. Bessa's candle was still burning. They left by the front door, Tessaly and Mira first, Cael last with the door pulled shut behind him. The street was empty. The harbour carter had moved further south.

Tessaly made the final entry: *0029 / clean exit / 1:53 bell / marker: pending.*

At the Night Market, twenty minutes later: Mira at the edge of the stalls with Tessaly's sealed note. Deception to blend at this hour, DC 12: **d20+7 → rolls=[9] mod=+7 total=16**. She found Rue Ashen at a lamp-oil stall — a stout woman with the specific expression of someone who had been expecting a specific messenger and was not going to show it. Mira delivered the note with the easy confidence of a factor's clerk running a late errand.

Rue read it without opening it to the light. She reached into her coat. She produced a small folded square of paper and pressed it into Mira's palm.

*"For Ink,"* Rue said. *"Tell her the watch on Pale's accounts has moved north. She'll know what that means."*

Mira did not say she didn't know what it meant. She said, *"I'll tell her."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
At Cordwainer Lane: Tessaly updated the final entry. *Rue Ashen — marker issued / intel: Pale accounts surveillance north / source: Ashen / date: PC 188.* She wrote Pale's name last. The ledger entry was complete before she said anything to the party.

She closed the ledger. She put it in the satchel. She looked at the three of them.

*"Clean entry. Clean exit. Bessa's question: partial answer given honestly. Rue's marker: received. The job is recorded."*

She did not say well done. She said it was recorded.

That was enough.
