## Setting & Background

Four guild professionals steal a merchant's operational ledger from a locked counting room in Havenmere's harbour district before dawn, and learn the clerk who could have stopped them chose not to.

**Havenmere** — a mid-sized port on the Straits of Ergoth, Age of Might, PC 188. The harbour district is called the Wharfside: warehouses, counting houses, chandlers, a Watch station three blocks north. Fenwick Gale runs an import business from a building on Anchor Row — legitimate on the surface, a pass-through for three guild members' financing on the inside. His ledger names them.

**The counting house:** Ground floor is the public trading floor (closed at night). First floor is Gale's private office. Counting room is behind the office — barred from inside, with a high, narrow window to the alley. Gale's clerk Bessa Tor sleeps in a cot in the store-room adjacent to the trading floor. She has a key.

Tessaly has known about the ledger for six weeks. She identified the exposure three days ago when one of the named guild members was arrested on unrelated charges and the Watch began asking about his finances. If the Watch pulls Gale's books as part of an asset inquiry, three guild members are named as investors in a scheme that does not exist in the public record. The ledger has to go.

Tessaly issued markers to Darro, Mira, and Cael. They assembled at her counting-house office on Cordwainer Lane two days ago. This is the first time all four have worked a job together.

Bessa Tor has been Gale's clerk for four years. She knows exactly what the ledger contains. She has been waiting for someone to do something about it — not because she is loyal to the guild, but because the three guild members named in the ledger are also the three people who helped her brother disappear from a dangerous situation two years ago. She has not told Gale. She has not told the Watch. She has been waiting.

When she realizes what the party is here for, she gives them the ledger. She does not ask for anything. She asks one question: *"Are they safe now?"* Her arc-completion is this question, asked without conditions.

### Scene 1 — The Planning Room
Tessaly lays out what she knows at Cordwainer Lane. The party has the floor plan (obtained by Mira during a legitimate delivery to Gale's office last week). Darro has already cased the external locks. The window to the alley is possible — fifteen feet up, eight inches wide, not designed to open from outside. The counting room bar is the problem: it can only be lifted from inside.

**DM note:** This is the party formation scene. All four PCs have their first joint job. The session is designed to be clean — no unnecessary violence, minimal Watch exposure, professional problem-solving. Let the party plan.

### Scene 2 — The Approach
After midnight. The Wharfside is quieter but not empty. Three Watch patrols on rotation (Tessaly's intelligence: one passes Anchor Row at 1, 3, and 5 bells). A harbour carter is still working two blocks south. Gale's building is dark except for a candle in the store-room window — Bessa is awake.

**Pressure:** The Watch patrol window is 90 minutes. They have from 1 bell to 3 bell, then from 3 to 5. Missing a window puts them on the street when the patrol is there.

### Scene 3 — The Entry Problem
The locked counting room. Two approaches: (A) get someone in through the alley window (Darro, Second-Story Work, Athletics DC 14 to squeeze through the narrow frame — advantage if he uses a tension hook to widen the gap), or (B) get Bessa to open it. The problem with option B is waking Bessa without alerting Gale, who sleeps directly above.

**Bessa is already awake.** She heard them in the alley. She has been sitting with the key in her hand for ten minutes. She comes out when Mira's minor illusion puts a soft tap on the store-room door — not the party's plan, but it works.

### Scene 4 — Bessa's Question
Bessa opens the store-room door. She does not scream. She looks at the party with the expression of someone who has been waiting a long time for an answer. She says: *"Gale's ledger. You're here for it."* It is not a question.

She has the counting-room key. She gives it without conditions. Before she goes back to her cot, she asks: *"Are they safe now?"* — meaning the three named guild members, not by name but clearly understood. If anyone answers honestly ("Yes" or "I don't know"), she nods. She goes back to her cot. She does not ask for a marker. She does not ask for payment. She is already protecting them by sleeping through this.

**Arc-completion condition:** Bessa gives the key and asks her question. No roll required. It fires when the party demonstrates they are here for the ledger and not for Gale. She does not need to be persuaded. She has been persuaded for two years.

### Scene 5 — The Exit
The ledger retrieved. The window re-latched (Darro). The store-room exactly as found. They are on the street before 3 bells. Tessaly makes one notation in her ledger: the job number, the date, the disposition. Clean entry. Clean exit.

**Rue Ashen (fence contact):** Tessaly hands Mira a small sealed package — a secondary objective she did not mention in the briefing: a note for Rue Ashen at the Night Market, confirming they have the ledger. Rue is a fence and intelligence contact who has been waiting for word from Tessaly. If Mira delivers the note (Deception DC 12 to blend at the Night Market at this hour), Rue gives Tessaly a marker in return. Rue earns trust. The marker goes both ways.

**Marker condition:** Rue Ashen's marker is issued when the note is delivered. Tessaly adds the entry to her ledger. This is the first marker of the campaign.

She does not hold a guild marker; she holds the more valuable thing: foreknowledge and silence. Token fires when the party exits clean and Bessa's cot is undisturbed at dawn.

## Two paths to the ledger

- **Path A (window):** Alley → window → Counting Room (bypasses the bar from inside)

## Noise economy

- Front door forced: automatic alert to Gale (2 floors up, sleeping light)

## DM notes

The building is designed for a two-approach play — the party can split, with Darro going up the alley while Mira handles the front, or converge on Bessa first. Bessa is the intended resolution path; the window is the backup. Both work. The session does not require combat.

Gale's bedroom (05) is accessible but there is no narrative reason to go there. If the party enters, he is asleep with a light snore. He has a cash box under the bed (15 gp, not the objective). Waking him is the failure condition.

### Scene 1 — The Trading Floor

> Bolts of cloth stacked by grade, a glass case of spice samples, everything locked and covered for the night. The floor smells of salt and cedar. A single strip of candlelight shows under the store-room door to the left. Someone is awake in there.

#### Features

- **The front door.** DC 14 Thieves' Tools to open silently; Darro reduces to DC 10. Forced entry makes a scrape audible on the floor above (Gale's Perception DC 8).
- **The display case.** Locked (DC 12 Thieves' Tools). Contains samples only — no value to the job.
- **The store-room door.** Unlocked, opens inward. The candlelight is visible before the door is touched.
- **The stair.** Open access to the first floor. The third step creaks. Darro notes this on entry (passive Perception 14 — his is 14 exactly).

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The trading floor has no encounter. It is the threshold — the commitment point. Once they are inside, the Watch patrol on the street is no longer an immediate problem.

The candle under the store-room door is the first signal that Bessa is awake. A character who moves to the store-room door and listens (passive Perception 12) hears quiet movement — not the stillness of sleep.

### Memory Fragment (optional — if the party lingers)
> *The spice samples in the case are labeled in two hands: Gale's formal script, and a second, smaller one correcting his prices downward. The smaller hand has been there for four years.*

### Scene 2 — The Store-Room

> A cot against the far wall, a candle burning low on an upturned crate. The woman sitting on the cot is fully dressed. She has a key in her hand. She is not surprised to see you.

#### Features

- **Bessa Tor.** Fully dressed, awake, key in hand. She heard them in the alley. She has been sitting here for ten minutes. Full NPC file: `npcs/bessa-tor.md`.
- **The key.** Counting-room key. Heavy iron. It is already in her hand; she does not reach for it.
- **Stock inventory.** Crates of sample goods, an invoice ledger (not the target — this is the stock book). Investigation DC 11 finds a notation in Bessa's hand: amounts adjusted downward with a note, *"actual."*
- **The candle.** Nearly at its end. Bessa has been awake since midnight. She does not relight it.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

Bessa's arc-completion fires in this room. She is the resolution — not an obstacle. The party does not need to persuade, bluff, or threaten her. She already knows why they are here and has already decided.

If the party attempts a stealth approach (treating her as a sleeping occupant), she speaks first: *"I know what you're here for."* She is not confrontational. She is waiting.

The key to the counting room is hers to give. Gale gave it to her three years ago because she was the one who caught errors in the books. He trusts her with the counting room because he trusts her with his numbers. She has been very careful not to betray either trust separately.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The key was given on a Tuesday in winter, two years before the three names appeared in the ledger. Bessa had not yet understood what she was guarding. By the time she understood, it was too late to un-know it.*

### Scene 3 — Gale's Office

> A desk with three locked drawers, a wall of correspondence sorted by date. The room smells of cold pipe tobacco. One drawer is new — the lock is fresh, the wood unstained by years of use. Someone recently had reason to secure something they didn't used to lock.

#### Features

- **The fresh-locked drawer.** DC 13 Thieves' Tools (Darro: DC 9). Contains a private cash account — 40 gp in coin, a receipt for a Watch sergeant's fee (20 gp, dated six weeks ago). This is not the target, but it is evidence of what Gale has been buying.
- **The correspondence wall.** Investigation DC 13: three letters from a solicitor's office discussing "pending asset enquiry." The letters are two weeks old. The enquiry has started.
- **The door to the counting room.** Iron-barred from inside. Without Bessa's key, the only other approach is the alley window.
- **The stair up.** Gale's bedroom. Soft snoring audible. Passive Perception 12 confirms he is asleep.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The office is not required to complete the job — the ledger is in the counting room, and the path is through Bessa or the window. But a thorough party will find the fresh-locked drawer and the solicitor's letters, which confirms Tessaly's intelligence: the Watch inquiry is already open.

If Tessaly sees the solicitor's letters, she photographs them mentally (Tessaly's Investigation is +7). She does not take them. She records the date and the solicitor's name in her ledger at Scene 5. This is a documentary-witness beat.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The fresh lock was installed fourteen days ago. The locksmith who fitted it is the same one Tessaly uses. Havenmere is not a large city.*

### Scene 4 — The Counting Room

> A single shelf of bound ledgers, a high narrow window showing alley-dark, an iron bar leaning against the wall. The target book is obvious: it is the only one with a cloth strap rather than a clasp — the kind of binding that opens fast. Three names on the index tab, in Gale's hand.

#### Features

- **The ledger.** On the shelf, cloth-strapped, index tab visible. Three names written in Gale's formal script — the named guild members. The ledger is approximately 200 pages and would take an hour to copy; the objective is the entire physical book.
- **The high narrow window.** Latched from inside; can be relatched after exit. Athletics DC 12 to land quietly after a drop from outside (Darro advantage).
- **The iron bar.** Already leaning against the wall — Bessa removed it when she came to open the door. She anticipated this.
- **Secondary materials.** Two other ledgers on the shelf are Gale's regular stock accounts. Not the objective but Investigation DC 14 reveals one of them shows the same solicitor's name from the office below, as a fee recipient.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The counting room is where the job completes. There is nothing left to resolve here — the ledger is accessible, the bar is down, the window is available for secondary entry or confirmation.

If Darro entered through the window before Bessa was found, he is already in this room when the rest of the party comes through the door. His having noted and relatched the window is the clean-exit signal.

Tessaly does not open the ledger. She puts it in her satchel and notes the time. This is the first entry in the ledger of Campaign 5.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Bessa has corrected three entries in this ledger, all years ago, all for arithmetic errors. She did not correct the three names. They were not errors.*

### Scene 5 — Anchor Row (The Exit)

> The street is empty. The harbour smell comes off the water two blocks south — salt, fish, the low tide. Three bells will ring from the Watch tower in twenty minutes. The ledger is in Tessaly's satchel. She writes one line in her own ledger before she starts walking.

#### Features

- **The Watch window.** They have until 3 bells. If they are on the street before then, the patrol does not see them. If delayed past 3 bells, Stealth DC 13 to avoid the patrol (group check; failure means Cael has to be visible as a distraction while the others disperse).
- **The Night Market approach.** Two blocks east toward the harbour, a side market operates past midnight. This is where Rue Ashen works. Mira can deliver Tessaly's note here (Deception DC 12 to blend without Watch attention).
- **Tessaly's ledger notation.** She writes before she speaks. This is her first entry for Campaign 5. If the party watches, she writes the job number, the date, and the word "complete." Not the names. Never the names.
- **Rue Ashen.** Full NPC file: `npcs/rue-ashen.md`. She receives the note, confirms receipt, and issues a marker to Tessaly. She says: *"Tell her I've been waiting for this."* She means the job confirmation, but the phrasing has more weight than she intends.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

Scene 5 is the landing. The job is done. Let the party feel it land before any new information arrives. Tessaly's notation fires before she speaks. Darro relatches the window notch in his mental checklist. Mira walks as whoever the Night Market needs her to be.

The Rue Ashen marker is the first trust anchor of the campaign. It is clean — no ambiguity, no cost. It is what a successful first job feels like before everything complicates.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Bessa Tor remained at her cot until dawn. She did not check the counting room. She did not need to. Some things you know are done without needing to verify.*

## NPCs

# Bessa Tor

## Appearance

A woman in her mid-thirties. Practical clothes — she sleeps in what she would wear to work, because she is always available to Gale if something comes up at night. Brown hair going grey at the temples, a permanent slight frown that is concentration, not displeasure. She has ink on her left thumb. She is holding a key.

## Background

Bessa Tor has been Fenwick Gale's clerk for four years. She is meticulous with figures and careful with money that is not hers. She has caught three significant errors in Gale's ledgers and corrected them without mentioning it to him, because she knew he would be embarrassed and embarrassed employers become difficult.

Two years ago, three guild members helped her brother Fen leave Havenmere under circumstances that required new papers and a new name. She does not know the guild by its formal name. She knows three people who appeared at her door in the middle of the night and solved a problem she could not solve. Two of them are named in Gale's counting-room ledger. She found out six months ago.

## Stat Block

She is not a combatant. If threatened, she does not run — she goes very still. She will not betray the three named people regardless of the pressure applied. Her silence has been tested; it has held.

- **AC** 11 | **HP** 6 | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Str** 9, **Dex** 12, **Con** 10, **Int** 16, **Wis** 15, **Cha** 12
- **Skills:** History +5, Insight +4, Investigation +5
- **Passive Perception** 12

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Bessa's response |
|---|---|
| Enters store-room treating her as sleeping | She speaks first: "I know what you're here for." |
| Asks why she is awake | "I've been awake since midnight. I heard the alley." |
| Tries to bluff about their purpose | She gives them a long, patient look and says nothing. |
| Admits they are here for the ledger | She stands up and holds out the key. No conditions. |
| Asks why she is helping | "I'm not helping you. I'm helping them." — meaning the named three. |
| Threatens her | She does not move. "You can take the key or not. I'm not going to stop you either way." |
| Asks if she wants anything | "I want to know if they're safe." This is her question. |

#### Arc-Completion
**The moment:** She gives them the counting-room key. She asks: *"Are they safe now?"* She does not wait for reassurance — she is asking because she has a right to know and has not been able to ask anyone until this moment.
**The condition:** The party demonstrates they are here for the ledger specifically — not to harm Gale, not to threaten Bessa, not for any objective other than the removal of those three names from Gale's records.
## Private Knowledge

She knows the solicitor's letters in the office above mean the Watch inquiry is already open. She did not act on this because acting would require going to someone — the guild, the Watch, an anonymous letter — and each path risked the three people she is protecting. She has been waiting for someone with the right resources to move faster than the inquiry.

She did not know this moment was coming. She was simply ready when it arrived.

## Notes

Bessa is not a guild contact. She does not want to be. She holds no marker and does not want one. Her involvement ends tonight. She will continue to work for Gale because leaving would require explanation and explanation would require names. She will correct his arithmetic for however many more years he employs her.

She is the campaign's first demonstration that protection is already happening — that the guild's work has consequences that outlast the jobs.

# Rue Ashen

## Appearance

Early forties. She runs a perfume and candle stall at Havenmere's Night Market — a legitimate business that keeps odd hours because her clientele keeps odd hours. She wears her grey hair loose, which is practical at the Night Market and also means no one looks at her face. She has a marker coin in the left pocket of her apron at all times.

## Background

Rue has been Tessaly's fence contact for seven years. She moves information more than goods — she knows who is buying what intelligence in Havenmere, who is paying the Watch for what, and which solicitors are working both sides of an asset inquiry. She is not a guild member. She has a working arrangement with Tessaly that runs on mutual benefit and the explicit understanding that neither of them sells names.

She has been waiting for word about the Gale ledger for six weeks. She told Tessaly about the Watch inquiry before Tessaly told her. This is normal; Rue's information runs a week ahead.

## Arc-Completion

**Adventure:** 0031 (planned — she learns about the false identity operation)

### Watch Patrol

# Combat Encounter — Watch Patrol

## Trigger

This encounter fires only if the party is on the street between 3 bells and 5 bells AND fails a group Stealth DC 13 check. It is the failure-state encounter — the job going wrong at the exit. It should not feel like a routine fight.

## Composition

**Two Watch Guards** (CR 1/8 each — SRD Guard reskin)

- **AC** 16 (chain shirt + shield) | **HP** 11 (2d8+2) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Str** 13, **Dex** 12, **Con** 12, **Int** 10, **Wis** 11, **Cha** 10
- **Passive Perception** 12
- **Actions:** Spear +3, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60, 1d6+1 piercing. Shield Bash (bonus): DC 11 Str save or target is pushed 5 ft.
- **Morale:** Will call for reinforcements if one is downed. Will retreat to the station three blocks north if both are outmatched. They do not fight to the death for a routine street patrol.

**XP:** 50 each (100 total). Not a significant encounter — it is a cost.

## Tactics

The patrol calls out first: *"Hold there. What's your business?"* This is standard. They do not draw weapons unless the party runs or acts hostile. Cael is the most visibly armed; they address him first.

**Social resolution (preferred):** Mira's Persuasion or Deception (DC 14) can explain the party as late-night workers from one of the harbour concerns. On a failure, they ask for names — which triggers the "never sell a name" code for Cael and Tessaly. This is the designed friction.

**Physical resolution:** If combat is joined, the guards call out (loud — audible 200 ft.) at the end of round 1. Reinforcements arrive in 3 rounds (4 more guards, same stat block). The party has 3 rounds to disengage.

## Encounter notes

This encounter is designed to be rare (group Stealth DC 13 means the party will usually avoid it at this level) but meaningful when it fires. The interesting moment is the social beat — two guards asking for names, one professional code saying don't give them.

If the party avoids the encounter entirely: that is the correct outcome. A clean exit is better than a fight they should not have needed.

**Damage budget:** 2 guards, 4 rounds max = ~8d6+8 total threat (adjusted for Cael's AC 16 and counter-threat). Medium difficulty for this party. Designed to cost resources without threatening the job outcome.

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
