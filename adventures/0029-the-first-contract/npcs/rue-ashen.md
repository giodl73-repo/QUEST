# Rue Ashen

## Appearance

Early forties. She runs a perfume and candle stall at Havenmere's Night Market — a legitimate business that keeps odd hours because her clientele keeps odd hours. She wears her grey hair loose, which is practical at the Night Market and also means no one looks at her face. She has a marker coin in the left pocket of her apron at all times.

## Background

Rue has been Tessaly's fence contact for seven years. She moves information more than goods — she knows who is buying what intelligence in Havenmere, who is paying the Watch for what, and which solicitors are working both sides of an asset inquiry. She is not a guild member. She has a working arrangement with Tessaly that runs on mutual benefit and the explicit understanding that neither of them sells names.

She has been waiting for word about the Gale ledger for six weeks. She told Tessaly about the Watch inquiry before Tessaly told her. This is normal; Rue's information runs a week ahead.

## Arc-Completion

**Adventure:** 0031 (planned — she learns about the false identity operation)
**Adventure 0029 role:** Trust marker delivery. She receives the note confirming the ledger acquisition and issues a marker to Tessaly. She says: *"Tell her I've been waiting for this."*

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Rue's response |
|---|---|
| Delivers the note from Tessaly | Reads it, pockets it, produces a marker. "Tell her I've been waiting for this." |
| Asks what the note said | "It said what it needed to say." She will not repeat it. |
| Asks for information about the Watch inquiry | She knows the solicitor's name. She will tell Tessaly directly; she will tell the party if Tessaly is present and nods. |
| Approaches without the note | She is cordial. She is selling candles. She does not initiate conversation about guild business. |

## Notes for the DM

Rue appears only briefly in Session 1 — she is the clean close of a clean job. She will appear more substantially in later sessions when her intelligence network becomes relevant to the campaign's investigation arc.

Her marker to Tessaly is the campaign's first. The phrase "I've been waiting for this" will carry more weight in Session 4 when the audit begins and Tessaly realizes Rue has been watching Orren Pale too.
