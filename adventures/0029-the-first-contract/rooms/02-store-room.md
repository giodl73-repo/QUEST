# Room 02 — The Store-Room

## Read-aloud
> A cot against the far wall, a candle burning low on an upturned crate. The woman sitting on the cot is fully dressed. She has a key in her hand. She is not surprised to see you.

## Features

- **Bessa Tor.** Fully dressed, awake, key in hand. She heard them in the alley. She has been sitting here for ten minutes. Full NPC file: `npcs/bessa-tor.md`.
- **The key.** Counting-room key. Heavy iron. It is already in her hand; she does not reach for it.
- **Stock inventory.** Crates of sample goods, an invoice ledger (not the target — this is the stock book). Investigation DC 11 finds a notation in Bessa's hand: amounts adjusted downward with a note, *"actual."*
- **The candle.** Nearly at its end. Bessa has been awake since midnight. She does not relight it.

## GM Notes

Bessa's arc-completion fires in this room. She is the resolution — not an obstacle. The party does not need to persuade, bluff, or threaten her. She already knows why they are here and has already decided.

If the party attempts a stealth approach (treating her as a sleeping occupant), she speaks first: *"I know what you're here for."* She is not confrontational. She is waiting.

The key to the counting room is hers to give. Gale gave it to her three years ago because she was the one who caught errors in the books. He trusts her with the counting room because he trusts her with his numbers. She has been very careful not to betray either trust separately.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The key was given on a Tuesday in winter, two years before the three names appeared in the ledger. Bessa had not yet understood what she was guarding. By the time she understood, it was too late to un-know it.*
