# Operational Pressure Table

Roll d6 at the transition between each scene (at the natural break when the party moves or pauses). Stop rolling once the party is on the street in Scene 5.

## Pressure Table (d6)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Nothing.** The night holds. |
| 2 | **A cart on the street.** A harbour carter passes Anchor Row heading south — lantern, horse, no attention to the buildings. Stealth DC 8 to avoid notice (they are not looking). |
| 3 | **The candle flickers.** Bessa's candle in the store-room gutters. For one moment the strip of light under the door goes dark. Then it returns. It means nothing, but someone notices. |
| 4 | **A gull on the roof.** Something lands on Gale's building above. The sound carries — three slow footsteps, then silence. Passive Perception 10: it is a bird, not a person. |
| 5 | **The Watch moves early.** The patrol is 4 minutes ahead of Tessaly's intelligence this window. The party has less time than planned. Adjust the Scene 5 window: they must be on the street in 10 minutes instead of 20. |
| 6 | **Gale stirs.** Movement on the second floor — a creak, then quiet. He has not woken, but he shifted. The party has 2 minutes before his next potential waking check (Perception DC 9 if they make noise in the next 2 minutes). |

## Pacing Guidance

An efficient party should trigger 2–3 pressure rolls total across the session. Do not force rolls; use them at natural decision points (entry, Bessa encounter, exit window) to maintain awareness that time is moving.

**Do not trigger 5 and 6 in the same window** — stacking two escalations makes the session feel punitive rather than tense.
