# Combat Encounter — Watch Patrol

## Trigger

This encounter fires only if the party is on the street between 3 bells and 5 bells AND fails a group Stealth DC 13 check. It is the failure-state encounter — the job going wrong at the exit. It should not feel like a routine fight.

## Composition

**Two Watch Guards** (CR 1/8 each — SRD Guard reskin)

- **AC** 16 (chain shirt + shield) | **HP** 11 (2d8+2) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Str** 13, **Dex** 12, **Con** 12, **Int** 10, **Wis** 11, **Cha** 10
- **Passive Perception** 12
- **Actions:** Spear +3, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60, 1d6+1 piercing. Shield Bash (bonus): DC 11 Str save or target is pushed 5 ft.
- **Morale:** Will call for reinforcements if one is downed. Will retreat to the station three blocks north if both are outmatched. They do not fight to the death for a routine street patrol.

**XP:** 50 each (100 total). Not a significant encounter — it is a cost.

## Tactics

The patrol calls out first: *"Hold there. What's your business?"* This is standard. They do not draw weapons unless the party runs or acts hostile. Cael is the most visibly armed; they address him first.

**Social resolution (preferred):** Mira's Persuasion or Deception (DC 14) can explain the party as late-night workers from one of the harbour concerns. On a failure, they ask for names — which triggers the "never sell a name" code for Cael and Tessaly. This is the designed friction.

**Physical resolution:** If combat is joined, the guards call out (loud — audible 200 ft.) at the end of round 1. Reinforcements arrive in 3 rounds (4 more guards, same stat block). The party has 3 rounds to disengage.

## Encounter notes

This encounter is designed to be rare (group Stealth DC 13 means the party will usually avoid it at this level) but meaningful when it fires. The interesting moment is the social beat — two guards asking for names, one professional code saying don't give them.

If the party avoids the encounter entirely: that is the correct outcome. A clean exit is better than a fight they should not have needed.

**Damage budget:** 2 guards, 4 rounds max = ~8d6+8 total threat (adjusted for Cael's AC 16 and counter-threat). Medium difficulty for this party. Designed to cost resources without threatening the job outcome.
