# Room 04 — The Letter's Corner

## Read-aloud

> A stone shelf with a ceramic holder on it. Scorch marks on the shelf and the wall behind it. A bowl with old ash at the shelf's edge — the ash from the first burning attempt, which is still there. The letter is in the holder, slightly warm, unmarked.

*(50 words)*

## Features

- **The dispensation letter** (in ceramic holder). See `treasures/dispensation-letter.md`. This is the session's central object. Warm, indestructible, slightly smaller than a playing card.

- **Scorch marks** (shelf and wall behind). Thirty years of fire attempts. Some scorch marks are old and faded; some are more recent. The most recent is from approximately six months ago — Aldric tried again when he heard word that a new party had been hired to clear the building. He thought maybe it would work this time. It didn't.

- **Bowl of ash** (shelf edge). The ash from the first burning attempt — the one Velantha performed before the ritual. The letter was buried in this ash afterward. Aldric has kept the ash as evidence that the destruction was genuine and still failed. *Relic sign: indestructibility demonstration sign — the ash proves the attempt was real.*

- **Ceramic holder** (crack repaired twice, visible). Aldric broke the holder twice in frustration. The first time, he put the letter directly on the shelf and tried to pretend it wasn't there for six months. Then he made a new holder. Then he broke that one. The current holder is the third. The repair marks are visible.

## Encounter

**The indestructibility test** (if party examines the letter or tries to destroy it). Mechanics in `encounters/aldric-statement.md`. Aldric will have followed the party to R4 if they came here — he watches. He does not stop them. He says: *"I've tried everything."*

Resource cost: if a party member spends a spell slot to try to destroy the letter, the slot is spent and the letter is unchanged. If they use mundane fire: the fire doesn't catch, but they expend a torch.

## Treasure

The letter. In the holder. Party may take it after Aldric's arc-completion — he will not stop them. He is glad to be rid of it and unable to be rid of it simultaneously.

## Connections

- **West (nook):** R2 (Main Sacristy)

## GM Notes

The bowl of ash is the room's best detail. Velantha burned it — she was trying to destroy the evidence of the unauthorized act before she performed the act itself. The burning failed. The ash is evidence of the failure. Aldric has kept the ash because it proves the destruction was attempted in good faith. Both of them tried to close the record. The record would not close.
