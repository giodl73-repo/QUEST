# Room 02 — Main Sacristy

## Read-aloud

> Walls covered in handwriting — floor to ceiling, organized in columns, the same shorthand repeated in different inks at different ages. A gully dwarf at a writing table, quill down, watching you. In the far corner: a ceramic holder on a stone shelf. A piece of parchment inside it. He says: "I thought you'd come eventually."

*(58 words)*

## Features

- **Aldric Voss** (at writing table). See `npcs/aldric-voss.md`. He is not hostile. He has been waiting.

- **Handwritten copies** (all walls). Thirty years of Morren's account, copied in Red Robe shorthand, organized by date. The oldest copies (near the floor) are faded; the newest (at eye level) are fresh. **DC 12 History or Arcana** to recognize the notation as Red Robe field station shorthand. **DC 10 Investigation** to count approximate years of copies: thirty. *Relic sign: copying sign — the response to the indestructible letter is making more records.*

- **The ceramic holder and the letter** (corner shelf). Visible from the entrance. **DC 10 Perception** to notice the parchment inside is slightly warmer than the room's temperature. Aldric will not ask the party not to examine it. He will watch. He will say nothing about it until they touch it or ask.

- **Writing table** (Aldric's workspace). A partial copy in progress — mid-sentence, quill set down at the word "permission." The copy's subject: Morren's Stage 5 (the authorization notation). *He was copying the part about the mark when the party arrived.*

- **Archive arch** (south wall). Connects to R3 (Copy Archive). DC 10 Perception to notice it extends.

- **Corner nook** (east side). Connects to R4 (Letter's Corner — where the destruction attempts happened). Visible as a recess.

## Encounter

**Aldric's statement.** See `encounters/aldric-statement.md`. Social encounter, primary session content. Hint 3 delivered here.

**Maret's spotlight:** After Aldric's statement, he asks: *"What do you practice, and who gave you permission?"* He directs this at whoever in the party seems most like a practitioner — he has been watching them since they entered. He will aim it at Maret if she shows any sign of practice (has she performed her three breaths since entering? has she positioned herself carefully?). If not obvious, he asks the party generally.

**Renn's parallel (Option C candidate):** When Aldric describes giving Velantha the unauthorized working, Renn has the option to say: *"She used something that wasn't prayer. I know what that costs."* This is the designed entry-point. The DM should give Renn space to say this after Aldric delivers Hint 3. Whether Renn takes it or not: log either way.

**Letter indestructibility test:** When the party examines the letter or tries to destroy it. See `encounters/aldric-statement.md` for mechanics. Resource cost fires here.

## Treasure

**Velantha's dispensation letter.** In ceramic holder. See `treasures/dispensation-letter.md`.

**Aldric's copies (given by Aldric as part of arc-completion):** He selects one complete copy and hands it to the party. *"This is everything Morren told me. The Crypt has Velantha's version. They will not match exactly. She softened some things."*

## GM Notes

This is the session's only significant room. Everything else exists to give the party space to explore while receiving what Aldric has.

**Aldric mid-sentence at "permission."** This is not accidental. He has been copying the part about Velantha showing Morren the focus and saying "it belongs to someone who knows what it is" — which means he was copying the part where she authorized *him*, implicitly, by pointing at the mark. The word he stopped on: "permission." Let that land if the party notices.

**The letter is warm.** When Renn touches it: warm. The DM does not explain. Renn understands.

### Memory Fragment
*He started copying the first day. He thought he would finish and then stop. He has not finished. He has not stopped. There is always more to copy — every time he reaches the end, he understands something differently than the last time, and the new understanding requires a new copy. He has thirty years of understanding Velantha's choice differently.*
