# Room 03 — Copy Archive

## Read-aloud

> A side room where the copies are organized more formally — by date, by version, with annotations in the margins in a second hand. One congregation member is seated here, reading slowly. They look up when you enter, look back down.

*(40 words)*

## Features

- **Organized copies** (date-organized, annotated). Aldric's copies organized in sections. The second-hand marginal annotations: Morren's corrections, delivered over twenty years. Where Aldric got a detail wrong, Morren came here and wrote the correction. The archive is collaborative. *Relic sign: shorthand convergence sign — the same notation as the ritual focus's base mark, in the same handwriting throughout.*

- **Congregation reader** (one member, non-combatant). Can read Aldric's shorthand. Has been learning to read it for years. Is currently on Stage 4 of the account. If asked: *"He taught me to read it so the account would not disappear when he does."*

- **Connecting door** (south wall). Connects to R5 (Aldric's Rest). Loop completion.

- **The oldest copy** (in a corner, earliest date). Nearly illegible from age and handling. The margins are blank — he had not yet started receiving Morren's corrections. The first copy is the most certain-sounding and the least accurate. *Documentation-arc beat for Calla, if she is in this room: she is looking at the written record she cannot make of Morren's account. Aldric made a written record. It is thirty years old and less accurate than what she received in silence.*

## Encounter

None. Atmospheric. The congregation member reading is the session's only additional presence.

## Connections

- **North:** R2 (Main Sacristy)
- **South door:** R5 (Aldric's Rest)

## GM Notes

This room exists for Calla and for Renn. Calla sees the written record she cannot make — and sees that it is less accurate than what she received. Renn sees thirty years of someone making unauthorized records in a room no one sanctioned. Both characters have a beat here if the DM plays it.

### Memory Fragment
*He organized the copies after the fifth year, when he realized he was going to keep making them. Before that they were in a pile. The organizing helped. It made the project feel like what it was: a record, not a symptom.*
