# Room 05 — Aldric's Rest

## Read-aloud

> A small room. A mat on the floor — congregation-made, the same material as the nave's. One candle in a nail-holder, same as the nave's. A single book: the Orders' field manual for Red Robe station procedures, thirty years out of date. The mat is worn in the pattern of someone who sits here for long periods.

*(58 words)*

## Features

- **The Orders' field manual** (one book). The only Orders document in the building. Thirty years out of date. Aldric consults it occasionally — not for procedure but for the feeling of having consulted it. **DC 12 Investigation** to note: several pages have been annotated with question marks in Aldric's shorthand. The annotations are: *"Does this still apply?" next to every rule about non-humanoid recipients of High Sorcery workings.*

- **The congregation-made mat** (floor). He asked the congregation for a mat when the original one wore out. They made him one. It is the same material as the nave's. He has been sleeping on congregation-made equipment for twenty years.

- **The worn spot** (where Aldric sits). He faces the east wall (Crypt direction) when he sits. He has been looking at the Crypt direction for thirty years. He knows something is there. He has never gone to look.

## Encounter

None. Atmospheric room.

## Connections

- **North door:** R2 (Main Sacristy)
- **East door:** R3 (Copy Archive) — loop

## GM Notes

This room exists for Maret specifically. The Orders' field manual with its "Does this still apply?" annotations is Aldric's thirty-year version of Maret's question: what does practice require when the institution that sanctioned it no longer speaks to the situation? He has been asking this question in the margins for thirty years.

If Maret examines the manual, she sees the annotations. She does not comment. She puts it back.

### Memory Fragment
*He looked at the Crypt direction every day. He knew something was there — she had said "I left something in the crypt." He had not gone. It wasn't time. He was still copying. When it was time, he would know.*
