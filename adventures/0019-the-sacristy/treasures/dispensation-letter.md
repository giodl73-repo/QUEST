# Velantha's Dispensation Letter

*A piece of parchment in a ceramic holder that has been through fire thirty times and is unchanged — the letter Aldric wrote to let Velantha do the thing he knew was prohibited.*

## Appearance

Standard Red Robe field parchment, approximately one-quarter page in size. Written in Aldric's shorthand, in the formal authorization register of the Orders: *"The bearer, Sister Velantha of Mireth, is authorized to access and apply the working designated [specific notation] under the direct supervision of this station's field wizard. Aldric Voss, Red Robe, Mireth station."* It is dated. It bears Aldric's notation mark. It is forged — he had no authority to issue field authorizations unilaterally. He wrote it anyway.

The parchment is clean. Thirty years of attempted destruction have left no mark on it. Not a scorch, not a water stain, not a dissolution trace. It sits in a ceramic holder that has been cracked, repaired, and replaced twice. The letter is unchanged.

It is slightly warm.

## Presence / Desire

The letter does not want to be used as a weapon. It is not a weapon — it is a record. It was written with genuine belief (Aldric believed Velantha was right) and genuine cowardice (he never told the Orders; he let the false miracle stand). Both qualities are present in the parchment and the person who reads it with care can feel them: the certainty of the authorization and the smallness of the person who wrote it in secret.

In the hands of someone who has made an unauthorized act for what they believed was right, the letter is warm. In the hands of someone who has never done anything without permission, it is cool. It does not judge. It simply records what it is.

## Provenance

**1. Aldric Voss (wrote it ~30 years ago; has been trying to destroy it ever since).**
He wrote it in forty minutes. He knew it was wrong by the Orders' classification. He wrote it because Velantha needed authorization to get the working and she was going to do the right thing regardless. He thought he was helping. When she died, he understood what he had helped her do and could not decide whether it was something to be proud of. He has been deciding for thirty years.

**2. Velantha (used it once; tried to burn it before the ritual).**
She used it to obtain the working from the Orders' reference library (Aldric's field access, forged authorization). She burned it before she began the ritual — or thought she did. She told Aldric: "I tried to burn the paper." Aldric went to where she had burned it. The ash was there. The letter was in the ash, unchanged.

**3. Aldric Voss again (has it now; will have it until the party takes it or the campaign ends).**
He has the letter. He has tried to destroy it 30 times across 30 years. It will not be destroyed. He keeps it in the ceramic holder because it deserves containment if not destruction. He does not display it. He does not hide it. He has accepted it.

## Properties

### Rarity
Uncommon. **No attunement required.** The letter's properties are passive; they apply to any creature that handles it.

### Properties (passive)

- **Indestructibility.** The letter cannot be destroyed by any mundane or magical means available to a tier-1 party. Fire does not catch. Water does not dissolve it. Spells of the destruction category (Shatter, Fire Bolt, Disintegrate if available) do not affect it. A creature that spends a spell slot attempting to destroy the letter: the slot is spent; the letter is unchanged. **DC 10 Arcana** to understand why: this is not magical resistance — the letter's indestructibility is a consequence of its being permanently, legally, and cosmically true. The act it authorizes was performed and cannot be undone. The authorization that records it shares that permanence. It is part of the world now.

- **Recognition warmth.** Warm in the hands of anyone who has made an unauthorized decision for what they believed was the right reason: Aldric (30 years), Velantha (it was warm for her), Renn Ashkeld (his pact), anyone in the congregation. Not warm for someone who has never acted outside authorization. The warmth is not a reward; it is a recognition.

- **Evidence.** Any Red Robe, White Robe, or Black Robe examining the letter and succeeding at DC 10 Arcana identifies it as a forged field authorization from the Mireth station. They know immediately that Aldric Voss issued an unauthorized authorization. This is the evidence of his act. The party now carries it.

### What it cannot do

It cannot be attuned. It cannot be equipped as a weapon. It cannot be used as a spellcasting focus. It is a piece of paper that records something that happened and cannot be made not to have happened.

## Hooks

1. **The finale decision.** The party carries the letter to the keystone (0021). What they do with it — show it to Doran, present it as the foundation of the congregation's legitimacy, or keep it — is part of the choice. Route D: the letter is evidence that Velantha's act was unauthorized AND right. Both things are true. The letter holds both.

2. **Renn's parallel.** The letter is warm in Renn's hands. He made an unauthorized transaction for a reason he believed was right. The letter recognizes this without comment. He holds a piece of evidence that he is doing the same thing Aldric did — and that the doing of it means the record cannot be destroyed.

3. **The Orders.** If the party ever encounters a member of the Orders of High Sorcery — unlikely at level 3, but possible in the campaign's epilogue — the letter is a serious matter. An unauthorized field authorization is grounds for censure of the issuer's station. Aldric is dead and returned; the censure would be posthumous. The letter is still evidence.

## GM Notes

The letter's indestructibility is the session's mechanical payoff. Let the party try to burn it. Let them try a spell. Let Aldric watch with the patience of someone who has watched this thirty times. Let him say: *"I've tried everything."*

Do not rush this. The indestructibility demonstration is the session's resource cost and its most important relic sign. Let the party exhaust at least one option before accepting it. The moment they accept it, the letter's nature has been established: it is permanent. It records something that is permanent. The party now carries permanence.
