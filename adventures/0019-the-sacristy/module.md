## Setting & Background

**Place:** The sacristy of the Halted Spire — a small room adjacent to the chancel, accessible from the crossing (0018 R5) once the east passage is cleared. The room smells of old ink and cold stone. The walls are covered, floor to ceiling, with Aldric Voss's handwritten copies of Morren's oral account — thirty years of copies in Red Robe shorthand, organized by date, a living archive. In one corner: a stone shelf with a single object in a ceramic holder. The letter.

**Atmosphere:** Aldric is mid-sentence when the party enters — he was composing a new copy. He stops. He looks at them. He says: *"I thought you'd come eventually."* He has been composing his response to this visit for thirty years.

The party cleared the east passage from 0018's crossing (10 minutes of work) and entered. Aldric knows they met Morren — the Bell Tower congregation member who visited during S4 told Sharr, who told the congregation, and the word reached the Sacristy. He knows what they heard. He has been writing his version for thirty years.

**Renn Ashkeld.** He smelled old ink from this room in S4. When he enters and sees the walls of handwritten copies, he understands: Aldric has been doing what he does — making a record of something that matters, in a place where no one officially sanctioned it. The parallel is complete. Renn's pact / Velantha's working / Aldric's copying: three acts of the same category. He will have the option to say so.

**Aldric** is approximately 60 years old (died at 30; has been in the building for 30 years). He is present as an undead whose specific motivation has kept him here: he owes a debt, and he has not yet paid it. His debt: he let the false miracle narrative take root without correcting it. Velantha asked him to tell the Orders' man the mark was Aldric's — and someone from the Orders would eventually find the mark. That person is now here.

He is not hostile. He is not frightened. He has been composing his statement for thirty years and he is ready to give it.

His arc-completion fires when the party asks what he regrets — not about the act, but about the story. *"I regret that I let them call it a miracle. She would have hated that. She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine."*

Aldric's question: *"What do you practice, and who gave you permission?"*

This is the session's confrontation for Maret. Aldric is a Red Robe — a member of an institution that requires formal permission for every magical act. He gave Velantha an unauthorized tool and she died for it. He has been in this building for thirty years without institutional sanction, copying an oral account no one asked him to copy. He asks Maret his question not as an accusation but as a genuine inquiry: he wants to know if she has faced the same question he has been living with.

Maret's answer: her practice came from Osel. Osel had no institutional affiliation. Neither does Maret. She has been practicing for six years without knowing what her practice is for. She has never been asked to justify it to anyone. She answers honestly.

**Craft-witness third formal activation:** Aldric's copying practice and Maret's breath-work practice are structurally parallel — both are daily devotional acts performed without institutional sanction, both are responses to something witnessed (Aldric witnessed the ritual; Maret was given the practice by someone who never explained its purpose). When Maret answers his question, Aldric recognizes the parallel before she does. He says: *"Then you know what it costs."*

**The letter's indestructibility test:** The party will want to try to destroy the letter themselves (it's evidence; it might be dangerous). When they attempt to burn it (or use any destructive method):
- A torch placed against it: the flame simply doesn't catch. The parchment doesn't react.
- A spell: **DC 10 Arcana** to understand why a spell doesn't work on it (relic-level indestructibility; not magical resistance — something else). Attempting to use a spell slot to destroy it: the slot is spent, the letter is unchanged.
- Aldric watches all of this and says: *"I've tried everything. It was there when she burned it. It was still there after."*

This test: (a) costs HP or slots if the party is aggressive about it; (b) establishes the letter's nature; (c) is the session's resource cost.

# Velantha's Dispensation Letter

*A piece of parchment in a ceramic holder that has been through fire thirty times and is unchanged — the letter Aldric wrote to let Velantha do the thing he knew was prohibited.*

## Appearance

Standard Red Robe field parchment, approximately one-quarter page in size. Written in Aldric's shorthand, in the formal authorization register of the Orders: *"The bearer, Sister Velantha of Mireth, is authorized to access and apply the working designated [specific notation] under the direct supervision of this station's field wizard. Aldric Voss, Red Robe, Mireth station."* It is dated. It bears Aldric's notation mark. It is forged — he had no authority to issue field authorizations unilaterally. He wrote it anyway.

The parchment is clean. Thirty years of attempted destruction have left no mark on it. Not a scorch, not a water stain, not a dissolution trace. It sits in a ceramic holder that has been cracked, repaired, and replaced twice. The letter is unchanged.

It is slightly warm.

## Presence / Desire

The letter does not want to be used as a weapon. It is not a weapon — it is a record. It was written with genuine belief (Aldric believed Velantha was right) and genuine cowardice (he never told the Orders; he let the false miracle stand). Both qualities are present in the parchment and the person who reads it with care can feel them: the certainty of the authorization and the smallness of the person who wrote it in secret.

In the hands of someone who has made an unauthorized act for what they believed was right, the letter is warm. In the hands of someone who has never done anything without permission, it is cool. It does not judge. It simply records what it is.

## Provenance

**1. Aldric Voss (wrote it ~30 years ago; has been trying to destroy it ever since).**
He wrote it in forty minutes. He knew it was wrong by the Orders' classification. He wrote it because Velantha needed authorization to get the working and she was going to do the right thing regardless. He thought he was helping. When she died, he understood what he had helped her do and could not decide whether it was something to be proud of. He has been deciding for thirty years.

**2. Velantha (used it once; tried to burn it before the ritual).**
She used it to obtain the working from the Orders' reference library (Aldric's field access, forged authorization). She burned it before she began the ritual — or thought she did. She told Aldric: "I tried to burn the paper." Aldric went to where she had burned it. The ash was there. The letter was in the ash, unchanged.

**3. Aldric Voss again (has it now; will have it until the party takes it or the campaign ends).**
He has the letter. He has tried to destroy it 30 times across 30 years. It will not be destroyed. He keeps it in the ceramic holder because it deserves containment if not destruction. He does not display it. He does not hide it. He has accepted it.

## Properties

### Rarity
Uncommon. **No attunement required.** The letter's properties are passive; they apply to any creature that handles it.

### Properties (passive)

- **Indestructibility.** The letter cannot be destroyed by any mundane or magical means available to a tier-1 party. Fire does not catch. Water does not dissolve it. Spells of the destruction category (Shatter, Fire Bolt, Disintegrate if available) do not affect it. A creature that spends a spell slot attempting to destroy the letter: the slot is spent; the letter is unchanged. **DC 10 Arcana** to understand why: this is not magical resistance — the letter's indestructibility is a consequence of its being permanently, legally, and cosmically true. The act it authorizes was performed and cannot be undone. The authorization that records it shares that permanence. It is part of the world now.

- **Recognition warmth.** Warm in the hands of anyone who has made an unauthorized decision for what they believed was the right reason: Aldric (30 years), Velantha (it was warm for her), Renn Ashkeld (his pact), anyone in the congregation. Not warm for someone who has never acted outside authorization. The warmth is not a reward; it is a recognition.

- **Evidence.** Any Red Robe, White Robe, or Black Robe examining the letter and succeeding at DC 10 Arcana identifies it as a forged field authorization from the Mireth station. They know immediately that Aldric Voss issued an unauthorized authorization. This is the evidence of his act. The party now carries it.

### What it cannot do

It cannot be attuned. It cannot be equipped as a weapon. It cannot be used as a spellcasting focus. It is a piece of paper that records something that happened and cannot be made not to have happened.

## Hooks

1. **The finale decision.** The party carries the letter to the keystone (0021). What they do with it — show it to Doran, present it as the foundation of the congregation's legitimacy, or keep it — is part of the choice. Route D: the letter is evidence that Velantha's act was unauthorized AND right. Both things are true. The letter holds both.

2. **Renn's parallel.** The letter is warm in Renn's hands. He made an unauthorized transaction for a reason he believed was right. The letter recognizes this without comment. He holds a piece of evidence that he is doing the same thing Aldric did — and that the doing of it means the record cannot be destroyed.

3. **The Orders.** If the party ever encounters a member of the Orders of High Sorcery — unlikely at level 3, but possible in the campaign's epilogue — the letter is a serious matter. An unauthorized field authorization is grounds for censure of the issuer's station. Aldric is dead and returned; the censure would be posthumous. The letter is still evidence.

## GM Notes

The letter's indestructibility is the session's mechanical payoff. Let the party try to burn it. Let them try a spell. Let Aldric watch with the patience of someone who has watched this thirty times. Let him say: *"I've tried everything."*

Do not rush this. The indestructibility demonstration is the session's resource cost and its most important relic sign. Let the party exhaust at least one option before accepting it. The moment they accept it, the letter's nature has been established: it is permanent. It records something that is permanent. The party now carries permanence.

## Appearance

He looks like someone who has been sitting at a writing table for thirty years: the slight curve of his back, the ink-stained fingers, the way he holds his quill even when not using it. He is not a large presence. He is a precise one.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 8, AC 10. He does not fight; he is dead and returned specifically to be here, and fighting would accomplish nothing he needs accomplished.

## Public Knowledge

- He gave Velantha the prohibited working. He knew it was prohibited for non-humanoid use. He gave it anyway.
- The dispensation letter: he wrote it, he cannot destroy it, it will not burn.
- He has been copying Morren's account for twenty years (the congregation gave him the account; Morren corrects his copies when he gets something wrong).
- She left something in the Crypt. She told him: "If anyone comes far enough, give them the copy and tell them to go to the Crypt."
- He regrets something. It is not the act. It is the story.

## Private Knowledge

- He was in love with the idea of what Velantha was doing — not with her, but with the act. He had been a Red Robe field wizard who had done nothing interesting for five years. She came to him with an impossible request and a completely clear moral logic. He said yes before she finished the sentence. He has wondered ever since if he would have said yes for the wrong reasons (excitement) as much as the right ones (she was correct about the gully dwarves).
- He watched her die. He stayed to honor that.

## Information Readiness Condition

### Aldric Statement

# Encounter — Aldric's Statement

## Type

Social encounter (extended, sequential). Aldric gives the party his thirty-year account. Maret's spotlight. Renn's optional disclosure. Letter indestructibility test. Arc-completion on "what do you regret."

## Sequence

### 1. Opening

Aldric says: *"I thought you'd come eventually. Morren told me — eventually, through the building. Sit. I have a copy for you when we're done."*

He stands, comes to the table. He is not performing; he is reporting. He has been composing this statement for thirty years.

### 2. Hint 3 Delivery

*"I gave her the ritual. I knew the prohibition. It was classified for humanoid use only; gully dwarves were specifically excluded in the revision after the Cataclysm — someone decided they were not capable of consent. I did not agree with that classification. She did not ask me to agree; she asked me to give her the working. I gave it to her. I wrote the authorization letter. I knew it was forged under the Orders' rules. I gave it anyway because she was right about the gully dwarves and I was a coward about the Orders."*

Hint 3 delivered.

### 3. The Letter

He takes the party to R4, or they may have already gone there. He watches them examine the letter.

**If they try to destroy it:**
- Mundane fire: flame doesn't catch. *"I've tried everything."*
- Spell slot: slot spent, letter unchanged. *"I've tried everything."* Same line. Same tone.
- **DC 10 Arcana** to understand the indestructibility: the act it authorized was performed and cannot be undone; the authorization shares that permanence.

He does not say "I told you so." He is not satisfied by their failure to destroy it. He has simply accepted it.

### 4. Maret's Spotlight

After the letter test, Aldric looks at the party and specifically at Maret (the party member who carries a daily practice most visibly, even if she has not performed it in the sacristy).

*"What do you practice, and who gave you permission?"*

Maret answers honestly: Osel gave her the practice. Osel had no institutional affiliation. She has been practicing for six years without knowing what it is for.

Aldric listens. He says: *"Then you know what it costs."*

**Craft-witness third formal activation (Maret's third spotlight session):** Aldric's copying practice and Maret's breath-work are structurally parallel — both daily devotional acts performed without institutional sanction. He recognized the parallel in her before asking the question. The question was not rhetorical; it was the question he has been asking himself for thirty years. Maret answered it honestly. That is the activation.

### 5. Renn's Optional Disclosure

After Hint 3, when Aldric has described giving the unauthorized working: Renn has the option to say: *"She used something that wasn't prayer. I know what that costs."*

If he says it: Aldric looks at him with the complete attention of someone who understands what category of person makes that statement. He does not ask Renn to explain. He says: *"Yes."* One word. Then he continues.

If Renn does not say it: no negative consequence. The moment passes. Renn still holds the parallel. He may speak it in the Crypt (0020) or not at all.

### 6. Arc-Completion Gate

**The question that fires it:** "What do you regret?" — directed at the act OR the story. If the party asks about the act ("do you regret giving her the ritual?"), Aldric says: *"No. She was right."* Arc does not fire.

If the party asks about the story ("do you regret what happened after? how it was told?"), or if they ask generally "what do you regret?": **Arc fires.**

### 7. Arc-Completion

*"I regret that I let them call it a miracle. She would have hated that. She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine."*

He picks up the copy he selected. He holds it out. *"This is everything Morren told me. The Crypt has Velantha's version. They will not match exactly. She softened some things."*

He picks up his quill. He begins writing. *"I never found the right place to put that line. I hope you can."*
