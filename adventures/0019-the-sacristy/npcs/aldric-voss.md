# Aldric Voss, the Copyist

## Appearance

Male gully dwarf, 60 years (died at 30; has been here for 30 years). He looks like someone who has been sitting at a writing table for thirty years: the slight curve of his back, the ink-stained fingers, the way he holds his quill even when not using it. He is not a large presence. He is a precise one.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 8, AC 10. He does not fight; he is dead and returned specifically to be here, and fighting would accomplish nothing he needs accomplished.

## Public Knowledge

- He gave Velantha the prohibited working. He knew it was prohibited for non-humanoid use. He gave it anyway.
- The dispensation letter: he wrote it, he cannot destroy it, it will not burn.
- He has been copying Morren's account for twenty years (the congregation gave him the account; Morren corrects his copies when he gets something wrong).
- She left something in the Crypt. She told him: "If anyone comes far enough, give them the copy and tell them to go to the Crypt."
- He regrets something. It is not the act. It is the story.

## Private Knowledge

- He was in love with the idea of what Velantha was doing — not with her, but with the act. He had been a Red Robe field wizard who had done nothing interesting for five years. She came to him with an impossible request and a completely clear moral logic. He said yes before she finished the sentence. He has wondered ever since if he would have said yes for the wrong reasons (excitement) as much as the right ones (she was correct about the gully dwarves).
- He watched her die. He stayed to honor that.

## Information Readiness Condition

Aldric will give the full account when asked. He does not need to be persuaded; he has been waiting. The sequence:
1. **Hint 3:** *"I gave her the ritual. I knew the prohibition. I gave it to her anyway because she was right about the gully dwarves and I was a coward about the Orders."*
2. **Seeds:** The letter cannot be destroyed; the copies; Velantha left something in the Crypt.
3. **Arc-completion:** When the party asks what he regrets (not about the act — about the story).

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** The party asks what he regrets — specifically about the story, not the act.

**The act:** *"I regret that I let them call it a miracle. She would have hated that. She never asked for a cathedral. She asked for medicine."*

He says this once. He looks at his current copy on the table. He adds: *"I have been trying to write that into the record for thirty years. I cannot find the right place to put it."* He gives them the copy. He picks up his quill. He begins writing again.

**Why this matters:** Aldric's arc-completion is not about the unauthorized act — that arc-beat was completed when he said yes to Velantha. His arc is about the story told afterward: the false miracle, the cathedral, the congregation's sincere faith built on incomplete information. He let it happen. He could have corrected it. He didn't. The line he says to the party — *"she never asked for a cathedral, she asked for medicine"* — is the line he has been trying to put into his copies for thirty years, in the right place, and has never found. When he says it to the party, he has found the right place: it goes to the people who will carry the account forward.
