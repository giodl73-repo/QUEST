# Brek Aldren

**Role:** Unit scout. Dorn's unit bond (one of Carra, Fest, Oln) — specifically, Brek is in the same file as Dorn's named soldiers, close enough that Dorn has been unconsciously tracking his position. Brek is 22. This is his second tour.

## Stat block

Scout (MM p.349): AC 13, HP 16, Speed 35 ft. Multiattack (2). Shortsword +4, 1d6+2. Longbow +4, 1d8+2. Keen Hearing/Sight (advantage on Perception). Camouflage (Stealth advantage in natural terrain).

## Emotional position

Brek was captured because he followed orders — the advance route took him east of the ford and directly into the unmarked guards. He didn't understand why they were there. He still doesn't. He heard one thing: "The commander knows this route is flagged. She won't look east."

He does not know what this means. He knows it was said about Varet.

## Arc-completion conditions

Brek's arc fires when he tells Varet directly what he heard. The firing condition is simple: Varet asks him. He will not volunteer it — he is 22 and scared and she is his commanding officer. But if she asks: he tells.

His arc-completion line: *"They said you wouldn't look east, Commander. That you knew the route was flagged. I don't think they meant it as a compliment."*

## What he knows

The phrase "the commander knows this route is flagged." Spoken between the two guards. Not the entire plan. Just that phrase. It implicates Varet in a way that isn't true — and that is important. Someone is using Varet's name to indicate the patrol is managed. She is not managing it. She doesn't know.

**H2 delivery mechanism:** If Brek tells Varet, she writes: "Command apparently believes I know the route is flagged. I do not know what 'flagged' means in this context. Discrepancy: orders came through Veth; Veth would not have told this to guards; the information came from above Veth."

## Voice

Young. Tries to sound steady. Fails slightly. Uses rank consistently — "Commander," "Sergeant" — even under stress. It is the only structure he has right now.
