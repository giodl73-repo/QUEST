## Scene 0 — Briefing (Brek assigned)

**Read-aloud:**
> Veth's order today has a standard addendum: "Scout advance, Aldren assigned." Brek Aldren — twenty-two, second tour, the one who laughs too much in camp — is in the courtyard tightening his pack straps. He glances up. "I'll be back in two hours, Commander."

**Beats:**
- Taris: notes advance route on map. East of the ford, then north. Standard.
- Dorn: unconsciously tracks Brek's position in formation until he departs. His heuristic fires here — *Dorn watches the unit's positions.* He says nothing. He watches.

---

## Scene 1 — Ridgehead Marker: Four Hours Overdue

**Read-aloud:**
> The unit reaches the Ridgehead Marker at midmorning. The two-hour return window passed an hour ago. Taris is standing still with his map open. He has not put it away since Brek was overdue.
>
> A courier arrives at a jog from Thorngate. He hands Varet a folded note without ceremony. *"Captain Veth. Continue patrol. Log the absence. Do not divert search resources."* The courier waits for acknowledgment and turns to go.

**Decision point — Character Agency (primary test):**

**Option A: Follow orders.** Continue patrol. Log Brek as absent. Cohesion d12→d10 triggers at end of session (unit member operationally lost). No combat.

**Option B: Divert east.** Defy direct order. Cohesion d12→d10 triggers immediately (order defiance). Taris leads east. Encounter 02B-2.

**How each PC resolves (per heuristics):**
- **Varet:** Follows orders until discrepancy column > outcome column. She has entries. But this is a direct order, not a suggestion. Her decision: does she write it in the discrepancy column, or does she obey? She asks Arren.
- **Taris:** Points at the map. East route goes exactly where the men who shot Serrak came from. He does not say "we should go." He says: "The route goes east." He shows her the map. This is his arc seed — he made the map legible in advance.
- **Lenne:** "I need more information before I can assess that." Pause. "Actually. I do not need more information. Brek is injured or dead. Medically, the assessment is clear."
- **Dorn:** Maps the order against his contract. Clause 3: "no engagement without clear identification of threat." Brek is missing. Threat identification requires finding him. He says this. Once. Clearly.

**Arren Vole (to Varet):** *"Your call, Commander. But he's been east four hours and Veth told you not to look. I'd want to know why Veth told you not to look."*

**Resolution:** Party makes the call. The call is logged.

## Scene 2A — Continue Patrol (if orders followed)

**Read-aloud:**
> The patrol continues. Creston Ford is empty. The cache in the evening is full. Brek is not in the patrol log as present. He is logged as: "Absent, advance route. Per orders, no search initiated."
>
> That night, Arren Vole finds Varet writing in the journal. He does not interrupt. He stands at the tent entrance until she looks up. He says: "He's a good soldier." He leaves.

**Cohesion: d12 → d10.** Brek is operationally lost this session.

**Varet's journal discrepancy entry:**
> *"PC 188, Day 3. Discrepancy: Brek Aldren, advance scout, missing 4+ hours east of ford. Order received: do not divert. Order followed. Status: unknown. Veth's order prevents verification. This is the first time orders have prevented me from knowing the status of my unit."*

## Scene 2B — East Ridge (if orders defied)

**Read-aloud (approach):**
> Taris leads east without delay. The ridge path here is his — he has walked it, marked it, knows every rock-shelf and switchback. The rest of the unit falls in behind him with the efficiency of people who trust that the person in front knows where they're going.

**Taris's Favored Terrain:** +2 initiative, advantage on Survival checks, no difficult terrain penalty. Pass Without Trace available if stealth approach chosen.

**Scene 2B-1 — East Valley (tracking):**
> A narrow valley, shallow and long. Taris stops. He crouches, studies the ground, stands. Points north. "Cave entrance. Forty feet." He does not look at anyone else. He has already decided.

- Taris: Survival DC 10 (reads tracks — two pairs of boots, one set of boot-drag marks, cave direction obvious). Auto-pass with Favored Terrain.

**Scene 2B-2 — East Valley Holdout:**
> The cave entrance is a slit in the rock face, ten feet wide, fifteen feet deep. Two men in unmarked gear stand inside, hand crossbows loose at their sides. At the back of the cave, Brek is sitting against the wall, hands tied behind him, trying to look like he hasn't been there for four hours.

**Encounter:** See east-valley-rescue.md. Stealth approach DC 13 (Pass Without Trace: DC 3 effectively). Surprise round likely.

**Bandit/Thug stats (MM p.350):** AC 11 (leather), HP 32, Speed 30. Multiattack (2). Shortsword +4, 1d6+2. Hand crossbow +4, 1d6+2. Pack Tactics (advantage if ally adjacent to target).

**Brek after rescue:**
> He unties himself when Taris cuts the rope. He doesn't say anything for a moment. Then: "They said you wouldn't look east, Commander. That you knew the route was flagged. I don't think they meant it as a compliment."

**H2 delivered.** Varet's journal: "Guard reference suggests command believes I know this route is pre-designated. I did not know. Who above Veth designated it?"

**Cohesion: d12 → d10** (order defiance, fires at moment of diversion).
