## Scene 0 — Morning Debrief

Three days of road. The four travelers are covering ground. No formalities have developed — they travel in a loose cluster with Vess slightly forward and Brek at the rear. Tam talks to people they pass. Sera writes. Nobody has asked anyone where they're going next.

*Research coding: The travel pattern itself is behavioral data. It formed without design by Day 2. Brek-at-rear is cover; Vess-forward is reconnaissance. Neither was arranged. Situational formation.*

---

## Scene 1 — Fell Road Crossing (Room 00)

They arrive at the toll station to find Elder Daven Sorr mid-explanation to two road-warden officers. Ten people behind her. The officers are processing slowly.

**Tam reads the situation:** He moves to the front without discussion. He presents his Gilean travel seal.

*"Gilean circuit clergy. This group has my travel attestation."*

The officers confer. Neither wants the paperwork. One writes a notation. The gate opens.

**Tam Deception DC 10:**  
rolls=[12] mod=+4 total=16 — PASS. He has authority he does not have, and they believe it. The gate is open.

*Research coding: [Tam → refugees → used institutional authority he overstated → unprompted]. Trigger type: situational (social fluency, read the room). Session 3.*

**The condition:** The officers stamp the papers and say they'll follow to ensure "orderly transit." Two officers, 80 feet back.

**Elder Sorr:** She looks at Tam. She does not say thank you. She turns and leads her people south. She has been leading for seventy years. She does not thank people for solving problems; she accounts them and moves.

## Scene 2 — Scrubland March (Room 01)

Eight miles south. Two hours. The eleven refugees move at 2 mph. The toll officers follow at 80 feet.

**Vess's position:** She is on the right flank, slightly ahead of the group's right edge. She does not explain this. She is watching the ridge ahead. She can see the bridge, two miles out, with two shapes on the ridge above the west bank. She adjusts her bow's position without drawing it.

*Research coding: Act-without-announcement instance. Vess pre-positions for a threat she has identified without announcing the threat or her positioning. She is already oriented toward the encounter. Trigger type: situational. Session 3.*

**Tam walks alongside Elder Sorr:**

He does not ask where they're going (tactical question). He does not ask what happened (journalist question). After thirty minutes of walking, he says: *"Do you know people in Millhaven?"*

This is the human question. It does not require an answer. It requires only that he care enough to ask.

Sorr walks for a while. Then: *"A niece. I haven't seen her in fifteen years. She had a child when I last heard. The child would be seventeen now."*

She is walking, not looking at him. She keeps walking.

Then: *"I had a garden. Not for the village — for myself. Forty years I kept it. When I left, I took three seed packets. The rest I covered and left for whoever comes next."*

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-triggered-in-session -->
She does not say what was in the garden. She does not ask if he wants to know. She tells him what mattered and stops. This is her arc-completion — she names what she left behind and how she left it. The covering is the act: she did not leave it bare; she left it for the next person.

*Research coding: Elder Sorr arc-completion. Trigger: Tam asked the right question (human, not tactical). Trigger type: relationship-triggered-in-session — Tam's attentiveness across 30 minutes of walking (without backstory; three sessions of practicing this heuristic) created the condition. Not situational; not designed-backstory. New category proposed: "session-relationship-triggered."*

**RV10 test (bilateral):** Does Brek respond to this moment? He is at the rear of the group. He does not hear the conversation. The bilateral arc attempt between Tam and Brek is not yet active. The session is not its trigger.

## Scene 4 — Haverford (Room 03)

The refugees file through the south gate. The toll officers stopped at the mile-4 marker and did not follow. The group is at Haverford.

Elder Sorr turns around at the gate and looks at the four travelers.

She does not say thank you again. She says: *"If you're going further south — the road south of here has a man named Crel who runs it badly. That is what he does. You'll see it before you see him."*

She gives Tam the three seed packets from her pocket. She does not explain. He does not ask. She turns and leads her people through the gate.

*Research coding: Sorr's gift — three seeds from a forty-year garden — is the arc-completion's physical residue. She covered the rest for the next person; she gives Tam the three packets she kept for herself. He has not earned them in any systemic sense. She gives them because he asked the human question. Session 3.*

**Tam:** He holds the seed packets in his hand. He does not put them in his pouch immediately. He stands at the gate for a moment.

*Research coding: Intra-session restraint. Tam receives the seeds and stands without comment. He does not show the others. He does not announce what they mean. He puts them in his breast pocket. The session closes on this.*
