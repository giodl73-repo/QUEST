## Setting & Background

A group of eleven refugees needs escort through eight miles of hostile ground; the four travelers can only fully protect them if all four commit — and one of them would rather not.

**The Fell Road crossing, Mile 44** (workshop-canon, south Abanasinia). A stretch of road that cuts through scrubland between two river crossings. It is currently controlled by a local authority (the Stonehaven road-toll operation) that has been stopping and searching travelers — refugees in particular. The refugees are eleven people: six adults, three children, two elderly. They left their village (Davan's Reach, 40 miles east) after the local labor levy became untenable. They are trying to reach family in Millhaven (south). They have legitimate travel papers. The papers will be questioned anyway.

The refugees are at the Fell Road approach when the four travelers arrive. Elder Daven Sorr — a woman in her seventies — is explaining to the toll officers why their papers are valid. It is not going well. The toll officers are not acting illegally; they are acting in bad faith. The situation will end in detention or a bribe neither party has money for.

1. **Encounter at the crossing** — the toll situation. Four travelers arrive mid-confrontation. The refugees are being processed. The toll officers are doing what their authority allows them to do, just barely.
2. **Tam's intervention** — Tam reads the situation immediately. His heuristic: social fluency, reading the gap between what someone says and what they mean. He does not ask the other three if he can intervene. He intervenes. He produces his Gilean travel seal (which carries institutional weight for road-circuit clergy). He does not claim authority he doesn't have — he creates social ambiguity about whether denying him is worth the paperwork.
3. **The escort commitment** — the four must accompany the refugees through eight miles to the next waypoint. The toll officers will not detain them, but two officers follow. If the escort breaks formation, the officers will close in. All four must stay with the group.
4. **The eight miles** — movement under pressure. One hostile encounter: two road agents (unaffiliated with the toll operation) target the group at the Fell Road bridge. The party defends. Brek anchors the bridge.
5. **The crossing** — the refugees reach the waypoint town of Haverford. The toll officers stop following. The crossing is complete.

**Elder Daven Sorr** — arc-completion NPC. Fires when Tam asks her where they're going and why they left — without needing to know. See npcs/elder-daven-sorr.md.

No seeds. Last advisory session. RV10 test: does bilateral arc-completion attempt between Tam and Brek? They have now been through S1 (cooperative labor), S2 (Brek's act), and S3 (shared escort commitment). If bilateral is going to fire early, this is the earliest opportunity.

S3 spotlight: Tam. Reciprocal Acts expected this session: Tam → refugees (intervention), Brek → group (bridge anchor), Vess → refugees (route scouting), Sera → Tam (covers documentation so Tam can talk without being observed). Log all instances with trigger types. Last advisory session before binding begins at S4.

## NPCs

# Elder Daven Sorr

**Role:** Refugee elder. Arc-completion NPC. Names where they're going and why they left — when Tam asks without needing to know.

## Appearance

Seventies, small, with the upright posture of someone who has been the person others wait for her entire life. She wears practical traveling clothes. She does not complain about the road. She does not move fast, but she does not stop.

## Emotional position

Daven Sorr has been the elder of Davan's Reach for thirty years. The village is mostly gone now — the labor levy took the young people, the crop failures took the margin, and she was the last one left who remembered how it was before. She is not bitter. She is tired of explaining herself to people who will decide what they will decide regardless of her explanation. She expected the toll officers to be difficult. She expected to be delayed. She did not expect the four travelers.

She does not ask for the escort. She does not thank them for it. She assumes they have reasons and that the reasons are their own. She is walking. That is sufficient.

## Arc-completion

**Trigger:** Tam asks her something that shows he does not need to know. Not "where are you going?" (tactical). Something like: *"Do you know people in Millhaven?"* or *"Has anyone there been waiting for you?"* The key signal: he is asking because he is curious about her, not because he needs the information.

**Tam's heuristic:** He pays attention to what people don't say. He will notice that Sorr has not explained herself to the four travelers despite having explained herself to everyone else on this road. He does not ask the tactical question. He asks the human one.

**Arc-completion:** She tells him why they left. Not the tactical version (labor levy, crop failure) — she says: *"I had a garden. Not for the village — for myself. Forty years I kept it. When I left, I took three seed packets. The rest I covered and left for whoever comes next."* She does not describe what was in the garden. She names the act: she covered it for the next person. That is why she left, and how.

**RV12 test:** The arc fires because Tam asked the right question, not because he has a backstory connection to her. He has no designed stakes in her story. This is pure situational investment firing NPC arc-completion. Positive RV12 evidence if it fires before session 6.

## What she knows

- Millhaven is 60 miles further south. She has family there — a niece who she has not seen in fifteen years.
- The labor levy was organized by the Stonehaven authority (Overseer Crel). She knows his name. She does not say it unless asked directly.
- She has heard of trouble on the road south of here but does not know details.

## Voice

Measured. She does not raise her voice. She answers questions with exactly as much as she thinks you can use. If you ask more than you need: *"You'll learn it when you need it."*

## Research coding

Arc-completion trigger type: relationship-driven (triggered by Tam's attentiveness) but not backstory-driven. Code as "relationship-triggered-in-session" — a new category if this fires. It is neither "situational" (no external event triggers it) nor "designed relationship" (no backstory connection). The session-to-session relationship established the conditions.

# Road-Warden Officer (×2)

**Role:** Toll station officers. Antagonist NPC without malice — they are doing their job selectively.

## Stat block (if needed)

Scout (MM p.349): AC 13, HP 16, Speed 30 ft. Multiattack (2). Shortsword +4, 1d6+2. Longbow +4, 1d8+2. Skills: Nature +4, Perception +6, Stealth +6, Survival +4.

## Emotional position

They have a quota. Refugees with borderline papers are the easiest way to meet it — slow down the processing, find documentation gaps, accept transit fees in lieu of full detention. They are not cruel. They are indifferent. The distinction is real but does not help the refugees.

They do not want to fight anyone. They do not want to document an incident with a Gilean cleric. They do not want to explain to their captain why they engaged a half-orc in a road altercation.

## Behavior

- Slow-processing the papers when the travelers arrive.
- Back down immediately if Tam produces the Gilean seal and makes the social check.
- Follow the group for 4 miles as a power demonstration. Stop at the mile-4 marker. Write in the ledger. Return north.
- If combat is initiated against them by PCs: this is a serious mistake. They have colleagues and they file reports.

## Voice

Bureaucratic. *"We'll need verification of purpose of travel."* They are not performing; this is how they process people.

## DM note

These officers have no connection to the road agents at the bridge. The road agents are unaffiliated criminals who have spotted a slow refugee group. The toll officers would not cooperate with road agents; they would arrest them if they knew.

### Fell Road Bridge Combat

# Fell Road Bridge Combat

## Setup

2 Road Agents (Bandit stat blocks, MM p.343) vs. 4 L3 PCs.

## CR / XP Budget

- 2 × Bandit (CR 1/8, 25 XP each) = 50 XP
- Adjusted for terrain advantage (high ground first round, bridge chokepoint): ×1.15 = ~58 XP
- **Total:** Far below easy threshold (525 XP for L3 party)
- **Assessment:** Deliberately light. This encounter tests PC heuristics in a tactical context, not HP attrition. The question is not whether they win but how each PC responds.

## Bandit stat blocks

**Road Agent (×2):** AC 12, HP 11, Speed 30 ft. Scimitar +3 1d6+1 slashing. Proficiency: +2. Passive Perception 11. No special abilities.

## Tactical behavior

The agents expected a slow group of civilians with no escort. They did not expect Brek. When Brek Rages: one agent will attempt to withdraw (Disengage + move south to the ridge). The other fights briefly, then runs if the first falls.

## Key decision points

1. **Brek Frenzy?** If he uses it: he drops both agents fast but takes 1 exhaustion. If he doesn't: 2-3 rounds of normal combat. Either is valid.
2. **Vess engagement:** She is pre-positioned. Her Sneak Attack fires if Brek is adjacent to an agent. Does she advance, or does she maintain her watch-position?
3. **Sera's defensive magic:** Does she cast Mage Armor on someone? Shield as a reaction for an ally? Her arc as a protective wizard is the RV9 secondary test.

## Resolution note

Do not stretch this combat. Two rounds maximum of meaningful decision-making; if the agents attempt to flee, let them go (or let Vess's Shortbow handle a fleeing agent if she chooses). The session is about the refugees, not the combat.

### Toll Station Social

# Toll Station Social Encounter

## Setup

2 Road-Warden officers (Scout stat blocks) vs. 11 refugees + 4 travelers. Social encounter — no combat.

## Mechanics

- **Tam's Gilean seal:** Institutional authority implies he can attest to the group's purpose. He cannot, technically. But the officers don't know the exact limits of what a Gilean circuit-cleric can attest to.
  - Deception DC 10 (Tam +4): implies authority he does not have.
  - OR Persuasion DC 12 (Tam +4): makes it more trouble than it's worth to detain a Gilean cleric's party.
  - Either succeeds on 6+ (Deception) or 8+ (Persuasion).
- **Failure consequence:** Transit fee of 2 gp per traveler. 11 refugees × 2 gp = 22 gp. The four PCs could cover this. Combined party resources allow it.

## Road-warden behavior

They are doing their job in bad faith. They know the papers are valid. They are making it slow and expensive because that is what they can do. They will not fight a Gilean cleric. They will not fight a half-orc who is visibly armed and not alarmed. They will back down when backing down is the easier path.

## Research coding

If the four PCs pay the transit fee collectively: log as group Reciprocal Act. [Group → refugees → transit fee paid → unprompted collective action]. Trigger type: situational.

If Tam alone intervenes and the others watch: the others watching without intervening is also data — operational deference to the PC best suited for the task, not disengagement.
