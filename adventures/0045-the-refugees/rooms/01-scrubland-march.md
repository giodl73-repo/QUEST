# Room 01 — Scrubland March

## Read-aloud

> Four miles of scrubland. Low brush, dry grass, the occasional limestone outcrop. The toll officers follow at a distance. The refugees move in a cluster — the children in the middle, the able-bodied adults on the outside. Elder Sorr walks at the front without complaint. She does not ask for help. She does not slow the group's pace.

## DM notes

- **Group management:** The eleven refugees move at 2 mph. The pace is determined by the elderly and the children. No one can rush them without causing harm. All four PCs must stay with the group — if anyone moves ahead to scout beyond visual range, the officers close in.
- **Vess's position:** She walks the group's right flank, slightly forward of the group's right edge. She does not explain why. She is watching the ridge. From here she can see the bridge, still 4 miles ahead, with the road agents already on the ridge (Perception passive 16 — automatic). She says nothing. She adjusts her position.
- **Elder Sorr walk-alongside opportunity:** If Tam walks alongside Sorr, she will eventually speak. She does not initiate conversation. If he asks something genuine — *not* "where are you going?" (tactical) but something that shows he doesn't need to know (per heuristic): arc fires. See npcs/elder-daven-sorr.md.
- **Toll officers dropping back:** At mile 4, the officers stop at a road marker and do not continue. They watch the group proceed south. One of them writes something in a field ledger. Then they turn north.
