# Road-Warden Officer (×2)

**Role:** Toll station officers. Antagonist NPC without malice — they are doing their job selectively.

## Stat block (if needed)

Scout (MM p.349): AC 13, HP 16, Speed 30 ft. Multiattack (2). Shortsword +4, 1d6+2. Longbow +4, 1d8+2. Skills: Nature +4, Perception +6, Stealth +6, Survival +4.

## Emotional position

They have a quota. Refugees with borderline papers are the easiest way to meet it — slow down the processing, find documentation gaps, accept transit fees in lieu of full detention. They are not cruel. They are indifferent. The distinction is real but does not help the refugees.

They do not want to fight anyone. They do not want to document an incident with a Gilean cleric. They do not want to explain to their captain why they engaged a half-orc in a road altercation.

## Behavior

- Slow-processing the papers when the travelers arrive.
- Back down immediately if Tam produces the Gilean seal and makes the social check.
- Follow the group for 4 miles as a power demonstration. Stop at the mile-4 marker. Write in the ledger. Return north.
- If combat is initiated against them by PCs: this is a serious mistake. They have colleagues and they file reports.

## Voice

Bureaucratic. *"We'll need verification of purpose of travel."* They are not performing; this is how they process people.

## DM note

These officers have no connection to the road agents at the bridge. The road agents are unaffiliated criminals who have spotted a slow refugee group. The toll officers would not cooperate with road agents; they would arrest them if they knew.
