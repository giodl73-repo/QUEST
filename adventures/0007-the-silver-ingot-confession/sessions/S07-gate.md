## Innovation flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Campaign-debt confession (S07-01)
Dimension: Atmospheric Landing / Character Agency
What happened: Grom's Silver-Ingot Confession named not only Aelwen's shame (the module's explicit target) but his own shame at the cold-vein site, when he could not bless the ore. "I was slow and I was proud and I called it patience." This was not in any scaffold version. It emerged from the failed pre-blessing (Religion 8 at Scene 01) being carried forward as unfinished business into the confession itself. The ingot carries two shames: hers and his.
Why the rubric did not anticipate it: Atmospheric Landing anchors capture specific sensory images and finder-vs-receiver. They do not anticipate that a mechanic failure (pre-blessing roll) can be carried forward as emotional content and discharged three scenes later in a binding confession. The five-scene arc (fail → carry → wait → discharge) is not an anchor in any current dimension.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Act-without-announcement (S07-02)
Dimension: Character Agency / Atmospheric Landing
What happened: Thera cut bread long-ways on the road home. She was aware of doing it. She did not explain it to the party. Grom watched. Said nothing. The first time she did the thing she had been not-doing for twenty-three years, she did not announce it. No verbal disclosure; no scene structure; just the act. The S06 naming of Adda produced a behavior change in S07 without any additional scene.
Why the rubric did not anticipate it: Character agency anchors capture decision-point choices (attune/refuse; open/close; speak/stay). They do not anticipate that a prior session's verbal disclosure (Thera naming Adda in S06) generates a cross-session behavioral consequence (bread long-ways in S07) that reads as agency without being a choice-moment.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Archive-placement as closure (S07-03)
Dimension: Character Agency / Atmospheric Landing
What happened: Kessa left Vethrenn's commonplace book — with her answer written in the margin — on the workshop archive shelf. She did not take it. She answered a two-hundred-year-old question and put the answer where someone else could find it. The closure was not possession (sheet-deep-reader's usual register); it was placement. The catalog is complete; the archive is the right home.
Why the rubric did not anticipate it: Sheet-deep-reader captures PC-sheet-grounded choices that deepen understanding. Archive-placement-as-closure is not a choice the rubric has seen: the PC has the information; she gives it back to the world rather than keeping it. This is the sheet-deep-reader pattern inverted — the reader becomes the archivist.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Hints delivered simultaneously as campaign resonance (S07-04)
Dimension: Atmospheric Landing / Module Fidelity / Surprise
What happened: All four hints (Fresco-Heal; Vethrenn's margin; Reorx's Judgment; Aelwen's Confession) were delivered in the same scene (Scene 06, ingot-forging), converging at the moment the silver flowed clean. Each hint was planted in a different session (S01, S01, S04, S03). The simultaneous convergence was not scripted — it emerged from the design of the rite + the specific dice outcome (Religion 23 → Route D) + the spatial logic of the forge (all four PCs present). Seven sessions of campaign debt discharged in one moment.
Why the rubric did not anticipate it: Module Fidelity captures whether planned manifest symptoms land. It does not capture multi-session hint convergence as a resonance event. The rubric has no anchor for "all campaign hints delivered simultaneously at a single structural moment." This is a different phenomenon from any single session's manifest delivery.
```
