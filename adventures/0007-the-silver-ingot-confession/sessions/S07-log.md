# S07 LOG — The Silver-Ingot Confession

## Session Summary

Aelwen of Vaenshold made six moon-silver objects in PC 179 and could not forge the seventh — the ingot of cold-vein silver that would have released what she made. She chose the pine tree instead. Two hundred years later, the Varduin Muster entered her workshop at dawn with the material in Grom's hands and no reason left not to finish it.

Three PCs passed the cold-vein mining checks; Grom failed his — he is a forge-priest, not a miner; Aelric took his pickaxe. At the vein site, Grom tried to bless the ore and the words did not come. He said: "The words will come when the forge is ready." He was correct.

The workshop door opened outward. Grom recognized the fresco of Ilendra's parents from S01's Perception CRIT 23. He said "I know them." Kessa's commonplace book warmed at the threshold and she read Vethrenn's note one final time and said "I know how." The sixth vault-indentation was rectangular and empty and ingot-shaped and had been waiting since PC 179.

The long night: Kessa wrote her answer into Vethrenn's margin. Aelric cleaned the armor without playing the Minute. Thera read the letter in Aelwen's workshop to see if the place changed what it said. Grom greeted the forge-hearth four times: *"Thou'rt seen."* He did not try to bless the ore again.

At dawn, Grom performed the Silver-Ingot Confession. He spoke to the silver the way Duren taught him to speak to difficult metal — directly, without ceremony. He named Aelwen's shame. Then he named his own, at the vein site, when he could not find the words: *"I was slow and I was proud and I called it patience."* He lifted the silver. It flowed clean.

Religion 23 — full Route D. The ingot formed whole. The fresco changed. Thera asked "Did that change?" Aelric said "Yes." Hint 4 auto-delivered at passive 15: Aelwen's voice carrying the intention across two hundred years: *"I should have forged an ingot instead."* Kessa: *"She did. We did it for her."*

Then Grom destroyed the Pride-Circlet in the same forge, same dawn (Religion 15 — exactly passes). The last coil, ended cleanly.

Ingot placed in the sixth vault-space. Kessa left Vethrenn's commonplace book with her answer written in the margin, on the archive shelf, findable by whoever comes next. Aelric told the steward: *"We completed work that was left unfinished two hundred years ago. The estate can now rest."* On the road home, Thera cut a loaf of bread long-ways. She was aware of doing it. She did not explain.

### Scene 3.0 — Aelwen's Workshop (Approach and Entry)

The door opens outward. The inside smells of two hundred years of locked air and the mineral ghost of moon-silver. Not warm; not cool. Old and present. On the north wall, directly visible from the threshold: a fresco. Two faces.

**Grom is through the door first.**

He stops. He looks at the fresco. He recognizes the faces. He saw them in S01, Room 06, the Sarcophagus Chamber, via a Perception CRIT 23. He is seeing Ilendra's parents in their original painting, two centuries earlier, in the house where the grief began. He says: *"I know them."*

The party asks. He tells them about S01. The Crit; the tomb; the faces of the people who loved Ilendra. These are those people, here, now.

**⭐ Hint 2 — Vethrenn's margin, full delivery at threshold:**

Kessa's commonplace book warms in her pack the moment she crosses the threshold. She opens it. The margin-note surfaces. She reads it aloud — Vethrenn's words, one more time. Then closes the book. Then: *"I know how."* She does not explain immediately. The party asks. She tells them: *"The ingot. The cold-vein silver. Grom names the shame aloud while he forges it. That is how. That is what Vethrenn suspected was possible. We do it tonight."*

The empty vault: six indentations, five artifact-shaped, one rectangular. Ingot-shaped. Waiting.

### Scene 4.0 — The Pine Tree

Thera walks around the workshop to look. Grom follows — he always follows where grief leads.

Thera: *"What happened here?"*

Kessa answers directly: *"Aelwen hanged herself from the original tree. In PC 179. Heva planted a replacement. Vaenshold-family silversmith tradition: you do not leave a place empty if it was a good place before the grief. Heva was eighteen."*

Grom looks at the tree. He does not touch it. He does not speak a benediction. He turns and goes back inside. The forge will not heat itself.

Aelric stands at the pine tree for forty-five seconds. He is thinking about Varran and armor and the weight of things that are inherited. He goes back inside.

Thera stays an extra moment. She thinks about bread cut long-ways. She goes back inside.

### Scene 5.0 — Forge Preparation (Night Before)

Grom builds the fire in layers. The forge will reach temperature in three hours. He checks it four times before dawn. He does not need to be watched but does not send the party away.

**Kessa** opens Vethrenn's commonplace book to the margin-note. She reads it one final time. Below Vethrenn's words, in the margin, she writes in her silver-ink pen:

> *"She was right. Here is how: you name the shame over hot metal, in the place where the grief began. You do not ask for anything. You name it and forge it and that is the releasing. — K., PC 20, Vaenshold workshop."*

She closes the book. The question is answered. The book is closed.

**Aelric** cleans the Varran-armor in an alcove. Not aggressively. He is thinking about the Minute. He does not play it tonight. He will know what he is bringing to the forge in the morning.

**Thera** sits on the workbench where the cold-vein silver once sat. She takes the letter out. Reads it in Aelwen's workshop to see if the place changes what it says. It does not. She puts it back.

**Grom** checks the temperature at 1 a.m., 2 a.m., 3 a.m., 4 a.m. At each check: thumb on the inside-curve of the firebox. Honest. *"Thou'rt seen."* Four times. He does not try to bless the ore again. The words will come.

### Scene 6.0 — The Silver-Ingot Confession (Dawn)

The forge reaches working temperature. Grom checks the inside-curve: blue-white, exactly right. He places the cold ore in the crucible. *"Does anyone need to leave the room for this?"* No one does.

Aelric casts Bless (L1; concentration; Grom target).

**⭐ GROM'S CONFESSION (Option C — binding):**

> *"Things were made to hold grief instead of releasing it. Aelwen made this cold-vein silver into a lock on a door that should have been a key. She was right to make it — she was wrong to stop. The silver wanted to move and she stilled it. I name that. I name the making as grief-coiled, not forged-with-intent.*
>
> *The tool of release could have been made. She had the ore. She had the temperature. Vethrenn's margin gave her the words and she answered it true, and she still put down the hammer. The tool could have been made forty years back. I name that gap. Forty years of a thing that wanted working, un-worked.*
>
> *Shame held the hammer still. Hers first. Then mine at the site, when I could not bless the ore because I knew I was standing where she had stood and I did not know how to finish a debt that was not mine to start. I name that too. I name it mine. I was slow and I was proud and I called it patience.*
>
> *Reorx. Thou'rt seen. The coil is uncoiled. We are finishing it now."*

He lifts the silver. It flows clean.

### Scene 7.0 — Aftermath

Grom places the ingot in the sixth vault-space. It fits. He closes the vault door. Does not lock it. Leaves it findable.

Kessa puts Vethrenn's commonplace book on the archive shelf with her answer written in the margin. She may come back for it. She does not know yet.

The steward's question: *"Did you find what you needed?"*

Aelric, formal and precise: *"We completed work that was left unfinished two hundred years ago. The estate can now rest."*

The steward writes in his maintenance-log. Locks the gate.

On the road home: Thera cuts a loaf of bread long-ways. She is aware of doing it. She does not explain this to the party. Grom watches. Says nothing. He notes it the way he notes inside-curves: honest work.

**The Moon-Silver Cycle is complete.**

Seven adventures. Seven emotions. Six moon-silver forgings from three veins. A fresco that no longer holds what it was holding. A forge-priest who named a shame that was not his to start. A wizard who answered a two-hundred-year-old question. A rogue who cut the bread long-ways. A knight who held the reliquary without opening it, because the Measure says you honor what you committed to.
