**Verdict: PASS the binding threshold (56+) but BELOW the shippable threshold (65).** This is correct behavior for a no-combat finale. The adventure is not trying to be a dungeon; Jaquays and Gygax score it as a dungeon (where it fails) rather than as what it is (a rite of completion). The emotional and lore scores are universally high. The structural scores (Dungeon, Encounter, Table, Agency) are universally low — and intentionally so.

**One-pass needed before play on Table-readiness.** All five reviewers agree on the single P0 fix.

## Strongest Axes

1. **Premise clarity (9.2)** — *"Forge the seventh artifact Aelwen could not."* All five reviewers cite this as the clearest hook in the cycle.
2. **Curse/consequence (8.6)** — The ingot documents the shame naming permanently. Stuart: "Curse as reckoning." Schick: "a quarter-pound of metal with a voice is Blackrazor's kin."
3. **Lore grounding (8.0)** — Ilendra's parents → Ilendra's grief → Aelwen's shame → the party's undoing. Two-hundred-year genealogy.

## Weakest Axes (structural; expected for a no-combat finale)

1. **Encounter craft (2.8)** — Correct. This is intentional. A finale rite should not be a fight.
2. **Table-readiness (4.6)** — The critical gap. All five reviewers flag it. This is fixable.
3. **Dungeon integrity (4.4)** — Also intentional. Linear processional by design. Jaquays is right; this is not a dungeon. Jaquays is not wrong to score it low.
4. **Player agency (4.6)** — All three routes (A/C/D) converge at the same forge. Intentional; the finale's agency is in the texture of the moment, not the path.

## Convergent P0 (ALL FIVE reviewers agree)

### P0.1 — Scaffold Grom's confession words

**The single most important fix.** All five reviewers flag that Scene 06's Grom Option C has no fallback. If the subagent is unavailable, or if the player is uncertain, the DM is told to ask the player and take the answer as binding — which stalls if the player freezes.

**Fix:** Add three scaffold versions of Grom's confession to Scene 06:
- **Short (20 words):** For a player who needs a starting point.
- **Medium (60 words):** For a player who wants the full arc.
- **Long (150 words):** For a player who wants to inhabit the moment completely.

All three must name the three things: (a) things were made to hold grief rather than release it; (b) the tool of release could have been made; (c) shame prevented it.

### P0.2 — Religion DC 12 failure state

Moldvay + Schick + Stuart flag that "DC 12 is accessible — this is not a challenge" is wish-fulfillment prose, not procedure. What if Grom rolls a 7?

**Fix:** Add a graceful failure path: if Religion DC 12 fails, the ingot cracks along an invisible seam. It still speaks (Hint 4 auto-delivers at passive 15), but quieter. The fresco still changes, but less clearly — *"the faces look almost as if they have been told the truth."* Route C outcome rather than Route D. Still a valid ending. The campaign still closes.

## Divergent Suggestions (single lens)

- **(Jaquays)** Open the manor house as optional explorable space (3 rooms; one Ilendra-archive clue).
- **(Gygax)** Add a wandering check during Scene 05 overnight (Solamnic patrol, not hostile, creates mild time-pressure).
- **(Schick)** Give the pine tree mechanical weight (carving in the bark; Aelwen's final mark).
- **(Stuart)** Write a DM-letter (not in-module) explaining how to hold space for the rite. "This is not a scene you run; it is a scene you witness and protect."
- **(Moldvay)** Reformat Scene 05 as checklist procedures; add one-page cold-run cheat sheet.

## Go/No-Go

**GO with P0 fixes applied.** Two edits to Scene 06 (scaffold versions + failure state) take 10 minutes. After those, the adventure is table-ready even without a subagent. The structural weaknesses (Dungeon integrity, Encounter craft, Agency) are not weaknesses — they are the design. This is a rite, not a dungeon. It earns its finale position.

After P0 fixes, expected weighted mean: ~63-65 (table-readiness rises from 4.6 to 7-8; other scores unchanged).
