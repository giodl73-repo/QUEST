## Scene 0 — Veth Calls Varet In

Veth's office. Morning. He dismissed his aide and closed the door. He did not offer Varet a seat. He did not sit himself. He stood at his desk with both hands on the surface and looked at the wall behind her.

*"I'm going to tell you things I shouldn't tell you. I'm telling you because your sergeant's man read a contract clause in front of a lieutenant, and I've run out of time."*

Arren Vole stood outside the closed door. He did not listen. He stood where he could see anyone approaching.

**Varet Insight DC 14 (+4):** rolls=[5] mod=+4 **total=9** — FAIL. She could not confirm if he was performing or present. He seemed credible — genuinely distressed — but she could not verify it. She wrote: *"Veth: seemingly credible. Distress seems genuine. Cannot confirm."*

The Insight failure held the uncertainty intact. Good. She needed to act on incomplete information. The journal would hold the uncertainty on record.

## Scene 1 — Veth's Disclosure (H3 + H4 full delivery)

**Veth (extended):**

*"Lord Herath Sorn has a payment record on me from seven years ago. A shipment I accepted and didn't report. It was small. It was a mistake. He's held it since. The orders you've been following — the cache, the transfer coordinates — those are his."*

He paused. He still had not looked at her directly.

*"Your unit was assigned to this route specifically. There's a complaint in your service record from three years ago. An officer. A supply irregularity you reported. The word used — in the brief I received — was 'manageable.' You were described as unlikely to escalate. They were wrong."*

He finally looked at her.

*"There's a document in Sorn's correspondence that calls your unit a 'temporary asset.' I saw it once. I didn't ask what it meant."*

**H3 delivered fully:** *"The unit was assigned here because command expected you to fail or disappear."* The phrase is in a brief Veth received. He reads it because he is done with euphemism.

**H4 planted:** Veth is blackmailed, not corrupt by choice. Sorn is the coercer. Who is above Sorn? Not yet answered.

Varet asked three questions. Per heuristics: evidential, not emotional.
1. "Do you have the payment documentation?" — Veth: "No. Sorn has it."
2. "Do you have the complaint record?" — Veth: "I have a copy. I'll give it to you."
3. "What does 'temporary asset' mean?" — Veth: "I don't know. I didn't want to know."

He handed her the complaint record copy. Her own complaint, annotated *"dismissed, officer transferred, file closed."* The annotation was not in her handwriting. Someone added it after she filed it.

She looked at the annotation for a long time. Then she said: *"Thank you, Captain."*

She left.

## Scene 2 — Varet Writes

Courtyard bench. She sat down and opened the journal. She wrote for eleven minutes. Nobody spoke to her. Arren Vole stood at the far wall and did not approach.

When she stopped writing, she closed the journal. She looked at Arren. She said: *"I need everyone in the courtyard in ten minutes."*

**Journal Entry 5 (written aloud by the DM as Varet's voice):**

```
Day 5. Veth disclosure (private). Not logged in official record pending verification.
H3 CONFIRMED: Unit assigned because complaint record flagged as "manageable."
  Command expected no escalation. This was a selection criterion, not an accident.
  My complaint was dismissed and annotated after I submitted it.
  The annotation is not in my handwriting. I did not dismiss my own complaint.
H4 SEED: Veth is being blackmailed by Sorn. Not corrupt by choice. Compliant by coercion.
  Who is above Sorn? Not yet known.
Complaint copy received: my own filing. Annotation added post-submission.
  Discrepancy: my own record was modified without my knowledge.
DISCREPANCY count: 6. OUTCOME count: 2.
RATIO STATUS: 6:2. The ratio has been inverted since Day 4.
  Per my own standing rule — written in this journal's front matter three years ago —
  the arc fires when discrepancies exceed outcomes.
  The arc fires.
  I am the unit's commanding officer. I have the authority to act.
  I am going to act.
```

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
*"The arc fires."* She wrote this about herself, referring to her own rule, in the third person for exactly three words and then switched back to first. It was not drama. It was the precision of a woman who had been recording decisions for nine years, applying her own framework to the most consequential decision she had made in nine years.

She did not mark the moment. The sentence was the moment.

**RV6 CONFIRMED:** The journal's inversion moment is a reception event — she acknowledges the framework and the arc. This is not a fact in the military sense. It is a professional self-assessment written in her own voice in her own journal. The documentary-witness accumulates to the point of self-referential firing. **RV6 verdict active.**

**RV7 TEST:** The spine of this campaign is duty and obedience, not grief and loss. The inversion moment fired from a professional rule she set for herself three years ago. No grief. No loss of a loved one. A rule. A ratio. An obligation to her own record-keeping. **RV7 evidence: duty-spine produces equivalent emotional density to grief-spine at the same campaign moment (session 5 of 7, primary arc beat).**

## Scene 3 — Taris Shows the Map

Courtyard. The unit gathered. Taris had the map open before anyone else was ready.

He did not speak. He handed it to Varet.

**Taris Perception passive (+6):** rolls=[16] mod=+4 total=20 — He had been making the map legible for this moment. It was legible.

The map showed: Day 1 trail compaction. Day 1 cairn waymark (unidentified symbol). Day 1 cache coordinates (SC-1). Day 2 overnight track notation. Day 2 cache bracket annotation. Day 3 east-valley holdout site. Day 3 Brek rescue location. Day 4 Sorn factor-house coordinates. Toll system notations (CF ford, RH toll). The formal objection route mark.

Three weeks of patrol rendered as a single coherent pattern.

Taris: *"I made it legible when I saw what it was. I didn't know when I'd need to show it."*

**This is Taris's arc-completion.**

Seven words. That was the explanation. The map was already the argument.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
He had been carrying this argument for three sessions on a piece of paper he updated every night. The showing was not a reveal. It was a handoff. The argument moved from his custody to hers. He did not need it anymore; she needed it now.

**Research coding:** craft-witness arc-completion. The map is Taris's professional practice made into evidence. He made it legible. He handed it. Two acts. No speech.

## Scene 4 — Lenne Hands Over the Log

After the map moment. Lenne approached. She held out the medical log without announcement.

*"These are the ones I couldn't explain with the briefing I was given."*

She named three entries aloud: Serrak's wound (military-spec bolt, east of route, cause unclear), the Day 2 cache medical stores (3× allocation, cause unclear), the Day 4 transfer (medical stores to civilian estate, cause unclear).

*"Three 'cause: unclear' entries. I don't write that unless I can't explain it. I can't explain any of these with what I've been told."*

**Pre-arc-completion.** The full arc fires in S7 (she hands the log to the tribunal, not just to Varet). This scene is the last accumulation before the delivery.

<!-- SURPRISE duty-triggered -->
She had been building toward this handover since Day 1. She did not know she was building toward it. She was logging what she couldn't explain. When she had enough that couldn't be explained, she gave it to the person with the authority to do something about it. The accumulation was implicit; the handover was explicit.

The arc is not the speech. The arc is the document transfer. "These are the ones I couldn't explain." That is the sentence.

## Scene 5 — Varet's Addendum and the Unit

After Taris showed the map and Lenne handed the log, Varet sat down again.

She wrote an addendum to Entry 5:

```
Addendum: Taris showed map. Five sessions of patrol rendered as pattern.
  The pattern is legible. It has been legible for two sessions.
  He was making it legible in advance. Without being asked.
Lenne's log: 3 "cause: unclear" entries. Medical evidence.
  She prepared this without being asked.
The unit is ready to act. I am the one with the authority to act.
[MARGIN NOTE: Arren said "someone is using us to run a route" on Day 2.
  He was right. He knew before I did.
  He has been waiting for me to catch up.
  I've caught up.]
```

**RV7 CONFIRMED:** The margin note "I've caught up" is the emotional center of the session. Not grief. Not loss. A commander acknowledging that her NCO was right, that she was behind, and that she has now arrived at the understanding he had two sessions ago. The emotional density is equivalent to a grief-arc resolution — but the content is professional mutual recognition.
