# Captain Osren Veth — Disclosure Scene

## Arc-completion conditions

Veth's arc fires here. He has been carrying this for seven years. The trigger was Dorn's contract reading in S4 — when Dorn read the contract in Ossren's presence, Veth understood that the unit was not going to be silent, and silence was the only thing protecting him.

His arc-completion: *"I was told your unit was assigned here because you filed a complaint three years ago that was suppressed. You were described to me as — the word used was 'manageable.' I didn't ask what that meant. I should have asked."*

He says this while looking at the wall behind Varet. He cannot look at her when he says it.

## What he tells her

1. Lord Herath Sorn has a payment record from seven years ago — a shipment that Veth accepted and did not report. Small, at the time. He has been compliant since to keep it quiet.
2. Varet's unit was assigned to this route specifically. The complaint she filed three years ago (workshop-canon: she reported an officer for using unit supplies for personal trade; report was dismissed, officer transferred) flagged her record as "history of insubordination — unlikely to escalate." She was chosen because they expected her to follow orders and not dig.
3. There is a document in Sorn's correspondence that references the unit as a "temporary asset." Veth saw it once. He does not know what it means. He suspects.
4. He does not know who Sorn works for. He has never asked. He has learned not to ask.

## H3 delivery

> *"The unit was assigned here because command expected you to fail or disappear."* He says this explicitly. He is not being cruel. He is being accurate. The phrase is in the brief he received. He reads it because he is done with euphemism.

## H4 seed

Veth is not corrupt in the way that would make him a clean villain. He is trapped. The inversion — that Sorn himself is under pressure from above, that this is a chain of compelled compliance all the way up — is not yet visible to Varet. It comes in S6.

## Voice

This is the quietest Veth has been. He speaks without his usual clipped precision. He sits at his desk and he talks to the wall. He is done.
