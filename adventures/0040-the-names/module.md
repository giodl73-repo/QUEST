## Scene 0 — Veth Calls Varet In

**Read-aloud:**
> Veth's office at Thorngate. He dismisses his aide and closes the door. He does not offer Varet a seat. He does not sit himself. He stands at his desk and puts both hands on the surface and looks at the wall behind her.
>
> "I'm going to tell you things I shouldn't tell you. I'm telling you because your sergeant's man read a contract clause in front of a lieutenant, and I've run out of time."

**Beats:**
- Arren Vole stands outside the closed door. He does not listen. He stands where he can see anyone approaching.
- Varet: does not speak until Veth finishes. Her Insight runs automatically — DC 14: Veth is not performing. He is in genuine distress.

## Scene 1 — Veth's Disclosure (H3 + H4 seed)

**Read-aloud (Veth, extended):**
> "Lord Herath Sorn has a payment record on me from seven years ago. A shipment I accepted and didn't report. It was small. It was a mistake. He's held it since. The orders you've been following — the cache, the transfer coordinates — those are his."
>
> He pauses. He still has not looked at her directly.
>
> "Your unit was assigned to this route specifically. There's a complaint in your service record from three years ago. An officer. A supply irregularity you reported. The word used — in the brief I received — was 'manageable.' You were described as unlikely to escalate. They were wrong."
>
> He finally looks at her.
>
> "There's a document in Sorn's correspondence that calls your unit a 'temporary asset.' I saw it once. I didn't ask what it meant."

**H3 delivered fully:** *"The unit was assigned here because command expected you to fail or disappear."*
- **v1.3 fallbacks:** (a) Veth says it explicitly; (b) the brief document itself is available (Veth offers it); (c) Arren Vole can confirm Varet told him afterward.

**H4 planted:** Veth is blackmailed. Sorn is the coercer. Who is coercing Sorn? Not yet answered.

**Varet's Insight (social roll, DC 14):**
**🎲 Varet — Insight vs. Veth's honesty** — DC 14, Wis +4
- On 14+: "He is telling the truth. He is also afraid. This is not performance."
- On 13 or lower: he seems credible; uncertainty about his full motive remains.

**Varet's response (per heuristics):**
She does not ask emotional questions. She asks evidential ones:
1. "Do you have the payment documentation?" — Veth: "No. Sorn has it."
2. "Do you have the complaint record?" — Veth: "I have a copy. I'll give it to you."
3. "What does 'temporary asset' mean?" — Veth: "I don't know. I didn't want to know."

**Veth hands Varet the complaint record copy.** It is her own complaint, annotated "dismissed, officer transferred, file closed." The annotation is not in her handwriting. It was added after she submitted it.

## Scene 2 — Varet Writes

**Read-aloud:**
> Varet comes out of Veth's office and sits down on the bench outside. She opens the field journal. She writes for eleven minutes. No one speaks to her. Arren Vole stands at the far wall and does not approach.
>
> When she stops writing, she closes the journal. She does not say what she wrote. She stands up. She looks at Arren. She says: "I need everyone in the courtyard in ten minutes."

**The journal entries (written aloud by DM playing Varet):**

```
Day 5. Veth disclosure (private). Not logged in official record pending verification.
H3 CONFIRMED: Unit assigned because complaint record flagged as "manageable."
  Command expected no escalation. This was a selection criterion, not an accident.
H4 SEED: Veth is being blackmailed by Sorn. Not corrupt by choice. Compliant by coercion.
  Who is above Sorn? Not yet known.
Complaint copy received: my own filing, annotated after submission. 
  Annotation: "dismissed." I did not annotate it. Someone did.
DISCREPANCY: Field journal now has 5 discrepancy entries. 3 outcome entries.
  The ratio has inverted. Per my own rule: the arc fires.
```

**This is the inversion moment.** Varet's heuristic says: *follows orders until the discrepancy column has more entries than the outcome column.* It now does. The arc is in motion.

## Scene 3 — Taris Shows the Map

**Read-aloud:**
> In the courtyard. The unit gathered. Taris has his map open. He does not speak. He hands it to Varet. She looks. The map shows: Ridgehead trail traffic (Session 1). East valley holdout site (Session 3). Sorn factor-house coordinates (Session 4). The toll system notations. Three weeks of patrol marked as a single coherent pattern.
>
> Taris: "I made it legible when I saw what it was. I didn't know when I'd need to show it."

**This is Taris's arc-completion.** He has been carrying the evidence for three sessions on a piece of paper he updated every night. Showing it is the act. He does not explain what it means. He does not need to.

**<!-- SURPRISE --> candidate:** Taris does not speak a speech. He shows the map and says seven words. That is sufficient. The map is the argument.

## Scene 4 — Lenne Hands Over the Log

**Read-aloud:**
> Lenne approaches after the map moment. She holds out her medical log. She does not offer it as evidence or as an argument. She says: "These are the ones I couldn't explain with the briefing I was given." She names three entries: the Serrak wound, the cache medical stores, the Ossren transfer.
>
> That is the handover. She does not elaborate. The log speaks.

**Pre-arc-completion beat.** Lenne's full arc fires in S7 (she hands the log to Varet at the tribunal). This scene is the accumulation completing.
