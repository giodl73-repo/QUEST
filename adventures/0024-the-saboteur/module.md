## Setting & Background

Half the west wall's arrow stock has been deliberately shaft-rotted, Gate-Captain Hessa Var asks the party to investigate quietly before Asholt finds out, and the investigation reveals both a saboteur and Hessa's own quiet complicity through a message network she thought was harmless.

**Thorngate Keep, Day 14 of the siege.** The west wall's arrow cache: 200 arrows, half now useless — shaft-rot has set in on one full rack. The sabotage is systematic, not accidental. Someone inside the keep worked against the garrison. Hessa Var, who controls what passes through the gate, found it this morning. She knows something she hasn't said.

- **Party HP:** All full (between-session carry-over; S2 ended with Davan 23/28 + 1 hit die = back to 28/28).
- **Supply Die:** d8. **S3 garrison potions:** 1.
- **Sava spell slots:** 3×L1, 2×L2.
- **Caelith Portent:** [6, 7] carried from S1. Third session. **Must fire this session.**

**Design intent:** The combat (the turned garrison member) should cost real HP — 20-30% of party total. Sava's 1-potion constraint is the session's mechanical pressure point. The Supply Die should degrade to d6 after this session.

Hessa Var finds the party before the morning briefing. She does not go to Asholt. She says: *"The west wall arrow stock. Half of it's been tampered with — shaft-rot, and it didn't happen overnight. I need you to find who did it before the Warden does. I have a reason for asking you instead of him."*

She will not say the reason yet. She is testing whether the party will proceed on trust before she reveals her own exposure.

Investigation, tracking, evidence recovery inside the keep. This is Talis's domain without needing to climb anything. Her Perception (passive 16), Survival (+4), and Stealth (+7) are all relevant. The investigation runs through three locations: the west wall cache (where the sabotage occurred), the postern log (where movement is recorded), and the suspect's quarters. The tracks and the evidence are physical; Talis reads them.

**Garrison member Wil Fen** — a crossbow-trained wall-watcher, 34, has served at Thorngate for 7 years. His mother is at Varduin-by-the-Pines. The Reconstitution-adjacent network sent a message: if the west wall cache is disabled before the next assault, his mother would not be bothered. Wil did not volunteer. He was given two days to do it or decline. He did it.

Wil knows he is about to be found. He will fight rather than be taken because he believes the alternative is worse — not for himself, but for his mother. He has not told Hessa, who does not know about the coercion.

**Gate-Captain Hessa Var** has been passing messages through the gate at night to a contractor network contact — a person she knew from before the siege who she believed was a trade intermediary. The messages were garrison status updates: supply levels, wall condition, shift rotations. She told herself this was harmless. She was receiving coin for it. The contractor is the same network that hired Dex for the supply cart ambush.

She does not know Wil was coerced through the same network. She does not know her messages contributed to the intelligence picture that made the coercion possible. When the party discovers this connection, she will have to face it.

**Hessa's token condition:** The party gives her 4 hours to address her own situation (go to Asholt herself) before they report it. If they do this, she goes. She tells Asholt everything. He relieves her of gate command for 48 hours and reinstates her. She gives the party her token.

The investigation produces the moment where Davan's Orel story surfaces for the first time. The parallel: Wil's sabotage is provably wrong (the evidence is unambiguous) AND the reason for it is sympathetic (coercion, his mother, not ideology). When Davan is holding the evidence and knows what Wil did and why, he must decide whether to present the complete picture to Asholt or just the finding.

This is the Orel mirror: correct testimony, wrong person outcome. *He gave correct testimony that ruined his brother. He is now holding correct evidence that will ruin someone who didn't want to do this.* The session does not resolve this — it surfaces it. The accounting speech's first content comes from here.

**PC-authored accounting speech, first layer:** Davan witnesses the Wil/Hessa situation and has a private moment (in-fiction, not announced to party) where the shape of his S7 speech begins forming. Note in handoff: *"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: the cost of correct evidence."*

**Design mandate:** Caelith's Portent [6, 7] has been carried for three sessions. This session, one die fires. The designed condition:

Wil's combat: he is a crossbow-trained wall-watcher who will attempt to flee over the west wall if cornered. The party needs to prevent this — either by blocking the route or by making a key roll. Design a critical moment where replacing an enemy attack roll, enemy saving throw, or party skill check with a 6 or 7 is mechanically meaningful:

- **Wil's escape attempt** (DC 14 Athletics to vault the wall ledge before the party can stop him): if Wil rolls a 14 Athletics and succeeds, he escapes. Caelith can replace Wil's roll with his Portent 7 (7 < 14 = Wil fails). Framed in-voice: *"I see this moment before it happens."*
- This is the first in-fiction Portent deployment. Log verbatim.

### Scene 1 — Hessa's Request (Room 01)

Early morning, west wall base. Hessa shows the party the rotted shafts. She gives them the investigation: find who did it, bring it to her first.

**Talis's scene:** She reads the cache. DC 12 Survival: the rot is targeted — the middle-tier racks only, consistent with someone who knew the storage rotation and could move fast. This is not random attrition; someone with garrison knowledge did this deliberately.

**The postern log:** Hessa's own log shows a discrepancy — a night passage two days ago, logged as "maintenance," no countersignature. DC 14 Investigation (any PC): the handwriting on the maintenance log matches Wil Fen's duty signature.

### Scene 2 — The Investigation (Rooms 02-03)

Talis leads. Three points of evidence:

1. **West wall cache (Room 02):** The rot agent — a fungal compound applied by brush. DC 14 Survival or Alchemy check: this is not a commonly available compound; it requires preparation time and specific materials. Someone planned this.

2. **The postern night log:** The maintenance entry. Wil's handwriting. Talis finds a faint boot-print near the log lectern — Wil's boot size (she has his size from the duty roster Hessa provides). DC 12 Perception: the print.

3. **Wil's quarters (Room 03):** A letter, half-burned, in the cold hearth. Not fully destroyed. DC 13 Investigation to recover legible sections: the coercion terms — his mother's address in Varduin, the demand, the window. The letter is not signed. The device on the seal is the contractor's token device (same one Dex carried — Talis recognizes the description from the S2 choke point context).

### Scene 3 — Confronting Wil (Room 04, primary encounter)

The party finds Wil on the west wall — on watch, alone, aware they've been investigating. He sees them coming and makes a decision.

He will fight. Not from ideology — from fear of what happens to his mother if he's taken. He has a crossbow and the wall ledge behind him.

**Caelith's Portent fires here:** Wil's escape attempt — he tries to vault the wall ledge when cornered. Caelith replaces the roll with his Portent 7. Wil fails. He does not escape.

See `encounters/saboteur-confrontation.md`.

### Scene 4 — Hessa's Disclosure (Room 01)

After Wil is taken (alive — the party has evidence he was coerced): Hessa is told. The party shows her the contractor device on the letter seal. She goes still.

*"I know that device."*

She tells them about the messages. She has been passing status updates for six months — before the siege began. She did not know what she was feeding. She stops herself mid-sentence and says: *"I need to tell the Warden myself. Give me four hours."*

**Token window:** If the party gives her four hours → Hessa goes to Asholt, confesses, is relieved of gate command for 48 hours, reinstated. She gives the party her token.

**If the party reports immediately:** No token. Hessa's confession happens anyway (she goes immediately rather than waiting), but the relationship is closed — she trusts their competence, not their judgment.

### Scene 5 — Davan's moment (corridor, between scenes)

Between Scene 4 and any reporting to Asholt: Davan holds the evidence. The half-burned letter. Wil Fen's coerced signature. His mother's address in Varduin.

*The correct evidence. The wrong person.*

He holds it for a moment longer than he needs to. He has given correct testimony before. He knows what it does to people who didn't want to be guilty. He puts the letter in his pocket (not to conceal it — to carry it correctly).

**PC-authored accounting speech, first content:** This moment is the first layer. Note in handoff: *"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: 'correct evidence, wrong person' — the thing he has held before."*

The west wall positions are investigation terrain during Scenes 1–2 and combat terrain during Scene 3. The two-path design means the party can split: send Talis up the wall direct while Davan circles via interior. He cannot see Path B (interior traverse) until the party is already at the Arrow-Walk. Path B gives the party an approach without Wil seeing them coming — relevant if the party wants to close the escape route before the confrontation.

### Scene 1 — West Wall Base

> Stone alcove, west wall base. Two arrow racks — one intact, one wrong. Lower-tier shafts grey-green where they should be pale, wood soft at the grip. On the postern lectern, the night log lies open to this morning. Hessa stands behind you, saying nothing yet.

#### Features

- **Arrow cache (two racks):** Upper rack — 100 arrows, intact, oiled, serviceable. Lower rack — 100 arrows, approximately half showing shaft-rot: grey-green discoloration, soft grain, will snap on draw. The rot is localized to the lower-tier rack only. **DC 12 Survival (Talis):** The rot is targeted — the lower rack is the middle-tier storage rotation, the first rack drawn during an assault. Someone with garrison knowledge picked the highest-impact rack and applied the compound deliberately. This is not accidental attrition. *[Talis spotlight: if she makes this check, she can identify the application method — brush-applied, not organic spread.]*

- **Postern night log:** A leather-bound log on the stone lectern by the postern door. Entries in multiple hands — every postern passage recorded. **DC 14 Investigation (any PC):** Two days ago, an entry reads "maintenance — west wall postern access, dawn watch" with no countersignature. The handwriting does not match Hessa's duty notation style. Cross-referencing the duty roster Hessa provides: the handwriting matches **Wil Fen's duty signature** — a crossbow-trained wall-watcher who had the dawn watch that night. *[Fallback: if DC 14 is missed here, Talis finds Wil's boot-print near the lectern in Room 02.]*

- **Hessa's opening:** She does not explain her reason for asking the party instead of Asholt until Scene 4. If pressed here, she says: *"I have a reason. Find who did it first."* DC 12 Insight: she already suspects someone; she is not certain; she is testing whether the investigation confirms or contradicts her suspicion.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

**Scene 1 is Talis's scene.** She reads the physical evidence; let her describe what she finds. If she makes the DC 12 Survival check, give her the full read: targeted application, lower rack specifically, garrison knowledge required. This is the investigation's first proof that the saboteur is internal, not external.

**The log discrepancy:** The DC 14 Investigation is the session's first direct pointer to Wil Fen by name. If the party misses it here, the postern log's value as evidence is not lost — the boot-print in Room 02 (DC 12 Perception) provides the same connection. Do not force the log; let the rolls determine sequence.

**Hessa's position:** She is standing back, watching the party work. She has already looked at the log this morning. She knows the handwriting — she just does not say so yet. If a PC brings the duty roster to compare handwriting in her presence, DC 12 Insight: she does not look surprised. She was waiting for someone to do that.

**Countersignature protocol:** Garrison postern entries require a second officer to countersign night passages. Wil's entry is unsigned. The protocol exists specifically because Hessa installed it after a message was misattributed six months ago. The party can learn this from any garrison officer who isn't Hessa. The implication — Wil knew the protocol would be checked, and did not care enough (or was too rushed) to forge it — is left for the party to surface.

### Scene 2 — West Wall Walk

> Forty feet of west-facing battlements, lower tier. The wall here smells wrong — something green and damp under the stone-and-mortar cold. Near the middle merlon, a faint stain on the walkway stone: a brush-mark, dried. Below the postern log lectern at the south end, a single boot-print in dried mud that does not belong to last night's watch rotation.

#### Features

- **Fungal compound stain (middle merlon):** A faint brush-mark on the walkway stone near the third merlon from the north. Not a spill — a deliberate application stroke, lateral, consistent with brush use. **DC 14 Survival or DC 14 Alchemist's Supplies check:** The compound is a prepared fungal accelerant — not commonly available, requires specific organic materials and preparation time (at least one day). Someone planned this, acquired materials outside standard garrison supply, and applied it with a brush in a single pass. *[This confirms the DC 12 Survival finding from Room 01 with physical evidence.]* DC 12 Perception (auto-notice if actively searching, no roll): the stain is visible in good light.

- **Boot-print (south end, near log lectern):** A single dried-mud boot-print near the foot of the postern log lectern. **DC 12 Perception:** The print is fresh relative to last night's watch rotation, left boot, size consistent with a man of approximately 5'10". Hessa's duty roster (which she provides) lists boot sizes for the garrison's issued kit. Wil Fen: issued size 11, left boot match. *[If the postern log discrepancy was missed in Room 01, this is the path to Wil's name. If the log was already found, this is corroboration.]*

- **Battlements (west face):** 3-foot merlons, west-facing. The wall faces the interior of the Needle Pass, not the siege lines — this is the protected face. No enemy fire risk during the investigation. The ledge above (Room 04) is visible from here: the iron ring ladder begins at the north end of the walk.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

**Talis's tracking sequence:** This room is the middle beat of Talis's three-room investigation arc (Room 01 → Room 02 → Room 03). The fungal compound and the boot-print are two independent pieces of evidence that both point toward the same person. If Talis has made at least one of the Room 01 checks, she arrives here already tracking an internal actor. The compound confirms methodology; the print confirms identity.

**The fungal compound specifics (if a PC asks):** It is a preparation made from a specific shelf fungus — Orrwood mold, common in the mountain passes below the Needle. Not in garrison supply. Not sold in Thorngate's garrison stores. Someone sourced it on the outside or knew where to forage it. This detail is available if the party asks Talis to elaborate on the Survival/Alchemy read — it does not require a second check, just the question.

**Approach to Room 04:** The iron ring ladder at the north end of this walk is visible. During the investigation, Wil is not here — he is on watch at Room 04. The ladder shows recent use (iron rings cold and slightly worn from a recent climb). DC 10 Investigation (no roll if Talis is tracking): someone went up this way, recently.

**Connecting to Wil's quarters:** From here, a PC can go up (Room 04, confrontation — the party should not go here yet without searching Room 03 first) or down-and-around to Room 03. The GM should allow the party to sequence as they choose. If they go to Room 04 before Room 03, Wil is there and the confrontation begins early — without the coercion letter. See GM Notes in Room 04 for how to handle early confrontation.

### Scene 3 — Garrison Quarters (West Wing)

> Eight bunks, west wing. Seven squared: kit aligned, boots under the frame. One is not — the far bunk, closest to the wall stairs. Beneath the window, a cold hearth holds ash and something that did not finish burning. A curl of paper: black at the edges, pale at the center.

#### Features

- **Wil Fen's bunk (far end):** Kit is squared — boots under the frame, crossbow bolts counted and aligned. Everything in order except: the pillow is turned over, and a folded piece of cloth (a Varduin-style kerchief, blue wool, woman's work) is tucked under the mattress seam. This is not contraband; it is personal. A PC who examines the kerchief: it is old, worn, and clean — kept carefully. It identifies him as someone who holds Varduin close. *[Contextual setup for the coercion reveal: his mother is at Varduin.]*

- **Half-burned letter (cold hearth):** A curl of paper in the ash, partially burned from one end. **DC 13 Investigation to recover legible sections** (the paper is fragile; a failed check destroys another section — reduce legibility, but the core content survives even on failure as a partial read): The legible sections contain: (1) a Varduin street address, (2) the phrase "two days or the arrangement changes," and (3) a wax seal at the base of the fragment, partially intact. The seal device is a quartered disc with an inner ring — the same contractor token device that Dex carried during the S2 choke point ambush (Talis recognizes the description from the ambush context). *[This is the Hessa link: same contractor network.]*

- **Seal identification (no check required if Dex's token was recovered):** If the party recovered Dex's contractor token in S2, Talis or any PC can match the device by direct comparison. If Dex escaped with the token, **DC 14 Investigation** to identify the device from description alone (Talis's notes from the ambush context give her +2).

- **Other bunks:** Seven regulation-standard. Nothing notable. No contraband.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

**Davan's beat (Scene 5 setup):** This is the room where Davan picks up the evidence. The half-burned letter — with its Varduin address and coercion terms — is the object he carries into Scene 5. The GM should give Davan a moment here, before the party moves on: he reads the address, reads "two days or the arrangement changes," and holds the letter for a beat longer than is strictly necessary. *Do not narrate his internal state — he is a PC. But the GM can note the silence and leave it there.*

**Davan's Orel mirror (for GM awareness):** The coercion terms are unambiguous — someone threatened his mother to make him do this. The evidence of the sabotage is also unambiguous. Davan knows this combination: correct evidence, sympathetic actor, unavoidable outcome. The GM should not underline this connection aloud; let the scene breathe. The accounting speech's first layer forms here.

**If the party misses DC 13:** The letter is not fully destroyed. Even a failed check recovers the Varduin address (enough to establish coercion context) and the partial seal. The coercion terms are the campaign-relevant content; reduce the exact phrasing but preserve the emotional architecture. The seal device must be recoverable regardless of roll — it is the Hessa link.

**Sequence note:** If the party comes here before Room 02, they will have the coercion letter without the boot-print confirmation. The letter identifies motive and network; the boot-print identifies the person. Both pieces point to Wil independently. The GM should allow either sequence to work.

**Other garrison members:** The west wing has 1-2 off-watch garrison members sleeping or present during the day. They are not hostile; they are not paying attention. A PC who searches Wil's bunk openly (rather than discreetly) may attract a passive comment: *"That's Wil's bunk."* Nothing more — garrison members do not interfere with investigation, but they notice. Note in session log if the search was discreet or visible.

### Scene 4 — West Wall Upper Ledge

> The upper ledge faces west. Wil Fen is here — crossbow raised, back to the inner wall-face. He has been waiting. Behind him: three feet of merlon, then a fifteen-foot drop to the Needle Pass below. He watches you clear the ladder and does not lower the bow.

#### Features

- **Ledge geometry:** 10-foot-wide patrol ledge, battlements on the outer (west) face, wall-face on the inner side. The iron ring ladder from Room 02 exits here at the south end. The battlement traverse to Arrow-Walk (0022-06) exits at the north end. Wil occupies the center — approximately 20 feet from the ladder, 20 feet from the traverse. Both exits are possible escape routes; his preferred route is over the west-facing merlon (the wall ledge drop to the Needle Pass below).

- **Wall ledge (escape route):** The west-facing merlons are 3 feet high with 2-foot gaps. The outer face drops approximately 15 feet to the packed earth of the Needle Pass (not siege-line territory; the safe side). Escaping over this ledge requires **DC 14 Athletics** — the drop is manageable but the merlon is awkward to vault while under pressure. *[This is Caelith's Portent moment: see Portent design note below.]*

- **Crossbow position:** Wil has full line of sight down the iron ring ladder and across the ledge. He has 3/4 cover from the inner wall-face (+5 AC) while maintaining his crossbow position. He is not in cover from the battlement traverse approach (Path B): a party member coming from Arrow-Walk arrives behind his cover position.

- **Wil's opening:** He does not fire immediately. He says: *"I know what you found. I know what it looks like."* He is watching their faces to see if they know about his mother. If they do not address the coercion, he fights. If any PC names the Varduin address, he hesitates — a visible hesitation, crossbow still raised, but the decision is no longer made.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

**Portent design (Caelith's mandate — this session must fire):**

Wil's escape attempt is the designed Portent moment. When cornered — his crossbow position is broken, or the party blocks both exit paths — Wil makes a decision to vault the west merlon and drop to the Needle Pass below. This requires **DC 14 Athletics**.

**The Portent condition:** Caelith replaces Wil's Athletics roll with Portent die [7]. (7 < 14 = Wil fails.) He clips the merlon, drops awkwardly, and is caught — either he falls prone below the wall where a PC can reach him, or he lands badly and is grappled. He does not escape.

**Framing guidance:** Caelith has carried [6, 7] for three sessions. His player should have the fictional framing ready: *"I see this moment before it happens."* Log the exact words verbatim in the session log. This is the first in-fiction Portent deployment — it is a campaign-permanent beat.

**If Caelith does not volunteer the Portent:** The GM should create a beat where Wil's roll is visible and pending — announce "Wil attempts to vault the merlon, DC 14 Athletics" and pause. This gives Caelith's player the space to intervene before the roll. Do not roll for Wil until Caelith has had the opportunity to respond.

**If Wil escapes (contingency — Portent not used):** Wil drops to the Needle Pass and runs south. He knows the terrain; he has 7 years on this wall. # Hessa Var (Gate-Captain)

## Appearance

Forties. Compact, steady-handed. Gate-Captain's insignia on her collar. She carries a ledger at her hip — not paperwork, habit. Her face does not show what she knows. You can tell that she has had practice keeping it that way.

## Role in this session

Hessa gives the party the investigation and holds back her own exposure until Scene 4 (Disclosure). She is testing the party's judgment in Scene 1: will they proceed on trust before she reveals her reason for asking them instead of Asholt? She already suspects the contractor network's involvement — she just doesn't know yet that she is part of it.

## Public knowledge

- Gate-Captain Hessa Var is responsible for postern access — who goes in and out, when, under what authorization.
- She found the rotted arrow stock this morning on her inspection round.
- She did not go to Asholt. She came to the party.
- She has installed a countersignature protocol for night postern passages. The protocol exists because of a misattributed message six months ago.

## Private knowledge

- For six months before and during the siege, Hessa has been passing status updates through the postern night gate to a contractor-network contact. Supply levels, wall condition, shift rotations. She received coin.
- She believed the contact was a trade intermediary she knew from before the siege — someone who would sell the information to merchants adjusting their contracts, not to a military adversary.
- She did not know the contractor network was the same network that hired Dex for the supply cart ambush.
- She did not know her status updates created the intelligence picture that enabled the coercion of Wil Fen (the network used garrison shift and supply information to identify vulnerable garrison members).
- She does not know Wil was coerced through this network.

## Behavior matrix

### Scene 1 — The hook

She gives the investigation without explanation. She says: *"I have a reason for asking you instead of him. Find who did it first."*

If pressed: she does not give her reason. She is testing whether the party will act on trust. DC 12 Insight: she already suspects someone. DC 14 Insight: she suspects the contractor network specifically — her posture tightens when the subject of the postern log comes up.

She provides: (1) the duty roster with signatures and boot sizes; (2) access to the west wall base, west walk, and garrison quarters; (3) her own availability for Scene 4.

### Scene 4 — The disclosure (trigger: contractor device on letter seal shown to Hessa)

When the party shows her the letter seal (Room 03), she goes still.

*"I know that device."*

She tells them: six months of status updates. A contractor contact. Coin received. She did not ask questions she did not want answered. She stops herself mid-sentence and says: *"I need to tell the Warden myself. Give me four hours."*

**Four-hour request:** If the party grants it, she goes to Asholt and tells him everything within the window. He relieves her of gate command for 48 hours and reinstates her. The token condition is met.

**If the party reports immediately:** Hessa confesses anyway — she goes to Asholt herself, immediately, rather than waiting. But the relationship is closed. She trusts their competence; she no longer trusts their judgment.

### What Hessa tells Asholt (whether party grants four hours or not)

She tells him everything: six months, the contact, the coin, the specific types of information she passed. She does not minimize. She does not ask him not to relieve her. She says: *"I knew what I was doing and I told myself it didn't matter. That's the part I can't fix by telling him."*

This line is spoken to Asholt. The party may or may not hear it — depending on whether they are present.

## Arc-Completion

**Arc-completion line (campaign-permanent):**

*"I knew what I was doing and I told myself it didn't matter. That's the part I can't fix by telling him."*

Spoken to Asholt, in the Warden's office, after she has told him everything.

**Arc-completion condition:** She goes to Asholt voluntarily, within four hours, without being compelled, and gives him the complete account. This requires the party granting the four-hour window.

**What closes the arc:** She does not ask for mercy. She does not hedge. She names her own complicity — not the contractor's manipulation of her, not her belief that it was harmless — but the specific choice to not ask questions. That is the arc: from *"I told myself it didn't matter"* to saying aloud that she knew.

**Token condition:** Asholt reinstates her 48 hours later (start of S4). She gives the party her token — a gate-key token, a flat iron disc with the postern device on one face — at the start of the next session.

## Information-holder

Hessa holds one piece of intelligence not available from the letter or the investigation: the contractor contact's name. She gave the information; she knows who she gave it to. She will give this name to the party after her arc-completion — after she has told Asholt and been reinstated. Before that, she is not ready to name the contact (she is still processing her own exposure). After: *"His name is Orren. He's not Reconstitution. He works for whoever pays cleanly."*

This is Seed 0025/0026 material: Orren is a contractor handler, not a field operative. His identity does not resolve in this session.

## Notes for future sessions

- Hessa's gate command is restored at S4 start (48-hour relief).
- The token she gives is campaign-significant: she is the third token, and she gives it after holding herself accountable rather than after proving competence.
- Her arc-completion line is verbatim above. Do not paraphrase it.

# Wil Fen (Garrison Watcher)

## Appearance

Thirty-four. Medium build, seven years of wall-watch in his posture — he stands with his back to whatever wall is behind him, not from training, from habit. His crossbow bolts are aligned in their quiver: he counts them before every watch. He looks like someone who has been waiting for something to happen and it has now happened.

## Role in this session

Wil is not a villain. He is a garrison member who committed deliberate sabotage under coercion. He chose his mother over the garrison. He knows this will destroy his life at Thorngate; he made the calculation anyway. When the party finds him, he fights because he believes being taken means his mother loses her protection — not because he wants to hurt anyone.

He is the session's moral hinge, not its antagonist.

## Public knowledge

- Wil Fen is a crossbow-trained wall-watcher with a seven-year service record at Thorngate. No disciplinary history. Reliable.
- He had the dawn watch two nights ago (postern log).
- He has a mother at Varduin-by-the-Pines.

## Private knowledge

- Two days before the session, Wil received a message — passed through the postern gate to him specifically, by name. His mother's address in Varduin. The demand: disable the west wall arrow stock before the next assault. The window: two days.
- He does not know who sent the message. He knows the seal device on the letter. He knows what the device means: the same contractor network that has been passing messages through the postern for months (he has seen the seal on other correspondence that passed through the gate).
- He did not volunteer. He did not look for a way out. He had two days and he used them.
- He applied the fungal compound himself — he sources it from the pass below during perimeter walks; Orrwood mold grows on the north-facing rock faces at lower elevation. He applied it with a brush from the garrison's maintenance kit.
- He has not told anyone. Not Hessa, not another garrison member.

## Coercion terms

The message specified: if the west wall cache is disabled before the next assault, his mother would not be bothered. No specific threat named — just "the arrangement changes." The letter is now partially burned in his cold hearth (Room 03).

**What "the arrangement changes" means:** Wil does not know exactly. He did not ask. The people who passed the message through the postern have been there for months; his mother is at a specific address in Varduin; the network demonstrated they knew the address. He chose not to find out what the alternative looked like.

## Fighting behavior

Wil fights from the upper ledge (Room 04) with his crossbow. He uses his wall position for cover and his Cunning Step to maintain distance. He is trying to stay free, not to kill the party — he fires to wound, not to down (GM note: he targets PCs who are closing, not spellcasters in the back).

**Escape attempt:** When reduced to 9 HP or below, or when two PCs are in melee range, Wil attempts to vault the west merlon (DC 14 Athletics). He has practiced this — he knows the drop angle, the landing zone. He was prepared to use it.

**Caelith's Portent fires here:** See `encounters/saboteur-confrontation.md` and `rooms/04-west-wall-confrontation.md`.

## When he stops fighting

Wil stops fighting when **both** conditions are met:

1. His escape attempt fails (he is still on the ledge, or he has been caught below).
2. Any PC names his mother's situation — specifically, names the Varduin address or says words to the effect of *"we know about your mother"* or *"we read the letter."*

**What stopping looks like:** He does not lower the crossbow when the escape fails. He lowers it after a PC speaks. Then he sits down, back to the inner wall-face. He says: *"She's at Varduin. I had two days."* He does not elaborate unless asked. He is not relieved — he is emptied out.

**If the party does not name the coercion:** He fights until incapacitated. When he comes around, he gives the same information — but not as a decision. He tells them because he is too tired not to.

## Arc-completion

**Arc-completion condition:** A PC names his mother's situation to his face, before or during fighting. He stops fighting voluntarily.

**Arc-completion line:**

*"She's at Varduin. I had two days."*

This is the first thing he says when he lowers the crossbow. It is the whole story in two sentences. Campaign-permanent if spoken in this context.

**What the arc-completion means:** He chose to trust the party with the one thing he was protecting by fighting. He does not ask them to protect his mother (he does not believe they can). He tells them because they knew to ask.

## Information-holder

Wil holds one piece of intelligence beyond the investigation evidence: **the contractor network's message drop point inside the Needle Pass.**

The message that coerced him was left at a specific location — a stone marker on the north face of the Needle Pass, south of the postern, at the base of the rock cut. Messages are placed and retrieved from a hollowed stone at this marker. This is how the contractor contact communicated with both Hessa (unknowing intermediary) and with Wil (direct coercion).

**Readiness condition:** Wil gives this information if the party treats him as a person rather than a prisoner — specifically, if any PC acknowledges the coercion context before asking what he knows. The question *"Where did the message come from?"* asked after naming his mother receives the answer. The same question asked as an interrogation does not.

*"There's a stone marker, south of the postern, north face of the pass. Messages go in a hollow at the base. I don't know who checks it."*

This is Seed 0025/0026 material: the message drop point is a physical location that can be watched.

- He cannot continue his garrison service at Thorngate regardless of the party's advocacy (Asholt will require it; the sabotage is unambiguous). His future is not in the party's hands; his treatment in the reporting is.

## Portent Condition (Campaign Mandate)

**Caelith's Portent [7] fires on Wil's escape attempt.**

**Trigger:** Wil declares the escape attempt (action to vault the merlon). Before the Athletics roll is made, the GM announces the DC (14) and pauses. This pause is the Portent window.

**Caelith's action:** He may replace Wil's roll with his Portent die [7]. 7 < 14 = Wil fails. He clips the merlon, loses his footing on the outer face, and drops badly — landing prone at the base of the wall (inner or outer side, GM's choice based on positioning). He takes 1d6 falling damage (half drop) and is prone.

**In-voice framing:** *"I see this moment before it happens."* Log verbatim. This is the first in-fiction Portent deployment in C4; it is campaign-permanent. The [6] die is carried to S4.

**If Portent is not offered:** Roll Wil's Athletics openly. On 14+, he escapes (GM note: he can still be caught in the Needle Pass). On 13-, he clips the merlon and falls. In either case, continue the session.
