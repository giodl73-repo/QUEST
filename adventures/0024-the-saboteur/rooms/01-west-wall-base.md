# Room 01 — West Wall Base

## Read-aloud

> Stone alcove, west wall base. Two arrow racks — one intact, one wrong. Lower-tier shafts grey-green where they should be pale, wood soft at the grip. On the postern lectern, the night log lies open to this morning. Hessa stands behind you, saying nothing yet.

## Features

- **Arrow cache (two racks):** Upper rack — 100 arrows, intact, oiled, serviceable. Lower rack — 100 arrows, approximately half showing shaft-rot: grey-green discoloration, soft grain, will snap on draw. The rot is localized to the lower-tier rack only. **DC 12 Survival (Talis):** The rot is targeted — the lower rack is the middle-tier storage rotation, the first rack drawn during an assault. Someone with garrison knowledge picked the highest-impact rack and applied the compound deliberately. This is not accidental attrition. *[Talis spotlight: if she makes this check, she can identify the application method — brush-applied, not organic spread.]*

- **Postern night log:** A leather-bound log on the stone lectern by the postern door. Entries in multiple hands — every postern passage recorded. **DC 14 Investigation (any PC):** Two days ago, an entry reads "maintenance — west wall postern access, dawn watch" with no countersignature. The handwriting does not match Hessa's duty notation style. Cross-referencing the duty roster Hessa provides: the handwriting matches **Wil Fen's duty signature** — a crossbow-trained wall-watcher who had the dawn watch that night. *[Fallback: if DC 14 is missed here, Talis finds Wil's boot-print near the lectern in Room 02.]*

- **Hessa's opening:** She does not explain her reason for asking the party instead of Asholt until Scene 4. If pressed here, she says: *"I have a reason. Find who did it first."* DC 12 Insight: she already suspects someone; she is not certain; she is testing whether the investigation confirms or contradicts her suspicion.

## Memory Fragment

> The log has been kept since the siege began. Every entry is written the same way — time, name, purpose, countersignature. The missing countersignature is the loudest thing in the room.

## GM Notes

**Scene 1 is Talis's scene.** She reads the physical evidence; let her describe what she finds. If she makes the DC 12 Survival check, give her the full read: targeted application, lower rack specifically, garrison knowledge required. This is the investigation's first proof that the saboteur is internal, not external.

**The log discrepancy:** The DC 14 Investigation is the session's first direct pointer to Wil Fen by name. If the party misses it here, the postern log's value as evidence is not lost — the boot-print in Room 02 (DC 12 Perception) provides the same connection. Do not force the log; let the rolls determine sequence.

**Hessa's position:** She is standing back, watching the party work. She has already looked at the log this morning. She knows the handwriting — she just does not say so yet. If a PC brings the duty roster to compare handwriting in her presence, DC 12 Insight: she does not look surprised. She was waiting for someone to do that.

**Countersignature protocol:** Garrison postern entries require a second officer to countersign night passages. Wil's entry is unsigned. The protocol exists specifically because Hessa installed it after a message was misattributed six months ago. The party can learn this from any garrison officer who isn't Hessa. The implication — Wil knew the protocol would be checked, and did not care enough (or was too rushed) to forge it — is left for the party to surface.
