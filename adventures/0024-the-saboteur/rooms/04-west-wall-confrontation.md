# Room 04 — West Wall Upper Ledge

## Read-aloud

> The upper ledge faces west. Wil Fen is here — crossbow raised, back to the inner wall-face. He has been waiting. Behind him: three feet of merlon, then a fifteen-foot drop to the Needle Pass below. He watches you clear the ladder and does not lower the bow.

## Features

- **Ledge geometry:** 10-foot-wide patrol ledge, battlements on the outer (west) face, wall-face on the inner side. The iron ring ladder from Room 02 exits here at the south end. The battlement traverse to Arrow-Walk (0022-06) exits at the north end. Wil occupies the center — approximately 20 feet from the ladder, 20 feet from the traverse. Both exits are possible escape routes; his preferred route is over the west-facing merlon (the wall ledge drop to the Needle Pass below).

- **Wall ledge (escape route):** The west-facing merlons are 3 feet high with 2-foot gaps. The outer face drops approximately 15 feet to the packed earth of the Needle Pass (not siege-line territory; the safe side). Escaping over this ledge requires **DC 14 Athletics** — the drop is manageable but the merlon is awkward to vault while under pressure. *[This is Caelith's Portent moment: see Portent design note below.]*

- **Crossbow position:** Wil has full line of sight down the iron ring ladder and across the ledge. He has 3/4 cover from the inner wall-face (+5 AC) while maintaining his crossbow position. He is not in cover from the battlement traverse approach (Path B): a party member coming from Arrow-Walk arrives behind his cover position.

- **Wil's opening:** He does not fire immediately. He says: *"I know what you found. I know what it looks like."* He is watching their faces to see if they know about his mother. If they do not address the coercion, he fights. If any PC names the Varduin address, he hesitates — a visible hesitation, crossbow still raised, but the decision is no longer made.

## Memory Fragment

> The upper ledge has the best view of the Needle Pass below — you can see the road to Varduin from here, in clear weather, as a pale line through the pass. He would have stood here every watch and looked at it.

## GM Notes

**Portent design (Caelith's mandate — this session must fire):**

Wil's escape attempt is the designed Portent moment. When cornered — his crossbow position is broken, or the party blocks both exit paths — Wil makes a decision to vault the west merlon and drop to the Needle Pass below. This requires **DC 14 Athletics**.

**The Portent condition:** Caelith replaces Wil's Athletics roll with Portent die [7]. (7 < 14 = Wil fails.) He clips the merlon, drops awkwardly, and is caught — either he falls prone below the wall where a PC can reach him, or he lands badly and is grappled. He does not escape.

**Framing guidance:** Caelith has carried [6, 7] for three sessions. His player should have the fictional framing ready: *"I see this moment before it happens."* Log the exact words verbatim in the session log. This is the first in-fiction Portent deployment — it is a campaign-permanent beat.

**If Caelith does not volunteer the Portent:** The GM should create a beat where Wil's roll is visible and pending — announce "Wil attempts to vault the merlon, DC 14 Athletics" and pause. This gives Caelith's player the space to intervene before the roll. Do not roll for Wil until Caelith has had the opportunity to respond.

**If Wil escapes (contingency — Portent not used):** Wil drops to the Needle Pass and runs south. He knows the terrain; he has 7 years on this wall. The party would need to pursue via the postern door — losing 1-2 rounds. Note in session log; the confrontation continues in the Needle Pass exterior. His capture is still the design intent; adjust as needed.

**Early confrontation (party comes here before Room 03):** If the party reaches Room 04 before searching Room 03, they have the physical evidence (log discrepancy, boot-print, fungal compound) but not the coercion letter. The confrontation can still happen — Wil's motivation is still discoverable through dialogue if the party takes him alive. However, the coercion reveal will come from Wil himself rather than from the letter Davan holds. Note in session log which sequence occurred; both paths are valid.
