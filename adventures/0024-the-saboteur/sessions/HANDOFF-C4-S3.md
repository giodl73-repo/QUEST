## Design obligations for S4

**Must address (from panel + handoff):**

1. **Talis character-specific beat.** Three sessions of Ranger-competency without a Talis-Oremm-specific moment. Her grief-paragraph and Asha's cord have not been touched. The lower keep assault should create one beat where Talis's specific history makes contact with what she sees. *Design note: refugees in the lower keep. Talis's grief-paragraph almost certainly involves someone she was supposed to protect. If a refugee is in a position that mirrors that situation, her sheet activates.*

2. **Caelith's notebook: design the scene where it opens.** The contractor drop point is intelligence the party needs. S4 should include a natural moment where Caelith decides whether to share it — not a forced reveal, but a situation where withholding it would cost something visible. His institutional-analysis heuristic will decide; the module needs to create the pressure.

3. **Caelith Portent [6] must have a designed use case.** A die of 6 is below AC for most garrison combatants (AC 13-18) but useful against enemy saving throws (DC 8-12 range) or contested ability checks. Design S4's encounter with at least one condition where a Portent [6] replaces an enemy's saving throw or low-DC check. If Caelith voices the Portent before firing again (I-C4-S3-02 second instance), the v1.4 cross-character compound path is open.

4. **Encounter pressure.** S3 combat was 0 party damage; S2 combat was 5 HP damage to Davan. S4 garrison potion is available; it should be used. Design the lower keep encounter to produce meaningful HP cost. Supply Die is d6; garrison is visibly thin. The encounter should feel it.

5. **Davan Oath collision named aloud.** S4 is the spotlight adventure for Davan. His Oath says the Measure is the Measure — correct evidence, correct action, correct consequence. The lower keep assault creates conditions where the Measure's correctness may harm people Davan did not intend to harm. The accounting speech's second layer is when Davan names the collision aloud — not in the corridor, but to another person.

6. **Dene's first-response-human-not-tactical token condition.** Dene Flor's token fires when her first response to the refugees is human, not tactical. The lower keep must put her in that situation. She will be there when the refugees are found; the module must give her a beat.

7. **Hint 2 auto-delivery.** The lower keep door opens during assault chaos. Sava heard sounds from below in S1. When the door opens, H2 delivers: the refugees are here. This is an automatic delivery — no check required if the party enters the lower keep. Ensure the module flags this as an auto-delivery on door-open.

8. **Reconstitution doctrinal texture.** Final flag from Lorekeeper. One line from a Reconstitution member or a document the party finds — what they believe about Solamnic authority, what the keep represents to them. Not a lore lecture; one sentence from a character who holds the belief.
