## Scenes

### Scene 3.1 — Hessa's Request

Hessa found the party before morning briefing. She did not go to Asholt. She showed them the west wall arrow cache — half the shafts destroyed by fungal rot, applied by brush, systematic. She said: *"I need you to find who did it before the Warden does. I have a reason for asking you instead of him."* She would not say the reason.

### Scene 3.2 — The Investigation (Rooms 01-03)

**🎲 Talis Survival DC 12 (targeted rot)** — `1d20+4` → rolls=[17] mod=+4 **total=21 ✓**

Talis reads the cache in ninety seconds. *"This isn't rot — it's applied. Middle-tier racks only, consistent with knowing the rotation. Someone who lives here did this fast and knew where to look."* She does not editorialize further.

**🎲 Caelith Investigation DC 14 (postern log)** — `1d20+5` → rolls=[15] mod=+5 **total=20 ✓**

The maintenance log entry. Two days ago. The handwriting: Caelith cross-references against the duty roster Hessa provides. *"Wil Fen. West wall watch, seven years."* He closes the log.

**🎲 Talis Perception DC 12 (boot-print near log)** — `1d20+4` → rolls=[5] mod=+4 **total=9 (miss)**

She looks at the floor near the log lectern and doesn't find what she is looking for.

**🎲 Pressure roll** — `1d6` → rolls=[2] **total=2 (low — nothing fires)**

**🎲 Caelith Investigation DC 13 (burned letter, Wil's bunk)** — `1d20+5` → rolls=[12] mod=+5 **total=17 ✓**

The cold hearth. A letter, half-burned, recoverable. The coercion terms: his mother's address in Varduin-by-the-Pines, the demand to disable the west wall cache, a two-day window. The seal's device on the surviving corner: the contractor's token device — the same one described in the S2 choke point context. Same network.

Caelith reads it twice. He does not comment. He hands it to Davan. <!-- SURPRISE: Caelith hands the evidence to Davan rather than Sava or Talis — his institutional-analysis reads that this decision belongs to the person who understands what correct evidence does to a person -->

### Scene 3.4 — Hessa's Disclosure

Hessa is told. The party shows her the contractor device on the letter seal.

She goes still. *"I know that device."*

She tells them about the messages — garrison status updates, six months, before the siege. She told herself it was harmless. The coin helped. She trails off and says: *"I need to tell the Warden myself. Give me four hours."*

The party gives her four hours. They don't discuss it. It is the correct thing.

Hessa goes to Asholt. Tells him everything. He relieves her of gate command for 48 hours — not a punishment, a procedure. He reinstates her the following morning.

**Hessa's arc-completion fires:**

After reinstatement, she finds Sava at the healer's station. She gives the token to Sava rather than to the party collectively — she trusts Sava to carry things correctly. She says:

*"I knew what I was doing and I told myself it didn't matter. That's the part I can't fix by telling him."*

She doesn't wait for a response. She goes back to the gate.

**Hessa's token: EARNED.** ✓

### Scene 3.5 — Davan's Moment (corridor)

Between Hessa's disclosure and the report to Asholt, Davan stands in the corridor outside Wil's bunk room holding the half-burned letter.

The correct evidence. A man who didn't want to be guilty. His mother's address in a village Davan knows.

He stands there longer than he needs to. He is not deciding what to do with the letter — it goes to Asholt, of course. He is deciding something else.

He puts the letter in his breast pocket, beside Tomek's token. Not to conceal it. To carry it correctly.

**PC-authored accounting speech — first content:** *"The cost of correct evidence."* Note in handoff from this session forward.
