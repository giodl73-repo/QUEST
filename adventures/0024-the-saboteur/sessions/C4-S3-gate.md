## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: I-C4-S3-01 — Combat-end through naming rather than damage
Dimension affected: Character Agency / Atmospheric Landing
What happened: Scene 3.3 — Davan says "We found the letter. We know about Varduin. We know about your mother.
We know you didn't want to do this." Wil stops. Combat ends. Two rounds, 0 party damage, Wil alive at 12/18 HP.
Wil's dual stop-condition (escape fails AND coercion named) fired simultaneously.
Why the rubric missed it: Character Agency 10-band anchors on "the choice was correct per character sheet, not per
optimal play." This is that. But it is also a new TYPE: a combat-resolution mechanic replaced by a speech act.
The naming IS the mechanical resolution. Not act-without-announcement (which is cross-session behavioral
consequence without speech); not sustained behavioral pattern (which is multi-scene inaction). This is
active combat-end through naming — one specific sentence, at the correct moment, from the correct PC.
Different from S3's Mira faction-pivot (C2-S03; Route D offer as combat-adjacent), which was pre-combat.
This is mid-combat, Round 2, with a live enemy at 12 HP, grappled but capable of further action.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: I-C4-S3-02 — Portent voiced before firing
Dimension affected: Mechanical Fairness / Surprise
What happened: Scene 3.3 — Caelith holds his action and says "I see this coming" before the Portent fires.
He is holding the die. He announces the preparation before the deployment. When Wil's escape would succeed
(natural 15+3=18), the Portent [7] replaces it (total 10 < DC 14). The mechanic had been carried for three
sessions; this is the first time it has a voice in the fiction.
Why the rubric missed it: Mechanical Fairness at 9+ requires mechanics that compound narratively. The current
anchor is single-character framed (a PC's feature shaping their own rolls). The v1.4 cross-character compound
(PC applies feature to ANOTHER PC's roll) was ratified from Portent-as-benediction (Kessa → Grom). This is
different: the same mechanic is announced in-voice BEFORE deployment, giving it fiction-layer framing for the
first time. The announcement makes the Portent visible as a character-specific act rather than a mechanical
substitution. Possible rubric anchor for future cross-character Portent (v1.4 compound path).
```
