## Critique

### Overall assessment

Davan's session. Fully, properly his. The campaign has been building toward a moment where his tribunal-grief — the experience of watching correct evidence destroy a person — makes contact with the present. Scene 3.5 (the corridor) is that contact: he holds the burned letter and Tomek's token in the same breast pocket, carrying both pieces of evidence correctly. He does not announce what this means. He does not need to. The Roleplayer's only concern is that Sava and Talis are doing strong work within their established registers but haven't broken new ground since their respective arc beats in S1 and S2. Three sessions in, two PCs have had their moments; two are still operating from their established mode rather than extending it. That is not a failure at session 3 — but it will be a failure by session 5 if the module doesn't pull Talis and Caelith into territory their sheets haven't shown us yet.

### What worked

- **Scene 3.5 — Davan in the corridor:** *"He puts the letter in his breast pocket, beside Tomek's token. Not to conceal it. To carry it correctly."* This is the session's best beat. The letter and the token are both pieces of evidence about people who were in impossible positions — Wil coerced, Tomek watching the garrison he loves atrophy. Davan carries them the same way. His character sheet grief-paragraph (the tribunal; accurate testimony that cost someone; the Oath that tells him this was correct) is present in this gesture without being named. The accounting speech begins here. *"The cost of correct evidence."* The Roleplayer does not need to hear it explained; the corridor scene says it.

- **Scene 3.4 — Hessa's token to Sava:** *"I knew what I was doing and I told myself it didn't matter. That's the part I can't fix by telling him."* This is the session's second-best line and it belongs to an NPC. Hessa's arc-completion fires from character logic, not from the module telling the DM to deliver it. She says the thing she could not say to Asholt — the thing reinstatement can't fix — to Sava, not to the party, because Sava is the person who carries things correctly. The healer-to-healer trust chain (Maret S2, Hessa S3) is the session's structural throughline and it landed. Hessa gives the token to the right person without explaining why. The Roleplayer notes: this is two consecutive sessions where the garrison's named healer recognizes Sava's competence before any other PC's. That is not random. That is character-sheet-deep.

### What needs work

- **Talis in Scenes 3.2 and 3.3 — competent, not Talis:** Talis leads the investigation in Scene 3.2 (Survival 21, reads the rot in ninety seconds) and fires her shortbow in Scene 3.3 (miss, total 11 vs AC 13). Both actions are correct for the situation. Neither requires Talis Oremm, specifically. Her character sheet has content — Asha's cord, her grief-paragraph around what happened to the person she was supposed to protect, her specific register around evidence and what happens to people who don't believe in it. None of that touched Scene 3.2 or 3.3. The rot-reading is a Ranger's competency read; the shortbow miss is a dice outcome. By session 4, Talis needs a beat that requires her sheet, not just her class.

- **Caelith's institutional-analysis is consistent but unexplored:** Scene 3.2 (hands evidence to Davan) is the session's most interesting Caelith moment and it happens in a comment annotation, not in described action. *"His institutional-analysis reads that this decision belongs to the person who understands what correct evidence does to a person."* That framing is correct and specific to Caelith. But it is DM-narrated interiority, not shown behavior. The handoff notes that Caelith has not shared the contractor message drop point with the party — this is intelligence he is holding, alone, in his notebook. The Roleplayer wants to know: why? What is Caelith's relationship to information that should be shared but isn't? His character sheet presumably has something to say about this. If S4 doesn't surface it, the notebook becomes an unexamined secret rather than a character beat.

### The question I'd push on

Hessa says *"I knew what I was doing and I told myself it didn't matter. That's the part I can't fix by telling him."* She does not wait for a response. She goes back to the gate. Sava receives the token and the line. Does Sava respond, internally or externally? The session log does not record it. The Roleplayer's concern is the same as C4-S2 (Maret's token placed beside suture kit without announcement): the act-without-announcement is correct and specific. But Sava now has two tokens — Maret's and Hessa's — from two garrison healers who recognized her specifically. At what point does Sava acknowledge this to herself, even privately? The Roleplayer is not asking for a scene announcement. But a single line of Sava's interiority — not what she does with the tokens, but what she holds about them — would close the loop on two consecutive healer-recognition beats without violating the restraint that makes them work.
