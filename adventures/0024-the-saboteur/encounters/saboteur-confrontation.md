# Encounter — Saboteur Confrontation

## Overview

Wil Fen is a crossbow-trained garrison wall-watcher who has served at Thorngate for seven years. He did not volunteer to commit sabotage; he was coerced under threat to his mother in Varduin. He knows the party has been investigating. He is on the upper ledge, back to the wall, crossbow raised. He fights not from ideology but from the belief that being taken means his mother loses her protection.

**Design intent:** This combat should cost 20-30% of party HP total (~23-28 HP across four PCs with 97 total HP at session start). Sava spends 1-2 L1 slots. The Supply Die degrades to d6. Wil survives — taken alive for the coercion reveal and the information-holder beat. Caelith's Portent [7] fires here.

**XP budget:** 1 × Garrison Watcher (CR 1/2, 100 XP) = 100 XP × 1.5 (solo modifier) = **150 adjusted XP.** Easy-to-Medium (party is L3, 4 PCs). The difficulty is tactical: crossbow position + wall geometry + escape-or-fight tension.

## Wil Fen — Garrison Watcher (CR 1/2, 100 XP)

*Coerced garrison member. Not a villain. Crossbow focus, wall positioning, desperate.*

**AC** 14 (chain shirt + Dex) · **HP** 18 (4d8) · **Speed** 30 ft.

| Str | Dex | Con | Int | Wis | Cha |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 12 (+1) | 14 (+2) | 10 (+0) | 10 (+0) | 12 (+1) | 9 (−1) |

**Saves:** Dex +4, Wis +3
**Skills:** Perception +3, Athletics +3, Stealth +4
**Senses:** passive Perception 13
**Languages:** Common

**Heavy Crossbow.** +4 to hit, range 100/400 ft., 1d10+2 piercing. (Reload: can only fire once per turn unless he uses his action to reload; he carries 12 bolts.)
**Shortsword.** +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., 1d6+1 piercing. (Fallback if party closes to melee.)

**Wall Position (passive):** While occupying his prepared position (inner wall-face, 3/4 cover), Wil has +5 AC against attacks from the ladder approach. He loses this bonus against attackers coming from the battlement traverse (Arrow-Walk path).

**Cunning Step (bonus action):** Once per round, Wil may use a bonus action to Disengage or take the Dodge action. He uses this to maintain distance and position the escape attempt.

**Escape Attempt:** When Wil is reduced to 9 HP or below OR when his cover position is broken, he uses his action to attempt to vault the west merlon (DC 14 Athletics) and drop to the Needle Pass (15-foot drop, 1d6 falling damage on success). See Portent condition below.

## Portent Condition (Campaign Mandate)

**Caelith's Portent [7] fires on Wil's escape attempt.**

**Trigger:** Wil declares the escape attempt (action to vault the merlon). Before the Athletics roll is made, the GM announces the DC (14) and pauses. This pause is the Portent window.

**Caelith's action:** He may replace Wil's roll with his Portent die [7]. 7 < 14 = Wil fails. He clips the merlon, loses his footing on the outer face, and drops badly — landing prone at the base of the wall (inner or outer side, GM's choice based on positioning). He takes 1d6 falling damage (half drop) and is prone.

**In-voice framing:** *"I see this moment before it happens."* Log verbatim. This is the first in-fiction Portent deployment in C4; it is campaign-permanent. The [6] die is carried to S4.

**If Portent is not offered:** Roll Wil's Athletics openly. On 14+, he escapes (GM note: he can still be caught in the Needle Pass). On 13-, he clips the merlon and falls. In either case, continue the session.

## Positioning (Upper Ledge)

**Round 1:**
- Wil: center of ledge, inner wall-face, 3/4 cover (ladder approach only). Crossbow ready.
- Party: arriving via ladder (south) or battlement traverse (north).
- Wil fires at the first PC who clears the ladder or traverse.

**Round 2+:**
- Wil tracks the nearest threat with crossbow. He does not move from cover unless a PC reaches melee range — then he uses Cunning Step to disengage and back toward the escape merlon.

**Escape threshold:** Wil attempts the escape (vault west merlon) when:
- He drops to 9 HP or below, OR
- Two PCs are within melee range simultaneously (he can't disengage from both).

## Tactical notes

**Talis on the upper ledge:** Her Shortbow has range advantage on this target. She does not have Wil's cover penalty from this position. She can fire from the battlement traverse (Path B) before Wil knows she's there — **Advantage** on her first attack if she approaches via Arrow-Walk without announcing.

**Caelith's positioning:** He should be set back — the ledge is 10 feet wide and long-range from the ladder access. His cantrips have full range. The Portent window is the critical beat; he does not need to be in danger to use it.

**Davan at the front:** He is the melee anchor — drawing Wil's attention and crossbow fire while the party flanks. Wil will prioritize Davan because he represents the most direct capture threat. **Expected HP cost: Davan takes 1-2 crossbow hits (1d10+2 each, expected 7-8 HP per hit). At 28/28 entering combat, Davan should exit at ~12-18 HP.**

**Sava's resource moment:** One crossbow hit on a secondary PC (likely Caelith or Sava if they close range) is the designed healing trigger. With 3×L1 slots and 1 garrison potion, Sava's conservation decision matters. If the combat costs 25+ party HP total, the 1-potion limit is real pressure.

## Morale

**Wil stops fighting when:**
1. His escape attempt fails (Portent or failed Athletics), AND
2. Any PC names his mother's situation — specifically: names the Varduin address, or says words to the effect of "we know about your mother" or "we read the letter."

**What "stopping fighting" looks like:** He does not lower the crossbow first. He lowers it after a PC speaks. Then he sits down on the ledge, back to the inner wall-face. He does not run. He is done.

**What he says:** *"She's at Varduin. I had two days."* That is the first sentence. Everything else follows from there, if the party asks.

**If the party does not name the coercion:** Wil fights until incapacitated. When incapacitated, the coercion reveal still happens — it is information the party needs, and Wil gives it when he is no longer fighting. But the tone is different: he gives it as a defeated man rather than a man who decided to trust.

## End states

**Wil taken alive (intended):** The party has a garrison member who committed deliberate sabotage under coercion, with a contractor-network connection, and a living connection to the threat on his mother. He gives the contractor network's message drop point if treated as a person. See `npcs/wil-fen.md` for information-holder conditions.

**Wil incapacitated (acceptable):** Same information recovery as above, different tone. Note in session log: party chose incapacitation over negotiation.

**Wil escaped (unintended — Portent not fired):** He knows where the party is. He does not leave the Keep — he has no exit strategy (his plan was always to be caught eventually; the sabotage is done). He is found in the Needle Pass or returns voluntarily. Handoff note: confrontation deferred.
