# Room 02 — First Landing

## Read-aloud

> Four gully dwarves on the landing, watching you come up the stairs. They don't move toward you. Behind them, another stairway rises. The walls here are covered in the same marks as below — but here, among the scratch-tallies, there are also small shrines: a stone, a cloth scrap, a candle. The practices of the nave congregation, reproduced here in the tower. The same kind of arrangement. A different kind of feeling.

*(73 words — trim:)*

> Four gully dwarves on the landing, watching. Behind them, stairs rise further. The walls have the same scratch-tallies as below — but also small shrines here: stone, cloth scrap, candle. The same arrangements as the nave. A different feeling to them, something taut.

*(45 words)*

## Features

- **Shrine arrangements** (three, along the east wall). The same form as the nave congregation's shrines — stones, cloth, candle — but something in their arrangement is tighter, more compressed. **DC 12 Insight** to read: these were not assembled in leisure; they were assembled by people who were also angry while assembling them. The practice and the resentment are both present in the same object. *Relic sign: eleven-and-twenty-six sign — one of the shrines has eleven small stones arranged separately from a larger group (twenty-six). This is not explained in the room; it is observed.*

- **North side passage** (connects to R4 Tally Wall Alcove). Clear and accessible from R2. **Sensory cue:** through the passage, the smell of something older — the tally marks near R4 are the oldest in the tower; the air around them is different.

- **The four congregation members** (non-combatants initially). They are watching. They will not attack unless Sharr commands or the party advances aggressively toward the upper stairs. If Renn uses Awakened Mind: they respond with images of waiting — long waiting, thirty years of it.

- **Sharr's voice** (from R3 above, before he descends if warned). He does not shout; he speaks in measured Common: *"Tell me why you're here."* He can hear the party from R3 through the open stairway.

## Encounter

**Initial contact** — social/tension. See `encounters/sharr-confrontation.md`. Sharr may speak from above (R3) before descending. The four congregation members on R2 are the first visible response to the warning from R1. They follow Sharr's lead.

**If the party pushes past R2 without answering Sharr:** The four congregation members escalate — one of them gets in front of the stairs. Not combat yet; but Sharr descends from R3 and the encounter becomes the full confrontation.

## Treasure

None directly. The eleven/twenty-six stone arrangement is the relic sign — evidence of the cord's arithmetic, not the cord itself.

## GM Notes

R2 is the session's social staging area. Sharr uses it as his extended space — he can hear from R3 and descend when he's ready.

**The shrines here matter.** The Bell Tower congregation performs the same devotional practices as the nave congregation. They are not a separate faith — they are the same faith in a different emotional register. When the party sees the shrine arrangements here after two sessions of seeing them in the nave and aisle, the comparison should land. The Nave's faith is practiced in grief and gratitude. The Tower's faith is practiced in grief and resentment. The form is identical.

**Dren's crack is close.** He is aware of the congregation's resentment from Veth's warning. When he sees the shrine arrangements here — the same as the nave's, but taut — the parallel to Aldis is closer. He doesn't say anything yet. The naming requires Sharr to articulate the specific resentment clearly. That happens in R3.
