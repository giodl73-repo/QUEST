# Room 06 — Bell Platform

## Read-aloud

> Open sky. The bell above you, enormous and still. The belfry's stone arches frame the four directions: north toward the unfinished nave roof; east toward the hills; south toward Mireth-on-the-Hill below; west toward the cathedral's scaffolding. The view from here is everything — the whole building, the whole town, the hill, the distance. Sharr is here if he climbed. If not, it is empty.

*(65 words — trim:)*

> Open belfry. The bell, enormous and still. Four stone arches frame the four directions — nave roof north, hills east, Mireth south, scaffolding west. The full building and the full town visible from here. Sharr is here if he retreated. If not: it is empty and the view is everything.

*(51 words)*

## Features

- **The bell** (above, hung correctly). The largest object in the building. Cast iron, suspended from a properly-made frame. The clapper is present. **DC 10 Perception** to confirm: there is no physical reason it cannot ring. It has never been rung in thirty years of congregation occupation. If a party member rings it — this is a session-defining decision. See GM Notes.

- **The view** (all four arches). The party can see: the Halted Spire's full unfinished shape from above (nave, gatehouse, the incomplete sections); Mireth-on-the-Hill below with the town's buildings visible; the hills to the east where the gully dwarf settlements are; the scaffolding on the cathedral's western face. This is the session's horizon-broadening moment — the full scope of what is at stake in the finale is visible.

- **Ledge and drop** (all four arches, 40 ft above ground). No railings. **DC 12 Dex save** if forcibly moved toward an arch opening; 4d6 bludgeoning damage on a fall. Sharr's Shove ability makes this relevant if combat happens here.

- **Sharr's final position** (if he retreated here). He stands near the north arch, looking at the nave below. He does not attack. He says: *"You can see all of it from here. I have been looking at it for thirty-two years."*

## Encounter

**Sharr at R6 (if retreated):** He is not fighting from here. His morale broke below 8 HP. He is not surrendering — he is done fighting. The party can still receive the cord here if they approach without aggression. He will give it to them if the bilateral arc fired at any point, even if it fired after combat began.

**The bell decision:** If a party member reaches for the bell's rope mechanism and rings it — this is the session's most significant off-book moment. The bell has not rung in thirty years. The congregation in the nave, the tower, the alcove, and anywhere else in the building will hear it. The nave congregation does not know what it means. The tower congregation does. What happens next is not scripted; the DM adjudicates based on whether the party has earned it. If the bilateral has fired and Sharr is present: he looks at the bell when it rings. He says nothing.

## Treasure

If Sharr is here: the promise-cord. He will give it when ready. The cord's reaction moment fires here if Dren or Renn holds it — a knot tightens over the approximate location of Aldis's insignia (Dren) or the warm copper coin (Renn).

## GM Notes

R6 is the session's possible climax — not its required one. The main arc fires in R3. R6 is where the full picture becomes visible.

**Ringing the bell.** This is not designed as a route-determining action, but it is a player-created moment with consequences. A party that rings the bell after the bilateral arc has fired, in Sharr's hearing, has produced something the module did not anticipate. The Improviser lens will flag this. The DM should not block it. Whatever it means — that is up to what has happened in this session.

**The view from R6.** The party can see everything from here. This is not an information-dump — it is a spatial understanding. Looking down at the nave from the bell platform, with the full building visible, is different from standing in the nave. The cathedral is enormous. The congregation has maintained it, and it is enormous, and they have done this for thirty years. That fact changes from abstract to physical when seen from R6.
