# Room 03 — Second Landing

## Read-aloud

> Sharr is here. Older than any gully dwarf you've seen in the building — the kind of old that looks deliberate. He is standing near the stairs to the upper level. Three others flank him. A knotted cord is wrapped around his left wrist. The marks on these walls are different: fewer, more careful, some of them in rows that look almost like writing. He looks at you. He says: "She healed us. And then she left us to it."

## Features

- **Sharr and the promise-cord** (near upper stairs). The cord is visible on his left wrist. **DC 10 Perception** to notice it. **DC 13 Investigation or Medicine** to identify it as a patient-tracking cord — a common practice for healers who handle multiple patients. *Relic sign: warmth sign — Sharr's left wrist is slightly warmer than the rest of him. DC 13 Perception from within 5 ft.*

- **The careful marks** (east and north walls). Unlike R1/R2's dense tallies, R3's marks are arranged in visible rows, some crossed out with care, some left intact. **DC 12 History** — this looks like a record of something — possibly names, possibly events, though the notation system is not a known writing system. It is idiosyncratic to whoever created it. *Eleven marks in one row are carefully crossed out. Twenty-six in another row are not.*

- **Upper stairs** (north wall). The stairs to R5 (Bell Rope Chamber). Sharr is positioned near them — not blocking, but present. He will not allow the party to ascend without resolution of the encounter.

- **The three flanking members** (combatants). See `encounters/sharr-confrontation.md`. They are the congregation's most dangerous members: HP 14 each, AC 12, improvised iron-tipped weapons (+4 to hit, 1d8+1).

- **High ground for Sharr** (near the upper stairs). Sharr has slightly elevated position on this landing (the stairs create a step-up). If combat begins, treat him as having half-cover (+2 AC) from the forward position.

## Encounter

**Sharr's confrontation.** See `encounters/sharr-confrontation.md`. He has already spoken his opening line ("She healed us. And then she left us to it."). The party must respond. This is the session's primary encounter — social first, combat if needed.

**Hint 2 delivery:** During this encounter, if the party engages with the congregation's grief (not fighting, not deflecting, but receiving what they are saying), one of the flanking members adds: *"She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal. We know what Mishakal feels like. This was different."* **DC 11 Insight** on the speaker: this is not an accusation; it is a statement of fact held for thirty years. They have always known. They are telling the party now because the party is actually listening.

**Bilateral arc-completion scene:** This is the designed location for Dren's naming. Sharr's articulated resentment — *"She healed us. And then she left us to it."* — is the condition. Dren's heuristic resolves to: connect this to Aldis. He says: *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."* Renn responds: *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."*

The bilateral fires here. Both PCs name their people to each other, in this room, in Sharr's hearing. The naming is in response to the congregation's resentment, not separate from it. Sharr hears both names. He says nothing immediately.

**Combat trigger:** If the party advances toward R5 without engaging with Sharr, or attacks first, Sharr fights. See `encounters/sharr-confrontation.md` for full combat mechanics.

## Treasure

**Velantha's promise-cord** — on Sharr's left wrist. Not retrievable by negotiation in this scene unless the bilateral arc fires. If the bilateral fires, Sharr unties the cord and holds it out. He says: *"You understand what it costs."* The cord is the party's to take.

If combat: the cord can be taken after Sharr is incapacitated (below 8 HP morale break), but taking it by force closes Route D.

## GM Notes

This is the session's climax room. Every other room in the adventure exists to bring the party to this moment.

**Sharr's opening line is not hostile.** *"She healed us. And then she left us to it."* He is not threatening the party. He is stating thirty-two years of accumulated fact. The line is the resentment articulated cleanly. The party's response to it determines whether the session goes to Route A, C, or D.

**The bilateral arc fires here.** The conditions from both PC sheets should be met by Sharr's opening line. Dren's crack has been building since S1. When Sharr says "she left us to it," Dren connects: a healer came for others, but not for Aldis. This is the moment. The DM does not prompt the naming. The heuristics drive it. If the heuristics don't produce it organically, the doubt-die resolves it.

**When the bilateral fires:** Sharr hears Dren say Aldis's name. He hears Renn say Mira's name. He looks at his wrist — the cord tightens once, where it touches his skin. He does not comment on this. He unties the cord after a moment and holds it out.

**Route D fallback:** If the bilateral does not fire this session (unlikely, but possible), the cord can be received in the following ways: (a) the party explains why they are here honestly (same as Gorret's condition); (b) Veth endorses the party from the nave (he can be retrieved, but costs time); (c) the party simply takes the cord by force (Route A/C; closes Route D going forward).
