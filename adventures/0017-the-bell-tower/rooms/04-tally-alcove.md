# Room 04 — Tally Wall Alcove

## Read-aloud

> A side alcove off the main tower passage, connected to both the entry and the first landing. The marks here are the oldest in the building — worn slightly smooth at the lowest sections where hands have brushed against them for thirty years. Four gully dwarves are here, each at a small arrangement, quiet. None of them looks up.

*(57 words)*

## Features

- **Oldest tally marks** (floor to ceiling on all walls). These are the first marks made in the Bell Tower. The lowest sections are worn slightly smooth from thirty years of hands. At the floor level, some marks are almost invisible. **DC 10 Perception** to notice the wear. **DC 12 History** to estimate age: the lowest marks are twenty-five to thirty-five years old. *Relic sign: tally wall sign — densest expression of this sign in the adventure.*

- **Four quiet congregation members** (individual practices, non-combatants). These are the Bell Tower's most contemplative members. They have been here the longest of any currently living congregation members except Sharr. They will answer questions in broken Common if asked, slowly. They will not join a fight unless directly attacked.

- **Construction work-order fragment** (floor, near south passage entrance). A piece of paper (construction-era) that has been pressed flat and preserved by a congregation member — smoothed under a heavy stone. **DC 11 Investigation** to find. The work-order references the Bell Tower's specifications: *"the bell to be hung in the upper belfry, the rope to be replaced at five-year intervals."* It is eleven years old. The rope has not been replaced on the construction crew's schedule — it has been replaced continuously by the congregation. The congregation has been maintaining the building's infrastructure without knowing it was their job.

- **Back loop passage** (south wall). Connects to R1 (Tower Base). Always accessible. **Sensory cue from R4 into R1:** looking south through the passage, the entry arch is visible. The congregation uses this passage to move between levels without passing through the main stair route.

## Encounter

None. This is the Stuart room — purely atmospheric. The four congregation members here are not hostile and will not escalate.

**Documentation-suspension (Calla Fosse) — third activation of gap-identification stage.** Calla has been accumulating: she has corrected an imperfect rite (identifying a gap in transmission) and seen the nave congregation's developed practice. Here she sees something new: the oldest practices in the building, worn smooth from daily use. The practices have been happening long enough to polish stone. She can describe this. She can compare it to the nave's development. She cannot yet name what all three expressions of the practice are versions of — but she is closer. The gap she is identifying is getting smaller.

## Treasure

The construction work-order fragment: evidence, not a magical item. Job: confirms the congregation has been maintaining the building.

## Connections

- **North passage:** R2 (First Landing) — always accessible
- **South passage:** R1 (Tower Base) — loop completion; always accessible

## GM Notes

R4's job is two things: the oldest marks (relic sign), and the flanking route. A party that entered via R4 from R1 (having noticed the south passage with DC 12) arrives behind R2 without triggering the R1 lookout — this is the Jaquays reward for exploration.

**The worn marks.** The congregation has been brushing against these marks every day for thirty years. The wear is not damage; it is the record of daily presence. The marks and the wear together say: this is what thirty years of daily returning looks like.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the floor-level marks)*
*The first mark was made standing up. By the third year, making the marks required kneeling — the rows had reached knee height. By the tenth year, the marks were above reach and she had to climb to make them. She did not stop. The marks are still there, above reach, faded.*
