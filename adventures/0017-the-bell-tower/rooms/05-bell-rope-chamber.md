# Room 05 — Bell Rope Chamber

## Read-aloud

> The bell rope hangs in the center of the shaft — not hemp, not the original; this rope is braided from cloth strips, thread, and what looks like hair, wound together over years. Above, through the shaft, the bell is visible. It does not move. Three congregation members are here, two of them working at the rope — adding new strips, tightening old ones. The work is careful. They don't stop when you enter.

*(74 words — trim:)*

> The bell rope hangs in the shaft: not hemp — braided cloth strips, thread, and hair, wound over years. Above, the bell is visible and still. Three congregation members are here; two are adding new strips to the rope with careful hands. They don't stop when you enter.

*(50 words)*

## Features

- **The bell rope** (center shaft). Braided from cloth strips, thread, and hair over thirty years. New material is added regularly — the rope has been continuously maintained for as long as the congregation has been here. **DC 10 Perception** to notice it is not original rope. **DC 13 Investigation** to see layers of age in the braiding — the innermost layers are the oldest, the outermost fresh. *Relic sign: bell rope sign — this is the relic manifest's accumulation sign.*

- **The bell** (shaft, 15 ft above). Visible from R5. Cast iron, correctly hung. **DC 12 Perception** to notice: the bell's clapper is present and properly positioned. There is no physical reason the bell cannot ring. It has not rung since the congregation occupied the tower. The congregation has never tried to ring it. When asked why: *"It is not for ringing."* They have no other explanation.

- **The ladder** (north wall). Wooden ladder, congregation-made, 10 ft tall. Leads to R6 (Bell Platform). **DC 10 Athletics** to ascend safely. The ladder is reliable — the congregation has been maintaining it as long as the rope.

- **The rope as traversal.** The braided rope hangs from the bell mechanism above. A creature can grab the rope and swing across the shaft (**DC 13 Athletics** to cross safely, landing on the east ledge near the ladder base). The rope will hold a Medium creature's weight — the congregation uses it for moving between rope-work positions. In combat, the rope can be swung as a way to cross between Sharr's position (if he retreats to R5) and the ladder.

- **Three congregation members** (two working at rope, one watching). If Sharr's encounter in R3 resolved socially, these members are calm. If it resolved through combat, they are defensive — HP 12, AC 11, improvised tools (+3 to hit).

## Encounter

**Conditional combat** — if the party fights through R3 and chases Sharr to R5, he makes his stand here. The verticality of the bell shaft (the rope crossing option, the ladder to R6, the 15 ft ceiling) creates a different tactical geometry than R3.

If Sharr is retreating at half HP (8 HP), he climbs the ladder to R6. The party can follow or not.

**Bilateral arc's secondary location:** If the bilateral arc did not fire in R3, and the party is pursuing rather than fighting, Sharr stops at R5 and waits. He says: *"Tell me why you're still here."* The bilateral can fire here instead — same conditions, different room.

## Treasure

None directly. The cord is with Sharr (who may have retreated here).

## Connections

- **Down (stairs):** R3 (Second Landing) — always passable
- **Up (ladder):** R6 (Bell Platform) — DC 10 Athletics; no door

## GM Notes

R5 is the tactical continuation of the R3 encounter. The rope traversal and the ladder to R6 create options that the party should feel: this is a three-dimensional space. Sharr will use the ladder to escape to R6 if cornered. The party can follow or let him go.

**The bell that doesn't ring.** The congregation has never tried to ring it. Ask them why and they say "it is not for ringing." This is not superstition — it is simply not something they have ever done. The bell was there before they arrived. It has been there while they braided their rope. It will be there when they are gone. Whether it rings is not their question.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the braided rope)*
*The first strip was a cloth from a shirt she was no longer wearing. The second was from a blanket. She had not thought about what she was making until the third strip, when she understood: she was making the rope that should have been here. The original rope had rotted. She replaced it. She has been replacing it ever since.*
