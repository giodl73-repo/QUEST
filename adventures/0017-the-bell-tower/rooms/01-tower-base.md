# Room 01 — Tower Base

## Read-aloud

> The passage opens into the tower's ground floor. Stairs rise immediately to the left. The walls are covered in marks — scratch-tallies, dense and overlapping, some crossed out. A single gully dwarf stands near the arch, watching you enter. He doesn't attack. He turns and shouts something up the stairs in a language you don't share.

*(51 words)*

## Features

- **Scratch-tallies** (all walls). Dense, overlapping, some crossed out, some rows covering older rows. **DC 10 Perception** to notice the scale — this is not one season's marks; the lowest layers are old enough that the scratches have worn slightly smooth. **DC 13 Investigation** to count: inconclusive. Too many overlapping marks. The count cannot be accurately made from here. *Relic sign: tally wall sign (first encounter).*

- **The lookout** (near entry arch). One congregation member. Non-combatant. He shouts a warning up the stairs immediately — there is no stealth possible from the front approach. **DC 15 Stealth** to approach via R4 (loop from R1's south side) without triggering the warning. If the party enters via R4 first (having navigated the loop), this lookout does not see them.

- **South passage** (floor level, south wall). A low passage connecting to R4 (Tally Wall Alcove). **DC 12 Perception** from R1 to notice. The passage is narrow (duck to enter). **Sensory cue at entrance:** the marks on the south wall are densest near this passage; the tally wall starts here, at the oldest marks.

## Encounter

**Lookout warning (triggered immediately):** The lookout cannot be silently bypassed from the front approach. His warning brings R2's four congregation members to the top of the stairs — not descending, but visible and watching. The social situation in R2 begins with the congregation having 30 seconds of preparation. Route D note: this is not hostile — it's their standard response to visitors. Sharr will judge from R3 what to do next.

## Treasure

None.

- **South (floor passage):** R4 (Tally Wall Alcove) — DC 12 Perception to notice; always passable once found

## GM Notes

R1 is a threshold and an alarm. Its job: deliver the tally wall impression and ensure the party cannot approach R2 silently from the front.

**The tally walls here are the densest.** The congregation has been marking this room since they arrived. The oldest marks are at floor level, worn slightly smooth. A curious party member can observe that the marks are not uniform — some are careful single lines, others are slashes, others appear to have been made quickly and revisited. Thirty years of marks made under different emotional conditions look different.

**The Gully Dwarf Gloss** — MANDATORY this session. When the DM narrates this room (or the session summary), include: *"Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by every Krynnish faction to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory. The congregation in this tower has been marking these walls for thirty years."* One sentence. Every session now.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the tallies)*
*The first mark was made on the third day. Not counting days — just marking that there had been a day, and then another, and then another. After a week the marks had a shape that wasn't counting. The shape didn't have a name. It still doesn't.*
