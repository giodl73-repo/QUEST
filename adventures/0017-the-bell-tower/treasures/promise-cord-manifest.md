# Relic Signs Manifest — Velantha's Promise-Cord

The dungeon housing this relic MUST express the presence of the congregation's held resentment and the cord's record-keeping function in at least **3 of the following ways**, distributed across at least **2 rooms**.

## Required (≥3, distributed across ≥2 rooms)

- [ ] **Tally wall sign:** The Bell Tower's walls are covered in scratch-tallies. These are not the careful thirty-row tally of the wall-walk in 0015 — these are overlapping, some crossed out, some dense. The tallies are not counting years. They are counting something the congregation cannot name. At least one room must show the tallies prominently, with a DC 10 Perception to notice them and DC 13 Investigation to try to count them (inconclusive — too overlapping to count accurately).

- [ ] **Bell rope sign:** The bell rope has been replaced with something braided from rags, cloth strips, and hair over thirty years. This sign must appear in the room with the bell rope (upper levels). The rope braiding is an act of ongoing maintenance — they keep rebraiding it, adding new strips. The bell does not ring. The rope is the congregation's version of the cord: knotted and rebraided, accumulating.

- [ ] **Eleven-and-twenty-six sign:** At least one room should have evidence of the cord's arithmetic: eleven cut knots visible (or felt) as distinct from the intact ones. This can be expressed as: a congregation member who mentions "eleven" in conversation; or a visible recess in a shrine where eleven small stones are placed separately from twenty-six others; or Sharr counting on his fingers during the confrontation.

- [ ] **Warmth sign:** The promise-cord is warm in the hands of someone who holds unfulfilled promises. If the party handles it, this is detectable without a roll. But the cord should also express warmth before the party handles it: Sharr's left wrist, where he keeps it knotted, is slightly warmer than the rest of him. **DC 13 Perception** to notice from within 5 ft of Sharr.

- [ ] **Reaction moment:** When the bilateral arc fires (Dren names Aldis, Renn names Mira), a knot in the cord tightens. This fires passively — no roll, no announcement. The DM describes: "The cord, wherever it is in the room, tightens slightly in one place." If Sharr is holding it, he looks at his wrist. He doesn't say anything.

## Required (always)

- [ ] **The cord is present and observable in the session before the bilateral arc fires.** The party should see the cord on Sharr's wrist before the confrontation's peak. The object is introduced before it becomes significant.

- [ ] **At least one room expresses what the cord represents architecturally:** a space that has been marked, tallied, and accumulated for thirty years without ever being able to say clearly what was being counted.

## Suggested (optional)

- Sharr performing a version of the knot-tying rite before the confrontation — retying the congregation threads — while the party approaches. They are doing the same thing the nave congregation does with the rite, but with the cord rather than the cup.
- One of the eleven cut knots is associated with a patient whose name the congregation remembers. If the party asks about it, they get a name and one sentence: the patient was healed, lived two more years, then died of something else. The healing was real. The death came anyway.
