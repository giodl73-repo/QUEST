# Velantha's Promise-Cord

*A length of knotted string, some knots cut, some open — a healer's patient record that knows which promises were kept.*

## Appearance

Hemp cord, approximately three feet long, stained brown from handling. Thirty-seven knots along its length. Some are tight and clean. Others are cut — the cord continues beyond a severed knot, the ends frayed and held together by the knot next to them. Eleven knots are cut. Twenty-six are intact. Velantha tied a knot for each patient she tracked over twenty years. A cut knot means the patient died. An intact knot means they lived.

The aggrieved congregation of the Bell Tower has added one thing: braided into the cord at intervals are small threads of cloth, each one from a different garment. One thread per congregation member who reached the tower. Fifteen threads, knotted loosely around the cord's intact knots, as if the congregation is holding on.

## Presence / Desire

The cord wants to be held by someone who knows what it means to promise something and have it matter. Not to perform promises — to mean them. It is heaviest in hands that have made promises to people who are gone. It is lighter, almost weightless, in hands that have never promised anything at risk.

When held by someone with an unfulfilled promise — a debt to the dead, a word given to someone who could not wait for it — one of the intact knots tightens slightly. The knot does not identify the promise. It does not name the person. It simply confirms: you have something you owe.

## Provenance

**1. An unnamed ropemaker near Mireth-on-the-Hill (made ~50 years ago; sold at a market stall).**
Hemp cord. Priced for household use. The ropemaker made three coils that day; this length was cut from the middle one. It was already the right length for tracking patients when Velantha bought it. She needed something that could be knotted while she worked.

**2. Sister Velantha (used ~20 years; died at the ritual site ~30 years ago).**
She knotted it for twenty years. The first knot is near the end — an early patient whose name she could not remember later, which is why she started the cord. By the end: thirty-seven knots, eleven cut. She knew every name. The cord was in her satchel when she left for the ritual. She did not bring it into the ritual space; she left the satchel at the threshold. The cord was inside.

**3. The Bell Tower congregation (kept ~30 years; currently with Sharr).**
They found it in the satchel — the field satchel, now with the nave congregation, which they raided for relics in the congregation's early years before the two groups organized separately. They took the cord. They have kept it in the Bell Tower for thirty years. Sharr has been the cord's keeper for the last twelve years. He keeps it knotted around his left wrist when he is not performing the rite. When he is performing the rite, it hangs from the bell rope.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a creature that has made a promise (kept or failed) to a named person. The attunement condition is a personal history, not a class or race. Anyone who has ever promised something to someone — and remembers it — can attune.

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Promise-tracking.** When the attuned creature makes a formal commitment (not a casual statement — a genuine promise to a specific creature), an open knot in the cord tightens on its own. This is not magical compulsion; the knot tightening is acknowledgment, not enforcement.
- **Kept-promise resonance.** When the attuned creature fulfills a promise, the relevant knot relaxes and becomes loose — not cut, but no longer tight. The attuned creature knows, without checking, that the promise has been honored.
- **Dead reckoning.** Once per long rest, the attuned creature can focus on the cord and know the number of open promises they currently hold — not their content, not the names, just the count. The knowledge is not judgment; it is simply the record.
- **Eleven cut knots (passive).** A creature holding the cord can feel the eleven cut knots as distinct from the intact ones. **DC 11 Medicine or Insight** to count them correctly. These were Velantha's patients who died. The cord carries the count permanently.

### Relic Test

If the attuned creature cuts a knot to deliberately end a promise they have not fulfilled — abandoning a commitment by destroying its record — the cord becomes ordinary hemp. The tightening stops. The knot count is gone. The congregation's fifteen threads fall loose.

The DM adjudicates what constitutes deliberate abandonment. Breaking a promise through failure or circumstance does not trigger the test. Cutting the cord to avoid the obligation does.

### Ending the Relic Test consequence

Permanent for this cord. Another cord could be made, but not this one — not with thirty years of congregation threads, not with eleven cut knots and twenty-six intact ones. The congregation would not need to be told. They would find the threads on the floor.

## Hooks

1. **The congregation wants it.** Sharr wears it. Twelve years. Taking it requires either Sharr's cooperation (Route D) or force (Route A). The cord will not be transferred to the party until Sharr understands they can be trusted with it — which requires the bilateral arc to fire.

2. **Eleven cut knots.** Velantha had twenty-six patients who lived and eleven who died. The Bell Tower congregation can name some of the eleven — they knew them. If the party asks about the cut knots, they learn something about what Velantha's healing could and could not do.

3. **The bilateral arc.** When Dren or Renn holds the cord (if they do), one of the open knots tightens. Both of them have made a promise to a person who is gone — Dren to Aldis (I'll find out what happened), Renn to Mira (I'll make the power worth something). The cord knows. The DM does not explain. The knot tightens once, when they first hold it.

## GM Notes

The promise-cord is the session's most personal relic. The field satchel was about care that persists. The measuring cup was about calibration without instruction. The promise-cord is about the record — what was promised, what was kept, what was lost.

When Dren first holds the cord (if he holds it): a knot tightens. He will not know which promise it is responding to. He has Aldis's insignia in his left breast pocket. The cord tightens over the pocket's approximate position. The DM does not comment.

When Renn first holds the cord: a knot tightens. He has the warm copper coin in his coat — the one that was warm when he made the pact. The cord tightens near where the coin is.

Do not explain the tightening. Do not identify the promise. The cord does not judge. It counts.
