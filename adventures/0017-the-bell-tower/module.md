## Setting & Background

**Place:** The bell tower of the Halted Spire — accessible from the upper gallery (R6 of 0016) via the east arch, once the debris is cleared. The tower is the cathedral's tallest unfinished structure: three internal levels plus an open bell platform at the top. The bell exists. It is hung correctly. It does not ring. The rope that should ring it has been replaced with something braided from rags, cloth strips, and hair over thirty years by the people who live here.

**Atmosphere:** The walls of the Bell Tower are covered in scratch-tallies. Not the careful thirty-row tally of the wall-walk (R4 of 0015) — these are different: dense, overlapping, some scratched over others, some crossed out. Thirty-two years of marks made by people who were counting something they could not name. The bell rope hangs in the center of the shaft, braided and rebraided. The bell above does not move in the wind that comes through the open belfry.

The Bell Tower congregation numbers approximately fifteen. They are the aggrieved ones — gully dwarves Velantha healed whose lives after healing remained hard. They kept faith for thirty years. They also kept anger for thirty years. Both are true simultaneously.

They came here because Velantha healed them. They are angry not that she failed but that she succeeded and the success was not enough. The healing was real. The poverty, the loss, the hard years after — those were also real. The congregation in the tower has been performing the same devotional practices as the nave congregation for thirty years, and the practices have not made the hard years easier. Faith can coexist with resentment. They know this.

**They are not immediately hostile.** They are wary, defensive, and quick to escalate if the party moves aggressively. Their leader — **Sharr** — is the congregation's most articulate voice for their specific grievance. He is also the most dangerous combatant in the building.

**The ritual focus is here.** The aggrieved congregation has one of Velantha's healing tools: a small carved stone focus that was used to channel the High Sorcery working. They kept it because it was warm when they found it. They have no idea what it is — they treat it as a relic, the same way the nave congregation treats the cup. But the Orders would recognize it immediately from its mark.

The party has heard from Veth (S2): *"Some of us came here angry. Some are still angry. They are in the tower."* They have cleared the debris from the R6 east passage (via Maret's failed attempt in S2 and subsequent effort, or through another path). They enter the Bell Tower.

**Doran's pressure** is building. She hired the party to clear the site. They are now two sessions in and have engaged with three sections of the congregation instead of clearing them. She sends word (via a message delivered to the gatehouse) that she needs a status report within three days. The party must decide what to tell her — and what to do about the Bell Tower — before that deadline.

**Structural requirement:** The Bell Tower congregation's specific resentment — *"she healed us but she could not save us from everything that came after"* — must surface both Dren's and Renn's grief simultaneously. They have each been carrying a version of this for two sessions.

**Naming order:** Dren names first. When Sharr or another congregation member articulates the resentment clearly, Dren connects it to Aldis. He says: *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."* Renn hears this and names Mira in response: *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."*

**Conditions from PC sheets:**
- Dren's condition: the congregation articulates "she healed us but couldn't save us from everything." Dren makes the connection to Aldis dying of plague while a healer was elsewhere.
- Renn's condition: after Dren names Aldis, Renn names Mira. Bilateral — Renn cannot name first; Dren's naming creates the conditions for Renn's.

The DM should not prompt either naming. The naming conditions are in the PC sheets. If the scene is played correctly (the congregation's resentment is articulated clearly and honestly), the bilateral fires from the heuristics.

**Primary delivery:** During the encounter with Sharr and the congregation, one of the aggrieved members says — either in dialogue or in the confrontation's emotional peak: *"She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal. We know what prayer feels like. This was different."*

**Delivery mechanism:** This is delivered if the party engages with the congregation's grief rather than fighting through them. DC 11 Insight from any PC: the statement is not an accusation — it is a description. They have known for thirty years that what they received was not divine grace. They never knew what it was instead.

**Recovery path:** If the session's dialogue does not deliver Hint 2 (party fights their way through without conversation), the ritual focus in the tower provides mechanical confirmation in 0018: the elder can identify what the focus was used for, which delivers Hint 2 from the object rather than the testimony.

**Sharr** (Bell Tower leader):
- CR: approximately 1 (custom combatant)
- HP: 16, AC: 12
- Attacks: Iron nail-flail (+5 to hit, 1d8+2 bludgeoning)
- Special: **Shove (bonus action)** — after landing a melee attack, Sharr can attempt to shove the target (Athletics +4 vs target's Str/Dex save). On success, target moves 5 ft toward the nearest ledge. If already adjacent to a ledge, they must make DC 12 Dex save or fall 10 ft (1d6 bludgeoning) to the level below.
- Morale: fights until reduced to 8 HP, then backs off and demands the party explain why they're here.

**2-3 aggrieved congregation members** (CR ¼ each, as gatehouse):
- HP: 12, AC: 11. Improvised weapons +3 to hit, 1d6 bludgeoning.

This gives: approx. 200 XP (Sharr CR 1) + 50-75 XP (congregation members). Medium-difficulty encounter for party of 4 at level 3.

- **Route A:** Fight through the Bell Tower, take the promise-cord, exit. The congregation is damaged or scattered. The Nave congregation will know by morning.
- **Route C:** Survive the Bell Tower without destroying the congregation; acknowledge their resentment; leave without taking the cord. The congregation remains in the tower but is no longer hostile. The party has not gained the cord but has not lost Route D.
- **Route D:** The bilateral arc fires; the congregation recognizes that the party understands what they're carrying; Sharr allows the party to take the cord (or shows them where it is). The resentment is not resolved — it is witnessed.

- **aggrieved-faction-exists** (0016): confirmed. The tower congregation is real, dangerous, and distinct from the nave.
- **congregation-was-organized** already retrieved in 0016.

- **velanthas-unkept-promise** (→ 0020): the congregation names what she promised and couldn't deliver. This is the specific grievance. Velantha addresses it in the Crypt.
- **ritual-focus-in-tower** (→ 0018): the congregation's sacred object is the ritual focus. They don't know what it is. The party may or may not learn this session; the elder in the Transept will identify it.

### Scene 1 — Tower Base

> The passage opens into the tower's ground floor. Stairs rise immediately to the left. The walls are covered in marks — scratch-tallies, dense and overlapping, some crossed out. A single gully dwarf stands near the arch, watching you enter. He doesn't attack. He turns and shouts something up the stairs in a language you don't share.

#### Features

- **Scratch-tallies** (all walls). Dense, overlapping, some crossed out, some rows covering older rows. **DC 10 Perception** to notice the scale — this is not one season's marks; the lowest layers are old enough that the scratches have worn slightly smooth. **DC 13 Investigation** to count: inconclusive. Too many overlapping marks. The count cannot be accurately made from here. *Relic sign: tally wall sign (first encounter).*

- **The lookout** (near entry arch). One congregation member. Non-combatant. He shouts a warning up the stairs immediately — there is no stealth possible from the front approach. **DC 15 Stealth** to approach via R4 (loop from R1's south side) without triggering the warning. If the party enters via R4 first (having navigated the loop), this lookout does not see them.

- **South passage** (floor level, south wall). A low passage connecting to R4 (Tally Wall Alcove). **DC 12 Perception** from R1 to notice. The passage is narrow (duck to enter). **Sensory cue at entrance:** the marks on the south wall are densest near this passage; the tally wall starts here, at the oldest marks.

#### Encounter

**Lookout warning (triggered immediately):** The lookout cannot be silently bypassed from the front approach. His warning brings R2's four congregation members to the top of the stairs — not descending, but visible and watching. The social situation in R2 begins with the congregation having 30 seconds of preparation. Route D note: this is not hostile — it's their standard response to visitors. Sharr will judge from R3 what to do next.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- **South (from 0016):** R6 east arch — entry passage (cleared)
- **Up (stairs):** R2 (First Landing) — always passable
- **South (floor passage):** R4 (Tally Wall Alcove) — DC 12 Perception to notice; always passable once found

#### GM Notes

R1 is a threshold and an alarm. Its job: deliver the tally wall impression and ensure the party cannot approach R2 silently from the front.

**The tally walls here are the densest.** The congregation has been marking this room since they arrived. The oldest marks are at floor level, worn slightly smooth. A curious party member can observe that the marks are not uniform — some are careful single lines, others are slashes, others appear to have been made quickly and revisited. Thirty years of marks made under different emotional conditions look different.

**The Gully Dwarf Gloss** — MANDATORY this session. When the DM narrates this room (or the session summary), include: *"Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by every Krynnish faction to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory. The congregation in this tower has been marking these walls for thirty years."* One sentence. Every session now.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the tallies)*
*The first mark was made on the third day. Not counting days — just marking that there had been a day, and then another, and then another. After a week the marks had a shape that wasn't counting. The shape didn't have a name. It still doesn't.*

### Scene 2 — First Landing

> Four gully dwarves on the landing, watching. Behind them, stairs rise further. The walls have the same scratch-tallies as below — but also small shrines here: stone, cloth scrap, candle. The same arrangements as the nave. A different feeling to them, something taut.

#### Features

- **Shrine arrangements** (three, along the east wall). The same form as the nave congregation's shrines — stones, cloth, candle — but something in their arrangement is tighter, more compressed. **DC 12 Insight** to read: these were not assembled in leisure; they were assembled by people who were also angry while assembling them. The practice and the resentment are both present in the same object. *Relic sign: eleven-and-twenty-six sign — one of the shrines has eleven small stones arranged separately from a larger group (twenty-six). This is not explained in the room; it is observed.*

- **North side passage** (connects to R4 Tally Wall Alcove). Clear and accessible from R2. **Sensory cue:** through the passage, the smell of something older — the tally marks near R4 are the oldest in the tower; the air around them is different.

- **The four congregation members** (non-combatants initially). They are watching. They will not attack unless Sharr commands or the party advances aggressively toward the upper stairs. If Renn uses Awakened Mind: they respond with images of waiting — long waiting, thirty years of it.

- **Sharr's voice** (from R3 above, before he descends if warned). He does not shout; he speaks in measured Common: *"Tell me why you're here."* He can hear the party from R3 through the open stairway.

They are not a separate faith — they are the same faith in a different emotional register. When the party sees the shrine arrangements here after two sessions of seeing them in the nave and aisle, the comparison should land. The Nave's faith is practiced in grief and gratitude. The Tower's faith is practiced in grief and resentment. The form is identical.

When he sees the shrine arrangements here — the same as the nave's, but taut — the parallel to Aldis is closer. He doesn't say anything yet. ### Scene 3 — Second Landing

> Sharr, older than the rest, stands near the stairs. Three congregation members flank him. A knotted cord on his left wrist. The walls here have fewer marks — more careful, some almost like rows of writing. He looks at you: "She healed us. And then she left us to it."

#### Features

- **Sharr and the promise-cord** (near upper stairs). The cord is visible on his left wrist. **DC 10 Perception** to notice it. **DC 13 Investigation or Medicine** to identify it as a patient-tracking cord — a common practice for healers who handle multiple patients. *Relic sign: warmth sign — Sharr's left wrist is slightly warmer than the rest of him. DC 13 Perception from within 5 ft.*

- **The careful marks** (east and north walls). Unlike R1/R2's dense tallies, R3's marks are arranged in visible rows, some crossed out with care, some left intact. **DC 12 History** — this looks like a record of something — possibly names, possibly events, though the notation system is not a known writing system. It is idiosyncratic to whoever created it. *Eleven marks in one row are carefully crossed out. Twenty-six in another row are not.*

- **Upper stairs** (north wall). The stairs to R5 (Bell Rope Chamber). Sharr is positioned near them — not blocking, but present. He will not allow the party to ascend without resolution of the encounter.

- **The three flanking members** (combatants). See `encounters/sharr-confrontation.md`. They are the congregation's most dangerous members: HP 14 each, AC 12, improvised iron-tipped weapons (+4 to hit, 1d8+1).

- **High ground for Sharr** (near the upper stairs). Sharr has slightly elevated position on this landing (the stairs create a step-up). If combat begins, treat him as having half-cover (+2 AC) from the forward position.

#### Encounter

**Sharr's confrontation.** **Encounter — Sharr's Confrontation**

**Type**

Social encounter, potentially combat. The session's primary encounter. Resolves socially via the bilateral arc-completion; resolves through combat if the party advances aggressively.

**Setup**

Sharr stands near the upper stairs in R3. Three flanking congregation members — the tower's most experienced combatants — are distributed around the landing. Sharr speaks first:. He has already spoken his opening line ("She healed us. And then she left us to it."). The party must respond. This is the session's primary encounter — social first, combat if needed.

#### GM Notes

This is the session's climax room. Every other room in the adventure exists to bring the party to this moment.

**Sharr's opening line is not hostile.** *"She healed us. And then she left us to it."* He is not threatening the party. He is stating thirty-two years of accumulated fact. The line is the resentment articulated cleanly. The party's response to it determines whether the session goes to Route A, C, or D.

**The bilateral arc fires here.** The conditions from both PC sheets should be met by Sharr's opening line. Dren's crack has been building since S1. When Sharr says "she left us to it," Dren connects: a healer came for others, but not for Aldis. This is the moment. The DM does not prompt the naming. The heuristics drive it. If the heuristics don't produce it organically, the doubt-die resolves it.

**Route D fallback:** If the bilateral does not fire this session (unlikely, but possible), the cord can be received in the following ways: (a) the party explains why they are here honestly (same as Gorret's condition); (b) Veth endorses the party from the nave (he can be retrieved, but costs time); (c) the party simply takes the cord by force (Route A/C; closes Route D going forward).

### Scene 4 — Tally Wall Alcove

> A side alcove off the main tower passage, connected to both the entry and the first landing. The marks here are the oldest in the building — worn slightly smooth at the lowest sections where hands have brushed against them for thirty years. Four gully dwarves are here, each at a small arrangement, quiet. None of them looks up.

#### Features

- **Oldest tally marks** (floor to ceiling on all walls). These are the first marks made in the Bell Tower. The lowest sections are worn slightly smooth from thirty years of hands. At the floor level, some marks are almost invisible. **DC 10 Perception** to notice the wear. **DC 12 History** to estimate age: the lowest marks are twenty-five to thirty-five years old. *Relic sign: tally wall sign — densest expression of this sign in the adventure.*

- **Four quiet congregation members** (individual practices, non-combatants). These are the Bell Tower's most contemplative members. They have been here the longest of any currently living congregation members except Sharr. They will answer questions in broken Common if asked, slowly. They will not join a fight unless directly attacked.

- **Construction work-order fragment** (floor, near south passage entrance). A piece of paper (construction-era) that has been pressed flat and preserved by a congregation member — smoothed under a heavy stone. **DC 11 Investigation** to find. The work-order references the Bell Tower's specifications: *"the bell to be hung in the upper belfry, the rope to be replaced at five-year intervals."* It is eleven years old. The rope has not been replaced on the construction crew's schedule — it has been replaced continuously by the congregation. The congregation has been maintaining the building's infrastructure without knowing it was their job.

- **Back loop passage** (south wall). Connects to R1 (Tower Base). Always accessible. **Sensory cue from R4 into R1:** looking south through the passage, the entry arch is visible. The congregation uses this passage to move between levels without passing through the main stair route.

#### Encounter

None. This is the Stuart room — purely atmospheric. The four congregation members here are not hostile and will not escalate.

**Documentation-suspension (Calla Fosse) — third activation of gap-identification stage.** Calla has been accumulating: she has corrected an imperfect rite (identifying a gap in transmission) and seen the nave congregation's developed practice. Here she sees something new: the oldest practices in the building, worn smooth from daily use. The practices have been happening long enough to polish stone. She can describe this. She can compare it to the nave's development. She cannot yet name what all three expressions of the practice are versions of — but she is closer. The gap she is identifying is getting smaller.

#### Treasure

The construction work-order fragment: evidence, not a magical item. Job: confirms the congregation has been maintaining the building.

#### Connections

- **North passage:** R2 (First Landing) — always accessible
- **South passage:** R1 (Tower Base) — loop completion; always accessible

#### GM Notes

R4's job is two things: the oldest marks (relic sign), and the flanking route. A party that entered via R4 from R1 (having noticed the south passage with DC 12) arrives behind R2 without triggering the R1 lookout — this is the Jaquays reward for exploration.

**The worn marks.** The congregation has been brushing against these marks every day for thirty years. The wear is not damage; it is the record of daily presence. The marks and the wear together say: this is what thirty years of daily returning looks like.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the floor-level marks)*
*The first mark was made standing up. By the third year, making the marks required kneeling — the rows had reached knee height. By the tenth year, the marks were above reach and she had to climb to make them. She did not stop. The marks are still there, above reach, faded.*

### Scene 5 — Bell Rope Chamber

> The bell rope hangs in the shaft: not hemp — braided cloth strips, thread, and hair, wound over years. Above, the bell is visible and still. Three congregation members are here; two are adding new strips to the rope with careful hands. They don't stop when you enter.

#### Features

- **The bell rope** (center shaft). Braided from cloth strips, thread, and hair over thirty years. # Velantha's Promise-Cord

*A length of knotted string, some knots cut, some open — a healer's patient record that knows which promises were kept.*

## Appearance

Hemp cord, approximately three feet long, stained brown from handling. Thirty-seven knots along its length. Some are tight and clean. Others are cut — the cord continues beyond a severed knot, the ends frayed and held together by the knot next to them. Eleven knots are cut. Twenty-six are intact. Velantha tied a knot for each patient she tracked over twenty years. A cut knot means the patient died. An intact knot means they lived.

The aggrieved congregation of the Bell Tower has added one thing: braided into the cord at intervals are small threads of cloth, each one from a different garment. One thread per congregation member who reached the tower. Fifteen threads, knotted loosely around the cord's intact knots, as if the congregation is holding on.

## Presence / Desire

The cord wants to be held by someone who knows what it means to promise something and have it matter. Not to perform promises — to mean them. It is heaviest in hands that have made promises to people who are gone. It is lighter, almost weightless, in hands that have never promised anything at risk.

When held by someone with an unfulfilled promise — a debt to the dead, a word given to someone who could not wait for it — one of the intact knots tightens slightly. The knot does not identify the promise. It does not name the person. It simply confirms: you have something you owe.

## Provenance

**1. An unnamed ropemaker near Mireth-on-the-Hill (made ~50 years ago; sold at a market stall).**
Hemp cord. Priced for household use. The ropemaker made three coils that day; this length was cut from the middle one. It was already the right length for tracking patients when Velantha bought it. She needed something that could be knotted while she worked.

**2. Sister Velantha (used ~20 years; died at the ritual site ~30 years ago).**
She knotted it for twenty years. The first knot is near the end — an early patient whose name she could not remember later, which is why she started the cord. By the end: thirty-seven knots, eleven cut. She knew every name. The cord was in her satchel when she left for the ritual. She did not bring it into the ritual space; she left the satchel at the threshold. The cord was inside.

**3. The Bell Tower congregation (kept ~30 years; currently with Sharr).**
They found it in the satchel — the field satchel, now with the nave congregation, which they raided for relics in the congregation's early years before the two groups organized separately. They took the cord. They have kept it in the Bell Tower for thirty years. Sharr has been the cord's keeper for the last twelve years. He keeps it knotted around his left wrist when he is not performing the rite. When he is performing the rite, it hangs from the bell rope.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a creature that has made a promise (kept or failed) to a named person. The attunement condition is a personal history, not a class or race. Anyone who has ever promised something to someone — and remembers it — can attune.

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Promise-tracking.** When the attuned creature makes a formal commitment (not a casual statement — a genuine promise to a specific creature), an open knot in the cord tightens on its own. This is not magical compulsion; the knot tightening is acknowledgment, not enforcement.
- **Kept-promise resonance.** When the attuned creature fulfills a promise, the relevant knot relaxes and becomes loose — not cut, but no longer tight. The attuned creature knows, without checking, that the promise has been honored.
- **Dead reckoning.** Once per long rest, the attuned creature can focus on the cord and know the number of open promises they currently hold — not their content, not the names, just the count. The knowledge is not judgment; it is simply the record.
- **Eleven cut knots (passive).** A creature holding the cord can feel the eleven cut knots as distinct from the intact ones. **DC 11 Medicine or Insight** to count them correctly. These were Velantha's patients who died. The cord carries the count permanently.

### Relic Test

If the attuned creature cuts a knot to deliberately end a promise they have not fulfilled — abandoning a commitment by destroying its record — the cord becomes ordinary hemp. The tightening stops. The knot count is gone. The congregation's fifteen threads fall loose.

The DM adjudicates what constitutes deliberate abandonment. Breaking a promise through failure or circumstance does not trigger the test. Cutting the cord to avoid the obligation does.

### Ending the Relic Test consequence

Permanent for this cord. Another cord could be made, but not this one — not with thirty years of congregation threads, not with eleven cut knots and twenty-six intact ones. The congregation would not need to be told. They would find the threads on the floor.

## Hooks

1. **The congregation wants it.** Sharr wears it. Twelve years. Taking it requires either Sharr's cooperation (Route D) or force (Route A). The cord will not be transferred to the party until Sharr understands they can be trusted with it — which requires the bilateral arc to fire.

2. **Eleven cut knots.** Velantha had twenty-six patients who lived and eleven who died. The Bell Tower congregation can name some of the eleven — they knew them. If the party asks about the cut knots, they learn something about what Velantha's healing could and could not do.

3. **The bilateral arc.** When Dren or Renn holds the cord (if they do), one of the open knots tightens. Both of them have made a promise to a person who is gone — Dren to Aldis (I'll find out what happened), Renn to Mira (I'll make the power worth something). The cord knows. The DM does not explain. The knot tightens once, when they first hold it.

## GM Notes

The promise-cord is the session's most personal relic. The field satchel was about care that persists. The measuring cup was about calibration without instruction. The promise-cord is about the record — what was promised, what was kept, what was lost.

When Dren first holds the cord (if he holds it): a knot tightens. He will not know which promise it is responding to. He has Aldis's insignia in his left breast pocket. The cord tightens over the pocket's approximate position. The DM does not comment.

When Renn first holds the cord: a knot tightens. He has the warm copper coin in his coat — the one that was warm when he made the pact. The cord tightens near where the coin is.

Do not explain the tightening. Do not identify the promise. The cord does not judge. It counts.

### Sharr Confrontation

# Encounter — Sharr's Confrontation

## Type

Social encounter, potentially combat. The session's primary encounter. Resolves socially via the bilateral arc-completion; resolves through combat if the party advances aggressively.

## Setup

Sharr stands near the upper stairs in R3. Three flanking congregation members — the tower's most experienced combatants — are distributed around the landing. Sharr speaks first:

*"She healed us. And then she left us to it."*

He waits for a response. He is not asking for an apology. He is not asking the party to fix it. He is asking whether they are capable of receiving this statement without deflecting.

## Combatants (if triggered)

### Sharr
See `npcs/sharr.md`. **HP 16, AC 12.** Nail-flail +5, 1d8+2. Shove bonus action (Athletics +4, pushes toward ledges). Morale break at 8 HP.

### Three aggrieved congregation members
**HP 14 each, AC 12.** Iron-tipped weapons +4 to hit, 1d8+1 bludgeoning. No special abilities. Morale: break if Sharr retreats or if two of three are incapacitated.

**🎲 Stat blocks:**
**Sharr — CR 1, 200 XP.** **AC** 12 · **HP** 16
**Aggrieved Member × 3 — CR ¼, 50 XP each.** **AC** 12 · **HP** 14

**Total XP if all defeated:** 350 XP (medium encounter for party of 4, level 3).

## Social Resolution

**The bilateral arc-completion path (Route D):**

Sharr articulates the resentment. One of his flanking members adds: *"She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal. We know what Mishakal feels like. This was different."* (Hint 2 delivery — DC 11 Insight to receive without deflecting.)

Dren connects: Aldis died asking for a healer who went elsewhere. He says: *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."*

Renn responds: *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."*

The bilateral fires. Sharr hears both names. He looks at the cord. He unties it. *"You understand what it costs."*

**The honest-answer path (Route C/D partial):**
If the bilateral does not fire, the party can still receive the cord by answering Sharr's implicit question: "Do you understand why she couldn't come back?" A correct, honest answer (not a deflection, not a defense) will move Sharr to a version of the arc-completion without the bilateral. Weaker, but functional.

**Deflection paths (Routes A/C):**
- Defending Velantha: *"I know she couldn't. I'm not asking you to fix it. I'm asking if you can hear it."* — he is patient; the scene can continue.
- Institutional framing ("we're here for the cathedral"): Sharr's posture changes; one flanking member moves closer to the stairs; the scene is closer to combat.
- Silence or advance without response: combat triggers.

## Combat Trigger Conditions

Combat triggers if:
- Party advances past Sharr toward the upper stairs without resolving the encounter
- Party attacks first
- Party takes the cord by force or grab attempt
- Party explicitly refuses to engage with the congregation's stated grievance

## Tactical Notes

**Verticality is the encounter's key mechanic.** Sharr's Shove ability, combined with the staircase edges in R3 and the open arches in R6, means the party must manage their positioning. Dren's platform: secure position first — he should have his back to a wall. Maret: Patient Defense as a bonus action prevents Shove from landing cleanly (Shove requires hitting, then Shove vs Dex; Dodge gives disadvantage on the attack roll). Renn: Eldritch Blast from range keeps him away from edges; Agonizing Blast deals real damage (7-13 per bolt). Calla: Faerie Fire targets Sharr's Con save (DC 13) — Sharr's Con is likely +1 or lower; reasonable chance to land.

**Combat pacing:** The encounter should feel dangerous. Sharr's Shove landing once (moving a PC 5 ft toward a ledge) creates real pressure even if the fall doesn't happen. The party has 250 XP and full resources; this encounter should drain at minimum: 1 spell slot, some HP damage, at least 1 Ki or Superiority die.

## Route Implications

- **Route D:** Bilateral fires; cord given freely; Sharr steps aside; upper levels accessible; Nave congregation's trust maintained.
- **Route C:** Party survives without destroying the congregation; acknowledges the resentment; leaves without the cord OR accepts a partial transfer (Sharr keeps it but the party has heard him); Bell Tower congregation intact; campaign continues.
- **Route A:** Party fights through; takes cord by force; Nave congregation will know by morning; Route D begins closing for the finale.

## Post-encounter: congregation behavior on return

- If Route D: Sharr and the Bell Tower congregation remain in the tower. They are not friends, but they are not enemies. The party can return.
- If Route C: Similar. The congregation is wary but present.
- If Route A: The congregation retreats deeper into the tower or to the bell platform. The cord is with the party. The Nave congregation's relationship to the party cools.
