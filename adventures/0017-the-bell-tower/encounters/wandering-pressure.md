# Wandering Pressure — The Bell Tower

*Roll Cold Pulse (d6) every 10 minutes. Roll Wandering Encounter (d6) every 30 minutes.*

*Justification: the Bell Tower is an active, inhabited vertical space with congregation members on all levels. Temporal pressure comes from the congregation's awareness of the party's movement and from the structural age of the building.*

---

## Cold Pulse Table (d6, every 10 min — atmospheric)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | The tower groans — not structural failure, just the building settling. Small grit from the mortar above. |
| 2 | A congregation member on an adjacent level speaks the verse: *"She came to us in the dark, and she had what we needed, and she gave it."* It sounds different here — quieter, tighter. |
| 3 | Sharr's voice from above or below (whichever level the party is not on): *"Tell me why you're still here."* He does not descend. He is listening. |
| 4 | The bell rope, visible from R5 or R3's upper stair, moves slightly — not rung, just disturbed by air movement through the belfry arches above. |
| 5 | A congregation member in R4 (tally alcove) is adding a new mark to the wall. The scratching sound carries through the stone. |
| 6 | Wind through the upper belfry moves the braided rope so it brushes the shaft wall once, audible two levels down. The sound is like a single low note. |

---

## Wandering Encounter Table (d6, every 30 min — mechanical)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | A congregation member from R3 descends to check on Sharr's status. Non-combatant, moving with purpose. If the party has been violent: this member has a weapon. If the party has been peaceful: this member is carrying a cup of water for Sharr, which they leave at the base of R3's stairs. |
| 2 | Doran's message arrives. A messenger from Mireth-on-the-Hill has left a note at the gatehouse threshold: *"Report expected. Three days. — D."* The note finds its way to the party via a nave congregation member who passes it up through the building. The deadline is real. |
| 3 | The ritual focus falls. The congregation member in R3 who holds it — not Sharr, a younger member — has been carrying it in their hand, and it slips. It hits the floor. The sound is a clear, clean note. The aggrieved congregation goes still for a moment. The focus is warm when the party retrieves it (DC 10 Perception). *Seed activated: ritual-focus-in-tower.* The congregation member picks it up quickly and does not explain. |
| 4 | A second congregation member from outside the tower — from the nave — appears at R1's entry arch. They have come to check if the Bell Tower congregation is all right. They look at the party. They look at the stairs. They go back to the nave. |
| 5 | The tally marks. A congregation member in R4 is counting the marks — their lips are moving. They have been counting for twenty minutes and have not finished and will not finish. They look up when the party passes. They say, in Common: *"Thirty-two years. Some of us counted every day."* |
| 6 | Sharr performs the knot-tying rite. He reties the congregation threads on the promise-cord — each one, slowly, while the party watches or doesn't watch. When he is done, one of the open knots is slightly tighter than before. He looks at it. He does not comment. *Relic sign fired: reaction moment pre-echo.* |
