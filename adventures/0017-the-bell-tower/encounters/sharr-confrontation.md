# Encounter — Sharr's Confrontation

## Type

Social encounter, potentially combat. The session's primary encounter. Resolves socially via the bilateral arc-completion; resolves through combat if the party advances aggressively.

## Setup

Sharr stands near the upper stairs in R3. Three flanking congregation members — the tower's most experienced combatants — are distributed around the landing. Sharr speaks first:

*"She healed us. And then she left us to it."*

He waits for a response. He is not asking for an apology. He is not asking the party to fix it. He is asking whether they are capable of receiving this statement without deflecting.

## Combatants (if triggered)

### Sharr
See `npcs/sharr.md`. **HP 16, AC 12.** Nail-flail +5, 1d8+2. Shove bonus action (Athletics +4, pushes toward ledges). Morale break at 8 HP.

### Three aggrieved congregation members
**HP 14 each, AC 12.** Iron-tipped weapons +4 to hit, 1d8+1 bludgeoning. No special abilities. Morale: break if Sharr retreats or if two of three are incapacitated.

**🎲 Stat blocks:**
**Sharr — CR 1, 200 XP.** **AC** 12 · **HP** 16
**Aggrieved Member × 3 — CR ¼, 50 XP each.** **AC** 12 · **HP** 14

**Total XP if all defeated:** 350 XP (medium encounter for party of 4, level 3).

## Social Resolution

**The bilateral arc-completion path (Route D):**

Sharr articulates the resentment. One of his flanking members adds: *"She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal. We know what Mishakal feels like. This was different."* (Hint 2 delivery — DC 11 Insight to receive without deflecting.)

Dren connects: Aldis died asking for a healer who went elsewhere. He says: *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."*

Renn responds: *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."*

The bilateral fires. Sharr hears both names. He looks at the cord. He unties it. *"You understand what it costs."*

**The honest-answer path (Route C/D partial):**
If the bilateral does not fire, the party can still receive the cord by answering Sharr's implicit question: "Do you understand why she couldn't come back?" A correct, honest answer (not a deflection, not a defense) will move Sharr to a version of the arc-completion without the bilateral. Weaker, but functional.

**Deflection paths (Routes A/C):**
- Defending Velantha: *"I know she couldn't. I'm not asking you to fix it. I'm asking if you can hear it."* — he is patient; the scene can continue.
- Institutional framing ("we're here for the cathedral"): Sharr's posture changes; one flanking member moves closer to the stairs; the scene is closer to combat.
- Silence or advance without response: combat triggers.

## Combat Trigger Conditions

Combat triggers if:
- Party advances past Sharr toward the upper stairs without resolving the encounter
- Party attacks first
- Party takes the cord by force or grab attempt
- Party explicitly refuses to engage with the congregation's stated grievance

## Tactical Notes

**Verticality is the encounter's key mechanic.** Sharr's Shove ability, combined with the staircase edges in R3 and the open arches in R6, means the party must manage their positioning. Dren's platform: secure position first — he should have his back to a wall. Maret: Patient Defense as a bonus action prevents Shove from landing cleanly (Shove requires hitting, then Shove vs Dex; Dodge gives disadvantage on the attack roll). Renn: Eldritch Blast from range keeps him away from edges; Agonizing Blast deals real damage (7-13 per bolt). Calla: Faerie Fire targets Sharr's Con save (DC 13) — Sharr's Con is likely +1 or lower; reasonable chance to land.

**Combat pacing:** The encounter should feel dangerous. Sharr's Shove landing once (moving a PC 5 ft toward a ledge) creates real pressure even if the fall doesn't happen. The party has 250 XP and full resources; this encounter should drain at minimum: 1 spell slot, some HP damage, at least 1 Ki or Superiority die.

## Route Implications

- **Route D:** Bilateral fires; cord given freely; Sharr steps aside; upper levels accessible; Nave congregation's trust maintained.
- **Route C:** Party survives without destroying the congregation; acknowledges the resentment; leaves without the cord OR accepts a partial transfer (Sharr keeps it but the party has heard him); Bell Tower congregation intact; campaign continues.
- **Route A:** Party fights through; takes cord by force; Nave congregation will know by morning; Route D begins closing for the finale.

## Post-encounter: congregation behavior on return

- If Route D: Sharr and the Bell Tower congregation remain in the tower. They are not friends, but they are not enemies. The party can return.
- If Route C: Similar. The congregation is wary but present.
- If Route A: The congregation retreats deeper into the tower or to the bell platform. The cord is with the party. The Nave congregation's relationship to the party cools.
