# Sharr, the Oldest in the Tower

## Appearance

Male gully dwarf, 60+ years, thin in a way that suggests long effort rather than deprivation. He stands with the posture of someone who has been waiting a long time and has learned to make waiting look like standing. The promise-cord is knotted around his left wrist; he does not remove it except during the braiding rite in R5. His left wrist is slightly warmer than his right.

## Stat Block

**HP** 16 · **AC** 12 · **Speed** 20 ft.

- **Nail-flail** +5 to hit, 1d8+2 bludgeoning.
- **Shove (bonus action, after landing a melee attack):** Sharr shoves the target (Athletics +4 vs target's Str/Dex save). On success, target moves 5 ft toward the nearest ledge. If already within 5 ft of a ledge, target must make DC 12 Dex save or fall to the next level down (10 ft = 1d6 bludgeoning; 40 ft from R6 = 4d6).
- **Morale break:** At 8 HP or fewer, Sharr stops attacking and retreats — first to R5, then R6 if pursued. He does not fight from R6 but will give the cord if the bilateral arc has fired.

**Immunities/Resistances:** None.

## Public Knowledge

- He has been here thirty-two years — longer than any other living tower member.
- The cord on his wrist was Velantha's. He keeps it because it was hers and he was healed and he has not found a better way to say what he thinks about that.
- He knows what Velantha used — not its name, but its feel. She used something that was not prayer. He has known this for thirty-two years.
- He does not want to fight the party. He wants to know if they are capable of understanding what the congregation has been carrying.

## Private Knowledge

- Sharr was healed of a severe burn that covered his left arm. The healing was complete. His left arm is entirely functional. It is the arm the cord is wrapped around.
- Velantha looked at his arm when she healed it and said: *"I'm sorry I can't make the scar lighter. I can only make the function back."* He has thought about this sentence for thirty-two years. She was honest about what she could and couldn't do. That is what he cannot forgive or forget: she was honest, and the honesty was not enough.
- He does not want the party to fix this. He wants them to understand it. These are different things.

## Information Readiness Condition

Sharr holds the ritual-focus-in-tower seed and Velantha's-unkept-promise seed. He will transfer them when:

- **Ritual focus:** If the party asks about the congregation's relics (not the cord — the other object they keep). He will show them the ritual focus — a carved stone, warm, marked on the base. He does not know what the mark means. *"We found it near where she stood. It was warm. We kept it."*
- **Unkept promise:** If the bilateral arc fires, or if the party asks directly what Velantha promised that she couldn't deliver. He says: *"She told some of us she would come back. She didn't come back. She died here instead of coming back."*

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** The bilateral arc fires in R3 (Dren names Aldis; Renn names Mira in response). Sharr hears both names.

**The act:** He unties the cord from his left wrist. He holds it out. He says: *"You understand what it costs."*

He does not say more. The cord is the party's to take. He turns and looks at the upper stairs.

**Why this is the completion:** Sharr has been keeping the cord for thirty-two years because he had no one to give it to. Not to hand off to a successor — to give it to someone who understood why it existed. The cord tracks what was promised and what was kept. The party, by naming their own unfulfilled promises, have demonstrated they know what the cord means. That is the condition.

**Alternative condition:** If the bilateral does not fire, Sharr will accept an honest answer to: *"Do you understand why she couldn't come back?"* If the party says yes and explains it correctly (she used the High Sorcery working and died; she did not choose not to return), he will give the cord. This is a weaker version of the arc-completion — the cord is transferred but the arc does not fully close.
