# Premise — The Bell Tower

**Logline:** The party enters the part of the cathedral where Velantha's faithful are still angry — and anger, held for thirty years, has edges.

## Setting

**Place:** The bell tower of the Halted Spire — accessible from the upper gallery (R6 of 0016) via the east arch, once the debris is cleared. The tower is the cathedral's tallest unfinished structure: three internal levels plus an open bell platform at the top. The bell exists. It is hung correctly. It does not ring. The rope that should ring it has been replaced with something braided from rags, cloth strips, and hair over thirty years by the people who live here.

**Atmosphere:** The walls of the Bell Tower are covered in scratch-tallies. Not the careful thirty-row tally of the wall-walk (R4 of 0015) — these are different: dense, overlapping, some scratched over others, some crossed out. Thirty-two years of marks made by people who were counting something they could not name. The bell rope hangs in the center of the shaft, braided and rebraided. The bell above does not move in the wind that comes through the open belfry.

## The Congregation in the Bell Tower

The Bell Tower congregation numbers approximately fifteen. They are the aggrieved ones — gully dwarves Velantha healed whose lives after healing remained hard. They kept faith for thirty years. They also kept anger for thirty years. Both are true simultaneously.

They came here because Velantha healed them. They are angry not that she failed but that she succeeded and the success was not enough. The healing was real. The poverty, the loss, the hard years after — those were also real. The congregation in the tower has been performing the same devotional practices as the nave congregation for thirty years, and the practices have not made the hard years easier. Faith can coexist with resentment. They know this.

**They are not immediately hostile.** They are wary, defensive, and quick to escalate if the party moves aggressively. Their leader — **Sharr** — is the congregation's most articulate voice for their specific grievance. He is also the most dangerous combatant in the building.

**The ritual focus is here.** The aggrieved congregation has one of Velantha's healing tools: a small carved stone focus that was used to channel the High Sorcery working. They kept it because it was warm when they found it. They have no idea what it is — they treat it as a relic, the same way the nave congregation treats the cup. But the Orders would recognize it immediately from its mark.

## Hook

The party has heard from Veth (S2): *"Some of us came here angry. Some are still angry. They are in the tower."* They have cleared the debris from the R6 east passage (via Maret's failed attempt in S2 and subsequent effort, or through another path). They enter the Bell Tower.

**Doran's pressure** is building. She hired the party to clear the site. They are now two sessions in and have engaged with three sections of the congregation instead of clearing them. She sends word (via a message delivered to the gatehouse) that she needs a status report within three days. The party must decide what to tell her — and what to do about the Bell Tower — before that deadline.

## Central Artifact (seed)

**Velantha's promise-cord** — a length of knotted tracking string she used to track her patients. Some knots have been cut (patients who died). Others are open (patients who lived). An open knot tied around a kept promise tightens on its own.

## Themes

- Faith and resentment as simultaneous truths — not contradictions; both real
- What healing cannot fix (Velantha saved lives; she could not save the lives those lives would live)
- The bilateral arc: Dren's crack and Renn's parallel fire here
- The first real threat in the campaign — the party should be tested

## The Bilateral Arc-Completion (Design Mandate)

**Structural requirement:** The Bell Tower congregation's specific resentment — *"she healed us but she could not save us from everything that came after"* — must surface both Dren's and Renn's grief simultaneously. They have each been carrying a version of this for two sessions.

**Naming order:** Dren names first. When Sharr or another congregation member articulates the resentment clearly, Dren connects it to Aldis. He says: *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."* Renn hears this and names Mira in response: *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."*

**Conditions from PC sheets:**
- Dren's condition: the congregation articulates "she healed us but couldn't save us from everything." Dren makes the connection to Aldis dying of plague while a healer was elsewhere.
- Renn's condition: after Dren names Aldis, Renn names Mira. Bilateral — Renn cannot name first; Dren's naming creates the conditions for Renn's.

The DM should not prompt either naming. The naming conditions are in the PC sheets. If the scene is played correctly (the congregation's resentment is articulated clearly and honestly), the bilateral fires from the heuristics.

## Hint Delivery (Hint 2)

**Primary delivery:** During the encounter with Sharr and the congregation, one of the aggrieved members says — either in dialogue or in the confrontation's emotional peak: *"She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal. We know what prayer feels like. This was different."*

**Delivery mechanism:** This is delivered if the party engages with the congregation's grief rather than fighting through them. DC 11 Insight from any PC: the statement is not an accusation — it is a description. They have known for thirty years that what they received was not divine grace. They never knew what it was instead.

**Recovery path:** If the session's dialogue does not deliver Hint 2 (party fights their way through without conversation), the ritual focus in the tower provides mechanical confirmation in 0018: the elder can identify what the focus was used for, which delivers Hint 2 from the object rather than the testimony.

At minimum: Sharr (+5 to hit, Shove as bonus action to push toward ledges), plus 2-3 aggrieved congregation members. The party should spend at least one spell slot, one Ki point, and take some HP damage this session.

- **Gully dwarf gloss in session log** — mandatory (S3 log first paragraph must contain it)

## Encounter Design (Combat Mandate)

**Sharr** (Bell Tower leader):
- CR: approximately 1 (custom combatant)
- HP: 16, AC: 12
- Attacks: Iron nail-flail (+5 to hit, 1d8+2 bludgeoning)
- Special: **Shove (bonus action)** — after landing a melee attack, Sharr can attempt to shove the target (Athletics +4 vs target's Str/Dex save). On success, target moves 5 ft toward the nearest ledge. If already adjacent to a ledge, they must make DC 12 Dex save or fall 10 ft (1d6 bludgeoning) to the level below.
- Morale: fights until reduced to 8 HP, then backs off and demands the party explain why they're here.

**2-3 aggrieved congregation members** (CR ¼ each, as gatehouse):
- HP: 12, AC: 11. Improvised weapons +3 to hit, 1d6 bludgeoning.

This gives: approx. 200 XP (Sharr CR 1) + 50-75 XP (congregation members). Medium-difficulty encounter for party of 4 at level 3.

## Routes

- **Route A:** Fight through the Bell Tower, take the promise-cord, exit. The congregation is damaged or scattered. The Nave congregation will know by morning.
- **Route C:** Survive the Bell Tower without destroying the congregation; acknowledge their resentment; leave without taking the cord. The congregation remains in the tower but is no longer hostile. The party has not gained the cord but has not lost Route D.
- **Route D:** The bilateral arc fires; the congregation recognizes that the party understands what they're carrying; Sharr allows the party to take the cord (or shows them where it is). The resentment is not resolved — it is witnessed.
