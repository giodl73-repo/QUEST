# S3 LOG — The Bell Tower

## Session Summary

*Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by every Krynnish faction to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory. The congregation in this tower has maintained the same walls, the same marks, the same braided rope for thirty-two years.* The Witnesses cleared the debris in the R6 east passage (Maret's second attempt fumbled; Dren cleared it) and entered the Bell Tower. Maret flanked via the tally alcove's south loop; Dren entered through the base with Renn. The confrontation with Sharr went social first — Calla misread his opening line as hostile — then escalated to brief combat when the party's hesitation registered as refusal. Renn's Eldritch Blast reduced Sharr to 3 HP in the first round; morale broke. In the stillness after, Dren named Aldis. Renn named Mira. Sharr untied the cord. The ritual focus fell from a congregation member's hands and was warm. Hint 2's primary delivery failed (Renn missed by 1); the recovery path through the ritual focus is active.

### Setup
> Sharr, older than the rest, stands near the stairs. Three congregation members flank him. A knotted cord on his left wrist. The walls here have fewer marks — more careful, some almost like rows of writing. He looks at you: "She healed us. And then she left us to it."

Actually fired in the second saying of the line — after combat — when Sharr backed against the wall and repeated his statement slower. She triggered the combat by misreading the scene. The session's first party damage (3 HP to Calla from an aggrieved member) resulted from her misread. The verbal-documentation-arc practitioner who is best at listening failed to listen at the crucial moment. (Scene 3.3)

The combat was real — the threat was real — and it ended in one round. The party's investment in AC paid off. (Scene 3.3)

## Questions this session raised about the module

- **Sharr's morale break (8 HP) may be too high.** With Renn's EB dealing 11 damage and Maret's 2 damage totaling 13, Sharr broke morale after 1 round without any opportunity to use his Shove ability. The Shove was the designed threat; it never fired. For future combatants with signature abilities, consider whether the morale threshold prevents the ability from having a session impact.
- **Calla's Faerie Fire exactly meeting DC 13 (Con save success) is the third consecutive session where a designed concentration spell fails.** S1: Faerie Fire vs Ghoul Con 17 (success). S2: no combat spell. S3: Faerie Fire vs Sharr Con 13 (success). The party's concentration strategy is being consistently denied. This may need a different approach in S4+.
- **The bilateral arc firing in post-combat stillness** rather than in the social encounter is dramatically superior to the designed version. Consider designing future arc-completion scenes with a combat resolution path that still produces the arc — not as fallback but as an equally valid path.
