## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Bilateral arc fires in post-combat stillness
Dimension affected: Atmospheric Landing, Character Agency, Surprise
What happened: The bilateral arc was designed to fire through social engagement with Sharr's
articulated resentment. In play, combat began first (Calla misread Sharr's opening line). The
bilateral fired AFTER combat — in the stillness when Sharr, at 3 HP, repeated his statement
slower. The combat was not an obstacle to the arc; it was a necessary precondition. The party
had to survive the resentment before they could receive it.
Why the rubric missed it: The designed-trigger vs. actual-trigger pattern (I-C3-S1-02, adopted
v1.7) anticipated this. But v1.7 documents the arc firing at the consequence rather than the
cause. This is a further refinement: the arc firing after an encounter that EMBODIES the
emotional content, not just expresses it. The combat was the resentment made physical;
the naming was possible only after surviving it.
```
