## Critique

### Overall assessment

The most accessible session of the campaign. The gully dwarf gloss is in the first paragraph. The combat is followable (one round, clear sequence). The bilateral names two people — Aldis and Mira — and a new reader understands what each name means because the session log established it three paragraphs earlier. The Fresh Face approves.

### What worked

- **The bilateral exchange (Scene 3.3):** *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."* / *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."* A reader who knows nothing about D&D can follow this exchange. Two people named something. The connection is human. The cord tightened. No jargon. No class feature names. Just names and a cord.
- **The gully dwarf gloss:** Finally in the first paragraph. *"Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by every Krynnish faction to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory."* The session's emotional payoff (the bilateral, the cord, thirty-two years of marks) lands harder because this sentence is there.

### What needs work

- **"Eldritch Blast" not translated.** Scene 3.3: *"Renn: EB 22 hit, 11 damage."* The EB is abbreviated in the dice line; the narrative says "a bolt of dark force from Renn's outstretched hand" — correct. But the dice line itself is readable by D&D players and opaque to others. The log's dice lines should always have a narrative translation nearby. S2 had this pattern right; S3 slipped.
- **Hint 2 failure not explained for a new reader.** The session log notes "Hint 2 primary delivery: FAILED — recovery path active." A fresh reader doesn't know what Hint 2 is or why its failure matters. A one-sentence explanation would help: "The party heard someone say Velantha had used something other than prayer — but they couldn't tell if it was an accusation or a fact. They'll need to find out another way."

### The question I'd push on

**A new reader knows the following after 3 sessions:** A healer died here. The congregation kept faith. Some of them are angry. The party is investigating. The healer used something other than prayer (maybe — Renn wasn't sure). A knotted cord has been given. This is enough to stay invested. Is it enough to understand what the finale will require? The Fresh Face says: by S4, a new reader should be able to formulate the central question ("can this place be sacred if its founding act was not divine?"). Right now they have all the pieces except the last one: what exactly did Velantha do, and was it wrong?
