## Critique

### Overall assessment

The bilateral is the campaign's best character moment so far. Not because of what was said — though both lines are correct — but because of the sequence: combat first, naming second. The party had to survive the resentment as a physical fact before they could receive it as an emotional one. Dren waited for three sessions and named Aldis in the exact right moment. Renn named Mira in response without explaining the pact. Both lines were exactly what the sheets promised.

### What worked

- **Dren's naming (Scene 3.3):** *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."* This is a correct Dren sentence. Short. Finished. Contains the specific fact (plague, not combat). Contains the institutional critique (cleric allocation). Does not contain self-pity. He named Aldis while holding the pocket with the insignia. The Roleplayer would have had him take the insignia out — but holding the pocket closed is also Dren. He is not displaying the grief; he is acknowledging it.
- **Renn's response (Scene 3.3):** *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."* He said *also*. He placed himself in the same category as Dren without elaborating. He did not explain the pact. He did not perform his grief. He named her and stopped. This is the correct use of the bilateral — one naming creates the condition for the other; the second naming is smaller, not larger.

### What needs work

- **Calla's fumble (Scene 3.3):** Insight 5 — she misread Sharr's opening. She triggered the combat by misreading. The Roleplayer asks: what does Calla feel during the combat she caused? The session log shows her casting Faerie Fire (which failed) and taking 3 damage. But no interior note. One sentence in the log would have closed this: "Calla, who caused this, does what she can." Not an explanation — an acknowledgment. She knows she misread it.
- **Renn and the pact:** He named Mira without referencing the pact. This is correct for Session 3. But eventually Renn's secret needs a designed entry-point — a scene where someone asks the right question and he has to decide whether to answer. Design that scene into 0019 (Sacristy, where Aldric Voss will understand unauthorized magical transactions). The conditions for Renn's disclosure are in that room.

### The question I'd push on

**Has Dren's bilateral arc completed, or is it still open?** He named Aldis. The arc-completion section in his PC sheet says: "Dren makes the connection to Aldis and names him aloud to another party member." He named Aldis to Sharr, with Renn present. Is the arc complete? The Roleplayer says: the arc closes when the insignia is no longer just a carried object — when Dren does something with it. He still hasn't found Aldis's family. He still hasn't decided what to do with the insignia. The naming was one act; the completion requires another.
