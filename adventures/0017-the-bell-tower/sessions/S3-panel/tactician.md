## Critique

### Overall assessment

Finally. Calla took 3 damage. A flanking member hit AC 14 and the spell slot was spent. The panel mandate was met. My concern this session: Sharr's Shove ability — the designed mechanical threat — never fired because he broke morale before Round 2. The encounter was real, but the session-defining ability was not tested.

### What worked

- **Scene 3.3 (initiative order):** Sharr went second (18), after Maret (19). He got his attack before most of the party. The attack missed, but Calla's flanking member landed (3 damage). First party HP loss in 3 sessions. The combat felt dangerous for exactly one round.
- **Dren's choice (scene 3.3):** He went last (init 3). By the time his turn arrived, Sharr had broken. He waited. He named Aldis instead of swinging. From the Tactician's perspective: this is the session's best tactical decision. The greatsword is a resource; using it on a broken morale is wasted. He used the pause to name a person instead. Efficiency.

### What needs work

- **Shove never fired.** The Shove ability (Athletics +4, push toward ledges) required Sharr to land a melee hit first. He attacked once (miss), then broke morale. The verticality threat — the dungeon's designed danger — was never tested. **Fix for S4+:** If a boss has a signature ability that requires landing a hit, ensure they have at least 2 rounds of combat. Either raise morale threshold or add a minimum engagement clause ("Sharr will not retreat until he has landed at least one Shove, regardless of HP threshold").
- **Faerie Fire pattern.** Three sessions, three concentration spells attempted, all failed at exactly or above DC. This is statistically unusual (Sharr Con +0 = base +0, so DC 13 means 40% success rate; exact DC success once is 5%). Log the pattern for S4 planning. Calla needs a backup option when concentration fails.

### The question I'd push on

**Does 0018 (Transept Witness) have a combat encounter?** The campaign spine suggests no. That means S4 will be the third consecutive session without a testing combat after S3's single-round fight. The party needs resource depletion before the campaign's midpoint. Design at least one mechanical hazard in 0018 — not a fight, but a navigation challenge that costs a Ki point, spell slot, or HD.
