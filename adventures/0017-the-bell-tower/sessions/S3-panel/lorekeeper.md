## Critique

### Overall assessment

The gully dwarf gloss appeared in the session log's first paragraph — three sessions, finally applied. The setting is properly anchored: the Bell Tower congregation maintains the same devotional forms as the nave congregation while holding resentment that the nave congregation has processed differently. Both groups are gully dwarves doing what Krynnish society has decided they cannot do. The session earned its highest lore marks of the campaign.

### What worked

- **Session log opening (Session Summary):** *"Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by every Krynnish faction to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory. The congregation in this tower has maintained the same walls, the same marks, the same braided rope for thirty-two years."* This is exactly the gloss the Lorekeeper has been requesting. It appears in the session summary, not buried in a room description. Any reader arriving at S3 cold now has the context to understand why the tally wall is extraordinary.
- **Sharr's burn scar (npcs/sharr.md):** His left arm — healed by Velantha, cord wrapped around it for thirty-two years — is the physical record of what she did for him. The cord on the healed arm is the session's best setting-specific detail. Velantha fixed function, not appearance. She was honest about it. He has held that honesty for thirty-two years.

### What needs work

- **Hint 2 not received:** The congregation member said the Hint 2 line clearly (*"She used something. We felt it. It wasn't Mishakal."*). Renn heard it as accusation. The Lorekeeper notes: in a post-Cataclysm world where Mishakal's grace has returned, a gully dwarf community would have specific experiential knowledge of what prayer feels like vs. what High Sorcery feels like. The statement is not theological — it is empirical. The failure to receive it (Insight 10 vs DC 11) is correct dice, but the session log should note that the statement was specific and factual. Renn's misread was a character failure, not a scene failure. Log it as such next session.

### The question I'd push on

**Is the congregation's rite a Mishakal rite?** The Lorekeeper raised this in S2. The bell tower's rite is the same form as the nave's. Both groups learned from Velantha's example, not from Mishakal's formal liturgy. The congregation has independently recreated something that resembles Mishakal's communal healing rites (water, sharing, communal verse) without knowing that's what they've done. Design an answer by the Keystone: when the cathedral is consecrated (or given), does Mishakal recognize the congregation's thirty years of practice as legitimate worship? This theological question is the campaign's deepest lore implication.
