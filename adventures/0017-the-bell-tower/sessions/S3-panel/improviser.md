## Critique

### Overall assessment

Calla's fumble triggering the combat is the best accidental yes-and of the campaign. She misread the situation (5 vs DC 11), the misread produced a real consequence (combat, 3 HP damage), and the DM let the consequence stand. The combat she caused was the necessary precondition for the bilateral. The Improviser gives this session its highest surprise score: 9/10.

### What worked

- **The bilateral in post-combat stillness:** Not scripted, not designed as a combat-then-naming sequence, but better than the designed version. Sharr repeated the line slower after breaking morale. That second saying — same words, different pace — is what produced the firing conditions. The DM did not manufacture this; it emerged from Sharr's character logic (a person who has been saying the same thing for thirty-two years would say it again when given a second chance). This is yes-and at the level of NPC character logic.
- **Dren not swinging (Scene 3.3):** His initiative was 3; Sharr broke before Dren's turn. The DM let him wait rather than finding something for him to do. The waiting was the correct choice. The DM recognized that Dren's action was coming and let the scene breathe rather than filling the dead turn with mechanics.

### What needs work

- **The ritual focus falling (Scene 3.4):** A congregation member dropped the ritual focus during the party's R5 visit. Good — the Wandering Encounter result produced a scene-defining moment. But the session log notes only that the focus was warm and marked. The DM did not ask the congregation member WHY they were carrying it or WHY they fumbled it. A yes-and DM would have had the congregation member say something when they picked it up. "She left it." (which they did say) is good. One more word would have been better: what was their relationship to it?

### The question I'd push on

**Does the bell ring before the campaign ends?** Three sessions in the Bell Tower across the campaign (S3, plus R6 sighting from the gallery in S2). The bell is there. It is correctly hung. It has not rung in thirty-two years. The Improviser is watching this. When the party ultimately encounters the finale (0021), and the congregation is assembled, and the choice is being made — if someone rings the bell, what happens? Design an answer before the Keystone session. Not what happens mechanically. What happens emotionally. The session log noted "no one rings it" at R6. That restraint was correct for S3. It will not be correct forever.
