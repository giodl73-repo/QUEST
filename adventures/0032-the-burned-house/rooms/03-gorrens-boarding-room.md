# Room 03 — Gorren's Boarding Room

## Read-aloud
> A single room with a cot and a locked box under it. The window shutter is closed. When the knock comes, there is a pause — not the pause of someone waking, but of someone deciding whether to answer. Then the door opens, knife in hand. The face above the knife is unshaven, exhausted, and relieved.

## Features

- **Gorren Dast.** Fully awake, clearly has not been sleeping properly, holding a short knife at his side (not raised, but not sheathed). Full NPC file: `npcs/gorren-dast.md`.
- **The locked box.** Under the cot. He has not opened it in three days. Investigation DC 12: it is a document box — the kind used for papers, not valuables.
- **The room's state.** Five days of bread crusts, an empty water pitcher refilled twice. He has been here and nowhere else. The window has been shuttered since he arrived.
- **His first question.** He asks: *"Is Tessaly alive?"* before anything else. This tells the party two things: he knows Tessaly's name, and he was worried about her before he was worried about himself.

## GM Notes

Gorren's first question establishes what he cares about. He is not asking whether he is safe — he already knows he is in danger. He is asking whether the person who organised the guild's record-keeping is still in a position to receive information. His concern is operational, not personal. Score this at handoff.

The locked box: it contains Gorren's own operational notes — locations, contact schedules, nothing that names Pale directly. He has not shown it to anyone. He will give it to Tessaly with the key. This is a secondary information source for S6.

When Tessaly shows him the ledger (Scene 4), give him the moment. He reads. He sees the date. He makes the connection. The recognition registers before the words do — passive Perception DC 10 to notice his expression change before he speaks.
