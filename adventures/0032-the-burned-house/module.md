## Setting & Background

The party investigates the raid that burned the guild safe house and killed a member, and the surviving member names the leak without being asked once he sees the evidence Tessaly has been keeping.

**The burned safe house**, Havenmere South Quarter — a rented ground-floor room in a tenement block on Chandler's Cut, three blocks from the Wharfside. The room burned six days ago. The Watch listed it as accidental fire; the surviving guild member knows it was not.

**Gorren Dast**, safe house member and the only one who got out, is currently in a boarding room two streets away. He has not contacted the guild. He does not know if the guild is safe to contact.

Tessaly has a ledger entry she did not fully understand when she made it: a meeting she recorded eight months ago between herself and a runner, at a location she coded in the ledger as "C4-2" — which resolves to the safe house. The entry records information she passed to the runner (a counter-surveillance observation, routine). She passed it cleanly. But in the current context, with Pale selling names, the entry is evidence that the safe house location passed through her records in a form that Pale could have read.

She has not decided whether she leaked it accidentally. The ledger is her professional record. If it shows she was the source, she will accept that. The session is about finding out.

**This is the RV1 test:** Does sheet-deep-reader fire from Tessaly's ledger without grief? She cares about the ledger because it is her professional record — the accurate accounting of eleven years of work. She does not grieve the burned house or the dead member (she barely knew him). The ledger matters because it is the truth and she needs to know if the truth makes her responsible.

### Scene 1 — Tessaly Opens the Ledger
At Cordwainer Lane. Tessaly spreads the ledger on the table and turns to the C4-2 entry. She does not explain immediately. She points to the entry and says: *"This is the location. I recorded it. Pale had access to my records once — eighteen months ago, during a joint billing review. If he read this entry, he had the location."*

She is not asking for reassurance. She is presenting the evidence to the party as she would present it to herself: here is what I know, here is what it might mean, here is what I need to find out. The ledger is the starting point.

**DM note (RV1 setup):** This is the sheet-deep-reader test moment. Tessaly's ledger is her loyalty object. She is using it as an investigative tool, not as a grief object. She is not upset — she is working. Score this at handoff.

### Scene 2 — The Burned Room
The safe house room is still a ruin — the Watch sealed it after the fire but has not cleared it. Investigation DC 12: the fire started in two places simultaneously. This is arson, not accident.

Investigation DC 15: the point of entry was the rear window, not the front door — the raiding party knew the layout. They came in through a window that faces an alley. The layout is not in any written document except Tessaly's ledger.

Investigation DC 17 (Tessaly, with +7): the entry point is consistent with someone who had read a written description of the building rather than having cased it. The marks at the window suggest they were following a map or notes, not professional thieves — Watch auxiliaries, not guild operatives.

### Scene 3 — Finding Gorren
Rue Ashen knows where Gorren is — she passes the boarding room address to Tessaly in her usual channel. Gorren is in the boarding room, hasn't left in five days, has been subsisting on bread from the building's common kitchen.

When the party arrives: he opens the door with a knife in his hand (he does not attack — he has been waiting for someone with the wrong intentions and is relieved to see someone with the right ones). He does not know whose intentions are right. He asks: *"Is Tessaly alive?"*

### Scene 4 — The Evidence
Tessaly shows Gorren the ledger entry. She does not explain what she thinks it means. She shows him the C4-2 entry — the location code, the date, the meeting recorded.

Gorren reads it. He looks at the date. He says: *"That's three days before Pale told me the safe house was compromised."*

He stops. He did not mean to say that last part. He looks at Tessaly. Then he says the rest: *"Pale told me the safe house was compromised from a Watch observation. He said it was random. He said to stay until he could arrange a new location. He was lying. He had the location from you, from that entry, and he sold it. He arranged the raid. I don't know why Orren — I don't know why he would burn his own house. But he did."*

**Arc-completion condition:** Gorren names Pale without being asked when he sees the evidence Tessaly has been keeping. The ledger is the trigger. He did not plan to say this — the date in the ledger connected a fact he had been holding separately. The evidence makes the connection inescapable and he names it because the alternative is sitting in the boarding room for another five days with no one to tell.

### Scene 5 — The Decision
Tessaly writes the entry. Gorren's account. Pale's statement. The date alignment. She writes it before she speaks to the party.

Then she says: *"The ledger is clean. I recorded correctly. He read it. That is what happened."*

She does not apologise for the ledger. She does not apologise for the entry. The ledger is accurate. It recorded a routine meeting. Pale exploited that accuracy. The ledger is not at fault. Neither is she. But the member died. She knows this.

**Gorren's marker:** He does not have a formal guild marker. He gives Tessaly the key to a secondary location he was keeping as a private emergency resource — a storage room on Dock Road that no one knows about, including Pale. *"If you need a place that he doesn't know,"* he says. He hands her the key. It is the trust marker.

## DM notes

The burned room is the investigation scene — it yields its information through skill checks rather than through occupants. The rear alley is the key physical finding: the window entry marks are there and they tell the story.

The session moves through three locations (Tessaly's office, the burned room, Gorren's boarding room) with the office serving as bookend. This is not a dungeon; it is an investigation across an urban district. Distance between locations is 5-10 minutes of walking. No encounter is anticipated between locations unless the pressure table triggers.

### Scene 1 — Tenement Front (Chandler's Cut)

> A Watch seal on the ground-floor door — a wax-and-ribbon closure, official, about five days old. The building above is occupied; someone is cooking on the second floor. No one is watching the entrance.

#### Features

- **The Watch seal.** Breakable (DC 12 Thieves' Tools; Darro: DC 8). Breaking it is evidence of entry — a Watch constable who checks the door later will notice. Not breaking it means going through the rear alley instead.
- **The alley approach.** The rear alley gives access to the burned room's window without the seal. Darro can open the window from outside (Thieves' Tools DC 11). This is the preferred approach.
- **The building's other occupants.** The tenement has four other units. None have anything to do with the safe house. If the party is loud or breaks the seal, a second-floor occupant may look out a window (Perception DC 8 for her to notice activity at the sealed door).
- **The Watch notation.** On the seal: *"Fire investigation concluded. Cause: accidental. Clearance pending."* Investigation DC 11: "clearance pending" means no one has claimed the room's contents. The space has not been cleared.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This room is the approach decision — seal or alley. The session is designed to steer toward the alley (cleaner, no Watch complication), but the seal approach is available and costs only the risk of the second-floor occupant noticing.

The sealed door's "accidental" conclusion is the first confirmation that the Watch's official record is wrong. It seeds the investigation in the burned room.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The Watch seal was applied by a constable who had no reason to look closely. The conclusion of "accidental" came from the reporting officer, not the constable. The officer was following instructions she had received from the South Station.*

### Scene 2 — The Burned Room

> The floor is scorched black in two places — one near the door, one beneath the window. The wall above the window is burned worse than the wall above the door. Not one fire spreading: two fires, lit separately. The window frame is splintered on the outside edge, not the inside.

#### Features

- **Two fire origins.** Investigation DC 12 (Tessaly auto-succeeds): the char patterns show two ignition points. Arson.
- **Window entry marks.** Investigation DC 15 (Tessaly auto-succeeds): the window frame is broken inward — the entry came from outside. The break pattern shows controlled force at a specific point, not random damage.
- **Map-follower evidence.** Investigation DC 17 (Tessaly rolls — needs 10): the entry point selection is too precise for someone who cased the building. The window is not visible from the street; a caser would choose the door. Whoever chose this window was following a description of the building, not their own observation.
- **What remains.** A few fragments of a cot's frame. A coat hook with nothing on it. A water jug, melted. Nothing that tells you who lived here. The room held nothing personal.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The burned room yields three findings: arson (DC 12), external entry (DC 15), map-follower pattern (DC 17). Each is a layer of the investigation. Tessaly's Investigation (+7) makes the first two automatic and the third a real roll.

The map-follower finding is the critical one — it is the evidence that points to a written source, which points to her ledger. This is the session's investigative spine. Without the DC 17 finding, the investigation still reaches Gorren, but the ledger connection requires his testimony rather than the physical evidence.

If the DC 17 fails: Tessaly doesn't make the ledger connection herself. Gorren's testimony in Scene 4 still names Pale, but the ledger's C4-2 entry becomes something she has to connect retrospectively rather than leading with. Both paths work — the DC 17 just changes who makes the connection first.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The room held two people. One got out the front before the window-fire. One did not. The Watch record has no name in the casualty column — the column is blank, because nobody claimed the body, because the body burned and nobody wanted to explain why they knew who it was.*

### Scene 3 — Gorren's Boarding Room

> A single room with a cot and a locked box under it. The window shutter is closed. When the knock comes, there is a pause — not the pause of someone waking, but of someone deciding whether to answer. Then the door opens, knife in hand. The face above the knife is unshaven, exhausted, and relieved.

#### Features

- **Gorren Dast.** Fully awake, clearly has not been sleeping properly, holding a short knife at his side (not raised, but not sheathed). Full NPC file: `npcs/gorren-dast.md`.
- **The locked box.** Under the cot. He has not opened it in three days. Investigation DC 12: it is a document box — the kind used for papers, not valuables.
- **The room's state.** Five days of bread crusts, an empty water pitcher refilled twice. He has been here and nowhere else. The window has been shuttered since he arrived.
- **His first question.** He asks: *"Is Tessaly alive?"* before anything else. This tells the party two things: he knows Tessaly's name, and he was worried about her before he was worried about himself.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

Gorren's first question establishes what he cares about. He is not asking whether he is safe — he already knows he is in danger. He is asking whether the person who organised the guild's record-keeping is still in a position to receive information. His concern is operational, not personal. Score this at handoff.

The locked box: it contains Gorren's own operational notes — locations, contact schedules, nothing that names Pale directly. He has not shown it to anyone. He will give it to Tessaly with the key. This is a secondary information source for S6.

When Tessaly shows him the ledger (Scene 4), give him the moment. He reads. He sees the date. He makes the connection. The recognition registers before the words do — passive Perception DC 10 to notice his expression change before he speaks.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Gorren was in the front room when the fire started at the window. He went out the front before he understood what was happening. He has been replaying the two seconds of decision it took him to go to the front door rather than back to help his partner. He has not yet resolved what to do with those two seconds.*

### Scene 4 — Chandler's Cut (Transit)

> The street between the burned house and Gorren's boarding room is two blocks of ordinary Havenmere morning — a fishmonger setting up, a cobbler opening his shutters, a child running an errand. Nobody looks at the party. They are four people in working clothes moving at a purposeful pace. They are invisible.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This is a walking-beat room — no encounter, no roll. It exists to give the session a breath between the burned room and Gorren. The observation moment is for the four loyalty-object checks to register as a group: the party in transit, all professionally grounded, the work proceeding.

If the pressure table fires here, it is a Watch sighting — not combat, just a patrol that requires the party to split briefly and reconverge two streets over.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Every person the party passes on Chandler's Cut has no idea a guild member died in a building two blocks away. This is how the city works. The city is full of things that happened inside it that it does not know about. The guild has been one of those things for forty years.*

### Scene 5 — Cordwainer Lane (Session Close)

> Tessaly spreads the ledger on the table. She does not speak yet. She writes the entry: Gorren's account, the date alignment, Pale's name. The third entry. She closes the ledger. She says: "The ledger is clean. I recorded correctly. He read it. That is what happened."

#### Features

- **The ledger entry.** Third time Pale's name appears. Tessaly writes it cleanly — no qualification, no blame. Factual recording.
- **The three facts laid out.** She names them to the party: (1) The location was in my records. (2) Pale had access eighteen months ago. (3) Gorren's date confirms he sold it. She presents these the way she would present any account: in order, without embellishment.
- **Gorren's key.** The secondary location key, if he gave it. She holds it for a moment. Then she gives it to Darro — for safekeeping, because he is the one who thinks about locks and exits. She does not explain. She just gives it to the right person.
- **What she does not say.** She does not say the member's name. She does not have a record of the name — the safe house did not use real names in the assignment log. She looks at the space in the ledger where the name should be. Then she turns the page.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The empty name space is the session's emotional center — not grief, exactly, but recognition. Tessaly's professional code says record everything. The name is not in the record because the record was designed to protect people from having their names in it. The protection worked for the record. It did not protect the person.

This is not a grief beat. It is a professional-code beat: the limits of the system she has built. Score at handoff — is her response to the empty space professionally-triggered (she identifies a gap in the record system) or does something else fire?

Gorren's key going to Darro is an act-without-announcement beat: Tessaly gives the right object to the right person without explaining the logic. Darro accepts it and files it in his mental checklist. Nobody comments.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *The ledger has one blank space in eleven years: the name of the person in the safe house. Every other entry has a name. This one has a code. The code was the protection. The protection was not enough.*

## NPCs

# Gorren Dast

## Appearance

A man in his late thirties. Solid build, going to softness from five days of bread and inactivity. Stubbled. He has the look of someone who has been running a background calculation since the fire and has not resolved it. He carries a short knife the way experienced people carry something they use for work: handle-down, unhurried.

## Background

Gorren Dast ran counter-surveillance for the guild safe house network for four years. Not glamorous work — he watched people watching other people, and he reported what he saw. He was good at it because he was patient and he did not invent patterns that weren't there.

He was at the safe house the night of the fire because he had drawn the overnight shift. He got out because he reacted to the window fire before the door fire fully caught. His partner was in the back room. He did not go back.

He has been sitting with that fact for five days.

## Stat Block

- **AC** 12 | **HP** 16 | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 13, **DEX** 14, **CON** 12, **INT** 12, **WIS** 14, **CHA** 10
- **Skills:** Athletics +3, Insight +4, Perception +6, Stealth +4
- **Passive Perception** 16

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Gorren's response |
|---|---|
| Knocks on his door | Pauses 30 seconds. Then opens with knife visible. |
| Identifies as guild-sent | First question: "Is Tessaly alive?" |
| Shows him the ledger entry | He reads the date. His expression changes. He makes the connection. |
| Asks who sold the location | He answers before they finish asking. |
| Asks what he needs | "A reason to leave this room." |
| Asks about his partner | A three-second pause. "He was in the back room." He does not say more and should not be pressed. |

#### Arc-Completion
**The moment:** He names Pale. He gives the statement in sequence: Pale's warning, the date, the conclusion. He does not ask anyone to believe him. He is not asking for anything. He is completing a report to the only person with the records to verify it.
**The condition:** Tessaly shows him the C4-2 entry in the ledger. The date is visible. He connects the date to Pale's statement ("three days before Pale told me the safe house was compromised").
## The thing he is not saying

He got out the front. His partner did not. He has not decided whether to count this as a professional decision (fastest route, correct choice under pressure) or a failure (he could have gone back). He is not going to decide tonight. The party should not try to help him decide — if they do, he closes. He will process this himself, eventually, in his own time.

## Notes for the DM

Gorren's arc-completion is the RV2 cross-check: he is not a morally clean NPC (he did work for a compromised operation for four years, he got out and his partner didn't), but his arc fires by character logic. The pattern from S2 (Aldras) and S4 (Gorren) should both score as character-logic arc-completion in the handoff. If both fire the same way despite different moral registers, RV2 is confirmed.

This is consistent with her standard intelligence function. She has her own reasons to want Gorren found — he knows things that are relevant to the Revenue Intelligence investigation she is monitoring.

Her arc-completion (S3 per original file, now updated: fires in S5 when she connects her intelligence on Pale with the party's investigation) remains pending.

### Watch Surveillance

# Combat Encounter — Revenue Intelligence Surveillance

## Trigger

Officer Sevarr from S2 is now aware that Aldras escaped. She has deployed a watcher on the burned-house block (connecting the extraction to the fire investigation). Trigger: roll 1d4 when the party approaches the burned house — on a 1, the watcher is in position.

## Composition

**One Revenue Intelligence Watcher** — Spy stat block (SRD, CR 1)

- **AC** 12 | **HP** 27 (6d8) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Skills:** Deception +6, Perception +4, Stealth +4
- **Passive Perception** 14
- **Actions:** Shortsword +4, 1d6+2 × 2 (multiattack). Cunning Action.
- **XP:** 200

The watcher's goal is identification, not interdiction. She is recording who investigates the burned house, photographing them mentally, and reporting back to Sevarr.

## Non-Combat Resolution (preferred)

Tessaly spots her (Investigation DC 14 — noticing the watcher's unusual stillness in a doorway). She signals the party to split: she and Cael continue past, Darro and Mira approach separately via the alley. The watcher sees Tessaly but cannot confirm she was heading for the burned house.

If the watcher is not spotted: she records Tessaly's description and the party's approach path. Sevarr has them in a week. This does not trigger immediate consequences but seeds S5.

## Encounter notes

Combat is unlikely — the watcher is professional, her goal is intelligence, and engaging the party would blow her cover. If cornered, she identifies herself as a Revenue Intelligence officer (true) and demands to know their business at a sealed site (legal authority). This is the Cael moment: a Watch-adjacent authority demanding names. His code says: don't sell a name. The party has to manage this without naming themselves.

Mira's Deception DC 16 provides a cover explanation. Cael's Intimidation DC 14 creates enough uncertainty that the officer decides the encounter is not worth the confrontation. Either works.

**XP budget:** 200 if combat (unlikely). Scene XP: 50 for a successful identification-evasion regardless of method.
