# C5-S4 Session Log — The Burned House

**Date in world:** PC 188, eight days after C5-S3. Morning.
**Spotlight:** Tessaly Ink
**Seed:** C5S4-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro before the brief. Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond. The set was complete. He rolled it and set it on his left hip. The morning was grey; the boarding house window faced east and the light was flat.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly opened the ledger to the C4-2 entry before anyone else arrived. She sat with it for three minutes. The entry was eight months old: a meeting she had recorded, a runner, a counter-surveillance observation that had seemed routine. She was re-reading it as a document that might implicate her. She read it the way an auditor reads — for what is present, not for what she remembered thinking at the time.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira checked the paper case. She opened it briefly — the framework was growing. A fourth component had been added since S3: Tessaly's entry format from eight months ago (the C4-2 entry), photographed during this morning's ledger review while Tessaly's back was turned. Mira had copied the date format, the meeting code structure, the runner attribution convention. She added it to the paper case without comment. She had needed to see an older entry to understand how the conventions had evolved. She closed the jacket.

## Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane: The Evidence

Tessaly spread the ledger on the table. She did not explain why. She turned to the C4-2 entry and placed two fingers on it.

*"This is the safe house location. I recorded it. The meeting was eight months ago — a routine counter-surveillance observation, nothing sensitive. Pale had access to my records once, eighteen months ago, during a joint billing review. If he read this entry, he had the location."*

She was not asking for reassurance. She was presenting the evidence to the party as she would present it to herself.

*"This is what I need to find out. Whether the entry is the source. If it is, I recorded it, and I am responsible for the consequences. That is what the ledger is for."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
This is the RV1 primary test: Tessaly's ledger as professional instrument, not grief object. She barely knew the dead member. The ledger matters because it is the accurate record of what happened. She is using it the way a professional uses documentation: to determine facts. The emotional register is not distress — it is the particular focus of someone who is prepared to accept bad news if the evidence shows it.

## Scene 2 — The Burned Room

The safe house ruin was six days old. Watch seal on the door — Darro's lock pick, DC 8 (standard seal; expertise applied): automatic. The room was black along two walls. Ash and char smell that had not dissipated.

Tessaly's Investigation DC 12: **d20+7 → rolls=[16] mod=+7 total=23**. The fire had started in two places simultaneously — two separate ignition points, visible from the scorch patterns. This was arson, not accident. The Watch's "accidental fire" listing was wrong or deliberate.

Tessaly's Investigation DC 15 (entry point): **d20+7 → rolls=[5] mod=+7 total=12**. She found the entry point at the rear window. The window faced an alley. The entry marks — she crouched and looked at them for two minutes — suggested someone who had approached from a specific angle, not a professional thief's approach. She could not read the full pattern from this angle; the light was wrong.

*"The entry was from the rear window,"* she said. *"The approach angle is specific."*

She did not have the full interpretation. She recorded what she had.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
She wrote in the ledger margin at the window: *burned room / entry point: rear window, east alley approach / arson confirmed: two ignition points / interpretation: partial.* The notation before she spoke was automatic — the writing is how she processes.

## Scene 3 — Finding Gorren

Rue Ashen's channel: the boarding room address on Dock Road, two streets from the Wharfside. Cael's Perception on approach: **d20+3 → rolls=[2] mod=+3 total=5**. He missed something — a figure at the end of the street who had been watching the boarding house entrance for approximately the same amount of time the party had been watching from the other direction. It resolved itself: the figure was another Watch contact of Rue's, running observation on Gorren, not hostile. Tessaly clocked it on her own passive Perception (14 passive). She filed it without comment.

Gorren opened the door with a knife in his hand. He had been in the boarding room for five days. He had been subsisting and waiting. His Insight on the party: **d20+4 → rolls=[11] mod=+4 total=15**. He read them as not threatening. The knife went down but stayed in his hand.

*"Is Tessaly alive?"*

*"Yes,"* she said.

He stepped back. He let them in.

---

## Scene 4 — The Evidence

Tessaly opened the ledger to the C4-2 entry. She set it on the table in front of Gorren. She did not explain what it meant. She showed him the entry: location code, date, meeting recorded.

Gorren read it. He looked at the date. He looked up. He looked back at the date.

*"That's three days before Pale told me the safe house was compromised."*

He stopped. He had not meant to say that. He could see himself having said it. He looked at Tessaly. Then he said the rest:

*"Pale told me the safe house was compromised from a Watch observation. He said it was random, a beat patrol that caught a glimpse. He said to stay until he could arrange a new location. He arranged the raid instead. He had the location from you — from that entry — and he told me it was Watch. He used the same location he got from your record and then told me the Watch had found it independently."*

He stopped again.

*"I don't know why. I cannot understand the mathematics of it — he burned a house he knew about, killed a member he was running, and lied about the source. I cannot make that add up to profit."*

**Arc-completion fired.** Gorren named Pale when he saw the evidence Tessaly had been keeping. The date in the ledger connected a fact he had been holding separately for six days. The evidence made the connection inescapable. He named it because the alternative was another five days in the boarding room with no one to tell.

Tessaly's Insight on Gorren's account: **d20+4 → rolls=[19] mod=+4 total=23**. She read him completely. He was afraid. He was telling the truth. He had not intended to name Pale — once he read the date, the naming was not a choice. It was a conclusion.

---

## Scene 5 — The Decision

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly wrote the entry before she spoke. Gorren's account — the date alignment, Pale's statement, the raid arranged from the same location. She wrote Pale's name in the record for the third time. She wrote it the same way she had written it the first two times: last, after all the confirming details.

Then she closed the ledger. She said:

*"The ledger is clean. I recorded correctly. He read it. That is what happened."*

She did not apologise for the ledger. She did not apologise for the entry. The ledger was accurate. It had recorded a routine meeting. Pale had exploited that accuracy. The ledger was not at fault. Neither was she. But the member died. She knew this. She recorded it and she knew it.

*"He has my entry in his records somewhere. He knows my coding conventions. He has been inside my documentation once. He will have used it."*

She looked at Mira.

*"The forgery cannot use my actual conventions directly. He will recognize them."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira's hand went to the paper case. A beat — three seconds. Then she withdrew her hand. She did not say: I have already noted your conventions, I can work around this, I have been building the framework. She said: *"I understand."*

She updated the paper case that evening. The entry-format component from S4's morning — the copied conventions — was annotated with a single note: *must diverge at C4-2 era formatting; Pale knows this register.* The framework had been refined without announcement.

Gorren gave Tessaly the key to the Dock Road storage room. *"If you need a place he doesn't know."*

She wrote the key's address in the ledger before she took the key.
