# Encounter — The Last Splinter Cell

**Location:** The farmland approach to Thornmere, one mile from the village.
**Trigger:** The three remaining agents step from the treeline as the party approaches.

**Context:** This is the last active splinter cell. The other two have stood down (Vorn's decision, transmitted through her network). These three did not. They have been watching Pellan for four days. They know the party is coming. They have decided to make a stand because the alternative is returning to a now-defunct organization with nothing to show for two years of field work.

They are not unreasonable. They are tired. They have been in the field for two years. Their leader (the cell coordinator) made a decision to keep going when Vorn stood the network down, and now she is not sure it was the right decision. If the party offers the stand-down (the same offer Thessaly gave the first splinter cell outside the White Quill), two of the three will take it. The coordinator will not, initially.

## Stat blocks

**Splinter Cell Coordinator (Cell Leader):** CR 2, AC 13 (leather + Dex), HP 35.
STR 14, DEX 16, CON 13, INT 12, WIS 12, CHA 11.
**Attacks:** 2× shortsword +5 (1d6+3) | hand crossbow +5 (1d6+3).
**Evasion:** As rogue, halves damage on successful Dex save; immune to damage on successful save with evasion.
**Cunning Action:** Dash/Disengage/Hide as bonus action.

**2× Splinter Cell Agent:** Same as previous splinter cell stats (Thug). AC 11, HP 32. 2× Mace +4 (1d6+2). Pack Tactics.

**Adjusted XP:** 1 Coordinator (450 XP) + 2 Agents (200 XP) = 650 base. ×2 multiplier (3 enemies) = 1300 adjusted. Per PC (5): 260 XP. **Medium encounter.**

## Route D stand-down (preferred)

**Offer:** Any PC says (some version of): *"The main faction stood down. Vorn stood down. You're the last cell. There's nothing left to defend."* 

**Persuasion DC 12** (the agents are tired and the offer is reasonable) or **roleplay threshold** (if the party explains what they are building — the new compact, the witness-list, that Vorn is collaborating).

**Response:** Both agents immediately lower their weapons — they have been waiting for an excuse to stop. The coordinator hesitates. She says: *"We've been in the field for two years. If we stand down, what was it for?"*

If a PC answers honestly — "it was for the right problem with the wrong method; the problem is still being solved" — she stands down. Persuasion DC 10 (she wants to be convinced; she is not being difficult; she is asking a real question). If answered: she sheathes her sword. She says: *"Fine."* She means it.

**Pellan, from his doorway:** He has been watching. He comes out. He says: *"That's the answer."* He goes back inside to get the shard.

## Combat (if stand-down refused)

**Tactics:** Coordinator uses Cunning Action to stay mobile; she focuses on Thessaly (softest target, most shards). Agents use Pack Tactics on whoever the coordinator attacks.

**Lenne's role:** Projected Ward fires if Thessaly takes a hit. This is the session where Lenne's Ward gets genuinely used in a consequence-having situation.

**Orik's role:** Battle Master. Commander's Strike to set up Lenne's Counterspell or protect Thessaly. Menacing Attack on the coordinator (frighten her, break Pack Tactics coordination).

**Retreat trigger:** Coordinator at ≤10 HP. She disengages and runs. The agents stand down. Pellan gives the shard after.

## What this combat is for

The splinter cell is the last mechanical threat of the campaign. Every combat the party has had — the Kharolis road (S02), the chapel entrance (S03), the White Quill entrance (S04) — has been escalating toward either confrontation or stand-down. This encounter presents both options simultaneously and lets the party choose.

Route D is cleaner. Route C gets the shard. Either way: this is the last fight.
