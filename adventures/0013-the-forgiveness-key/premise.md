# Premise — The Forgiveness Key

## Hook

Vorn Haleth's note, hand-delivered: *"The Clause of Forgiveness is held by a retired Orders investigator named Pellan Ashwick. He has been living in a village called Thornmere, forty miles east of Palanthas, for twenty years. He recovered the shard during an investigation into relic theft and kept it because he could not determine its rightful owner. He knows it is a compact shard — I told him, eight months ago. He said: 'Then it should go to someone who can use it correctly. I'll know them when I see them.' I believe he meant someone who has already forgiven something they didn't have to forgive."*

The party travels to Thornmere. They find Pellan — but they also find three members of the last remaining active splinter cell, who got Vorn's information before she sent it to the party. The splinter cell has been watching Pellan for four days. They are not subtle about it; Pellan knows they are there. He has been waiting for the party to arrive, because he knew the party would come if Vorn sent word.

## Setting

Thornmere — a small agricultural village, forty miles east of Palanthas. A market square, a grain mill, a waystation-inn, and Pellan Ashwick's cottage at the north edge. The surrounding farmland is open — long sightlines, little cover. If the splinter cell has been there four days and Pellan knows it, the encounter on the approach road will not be a surprise for anyone.

## Artifact

**The Clause of Forgiveness** — *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."* The shard is a dull finish rather than polished — absorbs light rather than reflecting it. It is room temperature. It does not vibrate, glow, or feel heavy. It feels, when held, exactly like a piece of stone. The magic is inward: an attuned creature can perform a forgiveness they were unwilling to perform before, once per long rest — not forced, but enabled. The capacity was always there. The shard removes the barrier.

**The forgiveness requirement.** Pellan will know who to give it to by watching whether they have forgiven something they didn't have to forgive. This is not a test he administers; it is a recognition he waits for. All four original PCs (Thessaly, Orik, Sera, Calder) have done this by Session 6:
- Thessaly: named the Test wound to Vorn (forgiving herself enough to name it publicly)
- Orik: named the hall to Lenne (forgave himself enough to speak it as explanation, not self-punishment)
- Sera: saw the settlement safe (forgave herself for leaving; forgave them for not waiting)
- Calder: received Sylaren's note and returned it (forgave the protocol's designers enough to stop carrying the weight as guilt)

Pellan will recognize this immediately when they arrive. He will say: *"Yes. You're the ones."*

## Themes

**Forgiveness** — the emotion. The clause says: *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."* The compact Sylaren was building was not built on rage at the Orders; it was built on the desire to fix the structure. The party has been doing the same.

**All four original PCs** — Thessaly, Orik, Sera, Calder each have a moment. Lenne is present and also has a moment, but her spotlight is the finale. This adventure closes each original PC's arc before the finale.

**Combat as load-bearing** — the splinter cell is the last active cell (the other two have stood down). This combat is the adventure's resolution, not its complication. The party clears the threat; Pellan gives them the shard. The combat is structurally simple but emotionally significant: it is the last time the party will fight as a group before the finale.

## Three-route outcomes (A/C/D)

**Route A (Charter/Fallback):** The party meets Pellan, he reads them correctly, he gives the shard after a brief conversation. The splinter cell is handled diplomatically (Persuasion DC 15; the last three know Mira stood down and are tired). Clean.

**Route C (Default):** The splinter cell attacks. Combat in the approach to Thornmere (open farmland; no cover; the party has to deal with an ambush). Pellan watches from his cottage doorway. After the combat: he gives them the shard without a word.

**Route D (Inversion/Optimal):** Pellan sees the party handling the splinter cell — not just defeating them, but offering them the out that Mira gave Osvin and the Quiet One at the very beginning. *"The main faction stood down. This is your chance to stand down too."* If the splinter cell accepts: they lay down their weapons. Pellan comes out of the cottage. He sees what just happened. He says: *"Yes. You're the ones."* He gives them the shard.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5. One genuine combat encounter: the splinter cell (3 agents, last active cell). Hard if they fight; Easy-Medium if the party uses the stand-down offer first. Lenne's Projected Ward gets a genuine use; Orik's Battle Master has room to shine.

## Constraints

- Room count: 4 (farmland approach, village square, Pellan's cottage, grain mill — optional secondary location)
- This is the last adventure before the finale. Keep it clean. No new lore, no new hooks. The purpose is to close the arc of each original PC and get the sixth shard.
- P0: the forgiveness condition should not be a puzzle — Pellan recognizes them immediately. The adventure's tension is the splinter cell, not the shard acquisition.
- The DM-letter is not for Pellan's arc-completion (it doesn't require hold-space — it's recognition, not naming). It's for the moment after the shard is given, when each PC understands they have already done the thing that earned it.
