# The Clause of Forgiveness

*The sixth shard. Dull finish. Absorbs light. Feels like a stone.*

## Appearance

Obsidian, same family. Dull finish — absorbs light rather than reflecting it. The inscription is shallow but clear: *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."* Archmage Orien of the White Robes (PC 192–220; died in the Cataclysm). Sigil: an open hand, palm up. Not the clenched hand of the Reckoning or the closed hand of the Grief. Open.

When held: it feels exactly like a stone. No warmth. No cold. No weight anomaly. No vibration. The magic is entirely inward.

## Presence / Desire

The Clause of Forgiveness does not reach outward. It waits. It has been waiting in Pellan Ashwick's cottage for twenty years and it has not shown signs of impatience. This is appropriate: forgiveness cannot be rushed.

## Provenance

1. **Archmage Orien of the White Robes (PC 192–220):** Swore the clause. He was the most junior signatory; he was 26 years old. He died in the Cataclysm. He never saw whether the compact worked.
2. **Tower of High Sorcery, Istar (destroyed PC 220).**
3. **Multiple intermediate holders (~200yr):** The shard changed hands many times. It is the least dramatic of the shards — no effects, no weight, no temperature. It tends to be overlooked.
4. **Pellan Ashwick (20 years ago – present):** Recovered it during an investigation into relic theft. Kept it because he couldn't determine its rightful owner. Recognized it immediately when Vorn told him what it was. Has been waiting.

## Properties

**Passive:** Room temperature. No detectable magical effect when held (Detect Magic: faint transmutation aura, easily missed). Feels like a stone.

**Arcana DC 14:** The inscription reads as the Forgiveness clause. Thessaly: automatic.

**Active (attuned only):** Once per long rest, the attuned creature can perform a forgiveness they were previously unable to perform — not forced, not compelled, but enabled. The capacity was always there; the shard removes the barrier.

## Curse

**The obligation of openness:** An attuned creature is unable to hold a grudge as a motivating force. They may still feel anger; they cannot use anger as the foundation for a commitment. If the attuned creature enters a binding agreement (oath, contract, compact) that is based primarily on anger — wanting to harm someone, wanting revenge — the shard makes this visible: the agreement will feel wrong in a specific way. The creature does not have to break the agreement; the shard does not enforce. It shows what is there.

**Ending:** Releases when the compact is spoken whole.

## GM Notes

- **Pellan's decision.** He has been living with this shard for twenty years. He knows what it does. He gave it twenty years because he was waiting for the right people — not people who understood forgiveness theoretically, but people who had already done it. The party qualifies.
- **The inscription read-aloud.** Thessaly reads it automatically. She says: *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."* She will think of the compact she is building. Then: *"That's the point."* Because it is.
