# Pellan Ashwick

*Retired Orders investigator, 68. He recovered the Clause of Forgiveness during a relic investigation twenty years ago and kept it because he couldn't find its rightful owner. He has been waiting for the right people to claim it.*

## Appearance

Pellan is stocky and unhurried. He was an investigator for thirty years; he knows how to read people, and he doesn't waste time pretending he can't. He has a vegetable garden at his cottage. He came out to water it when he saw the party approaching and kept watering it while they talked, because the tomatoes were due.

## Stat block

**Non-combatant (retired).** AC 12 (formerly better), HP 14. He has a shortsword. He won't use it unless someone threatens the tomatoes specifically.

## What he knows

- The shard is a compact fragment. Vorn told him eight months ago.
- The forgiveness clause's mechanism (he has held it for twenty years; he understands what it does).
- The splinter cell that has been watching him for four days. He was not going to do anything about them; they are not his problem; he was waiting for the party.
- Which PC earned the shard: all four original PCs have already done the thing it requires. He will recognize this within two minutes of meeting them.

## Emotional position

**What he wants:** To give the shard to the right people and go back to his garden.

**What he can't say:** He is moved by the fact that they came. He has been waiting twenty years for someone to show up who qualifies, and now they have. He does not have the vocabulary for how this feels; he is a retired investigator, not a poet. He will say: *"Yes. You're the ones."* and that will have to be enough.

**The condition:** He reads the party for two minutes. He recognizes the forgiveness that has been done. He gives the shard.

## Arc-Completion

**The moment:** He looks at each of them in turn — Thessaly (who named the wound), Orik (who named the hall), Sera (who saw the settlement safe), Calder (who returned the note). He says: *"Yes. You're the ones."* He goes inside. He comes back with the shard in a cloth wrapping. He gives it to whoever is standing at the front. He says: *"The inscription is about the covenant. Make sure yours isn't built on anger. You'll know if it is."* He goes back to the tomatoes.

**What happens if condition never met:** Pellan says: *"Come back when you've had to forgive something you didn't owe."* He goes back inside. Route C (the splinter cell) is still available; the combat resolves; he gives the shard afterward because the combat itself required forgiveness to resolve cleanly.

## GM Notes

- Pellan is not complicated. He is a retired investigator who has been waiting twenty years. He knows what he is looking for. He finds it. He gives the shard. The complication is external (the splinter cell), not internal.
- His recognition of the party is immediate and genuine. He was not skeptical that the right people would come; he was patient that they hadn't yet. Now they have. He is satisfied.
