# The Forgiveness Key — DM Module

*A retired investigator who has been waiting twenty years for the right people. The last active splinter cell. An open field with long sightlines. Six shards.*

**Tier:** 2  |  **Party:** 5 PCs, Level 5  |  **Expected playtime:** 1 session

## Summary

The Clause of Forgiveness is at Pellan Ashwick's cottage in Thornmere. Pellan will give it to the party as soon as he sees them — he has been waiting twenty years for someone who has already forgiven something they didn't have to forgive. The party qualifies.

The encounter is the last active splinter cell: three agents who didn't stand down when Vorn did. Route D: the party offers the same stand-down they gave the first agents at the White Quill entrance. Route C: combat. Either way: the last fight.

This is the last adventure before the finale. It closes each original PC's arc.

---

## Hook

Vorn Haleth's note: *"The Clause of Forgiveness is held by Pellan Ashwick, retired Orders investigator, Thornmere, forty miles east of Palanthas. He's been waiting for the right people. He'll know you when he sees you."*

## Setting & Background

**Thornmere** — Small agricultural village. Market square, grain mill, waystation-inn. Open farmland with long sightlines.

**Pellan Ashwick** — Retired investigator, 68. Recovered the Clause of Forgiveness during a relic investigation 20 years ago. Has been waiting. Not complicated.

**The splinter cell** — Last three agents of the Unbound Conclave's field network. Two active cells have stood down. These three did not. They have been watching Pellan for four days. They are tired.

## Scenes

### Encounter — Splinter Cell Last Stand

**Read-aloud:**

> Open farmland. Long sightlines. Three figures step from the treeline on the road's east shoulder. They are not hiding. The center one says: *"We know who you are. We know what you're carrying. And we know Vorn stood the network down. We didn't."*

**Route D (preferred):** Any PC offers stand-down terms — "The main faction stood down. There's nothing left to defend." Persuasion DC 12 or roleplay. Two agents lower weapons immediately. Coordinator hesitates. Answer her honestly ("right problem, wrong method; the problem is still being solved") — Persuasion DC 10, she wants to be convinced. She sheathes her sword. Pellan comes out: *"That's the answer."*

**Combat (Route C):** See Encounters Appendix.

### Scene 01 — Village Square (after encounter)

**Read-aloud:**

> Thornmere. A man at the north edge is watering his tomatoes. He looks at each of you in turn. He says: *"Yes. You're the ones."* He puts down the watering can. He goes inside.

Pellan recognized the party immediately. He is getting the shard.

### Scene 02 — Pellan's Cottage

**Read-aloud:**

> Pellan returns with a cloth-wrapped object. *"I've had this for twenty years. I kept it because I couldn't find its rightful owner. I found you now."* He gives it to whoever is at the front. *"The inscription is about the covenant. Make sure yours isn't built on anger."* He returns to the garden.

**Features:** The shard (room temperature; dull finish; feels like a stone). Pellan gives it without ceremony. Thessaly reads the inscription: *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."*

**Per-PC recognition:** Each original PC has already done the thing the shard requires:
- Thessaly: named the Test wound (forgiving herself enough to say it aloud)
- Orik: named the hall (as explanation, not self-punishment)
- Sera: saw the settlement safe (forgave herself for leaving)
- Calder: returned Sylaren's note (stopped carrying the weight as guilt)

None of them need to say this. Pellan already knew.

### Scene 03 — Grain Mill (Optional, Sera's perch)

Available if combat retreats into village. High point (Athletics DC 10; 300-ft sightline).

### Scene 04 — Pellan's Garden (Aftermath)

**Read-aloud:**

> Pellan is checking the tomato stakes. He says: *"Seventy-nine days? Then you'd better go."*

Thessaly corrects the number. He nods. Calder talks to Pellan about the garden. Orik checks the cottage structure. Sera looks toward Palanthas. Lenne counts her remaining notebook pages.

## Treasures

### The Clause of Forgiveness

*Dull finish. Absorbs light. Feels like a stone. The magic is inward.*

**Front:** *"Let no covenant hold anger as its foundation."* Archmage Orien of the White Robes (PC 192–220). Sigil: open hand, palm up.

**Passive:** Room temperature. No active effect when held. Detect Magic: faint transmutation aura (easily missed).

**Active (attuned):** Once per long rest, perform a forgiveness previously impossible. Not compelled — enabled.

**Curse — The obligation of openness:** Attuned creature cannot hold anger as the foundation of a binding commitment. The shard makes this visible if they try.

## Encounters Appendix

### Splinter Cell Last Stand (Route C)

**Cell Coordinator (1):** CR 2, AC 13, HP 35. 2× Shortsword +5 (1d6+3). Evasion (Rogue). Cunning Action.

**Splinter Cell Agent (2):** AC 11, HP 32. 2× Mace +4 (1d6+2). Pack Tactics.

**Adjusted XP:** 1300 (Medium for 5 PCs). 260 XP/PC.

**Stand-down route:** Any PC invokes the Mira stand-down or explains the new compact honestly → Persuasion DC 12 (agents) + DC 10 (coordinator, she wants to be convinced). If stand-down: no combat; Pellan comes out; gives shard.

**Combat tactics:** Coordinator focuses on Thessaly (shards). Pack Tactics amplifies. Lenne's Projected Ward fires for Thessaly. Orik's Menacing Attack + Commander's Strike creates space.

**Retreat trigger:** Coordinator at ≤10 HP → disengages; agents stand down; Pellan gives shard after.

### Three-Route Outcomes

| Route | Condition | Shard? | Pellan as witness? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (Charter)** | Pellan reads party; gives shard without incident | Yes | No additional role |
| **C (Default)** | Combat; party defeats splinter cell | Yes (after combat) | No additional role |
| **D (Inversion)** | Party offers stand-down; coordinator accepts | Yes (Pellan comes out immediately) | He witnesses the stand-down; he is moved by it |

No DM-letter needed — Pellan's recognition is immediate and non-hold-space. The emotional weight is in the party's private recognition that they have already done the thing the shard required.
