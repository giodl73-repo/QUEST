# The Clause of Grief

*The third shard. It makes you feel it again. Whatever it was.*

## Appearance

Obsidian, same family as the others. Slightly smaller than the Clause of Ambition. The inscription is the deepest-cut of any shard so far — the letters were carved with intent. *"Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks."* Below: the sigil of Archmage Davan of the Black Robes (PC 195–219), who died one year before the Cataclysm. Her sigil is a small closed hand — a fist, but not aggressive: the gesture of holding something carefully.

The shard is cold. Not supernaturally cold — just always slightly below room temperature, as if it is keeping something in reserve.

## Presence / Desire

The Clause of Grief wants to be witnessed. It does not want to cause pain — it wants the pain to be seen. It has been in a stone box in a ruined chapel for eight years because the man who found it understood immediately that this was not something to be used. He was right. The shard is not a weapon. It is a reckoning.

## Provenance

1. **Archmage Davan of the Black Robes (PC 195–219):** Swore the Clause at the Conclave Compact signing. She was the only Black Robe signatory. She insisted on this clause specifically because the White Robes had recently demanded access to a Black Robe research tower during a crisis and then left without acknowledgment of what the tower's scholars had given up. She swore it in her own name and meant every word.
2. **Tower of High Sorcery, Istar (PC 219–220):** The Cataclysm. The shard scattered.
3. **Four intermediate holders (PC 220 – ~PC 320):** Each held the shard for a short time and then gave it away. Nobody kept it long. Nobody wanted to.
4. **An unnamed pilgrim, 20 years ago:** Found the shard in a riverbed near the Qualimosti border and brought it to the ruined chapel as an offering to the departed gods, not knowing what it was.
5. **Morreth of the Kagonesti (12 years ago – present):** Found it in the foundation stones during a repair. Held it for three days. Put it in a stone box. Has not held it since.

## Properties

**Passive:** Always slightly cold to the touch.

**Active (1 minute):** Any creature who holds the shard for 60 consecutive seconds must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 14) or immediately relive their worst personal loss — not a vision; a re-experience, full sensory, present tense, approximately 10 seconds of subjective time. The creature knows it is a memory; it does not feel like one. After the experience: the creature has the Stunned condition for 1 round, then is shaken (disadvantage on Wisdom saves for 1 hour).

**Active (Route D exception):** If the holder has named their grief aloud to at least one other person within the past 24 hours, without being prompted, the shard shows them the lost thing as it is *now* — not the moment of loss but the current state. The settlement, intact. The hall, rebuilt. The loss is not erased; the shard shows the thing survived the losing.

**Passive (inscription reading):** Any Arcana-proficient creature who reads the inscription understands its compact context: the Black Robes demanded acknowledgment of what the Orders cost those who served them. The clause is not an accusation; it is a requirement of honesty.

## Curse

**The weight of witness:** A creature who attunes to the shard occasionally — once per week, randomly — relives a memory of someone else's grief. Not their own: a stranger's. A brief flash, 2-3 seconds, of a specific moment of someone else's loss. The creature can make a DC 12 Wisdom save to resist the flash; failure means they experience it fully and are distracted (disadvantage on the next Perception or Investigation check). The memories are real — they belong to former holders of the shard.

The attuned creature accumulates a small archive of strangers' grief. They do not know the names or contexts; only the moments. This is the Clause's function: to make the holder unable to forget that grief is not abstract.

**Ending:** Releases when the compact is spoken whole.

## GM Notes

She will relive Volenn's death — not as a gradual illness, but the specific moment she found out: arriving at the tower and realizing, from the silence, before anyone said anything. - **Morreth's stone box.** The box is carved stone, latched with a wooden toggle bar. It is not locked. Morreth does not need it to be locked; nobody reaches for the box after they see what it did to him the first day.
