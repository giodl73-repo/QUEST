# DM Letter — Scene 04 (The Sanctuary: Two Namings)

*This letter is for you, the DM. Read it before the session.*

---

## What this scene is

Two people are going to name something they have been carrying. They name it in order. The first naming enables the second.

Sera names the settlement — the note nailed to a tree, the headman's words, the years of not knowing if they were okay. She has never said this aloud. She has given space to Thessaly twice and Orik once without taking any for herself.

Then Morreth names Ven. He has been holding this name for eight years. He gives it after Sera gives hers — because she gave hers. Her naming is the proof he was waiting for.

Your job: hold the space for both. Witness them. Do not add atmosphere between the words.

---

## What to do before it starts

- Read Sera's grief paragraph: the settlement, the note, twenty-three years of tracking people she protected and losing track when they moved on. The specific note: *"Didn't know when you'd return. Couldn't wait. Thank you for the previous seasons."* Nailed to a tree.
- Read Morreth's arc-completion text (in `npcs/morreth.md`). Specifically: *"Ven. His name was Ven."* He says exactly these words, in this order. Do not improvise them.
- Lenne's experience with the shard (Scene 03) has already established what the party is asking Sera to hold. That setup is complete. This scene is the delivery.

---

## How Sera reaches the naming

Sera does not name the settlement because she is asked to. She names it because she sees that this is what Morreth is waiting for — not prompting, not asking, just waiting for someone to do the thing he cannot ask a stranger to do.

She will understand this before she speaks. She has been reading perimeters her whole career. She reads this one.

**The condition will be met when:** Sera speaks, in first person, the specific loss — not "I lost a settlement" but the actual content: the note, the headman, the years of not knowing. If the player is specific, the arc-completion fires. If the player says something general ("I've lost things too"), hold the space and wait — she may go further.

---

## What to do during

**When Sera begins to speak:** Stop. Do not add narration. Do not add atmosphere. She is doing the scene.

**Let her finish completely.** If she names the note specifically, the shard will matter more when she holds it. If she names the years of tracking, Morreth will hear that she understands what it means to tend something without knowing if it matters.

**After she finishes:** Morreth is quiet for a moment. This pause is correct — do not fill it. Then he stands up from the chair. He goes to the stone box. This is silent — no dialogue yet.

**When he gives her the shard:** *"Yes. Take it."* Two words. Then: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."* These are exact. Use them exactly.

**After he speaks:** He straightens the carving on the bench. He picks up a tool. The conversation is over because it is complete. Do not add more dialogue. The scene ends when he turns to the work.

**When Sera holds the shard (Route D):** She waits for the worst memory. Instead: a quality of light in the obsidian surface — not an image, a quality. Warmth where there should be cold. Something alive. She is looking at the settlement, safe. You can narrate this as: *"The shard is cold. You wait for the loss to come again. Instead: warmth, briefly, and the specific shape of a place you stopped looking for. They were fine. They continued. They did not need you to have stayed."*

**If she cries or doesn't:** Either is correct. Either way, don't narrate her emotional response for her. Narrate what the shard does. Let her player hold the rest.

---

## What ends the scene

The scene ends when Morreth turns to the work — when the conversation is complete. After that: the party has the shard, Morreth has said Ven's name, and the chapter closes.

---

## What happens if Sera doesn't name it

Route A: Morreth gives the shard in exchange for the promise — the chapel in the witness-list. This is a valid ending. The promise should be a real promise.

Route C: The agents arrive. The arc-completion requires calm. Under combat, it won't fire.

Sera's grief is still there. It may surface in 0011 or 0013 instead. The grief-paragraph is patient.
