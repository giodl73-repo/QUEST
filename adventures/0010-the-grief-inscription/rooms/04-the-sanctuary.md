# Scene 04 — The Sanctuary

**Location:** The chapel's inner room, behind a short corridor from the main space. **Time:** Day 3, late afternoon.

**Read-aloud:**

> A smaller room behind the main space. Morreth uses it as a workshop — there are woodworking tools on a bench, wood shavings, a half-carved lintel piece. There is also a chair that faces east. He sits here, apparently, in the evenings. The collapsed east wall has a gap here — a window-sized breach that looks into the ruins behind the chapel. The light through it is angled and specific.

**Features:**

- **Morreth's workshop.** The half-carved lintel piece is for the east wall's temporary breach closure — he is planning to seal it properly. The wood is local oak. The carving is more complex than the main lintel: a pattern of interlocking curves that is Kagonesti for "this place is remembered."
- **The chair.** Faces east, toward the ruined section. Morreth sits here in the evenings. He does not read; he just sits and looks at the ruins. He has been doing this for twelve years. It is not sadness, exactly — it is the Kagonesti practice of keeping witness.
- **The gap in the east wall.** Where the Ruins (Scene 05) are visible. A small figure of carved wood sits in the gap — a standing figure, very simple, Kagonesti style. It has been there long enough to weather. Morreth put it there so the gap "wasn't just an absence."
- **The arc-completion space.** This is where Morreth will be when the arc-completion fires — if it fires. He retreats here when he wants to think. If Sera names her grief in the main space (Scene 03), he will be in the doorway of this room, listening. If she names it here, he will be in the chair.

**Arc-completion — available here:**

If the party has reached this room (via main path or ruins bypass), and Morreth is present, and Sera names her grief without being asked — the specific grief, the settlement, the note, the years of not knowing — Morreth will stand up from the chair. He will go to wherever the stone box is (he carries it to the workshop sometimes when he is working late; otherwise it is on the altar). He will bring the shard to Sera.

He gives it to her. He says: *"Yes. Take it."*

And then: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."*

He straightens the carving on the bench. The conversation is complete.

**When Sera takes the shard (Route D):**
She holds it. The cold is immediate. She waits for the reliving to come.

Instead: a quality of light, just beyond the obsidian's surface. Not an image — a quality. The settlement, going about its work. The people she was tracking, unhurt. The headman writing in his accounts. The note on the tree, weathered now, barely readable. Everything she feared had happened to them: fine. Continued. Not needing her.

The shard is cold in her hands. She is crying, possibly, or not. Either way.

**P0 fallback — if arc-completion doesn't fire here:**
Route A: Morreth gives the shard to the party in exchange for a specific promise — that the chapel will be named in the new compact's witness-list. The party can make this promise. They should make this promise.

**P0 failure state for Route C:** Mira arrives via the ruins breach. Combat in the sanctuary. Morreth steps between the combat and the stone box and stays there. The party must clear the agents before the shard changes hands. Even under pressure, the shard can be recovered; the arc-completion requires calm.

**DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-04.md`. This scene requires hold-space for two moments: Sera naming the settlement, and Morreth naming Ven. Both moments require the DM to stop and witness.

**Connections:** Scene 03 (interior); Scene 05 (ruins, via gap in east wall).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The carved figure in the gap has been there for ten years. Morreth put it there after a winter when the wind through the breach was particularly persistent and he found himself avoiding the room. He thought: if the gap is just an absence, I avoid it. If the gap has someone in it, I look at the gap. He made the figure in an afternoon. He does not know if this was wisdom or stubbornness. It worked.*
