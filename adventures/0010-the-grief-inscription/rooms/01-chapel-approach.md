# Scene 01 — Chapel Approach (Road Encounter)

**Location:** Qualimosti borderland road, one mile from the chapel. **Time:** Day 3 travel.

**Read-aloud (road encounter):**

> The road bends around a stand of older trees — Qualimosti-border growth, the kind that has been there long enough to remember when these roads were maintained. Three figures step from the shadows: Osvin with his mace, the Quiet One with rope, and someone new — a woman in unmarked clothes with a small flame pin on her collar. She says: "I'd like to try something different this time. Can we talk?"

**Road Encounter — Unbound Conclave (3 agents, Hard):** Mira Coldsteel opens with a negotiation offer. She knows the party has two shards; she wants all seven. This is not necessarily combat — it can be a social encounter with combat as the failure state.

**Social resolution (Persuasion DC 15 or roleplay threshold):** The party explains Route D (new compact, mortal-spoken, witnessed by all Orders including former Unbound). Mira goes quiet. *"That would actually fix it."* She tells Osvin and the Quiet One to stand down permanently. Arc-completion fires. Proceed to chapel without combat.

**Combat resolution:** See `encounters/road-encounter-mira.md`. Hard encounter for 5 PCs (Mira's Fireball + Counterspell + Web creates genuine threat). Lenne is the tactical counter to Mira; she knows what to expect from a Red Robe trained mage.

**Read-aloud (chapel arrival, post-road):**

> The chapel is pre-Cataclysm stone, partially collapsed on the east side where the roof came down. The west wall is intact and the door is in it — wood, unlatched, with a new lintel that was clearly carved recently by someone who knows joinery. There are fresh-swept flagstones visible through the doorway. Someone has been taking care of this place.

**Features:**
- **East ruins.** Visible from the approach. The collapsed section forms a ramp of rubble that Sera can climb (Athletics DC 10) for a sniper position. Or: the ruins path bypasses the front entrance.
- **Fresh lintel.** Morreth carved it. Investigation DC 10: the wood is three years old; the carving style matches the carved toggles in Morreth's hair. He made this himself.
- **Offering flagstones.** Outside the door, three flat stones with offering bowls (one cracked, two whole). Something was recently left in them: dried flowers, pre-Cataclysm era style.

**GM Notes:**
- **Orik reads the approach** (signature move: counts exits, notes topology). He will note: main door, ruins path (east), collapsed roof sections. *"She knows this place better than we do. The lintel is recent."*
- **Lenne reads Mira** (she reads objects first, then people — but Mira is carrying a mage's posture that Lenne recognizes immediately). She will say, quietly: *"She's Red Robe trained. Or was."*

**Connections:** Scene 02 (Exterior, main path); Scene 05 (Ruins, east path).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The original lintel fell nine years ago during a winter storm. Morreth spent three weeks finding the right piece of wood and two more carving it. He did not carve anything symbolic or decorative — the gods don't need decoration, they need the door to stay up. The lintel is plain, level, and very well-joined. He is pleased with it.*
