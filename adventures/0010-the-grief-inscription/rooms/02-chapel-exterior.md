# Scene 02 — Chapel Exterior

**Location:** Outside the chapel's west wall. **Time:** Day 3, afternoon.

**Read-aloud:**

> Morreth is here — sweeping the flagstones with a short-handled broom, unhurried. He sees you and does not stop sweeping. He finishes the section he was working on, props the broom against the wall, and turns to look at you the way someone looks at a thing they have been expecting to arrive eventually.

**Features:**

- **Morreth.** He was expecting this, or something like it. He has been watching the grey-robed people watch his chapel for six days; this party is not them. He is cautious but not hostile. He will answer questions. He will not invite the party in immediately — he wants to understand what they came for first.
- **The offering bowls.** Three stone bowls on the flagstones. The dried flowers in them are Kagonesti-traditional; Morreth put them there this morning. He replaces them weekly.
- **The chapel's maintenance.** Investigation DC 10: the flagstones have been swept recently; the mortar between them has been freshly pointed; the west wall has three patches of newer stone where repairs were made. This chapel has received consistent care over years. It is not restored — it is maintained.
- **Morreth's broom.** The handle is carved with a small pattern — waves, repeated. Perception DC 12 or Investigation DC 10: the pattern is a Kagonesti grief-symbol, the mark for "the ones who went before." He carved this handle himself.

**Encounter:** None. If the party mentions what happened on the road (Mira; the Unbound Conclave), Morreth listens, then says: *"They've been watching for six days. They didn't come in."* He says this as if it is significant — and it is: he thinks the Unbound Conclave can feel what is in the stone box, and he does not think they want to feel it.

**GM Notes:**
- **Morreth's opening:** He will say, first: *"You're not the grey-robed ones."* He already knows. Then: *"What brought you to a chapel nobody visits?"* He is not suspicious — he is curious. He does not get many visitors who arrive by accident.
- **Sera and Morreth.** She will recognize the maintenance pattern: someone taking care of a place they were not asked to take care of, for reasons they have not explained to anyone. She won't say this. She will be quiet while the others talk.
- **Lenne and the flagstones.** She will notice the recent repair work and want to examine the mortar. This is her decision-order: read the object. Let her. Morreth notices her noticing and does not interrupt.
- **Shortcut:** If the party explains the compact and that they have two shards, and Morreth reads the reconstruction document (which Thessaly carries), he will invite them inside immediately — the shortcut to Scene 04.

**Connections:** Scene 01 (approach); Scene 03 (interior, via main door).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Morreth has been sweeping this particular section of flagstones for twelve years. He knows which stones are level and which have a slight tilt. He sweeps with the tilt, not against it. He learned this in the second year. He has not thought about why he learned it since; it is just how you sweep these stones.*
