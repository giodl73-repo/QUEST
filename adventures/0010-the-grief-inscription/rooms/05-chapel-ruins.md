# Scene 05 — Chapel Ruins (Optional)

**Location:** The collapsed east section of the chapel, exterior. **Time:** Day 3, afternoon.

**Read-aloud:**

> The east section of the chapel came down some time before Morreth arrived. The rubble has been partially cleared — enough to walk through — and a low fieldstone wall defines the perimeter. Wildflowers grow in the gaps between the larger stones. From the highest point of the rubble, you can see the approach road in both directions.

**Features:**

- **The high point.** Sera can reach it in one move (Athletics DC 10 if speed-walking). From here: the approach road is visible for 400 feet in each direction. Perception DC 12: if Mira's agents are approaching, they are visible here two rounds before they arrive at the chapel. This is Sera's early-warning position.
- **The ruins themselves.** Partially cleared — enough to move through. Investigation DC 12: the clearance was done by hand, stone by stone, without tools. Morreth spent a season doing this early in his tenure. The cleared path goes from the ruins' entry to the sanctuary gap in the east wall.
- **The carved figure visible from here.** The simple figure Morreth placed in the sanctuary gap is visible from the ruins. From this angle, it faces outward — toward whoever might be in the ruins, not toward the interior. This is intentional: the figure faces where the absence is.

**Encounter:** None at baseline. If Route C activates and Mira arrives via the road: the ruins are the agent's likely approach vector (they will use the rubble as cover). Sera's position on the high point is critical for early detection.

**GM Notes:**
- **This scene exists for Sera.** Her perimeter-scan instinct will bring her here before anyone asks. She will establish the early-warning position before the rest of the party enters the chapel. She will stay here, quietly, watching the road, while the others talk to Morreth.
- **The carved figure.** If Sera examines it: she notes that it faces outward. Toward where someone might come from. Toward the approach. Morreth put someone in the gap to watch the road. She understands this without needing to say it.
- **act-without-announcement watch.** Sera may spend a significant portion of the session in this position, watching the road, while the party handles Morreth inside. This is her mode of care: she is watching so the others don't have to. She does not announce this. Let it happen.

**Connections:** Scene 01 (approach, path around east wall); Scene 04 (sanctuary, via gap in east wall).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Morreth cleared the ruins in his second and third year. He did not have tools adequate for the large stones so he moved what he could and left the rest. He thought he would get the proper tools eventually and do it right. He has not gotten the proper tools. He has stopped thinking about it. The partially-cleared path is perfectly functional. This was a lesson he did not set out to learn.*
