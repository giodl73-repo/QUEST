# Scene 03 — Chapel Interior

**Location:** Inside the chapel's main space. **Time:** Day 3, afternoon.

**Read-aloud (if party negotiated with Mira — arrived clean):**

> The chapel's interior is dim and clean. Pre-Cataclysm stone, the kind built for ceremony. The east section is closed off with fieldstone — keeps the weather out. On the altar: three fresh offering bowls, a carved panel frame holding only its absence, and at the center, a stone box latched with a wooden toggle bar — slightly colder than the air around it. You notice this before anyone speaks.

**Read-aloud (if party fought Mira — arrived shaken):**

> The chapel's interior is dim and cold — colder than the autumn air outside. Your breathing slows. The pre-Cataclysm stone holds silence like a cup. The altar is simple: offering bowls, an empty frame, and a stone box with a faint surface-cold you feel before you cross the threshold. Your hands are still shaking from the fight. Here, in this place, the shaking stops.

**Features:**

- **The altar and stone box.** The stone box is on the altar. It is carved with no decoration — functional, intentional. The wooden toggle bar is not locked. Morreth did not need to lock it; nobody reaches for the box a second time after finding out what happens when you hold the shard. The box is cold to the touch (Symptom 1 for anyone who touches the box surface).
- **Morreth's fieldstone wall.** Closing off the collapsed east section. It is structurally rudimentary but weather-tight. He mixed the mortar himself. Lenne will examine this. Investigation DC 12: the mixture is correct for the climate; he researched this before starting.
- **The carved panel frame.** Empty. Whatever icon it held was lost in the Cataclysm or its aftermath. The frame is Kagonesti woodwork — not Qualinesti craftsmanship; somebody carried this here. It is very old.

**Lenne and the shard (Symptom 2 — primary manifest):**

Lenne will open the stone box and pick up the shard. This is her decision-order: she reads objects before she reads situations. She will hold it without asking. Nobody will stop her because nobody yet knows what the shard does.

**P0 fallback — if Lenne doesn't pick it up immediately:**
If Lenne asks questions first or hesitates, Morreth says: *"The only way to know what it does is to hold it. That's how I learned."* He does not stop her from trying. If still hesitant: *"It won't hurt you for sixty seconds. Hold it that long and you'll understand what I couldn't put down."*

**P0 fallback — if no PC holds the shard in Scene 03:**
Symptom 2 fires anyway in Scene 04, before the arc-completion. When Sera names the settlement, Morreth brings the shard from the sanctuary and says: *"Before you take this — you need to know what it is."* He holds it out to Sera directly. She holds it; she relives her worst loss (Wisdom DC 14, or the Route D version if the naming just happened). The demonstration is delayed but not skipped — the party must witness the cost before the gift is given.

At 60 seconds: she relives the moment she arrived at Volenn's tower after traveling for a week, and realized from the silence — before anyone said a word, before she found the note — that he was gone. The specific quality of silence. The moment before knowing, when knowing was already irreversible.

She drops the shard. She sits down on the floor. She is not hurt — she is simply not standing up for a moment.

When she recovers (1 round): she says, very quietly: *"Don't hold it for more than a minute."* She puts the shard back in the box herself.

The party now knows what they are asking Sera to hold.

**Encounter:** None at baseline. If Route C timer fires and Mira arrives at the chapel: the chapel interior becomes a combat scene. See encounters/chapel-confrontation.md.

**Connections:** Scene 02 (exterior); Scene 04 (sanctuary, short corridor).

**GM Notes:**
- **Do not prevent Lenne from picking up the shard.** The demonstration is necessary. Thessaly may try to warn her — but Thessaly's Arcana read is "compact shard, dangerous" not "will make you relive your worst loss immediately." Let Lenne make her choice. Let the party watch.
- **After Lenne recovers:** Morreth, who has been watching from the doorway, says: *"Three days. I didn't know what was happening. I couldn't put it down."* He pauses. *"He died twenty-two years ago. I still knew his face when I finally let go."* He does not say the name yet. He is saving the name.
- **Calder and the inscription.** After Lenne recovers and the shard is back in the box, Calder will look at the inscription through the stone box lid (it's visible through a carved gap). He reads it quietly: *"Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks."* He does not say what he is thinking. (He is thinking: they didn't come. When the plague came. They didn't come.)

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The offering bowls on the altar hold dried flowers that Morreth finds in the borderland meadows. He does not know what flowers the gods of the departed preferred. He uses whatever is in season. This morning he used late-blooming thistle, which is probably not ceremonially appropriate, but it was what was there, and the gods who received offerings of whatever-was-there for three thousand years probably understand.*
