# Mira Coldsteel

*Unbound Conclave mage-agent. Red Robe (former). 38. She is the reason Osvin and the Quiet One have not made another frontal assault.*

## Appearance

Mira Coldsteel is contained. Everything about her is deliberate — how she stands, how she speaks, what she shows. She wears unmarked working clothes rather than robes; the only Red Robe signifier is a small flame pin on her collar that she removes when she expects combat. She has the eyes of someone who has been calculating for a long time.

She is not cruel. She is operational.

## Stat block

**Combat-capable.** AC 12 (Mage Armor 15) | **HP** 45 | Speed 30 ft.
**INT** 17 (+3) | **WIS** 12 | **CON** 13

**Spells:** Fireball (L3, primary offensive), Counterspell (reaction), Shield (reaction), Web (battlefield control), Suggestion (social/non-combat), Hold Person (concentration).

**Combat approach:** She opens with Fireball if the party is clustered. She Counterspells any Counterspell targeting her Fireball. She uses Web to split the party (Orik and Lenne on opposite sides). She is not here to kill — she is here to recover the shard. If the shard can be recovered without combat, she prefers that.

## What she knows (public)

- She is Unbound Conclave. She does not hide this.
- She wants the shards to reconstruct the compact under a leadership structure that "actually serves mages" rather than the ghost of a pre-Cataclysm institution.
- She knows the party has two shards.
- She does not know about Lenne.

## What she knows (private)

- She left the Orders twelve years ago after her tower was used as a staging ground for an Order operation she was never consulted about. The operation destroyed three years of her research. The Orders said "the greater work requires sacrifice." She said "not mine."
- She is not wrong about the compact's flaws. The clause Davan swore ("Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks") was written for exactly the kind of operational erasure that drove Mira out. She finds it bitterly ironic that this is the shard she has been sent to retrieve.
- She has been watching the compact-wardens since Korrath. She is increasingly unsure the Unbound Conclave's use of the compact would be better than the party's.

## Emotional position

**What she wants:** The compact assembled under a structure that can't repeat what was done to her tower.

**What she can't say:** She is not entirely sure the Unbound Conclave is that structure. She joined them because they were the only people who were angry about the right things. That's no longer sufficient.

**Arc-completion (available post-combat or Route C):** If the party defeats the agents and takes the time to explain what they intend to do with the compact — specifically, Route D (a new compact spoken by a mortal, witnessed by all three Orders including former Unbound) — Mira goes quiet. She says: *"That would actually fix it."* She does not join them. But she tells Osvin and the Quiet One to stand down, permanently, for this campaign. The agents stop pursuing.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Response |
|---|---|
| Offers to fight | Counterspells the first offensive spell; holds Fireball until she has a clear target cluster |
| Offers to negotiate | Listens. She is not unreasonable. But she needs the shards as leverage; she won't give that up without something. |
| Explains Route D | Goes very still. This is new information. She has not considered that a mortal-spoken compact could be binding. |
| Lenne engages her mage-to-mage | Respects this immediately. Mira knows Red Robe work; she reads Lenne as an Abjuration specialist within two exchanges. |
| Party retreats cleanly | Does not pursue. She is not here to kill; she is here to retrieve. A clean retreat means the encounter resets. |

## GM Notes

- Mira is the most narratively complex enemy in the campaign. She is not a villain. She is someone who was wronged in exactly the way the compact is supposed to prevent, and who is now trying to fix it through a method that won't work. The party can reach her if they try.
- Lenne vs. Mira is a natural Counterspell duel. Both are Red Robe trained (Lenne's school is different but the tradition is shared). They will recognize each other as practitioners.
- If Mira's arc-completion fires: she becomes an off-stage ally. The Unbound Conclave stops being a direct combat threat. Future adventures replace the Conclave antagonist role with a different faction (or Mira's own complications).
