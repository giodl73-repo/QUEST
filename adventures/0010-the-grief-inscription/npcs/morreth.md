# Morreth

*Kagonesti elder, 68. Twelve years tending a ruined chapel to gods who no longer answer. He is not waiting for them to return. He is just keeping the chapel clean.*

## Appearance

Morreth is a Kagonesti — one of the wild elves of Krynn, distinct from the city-dwelling Qualinesti and Silvanesti. He is lean and weathered, with the specific look of someone who has spent decades outdoors not by accident but by choice. His grey hair is braided and pinned with carved wooden toggles. His clothes are practical: a patched robe of undyed wool, boots that have been re-soled twice. He does not carry a weapon. He carries a broom.

The chapel is his. Not legally — he has no deed. But he found it when it was a ruin and he has been maintaining it for twelve years, and at this point the distinction doesn't feel meaningful.

## Stat block

**Non-combatant.** AC 11 | HP 12. He will not fight. If threatened, he will step between the threat and the stone box and stay there. He is not protecting the shard — he is protecting what the shard represents: that grief deserves a proper container, and this chapel has been that container for twelve years.

## What he knows (public)

- He found a stone with inscriptions in the foundation eight years ago, during a repair.
- It made him feel things he thought he had finished feeling.
- He put it in a stone box because it was not safe to leave out.
- He will show it to visitors who ask respectfully. He will not give it away.
- He has been tending this chapel since leaving the Qualimosti forest after a disagreement with the Speaker's council about who decides when a place is worth keeping.

## What he knows (private)

- The person he relived losing when he held the shard: his partner Ven, who died of a fever twenty-two years ago. He held the shard for three days and relived the night Ven died, cycling through it, until he forced himself to put the shard down. He has not said Ven's name aloud in eight years, not because he is ashamed but because the name has weight and he only spends it carefully.
- He knows the gods are gone. He does not know if they will return. He does not tend the chapel because he expects them to return. He tends it because *someone should*.
- He has been watching the Unbound Conclave's agents watch the chapel for six days. They came, examined the chapel from the road, and left without entering. He does not know why they did not come in.

## Emotional position

**What he wants:** For the shard to go with people who understand what grief requires. Not people who want to use it — people who have been paying attention to their own griefs the way he has paid attention to the chapel's.

**What he can't say:** He cannot ask the party to prove they understand grief. He does not have the right to demand that of strangers. He can only watch.

**The condition:** He gives the shard when someone names a grief they have been carrying — without being asked, without being prompted. The naming has to be voluntary. He cannot explain this condition because explaining it would corrupt it. He simply waits to see if anyone does it.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Morreth's response |
|---|---|
| Asks to see the shard | Shows them the stone box. Does not open it immediately. Tells them what happened to him when he found it. |
| Explains the compact and what the shards are for | Listens carefully. Does not immediately respond. Then: "The Black Robe who swore that clause. She was right to." Considering. |
| Asks to take the shard | "I'll think about it." He is not stalling; he is genuinely thinking. He will watch them for the rest of the visit. |
| Lenne picks up the shard and relives Volenn's death | He watches without intervening. When she recovers: "Did you know that would happen?" Neutral, not unkind. |
| Any PC names a grief without being asked | He goes to the stone box. Opens it. Gives the shard to whoever named the grief. Says nothing beyond: "Yes. Take it." |
| Agents arrive and the party defends the chapel | Afterward, he is shaken. He has been alone here for twelve years. Seeing the party protect it moves him more than he shows. This opens Route A if Route D hasn't fired. |

## Arc-Completion

**The moment:** When Sera (or any PC) names a grief without being asked — the specific grief, not a general statement of sadness — Morreth goes to the stone box without speaking. He opens the toggle latch. He removes the shard, still in its cloth wrapping. He holds it out to whoever named the grief. He says: *"Yes. Take it."* And then, after they take it: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."* He straightens the offering bowl on the altar. He picks up his broom. The conversation is over because it is complete.

**What it produces:** Clause of Grief in party's hands. Morreth has spoken Ven's name. Both the party and the elder have named something they were carrying. The arc-completion is bilateral — he gives the shard; he receives the witness of having named Ven.

**What happens if condition never met:** Route A (charter) or Route C (agents arrive; chaos). The chapel is survivable without Route D. But Morreth keeps the shard in the box and Ven stays unspoken.

## GM Notes

- **Do not prompt the arc-completion.** Morreth does not say "tell me about your grief." He does not ask. He waits. If the party reads him correctly, they understand what he is waiting for. If they don't, they try Route A. Either is valid.
- **Lenne's experience with the shard** is the setup for Sera's arc-completion. After the party watches Lenne drop the shard and recover (Symptom 2), they understand what they are asking Sera to hold. Let this land before moving to the arc-completion scene.
- **DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-04.md`. Morreth speaking Ven's name after Sera speaks is the second half of the arc-completion. Do not rush it.
