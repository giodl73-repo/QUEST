# Wandering Pressure — The Grief Inscription

## Chapel pressure (no clock; atmosphere only)

The chapel has no hard time-pressure once the party is inside — Morreth is patient and the shard is not going anywhere. Pressure comes from the possibility that Mira (if not resolved on the road) returns with better preparation.

**Route C timer:** If Mira fled the road encounter: she regroups and sends the Quiet One ahead to observe (Route C marker: Sera's Perception DC 14 from the ruins high point sees a grey-robed figure on the road 30 minutes after the party enters the chapel). If the party is still in the chapel 90 minutes after arriving: Mira attempts the ruins path (Sera's passive 16 detects her; 2 rounds warning).

## Chapel Cold Pulse (d6, every 30 minutes)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | Light shifts through the gap in the east wall. The carved figure casts a long shadow across the sanctuary floor. Morreth does not comment. |
| 2 | The offering bowl on the far left of the altar cracks — a hairline crack, audible in the chapel's silence. Morreth: *"Third one this year."* He replaces it from a box under the altar bench. He has seven spares. |
| 3 | Pine resin smell. There is a pine tree at the chapel's north corner. Orik's hands go still for a moment. |
| 4 | Calder finds himself reading the inscription again. He has read it three times. He does not say what he keeps thinking. |
| 5 | Lenne catches herself about to reach for the stone box again. She stops herself. She is irritated with herself for wanting to. She wants to examine it properly, which is different from wanting to hold it. |
| 6 | Sera, in the ruins, hears the road. Nothing on it. She counts anyway: one minute of empty road. One. Two. Three. This is a thing she does. |
