# Encounter — Road Encounter (Mira Coldsteel + Osvin + Quiet One)

**Trigger:** Always fires on the approach road. Mira opens with a negotiation offer.

**Context:** Three agents. Mira has been planning this since the Kharolis foothills. She has a clear operational goal: recover all the shards. She is intelligent enough to know that frontal assault against a 5-person party with two wizards is suboptimal. She tries the talk first.

## Social resolution (no combat)

**Mira's opening:** *"I'd like to try something different this time. Can we talk?"* She gestures for Osvin and the Quiet One to stay back. She is genuine — she wants information, not a fight.

**What she proposes:** She will stop pursuing the party for this session if they share the reconstruction document. She wants to understand what the compact said. She is not proposing to stop the Unbound Conclave's mission — just to pause.

**Route D reveal:** If any PC explains that the party's intent is a new compact spoken by a mortal, witnessed by all three Orders including former Unbound, Mira goes quiet for 3-4 seconds. Then: *"That would actually fix it."* She tells Osvin and Quiet One: *"We're done here. Both of you, stand down."* Arc-completion fires. No combat. Proceed to chapel.

**Social skill threshold:** Persuasion DC 13 (the party seems to understand the problem) or a clear description of Route D (no roll needed if the explanation is specific).

**Combat trigger — explicit:** If Persuasion DC 13 fails, OR if the party is evasive/hostile, Mira says: *"Then we do this the hard way."* She immediately casts Web on the party cluster (no further negotiation). Initiative rolls begin. There is no ambiguous middle state — either she stands the agents down or she casts the first spell. The DM should not leave the encounter floating between social and combat resolution.

## Combat (if social fails)

**Encounter balance:**
- Mira Coldsteel (see `npcs/mira-coldsteel.md`): AC 15 (Mage Armor), HP 45, Fireball/Counterspell/Web
- Osvin: AC 11, HP 32, Multiattack 2×mace (+4), Dagger of Warning (advantage initiative)
- Quiet One: AC 11, HP 32, Multiattack 2×mace (+4), Bola (Dex DC 13 or restrained)

**XP:** Mira CR 4 (1100 XP) + 2× Thug CR 1/2 (200 XP) = 1300 base. ×2 multiplier (3 enemies) = 2600 adjusted. Per PC (5): 520 XP. **Hard encounter.** First genuinely dangerous combat of campaign 2.

**Encounter-balancer check:**
- Fireball (8d6, DC 14 Dex) = avg 28 damage to all in 20-ft sphere. Party average HP ~40. One hit = majority of Sera's HP. **Red flag: Sera instakill risk.** Mitigated by: Lenne's Projected Ward (can absorb ~22 HP of Fireball damage for one ally); Calder's Bless; party spread.
- Action economy: Mira 1 action + Counterspell reaction; Osvin 2 attacks; Quiet One 2 attacks = 5 meaningful actions/round vs. party 5 actions. Even action economy. Fair.
- Tactical choices: (1) Priority target = Mira (disabling her removes Fireball threat); (2) Terrain = trees on road shoulder (half-cover DC 10 Stealth to use); (3) Lenne vs. Mira Counterspell duel; (4) Non-combat path was offered — combat is the failure state.

**Tactics:**
- **Round 1:** Mira casts Web (concentration) at the party cluster. Osvin and Quiet One advance. If Web hits 2+ PCs: advantage for Osvin/Quiet One on restrained targets. If Web is Counterspelled (by Lenne or Thessaly): Mira casts Fireball instead on round 2.
- **Round 2:** If she has concentration on Web: she advances and holds Fireball for a turn where 3+ PCs are adjacent. If no Web: Fireball on the cluster.
- **Retreat trigger:** Mira at 20 HP or fewer OR Counterspell depleted. She disengages, casts Misty Step (bonus action), says: *"We'll finish this later."* Osvin and Quiet One follow. They do not pursue.

**Post-combat:**
If Mira fled: she is a recurring threat in 0011 unless her arc-completion fires elsewhere (possible in a side scene before they leave).
If Mira was captured: she will talk. Her intel: 4 shards unaccounted; she does not know the Qualimosti elder has one; she has a list of three other locations. This accelerates the party's research.

## Lenne vs. Mira (Counterspell duel)

Both are Red Robe trained (Mira from the former Orders; Lenne from Volenn). They will recognize each other.

**First exchange:** Mira casts Web (L2). If Lenne Counterspells: automatic success (Web is L2, Counterspell auto-succeeds against L2 spells). Mira notes this and says, after the spell fizzles: *"Abjuration? Volenn's style."* She is not asking — she recognized the school from how the Counterspell felt.

**Lenne's response** (if she addresses this): *"You knew him?"* Mira: *"By reputation. He was good. Left before the Orders could use him badly."* There is no hostility in this — it is an acknowledgment.

This exchange can happen mid-combat, in the space between spells. It is not an opportunity to stop combat; it is a human moment in a dangerous situation.
