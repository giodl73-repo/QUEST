## Summary

Three days southwest of Volenn's tower, a ruined chapel on the Qualimosti borderlands. An elder named Morreth has been maintaining it alone for twelve years. He found the Clause of Grief in the foundation stones eight years ago, held it for three days, and put it in a stone box. He has not held it since.

The Unbound Conclave has the same information the party does, plus a mage-agent: Mira Coldsteel. She tries talking first this time. Route D available from the road: explain the new compact plan; she stands her agents down permanently.

Inside the chapel: Lenne will pick up the shard. She will hold it for more than a minute. She will relive Volenn's death. The party will watch. They now know what they are asking Sera to hold. Morreth has been waiting for someone to name a grief without being asked. Sera is the one.

## Setting & Background

**The chapel** — Pre-Cataclysm stone, partially collapsed east section. Morreth has maintained the west section: new lintel, swept flagstones, fresh offering bowls. The gods this chapel served departed in the Cataclysm three hundred years ago — the punishment the gods sent when the Kingpriest of Istar demanded their power. Morreth tends the chapel not because the gods might return but because *someone should*.

**Morreth** — Kagonesti elder, 68. Left the Qualimosti forest twelve years ago after a disagreement about what places are worth keeping. Has been here since. Lost his partner Ven to a fever twenty-two years ago. Has not said Ven's name aloud in eight years.

**The Clause of Grief** — Inscribed: *"Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks."* Anyone who holds it for more than 60 seconds relives their worst loss in present tense. Morreth held it for three days. Route D exception: if the holder has already named their grief aloud to another person without being prompted, the shard shows the lost thing as it is *now*, not as it was at the moment of loss.

**The Unbound Conclave** — Now three agents: Osvin and the Quiet One (recurring) and Mira Coldsteel (new, Red Robe mage). Mira is operational and not unreasonable. She offers to talk.

### Scene 03 — Chapel Interior

**Read-aloud:**

> Pre-Cataclysm stone, dim and clean. The collapsed east section has been closed with fieldstone. On the altar: offering bowls, an empty icon frame, and at the center, a stone box latched with a wooden toggle bar.

**Features:** Stone box on altar (wooden toggle latch, not locked; cold to the touch — Symptom 1). Morreth's fieldstone wall. Empty icon frame (old Kagonesti woodwork, very old, carried here).

**Lenne and the shard (Symptom 2 — mandatory):** She opens the box and picks up the shard. Nobody stops her — nobody knows yet. At 60 seconds: she relives finding out Volenn was gone — the specific quality of silence in the tower before anyone spoke. She drops the shard. She sits on the floor. When she recovers (1 round): *"Don't hold it for more than a minute."* She puts it back.

Morreth, from the doorway: *"Three days. I didn't know what was happening. I couldn't put it down. He died twenty-two years ago. I still knew his face when I finally let go."* He does not say the name yet.

Calder reads the inscription quietly (Symptom 3): *"Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks."*

**GM Notes:** Do not prevent Lenne from picking up the shard. The demonstration is necessary. The party must understand what they are asking Sera to hold before Sera can choose to hold it.

### Scene 04 — The Sanctuary

**Read-aloud:**

> A smaller room behind a short corridor. Morreth uses it as a workshop — woodworking tools, wood shavings, a half-carved lintel piece. A chair faces east toward the ruins. A gap in the east wall — a window-sized breach — has a small carved figure sitting in it, weathered, facing outward. Toward whoever might be in the ruins. Toward the approach.

**Features:** Morreth's workshop (half-carved lintel: Kagonesti pattern for "this place is remembered"). Chair that faces east. The carved figure in the gap. The stone box (he sometimes brings it here when working late).

**Arc-completion — available here:** When Sera (or any PC) names a grief without being asked — specific, first person, the actual loss — Morreth goes to the stone box. He gives the shard. He says: *"Yes. Take it."* Then: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."*

He straightens the carving on the bench. The conversation is complete.

**When Sera holds the shard (Route D):** She waits for the worst memory. Instead: warmth, briefly. A quality of light in the obsidian surface — something alive. The settlement, safe. Continued. Fine without her.

**P0 fallback:** Route A — Morreth trades for a promise: the chapel named in the new compact's witness-list. This is a real promise. Make it.

**DM-letter:** See `dm-letter-scene-04.md`. Read before the session. Two namings. Hold space for both.

## Treasures

### The Clause of Grief

*Third shard. Obsidian, slightly smaller than the Ambition. Always cold. Inscribed: "Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks." Archmage Davan of the Black Robes (PC 195–219) — small closed-hand sigil.*

**Active (1 minute):** Hold it for 60 seconds → Wisdom save DC 14 or relive worst personal loss in present tense, full sensory, 10 subjective seconds. Stunned 1 round after. Disadvantage on Wisdom saves for 1 hour.

**Route D exception:** If holder named their grief aloud to another person within 24 hours (unprompted): the shard shows the lost thing as it is *now*, not at the moment of loss.

**Passive:** Always cold. Arcana DC 12 to identify as compact shard. Reading the inscription: Arcana-proficient creatures understand it as a Black Robe demand for accountability.

**Curse — The weight of witness:** Attuned creature relives a stranger's grief once/week (DC 12 Wis save to resist). Accumulates an archive of others' losses across former holders. This is the clause's function: the holder cannot forget that grief is not abstract.

**Provenance:**
1. Archmage Davan of the Black Robes (PC 195–219) — swore the clause; died one year before the Cataclysm
2. Tower of High Sorcery, Istar (destroyed PC 220)
3. Multiple intermediate holders (~200 years; none kept it long)
4. Unnamed pilgrim (20 years ago) — offered it to the chapel as a gift
5. Morreth (12 years ago – present) — found in foundation stones; held 3 days; stone box

## Encounters Appendix

### Road Encounter — Mira Coldsteel + Osvin + Quiet One (Hard)

**Social resolution:** Persuasion DC 13 or Route D description → Mira stands agents down permanently.

**Mira Coldsteel:** AC 15 | HP 45 | Fireball +6 (8d6, DC 14 Dex save, 20-ft sphere) | Counterspell (reaction, auto-succeeds vs. L2 or lower, Arcana DC 13 vs. higher) | Web +6 (L2, DC 14 Str, concentration) | Misty Step (bonus action teleport 30 ft.) | Shield (reaction, +5 AC until next turn)

**Osvin:** AC 11 | HP 32 | 2× Mace +4 (1d6+2) | Pack Tactics | Dagger of Warning (advantage initiative for self + allies within 30 ft.)

**Quiet One:** AC 11 | HP 32 | 2× Mace +4 (1d6+2) | Pack Tactics | Bola (ranged, Dex DC 13 or restrained 1 round)

**Adjusted XP:** ~2600 (Hard for 5 PCs). Per PC: 520 XP.

**Fireball threat to Sera:** Average 28 damage (DC 14 Dex). Sera HP 44; one hit = majority of HP. Lenne's Projected Ward: uses her reaction to absorb up to 22 HP of that damage for one ally.

**Key tactical note — Lenne vs. Mira Counterspell duel:** Mira opens with Web (L2). Lenne Counterspells automatically (Web is L2). Mira says: *"Abjuration? Volenn's style."* Lenne: *"You knew him?"* Mira: *"By reputation. He left before the Orders could use him badly."* This exchange happens mid-combat, between spells.

**Retreat trigger:** Mira at 20 HP or fewer, or both Counterspells spent → Misty Step + disengage. *"We'll finish this later."* Osvin and Quiet One follow.

**Capture yield (Mira):** Intel on 4 unlocated shards; 3 known locations; confirms Unbound Conclave does not know Morreth has one.
