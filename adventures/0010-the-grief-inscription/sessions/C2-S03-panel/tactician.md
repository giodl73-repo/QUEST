## Critique

### Overall assessment

Second consecutive zero-combat session. The Tactician is tracking this with increasing concern. Two Wisdom saves were logged — Lenne's honest DC 14 fail ([3]+1=4) and Sera's Route D gift — but neither was a combat roll. The Tactician notes that Lenne's failed save is the session's only moment where mechanics told the party something they didn't want to hear (she relived Volenn's death; she was shaken 1 hour). This is a real mechanical consequence. The session earns the Mechanical Fairness 9 specifically because that save was not soft-pedaled.

The Mira Coldsteel arc-completion in Scene 01 — Route D explanation triggering permanent stand-down — is a campaign-level design decision that has significant Tactician implications: the campaign's primary antagonist faction is now neutralized at Session 3 of 7. This closes the combat-threat door ahead of schedule.

### What worked

- **Lenne's Wis save DC 14 → [3]+1=4 FAIL.** This is textbook honest resolution. The module set a DC, the runner rolled honestly, the result was a fail, and the consequence was real (relived the death; shaken 1 hour; Wis disadvantage active for the scene). The Tactician specifically notes: the Disadvantage condition on subsequent saves was logged. This is mechanical follow-through. The artifact's curse works as designed and was not softened.

- **Mira's stand-down: no combat.** From a purely Tactician standpoint, this is the correct resolution for a route-D party against a frightened-of-violence antagonist faction. Mira's opening line (*"Can we talk?"*) was a genuine social encounter, not a DM shortcut. The party chose to explain Route D specifically; Mira was designed to receive this. No fudge. Clean.

### What needs work

- **The Shard-Grief encounter (Lenne's test, Sera's gift) should have had a combat backstop.** Morreth warned the party not to hold the shard for more than a minute. Lenne held it. She failed. This is good. But if the entire party had held the shard simultaneously and all failed, what was the combat consequence? The module apparently doesn't have one. The Tactician wants a session where the artifact's curse has teeth beyond "shaken 1 hour."

- **Three sessions in; two near-zero-dice sessions.** The first combat was a 1.5-round Medium encounter. The Tactician is done being patient. S04 needs a Hard encounter or the campaign's mechanical layer will atrophy.

### The question I'd push on

Mira Coldsteel stood her agents down. She will witness the compact. This is a campaign-permanent fact. The Tactician's question: is there now *any* faction that will oppose the party physically in the remaining four sessions? If the Unbound Conclave is neutralized, what replaces it as the campaign's tactical pressure?
