# S03 LOG — The Grief Inscription

## Session Summary

Mira Coldsteel met them on the road. She opened with a negotiation offer; Thessaly explained Route D cleanly — new compact, mortal-spoken, all three Orders as witnesses including former Unbound. Mira said: *"That would actually fix it."* She stood her agents down permanently and asked to be a witness at the compact. No combat. The Unbound Conclave stops being a direct threat.

At the chapel, Morreth led them inside immediately when Thessaly produced the reconstruction document. He had been waiting for people who understood what the chapel held. In the interior, Lenne opened the stone box and held the shard. One minute. Wis save 3 vs DC 14 — fail. She relived the specific quality of the silence in Volenn's courtyard when she arrived and knew, before anyone spoke, that he was gone. She put the shard back. She sat on the floor. The party watched. They understood what they were asking Sera to hold.

In the sanctuary, after a long silence, Sera named the settlement. The note specifically — *"Didn't know when you'd return. Couldn't wait. Thank you for the previous seasons."* The headman's words. The years of tracking people she had been supposed to protect, checking if they were okay, never checking on this settlement because she didn't want to know they were fine without her. Morreth picked up the stone box. He gave Sera the shard. When she took it: warmth instead of the expected loss. The settlement, intact. The headman writing in his accounts. The note, weathered, barely readable. Everything she feared: fine.

Then Morreth said: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."*

He returned to work.

Three shards. Eighty-three days to convergence. The road leads northeast.

### Scene 04 — The Sanctuary

A long silence. Sera came in from the ruins perimeter. She read the room — Lenne on the floor; the stone box on the bench; Morreth in the chair. She sat near the door. Another silence.

Then Sera named the settlement. The note specifically. The headman's words. The years of checking roads and holds and markets, tracking people she was supposed to protect. The one she never checked because she didn't want to know they were fine without her.

Morreth stood up from the chair. He carried the stone box to Sera. He opened it. He held out the shard. *"Yes. Take it."*

Sera took the shard. She waited for the worst memory. Instead: warmth, briefly. The settlement, intact. The headman writing. The note on the tree, weathered and barely readable. They were fine. They continued. Fine without her.

**Symptom 4 (Route D gift) delivered.** The shard delivered the gift rather than the reliving because Sera had named her grief without being asked.

Then Morreth: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."* He returned to the carving. The conversation was complete.

Orik checked the doorframe. Inside curve: true. He said nothing.
