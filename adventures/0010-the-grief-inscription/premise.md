# Premise — The Grief Inscription

## Hook

From Tessamine's reconstruction document: *"Clause of Grief — last located: ruined chapel to the departed gods, Qualimosti borderlands. Recovered from the rubble by unknown hands, date unknown."* Lenne's research adds: *"A Kagonesti elder has been tending a ruined chapel fourteen miles west of the Qualimosti tree-line for at least a decade. Pilgrims who pass mention 'a stone box on the altar he doesn't let anyone touch.'"*

The party travels three days southwest from Volenn's tower. The Unbound Conclave has also decoded Tessamine's records. They are on the same road, half a day ahead, and now have a mage-agent with them.

## Setting

A ruined chapel to the departed gods of Krynn — the gods who vanished during the Cataclysm — in the Qualimosti borderlands. The chapel is pre-Cataclysm stone, partially collapsed, partially maintained. The Kagonesti elder Morreth has been tending it for twelve years. He carved new lintels when the old ones fell. He sweeps the floor. He replaces the offering bowls when they crack. The gods he tends no longer answer, but the chapel is clean.

## Artifact

**The Clause of Grief** — a compact shard, the same obsidian origin as the others. Inscribed: *"Let no Order forget the cost of what it asks."* Anyone who holds it for more than one minute relives their worst loss as if it just happened — not a vision, a re-experience: present tense, full weight. Morreth held it for three days when he first found it, buried in the chapel's foundation stones. He put it in a stone box. He does not hold it anymore.

**Route D exception:** If the person holding the shard has already named their grief aloud — to another person, without being asked — the shard delivers something different: a glimpse of what was lost, as it is *now*, not as it was at the moment of loss. The settlement, safe and moved on. Volenn's tower, still standing. The hall, rebuilt. The grief is not erased; the shard shows that the thing loved did not end with the loss.

See `treasures/clause-of-grief.md`.

## Themes

**Grief** — the emotion of this adventure. The elder grieves the gods. The party carries their separate griefs. The shard is a mirror that shows grief back to whoever holds it. The question of the adventure: can Sera name what she lost without having it taken from her again?

**Sera's spotlight** — her grief is the settlement that moved on without her. A note nailed to a tree. She has never named this aloud. She has come close twice (S01: she gave Thessaly space; S02: she gave Orik space; she did not take any for herself). Morreth's arc-completion condition is the structural invitation she has been waiting for.

**Lenne and the shard** — she will pick it up immediately (she reads objects before people). She will hold it for more than a minute without anyone stopping her, because no one yet knows what the shard does. She will relive Volenn's death. This happens in Scene 03. It establishes the stakes before Sera's arc-completion.

## Three-route outcomes (A/C/D)

**Route A (Charter/Fallback):** The party explains the compact and what the shards are for. Morreth is moved by the idea of a covenant that required the Orders to remember grief — that is exactly what he has been tending alone. He trades the shard for a specific promise: that the chapel will be named in the new compact's witness-list, if a new compact is spoken. This is a real promise. The party can make it.

**Route C (Default):** The Unbound Conclave's mage-agent arrives at the chapel during Scene 03 or 04. Three-way confrontation. Lenne counterspells; Orik engages; the shard changes hands in the chaos. Party recovers it but Morreth's arc-completion doesn't fire — he doesn't see what he needed to see.

**Route D (Inversion/Optimal):** Sera names the settlement. Not the grief — the specific thing: the settlement, the headman's note, the years of tracking people she had promised to protect and then losing track of them when they moved on. Morreth receives it without comment. He goes to the stone box. He opens it. He gives Sera the shard. When she takes it, she does not relive her worst loss — she sees the settlement as it is now: moved on, intact, fine without her. The shard gives her the one thing she never let herself imagine: they were okay.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5 (party of 5 with Lenne). Two encounters expected: road confrontation (Unbound Conclave, now 3 agents including a mage) and optional chapel guardian (two ex-soldiers, non-hostile if respectful). Lenne's Counterspell and Projected Ward get their first real workout against the Conclave mage.

## Constraints

- Room count: 5 keyed locations (road/approach, chapel exterior, chapel interior, the sanctuary with the stone box, the ruins behind — optional)
- The shard's curse (relive worst loss) must be demonstrated on Lenne before the arc-completion. The party needs to understand what they are asking Sera to hold.
- Route D is the only route where Sera names the settlement. It cannot be prompted; it must be chosen.
- The Unbound Conclave now has three agents: Osvin (recovered), Quiet One (recovered), and Mira Coldsteel (Red Robe mage-agent, new). Mira makes this a Hard encounter for the 5-PC party.
