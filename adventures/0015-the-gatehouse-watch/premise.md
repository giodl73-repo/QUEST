# Premise — The Gatehouse Watch

**Logline:** The party enters a half-built cathedral expecting an empty problem and finds a congregation — gully dwarves, confused and scared, who have come here because a healer told them this place would matter someday.

## Setting

**Place:** The outer gatehouse of the Halted Spire — an unfinished Mishakal cathedral on the hill above **Mireth-on-the-Hill**, Abanasinia. Post-War of the Lance era. The main cathedral body looms behind the gatehouse: scaffolding, rough-cut stone, open sky where the roof should be.

**The gatehouse itself:** Two stories. The lower level includes a portcullis passage, a guardroom, and a waiting chapel (intended for pilgrims; never completed). The upper level is a wall-walk overlooking the approach road. A collapsed section of the outer wall provides a second entry the congregation uses. The gate hinges have been oiled recently — not by the construction crew.

**Atmosphere:** Smells of fresh earth and tallow. Small shrines assembled from construction offcuts in the corners of the guardroom — a candle stub, three smooth stones, a piece of cloth knotted around a nail. Still being assembled. The newest arrivals are here; they haven't learned the congregation's full rites yet. They are doing what they can with what they know.

## Hook

**Magistra Doran's commission** — the party has been hired to assess and clear the cathedral site so construction can resume. They have been told: workers fled, the site is dangerous, something is wrong. They have not been told what.

They enter through the main gate. The gate is unlocked. It should not be unlocked — the construction crew secured it when they left. The hinges move without sound.

Inside the gatehouse, they find gully dwarves.

## The Congregation in the Gatehouse

The gully dwarves here are the most recently arrived — three weeks to two months, compared to some who have been in the cathedral for years. There are **seven of them** in the gatehouse section. They are not organized into the congregation's full rites. They arrived because word reached their community (a settlement in the hills northwest of Mireth) that the place Velantha died was nearby. They came. They are still figuring out what to do now that they are here.

**They are not attacking the party.** They will defend themselves if threatened. Two of them — **Grisk** and **Torva** — are the most fearful and the most likely to escalate. The others will follow the lead of **Mig**, the one among them who came with a purpose: she carries a cracked cup she believes Velantha used, and she intends to leave it at the Nave shrine. She does not know where the Nave shrine is yet.

Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by Krynnish society to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory. The congregation in this gatehouse has maintained a daily practice for thirty years.

**The field satchel is here.** Mig is carrying it in addition to the cup. She found it outside the gate three days ago — it was placed there by someone from the inner congregation who wanted the newcomers to have it. The herbs inside are impossibly fragrant. She does not know why. She will not part with it easily, but she is not irrational.

## Central Artifact (seed)

**Velantha's field satchel** — the bag she carried into the gully dwarf camp the night of the ritual. One interior compartment holds dried herbs that should have become dust thirty years ago. They are still fragrant. Faint warmth in the leather. The congregation has kept it as a relic.

Full artifact file: `treasures/field-satchel.md`

## Themes

- First contact — who gets the benefit of the doubt, and at what threshold
- The gap between what a commission says and what a situation is
- Something kept in faith for a long time, without knowing if it was worth it
- Dren's specific crack: a healer came here for gully dwarves

## Constraints

- **6 rooms**, one session (3-4 hours)
- **Tier 1; party of 4 at level 3**
- Non-linear map: the gatehouse has multiple levels, the collapsed wall provides a second entry, the door to the Nave is gated by the portcullis mechanism
- At least one purely atmospheric room (the waiting chapel — never completed, never used, but the congregation has begun to use it)
- Temporal pressure table: the cathedral is structurally unstable — falling stone and the sounds of the inner congregation create pressure without requiring wandering monsters at every turn
- Combat encounters: at minimum, one encounter with defensive gully dwarves and one encounter with something that entered the incomplete structure before the congregation arrived (see Encounters below)
- Curse symptoms from `field-satchel-manifest.md` distributed across ≥2 rooms (gatehouse section; full 3-room distribution across the whole cathedral begins in 0016)
- Read-aloud ≤ 60 words per room
- Every named NPC has an `npcs/<slug>.md` file

## Encounters

**Primary encounter — Grisk and Torva (defensive gully dwarves):**
CR equivalent: 2 gully dwarves with improvised weapons and terrain advantage (the wall-walk gives higher ground). They will back down if the party stops advancing. They will not back down if the party keeps advancing. This encounter is social before it is combat.

**Secondary encounter — the squatters:**
Before the congregation arrived, a pack of **giant rats** nested in the guardroom's lower storage (a crawl space under the guardroom floor, accessed through a grate). The congregation has been coexisting with them uneasily. The rats attack the party as a swarm when the grate is disturbed. CR 1/4 × 8; treat as swarm of rats (CR 1/4). The congregation did not plant this problem; it was here before them.

**Wandering pressure:** Every 30 minutes (3× per session): d6 — 1-2 a stone falls somewhere in the upper level (Dex save DC 10, 1d6 damage; telegraphed by a groan in the structure first); 3-4 sound from deeper in the cathedral (the inner congregation's rites, distant); 5-6 Mig approaches the party, cup in hand, to ask if they know the way to the Nave.

## The Real Decision

The party's first choice in this campaign: **how do they treat Mig?**

1. **Negotiate in good faith.** Mig will explain (in broken Common and mime) where she is going and why. She will give them the satchel if they help her reach the Nave shrine safely. Route D opens.
2. **Take the satchel by force.** Mig fights. All seven gully dwarves escalate. The satchel is recovered; the congregation in the inner cathedral will know by morning. Route D begins closing.
3. **Report to Magistra Doran without engaging.** The party retreats with information. Doran wants them gone. This delays the campaign but does not close any routes.
4. **Help Mig find the Nave entrance.** The party guides her to the door leading deeper into the cathedral. They have now made first contact with the inner congregation under Mig's introduction. This is the smoothest Route D entry and unlocks a faction connection for 0016.

**Fighter spotlight (Dren Holt):** Dren's crack activates here. When he understands what Mig is doing — that she came because a healer came for her people — the DM should note the pause before "healer" and play it. He does not say anything yet. He notes it. The notation is the moment.

## Hint Delivery

**Hint 1 delivered this session:** The congregation's existence is undeniable by end of session. The party knows gully dwarves are living in the cathedral and have been there longer than three weeks. Whether they understand *why* is based on how they engaged with Mig. If they engaged: they know Velantha healed gully dwarves. If they did not: they know gully dwarves are here for a reason they have not yet learned.

Recovery path: 0016 (nave congregation's oral tradition will deliver this without requiring the party to have gotten it from Mig).
