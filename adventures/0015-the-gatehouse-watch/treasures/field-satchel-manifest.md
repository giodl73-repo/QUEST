# Relic Signs Manifest — Velantha's Field Satchel

The dungeon housing this relic MUST express the presence of thirty years of congregational faith in at least **3 of the following ways**, distributed across at least **2 rooms**. `dungeon-smith` reads this file and honors it.

These signs communicate the following truth to observant players before they understand it consciously: *something has been tended here, carefully, for a long time.*

## Required (≥3, distributed across ≥2 rooms)

- [ ] **Olfactory sign:** The gatehouse smells of dried herbs that should not be present — not strongly, just at the edge of noticing. There are no visible herbs in the room where the smell is strongest. The smell comes from the satchel; it has permeated the space nearest where Mig rests.
- [ ] **Maintenance sign:** Something in the gatehouse has been maintained that no construction crew left maintained. The gate hinges have been oiled. A cracked floor stone has been swept clean of debris daily. The tallow candles in the congregation's shrines are fresh — not the construction crew's. Someone has been tending this space.
- [ ] **Devotional arrangement:** At least one room contains a clearly deliberate arrangement of objects — not random debris, but a small shrine assembled from construction offcuts. Three stones, a candle stub, a piece of cloth knotted around a nail. The arrangement is not a human religious form. It is something worked out by people without a formal tradition, doing what seemed right.
- [ ] **Warmth anomaly:** A room that should be cold (stone, open to drafts, unheated for months) is not as cold as it should be. A character who makes a DC 12 Perception check notices that the warmth is localized — it comes from where the satchel is stored or carried. A character who touches the satchel for the first time notices immediately.
- [ ] **Kept record:** A small, flat stone in one of the congregation's shrine areas has marks scratched into it — tally marks, not letters. Thirty rows of marks. One for each year. The marks were scratched with something careful: a nail or a sharp piece of construction offcut. A character who counts them will count thirty.

## Required (always)

- [ ] **The satchel is present and discoverable before the encounter resolves.** Players should have the opportunity to observe and interact with the satchel during the session regardless of how they approach Mig. If combat occurs, the satchel is set down at the start; it is not a combat-loot item. If negotiations occur, Mig shows it voluntarily.

- [ ] **One room contains evidence that the congregation arrived here, not that they were always here.** Fresh earth at the threshold, new scuff marks in old dust, a path worn through debris that is weeks old rather than years. The gatehouse group arrived recently; their presence should read as new arrival, not long establishment. This distinguishes them from the inner congregation (which has been here for years and whose marks will be deeper in the cathedral).

## Suggested (optional)

- A child-sized handprint in fresh tallow on one of the shrine stones — the congregation includes young gully dwarves; one of them put their hand in the candle wax by accident. This is not sinister. It is domestic.
- One of the congregation members (not Mig) is performing a repetitive motion — smoothing a stone, sorting pebbles by size — that is clearly a form of prayer they have worked out for themselves. Not a formal rite. A personal practice.
- The satchel rests on a piece of cloth that has been carefully folded. The cloth is worn but clean. It was cleaned recently. Someone washes it. This is detail that rewards players who look.

## Reaction moment (when the satchel is first picked up by a non-congregation member)

When the party first handles the satchel, they feel the warmth. This is the relic's reaction moment. The DM describes: *"It is warm. Not from being near a fire — there is no fire. Warm like something recently held."*

Do not explain the warmth here. Do not attribute it. Let it land and move on. Players who ask will ask the congregation. That question is the door to Hint 1.
