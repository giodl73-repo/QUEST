# Mig, Keeper of the Satchel

## Appearance
Short even for a gully dwarf, which places her at approximately 3.5 feet. Her hands are rough-skinned from work she does not name. She carries the field satchel over one shoulder and a cracked clay cup in the other hand. The cup is old — not Velantha's cup (that is in R2 of the Nave; Mig does not have it yet). This cup is Mig's. She carries it because she intends to leave it at the Nave shrine when she arrives. She has carried it for three weeks. She has not been able to get through the inner door yet.

## Stat Block

Non-combatant. HP 6, AC 10. Speed 20 ft. She does not fight unless directly and physically attacked; if attacked, she sets the satchel down first, then defends herself with the cup as an improvised weapon (1 damage). She will not let go of the satchel under any circumstances short of unconsciousness.

## Public Knowledge (what she will share)

- She is here because Velantha healed her people thirty years ago. She was not alive then. Her grandmother was one of the healed. She grew up with the story.
- The satchel was Velantha's. She found it outside the gate three days ago — placed there by someone from the inner congregation, who knew the gatehouse group was arriving.
- The herbs in the satchel should not still be fragrant. They have been fragrant for thirty years. She does not know why. She has not investigated why. It is enough that they are.
- She wants to reach the Nave shrine and leave her cup there. That is the full extent of her plan.
- There is someone inside who knows everything. She gestures toward R5 and the direction of the Nave. She does not know his name — she has only heard of him from others in the community.

## Private Knowledge (what she holds)

- She suspects the satchel is drawn toward the Nave shrine. She felt it when she first picked it up — a pull, like looking at something you have been told to bring somewhere. She has not said this to anyone.
- She has more faith in the congregation's practice than she has ever articulated. She would not describe it as faith. She would describe it as showing up. The distinction matters to her, though she does not have the vocabulary for why.

## Information Readiness Condition

Mig holds the information about the elder (see seed: congregation-has-elder) and will transfer it when: the party demonstrates they want to understand the congregation's history, not just solve a problem. The specific act: asking her why she is here (not what she is doing or where she is going, but *why*). On receiving this question, she answers at length — not in Common necessarily, but in enough Common + gesture + demonstration that the party understands: *someone came for us. We come back.*

The elder information follows naturally from this: *the one inside knows more than I do. He was there.*

## Behavior Matrix

| Party action | Mig's response |
|---|---|
| Party enters and stops | She watches. Tilts head. Waits. |
| Party asks what she is holding | She shows them the satchel. Holds it up briefly. |
| Party asks what she is doing here | She points to the satchel, points toward R5. Then: "Shrine." |
| Party asks why | She sets the satchel down. Looks at it. Looks at the party. Says: "She came for us." Picks the satchel back up. |
| Party reaches for the satchel without asking | She steps back. Grisk reacts. |
| Party offers to help her reach R6 | She looks at them for a moment. Then she starts moving toward R5. She has been waiting three weeks for this. |
| Party refuses to help but does not drive her out | She stays. She waits. She is patient. She has been patient for three weeks. |
| Party drives her out by force | She goes through the collapsed wall exit in R3. She will return. |

## Arc-Completion

**Condition:** The inner door (R6) is opened and Mig steps through with the party's assistance or cooperation.

**The act:** Mig crosses the threshold. She does not announce that her arc is complete. The DM reads: *"She steps through, and does not look back."* After crossing, she sets the clay cup on the Nave shrine (in adventure 0016). That is the full completion. The cup on the shrine is the act she came to perform. The arc closes when it is performed.

**If the door opens without her present:** Her arc is deferred to 0016 — she will find the door open on her next approach and cross then. The arc still closes; it closes later.
