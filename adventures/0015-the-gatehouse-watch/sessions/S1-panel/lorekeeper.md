## Critique

### Overall assessment

This session could not have happened in any other setting. The gully dwarves' thirty-year vigil is only remarkable in a world that decided they were incapable of it — and the session honors that without explaining it in the log's narrative. The Lorekeeper's concern: "gully dwarves" appears without in-fiction gloss in the session log. The premise.md received the P0.3 fix, but the log itself uses the term as if the reader knows why a gully dwarf congregation is surprising. Mishakal also goes unglosed. Neither is a failure — the log is a DM record, not a player-facing document — but the module's first delivery to players should catch both.

### What worked

- **Scene 1.2 (V.S. initials):** *"Scratched, the way a person marks a thing they expect to get back."* This is Dragonlance-native prose register. The detail — not stamped, not dyed, but scratched — does lore work without citation. Someone who had read Velantha's history (which the party hasn't) would know what this means. Someone who hasn't read it would know that someone cared enough to mark this thing in the hardest, most permanent way available to them. The setting rewards attention without requiring prior reading.

- **Scene 5 (Mishakal's half-carved symbol):** *"A carved Mishakal symbol half-finished on the east wall — the chisel marks end mid-stroke."* The stopping-mid-stroke is the correct image for a post-Cataclysm setting: things were being built and they stopped. The symbol being Mishakal's — goddess of healing, whose cathedral this is — is set-appropriate without being explained. The Lorekeeper approves of this restraint: it trusts the setting to speak through image rather than exposition.

### What needs work

- **"Gully dwarves" unglosed in session narrative:** The log uses "gully dwarves" in the session summary and throughout the narrative without explaining why their 30-year vigil is extraordinary. The premise.md received the in-fiction gloss (*"Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by Krynnish society to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory"*) after the persona review. But the session log — which future readers will use to understand what happened — doesn't carry that context. A Lorekeeper correction: the session summary should include one line of why this is remarkable. Suggested addition: *"Seven gully dwarves — Aghar; the creatures every Krynnish faction has agreed cannot maintain purpose or faith — had been here, keeping faith, for thirty years."*

- **Mishakal unglosed at first use:** The session mentions "Mishakal's half-carved symbol" without introducing Mishakal to a reader who doesn't know. The accessibility policy (CLAUDE.md) requires first-use glossing of setting-specific terms. The symbol is central to the chapel scene's emotional resonance. Without knowing Mishakal is the goddess of healing — and that the cathedral is being built for her — the symbol is generic. A one-sentence gloss at first use in the log would satisfy the policy.

### The question I'd push on

**Does Renn register the High Sorcery connection in the V.S. initials?** Renn made a pact outside the Orders' sanction. Velantha used an Orders-prohibited ritual. The V.S. initials are on a healer's bag. Renn has the class features of an unauthorized magic user and he found evidence of an unauthorized healer. The Lorekeeper wants to know: does Renn's background make him more likely to recognize the nature of the V.S. ritual in a future session? His character sheet says he found his pact in a collapsed Istaran wayhouse — which is High Sorcery territory. When the party eventually learns what Velantha did, Renn will understand it in a way no other PC will. Design that understanding into his 0017/0018 scenes.
