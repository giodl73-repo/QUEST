## Critique

### Overall assessment

The Ghoul encounter had the right shape — initiative order mattered, AC differential mattered — but the encounter's designed threat (the paralysis on Claw hits) never fired. A Ghoul that never lands a Claw is a haunted-house decoration. The social encounter is outside my usual territory but the Awakened Mind social bypass deserves a tactical note: Renn made a class-feature decision that reshaped the entire encounter architecture, and that's legitimate tactical play.

### What worked

- **Scene 2.2, Round 1 (Maret):** Initiative 19 placed Maret ahead of the Ghoul (13). She got two strikes in before it could attack. Total damage: 5. The Ghoul's first action was a miss (AC 16 > Claws +4 = 10). The initiative order was the session's only real tactical decision — it mattered. Maret's AC investment (16 Unarmored Defense) protected her from the Ghoul's primary threat without a Con save. That's a legitimate design payoff.

- **Scene 2.2, Round 2 (Dren):** The greatsword at 13 vs. AC 12 hit, delivering the killing blow. Two rerolls via Great Weapon Fighting (two 1s → rerolled). The reroll mechanic fired in the session's only substantive combat, which is when you want it. The kill came from the back of initiative order — the player who rolled worst on initiative delivered the final hit. Good spread.

### What needs work

- **Ghoul's Claws never landed.** The Ghoul's Claws (+4 to hit) rolled 10 and 6 across two rounds against Maret AC 16. Neither hit. The DC 10 Con save — the Ghoul's actual threat — never had to be called. For the Tactician, this is the session's structural miss: the designed danger of the encounter was never tested. We don't know if Maret (Con +2, no Str/Con proficiency on saves) would have made that save. We should know by S2.

- **Faerie Fire failed, leaving Calla mechanical-idle for Round 2.** The Ghoul made its Con save vs. DC 13 (rolled 17). Calla passed a Bardic Inspiration die and held action in Round 2. Her mechanical contribution to the combat was one failed concentration spell and one BI die. At L3, College of Lore Bard has better options. The module should note: the Ghoul's Con modifier is 0; Faerie Fire DC 13 is correctly calibrated, but the party should know this fight can be won on concentration if the save drops. It didn't drop. That's dice. But Calla's Round 2 holding was the session's weakest tactical moment.

### The question I'd push on

**Does the Ghoul need a higher retreat threshold?** The module sets retreat at HP ≤ 10, but the Ghoul died at 3 HP in Round 2 after never retreating. At level 3 with four PCs, the Ghoul's action economy (one Bite + one Claws) is outpaced immediately. The designed threat (paralysis) requires a Claw hit, which requires hitting AC. Against a party with AC 16, 16, 14, 14, the Ghoul's +4 to hit gives roughly 45% hit rate — below the expected 65% for CR-matched encounters. For S2, if the Ghoul ever has a resurrection or the party encounters it again, consider whether the encounter needs a second combatant to create real action-economy pressure.
