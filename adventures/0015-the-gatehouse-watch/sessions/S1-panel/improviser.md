## Critique

### Overall assessment

The DM said yes four times. Every yes had a consequence. The Improviser is satisfied on the core criterion. The best moment — Renn bypassing the social encounter with Awakened Mind — is not just a yes; it's the DM recognizing that a class feature is a form of player problem-solving and letting it solve the problem. The Improviser's concern: the unnamed congregation member watching Maret in Scene 5 was improvised and correct, but the log doesn't show the DM checking whether this was earned within fiction. Was this character there from the start or summoned when the scene needed them? The answer matters.

### What worked

- **Scene 3 (Awakened Mind social bypass):** The module's social encounter was designed around Mig's non-verbal communication (tilting her head, pointing at the satchel) and the party needing to interpret her. Renn said: no, I'll talk directly. Awakened Mind sent a telepathic message to Mig that bypassed the language barrier entirely. The DM let it work. *"Mig (through Renn's telepathic channel, responding in broken images and emotion rather than words — she does not have the concept of telepathy but responds instinctively)"* — this is yes-and at its best. The DM didn't just let the mechanic fire; they handled Mig's response non-verbally to honor the character's communication limitations while still giving the information. The yes was earned and its consequence was the social encounter's resolution.

- **Scene 5 (Unnamed congregation member and Maret's breath-work):** The module notes the congregation has unnamed members; it doesn't script one of them watching Maret's practice and performing a parallel ritual. The DM invented this within fiction — a gully dwarf woman, older than Mig, stone-turning with a rhythm — and the invention was fair. The consequence was the craft-witness firing. A pure yes-and invention that served the session's research question.

### What needs work

- **Dren's second grate Perception attempt:** Dren fumbled his first Perception check for the grate (total 4, DC 10). The DM then narrated: *"Mig, seeing Dren's eyes find the grate, shakes her head once at it. Dren follows their glances down."* This gave Dren a second bite at the apple via congregation body language. The Improviser's verdict: this is fair adjudication — the context changed between the first and second checks. Dren wasn't watching the floor; he was watching the congregation; the congregation directed his attention. But the log notes it as a "creative ruling" and uses a prior roll (13 from the Ghoul initiative slot) as the resolution. That's improvising with the available dice rather than calling for a new roll. Defensible. Improviser verdict: yes, but flag this for the DM. If the party learns they can always get a second attempt by reading the room, that's not just improvising — it's an unwritten mechanic.

- **Calla's Round 2 Faerie Fire hold:** After Faerie Fire failed (Ghoul Con save 17), Calla held her action in Round 2. The log says *"she held her action."* What was she ready to trigger? The log doesn't say. An improviser would have Calla declare the readied action trigger. The session quietly glossed it. In future combat rounds, if a PC holds an action, the trigger should be in the log.

### The question I'd push on

**Did Mig going through the door produce a consequence for the rest of the Nave that the DM will have to improvise?** Route D was achieved: Mig stepped through. The Nave congregation's representative appeared at the threshold. The session ended. But the inner congregation now knows the party opened the door, helped Mig through, and killed the Ghoul in the crawlspace. What did the inner congregation do overnight? Did they extend trust? Did they send another representative to the gatehouse? The Improviser wants to see the consequence of Route D show up in S2's opening — not as a combat encounter but as a changed social situation. The DM said yes to Route D; the yes should have a morning-after.
