## Critique

### Overall assessment

All four PCs said or did something only they could have said or done. Dren's crack — the pause before "healer" held through four scenes without being externalized — is this session's most important character work. The session also produced the workshop's first restraint-moment in a new campaign: Renn finding the V.S. initials and returning the satchel without comment. The Roleplayer has one complaint: Calla's grief-thread (Tarrin, the missing witness) was seeded in the log's prep but didn't surface in play. She held the shrine observation. She didn't connect it to him. That's appropriate for S1, but I'll be watching.

### What worked

- **Scene 6 (Dren's crack):** *"The pause before the word 'healer' is there, held, and released without speaking."* This is the session's best character moment — a grief-thread activated not by a dice roll, not by a named scene, but by watching the healer's bag reach the destination the healer never reached herself. The log narrates: *"He does not say anything."* That silence is correct. That silence is Dren. The log cites his Playstyle Heuristic ("defers to party intelligence on context"), which drove the Route D decision, and then separately documents the emotional consequence. Two things happening in the same beat without conflating them. Good.

- **Scene 3 (Renn and the V.S. initials):** *"Renn does not say what this means to him. He hands the satchel back to Mig carefully."* Renn made an unauthorized pact to help someone; Velantha made an unauthorized ritual to help someone. The parallel is present in the log without being stated. Renn's restraint — finding evidence of someone who did what he did and not saying so — is played from his character sheet's Secret ("he does not know the cost yet") and his Flaw ("dishonest about everything else"). The session gave him a mirror and he didn't pick it up. That's exactly right for session 1.

### What needs work

- **Scene 2.1 (Calla's documentation-suspension):** The log documents Calla's Insight fumble as a documentation-suspension beat — she cannot categorize the shrine arrangements. Correct and earned. But Calla's grief-thread is about *Tarrin*, who closed his eyes at the founding verse, and she no longer knows if she's telling it right without him as a witness. The shrines are a practice without a formal tradition — which is exactly what Calla's oral tradition has become since Thornfield burned and Tarrin died. The log doesn't make this connection. The connection exists in the character sheet. The session held the observation without pulling the thread that would have made it personal. This is appropriate for S1 — don't force it — but the Roleplayer is flagging it for S2 or S3: the shrine arrangements should eventually land on Calla not just as a documentation problem but as a recognition that someone else is keeping faith without knowing if they're doing it right.

- **Scene 2.2 combat (all PCs):** Third-person discipline holds throughout. *"Maret is simply in a different position than she was one breath ago."* Good. But the combat scenes flatten the PCs slightly — they are class features and dice results, not characters. Dren's greatsword misses in Round 1 and the log records it cleanly. Maret's strikes are narrated with technical precision. Neither of these is wrong. But in S2, at least one combat beat should carry a character-interior note: what is Maret thinking when she hits the Ghoul? The combat log is accurate; it is not yet alive.

### The question I'd push on

**Calla's verbal documentation-arc needs its first articulated observation.** The arc fires from a fumble in S1 — she cannot categorize what she's holding. That's the arc's opening condition. What makes the arc interesting is not indefinite suspension but eventual speech: a session where Calla says aloud what she has been holding, to someone, and finds that saying it changes it. When does she say it? The arc has a beginning; it needs a designed endpoint, or it becomes permanent background texture rather than a character arc. Push the DM on this before S3.
