## Verdict: FAIL

The dungeon is well-built and the agency structure is the best I've seen from this workshop — three routes with real cost distinctions is legitimate design. But I can't pass an adventure where a level 3 party faces nothing harder than CR ¼ and the central treasure has no drain mechanic. This is a dungeon without attrition. I want honest cost.

## Strongest axes

**Agency (9/10):** Route A, C, and D are genuinely differentiated. Route A closes the gate and calls in Doran — a real choice with a real cost (the congregation is erased). Route D surrenders the building — the party does less work and gets less of what they came for. The portcullis mechanism giving the party a Route A tool before they understand what it does is good design. Counter-evidence: the routes are finale-level only; mid-adventure choices (search vs don't search R3) have no mechanical consequence beyond information.

**Dungeon (8/10):** The topology is sound. Loop confirmed (R1→R3→R2→R1 via arch and grate), vertical confirmed (R2→R4 stairs), conditional shortcut confirmed (R4 rope ladder after Torva trusts the party). Two meaningful approaches to the main social arena (front gate vs collapsed wall). The flanking approach yields a meaningfully different first contact. Counter-evidence: only one conditional shortcut; R5 and R6 are a linear corridor once the party reaches the chapel.

## Weakest axes

**Encounter (4/10):** Three encounters, all CR ¼. Against a level 3 party, this means: Grisk (HP 12, AC 11) falls in one hit from Dren's greatsword. The rat swarm (HP 24) is gone in one Eldritch Blast plus one hit. Torva on the wall-walk throws debris for 1d4. There is no attrition in this dungeon. A party that burns no resources here — and they won't — arrives at adventure 0016 at full strength with no sense that danger is real. The XP budget table in the cheatsheet says "Easy 300 · Medium 600" but this adventure delivers approximately 150 XP in combat if the party fights everything. That is below Easy.

**Curse/Consequence (5/10):** The satchel helps the party. Preserved herbs, advantage on Medicine, auto-stabilize. The relic test (if you use it to poison someone, the warmth fades) is a moral weight but not a mechanical one — a level 3 party doesn't use their healer's bag as a poison delivery system. There is no cost to possessing the satchel. In six previous campaigns this workshop has never produced a central treasure that's purely beneficial. This is the first one. I understand the narrative reason. I still dock the score.

**Table (5/10):** The module_binder inlined encounter files without stripping their headers. Scene 2's Encounter section contains `## Type` and `## Setup` at H2 level mid-scene. A DM running cold will see major section headers appearing inside what should be a room description. This is a formatting failure that needs to be fixed before anyone runs this at a table.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — Encounter pressure: zero attrition for a level 3 party**

The adventure has no encounter at or above CR 1. Grisk (CR ¼) and Torva (CR ¼) are each a single hit. Against four level 3 characters with proper action economy, this is not combat — it's paperwork. **Fix:** Escalate one encounter. Option A: Add one **Ghoul** (CR 1, 200 XP) in the crawlspace under R2/R3 — drawn to the half-consecrated space before the congregation arrived; the congregation is afraid of it; it's the reason the grate is left closed. This single addition raises the adventure to Easy-Medium, creates a tactical problem (the Ghoul's paralysis ability against level 3 PCs), and explains the congregation's unease around the grate without requiring new rooms. Option B: Make Torva's throwing attack wall-walk more dangerous (DC 13 Con save or disadvantaged on attacks from falling mortar — terrain hazard at CR equivalent of 1).

**P0.2 — Module formatting: H2 headers inside scene bodies**

A module_binder bug. Fix: strip encounter file H1/H2/H3 headers during inlining, converting them to bold text or H5. This is a code fix, not a design fix.

## Suggestions

1. The wandering pressure table's Roll 1 (structural hazard, 1d6 damage) is the only mechanical threat beyond combat. Consider making it more frequent — every 20 min rather than 30 min — to give the dungeon some teeth.
2. The portcullis mechanism is a good Gygaxian tool. Make sure the DM notes in the cheatsheet that using it to seal the congregation inside closes Route D permanently. Currently this is only in the scene GM Notes; it should be in the triggers table.
