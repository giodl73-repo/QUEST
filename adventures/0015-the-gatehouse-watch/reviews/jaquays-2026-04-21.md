## Verdict: PASS

The topology is the best this workshop has produced at the dungeon-as-machine level. The flanking route gives you a meaningfully different encounter. The faction repositioning between visits is designed in, not improvised. Grisk on the wall-walk instead of the collapsed section on return is a small thing that signals the dungeon is alive. Pass.

## Strongest axes

**Dungeon (9/10):** The loop exists and is exploitable (R1→R3→R2→R1 via the low arch and the floor grate). The vertical exists and matters (Torva retreats to R4; the wall-walk changes the tactical geometry of the Torva encounter). The conditional shortcut is faction-gated, which means it rewards social play rather than just Perception rolls. The collapsed wall provides a second exterior entry that changes first-contact dynamics entirely — a party that enters from the south road, not the front gate, catches Grisk alone and creates an entirely different opening scene. Every branch has a sensory cue at its entrance (draft + cold mortar at the arch; candle and voices at the main passage). Counter-evidence: R5 and R6 are a linear one-two after the chapel arch; no branching once the party passes the guardroom eastward.

**Encounter (7/10):** The flanking approach yielding a one-on-one Grisk encounter rather than a room-full-of-gully-dwarves encounter is exactly how I want encounter dynamics to work — player skill (noticing the arch, choosing the approach) changes the shape of the encounter, not just the tactics within it. The congregation's behavior change between visits (Grisk moves to R4 to watch from higher ground after first contact) is designed in, noted in the map, and referenced in the scene. The rat swarm is triggered, not placed — it lives in the dungeon and fires when the party disturbs its lair, which is the right way to design a secondary encounter. Counter-evidence: only one patrol-repositioning NPC; the four unnamed congregation members are static throughout.

## Weakest axes

**Curse (6/10):** The relic's warmth-toward-need is interesting but it has no faction behavior implication. The satchel is drawn toward healing activity — fine. Does the congregation notice? Does Grisk react to the party more warmly if one of them is visibly injured when they enter? The relic's Presence/Desire could drive faction behavior, but the module doesn't connect them. The congregation's behavior matrix should have a note: if the party includes someone wounded or tending wounds, Mig's initial posture softens.

**Agency (8/10):** Three routes are clean. The R3 flanking approach is the hidden fourth option that isn't named in the route structure — it should be. A party that enters via R3, catches Grisk alone, negotiates successfully, and gets Mig's cooperation without ever having to face the full guardroom has taken a genuine alternative path. Name it in the agency structure.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — The four unnamed congregation members are Jaquays-dead**

They are present, they follow Mig's lead, they flee through R5 if combat lasts more than 2 rounds. None of them have names, individual behavior, or a reason to be distinct from each other. In a dungeon where faction behavior is a first-class mechanic, unnamed characters who move in lockstep are wasted opportunity. **Fix:** Give at least two of the four congregation members one trait that distinguishes them — a distinctive action, a reaction that differs from Mig's lead, a private relationship to the satchel. They don't need full NPC files; one sentence each in the guardroom GM Notes is sufficient. The congregation should feel like people, not extras.

## Suggestions

1. The rope ladder shortcut (R4→R3) is the only conditional shortcut. Consider whether there's a second — perhaps a section of the R2 floor near the grate that the congregation has reinforced with planks, making the crawlspace traversal safer and faster for a party that befriended Mig. Same conditional structure, different activation.
2. The Nave door mechanism (portcullis in R1 releases R6's frame bolts) is a good Jaquaysian hidden connection. Make sure it appears in the sensory layer of R1 — a cable running along the ceiling ceiling that a Perception DC 11 party notices without rolling.
