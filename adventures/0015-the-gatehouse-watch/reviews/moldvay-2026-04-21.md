## Verdict: FAIL

The adventure is well-designed and well-written. The DM running it cold will enjoy reading it. The DM running it cold at 9pm on a Thursday while players are asking questions will hit a wall in Scene 2 where the compiled module has raw H2 headers mid-scene. That's the only reason this fails. Fix the compilation artifact and this passes.

## Strongest axes

**Agency (8/10):** Three routes, clearly named, with costs and gains stated. The DM Cheatsheet's Scene Triggers table is exactly what a DM needs at the table — "If the party does X, handle it like this" in one line per row. The portcullis mechanism having dual function (close the gate AND release the inner door) is the kind of procedural elegance that makes a module shine. Counter-evidence: the Scene Triggers table in the cheatsheet is hardcoded to this specific adventure's NPCs and rooms. When the module_binder compiles a different adventure, it will still print "Grisk interposes" — this needs to be adventure-specific or absent.

**Table (4/10) — but would be 8/10 if the formatting were clean:** The premise, hook, and background are clear. Read-alouds are under 60 words (except Scene 1 at 61 — one word over). DCs are inline in features. The wandering pressure table is in the module. Stat blocks are present. NPC behavior matrices are in the NPCs section. All of this is correct. But:

- Scene 2's Encounter section opens with `**Primary social encounter:** # Encounter — Gatehouse Defense` followed by a raw `## Type` header at H2 level. A DM reading this sees a major section header in the middle of what should be room content.
- Scene 3 has the same artifact — the full encounter file header structure appears mid-scene.
- These H2 headers will appear in any markdown renderer as same-level siblings to `## Map` and `## Treasures`. A DM running cold at a screen will be confused about which heading level they are at.

This is a module_binder compilation artifact, not a design flaw. But the output is what the DM reads.

## Weakest axes

**Curse (6/10):** The relic test is explained clearly (intent to harm → attunement ends). The ending condition is clear (another creature can attune; warmth may return). The passive benefit is clearly differentiated from the attuned benefit. Moldvay approves of the format. But the "relic test" framing has no mechanical trigger visible to the player — there is no roll, no save, no explicit moment. The DM adjudicates "intent." In a rules-clear module, adjudication calls should be flagged: "The DM adjudicates harmful use. This check fires *after* the action, not before." The note is buried in GM Notes rather than adjacent to the stat block where a DM would see it during play.

## P0 Issues

**P0.1 — H2 encounter headers mid-scene break table-readiness**

The module_binder inlines encounter files including their `## Type` and `## Setup` headers, which appear at H2 level inside scene bodies. Scene 2's encounter section shows:

```
**Primary social encounter:** # Encounter — Gatehouse Defense

## Type
Social encounter...
```

This is not a DM-readable format. **Fix (module_binder):** Strip or downgrade H1/H2/H3 headers from inlined encounter content. Convert `## Type` → `**Type:**`, `## Setup` → `**Setup:**`, etc. This is a one-line change in `_inline_encounter`.

**P0.2 — Scene 1 read-aloud is 61 words (one over the 60-word limit)**

"The gate stands open. The portcullis is raised..." counts 61 words in Python's `split()`. **Fix:** Remove one word. Suggested: "Someone oiled these hinges recently." → "The hinges move without sound." (saves 3 words, is more evocative).

## Suggestions

1. The NPCs section in the module has Mig and Grisk in full. The four unnamed congregation members are not in the NPCs section at all. For a DM running cold, a one-line "Unnamed congregation member — non-combatant, HP 6, follows Mig" entry in the NPCs section would complete the encounter reference.
2. The wandering pressure table is in the module. Good. It would be better if the DM Cheatsheet's Quick Reference table included the wandering pressure interval ("Roll Cold Pulse every 10 min; Roll Wandering every 30 min") rather than requiring the DM to remember to check the Wandering Pressure section.
