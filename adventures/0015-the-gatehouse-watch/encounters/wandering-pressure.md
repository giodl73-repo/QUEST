# Wandering Pressure — The Gatehouse Watch

*Roll Cold Pulse (d6) every 10 minutes of in-game exploration. Roll Wandering Encounter (d6) every 30 minutes.*

*Justification for temporal pressure in a non-sealed setting: the gatehouse is an unstable structure with an active congregation whose behavior changes on awareness of the party. Time pressure comes from structural hazard, congregation movement, and the possibility that word will reach the inner congregation before the party is ready.*

---

## Cold Pulse Table (d6, every 10 min — atmospheric, no mechanical effect)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | A groan from the upper level — stone settling. Small grit falls from the ceiling in the current room. Nothing moves. |
| 2 | Through the inner door (R6), the sound of the inner congregation's rite: distant, rhythmic, low. It is audible for approximately thirty seconds, then fades back to ambient. |
| 3 | Wind through the collapsed section (R3) moves something — a loose construction plank scrapes across stone. It is the sound of an empty building. |
| 4 | One of the unnamed congregation members has moved since the party last looked. They are in a different position, watching. They are not advancing. They are watching. |
| 5 | The herb smell intensifies briefly — stronger than before — and then settles back. No visible cause. |
| 6 | A distant sound of tapping, regular, from somewhere in the cathedral above. Construction? Wind against scaffolding? It stops before the party can locate it. |

---

## Wandering Encounter Table (d6, every 30 min — mechanical, may require rolls)

| d6 | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Structural hazard.** A ceiling stone in the current room (or nearest room) has been loosened by vibration or age. A crack widens — audible. Each creature in the room makes a DC 11 Perception check; on success, they notice the crack in time to step aside. On failure: DC 10 Dexterity save or 1d6 bludgeoning damage from falling debris when the stone drops (1 round after the crack). |
| 2 | **Grisk repositions.** If Grisk has not been defeated or fled, he has changed position since the party last saw him. They encounter him now, in a different room from where they left him, watching from a new angle. *If this roll occurs after the social encounter resolved peacefully:* Grisk makes eye contact and does not move. If it occurs during hostile conditions: he blocks the party's line of retreat. |
| 3 | **Rats stir.** Scratching under the floor is audible in the current room, even if the grate has not been disturbed. If the grate has already been triggered and cleared: the scratching is coming from further along the crawlspace — the rats are moving. If the grate has not been triggered: this is a warning before the trigger. |
| 4 | **Mig approaches.** If social encounter has resolved (any route): Mig approaches the party wherever they are, carrying a clay cup of water from a construction-era cistern she found. She offers it. No ulterior motive. She is doing what she knows how to do in the presence of people who might be guests. *If the party accepts the water:* the congregation's posture toward the party relaxes noticeably. Grisk sits down somewhere. |
| 5 | **Unnamed congregation member tries to leave.** One of the unnamed congregation members moves toward R3 to exit via the collapsed wall — they have an errand or they are anxious and need air. If the party blocks the exit, the congregation member calls for Grisk (not hostile, just alarmed). If the party lets them pass: they return in 10 minutes. If the party follows: they are going outside to collect water from a rain-catch vessel they've set up against the outer wall. |
| 6 | **The rite begins in R2.** The congregation (those in R2) begins a communal practice: stones placed in a particular arrangement, a repeated low sound, six minutes of focused activity. The congregation is not hostile. They are performing their rite in the space the party is standing in. The party can wait (the rite ends naturally), participate (no roll needed — Mig will indicate what to do with a gesture), or interrupt (the congregation stops, and the goodwill accumulated so far drops by one degree). |
