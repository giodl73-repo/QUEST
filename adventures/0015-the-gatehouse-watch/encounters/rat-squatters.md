# Encounter — Rat Squatters

## Type
Combat encounter (triggered). Rats nested in the crawlspace beneath R2 and R3, nearer the R3 side. The Ghoul (see `encounters/ghoul-in-crawlspace.md`) nests nearer the R2 side; if the grate is opened from R2, the Ghoul emerges first. The rat swarm triggers if the party enters the crawlspace from R3's side, or if the Ghoul has already been defeated and the grate is opened.

## Setup

The rats have been here longer than the congregation. They entered through the collapsed outer wall in R3 and found the crawlspace under the guardroom floor useful for nesting. The congregation has been coexisting with them — they do not like the rats, but they have not been able to clear them, and the rats have not been actively attacking.

The encounter triggers when the iron floor grate (in R2 or R3) is disturbed.

## Combatants

### Swarm of Rats
*Use SRD "Swarm of Rats" stat block.*
- **HP:** 24 · **AC:** 10 · **Speed:** 30 ft
- **Attack:** Bites +2, 2d6 piercing (or 1d6 if below half HP)
- **Condition immunities:** charmed, frightened, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, prone, restrained, stunned
- **Swarm:** Can occupy another creature's space; can move through tiny gaps

## Trigger

The grate is lifted, or a creature squeezes into the crawlspace. The rats surge up through the grate opening immediately. On initiative, the swarm enters whichever room the grate is in (R2 or R3) and targets the nearest creature.

## Duration

The swarm is driven off (retreats into crawlspace) when reduced to 12 HP or fewer. It does not leave the crawlspace again this session unless the grate is disturbed a second time (it will not reinitiate; it only defends its lair).

## Congregation behavior during encounter

The congregation in R2 reacts to the rats with unhappy familiarity rather than panic. They climb onto the bench frames (which are sturdy enough) and wait. Grisk pulls his feet up. Mig picks up the satchel before doing anything else. The unnamed congregation members press against the walls.

This is not a helpful reaction, but it is a realistic one. The congregation is not going to help fight the rats. If the party defeats the rats, the congregation's regard for the party increases — they have solved a problem the congregation couldn't solve.

**Faction effect:** Defeating the rat swarm without making the congregation feel targeted counts as a goodwill gesture. If the social encounter in R2 has not yet resolved, defeating the rats first gives the party advantage on any subsequent social check with Mig (she saw them solve a problem that has been annoying her for weeks).

## Post-encounter

The crawlspace is now accessible without triggering the rats again. It connects R2 and R3 (see map). A Medium creature squeezes through (disadvantage on attack rolls while in the crawl). The crawl is approximately 20 ft long.

**Optional loot:** In the crawlspace, alongside the rat nest: a construction tool pouch left by a worker who must have dropped it through the grate. Contains: 2 cold chisels, a wax marker, and 3 gp in mixed coin. *Incidental item purpose: evidence of normal construction activity before the site turned.*
