## Setting & Background

**Place:** The outer gatehouse of the Halted Spire — an unfinished Mishakal cathedral on the hill above **Mireth-on-the-Hill**, Abanasinia. Post-War of the Lance era. The main cathedral body looms behind the gatehouse: scaffolding, rough-cut stone, open sky where the roof should be.

**The gatehouse itself:** Two stories. The lower level includes a portcullis passage, a guardroom, and a waiting chapel (intended for pilgrims; never completed). The upper level is a wall-walk overlooking the approach road. A collapsed section of the outer wall provides a second entry the congregation uses. The gate hinges have been oiled recently — not by the construction crew.

**Atmosphere:** Smells of fresh earth and tallow. Small shrines assembled from construction offcuts in the corners of the guardroom — a candle stub, three smooth stones, a piece of cloth knotted around a nail. Still being assembled. The newest arrivals are here; they haven't learned the congregation's full rites yet. They are doing what they can with what they know.

**Magistra Doran's commission** — the party has been hired to assess and clear the cathedral site so construction can resume. They have been told: workers fled, the site is dangerous, something is wrong. They have not been told what.

They enter through the main gate. The gate is unlocked. It should not be unlocked — the construction crew secured it when they left. The hinges move without sound.

Inside the gatehouse, they find gully dwarves.

The gully dwarves here are the most recently arrived — three weeks to two months, compared to some who have been in the cathedral for years. There are **seven of them** in the gatehouse section. They are not organized into the congregation's full rites. They arrived because word reached their community (a settlement in the hills northwest of Mireth) that the place Velantha died was nearby. They came. They are still figuring out what to do now that they are here.

**They are not attacking the party.** They will defend themselves if threatened. Two of them — **Grisk** and **Torva** — are the most fearful and the most likely to escalate. The others will follow the lead of **Mig**, the one among them who came with a purpose: she carries a cracked cup she believes Velantha used, and she intends to leave it at the Nave shrine. She does not know where the Nave shrine is yet.

Gully dwarves — Aghar, in the old tongue — are considered by Krynnish society to be incapable of sustained purpose, faith, or memory. The congregation in this gatehouse has maintained a daily practice for thirty years.

**The field satchel is here.** Mig is carrying it in addition to the cup. She found it outside the gate three days ago — it was placed there by someone from the inner congregation who wanted the newcomers to have it. The herbs inside are impossibly fragrant. She does not know why. She will not part with it easily, but she is not irrational.

**Hint 1 delivered this session:** The congregation's existence is undeniable by end of session. The party knows gully dwarves are living in the cathedral and have been there longer than three weeks. Whether they understand *why* is based on how they engaged with Mig. If they engaged: they know Velantha healed gully dwarves. If they did not: they know gully dwarves are here for a reason they have not yet learned.

Recovery path: 0016 (nave congregation's oral tradition will deliver this without requiring the party to have gotten it from Mig).

- **congregation-has-elder** — Mig refers to "the one who knows everything" who lives "inside, deep." Not a name. A direction.
- **satchel-herbs-impossibly-fresh** — The party has (or has observed) the satchel. Something kept these herbs for thirty years.
- **congregation-was-organized** — The gatehouse group defers to the inner congregation's authority. They are newcomers; the real congregation is further in.

### Scene 1 — Gate Passage

> The gate stands open. The portcullis is raised; the mechanism chain shows no rust. The hinges move without sound. The passage smells of tallow and something drier — dried herbs, though there are no herbs here. A low stone arch on the south wall is half in shadow. Beyond: candlelight, and the sound of low voices.

#### Features

- **Portcullis winch** (east wall, mounted). Iron handle, functional. Operates the portcullis (west gate) AND releases the door frame bolts on the inner door in R6 — the two mechanisms were designed as one system. Lowering the portcullis closes the external gate but **releases R6's mechanical lock**, allowing the door to be opened from R5's side with only the congregation's bar to clear. **DC 11 Investigation** to notice the connecting mechanism cable running along the ceiling toward the interior. **DC 13 History or Arcana** to understand that this is a standard double-gate security design (lock one end open, the other end locked).
- **Oiled hinges** (gate arch). The oil is fresh — three days at most. Functional observation, not mechanically significant; confirms someone has been maintaining this space.
- **Low arch** (south wall). 4 ft clearance. Requires crouching. **DC 12 Perception** to notice it from the passage. The arch leads to R3 (Collapsed Section). At the arch entrance: a draft and the smell of night air and cold mortar, distinct from the tallow smell of R2.
- **Child-sized handprint** (gate arch stone, inner face). Tallow, pressed deliberately. **DC 13 Perception** to notice. No mechanical significance; it is a child's mark on the threshold of a place they were told matters.

#### Encounter

None. Unless the party is fleeing R2 with the congregation in pursuit — in which case Grisk may attempt to use the portcullis winch to seal the external gate and trap the party inside.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- **West:** External approach road (main gate, portcullis)
- **East:** R2 (Guardroom) — main passage, open
- **South:** R3 (Collapsed Section) — low arch, DC 12 Perception to notice, 4 ft clearance

#### GM Notes

This room is a threshold and a tool. Its job is to deliver three things immediately: (1) the party knows someone is maintaining this space, (2) the party hears and smells the congregation before they see them, (3) the party has access to the portcullis mechanism before they understand what it does.

**The portcullis mechanism is a Route A tool.** A party attempting Route A — clear the site, restore construction — will eventually use the winch to lower the portcullis (sealing the external gate) and lock the congregation inside while Doran's people finish the job. Make sure the party notices the mechanism before they reach R6. A party that lowers the portcullis without realizing it releases R6 has created a situation — the inner door can now be opened, which the congregation will notice.

**Relic sign present:** The olfactory sign — herbs where there are no herbs — is detectable here (passive Perception 11; or DC 11 if the party is actively searching). The smell is stronger in R2 and strongest in R5.

**Dren Holt spotlight note:** Dren checks this room automatically (his Playstyle Heuristic: checks every room before the party enters). He will note the oiled hinges. He will note the portcullis mechanism. He will position himself at the arch threshold before the party moves forward. Play the thoroughness; it is who he is.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the arch)*
*A woman's hands, checking the gate latch quickly before moving through. The same gesture she made at the camp entrance, at the tavern door, at every threshold she was likely to leave from and might not return to. The habit of someone who checks, just in case.*

### Scene 2 — Guardroom

> Seven gully dwarves in a stone room made into living quarters: bedrolls, candle-nail holders, three arrangements of stones and scraps on the cleared floor. The air smells of tallow and something dried. Near the south wall, a gully dwarf holds a worn leather satchel and watches you enter.

#### Features

- **Three shrine arrangements** (floor, central space). Each: three smooth stones, a candle stub, a piece of cloth knotted around a nail. Distinctly deliberate. **DC 13 Religion** — these are not a formal religious tradition; they are devotional practice worked out from first principles. **DC 10 Insight** — the arrangements are recent (days, not months) but the congregation's behavior around them is practiced (they step around them without looking, the way you step around a habit). *This is where the maintenance relic sign lands.*

- **Mig and the field satchel** (south wall). The satchel is warm to the touch; anyone who takes it from Mig's hands will notice immediately. See `npcs/mig.md` for behavior matrix. See `treasures/field-satchel.md` for relic mechanics. **Retrieval path 1:** Negotiate with Mig (social encounter). **Retrieval path 2:** Take by force (combat; closes Route D; congregation in R2 escalates). Note: these are meaningfully different — Path 1 opens Route D; Path 2 closes it.

- **Iron floor grate** (south wall, near where Mig sits). Recessed iron grate, ~2 ft square, giving access to the crawlspace beneath the guardroom floor. **DC 10 Perception** to notice. **DC 12 Strength** to lift. The crawlspace connects to R3 (Collapsed Section). A Ghoul and a rat swarm both inhabit the crawlspace; the rats nest nearer R3, the Ghoul nearer R2. Disturbing the grate triggers the Ghoul first. *The congregation are afraid of what is under the grate; Mig sits near it precisely to watch it. They will not volunteer this information; if asked why she sits there, she points at the grate and shakes her head once.*

- **Warmth anomaly** (near south wall, where Mig rests). The room should be cold — stone, no hearth, open drafts from the R3 connection. Near Mig, it is not as cold as it should be. **DC 12 Perception** to notice the localization of warmth. Traces to the satchel once the party handles it. *Relic sign: warmth anomaly.*

- **Stairs** (north wall). Stone stairs, no door, ascending to R4 (Wall-Walk). Sound of wind from above.

#### Treasure

##### Appearance

The satchel is old work — tanned hide, double-stitched at the stress points, the leather cracked at the fold lines but still supple where it should be supple. Three compartments: a main body for cloth and bandages, a flat pocket for documents, and a narrow inner sleeve whose lining has been replaced twice (the stitching is different thread from the outer body). The inner sleeve is where the herbs are. They should be dust. They are not. The brass clasp is tarnished green at the edges but closes cleanly. On the underside of the flap, faint and almost gone, the letters **V.S.** are scratched with something sharp — not stamped, not dyed. Scratched, the way a person marks a thing they expect to get back.

The satchel is warm. Not hot, not alarming — warm, like a hand held briefly in another hand. The warmth does not fade in cold rooms. It does not intensify in hot ones. It is simply, persistently, there.

##### Presence / Desire

The satchel does not want to be stored. It is patient — thirty years in gully dwarf hands has given it patience — but it has the quality of readiness about it: when picked up by someone in a hurry, it feels lighter than it should; when set down in a corner, it seems somehow closer to the room's center the next time the holder looks. It is drawn to activity, to need, to the immediate present of someone requiring tending.

It is warmest in the hands of people who are afraid and helping anyway. It is cooler — not cold, just less warm — in the hands of someone who has no intention of using it. It does not judge that person. It simply waits for a different holder.

The congregation has noted, over thirty years, that no keeper of the satchel has died of fever. They believe this is the satchel's doing. They may be right.

##### Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a creature that has tended to another creature's wounds at least once (no restriction on class, race, or alignment — the attunement condition is an act, not an identity).

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Preserved herbs.** Dried herbs placed in the satchel's inner sleeve compartment retain full potency indefinitely. When the attuned creature uses an Herbalism Kit in conjunction with these herbs, they add 1d4 hit points to any hit points restored.
- **Healer's touch.** Once per long rest, as a bonus action, the attuned creature can stabilize a dying creature within reach. No Medicine check required. The dying creature is automatically stable.
- **Practiced hand.** The attuned creature has advantage on Medicine checks.

### Passive benefit (no attunement required)

- **Preserved herbs** are accessible to any creature using the satchel's herbs, attuned or not. The 1d4 bonus applies to anyone.

### Relic Test

The field satchel is not cursed. It has a test.

If the attuned creature uses the satchel or its contents to deliver a harmful substance to another creature — poison, a damaging preparation, anything intended to injure rather than heal — the attunement ends immediately. The satchel cannot be re-attuned by that creature. The herbs in the inner sleeve decay to dust over the following 24 hours, releasing their thirty-year-old fragrance for the last time. The warmth fades. The satchel becomes ordinary leather with a brass clasp and faint initials that mean nothing anymore.

The DM adjudicates what constitutes "harmful use." The test is not about accident or ignorance. It is about intent. A healer who applies an herb incorrectly and harms a patient does not fail the test. A creature who deliberately delivers poison through the satchel does.

*Workshop note:* This is not mechanical compulsion — the satchel does not prevent the action. It responds after. The congregation would consider this a form of Mishakal's observation, though Velantha herself never framed it that way.

### Ending the Relic Test consequence

The test consequence (decay and ordinariness) is permanent for the individual who triggered it. Another creature can attune to the satchel — but the herbs will not grow back. Whatever was in the sleeve when it decayed is gone. The warmth, however, may return for a new keeper. The congregation believes it will. The DM decides if they are right, based on the campaign's route and the new keeper's intent. — held by Mig; warm, latched, herbs-fragrant. Two retrieval paths (see Features above).

**Incidental item:** A small carved stone, gully-dwarf work, in the shape of a cup. Not the satchel; a separate object, recently made by one of the unnamed congregation members. If examined: **DC 13 Investigation** — the carving is crude but the shape is deliberate. The object is a devotional representation of the measuring cup (in R2 next session). *Plant for the congregation's relic culture; not mechanically significant this session.*

#### GM Notes

This is the session's main social arena. Everything happens here or radiates from here.

**Mig's opening position:** She does not move when the party enters. She has seen construction workers before; they come in, see the congregation, leave faster than they arrived. She is giving the party time to make the first move. Do not have her speak first. Let the party speak or act first. If they say nothing for thirty seconds of real time, she tilts her head — a question without words.

**Dren Holt's crack activates here.** The moment Dren understands Mig is holding a healer's bag — that she came here because a healer came for her people — play the pause. Do not narrate it. Just note it. Play the voice-tag: the slight pause before "healer" if the word comes up. He does not say anything. He notes it. This is the seed.

**Seeds planted:**
- *congregation-has-elder:* Mig, if the party engages her, refers to "the one who remembers everything" who lives "inside, deep." She does not name him. She gestures vaguely toward R5 and beyond.
- *satchel-herbs-impossibly-fresh:* The warmth is felt on first contact. The impossible freshness is noticed if the satchel is opened (the party must open it). **DC 11 Nature or Medicine** to recognize the herbs as dry goods that should not still be fragrant.
- *congregation-was-organized:* The four unnamed congregation members consistently look to Mig before reacting to party actions. And Mig herself, when asked anything about "who is in charge here," glances toward R5 (the direction of the inner congregation) before answering.

**Bard documentation-suspension (Calla Fosse):** Calla will try to categorize the shrine arrangements. The DM should note that she can't — these are not a known religious form. They are something the congregation worked out themselves. This moment is the first documentation-suspension beat for her arc. She observes. She holds the observation. She cannot file it. Play the stillness.

**If the party leaves and returns:** The shrine arrangements have been added to. One new stone. The candles have been replaced. The congregation has continued their practice in the party's absence. The room reads as maintained, not abandoned.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers near the shrine arrangements)*
*The first time a stone was placed here, it was just to have something to look at while she thought. Then a second stone, because the first had company in her mind. Then the cloth, because something needed to be tied. The arrangement grew the way a habit grows — not decided, arrived at.*

### Scene 3 — Collapsed Section

> A section of outer wall, long-collapsed, rubble worn smooth from use. The gap is step-through-sized. Beyond: the hill and road below. Inside: cold air, mortar dust, a low arch north, and a gully dwarf in the rubble who was watching the road and is now watching you.

#### Features

- **Collapsed wall gap** (south). The congregation's primary entrance. The rubble is smooth and packed — this gap has been used regularly for weeks to months. Anyone approaching the cathedral from the south road can enter here without passing through R1. *This is the flanking route to R2; the party can use it to approach the congregation from an unexpected direction.*

- **Low arch** (north wall). Connects to R1 (Gate Passage). 4 ft clearance; requires crouching. **DC 10 Perception** from R3 to find the arch (it is visible and unobstructed; the check is lower from this side). The arch is the reason R3 and R1 are linked — this was the guardroom's secondary internal access, a standard design for gate security.

- **Iron floor grate** (east wall, floor level). Same grate system visible from R2. Can be opened from this side as well — **DC 12 Strength**. The crawlspace is narrow; Medium creatures squeeze (disadvantage on attack rolls). Rat swarm lair. *The congregation uses this side of the grate to exit toward the collapsed wall without going through R2 — a practical shortcut that the party can also use.*

- **Rubble pile** (west wall). Debris from the original collapse. **DC 13 Investigation** to search usefully; yields: a small carved stone in the shape of a cup (same as the one in R2 if the party finds both — a second devotional object). Also: a fragment of a construction work-order in Abanasinian Common; the work-order is dated eleven years ago, references "the gatehouse threshold stones" and is signed by a foreman whose name is now largely illegible. *Not a key; evidence of the original commission.*

- **Grisk** (starting position). He is here when the session opens, watching the road through the collapsed wall gap. He hears the party enter R1 and moves to R2 to warn Torva. If the party enters through the collapsed wall *before* entering R1, they catch Grisk before he can warn anyone. This is the flanking approach's tactical advantage. See `npcs/grisk.md`.

Here, he just wants to know which direction the party is going.

### Scene 4 — Wall-Walk

> Open parapet, sky overhead. Below: the guardroom, road, hill. The cathedral above — close enough to see unset stones and open sky where the roof should be. Larger than it looked from below. South parapet: a rope coiled on an iron post. East parapet face: a scratched stone.

#### Features

- **View of the cathedral** (parapet, all sides). From here the party can see the full scope of the Halted Spire for the first time: the Nave's open roof, the unfinished Bell Tower in the distance, the scaffolding reaching up. **DC 13 Investigation** from this vantage: the party can identify at least two more entry points into the cathedral body visible from here — a door in the Nave's west wall (unfinished, covered with construction canvas) and a high opening in the Bell Tower. *This seeds awareness of the larger cathedral geography before dungeon-smith designs subsequent adventures.*

- **Tally stone** (east parapet face). A flat stone, mounted level with the parapet top, with marks scratched into it in rows. **DC 10 Perception** to notice. Counting the marks: thirty rows of marks; the marks in each row are not uniform (some rows have more than others, as if tracking days rather than years). Total marks: approximately 300. If a character counts and interprets the rows: **DC 12 History or Insight** — thirty rows of marks for thirty years. Someone has been counting. *Relic sign: thirty-year tally. Answers the question "how long have they been here?" without anyone having to ask.*

- **Rope and iron post** (south parapet). A rope of reasonable quality, one end tied to the iron post. The other end disappears over the south parapet. **DC 10 Investigation** or simple looking over: the rope descends to R3 (Collapsed Section), approximately 15 ft. The rope is Torva's. She will not say so until she shows the party the shortcut (after peaceful intent demonstrated). If the party uses the rope without Torva's introduction, she watches and says nothing, but her posture shifts. *Conditional shortcut: the rope is there; Torva's offering to show it is the social reward for peaceful engagement.*

- **Torva's position** (when she retreats here). If Torva moved to the wall-walk during the R2 encounter, she is watching from the north parapet when the party ascends. She has the high ground. She is not attacking. She is watching to see if the party follows her. # Velantha's Field Satchel

*A worn leather bag, latched with a tarnished brass clasp, that smells of dried herbs from thirty years ago as if they were cut this morning.*

## Appearance

The satchel is old work — tanned hide, double-stitched at the stress points, the leather cracked at the fold lines but still supple where it should be supple. Three compartments: a main body for cloth and bandages, a flat pocket for documents, and a narrow inner sleeve whose lining has been replaced twice (the stitching is different thread from the outer body). The inner sleeve is where the herbs are. They should be dust. They are not. The brass clasp is tarnished green at the edges but closes cleanly. On the underside of the flap, faint and almost gone, the letters **V.S.** are scratched with something sharp — not stamped, not dyed. Scratched, the way a person marks a thing they expect to get back.

The satchel is warm. Not hot, not alarming — warm, like a hand held briefly in another hand. The warmth does not fade in cold rooms. It does not intensify in hot ones. It is simply, persistently, there.

## Presence / Desire

The satchel does not want to be stored. It is patient — thirty years in gully dwarf hands has given it patience — but it has the quality of readiness about it: when picked up by someone in a hurry, it feels lighter than it should; when set down in a corner, it seems somehow closer to the room's center the next time the holder looks. It is drawn to activity, to need, to the immediate present of someone requiring tending.

It is warmest in the hands of people who are afraid and helping anyway. It is cooler — not cold, just less warm — in the hands of someone who has no intention of using it. It does not judge that person. It simply waits for a different holder.

The congregation has noted, over thirty years, that no keeper of the satchel has died of fever. They believe this is the satchel's doing. They may be right.

## Provenance

**1. An unnamed leatherworker in Mireth-on-the-Hill (made ~50 years before campaign; sold at market).**
Ordinary craft. Good hide, double-stitched. The leatherworker made three that season; this was the second. She sold it to a healer woman for six copper. She did not remember which healer, afterward. The satchel was not remarkable then.

**2. Sister Velantha (carried ~20 years; died at the ritual site ~30 years ago).**
Velantha replaced the inner sleeve lining twice. She marked it **V.S.** the second year she had it, after losing it in a crowd at a market and finding it again three stalls down. She carried every healing mission she undertook for two decades with this bag on her shoulder. The night she performed the ritual — her last night — she set it down inside the gatehouse threshold before she went further in. She did not take it into the ritual space. She left it where someone would find it. The herbs were dry and ordinary when she set it down.

**3. Dova, First-Keeper of the congregation (kept ~5 years; died ~24 years before campaign).**
Dova was the gully dwarf who came to the site first, alone, a year after Velantha died. She gathered what was left: the satchel, the measuring cup, the other tools the congregation would eventually assemble. She kept the satchel at her side and prayed over it each morning — not with words, since she had no formal theology; with attention, the way she paid attention to small things she did not want to lose. The warmth began under Dova's keeping. The herbs stopped decaying under Dova's keeping. She died of old age in the nave, surrounded by the congregation she had formed, with the satchel under her hand.

**4. Seven keepers since Dova, up to the present day.**
The warmth has not faded. The herbs are thirty years old and smell like this morning. Currently held by **Mig** — the most recently appointed keeper, who carried it out of the gatehouse three days ago when her group arrived, because she wanted to leave an offering at the Nave shrine and was not sure which of the objects in the outer stash was most appropriate. She chose the one that felt warm.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity
Uncommon. **Attunement required** by a creature that has tended to another creature's wounds at least once (no restriction on class, race, or alignment — the attunement condition is an act, not an identity).

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Preserved herbs.** Dried herbs placed in the satchel's inner sleeve compartment retain full potency indefinitely. When the attuned creature uses an Herbalism Kit in conjunction with these herbs, they add 1d4 hit points to any hit points restored.
- **Healer's touch.** Once per long rest, as a bonus action, the attuned creature can stabilize a dying creature within reach. No Medicine check required. The dying creature is automatically stable.
- **Practiced hand.** The attuned creature has advantage on Medicine checks.

### Passive benefit (no attunement required)

- **Preserved herbs** are accessible to any creature using the satchel's herbs, attuned or not. The 1d4 bonus applies to anyone.

### Relic Test

The field satchel is not cursed. It has a test.

If the attuned creature uses the satchel or its contents to deliver a harmful substance to another creature — poison, a damaging preparation, anything intended to injure rather than heal — the attunement ends immediately. The satchel cannot be re-attuned by that creature. The herbs in the inner sleeve decay to dust over the following 24 hours, releasing their thirty-year-old fragrance for the last time. The warmth fades. The satchel becomes ordinary leather with a brass clasp and faint initials that mean nothing anymore.

The DM adjudicates what constitutes "harmful use." The test is not about accident or ignorance. It is about intent. A healer who applies an herb incorrectly and harms a patient does not fail the test. A creature who deliberately delivers poison through the satchel does.

*Workshop note:* This is not mechanical compulsion — the satchel does not prevent the action. It responds after. The congregation would consider this a form of Mishakal's observation, though Velantha herself never framed it that way.

### Ending the Relic Test consequence

The test consequence (decay and ordinariness) is permanent for the individual who triggered it. Another creature can attune to the satchel — but the herbs will not grow back. Whatever was in the sleeve when it decayed is gone. The warmth, however, may return for a new keeper. The congregation believes it will. The DM decides if they are right, based on the campaign's route and the new keeper's intent.

## Hooks

1. **The congregation wants it back.** Mig was appointed Keeper. Whatever the party does with the satchel, Mig knows it exists and where it went. If the party recovers the satchel without her cooperation, the inner congregation will know by the next morning. This is a relationship, not a retrieval quest.

2. **The Orders would recognize it.** Aldric Voss, in the Sacristy (0019), will identify the **V.S.** initials immediately and the inner sleeve lining (he replaced it the second time). He will say: *"She left that at the threshold. She told me once she always left her bag where she could find it when she came back."* He does not say she did not come back. He does not need to.

3. **The preserved herbs are evidence.** A wizard examining the herbs will determine that they have been preserved by faith-generated magic — not a High Sorcery working, not a divine miracle, but something that accumulates from repeated acts of devotion over years. The nature of that magic raises questions. The Orders would be interested. Whether the party tells them is a choice.

## GM Notes

The satchel is the campaign's first physical evidence. Handle it with the weight that deserves.

When the party first picks it up, they feel the warmth. Do not explain the warmth. Let them ask. The answer is thirty years of gully dwarves carrying it because a healer left it behind for someone to find. That answer lands harder if it is discovered than if it is told.

The **V.S.** initials should not be immediately legible — the scratching is faint. A player who examines the satchel closely (Investigation DC 12) finds the initials. A player who then asks the congregation what V.S. stands for has just opened the door to the campaign's core mystery at their own initiative. Let that happen. Do not prompt it.

Do not attach mechanical significance to the warmth beyond what is listed. Its meaning is narrative. The warmth is thirty years of faith. It does not need to do anything else.

*Non-combatant. See `npcs/mig.md`. She does not fight. If combat begins, she sets the satchel down, picks it back up, and waits. She will not leave unless the party explicitly and aggressively threatens her directly. She holds her ground because the satchel is here and she has not delivered it to the Nave shrine yet.*

## Trigger conditions

Combat is triggered if the party:
- Advances within 5 ft of Mig or the shrines without having exchanged any communication
- Reaches for the satchel without asking
- Draws weapons on entering R2
- Continues advancing after Grisk's warning sound

Combat is **not** triggered if the party:
- Stops moving and waits
- Says anything (words or gestures) that indicates "we are not here to drive you out"
- Asks a question
- Shows empty hands

## Social resolution

**The encounter resolves socially when:** Mig understands the party is not the same as the workers who fled. Her threshold is low — she has seen workers flee; she has not seen someone stop and ask. Any party action that constitutes "asking" (in any form) de-escalates.

**First meaningful exchange:** The party says or does something that is not an advance. Mig tilts her head — a question. If the party asks what she is doing (in any words): she holds up the satchel slightly and points toward R5 (the direction of the inner cathedral and the Nave shrine). If the party does not understand: she mimes putting the satchel down on the floor and picking it back up — *I am bringing this somewhere. That is all.*

**Social check (optional):** DC 12 Persuasion or Insight to understand what Mig wants specifically enough to help her reach R6. On success: the party understands she wants to reach the Nave shrine. On failure: they know she wants to go further in, but not why.

**Grisk de-escalation:** Grisk does not de-escalate through Persuasion. He de-escalates when Mig de-escalates — he follows her lead. If Mig relaxes, Grisk takes two steps back. That is the signal.

## Route implications

- **Route A:** Combat resolved (Grisk and Torva subdued or fled), satchel recovered without Mig's cooperation. Congregation alive but hostile. Inner cathedral access will be contested.
- **Route C:** Social resolution; party has the satchel by exchange or agreement but did not commit to helping Mig through R6. Congregation neutral.
- **Route D:** Social resolution; party agrees to help Mig reach R6. Congregation cooperative. Mig introduces the party to the inner congregation at the threshold.

## Post-encounter: congregation behavior on party return

If social (any route): The congregation will be in different positions when the party returns — evidence that they have continued their practice. Grisk will be on the wall-walk instead of R3.

If combat occurred: The congregation has withdrawn deeper into the cathedral. The gatehouse is clear. The inner door is barred from both sides (the unnamed congregation members barred it from R5 before fleeing). Route D is not available.

### Ghoul In Crawlspace

# Encounter — Ghoul in the Crawlspace

## Type
Combat encounter (triggered). A Ghoul nested in the crawlspace beneath R2 and R3. It pre-dates the congregation's arrival. The congregation is afraid of it. This is why the grate is always closed and why Mig sits near it rather than away from it — she keeps watch.

## Setup

The Ghoul entered through the collapsed outer wall (R3) before the congregation established themselves here, and found the crawlspace beneath the guardroom a useful lair. The congregation has been coexisting with it in fear for weeks. They have not been able to drive it out. It has not attacked them unprovoked — it seems to sense that they are persistent, and it waits.

The Ghoul stirs when the grate is disturbed. On the round the grate is opened, roll initiative. The Ghoul emerges into whichever room opened the grate on its first turn.

**Congregation behavior during encounter:** They are already afraid of what is in the crawlspace. When the grate opens, the congregation scrambles to the far wall immediately — even before the Ghoul emerges. Mig picks up the satchel first. If the party kills the Ghoul, the congregation's regard for them increases substantially: they have solved the problem the congregation feared most.

**If the party defeats the Ghoul:** The crawlspace is now accessible. The congregation visibly relaxes. Mig offers the party food (dried rations from their supplies) — the first time she has offered anything voluntarily. If the social encounter has not yet resolved, defeating the Ghoul first gives advantage on any subsequent social check with Mig.

## Combatant

### Ghoul
*Undead, drawn to the incomplete sacred space before the congregation arrived. HP 22. AC 12. Speed 30 ft.*
- **Immunities:** poison; charmed, exhaustion, poisoned. **Darkvision** 60 ft.
- **Bite** +2 to hit, 2d6+2 piercing.
- **Claws** +4 to hit, 2d4+2 slashing; non-elf humanoid target: DC 10 Con save or **paralyzed** 1 minute (save repeats at end of each turn).

**Paralyzed condition (inline):** Incapacitated; can't move or speak; automatically fails Str/Dex saves; attack rolls against it have advantage; any attack that hits from within 5 ft is a critical hit.

## Tactical notes

The Ghoul will try to paralyze the most heavily armored PC first (Dren Holt) to remove the front-liner. If paralyzed, Claws become auto-crits from adjacent attackers — this is genuinely dangerous for a level 3 party. The DM should not pull punches: the Ghoul is the dungeon's only real threat and should play as one.

**Retreat:** The Ghoul retreats into the crawlspace (dropping prone to squeeze through the grate) if reduced to 10 HP or fewer. The crawlspace is its home; it will defend the entrance from inside.

## XP

200 XP. With the rat swarm (50 XP) and the Gatehouse Defense social/conditional encounter (50–100 XP), total adventure XP is approximately 300–350 — Easy threshold for a party of 4 at level 3. Appropriate for a campaign opener.

### Rat Squatters

# Encounter — Rat Squatters

## Type
Combat encounter (triggered). Rats nested in the crawlspace beneath R2 and R3, nearer the R3 side. The Ghoul (see `encounters/ghoul-in-crawlspace.md`) nests nearer the R2 side; if the grate is opened from R2, the Ghoul emerges first. The rat swarm triggers if the party enters the crawlspace from R3's side, or if the Ghoul has already been defeated and the grate is opened.

## Setup

The rats have been here longer than the congregation. They entered through the collapsed outer wall in R3 and found the crawlspace under the guardroom floor useful for nesting. The congregation has been coexisting with them — they do not like the rats, but they have not been able to clear them, and the rats have not been actively attacking.

The encounter triggers when the iron floor grate (in R2 or R3) is disturbed.

## Combatants

### Swarm of Rats
*Use SRD "Swarm of Rats" stat block.*
- **HP:** 24 · **AC:** 10 · **Speed:** 30 ft
- **Attack:** Bites +2, 2d6 piercing (or 1d6 if below half HP)
- **Condition immunities:** charmed, frightened, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, prone, restrained, stunned
- **Swarm:** Can occupy another creature's space; can move through tiny gaps

## Trigger

The grate is lifted, or a creature squeezes into the crawlspace. The rats surge up through the grate opening immediately. On initiative, the swarm enters whichever room the grate is in (R2 or R3) and targets the nearest creature.

## Duration

The swarm is driven off (retreats into crawlspace) when reduced to 12 HP or fewer. It does not leave the crawlspace again this session unless the grate is disturbed a second time (it will not reinitiate; it only defends its lair).

## Congregation behavior during encounter

The congregation in R2 reacts to the rats with unhappy familiarity rather than panic. They climb onto the bench frames (which are sturdy enough) and wait. Grisk pulls his feet up. Mig picks up the satchel before doing anything else. The unnamed congregation members press against the walls.

This is not a helpful reaction, but it is a realistic one. The congregation is not going to help fight the rats. If the party defeats the rats, the congregation's regard for the party increases — they have solved a problem the congregation couldn't solve.

**Faction effect:** Defeating the rat swarm without making the congregation feel targeted counts as a goodwill gesture. If the social encounter in R2 has not yet resolved, defeating the rats first gives the party advantage on any subsequent social check with Mig (she saw them solve a problem that has been annoying her for weeks).

## Post-encounter

The crawlspace is now accessible without triggering the rats again. It connects R2 and R3 (see map). A Medium creature squeezes through (disadvantage on attack rolls while in the crawl). The crawl is approximately 20 ft long.

**Optional loot:** In the crawlspace, alongside the rat nest: a construction tool pouch left by a worker who must have dropped it through the grate. Contains: 2 cold chisels, a wax marker, and 3 gp in mixed coin. *Incidental item purpose: evidence of normal construction activity before the site turned.*

## Wandering Pressure

# Wandering Pressure — The Gatehouse Watch

*Roll Cold Pulse (d6) every 10 minutes of in-game exploration. Roll Wandering Encounter (d6) every 30 minutes.*

*Justification for temporal pressure in a non-sealed setting: the gatehouse is an unstable structure with an active congregation whose behavior changes on awareness of the party. Time pressure comes from structural hazard, congregation movement, and the possibility that word will reach the inner congregation before the party is ready.*

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
