# Room 06 — Inner Door

## Read-aloud

> The door at the end of the passage is oak, iron-fitted, old enough to have settled into its frame. Two things hold it closed: iron bolts set into the frame above and below — mechanical, part of the original construction — and a wooden bar laid across the brackets from this side. A bar the construction crew did not put there. Beyond the door, through a gap at the threshold: the herb smell, strong enough now to be unmistakable.

## Features

- **Frame bolt mechanism** (door frame, top and bottom). Iron bolts that slide from the frame into the door edge — original security design. Connected by cable to the portcullis winch in R1. When the portcullis is lowered (R1 winch), these bolts **retract**, unlocking this mechanism. *The portcullis and this door were designed to be controlled together: lower the gate, and the inner door can be opened. The construction crew would have used this to manage pilgrim flow.* **DC 13 Investigation** from R6 to understand the cable mechanism; the party may also figure it out from R1's winch inspection (DC 11 from that end).

- **Wooden bar** (brackets, this side). A roughly-cut construction plank laid across two iron brackets. Not the original design. **DC 11 Investigation** — the brackets were always there; the bar was added recently, within the past few weeks. The congregants can confirm: they added it to keep things from the inner cathedral from entering the gatehouse without their knowledge. *The bar's purpose is protective, not hostile. The congregation is also keeping something out.*

- **The threshold gap** (door base). Approximately 1 inch between door base and stone floor. The herb smell originates from here — concentrated, pushing through the gap from the Nave side. **DC 10 Perception** to detect the smell as originating here specifically. A torch held near the gap shows the direction of air flow: from the Nave toward the gatehouse. The inner cathedral is slightly warmer.

- **Field satchel warmth** (if carried). If the party has the satchel, it is warmest here — noticeably, unmistakably warmer than in R2 or R5. The relic is drawn toward the Nave shrine. **No roll required.** Anyone carrying the satchel feels this. *The satchel and the Nave shrine are in proximity for the first time in thirty years.*

## Encounter

None. The decision to open or not open the door is the adventure's primary choice point. No creature pressures the party here.

## Treasure

The door itself is not treasure. What is accessible through it is the whole campaign.

**Reaction moment:** When both mechanisms are cleared and the door is opened for the first time, the herb smell intensifies — a wave of fragrance from the Nave, where the congregation has placed offerings. The wave is brief; it settles. Then: the sound of the inner congregation is audible for the first time from within the gatehouse, not muffled by stone. Distant, rhythmic, low. They are performing a rite.

The party has opened the connection between the gatehouse and the inner cathedral. The inner congregation will know the door has been opened. This is not bad or good; it is consequential.

## Connections

- **East:** Nave (adventure 0016) — **campaign lock; door can be opened during 0015 (see below) but the Nave section is not keyed until 0016**

## Opening conditions

The inner door requires **both** locks cleared:
1. **Frame bolts:** Lower the portcullis from R1 (R1 winch). This retracts the bolts. The party can return to R1 to do this, or Mig can show them a workaround — a manual release pin inside the door frame bracket (**DC 15 Investigation** to find without guidance; Mig points to it if she is with the party and they ask how to open the door).
2. **Wooden bar:** Lifted from the brackets from this side. Requires no roll. Takes one action.

**If the party opens the door during this session:** The adventure ends with the door open. The party has made first contact with the inner cathedral. Roll Wandering Pressure immediately: the inner congregation sends one representative — a gully dwarf from the Nave congregation — to the threshold to see who opened the door. This is not combat. It is introduction.

**If the party does not open the door:** The adventure ends with the door closed. The party knows the door exists and how to open it. Adventure 0016 begins with the party returning and choosing to go through.

## GM Notes

This room is the session's decision point and its most honest moment. The party has spent the session learning what is in the gatehouse. Now they are at the threshold of something larger. 

**Route A parties** will not open the door here — they will note it, understand the mechanism, and report to Doran. The door information is the primary deliverable of a Route A playthrough.

**Route C parties** may try to open the door without Mig — possible, but the inner congregation will send a representative who has not been prepared for this party by Mig's introduction. The encounter in 0016 will be colder.

**Route D parties** — particularly if Mig is with them — will open the door with Mig present. Mig steps through. She has waited three weeks to do this. The DM does not narrate her reaction extensively. One sentence: *"She steps through, and does not look back."*

**The satchel's warmth here is the session's payoff for players who tracked the relic sign.** They noticed the warmth in R2. They noticed it intensify in R5. Here, it is the strongest it has been. Something in the Nave is drawing it. This is evidence, not yet explanation. Let it accumulate.

**Dren Holt at the threshold:** He will check the bar, check the mechanism, note the smell. His Playstyle Heuristic 1: secure the position before engaging. He has not yet processed the crack that activated in R2. He is still doing the work. Let him do the work. The processing comes in 0017.
