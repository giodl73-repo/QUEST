# Room 01 — Gate Passage

## Read-aloud

> The gate stands open. The portcullis is raised; the mechanism chain shows no rust. The hinges move without sound. The passage smells of tallow and something drier — dried herbs, though there are no herbs here. A low stone arch on the south wall is half in shadow. Beyond: candlelight, and the sound of low voices.

*(56 words)*

## Features

- **Portcullis winch** (east wall, mounted). Iron handle, functional. Operates the portcullis (west gate) AND releases the door frame bolts on the inner door in R6 — the two mechanisms were designed as one system. Lowering the portcullis closes the external gate but **releases R6's mechanical lock**, allowing the door to be opened from R5's side with only the congregation's bar to clear. **DC 11 Investigation** to notice the connecting mechanism cable running along the ceiling toward the interior. **DC 13 History or Arcana** to understand that this is a standard double-gate security design (lock one end open, the other end locked).
- **Oiled hinges** (gate arch). The oil is fresh — three days at most. Functional observation, not mechanically significant; confirms someone has been maintaining this space.
- **Low arch** (south wall). 4 ft clearance. Requires crouching. **DC 12 Perception** to notice it from the passage. The arch leads to R3 (Collapsed Section). At the arch entrance: a draft and the smell of night air and cold mortar, distinct from the tallow smell of R2.
- **Child-sized handprint** (gate arch stone, inner face). Tallow, pressed deliberately. **DC 13 Perception** to notice. No mechanical significance; it is a child's mark on the threshold of a place they were told matters.

## Encounter

None. Unless the party is fleeing R2 with the congregation in pursuit — in which case Grisk may attempt to use the portcullis winch to seal the external gate and trap the party inside.

## Treasure

None.

## GM Notes

This room is a threshold and a tool. Its job is to deliver three things immediately: (1) the party knows someone is maintaining this space, (2) the party hears and smells the congregation before they see them, (3) the party has access to the portcullis mechanism before they understand what it does.

**The portcullis mechanism is a Route A tool.** A party attempting Route A — clear the site, restore construction — will eventually use the winch to lower the portcullis (sealing the external gate) and lock the congregation inside while Doran's people finish the job. Make sure the party notices the mechanism before they reach R6. A party that lowers the portcullis without realizing it releases R6 has created a situation — the inner door can now be opened, which the congregation will notice.

**Relic sign present:** The olfactory sign — herbs where there are no herbs — is detectable here (passive Perception 11; or DC 11 if the party is actively searching). The smell is stronger in R2 and strongest in R5.

**Dren Holt spotlight note:** Dren checks this room automatically (his Playstyle Heuristic: checks every room before the party enters). He will note the oiled hinges. He will note the portcullis mechanism. He will position himself at the arch threshold before the party moves forward. Play the thoroughness; it is who he is.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the arch)*
*A woman's hands, checking the gate latch quickly before moving through. The same gesture she made at the camp entrance, at the tavern door, at every threshold she was likely to leave from and might not return to. The habit of someone who checks, just in case.*
