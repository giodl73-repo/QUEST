# Room 05 — Waiting Chapel

## Read-aloud

> A room that was becoming a chapel and stopped. The floor stone is unpolished. The bench frames are wood without planks. On the east wall, a carving of Mishakal's symbol — the blue infinity, Krynn's sign of healing — half-finished; the chisel marks end mid-stroke. The congregation has been using this room: four small shrine arrangements on the floor. A child-sized handprint pressed in tallow on the northeast wall. The smell of herbs is strongest here, and there are no herbs anywhere.

## Features

- **Half-carved Mishakal symbol** (east wall). The blue infinity symbol, standard mark of Mishakal's faith. The carving is precise for the first third — a craftsperson knew what they were doing. It stops abruptly, mid-stroke; the chisel line trails off. **DC 11 Religion** — this is Mishakal's mark; the cathedral is being built for her. **DC 13 History** — the chisel marks stopped suddenly; this is not a planned pause, it is the moment the workers fled. *The half-finished symbol is the structural image of the whole campaign: something sacred, stopped before it was complete.*

- **Four shrine arrangements** (floor, spread). Same construction as R2's shrines but more carefully made. Stones are smoother (chosen, not grabbed). Cloth is folded before knotting. One arrangement includes a small fragment of dried flower — not the satchel's herbs, something brought from outside. **DC 12 Religion** — these arrangements are more developed than the ones in R2; this room has been used longer. The congregation has been working toward a form they haven't arrived at yet.

- **Child-sized handprint** (northeast wall, tallow). At knee height for an adult. Pressed deliberately — the size and shape suggest the child did this intentionally rather than accidentally. The tallow is slightly damp: recent (within the week). *Relic sign: maintenance sign (someone cares for this space); also establishes that the congregation includes children, which has implications for Route A.* **No mechanical significance. Do not assign a roll.**

- **Iron ring** (north wall). A construction-era mounting ring, intended for hanging a door or a curtain that was never installed. The congregation has hung a small bundle of cloth scraps from it — offerings, tied loosely. If taken down: the cloth scraps are cut from multiple different pieces of fabric, none matching each other, from at least six different garments. **DC 13 Investigation** — each scrap is from a different person; this is a collective offering, each person contributing something of their own.

- **The herb smell** (throughout). This room has the strongest concentration of the olfactory relic sign. The smell is coming from R6 — from the Nave beyond the inner door, where the congregation has placed offerings near the Nave shrine. The party can now determine directionally where the smell originates. **DC 11 Perception** from this room locates the source: through the inner door.

## Encounter

None. This is the Stuart room — purely atmospheric. No creature will attack the party here unless the party has made the whole congregation hostile. If Mig is with the party (Route D), she pauses here before approaching R6 and touches one of the shrine arrangements lightly before continuing. She does not explain. The DM does not explain.

## Treasure

The cloth-bundle offering (if taken from the iron ring): worthless in gp terms; meaningful in context. The party will understand what it is later (when they understand who brought it and why). For now: incidental. **Job:** evidence of a collective practice.

## GM Notes

This is the campaign's thematic image made physical: something sacred, stopped mid-completion, now being tended by people who have no formal tradition but are trying anyway. Every element in this room reinforces the campaign's central question without stating it.

**Bard documentation-suspension fires here.** Calla Fosse's oral history practice depends on categories — she can hold and recite a tradition if she knows what tradition it is. These shrine arrangements are not a tradition she has encountered. She can observe them. She can describe them. She cannot categorize them, which means she cannot hold them in the same way she holds everything else. Play the stillness. She observes. She does not speak. If another party member asks what these are, she says: "I don't know." This is remarkable — she always knows what things are. The DM notes this in the session log as a documentation-suspension beat.

**The half-carved symbol is the Dren Holt mirror.** The symbol was stopped mid-stroke by people who fled. The congregation came back to tend the space anyway. This is the other side of Dren's crack: people who kept showing up even when the structure that was supposed to support them stopped. The DM does not make this connection explicit. Let it sit.

**If Mig is present:** She pauses at the shrine and touches one arrangement. The specific touch (one stone, briefly) is a private rite. She continues. Do not interpret it to the party.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers near the half-carved symbol)*
*The chisel was still in the stone when the workers left. No one picked it up on the way out. The congregation found it three months later, tried to understand what it was for, and eventually moved it to the corner where it wouldn't be stepped on. No one knew how to use it. The incomplete symbol stayed incomplete.*
