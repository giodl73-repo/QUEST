# Room 03 — Collapsed Section

## Read-aloud

> A section of the outer wall has given way — not recently; the rubble has settled and been climbed over enough times to pack smooth. The gap is large enough to step through sideways. Beyond it: the hill, the road below, the town. Inside: cold air and mortar dust, a low arch connecting somewhere to the north, a gully dwarf crouched in the rubble who was watching the road and is now watching you.

> A section of outer wall, long-collapsed, rubble worn smooth from use. The gap is step-through-sized. Beyond: the hill and road below. Inside: cold air, mortar dust, a low arch north, and a gully dwarf in the rubble who was watching the road and is now watching you.

*(50 words)*

## Features

- **Collapsed wall gap** (south). The congregation's primary entrance. The rubble is smooth and packed — this gap has been used regularly for weeks to months. Anyone approaching the cathedral from the south road can enter here without passing through R1. *This is the flanking route to R2; the party can use it to approach the congregation from an unexpected direction.*

- **Low arch** (north wall). Connects to R1 (Gate Passage). 4 ft clearance; requires crouching. **DC 10 Perception** from R3 to find the arch (it is visible and unobstructed; the check is lower from this side). The arch is the reason R3 and R1 are linked — this was the guardroom's secondary internal access, a standard design for gate security.

- **Iron floor grate** (east wall, floor level). Same grate system visible from R2. Can be opened from this side as well — **DC 12 Strength**. The crawlspace is narrow; Medium creatures squeeze (disadvantage on attack rolls). Rat swarm lair. *The congregation uses this side of the grate to exit toward the collapsed wall without going through R2 — a practical shortcut that the party can also use.*

- **Rubble pile** (west wall). Debris from the original collapse. **DC 13 Investigation** to search usefully; yields: a small carved stone in the shape of a cup (same as the one in R2 if the party finds both — a second devotional object). Also: a fragment of a construction work-order in Abanasinian Common; the work-order is dated eleven years ago, references "the gatehouse threshold stones" and is signed by a foreman whose name is now largely illegible. *Not a key; evidence of the original commission.*

- **Grisk** (starting position). He is here when the session opens, watching the road through the collapsed wall gap. He hears the party enter R1 and moves to R2 to warn Torva. If the party enters through the collapsed wall *before* entering R1, they catch Grisk before he can warn anyone. This is the flanking approach's tactical advantage. See `npcs/grisk.md`.

## Encounter

**Grisk (starting position):** A brief social encounter when Grisk realizes the party has entered via the collapse. He is startled but not immediately hostile. His first response is to put himself between the party and the arch to R1 (to block access to the guardroom). If the party advances without speaking, he calls out — not words, just a warning sound. *This is the version of the encounter that rewards the flanking approach with a one-on-one interaction rather than a room full of gully dwarves.* See `encounters/gatehouse-defense.md`.

## Treasure

None directly. The rubble pile search yields the devotional carving and construction work-order fragment (see Features).

## Connections

- **North:** R1 (Gate Passage) — low arch, 4 ft clearance, **DC 10 Perception** to notice from this side
- **East (floor):** R2 (Guardroom) — iron floor grate, crawlspace, narrow
- **South:** External (collapsed wall gap, open)
- **Up (parapet):** R4 (Wall-Walk) — **conditional**; rope ladder accessible only after Torva shows it (or DC 15 Athletics to find and descend safely from R4 side)

## GM Notes

This room rewards players who approach from the outside rather than through the front gate. The tactical payoff is catching Grisk alone rather than in a room full of gully dwarves. The informational payoff is the work-order fragment: eleven years ago, this was a normal construction project. The congregation arrived later.

**The flanking route and Route D:** A party that enters here first is more likely to have a one-on-one encounter with Grisk, which is more likely to go socially rather than tactically. A party that enters through R1 first sees the whole congregation simultaneously, which is more likely to trigger a defensive response. Design note: the flanking route gently favors Route D — not deterministically, but by creating conditions where the first contact is more manageable.

**Grisk's information:** If Grisk is engaged socially (rather than in combat), he will not explain anything. He will gesture toward R2 and toward the road alternately — *"go back or come forward, but don't stand here."* His arc-completion is in R2 or R4 (when asked *why* he is here, not what he is). Here, he just wants to know which direction the party is going.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the collapsed gap)*
