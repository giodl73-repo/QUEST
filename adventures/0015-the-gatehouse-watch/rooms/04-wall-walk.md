# Room 04 — Wall-Walk

## Read-aloud

> Stone parapet, open to the sky. Below: the two rooms you came from; the road; the hill. Above: the unfinished cathedral, close enough now to see the individual stones of the unbuilt wall, the scaffolding, the open spaces where the roof should be. The cathedral is enormous. You had not understood, from below, how enormous. On the south parapet: a rope coiled around an iron post. On the east parapet face: a stone with marks scratched into it.

## Features

- **View of the cathedral** (parapet, all sides). From here the party can see the full scope of the Halted Spire for the first time: the Nave's open roof, the unfinished Bell Tower in the distance, the scaffolding reaching up. **DC 13 Investigation** from this vantage: the party can identify at least two more entry points into the cathedral body visible from here — a door in the Nave's west wall (unfinished, covered with construction canvas) and a high opening in the Bell Tower. *This seeds awareness of the larger cathedral geography before dungeon-smith designs subsequent adventures.*

- **Tally stone** (east parapet face). A flat stone, mounted level with the parapet top, with marks scratched into it in rows. **DC 10 Perception** to notice. Counting the marks: thirty rows of marks; the marks in each row are not uniform (some rows have more than others, as if tracking days rather than years). Total marks: approximately 300. If a character counts and interprets the rows: **DC 12 History or Insight** — thirty rows of marks for thirty years. Someone has been counting. *Relic sign: thirty-year tally. Answers the question "how long have they been here?" without anyone having to ask.*

- **Rope and iron post** (south parapet). A rope of reasonable quality, one end tied to the iron post. The other end disappears over the south parapet. **DC 10 Investigation** or simple looking over: the rope descends to R3 (Collapsed Section), approximately 15 ft. The rope is Torva's. She will not say so until she shows the party the shortcut (after peaceful intent demonstrated). If the party uses the rope without Torva's introduction, she watches and says nothing, but her posture shifts. *Conditional shortcut: the rope is there; Torva's offering to show it is the social reward for peaceful engagement.*

- **Torva's position** (when she retreats here). If Torva moved to the wall-walk during the R2 encounter, she is watching from the north parapet when the party ascends. She has the high ground. She is not attacking. She is watching to see if the party follows her. If they do, she retreats to the south parapet and puts the rope between her and them. *The rope-as-barrier becomes the rope-as-gift if the encounter goes well.*

## Encounter

Torva (if she retreated here). The wall-walk encounter is social: Torva at the south parapet, party ascending from R2. She will not attack first. If the party asks why she is here (not what she is, but why — the specific question that triggers Grisk's arc-completion also works for Torva): she says, in broken Common: "She came for us. We come back." If the party has already gotten this from Grisk in R3, Torva's version is slightly different: *"She came. So we came."* She sits down after saying it, which is how you know the encounter is over.

*This is a conditional encounter: only happens if Torva retreated from R2. If the party handled R2 peacefully and Torva stayed in R2, this room contains only the features and the view.*

## Treasure

None directly. The tally stone is not removable (it is mounted in the parapet face).

## Connections

- **Down (stairs):** R2 (Guardroom) — stone stairs, always accessible
- **Down (south parapet, rope):** R3 (Collapsed Section) — **conditional shortcut**: rope accessible always (Perception DC 10); using it safely requires Torva's guidance OR DC 13 Athletics (15 ft descent, not difficult but the rope is old)

## GM Notes

This room does two things: it shows the party how large the problem is (the full cathedral), and it resolves the Torva encounter if she came here.

**The cathedral view is the session's horizon-broadening moment.** The party has been dealing with a room full of gully dwarves. From here, they see that the gatehouse is a threshold — there are five more sections beyond this, each larger. The session does not require them to process this. They should just see it.

**Dren Holt on the wall-walk:** He will scan the cathedral for exits, entry points, and threat vectors — Playstyle Heuristic 1 (secure the position before engaging). Play this. He counts the entry points he can see. He notes the Bell Tower gap. He does not say this aloud unless someone asks.

**The tally stone is the most important feature in this room.** If a player counts the marks and understands them, they have just learned that someone has been here for thirty years without being told. This is the cleanest form of information delivery: the player does the work and arrives at the answer themselves. Do not read the count aloud unless a player asks. Wait for the ask.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers at the tally stone)*
