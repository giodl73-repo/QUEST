# Room 03 — Outside the Walls

## Read-aloud

> Open ground. The keep is at your back. The cold is different out here — no stone to hold the heat, no walls to break the wind. The tree line is three hundred feet north. Somewhere between here and there, a patrol is moving. You cannot see them yet.

## Features

- **50 feet of open ground:** No cover between the cliff-gap egress and the tree line. The party must cross this in the dark. **DC 12 Stealth (group check, advantage due to pre-dawn dark):** If the group check fails, the patrol in Room 04 is alerted (they are at disadvantage on their attack rolls for the first round of combat because they expected garrison soldiers, not infiltrators moving fast). **If the group check succeeds:** The party reaches the tree line unseen.

- **What the cold does:** The party is in pre-dawn Kharolis foothills without shelter or fire. No mechanical effect for one scene, but: at the start of Room 04, each PC has used one hit die of warmth from their overnight rest. This is flavor, not mechanics — but it makes the scene feel right.

- **The tree line at 300 feet:** Dense pine, with underbrush. Light enough to see by at this hour — the moon is down but the sky is lightening. DC 12 Perception to see the patrol's movement before it reaches them. DC 14 to count: there are two soldiers (not three as the party might assume from earlier encounters).

- **Luca's tracks:** They lead directly north across the open ground to a point 20 feet east of center in the tree line. He crossed without cover. He either got lucky with the patrol rotation or he waited long enough to know exactly when to go. **DC 12 Survival:** He went fast — the prints are far apart (running stride, not walking). He was confident in his timing. Or he was reckless. Hard to say which from the prints.

- **What the party cannot see:** Luca himself. He is inside the tree line, at the camp perimeter (Room 04), with his brother. He is not in distress — he is exactly where he planned to be. He does not know the party is out here.

## Encounter

If the group Stealth check is failed: the patrol arrives at the tree line's edge and spots the party crossing. This triggers the Room 04 encounter in open ground rather than at the camp perimeter — less tactical cover for the party, same patrol composition.

If the group Stealth check succeeds: no encounter in this room.

## Connections

- South: Room 02 Cliff-Gap Egress (10-ft scramble back up)
- North: Room 04 Reconstitution Camp Perimeter (cross the gap and tree line)

## GM Notes

**The exposure is the point.** The party is outside the walls, depleted, at pre-dawn. This room is the physical embodiment of their vulnerability. The Supply Die at d4 means they have essentially no garrison backing. They are on their own.

**Let the cold land.** A brief description of what pre-dawn foothills feel like — the frost underfoot, the breath visible, the silence — before the party moves north is the right atmospheric beat. Caelith especially: his history notes him as someone who observes conditions before acting. He would note the wind direction (west, carrying their sound away from the picket).

**The group Stealth check:** This is a single check — the party's worst result (not an average). If Caelith is present (AC 12, bulky messenger case), he is the likely weak link. If Davan came through in armor, he is definitely the weak link — but if he stripped armor, his Stealth is not worse than Caelith's. Use the actual party composition to determine the check modifier.

## Memory Fragment

> The pass has been defended for two hundred years because of this gap of ground — fifty feet that cannot be held by either side, that both sides leave empty by convention. The convention has never been written down. Both sides simply understand that the open ground is no one's to hold.
