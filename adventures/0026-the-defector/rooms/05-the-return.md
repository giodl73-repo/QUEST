# Room 05 — The Return

## Read-aloud

> Inside the walls at dawn. The sky is grey-pink beyond the northeast parapet. Luca is over the wall before the light catches him. Behind the keep's eastern face, in the lee of the stone, a small fire. Dene has moved the refugees outside — emergency protocol. Helin sits near the fire.

## Features

- **Luca's return:** He climbs the rope last, with the party's help. He is cold and scratched from moving through underbrush. He is not apologetic. He is the opposite of apologetic — he has the bearing of someone who did a necessary thing and is prepared to account for it.

- **Character-logic condition (no roll):** The party's FIRST response to Luca sets the token condition.
  - **Human (token fires):** Ask about his brother. Say his name. Make space for him to say what he needs to say. Ask "are you all right" as a genuine question.
  - **Tactical (token window closes):** "Did you compromise security." "You violated your watch post." "What did you give them." Any question whose first frame is institutional rather than personal.
  - **Ambiguous:** DM judgment. Caelith's heuristic specifically directs him to ask a non-tactical question first. If Caelith speaks first (per his heuristics), the condition fires on his question.

- **Caelith's question (spotlight, campaign-permanent):** When Luca is over the wall and the party is standing with him in the dawn, Caelith speaks first. His institutional-lens question:

  *"What did you need to know?"*

  This is the question his entire operating mode has been building toward since S1. Not "why did you go." Not "what did you find." What did you need to know — as though the information-gap is the real subject, which is also the real subject, because Caelith understands that institutional rules exist to prevent exactly the kind of knowing that changes what people are capable of deciding.

  **Luca's answer (campaign-permanent):**

  *"Whether Aldren was on the wrong side, or just the other side."*

  He says it simply, like a man who has had the answer ready for three years and is now finally saying it out loud. He does not elaborate. He does not need to.

  **Davan witnesses this exchange.** The accounting speech's architecture comes from it: the distinction between wrong and other. This is the frame Davan will use at the parley.

- **Luca's token delivery:** The PC who spoke to him without a tactical frame — or, if Caelith spoke first, Caelith — receives Luca's token. It is a wooden disc with a bowshot carved on one face: the garrison archer's mark.

- **Helin's Hint 3 delivery:** She is at the fire. She has been watching the party return. When Luca is accounted for and the party is standing near the fire, she speaks to Davan — or to the group, but she looks at Davan.

  **Hint 3 line (campaign-permanent):**

  *"She said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people. She never came. This is her coming back."*

  The subject is Saren Coldforge. If the party does not understand the pronoun, she continues: *"The knight who objected when Asholt sheltered us. She said the Oath didn't cover people who couldn't swear it. She left when Asholt said he'd shelter us on his own authority anyway."* Then: *"I have been thinking for twenty years about whether she was wrong about the Oath or wrong about us. She was wrong about us. I still don't know about the Oath."*

- **Aldren's Hint 4 (via Luca):** After Helin speaks, Luca gives the party Aldren's information if they haven't already overheard it. *"She hasn't asked for permission to finish it. Aldren doesn't understand why."* Then, quieter: *"I think I might."* He does not explain this. The party carries it.

- **Dawn:** By the time the fire has burned down and the conversation is done, the wall-walk is changing watch. The party is inside before the garrison counts them. Or not — DM call based on timing.

## GM Notes

**Give this room time.** The session's emotional weight lands here: Luca's token condition, Caelith's spotlight question, Helin's Hint 3 delivery, and the Hint 4 pre-plant all happen in a single pre-dawn scene. The fire is the center. The people around it are cold and tired and have just done something that will not be undone. Let the scene breathe.

**Caelith's heuristic fires here.** His voice tags include: "pauses at the point in any conversation where someone is about to follow an order they don't agree with." That is exactly what Luca did — he followed his own knowledge rather than his orders. Caelith recognizes this pattern. His question is not hostile; it is the response of a man who understands the difference between institutional disobedience and principled action, and is asking which one this was.

**Davan's witness moment.** He does not speak during the exchange between Caelith and Luca. He is holding Luca's answer: *"Whether Aldren was on the wrong side, or just the other side."* That is the Orel question too. That is the question he has been not asking for five years. He does not name this aloud in S5 — but it has landed. Log it in the handoff: "Davan has the frame."

**Helin to Davan:** She looks at him because she has been watching him since S4. She has decided he is worth the information. This is not an accusation — she is giving him something she has been carrying for twenty years. He does not need to respond. He can simply receive it. That is the correct response.

**Reporting to Asholt:** The party must eventually tell Asholt that Luca was outside the walls. Whether they report immediately or wait is their choice. If they wait until mid-morning, the watch change will have been counted and Luca's absence was noted by the replacement. He will be asked directly. The question is whether the party frames it before or after Asholt hears it from his own garrison chain.

## Memory Fragment

> The refugees' outdoor fire is the first open fire at Thorngate in nineteen days that is not on the wall. It is the wrong fire in the wrong place by every garrison protocol. The smell of it is pine smoke and cold air, and it smells like someone has decided that certain protocols are for a keep under different circumstances than this one.
