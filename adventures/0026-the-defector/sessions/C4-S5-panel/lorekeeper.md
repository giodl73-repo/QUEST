# Lorekeeper — C4-S5

The Reconstitution doctrinal texture obligation is resolved. Caur's line — "we're not here for the pass, we're here because she told us there was a question that needed answering" — is not policy, it is lived experience. That is the right register. Solamnic factions do not have ideological briefings; they have commanders whose authority they trust. Caur trusts Saren's purpose without knowing it. That is authentic.

Helin's testimony is grounded in Solamnic heraldic and ethical tradition: the argument between Saren and Asholt twenty years ago is a real argument within the Order — what the Oath demands vs. who the Oath is for. The lore distinguishes between the Oath's letter (what a knight must do) and its purpose (why a knight does it). Saren defended the letter. Asholt served the purpose. Both were within the Solamnic tradition. Helin names this without knowing the vocabulary; she says "right about the Oath, wrong about the people" and that is the correct formulation.

The cliff-gap as a garrison feature: its existence is consistent with how keeps like Thorngate are built — secondary exits for supply scouts, now overlooked because the garrison has been under siege for three weeks. Talis finding it in S1 (passive 16) and using it in S5 is internally consistent world-building.
