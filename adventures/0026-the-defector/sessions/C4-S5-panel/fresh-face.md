# Fresh Face — C4-S5

Caelith's question — "What did you need to know?" — is immediately accessible. You don't need to know anything about the campaign to understand that this is the question that treats Luca as a person with a comprehensible reason rather than a soldier who broke protocol. A fresh reader understands it.

Luca's answer — "He's all right. He didn't know I was here" — is accessible without context. A brother went over a wall to check if his brother was alive. He came back. The answer he got was: alive, and not knowing I was there. A fresh reader understands the exact weight of "he didn't know I was here" — there is relief in it, and there is also the fact that nothing was said, nothing was changed, the brothers are still on opposite sides of this wall.

Helin's H3 delivery requires a tiny bit of context — "she said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people" requires the fresh reader to understand that Saren left for a reason. The module grounds this: Helin says "Saren Coldforge left Thorngate twenty years ago when Asholt gave shelter to travelers from Lemish." Fresh reader gets the context from the next sentence.

One minor accessibility gap: the cliff-gap was planted in S1. A fresh reader arriving at S5 doesn't know the significance of Talis leading the descent. One line of dialogue from Talis — "I found this on our first day" — would close the gap without slowing the scene.
