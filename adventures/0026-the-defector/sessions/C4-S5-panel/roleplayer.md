# Roleplayer — C4-S5

"What did you need to know?" — Caelith's question to Luca is the best NPC interaction line in C4 so far. It is not the question any of the other PCs would have asked. It is specifically and only Caelith's question — the institutional-analysis voice that looks at an action and identifies the knowledge gap that motivated it. The question treats Luca as someone who had a comprehensible reason, not someone who made an error. That treatment is what fires Luca's token.

Helin's H3 delivery lands cleanly. Her lines are earned — she has been watching Davan for two scenes and she picked the right moment and the right listener. "She said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people" — this is a sentence that does everything: it tells us what Saren left for, what she has been carrying, and what she has not yet been able to say. Campaign-permanent.

Talis's one-line to the older girl: "She's been doing this a long time." This is the moment the panel has been asking for across four sessions. One sentence, no elaboration, no explanation — exactly right for her voice tag. It is also, quietly, recognition from one person who has been doing a long thing to another.

Caur's interview was excellently constructed. The DM had to invent a doctrinal-texture line under pressure; it worked: "a question that needed answering" echoes the campaign's theme without making Caur seem artificially insightful. He does not know the question. He was told to hold. He held.

Score: 77/80
