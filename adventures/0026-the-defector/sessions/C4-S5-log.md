# C4-S5 SESSION LOG — The Defector

*Pre-dawn, Day 24. Luca Bent goes over the wall before first light. Talis uses the cliff-gap she spotted on Day 1 to follow. Caelith asks the one question that gives the accounting speech its frame.*

---

## Scene 01 — Morning, The Sergeant's Fate (Opening)

The sergeant is alive. Sava found him in the cleared hall at first light of Day 19, barely conscious, stabilized by Spare the Dying. Davan had him moved to the garrison's cell — a room with a lock, a cot, and a bucket — not out of cruelty but out of logistics. An unconscious enemy in your hall is an unquantified variable.

He woke on Day 21. His name is Caur. He is thirty-eight years old. He has been in the Reconstitution's eastern division for eleven years. He was seconded to Commander Saren's force six months ago, along with sixty others.

Caelith interviews him — not an interrogation; he calls it an interview because that is what it is. He has his notebook. He asks structured questions. Caur answers some of them.

What Caur says, eventually, to Caelith's third question about why the Reconstitution is here:

*"We're not here for the pass. The pass changed hands before. It'll change hands again. We're here because she told us there was a question that needed answering before it could change hands."*

He does not elaborate on what the question is. He does not know. He was told there was a question. He has been answering it by standing in front of a wall for twenty-four days.

**🎲 Caelith Insight on Caur (is he telling the truth)** — `1d20+2` → rolls=[13] mod=+2 **total=15 (success)**

He is telling the truth. He genuinely does not know the question. He was told to hold. He held.

**Reconstitution doctrinal texture (campaign-permanent, three-flag obligation resolved):**

*"We're not here for the pass. We're here because she told us there was a question that needed answering before it could change hands."*

This is what the Reconstitution soldiers believe about why they are here. Not doctrine in the ideological sense — experiential doctrine: we hold because our commander said there was a reason. The belief is in the commander's purpose, not in an abstract principle.

<!-- SURPRISE: Caur's articulation of "a question that needed answering" — this is the Reconstitution's unknowing echo of everything the party has been accumulating. They are holding a siege for a question their commander has not named to them. -->

Caelith notes this in his notebook. He underlines "a question that needed answering." He looks at the line. He closes the book.

He also decides, in this moment, that the contractor drop point notation three pages back is relevant to what Caur just told him. The Reconstitution contracted the saboteur. Wil Fen was tasked with slowing the garrison's supply runs. The contractor was operating in service of someone who needed *time* — not a tactical advantage, but duration. Time for a question to be answered.

**Caelith shares the contractor drop point with the party.**

He does it in the corridor outside the cell, after the interview. He shows Davan and Sava the notation directly — the address, the schedule, the cipher mark that Wil Fen had been using. He says: *"The contractor isn't a supply-line threat. They're a timeline threat. Someone needed this siege to last long enough for something to happen."*

He does not say what. He does not know what. But the institutional pattern is visible and he names it.

**Caelith's notebook: contractor drop point shared.** The three-session carry is resolved.

## Scene 02 — Day 24, Pre-Dawn: Luca's Empty Post

The garrison watches run in two-hour shifts. Talis takes second watch with Luca by rotation. At the watch change — the coldest hour, when exhaustion sits deepest — she relieves the previous watchman and scans the northeast parapet.

Luca's post is empty. His bow is on the wall. His quiver is gone.

**🎲 Talis Perception (reading the departure)** — `1d20+6` → passive/active combined: she scans the parapet immediately. **Passive 16 activates for the cliff-gap: she sees the rope anchor at the northeast corner, exactly where she noted it on Day 1.**

She stands there for a moment. Not deciding — she has already decided. She goes back to the garrison hall and wakes Davan with a hand on his shoulder: *"Luca went over the wall. Pre-dawn. Cliff-gap, northeast corner. I'm going after him."*

She does not ask permission.

Davan says: *"Take Sava."*

Talis: *"I'll take Caelith."*

She is right. Caelith is lighter. He moves better in the dark. And she has a reason for choosing him that she does not state: she wants his eyes on this. His notebook. His questions.

The party does not report Luca's absence to Sergeant Irr or Warden Asholt. There is no discussion about whether to report it. There is a shared understanding — unannounced, uncoordinated, arrived at from different motivations — that reporting it would close a door that should stay open.

**Independent-motivation convergence (v1.9 Character Agency anchor): all four PCs independently choose not to report Luca's departure. Davan: reporting would mean Asholt sends Irr, who would bring Luca back forcibly. Sava: the garrison doesn't need another thing to hold against Luca. Talis: you don't report a thing you're about to go fix. Caelith: reporting it changes the variable before he understands the variable. Four motivations. One non-action. No coordination.**

## Scene 03 — The Cliff-Gap Egress (Room 01-02)

First light is two hours away. The northeast parapet is empty. The rope anchor Talis identified in Session 1 — a garrison iron ring, looped with a section of hempen rope, not long enough for a full descent but long enough for the first fifteen feet — is still there.

Luca's boot prints in the frost on the parapet go to the edge and stop.

**Talis goes first.** This is her route. She found it. She uses it.

**🎲 Talis Athletics (cliff descent, DC 12)** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[7] mod=+3 **total=10 (fail)**

The rope slips in her hands — the frost is deeper here, on the wall's shadow side, than she calculated. She drops the last four feet, landing hard on the exterior ledge. Nothing broken. Her wrists are cold. She looks up.

Sava:

**🎲 Sava Athletics (cliff descent, DC 12)** — `1d20+1` → rolls=[15] mod=+1 **total=16 (success)**

All four across without incident. The tree line is cold pine and frozen undergrowth. Talis leads, finding Luca's track in the snow — he came this way two hours ago, moving carefully, staying in shadow.

They follow the track to the Reconstitution camp perimeter. They can see the outer ring — a patrol path, two soldiers on rotation.

The patrol soldiers see them.

## Scene 05 — The Return (Room 05): Luca, Helin, Caelith's Question

Twenty minutes after the patrol engagement. The party, depleted and cold, is at the cliff-gap reentry point when they see Luca. He is climbing back up the cliff from below — moving carefully, the rope in his hands, his quiver over his shoulder.

He does not know they are here.

**🎲 Talis Perception (spotting Luca's return)** — `1d20+6` → rolls=[4] mod=+6 **total=10 (success)**

She spots him. She says nothing. She waits. Davan and Caelith see him a moment later.

Luca reaches the ledge and pulls himself up. He sees the party. He freezes.

A long moment.

Nobody speaks first. This is the token condition: the party's first response to his return. There are several tactical first responses available: *"You compromised security." "Did anyone follow you?" "You'll be reported."* None of these fire.

What fires, after a moment, is Caelith. He looks at Luca and says:

*"What did you need to know?"*

Not "why did you go." Not "what did you find." Not "are you hurt." The question that Caelith, looking at Luca's face — still open from whatever happened in the camp, not yet closed into the garrison-face he wears on the wall — decides is the right question.

**Luca's token condition fires: party's first response is human, not tactical. Caelith's question IS human — it is the only question that treats Luca's departure as a comprehensible act rather than a security breach.**

**Luca's arc-completion line (campaign-permanent):**

Luca is quiet for a moment. Then: *"He's all right. He didn't know I was here."*

That is the whole of it. His brother Aldren is alive. He did not find out what Aldren thinks about the siege, about Saren, about the question she has been sitting on for twenty days. He found out that Aldren is breathing and eating and walking the perimeter. That is what he needed to know.

**Luca's token earned.**

---

The party is inside the walls, through the postern gate, before first light. The refugees' emergency outdoor position — where Dene relocated them after the assault, a narrow courtyard off the lower keep — is visible as they pass. Helin is there.

She has been awake. She was awake when they went out. She watched the cliff-gap from below. She is standing at the courtyard wall with her arms folded and her eyes on Davan.

She does not wait to be asked. She says, to Davan specifically:

*"She said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people. She never came. This is her coming back."*

A pause.

*"Saren Coldforge left Thorngate twenty years ago when Asholt gave shelter to travelers from Lemish. She said his duty was to the Oath, not to every person who knocked. She was right about the Oath. She was wrong about the people. She has known that for twenty years."*

**Hint 3 delivered (campaign-permanent).** Helin speaks. She has been waiting to tell someone who could use it.

**🎲 Sava Insight (Helin's conviction)** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[10] mod=+3 **total=13 (success)**

Helin is not speculating. She was at the pass. She heard the argument. She knows Saren's voice when she is right and when she is wrong, because she heard both in the same conversation twenty years ago.

---

Luca, beside Talis, processes what Helin said. He has the intel from Aldren. He has been turning it over since he was in the camp. He says, to no one in particular — to the courtyard, to the dawn sky, to the group:

*"She hasn't asked for permission to finish it. Aldren doesn't understand why."*

A pause. Then, quietly: *"I think I might."*

He does not explain what he thinks. He is twenty-two. The party is left with his hesitation.

**Hint 4 pre-plant (campaign-permanent):** Saren is stalling. She has drafted final assault authorization three times and pulled it back. The camp is exhausted. Some units are rotating out. She is here for something she has not named. Luca thinks he understands what it is. He does not say it.

---

Davan stands at the courtyard wall as the sky begins to grey. He has been composing something. The second layer arrived this session: Caelith's question — "what did you need to know" — is the architecture. Not a speech about the Oath. A speech about the people in the question.

He takes out his notebook. He adds a line below the first one.

**PC-authored accounting speech current shape:**
*"The Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this.*
*The Oath tells you about the letter. It doesn't tell you about the people. Saren was right about one. Asholt was right about the other. So was I, at different times."*

The second sentence came from watching Caelith ask Luca "what did you need to know" and from hearing Helin name the gap. Davan did not compose it at a desk. He composed it in the cold, watching the sky change.

**Supply Die: d4 → EXHAUSTED.** The patrol encounter fired the last d4-tier resource. Sava used Healing Word; that is the last healing resource the garrison-supply has available. From this point: individual PC resources only.
