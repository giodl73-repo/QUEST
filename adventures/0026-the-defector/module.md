## Setting & Background

On day 24 of the siege, Archer Luca Bent is missing from pre-dawn watch; Talis uses the cliff-gap she spotted in S1 to get outside the walls; Luca comes back from the Reconstitution camp with his brother alive and a secret that reframes the entire siege — and Caelith asks him one question that gives the accounting speech its frame.

**Thorngate Keep, Pre-dawn of Day 24.** The keep has been in supply-crisis mode since the S4 refugee reveal. Asholt has not formally adjudicated Dene's situation; the refugees are still in the lower chambers; the supply die is at d4. The garrison is exhausted. Pre-dawn watch is the worst watch — the hour when alertness bottoms out and the cold is deepest.

**Hint 3 delivery:** Helin (from S4's refugee space) tells the party what happened twenty years ago. Saren trained under Asholt, objected when he sheltered non-Solamnic travelers, and left in protest. She has spent twenty years knowing she was wrong about the people. She is here to say it.

**Hint 4 pre-plant:** Luca's brother Aldren, from inside the Reconstitution camp, tells Luca: Saren has not requested final assault authorization from Reconstitution high command. She is stalling. The camp is exhausted. Some units are rotating out.

- **Party HP:** Whatever survived S4 + 1 hit die of recovery.
- **Supply Die:** d4. **1 emergency item (DM choice: either a second garrison potion or a short ration pack)**. After combat: d4 → exhausted (Supply Die reaches zero; the keep has no more reserves beyond individual PC resources).
- **Sava spell slots:** Carry-over from S4, reduced.
- **Caelith Portent:** [6] if not used in S4 (design for both). If available: this is the last designed Portent opportunity before the finale.

**Design intent:** The patrol combat is outside the walls, in hostile territory, with a depleted party. The damage should be real but not lethal. 2–3 patrol soldiers. The Supply Die reaching exhausted is the session's structural signal: the keep cannot hold much longer.

Talis is the first to notice at pre-dawn watch change: Luca Bent's post is empty. Not relieved — empty. His bow is on the parapet. His quiver is gone. His footprints go to the northeast corner and stop where the cliff-gap is.

**Talis's S1 passive 16 detection vindicated:** She found the cliff-gap in S1 as a passive perception result. She told the party it was there. Nobody has used it yet. Now it becomes the only way to get outside the walls quietly.

The spotlight is the conversation after Luca returns. Caelith speaks to Luca when he comes back through the cliff-gap. His institutional-lens question — the one his voice-tag has been building toward — fires here.

**Caelith's question:** *"What did you need to know?"*

Not "why did you go." Not "what did you find." What did you need to know. The distinction is Caelith's entire operating mode — he does not ask about motives; he asks about the knowledge-gap that drove the action.

Luca's answer gives the party Hint 4's frame. Davan witnesses this exchange. The accounting speech finds its architecture here.

Twenty-two years old. Youngest garrison member at Thorngate. Has been on the east wall since the siege began. His older brother **Aldren** serves in the Reconstitution as an archer — they joined different orders three years ago, before the schism became a siege. Luca has been watching the Reconstitution standard every dawn from the wall. In S1, the party saw this.

He did not go to defect. He went to see whether Aldren was alive and whether Aldren was the kind of person who was going to die on the wrong side of a wall that Luca was standing on. He came back. That is the whole of it.

**Luca's arc-completion condition:** A PC responds to his return without a tactical frame. Not "did you compromise security" — something human. If this fires, his token follows.

**Luca's arc-completion line (campaign-permanent):** *"He's all right. He didn't know I was here."*

When Talis's route takes the party through or near the refugees' emergency outdoor position (Dene relocated them per S4's post-assault protocol), Helin is present. She has been watching for an opportunity. She speaks when the party is on its way back with Luca — in Room 05 (the return scene, inside the walls at dawn).

**Hint 3 line (campaign-permanent):**

*"She said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people. She never came. This is her coming back."*

The subject is Saren Coldforge. Helin was at the pass twenty years ago with the non-Solamnic travelers Asholt sheltered. She heard the argument. She knows what Saren said and what Asholt answered. She has been waiting to tell someone who could use it.

Aldren is not in the party's encounter space — he is inside the Reconstitution camp. Luca carries the information back.

**What Aldren told Luca:** Saren has not filed for final assault authorization with Reconstitution high command. The formal request has been drafted three times and pulled back. The camp is exhausted — rotating units, supply problems of their own, a sergeant who has started asking when they are going home. Saren is stalling.

**Luca's framing of this (to Caelith or the party):** He does not know what to do with this information. He says: *"She hasn't asked for permission to finish it. Aldren doesn't understand why."* He pauses. *"I think I might."*

This is the Hint 4 pre-plant. Luca does not explain what he thinks. He is twenty-two. The party is left with his hesitation.

End-of-session handoff note: *"Davan is composing the accounting. Current shape: 'the Oath was supposed to tell me who deserves shelter. It doesn't tell me this.' And now the frame: Caelith's question — 'what did you need to know' — is the architecture Davan will use at the parley. Davan witnessed it. It has landed. He has not spoken it yet."*

## Scale

One square ≈ 10 feet. The cliff-gap ledge is approximately 5 feet wide and 8 feet deep — enough for two people. The no-man's-land gap is 50 feet (open ground; no cover). The camp perimeter patrol path is 20 feet from the tree line. The camp itself is 200 feet into the tree line — the party does not go there.

## DM notes

**Talis's vindication:** The cliff-gap was discovered by Talis in Session 1 via passive 16 Perception. She noted it. Nobody has used it until now. When she leads the party to this corner of the wall, there is no drama — she simply knows where it is. She can descend without an Athletics check (her Dex +3 and natural movement; the DC 12 is for less nimble PCs).

**Luca's tracks:** His footprints in the frost are visible at the northeast parapet (passive 12 Perception). They go to the corner and stop. The rope anchor — a piton he installed sometime in the last 24 hours — is in the wall above the gap. He planned this.

**Outside the walls at pre-dawn:** The advantage on Stealth checks at night is meaningful. The party is at serious risk if they are spotted — they are outside the walls, depleted, without garrison backup. The designed path keeps them at the camp perimeter (Room 04) and does not push them into the camp proper.

**Luca on the return route:** He has to come back over the wall. The rope is there. He can make the DC 12 Athletics, but not quietly — the party will need to decide whether to secure the rope from inside. If they are in Room 01 when he returns, someone can brace it. If not, he makes noise on the ascent (DC 10 Stealth; failure means a garrison member on the south-wall circuit hears it).

### Scene 1 — The Pre-Dawn Wall

> Northeast corner, the worst watch. Cold off the cliff face. Luca's post is empty. His bow is on the parapet stone, quiver gone. In the frost near the corner: boot prints, heading northeast. They stop where the wall meets the cliff overhang.

#### Features

- **Luca's empty post:** His shortbow rests on the parapet, not stored — he set it down deliberately. His personal quiver is gone. His watch-coat is also gone. He has been gone for at least an hour (the frost has reset over the earlier part of his prints). **DC 10 Survival (Talis, auto):** He left quietly and intentionally. This is not a panic exit. He had a plan.

- **The rope anchor:** A piton driven into the wall mortar above the cliff-gap, with a knotted rope descending into the darkness below. **DC 12 Investigation or Perception:** The piton is recent — the mortar dust is fresh, the rope coils are even, not tangled. He installed this before his watch shift. He prepared this in advance. **This was not an impulse.**

- **The cliff-gap (S1 seed vindicated):** Talis found this gap in S1. She noted the northeast corner had a rock-shelf outside the wall at a 15-foot drop — enough for a careful person to descend without major equipment. The gap is 2 feet wide where it opens in the wall's foundation stones. She fits. Davan does not without removing armor (he can, but it costs him 5 minutes and an action). Caelith and Sava fit with some care.

- **What the party can see from the parapet:** In the pre-dawn dark, nothing is visible beyond the no-man's-land except the faint outline of the tree line 300 feet north. **DC 14 Perception:** A single figure, moving low and fast, at the tree line's edge. Could be Luca. Could be a Reconstitution scout. The figure is gone in one round — the tree line is deep.

#### Encounter

None in this room. The encounter is in Room 04 (patrol).

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- South: Wall-walk, connects to garrison quarters (approach from below; alarm accessible)
- Northeast: Cliff-gap egress (Room 02) via rope descent

#### GM Notes

**Let Talis lead.** This is her scene architecturally — she found the gap in S1, she is reading the tracks now. If the DM is running solo, Talis does not announce her own competence; she acts. Her passive Perception is 16. She already sees the rope when the party arrives. She already knows where the tracks go. Her move toward the gap is the prompt for the rest of the party.

**Davan's armor dilemma:** He cannot fit through the gap in full armor. The party must decide: he strips down (5 minutes, quiet if he is careful, but he will be significantly less durable in Room 04) or he holds the rope from inside while the others go. This is a real choice. If Davan stays, he is present for Luca's return (Room 05) but not for the patrol encounter. If he goes, the party has their heaviest combatant in Room 04 but at AC 12 instead of 18.

**The pre-dawn window:** The party has approximately 90 minutes before full dawn and the change of watch. If they are not back by then, the gap will be in daylight — DC 16 Stealth instead of DC 12. Time pressure is real but not catastrophic.

### Scene 2 — The Cliff-Gap Egress

> Rock shelf, five feet wide, under an overhang. Frost on the stone. Below: a ten-foot scramble to the ground. The keep's outer wall is at your back. The tree line is north, three hundred feet of open ground away. Somewhere in that dark, a garrison archer went over this wall.

#### Features

- **The ledge:** 5 feet wide, 8 feet deep, sheltered by the rock overhang above. Not visible from the Reconstitution forward picket at the tree line (the overhang blocks the sightline). From here, the party can observe the gap before crossing it. **DC 14 Perception:** Two figures visible at the tree line's edge, moving slowly — patrol, not attack formation. Standard rotation interval (one pass per 4–5 minutes based on movement speed).

- **The descent from Room 01:** DC 12 Athletics for most party members. Talis: automatic (her movement; no check required). Davan without armor: DC 10. Caelith and Sava: DC 12 (failure = 1d4 falling damage, not serious, but noisy — DC 12 Stealth check after a failed Athletics check to avoid alerting the picket).

- **Luca's boot-prints on the ledge:** Visible in the frost. He came here. He waited briefly (the indentations are deep — he crouched). Then he crossed the gap. **DC 12 Survival (Talis):** He waited for the patrol gap. He counted the rotation. He was patient. He has been thinking about this for a long time.

- **The 10-foot scramble to ground:** DC 10 Athletics for all party members. This is easier — the rock shelf steps down naturally. Failure: 1d4 falling damage; noise as above.

- **Wind direction:** West to east, light. Carries sound away from the Reconstitution picket (toward the cliff face). This is marginal advantage: the party's voices carry less than normal. Add +2 to any Stealth checks made while whispering.

#### Encounter

None. Room 02 is the decision point and the transition space.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- Up: Room 01 Pre-Dawn Wall (rope ascent; DC 12 Athletics)
- Down: Room 03 Outside the Walls (10-ft scramble; DC 10 Athletics)

#### GM Notes

**This is the commitment point.** Once the party descends to Room 03, they are outside the walls with a depleted supply die, reduced HP, and an unknown patrol schedule. The party should feel this choice. Give them a moment on the ledge. Let Talis describe what she sees before they go further.

**Davan's presence or absence:** If he stayed in Room 01 to manage the rope, he is not here. If he stripped armor and came through, he is here at AC 12. Either way, note it in the session log — it affects the Room 04 patrol encounter tactics.

**Re-entry:** On the way back, the party needs someone to brace the rope from above (Room 01) for Luca to ascend quietly. If everyone is outside, Luca has to free-climb (DC 12 Athletics, noisy on a failure). Plan for this before descending.

### Scene 3 — Outside the Walls

> Open ground. The keep is at your back. The cold is different out here — no stone to hold the heat, no walls to break the wind. The tree line is three hundred feet north. Somewhere between here and there, a patrol is moving. You cannot see them yet.

#### Features

- **50 feet of open ground:** No cover between the cliff-gap egress and the tree line. The party must cross this in the dark. **DC 12 Stealth (group check, advantage due to pre-dawn dark):** If the group check fails, the patrol in Room 04 is alerted (they are at disadvantage on their attack rolls for the first round of combat because they expected garrison soldiers, not infiltrators moving fast). **If the group check succeeds:** The party reaches the tree line unseen.

- **What the cold does:** The party is in pre-dawn Kharolis foothills without shelter or fire. No mechanical effect for one scene, but: at the start of Room 04, each PC has used one hit die of warmth from their overnight rest. This is flavor, not mechanics — but it makes the scene feel right.

- **The tree line at 300 feet:** Dense pine, with underbrush. Light enough to see by at this hour — the moon is down but the sky is lightening. DC 12 Perception to see the patrol's movement before it reaches them. DC 14 to count: there are two soldiers (not three as the party might assume from earlier encounters).

- **Luca's tracks:** They lead directly north across the open ground to a point 20 feet east of center in the tree line. He crossed without cover. He either got lucky with the patrol rotation or he waited long enough to know exactly when to go. **DC 12 Survival:** He went fast — the prints are far apart (running stride, not walking). He was confident in his timing. Or he was reckless. Hard to say which from the prints.

- **What the party cannot see:** Luca himself. He is inside the tree line, at the camp perimeter (Room 04), with his brother. He is not in distress — he is exactly where he planned to be. He does not know the party is out here.

#### Encounter

If the group Stealth check is failed: the patrol arrives at the tree line's edge and spots the party crossing. This triggers the Room 04 encounter in open ground rather than at the camp perimeter — less tactical cover for the party, same patrol composition.

If the group Stealth check succeeds: no encounter in this room.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- South: Room 02 Cliff-Gap Egress (10-ft scramble back up)
- North: Room 04 Reconstitution Camp Perimeter (cross the gap and tree line)

#### GM Notes

**The exposure is the point.** The party is outside the walls, depleted, at pre-dawn. This room is the physical embodiment of their vulnerability. The Supply Die at d4 means they have essentially no garrison backing. They are on their own.

**Let the cold land.** A brief description of what pre-dawn foothills feel like — the frost underfoot, the breath visible, the silence — before the party moves north is the right atmospheric beat. Caelith especially: his history notes him as someone who observes conditions before acting. He would note the wind direction (west, carrying their sound away from the picket).

**The group Stealth check:** This is a single check — the party's worst result (not an average). If Caelith is present (AC 12, bulky messenger case), he is the likely weak link. If Davan came through in armor, he is definitely the weak link — but if he stripped armor, his Stealth is not worse than Caelith's. Use the actual party composition to determine the check modifier.

### Scene 4 — Reconstitution Camp Perimeter

> Tree line. Two soldiers on patrol rotation, moving east. Between the trees, firelight — the camp, fifty yards deeper in. At the camp's edge, two figures standing close together, not moving. One of them has a Thorngate quiver.

#### Features

- **The two patrol soldiers:** Standard rotation, moving east along the outer perimeter. They do not expect infiltrators from the keep side — their training is focused outward, toward the Needle Pass. **Passive Perception 12.** If the party is in cover at the tree line and not making noise, the patrol passes within 20 feet without detecting them.

- **Luca and Aldren:** The two figures at the camp's edge are Luca Bent (garrison quiver visible) and Aldren Bent (Reconstitution tabard). They are standing close, speaking in low voices. They are not arguing — this is a conversation that has been building for three years. They are doing it in the ten minutes Luca has before he needs to leave.

- **The camp itself (not keyed, not entered):** Firelight, tents, the smell of cook-smoke. The party can see its edge from here. It is larger than a garrison force — 200+ soldiers at minimum. And yet: the tents nearest the perimeter are two-thirds full. Some have been struck. **DC 14 Perception:** Unit markers on the tent-pegs indicate rotations — some units are not here that were here two weeks ago. The camp is smaller than it was.

- **Aldren's information (what he tells Luca, audible if the party is within 30 feet and makes DC 12 Perception):** *"She drafted the final-assault request three times. Pulled it back each time. Sergeant Harven has started asking the question — 'what are we waiting for.' She hasn't answered him."* Then: *"The units from Solanthus rotated out last week. She didn't stop them."*

- **What the party must decide:** Engage the patrol now (before Luca's conversation ends) or wait and risk being spotted when Luca moves. If they wait: DC 12 Stealth to hold position while Luca finishes (advantage if in cover). If the Stealth fails, the patrol spots them — combat, 2 soldiers.

#### Encounter

**Encounter — Camp Patrol**

**Overview**

Two Reconstitution patrol soldiers on standard perimeter rotation at the camp's outer edge. They are not on high alert — their training is focused outward (toward the pass), not toward the keep. They are cold, tired, and in the last hour of a night watch. Their morale breaks quickly if things go badly for them.

**Design intent:** Moderate encounter — real HP cost but not lethal. Outside the walls, depleted party. 2 soldiers (CR 1/2 each). The difficulty is situational: the party is in hostile territory, cannot raise an alarm, and must deal with these soldiers without drawing camp attention. XP budget: 2 × 100 = 200 XP × 1.5 (situational multiplier, outside walls) = **300 adjusted XP.** Medium threshold for L3 party of 4. Intentionally moderate — the session's structural weight is not this combat, it is Room 05.

---.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- South/West: Room 03 Outside the Walls (reverse route through tree line)
- Deeper North: Reconstitution camp proper (not entered; background only)

#### GM Notes

**Let the party see Luca's conversation before they engage.** The tactical situation allows 1–2 rounds of observation before the patrol completes its rotation. Use this time. The party is watching a garrison soldier and a Reconstitution soldier standing close in the dark, talking quietly. That image carries weight. Do not rush past it.

**Aldren is not an enemy.** He does not fight the party — he does not know they are there. When the combat starts (if it starts), Aldren and Luca separate immediately. Luca does the right thing: he moves away from the camp perimeter and toward the return route. He does not fight. Aldren retreats into the camp. He and Luca have already said what they needed to say.

**The depleted-party dynamic:** Two patrol soldiers against a party that is depleted and resource-limited. The encounter is designed to be moderate (not lethal), but outside the walls with a d4 Supply Die, even a moderate combat costs. Sava's emergency item decision comes here — if a PC drops below 10 HP, she uses it. If not, she holds it. There may not be another opportunity.

**Hint 4 availability:** Aldren's information is audible if the party positions for it. If they engage the patrol before overhearing, they get the information secondhand through Luca in Room 05. Either way, the Hint 4 pre-plant delivers. But overhearing it live is a richer version.

### Scene 5 — The Return

> Inside the walls at dawn. The sky is grey-pink beyond the northeast parapet. Luca is over the wall before the light catches him. Behind the keep's eastern face, in the lee of the stone, a small fire. Dene has moved the refugees outside — emergency protocol. Helin sits near the fire.

#### Features

- **Luca's return:** He climbs the rope last, with the party's help. He is cold and scratched from moving through underbrush. He is not apologetic. He is the opposite of apologetic — he has the bearing of someone who did a necessary thing and is prepared to account for it.

- **Character-logic condition (no roll):** The party's FIRST response to Luca sets the token condition.
  - **Human (token fires):** Ask about his brother. Say his name. Make space for him to say what he needs to say. Ask "are you all right" as a genuine question.
  - **Tactical (token window closes):** "Did you compromise security." "You violated your watch post." "What did you give them." Any question whose first frame is institutional rather than personal.
  - **Ambiguous:** DM judgment. Caelith's heuristic specifically directs him to ask a non-tactical question first. If Caelith speaks first (per his heuristics), the condition fires on his question.

- **Caelith's question (spotlight, campaign-permanent):** When Luca is over the wall and the party is standing with him in the dawn, Caelith speaks first. His institutional-lens question:

  *"What did you need to know?"*

  This is the question his entire operating mode has been building toward since S1. Not "why did you go." Not "what did you find." What did you need to know — as though the information-gap is the real subject, which is also the real subject, because Caelith understands that institutional rules exist to prevent exactly the kind of knowing that changes what people are capable of deciding.

  **Luca's answer (campaign-permanent):**

  *"Whether Aldren was on the wrong side, or just the other side."*

  He says it simply, like a man who has had the answer ready for three years and is now finally saying it out loud. He does not elaborate. He does not need to.

  **Davan witnesses this exchange.** The accounting speech's architecture comes from it: the distinction between wrong and other. This is the frame Davan will use at the parley.

- **Luca's token delivery:** The PC who spoke to him without a tactical frame — or, if Caelith spoke first, Caelith — receives Luca's token. It is a wooden disc with a bowshot carved on one face: the garrison archer's mark.

- **Helin's Hint 3 delivery:** She is at the fire. She has been watching the party return. When Luca is accounted for and the party is standing near the fire, she speaks to Davan — or to the group, but she looks at Davan.

  **Hint 3 line (campaign-permanent):**

  *"She said she'd come back when she could say she'd been wrong about the people. She never came. This is her coming back."*

# Encounter — Camp Patrol

## Overview

Two Reconstitution patrol soldiers on standard perimeter rotation at the camp's outer edge. They are not on high alert — their training is focused outward (toward the pass), not toward the keep. They are cold, tired, and in the last hour of a night watch. Their morale breaks quickly if things go badly for them.

**Design intent:** Moderate encounter — real HP cost but not lethal. Outside the walls, depleted party. 2 soldiers (CR 1/2 each). The difficulty is situational: the party is in hostile territory, cannot raise an alarm, and must deal with these soldiers without drawing camp attention. XP budget: 2 × 100 = 200 XP × 1.5 (situational multiplier, outside walls) = **300 adjusted XP.** Medium threshold for L3 party of 4. Intentionally moderate — the session's structural weight is not this combat, it is Room 05.

## Tactics

### Round 1 (if party engages first)

- Patrol is in standard formation (8 feet apart, moving east). If the party engages from cover before the patrol spots them, the party has a surprise round.
- During the surprise round: the patrol has not yet acted. Both soldiers are eligible for Sneak Attack (Talis) or advantage (any attacker from behind cover while unseen).
- If one soldier drops to 0 HP in the surprise round: the other makes a Wisdom saving throw (DC 13) before raising an alarm. On a success, they call the alarm. On a failure, they are stunned by the speed of the action and do not call the alarm this round.

### Round 1 (if patrol spots the party first)

- One soldier raises the alarm (action) while the other draws weapon.
- Camp reinforcements in 3 rounds unless the alarm-caller is silenced (incapacitated, grappled, or Caelith uses Suggestion if he still has a slot).

### Round 2+

- Both soldiers fight. They do not retreat — they know the camp is behind them. They will die holding their position rather than let unknown infiltrators reach the perimeter.
- If one is incapacitated: the other fights harder (disadvantage on Wisdom saves against being frightened; they are now motivated by their companion's fall).

## Portent Condition (Final Opportunity)

**Caelith's Portent [6] may fire here — last designed opportunity before the finale.**

**Best trigger:** The patrol soldier's alarm-call Wisdom save (DC 13). Caelith can replace the roll with his Portent [6]. A 6 fails the DC 13 save — the soldier does not call the alarm. This is the cleanest use: tactical, clear consequence, campaign-significant because it determines whether reinforcements arrive.

**Alternative trigger:** The alarm-call itself, if the party chooses to grapple the caller. Replacing the soldier's Strength check to break the grapple (DC 14 Str vs. Talis or Davan) with Portent [6] keeps them secured.

**If Portent is not used here:** The die expired with the last long rest (never happening under siege). It is gone. This is acceptable — the campaign can complete without it. Note in session log and handoff: Portent [6] expired or final use.

## Silence constraint

The party is 200 feet from the camp. Any loud noise (shouting, metal on metal, a spell with verbal components at normal volume) risks camp attention. **DC 14 Perception** for any camp soldier awake and not in a tent. At pre-dawn (90% of soldiers in tents), this DC effectively applies only to the handful on interior watch.

**Talis's Stealth approach:** She can close to melee range before the patrol sees her with DC 12 Stealth (advantage, night, pre-dawn). Her shortbow has advantage if she fires from concealment and is not yet detected.

**Caelith's Magic Missile:** No verbal component in standard 5e text (it is a spoken spell in lore, but mechanically silent in SRD terms; DM call, but the designed default is that it functions as a cast — low sound, not a shout). He can use it without triggering the noise constraint.

## End states

**Patrol incapacitated (no alarm):** The party has until dawn to recover Luca and return. They leave the patrol soldiers unconscious (not dead — up to the party). The soldiers wake when the camp's morning watch begins (approximately 90 minutes).

**Alarm raised, reinforcements at 3 rounds:** The party must disengage immediately — they cannot fight 4 more soldiers in this condition. Luca follows; Aldren stays. The party takes the return route (Room 03 → 02 → 01). If they are fast (DC 14 Athletics, or DC 10 with Talis leading), they reach Room 02 before the reinforcements reach the tree line.

**A patrol soldier is killed (party choice):** Note in session log. The Reconstitution will know a garrison infiltration happened. Saren's response to this information is an S6 complication — she will send a formal message demanding acknowledgment. This is a consequence, not a catastrophe.
