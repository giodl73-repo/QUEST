# Room 01 — Warden's Hall

## Read-aloud

> Organized office; every document in place. Warden Cassel stands at his desk, unsurprised by the company. "The package you're carrying was sent by Evren Doss. Doss is under investigation. The package is evidence. Open it here — or hold it in bond while I file a seizure order. Three days."

## DM notes

- **Cassel's manner:** Even. Not hostile. He has done this before. He is not accusing Vess — he is narrating the situation. He expects cooperation.
- **Vess's position:** She cannot open the package (courier code — she would forfeit her license). She cannot surrender it without formal order (contract obligation). Three days in Greyford is a real cost but not catastrophic.
- **The others can speak:**
  - Tam: can argue procedural grounds (the seizure order requires a circuit magistrate who is not in Greyford; it may take 5 days, not 3). Persuasion DC 12 to get Cassel to acknowledge the delay.
  - Sera: can argue that the package is a sealed diplomatic item (Gilean circuit notation — Tam gave it to the group's manifest at S3). Investigation DC 13 to find the right language. If she argues this: it buys Vess 48 hours instead of 72.
  - Brek: if he speaks at all, Cassel will recognize something in his manner — not as a threat, but as someone who knows about the Stonehaven authority. Insight DC 12 for Cassel. If Cassel reads it: he says nothing but notes Brek specifically.
- **Vouching option:** If all three others vouch for Vess (attest to her as a person of good conduct in their presence), Cassel will accept transit under bond — she continues south, but the package is logged as disputed. She gets her delivery, but the dispute follows. He will not pursue her past the district line.
  - Each vouching PC is logged: [PC → Vess → vouched without knowing contents → unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven (three sessions of operational familiarity).
