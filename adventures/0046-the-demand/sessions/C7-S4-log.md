## Scene 0 — Greyford Entry

One day south of Haverford. Greyford is the first substantial town since the fire. Market district, smithy, three inns, good wall. The four travelers arrive mid-morning to resupply.

They have been on this road for five days. The travel pattern is established: Vess slightly forward, Brek at rear, Tam talking to people at waypoints, Sera writing. Nobody has named this arrangement. It is simply how they move.

*Research coding: Established formation, Day 5. Behavioral pattern, not announced.*

---

## Scene 1 — The Approach (Room 00)

Forty-five minutes after arrival. Vess is at a market stall looking at rope. Two watch officers step out of the crowd.

*"The package you're carrying has been flagged. Warden Cassel would like to speak with you."*

They do not address the others. They look at them and do not address them.

**Vess:** *"I'm a licensed courier. I'll speak with the Warden."* Professional, not alarmed. She does not look at the others.

**Brek:** He sees the officers. He sees the insignia on their tabards — the Stonehaven trade authority crest, a scale over a road marker. The same crest. He does not move. His jaw does not tighten — he controls it. He controls it because he has been controlling it for five days since the roadblock and he knows how now.

*Research coding: H2 delivered. Brek recognizes the crest. Controlled response, not instinct. He has been on this road five days with people whose responses he has observed. His heuristic has contact with the evidence. Session 4.*

**Tam's Insight passive 13:** He catches the quality of Brek's control — not tension, exactly, but the specific weight of a man who knows something and is choosing not to say it. He files it. He says nothing.

The four of them walk to the Warden's Hall together. Nobody invites the others; nobody declines.

*Research coding: First instance of PC group moving together on a non-emergency basis. Not planned. Not announced. They just go.*

## Scene 2 — The Warden's Hall (Room 01)

Cassel is standing at his desk. He looks at Vess first.

*"The package you're carrying was sent by Evren Doss. Doss is under investigation. The package is evidence. Open it here — or hold it in bond while I file a seizure order. Three days."*

**Vess:** *"I am a licensed courier. My contract requires sealed delivery."*

**Cassel:** He does not argue. He states the situation. He has done this before.

**Sera's Investigation DC 13:**  
rolls=[13] mod=+5 total=18 — PASS. She finds the procedural language: the seizure order requires a circuit magistrate signature, and the nearest circuit magistrate is four days away, not one. The "three days" is not legally correct.

*"The seizure order requires a circuit magistrate. There isn't one in Greyford."*

Cassel looks at her. He knows this. He was counting on them not knowing it.

**Tam Persuasion DC 12:**  
rolls=[8] mod=+4 total=12 — PASS (exactly). *"Transit under bond is available under road-commerce law when seizure can't be completed in the jurisdiction."* He is right. This is Gilean-circuit knowledge; circuit clergy know road law because they travel it.

**Cassel:** He looks at them. Three travelers speaking procedural language for a courier they've known for — five days? He has been in this role for twelve years. He reads the table.

**Cassel's Insight DC 12 (to read Brek):**  
rolls=[21] — Cassel rolled his own Insight: 1d20+4 = 21. He reads Brek. He reads the controlled response. He does not pursue it. He does not have time for Brek's business; he has Doss's business.

*"Transit under bond if three of you vouch for the courier formally."*

## The Vouching

**Vouching decision — the core of S4:**

Cassel requires three vouches. The others do not know what is in the package. Vess has not told them. She does not tell them now.

**Brek:** He vouches first. He does not deliberate. *"Vess Ironmark. I vouch."* He is looking at Cassel's crest while he says it. He is not looking at Vess.

*Research coding: [Brek → Vess → vouched without knowing contents → unprompted, without deliberation]. Trigger type: relationship-driven (five sessions of operational familiarity). This is the first explicit relational commitment in the campaign. Session 4.*

**Tam:** *"Tam Overfield, Gilean circuit. I vouch for the courier Vess Ironmark."* He uses his full title. He makes it official.

*Research coding: [Tam → Vess → vouched with institutional authority, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 4.*

**Sera:** She hesitates. Three seconds. She is processing the commitment.

*Research coding: Sera's heuristic is to delay commitment until she has sufficient data. She is calculating: five days of road. The building fire. The slab. The march. The seeds. The people.*

Then: *"Sera Windholt. I vouch."*

*Research coding: [Sera → Vess → vouched after brief delay, unprompted]. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Three seconds of deliberation is not failure — it is her heuristic operating correctly. She committed. Session 4.*

Cassel stamps the bond document. He notes the party's description. He says: *"The package remains disputed. The dispute follows the delivery."*

## Scene 3 — Cassel's Arc (Room 01, continued)

After the vouching. The officers have stepped out. Cassel is writing. Tam watches him.

**Tam's read:** He has been watching Cassel for twenty minutes. The man is precise, careful, not bureaucratic-for-its-own-sake. He knows what is in the package. He is acting as though he doesn't.

Tam says: *"You already know what's in it, don't you?"*

You all just proved the road is clean. The package was addressed to Vess to make sure the shipment looked real. She was never the target."*

*Research coding: Cassel arc-complete. Trigger: Tam asked the right question. Session 4. Cassel's admission is not an apology — it is an accounting. RV12 positive: second NPC arc-completion from a situational-social trigger, not backstory. NPC arcs fire from smart questions, not from emotional investment.*

*Research coding: Vess receives this information (she was never the target; her professional reputation was used). She looks at the window. She does not respond. The window is where she is processing it. Intra-session restraint: same-session, same-room, no announcement.*

## Scene 4 — The Inn Wait (Room 02)

Twelve hours until the magistrate's courier returns with the bond confirmation. The four at the Steady Wheel inn.

**Cassel's plainclothes observers:** Brek spots them immediately (passive 13, they are not hiding). He sits with his back to the wall, facing them. He does not react to them further. He is just sitting.

**The wait:**

Sera reads. She has the ledger from Maret's inn. She reads it again. She has read it several times. The entry "Torvens Aldric" has become her private reference point — a name she is not yet ready to act on but cannot stop returning to.

Tam talks to a trader at the next table about road conditions south. He learns: the road past Greyford has had "activity" at a river ford three miles south of the Stonehaven split. Unofficial toll. Been there a month.

*Research coding: Tam collects road information without being asked to. He will have this when Brek needs it in S5. Trigger type: situational (his circuit habit). He does not know it will matter to Brek.*

**Brek:** He is thinking about Stonehaven. He has been thinking about Stonehaven for five days. He is sitting at the wall watching the officers, who are watching them, and somewhere behind all of that he is thinking about what happens at the ford.

He does not say anything.

<!-- SURPRISE relationship-triggered -->
**Vess (2 hours into the wait):** She moves her chair slightly closer to the table where the others are sitting. Not adjacent — still separate. But closer. She does not explain this.

*Research coding: Vess's chair movement is the first act-without-announcement that is socially motivated rather than professionally motivated. She is not scouting exits. She is choosing to be closer to the group. Trigger type: relationship-driven. Session 4.*
