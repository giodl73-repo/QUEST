## Setting & Background

A local authority wants Vess specifically — for the package she's carrying — and the others don't know what's in it.

**The town of Greyford, Mile 68** (workshop-canon, south Abanasinia — two days south of Haverford). Greyford is a substantial market town that serves as a regional hub for the Stonehaven authority. The town watch is an extension of the Stonehaven toll operation. The Overseer for this district is **Warden Cassel**, a careful, methodical man who has been waiting for a specific package to come through this road.

The four travelers enter Greyford for resupply. Within an hour of arrival, Vess is approached by two town watch officers: *"The package you're carrying has been flagged. Warden Cassel wants to speak with you."* The others are not addressed — but they are standing there when it happens.

1. **The approach** — the watch officers find Vess in the market district. Professional, not aggressive. They have her description (she's been on this road before; she was noted). The others are with her.
2. **The meeting with Cassel** — Warden Cassel is measured, organized, and has been waiting for this package to appear on this road. He explains: the package's sender is under investigation for road-toll evasion and illegal goods transport. He is not accusing Vess of wrongdoing — she is a courier. But he needs the package, or he needs her to open it here so he can verify contents.
3. **Vess's dilemma** — she cannot open the package (courier code). She cannot surrender it (contract). She can be detained while a formal seizure order is filed (3 days in Greyford). The others do not know what's in it.
4. **H2 delivery** — Brek recognizes Cassel. He has encountered this authority before — Cassel works for the same organization whose representative wronged Brek's family. He doesn't announce this. He watches Cassel very carefully.
5. **Resolution** — the others can help Vess negotiate for transit under bond (they vouch for her), pay for a legal review (costs 15 gp and 12 hours), or physically extract from the situation. Cassel's arc fires when he admits the package was never meant for Vess — he was testing the road for someone else.

**Warden Cassel** — arc-completion NPC. Admits the package was never meant for Vess; he was using her delivery as a test shipment to trace a larger network. Fires when someone asks the right question. See npcs/warden-cassel.md.

- **H2 delivery:** Brek has encountered Cassel's organization before. He knows Cassel is connected to Stonehaven authority. He says nothing — yet. Log as H2 planted-silent.

First binding session (Gate 74 target). Vess spotlight. H2 delivered via Brek's recognition (silent, situational). The four travelers must now act collectively for Vess. This is the first time the group is required to act for a specific member rather than for an external problem. Reciprocal Acts expected: who vouches for Vess without knowing what she's carrying?

## NPCs

# Warden Cassel

**Role:** Local authority. Arc-completion NPC. Admits the package was never meant for Vess — he was testing the road for someone else.

## Appearance

Fifties, precise, with the organized bearing of someone who values accuracy over impressiveness. His office is exactly right. His uniform is worn but maintained. He has the look of a man who has filed a great many reports and read a great many more.

## Emotional position

Cassel has been in this role for twelve years. He is good at it. He does not abuse his authority — he uses it carefully because careless use creates documentation problems. He is investigating the Evren Doss network because it has been running illegal goods through this road for three years and the documentation trail is finally closing. Vess's package is a test shipment he arranged — a decoy package to trace which route the network uses and which officials look the other way.

He did not expect to feel anything about this. He is beginning to feel something, because the courier he tagged is a professional who is just doing her job, and the people around her are not what he expected.

## Arc-completion

**Trigger:** Someone asks the right question. Not "what's in the package?" but something like: *"If this is about Evren Doss, why didn't you move against him directly?"* Or: *"You already know what's in it, don't you?"*

Tam's heuristic fires for this: he reads the gap between what someone says and what they mean. Cassel knows what is in the package because he arranged the contents. The question that exposes this is the one that recognizes the gap.

**Arc-completion line:** *"The package isn't the point. Evren Doss is the point. What I needed to know was whether this road has been compromised — whether someone between Doss and the delivery point opens sealed packages. You all just proved the road is clean. The package was addressed to Vess to make sure the shipment looked real. She was never the target."*

He does not apologize. He explains. He is not cruel — he is methodical. But the methodical use of a person as an instrument requires naming it when the instrument is standing in front of him.

**RV12 test:** Cassel's arc fires because someone asked a question smart enough to expose his method. Not because of backstory — the travelers have been on this road for four sessions and none of them are from his world. Pure situational intelligence triggering arc-completion.

## What he knows

- Evren Doss is a smuggling coordinator operating out of a town two days north of Stonehaven.
- The package contains sealed trade documents — dry, legal, nothing dangerous. It was chosen to look valuable without being valuable.
- Brek Ashfall: Cassel has seen that jaw-tighten before, on people who have history with the Stonehaven authority. He notes it. He does not pursue it. He has enough to do.
- The Stonehaven Overseer (the man Brek is going to find) is not involved in this investigation. But the two operations are adjacent. Cassel will say this if asked.

## H2 seed for Brek

Cassel's organization (the Stonehaven trade authority) is the same organization that produced the man who wronged Brek's family. Cassel is not that man — but Cassel is the system that man operates within. Brek's recognition is not of Cassel personally; it is of the crest, the method, the organization.

## Voice

Even. He says what is true and does not inflate it. When he makes the admission: *"She was never the target."* Clean, without qualification.

### Watch Confrontation

# Watch Confrontation (Social Encounter)

## Setup

Warden Cassel (NPC) + 2 Watch Officers (Soldier stat blocks) vs. 4 L3 PCs. Social/legal encounter. Combat should not occur unless PCs initiate, which would be a strategic error.

## Cassel's mechanics

- **INT 14, WIS 15, CHA 13**
- Insight +4, Investigation +4, Persuasion +3
- He reads the party: passive Insight 15. He will note Brek's jaw-tighten at the crest.
- His Persuasion DC for convincing Cassel of anything is DC 14 — he is not easily moved, but he is persuadable with good evidence.

## Resolution tracks

**Track A — Vouching:** All three others vouch for Vess. Cassel accepts transit under bond. DC 0 if all three vouch; DC 10 Persuasion if only two vouch; DC 14 if only one.

**Track B — Legal argument:** Sera finds procedural language, Tam argues delay. DC 12 (Persuasion or Investigation), with advantage if both succeed. Result: transit under 48-hour review instead of 72.

**Track C — Payment:** 15 gp, no roll. Cassel accepts. Vess's record cleared for this road.

**Track D — Extraction:** Does not involve Cassel. See rooms/03-town-gate.md.

## Downstream effects

- If Track A: Vess's package is logged as "disputed, transit under bond." This follows her to Stonehaven. Relevant in S5.
- If Track C: The cleared record means no flagging at Stonehaven entry. Relevant in S5.
- If Track D: Flagged status persists. Stonehaven entry will be harder.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
