# Patrick Stuart on *The Silversmith's Mirror*

## Overall verdict

This module knows exactly what it is about. Aelwen's Mirror is not window-dressing — it is the entire vessel. A grief-object that asks the party (and DM) to articulate what they are willing to lose for being truly seen. Mira's thirty-year refusal is love made visible. The self-image replacement is not mechanical penalty but a wound that will haunt anyone who touches it. The journal page naming *"an ingot I should have forged instead"* is the kind of seed-work that suggests Aelwen doubting herself at the end — not a breadcrumb, but a completed character's uncertainty.

Where this module falters, it falters in texture. Scene 2 is a counter and a rack and a closed ledger. Scene 6 is a six-foot-square dry room with a stand and a cloth. These are functional. They are not *present*. The Mirror deserves a room that knows what it is holding. Mira's shop deserves a grief-smell, a particular silence, a detail that proves thirty years of refusal have worn the stone.

## The Mirror as object

The mirror refuses to reflect anyone *without first pausing*. It is waiting for *"the waiting that is not waiting."* Aelwen wrote that. This is not a magic item; it is a moral test with a silver face.

The Blank-Mirror Trap is the module's deepest gesture. If no one alive has loved you most — if your most-lover has already attuned and taken their seeing with them — the mirror shows nothing. The bearer who attunes to a blank surface is **permanently bound** and loses 30 days of love-capacity. Not memory. Capacity. For thirty days, warmth does not return, grief does. You still remember loving her. You just cannot feel what love is anymore.

This is not a curse in the D&D sense. This is a theological proposition in mechanical form.

The self-image replacement is nearly as strong. *"The bearer can no longer picture themselves 'from outside' without the most-lover's gaze coloring the image."* Dreams show the reflected view. Self-description subtly shifts toward what the reflection revealed. The bearer's sense of who they are *belongs to the reflection*. This is not restored by Greater Restoration. It is not restored by time. It is the cost of having been seen.

**Aelwen is in this room.** The smith-mark pulses when a Vaenshold-line person holds the handle. The obsidian was bought at cost because obsidian *holds* what silver *shows*. The polishing-lines do not perfectly converge — Aelwen could not finish, or chose not to. These are the details that matter.

But Mira's shop upstairs? Her counter is smooth-worn oak. There is a half-worked ingot on the workbench. The rack has six pendants, four pins, two cups. This is *inventory*, not *presence*. Thirty years of refusal should have soaked the walls. Where is that?

## What works

1. **Mira's long sentence in Scene 4.** One exhalation. Ritual, not dialogue. *"Do not interrupt. Let them sit."* The module knows this is a ceremony, not a speech.

2. **Mira is not grateful.** She did not hire you. She will not thank you. She is leaving regardless. She respects competence; she will not repay it. This is honest.

3. **The journal page.** Aelwen naming her own failure — *"I think I should have forged an ingot instead. I did not have the courage. I have been a coward. I have been ashamed. I have been weighed, and found lacking. I will go to the pine by the back of the house now."* This is a writer who understood what it is to end oneself in a sentence.

4. **Kann is commercial.** He will bargain. He will flee at HP ≤ 8. He is not Keloth; he never was Keloth. His moon-silver finger-bone — carrying his dead wife's last minute — he has never attuned. He has refused to hear it. A hostile transactor who has, himself, refused a mirror. The parallel is real.

5. **Seralen's note.** *"Do not look. Do not let Mira look."* Two sentences. Everything Mira's mother could not say alive. Preserved in a drawer for 30 years.

6. **The cross-adventure phrase recovery.** The S02 signal that the party missed on dice — *"I will not look at the rose again"* — now sitting in Mira's ledger margin. Passive Perception 15 fallback. The DM has three routes. The signal does not die.

7. **Option C trusts the PC's voice absolutely.** Spawn the attuner. Let them describe their own reckoning. The response is binding. This is the module refusing to author the PC's grief for them.

8. **Mira's departure in Scene 7.** She does not stay. She does not die. She leaves with the apprentice for Stonfold. The party does not save her from her choice.

## What fails

1. **Scene 2 is functional, not present.** The shop is a counter, a workbench, a rack of goods. Mira is standing silent with a hammer. This is a tableau, not a room. Thirty years of grief have not worn the walls. Where is the silver-oil smell? Where is the dust that won't settle? Where is the clockwise-circle motion Seralen made with the grey rag — not in the Memory Fragment, but in what the party *sees*?

2. **Harel is 15 and nearly a cipher.** She is *"orphaned, observant, has counted six days"* — good — but she has no voice that lands. In Scene 5 she throws a stool. This is the module signaling "she is real" without actually giving her to us. One character-revealing line in Scene 2 (she sets down the cup, she says something specific, she looks at Kessa) and her terror in Scene 5 becomes catastrophic rather than plot-functional.

3. **The five other artifacts are named and not felt.** Wrath-Coin. Pride-Circlet. Thread-That-Frays. An unnamed fifth. These are seeds for #0004-#0007, which is good *planning*. But in *this* session, they are a sentence: *"Three more I only suspect."* Aelwen's vault should haunt Mira's shop. The party should sense *the shape* of what is coming.

4. **Minor loot is scattered and mechanical.** Kann's kit: shortsword, light crossbow, 12 gp. Pevra's kit: scimitar, crossbow, 4 gp, worry-stones. These are treasure-rolls, not wounds. Each death should tell its story. Pevra's worry-stones should be the only real loot off her body; the rest is nothing. Vend has nothing that names him. *Make it hurt that he had nothing.*

5. **The Reorx-shattering rite is undercooked.** Religion DC 15. On success, obsidian shatters; silver un-forges; a small bright vapor escapes. This is a skill check with set dressing. The rite should cost the world something — a hearth that goes dark three towns away; a bell that cracks in a Solamnic chapel; Aelwen herself, one last time, in the wind in the pine by the back of the Vaenshold workshop where she hanged.

6. **Reciprocal Recognition is structurally impossible.** All the reflecteds are dead (Vethrenn, Adda, Duren, Doriel, Varran, Seralen). The ending-curse path that requires the reflected person *alive, present, willing* cannot fire. This is not a failure of design exactly — it is a refusal — but the module should *say* this. Currently it reads as a placeholder.

## Specific recommendations

1. **Rewrite Scene 2's features section with sensory specificity.** The smell of silver-oil and old woodsmoke. A pale rectangle on the wall where Seralen's apron hung until six years ago. The rack's clockwise-polish sheen. A spider that has not been swept from the corner behind the counter in years. Something that tells us thirty years of refusal have happened *here*.

2. **Give Harel one character-revealing line in Scene 2.** Not plot. Character. She hands a cup to Kessa and says: *"My mother polished with a different motion. I cannot make myself do Mira's."* Now her Scene 7 polishing-cup is a lifetime, not a device.

3. **Put one of the other artifacts in Mira's shop.** Not attuned. Not known. A pendant among the rack, forged by Aelwen PC 180, bearing a wrath-mark — Kessa may catch it on Arcana DC 14. If she asks Mira: *"I did not make that. It came to me from my grandmother. I do not know what it is for. I have been afraid of it since I was a child."* The vault widens.

4. **Rewrite loot as wounds.** Pevra's body has *only* the worry-stones and a scrap of ledger-paper with three lines crossed out. Lorn's body has the map and one folded letter never sent — to his sister in east Neraka, unfinished: *"I have been told to tell you—"* Vend's body has nothing. Gorr's body has three gp and a child's drawing of a horse. *Each death should name itself.*

5. **Expand the forge-rite.** The obsidian cracks along Aelwen's incomplete polishing-lines. The silver burns black. The pine by the back of the Vaenshold workshop — eighty miles away — catches a sudden wind that no one but a Wizard of High Sorcery would note. If Kessa is present, she feels Vethrenn's commonplace-book go warm in her pack.

6. **Add a post-session moment for Harel at Stonfold.** In the epilogue, Harel will choose what she carries from Crystalmir. Let the party witness the choice. A silver cup? Seralen's threadbare grey rag? The apprentice's hammer? This is a seed for #0006 — Harel at Stonfold knowing what she chose when her life changed.

## Final line

*The Silversmith's Mirror* understands the difference between a quest and a reckoning; what it still lacks is the particular weight of a room that knows it is holding what cannot be held.
