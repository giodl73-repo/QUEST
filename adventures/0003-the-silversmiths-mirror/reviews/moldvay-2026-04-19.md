# Tom Moldvay on *The Silversmith's Mirror*

## Overall verdict

This is a clean, teachable adventure — the kind that lives at your table without apology. Every room earns its space. The map is legible, the read-alouds know when to shut up, and a DM can run Scene 1 cold without flipping to an appendix. The emotional core (Mira's refusal, the Mirror's waiting) doesn't preach; it *plays*. This is how you build a treasure that carries the story.

## Self-containment check

The module is **table-ready**. A DM can run it cold from `module.md`.

**Stat blocks:** All inlined at point-of-use. Scene 1 gets Lorn. Scene 5 gets full roster (Kann, Pevra, Lorn, Vend, Gorr) + environmental details (darkvision rules, sight-range penalties, night mechanics). Mira and Harel get combat stats inline in Scene 5 descriptor. Encounters Appendix duplicates Scene 3 and Scene 5 for "table-flip" access — redundant but deliberate.

**SRD references:** Attunement rules inlined in Cheatsheet with fast-attune homebrew called out. Conditions (Prone, Surprised, Restrained) inlined with mechanical definitions. Save mechanics noted: *"None required this adventure; attunement voluntary; Blank-Mirror Trap not dice-gated."* XP budget complete with per-character thresholds, encounter multipliers, total session XP. No flipping required.

**DCs visible at point-of-use:** Scene 1 arch-mark is "Passive Perception 10"; Scene 2 ledger is "Investigation DC 13"; Scene 5 has "Perception DC 10 during combat" for Kann's finger-bone. Cheatsheet repeats all DCs organized by check type — a safety net for DMs who miss the inline callout. Scene 3 stealth vs. scouts: "Group Stealth DC 12 → surprise." Scene 6 hidden door: "Investigation DC 13" or Mira opens. Scene 4 Mira insights layer DC 15 / DC 18 / DC 13 in escalating specificity.

**Read-alouds preserved:** Scene 1 opening is 52 words (under Moldvay's 60-word rubric). Scene 2 shop is 59 words. Scene 5 night opening is 49 words. Scene 6 chamber is 37 words + 6-word follow-up. Each read-aloud informs spatial sense without burying encounter mechanics. None repeat the map key's information.

**No "see file X" stubs:** Grep finds zero instances of *"see appendix"* or *"see file"* in the rooms section. Compile-check line at EOF confirms: *"rooms 1-7 inlined; map inlined; stat blocks inlined in rooms AND Encounters Appendix; treasures inlined with full mechanics; NPCs inlined."*

**Treasure mechanics fully inlined:** Mirror's attunement rules (fast-attune, voluntary, one look) in Treasures section. Blank-Mirror Trap mechanics explicit — no living most-lover, mirror shows nothing, attunement permanent, 30-day love-capacity loss, can't attune other items. Ending-curse paths (Reorx-rite at forge Religion DC 15; reciprocal recognition; blank trap irrevocable) all spelled out. Minor loot table shows value + scene + purpose; four "KEY items" called out for campaign continuity.

**Cheatsheet structure:**
- Quick Reference DC table (10 checks; all scenes; consequence per DC).
- Key Saves (explicit: none; attunement voluntary; Blank Trap unrolled).
- Attunement Rules inline (fast-attune, 3-item limit, curse reveal on attunement, breaking mechanics).
- Conditions Used inline (Prone, Surprised, Restrained; explicit exclusions).
- XP Budget (per-character thresholds; encounter multipliers; session total).
- Scene Triggers (13 conditional rules).
- Campaign-continuity entries.
- Rubric v1.2 Tracker.

No "see section X for details" in the cheatsheet. Each entry is self-contained.

## What works

1. **Layered choice architecture.** Three overlapping decisions (Mira's fate, Mirror-looking, Mirror disposition) each route the #0007 finale differently, and the party doesn't know they're routing. It's not a railroad disguised as choice; it's genuine branching that doesn't require the DM to improvise new scenes.

2. **Read-alouds that teach the setting.** Scene 1's "stones worn smooth by three centuries of hands" tells you *why* this town matters without a paragraph about Abanasinian history. The arch mark is found via mechanics, not told.

3. **Stat blocks that fit the encounter.** Kann's Commercial Disengage reaction isn't gamey; it's *him* — he surrenders rather than dies because he's commercial, not zealot. Mira's AC 10, HP 8, one swing is not a cop-out; it's a constraint that forces the party to make her protection a priority, not a side-effect.

4. **Treasure as a lock, not a prize.** The Mirror doesn't just sit in Scene 6 waiting to be picked up. It's guarded by Mira's 30-year refusal, by mechanics (fast-attune, permanent curse), by a Blank-Mirror Trap that requires reading the party's character sheets. The party's decision to look (or not) *becomes the story*.

5. **Campaign continuity woven in, not bolted on.** Aelwen's journal page, Kann's Stonfold finger-bone, Felor's name, Tova Stonfold's existence — all emerge from play. The cheatsheet tells the DM what to write to CLAUDE.md post-session; the module hands off to #0004 cleanly.

6. **Scenes that do double work.** Scene 1 is tavern-entry *and* surveillance reveal *and* anchor-level mark. Scene 3 (conditional) is combat *and* intel-grind *and* Idra-grief-thread. Scene 4 is exposition *and* Mira's reveal *and* Kessa's mirror-hook. No fat.

7. **Town as a real place.** The wandering-pressure tables are light (d6 per hour; d6 per 3 hours) and flavored as townfolk, not encounters. Belg is a child who noticed the "bad men," not a quest-giver. Rillina is nervous *because Kann is actually at the inn*, not because the plot needs her to be.

8. **Inline everything.** The cheatsheet doesn't say "attunement works like 5e DMG page 138." It says *how*. DCs aren't buried; they're on the scene. Conditions are spelled out. XP is pre-calculated. A DM literally doesn't have to open another book.

## What fails

1. **Premise clarity loses a point because "the party is hired to protect Mira" is the *premise hook*, but "Mira refuses the Mirror" is the real emotional premise** — and a DM reading the first two paragraphs doesn't know that yet. The adventure teaches it in play, which is good, but a DM pitching the session to players needs the *why* up front.

2. **Scene 3 (Scouts) is marked conditional but has no fallback for DMs who forget the branching.** If the party doesn't spot surveillance in Scene 1, Scene 3 becomes "empty alleys." That's fine; the module handles it (Tier C assault, no party advantage). But a DM on their first read might miss the trigger entirely. The map could call out: *[3] fills with scouts ONLY if: party obvious in Scene 1 OR Kann is warned later.* One line saves confusion.

3. **Harel is under-equipped for the moment.** She's 15, orphan, trained silversmith. In Scene 5 she throws a stool (Improvised +0, 1d4). The module says *"she has thrown stools before, apparently"* — a joke that undercuts her agency. A line like *"Harel picked up stool-throwing in childhood; she's practiced"* gives her competence without asking the DM to improvise her background.

4. **Mira's stat block says AC 10, HP 8, one swing — good — but doesn't say what happens if she *does* connect.** Does she drive the attacker back? Is she standing on the stair? The module says she fights "if a Bone-Collector reaches Harel's stair" but doesn't describe the spatial positioning. A DM running Scene 5 at pace needs to know: *if Mira's hammer hits, does it block the stair, or does it just wound?*

5. **The Blank-Mirror Trap is mechanically clear but psychologically under-explored.** The module tells DMs: "If the PC's most-lover is dead, the Mirror is blank. Blank-attunement costs 30 days of love-capacity." But it doesn't give the DM a read-aloud for the moment the PC sees a blank mirror. What does blank *feel* like? A single sentence — *"You lift the mirror. Your face resolves — and then fades. The face never comes. The silver stays empty."* — would give DMs the voice to deliver this moment.

6. **Campaign-continuity tracker is strong, but the module doesn't emphasize hard enough that these four entries are *binding on the DM* — not suggestions.** A DM finishing Scene 7 might not realize that "write Aelwen's Mirror disposition to CLAUDE.md" isn't optional. It routes the entire #0007 finale.

7. **Scene 6 Option C (subagent prompt) is sophisticated but has no fallback for DMs who don't have access to an Agent tool.** The prompt says *"spawn them as a subagent via the Agent tool."* In a solo DM context, the DM has to improvise the moment. The module should say: *"If subagent unavailable, use the PC's grief-paragraph from their sheet and ask: 'What do you see?' Let them answer in-character. Mirror is real once they commit to the reflection."*

8. **Read-alouds are good but Scene 4 (Mira's disclosure) has no read-aloud at all.** The scene is all mechanics: Mira's Insight checks (DC 15, 18, 13). But how does *Mira say* it? A DM should be able to copy-paste Mira's disclosure into the session without writing her speech.

## Specific recommendations

1. **Revise Premise to lead with emotional truth.** Top of Summary: change from *"The Varduin Muster arrives to protect Mira"* to *"Mira Vaenshold has held a mirror she refuses to look into for thirty years. Now the people who want to use it have found her. And she's about to leave whether the mirror stays or not."* Then: *"The Muster is hired to protect her against Bone-Collector remnants. The real story is what Mira chooses when the mirror is finally opened."*

2. **Add a one-line trigger callout in Map section.** Under [3] Alleys marker, add: *"NOTE: Scene 3 has scouts ONLY if party was obviously surveillance-aware in Scene 1, OR if Kann is warned in Scene 3 before the party arrives. Otherwise [3] is empty; Scene 5 becomes Tier C (attackers have surprise)."*

3. **Expand Harel's prep line in Scene 5.** Instead of *"she has thrown stools before, apparently,"* write: *"Harel grew up in the plague-year orphanage. She learned young to throw whatever was nearest. Stool, pot, firewood. She will not die like a victim."*

4. **Add spatial-positioning to Mira's combat clause.** Change *"takes position in the hearth-room"* to: *"Stands at the foot of the stair leading up to Harel's room, hammer raised. She fights only if a Bone-Collector tries to pass her. If her swing connects, it's a block — she doesn't yield; she commits her weight to holding the stair. If she misses or her hammer is knocked aside, she's overwhelmed on the next attack unless a PC reaches her."*

5. **Add a read-aloud for Blank-Mirror attunement.** In Scene 6, after the Option C setup, add: *"If the PC sees a blank mirror, read aloud: 'You lift the mirror to your face. For two heartbeats, the silver is empty. No face comes. No one is there. The mirror stays blank. And you feel—' [let the PC describe the feeling in-character before delivering the 30-day cost]."*

6. **Flag campaign-continuity as binding.** Under "Campaign-continuity entries" in Cheatsheet, add a bold header: *"**POST-SESSION (MANDATORY):** Write all four entries below to CLAUDE.md. They gate #0007."*

7. **Add fallback language for Option C subagent.** In Scene 6, after the subagent prompt: *"If Agent tool unavailable, run the moment as: You hold the mirror to your face. Waits 2-3 heartbeats. Then ask the PC, in-character: 'What do you see?' Their answer is binding — write it verbatim in the session log."*

8. **Write Mira's Scene 4 disclosure as dialogue ready to read aloud.** Scene 4 (after the three Insight checks, DC 18 being the "unlock") should include: *"Mira is quiet for a long moment. Then: 'My mother Seralen held my hand while she died. Held it. I was 32. I have not said her name aloud in thirty years. I have counted the days. Every morning I count. I do not know whose face the mirror would show me. Hers. Or no one. I am not willing to find out. But you are here. And I am sixty-two. If the mirror is going to be opened, it is now, or it is never.'"*

## Final line

This is a module that trusts the table and knows when to shut up — Moldvay's instinct exactly. Run it cold.
