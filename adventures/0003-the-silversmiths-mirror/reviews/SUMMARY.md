## Aggregate Verdict

**Strong draft on the edge of shippable.** Moldvay and Stuart both rate the adventure shippable on their weights — Moldvay for craft and table-readiness, Stuart for moral/artifact weight. Gygax, Jaquays, and Schick all land at "strong draft, one more pass" — not because the adventure is weak, but because each of their preferred axes (dungeon integrity, topology, signature-room craft) is where #0003 scores lowest. The design is working; the revisions are about tightening texture, not rebuilding foundation.

## Strongest axes (above 8.0 avg)

1. **Curse/consequence (9.0)** — the Blank-Mirror Trap and permanent self-image replacement are the module's deepest design move. Stuart calls it "a theological proposition in mechanical form." Consensus: this is shippable.
2. **Premise clarity (8.6)** — Kessa's hook lands cold; Mira's 30-year refusal is irresistible. Moldvay notes the *emotional* premise ("Mira refuses the Mirror") is buried under the *hook* premise ("protect the silversmith") — easy fix.
3. **Treasure-as-story (8.6)** — the Mirror IS the session. Stuart scores 10; Gygax scores 7 (his objection: the Mirror has no gp valuation and violates treasure-as-XP).
4. **Lore grounding (8.2)** — moon-silver vein, Vaenshold line, S01/S02 continuity all earned. Stuart: "Aelwen is in this room."
5. **Table-readiness (8.0, divisive)** — Moldvay 10, Stuart 6. The bimodal score is persona-philosophical; every other reviewer lands 8.

## Weakest axes (below 7.5 avg)

1. **Dungeon integrity (6.6)** — consensus weak spot. Gygax: *"This is not a dungeon; it is a town-encounter with a combat scene bolted on."* Jaquays: the alleys-loop is fragile; [6] has no secondary approach. Schick: no verticality; the Mirror's room is generic.
2. **Player agency (7.2)** — Gygax: every move is gated by Mira's NPC-agency. Jaquays: Scene 7's outcome is not negotiable — Mira *will* leave. Agency shapes *how*, not *whether*.
3. **Encounter craft (7.4)** — Scene 5 is the only real combat; competent but generic (Gygax); thin variety (Jaquays, Schick). The prep-tier branching (A/B/C) is universally praised.

## Convergent critiques (3+ reviewers)

1. **Scene 6 (Lower Chamber) is generic — needs signature texture.** Schick calls for rewriting Scene 6 as a signature room (obsidian dust, candlestub patterns, engraved stand). Stuart: *"The Mirror deserves a room that knows what it is holding."* Moldvay implicit in his "Scene 4 needs a read-aloud" critique.
2. **Scene 3 (Scouts) is too optional.** Jaquays, Schick, Moldvay all note: if the party never scouts the alleys, the map collapses to a corridor and the assault becomes Tier C. Recommended fix: have Harel pull a PC aside in Scene 2 with intel, turning Scene 3 from soft-gate to branch-choice.
3. **Mira's Scene 4 long-sentence is high-risk.** Gygax: break it into three question-triggered speeches. Moldvay: add a read-aloud version of her spoken disclosure. Stuart: it *works* as ritual; don't break it. **Divergent recommendation** — authorial call to make.
4. **Option C needs a no-subagent fallback.** Moldvay explicit: *"If Agent tool unavailable, ask the PC 'What do you see?' in-character; their answer is binding."* Gygax wants a mandatory read-step for the PC's sheet before attunement. Actionable fix.
5. **Blank-Mirror Trap needs a read-aloud.** Moldvay and Stuart both flag: the mechanics are clear but the DM has no voice for the moment. Recommended addition: *"You lift the mirror. Your face resolves — and then fades. The face never comes. The silver stays empty."*
6. **Loot should tell stories, not just numbers.** Stuart explicit: each body's loot should name itself. Schick implicit: the Mirror's "priceless" designation contrasts with the combatants' generic gp. Gygax counters from the opposite direction: he wants more measurable gp.
7. **Campaign-continuity entries should be flagged as binding.** Moldvay explicit: *"POST-SESSION (MANDATORY): Write all four entries to CLAUDE.md. They gate #0007."* Low-cost fix.

## Divergent critiques (unique to one reviewer)

- **Gygax:** temporal pressure is too mild; no wandering-monster escalation; treasure-as-XP violated (Mirror has no gp valuation).
- **Jaquays:** [6] Lower Chamber has no secondary approach — no crack in the wall, no shaft, no vent. Topology fails her verticality test.
- **Schick:** Kann's motive should be personal (he has a moon-silver artifact he refuses to attune — make *that* why he wants the Mirror, not the Istaran contract). Also: put one of the *other five artifacts* in Mira's shop (a wrath-marked pendant Kessa can Arcana-catch) to let the Aelwen-vault widen.
- **Moldvay:** Harel needs one character-revealing line in Scene 2; Mira needs spatial-positioning for her combat stat (blocks the stair, commits her weight); the "she has thrown stools before, apparently" joke undercuts Harel's agency.
- **Stuart:** Scene 2 needs sensory presence (silver-oil smell; pale rectangle where Seralen's apron hung; clockwise-polish sheen). Scene 6 needs obsidian dust and incomplete polishing-lines. Pevra's body should have *only* Idra's worry-stones and one crossed-out ledger line; Vend's body should have nothing and the absence should hurt. The Reorx-rite should cost the world — a pine catching wind 80 miles away where Aelwen hanged.

## Recommended revision pass (action items by priority)

**P0 — Convergent, low-cost, high-impact:**

1. **Scene 2/6: Add sensory texture** (Stuart + Schick) — silver-oil smell, dust, obsidian-residue, Aelwen's fingerprint marks on the stand. One paragraph per scene.
2. **Blank-Mirror read-aloud** (Moldvay + Stuart) — add a 2-3 sentence read-aloud block in Scene 6 for blank-attunement.
3. **Option C no-Agent fallback** (Moldvay) — add 1-2 lines in Scene 6 for DMs without subagent tooling.
4. **Campaign-continuity binding callout** (Moldvay) — bold "POST-SESSION (MANDATORY)" header in cheatsheet.
5. **Scene 3 unavoidable-ification** (Schick + Moldvay + Jaquays) — Harel or Mira pushes intel to a PC in Scene 2.
6. **Harel character-line in Scene 2** (Moldvay + Stuart) — one sentence in which she is specifically a person, not a plot-token.
7. **Mira combat-spatial clause** (Moldvay) — clarify she blocks the stair; commits her weight; overwhelmed next turn if she misses.

**P1 — Divergent but worth considering:**

8. **Scene 4 Mira read-aloud speech** (Moldvay) — write her DC-18-unlock disclosure as ready-to-read dialogue. (Gygax would prefer breaking the long sentence; Stuart warns against. Author's call.)
9. **Kann's moon-silver finger-bone description** (Gygax + Stuart) — expand from "30 gp raw" to a paragraph that describes what Kann refused.
10. **Second artifact in Mira's shop** (Schick) — put a wrath-marked pendant on the rack; let Kessa Arcana-catch it.
11. **Reorx-rite cost-to-the-world** (Stuart) — expand the forge-rite success narration with world-impact detail (wind in the pine, Vethrenn's book going warm).

**P2 — Structural, high-effort, persona-specific:**

12. **Secondary approach to [6]** (Jaquays) — crack in eastern wall with Strength-squeeze; unlocks after Scene 3 if Kessa reads the foundation.
13. **Temporal-pressure escalation** (Gygax) — add Pressure Ticks to Town Pulse 5-6 outcomes; three ticks → Kann arrives early.
14. **Rewrite minor loot as wounds** (Stuart) — each body has exactly one object that names them.

## Divergences worth naming

**Mira's long sentence (Scene 4):**
- Gygax: break it into three shorter speeches.
- Stuart: it works as ritual; do not break it.
- Moldvay: keep it, add a read-aloud version for the DM's voice.

**Recommendation:** Keep Mira's long sentence as-written (Stuart is right about its ritual function), but add Moldvay's read-aloud version as an *alternate* path for tables that need the compressed form.

**Table-readiness (6 vs. 10):**
- Moldvay: 10 — cheatsheet inlines everything; no stubs.
- Stuart: 6 — the prose of Scene 4 is not ready to *speak* at the table.

**Recommendation:** Both are right on their own terms. Moldvay scores the procedural correctness; Stuart scores the voice. Add Moldvay's read-aloud Mira speech to close Stuart's gap without breaking Moldvay's praise.

## Go/No-Go verdict

**GO to play at S03** with P0 revisions applied as light authorial pass (estimated 45-60 minutes of module.md edits). The adventure is operating at the "strong draft on the edge of shippable" mark; none of the convergent critiques invalidate the core design, and the Moldvay/Stuart shippable rating captures that the emotional+craft foundations are intact.

**Post-S03**, the persona panel should reconvene to score the *played* session — where the real test of Stuart's "presence" critique and Gygax's "player agency" critique happens at the table.
