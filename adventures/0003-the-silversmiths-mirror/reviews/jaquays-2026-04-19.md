# Jennell Jaquays on *The Silversmith's Mirror*

## Overall verdict

You've built something genuinely uncommon: a town-plus-shop that honors geography instead of treating it as backdrop. Mira's place has multiple approaches — you enter through the market, branch into alleys, or breach the back — and the verticality from shop counter down through living quarters to the hidden chamber below creates real navigational stakes. The party can map this in their head and *use* the map to win. But the whole structure depends on one scene (Scene 4) that works only if the DM doesn't rush it, and the topology falls apart if your players never scout the alleys. You've done the hard work; it lives or dies on table discipline and player curiosity.

## Topology analysis

The town-shop is the dungeon. Let me map what you've given me:

**Ground level (town):**
- [1] Market Square: entrance point, well-central, three children playing, one Bone-Collector scout (Lorn). Town-NPCs named; this is lived-in.
- [3] Alleys: Z-path (narrow, three turns) connecting market to back-alley service door. Scouts hide here. Smell of old mortar distinguishes it from the market.

**Shop level ([2]):**
- Upper counter + workbench. Front door (bell) from market. Back service door (unlocked, kicks open) from alleys. Door behind counter leads to private space [4]. Harel upstairs.

**Living level ([4]):**
- Mira's hearth-room, three chairs, cupboard (Aelwen's journal page inside). Stair down to kitchen and cellar.

**Cellar → Lower Chamber ([6]):**
- Kitchen trapdoor (unlocked) to cellar. Cellar to [6] via hidden door (silver-filigree latch, DC 13 Investigation OR Mira opens). [6] is 6×6, plain oak stand, cloth-covered mirror, Seralen's "Do not look" note in drawer.

**The loop:** [1] Market ↔ [2] Shop (front door) ↔ [3] Alleys (back service door) ↔ [1] Market. This is real. The party can map it. They can route encounters, plan ambushes, use the alleys to flank.

**The verticality:** Counter [2] → through door → stair to [4] Hearth → stair down to kitchen → trapdoor to cellar → hidden door to [6]. Three **real** levels. But only one vertical connection per level (no shaft, no shortcut bypass). The hidden door to [6] is a bottleneck — you cannot avoid Mira or the Investigation check. This is intentional and correct.

**What works in the topology:**
- The loop is genuine. Party can enter market, drift to alleys, return via back door. Combat in Scene 5 uses all three approaches (Kann front, Pevra back, Lorn roof-to-upstairs-window). The map teaches itself through assault logistics.
- The vertical descent is *earned*. Getting to [6] requires: (a) defeating Kann's assault, (b) either passing Mira's trust (Scene 4) to have her open the door, or (c) rolling Investigation 13 in the cellar. Two valid paths; one requires social success, one requires skill-check success.
- Horizontally, the alleys are *distinguished* from the market (smell, narrowness, cover). This is the Jaquaysing — place has geography beyond room-description.

**What fails in the topology:**
- **Scene 3 is optional.** If the party never scouts the alleys, Scene 3 is empty; the assault in Scene 5 becomes Tier C (no intel, attackers surprise). The loop exists only if the party discovers it. A group that goes Market → Shop → Hearth → Cellar → Combat, without ever entering the alleys, has collapsed the map to a corridor with a side-room. You mitigate this with GM notes ("Let Scene 1's choice matter") but you cannot force it.
- **The hidden door to [6] is a hard gate.** No party will blast through it without investigation or NPC permission. This is not bad per se — it's a threshold — but it means [6] has no approach that bypasses the cellar. There is no way to reach the chamber from outside, no way to tunnel in, no way to climb through a window. [6] is a room, not a location with geography.
- **The back-door break-in (Scene 5, Pevra's entry) is the only real "use-the-map-to-win" moment.** If the party figured out the back alley in Scene 3, they can pre-position a defender there in Scene 5. Otherwise, Pevra's breakthrough is a surprise. The mechanics of *using* the loop for tactical advantage exist only if discovered in advance.

**Verticality verdict:**
Three levels, achieved. One stair, one trapdoor, one hidden door. No shafts, no leaps, no shortcuts that unlock new paths retroactively. The vertical movement is *sequential* — you must pass through each level to reach the next. This is not Caverns of Thracia; it's a linear descent with lateral branches. It works for the session-pacing and Mira's arc, but it is not particularly vertical in the way I value it.

## What works

1. **Mira's monologue in Scene 4** — one long sentence, load-bearing. If the DM reads it slowly and does not interrupt, it lands. This is restraint; this is craft.
2. **The scout map (Scene 3 loot).** Concrete payoff for scouting. Players who invest in alleys-reconnaissance get the Bone-Collector's own tactical diagram. Scene 5 prep tiers are *built on this artifact*. Well done.
3. **The blank-mirror trap.** Kessa's Vethrenn hook — was Vethrenn's love transmission or love? The curse mechanism is specific and targets PC sheets directly. This is not a generic "roll saves" trap.
4. **NPCs are named and have real decisions.** Mira will leave Crystalmir regardless of party outcome. Harel is not a quest-object; she's a girl who throws stools. Even Kann has a commercial logic (survives on practical head-clearing). Inhabitants move; they're not furniture.
5. **The town is lived-in.** Townsfolk have names (Belg, Hela, Orin, Tam, Poll). A child notices the party. A cloth-merchant will speak about Mira. The well is the town's name-giver. This is not a random-encounter table; it's a place.
6. **Rubric v1.2 compliance** — anchor-level symptoms have passive-Perception fallbacks; per-PC reception tracking; Memory Fragments in each scene; DM-obligation callouts. You've built recovery-paths for missed signals.
7. **The cross-adventure phrase recovery.** Mira's ledger contains *"I will not look at the rose again"* — the same phrase (uncredited) that appears in S02's Keloth-vault ledger. A careful Kessa (passive Perception 15) will notice. Or Mira will point: *"the middle page is older than I am; you may look."* Two paths; one active, one passive. This is how you re-land a signal.

## What fails

1. **The alleys-loop is fragile.** If the party ignores Scene 3, they've collapsed the map. You mitigate with GM-notes and random-encounter triggers. But a group that commits to Market → Shop → Descend → Combat without ever checking the alleys will play a linear module with a loop you drew that they never traced. This is not a design failure; it's a GM-execution dependency.

2. **[6] Lower Chamber has no secondary approach.** There is no clever way to reach the mirror except through Mira's cellar. There is no window you can smash, no tunnel, no shelf you can climb. The room is *under* the shop but has no spatial relationship to it. If the party never breaches the kitchen trapdoor (they might choose not to), they never see [6]. This is fine for story-gating; it's not spatial.

3. **The hidden door to [6] is pure DC-check gating.** Investigation DC 13 to spot the filigree latch. Mira can open it, but she will not — Scene 4 disclosure explicitly does *not* include opening the lower chamber. So: Investigation check or wait for morning. No third path. No way to persuade Mira early. This is a threshold, not a choice.

4. **Encounter variety is thin.** Scene 3 is scouts (optional). Scene 5 is the Bone-Collector assault (mandatory). That is two combats. Everything else is social / discovery / decision. The assault is well-designed (Tier A/B/C scaling is excellent), but if your players breeze through or avoid Scene 3, the session has *one* real combat.

5. **No wandering consequences for lingering.** Scene 5's Night Assault is scripted to happen after midnight. The module says: roll Town Pulse once per hour during the day/evening. But if the party sits in the shop for 6 hours without moving, the pressure does not escalate. Kann is approaching; the town-watch is not mobilizing; the NPCs do not panic. The temporal pressure is atmospheric, not mechanical.

6. **Mira's arc is pre-decided.** Mira *will* leave Crystalmir. The Mirror will go with her, or the party will take it, or they'll destroy it. They do not get to convince Mira to stay, reopen the shop, and keep the artifact in town. This is narratively right (Mira's arc is complete) but it removes one class of player-agency: negotiating a different outcome with an NPC.

## Specific recommendations

1. **Consider a second entry to [6] for skilled parties.** Example: a crack in the lower-chamber's eastern wall (tight squeeze, no armor, Strength DC 12) that connects to the cellar's foundation-breach. Undetectable from the cellar side without tools. Tight enough that Kann's assault cannot use it. This gives a rogue-and-light-party a way to outmaneuver the hidden door, and rewards the kind of imaginative spatial-thinking I prize.

2. **Escalate the temporal pressure in Town Pulse.** Example: On the hour, roll Town Pulse. On a 5-6, add a **Pressure tick**: Kann's scouts are sighted outside town, fires lit on the western ridge, town-watch gathering equipment, Mira saying *"tonight, maybe sooner."* Three Pressure ticks → Kann arrives (assault begins). This makes lingering mechanically costly.

3. **Give the alleys a reason to be visited twice.** Scene 3 is the party scouting for intel. But what if a successful-enough Scene 3 allows the party to place an *ambush* — lure Kann into the alleys for a pre-emptive fight? Example: Pevra (if surrendered and treated well) agrees to lead Kann toward the shop via the alleys, and the party waits in cover. This turns Scene 3 scouting into a branching tactical choice.

4. **The lower chamber feels isolated.** Why does [6] have *no* connection to the shop's footprint above? Example: Add a detail that the lower chamber's ceiling is the hearth-room's floor (they can hear Mira moving upstairs). Or: a small iron vent from the chamber to the shop's chimney. Something that makes [6] *spatially part of* the structure instead of simply *below* it.

5. **Reward scouting of Mira's shop itself.** If a party tries to peer through the peephole, pick the lock, or otherwise scout *before* Mira invites them in, let them discover Aelwen's journal page evidence or hear Harel moving upstairs. This gives investigators early payoff and makes Scene 2 a place of discovery rather than only social negotiation.

## Final line

You've built a genuine place — not a corridor with decorations — and you've trusted your players to map it; the module just asks your table to be curious enough to explore it before committing to the descent.
