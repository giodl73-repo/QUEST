## Critique

### Overall assessment

A tactically clean session. Prep-tier machinery worked as designed — Thera's CRIT 23 Scene 1 produced full scene-5 prep, the night assault resolved in two rounds at zero party damage, dice disciplined throughout. The only mechanical edge-case that hurt was a *positional* anchor-symptom failure that the rubric's v1.2 passive-fallback did not anticipate. Otherwise: this is how the math is supposed to run.

### What worked

- **Scene 5 two-round zero-damage resolution.** Attackers: Kann + Lorn + Vend + Gorr (400 XP medium, down from 500 because Pevra was captured Scene 3). Prep tier was Partial-plus — verbal intel from Pevra but no map. Party still pre-positioned correctly: Aelric front, Grom central, Kessa line-of-sight, Thera upstairs. Round 1: Thera one-shots Lorn (22 vs 13, 16 damage vs 16 HP) through the window; Aelric javelin + Kessa Magic Missile drop Kann to 5 HP from the street. Round 2: Kann flees at Dash, Kessa finishes him with second missile; Grom Guiding Bolt (23 to hit) drops Vend. Gorr surrenders. Four attackers neutralized in 12 rounds of action economy total. Mechanics respected.
- **Scene 7 Portent rescue.** Grom Religion 8 (fail); Kessa Portent [14] replacement; effective 17 (pass). The die was pre-rolled at session start; Kessa held it for "clutch save" per her PC sheet; she spent it on another PC's ritual. Clean Divination School mechanics applied to a non-combat Religion roll. Dice integrity preserved; emergent story produced.

### What needs work

- **Scene 6 filigree-dim anchor-symptom positional failure.** Module specifies the shop signage dims "upstairs" when the Mirror's cloth lifts. Passive-Perception DC 14 fallback. Nobody was upstairs (all party in chamber; Mira + Harel in hearth; Harel's passive 10 < 14). The symptom had a v1.2-compliant passive-fallback *documented*, but the fallback can only fire if someone is in-position to roll. This is a distinct failure mode from the one v1.2 was designed to fix (single-point misses on gated rolls). Rubric doesn't capture it.
- **Scene 3 tension valve released early.** Pevra solo-ambushed; 16-damage one-shot from Thera put her down before Round 2 started; Aelric's subsequent grapple was unopposed. Total tactical challenge: one Stealth check + one attack roll. For a 4×L3 party at Tier 1, this is under-dangerous. Module specifies partial intel tier would have Pevra + Lorn both present; Lorn's rotation to alleys happens "at dusk" but the party arrived pre-dusk and engaged Pevra alone. Suggest: tighten Lorn's rotation to "when Pevra signals" so Lorn arrives mid-ambush if Pevra fails to signal.

### The question I'd push on

**Why did Grom's Reorx-rite need Portent in the first place?** Grom's Religion modifier is +3. DC 15. Expected average roll: 10.5 + 3 = 13.5. Miss-rate: ~60%. For a campaign-defining ritual at a proper forge, 60% miss is fragile. The module should either (a) give Grom advantage in forge-conditions, (b) halve the DC on successful preparation (ingredient ritual + dawn + consent), or (c) let the rite take multiple attempts. As-written, it took a Portent die to succeed — which is a Divination wizard's lottery ticket, not a forge-priest's domain. The priest's move was insufficient; the wizard rescued it. That's backwards.
