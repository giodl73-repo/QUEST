## Critique

### Overall assessment

A new player could follow this session. The module ran cold — P0 revisions inlined the tricky bits (Scene 2 sensory texture, Harel's aside, Scene 6 chamber detail, Blank-Mirror read-aloud, Option C no-Agent fallback, Mandatory continuity callout). The setting glosses held ("silversmith's kin"; "est sularus oth mithas"; "Vaenshold"). The cross-adventure phrase recovery landed cleanly because the module flagged it three ways (Investigation 13, passive 15, DM-prompt fallback) and the session didn't need any of the fallbacks — the active check hit. This is the first session the anthology-floor + campaign-reward policy has been *tested at a table with campaign depth already loaded,* and it passed for a reader who came in cold.

### What worked

- **The glosses landed naturally in play.** Mira addressed Kessa as *"silversmith's kin"* (Scene 2); the DM did not gloss it because the module had already primed the reader in the accessibility policy. Aelric spoke the Oath (*"Est Sularus oth Mithas"*) in Scene 5 with the translation (*"my honor is my life"*) present in the cheatsheet for on-demand lookup. A fresh face needed to look up exactly zero terms in play; everything was inline or context-deliverable.
- **The seven-scene structure has a legible arc.** Arrival → meet-the-keeper → scout → disclosure → assault → chamber → dawn. A new player can name each scene's purpose without notes. Compare: S02's route-trap-ambush-waystation-vault-raid structure had six named locations but no clean arc-shape; S03's three-act-structure (establish → crisis → reckoning) holds together.
- **No rule-lookup required during Scene 5 combat.** Stat blocks for all five attackers (reduced to four post-Pevra-capture) inlined. Commercial Disengage reaction called out. Darkvision asymmetry (Grom + Kessa have it; Aelric + Thera don't) called out. Candlelight + moon conditions spelled out. The DM did not pause once for mechanics lookup. Cheatsheet handled attunement, conditions, XP budget inline.
- **The Blank-Mirror Trap was clearly enough communicated that four PCs declined it.** The module's treasure-section explains: if your most-lover is dead AND has not attuned this mirror, mirror shows their pre-death view; if no one alive has loved you most, mirror is blank; blank-attunement is permanent and costs 30 days of love-capacity. All four PCs visibly weighed their grief-paragraphs against the trap. Kessa articulated the Vethrenn blank-risk explicitly. This is a complex mechanic that a fresh-face table would ordinarily mis-execute; the module's clarity prevented that.

### What needs work

- **Scene 4's disclosure requires the DM to slow the tempo.** Mira's ~100-word long sentence only lands if the DM reads it without interrupting and the players don't talk over it. Module notes this explicitly (*"Do not interrupt. Let the players sit."*). But a first-time DM running this adventure may not know how to hold a silent 30 seconds of table-time. Suggest: module could add *"If the sentence lands silent, count ten heartbeats before moving on."* This turns it into an explicit procedural cue, not a vibe-check.
- **Portent is complicated for new wizards.** Kessa's Portent [14] rescue of Grom's rite worked cleanly, but the player playing Kessa needed to know: (a) Portent pre-rolls at start of session; (b) can be used to replace any d20 including another creature's; (c) declared after the roll but before outcome is announced. This is three sub-rules. The module's character-sheet already covers them, but the cheatsheet doesn't surface Portent in the scene-triggers. For a new Kessa-player, a one-line reminder in the DM cheatsheet — *"Kessa may spend her Portent die on any roll including NPCs' — consult her sheet"* — would prevent the move from being missed.

### The question I'd push on

**Does the campaign-awareness policy still hold as the campaign deepens?** S03 is the first session where campaign-specific context (S01/S02 artifacts, PC campaign-permanent states like Aelric's Varran-memories, cross-adventure phrase recovery, Mira's S01 seed) is actually *load-bearing* for comprehension. A fresh face reading S03-log.md cold would understand the Mirror and the Muster's choices, but would not feel the weight of the cross-adventure phrase landing (because they haven't read S02). The policy said anthology-floor + campaign-reward; this session's reward is larger than previous. By S05 the reward will be larger still. At some point the anthology-floor breaks — a fresh reader can't enter S06 without S05 context. We should decide *which session is the last anthology-floor-safe entry point* before we get there. My read: S03 is still safe; S04 will be.
