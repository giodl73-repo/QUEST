## Critique

### Overall assessment

The best session the Muster has played. Every PC was *themselves* — not a bundle of skills, not a class identity, but the specific person on the sheet. Kessa's scholarly restraint, Grom's craft-witness honor, Aelric's Measure-bound discipline, Thera's quiet-seriousness — four distinct voices produced four distinct decisions that *combined* into a single outcome none of them individually chose. That is character-made-choice at its best.

### What worked

- **Kessa catalog'd rather than attuned.** Her PC-sheet heuristic priority-1 is "Understand it." She could have rolled 1 on her d6 and still justified attunement as "I must understand this by experience." She didn't. She justified restraint: *"On my preliminary reading, this object will test whether anyone has loved me most. My mentor Vethrenn went silent a decade before I was born. I never met her. I do not know if what she felt toward me counts. I will not risk a blank."* The quote-Vethrenn-without-realizing-it is the sheet's own voice-tag firing. She then used her *silver-ink pen signature move* to write the observation. This is sheet-deep-reader behavior at full volume.
- **Mira writing Seralen's name in ink (Scene 7).** Unscripted. Module did not specify this. After the rite, Mira asked Thera for a piece of notebook, wrote *"For the Council file. A daughter's note. My mother's name was Seralen. I am grateful. — M. Vaenshold."* The ink-writing completes the arc Scene 4 opened when Mira said Seralen's name aloud for the first time in 30 years. That's how arcs close — not with dice, but with an NPC's small gesture. Grom put the note in his pouch with Duren's tongs. Two forge-priests, one note.
- **Grom's silent benediction Scene 4.** Insight 23 read Mira's 30-year grief; Grom's response was four words — *"Seralen was loved."* No dice. Mira closed her eyes for a long moment, then nodded. Craft-witness in action. Player-style cluster evidence.
- **Aelric's Measure-bound restraint at the chamber threshold.** *"The Measure does not call me here."* He did not enter Scene 6's chamber. A Solamnic paladin refusing to enter a room because it is not his business to be there — that's Oath discipline the module didn't ask for. He chose it.

### What needs work

- **The module's attunement architecture assumed a PC would take the offer.** Scene 6 had an Option C subagent prompt, a Blank-Mirror read-aloud (P0 revision), a whole apparatus. None of it fired. The four PCs collectively declined. This isn't a failure — it is the module's design meeting the players' truth and the players winning. But the module has *more design-weight on "a PC attunes"* than on "nobody attunes and they choose destruction." The destruction-path (Scene 7 option C) has a single Religion DC 15 check. The attunement-path has a subagent prompt, mechanics, campaign-continuity trigger. If no-attunement is a legitimate outcome — and it is — it deserves equal design-weight.
- **Thera was quieter than she should have been.** Her d6 rolled "profit" in Scene 6 and she took the hint. Fine. But she had one of the session's biggest in-character moments — picking up Seralen's *"Do not look"* note, folding it three times, tucking it in her pocket without saying why — and the scene didn't land on her the way it landed on Kessa or Grom. Her Adda-bread grief could have surfaced here and didn't. The doubt-die was honest; the atmosphere didn't chain to Thera.

### The question I'd push on

**What did Kessa *give up* by not attuning?** The Mirror was her spotlight artifact. The module pointed its whole mechanism at her (Vethrenn / Blank-Mirror Trap / scholarly conclusion). She declined. That's legitimate. But the session ends without her ever *seeing* the thing she came to Crystalmir to see. She catalogs its refusal instead of experiencing it. Is that a win for the character — proving she can resist her own curiosity — or a loss for the campaign, because the spotlight-PC's emotional arc for this adventure is that she *didn't learn what she came to learn*? The next session should probably address this explicitly. Kessa cannot go on being the PC who almost attunes and does not — the character will calcify into a signature refusal that reads as cowardice from the outside.
