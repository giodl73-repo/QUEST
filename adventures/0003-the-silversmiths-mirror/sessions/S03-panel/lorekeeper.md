## Critique

### Overall assessment

The Vaenshold line now has flesh. Aelwen in PC 179 with her journal page; Heva rescuing the page from the pyre; Rillina → Avel → Seralen → Mira across five generations; Stonfold smith-family confirmed by artifact (Kann's finger-bone) and name (Tova Stonfold, spoken by Mira). The Moon-Silver Cycle's setting is no longer a blueprint — it is a genealogy with voices. And the cross-adventure signal from S02 — *"I will not look at the rose again"* — finally surfaced. The echo lands in session three of seven. That is a long-signal design paying off exactly as intended.

### What worked

- **Kessa caught the cross-adventure phrase in Mira's ledger (Scene 4, Investigation 16).** The signal was planted in S01 (Vesk's phrase), appeared uncredited on Keloth's wall-ledger in S02 (missed on dice), and now appears in Mira's marginalia from decades ago — same buyer, same phrase, different silversmith, different century. *"Paid in full; I will not look at the rose again."* Mira said *nyet* when asked. Kessa wrote it verbatim into Vethrenn's commonplace book. The canon recovery path — rubric v1.2 anchor-level — worked on first attempt at the new adventure. This is the model for cross-adventure signaling going forward.
- **Aelwen's journal page, delivered by obligation.** *"I made six. I made the Rose first. I made the Reliquary second. I made the Mirror third. I made three more in secret after the Mirror and I can no longer remember what I named them. I think I should have forged an ingot instead."* Hint 4 pre-planted. Aelwen now has a voice and an unfinished work. The finale's Silver-Ingot Confession has its first seed.
- **The obsidian shattered along Aelwen's unconverged polish-lines.** Kessa identified the unconverged polish in Scene 6 via Arcana 24 — a lorekeeper's detail (moon-silver is normally fully converged; Aelwen stopped deliberately). That same detail was the *line along which the Reorx-rite destruction fell* in Scene 7. The setting-detail was not ornament; it was load-bearing. Aelwen's hesitation at the polish is what made the Mirror destructible at dawn. That is the setting producing mechanics, which is how a world should behave.
- **Stonfold smith-family confirmed twice.** Named by Mira (Tova Stonfold, her mother's cousin) in Scene 4; demonstrated by artifact (Kann's Stonfold-vein finger-bone, ID'd by Kessa) in Scene 5. Two independent confirmations of the non-Vaenshold smith-family's existence. Seed for #0006 + #0007 is not a single thread; it is a braid.

### What needs work

- **The filigree-dim anchor symptom did not land.** The Vaenshold smith-mark on the shop's street signage — the setting's most visible smith-mark across two scenes — was designed to dim by one perceptible degree when the Mirror is uncovered. It is a *canon signature*: the Vaenshold line's own mark responding to its making. Nobody was in-position to perceive it. Passive-Perception DC 14 fallback failed because no witness. The canon-detail was activated and nobody saw. This is a loss for the setting — the world reacted and the party did not witness the reaction.
- **The six artifacts are still mostly ghosts.** Aelwen made six: Rose, Reliquary, Mirror (seen), Wrath-Coin, Pride-Circlet, Thread-That-Frays, and one unnamed. Four remain *unseen*. The module mentioned them in a single sentence of Mira's disclosure. The Stuart review during design said "put one in the shop." I agreed. Nothing of that landed — the shop had standard pendants and cups, no secondary artifact for Kessa to Arcana-catch. The vein should have *scope* by the end of session three; instead it has three artifacts present and four in-name-only.

### The question I'd push on

**Is destroying the Mirror a canon act or a canon erasure?** Aelwen made six objects. The Vaenshold line refused all six for generations. Mira hands the Mirror to the party and they destroy it at Grom's rite. This is narratively satisfying. But canon-wise: is Aelwen's *third forging* — one of the six inscribed on the finale's Silver-Ingot Confession — now gone? Does #0007's inversion still account for six, or does it now account for five? The campaign-continuity entry should specify: *the Mirror is destroyed; the ingot-confession references it as a completed absence, not an uncompleted one.* Otherwise #0007's finale-logic breaks. This is a lore-trap that the campaign-planner should address before #0004 drafts.
