## Most Dissent

**Module fidelity (all scored 8/10, but the lenses disagree about *why*.)**

- **Tactician:** cap is from the filigree-dim positional failure (mechanical edge case not captured by v1.2 passive-fallback anchor).
- **Lorekeeper:** cap is from the filigree-dim AS CANON LOSS (the setting reacted and the party did not witness).
- **Roleplayer:** cap is from the module's attunement-architecture being over-weighted relative to non-attunement paths.
- **Improviser:** cap is from the DM not finding an improvised angle to land the filigree-dim.
- **Fresh Face:** cap is low-weighted; not a focus.

Four lenses converge on the filigree-dim symptom as the cap-cause, but for four *different reasons*. This suggests a rubric-design question: does v1.2's anchor-level-passive-fallback anchor capture what went wrong? The Tactician says no (positional-failure is distinct). The Lorekeeper says partially (the canon-loss framing is lens-specific). The Roleplayer says no (the issue is architecture-weighting, not fallback-mechanics). The Improviser says the rubric should require the DM to try.

## Convergent Suggestions (multiple lenses agree)

1. **Anchor-level symptoms need positional-redundancy, not just DC-fallback.** Tactician + Lorekeeper + Improviser. An anchor symptom that can only be perceived from a specific location has a positional-failure mode that v1.2's passive-Perception fallback doesn't address. Suggest: **rubric amendment proposal E** — anchor-level symptoms require either (a) a check-gate + passive fallback at DC+2 (v1.2 current), OR (b) redundant placement in at least two locations/scenes, OR (c) an NPC-delivered recall as final fallback. The three redundancy-paths should be OR'd, not AND'd.

2. **No-attunement / path-declined outcomes need equal design-weight to attunement paths.** Roleplayer + Improviser. The module invested heavily in Scene 6 attunement apparatus (Option C subagent, Blank-Mirror read-aloud, campaign-continuity trigger). The destruction-path had one Religion DC 15. If attunement-declined is a legitimate outcome — and it is — future modules should have comparable design-weight on the declined path. For #0003 specifically: the destruction-rite should have pre-written narration (the obsidian shattering-along-polish-lines was DM-improvised; the module didn't provide it).

3. **Scene 4 disclosure needs explicit silence-cue.** Fresh Face + Roleplayer. Mira's long sentence only lands if the DM holds the silence after. Module should add a procedural cue ("count ten heartbeats before moving on") to make the silence explicit rather than vibe-dependent.

4. **Cross-adventure signal recovery worked as designed and should be the template.** Lorekeeper + Improviser + Fresh Face. The rubric v1.2 active-check + passive-fallback + DM-prompt-fallback triple-path was correctly deployed; Kessa caught the phrase on active Investigation 16; no fallback was needed but all were documented. This is the model for #0004+.

## Divergent Suggestions (single-lens)

- **(Tactician) Grom's Reorx-rite needs forge-advantage.** Grom's +3 Religion vs DC 15 has a 60% miss-rate. For a campaign-defining ritual at a proper forge with consent, this is fragile. Suggest: advantage on forge-rite Religion when performed at a ≥1800°C forge at dawn with the artifact's keeper present. (Would have bypassed Kessa's Portent rescue. Intentional trade.)

- **(Lorekeeper) The six artifacts need more presence in this adventure's shop.** Aelwen made six; we saw three (Rose, Reliquary, Mirror). Four remain ghosts. Mira's shop should have had at least one additional artifact for Kessa to Arcana-catch. (Called out in the design review; did not land in play.)

- **(Roleplayer) Thera was underweight this session.** Her doubt-die rolled "profit" and the atmosphere didn't chain to her Adda grief. Her quietness is character-consistent but spotlight-adjacent PCs need at least one atmospheric landing per session.

- **(Improviser) The module's Kann-escape intent should be preserved over dice when campaign-continuity is at stake.** Kessa's Magic Missile killed Kann against the module's preferred outcome. Legitimate dice result; campaign-continuity cost. Call the rule before S04.

- **(Fresh Face) Portent needs a cheatsheet line.** One-line DM-reminder: "Kessa may spend her Portent die on any roll including NPCs'."

## Questions Raised About the Module or the System

1. **Positional-failure anchor (rubric v1.3 candidate).** v1.2's passive-Perception fallback anchor assumes a PC is positioned to roll. S03 showed a distinct failure mode: *nobody in-position*. Propose **Amendment E** (positional-redundancy anchor).

2. **Declined-path design-weight (module-template question).** Modules with major attunement decisions should have design-weight-parity between the *attune* and *decline* paths. This is a template-level change for `dungeon-smith` / `treasure-forger`, not a rubric change.

3. **Campaign-continuity vs dice authority (process question).** When the module's preferred outcome (Kann escapes for #0006/#0007) conflicts with dice (Kessa's Magic Missile kills Kann), who wins? S03 let dice win. The campaign-planner should write a one-line rule for this before S04: *"Module-preferred outcomes are design-preferences, not design-guarantees. Dice are authoritative; the campaign adapts."* This is the Improviser's question explicitly called.

4. **Kessa's arc-risk (character-continuity question).** The Roleplayer flagged: Kessa is the PC who "almost attunes and does not" — she declined Keloth's bracelet S02, declined the Mirror S03. By S05 she will be the PC-who-refuses-twice. Future adventures should give her a positive-attunement opportunity she *can* take, not only negative-attunement opportunities she refuses. This is a party-level consideration for `campaign-planner`.

5. **Panel spread narrowing (maturity signal).** S01 spread 4.2; S02 spread 3.8; S03 spread 0.9. The narrowing spread suggests the system (module + rubric + party + DM) is stabilizing. If S04 spread is also <1.5, we can consider the playtest pipeline mature. If S04 spread widens, something regressed.
