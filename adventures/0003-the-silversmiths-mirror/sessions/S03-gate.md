## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Positional-failure edge case for anchor-level passive-fallback
Dimension affected: Module Fidelity
What happened: Scene 6 — the filigree-dim anchor symptom triggers "upstairs" when the cloth is lifted in the lower chamber. Passive-Perception DC 14 fallback. Nobody was positioned upstairs at that moment (all party in chamber; Harel + Mira in hearth; Harel's passive 10 below DC 14). The symptom had a passive-fallback documented per v1.2 — but the fallback requires someone to be *positioned* to roll.
Why the rubric missed it: v1.2's anchor-level-passive-fallback anchor solves the "one-point-miss on a gated roll" problem (S02 cross-adventure phrase). It does NOT solve the "nobody-to-roll" problem. Positional failures are a distinct failure mode.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Finder-vs-receiver PARTIAL reception (pattern not fully forged)
Dimension affected: Atmospheric Landing
What happened: Scene 4 — Kessa caught the cross-adventure phrase in Mira's ledger (Investigation 16, active hit) but then rolled Investigation 9 on cross-referencing her own S02 notebook. She has the phrase; she does not yet have the *pattern-match* to her own S02 transcription. Partial reception: the finder has the signal; the receiver has only half of the meaning.
Why the rubric missed it: v1.2 finder-vs-receiver two-column tracking assumes both columns resolve within-scene. Partial-reception across scenes (or deferred-reception, where the finder becomes the receiver later via reflection) is a new sub-type the rubric did not anticipate.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Portent-as-benediction (Divination-for-another-PC's-ritual)
Dimension affected: Surprise / Mechanical Fairness / Character Agency (fuses three)
What happened: Scene 7 — Grom rolled Religion 8, failing his Reorx-rite. Kessa expended her held Portent [14] to replace the roll, framed in-character as *"Vethrenn. I have never met you. If this was part of what you wanted me to see — let it be through me."* The mechanical Divination move landed as a PC-to-PC benediction; the rite succeeded at 17.
Why the rubric missed it: Mechanical Fairness at 9+ requires mechanics that "compound narratively." This is a PC-to-PC compound, not a within-PC compound. The rubric's mechanical-fairness anchor is single-character framed. A cross-character mechanical benediction is a new type.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: No-attunement-collective-restraint (Option C moment declined by all 4 PCs)
Dimension affected: Character Agency / Module Fidelity
What happened: Scene 6 — all four PCs' doubt-dice rolled against looking into the Mirror (Kessa d6=1 catalog; Thera d6=1 profit; Grom d6=2 mend; Aelric d6=2 letter-of-Measure). The module's most-designed decision beat (Option C subagent spawn for attunement) did not fire. Collective restraint.
Why the rubric missed it: Module Fidelity measures whether designed beats land. The Option-C-spawn is a designed beat. But "designed beat declined by PCs acting in character" is not a fidelity failure — it is the highest form of character agency. The rubric currently has no vocabulary for this duality: a designed beat can legitimately fail because PCs chose it to.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Unscripted NPC narrative completion (Mira's ink-writing)
Dimension affected: Atmospheric Landing
What happened: Scene 7, post-rite — Mira asked Thera for a corner of her notebook, tore a small piece, wrote a single line in her own hand: *"For the Council file. A daughter's note. My mother's name was Seralen. I am grateful. — M. Vaenshold."* First time in 30 years Seralen's name has been written in ink anywhere. The module did not script this. Scene 4's spoken disclosure → Scene 7's written affirmation.
Why the rubric missed it: Atmospheric Landing's 10 band requires an inter-PC chain. This is an *NPC*-completion chain: Grom's silent benediction Scene 4 → Mira's spoken name Scene 4 → Mira's written name Scene 7. The rubric's chain-concept is PC-centric. NPC chains that close session-long arcs are uncaptured.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Heuristic crossover (Kessa behaves as craft-witness under blank-risk)
Dimension affected: Character Agency / Surprise
What happened: Scene 6 — Kessa's PC sheet heuristic-priority-1 is "Understand it." Her d6 rolled 1 (catalog, don't touch). Her articulated in-character reasoning framed the decision as craft-witness would (honor the object's refusal by not forcing it). This is the second player-style (`sheet-deep-reader` — pending approval) plus `craft-witness` (pending approval) showing crossover in play.
Why the rubric missed it: The two pending player-styles are tracked as separate clusters. A PC exhibiting BOTH patterns under pressure (sheet-hidden grief + craft-witness restraint) is a cross-style emergent behavior. Possible sub-style: *doctrinal-silence* (PC acts against their own primary heuristic in a way their *secondary* heuristic justifies, under existential artifact risk).
```
