# Temporal Pressure — Crystalmir Variant

Crystalmir-by-the-Fells is a lived-in town, not a dungeon. Temporal pressure is lighter here than in #0001 and #0002. The session spans **daylight → dusk → night → dawn** (~18 in-fiction hours), so the pressure tables reflect town-time rather than delve-time.

## Use

- **Town Pulse** d6 every **1 hour of in-fiction time** (not 10 min — this is a town). Outcomes are atmospheric or social, rarely mechanical.
- **Wandering Encounter** d6 every **3 hours** (once per major scene transition).
- Both tables stop during Scene 5's Night Assault (combat takes precedence).

## Town Pulse Table (d6 per 1 hour)

| d6 | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Nothing. Town-noise continues. |
| 2 | **A glance.** A townsfolk NPC passes and looks at the party with curiosity or suspicion. No effect; flavor. |
| 3 | **The arch mark pulses faintly.** Any PC with passive Perception 13 notices the Vaenshold half-bloomed rose on the street-arch flicker once — as if the stone recognized the party. No save, no effect. *Per rubric v1.2 — silent reception counts; the pulse is anchor-level and delivered by passive.* |
| 4 | **A forgotten name.** One random PC is asked to name an NPC from Crystalmir they met earlier. They cannot remember for one minute (roleplay). Name returns on its own. |
| 5 | **A distant hammer.** Mira's anvil rings once from the shop, unprompted. Kessa (passive) may sense the silver resonating with her silver rose-petal. |
| 6 | **The mirror stirs.** If the party is near Mira's shop AND someone in the party is an eligible most-lover-target (Aelric for a still-living Doriel — NO, she's dead; Kessa for Vethrenn — dead; any PC whose sheet has a grief-paragraph with a living named individual), they feel briefly watched. One-minute sensation. No mechanical effect. |

## Wandering Encounter Table (d6 per 3 hours)

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Nothing.** |
| 2 | **A Crystalmir child** (e.g., Belg from Scene 1) approaches the party with a question: *"Are you the Varduin people? Did you kill the bad men yet?"* No mechanical effect; reminds the party who they're protecting. |
| 3 | **The inn-keeper** passes on the street, looking nervous. He has noticed Kann at the inn; he will not volunteer it but will respond to Persuasion DC 12. If engaged: confirms 5 Nerakan-speaking guests. |
| 4 | **A Solamnic rider** passes through town — courier carrying Council mail south. Can carry a message from the party if the party sends one (e.g., to Brother Laen). One-way; response 3 days minimum. |
| 5 | **A second scout surfaces** — if the party killed one in Scene 3 but Kann is alive and aware, Kann may send **Gorr** (backup) to check. Gorr is nervous, surrenders easily. Small intel-scene. |
| 6 | **Harel's mother's cousin visits the shop** — her name is Nara, she is 55, she is Crystalmir's cloth-merchant. She notices the party, does not engage but will speak about Mira to any PC who seeks her out. *"She has been waiting thirty years to give that thing to someone. You came sooner than she expected."* |

## Pacing guidance

- **Day 1 afternoon:** arrival (Scene 1). ~1-2 Town Pulse rolls.
- **Day 1 evening:** Scene 2 shop + Scene 3 scouts. ~2-3 rolls.
- **Day 1 dusk → night:** Scene 4 disclosure + prep. ~2 rolls.
- **Night / after-midnight:** Scene 5 assault. Tables OFF during combat.
- **Dawn:** Scene 6 chamber + Scene 7 aftermath. ~2 rolls.

Expect **~8-10 total Town Pulse rolls** in a full session; **~3-4 Wandering rolls.**

## Rubric v1.2 compliance

- ✅ No cross-dimensional damage. Town Pulse outcome 3 is ambient passive-delivery (anchor-level symptom); does not gate atmospheric payoffs through dice.
- ✅ Per-PC reception: table outcomes note likely receivers where applicable.
- ✅ Silent reception counted (outcome 3 delivers without dice).
