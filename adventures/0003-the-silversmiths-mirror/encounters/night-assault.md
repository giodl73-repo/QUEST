# Encounter — The Night Assault (Scene 5 climax)

**Location:** Mira's shop — front door, back service door, upstairs window.
**Trigger:** After midnight (or earlier if Kann was warned during Scene 3).

## Adversaries

Full roster (if Lorn alive):
- **Kann** (CR ½, 100 XP) — directs from street; covers with crossbow
- **Pevra** (CR ¼, 50 XP) — back service door
- **Lorn** (CR ¼, 50 XP) — upstairs window (via roof)
- **Vend** (CR ⅛, 25 XP) — front door
- **Gorr** (CR ⅛, 25 XP) — alley watch

If Lorn was killed in Scene 3: reduce to 4 attackers. Gorr promoted to front door; no roof attacker.

## Stats

### Kann (CR ½, 100 XP)
- AC 14, HP 25, Multiattack 2, Shortsword +4 (1d6+2), Light crossbow +4 (1d8+2), Blade-throw +4 (1d4+2)
- Commercial Disengage reaction (see `npcs/kann-of-neraka.md`)

### Pevra (CR ¼, 50 XP)
- AC 12, HP 11, Scimitar +3 (1d6+1), Light crossbow +3 (1d8+1)

### Lorn (CR ¼, 50 XP — if alive)
- AC 13, HP 16, Stealth +6, Shortsword +4 (1d6+2), Longbow +4 (1d8+2)

### Vend (CR ⅛, 25 XP)
- AC 12, HP 11, Scimitar +3 (1d6+1). Silent.

### Gorr (CR ⅛, 25 XP)
- AC 12, HP 11, Scimitar +3 (1d6+1). Nervous; surrenders at HP ≤ 4.

## Encounter XP

- **With all 5 attackers:** 100+50+50+25+25 = 250 base × 2 (5 creatures) = **500 XP.** Medium-Hard for 4×L3.
- **With 4 (Lorn killed):** 100+50+25+25+25 = 225 base × 2 = **450 XP.** Medium.

## Preparation benefits (from Scene 1 + 3 play)

### Tier A — Full intel (ambushed Lorn + Pevra silently in Scene 3)
- Party has the scouts' map; knows entry points.
- Party can pre-position: Aelric at front, Grom at back, Thera at upstairs window, Kessa free.
- Round 1: party has **surprise** on attackers at each of their entry points (attackers expected sleeping defenders).
- Attacker composition: Kann + 3 (Lorn dead).

### Tier B — Partial intel
- Kann advances assault by 2-3 hrs (before party is fully ready).
- One PC pre-positioned; others waking Round 1.
- No surprise.

### Tier C — No intel
- Assault starts with boot-on-door Round 1.
- Attackers have **surprise** on defending PC (first round only).

## Tactics

### Round 1
- **Surprise round (whoever has it).**
  - Tier A: PCs each get a free action against an attacker at their position.
  - Tier B: no surprise, roll initiative.
  - Tier C: attackers take free actions.
- **Kann** fires crossbow into the front door from the street at Aelric (or front-defender) for 1d8+2.
- **Remnants** breach their respective entry points.

### Rounds 2-3
- Melee in the hearth-room (center of house).
- **Lorn** (upstairs) attempts to reach Harel's room to take her hostage. Thera or anyone upstairs must intercept.
- **Kann** enters the shop on Round 3 if his remnants are overwhelmed — "collecting the delivery himself."

### Mira's intervention
- Mira stays hearth-room. She picks up her silversmith's hammer if a Bone-Collector reaches the stair toward Harel's room. She fights with Commoner-variant stats: AC 10, HP 8, Hammer +1 (1d4).
- **She takes one swing.** If that swing misses or is deflected, she is overwhelmed on the attacker's next action unless a PC intervenes.

### Harel's defense
- If Lorn (or any attacker) reaches her room: she throws her stool. Improvised weapon +0 (1d4 bludgeoning). She will not hide; she will fight with what she has.

### Kann's fleeing
- Kann will attempt to flee if: HP ≤ 8 OR his remnants are all down OR the Mirror is obviously lost to him.
- **Commercial Disengage** reaction: Shortsword attack at advantage, then Disengage at regular movement. Can travel 30 ft out of melee.
- **Flee-route:** south through the alleys to a stabled horse. 1d4 rounds to reach it.

## Resolution branches

### Party wins cleanly
- Attackers dead or captured.
- Mira and Harel alive.
- Scene 6 opens.

### Party wins but Harel is harmed/killed
- Mira's Scene 7 arc collapses. She will not leave Crystalmir. She retreats into grief.
- Scene 6 still opens but Mira will not re-emerge; the party takes or destroys the mirror without her.
- Downstream: the Council contract is flagged as "fulfilled by loss"; a Solamnic post-mortem follows; Brother Laen's reputation is damaged.

### Kann escapes
- 3 remnants east; they may regroup in #0006 or #0007.
- Kann does not return to Neraka; he disappears.

### Kann captured alive
- Intimidation DC 14 or Persuasion DC 16: Kann gives up the Istaran client's name (Felor, Second House of Istar). This is a **direct plant for #0005's hook** (Lady Tiran connection).

## Loot

See Scene 5 Treasure section. Key items:
- **Kann's Stonfold moon-silver finger-bone** (if he dies or is captured). **Anchor seed for #0006/#0007.**
- **The scouts' map** (if not already held from Scene 3).
- Minor cash: 20-40 gp aggregate mixed Nerakan coin.

## Environmental factors

- **It is night.** Candlelight only (Mira's tallow candle in hearth-room). Disadvantage on ranged attacks at > 30 ft without darkvision.
- **Aelric and Grom have darkvision?** Aelric (human) does not. Grom (hill dwarf) does (60 ft). Kessa (Qualinesti elf) does (60 ft). Thera (lightfoot halfling) does not.
- **Moon.** Crescent; minimal outdoor light. The alleys are nearly pitch-dark.

## GM Notes

- **Scene 5 is not just combat — it is consequence.** The party's Scene 1 and Scene 3 choices determine what Scene 5 actually feels like. If they prepared, it is defensible; if not, it is scary.
- **Harel is not optional.** Her survival is Mira's arc. Plan to have one PC near her room.
- **Kann's flee is narratively preferred.** The campaign wants him to live for #0006 or #0007.
- **Rubric v1.2 compliance:** this encounter does not produce cross-dimensional damage. Standard tier-1 combat mechanics.
