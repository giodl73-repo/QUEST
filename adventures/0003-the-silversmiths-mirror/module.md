## Summary

A three-act adventure in the Abanasinian hill-town of **Crystalmir-by-the-Fells**. The Varduin Muster arrives to protect the hedge-silversmith **Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith** (Aelwen's great-great-granddaughter) from a remnant Bone-Collector cell led by **Kann of Neraka**. Mira holds, in a hidden chamber below her shop, **Aelwen's Mirror** — the silversmith's last forging, decades after the Silver Rose, a moon-silver artifact that shows the holder their own face as the person who loves them most sees them.

**Three overlapping decisions drive the session:**

1. **Does Mira live, die, or leave?** — she has planned to leave regardless.
2. **Does anyone look into the Mirror?** — the attuner's self-image becomes permanently the reflection.
3. **Where does the Mirror end up?** — taken (attuned or not), destroyed via Grom's Reorx-rite, or left with Mira.

Each routes the #0007 finale differently.

## Hook

The Rose Council's protection contract for Mira (formalized by Brother Laen post-S02) activates. Laen tells the party: **Bone-Collector remnants — 4-5 survivors from Keloth's cell** — have regrouped from the east-escapees and the Varduin-feint survivors and are closing on Crystalmir. They want the Mirror as a reliquary-tool. The Council has no agents to spare; the Varduin Muster is their nearest resource.

**Kessa has a second reason to accept.** Aelwen's Mirror is her grandmother-smith's *last* work — post-Rose, post-Reliquary, post-everything she made in grief. If **Vethrenn** (Kessa's never-met mentor, the White Robe whose commonplace book Kessa inherited) ever saw it, her notes would reflect it. Kessa has not dared to hope so. She goes to find out.

## Setting & Background

**Crystalmir-by-the-Fells** is a lived-in Abanasinian hill-town, ~800 inhabitants, ~3 days' ride south of Varduin. Slate roofs. Market square with a clear-water well that gives the town its name. Public stone arches carry the **Vaenshold half-bloomed-rose** smith-mark — the same sigil Aelwen used on her work.

**Time:** PC 20 (late Age of Might; the Cataclysm is 20 years away).

**Aelwen the Silversmith (PC 179, hanged)** was Kessa's great-great-great-grandmother-smith (via the silversmith line, not blood). Before her death, Aelwen forged **six moon-silver artifacts** — the Silver Rose (#0001), the Reliquary (#0002), the **Mirror** (this session), and three more she did not name in the surviving journals. Each is a grief's mirror. Her final journal page (rescued from her pyre by her daughter Heva) names "an ingot I should have forged instead" — the pre-plant for **Hint 4** at the #0007 finale.

**Mira** (62, last of Aelwen's line) has held the Mirror for 30 years in a chamber beneath her shop. She has never looked into it. She does not know whose face the Mirror would show her. Her mother **Seralen** died in Mira's arms 30 years ago; Mira has not said Seralen's name aloud since. She has planned this week — perhaps this session — to close the shop and leave for **Stonfold** (the rival smith-family's home, 80 miles southwest), where Seralen's cousin Tova Stonfold will take her and her apprentice Harel in.

**Kann of Neraka** — the Bone-Collector remnant leader — wants the Mirror for an **Istaran client** (a clerk named Felor, of the Second House of Istar, working on behalf of **Lady Tiran of the Purple Rose** — seed for #0005). Kann is commercial, not priestly. He will bargain if cornered.

**Campaign-awareness (retrievals + plants):**

- **Retrieves** from S01: Mira-as-named-person; half-bloomed-rose smith-mark (via arch + shop goods + Mirror handle).
- **Retrieves** from S02: the Mira-protection contract; Bone-Collector remnants; the missed cross-adventure phrase (*"I will not look at the rose again"*) recoverable via Mira's sales ledger (Investigation DC 13 / passive 15).
- **Plants** for #0004: **Thane Krun Ironhaft** in Mira's ledger (Thorbardin mine-magnate; unclaimed commission).
- **Plants** for #0005: **Felor / Lady Tiran** via Kann's letter shreds or interrogation.
- **Plants** for #0006 + #0007: **Tova Stonfold** (named by Mira) + **Kann's Stonfold moon-silver finger-bone** (physical non-Vaenshold-vein artifact).
- **Plants** for #0007 finale: **Aelwen's journal page** (ingot reference; Hint 4 pre-plant).
- **Hint 2 (Vethrenn warded margin)** — near-unlock only; ward weakens in Aelwen's workshop proximity. Full unlock reserved for #0004.

## Rooms / Scenes

### Scene 1 — Crystalmir Arrival

**Location:** Market Square [1]. **Time:** midday, Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The road lifts into Crystalmir at midday. Slate roofs. A market square with an old well at its center, stones worn smooth by three centuries of hands. Market stalls — wool, cheese, tin — under canvas sun-shades. The air smells of cooking-smoke and rain-wet stone. Above the street that descends toward the silversmith's shop, a carved stone arch: a half-bloomed rose, chisel-clean.

**Features:**

- **The market square** — ~60 townsfolk at midday; ordinary hill-town economy. Two well-gatherers share a bucket (*Hela* and *Orin*); a farrier's boy (*Tam*) watches a horse being shoed; three children play pebbles (*Belg, Mirra* — not Mira-the-silversmith, different — *and Poll*).
- **The well (center).** Waist-high stone; Crystalmir's name-giver. No mechanical property; flavor.
- **The arch above the silversmith-road.** Carved stone, centuries old, a **half-bloomed rose** — the Vaenshold smith-mark (same design Aelwen used on her work). Passive Perception 10: legible. Active Investigation DC 10: matches Abanasinian silver-guild markings. Active Arcana DC 12 (Kessa): she may recognize it as the precise mark used on the Silver Rose's petal-hinges (S01 recall). *Per rubric v1.2 anchor-level, offer Kessa the direct-comparison recognition whether or not she rolls.*
- **The silversmith's shop-sign.** *"VAENSHOLD · silver"* in real silver-filigree letters, inlaid decades ago. Passive Perception 10.
- **Bone-Collector surveillance — Lorn.** Kann's scout (CR ¼) poses as a traveler near the well, watching the silversmith's door. **Passive Perception 13** notices him watching one specific direction. **Active Perception DC 13** catches him directly. He carries a concealed shortsword. Mission is intel, not combat.

**Encounter:**

**None by default.** If the party is obviously scanning, Lorn withdraws into the alleys toward Scene 3. If the party does nothing threatening, Lorn stays and shadows them to Scene 2.

Lorn stat block (if needed):

> **Lorn — Bone-Collector Scout (CR ¼, 50 XP; Scout variant)**
> **AC** 13 (leather armor) · **HP** 16 (3d8+3) · **Speed** 30 ft
> Stealth +6, Perception +5, Survival +5
> **Multiattack:** 2 shortsword attacks.
> **Shortsword:** +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing.
> **Longbow:** +4 to hit, range 150/600, 1d8+2 piercing.

**Treasure:** None in the market; party may buy supplies.

**Connections:** North (road to Varduin), silversmith-road → Scene 2, alleys off market → Scene 3.

**GM Notes:**

- Play Crystalmir lived-in. Townsfolk are named; a PC who asks is told by name.
- **Gloss on first use:** "Vaenshold" — *"the silversmith-line's family name, fallen from holding-status generations ago; the mark has outlived the house."*
- Let Kessa connect the arch to her silver rose-petal (S01 R05 brazier) — campaign-aware beat; free, no roll, passive delivery.
- Lorn's disappearance is itself intel (makes Scene 3 available).

**Memory Fragment (optional):**

Belg is eight years old and has been telling the other children for weeks that Kann's men are "bad men." The adults have not taken him seriously. He was right.*

**Manifest symptoms landed:** Vaenshold mark on the arch (anchor-level, passive Perc 10); town's folklore ("silversmiths who do not look into their own work" — via Memory Fragment).

### Scene 2 — Mira's Shop, Upper Floor

**Location:** Mira's Shop [2]. **Time:** afternoon, Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The shop is small. Three paces across. The air carries silver-oil and old woodsmoke — the smell of a place that has not been aired out in a long time. A counter, a workbench behind it with a single silver ingot half-worked, a rack of finished goods — pendants, pins, two silver cups engraved with the same half-bloomed rose from the street-arch. On the back wall, above the workbench, a pale rectangle where something hung until very recently. The rack's silver gleams with a specific clockwise-polish sheen. A woman stands behind the counter with a silversmith's hammer in her left hand, unmoving. Her hair is grey-braided. Her eyes are a specific silver-grey. She says nothing.

**Features:**

- **The counter.** Smooth-worn oak. Pens and a **sales-ledger** (leather-bound; decades of entries). The ledger is closed at session start. If opened without asking: Mira's first word is *nyet* (no).
- **The pale rectangle on the back wall.** Perception DC 10 — outline of an apron that hung there for decades until a few years ago. Seralen's apron. Mira finally took it down when she could no longer bear seeing it; she has not put anything in its place. Active Investigation DC 12 finds the iron nail still set in the stone.
- **The rack's polish pattern.** Any PC (passive, no roll) who pays attention to the shop goods sees each piece polished in a consistent clockwise circle — Seralen's motion, continued by Mira for 30 years.
- **The workbench behind.** Half-worked ingot; smith's tools; a small anvil. Metal dust and oil. A scrap paper: customer order for wedding rings (flavor).
- **The rack** (behind Mira). Finished goods for sale: 6 pendants (5-20 gp each), 4 pins (3-8 gp), 2 cups engraved with the Vaenshold half-bloomed rose (**45 gp each**; Kessa's Arcana will confirm they bear the same mark as the Silver Rose AND Aelwen's workshop).
- **The door behind the counter.** Closed. Leads to Scene 4 (Mira's Hearth). Mira will NOT let the party past this door in Scene 2.
- **Harel** (apprentice, 15). Sits on a stool at the back polishing a pendant-chain. Speaks only when addressed.

**Dialogue beats:**

Mira's opener, after the party enters:

*"You are the Varduin party. Brother Laen sent word. You come sooner than I expected. Harel — tea."*

If Kessa is present and speaks first:

*"Silversmith's kin. You carry something of the vein. Put it on the counter."*

(Mira will name Kessa's silver rose-petal on sight. Recognition beat; she will not take the petal, she wants to see it, and she hands it back without comment.)

**Harel's aside (Scene-3 unavoidable-ification).** Before the party leaves Scene 2, Harel will catch one PC's eye on their way out the door — whichever PC has been kindest to her, or to Kessa if she showed no preference. She speaks quietly, not in Mira's hearing:

> *"I have counted six days. Two of them at the back alley at night. One at the inn window who does not drink. My mother polished with a different motion; I cannot make myself do Mira's, so I see the shop different. I see the back door at night. You should see it before the dark."*

Then she returns to her stool. **This converts Scene 3 from optional to actively offered.** If Scene 1 went poorly (no surveillance spotted), Harel's intel gives the party a second door into Scene 3. Her line about her mother's polishing motion is a character-seed — her mother taught her a motion before the plague-year killed her; she has kept it; it is her only inheritance.

**Mira will answer:**
- *Bone-Collector threat:* she will tell the party what she knows (4-5 remnants scattered; leader stays at the inn). Confirms Laen's warning; has made "some preparations" (no elaboration).
- *The Mirror:* she will **not** confirm or deny. Says *nyet*. *"I have work that is not yours."*
- *Her family line:* confirms she is the last Vaenshold silversmith. Will not say her mother's name. Does not mention the six artifacts.

**Insight reads:**
- **DC 15:** *"She is afraid, but not of the Bone-Collectors. She is afraid of something she is keeping."*
- **DC 18:** *"She has been afraid for thirty years; it is a shape, not a feeling."*

**Investigation beats:**
- **DC 10 on the rack:** finds the half-bloomed rose on the cups → Kessa cross-refs to silver rose-petal (campaign-aware beat).
- **DC 13 on the sales-ledger** (if permitted, or purchased-a-cup-and-asked-for-receipt): decades of entries. **Passive-Perception DC 15 fallback** (rubric v1.2 anchor-level). Key margin-note, different hand, decades old: *"paid in full; I will not look at the rose again."* Buyer described only as *"the man who would not give his name."* **This is the cross-adventure signal recovery path from S02.** Mira will say *"nyet"* if asked.

*Mira will not normally permit ledger-browsing in Scene 2. The recovery path is available cleanly in Scene 4.*

**Encounter:** None. Mira and Harel are non-combatants.

**Treasure:**

- Nothing forcibly taken (Mira calls the town-watch; party becomes antagonist).
- Purchased: any rack item at face value.
- **Harel** as a post-session seed.

**Connections:** Front door → Market (Scene 1); back service door → Alleys (Scene 3); counter door → Scene 4 (not opened in Scene 2).

**GM Notes:**

- Play Mira's recognition of Kessa with weight. She has seen two others in her life carry Vaenshold-silver trace (Seralen; Heva-via-portrait). Kessa is the third. She does not smile; she acknowledges.
- Harel is real. If addressed, let her answer. She knows (*"They have been watching for six days. I have counted"*) — accurately.
- Scene 2 ends when the party has what Mira will give (acceptance, threat intel, polite dismissal). She invites them back at dusk — "for the hearth."
- **Do not let Mira open the hidden door in Scene 2.** The chamber reveal is Scene 6.

**Memory Fragment (optional):**

> *Seralen — Mira's mother, dead 30 years — used to polish the silver goods on the rack herself, each morning, before opening. She polished by rubbing a specific clockwise circle on each piece, using a specific grey rag she had inherited from her own mother. Mira has continued the practice. She does not think about it; she does it. She did it this morning, as she does every morning. The rag is threadbare now. Mira has never bought a replacement.*

**Manifest symptoms landed:** half-bloomed rose mark on shop goods (anchor-level; active Arcana 10 / passive 12); cross-adventure phrase recovery hint (anchor-level; pass-gated in Scene 2, cleanly available in Scene 4).

### Scene 4 — Mira's Hearth (Disclosure, dusk)

**Location:** Mira's Hearth [4]. **Time:** dusk, Day 1.

**Read-aloud:**

> The door behind the counter opens. A small room: three chairs, a fireplace, a cast-iron kettle, a cupboard on the wall with a closed door. The fire is going. Mira sits in one chair. Harel is in the upper room — the party can hear her moving. Mira does not speak when the party enters. She gestures to the chairs. The fire is louder than the silence.

**Features:**

- **The fire.** Live. Pine-resin against hill-stone smell.
- **Three chairs.** Two for Mira's mother's and grandmother's use; one for Mira. Worn. She gestures for any three PCs to sit; fourth stands.
- **The cupboard.** Closed. Mira will open it if she decides to show Aelwen's journal page. Also contains: a box of Seralen's jewelry (unopened in 30 years); a candle stub; a book of Vaenshold-family prayers (private devotions, not liturgical).
- **The sales-ledger** (on the table, **already open** — Mira has selected a page in advance). If the party investigates without asking, Mira permits it. **THIS IS WHERE THE CROSS-ADVENTURE PHRASE CAN LAND.** Investigation DC 13 or **passive Perception 15** (rubric v1.2 anchor-level fallback): *"paid in full; I will not look at the rose again."*

**The disclosure scene:**

Mira takes a long breath. Then speaks — one long sentence, carefully prepared:

> *"My great-great-great-grandmother Aelwen made six objects before she hanged herself in the year seventy-nine before the Cataclysm, and she made them all from the vein my family has worked for five generations, and one of them is in my cellar, and I will not take it out, and I will not look at it, and I have spent thirty years training myself not to want to look at it, and I will tell you where it is, and you will decide what to do with it, and after tonight I will close this shop and go to Stonfold where my mother Seralen's cousin's people will take me, and this is what I have to say, and you will ask what you will ask, and I will answer what I can."*

Then she is quiet.

**What the party can ask and Mira's answers:**

- **"What is in the cellar?"** — *"A mirror. My great-great-great-grandmother's last forging."*
- **"What does it do?"** — *"It shows the holder their face as the person who loves them most sees them."*

## Treasures

### Aelwen's Mirror (central artifact)

*A palm-sized mirror, moon-silver handle, obsidian back, silver face polished to the edge of showing anything. It waits.*

**Appearance.** Four inches across the polished face; eight inches handle. Moon-silver handle engraved with Aelwen's smith-mark — a **half-bloomed rose opening** (the inverse of the Silver Rose's petal-hinges which close). Obsidian back — not Aelwen's usual material; she bought it specifically for this forging, at considerable cost, because obsidian *holds* what silver *shows*. Silver face polished to mirror-brightness with a slight hesitation at the edges; the polishing-lines do not perfectly converge. Weighs ~6 ounces. The handle is cold at first touch and warms to the holder within a few heartbeats. The face is room-temperature.

**Presence / Desire.** **The mirror refuses to reflect anyone without first pausing.** Those who lift it expecting a reflection get silence — the face stays blank for 2-3 heartbeats then resolves. Those who lift it expecting silence (as Mira lifts when she dusts the obsidian back, eyes averted) get a faint warming of the silver — but they do not see the face; they have not turned it toward themselves. The mirror is looking for a particular attention — one that is neither greedy (expecting a reflection that flatters) nor fearful (expecting one that accuses). Aelwen called this *"the waiting that is not waiting"* in her workshop notes.

When Aelwen's smith-mark on the handle is held to bare skin, the holder feels a faint pulse — the silver's awareness that it is being held by a kinsman or kinswoman of the maker. Mira has felt this her whole life; no one else still living has.

**Rarity.** Rare · attunement required · **no kin prerequisite.**

**Provenance.** Aelwen forged the Mirror across the year-before-her-death, in her final grief-period (PC 181 → PC 179).

- PC 181: Aelwen forges the Silver Rose for Baron Edric of Vaenshold. Begins to forget her daughter Heva's face.
- PC 180: Aelwen writes the never-sent letter to the Ergothian abbot; begins the Mirror in secret. Journals describe it as *"what I should have forged instead."*
- PC 180-179: Aelwen forges four more artifacts in secret (never delivered; locked in her workshop): the Wrath-Coin, the Pride-Circlet, the Thread-That-Frays, and one other she did not name in the surviving journals. Each grief's mirror.
- PC 179: Aelwen hangs herself. Heva Aelwensdaughter (18) enters the workshop, finds the six artifacts, rescues the **last journal page** from the burning pyre.

The Mirror passes through the Vaenshold line: **Heva → Rillina → Avel → Seralen → Mira.** **No one in the line has ever attuned it.** Each has been unable to look. The refusal has become the line's inheritance. Mira is the 6th-generation holder; last of the direct line; no children.

**Properties (mechanical).**

*Attunement.* Required. **One look** — not a short rest. The moment the holder turns the face toward themselves and sees the resolved reflection, attunement completes. **Fast-attune: combat-pace.**

The mirror shows the holder their own face **as the person who loves them most sees them** — *felt recognition*, not observation. Experience lasts as long as the holder can bear it. First-time: 1-2 seconds before looking away. Repeat: longer; Heva's journal described one holder at "a full minute, weeping steadily."

*Benefits (while attuned).*

- **Seen.** Advantage on Charisma checks with anyone who once loved the bearer (or whom the bearer once loved). Derives from the bearer's refreshed sense of their own worth.
- **Direction (1/long rest).** The bearer may hold the mirror toward a named specific person (living or dead) and see themselves *as that person sees them*. Living: current view. Dead: pre-death view. Living who has never known the bearer: the mirror darkens; no reflection.
- **Smith-mark pulse (passive, free).** Anyone of Aelwen's line feels a faint heartbeat-pulse holding the handle. Mira is the only living such person at campaign start.

*Curse (while attuned): **Self-image replacement.***

From attunement, the bearer's **internal self-image** — face in dreams, face mentally pictured when thinking of themselves — is **permanently replaced with the mirror's reflection**. The bearer can no longer picture themselves "from outside" without the most-lover's gaze coloring the image. Practical effects:

- Dreams featuring the bearer's face always show the reflected view.
- Self-description (in conversation, writing) subtly shifts toward what the reflection revealed. Words like "loved" or "missed" used about themselves without realizing.
- The bearer's self-image is no longer their own. It belongs to the reflection. **Permanent.** Greater restoration does NOT reverse it.

Not as brutal as the Silver Rose's memory-erosion or the Reliquary's memory-concentration — the bearer's actual memories are intact; their *sense of who they are* is relocated.

*The Blank-Mirror Trap (anchor-level warning).*

If the bearer's most-lover is dead AND has already attuned this same mirror (not applicable on first encounter), OR **no one living has ever loved the bearer most**, the mirror shows nothing. **A bearer who attunes to a blank mirror:**

1. Attunement is **permanent and irrevocable** — cannot be un-attuned even by forge-rite.
2. Bearer **loses the capacity to feel love for 30 days.** Not the memory of love; the capacity. Grief returns; warmth does not. Day 30: capacity restored; but the memory of those 30 days haunts.
3. If the bearer attunes a DIFFERENT artifact during those 30 days, the attunement fails (no love to anchor attachment).

**The Blank-Mirror Trap is Mira's actual fear.** She is 62; her most-lover (Seralen) died 30 years ago; she has not loved another as deeply. If Mira looked, she is not certain whose face would show. She is not willing to find out.

*Ending the curse.*

- **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** at dawn on a forge-fire ≥ 1,800°C, **Religion DC 15.** Destroys the obsidian back; silver un-worked; Mirror gone. Attunement ends; **self-image replacement persists (already permanent).**
- **Reciprocal recognition:** the attuner looks into the mirror in the presence of the reflected person — and offers them the mirror. The reflected accepts. Attunement becomes mutual; the curse is ended for the original attuner (self-image returns); the reflected person attunes (same terms apply). Requires reflected alive, present, willing. For dead reflecteds: impossible.
- **Blank-mirror attunement is irrevocable.** No ending.

### Key Saves

None required this adventure (no save-inducing magic or traps). The Mirror's attunement is **voluntary** — no save. The Blank-Mirror Trap is **not dice-gated** — it is determined from the attuner's PC sheet.

### Attunement Rules (inline — SRD)

- **Attunement** requires a **short rest (1 hour)** in normal 5e play; **Aelwen's Mirror uses fast-attune (one look, combat-pace)** — a homebrew deviation called out in the artifact's properties.
- A character may be attuned to **at most 3 magic items** at a time.
- A cursed item reveals its curse only on attunement unless otherwise specified. *Aelwen's Mirror reveals the Blank-Mirror Trap only on attunement.*
- Breaking attunement: normally spending a short rest 100 ft from the item while focusing. **Aelwen's Mirror's self-image replacement persists even if attunement breaks** — the alteration is already permanent.

### Conditions Used (inline — SRD)

- **Prone:** attacker has advantage on melee attacks against prone target; prone target has disadvantage on attacks; costs half movement to stand up.
- **Surprised:** creatures can't move or take an action on first turn of combat, and can't take a reaction until that turn ends.
- **Restrained:** speed 0; attacks against have advantage; target's attacks have disadvantage; disadvantage on Dex saves.

*This adventure does not use Paralyzed, Frightened, Poisoned, or other conditions. Exhaustion not triggered.*
