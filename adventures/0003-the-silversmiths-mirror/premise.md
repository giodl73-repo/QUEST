# Premise — The Silversmith's Mirror

**Campaign position:** #0003 of 7 in the **Moon-Silver Cycle**. Emotion: **Love**. PC spotlight: **Kessa Moonweave** (Vethrenn retrieves).

**Logline:** The mirror shows the holder their face as the person who loves them most sees them — and the silversmith who has refused to look into it for thirty years is about to lose the chance.

## Setting

**Place:** **Crystalmir-by-the-Fells**, an Abanasinian hill-town ~3 days' ride from Varduin. Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith runs a small silversmith's shop here. She has, in a lower chamber beneath the shop, **Aelwen's last forging** — a silver mirror she has hidden for thirty years.

**Time:** Late Age of Might, **PC 20** (continuous; approximately two weeks after S02). The Cataclysm is 20 years away.

## Hook

The Rose Council's protection contract for Mira (formalized via Brother Laen post-S02) activates. Laen tells the party: Bone-Collector remnants — 4-5 survivors from Keloth's cell, regrouped from the east-escapees and Varduin-feint survivors — are closing on Crystalmir. They want the mirror as a reliquary-tool. The Council has no agents to spare; the Varduin Muster is their nearest resource.

**Kessa has a second reason to accept.** Aelwen's Mirror is her grandmother-smith's **last** work — *post-Rose, post-Reliquary, post-everything she made in grief*. If Vethrenn (Kessa's mentor) ever saw it, her notes would reflect it. Kessa has not dared to hope so. She goes to find out.

## Central Artifact

**Aelwen's Mirror** — palm-sized; moon-silver handle; obsidian backing; polished silver face. Aelwen's *last* forging, made decades after the Silver Rose, after she had buried her daughter, after she had begun to forget her own face.

**Effect on looking:** the holder sees themselves as the person who loves them most sees them. Not as an observer — as *felt recognition*. The look lasts as long as the holder can bear it. First-time lookers stay 1-2 seconds.

**Presence / Desire:** the mirror refuses to reflect anyone *without first pausing*. Those who expect a reflection get silence. Those who expect silence get reflection. It is waiting for a particular kind of attention.

**Rarity:** Rare · attunement required · no kin prerequisite.

**Benefit:** advantage on Charisma checks with anyone who once loved the bearer (or whom the bearer once loved).
**Passive:** once per long rest, the bearer may direct the mirror at a specific named person and see themselves as *that person* sees them.

**Curse:** the mirror shows ONLY who loves the bearer most, and the mirror chooses. The bearer does not pick. If the bearer's most-lover has already died, the mirror shows the pre-death view (harrowing). If no one loves the bearer most (or the most-lover has already attuned to this same mirror), the mirror goes **blank**. **A bearer who looks into a blank mirror attunes permanently and irrevocably**, AND loses the capacity to feel love for 30 days.

**Ending:** forge-rite (Reorx-shattering) OR *reciprocal recognition* — the attuner looks into the mirror in the presence of the reflected person and offers them the mirror. In practice this is usually impossible (the reflected person is usually dead, distant, or unwilling).

Full artifact file: `treasures/aelwens-mirror.md`. Manifest: `treasures/mirror-manifest.md` (rubric v1.2 — anchor-level symptoms with passive-Perception fallbacks).

## Themes

- The vein of moon-silver is larger than any one smith (Aelwen is not the only maker).

## The Real Decision

Three overlapping choices:

1. **Does Mira live, die, or leave?** The Bone-Collectors attack; Mira's choices are to defend her shop, flee, or trust the party. Party's choice: protect her, help her leave, or take the mirror without her knowledge.
2. **Does anyone look into the mirror?** The mirror is locked in Mira's lower chamber. She has not looked in thirty years. A PC attuning is a session-defining beat. **Option C (per the pipeline) spawn** if a PC chooses to attune.
3. **Does the party take, destroy, or leave the mirror?** If taken, it joins the campaign's artifact inventory. If destroyed (Grom's rite), it's a #0003-level sacrifice. If left with Mira, the campaign's later finale changes.
