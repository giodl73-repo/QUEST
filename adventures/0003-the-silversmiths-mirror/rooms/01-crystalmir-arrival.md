# Scene 01 — Crystalmir-by-the-Fells, Market Square

## Read-aloud

> The road lifts into Crystalmir at midday. Slate roofs. A market square with an old well at its center, stones worn smooth by three centuries of hands. Market stalls — wool, cheese, tin — under canvas sun-shades. The air smells of cooking-smoke and rain-wet stone. Above the street that descends toward the silversmith's shop, a carved stone arch: a half-bloomed rose, chisel-clean.

## Features

- **The market square.** ~60 townsfolk present at midday. Ordinary hill-town economy. Two well-gatherers share a bucket; a farrier's boy watches a horse being shoed; three children play a game with pebbles. Normal life.
- **The well (center).** Waist-high stone. The well-water is the town's name-giver — "Crystalmir" for its clarity. No mechanical property; flavor.
- **The arch above the silversmith-road.** Carved stone; centuries-old; a **half-bloomed rose** — the Vaenshold smith-mark (same design Aelwen used on her work, per `treasures/aelwens-mirror.md`). Passive Perception 10: the mark is old and weathered but legible. Active Investigation DC 10: it matches the carving on shop-goods Thera has seen in other Abanasinian markets as a silversmith's guild-mark. Active Arcana DC 12 (Kessa): she may recognize it as the precise mark used on the Silver Rose's petal-hinges (S01 recall).
- **Bone-Collector surveillance.** At least one of Kann's remnants — **Lorn** (scout; CR ¼ Scout stats below) — is in the market posing as a traveler, watching the silversmith's shop-door. Passive Perception 13 notices him watching one specific direction. Active Perception DC 13 catches him. He is not armed visibly; he carries a concealed shortsword.
- **The silversmith's shop-sign.** At the head of the silversmith-road, visible from the market: *"VAENSHOLD · silver"* carved in silver-filigree letters above a door. The filigree is silver, not silver-colored — real silver, inlaid decades ago. Passive Perception 10 notices it is worth more than most shop signs.

## Encounter

**None by default.** If the party is obvious about scanning for surveillance (e.g., clearly suspicious behavior in the market), Lorn withdraws into the alleys toward Scene 3. If the party does nothing threatening, Lorn stays.

## GM Notes

- **Play Crystalmir lived-in.** This is not a dungeon. Townsfolk are named and purposeful (the farrier's boy is *Tam*; the well-gatherers are *Hela and Orin*; the three children are *Belg, Mirra* — not Mira-the-silversmith; different — *and Poll*). A PC who asks is told by name.
- **Let Kessa notice the arch's Vaenshold-mark.** This is a campaign-aware beat. Kessa carries the silver rose-petal from S01 R05 brazier; a direct side-by-side comparison (free, no roll) confirms the smith-mark link. *Per rubric v1.2, this is a passive anchor-level symptom and the DM should offer it whether or not Kessa rolls.*
- **Lorn's watch.** If the party does not spot him, he shadows them to Scene 2 and reports Kann. If they spot him, he retreats; his disappearance itself is intel (Scene 3 becomes available).
- **Gloss:** "Vaenshold" first-use — "the silversmith-line's family name, fallen from holding-status generations ago; the mark has outlived the house."

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Belg, one of the three children playing in the square, will ask the party — if any PC pauses near the well — whether they are "the Varduin party the steward sent for." Belg is eight years old and has been telling the other children for weeks that Kann's men are "bad men." The adults have not taken him seriously. He was right.*
