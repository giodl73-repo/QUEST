# Scene 05 — The Night Assault

## Read-aloud

> Past midnight. The fire has burned low. A single tallow candle in the hearth-room. Mira is awake in her chair; Harel is up in her bedroom; the party has chosen watch-positions. The first sound is a breath on the back service door — too controlled to be the wind. A boot on the stair from the alley. Above, a creak from the roof. They are here.

## Features & setup (varies by party prep)

The outcome of Scene 5 depends heavily on what the party did in Scenes 1 and 3.

### Preparation tier: FULL INTEL (ambushed Lorn + Pevra silently in alleys)

- Party has the scout's map: front door, back service door, upstairs window via roof.
- Party knows cell size: 5 remnants (Kann + Pevra + Lorn + Vend + one unnamed).
- Party can pre-position: Aelric at the front door; Grom at the back; Thera on the upstairs window (via roof access); Kessa free to cast and support.
- Surprise on round 1 for whichever attacker hits a PC-covered door.

### Preparation tier: PARTIAL INTEL (spooked a scout in alleys OR got ambient intel in Scene 1)

- Party knows Kann is at the inn and the assault is tonight.
- Does not know specific entry points.
- Kann advances the assault by 2-3 hours (before deep-night); party has less time to prepare.
- Only one PC can pre-position; others are waking.

### Preparation tier: NO INTEL (did nothing in Scenes 1 or 3)

- Party learns the assault is happening from the boots on the door.
- Kann assumes the party is here but underestimates them.
- Round 1: all attackers have surprise on the defending PC.

## Combatants

All options: **Kann + 3 remnants (Pevra + Vend + one other — "Gorr")** if Lorn was killed in Scene 3; OR **Kann + 4 remnants** if Lorn is alive.

Stats:
- **Kann** — CR ½, 100 XP. See `npcs/kann-of-neraka.md`. AC 14, HP 25, Multiattack. Carries his own moon-silver finger-bone (Stonfold smith-family).
- **Pevra** — CR ¼, 50 XP. Bandit variant. AC 12, HP 11, Scimitar +3, Light crossbow +3. Former Idra-apprentice; may hesitate if reminded.
- **Lorn** — CR ¼, 50 XP (if alive). Scout variant. AC 13, HP 16, Stealth +6.
- **Vend** — CR ⅛, 25 XP. Bandit. AC 12, HP 11. Silent; does not speak.
- **Gorr** — CR ⅛, 25 XP. Bandit. AC 12, HP 11. Nervous; may surrender early.

**Total encounter XP** (4 remnants + Kann): Kann 100 + 3×50 = 250 OR Kann 100 + 4×50 = 300 base. Adjusted by count:
- Full intel + Lorn dead (3 remnants + Kann = 4 creatures): 250 × 2 = **500 XP**. Medium-Hard for 4×L3.
- Full intel + Lorn alive (4 remnants + Kann = 5 creatures): 300 × 2 = **600 XP**. Hard.
- Partial/No intel: remove one party-preposition advantage; same XP but harder to resolve cleanly.

## Tactics

### Kann
- Stays **outside** with his crossbow, covering the street and the Mirra-shop front.
- Does not enter the shop in round 1 (he directs; he does not breach).
- Enters in round 3+ if his remnants are overwhelmed — "collecting the delivery himself."
- At HP ≤ 8, uses his Commercial Disengage reaction to retreat. Flees the town if possible.
- If captured: bargains. Gives up the Istaran client name (Felor, Second House of Istar) for his freedom.

### Remnants (Pevra, Vend, etc.)
- **Pevra** enters the back service door.
- **Vend** breaches the front door simultaneously.
- **Lorn** (if alive) climbs to the upstairs window.
- **Gorr** watches the alley (does not engage unless the party flees that way).

### The moon-silver flavor — Kann's finger-bone
During combat, if Kann is hit: his shirt opens slightly, revealing his own moon-silver finger-bone. Any PC who sees it (Perception DC 10 during combat, with +2 if in melee) and recognizes the alloy (passive-Perception 13 via Kessa, or active Arcana DC 10) can note: **"Kann carries a non-Vaenshold-vein moon-silver piece."** This is a **planted seed** for #0006 and #0007 — the party learns in-session that the vein has multiple smith-families.

## Mira and Harel during combat
- **Mira** takes position **at the foot of the stair leading up to Harel's room**, silversmith's hammer raised. She fights **only** if a Bone-Collector tries to pass her toward Harel. If she engages: use Commoner-variant stats (**AC 10, HP 8, Hammer +1 to hit, 1d4 bludgeoning**). If her swing connects, it is a **block** — she commits her weight to holding the stair; the attacker does not advance up. If she misses or her hammer is knocked aside, she is overwhelmed on the attacker's next action **unless a PC intervenes within one round**. **The party should prevent this.** Narratively: this is a woman doing the thing she trained herself not to do; her swing is wide, slow, ugly. She will not swing twice.
- **Harel** is upstairs, terrified. If Lorn reaches her window: Harel has a stool she will throw (Improvised weapon +0 to hit, 1d4 bludgeoning). She grew up in the plague-year orphanage before Mira took her in; she learned young to throw whatever was nearest — stool, pot, firewood. She will not die like a victim. If an attacker reaches her room, she stays behind the bed and keeps throwing. She will not run.

If Harel dies, Mira's Scene 7 arc collapses. She will not leave Crystalmir; she will not go to Stonfold; she closes the shop and retreats into grief. The party can still take or destroy the mirror, but Mira herself becomes post-session unreachable. **This is the adventure's worst outcome by Mira's lights.**

## Treasure (post-combat)

- Kann's kit: shortsword, light crossbow, 3 throwing blades, 12 gp mixed Nerakan coin.
- **Kann's moon-silver finger-bone** — **anchor-level seed for #0007.** 30 gp raw value. Carries Kann's dead wife's last minute. Unattuned.
- Pevra's kit: scimitar, light crossbow, 4 gp.
- Vend's kit: scimitar, 2 gp. Silent-to-the-end. He leaves nothing behind that names him.
- Lorn's kit (if killed): shortsword, longbow, 15 bolts, 5 gp, + the scouts' map (may already be in party possession from Scene 3).
- Gorr surrenders if offered; has 3 gp and no intel of value.

## Connections

- **After combat resolution:** either the party has won (and can approach Scene 6) or they have fled (adventure ends in retreat; run Scene 7 as recovery).
- **To Scene 6:** after victory, Mira opens the cellar trapdoor.

## GM Notes

- **Make the preparation matter.** If the party prepared well (FULL INTEL tier), the first round should reward them with surprise-attack advantages. If they did not, the first round punishes.
- **Kann's fleeing is a feature.** The campaign-structure benefits from Kann escaping — he can return in #0006 or #0007 as a minor second-wave. Don't push to kill him.
- **If Harel is reached:** give the party one round to respond. Do not auto-kill her in the attacker's turn. She is 15; she is terrified; she will throw a stool. Narrate her stool-throw in dialogue.
- **Mira's fighting is the session's lowest moment.** If it happens, narrate it as a woman doing the thing she trained herself not to do. Her hammer-swing is wide, slow, ugly. She will not swing twice.
