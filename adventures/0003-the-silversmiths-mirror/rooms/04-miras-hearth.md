# Scene 04 — Mira's Hearth (Disclosure, dusk)

## Read-aloud

> The door behind the counter opens. A small room: three chairs, a fireplace, a cast-iron kettle, a cupboard on the wall with a closed door. The fire is going. Mira sits in one chair. Harel is in the upper room — the party can hear her moving. Mira does not speak when the party enters. She gestures to the chairs. The fire is louder than the silence.

## Features

- **The fire.** Live; Mira has built it up for this conversation. Gives the room a particular Crystalmir smell — pine-resin burning against hill-stone.
- **The three chairs.** Two for Mira's mother and grandmother's use; one for Mira herself. Worn. Mira gestures for any three of the four PCs to sit. The fourth stands.
- **The cupboard on the wall.** Closed. Mira will open it if she decides to show the party Aelwen's journal page. The cupboard also contains: a small box of Seralen's jewelry (unopened in 30 years); a candle stub; a book of Vaenshold-family prayers (not liturgical; private devotions).
- **The sales-ledger (on the table by Mira's chair).** Already open — Mira has selected a page in advance. If the party investigates without asking, Mira permits it. **THIS IS WHERE THE CROSS-ADVENTURE PHRASE CAN LAND.** Investigation DC 13 OR passive-Perception 15 (rubric v1.2 anchor-level fallback) catches the uncredited entry: *"paid in full; I will not look at the rose again."*

## The disclosure scene

Mira takes a long breath. Then speaks — one long sentence, carefully prepared:

*"My great-great-great-grandmother Aelwen made six objects before she hanged herself in the year seventy-nine before the Cataclysm, and she made them all from the vein my family has worked for five generations, and one of them is in my cellar, and I will not take it out, and I will not look at it, and I have spent thirty years training myself not to want to look at it, and I will tell you where it is, and you will decide what to do with it, and after tonight I will close this shop and go to Stonfold where my mother Seralen's cousin's people will take me, and this is what I have to say, and you will ask what you will ask, and I will answer what I can."*

Then she is quiet.

### What the party can ask and what Mira answers

- **"What is in the cellar?"** *"A mirror. My great-great-great-grandmother's last forging."*
- **"What does it do?"** *"It shows the holder their face as the person who loves them most sees them."*
- **"Why can't you look?"** *"Because I do not know whose face it would show. I had a mother. She died. I do not know if anyone has loved me most since. I do not want to know."*
- **"The six objects — what are the others?"** *"I know three by name: the Rose, the Reliquary, the Mirror. Three more I only suspect. Her journals survive only in one page. I keep it in the cupboard. I can show it if you wish."*
- **"What about the second smith-family?"** *"Stonfold, southwest. Eighty miles. They are not friends. They compete for the vein's small yield. My mother's cousin is there — Tova Stonfold. She will take me in. She owes Seralen for something I never asked about."* (This plants **the Stonfold smith-family** by name for #0006.)
- **"Why are the Bone-Collectors coming?"** *"A client in Istar wants the Mirror. They have been trying for six years. Keloth's people are the latest."* (This confirms the **Lady Tiran seed for #0005** — Istaran client connection; Mira does not know Tiran's name.)
- **"Your mother Seralen — what happened?"** *"She died in my arms of a stroke, holding my hand. I was thirty-two. I have not said her name aloud since. She asked me, the day before she died, to promise I would not look in the mirror. I promised. I have kept the promise. Tonight is the first time I have said her name."* **This is Mira's emotional core. Do not rush.**

### The journal page

If the party names Aelwen with respect (any PC; Kessa is the most likely), Mira opens the cupboard and produces a single folded page, yellowed at the edges, smelling of pyre-smoke. Aelwen's handwriting.

**The page reads:**

> *"I made six. I made the Rose first. I made the Reliquary second. I made the Mirror third. I made three more in secret after the Mirror and I can no longer remember what I named them. I think I should have forged an ingot instead. I did not have the courage. I have been a coward. I have been ashamed. I have been weighed, and found lacking. I will go to the pine by the back of the house now."*
>
> *— Aelwen, PC 179*

**The pine by the back of the house** is where she hanged herself. Heva found her there.

**The ingot reference** is the **seed for #0007 finale** — Aelwen's Silver-Ingot Confession is exactly what she says here she should have forged. This is Hint 4's **pre-plant.**

### Insight DC 13 on Mira

A PC with Insight 13 sees: *"She will not survive this trust. She has ended herself with the telling."* It is not suicide; it is abdication. Mira has given the mirror to the party because she is 62, tired, and at the end of her role.

## GM Notes

- **Mira's long sentence is load-bearing.** Read it slowly. Do not interrupt. Let the players sit with it.
- **The journal page is a rev-2-commitment moment.** If a PC names Aelwen aloud with respect, the DM MUST give the page. This is not a trust-check; it is a DM obligation.
- **The cross-adventure phrase** is ready to land in this scene via the open ledger. Give the party time to ask about the ledger. If no one asks, Mira will say *"the middle page is older than I am; you may look."* Passive 15 catches. If the party's passive is 15+ (Kessa's is 12 or 13 unless she has good rolls), the phrase lands ambiently. **If neither path triggers:** Mira herself, on the party's way out, will say *"there is an entry in the ledger that will matter to the scholar"* and point to it. Rubric v1.2 recovery-path fulfilled.
- **Do NOT open the lower chamber in Scene 4.** The reveal is Scene 6. Scene 4 ends with Mira saying "I will open it at dawn, after tonight's work, if you are still willing."

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Aelwen, the night before she went to the pine: she stood at the workshop window and watched Heva sleep. Heva was eighteen. Aelwen knew she had failed her. The page the party holds is what Heva rescued — not all of the journals, one page — because it was the only page Aelwen had left on the workshop table after she closed her last ledger.*
