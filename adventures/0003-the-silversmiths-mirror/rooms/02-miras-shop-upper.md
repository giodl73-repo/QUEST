# Scene 02 — Mira's Shop (Upper Floor)

## Read-aloud

> The shop is small. Three paces across. The air carries silver-oil and old woodsmoke — the smell of a place that has not been aired out in a long time. A counter, a workbench behind it with a single silver ingot half-worked, a rack of finished goods — pendants, pins, two silver cups engraved with the same half-bloomed rose from the street-arch. On the back wall, above the workbench, a pale rectangle where something hung until very recently. The rack's silver gleams with a specific clockwise-polish sheen. A woman stands behind the counter with a silversmith's hammer in her left hand, unmoving. Her hair is grey-braided. Her eyes are a specific silver-grey. She says nothing.

## Features

- **The counter.** Smooth-worn oak. A small set of Mira's pens and a sales-ledger (leather-bound; decades of entries). The ledger is **closed** at session start. If opened without asking: Mira's first words are *nyet* (no).
- **The workbench behind.** Half-worked ingot; smith's tools; a small anvil. Fresh smith-work smell — metal dust and oil. A scrap of paper on the bench: a customer order for a pair of wedding rings (unrelated; flavor).
- **The rack (behind Mira).** Finished goods for sale: 6 pendants (5-20 gp each), 4 pins (3-8 gp), 2 cups engraved with the Vaenshold half-bloomed rose (45 gp each; Kessa's Arcana will confirm they bear the same mark as the Silver Rose AND Aelwen's workshop). Mira will sell any of these; they are shop-grade, not artifact-grade.
- **The door behind the counter.** Closed. Leads to Scene 4 (Mira's Hearth) — her private space. Mira will NOT let the party past this door in Scene 2. It opens in Scene 4 after trust is established.
- **Harel (apprentice).** 15, present. She sits on a stool at the back of the shop, polishing a pendant-chain. She speaks only when addressed. When addressed, she speaks plainly and quickly — she has not learned Mira's economy yet.
- **The pale rectangle on the back wall.** Perception DC 10: outline of an apron that hung on the wall for decades until a few years ago. Seralen's apron. Mira finally took it down when she could no longer bear seeing it; she has not put anything in its place. Active Investigation DC 12 finds the iron nail still set in the stone.
- **The rack's polish pattern.** Any PC (passive notice; no roll) who pays attention to the shop goods sees that each piece has been polished in a consistent clockwise circle. Stuart's hint: Seralen's motion, continued by Mira for 30 years.

## Dialogue beats (DM plays Mira and Harel)

**Mira's opener, after the party enters:**

*"You are the Varduin party. Brother Laen sent word. You come sooner than I expected. Harel — tea."*

Mira does not offer names. She does not ask for the party's names unless she decides to trust them.

**If Kessa is present and speaks first, Mira addresses her:**

*"Silversmith's kin. You carry something of the vein. Put it on the counter."*

### Harel's aside (Scene 3 unavoidable-ification)

**Before the party leaves Scene 2,** Harel will catch one PC's eye on their way out the door — whichever PC has been kindest to her, or to Kessa if she showed no preference. She will speak quietly, not in Mira's hearing:

*"I have counted six days. Two of them at the back alley at night. One at the inn window who does not drink. My mother polished with a different motion; I cannot make myself do Mira's, so I see the shop different. I see the back door at night. You should see it before the dark."*

Then she returns to her stool. **This converts Scene 3 from optional to actively offered** — the party has a named reason to scout the alleys. The GM-notes direction ("Let Scene 1's choice matter") is preserved, but if Scene 1 went poorly, Harel's intel gives the party a second door into Scene 3. Her line about her mother's polishing motion is a character-seed (her mother taught her a motion before the plague-year killed her; she has kept it; it is her only inheritance).

(Mira will name Kessa's silver rose-petal on sight. This is a recognition beat. She will not take the petal; she wants to see it, and she will hand it back without comment.)

**Mira will answer:**
- **About the Bone-Collector threat:** she will tell the party what she knows (4-5 remnants scattered in town; their leader stays at the inn; they watch her shop but have not moved). She confirms she received Laen's warning and has made "some preparations" (she will not elaborate).
- **About the mirror:** she will **not** confirm or deny. She says *nyet* and changes the subject. She says *"I have work that is not yours."*
- **About her family line:** she confirms she is the last Vaenshold silversmith. She does not say her mother's name. She does not mention the six artifacts.

### Insight DC 15 reads

A Kessa or Grom Insight check on Mira will return: *she is afraid, but not of the Bone-Collectors. She is afraid of something she is keeping.* No further detail on a DC 15 success; Insight DC 18 returns: *she has been afraid for thirty years; it is a shape, not a feeling.*

### Investigation on the shop

- **Investigation DC 10 on the rack:** finds the half-bloomed rose mark on the cups. Kessa cross-references to the silver rose-petal = campaign-aware beat.
- **Investigation DC 13 on the sales-ledger (if permitted to browse, or if bought a cup and asked for a receipt):** finds entries spanning decades. **Passive-Perception DC 15 fallback** per rubric v1.2 for the key entry: among the ledger's margin-notes, decades old, one entry is in a different hand and reads *"paid in full; I will not look at the rose again."* The buyer is described only as *"the man who would not give his name."* **This is the cross-adventure signal recovery path from S02.** Mira, if asked about this entry, will say *"nyet."*

Mira will not normally permit ledger-browsing in Scene 2. The recovery path activates in Scene 4 when Mira opens the ledger voluntarily OR in Scene 6 when Mira's trust is fully established.

## GM Notes

- **Mira's recognition of Kessa.** Play this beat with weight. Mira has seen two other people in her life carry Vaenshold-silver trace: her mother Seralen (who made a small spoon for her) and Heva-via-her-portrait (the line's recognition-memory). Kessa is the third. Mira does not smile; she acknowledges.
- **Harel is a real person.** 15-year-old, Crystalmir-born, plague-orphaned. If a PC addresses her, let her answer. She will offer her own opinion on the Bone-Collectors (*"They have been watching for six days. I have counted"*). She has counted accurately; she is observant.
- **Scene 2 ends** when the party has what Mira will give in this visit — her acceptance of the help, the bone-collector-threat intel, a polite dismissal. She will invite them back at dusk (Scene 4) "for the hearth."
- **Do not let Mira open the hidden door in Scene 2.** The chamber reveal is Scene 6.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Seralen — Mira's mother, dead 30 years — used to polish the silver goods on the rack herself, each morning, before opening. She polished by rubbing a specific clockwise circle on each piece, using a specific grey rag she had inherited from her own mother. Mira has continued the practice. She does not think about it; she does it. She did it this morning, as she does every morning. The rag is threadbare now. Mira has never bought a replacement.*
