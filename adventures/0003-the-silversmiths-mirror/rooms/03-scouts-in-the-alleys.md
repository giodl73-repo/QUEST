# Scene 03 — Scouts in the Alleys

## Read-aloud

> The alleys off the market are narrow — two arms' width between stone walls. Grey lichen on the north faces. Laundry overhead. Puddles from the morning's rain. A cat sits on a windowsill and does not move. Somewhere ahead, a boot scrapes once and is still.

## Features

- **The alleys.** A rough Z-path from the Market Square [1] to Mira's back service door [2]. Three turns; ~150 yds total. Partial cover (lichen walls, stacked crates at corners).
- **The cat.** On a windowsill. Does not move. If the party looks, it blinks once. Pure flavor.
- **Kann's remnants in the alleys:** up to 2 scouts scattered along the path. Composition depends on party approach:
  - If the party came here directly after spotting Lorn in Scene 1 AND made no attempt to engage: **Lorn is here + 1 other scout (Pevra).** Both can be caught by surprise.
  - If the party talked to Lorn or spooked him in Scene 1: **Lorn is gone east; 1 other scout (Pevra) watches a specific corner.**
  - If the party did nothing in Scene 1: **Lorn shadowed the party to Mira's Shop; the alley is empty in Scene 3.** Scene 3 becomes an intel-gathering scene only (no combat).

## Encounter

**See `encounters/scout-intel.md`** for the full intel-encounter text.

Variable composition based on party approach. Stats:

### Lorn (if present — CR ¼, 50 XP)
- AC 13, HP 16, Shortsword +4 1d6+2, Longbow +4 1d8+2. See `npcs/kann-of-neraka.md`.

### Pevra (if present — CR ¼, Bandit variant, 50 XP)
- AC 12, HP 11, Scimitar +3 1d6+1, Light crossbow +3 1d8+1.
- **Tactics:** Pevra retreats toward the inn (where Kann is) if reduced below 5 HP. If she escapes, Kann knows the party is hunting.

### If both scouts are ambushed silently
Party gains:
- Kann's location (the inn on the main street)
- Cell strength (5 remnants total)
- The night-assault plan (sometime after midnight; Bone-Collectors strike when the shop is asleep)

### If one scout is spooked and flees
Kann gets warned; the night-assault moves forward by 3-4 hours. Scene 5 fires earlier than planned.

### If the party does not engage scouts at all
Scene 3 is a short walk-through. The party gets ambient intel (they see a man in the inn window who matches Lorn's description) but no cell numbers.

## Intel outcomes

If the party captures a scout alive (Intimidation DC 12 OR Persuasion DC 14), they learn:
- Kann leads; 5 remnants including Kann; based at the Crystalmir Inn (on the main street)
- The assault is planned "tonight, deep" (after midnight)
- The client is in Istar — an Istaran faction wants the Mirror. The scout doesn't know the client's name (Kann keeps that close).

## Treasure

- Scout's kit: 2x shortsword or scimitar; 1x bow; light crossbow + 15 bolts; 3-5 gp mixed coin per scout.
- If Lorn is captured or killed: a folded map in his pocket sketching the silversmith's shop exterior with three X's marking planned assault entry points (front door, back service door, upstairs window via a nearby roof). This map is **crucial for Scene 5 defense preparation.**

## Connections

- **East (toward Market):** Scene 1.
- **West (to Mira's back service door):** Scene 2 (alternate entry).
- **Northwest (toward the Crystalmir Inn):** Kann's location; not visited in Scene 3 but relevant for the night-assault.

## GM Notes

- **Let the party's Scene-1 choice matter.** If they spotted and engaged Lorn cleanly in Scene 1, Scene 3 rewards them with a full intel haul. If they blundered, Scene 3 is a hostile ambush. If they did nothing, Scene 3 is empty but the night-assault is worse. Three-tier tactical consequence of a passive-Perception-scale decision.
- **Pevra is a name, not just a stat block.** She was Idra's apprentice (from S02). If the party mentions Idra, Pevra will flinch. She knows Idra is dead. She is here because she believes Kann can get her home; she does not fully commit to the cell anymore. If captured AND treated with respect, she will share more intel than a pure-mercenary scout would.
- **The map is the Scene-5 prep payoff.** If the party has it, Scene 5's Night Assault is mechanically easier (they know where the attackers come from; can set up).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Pevra's last conversation with Idra was the morning Idra left for Gavek's Rest. Idra told Pevra to mind the cell's ledger at Neraka while she was gone. Pevra did. When the news came that Idra was dead, Pevra continued minding the ledger for three days before she understood she was the only one left of the Ninth House branch of the cell. She is minding what is no longer there.*
