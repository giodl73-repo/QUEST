# Aelwen's Mirror

*A palm-sized mirror, moon-silver handle, obsidian back, silver face polished to the edge of showing anything. It waits.*

## Appearance

Four inches across the polished face; eight inches handle. Moon-silver handle, engraved with Aelwen's smith-mark (a half-bloomed rose — the same sigil she used on the Silver Rose's petal-hinges, but with the rose *opening* here, not closing). Obsidian back — not Aelwen's usual material; she bought it specifically for this forging, at considerable cost, because obsidian *holds* what silver *shows*. Silver face polished to mirror-brightness with a slight hesitation at the edges; the polishing-lines do not perfectly converge.

Weighs about six ounces. The handle is cold at first touch and warms to the holder within a few heartbeats. The face is room-temperature.

## Presence / Desire

**The mirror refuses to reflect anyone without first pausing.** Those who lift it expecting a reflection get silence — the face stays blank for two or three heartbeats, then slowly resolves. Those who lift it expecting silence (as Mira lifts it when she dusts the obsidian back, eyes averted) get a faint warming of the silver — but they do not see the face; they have not turned it toward themselves.

The mirror is **looking for a particular attention** — one that is neither greedy (expecting a reflection that flatters) nor fearful (expecting one that accuses). Aelwen called this *"the waiting that is not waiting"* in her workshop notes. Those who bring that attention get the clearest reflection.

When **Aelwen's smith-mark** on the handle is held to bare skin, the holder feels a very faint pulse — the silver's awareness that it is being held by a kinsman or kinswoman of the maker. Mira has felt this her whole life; no one else still living has.

## Rarity

**Rare.** Attunement required. No kin prerequisite.

## Provenance

Aelwen forged the Mirror in **PC 180** — actually, across the year-before-her-death, in her final grief-period. Timeline:

- **PC 181 (approx.):** Aelwen forges the Silver Rose for Baron Edric of Vaenshold. Begins to forget her daughter Heva's face.
- **PC 180:** Aelwen writes the never-sent letter to the Ergothian abbot, begging him to keep the Rose from Ilendra. She also begins, in secret, the Mirror. Her journals describe it as *"what I should have forged instead."*
- **PC 180-179:** Aelwen forges four more artifacts in secret (never delivered to clients; locked in her workshop): the Wrath-Coin, the Pride-Circlet, the Thread-That-Frays, and one other she did not name in the surviving journals. Each grief's mirror.
- **PC 179:** Aelwen hangs herself. Heva Aelwensdaughter (Aelwen's 18-year-old daughter) enters the workshop, finds the six artifacts, and rescues the **last journal page** from the burning pyre. The Mirror is among the six.

The Mirror passes through the Vaenshold silversmith line (Heva → Rillina → Avel → Seralen → Mira). **No one in the line has ever attuned it.** Each has been unable to look.

**Mira is the sixth-generation holder** and the last of the direct line (no children). The mirror has been in her cellar-chamber for 30 years.

## Properties (mechanical)

### Attunement

Required. Attunement takes **one look** — not a short rest. The moment the holder turns the face toward themselves and sees the resolved reflection, the attunement completes. **This is a fast-attune: combat-pace.**

The mirror shows the holder their own face **as the person who loves them most sees them** — not observer, *felt recognition*. The experience lasts as long as the holder can bear it. First-time lookers: 1-2 seconds before they look away. Repeat-lookers: longer; one described by Heva in her journal as holding for "a full minute, weeping steadily."

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **Seen.** Advantage on Charisma checks with anyone who once loved the bearer (or whom the bearer once loved). The advantage derives from the bearer's refreshed sense of their own worth.
- **Direction (once per long rest).** The bearer may hold the mirror toward a named specific person (living or dead) and see themselves *as that person sees them*. Living: their current view. Dead: their pre-death view. For living persons who have never known the bearer: the mirror darkens; no reflection.
- **Smith-mark pulse (passive, free).** Anyone of Aelwen's line (Mira is the only living such person at campaign start; any of Aelwen's descendants would qualify) feels a faint heartbeat-pulse when holding the handle. Recognition across generations.

### Curse (while attuned)

**Self-image replacement.**

From the moment of attunement, the bearer's **internal self-image** — the face they see in dreams, the face they mentally picture when thinking of themselves — is permanently replaced with the mirror's reflection. The bearer can no longer picture themselves "from outside" without the most-lover's gaze coloring the image. In practical terms:

- Dreams featuring the bearer's face always show the reflected view.
- The bearer's self-description (in conversation, in writing) subtly shifts toward what the reflection revealed. They will use words like "loved" or "missed" about themselves without realizing.
- **The bearer's self-image is no longer their own.** It belongs to the reflection. Permanent.

This is not quite as brutal as the Silver Rose's memory-erosion or the Reliquary's memory-concentration — the bearer's actual memories are intact; their *sense of who they are* is relocated. But it is a permanent alteration. Greater restoration does NOT reverse it.

### The Blank-Mirror Trap (anchor-level warning)

**If the bearer's most-lover is dead AND has already attuned this same mirror** (not possible on first encounter; relevant if the mirror has been attuned by a prior-bearer's most-lover), OR **if no one living has ever loved the bearer most**, the mirror shows nothing. **A bearer who attunes to a blank mirror:**

1. The attunement is **permanent and irrevocable** — cannot be un-attuned even by forge-rite.
2. The bearer **loses the capacity to feel love for 30 days.** Not the memory of love; the capacity. Grief returns; warmth does not. Day 30: capacity restored; but the memory of those 30 days haunts the bearer.
3. If the bearer attunes a DIFFERENT artifact during those 30 days, the attunement fails (no love to anchor attachment).

**The Blank-Mirror Trap is Mira's actual fear.** Mira is 62; her most-lover (her mother Seralen) died 30 years ago; she has not loved another person as deeply. If Mira looked into the mirror, she is not certain whose face would show. She is not willing to find out.

### Ending the curse

- **Reorx-shattering rite (Grom):** at dawn on a forge-fire ≥ 1,800°C, Religion DC 15. Destroys the obsidian back; silver is un-worked; the Mirror is gone. Attunement ends; self-image replacement persists (already permanent).
- **Reciprocal recognition:** the attuner looks into the mirror in the presence of the reflected person — and offers them the mirror. The reflected person accepts. The attunement becomes mutual; the curse is **ended for the original attuner** (self-image returns to the attuner); the reflected person attunes (same terms apply to them). This requires the reflected person be alive, present, and willing. For attunements where the reflected is dead, impossible.
- **Blank-mirror attunement is irrevocable.** No ending.

## Hooks

- **Aelric + Doriel.** If Aelric attunes, the mirror will show him Doriel's gaze — the woman he would have asked on his return, named in Varran's Minute. Doriel died three years after Varran. The image would be frozen at whatever view she held. Aelric did not know Doriel existed until the Minute; he knows her name only; her face is unknown to him. The mirror would show him *the face Doriel imagined him as* — which may be significantly different from his own.
- **Kessa + Vethrenn.** Vethrenn is dead (went silent PC 100). If Kessa attunes, the mirror may show her Vethrenn's gaze — the mentor she never met but who preserved the commonplace book for her. **Kessa may be the Blank-Mirror Trap case:** was Vethrenn's love for Kessa love or something more like transmission? The mirror would tell, brutally.
- **Thera + Adda.** Adda is dead (PC 3, winter-sickness). If Thera attunes, the mirror shows her Adda's gaze — the eight-year-old version of Thera her mother last saw. A heavy encounter.
- **Grom + Duren.** Duren is dead (PC 23, mine-collapse — soon to be revealed as murder in #0004). If Grom attunes, the mirror shows Duren's gaze. Duren's view of Grom was professional respect deepening to fatherhood. Heavy but not crushing.
- **Mira + Seralen.** Mira's most-lover was her mother. She has refused to look for 30 years.
- **The fifth artifact Aelwen made (in PC 180 secret forgings) — unnamed in surviving journals.** The party may learn of its existence but not its nature this session. Seed for #0007 finale — it is one of the six inscribed on the Silver-Ingot Confession.

## GM notes — Option C at attunement

**If any PC decides to attune the Mirror, spawn that PC as a subagent** (per `session-runner` Section 2.3 Option C). This is the session's canonical Option-C trigger. The subagent reads:
- Their PC sheet in full.
- Their grief-paragraph.
- A prompt describing what the Mirror is, what the Blank-Mirror Trap is, what it will show (if not blank), and what it costs (self-image permanent replacement).
- The other party members' in-scene voices (Grom's argument, Mira's silence, whatever the party has said).

The subagent's response is binding — whether to attune, what they see, what they say.
