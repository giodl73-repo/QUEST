# Curse Symptoms Manifest — Aelwen's Mirror

Cross-skill contract: `dungeon-smith` must honor ≥ 3 symptoms across ≥ 2 rooms/scenes. This manifest delivers **7 symptoms across 5 scenes** plus **1 cross-adventure recovery signal**. All anchor-level symptoms have passive-Perception fallbacks per rubric v1.2.

## Required (≥ 3, distributed across ≥ 2 scenes)

- [x] **Visual symptom (shop-signage filigree).** The silver-filigree letters above Mira's shop — which Aelwen's mother's great-great-grandmother carved — dim by one perceptible degree when the lower chamber's hidden door is breached. Primary: Perception DC 12. **Passive-Perception fallback DC 14** (rubric v1.2 anchor-level). Place: Scene 6 as the party approaches the hidden door OR Scene 5 if the assault breaches the cellar.

- [x] **Visual symptom (mirror's pause).** The mirror does not reflect immediately — it waits 2-3 heartbeats before resolving. Primary: anyone lifting the mirror notices (no roll). Anchor-level: the pause itself is the symptom; it tells the party the mirror is *choosing*.

- [x] **Textual symptom (attribution drift — continued pattern).** Aelwen's smith-mark on the Mirror's handle is a **half-bloomed rose opening** — the mirror of the Silver Rose's petal-hinges (which close). The same mark, inverted. A party that recalls the Silver Rose's petal-pattern (S01) can connect this. Primary: Kessa Arcana DC 10 OR History DC 12. **Passive-Perception 15 fallback** (she carries the silver rose-petal from S01 R05; direct comparison possible).

- [x] **Auditory symptom (cross-adventure phrase in receipt-book).** Mira's sales ledger contains an entry from decades ago with the same phrase verbatim: *"I will not look at the rose again."* Uncredited buyer (described only as "a man who would not give his name"; the transaction is for moon-silver ingots). **This is the S02 missed cross-adventure signal recovery path.** Primary: Investigation DC 13 on the ledger. **Passive-Perception 15 fallback** per rubric v1.2. Recovery mechanism: **Kessa's own notebook (from S02)** contains her transcription of Keloth's vault-ledger. Comparing the two books catches the phrase AND connects the cross-adventure signal. Place: Scene 4 (Mira's disclosure) or Scene 6 (lower chamber).

- [x] **Sensory symptom (dryness, continued cross-adventure).** The lower chamber is dry — even drier than Mira's cellar above it. Cross-adventure pattern matching the Rose Cairn (S01 R02 tomb) and Keloth's Hand lower level (S02). Primary: any character in the chamber notices (no roll). Pattern-recognition: Kessa Wisdom (Insight) DC 10 connects the three instances — *"moon-silver artifacts produce ambient dryness; this is the third."*

- [x] **Reaction moment (smith-mark pulse).** When a Vaenshold-line person holds the handle, the silver pulses faintly. Mira has felt this her whole life. No one else alive has. When Kessa examines the Mirror (or if the party tells Mira they've met other Vaenshold descendants — they have not), Mira can be asked and will acknowledge. Sensory, not dice-gated.

- [x] **Reaction moment (attunement blank-test).** At attunement, the mirror may go blank — the Blank-Mirror Trap. Mechanical: the attuning PC rolls nothing; the DM determines from the PC's PC-sheet whether their most-lover is alive + recognizable. See `aelwens-mirror.md` for the blank-cases.

## Required (always)

- [x] **Architectural expression of the mechanism.** The Mirror's hidden chamber is below Mira's living quarters — the mirror is physically *buried* under the silversmith's life. Stepping down to find it is stepping into Mira's refusal. The chamber's silver-filigree latch (which opens only to Mira's touch or Investigation DC 13) echoes the Mirror's own filigree handle.

- [x] **Earlier-scene foreshadowing.** Scene 1 (market arrival) establishes the Crystalmir town's *public* Vaenshold mark — carvings on old buildings using the same half-bloomed rose. Scene 2 (Mira's shop-upper) shows the same mark on shop goods. Scene 4 (hearth + disclosure) reveals the mark's origin. Scene 6 (lower chamber) delivers the mark in its truest form: on the Mirror itself.
