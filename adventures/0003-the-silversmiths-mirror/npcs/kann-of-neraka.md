# Kann of Neraka, Bone-Collector remnant leader

## Appearance

Lean, scarred across the left cheek (old wound from a Solamnic skirmish decades ago), forty-five years old. Nerakan by birth; Bone-Collector by enlistment, not faith. He is the cell's **practical** — the one who survived because he kept his head down while others performed their priesthood. Wears a plain traveler's cloak with a small moon-silver finger-bone on a cord inside the collar. No visible cell-insignia.

## Campaign-continuity note

**Kann's name appeared on the duty-roster in S02 Scene 5** — Keloth, Idra, Vesk (crossed out), **Kann**, Tull. Vesk died at the Rose Cairn pre-S01. Keloth and Idra died at S02's Scene 6. Kann and Tull were the two cell-members in Keloth's faction who **were not at Gavek's Rest** when the Varduin Muster arrived — they were west with the feint-wing at Varduin. Tull died in the Varduin raid (one of the four killed). **Kann escaped east.** He is one of the three east-escapees named in the S02 handoff.

Kann now commands **4 surviving remnants** (himself plus 3 others regrouped from the feint + the east-escapees):
- **Kann** (leader, CR 1/2 by homebrew — see stat block)
- **Pevra** (ex-apprentice of Idra; lieutenant; CR 1/4 Bandit variant)
- **Lorn** (Nerakan hunter; CR 1/4 Scout variant)
- **Vend** (silent; the one who loads the crossbows; CR 1/8 Bandit)

## Background

Kann was a road-guard in eastern Abanasinia twenty years ago; a caravan he guarded was hit by Solamnic-border skirmishers and he lost an ear and a wife in the same week. Keloth recruited him through Idra, whom Kann respected professionally (never romantically — Idra had her own complications). Kann served the cell for fifteen years **without ever attuning to any reliquary**. He saw the work from the outside. When Keloth died, Kann inherited the command by survival, not succession.

He does not share Keloth's priestly convictions. He does not believe grief-preservation is mercy. He believes the cell's work is **economic** — the artifacts are valuable; the clients (Nerakan nobles, Istaran faction-buyers, occasionally Solamnic discontents) pay well. With Keloth dead, the cell is broken as a religious organization. As a commercial operation, it is still functional — if Kann can deliver one more asset.

**His goal: the Mirror.** A client in Istar (connected to Lady Tiran's faction — this is a seed for #0005) has offered a substantial price for any of Aelwen's late-career works. The Mirror is the highest-value item known. Kann needs to retrieve it before the Varduin Muster arrives, OR kill the Muster and take it from them.

## What he wants this session

- **Primary:** the Mirror. Alive or dead; delivered to the Istaran client.
- **Secondary:** the Varduin Muster dead. They killed Idra. He is not motivated by vengeance (Idra was professional, not personal), but a cell that lets its killers live attracts no future clients.
- **Tertiary:** himself alive. Kann is not a zealot; if cornered, he will surrender to talk. He will bargain.

## Stat block (homebrew CR 1/2, 100 XP)

- **AC** 14 (studded leather) · **HP** 25 (4d8+4) · **Speed** 30 ft
- **Str** 12 · **Dex** 15 · **Con** 13 · **Int** 12 · **Wis** 13 · **Cha** 11
- **Skills:** Perception +3, Stealth +4, Survival +3, Investigation +3
- **Senses:** passive Perception 13
- **Languages:** Common, Nerakan
- **Practical:** Kann has disadvantage on saves vs. *commanded-to-surrender* spells; he surrenders voluntarily when cornered.

**Actions:**
- **Multiattack:** two weapon attacks.
- **Shortsword:** +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing.
- **Light crossbow:** +4 to hit, 80/320, 1d8+2 piercing.
- **Blade-throw (bonus action, 1/rest):** ranged +4, 1d4+2 piercing. He carries 3 throwing blades.

**Reaction — Commercial Disengage:** when reduced below 8 HP by an attack, Kann uses his reaction to make a shortsword attack at advantage and then Disengage at regular movement speed. He will try to escape rather than die.

## Voice & tactics

**Voice:**
- Calm, businesslike. Addresses opponents as "the Varduin contract" when not speaking to them directly; "friend" when speaking to them. The friend-form is not friendly.
- Uses the first-person plural: *"We have come for Aelwen's last work."* Speaks for the cell even when alone.
- Will bargain mid-combat: *"Give us the Mirror. We leave. We come no further. A fair trade."*
- Does not say Keloth's name. Keloth's death was a cost; Kann does not revisit costs.

**Tactics:**
- Kann's remnants strike **at night** (Scene 5 assault).
- Lorn (Scout) enters first via alleys [3]; disables any lookout.
- Pevra + Vend breach the shop's back door simultaneously with the front door.
- Kann stays on the street with his crossbow; covers the escape if needed.
- Kann will NOT enter the lower chamber ([6]) — he does not know it exists; Mira has not mentioned it to anyone.
- If the Mirror is taken during the fight, Kann Disengages immediately and flees the town. He considers that an acceptable delivery; the Mirror is recoverable.
- If Kann is captured alive (Intimidation DC 14 OR Persuasion DC 16): he will trade the name of the Istaran client for his freedom. The name: **a clerk named Felor, of the Second House of Istar.** Seeds Lady Tiran's faction for #0005.

## Secrets

- Kann has a small moon-silver finger-bone **that is his own.** He had it made thirty years ago, after losing his wife, by a hedge-silversmith in Stonfold. He has never attuned it; it carries his wife's last minute but he has refused to hear it. It is identical-in-spirit to Keloth's bracelet (Scene 6 of S02). If the party recovers it post-combat, they have another reliquary to decide about at the finale.

**Kann carries a non-Vaenshold-vein artifact, made by the Stonfold smith-family — this is a PLANTED SEED for #0006 and #0007.** The Mirror is not the only non-Vaenshold silver the party encounters this session.

## Post-session outcomes

- **Kann dead:** cell remnant gutted. Lorn + Pevra + Vend die with him or flee. Istaran client lead dies with Kann unless his finger-bone is recovered (the client's name is also engraved in tiny Nerakan on the finger-bone's cord).
- **Kann captured + bargained:** Lady Tiran's faction exposed early (seed for #0005 lights up prematurely; adjustable).
- **Kann escaped:** he regroups further east; returns in #0006 or #0007 as a minor second-wave threat. Reduces the finale's hint-count availability (if hints 1 + 2 catch, Kann's return still doesn't undo them; but his presence at the finale complicates the decision scene).

## GM notes

- **Kann is not Keloth.** Play him practical, not mystical. Where Keloth had priestly patience, Kann has commercial patience. Different rhythm; same dangerous outcomes.
- **The finger-bone is a quiet plant.** Don't foreground it. Let the party find it post-combat and ask.
- **Kann will recognize Aelric's plate** if close — he fought a Solamnic knight on the Neraka-border decades ago. He may mention it: *"I knew a knight with that shoulder-work. Kharolis-era. Are you his —?"* Leave the question open. (Optional: this is Varran. Kann was at the Neraka-border skirmish where Varran died. Kann did not kill Varran; Keloth did. But Kann was present. If Aelric picks up this thread, the session's emotional register shifts.)
