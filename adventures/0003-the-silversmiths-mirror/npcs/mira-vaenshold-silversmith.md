# Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith, 62, of Crystalmir-by-the-Fells

## Appearance

Slight, grey-braided, eyes a specific silver-grey that runs in her family-line. Hands that have worked silver for fifty years — knuckles enlarged, fingertips calloused but still sensitive. She wears a leather apron she has not replaced in thirty years; it carries thumb-prints of every ingot she has ever shaped. Her face does not hold expression easily; she has trained it out of doing so. When she smiles — rarely — it is startling.

## Background

Born PC 82 in Crystalmir-by-the-Fells; has lived here all her life. Great-great-granddaughter of **Aelwen the Silversmith of Vaenshold** (the forger of the Silver Rose; died PC 179). The silversmith line runs: Aelwen → **Heva Aelwensdaughter** (Mira's great-great-grandmother; PC 198-120) → Heva's daughter **Rillina** (Mira's great-grandmother; PC 140-78) → Rillina's daughter **Avel** (Mira's grandmother; PC 95-40) → Avel's daughter **Seralen** (Mira's mother; PC 62-18) → Mira (PC 82-present). Five generations since Aelwen.

The mirror came to Mira at **PC 50**, when her mother Seralen died. Seralen had it from Avel; Avel had it from Rillina; Rillina had it from Heva. **Heva received it from Aelwen directly** — it was Aelwen's last forging before her hanging in PC 179. Every silversmith since has refused to look into it. The refusal has become the line's *inheritance*.

Mira has been alone in the shop since Seralen's death (thirty years ago). She took in **Harel** (an orphan from Crystalmir's plague-year, PC 12; Harel is 15 now) eight years ago. Harel is being trained; Harel does not know the mirror exists.

## Voice & manner

- Economical. Nothing wasted. Where another woman would say "thank you," Mira nods.
- Uses her great-great-grandmother's contraction for "no" — a sharp *nyet* that sounds Nerakan but is actually Vaenshold-provincial. She does not gloss it.
- She will not speak of the mirror until she has decided whether to trust the party. Trust is slow; she measures each PC against a standard she will not name.
- She addresses Kessa as *"silversmith's kin"* on first meeting — recognizing the moon-silver petal Kessa carries (from the Rose Cairn). It is not a compliment; it is an acknowledgment.

## What she knows (and does not say)

- Aelwen made six moon-silver artifacts. Mira knows three by name (the Rose, the Reliquary — she does not know the Reliquary's current occupant; the Mirror). She suspects three more but does not know what they are. **This is exact campaign-truth: there ARE six, plus the seventh-as-ingot at the finale.**
- Aelwen's workshop still stands at the Vaenshold estate. Mira has never visited. Seralen told her it was "beyond her to enter."
- The second moon-silver smith-family — a hill-family from **Stonfold**, 80 miles southwest — has existed since Aelwen's time. Mira does not like them; they compete for the vein's small yield. She will mention them if asked; she will not volunteer them. (Seed for #0006; confirmed here.)
- Her mother Seralen **was a hedge-wizard**. Unsanctioned by the Wizards of High Sorcery. Seralen died in Mira's arms of a stroke while Mira held her hand. Mira has not spoken Seralen's name aloud in thirty years. (This is the **Scene 4 disclosure** — the emotional core of the adventure.)

## What she wants this session

- **Primary:** her apprentice Harel safe. Not the shop; not the mirror; not even her own life. Harel.
- **Secondary:** Harel never look into the mirror.
- **Tertiary:** the mirror finally leave her house — either destroyed or taken far. She has been its jailer thirty years; she wants release.
- **Not wanted:** to look into the mirror herself. She has spent thirty years holding that refusal. She will not reverse it under any pressure the party can apply.

## Stat block (treat as Commoner variant; non-combatant)

- **AC** 10 · **HP** 8 (2d6+1) · **Speed** 30 ft (slower in shop; lifetime's worth of sawdust in her knees)
- **Str** 9 · **Dex** 10 · **Con** 12 · **Int** 15 · **Wis** 16 · **Cha** 12
- **Skills:** Arcana +4, History +4, Insight +5, Investigation +4, Perception +5 (proficient)
- **Senses:** passive Perception 15 (proficient + Wis)
- **Languages:** Common, dwarf-pidgin (traveler's), can read moon-silver engravings like a woman reading her own letters
- **Silversmith's hammer** (improvised): +1 to hit, 1d4 bludgeoning. She will not use it unless there is no one else between Harel and death.

## How the DM plays her

**Decision order:**
1. Harel's safety.
2. The mirror stays unlooked.
3. The party is told what they need to know to make their own choices; no more.
4. Her own survival.

**Voice tags:**
- *Nyet* for "no."
- Addresses Kessa as "silversmith's kin" on first meeting; does not explain.
- Ends sentences early: *"I have said."* *"That is the work."* *"You will not need more from me."*
- Uses one long sentence per scene when she decides to explain something. Before that sentence and after it, short.

**Secrets:**
- She has, hidden in a cupboard in her own room (not the lower chamber), **Aelwen's last journal page** — not burnt, despite what Aelwen thought. Heva rescued it from the funeral pyre before the ink set. The page contains Aelwen's sketch of what *she would have forged next if she had the courage*. The sketch is of an **ingot, not a forged artifact.** *This hints at the finale's Silver-Ingot Confession without giving it away.*
- She will show this page only to **a PC who has named Aelwen aloud with respect** (Kessa qualifies; Aelric qualifies if he speaks of the Order's silence on Aelwen). She will show no one else.

## Scene-by-scene play

- **Scene 2 (shop upper):** terse, measuring. Lets them in. Offers tea. Asks why they have come. Will not discuss the mirror; will discuss the Bone-Collector threat. Commits to cooperate.
- **Scene 4 (hearth disclosure):** decides to tell them about Aelwen. One long sentence, perhaps two. Produces the journal page if the trust-gate is met. Does NOT open the lower chamber.
- **Scene 5 (night assault):** hides Harel. Fights with the silversmith's hammer if a Bone-Collector reaches Harel's room.
- **Scene 6 (lower chamber):** *after* the assault, she opens the hidden door — only if the party has defended the shop well and Harel is alive. Reveals the mirror. Does not look in. Asks the party what they will do.
- **Scene 7 (dawn):** delivers her own decision about her future. She will close the shop; move to Stonfold (competing smith-family who will take her in despite the history); Harel goes with her if Harel wants.

## Post-session seeds

- **Aelwen's last journal page** (if given to the party). Hints at the Silver-Ingot Confession without spoiling it.
- **The Stonfold smith-family** named. Seed for #0006.
- **Harel, age 15.** Apprentice. Could become a PC in a post-campaign era (she would be 20+ at the Cataclysm).
- **Mira's departure from Crystalmir** — the shop closes. The Mira-protection contract with the Rose Council is ambiguously fulfilled; Council may or may not extend it.

## GM notes

- **Play Mira's restraint.** Her silence is not evasion — it is a thirty-year practice of holding a thing she does not want to hand anyone. Do not make her chatty under pressure.
- **Harel is a person, not a plot device.** Let her have a voice in Scene 2 (brief; apprentice being introduced) and Scene 5 (terrified but not silent). Do not kill her unless the party fails catastrophically.
- **Mira is not grateful.** The party is doing a job; she did not hire them; she will not thank them for it. She will respect competence. She will not repay it.
