## Critique

The setting held across two sessions now, and held *cumulatively*. That is the harder test. I will explain what I mean.

### What worked

- **The moon-silver vein is now a cross-adventure structural fact.** The Silver Rose (#0001) and the Bone-Collector reliquaries (#0002) are forged from silver sourced from the same Vaenshold vein via the same silversmith-line (Aelwen → Heva → Mira). Kessa recognized the alloy on the Howl-Stones lieutenant's finger-bone ("same vein") and the party received this as continuity, not coincidence. The workshop has built a *physical infrastructure* underneath the artifact-lore. Commendable.
- **Gavek the dwarf was a proper setting beat.** A dwarf running an Istari wayhouse "not in his own name" during the late Kingpriest years, because the Kingpriest would have objected — this is Age-of-Might politics at the grassroots level. Grom's hearth-thumb identification worked because Grom's character sheet is *Thorbardin-inflected*. Setting hit setting cleanly.
- **The pre-Istaran Nerakan carvings at Howl-Stones** — another lore-layer. The stones predate the current Nerakan culture; the current Bone-Collectors have over-scratched the older marks with cruder work. This is attribution-drift operating at a geological time-scale rather than a generational one. Good depth.
- **Sir Venric's pyre.** A Solamnic death, handled with proper ritual (Grom's Reorx-rite in Dwarvish; Aelric's three-verse attention). The workshop is now treating Solamnic death-customs as a stable feature of its Dragonlance-native approach. No anachronism; no generic-fantasy drift.

### What needs work

- **The cross-adventure signal did not fire.** The wall-ledger's uncredited *"I will not look at the rose again"* entry — verbatim from S01's Vesk memory fragment, placed by design as the workshop's first explicit cross-session echo — was gated behind Kessa Investigation DC 13 and Perception DC 13. She rolled 12 and 11. Twice, by one. The signal is still on the wall; the party left without seeing it. This is the session's largest lore-landing miss. **Recommend: passive-Perception fallback on anchor-level cross-adventure signals.** (This matches the Gate's innovation flag.)
- **Reya's name was not recovered.** Mensa's notebook (Investigation DC 13 at the kitchen dresser) held Pella's mother's name — the most specific piece of setting-embedded character-lore in Scene 1. Thera rolled 7. The name sits in the waystation unread. Pella does not yet know her mother was named Reya. **A future session should return to the waystation; the notebook is still there.**
- **Jannet of Crownhold went uncaught.** The kitchen cameo (Investigation DC 15) connects Mensa's waystation to Aelric's family line. Aelric rolled 8. The connection is unactivated. Again, the waystation holds it; future revisit possible.
- **The workshop-canon file is doing its job.** I checked during review: Haena of the Fourth House, Sergeant Hern, Mira-no-kin, Sir Venric, Alys — all entered canonically post-S02 addition. This is the discipline that will matter if the workshop ever publishes. Keep it up.

### The question I'd push on

**What does the workshop do when its ambition outpaces its dice?** The cross-adventure signal on Keloth's ledger is the most sophisticated design element the workshop has produced — a textual artifact that carries meaning across two adventures, gated behind setting-knowledge (Vesk's memory fragment from S01). It failed on two consecutive Investigations by one point each. The setting had the goods; the dice said no. **I want a design rule:** when a signal's failure means the entire cross-adventure meaning is lost, give the signal multiple paths to land — passive Perception, triggered by proximity to an adjacent beat (e.g., a Cultist's casual reference), or re-discoverable on post-session transcription-review. The ambition is right. The single-gate is fragile.
