## Critique

A denser session than #0001. More NPCs to track; more named dead; a moral climax that requires the DM to deliver Keloth's offer in-voice; a reliquary mechanic that asks the attuning player to describe a minute they did not experience. A new player at this table would have been carried by the party's existing chemistry and Kessa's scholarly exposition — but not every first-time-player has that luxury.

### What worked

- **The duty-roster as in-fiction intelligence.** *"Keloth, Idra, Vesk (crossed out), Kann, Tull."* Five names, and the crossed-out one is a name the players know (Vesk from #0001). This is a new-player-friendly intelligence moment — no setting-knowledge required; you read names, you notice one is crossed out, you remember Vesk is dead. The drift is visible without lore.
- **Pella's voice was accessible.** Short sentences. Direct eye contact. One Nerakan word (*chal*) used without glossing — and the context carries it: *"You are chal knights? Papa said only chal knights come from Solamnia."* A new reader infers *chal = real/proper* from context in the same sentence. Clean.
- **Grom's forge-metaphor vocabulary is accessible,** and it has grown more consistent across two sessions. *"Ash and cinder,"* *"by the Black Anvil,"* *"needs hammering out."* A new player picks these up within a scene and can re-use them. The DM-as-Grom maintained register.
- **Mechanics-translated-to-fiction remains strong.** The crit on Kessa was narrated as *"a bolt strikes deep enough to fold her behind a stone"* — not "Kessa takes 15 damage." The in-fiction reader can sense Kessa is nearly dead without needing the number.

### What needs work

- **Scene 6 had a lot moving.** Keloth's offer; Pella's question; Grom's counter-argument; Aelric's decision; the attunement silence; Idra's move; combat opening. A new player would have gotten lost somewhere between *"trade accepted"* and *"combat open."* The module has been revised to include a **Keloth-Trade Decision Table** on the cheatsheet — and that helped — but the *transition* from trade-accepted to combat was DM-improvisation (Idra's move) and would not have been on the cheatsheet. New-player confusion likely.
- **Rubric-v1.1 per-PC reception tracking is invisible to players.** A DM fills out the 22-row table during play. A player at the table never sees it. If the player is new, they have no idea the DM is tracking this; they may wonder why the DM keeps asking them "what did you see?" Consider: during a trade-scene, a one-line PC-facing prompt — *"Each of you, describe what your character is noticing."* — makes the tracking visible as a conversational move rather than a DM's clipboard.
- **The attunement Minute is a player-facing dead zone in Option A.** The DM-as-Aelric played both the narrator of the Minute AND the experiencer. A new player at the table would see nothing — the DM goes still for sixty seconds, then describes a Minute. There is no *conversation.* The module's rev 2 commits to the DM asking the player what Varran said/did/thought/felt. In Option A, the player and DM are the same person. **This is a limitation worth documenting.** For a new player in Option A context, the Minute plays as a monologue; in Option C (future), it would play as a conversation.
- **Keloth's Nerakan formal register** ("Sir Knight"; "opposed" rather than "enemy"; "Idra of the Ninth House") requires a brief gloss for a reader who does not know Dragonlance's Order hierarchy. The module inlines *"Nerakan"* as "language of the eastern wastes," which is fine. It does not gloss "of the Ninth House" — that is a house-identifier common to Nerakan culture. **Glosses should be one-line inline; missing them is cumulative on a new reader.**
- **Scene 08's Sir Venric pyre** was beautiful and required the new player to understand that Solamnic funerals involve verse-readings, that Reorx-rites in Dwarvish are a dwarf-cleric's proper contribution, and that Aelric standing at attention for three verses is a *private* realization beat (Varran had no pyre). None of this is in-scene-explained. A new player sees a funeral; they do not see the *layers*.

### The question I'd push on

**Is the workshop writing for a reader with campaign memory, or for a reader who arrived at this session cold?** S02 is the second session; the adventure contains cross-adventure signals; the party carries S01 consequences; Aelric's dead brother is the artifact's occupant because S01 established Aelric's character sheet. A new player at S02 without S01 can follow most of the surface but will miss the structural reward — Varran's Minute means less if you did not play S01. **The workshop should decide:** is this a campaign (continuous reading required) or an anthology (each session re-entrant)? I have assumed campaign; the accessibility policy in CLAUDE.md treats it as anthology. The gap between those two answers is the Fresh Face's permanent discomfort.
