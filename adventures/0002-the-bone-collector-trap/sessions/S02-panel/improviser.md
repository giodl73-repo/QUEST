## Critique

The module left one important hole and the DM filled it in character. That is the test.

### What worked

- **Keloth's post-attunement decision to fight.** The module's Option A is "trade accepted; cell walks out; no combat." The DM adjudicated that Idra — Keloth's lieutenant — would not stand passive while a Solamnic knight attuned a cell-forged reliquary, and would move on Pella. Grom's Spiritual Weapon intercepted; combat opened; Keloth declared the trade complete but his cell's self-defense restored. **This is a DM saying yes to the fiction over saying yes to the module.** The hole was real; the fill was fair.
- **Grom's counter-argument at the altar** was not in the module. The module told the DM "play Keloth calmly; let the party decide slowly." It did not script Grom's intervention. The DM-as-Grom improvised a five-sentence counter-argument that landed in-character. Then the DM-as-Aelric heard it and still chose the trade. Two PC improvisations talking to each other inside a scripted scene — exactly the kind of PC-to-PC texture the Improviser lens is asking for.
- **The session's 8 `<!-- SURPRISE -->` tags.** Healthy tag-count for improvisation tracking. At least two are actionable for rubric-amendment (Investigation-gated misses; per-PC prediction lossiness).
- **Pella's *"You came"* after the fight** is an emergent beat. The module committed to her interior as certainty; the DM-as-Pella found the post-combat line unprompted. Nine-year-old silent, small hand on pauldron, two words. The module gave the certainty; the DM provided the silence and the line.

### What needs work

- **The Investigation fumble on the well-cover was a missed improvisation opportunity.** Thera rolled 1 on the straw pile. The DM played it straight: *"Just old hay."* That is correct but not improvisational. A livelier DM might have said: *"Thera, you dig through the straw and find... a dead rat. Old. It has been there long enough to be dry."* — still a fumble, still a miss, but with texture. The module's fumble-outcomes are binary. Improvisation asks for narration, not just DC-pass/fail.
- **Bandit B's surrender and intel-dump happened off-camera fast.** Intimidation 18 = auto-pass; intel delivered in bullet points. Under pressure, this is fine. In a richer session it would have been a scene — Bandit B's fear, his Nerakan accent, a small detail he volunteers after a pause. The module has the intel pre-written; it does not have Bandit B's *voice*. The DM didn't invent one.
- **Aelric attuning was played as solo silent interior, not as a scene.** Per the module rev 2 and rev-3-from-#0001, when a PC attunes an artifact with a Minute, the DM "asks the player to describe." In Option A's solo-DM mode, the DM-as-Aelric described the Minute to himself; there was no *collaborative* improvisation. This is a known Option-A limitation (surfaced in S02's log as a design note). Option C — agent-per-PC at key moments — would have let an Aelric-agent describe the Minute in their own voice, and *the other three PC-agents* would have watched with their own reactions. Worth reopening.

### The question I'd push on

**When the module leaves a hole (Keloth post-attunement), the DM filled it fairly. When the module gates a symptom (wall-ledger at DC 13), the dice said no and the beat was lost. Should improvisation backstop the dice in the same way it backstops the module?** The Keloth hole got a DM invention. The ledger miss got no DM invention — the DM did not narrate, for example, that Kessa "on the ride home, flipping through her notebook, notices the uncredited entry." That's a DM-can-do move that would preserve the cross-adventure signal. The module didn't authorize it; the module-hole for Keloth wasn't authorized either. **What's the rule?** I want an answer: *when the dice miss a high-value atmospheric beat, is DM-improvisation to recover it a yes or a no?* I think yes — but the workshop has not decided.
