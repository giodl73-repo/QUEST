## Strongest Consensus

**Character agency (9 unweighted).** All five lenses scored 9. The attune-or-not decision at the altar — with Grom's counter-argument, Aelric's nine-second silence, Pella's certainty — was the session's strongest beat. The workshop's "treasure IS the story" principle extended to *choice about treasure IS the story.*

**Atmospheric landing (9 unweighted).** Inter-PC chain (rubric v1.1 10-criterion): Aelric → Grom → Grom's Spiritual Weapon → Aelric. Four-link chain within Scene 6. Plus Pella's *"You came"* image. Rubric v1.1 amendments delivered.

## Most Dissent

**Module fidelity (7).** Lorekeeper weighted heavily (1.4) and pushed hardest on the cross-adventure signal miss. Improviser (weight 0.4) weighted lowest. Fresh Face (0.7) noted the Scene 6 transition chaos. Philosophical split: the *missed* cross-adventure signal is a module-design issue (Lorekeeper) OR a fix-on-the-fly opportunity (Improviser) OR a new-player-confusion risk (Fresh Face). Three lenses, three legitimate framings of the same fact.

**Table readiness (7).** Moldvay-equivalent (Fresh Face + Tactician) flagged the Option-A-plus-combat branch as a module gap. DM adjudicated fairly but ad-hoc.

## Convergent Suggestions (multiple lenses agree)

### Priority-1

1. **Passive-Perception fallback on anchor-level atmospheric symptoms.** Gate + Tactician + Lorekeeper + Improviser all surfaced this from different angles. The wall-ledger's cross-adventure signal is the workshop's most ambitious single design element; it failed on two consecutive gating rolls by one point each. **Unified fix:** for manifest symptoms tagged as "cross-adventure signal" or "anchor-level," add a passive-Perception fallback at DC = active-Investigation + 2 (so Investigation 13 becomes also passive-Perception 15). A party with a careful wizard (passive 15+) would auto-land these even on cold dice. **This is a candidate rubric v1.1 → v1.2 amendment** (see Innovation threshold check below).

2. **Document the Option-A-plus-combat branch in `encounters/vault-climax.md`.** Tactician + Fresh Face both surfaced this. **Unified fix:** add a sub-branch: "Option A accepted but an NPC on the cell's side triggers combat (e.g., Idra's move-on-Pella during the attunement silence). Keloth considers the trade complete but his cell's integrity restored; fights to death on principle." Pre-cover this decision so DMs do not have to invent.

### Priority-2

3. **Per-PC reception "finder vs. receiver" separation.** Roleplayer + Lorekeeper both noted that the module predicted Thera on the bread-scrap; actually Kessa found it and Grom received it. **Unified fix:** update the per-PC reception table in module.md to separate *who rolls the detection* from *who the beat lands on emotionally.* Two columns.

4. **Voice-tag reinforcement in early scenes.** Roleplayer specifically: Aelric's Solamnic formal register only appeared in Scenes 5-9; Thera's hill-vernacular was almost absent. **Unified fix:** module's per-scene GM Notes should include a one-line "voice-tag prompt" for each PC — e.g., "Grom may thumb the inside-curve here" in Scene 3 (already present); equivalent prompts for the other three PCs in other scenes.

### Priority-3 (per-lens; taste calls)

- **Tactician:** formalize Portent-under-use as a known lossy mechanic (2 wasted Portents in S02; dependent on draw quality).
- **Roleplayer:** commit in campaign-continuity docs that Aelric's pre-death-Varran memories are permanently gone (no retroactive narration).
- **Improviser:** DM-recovery rule for missed anchor-level symptoms (e.g., Kessa "on the ride home, flipping through her notebook, notices...").
- **Fresh Face:** declare whether the workshop is a *campaign* (continuous reading required) or an *anthology* (each session re-entrant) — the accessibility policy and cross-adventure signaling are in tension.
- **Lorekeeper:** add a "Recovery Path" note to each manifest symptom indicating how a party could *still land it post-session* if missed in play.

## Panel Verdict (unified)

S02 held quality at 65.0 / 80 — flat vs. S01. The workshop is *producing consistent output*; it is not yet *improving*. The rubric-amendment cycle (v1.0 → v1.1) produced measurable gain between S01's pre-v1.1 score (65) and... still 65. The amendments landed as designed (per-PC reception tracked; inter-PC chains fired) but were offset by Module-fidelity regression (cross-adventure signal missed).

**The v1.2 amendment opportunity is clear:** passive-Perception fallback for anchor-level symptoms. This is the Priority-1 convergent suggestion and maps to the gate's Innovation Flag #1.

**Cluster trigger (cross-session) may have fired** — see `playtest-innovation` pass for threshold check. Candidate themes: (a) PC-sheet-hidden content drives agency (S01 + S02); (b) craft-ritual PCs read atmosphere non-standard (Grom-forward, S01 + S02); (c) atmospheric beats miss on single-gate rolls (S01 + S02, 5 instances).
