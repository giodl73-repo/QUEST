## Critique

This was the most character-driven session the workshop has produced. I do not say that lightly. Four PCs, four distinct contributions to the Scene 6 climax, each traceable to their specific character sheet — not generic heroism.

### What worked

- **Aelric's decision to attune.** Grom counter-argued. Aelric listened, did not answer for nine seconds, and then spoke in his Solamnic formal register: *"I accept the trade. The child walks free. I attune."* This is the Oath working through Aelric in an ugly form — the Measure does not forbid sacrifice; it specifically asks for it. The player (DM-as-Aelric) understood this. The line lands because Aelric's character sheet has been building toward it since his brother's name was first written.
- **Grom's counter-argument** before Aelric's decision. *"If you take this, Varran is gone. Not just the body. The living-figure of him. You will only be able to remember him dying."* Grom's entire character sheet is "mend first, keep the party whole." This was the sheet arguing. The DM heard the sheet.
- **Pella walking silent to Aelric after the fight, putting a hand on his pauldron, saying: "You came."** This is the session's memory-worthy image. The module committed to Pella's interior as certainty (rev 2 addition), and the scene played that interior into prose. Nine-year-old, silent, small hand, and a two-word line that will outlive the session. I will quote it at a table next year.
- **Grom's closing line** at the way-house bench: *"You will have given up what you needed to give up. That is not the same as nothing."* This is character work at the exhale after the climax. Grom — who argued against attunement — nevertheless consoles Aelric for having attuned. That is how a forge-priest who has lost a forge-master counsels a knight who has lost a brother. The sheet did the work.
- **Thera giving Pella the letter "to P, when you are ten"** at the mid-scene pause, saying *"When you're ten."* Pella not reaching for it. That is two character sheets agreeing on a respectful silence. I commend it.

### What needs work

- **Aelric's first three scenes were voice-thin.** He only spoke when strictly called on. The DM-as-Aelric should have had at least one voice-tagged moment in Scenes 1-3 — a Measure-reference at Mensa's body, a Solamnic phrase at the Howl-Stones pre-fight, something. His Solamnic formal register is striking when it appears; it appeared only in Scenes 5-9.
- **Thera's voice-tags faded.** Her S01 hill-vernacular ("aye, well," "right then") was almost absent in S02. Given she grew up in a Solamnic way-house, she might register Sir Venric's pyre differently than the others — a local's grief, not a stranger's. The module did not prompt this and the DM did not play it.
- **Kessa's private arc (Vethrenn) did not surface.** The Mira-letter gives her a Vethrenn-adjacent lead, and she did not remark on it. Scene 6's wall-ledger — which was her best chance to connect S01's Vesk-phrase to the bone-collector's archive — was missed on dice. That's bad luck; but Kessa also did not FLAG her interest in the ledger to the party, and she had been carrying the Vethrenn-arc quietly since S01. This feels like the private-arc waiting for S03 to activate.
- **The grief-at-table note from S01 was half-answered.** S01's Roleplayer lens asked: "will a PC speak a lost loved one's name aloud to another PC?" S02's answer: **Aelric does, by implication — he attunes to Varran's minute, and Varran's name is the session's through-line.** Pella, too — she says *"Papa is not coming back"* to Thera, naming her lost father. This is the axis I asked about landing. **Not yet at table-level (Grom does not speak Duren aloud; Thera does not speak Adda), but improving.**

### The question I'd push on

**Is Aelric's attunement an irrevocable character-definition moment, or a reversible narrative beat?** The module says the memories are permanent — *greater restoration* cannot restore them. If future sessions need Aelric's pre-death-Varran memories (a flashback for a future arc), they cannot be accessed. Has the workshop committed to this? If yes, S03+ must respect it: the DM must not re-narrate Varran-as-living in any form. If no, the attunement's weight is undercut. I want this documented in the campaign continuity — **"Aelric remembers Varran only as dying"** — as a permanent campaign fact.
