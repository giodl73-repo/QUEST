## Critique

Dice were honest. Every roll traced. Bandit A's nat-20 crit on Kessa in Round 1 of the Howl-Stones dropped a wizard to 1 HP before the party could even take a turn, and the log traced every die. That is the dice-discipline I asked for in S01's review and you have held it in S02. Good.

### What worked

- **The crit consequence played forward.** 15 damage on Kessa from the crossbow crit forced Grom's bonus-action Healing Word on his turn — a resource he had to spend instead of saving for mid-fight. That is a tactical consequence of an honest die: one crit, one spell slot, cascading. No fudging.
- **Kessa's finisher on the fleeing Lieutenant** was a clean tempo play. Magic Missile auto-hits 3 darts for 1d4+1 each; at 1 HP the Lt dies to the first dart. Kessa committed a slot to prevent a Lieutenant escape that would have triggered stronghold Alert 45 minutes later. That is the kind of strategic spend a Tactician scores high on.
- **Grom's Spiritual Weapon bonus-action intercept** during Aelric's attunement silence is the session's single best tactical beat. L2 slot; spectral warhammer; hit on 21 vs Idra's AC 12; killed her on the intercept before she reached Pella. Grom's character sheet explicitly lists Spiritual Weapon; his heuristic is "keep the party whole." The spend matches the moment exactly.
- **Aelric's Divine Smite on Keloth at Round 2** resolved a deadly fight (climax was 1,150 adjusted XP at Routine) in two rounds after the attunement lifted. 17 damage in one hit (9 bludg + 8 radiant) via L1 slot. Tempo-correct.

### What needs work

- **Thera's Stealth is under-delivering.** Three Stealth rolls this session: Howl-Stones scout (9 vs DC 13, fail), patrol approach (12 vs DC 14, fail), Vault Approach (9 vs DC 15 kill-before-shout, fail). Her expertise modifier is +7 but she keeps rolling low single-digits on the d20. The dice are cold; not a design issue; but a DM should note that a rogue's Stealth is the party's most load-bearing stat and three fails in a single session is a pattern.
- **The crit on Round 1 Turn 1 was almost a TPK starter.** At 4×L3 with Kessa's 16 HP, a 15-damage opener on her is a 93% dent. Five more HP and she's down. The module's ambush design is fair, but a less-defended party (wizard without Mage Armor up) would have taken a dead PC to the opening turn. Consider a note in the trap-ambush file: "warn the player to cast Mage Armor before approach."
- **Portent went entirely unused.** Kessa's two pre-rolled 3s had no application — she can't force an enemy to fail on a 3 when a 3 alone doesn't miss a tier-1 attack (Bandit needs 12 to hit AC 12 with +3 attack; a 3 doesn't suffice; but enemies attacked AC 18 Aelric where a 3 would auto-miss... except the Lieutenant only rolled once and fumbled naturally). Kessa had no attack-rolls of her own to substitute either. **Two wasted Portents is not a character-sheet failure; it is a bad draw.** The Tactician notes this: if we roll 1-5 twice in future sessions, Portent will continue under-delivering. Not fixable; documentable.

### The question I'd push on

**Is the Option-A-plus-combat branch in Scene 6 a fair mechanical read?** Keloth accepts Aelric's attunement; Idra attacks; Keloth declares the trade completed but his cell's self-defense restored. Combat ensues at the rev-2-raised Routine baseline (1,150 XP). A less-resourced party would have TPK'd here — Aelric is out of action for 60 seconds post-attunement. The module presumed Option A = no combat. Rev 2 raised Routine to deadly. Combined, the "Option A + Idra-triggered combat" creates a no-win trap for a party that accepted the trade in good faith. **I want this branch explicitly in the module so a DM can adjudicate fairly without needing to invent.**
