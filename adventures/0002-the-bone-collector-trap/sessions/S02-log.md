# S02 LOG — The Bone-Collector Trap × The Varduin Muster

## Session Summary

The muster rode north with Brother Laen's safe-conduct and a stipend to deliver Pella's drawing. They found Mensa dead at the waystation and the trap-signal on the table. Grom read the body properly; Kessa found the bread Mensa had been kneading for her own brother, three weeks after Vesk's death.

Tracking went poorly — Grom lost the trail and the party lost half a day — but they reached the Howl-Stones by evening. Thera scouted but was seen; the ambush opened with a Bone-Collector's lucky crit that dropped Kessa to 1 HP before Grom's Healing Word reached her. The party rallied: the Lieutenant tried to flee east to warn Keloth and Kessa's Magic Missile finished her from 80 yards. Bandit B surrendered at 2 HP and talked.

At Gavek's Rest, Grom's hearth-thumb caught the dwarf-work under the burn-scars. Thera **did not find the well** under the straw pile — fumbled the Investigation — so the party took the frontal stair down. The Vault Approach guard shouted *"KELOTH! OST!"* before Thera could silence him; Keloth moved Pella to the Vault. The Cell-Block's interior was read carefully by Grom: a half-wiped chalk figure of a woman, tally-marks counting sixty-three weeks, and an old scrubbed blood-stain in Pella's cell that was not Pella's. Three prior prisoners. The cell-block was a record.

At the Vault, the iron door fell to Thera's picks. Keloth was behind the altar, hands folded, exactly as designed. Pella was bound to the iron box; she looked at Aelric and asked her question. Keloth made the offer. Grom argued against it — the trade is not mending, it is bookkeeping. Aelric listened to Grom and took the trade anyway.

But Keloth did not survive the trade. Aelric attuned to the Varran-reliquary at the altar, and in the sixty seconds of silence that followed, Idra — Keloth's lieutenant, reading the moment — drew her scimitar and moved on Pella. Grom's Spiritual Weapon intercepted. Combat opened with the attunement still in Aelric's head. When Aelric returned to his body, the room was in fight. He finished Idra with Divine Smite. Keloth did not surrender at half HP; he fought to death, claiming the trade had been broken by Idra's move. The party believed him. They killed him cleanly.

The shelf-reliquaries were recovered. The Mira-letter was recovered. The wall-ledger's uncredited *"I will not look at the rose again"* entry went unread by the party (Kessa's Investigation failed; her Perception on the ledger-brighten at reliquary-lift also failed). **The cross-adventure signal was in the room but did not fire this session.** Stuart's echo waits for S03.

On the ride home, the muster found Varduin's east wing burned and Sir Venric of the Sword on a pyre. Aelric attended without being asked. Pella, when asked, chose to stay with Brother Laen's elderly mother rather than travel with the party. Thera accepted this. The adventure closed on Grom sitting beside Aelric at Varran's absent-pyre spot in the way-house common room, not speaking.

## Questions Raised About the Module or the System

1. **Investigation-gated atmospheric beats can miss.** The cross-adventure signal was the module's most ambitious design element and failed to fire because Kessa rolled 7 + 11 on two consecutive crucial Investigations. **Innovation flag: should the highest-leverage manifest symptoms have a passive-Perception fallback (DC 15 passive) in addition to an active Investigation?**

2. **Keloth's tactical decision to fight after the trade** — he declares the trade complete (Aelric attuned) and considers his cell's self-defense restored. Idra moved; Grom's Spiritual Weapon killed her; Keloth considers the cell's integrity the new axis. This produced a fight the module's Routine-baseline handled cleanly. But a less prepared party would have struggled — Keloth at 33 HP + Shield-of-Faith + Inflict Wounds while Aelric is 60 seconds into attunement is a *tempo trap*.

3. **The Stealth shortcut was discoverable only by Investigation 13.** Thera fumbled. A party that depended on the shortcut would have been in trouble. The module's warning ("a party that doesn't investigate, doesn't find it") landed accurately — but this adventure was tight enough at Routine that the shortcut-miss did not matter. With more PC fatigue, it could have.

4. **Per-PC reception reality vs. design prediction.** The module's rubric-v1.1 per-PC table predicted Thera would receive the bread-scrap (mother-grief). Actually: Kessa found it and Grom received it. The prediction was half-wrong. **Innovation flag: per-PC reception predictions are lossy because they don't account for dice (who found it first) OR for chain-reception (who gets hit by another PC's finding).**

5. **Aelric's Minute was played solo.** The DM (Claude) played all four PCs per Option A. When Aelric attuned, there was no "player" to describe the Minute — the DM described it from the module's pre-written text. **This is a limitation of Option A we should note.** Option C (agent-per-PC for meaningful decisions) would have let the Aelric-agent describe the Minute in their own voice. **Worth re-opening.**
