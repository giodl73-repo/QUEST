## Dimensions Below Threshold (7/10 cutoff)

- **Module fidelity (7)** and **Table readiness (7)** are at floor. Raising either would require:
  - **Module fidelity:** Investigation-gated atmospheric beats need a passive-Perception fallback. The cross-adventure signal on the wall-ledger was a 7-on-Kessa's-roll-away from firing; a passive-Perception 15 fallback would have caught it.
  - **Table readiness:** add a "Trade-completed-but-cell-hostile" decision branch to `encounters/vault-climax.md`. The module presumed Option A was clean-no-combat; play showed otherwise.

## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Investigation-gated atmospheric beats miss on a single fail
Dimension affected: Module fidelity + Atmospheric landing
What happened: The wall-ledger's uncredited "I will not look at the rose again" entry is the workshop's first explicit cross-adventure signal (per Stuart's S01/S02 suggestion, ratified as rev 2 addition). It was gated behind Kessa Investigation DC 13 + Kessa Perception DC 13 (the ledger-brighten). She rolled 12 then 11. Both failed by 1. The signal was in the room and did not fire.
Why the rubric missed it: Module fidelity anchors assume "≥75% symptoms landed = 9+" at the session level. It does not address whether the highest-leverage symptoms have single-fail-points.
Proposed: for "anchor-level" atmospheric symptoms (cross-adventure signals; named-NPC reveals; reaction moments), mandate a passive-Perception fallback at DC = active-Investigation + 2. A party with passive-Perception 15 would auto-land what Kessa rolled 12 on.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Trade-completed-but-cell-still-hostile is a legitimate DM branch not in module
Dimension affected: Table readiness
What happened: Aelric attuned the Varran-reliquary mid-combat (standard Option A completion). Per module, Keloth should have "hands key, releases Pella, walks out rear tunnel." Instead, Keloth's lieutenant Idra moved on Pella during the attunement silence; Keloth declared the trade complete but his cell's self-defense restored; combat continued. DM adjudicated fairly, but the module didn't anticipate this branch.
Why the rubric missed it: Table readiness anchors assume module coverage of predictable decision-branches. This decision-branch is *plausible under Option A* and wasn't covered.
Proposed: add to vault-climax.md — "If Option A accepted but party or NPC triggers combat mid-attunement, Keloth considers his cell's integrity restored and fights to death. Idra's move-on-Pella is a plausible trigger if she acts before Keloth can stand down."
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Per-PC reception predictions are lossy
Dimension affected: Atmospheric landing
What happened: Module predicted Thera would receive the Mensa-bread-scrap beat (mother-grief). Actually, Kessa found it (she rolled 22 Investigation) and Grom received it (the weight landed on him — he stood silent). The predicted PC and the actual receiving PC diverged.
Why the rubric missed it: Per-PC reception anchors (rubric v1.1) predict reception per-PC. They don't account for dice (who finds the object first) or chain-reception (the finder vs. the receiver may differ).
Proposed: separate "finder" from "receiver" in per-PC tracking. Document who rolled the detection AND who the beat emotionally landed on.
```
