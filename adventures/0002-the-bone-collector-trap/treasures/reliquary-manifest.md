# Curse Symptoms Manifest — The Bone-Collector's Reliquary

Required by the cross-skill contract: `dungeon-smith` must honor ≥ 3 symptoms across ≥ 2 rooms.

## Required (≥ 3, distributed across ≥ 2 rooms)

- [x] **Visual symptom (silver-filigree bone-trophies).** The cell identifies itself by wearing small moon-silver-bound bones (finger-bones, etc.). Placed: Howl-Stones lieutenant (Scene 02), Vault Approach guard-post (Scene 05), Cell-Block mattress cache (Scene 07), Vault shelf (Scene 06).
- [x] **Textual symptom (attribution drift — erasure of names).** Keloth's Hand carved over Gavek's Rest on the stronghold lintel (Scene 03). Vesk's name crossed out on the cell's duty-roster (Scene 05). Keloth's own father's name unpicked from his robe-hem embroidery (Scene 06). The pattern of erasure is recursive — the cell erases its own members as it erases others.
- [x] **Auditory symptom (names unvoiced).** The Memory Echo-equivalent for this artifact is the Minute itself — sixty silent seconds for the witnesses, sixty first-person seconds for the attuner. The silence while the Minute plays IS the symptom. Whispered-into-wind carvings at the Howl-Stones (Scene 02 Memory Fragment) echo this: names spoken into wind, never said aloud.
- [x] **Sensory symptom (dryness).** The stronghold's lower level is **dry**. In a winter-wilderness setting with late snow, this is notable. Same strange dryness as Room 02 of #0001 (aside from the weeping wall). The pattern is emerging as a cross-adventure signal: **moon-silver work produces ambient dryness in its vicinity.** Not yet an innovation-logged pattern (that's for `playtest-innovation` post-S02) but flagged.
- [x] **Reaction moment (five reliquaries dim).** When the Varran-reliquary is lifted from the altar (Scene 06), the five other occupied reliquaries on the shelf **dim by one perceptible degree.** The Varran-reliquary was the anchor; removing it breaks a low ward. Perception DC 12 to catch. Rubric v1.1: per-PC reception tracking applies.
- [x] **Reaction moment (wall-ledger brightens) — rev 2 per Schick/Stuart.** At the same instant the Varran-reliquary is lifted, the **uncredited ledger entry** on the north wall (*"I will not look at the rose again"* — a phrase whose original speaker lives in one of the shelf-reliquaries) briefly glows warm-orange, then fades back to charcoal. Kessa Perception DC 13 specifically watching the ledger catches it. No other entry brightens. Whoever wrote the line was bound to the ward; the reliquary-lift disturbed their rest. **Cross-session signal:** the phrase is verbatim from Vesk's Memory Fragment in #0001 Scene 5. This is the workshop's first explicit cross-adventure textual echo (per Stuart's S01/S02 suggestion). A party that played S01 may catch it.
