# The Bone-Collector's Reliquary

*A human skull bound in moon-silver filigree, the eye-sockets stopped with small silver discs.*

## Appearance

A human skull, moderately weathered, of a man in his late thirties. The mandible is detached but held in place by fine silver wire. The whole skull is wrapped in a filigree of **moon-silver** — the same alloy as the Silver Rose of Vaenor, likely from the same vein. Two small silver discs, each smaller than a coin, are set into the eye-sockets. The filigree is worked in thread-fine Nerakan geometric patterns, beautiful if you do not pause to consider whose face the work has replaced. Weighs about three pounds. Cool to the touch, but not cold.

## Presence / Desire

The reliquary **wants to be held, listened to, and exchanged.** It is drawn, more than any other object the workshop has made, to the closest living relative of its current occupant. In the presence of Varran's brother Aelric, it will warm by a perceptible degree — the first heat anyone in the party has felt from moon-silver. It chimes — once, softly, inaudibly to anyone but the kin — the first time that kin is within five feet of it. The chime is not musical. It is the sound of a small silver disc being set into the bone of an eye-socket.

It has no voice of its own. It has the voice of its current occupant, reserved for the one moment that justifies the work.

## Provenance

### The reliquary itself (the object)

Forged by Keloth's master **Tarch** in approximately PC 40, one of perhaps a dozen Tarch made in his lifetime. Moon-silver from the Vaenshold vein (same source as Aelwen's Rose); Tarch traded six Nerakan bone-carvings for an ingot of the silver in PC 42, a transaction that the Vaenshold silversmith-line would later record as *"an obligation not fully understood."* Tarch died in PC 9; Keloth inherited his moon-silver tools.

### The current occupant (Varran)

- **Varran of Crownhold** — Knight of the Sword, Aelric's older brother, died **PC 22** at a Neraka-border skirmish, ambushed by a Bone-Collector cell scouting for raw material. Varran was alone when he fell; his body was never recovered by the Order.
- His death was witnessed by **Keloth**, then forty-two, newly inherited from Tarch, and by the scout who named Varran at the moment of death (required for the reliquary to hold the minute). The scout's name is lost to the cell's records. Keloth held the reliquary himself at the moment of attunement.
- The **Varran-minute** contains: dusk, cold, the specific weight of Solamnic plate in snow, a sergeant named Hern falling beside him, a sentence he thought but did not say — *"Paladine, carry my wife"* — though Varran never married. The woman he had meant was a Crownhold-local named **Doriel**, whom he had intended to ask on his return. She died three years later without knowing she had been on Varran's mind at the end.

### Prior occupants of this specific reliquary

Two, before Varran:
1. **Fern of the Sixth House** (Nerakan family, minor), died PC 41, age 22, last minute held her own mother's singing. Overwritten by Varran.
2. **Old Derrin**, a blacksmith of no particular note, died PC 31, age 70, last minute was a dry breath and a man thinking about the cold. Overwritten.

This reliquary has erased two last-minutes to arrive at Varran's. The silver remembers all three only as muscle-memory; none are recoverable.

## Properties (Mechanical)

### Rarity

**Rare** (appears uncommon to casual detection; Keloth knows the true rarity; Kessa can guess with Arcana DC 15).

### Attunement

Required. Attunement consumes the attuner's memory as below. **Prerequisite:** the attuner must be a blood relative or named-loved-one of the current occupant. (Non-kin attempting attunement: the reliquary grows cold in their hand and will not take; the attuner loses an attunement slot for 24 hours from the attempt, but nothing else.)

### Benefits (while attuned)

- **The Minute.** Once at the moment of attunement (and only once), the attuner experiences the occupant's final minute in first-person: sight, sound, body-sensation, thought. Sixty seconds of in-fiction time for the attuner; the rest of the party sees the attuner go still for exactly sixty seconds. The experience is perfect and non-negotiable — it contains what it contains.
- **Name of the Dying.** Permanent passive: while attuned, the attuner automatically knows the name of any creature that has died within 60 feet of them within the past minute.
- **Grief-Echo.** Once per long rest, the attuner may ask the reliquary for a factual detail about the occupant's death (who was present, where it happened, what weapon, etc.). The reliquary answers in the occupant's voice.

### Curse (while attuned)

**Memory Concentration.**

At the instant of attunement, **every memory the attuner has of the occupant, other than the Minute itself, is consumed.** Permanent. Not restorable by *greater restoration*. Not stored anywhere retrievable. The memories are the cost.

The attuner retains:
- The fact that the occupant existed.
- The name of the occupant.
- The Minute.

The attuner loses:
- Every specific memory of the occupant prior to their death. Every shared meal, every argument, every quiet moment. All of it.
- Any emotional associations the attuner had built up. The Minute's emotional weight replaces them.
- The attuner's sense of the person as a living figure. Henceforth, the occupant exists in the attuner's memory **only as dying.**

There is no mechanical penalty (no HP, no spell-slot drain). The cost is entirely narrative. The player will feel it or will not. The DM should not pretend it is a small thing.

### Ending the Curse

- **Lesser effects** (*remove curse*, *greater restoration*) **DO NOT** restore lost memories. The reliquary is not a curse in the spellcasting sense; it is a permanent memory-transaction.
- **The Reorx-shattering rite.** A forge-priest of Reorx can destroy the reliquary at dawn on a forge-fire of at least 1,800°C (dwarf-rated; Grom can reach this with forge-tools from Varduin or any Solamnic-adjacent smith). The rite consumes the moon-silver filigree; the skull is returned to bone-ash; the Minute is lost permanently; the attunement (if active) ends, but the memories are still gone (the shattering destroys the relic, not the transaction).
- **Re-occupation.** The reliquary can be overwritten. If anyone dies within 60 feet of the reliquary while their name is spoken aloud, their last minute replaces the current occupant's. Keloth has used this mechanism deliberately for decades. The party can, in theory, do the same — though the moral weight of choosing to harvest a stranger's death for this purpose is considerable.

## The Other Shelf-Reliquaries and the Empty One *(rev 2: per Schick)*

**The five occupied shelf-reliquaries** hold:

1. **Haena of the Fourth House** (Nerakan midwife, died PC 29, age 31). Hand-scratched house-rune at the temple. Her extended family lives in **Verrat, east of Neraka**. Her last minute is a woman at a difficult birthing who passed into death still holding the infant's shoulder; the infant survived. Returning Haena's reliquary to Verrat is a future adventure arc.
2. **Sergeant Hern of the Solamnic Sword** — fell beside Varran in PC 22. His last minute is brief: a sword-cut across the thigh, the certainty he would not stand again, a thought of his wife Andra and their son. His body was also not recovered; his family (Andra still lives at Tarbir Waystation in southern Abanasinia) presumes him dead but does not know how.
3-5. **Three anonymous Nerakan dead**, two men and a woman, spanning PC 15-PC 12. Their families are not documented in Keloth's records; returning them requires outside investigation. Keloth knows their names but will not share.

**The empty shelf-reliquary** is visibly expectant: **polished to mirror-brightness**, eye-sockets open and waiting, **a single silver thread laid across the mandible** as if the filigree work is still mid-forge. It is prepared for a specific future occupant Keloth has not yet named. The party can take it; it functions as a *future-use* tool (any named-dying within 60 ft, name spoken aloud, fills it). Kessa would recognize the preparation as Tarch-school work completed to the final step before naming.

## Hooks

- **Aelric's choice.** Primary; see Scene 06 GM Notes.
- **The five other reliquaries on the shelf.** Each holds a different death-minute. One holds a **Solamnic knight** named Hern (the same sergeant who fell beside Varran). Aelric has no blood-kinship with Hern; he could not attune Hern's reliquary even if he wanted to. But **recovering Hern's reliquary** and delivering it to Hern's family (who still live at a Solamnic waystation in southern Abanasinia) is a future adventure. Four of the five are Nerakan dead whose families are in Nerakan territories; returning them would mean crossing borders.
- **The empty reliquary.** One shell on the shelf is un-occupied. The party can take it. If anyone in the party dies in the future within 60 ft of it while their name is spoken, the reliquary will hold their final minute. A morbid tool the party may or may not carry.
- **Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith.** Keloth's letter demands her compliance in supplying moon-silver. If the party has taken the letter (combat-loot from Keloth), they know Mira is in Crystalmir-by-the-Fells and under threat. Seeds #0003.
- **Doriel.** Varran's almost-intended. The party may or may not learn her name (it's in the Minute). If they do, and they visit Crownhold, they may meet her grave — Doriel died in PC 19, three years after Varran, of a winter-illness. Her family would appreciate knowing a Knight of the Sword had thought of her. Future adventure.

## GM Notes

- **Let the party discuss the choice.** Aelric is the only one who can attune the Varran-minute. The rest of the party will weigh in. They do not get to decide for him.
- **If Aelric attunes at the altar in Scene 06:** play the sixty seconds in real time. Set a timer. The other three PCs are present and watching; they do not move. Ask the player, afterwards, to describe what Varran said, did, thought, felt. Preserve the description as a session-log appendix. The description is permanent record of the Minute; the player's other memories of Varran — as a DM-fiction construct — should be de-referenced by subsequent sessions. The DM should not re-narrate Varran doing other things.
- **The forge-shattering.** If the party chooses to destroy the reliquary (via Grom), this is a single-scene ritual, probably performed at Varduin's smithy on the return journey. Grom rolls **Religion DC 15** (his modifier +3 + Bless if anyone casts it); on success, the reliquary shatters and the Minute is lost. On fail, Grom tries again next dawn; no further penalty. **A dwarf forge-priest destroying a Bone-Collector reliquary is an act of theological significance** — make it weighty in the narration. Grom's character arc lands here if this is the chosen path.
- **What Aelric loses.** Be prepared, as DM, with a list of Varran-memories that will be consumed. Pre-generate two or three: the blue-leather saddle-lashing scene from Aelric's PC sheet; a shared training-ring fight; the news of Varran's death reaching Crownhold. These are the memories Aelric will no longer have. Make sure the player understands what is being traded. Do not let them into the Minute without them understanding.

## Rubric v1.1 compliance (atmospheric landing)

- **Per-PC reception:** this artifact is specifically per-Aelric. The other three PCs watch and cannot participate. Document.
- **Inter-PC chain:** Aelric's decision (attune / refuse / destroy) chains to Grom (performs the shatter or not) AND to Thera (guards Pella during the minute) AND to Kessa (records the minute in her notebook). The chain is structural to the artifact's design.
