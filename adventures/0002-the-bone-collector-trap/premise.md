# Premise — The Bone-Collector Trap

**Logline:** Compassion walks north with a child's drawing and finds the drawing already on the table.

## Setting

**Place:** Three locations across the Nerakan border.
1. **Mensa's Waystation** — small roadside inn, ~60 mi north-northeast of Varduin. Run by Vesk's sister **Mensa**. Pella has been living here.
2. **The Howl-Stones** — a pass through standing stones, ~15 mi further north. A Bone-Collector trap site.
3. **Gavek's Rest / "Keloth's Hand"** — ruined pre-Cataclysm Istari wayhouse, now the Bone-Collector cell's stronghold. ~10 mi east of the Howl-Stones.

**Time:** Late Age of Might, **PC 20** (continuous from S01; ten days after S01 closes). The Cataclysm is 20 years away.

## Hook

Thera Hillfoot has been carrying Pella's crayon drawing for ten days. She has said she will deliver it. At the way-house common room on the morning this adventure opens, she says she is leaving tomorrow — with or without the rest of the party. Brother Laen, quietly alarmed, offers the party an official Solamnic safe-conduct and 20 gp of travel-money if they go as a Varduin party rather than Thera alone. He has reasons of his own: the Council has received worrying reports from the Nerakan border about Bone-Collector activity since the Rose was vaulted.

## Central Artifact

**The Bone-Collector's Reliquary** — a human skull bound in moon-silver filigree (same alloy as the Silver Rose, same vein). Attunement plays the final minute of the skull's current occupant in their own voice. Cost: *all other memories of that person*, permanently consumed. Current occupant: **Varran of Crownhold**, Aelric's older brother (died PC 22 to this same cell).

Full artifact file: `treasures/reliquary.md`. Manifest: `treasures/reliquary-manifest.md`.

## Themes

- Compassion walks into traps (the drawing as bait).
- Grief-economies inverted (the Rose erases; the Reliquary gives-one-and-takes-four).
- A brother's last minute vs. a lifetime of small memories.
- The crafts of grief — silver, skull, forge — as tools for a moral decision.

## The Real Decision

Two overlapping choices:

1. **Attune the reliquary?** Aelric carries Varran's name. The reliquary holds Varran's last minute. Attuning gives him the minute and erases every other memory of Varran. Nobody else in the party has a named-dead the reliquary currently holds.
2. **Destroy the reliquary?** Grom (forge-priest of Reorx) can perform a shattering-rite at dawn that ends the artifact. The preserved minute is lost; nobody gets anything. The cell's tools of grief-harvesting are down to one fewer.

The party can also take the reliquary unattuned (as they did the Rose) and decide later. That's allowed. The curse does not compel.
