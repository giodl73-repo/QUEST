## Critique

The module runs cold. That is my first and most important observation, as it was last time, and it remains the first and most important observation about any module: could a DM sit down at a table with only this artifact, with four level-3 character sheets, and a d20, and run the session? Yes. This module can.

I note, specifically:

- The premise file is tight — well under the hundred-line working ceiling. It references the other files without duplicating their content. It states the stakes and the decision cleanly.
- The lint-report is useful not only as an artifact of self-review but as a *working transcript of the two fixes that had to be made*. Scene 06 and Scene 07 both had read-alouds over the sixty-word cap; both were trimmed. The trimmed versions are cleaner than the originals. I read the new Scene 06 read-aloud aloud: *"Low-ceilinged, stone-cold room. Along the east wall: a shelf of silver-bound skulls, spaced like a quiet procession. At the center, a low altar holds an iron box. Behind the altar stands a tall, soft-robed man, hands folded. On the altar lies a nine-year-old girl, bound at the wrists. She sees you. Her chin lifts. Pella."* Fifty-seven words. Cleanly delivered. A DM running this will not paraphrase. That is what the sixty-word cap was for.
- The DM cheatsheet has been upgraded since #0001. It now contains:
  - Key DCs organized by scene (every DC in the module appears here; cross-reference is clean)
  - Key saves (including Keloth's spell DC 11)
  - **Attunement rules inlined**, specifically noting that the Varran-reliquary's attunement is *instant, not short-rest-gated* — a rule the body of the module introduces that a DM would otherwise miss. Good catch.
  - Conditions used, inlined. Paralyzed, Frightened, Charmed, Command-Halt, Hold Person. No page-flipping.
  - XP budget.
  - **Scene Triggers — nineteen specific if-then pairs.** This is the module's most load-bearing section for an under-pressure DM. I commend it specifically.
  - **Downstream Consequence Tracking — eleven post-session states for #0003 continuity.** No other module I have edited had this discipline. Your workshop is learning.
  - **Per-PC Reception Tracking table — fifteen rows.** Rubric v1.1 compliance made legible. A DM can tick boxes during play.
  - **Inter-PC Chain Tracking.** Five specific chains to watch for.

The cheatsheet is now the most valuable single page in the module for a DM running cold. At some point you will want to split it out as a printable one-page card. I would recommend doing so before publication, not after.

Where I would push harder:

**The Scene 06 Decision section has four options (A–D) written in narrative prose.** This is clear on first read. Under table-pressure at the altar, with a player weighing the trade, the DM will want a one-line summary for each — a *decision table* — that they can reference while talking to the player. Add to the cheatsheet:

> **Scene 06 Keloth-trade decisions:**
> - **A Accept:** attune; Pella free; Keloth departs; no combat.
> - **B Refuse + fight:** initiative; Keloth Shield of Faith R1; Command R2; Inflict R3+; surrenders at HP ≤ 16.
> - **C Negotiate:** Persuasion DC 20 or grief-exchange offer.
> - **D Attack, then accept mid-combat:** Keloth re-offers at HP ≤ 16.

Four lines. Replaces the narrative prose for in-scene reference (not in the main body).

**Pella's stat block is inlined in the NPC section, which is correct, but I would inline it a second time in Scene 06.** A child's AC and HP is not a thing a DM expects to need, until a player attempts something that targets her. Having the stat block where the action is, even as a two-line reminder, prevents a pause.

**The premise.md still references some files that will not exist until S02 plays.** (`sessions/` is listed but not yet populated.) This is not a defect; the file honestly states what will exist where, and a DM preparing the session reads it correctly. But when S02 plays, update premise.md with a post-play note. Do not let the file remain in its *intent-to-be* state once it has been.

**The attribution drift pattern is scattered across six places** (Howl-Stones carvings, lintel, duty-roster, cell-mattress, Keloth's robe-hem, the wall-ledger Schick wants added). This is deliberate and good. I would add one line to the DM cheatsheet that *lists the six attribution-drift beats in sequence* so a DM can track which have landed and which have not. The pattern is strong; its execution depends on the DM noticing when a beat has fired.

## Specific suggestions

1. **Add the Keloth-trade decision table to the cheatsheet.** Four lines, replaces narrative in-scene.
2. **Inline Pella's 2-line stat block in Scene 06** as a reminder.
3. **Add an attribution-drift tracker to the cheatsheet.** Six rows, one per beat, with scene reference.
4. **Post-S02, update premise.md** to reflect what happened rather than what the adventure anticipated. Do not leave intent-documents in the state of their original intent after the session closes.

## Final note

This is a module a DM can run cold with confidence. Your rev-3 on #0001 taught you what a cheatsheet should be; your rev-1 on #0002 applies the lesson. That is exactly the kind of evolution this workshop's rubric system was built to enable. Keep going.
