## Critique

The stronghold is good. Not great yet — small — but good. The design does what it can with five rooms. That is worth saying first.

Let me list what I like. The loop at [5]→[6]→[7]→[5] is a *genuine* loop — two doors and a corridor, properly bidirectional once the iron door between the Vault and the Cell-Block is opened. The verticality is actually two verticalities: the stair between levels, and the well-shaft connecting the yard directly to the cell-block, which is the kind of layered vertical I advocate for in my old modules. The well is an earned shortcut — the party has to investigate the straw pile in the Stable Yard to find it. That is a Jaquaysed move. Parties that play lazily will take the stair; parties that play thoroughly will take the well; both are valid and produce different sessions.

**The Bone-Collectors' own refusal to use the well** ("Idra says the well is haunted; we don't open it") is a lovely diegetic detail. You have not only designed a shortcut, you have designed a *reason* the shortcut is available to the party and not to the inhabitants. A less careful designer would have left the well open and wondered why the Bone-Collectors hadn't secured it. You have answered the question in the fiction.

**The alarm bell as tempo control** is the single best tactical design in this module. The party has a real choice (ring/don't-ring) with a real mechanical consequence (harder climax / easier climax). That is what *Tactical* means in a dungeon — choices the party makes that shape what they face later. I commend this.

**Inhabitants that move.** Last time I complained that the Rose Cairn's attendants were static — rose when triggered, fell when done. In this dungeon, the patrol in the Stable Yard moves on a timer. Pella (off-screen) causes a distraction as a Wandering table outcome. Keloth surrenders at half HP and shifts the scene-type. The Alert state is an actual state that changes creature placements and counts. This is a living dungeon at tier 1 scale. Well done.

Where I would push harder:

**Only three stronghold-encounter locations (yard patrol, guard-post, vault-climax).** At five keyed stronghold rooms, you have one encounter per 1.67 rooms, which is reasonable. But two of those encounters — the patrol and the guard-post — are single Bandits at Routine; trivial mechanical weight. The party can silent-kill both and reach Keloth with full resources. I would add at least one minor encounter opportunity in Scene 06 *before* Keloth's offer — perhaps Idra is in the corridor between Cell-Block and Vault at Routine, and must be dealt with before the party reaches the altar. That gives the party one earlier commitment before the moral-climax scene opens.

**The mattress-cache in Scene 07** is good — a silver-filigree finger-bone that evidences a prior prisoner. But the cell-block is only three empty cells. At a stronghold that has been operating for nine years, there should be *marks* of multiple prior prisoners — tally-marks scratched in the wall, a second-cell chalk-drawing the party can't identify, a stain on the third-cell floor. This is not a call for more words; it is a call for more *layers* in the environment. A thorough party should be able to peel back the history of the stronghold through its small objects.

**The well-shaft has no lateral connection.** It drops straight down. I would have added a partial ledge halfway — a disused second-level landing, blocked with rubble, that a party could discover (via climbing) and explore later. A place the Bone-Collectors have walled off. This would suggest that Gavek's Rest had more rooms in its Istari prime than the party now sees — Bone-Collectors have sealed off three-quarters of the original structure. A future adventure's sequel-hook hiding in a wall.

**The trap-site at Howl-Stones** is a properly vertical encounter — three attackers on upper ledges, crossfire, the party forced to engage with the Z-axis. I commend this specifically. Tier 1 modules rarely feature working verticality in combat; yours does.

## Specific suggestions

1. **Add a Scene 06 pre-offer encounter.** Idra (or her equivalent) in the corridor before the altar, at Routine difficulty. The party should have one combat commitment before the offer lands.
2. **Add an interior layer to Cell-Block.** Tally-marks, stains, a second-cell chalk-drawing. The prior-prisoner history should be readable.
3. **Partial ledge in the well-shaft.** A sealed former landing, visible to a climber. Hook for later.
4. **Make the well-shaft a genuinely risky climb** at the Alert state — one Bandit stationed at the well-cover in Alert, because a smart cell-leader *would* post one there if the bell rang. Right now the Alert state adds creatures to other scenes but not to the well-entrance.

## Final note

The stronghold is tight, Jaquaysed at its scale, and better-inhabited than #0001's. At twice the size this would be a module worth studying. At its current size, it is good tier 1 work with a thoughtful shortcut. Keep scaling the lesson.
