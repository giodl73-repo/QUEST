## Critique

A genuine improvement over the first outing. The travel-plus-trap-plus-stronghold shape gives the party more ground to cross, more weather to feel, more chances to make decisions that cost them. I commend the five-mile distances measured in honest riding-time rather than fantasy-waved-away hours. A dungeon that knows where it is on a map is a dungeon that earns its name.

The **trap-as-compassion** structure is the design's best move. The party is choosing to walk into the trouble — and the module has positioned that choice so that refusing feels like abandoning a child. This is the kind of bind old-school design used to reach for and frequently missed. You have not missed.

My objections remain what they were last outing, and the design has not fully answered them.

**Five stronghold rooms is still thin.** Yes, you have added travel (Scenes 01, 02) and the stronghold is Jaquaysed at its small scale. But the party can descend, fight past one guard-post, and stand before Keloth within six minutes of real play if dice are kind. A proper dungeon is a gauntlet. Gavek's Rest is a theatre-set. It does the job the module wants; it does not bleed the party of resources. A level-3 party coming into this fight with full slots is an under-weighted climax.

**Your Cold Pulse redesign is correct and I approve it.** One-d-four cold, heavy-armor-immune, cloak-immune — that is a mechanic that costs without *interfering* with the atmospheric-landing rolls. This is the kind of rule-design your v1.0 lacked, and your v1.1 has fixed it. Good. I noted in the last review that mechanics must not destroy atmospheric payoffs; you have answered. Continue listening to this axis.

**The trade-at-the-altar is a moral bind, not a tactical one.** I have a grudging respect for this. A less sophisticated design would have made Keloth attack on sight and called it a boss fight. Your Keloth *talks* — offers a trade, surrenders at half HP, will die without defending himself if murdered-after-surrender. That is not weakness; that is the trap closing around the party's character. A Paladin player who kills Keloth anyway is telling the table who they are. That is old-school in the best sense — consequences for choices, not for rolls.

But — **the combat branch is too easy if the party fights fair.** Keloth plus Idra at Routine is 713 XP, hard but not deadly. Keloth plus three Cultists at Alert is 1050, deadly. A party that scouts well and silences the bell walks into the softer fight. I would make it harder: give Keloth two more Cultists at Routine (he is a priest with retainers; he does not work alone), and make the Alert-state a raid by the seven western cell-members returning from Varduin mid-combat. The Alert state should be **dangerous,** not just annoying.

**The reliquary mechanic is superb.** A curse that costs every other memory of the person it gives you. That is a real decision on a real scale. The player who attunes that reliquary has made a choice they will feel for the rest of their character's life. I would have built a dungeon specifically to deliver this artifact, and you have. The fact that Varran's last minute contains a wife's name Aelric did not know — Doriel — is a detail no other module has the patience for. I approve.

**Wandering pressure table upgrade** (specifically: Pella's off-screen distraction reducing the next guard-encounter by one) is the kind of diegetic consequence I asked for in the last review. You are listening. Keep doing it.

## Specific suggestions

1. **Raise the climax combat's baseline.** Keloth + Idra + 2 Cultists at Routine; + 3 Cultists + returning Lieutenant at Alert. The deadly-state should genuinely threaten a PC death.
2. **Add one more stronghold room.** A Bone-Collector bunk-room, or a ritual-preparation chamber where the moon-silver is worked. Six keyed stronghold locations is a reasonable floor; five is the floor of a theatre-set.
3. **The seven western cell-members** should appear at Varduin with mechanical detail. The module acknowledges the feint raid but treats it offstage. A session that ends with Varduin's east wing burned should have a combat scene showing who burned it. Include a two-paragraph sketch of Brother Laen's retrieval-team defense for Varduin as a narrative-level encounter the DM can describe on the ride home. Currently it is handed off as "resolved off-screen" — good for pacing, but weak for stakes.

## Final note

The workshop is learning. Two adventures in and the quality-gates are visibly working: the Cold Pulse fix is correct; the read-aloud discipline is being held; the manifest-symptom distribution is tight. I disagreed with your v1.0 on atmospheric-landing-vs-fairness and you amended. That is how a rubric evolves. Keep going.
