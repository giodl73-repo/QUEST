# Encounter — The Howl-Stones Trap

**Location:** Scene 02 — Howl-Stones pass.
**Trigger:** Party enters the pass. Full ambush if walked in openly; mixed-surprise if partially scouted; no ambush-surprise if full-party Stealth check succeeded (DC 13 group).

## Adversaries

- **2 × Bandit** (SRD, CR ⅛, 25 XP each) — the *crossfire* shooters, positioned on the stones' upper ledges.
- **1 × Bandit Captain-equivalent** (SRD Bandit Captain, CR 2 is too high for tier 1 party; use a **homebrewed lieutenant**: CR ½, 100 XP, stats below) — positioned on the far-end stone.

### Lieutenant (Bone-Collector Crossfire) — CR ½, 100 XP

- **AC** 15 (studded leather + shield)  ·  **HP** 22 (3d8+6)  ·  **Speed** 30 ft.
- **Str** 12 · **Dex** 14 · **Con** 14 · **Int** 10 · **Wis** 11 · **Cha** 11
- **Skills:** Athletics +3, Stealth +4, Perception +2
- **Senses:** passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Nerakan

**Actions:**
- **Multiattack.** Two weapon attacks.
- **Scimitar.** Melee. +4 to hit, 1d6+2 slashing.
- **Heavy crossbow.** Ranged (100/400). +4 to hit, 1d10+2 piercing.

**Reaction — Parry.** +2 AC on an incoming attack, once per turn.

## Encounter Math (party of 4 × L3)

- Base XP: 25 + 25 + 100 = 150.
- Adjusted (×2 for 3 creatures): 300 XP.
- Thresholds: Easy 300, Medium 600, Hard 900, Deadly 1600.
- **This is an easy-to-medium fight** — appropriate as an opener. Party should spend 0-1 spell slots and take minor HP loss.

## Tactics

- **Round 1:** if surprise, all three fire ranged. The lieutenant (heavy crossbow) targets Aelric or Grom (heaviest armor; she assumes they are the hardest to kill first); the two Bandits fire at Kessa (they see her as the soft target).
- **Round 2:** Bandits reload and fire again. Lieutenant, if engaged in melee, draws scimitar and holds her ground on the stone.
- **If reduced below 8 HP:** a Bandit surrenders if offered. The Lieutenant does NOT surrender; her orders are to die rather than be captured with information. She will leap from the stone to re-engage or to escape east (running toward the stronghold to warn).
- **If the Lieutenant escapes:** she reaches the stronghold in 45 minutes of in-fiction time. If the party's stronghold approach is less than 45 minutes behind her, they arrive AFTER she has warned Keloth. The stronghold moves to **Alert** status (see Scene 03/04/05 notes).

## Special — Terrain Advantage

- The stones provide **half-cover** (+2 AC, +2 Dex saves) for anyone behind them.
- The upper ledges provide **three-quarters cover** (+5 AC, +5 Dex saves). The Bandits on ledges start there; dislodging requires ranged attack, a spell that bypasses cover, or a climb (Athletics DC 13) to reach them.
- The whistling wind imposes **disadvantage on Perception (hearing) checks** throughout the pass. This affects both sides — the party's Stealth benefits from the wind-noise.

## If the Party Captures a Bandit Alive

- **Intimidation DC 12 or Persuasion DC 14:** the Bandit talks. Useful intelligence:
  - Cell is based at **Keloth's Hand** (their name for Gavek's Rest), half a day east.
  - Cell-leader is **Keloth**, a priest. Lieutenant at the stronghold is **Idra**. Three guards plus Keloth and Idra = five at the stronghold tonight.
  - Rest of the cell (seven members) is west on a "feint" — but the Bandit does not know where they went. They were ordered west yesterday.
  - The stronghold has a well in the stable yard. The Bandit does not use it — *"Idra says the well is haunted; we don't open it."*
  - Pella is alive, being held below, not harmed.
- **Failed Intimidation:** the Bandit lies about the cell size (says "ten" instead of "five") to scare the party off.

## Loot

- 2 × scimitar, 2 × dagger
- Lieutenant's heavy crossbow, 10 bolts
- Lieutenant's scimitar + studded leather + shield
- Mixed Nerakan coin: 12 gp
- Lieutenant's silver-bound finger-bone (cell-marker, 5 gp silver, **manifest-link**)
- Lieutenant's locked belt-pouch: **Pella's cut ribbon** (evidence) + small lock-box (Thieves' Tools DC 12 to open)

## Designer notes

- **The crossfire design** is Jaquaysed in its own way: the party must **actively engage verticality** to kill the ambushers — either climb, shoot, or bait them down. A tier-1 party that tries to play purely on the ground will struggle against the ledges.
- **Pella's ribbon** in the Lieutenant's pouch is the session's sharpest early-object — an unambiguous signal to the players that *they are tracking their child*, not just mercenaries.
- **Rubric v1.1:** this encounter is mechanically pure (no atmospheric-check-gating from the combat mechanics themselves). The cold of the pass is flavor only, not a save-required hazard during combat. Passes the cross-dimensional-harmlessness anchor.
