# Encounter — Stronghold Guards (patrol + guard-post)

Two possible sub-encounters in the stronghold, depending on party path. Either or both may fire.

## Sub-encounter A — Stable Yard Patrol (Scene 04)

**Trigger:** Party in the Stable Yard. Patrol enters 1d4 minutes after party arrival, then every 10 minutes.

- **Adversaries:** 1 × Bandit (CR ⅛, 25 XP). *On Alert:* 2 × Bandits.
- **Tactics:** The patrol Bandit walks a set circuit. If he spots the party, he makes for the **alarm bell** as his first priority. If prevented, he fights with scimitar.
- **If the bell is rung:** stronghold moves to **Alert**. All subsequent encounters gain +1 creature; Keloth prepares defenses; Pella is moved from Cell-Block to Vault as a hostage.

**Encounter math:** 25 XP × 1 = easy (trivial). On alert: 50 XP × 1.5 = 75 (still easy). Resource drain is minimal; time pressure is the mechanic.

## Sub-encounter B — Vault Approach Guard Post (Scene 05)

**Trigger:** Party descends the stair and engages the guard-post alcove, OR the guard spots them from the alcove.

- **Adversaries (routine):** 1 × Bandit (CR ⅛, 25 XP).
- **Adversaries (Alert):** 1 × Bandit + 1 × Bandit Lieutenant (homebrew CR ½, 100 XP — same stat block as the Howl-Stones lieutenant).

**Routine combat:** 25 XP × 1 = trivial. Consider this a chance to scout — ambush + one-round combat. Use Cunning Action / Divine Smite / Sneak Attack to end it cleanly.

**Alert combat:** 125 XP × 1.5 = ~190 XP. Still easy, but lieutenant's reaction-Parry makes it a slightly harder engagement.

### Tactics (routine)

- Single Bandit seated; reading the duty-roster. Perception +0 passive 10. Party Stealth DC 10 to approach; DC 15 to kill before he shouts.
- If he shouts: the cell-block Cultists come running (1 round); if bell is rung elsewhere, stronghold goes fully Alert.

### Tactics (Alert)

- Bandit stands forward; Lieutenant (Idra) stands back, ready to shout warning and fire heavy crossbow.
- Idra's objective: shout "**KELOTH! OST!**" on her first turn, then fire from cover. Her scream alerts Keloth; Keloth moves Pella to the Vault within 2 rounds.

## Sub-encounter D — Well-Guard at the Stable Yard (Scene 04, Alert only) *(rev 2: new per Jaquays)*

**Trigger:** Party attempts to use the well after the bell has rung OR the stronghold is otherwise alerted.

- **Adversaries:** 1 × Bandit (CR ⅛, 25 XP), posted at the well-cover, weapon drawn, watching.
- **Tactics:** The well-guard is looking upward toward the stair-arch; his back is to anyone approaching from the west. A Stealth DC 14 approach bypasses him; Stealth DC 16 kills him silently. If he spots the party, he blows his bone-whistle (alerts cell-block interior); combat then normal.
- **Design rationale:** a smart cell-leader would post a guard here if the alarm had rung. Keloth is smart. This closes the "stealth shortcut becomes easier when the bell rings" loophole.

## Sub-encounter C — Cell-Block Patrol (Scene 07, Alert only)

**Trigger:** Party in the Cell-Block when stronghold is Alert.

- **Adversaries:** 1 × Bandit.
- **Tactics:** Bandit patrols the cell-block. If he sees the party, he tries to retreat through the iron door to the Vault (to warn Keloth). If prevented, he fights. Will surrender if reduced below 4 HP — he does not share Keloth's zealotry, he just wants to live.

## Total stronghold encounter budget (excluding climax)

- Routine path: ~50 XP total across Scenes 03-05 (trivial).
- Alert path: ~200-300 XP total across Scenes 03-05 (easy-medium).
- The **real** encounter is Scene 06 (Keloth). These guard encounters are attrition, not climax.

## Loot (per guard)

- Scimitar or short-spear (5 sp each).
- Bone-whistle (3 gp curio, patrol-impersonation utility).
- 2-4 sp in coins.
- Sometimes a smaller silver-filigree token (5 gp silver, manifest-link).

## Designer notes

- **The bell is the ticking clock.** The party has real agency — ring the bell = hard climax; don't = easier climax. This answers the Tactician's S01 feedback about wanting real consequences from tactical choices.
- **The Bandits here are not zealots.** Only Keloth is. His cultists are hired help, mostly Nerakan locals who got into the work for coin. They will surrender at low HP. This differentiates them from the trap-ambushers (who were ideologically committed lieutenants).
- **Rubric v1.1:** no cross-dimensional mechanic here. Combat is combat; atmosphere is elsewhere.
