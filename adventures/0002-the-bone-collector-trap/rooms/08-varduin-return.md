# Scene 08 — The Return to Varduin

A closing scene for the ride home. Not a keyed dungeon location; a narrative frame for the session's landing.

## Read-aloud (as the muster crests the last rise above Varduin)

> You smell the smoke before you see the village. It is not a cooking-fire. The way-house's east wing is black against the morning — one wall still standing, the roof caved in. The main hall is intact. Horses are in the yard. A Solamnic banner you did not leave behind hangs from the gate.

## What happened in the party's absence

While the muster was north, the **seven western Bone-Collectors** the cell had spared for a feint raid struck at Varduin on the second night. Brother Laen had not expected this; but he had sent word south the moment the party left, and three **Knights of the Sword** from the nearest Solamnic holding arrived at the way-house on the first night. The raid was uneven:

- The raiders burned the east wing — Thera's old bedroom among the losses — and took two horses from the stable.
- They killed **two villagers** (a farrier named Tell and an old woman named Mira-no-kin who had taken in travelers for thirty years). Grom recognizes Mira-no-kin's name; she had ordered a hearth-fitting from the Reorx-circuit two years ago and Grom had sent her one.
- The Solamnic retrieval team lost **one knight** (Sir Venric of the Sword, age 29, unmarried, from a waystation south of Solanthus). His body lies in the way-house main hall, draped in his own cloak, awaiting the Order's pyre.
- Four of the seven raiders were killed. Three escaped east.
- Brother Laen is alive but has a split lip and a hand he is favoring. He has already sent the Council's report.

## Brother Laen on return (what he tells the party)

- *"You were right to go. I would not have survived the counterfactual where we let Thera ride alone."*
- *"My sister Wena has been named inside the Council. The letter arrived yesterday. They have asked me to withdraw from the clerkwork and serve at her cloister as a gesture of 'family consolidation.' I have not answered."*
- *"Sir Venric has been kind to me. I am going to the pyre in an hour. You do not have to attend."*

The party can attend Sir Venric's pyre (one hour in-fiction; atmospheric weight; Grom may lead a Reorx-rite if invited; Aelric will likely attend by Oath). Or they can keep their distance. Either is valid.

## What the party does with what they brought home

- **Pella.** Brother Laen's elderly mother (first mentioned in #0001's `npcs/brother-laen.md`) lives at the north end of the village. She offers, unasked, to take Pella in. Thera can accept on Pella's behalf, or refuse, or ask Pella directly — the module should prompt the party to choose, not the DM.
- **The Varran-reliquary.** If attuned: Aelric goes to find a quiet corner of the main hall to be alone. Grom sits outside that corner without being asked. If unattuned: it goes to Brother Laen's locked cabinet — the same cabinet the Silver Rose was filed into before the Council transported it to Palanthas. The Council will not move fast on this one; they are still reeling from the first.
- **The shelf-reliquaries.** If taken: Laen is visibly disturbed, asks the party to keep them out of sight until a Council delegate arrives (three days, maybe four). If destroyed (via Grom's shattering-rite, done at Varduin's smithy): Laen will not be told ahead of time but will understand after. He may quietly endorse the outcome.
- **The Mira-letter.** Laen reads it, sits down, reads it again. Agrees to a Council contract to protect Mira Vaenshold-Silversmith in Crystalmir-by-the-Fells. **This is adventure #0003's formal hook.**
- **Pella's decision.** If the party asks her what she wants, she will say: *"I go with you."* The DM should let this land without comfort. Whether the party allows it shapes future sessions.

## Encounter

None. The session lands in quiet. If the party goes looking for the three escaped Bone-Collectors, that is #0003 starting early.

## Treasure

- The Order will pay **15 gp per shelf-reliquary recovered**, reserved pending Council delegate.
- The Mira-letter is priceless; Laen does not pay for it, but the contract it generates does.
- Brother Laen's **20 gp safe-conduct stipend** is paid regardless.

## GM Notes

- **This scene is a landing, not a climax.** The adventure's emotional weight has already fired in Scene 06. Scene 08 gives the players time to absorb what they did. Do not introduce new crises.
- **Do not rush through Sir Venric.** If the party attends the pyre, let them stand in silence for a beat. Aelric will recognize — perhaps — that Varran's pyre was not held. His brother's body was never recovered; there was no pyre for him. Sir Venric gets one. This is the session's final atmospheric beat.
- **Grom's doubt from S01** resolves or does not, here. If the party destroyed the Varran-reliquary via his rite, Grom is quietly at peace; the Order's cover-up pattern has a counter. If the party returned it to the Council, Grom watches Laen accept it and says nothing. This is an open thread for #0003.
- **If the party adopted Pella:** Thera's character has changed. She is no longer only a freelance scout; she is a child's guardian. Adjust her Playstyle Heuristics for S03+.
- **Per-PC reception:** Aelric → the empty pyre-thought; Grom → Mira-no-kin's death; Thera → east-wing loss (her old bedroom); Kessa → the arrival of the Knights of the Sword (she has not seen many Solamnic knights in formal armor before; that is research material).

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Sir Venric of the Sword died on the third step of the way-house porch, blocking a Bone-Collector from reaching the children sheltering in the pantry. One of the children he saved was a cooper's daughter named Alys, seven. Alys will remember his face for the rest of her life and will not know his name. She will call him, when she is grown and telling her own children, "the tall knight on the porch." That is all she will be able to say of him. It is more than most men get.*
