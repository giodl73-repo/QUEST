# Scene 02 — Pursuit & The Howl-Stones

A two-beat scene: tracking the cell, then the ambush.

## Pursuit (beat 1) — read-aloud

> The trail goes north. Hoof-prints, maybe five riders. One set is smaller — a child's weight on a pony being led. Late snow holds the prints where open ground has lost them. The land lifts into low hills. By afternoon you see, ahead, the Howl-Stones: a pass between standing stones twelve feet high, set by hands older than any history.

## Pursuit — Features

- **Tracking the trail.** Wisdom (Survival) DC 12 to follow. On a **fail**, the party loses half a day circling; resume tracking from a higher DC (DC 15) at a waypoint. On a success, they reach the Howl-Stones by late afternoon.
- **The pony's pace.** Whoever is tracking (or rolling Investigation DC 10 on the prints) notices the smaller pony's prints are spaced unevenly — Pella has been *dismounting and refusing to remount*. She has been fighting them on the trail. She is alive. She is also slowing them down, which is why the cell took two days to cover fifteen miles. This is information the party *can use*: Pella is buying them time.
- **Howl-Stones at distance.** Visible from a mile off. The party can choose to approach openly OR to circle and observe. A circling approach (Stealth DC 13, all PCs roll) reveals that three figures are positioned **above** the stones, crouched among them. Open approach walks the party directly into the ambush with no warning.

## The Howl-Stones (beat 2) — read-aloud

> The pass is two hundred paces of narrow track between twelve-foot stones that whistle in the wind — faintly, like breath drawn through teeth. That is how the stones got their name. Old Nerakan carvings on some of them, half-worn. Nothing moves above. Nothing moves below. The wind does not stop.

## Features (at the stones)

- **The whistling stones.** Wind draws through natural hollows in the stones' flanks at certain angles. Audible from any direction. The sound is *not* cover for footsteps — it's steady. An attentive party (Perception DC 12 passively or actively) will notice that somewhere at the pass's far end, the whistling is *broken* for a moment — not a gust. A movement.
- **Old Nerakan carvings.** History DC 14 reads them as pre-Istaran Nerakan glyphs — warrior-memorials from a people who lived here centuries before the cell that now uses these stones. The Bone-Collectors have scratched newer marks over the older ones; the newer marks are crude. Glosses to land for new-readers: *"pre-Istaran Nerakans"* were the plains-people who predated the eastern wasteland's present inhabitants — a faded culture that the current Neraka is not descendant-of.
- **Pella's pony.** Tied to a stone at the pass's far end, alone, saddle on. A decoy. Pella is not here; she has already been taken to the stronghold, ahead of the ambush. Investigation DC 13 reads the pony's dampness — she has been standing there ninety minutes. Whoever tied her is gone. The cell has split: three ambushers at the Howl-Stones, the rest at the stronghold with Pella.

## Encounter — The Trap

**See `encounters/trap-ambush.md`.**

Three Bone-Collectors (2 × Bandit, 1 × Bandit Captain-equivalent as lieutenant) have positioned themselves on the stones' upper ledges for a crossfire.

- **If the party walked in openly:** full ambush. All three fire on round 1 with surprise.
- **If the party scouted (Stealth DC 13 group):** party catches the ambush flat-footed. Roll initiative normally; ambushers do not have surprise.
- **If any single PC scouted (Stealth DC 13 individual):** that PC is ware; others may still be surprised (individual initiative, mixed).

After the fight: one ambusher, if subdued alive (Knock-Out / non-lethal), carries a crude map and will talk (Intimidation DC 12 or Persuasion DC 14). The map is a rough sketch of Gavek's Rest's upper level. It shows the common room and stable yard but NOT the well-shaft — the Bone-Collectors themselves do not use the well; they consider it haunted and keep its cover weighted down. This is a **clue**: the party can learn the well exists but may not yet know it's a shortcut. That's for the stronghold scene to reveal.

## Treasure

- The ambushers carry standard kit: 3 × shortbow, 3 × scimitars, 12 gp aggregate in mixed Nerakan coin.
- The lieutenant (if killed/captured) has **a silver-bound finger-bone** on a cord around her neck — a cell-marker, denoting Keloth's faction. 5 gp silver value. **Manifest item: same moon-silver alloy as the Silver Rose and the Reliquary.** Kessa (Arcana DC 10 ritual Detect Magic) can confirm. This is evidence the cell has been working the same silver vein.
- The lieutenant's belt-pouch has **a small locked box** (Thieves' Tools DC 12). Inside: Pella's ribbon, cut. Proof she was here; proof she is alive; proof they wanted the party to find this.

## Connections

- South: back-trail to Mensa's Waystation.
- East: trail to Gavek's Rest / Keloth's Hand (Scene 03 onward).
- North: continues into Nerakan wastes (dead end for this session; seeds future adventure).

## GM Notes

- **Grom's mending impulse at the captured lieutenant.** If a PC is captured alive and bleeding, Grom may instinctively stabilize them. This is in-character. Let it happen; the captive, wounded and stabilized, is more cooperative than one on death's door.
- **Pella's ribbon is a gut-punch.** Describe it small: blue, frayed, knotted.
- **First-use gloss:** *"Nerakan wastes"* — eastern Krynn, sparse and hostile to most travelers, cradled between mountain ranges. Drop via Kessa's cataloging voice.
- **If the party refuses the combat path entirely** (walks around the Howl-Stones at cost of half a day): the ambushers eventually give up and return to the stronghold. The stronghold approach is harder (they're alert, Keloth has 2 lieutenants + 3 guards inside). The party chose safety over tempo; reward that by not killing them, but impose the harder stronghold.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The Nerakan carvings were made by a people whose grandmothers had stood under these stones to say the names of their dead aloud into the wind — because the wind carried names east, and east was where the dead had gone before there were Kharolis and Thorbardin to break the land. The people who made this place no longer exist. Their wind still knows the names.*

## Manifest symptoms landed at this scene

- **Attribution drift (pre-setup):** the Nerakan carvings over-scratched by cruder Bone-Collector marks. The older attribution is being erased. Echoes Aelwen's name being taken from inscriptions in #0001.
- **Moon-silver trace:** the lieutenant's bone-cord is the same alloy as the Rose and the Reliquary. Links the Bone-Collectors to the silver vein Aelwen used.

## Encounter math

XP threshold for 4 × L3 party: medium 600, hard 900.

Encounter base: 25 (Bandit) × 2 + 100 (Bandit Captain-equiv, CR ½) × 1 = 150 XP. Adjusted ×2 (3 creatures) = **300 adjusted XP**. Easy-medium. Appropriate opening fight; preserves resources for the stronghold.
