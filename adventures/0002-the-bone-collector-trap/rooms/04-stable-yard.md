# Scene 04 — The Stable Yard (Stronghold Upper)

## Read-aloud

> Roofless courtyard. Weeds up through the cobblestones. The far wall holds an iron alarm-bell on a frayed rope. Straw has drifted in the north corner, piled unnaturally high. A stone block sits in the yard's center — weathered, cracked across, no obvious purpose.

## Features

- **The alarm bell.** Iron, dented, functional. If struck, its sound carries into the Vault Approach (Scene 05) and alerts the entire stronghold. Rope is old and would snap on a hard yank (auto-success to ring). Climbing to the bell silently is DC 12 Stealth. Detaching the rope silently is DC 13 Sleight of Hand.
- **The straw pile (north corner).** Unnaturally high and concentrated. **Investigation DC 13** reveals it has been piled there to hide something. Clearing the straw reveals **the well-cover**: a circular stone slab over a hole in the floor.
- **The well-cover.** Heavy stone, ~80 lbs. **Strength (Athletics) DC 12** to lift (one character) or auto-succeed with two. Beneath: a circular shaft descending ~30 ft to a dry well-chamber that connects to the Cell-Block (Scene 07). Climb DC 10 with rope; DC 12 without (handholds in the stones).
- **Partial ledge in the well-shaft.** A climber descending the well will notice, halfway down (~15 ft), **a sealed second-level landing** — rubble-packed, about 3 ft wide, suggesting the shaft once opened onto an intermediate floor no longer accessible. Gavek's Rest in its Istari prime was larger than the Bone-Collectors' current footprint; this landing was part of a second interior level since walled off. **Hook for future adventure.** No immediate access this session; a character can note it and mark it for later.
- **(Alert only) Well-guard.** If the bell has rung OR the stronghold is otherwise alerted, **1 × Bandit** is posted at the well-cover, weapon drawn, watching the stair-arch. A smart cell-leader would post one there; Keloth is smart. Stealth to bypass requires DC 14; combat = 25 XP easy.
- **The stone block (yard center).** Cracked limestone, scorched. **Investigation DC 10** identifies it as an old chimney-base for a forge that used to stand here — probably Gavek's forge, dismantled long after his time. Nothing hidden inside; flavor only.
- **Patrol.** A Bone-Collector patrol sweeps the yard every 10 minutes of in-fiction time. See encounter below.

## Encounter

**Patrol.** Roll 1d4 for timing: the patrol is in the yard at minute **1d4** after the party enters, then every 10 minutes. Composition: **1 × Bandit** (CR ⅛). If discovered, the Bandit makes for the alarm bell; the party has 2 rounds of combat before the bell is rung unless they can stop him (Grapple, Stunning Strike, a crit that drops him, etc).

- If Thera (or anyone hidden, Stealth DC 14) can ambush the patrol: auto-surprise. One round of free action. Usually enough to silence the Bandit before the bell.
- If the bell IS rung: stronghold goes to Alert status (see Scene 03's notes).

## Treasure

- The Bandit carries a **bone-carved whistle** (3 gp curio value) on a thong — a cell-identification tool. If blown, it produces a tone the cell uses as a check-in signal. The party, once they have it, can *impersonate* a patrol check-in with Performance DC 13 or Deception DC 15.
- In the straw pile (when clearing): **2 sp and a child's glove**, too small for any adult hand. Pella's, recently lost. She has been through this yard, being taken below.

## Connections

- West (archway, rubble-topped): Common Room (Scene 03).
- Down (well-shaft, if uncovered): Cell-Block (Scene 07) — the **stealth shortcut**.

## GM Notes

- **The well is the earned shortcut.** A party that investigates the straw pile discovers it; a party that doesn't, doesn't. No push from the DM.
- **The bell is the session's ticking clock.** If it rings, the climax encounter adjusts upward: Keloth is alert, holds Pella as hostage at blade-point, and has 2 additional cultists with him in the vault. If it does not ring, Keloth is in the vault alone (or with one lieutenant) and Pella is in the cell-block, separate.
- **Thera will likely investigate the straw reflexively.** Her playstyle heuristic "checks pockets, rafters, floor-gaps reflexively" applies. Let her find the well.
- **Patrol-impersonation (whistle).** If the party uses the whistle later (at the Cell-Block or Vault), they can gain a round of advantage before combat. Reward this if attempted.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Pella fought them in this yard. She was nine, she was not larger than a goat, and she had kicked the tall one in the shin hard enough that he had limped. They had not been expecting a child who would kick. They had laughed, and then they had stopped laughing, and then they had taken her below. One of them had said: "Keloth will not laugh." Pella had not known who Keloth was. She had decided, in the carrying, that she would remember the name.*

## Manifest symptoms landed

- **Per-PC reception:** Thera → the child's glove in the straw (mother-grief trigger compounded with Pella-grief). Document.
- **Dryness / sensory:** the well-chamber below is dry — no groundwater, no dampness — in a land where late snow is still falling. Same strange dryness as #0001's Rose Cairn. Connection not yet named; may become a cluster signal across adventures.
