**Travel scale:** Varduin to Mensa's Waystation is ~60 mi (two days). Mensa's to Howl-Stones is ~15 mi (half-day). Howl-Stones to Gavek's Rest is ~10 mi (half-day).

**Weather:** late spring in Abanasinia is mild. North of the New-Sea-yet-to-be (pre-Cataclysm geography), the country gets colder quickly; the Nerakan border-lands are still carrying late snow at this season. Bring rations and cold-weather cloaks.

## Topology Summary

- **7 keyed locations** (2 external + 5 stronghold).
- **Loops:** [5]→[6]→[7]→[5] (full loop once the [6]↔[7] iron door is opened).
- **Vertical:** stair [3]→[5] AND well-shaft [4]↔[7].
- **Two paths to climax ([6]):** via [3]→[5]→[6] (frontal); via well [4]→[7]→[6] (stealth).
- **Earned shortcut:** the well. Requires party to discover the cover in [4] and choose to use it. Bypasses [3]'s combat and [5]'s guards.
- **Branch cues:** [3]'s archway is visible from the road (cold-air draft carries smoke and voices). [4]'s well-cover is not obvious — requires Investigation DC 13 to spot under old straw. A party that doesn't investigate the stable-yard takes the frontal path; a party that does earns the stealth route.

## Notes for the DM

- **First-use glosses to land** (per accessibility policy): "Bone-Collectors" on first mention (small Nerakan death-cult); "Nerakan" (people of the eastern wastes, mostly hostile to Solamnic influence); "Reorx-rite" (dwarven forge-priest ritual for mending or unmaking).
- Mensa's Waystation (Scene 1) is empty on arrival. Pella's aunt's body is inside. Not a combat encounter.
- Howl-Stones (Scene 2) is the trap. Bone-Collectors have chosen the ground.
- Gavek's Rest has ~5 active Bone-Collectors at session time (3 guards + Keloth + 1 lieutenant). Rest of the cell (~10 more) is west — the feint raid on Varduin.
