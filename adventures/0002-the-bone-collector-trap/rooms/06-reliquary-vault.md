# Scene 06 — The Reliquary Vault (Stronghold Lower) — climax

## Read-aloud

> Low-ceilinged, stone-cold room. Along the east wall: a shelf of silver-bound skulls, spaced like a quiet procession. At the center, a low altar holds an iron box. Behind the altar stands a tall, soft-robed man, hands folded. On the altar lies a nine-year-old girl, bound at the wrists. She sees you. Her chin lifts. Pella.

## Features

- **The shelf of skulls (east wall).** Six skulls, each bound in moon-silver filigree, spaced on a shelf. Five occupied reliquaries. One empty — **and it has been prepared**: polished to mirror-brightness, eye-sockets open and waiting, a single silver thread laid across the mandible as if the filigree work is mid-forge. An artifact waiting. (Kessa, Arcana Detect Magic: all six magical — 5 active, 1 dormant.)
- **The named shelf-reliquary.** One of the five occupied skulls bears a hand-scratched sigil at the temple — a Nerakan house-rune. Investigation DC 12 reveals the name: **Haena of the Fourth House, PC 29.** A Nerakan midwife's last minute. Her extended family still lives in the hill-village of **Verrat, east of Neraka.** Seeds a future delivery arc.
- **The wall-ledger (north wall).** A charcoal ledger runs the length of the north wall: names, dates, locations, in Keloth's hand. Some entries crossed out (overwritten). Some circled (given away). Decades long. Investigation DC 13: one entry in the middle section is **uncredited and in a different hand** — *"I will not look at the rose again."* A party that played S01 will recognize the phrasing from Vesk's memory fragment in the Rose Cairn. Neither Vesk nor Keloth wrote it here. Someone else did — long dead, held in one of the shelf-reliquaries. *Cross-adventure signal.*
- **The central altar.** Low stone. The iron box on it contains a seventh reliquary. **This is the Varran-reliquary.** Keloth wears a key at his neck.
- **Pella.** Bound by the wrists to the box's handles. Bruised. Thin. Not crying. *(Stats: AC 10 · HP 6 · Speed 25 ft. Child's bite +0, 1 piercing. Not a combatant. Full playsheet: `npcs/pella.md`.)*
- **Keloth.** Behind the altar. Unarmored. Soft-robed. Hands folded. He speaks first.

## Opening dialogue — Keloth's offer (DM plays Keloth)

**Keloth (calm, without threat-voice):** *"Put down your weapons and listen to me for thirty seconds. I will say my piece, and then you will do what you think is right. My name is Keloth. I am a priest. You did not come here for the child. You think you did. But you walked here because one of you —"* (his eyes, which have been on Aelric the entire time, do not leave Aelric) *"— needs a minute he has never had, and I am the man who can give it to him. I have on this altar the death-minute of Varran of Crownhold. Your brother. If you want him, I will give him to you. All I want in exchange is the child to walk out this door at your side, alive, today, and the work of my cell to continue unharried by your Order. Solamnia asks for too much. One minute for the child, and we are done."*

**He is not lying.** He is offering an exchange. The party can verify (Insight DC 13 — he's earnest). This is not a trap beyond the trap they are already in. This is the *moral choice* dressed as a trade.

**At this moment, Pella speaks.** The DM MUST narrate her interior-state as certainty — not a question, not a hope. She looks at Aelric, clearly, and asks: *"Are you the knight my papa said would come?"* **She has decided the answer is yes.** Her question is not a question. She is naming Aelric as what she has been waiting for. The DM should not soften this; a child who has been through two days of captivity has earned the right to decide who her rescuer is before they have proven it.

## The Choice (read this to the players as a DM-meta aside if helpful)

- **Option A — Accept the trade.** Aelric (or any PC) attunes to the Varran-reliquary. He hears Varran's last minute. He loses every other memory of Varran. Pella walks free. The cell is not pursued.
  - Keloth hands over the key.
  - Pella is released.
  - The party leaves.
  - Consequence: the cell continues operating. Aelric carries Varran's final minute and nothing else of him.
- **Option B — Refuse the trade; fight.** Combat. See `encounters/vault-climax.md`. Keloth will try to escape with the Varran-reliquary during the fight; will kill Pella if backed into a corner (he is a zealot).
- **Option C — Negotiate.** A very high Persuasion (DC 20) or a specific offer Keloth values (the Silver Rose returned to the Bone-Collectors, for example — though the party doesn't have it) might get Pella released without the reliquary exchange. The DM should be stingy; Keloth is a priest of a grief-god and has no interest in bargains smaller than an exchange of grief.
- **Option D — Attack, then negotiate mid-combat.** If Keloth is reduced to below half HP, he will drop to his knees and offer Option A again, as a surrender. If the party accepts, he hands over the Varran-reliquary, Pella, and his freedom. If refused, combat resumes.

## Encounter (if combat)

**See `encounters/vault-climax.md`.** *(Rev 2: climax baseline raised per Gygax/Jaquays convergent suggestion.)*

- **Keloth** (Cult Fanatic reskin, CR 2, 450 XP).
- **Idra, lieutenant** (Bandit Lieutenant stats from Howl-Stones, CR ½, 100 XP) — always present at the climax (moved up from deferred Alert-only role).
- **1 × Cultist** (Bandit, CR ⅛, 25 XP) — on Routine.
- **3 × Cultists + a returning second-wave Lieutenant** at Alert; the second-wave Lieutenant arrives at round 3 of combat, emerging from the cell-block door (having been roused by the bell).

**Adjusted XP:**
- **Routine:** Keloth + Idra + 1 Cultist = 575 base × 2 (3 creatures) = **1,150 XP. Deadly.**
- **Alert:** Keloth + Idra + 3 Cultists at start = 625 base × 2 = 1,250 XP. Second-wave Lieutenant at round 3 adds +100 base; total effective encounter weight ~1,450 XP (deadly+, TPK-risk).

**Keloth's tactics:**
- Round 1: casts **Shield of Faith** on himself (+2 AC → 15 for 10 min).
- Round 2: casts **Command** on the party's closest heavy-hitter (Aelric or Grom) to "Halt" — Wis save DC 11.
- Round 3+: **Inflict Wounds** (melee spell attack, 3d10 necrotic at L1).
- Throughout: never attacks Pella directly; uses her position on the altar as a psychological block.
- At half HP: surrenders with Option A restated.
- If cornered without surrender option: tries to grab the Varran-reliquary and flee via vault's rear exit (a hidden tunnel — the Bone-Collectors' escape route, collapses after use; flagged here but not part of the climax unless the fight reaches that point).

## Treasure (if combat route)

- **The five other occupied reliquaries** on the shelf (each holds a different dead's last minute; see `treasures/reliquary.md` Appendix for what they hold — four of the five are Nerakan, the fifth is a Solamnic knight unknown to the party; could be future adventure material).
- **One empty reliquary** (the one intended for the party). Moon-silver; 30 gp raw; mechanically identical to the Varran one but un-occupied. Can be used by the party if they wished to preserve a dying friend's minute later.
- **Keloth's belongings**: robe (fine weave, 20 gp); dagger (5 gp); a sealed letter to a Nerakan authority naming the Rose Council as targets-of-interest (5-month old; seeds #0003's Council-conflict line); 40 gp in mixed Nerakan and Abanasinian coin; the altar's iron-box key.

## Connections

- West (iron-banded door): Vault Approach (Scene 05).
- East (iron door to Cell-Block): cell-block (Scene 07). Keloth's key opens this too.
- Hidden rear tunnel (south wall): an escape route. Keloth knows it. ## GM Notes

- **Play Keloth calmly.** He is not a snarling villain. He is a genuine priest of a genuine small god, who believes the grief-economy he curates is a kindness. The offer he makes Aelric is sincere. He is the scariest kind of villain: the one who is neither wrong nor monstrous in his own terms.
- **Play Pella as a child, not an NPC token.** She sees Aelric's face; her father described Solamnic knights to her once; she asks, tiny-voiced: *"Are you the knight my papa said would come?"* This is the session's emotional hinge.
- **Let the party decide slowly.** The trade is a real trade. Do not pressure them. Keloth is patient.
- **If a PC attunes the Varran-reliquary at the altar:** the minute plays silently, in the attuner's head. The party sees the attuner go still for exactly sixty seconds. When it ends, ask the player to describe what Varran said, did, thought, felt. Write it into the session log. The memory of Varran the attuner had before this — all other memories — is gone.
- **If the party takes Pella and the Varran-reliquary but DOES NOT attune:** they have not accepted Keloth's trade. Keloth will consider the trade broken (if alive) — the cell will pursue. This is the "take-it-unattuned" option parallel to the Silver Rose's.

### Memory Fragment (optional — reserved for if a PC attunes)

> *Varran's last minute contains: the specific cold of a Nerakan dusk; a sergeant named Hern who fell beside him; a sentence he thought but did not say — "Paladine, carry my wife" — though Varran never married. The wife he had meant was the woman he had intended to ask, who was still alive at Crownhold when Varran died, and who died three years later without knowing she had been on his mind at the end. Her name was Doriel. Aelric never knew about her.*

## The Reaction Moments *(rev 2: now two simultaneous beats)*

At the instant the Varran-reliquary is lifted from the altar:

1. **The five occupied shelf-reliquaries dim by one perceptible degree.** The Varran-reliquary was the anchor. Perception DC 12 to catch.
2. **The charcoal wall-ledger briefly brightens** — as if an unseen hand drew a line through one name. Kessa (Perception DC 13, specifically watching the ledger) sees the uncredited *"I will not look at the rose again"* entry glow warm-orange for a single heartbeat, then fade. Other entries do not brighten. Whoever wrote that line was bound to the ward; lifting the reliquary has disturbed their rest.

## Manifest symptoms landed

- **Reaction moments (×2):** the shelf-dim + the ledger-brighten (above). Per-PC Perception rolls tracked.
- **Attribution drift (final landing):** Keloth's robe has, sewn into the hem, an embroidery of a name that has been unpicked. **"Keloth, son of —"** and then nothing. His own father's name has been removed, probably by his own hand. The pattern closes: even the cell-leader is participating in the erasure-economy.
- **Cross-adventure signal:** the *"I will not look at the rose again"* ledger entry — verbatim from Vesk's Memory Fragment in #0001 Scene 5. A prior victim of the cell's work whose last minute lives in one of the shelf-reliquaries. The phrase has traveled from a Rose Cairn brazier to a Nerakan vault ledger. *This is the workshop's first explicit cross-session signal per Stuart's S01/S02 convergent suggestion.*
- **Per-PC reception (rubric v1.1):** Aelric → the Keloth offer. Thera → Pella. Grom → the forge-destroyable reliquary. Kessa → the moon-silver evidence + the Mira-Vaenshold letter + the ledger-brighten. Document all four.
- **Inter-PC chain (rubric v1.1, 10-criterion):** Aelric's decision chains to Grom (shattering-rite), Thera (judgment of the call), Kessa (silver-vein analysis). Document any chains that fire.
