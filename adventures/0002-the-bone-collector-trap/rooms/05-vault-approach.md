# Scene 05 — The Vault Approach (Stronghold Lower)

## Read-aloud

> The stair opens into a dry stone corridor. Twenty feet ahead, a guard-post alcove — a wooden chair, a table with a clay cup still steaming, a short-spear leaning against the wall. At the corridor's far end, two doors: a plain wooden one left, an iron-banded door right. The air smells of old bone-meal and hearth-ash.

## Features

- **The guard-post alcove.** A Bone-Collector sits here on routine. One Bandit (CR ⅛). If he has been alerted (bell rung OR sentry from Scene 03 fled here), he is positioned defensively rather than lounging, and a lieutenant stands with him (one Bandit Captain stat equivalent, CR ½).
- **The left door (wooden, unlocked).** Leads to the **Cell-Block (Scene 07)**. Marked with a charcoal sigil of a hand — Keloth's house-mark.
- **The right door (iron-banded, locked).** Leads to the **Reliquary Vault (Scene 06)**. Keloth carries the key. Lockpick DC 13; door AC 17, HP 18 to break through.
- **The bone-meal smell.** Faint but distinct. Investigation DC 10 identifies it as real — the Bone-Collectors grind certain artifact-bones into meal for ritual purposes. Kessa (Arcana DC 13) recognizes the scent from a footnote in a Wizards of High Sorcery monograph on unsanctioned necrotic practice.
- **The guard-post's table.** Papers: a rough duty-roster (names of 5 cell members on rotation: **Keloth, Idra, Vesk, Kann, Tull**). Vesk's name is crossed out. Idra appears twice (as both cook and Wednesday-night watch — a small revealing inefficiency). The party now knows the cell's size and two specific names: Keloth (cell-leader) and Idra (lieutenant).

## Encounter

**Guard post.** 1 × Bandit (routine) OR 1 × Bandit + 1 × Bandit Captain-equivalent (if Alert).

- Routine combat: single guard. Easy encounter (25 XP base × 1 = 25 XP adjusted).
- Alert combat: guard + lieutenant. 25 + 100 = 125 base × 1.5 = ~190 adjusted XP. Still easy.

**Tactics:** If the guard spots the party, he shouts *"KELOTH! OST!"* — Nerakan for "Keloth, enemy!" — which alerts everyone on the lower level. The fight proceeds; but if any combatant is still conscious by round 3, Keloth has moved Pella to the vault (she becomes a human shield in Scene 06).

## Treasure

- Guard's kit: short-spear, 5 sp, a bone-whistle (same as Scene 04).
- The duty-roster: priceless intelligence. Players can identify specific names when they hear them later.

## Connections

- Up (stair): Common Room (Scene 03).
- Left (wooden door): Cell-Block (Scene 07).
- Right (iron-banded door, locked): Reliquary Vault (Scene 06).

## GM Notes

- **The wooden door (Cell-Block) is UNLOCKED.** This is a design choice: the Bone-Collectors do not fear their captive escaping through the lower level. Pella is chained; the door is a courtesy.
- **The iron-banded door (Vault) is LOCKED.** Keloth has the only key. The party can: pick it (Thera), break it (Athletics DC 17 or slow damage to HP 18), or wait for Keloth to open it (if they capture him alive).
- **Gloss:** *"Nerakan"* — language of the eastern wastes; "OST" means *enemy* or *intruder*.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Idra had taken the Wednesday watch because she did not like to eat meals with the others. She had been with the cell for eleven years and she was, most nights, the only one who asked whether Keloth slept. He did not sleep. She had stopped asking two years ago. When the man she had loved was killed in Neraka, Keloth had been the one who named him at the shrine. It had been a kindness. Idra still remembered it as one, even now, on the watch, alone, in a dry wayhouse in Abanasinia.*

## Manifest symptoms landed

- **Attribution drift (roster):** Vesk's name crossed out on the duty-roster. His name is being erased from the cell. The pattern of the workshop's central symptom — erasure of names — repeating.
- **Per-PC reception:** Aelric is the most likely to register this (a Solamnic reading the roster as an intelligence document). Document which PC picks it up.
