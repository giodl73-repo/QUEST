# Scene 03 — The Common Room (Stronghold Upper)

## Read-aloud

> The wayhouse was burned long ago — roof gone, timbers black-rimed with old soot and new ice. A stone hearth stands at the room's center, cold. An archway east leads to an open yard. Over the front lintel: carved letters, fresh-cut over older ones beneath. *KELOTH'S HAND.* Behind the newer word, older strokes are still legible: *GAVEK'S REST.*

## Features

- **The lintel inscription.** *KELOTH'S HAND* cut recently over *GAVEK'S REST*. History DC 12: Gavek's Rest was an Istari wayhouse on the pre-Cataclysm Istar-Neraka road, abandoned approximately 40 years ago when the Istari withdrew. Investigation DC 10: the newer carver used a different chisel — cruder; the older Istari work was masterwork. **The older name is being erased.** (Manifest symptom — attribution drift, same pattern as Aelwen's name in #0001 and the Nerakan carvings at Howl-Stones.)
- **The hearth.** Cold. Dragonfire-black soot in one layer and dry ash in another. **Grom, on approach:** if he runs a thumb along the inside-curve of the hearth-stone (signature move), he feels that this hearth was worked by a dwarf — Thorbardin-style inside-curve finishing. He did not expect that. Gavek's Rest, in its prime, had a dwarf on staff.
- **The archway east.** Open; rubble partially blocks the top. Leads to the stable yard (Scene 04).
- **The stair down.** In the northeast corner. Partially cleared of rubble; recent traffic. Descends to the Vault Approach (Scene 05).
- **The ruined roof.** Weather falls directly into this room. Old snowdrift in the northwest corner. Tracks: at least two Bone-Collectors came through here in the last hour. Investigation DC 12 reads the tracks.

## Encounter

None in this room, by default. The Bone-Collectors hold the lower level. *However:* if the party made noise at Howl-Stones (full combat without stealth, OR used fire/magic visible at distance), a **single Bone-Collector sentry** is stationed here and will engage or flee to warn the stronghold. Sentry = **1 × Bandit** (CR ⅛). If he flees, the stronghold is on **Alert** status for the rest of the adventure — all stronghold encounters gain +1 creature and guards are positioned defensively rather than on routine.

## Treasure

- Among the rubble: a **pre-Cataclysm Istari traveler's token** (1 gp silver; historical-value 20 gp to a scholar) — a coin-sized medallion stamped with a wayhouse-tree. Investigation DC 10 finds it.
- The old hearth, dug into, yields nothing (someone already searched).

## Connections

- East (archway): Stable Yard (Scene 04).
- Down (stair): Vault Approach (Scene 05).
- South (open, no door): the road outside.

## GM Notes

- **Do not signpost the well.** The well is in the stable yard and the party discovers it only on Scene 04's Investigation DC 13. If they take the stair straight down (natural choice), they take the frontal path and miss the stealth route. That's fine. Don't funnel them.
- **Grom's dwarf-work observation is free.** No roll needed. It is character-work; Grom knows inside-curve.
- **Gloss:** *"Istari"* — of Istar, the human religious empire that dominated the central continent in the late Age of Might. The Wayhouse is a relic of the Istari road network, abandoned decades ago.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *Gavek was a dwarf. He ran this wayhouse for eleven years, never in his own name — his name was on none of the Istari registers — because a dwarf running an Istari way-station during the later Kingpriest years was a quiet act of reconciliation that the Kingpriest would not have approved of. Gavek knew this. He ran the house anyway. When Istar fell, he was already back in the Kharolis holds, running a forge he never named after himself either. He would have recognized Grom.*

## Manifest symptoms landed

- **Attribution drift (landing):** Keloth's Hand over Gavek's Rest. Two-layer inscription. Grom's craft-reading recognizes the older dwarf-work. This is the same curse-pattern as #0001's Aelwen-over-uncredited — a name being taken from a place, replaced with a less-rightful name.
- **Per-PC reception (rubric v1.1):** Grom will most strongly receive this scene's atmospheric flag (dwarf-hearth). Document.
