# Scene 07 — The Cell-Block (Stronghold Lower)

## Read-aloud

> A narrow stone corridor, two oil-lamps high on the walls. Three cell-doors along the south wall. The first two: empty. The third, at the corridor's end: also empty — a wool blanket, a porridge bowl. A child was kept here. She has been moved.

## Features

- **The well-shaft (ceiling, west end).** If the party enters via the well from Scene 04, they drop down into this corridor's west end. A coil of rope already hangs from the ceiling — old, frayed, but functional. (Previous well-users from decades ago; not the Bone-Collectors.)
- **Three cells (south wall).** All empty. Each bears traces of prior occupancy — the cell-block has been used for years:
  - **Cell one.** A half-wiped chalk figure of a woman on the south wall, shoulder-high — the prior prisoner's attempted portrait, partially smudged by whoever came later. Investigation DC 12 to see it; DC 15 to read it as an attempt at someone loved.
  - **Cell two.** Tally-marks near the bolt, scratched with a fingernail or knife: sixty-three marks in groups of seven. A long-term prisoner counting weeks. Investigation DC 10.
  - **Cell three (Pella).** Held her until 15 minutes before the party arrived. Porridge bowl still warm (DC 10 Insight), blanket pulled back, a crayon broken on the floor — she did not come quietly. **A dark floor-stain** near the north wall — Investigation DC 13: old blood, years old, scrubbed but ingrained. Another prior prisoner. The cell-block is a record of them.
- **The iron door (east end).** Leads to the Reliquary Vault (Scene 06). Heavy; Keloth has the key. Same door as Scene 06's east exit — bi-directional.
- **The oil lamps.** Two, high on the wall. If broken, the oil will soak into the stone floor and any subsequent fire (a Cultist's torch, a Bone-Collector's lantern) can ignite the corridor (1d6 fire damage to anyone in it next round, then burns out). Environmental hazard worth flagging.
- **The corridor's west end.** A blank wall, except for the well-shaft overhead. Looks dead-end. Actually it is.

## Encounter

**If the party arrives via well (stealth path):** no encounter in this corridor. Proceed east to the Vault (Scene 06).

**If the party arrives via east door from Vault Approach (Scene 05):** same — no encounter here; the guard post was in the corridor (Scene 05).

**If the stronghold is on Alert:** one additional Cultist patrols this corridor. Routine combat (1 × Bandit, 25 XP).

## Treasure

- **Pella's crayon (broken).** Taken by Thera reflexively. 0 gp; immense sentimental value.
- **The oil lamps.** Full; could be taken for torch-fuel (2 sp each).
- **A small cache hidden in cell three's mattress** (Investigation DC 13 on the cot): a **silver-filigree finger-bone** — another cell-identifier like the one from the lieutenant at the Howl-Stones. 5 gp. Manifest: same moon-silver. Someone was held in this cell before Pella — not long ago. A person who did not leave.

## Connections

- East (iron door): Reliquary Vault (Scene 06).
- West (well-shaft, up): Stable Yard (Scene 04). Climb DC 10 with rope (already hangs); DC 12 without.

## GM Notes

- **Pella's broken crayon is the session's sharpest object.** Describe it specifically — red, snapped clean, not chewed — and let the players find out she broke it herself in frustration at being moved mid-drawing.
- **The cache in cell three is a weight.** Someone else was in this cell. Not long ago. The Bone-Collectors do not release prisoners. This cell-block has been used before. A thoughtful party will ask who.
- **Environmental fire hazard.** Do not foreground; let a clever player propose breaking the lamps.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

> *The prisoner before Pella was a woman in her fifties from a village west of Neraka. She had inherited her mother's ability to hear the names of the dead spoken on the wind. Keloth had wanted her to teach him. She had refused. She had lasted three months in that cell. She had not been a martyr about it; she had simply been stubborn about her mother's gift. When she died — old age, not violence — Keloth had been disappointed. He had taken her last minute anyway. Her name is on the third skull from the left in the Vault.*

## Manifest symptoms landed

- **Per-PC reception (rubric v1.1):** Thera → Pella's crayon (child-grief reinforcement). Grom → the cell-mattress cache (a prior prisoner he cannot mend). Document.
- **Secondary object:** the cell-cache silver-filigree bone connects the cell's grief-harvesting to people who are *not* soldiers — private individuals with abilities. This widens the moral stakes.
