# Scene 01 — Mensa's Waystation

## Read-aloud
> The waystation sits alone on the road, a low stone building with a squat chimney and a sign carved as a running horse. No smoke. No horses in the stable-yard. The door stands ajar. Two days of snow has drifted against it, undisturbed.

## Features

- **The door (ajar, unmoved).** Forced open from outside, at least two days ago. The snow has not been crossed since. **Investigation DC 10** reads this cleanly.
- **The common room (inside).** A hearth, cold. A table laid for one child's breakfast — porridge bowl, a small carved horse, a cup. The porridge frozen. *"Pella"* carved into the edge of the table in a child's hand with a small knife.
- **The second drawing.** Sitting on the table, beside the porridge: a crayon drawing **identical to the one Thera carries** — same waystation, same chimney-smoke-as-spiral, same three windows of uneven size, same block-letter signature *"for papa — pella."* Investigation DC 10 confirms same paper, same hand. Someone made Pella produce a duplicate and left it here.
- **The kitchen (inside door, left).** **Mensa's body.** Two days dead. A head-wound from behind; the kitchen knife still on the floor, clean. See `npcs/mensa.md` for her full write-up. Wisdom (Medicine) DC 12 tells: killed first, before whoever took Pella searched the house.
- **Pella's room (inside door, right).** A small cot. Blankets pulled back like a child rising in a hurry. A stuffed wolf missing one ear. A chalk-drawing of a woman (unlabeled — might be Adda, might be her mother, might be Mensa) on the wall beside the cot.
- **The fireplace.** Cold ash. Dug through by someone looking for something. A small iron box buried in the ash (**Investigation DC 13**) contains: 8 gp, a letter in Vesk's hand addressed to "P, when you are ten," and a pressed silver leaf (looks nothing like the Silver Rose's moon-silver; this is common silver).

## Encounter

None. Mensa is dead. The cell is long gone.

## Treasure

- **Vesk's letter to Pella** — sealed; unread. *"When you are ten"* inscribed on the outer fold. 0 gp; infinite weight. Thera will not open it until Pella is ten, unless the players decide otherwise.
- **8 gp** in the ash-box.
- **Pella's stuffed wolf** — Thera will pick this up without asking.
- **Pella's chalk drawing** — not taken; noted.

## Connections

- South: road back to Varduin.
- North: road to Howl-Stones pass (Scene 02).

## GM Notes

- **No funeral at the waystation.** The party has time pressure (Pella is the priority) and the cell's trail will fade in the snow. Grom may want to bless Mensa's body; this is a Reorx-rite beat — *"Thou'rt seen. Reorx keep thy forge warm."* One minute of in-fiction time. Forge-priest character-work.
- **The second drawing is the trap-hint.** A thoughtful player will realize: *they wanted us to know Pella was here. They made sure the drawing was on the table. They're expecting us.* This is the module telling the party, via a visible clue, what is happening — without requiring a check. A moment to let the table feel the trap tighten.
- **Gloss:** first mention of "Bone-Collectors" in DM narration. Keep it brief — *"a small death-cult out of Neraka, trade in artifacts of grief and loss"* — through a character who'd know (Kessa, or Aelric from Solamnic briefings).

### Memory Fragment (optional — read only if the party lingers)
> *Mensa, two days ago, had been singing. Pella did not often hear her aunt sing. Mensa had sung a song her own mother used to sing, in a voice Mensa did not know she had, and Pella had not understood the words but had known, without being told, that the song was about her mother. Pella fell asleep listening. When she woke, Mensa's singing had stopped.*

## Manifest symptoms landed at this scene

- **Orthographic drift (subtle):** the carving *"Pella"* in the table, and the crayon signature, are both in a child's block letters — but the second drawing's signature is *slightly different* from the first Thera has. One letter is reshaped. Investigation DC 15 catches that *someone else's hand* finished signing the second drawing. This is an early foreshadow for the manifest's forgery-attribution pattern.
- **Per-PC reception** (rubric v1.1): Thera will react strongest to the stuffed wolf (mother-grief trigger). Grom will react strongest to Mensa's body (mending he cannot do). Aelric will react strongest to the sealed letter (it mirrors his own letters to Varran). Kessa will read the chalk-drawing of the woman and wonder what grief-shaped this house. **Document per-PC reception in the session log.**
