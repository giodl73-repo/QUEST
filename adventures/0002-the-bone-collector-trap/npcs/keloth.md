# Keloth of Neraka, Bone-Priest

## Appearance

Tall, thin, black hair gone iron-gray at the temples. Fifty years old, moves like forty. No armor — he wears a soft charcoal-wool robe, unadorned, belted with plain leather. A moon-silver filigree bracelet on his right wrist (a small reliquary; holds his own mother's last minute, which he refuses to attune). Voice is low, even, a little tired. Looks you in the eye when he talks.

A Nerakan by birth, not by ideology. He came to the grief-priesthood through loss, not conviction. That is important.

## Background

Born in a Nerakan hill-village in PC 70. Lost his wife and two sons in a plague-year when he was thirty-two. The grief drove him into the fringe-priesthoods that operate at the edges of Nerakan polity — practitioners of ritual that the Dragonarmies-yet-to-come will outlaw, but which in the late Age of Might move relatively freely in the eastern wastes. He apprenticed to an old man named Tarch who taught him that grief could be harvested, concentrated, and held. Tarch died eleven years ago; Keloth inherited Tarch's methods and most of his moon-silver.

He has led this cell for nine years. Eleven members currently; five including himself at Gavek's Rest at session time. The others (seven) are west at Varduin, feinting. Keloth expected the party to come after Pella; he expected Varduin to be distracted.

He is a *priest* in the true sense. He believes the cell's work is a form of mercy — that preserving a dying person's last minute gives their grief a place to live outside their bodies, where it cannot poison the living. He thinks the Silver Rose of Vaenor and similar artifacts are *tools of this mercy*, mis-used by fools like the Rose Council who try to lock them away. He would not describe himself as evil. He is wrong about many things. He is not a villain who sneers.

## What he wants this session

- **Primary:** the Varran-reliquary (which he has) attuned by Varran's brother Aelric (which would be a ritual completion — the priest in Keloth believes this is what the reliquary was *made for*, and he has waited twenty years to find Varran's brother).
- **Secondary:** the cell's continued operation. He needs the Rose Council to leave him alone.
- **Tertiary:** Pella is leverage, not an objective. He does not want to harm her. He will, if the party forces him into a corner, but he will not enjoy it.

## How the DM plays him

**In the opening conversation at the altar (Scene 06 read-aloud):** he is calm, patient, direct. He does not threaten. He offers the trade plainly and waits. He does not raise his voice.

**If the party refuses and combat begins:** Round 1 he casts *Shield of Faith* on himself. Round 2 *Command* on the heaviest hitter (*"Halt"*). Round 3+ he will try to close for *Inflict Wounds* (3d10 necrotic, touch). He does not spray cantrips for flavor; he conserves slots.

**At half HP (16 or below):** drops to his knees, arms spread, and **restates the offer**: *"One minute. The child. We are done."* He means it. If accepted, he hands the key to Aelric, releases Pella, and walks out through the rear tunnel (not fighting back). He considers this a completed ritual, not a defeat.

**Morally complex move:** if the party attacks him after he surrenders, he does not fight back. He says, plainly: *"You are choosing your rage over your brother's minute and the child's walk. I will grieve that, but I will not stop you."* He does not cast. He will die without defending himself. This is a DM gut-check for the party.

**If the party accepts the trade:** he is gracious. He leaves with his five cell-members, his bracelet-reliquary, and his plans undisturbed. He will appear again in a later adventure. He takes neither the Varran-reliquary nor any of the shelf-reliquaries with him on exit — "those are bought with the child."

## Voice tags

- **Soft-voiced.** Never shouts.
- **Uses Nerakan formal register** — calls his own cultists by their full names ("Idra of the Ninth House, please stand to my right"). Gives the party the same respect. Calls Aelric "Sir Knight" without irony.
- **Never uses the word "enemy."** Uses "opposed" or "unaligned."
- **Pauses often before he speaks.** Chooses words.

## The moon-silver bracelet

His bracelet is a reliquary holding his own mother's last minute. Attunement available; Keloth has never attuned. He cannot bring himself to, and he will not let anyone else do so. If the party takes the bracelet from his body (combat loot), they can attune it; the cost is all their memories of *their* mother, and the payoff is the minute of Keloth's mother's death in her own voice. This is functionally useless to the party but reveals, if attuned, that Keloth's mother died forgiving him for an argument he still blames himself for. Future-adventure fuel.

## Secret

Keloth is not a good man, but he is not the cell's worst member. **Idra** (his lieutenant) is. She has been quietly taking reliquaries from the shelf and using them to recruit. Keloth suspects this; he has not confronted her. If the party kills Keloth and leaves Idra alive, Idra succeeds him and the cell becomes worse. If the party kills Keloth AND Idra, the cell fragments (the seven at Varduin will scatter). If the party accepts Keloth's trade, the cell continues as it is — Keloth constrains Idra, the work goes on.

**The party's choice matters downstream.** Keloth-alive with trade = stable cell under uneasy truce. Keloth-dead, Idra-alive = worse cell. Keloth-dead, Idra-dead = dispersed cell. DM tracks this in the session log for #0003 continuity.
