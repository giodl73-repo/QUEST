# Pella Vesk-daughter, age 9

## Appearance

Small for her age, but not thin. Brown hair in two uneven braids she did herself. A burn-scar across the pad of her left thumb — she touched the stove at seven and learned not to. Wears a linen smock dyed the color of bruised plums (Nerakan dye; Vesk sent it to Mensa two years ago and Mensa made her the smock). Dirty; bruised on one cheek; the bruise is two days old.

## Background

Born PC 29 in a waystation north of Neraka. Her mother died of fever when Pella was four; Pella barely remembers her, though she has a chalk-drawing she did at six that she tells people is her mother. Her father Vesk was away more than he was home — tomb-work, he called it. She called him Papa. He had not been home in three years when he died three weeks ago in a tomb called the Rose Cairn. Pella does not know this. She knows her father said he would come back. She believes he will. This belief is structural for her.

Mensa, her aunt, raised her since age five. Mensa was not warm but was competent. She taught Pella to read (Nerakan common-letters), to cook a thin porridge, to tie her own shoes, and to sign her name. Pella can draw well for her age. She has been drawing waystations and Papa's face since she was six.

## Voice & manner

- Does not cry easily. Has not cried during her capture.
- Asks questions. Specific, direct ones. *"Are you a Solamnic? Papa said Solamnic knights had silver on their collars."*
- Fierce when cornered. Kicked a Bone-Collector hard in the shin at the waystation. Bit Keloth's wrist in the cell-block. Still has a small mouthful of Keloth-blood on her teeth when the party finds her.
- Pronounces her own name *PELL-uh*, not *pay-LAH*. Important if any PC addresses her.
- Tired. Not broken.

## What she knows

- Her father Vesk is coming back. (She is wrong; Vesk died in the Rose Cairn three weeks ago. She does not know.)
- Mensa is dead. (She saw them kill Mensa. She has not cried about this but she has thought about it constantly.)
- Her papa drew her a small map once of where his work took him. It is in her pocket. The map shows a compass-point east and a crude squirrel (inside-joke between them). She has held this map for two days.
- The tall Bone-Collector is named Keloth. She decided to remember it. She will tell the party if they ask — and will tell them anyway if they don't.

## How the DM plays her

**Decision order (when the DM makes her choose):**
1. **Do not seem weak in front of these people.** Pella's stubbornness is her armor. She WILL NOT cry on cue.
2. **Find out about Papa.** If a PC has any information, she will pry until she gets it. She has been trained by Mensa that you get food by asking for it.
3. **Help the people helping her.** She will not be a helpless package if she can help it — she'll point, she'll warn, she'll kick a cultist in the knee if given an opening. She has roll +0 on everything (child); she can still attempt things.
4. **Protect the drawings.** If in her possession, the crayon-drawing (second copy, seized by the party from Mensa's waystation) is a thing she will reach for.

**Voice tags:**
- Short sentences. *"I can walk."* *"Papa told me about you."* *"That man is called Keloth."*
- Direct eye contact. Children who have been hit learn to read faces fast; Pella reads adult faces faster than the PCs realize.
- One Nerakan word used without glossing it: *"Chal."* (It means *real*. She uses it to confirm things. *"You are chal knights? Papa said only chal knights come from Solamnia."*)

## The truth about Vesk

If the party tells her her father is dead: she absorbs it without visible reaction. The DM should not narrate tears. Instead, narrate silence, and ask the player-in-scene what they see in Pella's face. This is a moment of atmospheric density the module should not pre-write. She will cry LATER, possibly during travel home, possibly days after, possibly never in the players' presence.

If the party **does not** tell her: she will continue to expect her father. This will be painful. The party has to decide what Pella gets to know and when.

## Stat block (if anyone tries to attack her)

AC 10 · HP 6 (child; 2d6-2) · Speed 25 ft.
Str 6, Dex 11, Con 8, Int 10, Wis 11, Cha 10.
**Child's bite** +0 to hit, 1 piercing (she bit Keloth this way). No other attacks. Not a combatant.

## Playstyle role in the adventure

- **Scene 06 (Vault climax):** she is present; a human presence in the moral calculation. The DM must play her visibly — breathing, watching, sometimes trying to speak. The trade Keloth offers is easier if she is a token and harder if she is a person.
- **The Aelric-recognition beat (rev 2 commitment per Stuart).** When Pella sees Aelric for the first time in the Vault, she asks *"Are you the knight my papa said would come?"* **The DM MUST narrate her interior as certainty, not hope.** She has already decided the answer is yes. Her question is not asking; it is naming. She is telling Aelric who he is going to be for her. The module commits to this: Pella does not wonder, she knows. A child who has been through two days of captivity has earned the right to decide who her rescuer is before they have proven it. The DM should not soften this with maybe or perhaps.
- **Post-session:** the party has to decide what to do with her. Options:
  - Take her to Varduin (Thera's hometown; good-aligned option; seeds a longer arc).
  - Find her remaining extended family (Vesk had no other siblings; there are cousins in Neraka, but delivering her to Neraka is a different adventure).
  - Foster with Brother Laen's mother in Varduin (Laen's mother lives alone; this is canonically an option).
  - Ask her what she wants (the hardest option for the DM; Pella may choose "I go with you" which changes the party composition).

## Secrets

- **Pella does not yet know her mother's name.** Mensa never told her. "Your mother" is all Mensa ever said. The name is in Mensa's notebook at the waystation. A party that takes the notebook (Investigation DC 13 at the waystation, which no one rolled against) learns her mother's name: **Reya**. When to tell Pella? DM's discretion; this is future-adventure material.

## GM Notes

- **Do not play her precociously.** Children say wise things sometimes and ordinary things usually. Pella is mostly an ordinary child in an extraordinary situation. The wisdom should be occasional, not relentless.
- **The map in her pocket is a key for #0003.** Vesk's sketchy east-and-squirrel map points to a Nerakan ruin the Bone-Collectors have not yet dismantled — Vesk was going to retrieve something there and did not. It is a hook, not a puzzle this session.
