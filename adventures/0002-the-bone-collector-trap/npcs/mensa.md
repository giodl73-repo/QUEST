# Mensa Vesk-sister, Waystation-keeper (PC 68 — PC 20)

## Appearance (as the party finds her)

Fifty-two at death. Narrow-faced, bone-structured woman with Vesk's same sharp chin. Gray-streaked dark hair pulled back. Wearing a wool apron over a plain dress, still dusted with flour from the morning's bread. Killed from behind with a single blow — the Bone-Collectors' knife is still on the floor, clean because they wiped it on her apron. Her body was laid face-down in the kitchen, hands not arranged, left where she fell.

A thoughtful character observing the body (Wisdom Medicine DC 12) concludes: killed quickly, without struggle, by someone standing behind her while she kneaded. She did not know they were there until she did.

## Background

Vesk's older sister, by five years. Raised in the same Nerakan hill-village; left Neraka at twenty when a road-quarrel she was not party to ended with three men dead and Mensa, a witness, unwelcome. She walked west across the Plains of Dergoth and stopped when she ran out of road in a pine-belt that had an empty waystation for sale. She bought it with a single silver-piece and a promise to the previous owner's widow that she would never sell to a Nerakan. The widow had been specific about that. Mensa had kept the promise for thirty years.

She ran the waystation alone, mostly. Vesk visited when his tomb-work brought him north-and-west; he brought Pella when Pella's mother died. Mensa did not particularly want a child. She took her anyway. She taught Pella to read, to cook, to sign her name. She was not demonstrative but was reliable.

She was not a warm woman. She was fair. Pella understood this without being told.

## What she was doing when she died

She was kneading rye-and-winter-savory bread, the same kind her brother Vesk preferred. She was going to send a loaf south with the next caravan, to an address in Abanasinia that she had kept for three years and had never sent anything to — she had been building up to it. The address was **Varduin-by-the-Pines, care of the way-house steward, to be held for V— Vesk, when he next passes through.** The loaf was still on the counter when the Bone-Collectors came in through the back door.

A party member investigating the kitchen (Investigation DC 12) will find this address written on a scrap, tucked under the bread's rising-cloth. It is the lever for a gut-punch: Vesk had been three weeks dead, and his sister was kneading a loaf to send him, not knowing.

## What she knew

- That her brother was in "tomb work" of a kind Mensa understood to be dangerous. She did not approve but did not intervene.
- That Pella was her obligation. She treated this obligation with competence.
- That the Nerakan Bone-Collectors were a shadow over Pella's existence. She kept a short-sword under the counter. She did not reach it in time.
- One additional thing the party can discover: in the small locked iron-box in the kitchen hearth (Investigation DC 13), along with 8 gp and Vesk's sealed letter to Pella, **Mensa's notebook** — which contains her own names-of-the-dead, written in the Nerakan mother-tongue:
  - Her mother (**Oneva**)
  - Her father (**Kalt**)
  - Pella's mother (**Reya**) — the name Mensa never told Pella
  - Her brother (**Vesk**) — the entry not yet written; Mensa was going to write it if she ever got the news, which she did not
  - Her own planned entry (blank) — she had pre-ruled the line for herself and written the date-field empty

## Role in the session

- **Scene 01 (Waystation)** — her body is the discovery that anchors Pella's kidnapping. The room treats her with respect: Grom may want to bless her; the party may spend a minute on her before pushing north. Do not rush this.
- **After the session** — the party must decide what to do with her body. The competent play is: bury her at the waystation under the pines before heading north (half-hour in-fiction; no mechanical cost; atmospheric weight). A less competent play is to leave her for the party to handle on return, which forces the return trip to deal with a two-week corpse and a waystation that has been searched by the Bone-Collectors. The DM should foreground neither option; let the party choose.

## Secrets

- **The waystation's original widow** — the one who sold Mensa the house thirty years ago with the promise about never selling to a Nerakan — was a retired Solamnic armswoman named **Jannet of Crownhold**. Same surname as Aelric's family seat. Possibly a great-great-aunt; possibly coincidence. Jannet is long dead. Mensa kept her portrait on the kitchen wall (a small cameo, no name-plate). The party can discover this (Investigation DC 15 in the kitchen). **Seeds a thread: Aelric's family had a connection to this waystation before his birth.**

## GM Notes

- **Play Mensa's absence as presence.** The kitchen is as she left it. The bread is on the counter. The fire was lit and is now cold. The waystation exists because of what she did and for as long as anyone remembers her. A pass of quiet in the kitchen, as Grom might recommend, is appropriate.
- **Pella's mother's name (Reya)** is in Mensa's notebook. The party can give it to Pella — or withhold it. This is character work.
- **Jannet of Crownhold** is a hook for #0003 or later, not this session. Do not over-play it.
