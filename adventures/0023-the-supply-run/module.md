## Setting & Background

The party leaves the walls for the first time to find the overdue supply cart, discovers it was ambushed by someone who is not the Reconstitution, and brings back enough to keep Maret Stave's triage point running — while a captured Reconstitution soldier says something that doesn't make sense yet.

**The Secondary Pass** — a narrow mountain trail that branches from the main road two miles south of Thorngate Keep, used for supply runs when the primary approach is watched. Day 8 of the siege. The cart left the supply depot at Irathon Waystation four days ago and has not arrived. Tomek's scout went to look two days ago and has not returned either.

The secondary pass winds through three terrain zones: the **low trail** (open, visible from above), the **choke point** (a narrows between two rock faces, ideal ambush ground), and the **upper ledge** (a shelf above the choke, used by watchers and archers). The cart is at the choke point, halted. The ambush happened there.

**The ambushers are not the Reconstitution.** Their tracks are wrong — lighter boots, no Solamnic-pattern kit, a different fire style. Someone else hit the cart before the Reconstitution siege line was even established. Someone who knew the route.

- **Party HP:** All four at full (28/28, 24/24, 28/28, 17/17) — S1 ended with 0 damage.
- **Supply Die:** d10 (degraded from d12 post-S1).
- **Sava spell slots:** Full (4×L1, 2×L2) — 0 used in S1.
- **Talis spell slots:** 2×L1 (used 1 for Hunter's Mark in S1).
- **Caelith Portent:** [6, 7] carried forward (no long rest between sessions).
- **S2 healing potions available (from garrison, d10 tier):** 5 (rolled from Supply Die).

**Design intent:** This is the first session where the party takes real damage. The ambush site combat should cost 15-25% of party HP total. Sava should need to spend at least 1 slot — ideally 2 — while still having enough in reserve to satisfy Maret's token condition (which fires on return, based on S1 conservation, not S2).

Before dawn briefing, Maret finds Sava at the healer's station. She says quietly:

*"The cart is four days late. I have five days of materials left. If you go out and find it, I don't need to tell the Warden how close we are."*

She is not asking Sava to lie to Asholt. She is asking her to solve it before he needs to know.

Sava goes. The others come because the party goes together now — they haven't discussed this explicitly, but by the third day in a siege you know who you leave through the postern with.

This is Sava's session. The supply run is a healer's errand — she is the one who understands what's at stake if the cart is lost. The combat at the ambush site will push her toward spending slots she has been conserving. The Maret token fires on return. The grief resonance (Elara, delayed, too late) is present throughout — the whole session is a version of the story Sava doesn't want to repeat.

**Sava's arc beat for this session:** She makes at least one decision to spend a healing slot on the supply run — not because the tactical situation demands it, but because she refuses to let the delay pattern repeat. She arrives on time. She has enough. She chooses to spend it.

### Scene 1 — The Postern (Room 01)

The party leaves before dawn. Tomek sees them go. He does not stop them. He does not order them back. He watches until they are through the postern.

**DM note:** Tomek's manner on their exit — noticing it, filing it, saying nothing — is a continuation of the S1 behavioral-stance pattern. He is still measuring. This is scene-setting, not a beat.

Passive Perception DC 12: the secondary pass branches left from the main road after a half-mile. The main road is watched from the ridge. The branch is in shadow.

### Scene 2 — The Low Trail (Room 02)

**Read-aloud:**
> The trail narrows between two cliff faces fifty feet high. Ahead: the sound of nothing. No cart wheels. No mule. The trail has been used recently — there are boot-prints going in that are not Reconstitution-pattern kit.

Talis leads. This is her terrain.

**DC 12 Survival (Talis, signature move: terrain-first):** The tracks are lighter than Reconstitution soldiers'. Shorter stride. Different boot construction — no military pattern. Two, maybe three people. The cart went in ahead of them.

**DC 14 Survival (any PC):** The tracks are recent — two, maybe three days old. Before the Reconstitution tightened the siege line. Whoever this was, they hit the cart before the siege was a factor.

**Pressure:** The low trail is exposed. A Reconstitution patrol on the northeast ridge could spot movement here. DC 14 Stealth (group check) to move without drawing fire. Failure: one arrow, +4 to hit, 1d8+2 — random target, from above. The patrol moves on regardless.

### Scene 3 — The Choke Point (Room 03, primary encounter)

**Read-aloud:**
> The trail pinches between two rock faces. Ahead: the cart, stopped. One wheel cracked. The mule is gone. Three supply crates — one overturned, two intact. No bodies. No blood. Whatever happened here was controlled.

The ambushers did not loot the crates. They took the mule and left the cart. This was not a robbery. This was a disruption.

**The remaining threat:** Two of the ambushers are still here, watching the cart. They expected the garrison to send a small patrol. They are not Reconstitution; they are **Reconstitution-adjacent** — irregular contractors, paid to slow Thorngate's resupply without leaving Reconstitution evidence. They have orders not to kill garrison soldiers if avoidable. They will fight if cornered; they will run if given a clear path out.

**Encounter:** See `encounters/ambush-remnant.md`. Designed to cost HP — first real combat damage of the campaign.

**The cart:** Two intact crates. Enough healing materials for 8 more days (doubles Maret's window). One crate has specific contents Maret asked for. The cracked wheel takes 20 minutes to repair (Athletics DC 12 with tools from the cart's own repair kit — which is still here).

**Tomek's scout:** Slumped against the north rock face. Alive — unconscious, mild concussion, 1 HP remaining. The ambushers hit him when he arrived and left him. He has been here for two days. He will recover.

### Scene 4 — The Upper Ledge (Room 04, optional)

If the party climbs to the upper ledge (Athletics DC 12):

- A fire ring, cold. Used for watching. Provisions for three days.
- A **Reconstitution prisoner** — one of the ambushers who didn't run and couldn't fight. He is not an irregular; he is a regular soldier who was here as a liaison. He saw the ambush, tried to stop it (he has orders to leave the garrison alone; this was not his operation), and was left behind by the irregulars.

**H1 partial delivery (this scene):** When the party shows him the Thorngate garrison insignia (on a shield, a collar, or just by asking what he knows about the Keep):

He says: *"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."*

He will not elaborate in front of the full party. If Caelith speaks to him alone (his institutional-lens heuristic fires: *"what does the Reconstitution actually want here?"*), he gives one more sentence: *"She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass."*

**Passive Perception DC 12:** His reaction when he sees the insignia is not what you'd expect from a soldier looking at the enemy — it's something closer to recognition. Not of the device itself. Of what it represents to someone.

### Scene 5 — Return and Maret's Token (Room 05)

The party brings back the cart (repaired wheel, two intact crates, Tomek's scout on the cart-bed). They come through the postern before noon.

Maret is at the station. She sees the crates. She looks at Sava.

She has been counting this since she asked. She already knows — not from the crates but from the fact that Sava came back, with enough, before the six-day window closed.

She gives Sava her token. She says: *"You count before you spend. I've not seen that in someone from outside."*

It comes from the cord under her healer's apron — the same cord described in her NPC file. No ceremony.

**Maret's token:** DELIVERED (Sava; S2 arc-completion for Maret Irr).

**DM note:** The token condition fires on the combination of S1 (0 slots) + S2 (returned with supplies). Sava may have spent slots in S2 combat — that's correct and expected. The token is about the pattern across both sessions, not this session alone.

## Loop

The upper ledge (04) can be accessed from the choke point (03) and provides an elevated view of the full secondary pass, including back toward the junction. A party that climbs up can see whether anyone approaches from either direction — tactical intelligence that rewards the climb.

## Vertical element

Room 03 (Choke Point) to Room 04 (Upper Ledge): 30-foot climb, Athletics DC 12. The upper ledge is 30 feet above the trail floor.

## Two paths to the supply cart (Room 03)

- **Path A (direct):** Postern → Low Trail Junction → Low Trail → Choke Point
- **Path B (upper approach):** Party could attempt to reach the upper ledge first (if they spotted it from the trail), then descend into the choke point from above — Athletics DC 14 to descend safely (lower DC for climb, higher for controlled descent). Arriving from above gives Surprise on the ambushers (they are watching the trail, not the ledge).

## Shortcut (unlocks through play)

If the party approaches via Path B (upper ledge first), they can descend with Surprise. The shortcut is earned by Talis noticing the fire ring's cold smoke from the trail (passive DC 14) and the party taking the climb before entering the choke point.

## Scale

One section = approximately 5–10 minutes of travel. Total round-trip from Thorngate: ~3 hours (1 hour out, 30 min at site, 1 hour back with cart — the cracked wheel slows return).

### Scene 2 — Low Trail Junction & Trail

> The road branches left into shadow. The main road continues south, visible, exposed. The branch is older — narrower, stone underfoot rather than gravel. Boot-prints in the trail dust, going in. Not Solamnic-pattern kit. Lighter boots. Different stride.

#### Features

- **Track analysis (Talis's scene):**
  - **DC 12 Survival (Talis, auto with signature move "read the ground"):** The tracks are not Reconstitution pattern — lighter boots, shorter stride, no military spacing. Two to three individuals. The cart's wheel-tracks go ahead of them.
  - **DC 14 Survival (any PC):** The tracks are 2-3 days old. They predate the Reconstitution's current siege-line tightening. Whoever hit the cart arrived before the siege was at full strength.
  - **DC 16 Survival (Talis only, takes a minute):** The lighter boots have a specific sole-wear pattern — high-arch, soft leather, the kind used by mountain guides or mercenary scouts. These are professionals, not regulars.

- **Cold smoke (shortcut trigger):**
  - **Passive Perception DC 14 (from the junction):** A faint smell of old smoke from above — a fire ring on a ledge, cold now. If noticed, the party can attempt to reach the upper ledge (Room 04) before entering the choke point.
  - If Talis makes DC 14 passive (her passive is 16): she spots it automatically. She says: *"There was a watch-fire up there. Recent. Cold."* Whether to climb before entering the choke is a party decision.

- **Exposure:** The low trail is visible from the northeast ridge. Anyone moving along it in daylight can be observed by the Reconstitution. This does not trigger combat — the Reconstitution's standing orders distinguish between garrison resupply parties and military threats — but it is visible. Note for the handoff: the Reconstitution knows the party made a supply run.

#### Encounter

None (approach/investigation scene). The pressure comes from the tracks and the exposure, not combat.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections

- **North (main road):** back to 01 Postern Exit
- **South (trail continues):** to 03 Choke Point
- **Up (cold smoke sighted, Athletics DC 12):** to 04 Upper Ledge (shortcut — approach choke from above with Surprise)

#### GM Notes

This is Talis's first solo-spotlight scene in the campaign. Her decision order fires: *terrain first* → *state what she sees, do not wait to be asked*. She reads the tracks, she reports the result, she moves.

Her voice-tag fires: "two, maybe three. Forty-eight hours old. Light boots — not military." She does not editorialize. She gives the read.

The cold smoke is the shortcut cue. If Talis sees it and the party climbs to Room 04 before the choke: they approach the ambushers from above with Surprise in combat. If they miss it: they enter the choke at ground level, no Surprise.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party pauses at the junction)*

### Scene 3 — The Choke Point

> The trail pinches between two rock faces. Cart stopped, one wheel cracked, mule gone. Two crates intact under canvas; one overturned, empty. No bodies. No blood. A man slumped against the north rock face: garrison kit, breathing. The ambush happened here. The people who did it are still watching.

#### Features

- **The cart:** Supply cart, one cracked wheel. Two crates intact (enough healing materials for 8 days). The empty crate held dry goods — not Maret's materials. Repair kit is under the driver's seat: Athletics DC 12 + 20 minutes to fix the wheel with the kit, or DC 16 without.

- **Tomek's scout (Aldric, garrison runner):** Slumped against the north rock face. 1 HP. Unconscious from a blow to the head, two days ago. He rode ahead of the cart, arrived, was struck before he could call out, and left alive. He will wake if stabilized (Spare the Dying; auto) and can speak within 10 minutes. He confirms: two people hit him from above, from the ledge. They were not in Solamnic kit.

- **Cover:** The choke point has 3/4 cover on both sides (rock faces). The ambushers have positioned at the south end of the choke — the exit — between the party and the road. If the party entered from above (Room 04 Surprise route), this positioning is revealed before combat; if they entered ground-level, they walk into it.

- **Ambusher positions:** Two mercenary scouts (stat blocks in encounters/ambush-remnant.md) at the south end of the choke. They expected a small patrol. They have orders not to kill garrison soldiers. They will fight defensively and attempt to disengage south toward Irathon Road if losing.

#### Encounter

**Primary combat encounter:** `encounters/ambush-remnant.md`. Two mercenary scouts. Medium difficulty. Designed to cost HP — first real damage of the campaign.

#### Treasure

- Two intact supply crates (Maret's healing materials — 8 days' worth; the party's mission objective)
- Scout's saddlebag (Aldric's): intact, includes a Rose Council correspondence packet (3 sealed letters — cannot be opened without Asholt's authorization; adds to the "Rose Council isn't responding" thread)
- Mercenary's belt pouch if defeated: 14 gp, a token with an unknown device (the contractor's payment marker — plants Hessa investigation seed for 0024)

#### Connections

- **North (trail, cold wind from the open pass):** back to 02 Low Trail — the way in; air moves from this direction; smells of open mountain
- **Up (rock face, north wall, handholds visible):** to 04 Upper Ledge — Athletics DC 12; ledge visible from the choke floor as a stone shelf catching the early light
- **South (Irathon Road, mule tracks in the dust):** to 05 Irathon Road — ambushers' escape route; the tracks are clearly marked; the road widens thirty feet ahead

#### GM Notes

**If party came from above (Room 04 first, Surprise route):** The ambushers are visible before combat begins; Surprise round available; combat starts with the party positioned above the ambushers (+2 to ranged attacks from height). The fight is easier.

**If party came ground-level:** No Surprise; normal initiative. The combat is tighter. This is designed to cost more HP.

### Scene 4 — Upper Ledge

> A stone shelf thirty feet above the trail. Cold fire ring, three days of provisions, sightline down the full pass. A man is here: garrison build, Reconstitution's grey-field device on his collar. He is not moving to fight. He is watching you decide whether he is.

A party here can see into the choke point (Room 03) below — including ambusher positions — before descending. **If the party reaches this room before Room 03:** they can plan the approach to the choke point with full tactical knowledge. Combat in Room 03 begins with Surprise.

- **The Reconstitution regular (Pren — see npcs/pren-reconstitution.md):** A Reconstitution regular soldier, left behind when the mercenaries fled north. He had orders to observe only — the contractor operation was not his. He tried to stop the cart ambush (he has standing orders: garrison resupply parties are not legitimate military targets). The irregulars ignored him. He is now sitting on a ledge with nowhere to go.

  His gear is intact. He is not bound. He chose to stay rather than run.

  **H1 partial delivery scene:**
  When the party shows him the Thorngate garrison insignia (on a shield, collar, or by asking what he knows about the Keep):
  
  He says: *"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."*
  
  He will not say more in front of the full party. If Caelith speaks to him alone (his institutional-lens heuristic: *"what does the Reconstitution actually want here?"*) — passive Insight DC 13 on Pren to see he wants to say more — he gives one additional sentence:
  
  *"She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass."*

#### Encounter

Social (Pren). What the party does with him is a choice:
- Take him as a prisoner back to Thorngate → Tomek receives him professionally; Asholt questions him; information available in S3
- Leave him on the ledge with his provisions → he will be gone by morning; the intelligence dies with the encounter
- Release him formally (Caelith's institutional framing: give him a 4-hour head start) → he is not the enemy; he tried to stop the ambush; he goes

Document the party's choice in the handoff. It shapes what Tomek thinks of them.

#### Treasure

None (Pren's kit is garrison-standard; party should not take it).

#### Connections

- **Down (Athletics DC 12, controlled descent):** to 03 Choke Point — delivers Surprise on ambushers if descending before combat

#### GM Notes

**If party climbs here before Room 03:** They see the ambushers' positions in the choke point, find Pren, and can descend with Surprise. The session's tactical challenge is reduced but the narrative gain (Pren, H1 partial delivery) is significant.

**If party goes to Room 03 first:** They encounter Pren after the combat. He comes down when he hears the fighting stop. He approaches with his hands visible.

**Pren's loyalty:** He is not a defector. He followed his orders (observe, do not engage garrison). The contractor operation violated his understanding of his command. He is not angry at the garrison. He is frustrated with the contractors.

**What "she came back for something that wasn't finished" means:** This is Saren Coldforge — Pren doesn't know her name but knows his orders come from a commander with personal history at Thorngate. This is H1's second layer: the grievance is not strategic, it's personal. The pass is not the point. *She* is the point.

### Memory Fragment *(read if party lingers on the ledge)*

> The fire ring is the size of a single person's comfort. Whoever sat here sat here alone, watching something they were ordered to watch but perhaps not ordered to feel. The provisions are field-standard and exactly right for three days. Someone packed this carefully before they sent Pren here.

## Overview

Two mercenary scouts who ambushed the supply cart are still watching it, waiting for the garrison to send a recovery patrol so they can report completion. They did not expect four people, including a wizard on high ground (if the party took the upper ledge route). They have orders not to kill garrison soldiers — this was a disruption operation, not an assassination. They will fight defensively and attempt to disengage south if losing.

**Design intent:** First session of real HP damage. Party should take 15-25% total HP. Sava spends 1-2 slots. The ambushers are professionals (not thugs) — they fight clean and retreat when the math is wrong. No deaths required; capture or retreat are both valid outcomes.

## Tactical notes

**Scout B's ambush position:** If not spotted before combat (DC 17 Perception), Scout B attacks from hidden position in Round 1 with Advantage on attack roll (hidden attacker). After attacking, he is revealed.

**The cart as cover:** Scout A will use the cart as 3/4 cover (+5 AC) while firing with the shortbow. Moving around the cart to engage Scout A in melee requires spending movement to circle the cart (additional 10 ft. of movement on approach). This is where Talis's high-ground position matters — she can fire over the cart without the cover penalty.

**Choke point geometry:** The pass is 15 feet wide at the narrowest. Two combatants can fight side-by-side; a third must be behind. This means the party cannot all engage simultaneously in melee — Davan at front, Sava back, Talis on the ledge above (if she climbed), Caelith in the rear. The geometry creates natural action-economy pressure.

**Sava's healing decision:** Scout B will prioritize Sava if he can reach her (she is the visible healer). An attack on Sava creates the scenario where Sava must choose to spend a healing slot on herself or on Davan if he takes damage. This is the designed moment for her arc beat this session.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.
