# C4-S2 LOG — The Supply Run

## Session Summary

Maret found Sava before dawn and said she had five days of healing materials left, and she didn't want Asholt to know. The party left through the postern. On the low trail, Talis spotted cold smoke from a ledge above the choke point and the party climbed — or most of them did. At the upper ledge they found a Reconstitution regular named Pren who had tried to stop the cart ambush and stayed. Caelith spoke with him alone. They descended with Surprise and drove off two mercenary scouts in two rounds. Davan took five damage — the campaign's first real HP cost. Sava found Aldric alive at one hit point and spent a spell slot without being asked to. The scouts escaped south. Caelith released Pren with a head start. The cart's wheel was repaired. The party came back through the postern before noon. Maret gave Sava her token, healer to healer.

### Scene 2.1 — Before Dawn (Room 01, Postern Exit)

**Read-aloud:**
> Before dawn. The postern opens onto the pass-side, the safe direction, the side the Reconstitution is not watching. Cold air. Stars still visible. The main road south is a darker line through grey stone. Tomek is on the central wall above you. He watches until you are through. He does not say anything.

Maret found Sava at the healer's station before the garrison woke for morning rotation. She handed her the ledger — six days, crossed to five — and said: *"The cart is four days late. I have five days of materials left. If you go out and find it, I don't need to tell the Warden how close we are."*

She was not asking Sava to lie. She was asking Sava to solve it.

Sava took the ledger and looked at the count. *Five days.* She did not look up. She said: *"Who else knows?"*

### Scene 2.4 — The Upper Ledge: Pren (Room 04)

**Read-aloud:**
> A stone shelf thirty feet above the trail. Cold fire ring, three days of provisions, sightline down the full pass. A man is here: garrison build, Reconstitution's grey-field device on his collar. He is not moving to fight. He is watching you decide whether he is.

Pren does not stand. He keeps his hands visible. He looks at Sava's Mishakal symbol first, then at Davan's garrison kit, then at Caelith.

Davan: *"On your feet."*

Pren stands. His hands stay visible.

Sava shows him her collar — the Thorngate garrison insignia that Maret had given her on arrival as a visiting healer identifier.

Pren's expression changes. Not fear. Recognition.

*"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."*

He stops. He will not say more in front of all three of them.

**🎲 Caelith Insight (Pren has more, DC 13)** — `1d20+3` → rolls=[10] mod=+3 **total=13 (success, exactly)**

Pren is quiet for three seconds. Then:

*"She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass."*

He will not say "she." He says "the commander." He does not know her name.

**H1 partial delivered:** *"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."* + *"She came back for something that wasn't finished."* Both lines logged. Caelith: *"I know."* He turns back to the ledge edge. *"We're going to let you go. Four-hour head start."*

Pren looks at Davan. Davan has not spoken since "on your feet." He nods once.

*"I'll be gone."*

### Scene 2.6 — After the Fight: Aldric and the Cart (Room 03)

Talis moves first to the north rock face — she spotted him before the combat, and she goes directly without being asked.

A garrison runner, slumped against the rock. Garrison kit. Breathing. 1 HP.

He is stable. He doesn't need to be stabilized — he's above 0. He needs to be brought back from 1.

This is the decision. He is fine at 1 HP in the sense that he will not die. The cart needs to be repaired. The return march takes an hour. Sava has four first-level slots. He is not dying.

She casts Cure Wounds.

**🎲 Sava Cure Wounds on Aldric (1d8+3, L1 slot)** — `1d8+3` → rolls=[7] mod=+3 **total=10**

*"Cart's here,"* Sava says. *"Two crates intact. You've been out two days."*

Sava spent the slot not from desperation but from refusal to leave a patient at 1 HP when she had the means. Elara's waystation was 2 hours away and she was delayed helping someone less urgent. She arrived too late.
