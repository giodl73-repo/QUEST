## Innovation Flags

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Intra-session act-without-announcement
Dimension affected: Atmospheric Landing, Character Agency
What happened: Scene 2.7 — Sava receives Maret's token without showing it to the party. She places it beside her suture kit. The log does not explain this. It is the correct place for something she trusts with her tools.
Why the rubric missed it: v1.5 act-without-announcement covers cross-session behavioral consequence. This is the intra-session variant: a named-arc event (the token) produces a behavioral response within the same session that requires no announcement. The token is received, processed, and stored in the same gesture. Not across sessions; within a scene. The v1.5 anchor requires cross-session; this does not qualify as v1.5. But it is the same phenomenon at shorter range.
```

```
INNOVATION FLAG: Failure-as-tactical-reframe
Dimension affected: Character Agency, Engagement, Surprise
What happened: Scene 2.3 — Talis fails three consecutive Athletics checks (5, 10, 10 vs DC 12). She says: "I'll stay on the ground and cover the south approach." She converts the failed climb into a deliberate tactical position — ground-level coverage of the choke point's north entrance — and in combat she fires from exactly that position. The repositioning produced better coverage than the ledge (she could engage enemies that exited toward the north; from the ledge she could only fire down).
Why the rubric missed it: Character agency scores choices that mattered. This choice was necessitated by dice failure; the agency was in the framing, not the initiative. The PC didn't choose to fail the climb; she chose how to interpret the failure as meaningful positioning. The innovation is in converting a mechanical failure into a characterological strength — distinct from framework-failure arc-activation (v1.7), which is about categorical vocabulary breaking; this is about tactical reframe of mechanical inadequacy.
```
