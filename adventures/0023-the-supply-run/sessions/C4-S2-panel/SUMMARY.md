## Most Dissent

**Surprise (8/10)** produced the widest spread of concern, for structurally opposite reasons:

- The **Improviser** found the 5 SURPRISE tags well-earned but noted uneven distribution across PCs (2 Sava-adjacent, 1 Caelith, 0 Davan) and flagged that 5 tags do not reach the 10-band threshold.
- The **Tactician** is not concerned about surprise count at all — the concern is that the Portent carry is the session's most telling absence, a high-weight class feature that has been atmospherically present for two sessions without mechanical consequence.
- The **Lorekeeper** and **Fresh Face** note the same Portent problem from opposite directions: the Lorekeeper wants to know what it means in fiction; the Fresh Face wants to know what it means in rules.

The philosophical disagreement: should Surprise be measured by how many tags the session generated (Improviser's read: 5 is good), or by whether the session's most telegraphed potential surprise (Portent [6,7] eventually firing) is building toward something mechanical (Tactician's read: not yet)?
