## Critique

### Overall assessment

The session's material texture is careful and earned. Maret's token warm from being worn is the kind of detail a setting-conscious writer lands once per adventure when they're working well; this session lands it and does not explain it. Pren leaving his provisions on the ledge when he departs is similarly unmarked. These are setting-density moments: the world makes a gesture and does not annotate it. What the session does not yet demonstrate is whether the Reconstitution itself is a Krynn-native faction with specific doctrinal texture, or whether it could be transplanted to any fantasy siege without loss. One scene's worth of Reconstitution-specific vocabulary would change this reading substantially.

### What worked

- **Scene 2.7 — the token warm from being worn:** *"She takes the cord from under her apron and removes the token. She hands it across. 'You count before you spend. I've not seen that in someone from outside.' The token is warm from being worn."* This is a Dragonlance-native detail in register if not in explicit lore — the token is a healer's professional credential passed body-to-body, not a formal insignia handed across a desk. The warmth is physical and correct. A token kept close to the body for professional reasons has a specific temperature. The session notes this without explaining it. That is exactly the right level of restraint. The token becomes a setting object, not merely a plot coupon, because it has been lived in.

- **Scene 2.1 — Maret approaching Sava rather than Asholt, and the ledger:** *"I have five days of materials left. If you go out and find it, I don't need to tell the Warden how close we are."* This interaction gives us Thorngate's internal power dynamics without a diagram: Maret (civilian healer authority) is above the supply chain in her domain; Asholt (garrison Warden) is above her in the command structure; the gap between their jurisdictions is exactly where Maret's request lives. This could only happen at a Solamnic garrison on a Solamnic pass. The access structures feel era-correct and institution-specific.

### What needs work

- **The Reconstitution: what are they, exactly?** Across two sessions, the Reconstitution has been described by what they do (siege-adjacent operations, organized supply-line interference, mercenary scouts, a commander with personal history at Thorngate) but not by what they are. In a Dragonlance setting, every organized faction has a theology, a prior history, or a relationship to one of the Orders. Keloth-era cells (Campaign 1) had Nerakan markers — the reader knew which doctrinal tradition they came from. The Reconstitution has "grey-field device on his collar" (Pren's insignia) and nothing else that ties them to a Krynn-specific lineage. By Session 2 of a campaign, the antagonist faction should have enough setting-texture to locate them in Ansalon's political geography. This is the session's most significant Lorekeeper gap.

- **Pren's Reconstitution regular is sympathetic but faction-anonymous:** *"She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass."* The extended H1 line is well-placed. But Pren's sympathy and his attempt to stop the cart ambush tell us he is a principled man within a faction he believes in — or at least tolerates. That is a rich character position. It is richer if we know why a principled person would join the Reconstitution. What does the faction's stated purpose mean to someone who agrees with it? What does Pren think the Reconstitution is *for*? A single line — even something he does not say but that his provisions-on-the-ledge implies — would give the faction its first interior character and distinguish it from generic-fantasy militia.

### The question I'd push on

Maret's healer-credentials token is a new workshop-canon object — it does not appear to derive from the SRD or Dragonlance sourcebooks. Has it been logged in `reference/dragonlance/workshop-canon.md`? The token's properties (warm from wearing, professional credential, passed body-to-body between healers of demonstrable equivalence) are distinctive enough to constitute a workshop canon artifact. If subsequent sessions treat the token differently — as a charm, a debt-marker, a simple coin — the setting-density of Scene 2.7 is retroactively undermined. Campaign 1's most resonant objects (the reliquary, the silver rose) held their meaning across sessions because they were tracked. This one needs a log entry before Session 3.
