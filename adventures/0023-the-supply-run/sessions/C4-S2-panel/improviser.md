## Critique

### Overall assessment

Five SURPRISE tags across a four-scene session is a genuinely good hit rate. Three of them came from players acting outside what the module's designed rails would produce — Talis's reframe, Caelith's institutional framing, Sava's token storage. The DM adjudicated all of them fairly, let them land without over-explaining, and the consequences were real (Pren gave the extended H1 line *because* of Caelith's framing; Talis covered the exit *because* she'd repositioned; the token means what it means *because* Sava didn't announce it). This is a session that earned its SURPRISE tags. My complaint is narrower: both scouts escaped, and that outcome has downstream costs that the module had flagged and not resolved. The DM's hands were tied by the dice, which is correct. But the module should have given the DM a better fallback.

### What worked

- **Scene 2.3 — Talis's failure-as-tactical-reframe:** *"I'll stay on the ground and cover the south approach."* Three failed climbs. No apology. No "I try again." She reads her own failure as information — the terrain is telling her something — and she finds a position the module didn't design. In the subsequent combat (Scene 2.5), she fires from exactly that ground-level position at the fleeing Scout B. The arrow misses (7+5=12 vs AC 14), but the position was correct. The DM did not redirect her, did not add consequences for not climbing, did not explain that the module expected everyone on the ledge. She went off-book and it mattered. This is the session's best improvised moment.

- **Scene 2.4 — Caelith's institutional framing as recognition signal:** The module presumably anticipated that PCs would ask Pren about the Reconstitution's goals. The module did not anticipate that Caelith would ask it in the institutional register — *"What does your command actually want here? Not the pass. What."* — which worked as a specific kind of recognition signal to a regular soldier who expects to be interrogated about personal loyalty. Caelith's framing said, implicitly: I am treating you as a professional in an institution, not as a defector or an enemy. Pren responded to that. The DM let the framing do its work rather than requiring a separate Persuasion roll. The Insight check (13 vs DC 13) was for reading Pren's readiness to speak; the institutional framing was the actual key. Good adjudication — the check and the approach were separate questions.

### What needs work

- **Both scouts escape, contractor's token gone:** This is the session's biggest unresolved off-book outcome. The players fought well; they drove off both scouts. They were not compensated for driving them off — the token went south, the Hessa seed is now circumstantial rather than physical. This is an honest dice outcome and I would not change it. But the module's own notes flag it: *"consider adding a backup physical token in the choke point debris."* That fix was identified by the session runner and not pre-implemented. From an Improviser's perspective, the DM said yes to the dice and the consequence is that the party did the right things and lost the evidence anyway. The downstream cost arrives at a moment where the party is currently winning — that is not inherently wrong, but it could feel like a railroaded setback at S3 if the Hessa investigation needs the token. Plant the backup. Let the improvised good combat outcome *not* cost them their intelligence.

- **Surprise rate: 5 tags, but 3 are Sava's:** Two of the five SURPRISE tags are Sava's (arc beat on stable Aldric; token storage). The innovation flags are both Sava-adjacent or Talis-adjacent. Caelith gets one (Pren extended line). Davan gets zero. From an Improviser's perspective, a session where one PC goes off-book twice, one PC goes off-book twice in the same direction (the arc beat and the token are the same throughline, not two separate improvisations), and one PC never ventures outside the module's stated options — that is an uneven yes-and distribution. Davan is playing to module. That is fine at session 2. It will not be fine at session 5.

### The question I'd push on

Caelith gave Pren a four-hour head start. The module presumably accounted for Pren's fate (gone). But the downstream consequence — a Reconstitution regular who tried to stop the cart ambush is now loose in the region, with four hours of lead time, knowing the party's faces and the garrison's supply situation — is either an open seed or a closed thread. The DM noted it in the handoff. If the module treats Pren as closed (left, done), that is a missed yes-and: Caelith's specific institutional recognition of Pren as "not my people, not my operation" is exactly the kind of judgment call that should have a downstream. Pren should come back — not because the module requires it, but because the improvisation earned it.
