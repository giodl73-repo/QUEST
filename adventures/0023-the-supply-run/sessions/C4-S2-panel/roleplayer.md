## Critique

### Overall assessment

This is Sava's session, fully and properly. Her grief-paragraph (Elara at the waystation; arrived too late; insufficient means) structures every choice she makes from Scene 2.1 to Scene 2.7 — and Maret's token is the moment the character sheet's quiet promise comes through. The session almost passes this lens on Sava's arc alone. The problem is Davan and Talis: both had moments, but only one of them was specific to their character. Caelith did something no other PC in the party could have done. Three of four PCs passed. One — Davan — remained a garrison-soldier-shaped silhouette.

### What worked

- **Scene 2.7 — Sava receives Maret's token:** *"You count before you spend. I've not seen that in someone from outside."* This line could only have been delivered to Sava, by Maret, at this moment, because of two sessions of consistent play. Sava's response — placing the token beside her suture kit, no announcement — is the session's best character work. Her suture kit is where she keeps what she trusts with patients. The token goes where her tools go. That is a character-sheet-deep gesture, not a plot beat. The grief-paragraph fires not as stated grief but as professional competence that is itself the resolution of the grief. Elara's waystation becomes, quietly, the standard Sava now meets consistently. This is exactly what the grief-paragraph is for.

- **Scene 2.4 — Caelith with Pren:** *"What does your command actually want here? Not the pass. What."* This is Caelith's voice-tag operating precisely: asks what the institution wants, not what the individual in front of him wants. No other PC in the party would have framed the question that way. A Fighter would have asked for intelligence. A Cleric would have asked after wellbeing or loyalty. Caelith's institutional framing was the recognition signal that made Pren speak. The character's specific register did specific work. That is a session moment only this PC could produce.

### What needs work

- **Davan in Scene 2.5 and 2.6 — "on your feet" and one nod:** Davan speaks twice this session. "On your feet" to Pren, and a single nod when Caelith announces the head start. Both are consistent with his character as a garrison soldier who does not volunteer more than necessary — but there is a difference between disciplined silence and vacancy. The session does not give Davan a moment where his *specific* grief-paragraph or bond or flaw shapes a decision. He repairs the cart. He takes a hit. He nods. None of that required Davan Fostermark, Fighter, specifically. A generic garrison guard could have done the same. At session three, this needs to be addressed — his character sheet has content. The module needs to pull on it.

- **Talis's failed climb reframe (Scene 2.3) — effective, but whose voice?** Talis saying *"I'll stay on the ground and cover the south approach"* is a good tactical-reframe moment and the innovation flag is correct to note it. But it is narrated in a general-competence register rather than a specific character register. What does Talis *feel* in that moment? Her character sheet implies a particular relationship to physical failure — does she have one? The moment works as a player-style instance (failure-as-tactical-reframe) but it does not tell us anything about who Talis is that her Ranger class description wouldn't tell us. The moment is characterized by what she *doesn't* do (no embarrassment, no apology) but not by what she does in her specific voice. The session log handles it correctly; the module needs to give Talis a beat that requires her grief-paragraph, not just her class identity.

### The question I'd push on

Maret's arc-completion fired — by character logic, correctly, at exactly the right moment — and Sava received it alone. No other PC was present for the full exchange. This is correct to the scene. But in a four-PC party, three PCs who weren't in the room for the session's most resonant character moment is a choice worth interrogating: does the party ever reflect on what the token means? Does Sava ever tell them? The token being placed beside the suture kit without announcement is wonderful *as a player-style instance.* But the Roleplayer's concern is that "without announcement" should eventually have a witness — at least internally, even between sessions. The gift's meaning should be shared with the party at some point, or it will calcify into a private Sava sub-plot that the rest of the table cannot access.
