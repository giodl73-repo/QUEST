# Pren (Reconstitution Regular)

## Appearance

Late twenties. Medium build, worn-in field kit, the Reconstitution's grey-field device on his collar — worn openly rather than hidden. Clean-shaved. Eyes that are watchful in the way of someone who has spent a lot of time on ledges in silence. He is not visibly afraid of the party. He is visibly tired.

## Stat Block

*Non-combatant in this encounter.* If forced: AC 14 (chain shirt), HP 18, Longsword +3 (1d8+1). He will not initiate combat. If attacked, he defends himself only long enough to disengage.

## Public knowledge

- He is a Reconstitution regular assigned as a liaison observer for the contractor operation.
- He had orders to observe only — the contractors' job was to delay the resupply without leaving Reconstitution evidence.
- He tried to stop the cart ambush. The contractors ignored him.
- He stayed on the ledge rather than returning to the siege line because he does not know what to report to his commander: that the contractors did their job in a way that exceeded their orders.

## Private knowledge

- His orders came down through a command chain that ultimately traces back to Commander Coldforge. He does not know her name. He knows his orders came from "the commander with history at the keep."
- He knows the Reconstitution is here for something personal to their commander, not for the pass. He does not fully understand this himself.
- He knows the contractor token (the one in the ambushers' belt pouches) is from a network that Coldforge's field adjutant uses for plausibly-deniable operations.

## H1 partial delivery

**Condition:** The party shows him the Thorngate garrison insignia (on any piece of kit) OR asks him directly what he knows about the Keep.

**His line:** *"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."*

This is all he says in front of the full party. No elaboration. He has been sitting on this information for two days.

**Extended delivery (Caelith alone, passive Insight DC 13 to notice he has more):**

Caelith's heuristic fires: *"what does the Reconstitution actually want here?"* If Caelith asks him alone — not as an interrogation but as an institutional analysis question — Pren gives one more sentence:

*"She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass."*

He will not say "she." He says "the commander." He does not know her name. He knows this is personal. He is choosing to give the party this because he tried to stop the ambush and this is the only thing he has to give.

## Behavior matrix

| Party action | Pren's response |
|---|---|
| Approach with weapons drawn | Hands up; does not flee; waits |
| Ask if he's a threat | "No. I tried to stop them." Factual. |
| Show him the garrison insignia | Pauses. H1 partial delivery: "Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here." |
| Ask him to elaborate in front of full party | "I've said what I can say." |
| Caelith alone, institutional question | One additional sentence: "She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass." |
| Ask what "she" or "the commander" means | "I don't know her name." (Honest) |

| Release him | Nods. Goes south. Gone by morning. |
| Leave him on the ledge | Still here at dusk. Gone by dawn. |

## Information-holder design

Pren is an information-holder who has crossed his own command structure's interests to give partial information. His information readiness condition: the party demonstrates they can receive the information correctly — specifically, that they ask *what* rather than *who*, and that they understand the distinction between the pass (tactical) and the unfinished thing (personal).

Caelith is the correct recipient: his heuristic is *"what does this institution actually want here?"* That question, asked of Pren directly, is the condition.

## No arc-completion

Pren does not have an arc-completion designed for this campaign. He is a one-session NPC. His job is H1 partial delivery. What happens to him is the party's choice; document in handoff.
