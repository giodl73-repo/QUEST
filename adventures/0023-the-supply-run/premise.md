# 0023 — The Supply Run

## The adventure in one sentence

The party leaves the walls for the first time to find the overdue supply cart, discovers it was ambushed by someone who is not the Reconstitution, and brings back enough to keep Maret Stave's triage point running — while a captured Reconstitution soldier says something that doesn't make sense yet.

## Setting

**The Secondary Pass** — a narrow mountain trail that branches from the main road two miles south of Thorngate Keep, used for supply runs when the primary approach is watched. Day 8 of the siege. The cart left the supply depot at Irathon Waystation four days ago and has not arrived. Tomek's scout went to look two days ago and has not returned either.

The secondary pass winds through three terrain zones: the **low trail** (open, visible from above), the **choke point** (a narrows between two rock faces, ideal ambush ground), and the **upper ledge** (a shelf above the choke, used by watchers and archers). The cart is at the choke point, halted. The ambush happened there.

**The ambushers are not the Reconstitution.** Their tracks are wrong — lighter boots, no Solamnic-pattern kit, a different fire style. Someone else hit the cart before the Reconstitution siege line was even established. Someone who knew the route.

## Resource state entering S2

- **Party HP:** All four at full (28/28, 24/24, 28/28, 17/17) — S1 ended with 0 damage.
- **Supply Die:** d10 (degraded from d12 post-S1).
- **Sava spell slots:** Full (4×L1, 2×L2) — 0 used in S1.
- **Talis spell slots:** 2×L1 (used 1 for Hunter's Mark in S1).
- **Caelith Portent:** [6, 7] carried forward (no long rest between sessions).
- **S2 healing potions available (from garrison, d10 tier):** 5 (rolled from Supply Die).

**Design intent:** This is the first session where the party takes real damage. The ambush site combat should cost 15-25% of party HP total. Sava should need to spend at least 1 slot — ideally 2 — while still having enough in reserve to satisfy Maret's token condition (which fires on return, based on S1 conservation, not S2).

## Hook

Before dawn briefing, Maret finds Sava at the healer's station. She says quietly:

*"The cart is four days late. I have five days of materials left. If you go out and find it, I don't need to tell the Warden how close we are."*

She is not asking Sava to lie to Asholt. She is asking her to solve it before he needs to know.

Sava goes. The others come because the party goes together now — they haven't discussed this explicitly, but by the third day in a siege you know who you leave through the postern with.

## Spotlight: Sava

This is Sava's session. The supply run is a healer's errand — she is the one who understands what's at stake if the cart is lost. The combat at the ambush site will push her toward spending slots she has been conserving. The Maret token fires on return. The grief resonance (Elara, delayed, too late) is present throughout — the whole session is a version of the story Sava doesn't want to repeat.

**Sava's arc beat for this session:** She makes at least one decision to spend a healing slot on the supply run — not because the tactical situation demands it, but because she refuses to let the delay pattern repeat. She arrives on time. She has enough. She chooses to spend it.

## The five scenes

### Scene 1 — The Postern (Room 01)

The party leaves before dawn. Tomek sees them go. He does not stop them. He does not order them back. He watches until they are through the postern.

**DM note:** Tomek's manner on their exit — noticing it, filing it, saying nothing — is a continuation of the S1 behavioral-stance pattern. He is still measuring. This is scene-setting, not a beat.

Passive Perception DC 12: the secondary pass branches left from the main road after a half-mile. The main road is watched from the ridge. The branch is in shadow.

### Scene 2 — The Low Trail (Room 02)

**Read-aloud:**
> The trail narrows between two cliff faces fifty feet high. Ahead: the sound of nothing. No cart wheels. No mule. The trail has been used recently — there are boot-prints going in that are not Reconstitution-pattern kit.

Talis leads. This is her terrain.

**DC 12 Survival (Talis, signature move: terrain-first):** The tracks are lighter than Reconstitution soldiers'. Shorter stride. Different boot construction — no military pattern. Two, maybe three people. The cart went in ahead of them.

**DC 14 Survival (any PC):** The tracks are recent — two, maybe three days old. Before the Reconstitution tightened the siege line. Whoever this was, they hit the cart before the siege was a factor.

**Pressure:** The low trail is exposed. A Reconstitution patrol on the northeast ridge could spot movement here. DC 14 Stealth (group check) to move without drawing fire. Failure: one arrow, +4 to hit, 1d8+2 — random target, from above. The patrol moves on regardless.

### Scene 3 — The Choke Point (Room 03, primary encounter)

**Read-aloud:**
> The trail pinches between two rock faces. Ahead: the cart, stopped. One wheel cracked. The mule is gone. Three supply crates — one overturned, two intact. No bodies. No blood. Whatever happened here was controlled.

The ambushers did not loot the crates. They took the mule and left the cart. This was not a robbery. This was a disruption.

**The remaining threat:** Two of the ambushers are still here, watching the cart. They expected the garrison to send a small patrol. They are not Reconstitution; they are **Reconstitution-adjacent** — irregular contractors, paid to slow Thorngate's resupply without leaving Reconstitution evidence. They have orders not to kill garrison soldiers if avoidable. They will fight if cornered; they will run if given a clear path out.

**Encounter:** See `encounters/ambush-remnant.md`. Designed to cost HP — first real combat damage of the campaign.

**The cart:** Two intact crates. Enough healing materials for 8 more days (doubles Maret's window). One crate has specific contents Maret asked for. The cracked wheel takes 20 minutes to repair (Athletics DC 12 with tools from the cart's own repair kit — which is still here).

**Tomek's scout:** Slumped against the north rock face. Alive — unconscious, mild concussion, 1 HP remaining. The ambushers hit him when he arrived and left him. He has been here for two days. He will recover.

### Scene 4 — The Upper Ledge (Room 04, optional)

If the party climbs to the upper ledge (Athletics DC 12):

- A fire ring, cold. Used for watching. Provisions for three days.
- A **Reconstitution prisoner** — one of the ambushers who didn't run and couldn't fight. He is not an irregular; he is a regular soldier who was here as a liaison. He saw the ambush, tried to stop it (he has orders to leave the garrison alone; this was not his operation), and was left behind by the irregulars.

**H1 partial delivery (this scene):** When the party shows him the Thorngate garrison insignia (on a shield, a collar, or just by asking what he knows about the Keep):

He says: *"Asholt's keep. He knows why we're here."*

He will not elaborate in front of the full party. If Caelith speaks to him alone (his institutional-lens heuristic fires: *"what does the Reconstitution actually want here?"*), he gives one more sentence: *"She came back for something that wasn't finished. Not the pass."*

**Passive Perception DC 12:** His reaction when he sees the insignia is not what you'd expect from a soldier looking at the enemy — it's something closer to recognition. Not of the device itself. Of what it represents to someone.

### Scene 5 — Return and Maret's Token (Room 05)

The party brings back the cart (repaired wheel, two intact crates, Tomek's scout on the cart-bed). They come through the postern before noon.

Maret is at the station. She sees the crates. She looks at Sava.

She has been counting this since she asked. She already knows — not from the crates but from the fact that Sava came back, with enough, before the six-day window closed.

She gives Sava her token. She says: *"You count before you spend. I've not seen that in someone from outside."*

It comes from the cord under her healer's apron — the same cord described in her NPC file. No ceremony.

**Maret's token:** DELIVERED (Sava; S2 arc-completion for Maret Irr).

**DM note:** The token condition fires on the combination of S1 (0 slots) + S2 (returned with supplies). Sava may have spent slots in S2 combat — that's correct and expected. The token is about the pattern across both sessions, not this session alone.
