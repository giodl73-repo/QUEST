# The Clause of Reckoning

*The fifth shard. Heavier than it looks. The last clause sworn at the compact signing.*

## Appearance

Obsidian, slightly smaller than the others. The inscription is the most recent-looking — it was the last clause added at the compact signing in PC 198, and the letters have a slightly different quality from the others, as if the hand that cut them was tired, or rushed, or very sure. *"Let the Orders answer to those they serve."* Archmage Toven of the Red Robes (PC 190–218, who also swore the Clause of Ambition) — his sigil here is the flame, but open-handed rather than closed. The other shard (Ambition) had a restrained flame. This one is open.

The shard is heavier than it should be for its size. Every person who has held it has noticed this. Nobody knows why.

## Presence / Desire

The Clause of Reckoning wants to be *enforced.* Of all the shards, this one has the most active presence — not warm, not cold, not vibrating, but weighted. It sits in Vorn's archive and it has been sitting there for twelve years and it has been heavy the whole time. It is waiting for something to be done with it.

## Provenance

1. **Archmage Toven of the Red Robes (PC 190–218):** Swore this clause last, at the compact signing. He swore it last because the other signatories had been debating for three days and he finally said: *"We swear this or we don't swear anything."* They swore it. He died in the Cataclysm.
2. **Tower of High Sorcery, Istar (PC 198–220):** Destroyed.
3. **Vorn Haleth's predecessors (unknown, ~200 years):** The shard passed through several hands, always ending up with researchers and scholars rather than practitioners. Nobody who has held it long-term has been comfortable with power. The weight seems to select for that.
4. **Vorn Haleth (12 years ago – present):** Purchased it from an antiquities dealer in Palanthas. She knew immediately what it was; she had been looking for it specifically. She has had it in a locked case in her archive ever since. She holds it sometimes, when she is working. She is not comfortable with what it asks.

## Properties

**Passive:** Heavier than it appears. This is not a magical effect; it is a characteristic of the stone. Every holder notices it.

**Passive:** Arcana DC 12 to identify as compact fragment. Thessaly/Lenne auto.

**Hint 4 delivery (inscription reading):** When the inscription is read aloud — *"Let the Orders answer to those they serve"* — in the context of Vorn's research (the party has seen her twelve years of notes), Thessaly or Calder (Arcana DC 10 or passive) understands: this clause requires the Orders to be accountable to people, not to the Orders themselves. The compact was a protection mechanism, not a governance framework. This is Hint 4.

**Route D activation:** When Thessaly names her Test wound to Vorn, the shard's weight becomes noticeable to everyone in the room — not lighter (that would be too neat) but *acknowledged*. The weight is correct. The reckoning is being performed.

## Curse

**The weight of responsibility:** An attuned creature becomes aware, once per day, of who they owe an accounting to — which person or persons is waiting for them to answer for something. This is not guilt; it is recognition. Specific: *"This person. This action. They are waiting."* The creature may perform the accounting or not. The shard does not enforce. It recognizes.

**Ending:** Releases when the compact is spoken whole.

## GM Notes

- **Vorn holds the shard sometimes while working.** The weight is not comfortable; it is honest. She has been doing research for twelve years into who the Orders owe an accounting to. The shard is the right instrument for that work.
- **Thessaly and the weight.** When she holds it: the weight. Who does she owe an accounting to? Her sister Mira, who doesn't know what the Test cost her. The shard does not name this aloud. Thessaly knows it.
