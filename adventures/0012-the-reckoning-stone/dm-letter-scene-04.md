# DM Letter — Scene 04 (Vorn's Study: Thessaly Names the Test)

*This letter is for you, the DM. Read it before the session.*

---

## What this scene is

Vorn has been doing research for twelve years into who the Orders owe an accounting to. She has concluded: the compact requires accountability, not governance. The clause she holds is the one that says *"let the Orders answer."*

Thessaly has been doing research for three years into how to fix the structure that allowed her to do wrong without consequence. She named her wound to Tessamine in Session 1. Tessamine is one of three people who know. She named it to Morreth (by implication) in Session 3, by being present while Morreth's arc-completion fired. She has not named it to anyone who is a direct peer — another mage, another person who understands what the Test is and what she sacrificed of someone else's to pass it.

Vorn is that person. She will ask, essentially: *"Do you know what accountability requires? Not theoretically. Of you."*

Thessaly will answer honestly. This is the arc-completion condition.

---

## What to do before it starts

- Read Thessaly's grief paragraph: the spell at the Test; Mira's diminished ability; her sister doesn't know; she thought no one seeing it meant it was contained.
- Read Vorn's arc-completion text (in `npcs/vorn-haleth.md`): *"My researchers in the field have been operating for two years... one of them frightened a family in Crystalmir... I did not adequately predict it."*
- These two people are mirrors. Vorn did the wrong thing for right reasons. Thessaly did the wrong thing for reasons she could justify. Both are trying to fix the structure that allowed them to be wrong. This scene is the recognition.

---

## What to do during

**When Vorn finishes assessing the party:** She will ask a version of this: *"You want to speak a new compact. You want me to trust that you'll speak it correctly. How do I know you understand what the reckoning requires — not of the Orders, but of you?"*

**If Thessaly answers:** Do not prompt her further. She knows what the answer is. She may need a moment of silence before she speaks. That is the moment you hold.

**If she names the Test:** Vorn is quiet. Longer than comfortable. This is her processing time. Do not fill it.

**When Vorn responds:** She names the Crystalmir family. *"I did not authorize it. I should have predicted it."* This is her accounting. It does not match Thessaly's in scale — Mira lost an ability; a family was frightened, not permanently harmed. The scales are different. The structure is the same. Both of them did what they could not fully control when they stopped paying attention.

**After she speaks:** She picks up the Clause of Reckoning. She gives it to Thessaly. *"This clause is for both of us."*

---

## What ends the scene

The scene ends when the shard is in Thessaly's hands. After that: the roof garden if the party wants, or they leave. Vorn will discuss the accountability language for the new compact if anyone asks. She has twelve years of work on this.

---

## What happens if Thessaly doesn't name the Test

Route A: Vorn gives the shard for documentation credit. This is a real exchange. It's enough. The party has the shard.

The scene remains open — Thessaly has not yet performed her reckoning publicly. The finale (Session 7) will provide the occasion if this one doesn't.

---

*Vorn has been doing the right work for twelve years in the wrong way. Thessaly has been doing the right work for three years carrying something she hasn't fully accounted for. They recognize each other. The shard is the accounting made concrete.*
