# S05 LOG — The Reckoning Stone

## Session Summary

Vorn Haleth received the party as expected. Orik read the building on entry (craft-witness x4 — fourth consecutive session, fully session-invariant). The staff member who received them was security-trained; Orik noted this without saying it.

In the Public Research Room: Hint 4 on the east wall, visible from the doorway. The twelve-year summary panel read: *"The compact was a protection mechanism, not a governance framework. The seven clauses are the minimum acknowledgment that the Orders must provide to the people who live under the consequences of what the Orders do."* Thessaly stopped in the doorway and read it. Calder read it the way he read the Sylaren amendment twice. Lenne recognized Vorn as a methodological peer; Vorn asked how the party had done the same work in less time. Lenne: *"I had better equipment."* Vorn almost smiled.

In Vorn's study, with the Clause of Reckoning visible through the glass case: Vorn asked whether the party understood what the reckoning requires — not of the Orders, but of them. The room was quiet. Then Thessaly named the Test wound — the spell, the cost, her sister Mira who doesn't know — to Vorn, a peer, the first peer who has heard it. She said it because the question deserved an honest answer, not because she was performing a requirement.

Vorn went very still. Then she named the Crystalmir family her researcher had frightened two months ago. She opened the case. She gave Thessaly the shard. *"This clause is for both of us."*

On the roof: Vorn revealed she knows where the Forgiveness shard is — she has known for eight months and withheld it from her field researchers because *"it requires something I didn't have to give."* Calder built the witness-list aloud: Mira, Deva, Vorn, Sevven's letter, Morreth's chapel. Lenne began drafting the accountability clause language with Vorn. Five shards. Seventy-nine days.
