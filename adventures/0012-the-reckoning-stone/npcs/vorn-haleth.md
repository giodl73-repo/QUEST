# Vorn Haleth

*Research archivist, 44. Twelve years studying the compact's history. She decoded Tessamine's records before the party did. She trained the splinter cells. She is not wrong about the problem. She is wrong about the solution.*

## Appearance

Vorn is precise and watchful — the specific watchfulness of someone who has been expecting a particular conversation for a long time and is assessing whether this is the one. She wears practical scholar's robes with a research organization's mark (her own; the name is "The Reckoning Archive"). Her hair is cut short for convenience. She has ink on her hands. She carries a notebook that she has been filling for twelve years.

She is not hostile. She is prepared.

## Stat block

**Combatant (reluctant).** AC 12 (Mage Armor 15) | HP 42. She is not a field mage; she is a researcher. Her offensive capacity is limited: Hold Person (concentration), Charm Person, Detect Thoughts. If combat triggers: she stays in her study and lets her staff handle the hallways. She will use Hold Person if directly threatened.

## What she knows (public)

- She has the Clause of Reckoning. She purchased it twelve years ago.
- She has spent twelve years researching the compact's history and purpose.
- She trained several research assistants who have been field-active. (She will not call them "splinter cells.")
- She knows Mira stood down the main operation. She disagrees with Mira's authority to do that.
- She has been expecting this party since Korrath.

## What she knows (private)

- She decoded Tessamine's records by finding a copy of the reconstruction document in a different archive eighteen months ago. She has had a complete reconstruction for eight months.
- Her twelve-year conclusion: the compact was a protection mechanism for ordinary people, not a governance structure for wizards. The Orders have been misusing it for three hundred years by treating it as legitimacy rather than obligation.
- She is afraid that the party will speak the old compact verbatim and restore the broken structure rather than fixing it. This is her primary concern. Everything she has done — the splinter cells, the field operations, the aggressive research posture — has been driven by this specific fear.
- She is not fully sure her methods are justified. She has been pushing this question away for twelve years. If someone asks her directly whether the field operations were necessary, she will not be able to say yes without qualification.

## Emotional position

**What she wants:** For the compact to be spoken correctly — as a protection mechanism, not a governance framework. She wants the accountability clause enforced. She wants ordinary people to be able to hold the Orders responsible.

**What she can't say:** She is not sure she trusts the people who will speak the compact. She has been the most rigorous researcher of the compact's history for twelve years. She has not found anyone she trusts to speak it correctly. Until now.

**The condition:** She gives the shard when Thessaly demonstrates that she understands what the compact costs the person who has to answer for it — not the Orders, but the person. Specifically: when Thessaly names what she owes and to whom. The Test. Her sister. The cost she has been carrying. Vorn will recognize this: someone who knows what the reckoning requires of them personally, not just institutionally.

## Arc-Completion

**The moment:** Thessaly tells Vorn about the Test — the spell, the sister, the cost, the fact that her sister doesn't know. She does not say it to argue her moral position. She says it because Vorn asked, essentially, *"Do you know what accountability requires?"* and Thessaly is answering honestly.

Vorn is quiet for a long moment. Then: *"My researchers in the field have been operating for two years. They have not physically harmed anyone. But they have threatened, they have broken and entered, and two months ago one of them frightened a family in Crystalmir who had nothing to do with any of this. I did not authorize that. I should have predicted it. I did not adequately predict it."* She picks up the Clause of Reckoning. She gives it to Thessaly. The weight is still there. *"This clause is for both of us."*

**What it produces:** Shard given. Vorn's research notes given (the full twelve years; she has a copy). Vorn agrees to be present at the compact speaking as a researcher-witness. She will not speak for any Order; she will speak for the people the Orders serve. She will help design the accountability language in the new compact.

**What happens if condition is never met:** Route A or C. The shard changes hands. Vorn is not a compact witness.
