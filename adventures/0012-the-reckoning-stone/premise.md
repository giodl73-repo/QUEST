# Premise — The Reckoning Stone

## Hook

Mira Coldsteel sends a brief message: *"The Palanthas contact for the splinter cells is Archivist Vorn Haleth. She runs a private research center in the Palanthas merchant district. She has the Clause of Reckoning and she has been studying the compact's history for twelve years. She is the one who decoded Tessamine's records before you did. She is also the person who trained the splinter cells. She will not stand down because I asked her to. She does not recognize my authority to stand anyone down."*

The party travels to Palanthas. The Clause of Reckoning is in Vorn Haleth's research center — a converted merchant house with a private archive, six permanent staff researchers, and a security arrangement that reflects someone who has been expecting this visit for years.

## Setting

Palanthas — the great northern city. The merchant district. Vorn Haleth's research center is a four-story building with no external sign. The researchers inside know what they are working on and why. The security is professional, not hostile: Vorn will receive the party. She has been expecting them.

## Artifact

**The Clause of Reckoning** — *"Let the Orders answer to those they serve."* Slightly smaller than the previous shards; the inscription is the most recent-looking of any (it was the last clause sworn at the compact signing, PC 198). The stone has a quality of weight — heavier than it should be for its size.

**Hint 4 delivery:** Vorn's research notes, visible in her archive, reveal what she has concluded after twelve years: *"The compact was not a political instrument. It was a protection mechanism. The seven clauses are not governance — they are the minimum acknowledgment that the Orders must provide to the people who live under the consequences of what the Orders do. The compact was not about the Orders' authority. It was about their accountability."* This is Hint 4: the compact's intent. The party receives it via the research notes (passive Investigation 10 or Vorn herself; she is proud of this conclusion).

## Themes

**Reckoning** — the emotion. The compact makes the Orders accountable. Vorn wants that accountability. So does the party. The difference is method.

**Thessaly's spotlight** — she is White Robe. Vorn trained the people who have been trying to steal the party's shards. Vorn has been right about everything the compact was supposed to do. She has been using wrong methods to achieve right ends. Thessaly's wound (the Test; the spell; her sister's diminished ability) is about exactly this: she did the wrong thing for reasons she could justify. She understands Vorn better than she wants to.

**The party's moral position** — they have Route D. They have Mira. They have Deva. They have four shards and four Order representatives. They can offer Vorn the one thing she has been working for: real accountability, built into the new compact, in a framework that cannot be controlled by any single faction. Can she accept this? Can she give up the Reckoning shard to the people who are actually capable of using it correctly?

## Three-route outcomes (A/C/D)

**Route A (Charter/Fallback):** Vorn trades the shard for recognition of her research in the new compact's documentation. She has twelve years of work that deserves acknowledgment. The party can acknowledge it. The shard changes hands professionally.

**Route C (Default):** Combat in the research center. Vorn's staff defends the archive. The party recovers the shard under pressure. Costly; one staff researcher is injured; Vorn feels the party has proven her right (the Orders, even the new ones, take what they want). She will not witness the new compact.

**Route D (Inversion/Optimal):** Thessaly tells Vorn about the Test — the spell, the cost, the sister who doesn't know. She names what she did wrong and what she is trying to fix. Vorn goes quiet. She recognizes this: someone who was wrong for reasons they could justify, trying to fix the structure that allowed them to be wrong. She gives the shard. She agrees to help design the accountability clause in the new compact. She becomes a contributor, not an obstacle.

## Tier and party

Tier 2, L5 (party of 5). One genuine combat encounter: the party must fight past Vorn's security team to reach the archive if Route C activates (or if the party triggers it). The combat is Hard — 5 trained guard-researchers (higher level than splinter agents). Lenne's Counterspell will be essential (Vorn's senior researcher is a mage).

## Constraints

- Room count: 5 (reception, public research room, private archive, Vorn's study, roof garden)
- Hint 4 must deliver this session — it is the final prerequisite confirmation for the finale.
- Vorn's arc-completion is available only via Route D. Route A gives the party the shard without Vorn's cooperation. Route D gives them Vorn as a collaborator for the finale.
- P0 self-review: every decision-point needs a fallback; the Hard encounter needs a clear Route C trigger condition.
