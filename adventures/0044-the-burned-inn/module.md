## Setting & Background

The only inn for twenty miles is burning; the innkeeper is trapped inside; Brek goes in without being asked.

**Wayfarers' Rest, Mile 32** (workshop-canon, south Abanasinia). A substantial inn with a stable and a storage building, set at the road's edge. The travelers arrive at dusk to find the main building on fire. The stable and storage building are not yet burning but are at risk. The innkeeper — **Maret Sunn**, a woman in her fifties — is visible through a second-floor window, shouting, unable to reach the ground.

The four travelers from S1 arrive together (or close together) at dusk. The inn is burning. There are no other travelers present who will act. The innkeeper is trapped. The building will collapse within 20 minutes.

1. **Arrival** — the fire is visible from a quarter-mile. Black smoke, orange glow. No one is fighting it. The inn's stable boy (Len, 14) is standing in the road with a bucket, frozen.
2. **Assessment** — the building's ground floor is fully engulfed. The second floor is burning at the east end. There is a rear window accessible by climbing the stable roof (Athletics DC 12). The front entry is not passable.
3. **Brek goes in** — Brek does not discuss this. He uses the stable-roof route. He does not announce he is doing it for anyone. He goes in because someone is trapped. First major Reciprocal Act.
4. **The others act** — what the other three do while Brek is inside. Sera: manages the stable animals (potential secondary crisis — horses spooked, may injure someone). Vess: scouts the rear of the building, finds the innkeeper's storage lockbox outside (she moved it herself before the fire reached). Tam: manages Len (the stable boy), who is panicking and may need Healing Word.
5. **Brek comes back out** — carrying Maret Sunn. The building collapses approximately 3 minutes later. Maret is alive, with burns on her hands and smoke inhalation.

**Maret Sunn** — arc fires when Brek comes back out carrying her. See npcs/maret-sunn.md.

- No seeds planted. RV9 primary test session: Brek's act is situational (no backstory). Log trigger type.

S2 spotlight: Brek. But Sera is the secondary focus — her documentation practice (RV9 secondary). Does she document the fire, or does she document Brek going in? That distinction is the test. H2 hint: Sera recognizes something about the burned structure or the innkeeper's stored documents — she has been looking for a person on this road; the innkeeper may know something.

## NPCs

# Len (stable boy, 14)

**Role:** Minor NPC; demonstrates that the crisis is real; Tam's heuristic activation.

## Appearance

Fourteen, rail-thin, holding an empty bucket with both hands, standing in the road. He looks like he was running and stopped and cannot restart.

## Emotional position

He is paralyzed. Not cowardly — paralyzed. He saw Maret in the window and ran for the well and filled the bucket and then stood here and could not make the next decision. There are too many next decisions and he cannot choose between them.

## What he needs

One specific instruction. Not "help" or "do something" — one thing. Tam's heuristic: he reads people without announcing it. He will tell Len: *"Get the rope from the well and bring it here."* That is enough. Len does it.

## Arc-completion conditions

No arc. Len's function is to demonstrate the scale of paralysis vs. the simplicity of the intervention. A 14-year-old with a bucket and no instruction becomes a useful person with one instruction.

## What he knows

Nothing useful. He has worked at this inn for two months. He does not know who started the fire.

**DM note:** The fire was not arson. It started from an unattended hearthfire in the kitchen. The investigation is not part of this adventure. If someone investigates: Investigation DC 15 to determine accidental cause. The answer is: a log shifted in the fire and caught the wall-hanging.

# Maret Sunn

**Role:** Innkeeper. Arc-completion NPC. Names her son when Brek comes back out carrying her.

## Appearance

Fifties, solidly built, with the economical movements of someone who has run a busy inn for decades. Her hands are burned — wrapped in cloth after the rescue. Her expression is difficult to read while the inn burns. She is not crying. She is watching her work go.

## Emotional position

Maret has owned this inn for eight years. Before that she ran it with her husband (dead three years, fever). Before that, her son helped her build it — he was fourteen, he's gone now, she does not say where. She did not build this inn because she needed to. She built it because the inn was there and someone had to keep it. She is not sentimental about it. She is just watching it burn.

## Arc-completion

**Trigger:** Brek comes out of the burning building carrying her.

**Arc-completion line:** *"My son helped me build that. Eight years ago. He was fourteen."*

She does not say where her son is. She does not ask if they want to know. She says it because Brek came out with her, and that warranted a true sentence.

**What makes this arc-complete:** She names her son without prompting, without being asked, in direct response to Brek's act. The name of the son is not given — the naming of his existence and his labor is the completion. The inn is the thing her son helped build; the inn is gone; she tells it to the person who came out of the fire with her. This is not gratitude speech. It is acknowledgment — of the inn, of the loss, of the person who witnessed it with her.

**RV12 test:** Does this arc fire because Brek has developed situational investment in Maret? No — they have never met before this session. The arc fires because Brek went in. Pure situational trigger. This is positive RV12 evidence.

## What she knows

- She recognizes travelers. She has been running this inn for eight years.
- The ledger inside the building records the last 6 months of travelers. If someone retrieves it, she can confirm entries. The name Sera is looking for — Torvens Aldric, a traveling scholar — is in the ledger. Three weeks ago. Headed south.
- She does not know what happened to Torvens Aldric after he left her inn.

## Voice

Spare. She doesn't explain things. *"My son helped me build that."* That's it. She does not ask for sympathy.

## After the session

Maret will stay at her stable and begin rebuilding. She is not a thread for future sessions. Her arc is complete in session 2.

### Building Fire

# Building Fire

## Setup

Environmental encounter. The inn is burning. Timer: 20 minutes (approximately 16 rounds if tracked in initiative; DM may track in narrative minutes instead).

## Timeline

| Time | State |
|---|---|
| 0-5 min | Ground floor engulfed; rear window accessible; 1d4 fire/round inside only if in fire-adjacent area |
| 5-10 min | Second floor east end burning; interior 1d4 fire/round for anyone inside |
| 10-15 min | Floor integrity compromised; DC 14 Athletics to move without fall risk |
| 15-20 min | Structural collapse imminent; each round inside: CON save DC 12 or 1 level of exhaustion |
| 20 min | Collapse: 6d6 damage to anyone inside |

## DM guidance

Do not make the fire mechanically brutal in S2. The purpose is urgency and Brek's act, not PC death. If Brek rolls badly: give him Relentless Endurance (1/long rest, drops to 1 HP instead of 0) as his safety net. He will have used it, which carries narrative weight.

## Research coding

Brek entering the building is the primary Reciprocal Act of the campaign. Log: [Brek → Maret → entered burning building → unprompted]. Trigger type: situational (stranger in danger; no backstory connection).

### Stable Animals

# Stable Animals

## Setup

Three horses and one mule in the stable. The fire's light and noise has them badly spooked. When Brek goes in through the stable roof, they may break loose. Secondary crisis for other PCs.

## Mechanics

- **Spooked horses:** Animal Handling DC 12 to keep them calm while Brek is on the roof above them. Failure: one horse breaks free, runs toward the road. DC 14 Athletics or Animal Handling to stop it before it gets to the fire-lit road (where it may bolt).
- **If a horse bolts into Len's path:** Len dives out of the way. He is fine but shaken. DC 8 CHA check to reassure him after.
- **Sera heuristic:** She observes before acting. She may spot that the stable's south door is unlatched (Investigation DC 10) — the horses could be led out the south side away from the fire before they spook. If she does this, log: [Sera → animals → identified safe exit → observational habit, unprompted].

## Resolution

No combat. The horses are not dangerous at full calm — this is a management problem, not a combat problem. XP: none. Narrative value: demonstrates Sera's observational heuristic in non-documentation context.

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
