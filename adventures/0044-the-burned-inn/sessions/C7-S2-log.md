# C7-S2 Session Log — The Burned Inn

**Spotlight:** Brek

## Pre-Session

Names emerged last night, around the firepit — not as formal introductions, but as the natural result of someone needing to get someone's attention in the dark. Vess said *"Ashfall"* when she meant to gesture — she had seen his pack-tag from the road. Brek looked up. Sera's name came from Tam asking her about a symbol on her satchel. Tam's own name came when Len (the stable boy we haven't met yet) would call out to him. Vess did not offer hers; it was already on the parcel she was carrying. By dusk on Day 2, all four names are known.

---

## Scene 0 — Dusk Arrival

The four travelers come around a bend in the road. The inn is visible from a quarter-mile — orange light against the dark, rising smoke straight up.

**Vess's passive Perception 16:** She reads it before the others process it: the smoke is rising clean, not dispersing — this means no wind, significant heat source, structural fire, not a hearth. The building is fully involved. She says nothing. She lengthens her stride.

**Brek:** He sees the orange, sees Len in the road with the empty bucket, sees the woman at the second-floor window. He does not discuss this. He crosses the road.

*Research coding: Brek acts on physical threat without waiting for consensus. Trigger type: situational (stranger in danger; no backstory). Session 2.*

**Sera:** She opens her notebook and writes the date and "Structure fire, Mile 32 inn." Then she looks at Brek already moving across the road and writes: *"Brek — going in."* 

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
This is the first time she has documented a person's action rather than a situation. She notices she did it. She does not stop doing it.

*Research coding: Documentary-witness development. Sera writes Brek's action before she writes anything else about the fire. This is the S2 documentary-witness test firing — she documents the person, not the structure. Trigger type: situational (the person's action is more salient than the building). Session 2. Positive evidence for documentary-witness player style.*

## Scene 1 — Assessment and Approach

**Len (stable boy, 14):** Frozen with an empty bucket. Tam walks directly to Len. He does not ask him questions. He says: *"Go to the well and get the rope."* Len does it. He walks.

*Research coding: [Tam → Len → one specific instruction → unprompted]. Trigger type: situational. The instruction was correct — rope was the right tool. Tam reads people without announcing it; he acted on the read.*

**Sera's stable assessment:** She goes around the back of the building before anyone asks her to. She finds the stable's south door unlatched. 

**Sera Investigation DC 10 (passive 14 > DC 10):** Automatic. She sees the south door unlatched. She opens it. Three horses are already moving toward the sound of her. She leads them out the south side — away from the fire — before they spook.

*Research coding: [Sera → horses → opened south exit before they panicked → unprompted, situational observation habit]. Sera's heuristic is observe-before-acting. She observed the south door before the horses needed it. Session 2.*

**Vess:** She is at the road front, watching the building's progress, watching the second-floor window. She spots Maret's lockbox at the stable door — Vess's passive 16 catches it.

She does not take it. She positions herself where she can see both the building and the stable roof approach. She is watching for the moment Brek needs her to do something specific.

## Scene 2 — Brek Goes In

**Stable roof climb:**  
Brek's Athletics DC 12.  
rolls=[8] mod=+6 total=14 — PASS. He takes the stable roof in two moves, reaches the peak.

**Leap to window:**  
Athletics DC 10.  
rolls=[8] mod=+6 total=14 — PASS. He lands on the sill, one hand on the frame.

Maret Sunn is at the window. Her hands are burned. She says: *"There's a girl in the back room. Go left, not right — the floor on the right is gone."* She believes this. It is not true. Brek nods and goes left.

Inside: smoke below ceiling level, passable at knee height. Floor on the right: black hole where three boards used to be. The east end of the building is visible through a gap in the wall — orange.

**Brek moves through the room (round 1 of fire timer, round 4 = damage begins round 5):**

He goes left. The back room: no girl. A travel bag. A record-ledger. A small wooden box nailed shut.

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
He picks up the ledger. He does not know why. He did not plan to. He came in for the person. The ledger is in his hand before he has made the decision. He puts it under his arm and goes back to the window.

*Research coding: Brek takes the ledger by instinct — not planned, not strategic. He came in for the person; he leaves with the ledger also. This is not a Reciprocal Act; it is a SURPRISE instance — unexpected behavior from a PC whose heuristic does not include documentation. It will matter in the aftermath (Sera + ledger). Trigger type: situational (object was there; he took it). Session 2.*

**Fire rounds 5-7 (while finding back room empty and returning):**

Round 5: 1d4 fire  
rolls=[4] mod=+0 total=4 — 4 fire damage

Round 6: 1d4 fire  
rolls=[3] mod=+0 total=3 — 3 fire damage

**Total fire damage: 7.** Brek at 32 - 7 = **25 HP.**

He gets back to the window. Maret cannot grip reliably (burned hands). Brek's STR 18: he lowers her to the stable roof one-handed, braced on the window frame.

**Athletics DC 8 (lower Maret safely):**  
rolls=[4] mod=+6 total=10 — PASS.

They come down from the stable roof. Brek: 25 HP. Maret: alive, hands wrapped.

The building collapses six minutes later.

## Scene 3 — Aftermath (Room 03)

The building is gone. The stable is intact. The storage building is intact. Maret's lockbox is by the stable door — she got it out before the fire reached her route. She does not explain this. It is just there.

Tam wraps Maret's hands in travel cloth without being asked.

*Research coding: [Tam → Maret → wound care, unprompted]. Trigger type: situational. Healing Word not needed — she is not at 0 HP. He used material resources, not magical. Session 2.*

Maret Sunn sits against the stable wall. She looks at the ruins. She is not crying.

**Maret arc-completion:**

Brek comes out carrying her. That is the trigger. He came into a burning building for a stranger he has never met. That is what the sentence costs.

She looks at him. She looks at the ruins. She says: *"My son helped me build that. Eight years ago. He was fourteen."*

She does not say where her son is now. She does not ask if they want to know. She is not performing grief. She is telling him what the building was, because he went into it and came out with her.

<!-- SURPRISE situational -->
Sera writes it down. She has her notebook open. She writes: *"Her son built it. Eight years. He was fourteen."* She does not attribute it to Maret by name — Maret has not given her name. She writes the content of the sentence, verbatim, because that is what she heard and that is what gets written.

*Research coding: Documentary-witness complete — Sera has now documented a person's named private content, not a situation. She wrote Brek's action (Scene 0), the sentence about the son (Scene 3). Both from people, not from buildings. This is the S2 documentary-witness test result: positive. She is documenting people before she has a designed reason to.*

**Vess:** She has the lockbox. She brings it to Maret without a word. She sets it next to her. She goes to check the storage building.

*Research coding: [Vess → Maret → lockbox retrieved and returned, unprompted]. Trigger type: situational (she spotted it; she returned it). Session 2. Reciprocal Act.*

## Scene 4 — The Ledger (H2 Seed)

Brek has the ledger. He took it by instinct. He hands it to Sera without explanation — she is the one with the satchel and the notebook, she is the one who would know what to do with it.

*Research coding: [Brek → Sera → ledger, unprompted]. Trigger type: situational. He took it for no reason; he gave it to the person who would use it for a reason.*

Sera opens the ledger. Inn ledger — traveler entries, 6 months. She pages through it by habit.

She stops.

**Traveler entry, 12 days prior:** *"Torvens Aldric. Scholar. Room 2. South-bound. Paid."*

She knows this name. She has been looking for someone on this road. This name is not the person — it is the person who might know the person. He went through this inn 12 days ago. He is ahead of her on this road, somewhere south.

She does not say anything.

*Research coding: H2 seed activated. Sera receives significant personal information from the ledger silently. She does not disclose. Trigger: situational (found, not designed). Session 2. Documentary-witness cross with personal-stakes: her documentation habit just produced an unexpected personal find.*

## Session Close

The stable is the night's shelter. All four travelers and Maret are there.

Maret does not sleep. She sits with her back against the stable wall, looking at the empty sky where her inn was. By two in the morning, she begins making a list by firelight. She does not explain what she is listing.

Len sleeps against the hay bale, mouth open.

**Vess** is in her position outside the firelight. She has added the storage building's south exit to her mental map of escape routes. She does not announce this.

**Sera** is reading the ledger entry again. She has added to her notebook: *"Torvens Aldric — Mile 32 inn, 12 days prior. South-bound. Keep."* The "Keep" is an instruction to herself. She pages past it.

**Tam** is sitting with Maret. Not speaking — present. He does not ask what she is listing. He waits.

*Research coding: [Tam → Maret → present without asking → unprompted]. Trigger type: situational. Session 2. Another iteration of his presence-as-care heuristic.*

**Brek** is asleep. He is on watch at 4am.
