# Room 03 — Pale's Subsidiary Counting House (Exterior Observation)

## Read-aloud
> Pell walks north at a steady pace, no check for tails. Two blocks, a right turn, into a counting house with a new sign — a trading company name that doesn't match the building's age. The sign is less than a year old. He goes in. Darro clocks the lock on the front: standard commercial, recent installation.

## Features

- **The tailing check.** Darro's Stealth DC 13 (Darro's Stealth is +8 — he succeeds automatically unless a complication fires). The party observes from across the street.
- **The counting house exterior.** Investigation DC 12 from across the street: the sign's paint style matches a property register style used by Pale's known legitimate business. Tessaly's Investigation (+7) auto-succeeds this.
- **What they observe.** Pell enters. A window on the second floor has a curtain move — someone is watching the street from upstairs. They are not watching the party specifically; they are watching generally. Passive Perception DC 10 to notice the curtain.
- **The thirty-minute wait.** Pell does not emerge in thirty minutes. He is meeting with someone. The party has no way to know who. They file the location and withdraw.
- **Cael's observation.** After the curtain moves: *"He knew."* Two words. He turns and starts walking back toward Cordwainer Lane.

## GM Notes

This is Cael's professional-reading moment — he identifies Pell as operating within an organised framework rather than acting independently. "He knew" means: Pell was following instructions. The counting house confirms the instructions came from Pale's structure. This is sheet-deep-reader: Cael reads professional behavior patterns the way Tessaly reads financial records.

If the party does not tail: they do not see the counting house this session. Tessaly's ledger gets the note "Pell — Pale-adjacent; test confirmed." The counting house is identified in S6 when Tessaly traces Pale's financial structure directly.
