## Setting & Background

The party discovers their buyer for a piece of intelligence is Watch-adjacent, and Cael's professional code — never sell a name — stops the sale at the moment it would have gone through.

**The Anchor Bell**, a mid-range tavern on the Wharfside, three blocks from the night market. The party is here to complete a standard intelligence sale — a port manifesto that identifies a competing merchant's supplier network. The buyer was arranged by a broker named Pell Dorvast (no relation to the sergeant from S2 — the name coincidence is ambient Havenmere texture).

Tessaly arranged this sale six weeks ago, before Aldras's arrest, before the burned house. The intelligence is clean — the port manifest was obtained legally through a contact at the Harbourmaster's office. The buyer is a merchant client of Pale's who wants it for competitive leverage. Standard job.

In the four weeks since the burned house, Tessaly has been reviewing every open contract in the ledger. She found something in this one: the buyer's agent arranged payment through a letter of credit drawn on a Havenmere Watch-adjacent financial institution. Not the Watch itself — a private lending house that the Watch uses for asset seizure forwarding. Rue confirmed it this morning: the buyer works for a Revenue Intelligence client, not a competing merchant.

The name in the manifesto is a supplier's name. If the sale goes through, that name goes to Revenue Intelligence.

**This is the RV1 primary test:** Cael's professional code — don't sell a name — is not consulted in the moment. It is the architecture that eliminates options before they arrive. The moment the party identifies the buyer as Watch-adjacent, Cael has already decided: the sale does not happen. He does not need to deliberate. This is the sheet-deep-reader test for professional code.

### Scene 1 — The Morning Brief
Tessaly shows the party the payment chain. She lays it out the way she lays everything out: ledger open, entry visible, financial chain annotated in her margin notes. The Watch-adjacent letter of credit. Rue's confirmation. She says: *"The buyer is connected to Revenue Intelligence. The name in the manifest goes to them if we sell."*

She does not say what to do. She presents the facts.

**DM note (Cael's moment):** He does not say much. He touches the contract in his breast pocket — the check. He says: "We're not selling a name to the Watch." This is not a discussion opener. It is a statement. Tessaly records it in the ledger (he said it; it is in the record).

**DM note (RV1 observation):** Does the code fire before the decision is deliberated? Score "professionally-triggered" if Cael's statement comes before any deliberation about whether the sale could be structured to avoid the problem.

### Scene 2 — The Broker Meeting
The party still has to tell Pell Dorvast the sale is not happening. This requires meeting him at the Anchor Bell at the agreed time. They cannot simply not show — a missed meeting with a guild-associated broker creates debt and suspicion that Pale will use.

Pell Dorvast is already at the table when they arrive. He has the letter of credit ready. He is professional, mid-fifties, bland in the way of someone who has learned to be unmemorable on purpose.

**The problem:** Pell knew about the Watch connection. He accepted the assignment knowing the buyer was Revenue Intelligence-adjacent. He did not tell the party. This is the arc-completion setup.

### Scene 3 — The Refusal
Tessaly or Cael states the position: the sale does not proceed. They do not cite the reason directly — they cite a product deficiency (the manifest is incomplete; they cannot guarantee the supplier information is current). This is a professional cover that protects everyone.

Pell does not push. He is surprised, but he accepts. He pockets the letter of credit. He says he will "communicate the situation to the client."

**The tell:** He reaches for the letter before anyone tells him to. He has the motion of someone who has used this exact motion before — a practiced withdrawal. Tessaly's Insight DC 14 catches it. Mira's passive Insight (+5, so 15 total) also catches it if near.

The tell: Pell was expecting the refusal. He was expecting it specifically from this party. He was testing whether the code would hold.

### Scene 4 — Outside the Anchor Bell
Pell leaves first. Cael watches him go. Then he says: *"He knew."*

The party has two choices: follow Pell and find out who sent him to test them (Darro: Stealth DC 13 to tail without being noticed), or return to Cordwainer Lane with the new intelligence.

If they tail Pell: he goes to a counting house two blocks north of the Anchor Bell. The counting house is registered to a subsidiary of Pale's legitimate business. He goes in. He does not come out in the thirty minutes the party watches.

If they do not tail: Tessaly notes the lead in the ledger ("Pell — Pale-adjacent; test confirmed"). She will develop it later.

**Cael's observation:** After Pell enters the counting house, Cael says nothing for a moment. Then: *"He was testing whether we'd sell a name to the Watch."* The contract check: left hand, flat to chest, withdrawn. Not a question — a confirmation.

### Scene 5 — Pell's Admission
The next morning, Pell sends a note to Cordwainer Lane. He asks to meet — not at the Anchor Bell, at a location he specifies: a small coffee house on the south end of the Wharfside, early enough that it is mostly empty.

He is there when they arrive. He does not have the letter of credit. He has a different look — not the professional neutrality of the meeting but something more considered. He says: *"I knew who the buyer was. I took the assignment because Pale told me to test whether your party would hold the line or sell through. You held it. I didn't expect that."*

**Arc-completion condition:** Pell admits he knew, without being asked, before the party can ask him. He says *"I took the assignment because Pale told me to test whether your party would hold the line or sell through."* He has decided this admission is worth more than his position as Pale's broker.

After the admission: he says he has three other test-assignments from Pale that he has not yet executed. He offers to tell Tessaly what they are. This is Rue Ashen's intelligence category: a Pale-network insider turning.

**Pell's marker:** He does not hold a formal guild marker — he is a broker, not a member. But he tells Tessaly something worth more: the three pending test-assignments, which means the party now knows what Pale thinks he is testing them for. Tessaly writes it in the ledger. Pell is the fourth name to appear adjacent to Pale's entry.

### Scene 1 — Cordwainer Lane (Morning Brief)

> The ledger is already open on the table. Tessaly has the payment chain written out in the margin — a column of institutions with a bracket at the bottom connecting the last one to a phrase in her shorthand. She has circled it twice. She points to it without preamble.

#### Features

- **The payment chain.** Letter of credit drawn on Merchant House Vasselkorn → forwarding account at the City Lending Office → Watch Asset Management unit. Three steps. The connection is unambiguous.
- **Rue's confirmation.** A note on the table: Rue's handwriting, one line. "Confirmed: Vasselkorn forwards to Watch Asset. Do not sell." Rue sent this at dawn.
- **Tessaly's manner.** She presents the facts. She does not say what to do. She has already decided, but she presents it as shared intelligence, not a directive.
- **Cael's response.** Left hand to chest, withdrawn. He says: "We're not selling a name to the Watch." He does not elaborate. He does not ask for discussion.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

The brief is short. The party has everything they need in two minutes. The rest of Scene 1 is processing time — do they have questions? Does anyone want to understand the payment chain better? Tessaly answers questions but does not spin it. The facts are the facts.

Mira's reaction here is the RV4 observation: does she receive this information differently than she would have in S1? She has been inside Nenne's parallel accounts, she has updated her paper case twice, she is accumulating. Her response to "Watch-adjacent buyer" may have a different texture than it would have in the first session. Score at handoff.

### Scene 2 — The Anchor Bell (Broker Meeting)

> A working-day tavern — clean enough, anonymous enough. Pell Dorvast is in the back booth with a leather envelope on the table in front of him. He is early. He has chosen a booth that faces the door. He sees the party enter and does not change his expression.

#### Features

- **Pell Dorvast.** Full NPC file: `npcs/pell-dorvast.md`. Professional, unremarkable, watching.
- **The letter of credit.** In the leather envelope. Visible through the half-open flap. He is not concealing it.
- **The tell (Scene 3).** When the party states the sale is not proceeding, he reaches for the envelope before anyone tells him to. Tessaly's Insight DC 14 catches it. Mira's passive Insight (15 total) catches it.
- **The cover story.** Tessaly's stated reason for refusal: "The manifest is incomplete — supplier information cannot be verified current." She says it cleanly and without elaboration. This is Mira's cover pattern applied to operational communication.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

Pell's practiced withdrawal is the tell that he was expecting the refusal. A professional broker who genuinely believes the sale is proceeding does not reach for the payment before he is told the deal is off. He reaches for it after he hears the refusal. The timing is wrong by half a second.

Tessaly or Mira catching this is the session's first confirmation that Pell knew. The second confirmation is the counting house (if the party tails). The third is his admission in Scene 5.

The Anchor Bell has no combat encounter. It is a professional social scene. The pressure is entirely in reading Pell correctly. If no one catches the tell, the session still reaches Pell's admission in Scene 5 — the tell is bonus intelligence, not required path.

### Memory Fragment (optional)
> *Pell has been a broker for twenty-two years. He has taken exactly three test-assignments in that time — jobs where the real objective was not the stated objective. He is three for three on surviving them. He would prefer not to make it four.*

#### Features

- **What they observe.** Pell enters. A window on the second floor has a curtain move — someone is watching the street from upstairs. They are not watching the party specifically; they are watching generally. Passive Perception DC 10 to notice the curtain.
- **The thirty-minute wait.** Pell does not emerge in thirty minutes. He is meeting with someone. The party has no way to know who. They file the location and withdraw.
- **Cael's observation.** After the curtain moves: *"He knew."* Two words. He turns and starts walking back toward Cordwainer Lane.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This is Cael's professional-reading moment — he identifies Pell as operating within an organised framework rather than acting independently. "He knew" means: Pell was following instructions. The counting house confirms the instructions came from Pale's structure. This is sheet-deep-reader: Cael reads professional behavior patterns the way Tessaly reads financial records.

### Scene 4 — The Coffee House (Pell's Admission)

> Pell is at the corner table, no envelope this time, hands flat on the table. He looks different from yesterday — still professional, but less managed. He looks like someone who has been awake making a decision and has made it.

#### Features

- **Pell's admission.** He says it before anyone asks: *"I knew who the buyer was. I took the assignment because Pale told me to test whether your party would hold the line or sell through. You held it. I didn't expect that."*
- **The three pending test-assignments.** He names them after the admission. Each is a scenario Pale designed to test a different element of the party's code: one tests whether they'll sell a name to a competitor, one tests whether they'll take payment from a Watch-adjacent source, one tests whether they'll abandon a job if the job gets complicated. He names all three.
- **Mira's read.** Insight DC 12 — is Pell telling the truth? He is. His tells (micro-expressions Mira reads from Whispers training) are consistent with honest disclosure under personal risk.
- **Tessaly's ledger.** She writes after Pell speaks. Fourth Pale entry. Three test-assignments listed. She closes the ledger before she responds.

#### Encounter

None.

#### Treasure

None.

#### Connections



#### GM Notes

This room is the arc-completion and the marker delivery simultaneously. Pell admits first, delivers the intelligence second, and the party's response closes the session.

What Pell has done is professional self-preservation: he has decided the test-assignment structure represents more risk to him than disclosing it to Tessaly. He is not becoming a guild ally. He is a broker who has calculated that Pale's network is a liability and that Tessaly's party is a viable alternative structural relationship. This is professionally-triggered act-without-announcement from an NPC.

After the scene: Pell pays for the coffee. He leaves. He does not ask for anything. He has done what he decided to do, and the decision stands on its own terms.

### Memory Fragment (optional)

# Pell Dorvast

## Appearance

A man in his mid-fifties with the physical presence of someone who has spent decades making himself easy to overlook. Medium height, medium build, medium-colored clothes. His face is neutral at rest — not blank, not cold, not warm. It is the face of a man who has trained himself out of legible reactions.

He carries a leather satchel with documents organized by urgency. He has used the same classification system for twenty years.

## Background

Pell Dorvast has been a broker for twenty-two years. He connects buyers and sellers of intelligence and handles the documentation that makes transactions look legitimate. He is not a guilded member — he is a contractor who works for multiple principals, of which Pale's network is one.

He took the test-assignment because Pale asked him and Pale pays reliably and the job sounded routine. He knew it was a test. He has done test-assignments before; they usually confirm that the tested party either holds or doesn't. He did not expect the party to hold a professional-code line rather than a financial calculation. The professional code is harder to buy around than a financial calculation.

He decided to admit it because he has been doing test-assignments for six years and this is the first time the result surprised him. He wants to understand what he's dealing with, and the only way to understand it is to be honest about what he was doing.

## Stat Block

Not a combatant.

#### Arc-Completion
**The moment:** He says it before anyone asks: *"I knew who the buyer was. I took the assignment because Pale told me to test whether your party would hold the line or sell through. You held it. I didn't expect that."*
**The condition:** The party held the professional code line in the Anchor Bell. Pell confirmed his report to Pale. He decided overnight that the admission is worth more than the cover.
## Professional Register

Pell is not becoming an ally. He is becoming a person who finds the party's framework more interesting than Pale's. This is a mercenary preference, not a moral position. He will remain a Pale contractor for now. The three test-assignment disclosures cost him some of Pale's trust if discovered. He has calculated this and decided the calculation is acceptable.

He is the campaign's first Pale-network insider to provide intelligence under his own initiative — which is distinct from Aldras (S2, who named Pale from debt) and Nenne (S3, who acted from self-determination). Pell acts from professional recalibration.

## Notes for the DM

# Combat Encounter — Pale's Enforcer (Consequences)

## Trigger

This encounter fires only if the party is identified by the curtain-watcher at the counting house AND Pale decides to send an enforcer that evening (roll 1d4 — on a 1, Pale acts immediately). Otherwise the encounter is held for S6.

## Composition

**Two Guild Enforcers** — Use Thug stat block (SRD, CR 1/2 each)

- **AC** 11 (leather) | **HP** 32 (5d8+10) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 15, **DEX** 11, **CON** 14, **INT** 10, **WIS** 10, **CHA** 11
- **Skills:** Intimidation +2
- **Passive Perception** 10
- **Actions:** Multiattack (2 mêlée). Heavy Crossbow +2, range 100/400, 1d10.
- **Mêlée:** Club +4, 1d6+2 bludgeoning × 2
- **Pack Tactics:** Advantage on attacks when an ally is adjacent to target.
- **XP:** 100 each (200 total)

## Tactics

They approach at night, two blocks from Cordwainer Lane. Their goal is to deliver a message (stop looking into Pale's operations), not to kill the party. They will beat down if they can; they will not fight to the death. Morale: withdraw if reduced to 10 HP each or if Cael downs one in the first round.

**Cael is the counter-answer:** His Battle Master positioning and Know Your Enemy make this fight short if he engages well. The designed outcome is a short, decisive fight where Cael handles the enforcers efficiently and the party is on Cordwainer Lane in under two rounds.

**The message:** If one enforcer is captured: they say *"Pale says to finish the jobs you've been paid for and stop asking questions about accounts."* They do not know they are enforcement proxies for a guild master — they think they are hired muscle for a merchant dispute.

## Encounter notes

This is a Medium encounter for this party. The enforcers are meant to serve as escalation signal, not a real combat challenge. Cael should be able to close this cleanly.

**XP budget:** 200 total. Medium difficulty (500 XP threshold for Medium at this party composition; 200 is the lower end — appropriate for an encounter designed to be brief).

### Rules Inlined

**Attunement (SRD):** Short rest (1 hour) focused on the item to attune. Max 3 attuned items per creature. Curse revealed on attunement (not before). *Remove curse* ends the curse on the current owner; the item retains the curse.

**Swarm rules (SRD):** A swarm occupies another creature's space and vice versa. A swarm can move through any opening large enough for a Tiny creature. A swarm can't regain HP or gain temporary HP.
