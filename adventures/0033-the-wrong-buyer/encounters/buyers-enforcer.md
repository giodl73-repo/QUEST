# Combat Encounter — Pale's Enforcer (Consequences)

## Trigger

This encounter fires only if the party is identified by the curtain-watcher at the counting house AND Pale decides to send an enforcer that evening (roll 1d4 — on a 1, Pale acts immediately). Otherwise the encounter is held for S6.

## Composition

**Two Guild Enforcers** — Use Thug stat block (SRD, CR 1/2 each)

- **AC** 11 (leather) | **HP** 32 (5d8+10) | **Speed** 30 ft.
- **STR** 15, **DEX** 11, **CON** 14, **INT** 10, **WIS** 10, **CHA** 11
- **Skills:** Intimidation +2
- **Passive Perception** 10
- **Actions:** Multiattack (2 mêlée). Heavy Crossbow +2, range 100/400, 1d10.
- **Mêlée:** Club +4, 1d6+2 bludgeoning × 2
- **Pack Tactics:** Advantage on attacks when an ally is adjacent to target.
- **XP:** 100 each (200 total)

## Tactics

They approach at night, two blocks from Cordwainer Lane. Their goal is to deliver a message (stop looking into Pale's operations), not to kill the party. They will beat down if they can; they will not fight to the death. Morale: withdraw if reduced to 10 HP each or if Cael downs one in the first round.

**Cael is the counter-answer:** His Battle Master positioning and Know Your Enemy make this fight short if he engages well. The designed outcome is a short, decisive fight where Cael handles the enforcers efficiently and the party is on Cordwainer Lane in under two rounds.

**The message:** If one enforcer is captured: they say *"Pale says to finish the jobs you've been paid for and stop asking questions about accounts."* They do not know they are enforcement proxies for a guild master — they think they are hired muscle for a merchant dispute.

## Encounter notes

This is a Medium encounter for this party. The enforcers are meant to serve as escalation signal, not a real combat challenge. Cael should be able to close this cleanly.

**XP budget:** 200 total. Medium difficulty (500 XP threshold for Medium at this party composition; 200 is the lower end — appropriate for an encounter designed to be brief).
