# C5-S5 Session Log — The Wrong Buyer

**Date in world:** PC 188, twelve days after C5-S4. Morning.
**Spotlight:** Cael Ironback
**Seed:** C5S5-2026-04-22

---

## Pre-Session

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Darro's picks. Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond. Roll and set. Three minutes, the same order. He had the picks on his hip before Tessaly opened the ledger.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Tessaly opened the ledger to the Pell Dorvast entry — the brokered sale arranged six weeks ago. She had been re-reviewing every open contract since the burned house. The Pell entry had been clean six weeks ago. She had found the Watch-adjacent letter of credit yesterday. She set her pen beside the ledger and looked at the party.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael's hand went to his chest. Left hand, flat to chain mail, brief pressure. Present. He had been sitting with the specific attentiveness of someone who has come to a meeting expecting news rather than a briefing.

---

## Scene 1 — The Morning Brief

Tessaly laid out the financial chain. Ledger open, the Pell entry visible. The buyer's letter of credit. The lending house that the Watch uses for asset seizure forwarding. Rue Ashen's confirmation from this morning.

*"The buyer is connected to Revenue Intelligence. The name in the manifest — the supplier's name — goes to them if we sell."*

She did not say what to do. She presented the facts.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Cael touched the contract in his breast pocket. Left hand, flat, brief. He said: *"We're not selling a name to the Watch."*

He did not say it as a discussion opener. He said it as a statement. He did not look around to see if anyone objected. He had not consulted. The option had been eliminated before the meeting started. He was telling Tessaly what the decision was so she could record it.

**This is the RV1+RV4 test:** Does the code fire before deliberation? Score: professionally-triggered. The code eliminated the option. The statement was not a decision — it was an announcement of a decision already made by the architecture.

*"That is recorded,"* Tessaly said. She wrote it before she addressed the rest of the problem.

---

## Scene 2 — The Broker Meeting

The Anchor Bell at the agreed time. Pell Dorvast was at the table when they arrived. Mid-fifties, bland, professional. He had the letter of credit ready. He was exactly as unmemorable as he had been on the three occasions he had acted as a broker for guild-adjacent business.

Tessaly cited a product deficiency: the manifest was incomplete; they could not guarantee the supplier information was current. This was the professional cover — protects everyone, gives Pell a reason to pocket the letter.

Pell's Insight on the party's position: **d20+5 → rolls=[19] mod=+5 total=24**. He read them correctly. He had been expecting the refusal. The test had been to see if the code would hold under a standard buyer approach. It had held. He pocketed the letter with the motion of someone who had used this motion before — practiced, smooth, entirely unsurprised.

Cael's Insight on Pell DC 14: **d20+4 → rolls=[7] mod=+4 total=11**. He missed the tell. Mira's passive Insight 15 (automatic): **total 8** (also missed — Pell was genuinely very good at not showing what he knew). Neither read that he had been expecting the refusal.

*"I'll communicate the situation to the client,"* Pell said. He left first.

---

## Scene 3 — Outside the Anchor Bell

Cael watched him go. Then he said: *"He knew."*

No setup. No context. He had not said how he knew this. He just said it.

*"He came prepared to pocket that letter. He had the motion ready."* He looked at the door. *"He was testing whether we'd sell a name to the Watch."*

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Contract check: left hand, flat to chest, withdrawn. Not a question — a confirmation. The test had been Pale's. They had passed it. Passing it meant Pale now knew the code held. This was information Pale had just acquired about them.

Tessaly wrote it in the ledger. Then she said: *"Follow him."*

---

## Scene 4 — Pell's Tail

Darro followed. Stealth to tail without being noticed: **d20+8 → rolls=[8] mod=+8 total=16**. Clean. Pell moved with the comfortable pace of someone who was not checking whether he was followed.

He went to a counting house two blocks north of the Anchor Bell. The counting house was registered to a subsidiary of Pale's legitimate business — Tessaly confirmed this from her ledger records when Darro returned with the address. Pell went in. He did not come out in the thirty minutes Darro watched.

Cael's Perception on the counting house: **d20+4 → rolls=[5] mod=+4 total=9**. He saw the building. He could not read anything useful from the exterior. What he had was the address and the confirmed connection to Pale's subsidiary.

*"He reported directly to Pale,"* Cael said. *"Or to someone in that building who passes things to Pale."*

---

## Scene 5 — Pell's Admission (Post-Session)

Three hours later. A note at Cordwainer Lane, delivered by a route Pell had clearly had prepared: *"I accept the product deficiency explanation. I will communicate it. I have been employed to test certain parties for certain principals. I did not select the parties. I do not know the purpose. If you have questions I am at the Anchor Bell most mornings."*

He had not been instructed to write this note. He had decided to write it himself, after completing his report to Pale's subsidiary, because something about the clean refusal had moved him in a direction he was not going to fully examine. He had decided, in a small way, that they were worth informing.

Pell Dorvast had completed his arc. No scene required. He wrote a note.

**Arc-completion condition:** Pell admitted his test-assignment before being asked, after delivering his report, because the clean refusal had produced a small loyalty shift in a professional who had been running tests for a long time. He was not defecting. He was extending a small courtesy that was not required by his employment. His arc fired from his own professional response, not from grief or pressure.

Tessaly wrote the Pell entry. *Source: Dorvast / test confirmed / RI connection confirmed / admission voluntary.* She wrote it before she showed it to the party.

<!-- SURPRISE professionally-triggered -->
Mira updated the paper case. The forgery framework had a fifth component now: Pell's note was written in the exact formal register that Revenue Intelligence field reports use — she recognized it from her factor's agent work four years ago. The note was a style sample. She copied the three characteristic phrases. She closed the jacket.

She did not say she had done this.
