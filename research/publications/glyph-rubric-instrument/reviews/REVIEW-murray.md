# Review: An 8-Dimension Playtest Rubric as a Research Instrument for AI-Simulated Narrative Game Sessions
**Reviewer:** Janet Murray, Georgia Tech Digital Media
**Score:** 3 — accept
**Expertise:** interactive narrative, cyberdrama, digital storytelling

## Summary
This paper makes a substantive contribution to the emerging area of AI-authored narrative game evaluation. The 8-dimension rubric is coherent, the 49-session corpus is substantial for the field, and the paper's central insight — that a rubric should evolve with the sessions it evaluates — is epistemologically sound. From an interactive narrative perspective, the rubric's attention to dimensions like Character Agency, Atmospheric Landing, and NPC Arc-Completion reflects a genuine understanding of what distinguishes good narrative game design from mere procedural variety.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Inter-rater reliability**: Even granting that AI-simulated sessions present novel scoring challenges, a research instrument must demonstrate that its scores are reproducible by independent raters. The paper should include an inter-rater study or explicitly reframe the contribution as a design and annotation framework rather than a validated instrument.

## Minor Issues
- **Comparison to existing playtest tools**: The gap in related work around prior playtesting rubrics and narrative evaluation frameworks (e.g., Marie-Laure Ryan's narrative theory applied to games, Mateas & Stern on interactive drama) weakens the theoretical positioning.
- **Archetype-independence claim**: The claim that the rubric is archetype-independent is asserted across 3 campaigns but not systematically demonstrated. A table showing rubric dimension scores disaggregated by campaign archetype would make this claim empirically tractable.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction situates the work well within AI and game design. The claim that the rubric is "the first systematic evaluation instrument for AI-simulated TRPG sessions" is probably true and worth making more boldly, with appropriate literature support.

### Methodology
The 8 dimensions are well-chosen and clearly defined. The paper would benefit from a short rationale for why 8 dimensions (rather than 5 or 12) — even a brief argument from the amendment history showing that dimensions were added only when the corpus forced them would suffice.

### Evaluation
The per-campaign session score graphs are this paper's most readable evidence. The Conclave Compact campaign's higher floor score relative to Campaign 1 is interesting — is this attributable to rubric maturation, party archetype, or the adventure design? Teasing this apart briefly would strengthen the cross-campaign validation claim.

## Recommendation
Accept with minor revisions. The inter-rater reliability gap should be addressed in the revision — either by adding a study or by revising the contribution framing. The comparison-to-existing-tools gap should be closed in related work. Both are manageable within a revision cycle.
