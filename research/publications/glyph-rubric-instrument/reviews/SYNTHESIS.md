# Review Synthesis: An 8-Dimension Playtest Rubric as a Research Instrument for AI-Simulated Narrative Game Sessions

**Venue:** CHI 2027
**Mean Score:** 2.8 (Bogost 3, Bernstein 2, Murray 3, Morningstar 3, Torner 3)
**Recommendation:** Revise and Resubmit

---

## P1 — Priority 1 (3+ reviewers, blocking)

**Inter-rater reliability is absent.**
All five reviewers named this. Bernstein called it blocking. The paper claims to offer a "research instrument" but provides no evidence that independent scorers would reach consistent scores. This is the single revision that must be completed before the paper can be accepted.

- **Required fix**: Recruit a second scorer to blind-rate 10–15 sessions. Compute inter-rater agreement (Cohen's kappa or Krippendorff's alpha). Report the number and discuss it. If agreement is high, the instrument claim is validated. If agreement is low, revise the contribution framing from "validated research instrument" to "design artifact and annotation framework under validation."
- **Fallback**: If a second-scorer study cannot be conducted in the revision window, the paper must substantially revise its contribution claim throughout — removing all uses of "research instrument" as a validated category and substituting language that acknowledges the current single-scorer limitation as an open validation question.

---

## P2 — Priority 2 (2+ reviewers, required for acceptance)

**Comparison to existing playtest tools is missing.**
Four of five reviewers noted the absence of related work on prior playtesting frameworks (Fullerton's game design workshop protocols, GBL rubrics, practitioner playtesting tools from Bully Pulpit, Pelgrane, the Gauntlet).

- **Required fix**: Add a related work section or substantially expand the existing one. The paper does not need to claim superiority — it needs to locate its contribution. A 2–3 paragraph treatment of the landscape, ending with a clear statement of what this paper adds, is sufficient.

---

## P3 — Advisory (1–2 reviewers, strengthens but does not block)

**Dragonlance setting limitations.**
Three reviewers noted the single-setting constraint. This is acknowledged in the paper but undertreated. A brief taxonomy of which rubric dimensions are setting-agnostic versus setting-inflected would improve transferability claims and preempt reviewer concerns at camera-ready.

---

## Convergent Strengths (Do Not Revise Away)

- 49-session corpus spanning 3 campaigns: all reviewers acknowledged this as a genuine strength. Protect it prominently in the abstract.
- Amendment pipeline (dynamic rubric evolution): Bogost, Morningstar, and Torner all cited this as the paper's most interesting methodological contribution. Make it more prominent.
- Archetype-independence across 3 distinct party types: Torner specifically noted this should be in the abstract. Move it forward.

---

## Divergent Notes

- Bogost and Murray are ready to accept with revisions; Morningstar is ready to accept. Bernstein is the hold-out at weak accept — his condition is specifically the inter-rater reliability study. Torner accepts conditionally on the same.
- No reviewer recommended rejection. The paper is viable at CHI if the P1 revision is executed.
