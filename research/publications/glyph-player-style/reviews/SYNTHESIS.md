# Review Synthesis: Emergent Player Styles in AI-Simulated TRPG: From Innovation Instances to Ratified Design Patterns

**Venue:** FDG 2027
**Mean Score:** 3.0 (Bogost 3, Juul 2, Morningstar 4, Torner 3, Sicart 3)
**Recommendation:** Accept with Revisions

---

## P1 — Priority 1 (3+ reviewers, blocking)

**Justify or calibrate the promotion threshold empirically.**
All five reviewers named the 3/2 threshold as unjustified. This is the paper's primary methodological vulnerability. The threshold determines which behaviors become ratified styles and which do not — a different threshold produces a different taxonomy.

- **Required fix (preferred)**: Conduct a threshold sensitivity analysis. Run the promotion pipeline at threshold values 2/1, 3/2, 4/3, and 5/4. For each: report the resulting style count, which styles appear or disappear, and the mean gate trajectory under each taxonomy. If the seven-style taxonomy is stable across adjacent thresholds, the threshold is defensible. If it is not stable, acknowledge this as a limitation and characterize the stability range.
- **Minimum acceptable fix**: Add a design rationale paragraph explaining what the threshold is trading off. E.g., "We chose 3 as the session-level minimum to distinguish a player style from an individual session habit, and 2 as the cross-session minimum to require replication across independent contexts." Acknowledge the threshold as a design decision subject to calibration rather than an empirically determined value.

---

## P2 — Priority 2 (2+ reviewers, required for acceptance)

**Distinguish act-without-announcement v1.5 from v1.12 variant more clearly.**
Three reviewers noted the act-without-announcement variants appear to be two related but distinct styles that are currently under-differentiated. The paper needs either:

- A clear distinguishing criterion: what makes v1.12 a distinct style rather than a variant of v1.5? (Morningstar's suggestion: restraint vs. action as the distinguishing axis is a good start)
- Or: merge the variants into a single style with a named sub-type structure, acknowledging that the style has contextual variants

**Add Bartle/Yee comparison to related work.**
Torner, Bogost, and Sicart all requested this. Any player-type taxonomy paper must engage with Bartle's four types and Yee's Motivational Model. The paper does not need to claim superiority; it needs to locate the contribution. A 1–2 paragraph comparison noting overlap and divergence is sufficient.

---

## P3 — Advisory (1–2 reviewers, strengthens but does not block)

**Design theory grounding (Sicart).**
The craft-witness and verbal-documentation-arc styles raise interesting questions about reflexivity and play-as-documentation that prior player-type models do not address. A brief connection to play theory would deepen the theoretical contribution.

---

## Convergent Strengths (Do Not Revise Away)

- Cross-archetype validation: all five reviewers cited this as the paper's strongest result. Do not compress it.
- RV1–RV12 systematic annotation: the annotation system gives the taxonomy an evidence base prior player-style taxonomies lack. Describe it fully.
- 4 PC-authored deliverables: Morningstar flagged this as underexploited. Elevate it to a named result.
- Innovation-to-style pipeline as method: Torner and Sicart both want this framed as a methodology contribution alongside the taxonomy. Do both in the abstract.

---

## Divergent Notes

- Juul (weak accept) is the hold-out: his condition is specifically the threshold justification. Path B (design rationale paragraph) should be sufficient to bring him to accept.
- Morningstar (strong accept) provides strong practitioner endorsement but his review does not substitute for addressing Juul's and Sicart's academic concerns.
- The paper is at FDG's acceptance threshold and should proceed after P1/P2 revision.
