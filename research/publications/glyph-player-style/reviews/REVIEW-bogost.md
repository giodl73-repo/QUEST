# Review: Emergent Player Styles in AI-Simulated TRPG: From Innovation Instances to Ratified Design Patterns
**Reviewer:** Ian Bogost, Georgia Tech / Washington University in St. Louis
**Score:** 3 — accept
**Expertise:** game criticism, procedural rhetoric

## Summary
This paper makes a genuine contribution to player-type taxonomy research in a setting — AI-simulated TRPG — where such taxonomy has not previously been attempted systematically. The seven identified styles are plausible, the promotion pipeline (innovation instance → threshold → ratified style) is principled in structure if not yet fully justified in its numerical threshold, and the cross-archetype validation claim is the paper's strongest empirical result. The Bartle/Yee comparison is conspicuously absent and should be included.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Promotion threshold justification**: The 3/2 threshold (3 innovations in a session OR 2 across 2+ sessions) is presented without empirical justification. Why not 4/3? Why not 2/1? A sensitivity analysis showing how the style count and composition change at adjacent threshold values would demonstrate that 3/2 is not arbitrary. This is the paper's primary methodological vulnerability.

## Minor Issues
- **Bartle/Yee comparison absent**: Any paper proposing a player-style taxonomy in 2027 must engage with Bartle's four types and Yee's Motivational Model. Not to claim superiority — but to locate the contribution. The paper should explain what AI-simulated TRPG styles share with and diverge from these frameworks.
- **Act-without-announcement variant distinction**: The paper has multiple act-without-announcement instances that appear to be variations (v1.5 baseline; v1.12 variant). The distinction between them is noted but not clearly argued. Readers should be able to distinguish these as meaningfully different styles.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
Good framing. The innovation-to-style pipeline as the paper's contribution is well-stated. The paper should open with a crisp definition of "player style" as used here — the term carries a lot of baggage from prior work.

### Methodology
The RV1–RV12 systematic annotation is the paper's methodological backbone and it is described clearly. The promotion pipeline diagram (if one exists) should be a figure in the paper, not prose description. The 4 PC-authored deliverables as an outcome measure are interesting and underexploited.

### Evaluation
The cross-archetype validation table is the paper's best evidence. The claim that all seven styles appear across at least two of the three campaigns is strong. A per-style confidence level (how many cross-campaign instances per style, not just whether they appeared) would make the claim more precise.

## Recommendation
Accept with revisions. The threshold justification and Bartle/Yee comparison are the required revisions. Both are manageable. The paper is a solid FDG contribution.
