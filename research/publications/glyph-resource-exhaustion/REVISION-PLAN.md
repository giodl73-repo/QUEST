# Revision Plan: glyph-resource-exhaustion

**Paper:** Resource Exhaustion as Narrative Amplifier: Mechanical Attrition in Long-Form Tabletop RPG
**Venue:** FDG 2027
**Mean Score:** 2.6
**Status:** Revise and Resubmit

---

## P1 Checklist — Must complete before resubmission

- [ ] **Address archetype confound**

  Choose one path:

  **Path A — Controlled comparison analysis (preferred if data available):**
  - Identify CA scores for non-resource-exhaustion campaigns in the corpus that share archetype properties with C4 (military) and C6 (craft-witness/unit-cohesion)
  - Compare CA trajectories: do military/craft-witness archetypes show CA gains similar to C4/C6 without the resource mechanic?
  - If resource-exhaustion campaigns show CA gains above the archetype baseline, report this as partial confound control
  - Add Table N: "CA Scores by Archetype and Resource Mechanic Condition"

  **Path B — Hypothesis paper reframing (required if Path A data is not available):**
  - Revise abstract, introduction, and conclusion to replace causal language ("resource exhaustion amplifies") with association language ("resource exhaustion is associated with Character Agency gain in two campaigns with military and craft-witness archetypes")
  - Add explicit confound acknowledgment in Section 4: "The two resource-exhaustion campaigns in this corpus are both archetypes predisposed to unit-cohesion and narrative depth. The Character Agency gains observed may reflect archetype properties, resource mechanic effects, or an interaction. Controlled replication is required to disentangle these."
  - Add Section 6: "Study Design for Causal Inference" — specify: resource-exhaustion campaign with a non-unit-cohesion archetype (e.g., balanced-classic or puzzle-focused party); comparison campaigns matched on archetype without resource mechanic; same evaluator, same rubric

  **Both paths require:**
  - Remove all causal language from the abstract ("resource exhaustion causes," "resource exhaustion produces") unless Path A provides partial confound control
  - The abstract must accurately represent the evidence level

---

## P2 Checklist — Must complete before resubmission

- [ ] **Validate Character Agency against an independent measure or acknowledge the gap**

  Choose one path:

  **Path A — Comparison within corpus:**
  - Compare CA score behavior across all campaigns in the corpus
  - Identify whether CA scores correlate with party archetype independent of resource mechanic
  - Report the analysis; if archetype is a strong CA predictor independent of the mechanic, this is evidence the confound is real

  **Path B — Explicit limitation acknowledgment:**
  - Add to limitations section: "The Character Agency score is derived from the same rubric co-developed alongside the campaigns being analyzed. If the rubric is sensitive to archetype properties, CA scores may reflect archetype rather than mechanic effects. Independent validation of CA as a mechanic-sensitive measure is future work."
  - Describe the independent CA validation study: what external measure of narrative engagement (player-authored content count, choice complexity rating) could be correlated with CA scores?

- The unit-cohesion mechanism description — the paper's strongest theoretical contribution; expand it, do not compress
- The design principles — elevate from conclusion to a dedicated section
