# Review: Resource Exhaustion as Narrative Amplifier: Mechanical Attrition in Long-Form Tabletop RPG
**Reviewer:** Jason Morningstar, Bully Pulpit Games
**Score:** 3 — accept
**Expertise:** tabletop RPG design, playtesting, narrative structure

## Summary
This paper describes something practitioners know and have no good way to articulate: that players who are running out of resources make more interesting decisions, and those interesting decisions make for more memorable stories. The C4 and C6 findings are modest but real. The unit-cohesion mechanism is the most interesting part of the paper — the idea that shared attrition creates shared narrative stakes is a design principle worth formalizing. The archetype confound is real and should be acknowledged; from a practitioner standpoint, however, it does not undermine the paper's value because the claim is not "this mechanic works in isolation" but "this mechanic amplifies the narrative properties that archetypes already support."

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Address archetype confound directly**: The paper should distinguish between "resource exhaustion amplifies narrative properties" (which may interact with archetype) and "resource exhaustion causes narrative properties" (which would require archetype control). The former is the more honest claim and is still a useful design principle.

## Minor Issues
- **Comparison to published resource-exhaustion game designs**: Torchbearer, Ironsworn, Burning Wheel, and 13th Age (Escalation Die) are all published games that use resource exhaustion or attrition as a narrative mechanic. A brief comparison to their design rationales would locate the paper's contribution.
- **Design principles section**: The paper's design principles (based on C4 and C6 observations) are presented at the end. From a designer's perspective, these are the most useful output. Make them more prominent.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
Good framing. The "meaningful scarcity" concept should be defined early and clearly.

### Methodology
The mechanic specification is adequate. The paper would benefit from a comparison table: what resource-management elements are present in resource-exhaustion campaigns vs. standard campaigns in this corpus?

### Evaluation
The +3.0 CA in C6 is striking. The paper should explain what specifically happened in C6 that produced this result — what choices did players (or simulated players) make that would not have been made in a non-resource-exhaustion context?

## Recommendation
Accept with revisions. The archetype confound acknowledgment is the required revision. The paper is a good FDG contribution with clear practical value for game designers.
