# Review: Resource Exhaustion as Narrative Amplifier: Mechanical Attrition in Long-Form Tabletop RPG
**Reviewer:** Evan Torner, University of Cincinnati
**Score:** 2 — weak accept
**Expertise:** LARP studies, game studies methods, Nordic freeform

## Summary
The paper makes an interesting claim about resource exhaustion as a narrative mechanism, but the two-campaign corpus with an uncontrolled archetype confound means the paper cannot support its causal claims. The unit-cohesion mechanism is the most theoretically interesting contribution and is underexplored. The Character Agency score is rubric-internal and not independently validated. I am sympathetic to the paper's ambitions but the current empirical framing overstates what two observations can show.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Archetype confound is unaddressed**: Campaign 4 (military) and Campaign 6 (craft-witness/unit-cohesion) are precisely the archetypes most predisposed to high Character Agency scores independent of resource mechanic. The paper cannot claim that resource exhaustion caused the CA gain without either (a) controlling for archetype, (b) showing the CA gain in resource-exhaustion campaigns exceeds what the same archetypes produce without resource exhaustion, or (c) reframing as a hypothesis paper with a clear confound acknowledgment and a proposed controlled study.
- **Character Agency score requires independent validation**: The CA score is derived from a rubric co-developed with the campaigns being analyzed. The paper needs to either validate CA against an independent measure or explicitly acknowledge that CA is a rubric-internal outcome and not an independently validated narrative engagement measure.

## Minor Issues
- **n=2 resource campaigns**: The paper should describe what additional campaigns would be needed to test the resource-exhaustion hypothesis in a controlled way. What archetype and adventure design conditions would constitute an adequate test?
- **Comparison to published resource-exhaustion designs**: Nordic freeform games (Undergångens Arvtagare, Dog Eat Dog) use resource exhaustion as a narrative mechanism explicitly. A comparison to these designs' stated rationales and observed player behavior would locate the paper in a broader tradition.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction's proceduralist framing is appropriate. The paper needs a clearer statement of its contribution claim: is it (a) "resource exhaustion causes narrative amplification" (causal claim requiring controlled study), (b) "resource exhaustion is associated with narrative amplification in two campaigns with confounded archetypes" (honest descriptive claim), or (c) "resource exhaustion is a plausible narrative amplifier; here is a hypothesis and a preliminary observation" (hypothesis paper framing)?

### Methodology
The paper should specify the archetype dimensions of C4 and C6 precisely and acknowledge how those dimensions might independently predict Character Agency outcomes.

### Evaluation
The +0.6 vs. +3.0 differential needs a dedicated discussion section. Why is C6 so much larger? Is it archetype, adventure design, session count, or accumulated resource pressure? This question is the paper's most important unresolved issue.

## Recommendation
Weak accept — revise. The archetype confound and CA validation are the two required revisions. Reframing as a hypothesis paper is the most honest path. The paper's core ideas are worth publishing if the framing accurately represents what the evidence can support.
