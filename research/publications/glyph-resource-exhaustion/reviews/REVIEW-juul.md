# Review: Resource Exhaustion as Narrative Amplifier: Mechanical Attrition in Long-Form Tabletop RPG
**Reviewer:** Jesper Juul, Royal Danish Academy / NYU
**Score:** 2 — weak accept
**Expertise:** game theory, player motivation, casual games

## Summary
The paper proposes a relationship between resource exhaustion mechanics and narrative engagement in TRPG. The core idea is interesting and the +0.6 / +3.0 CA differential is suggestive. However, the paper cannot support a causal claim with two observations and an archetype confound. The resource-exhaustion campaigns differ from the non-resource campaigns in at least two confounded ways: the mechanic itself, and the party archetypes. Without controlling for archetype, the paper has demonstrated a correlation in two data points, not a mechanism.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Causation concerns — archetype confound is unaddressed**: The two resource-exhaustion campaigns (C4 military, C6 craft-witness/unit-cohesion) are precisely the party archetypes most likely to show high Character Agency from archetype properties alone. A military party tracks unit-cohesion by design; a craft-witness party deepens narrative by design. The paper must address this confound before claiming that resource exhaustion caused the CA gain. The fix requires either (a) a resource-exhaustion campaign with a non-unit-cohesion, non-military archetype, or (b) a formal argument for why the CA gain is attributable to the mechanic rather than the archetype.

## Minor Issues
- **n=2 is not a corpus**: The paper should reframe its contribution as exploratory or hypothesis-generating. Two observations cannot support a causal claim. The paper should describe what additional campaigns would be needed to distinguish the mechanic effect from the archetype effect.
- **Character Agency is rubric-internal**: The CA score is derived from the same rubric used to evaluate all campaigns. If the rubric is sensitive to archetype properties, then CA scores may reflect archetype more than mechanic. An independent measure of narrative engagement would help.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction makes a clear and interesting argument that mechanics shape narrative engagement — this is a well-established game studies claim (Bogost, Juul, Sicart). The paper's specific contribution is the resource-exhaustion instantiation. Be more precise about what "narrative amplification" means.

### Methodology
The resource-exhaustion mechanic is described adequately. The paper should specify exactly what differs between resource-exhaustion and non-resource-exhaustion campaigns in terms of the session mechanics, so readers can understand what the "treatment" is.

### Evaluation
The +0.6 (C4) vs. +3.0 (C6) differential deserves more analysis. Why is C6 so much larger? Is it adventure design, archetype, or accumulated resource pressure across more sessions? This is an interesting question the paper does not address.

## Recommendation
Weak accept — revise. The archetype confound is the blocking issue. The paper is interesting and the hypothesis is worth pursuing, but the causal framing must be revised. Reframing as a hypothesis/exploratory paper with a clear description of the study needed to test the hypothesis would be a clean and honest revision.
