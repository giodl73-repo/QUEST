# Review Synthesis: Resource Exhaustion as Narrative Amplifier: Mechanical Attrition in Long-Form Tabletop RPG

**Venue:** FDG 2027
**Mean Score:** 2.6 (Bogost 3, Juul 2, Sicart 3, Morningstar 3, Torner 2)
**Recommendation:** Revise and Resubmit

---

## P1 — Priority 1 (3+ reviewers, blocking)

**Address the archetype confound.**
All five reviewers named the archetype confound. Juul and Torner are weak accepts specifically because of it. The resource-exhaustion campaigns (C4 military, C6 craft-witness) are the archetypes most predisposed to high Character Agency scores by design. Without controlling for this, the CA gain cannot be attributed to the resource-exhaustion mechanic.

- **Required fix (controlled analysis path)**: Identify the CA scores for non-resource-exhaustion campaigns with military or craft-witness archetypes. If such campaigns exist in the corpus, compare their CA scores to C4 and C6. If the resource-exhaustion campaigns show CA gains beyond the archetype baseline, this partially addresses the confound.

- **Required fix (hypothesis paper reframing path)**: Reframe the paper throughout from "resource exhaustion causes narrative amplification" to "resource exhaustion is associated with narrative amplification in two campaigns with military and craft-witness archetypes; the archetype confound requires controlled replication to disentangle." Add Section 6: "Required Study for Causal Inference" — specify the campaign design needed to test the hypothesis: a resource-exhaustion campaign with a non-unit-cohesion, non-military archetype.

- **The honest minimum**: The abstract and conclusion must not claim causation. Replace "resource exhaustion amplifies Character Agency" with "resource exhaustion is associated with Character Agency gain in two campaigns; archetype confound is acknowledged and requires controlled study to resolve."

---

## P2 — Priority 2 (2+ reviewers, required for acceptance)

**Validate Character Agency score against an independent measure.**
Torner and Juul both raised this. The CA score is rubric-internal — derived from the same rubric co-developed with the corpus. If the rubric is sensitive to archetype, CA scores may reflect archetype more than mechanic.

- **Required fix (comparison path)**: Compare CA score behavior in non-resource-exhaustion campaigns of similar archetypes. If CA scores for military/craft-witness campaigns without resource exhaustion are lower than C4/C6, this partially validates the rubric as sensitive to the mechanic.

- **Required fix (external measure path)**: Identify an independent measure of narrative engagement (session log word count of player-authored content, independent rater assessment of player choice complexity) and correlate with CA scores across the full corpus.

- **Minimum acceptable**: Add an explicit limitation acknowledging that CA is rubric-internal and may conflate archetype and mechanic effects. Describe the independent validation study as future work.

---

## P3 — Advisory (1–2 reviewers, strengthens but does not block)

**Compare to published resource-exhaustion game designs.**
Morningstar, Sicart, and Torner all asked for this. Torchbearer, Ironsworn, Burning Wheel, and Nordic freeform games (specifically those using resource depletion as a narrative driver) are the relevant comparands. A 1–2 paragraph comparison would locate the contribution and add credibility.

---

## Convergent Strengths (Do Not Revise Away)

- C4 +0.6 / C6 +3.0 CA differential: all reviewers cited this as the paper's primary empirical finding. Keep the numbers and contextualize them.
- Unit-cohesion mechanism: Bogost, Sicart, and Morningstar all named this as the paper's most theoretically interesting claim. Give it more space — it is underexplored relative to its importance.
- Design principles: Morningstar wants these elevated. Move the design principles from the conclusion into a dedicated section.

---

## Divergent Notes

- Bogost (accept, 3) and Sicart (accept, 3) are willing to accept with the archetype confound acknowledged as a limitation. Juul (weak accept, 2) and Torner (weak accept, 2) require either a controlled analysis or a hypothesis-paper reframing. Morningstar (accept, 3) accepts on design value grounds.
- The hypothesis paper reframing is the most tractable revision path. It requires no new data and honestly represents what two observations can show. FDG is an appropriate venue for a well-motivated hypothesis paper with preliminary data.
- No reviewer recommended rejection. The paper's ideas are worth publishing; the framing needs to match the evidence.
