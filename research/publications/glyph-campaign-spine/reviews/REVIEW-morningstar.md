# Review: The 7-Session Campaign Spine: Evidence-Based Architecture for Emotionally Complete Narrative Arcs
**Reviewer:** Jason Morningstar, Bully Pulpit Games
**Score:** 4 — strong accept
**Expertise:** tabletop RPG design, playtesting, narrative structure

## Summary
This paper describes a 7-session campaign spine and provides evidence from three complete campaigns that it produces emotionally satisfying arcs. As a game designer, I can say that this is harder than it looks. Running a single emotionally coherent campaign session is challenging. Running seven in sequence, each building on the last, each giving each PC their moment, each planting seeds that pay off later — and doing it across three different party archetypes — is a genuine design achievement. The paper describes the structural principles that made this possible, and that is a real contribution.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Operationalize emotional completeness more precisely**: For the academic audience, "emotionally complete" needs a definition that does not depend on rubric scores alone. From a practitioner standpoint, I know what it means when I see it — but the paper needs to say what it means in terms that an outsider can evaluate. Even a brief mapping of emotional completeness to prior narrative theory (Aristotle's catharsis, Murray's four pleasures) would be sufficient.

## Minor Issues
- **Same-author confound: acknowledge and address**: The paper should be upfront that three campaigns from one author is a small corpus with a confound. Propose a replication protocol clearly — what would a second designer need to know to run a campaign using this spine?
- **Comparison to adventure path design**: How does the 7-session spine compare to published adventure path structures (Pathfinder, Mörk Borg campaign guides, published D&D modules)? A brief comparison would locate the contribution for practitioners.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
Strong framing. The hint-system and PC spotlight rotation are the most novel elements and should be named in the abstract.

### Methodology
The spine structure is described clearly. I would like to see the full seed-tracking log for at least one campaign as an appendix — the seed-plant/retrieve structure is the spine's most technically interesting element and the paper would benefit from showing it in detail.

### Evaluation
The Route D/E distinction is important. Route D (player-authored emotional resolution without DM prompting) is the design ideal — the paper should say this plainly and argue for why player-authored resolution is the success criterion. The 5/7 Route D/E rate is the headline; frame it that way from the start.

## Recommendation
Strong accept. The paper is a practical, well-evidenced design contribution. The emotional completeness operationalization is the academic gap — it should be addressed in revision but it does not diminish the contribution's value.
