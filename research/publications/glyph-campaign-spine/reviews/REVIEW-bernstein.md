# Review: The 7-Session Campaign Spine: Evidence-Based Architecture for Emotionally Complete Narrative Arcs
**Reviewer:** Michael Bernstein, Stanford HCI Group
**Score:** 2 — weak accept
**Expertise:** crowdsourcing, human-AI interaction, evaluation methodology

## Summary
The paper presents an interesting design template for long-form narrative arcs and provides evidence from three campaigns. However, two methodological issues limit the contribution's claim strength: the emotional completeness operationalization is circular (rubric scores cannot validate the rubric-optimized spine), and the small n=7 with a single author-evaluator prevents generalization. Both issues are addressable in revision, but both are real.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **"Emotional completeness" operationalization is rubric-circular**: The paper's central claim — that the spine produces emotionally complete arcs — is evidenced only by rubric scores from sessions that the same author designed, ran, and evaluated. The rubric was co-developed with the campaigns that use the spine. This circularity is the paper's methodological Achilles' heel. The required fix: either a theory-grounded definition of emotional completeness that is independent of the rubric, or an external validation (blind rater study, comparison to independently scored narrative transcripts, or both).
- **Same-author confound**: The paper presents n=3 campaigns, all authored by the same person who designed and iteratively revised the spine. The spine's success may reflect the author's design skill rather than the spine's architectural properties. The paper should address this more directly — either by reframing the contribution as "one author's evidence-based design practice" or by describing a replication protocol that a different author could follow.

## Minor Issues
- **Comparison to published campaign frameworks**: The paper should locate its contribution relative to existing campaign design frameworks (Pathfinder Adventure Paths, D&D adventure module design guidelines, academic studies of serialized narrative).
- **Route A–E taxonomy definition**: The Route A–E categories are used throughout but defined informally. A formal definition table would make the taxonomy usable by other researchers.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction frames the contribution clearly. The paper should add a caveat at the end of the introduction acknowledging the single-author corpus and its implications for generalizability.

### Methodology
The spine's structural elements — 7 adventures, PC spotlight rotation, 4-hint system, seed-plant/retrieve structure — are described clearly. A visual diagram of the full spine structure (adventure slots, PC spotlight assignments, hint placements) would be a useful figure.

### Evaluation
The 7/7 completion and 5/7 Route D/E are presented as success metrics without discussion of what the two Route A–C sessions looked like and why they did not achieve Route D/E. Analyzing the two non-D/E sessions would make the spine's success conditions more transparent.

## Recommendation
Weak accept — revise. The emotional completeness operationalization and the same-author confound are the two required revisions. The paper is viable at CHI if these are addressed honestly and directly.
