# Review: The 7-Session Campaign Spine: Evidence-Based Architecture for Emotionally Complete Narrative Arcs
**Reviewer:** Janet Murray, Georgia Tech Digital Media
**Score:** 3 — accept
**Expertise:** interactive narrative, cyberdrama, digital storytelling

## Summary
This paper makes a substantial contribution to the design of emotionally complete long-form narrative arcs in AI-simulated settings. The 7/7 completion rate across three distinct campaigns with different party archetypes is impressive evidence for the spine's structural robustness. The distinction between Routes D/E (player-authored emotional resolution) and Routes A–C (DM-assisted resolution) is important and deserves more theoretical development. The paper's main gap is the operationalization of "emotional completeness" — a concept that is load-bearing throughout but is only defined by reference to the rubric scores that the spine was partly designed to produce.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **"Emotional completeness" not operationalized independently of the rubric**: The paper's central claim is that the spine produces "emotionally complete" arcs. The evidence for this claim is rubric scores on sessions designed using the spine. This is circular: the spine was designed to produce high rubric scores, and high rubric scores are taken as evidence of emotional completeness. The paper needs an independent operationalization — either a theoretical definition grounded in narrative theory, or an external measure (reader/player assessment, independent narrative scoring).

## Minor Issues
- **Same-author confound**: All three campaigns were authored and evaluated by the same author. This is acknowledged but understated. The paper should address whether the spine produces emotionally complete arcs in the hands of a different designer or DM, not just the designer who invented the spine.
- **Compare against published campaign frameworks**: The paper does not compare the 7-session spine against published D&D campaign structures (Pathfinder Adventure Paths, published TRPG campaign guides). Even a qualitative comparison would locate the contribution.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction frames the spine as a design solution to a specific problem: maintaining emotional throughlines across 7 sessions with 21 scenes each. The problem statement is clear. The paper would benefit from a stronger statement of what was tried and abandoned before the current 7-session structure — what is the spine's design rationale?

### Methodology
The Route A–E taxonomy is the paper's most important design contribution and is currently underspecified. What determines which route a session takes? Is it driven by adventure design, party behavior, or emergent interaction? A clear definition of each route and a per-session route log would make this empirically tractable.

### Evaluation
The 5/7 Route D/E sessions across three campaigns is the paper's strongest evidence for player-authored emotional resolution. The two Route A–C sessions should be analyzed in detail: what was different about them, and what does that imply for the spine's design?

## Recommendation
Accept with revisions. The emotional completeness operationalization is the critical revision item. The paper is a strong CHI contribution once this is addressed.
