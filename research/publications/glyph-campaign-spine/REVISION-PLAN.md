## P1 Checklist — Must complete before resubmission

- [ ] **Operationalize "emotional completeness" independently of the rubric**

  Choose one path:

  **Path A — Theoretical grounding (recommended):**
  - Select 1–2 prior narrative theories as the definitional basis (e.g., Murray's four pleasures of cyberdrama, Chatman's story/discourse structure, Labov's narrative evaluation clause)
  - Add Section 2.2: "Defining Emotional Completeness" — provide a theory-grounded definition with explicit criteria
  - Apply the definition to each of the three campaigns with specific textual examples from the session logs
  - Clarify that the rubric is a practical operationalization of these theoretical criteria, not their source

  **Path B — Empirical validation study:**
  - Recruit 5–10 readers (game designers, narrative researchers, or general readers) as blind raters
  - Provide 5–10 session transcript excerpts (final scenes from each campaign)
  - Raters assess "narrative completeness" and "emotional satisfaction" on a 5-point Likert scale without rubric context
  - Compute correlation with rubric scores (specifically the Emotional Resonance and Character Arc dimensions)
  - Add as Section 5.2: "External Validation of Emotional Completeness Measure"

  **Minimum acceptable (if neither A nor B feasible in revision window):**
  - Add explicit definitional paragraph at first use of "emotional completeness": derive definition from narrative theory
  - Add limitations note acknowledging that rubric-based operationalization is a practical proxy, not an independent measure
  - Remove "emotional completeness" from the title or qualify it: "Rubric-Validated Emotional Completeness" or similar

## P2 Checklist — Must complete before resubmission

- [ ] **Address same-author confound**

  Choose one path:

  **Path A — Replication by second author (preferred if feasible):**
  - Identify a second designer willing to run one 7-session campaign using the spine
  - Provide the spine template and required documentation
  - Add results as Section 5.3: "Replication Study"
  - Compare Route D/E rate and completion rate with the three original campaigns

  **Path B — Honest reframing + replication protocol:**
  - Add explicit acknowledgment in the methodology section: "All three campaigns were authored and evaluated by the same person who designed the spine. This is a single-author corpus; generalizability to other designers cannot be claimed without replication."
  - Add Section 6.2: "Replication Protocol" — specify what a second designer would need: spine template, rubric, hint-placement guidelines, PC spotlight rotation rules, seed-tracking format
  - Frame as a practical contribution: the spine is a design tool, not a validated general method; replication is the next step

- [ ] **Analyze the two non-Route-D/E sessions**
  - Identify the two sessions (across the three campaigns) that did not achieve Route D or E
  - Describe what happened: adventure design issue? party composition? session length?
  - Draw design implications: under what conditions does the spine not produce player-authored resolution?
