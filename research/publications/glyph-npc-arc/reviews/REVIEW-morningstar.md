# Review: Designing NPC Arc-Completion Conditions for Session-Invariant Emotional Payoffs in AI-Simulated TRPG
**Reviewer:** Jason Morningstar, Bully Pulpit Games
**Score:** 4 — strong accept
**Expertise:** tabletop RPG design, playtesting, narrative structure

## Summary
This paper describes what good TRPG designers do when they are working at their best, and it describes it precisely enough that another designer could replicate it. The character-logic-driven arc-completion template is a real design contribution — I recognize it from practice and I am grateful to see it formalized. The zero-DM-scripted finding is the headline result from a practitioner perspective: 40+ arc-completions and not one required the DM to intervene to make it happen. That is the design goal stated and achieved. This is one of the most practically useful papers I have reviewed in this domain.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **Drama management comparison should be expanded**: As the only academic-facing major issue, the paper should spend more time distinguishing its approach from drama management theory. For a practitioner reviewer this is not a concern, but the CHI audience will expect it. A clear paragraph explaining why drama management's central-narrative-controller model is not what this paper is doing would satisfy this.

## Minor Issues
- **Player study future work**: The absence of human player studies is not a flaw in this paper's design — AI-simulated sessions are what the corpus is. But a future-work section describing what a human player study would look like, and what it would add, would preempt reviewer concerns.
- **"Morally grey" NPC examples**: The paper makes a point of claiming the template handles morally grey NPCs. Show this with at least one worked example of a morally grey NPC arc specification and its completion in the corpus.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction is the paper's best section — clear, motivated, and honest about what the paper is and is not. Keep it.

### Methodology
The template specification is excellent. The paper should include the full arc specification for at least one canonical NPC from the corpus (Morreth, Aldric, or Lenne are obvious candidates from the session logs) as an in-body example. Readers will want to see the template instantiated before they can evaluate it.

### Evaluation
The 88% session coverage figure with zero DM scripting is the headline. The paper should describe the 12% failure cases explicitly — what is the design inference from sessions where no NPC arc fired? Is it a party composition issue, an adventure design issue, or a session duration issue? That analysis would make the template's limitations visible.

## Recommendation
Strong accept. This paper is a clear contribution to CHI's HCI and narrative design communities. The drama management comparison is the only academic gap; it is addressable in revision. The template, the corpus, and the zero-scripted finding are the paper's core and they are strong.
