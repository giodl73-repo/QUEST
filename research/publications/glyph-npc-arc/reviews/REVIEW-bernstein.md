# Review: Designing NPC Arc-Completion Conditions for Session-Invariant Emotional Payoffs in AI-Simulated TRPG
**Reviewer:** Michael Bernstein, Stanford HCI Group
**Score:** 2 — weak accept
**Expertise:** crowdsourcing, human-AI interaction, evaluation methodology

## Summary
The paper presents a useful design template for NPC arc-completion and a substantial corpus of instances. The zero-DM-scripted finding is genuinely striking and would be more convincing if supported by evidence that human players — not just rubric scores on AI-simulated sessions — found the payoffs emotionally resonant. As presented, the paper's "memorable payoff" claim is entirely self-referential: the rubric was designed to detect what the arc-completion template was designed to produce. This circularity is not fatal but must be acknowledged and addressed.

## Major Issues (P1 if 3+ reviewers or blocking)
- **No player study**: The paper claims NPC arc-completions produce "memorable emotional payoffs." This claim is backed exclusively by rubric scores from the same system that generated the sessions and designed the template. There is no independent evidence that any human (player or observer) found these moments resonant. The paper should either conduct a player study (even a small study with human DMs rating transcripts) or substantially revise the "memorable payoff" claim to "rubric-score-correlated payoff" throughout.

## Minor Issues
- **Drama management comparison**: The paper engages with drama management only briefly. CHI reviewers with interactive narrative backgrounds will expect a more substantive treatment.
- **"Session-invariant" formalization**: The term is used as a key property but never formally defined. Define it.

## Detailed Comments
### Introduction
The introduction makes a strong case for the design problem. The "memorable payoff" language should be qualified from the start — the paper can claim a rubric-correlated payoff without overclaiming memorability.

### Methodology
The template is clearly specified. I would like to see the arc specification documents for 3–4 NPCs from the corpus included as a supplementary appendix, so the reader can evaluate the template's expressiveness against actual instances.

### Evaluation
The per-campaign arc-completion count and session coverage statistics are presented clearly. A timeline figure showing which sessions fired arc-completions and which did not — across all three campaigns — would make the 88% figure more interpretable.

## Recommendation
Weak accept — revise. The player study gap is the key revision item. The paper can proceed to camera-ready if the "memorable payoff" claim is revised to accurately reflect what the evidence supports (rubric-correlated, not independently validated as memorable), and if a future-work section describes the player study design that would close this gap.
