## Abanasinia (seed region for 0001)

**Pre-Cataclysm (relevant to our adventure):** A sparsely-settled frontier province on the western edge of the old empire's reach. Most inhabitants are hill-folk, pastoralists, and a scattering of Solamnic waystations. Plainsfolk tribes (ancestors of the Que-Shu) roam the Plains of Dergoth to the south. A handful of minor Solamnic knightly holdings guard the passes into Kharolis.

**Post-Cataclysm (for later adventures):** Abanasinia becomes iconic in the War of the Lance — home to Solace, Haven, Que-Shu, Gateway, Crystalmir Lake. For adventure #0001 we stay pre-Cataclysm; the Cataclysm will strike ~100 years AFTER the tomb is sealed.

### The Rose Cairn (our dungeon's location)

A solitary hill on the northern edge of the Plains of Dergoth, about 40 miles south-southwest of a minor Solamnic waystation (un-named in our canon; feel free to invent if a skill needs one). Wind-scoured, scattered pines, a view of the Kharolis peaks to the south. Locally called "the Rose Cairn" because of a carved stone rose set into the cairn-stones above the tomb entrance.

**Why it matters to our adventure:** the tomb was sealed in ~PC 100, during the late Age of Might. A century later, the Cataclysm did not displace the cairn — an unusual fact in a land that was otherwise heavily reshaped. This is significant. Either Paladine himself protected the site (possible, given Ilendra was a Knight of the Rose) or something in the tomb kept the Cataclysm at bay (darker, more interesting).

### Nearest habitation (adventure use)

A hill-village, **TBD-named** when a starting scene needs it. ~8 miles northeast of the cairn. The Solamnic archivist who offers the party the hook lodges at the village's single inn (a fortified way-house the Order keeps).

## Plains of Dergoth

A short-grass plain stretching south from Abanasinia. Broken by occasional hills and standing stones. In the Age of Might, little-traveled except by herders and the occasional Solamnic patrol chasing raiders.

**Hazards (workshop note):** weather — sudden cold winds, late-spring snow. Predators — dire wolves, occasional ogre patrols in this era.
