# Conditions

Conditions alter a creature's capabilities. Effects stack; duration depends on the source. Unless otherwise specified, conditions end when their source ends.

## Blinded
- Can't see; auto-fails sight-based ability checks.
- Attacks against it have advantage; its attacks have disadvantage.

## Charmed
- Can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful abilities/magical effects.
- Charmer has advantage on social (Cha) checks against the charmed creature.

## Deafened
- Can't hear; auto-fails hearing-based ability checks.

## Frightened
- Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while source is in line of sight.
- Can't willingly move closer to the source.

## Grappled
- Speed becomes 0; can't benefit from speed bonuses.
- Ends if grappler is incapacitated or moved out of reach.

## Incapacitated
- Can't take actions or reactions.

## Invisible
- Impossible to see without special sense (heavily obscured for hiding purposes).
- Attacks against it have disadvantage; its attacks have advantage.

## Paralyzed
- Incapacitated; can't move or speak.
- Auto-fails Str/Dex saves.
- Attacks against it have advantage.
- Any attack that hits from within 5 ft is a critical hit.

## Petrified
- Transformed (along with nonmagical equipment) into solid inanimate substance.
- Weight × 10; ceases aging.
- Incapacitated; can't move/speak; unaware of surroundings.
- Attacks against it have advantage; auto-fails Str/Dex saves.
- Resistance to all damage; immune to poison and disease (current poison/disease paused).

## Poisoned
- Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

## Prone
- Only movement option is to crawl (or stand, costing half movement).
- Disadvantage on attack rolls.
- Attackers within 5 ft have advantage; attackers farther away have disadvantage.

## Restrained
- Speed 0; no speed bonuses.
- Attacks against it have advantage; its attacks have disadvantage.
- Disadvantage on Dex saves.

## Stunned
- Incapacitated; can't move; can speak only falteringly.
- Auto-fails Str/Dex saves.
- Attacks against it have advantage.

## Unconscious
- Incapacitated; can't move/speak; unaware.
- Drops what it's holding; falls prone.
- Auto-fails Str/Dex saves.
- Attacks against it have advantage.
- Any attack that hits from within 5 ft is a critical hit.

## Exhaustion (6 levels)

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Disadvantage on ability checks |
| 2 | Speed halved |
| 3 | Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws |
| 4 | Hit point maximum halved |
| 5 | Speed reduced to 0 |
| 6 | Death |

A long rest removes 1 level of exhaustion (requires food/drink).
