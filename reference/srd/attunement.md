# Attunement & Cursed Items

## Attunement

Some magic items require a creature to form a bond ("attune") with them before the item's properties can be used.

- **Time to attune:** a short rest (1 hour) focused on the item. Spell-focus rituals or combat interrupt the process.
- **Requirements:** some items require specific class, race, or alignment. A creature that doesn't meet them gets no benefit from attunement.
- **Limit:** a creature can be attuned to **no more than 3 magic items at once**. The creature can't attune to more than one copy of a single item.
- **Ending:** the creature can end attunement by spending another short rest focused on the item (unless the item is cursed). Attunement also ends if the creature no longer meets the prerequisites, dies, or if another creature attunes to the item.

## Cursed Items

A cursed item's curse lasts until removed. Notable properties:

- **Revealed on attunement:** a curse is not detected by a casual *detect magic*. Once a creature attunes, the DM may reveal the curse — or let the player discover it through play.
- **Removal:** *remove curse* ends the curse on the item's current owner but does not destroy it; the curse resumes if re-attuned. *Wish* or an equally powerful effect may destroy a curse. Some curses require specific rituals.
- **Compulsion to retain:** many cursed items prevent willing parting. The owner may be compelled to re-attune or refuse disposal.

## Curse Design Guidelines (workshop-specific, not SRD)

When `treasure-forger` makes a cursed artifact, ensure:

1. **Mechanical benefit** — attunement gives a real perk. No one falls for a curse that offers nothing.
2. **Creeping cost** — the curse charges slowly enough that the first session is pure upside. The cost emerges across play.
3. **Narrative hook** — the curse advances a story (memory loss, possession by a former owner, draws a hunter).
4. **Escape path** — document how the curse *can* be ended, even if hard. No unbreakable curses (they're boring).
5. **In-fiction foreshadowing** — the dungeon contains evidence of prior victims; the curse is deducible from clues, not sprung as a GM "gotcha".

## Magic Item Categories (short)

- **Armor, Shield** — fit a wearer; most don't require attunement.
- **Potion** — consumed on use; no attunement.
- **Ring** — usually requires attunement.
- **Rod, Staff, Wand** — most require attunement.
- **Scroll** — single use; no attunement.
- **Weapon** — attunement varies.
- **Wondrous Item** — catch-all; attunement varies.
