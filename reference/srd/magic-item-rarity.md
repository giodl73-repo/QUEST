# Magic Item Rarity

Each magic item has a rarity that suggests the tier of play it belongs in and its approximate value.

| Rarity | Party Level | Value (gp) |
|---|---|---|
| Common | 1st+ | 50 – 100 |
| Uncommon | 1st+ | 101 – 500 |
| Rare | 5th+ | 501 – 5,000 |
| Very Rare | 11th+ | 5,001 – 50,000 |
| Legendary | 17th+ | 50,001+ |
| Artifact | (unique) | priceless |

## Placement Guidance

- **Tier 1 (levels 1-4):** expect 0-2 uncommons; 1 rare as a campaign hook for a full tier.
- **Tier 2 (levels 5-10):** 2-4 uncommons, 1-2 rares, optional very rare climax.
- **Tier 3 (levels 11-16):** rares common; 1 very rare standard; a legendary as arc-climax.
- **Tier 4 (levels 17-20):** very rares standard; legendary uncommon-but-present; artifacts campaign-defining.

## Attunement Slot Pressure

Because a character can only attune 3 items, giving out attunement-required items is a design lever. A dungeon that drops 4 attunement-required items forces choice — which is often more interesting than more items.

## Workshop Note on Curses & Rarity

Cursed items typically **advertise their rarity one tier lower than their effective power** — a rare-tier cursed item might read as uncommon until attunement reveals the hidden mechanic. Use this asymmetry intentionally; don't just gate the curse behind a DC 20 check.

## Our First Dungeon (reminder)

`0001-tomb-of-the-silver-rose` is tier 1. The Silver Rose of Vaenor should read as **uncommon** (cursed tier-up convention → effectively rare). Incidental loot: mundane coins, non-magical signet, a lore-heavy letter.
