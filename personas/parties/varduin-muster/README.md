# The Varduin Muster

## One-line identity
Four odd-sorts drifted into Varduin-by-the-Pines over the same winter and discovered they had nowhere else to be.

## Formation

The muster was not recruited. It accumulated.

The first to arrive was **Thera Hillfoot**, who had always been in Varduin — born in a hill-croft two valleys east, raised in the back rooms of the way-house until the way-house was all she had. She drifted back through that winter because the drifts closed the passes and there was nowhere else to drift to.

**Grom Ironhand** arrived in the second thaw, pushing a hand-cart of forge-tools over a road that had buckled three times under worse weather. He had been walking the Reorx-circuit since his forge-master died; Varduin was where the road gave out. The way-house had a cold forge and Grom meant to leave it warm. He was still there when the spring winds came.

**Kessa Moonweave** walked into the village on foot, in Red robes too thin for Abanasinia, carrying a spellbook wrapped in oilcloth and a grim certainty that a signal her Portent had given her three months ago terminated at this exact bluff. She stayed at the way-house to watch for the signal again. It has not come.

**Aelric of Crownhold** came last, in weather that should have killed his horse. He was on errand for the Rose Council's clerkwork — a minor errand, the kind given to initiates — and he arrived to find three strangers in the way-house common room, playing dice with a set Thera Hillfoot had stolen from a Nerakan trader six years earlier. Aelric announced his errand formally. They listened. Then they asked if they could come along.

## Cohesion

The muster is bound by a small obligation and a larger silence. The obligation: Brother Laen's patronage pays for all four of them while the errand is open, and none of them has anywhere else to spend the spring. The silence: each of them is carrying a grief they have not yet spoken aloud at the way-house table, and each of them suspects — without evidence — that the others are doing the same.

They are not friends yet. They have not yet been through something together. This adventure is the first through-line. If Varduin is home for any of them now, it is because they all returned to it after.

## Group Dynamics

Aelric defers to Grom on matters of conscience and to Kessa on matters of fact. Thera defers to no one, openly, but she checks her watch when Aelric is speaking, which means she's taking him seriously. Kessa and Thera argue pleasantly; Kessa and Grom argue less pleasantly about how much can be mended. Grom treats all three of the others as apprentices he hasn't yet been given permission to train.

The party does not have a leader. Under pressure, Aelric leads by default because he moves first. After the adventure, this may change.

## Each PC carries a grief (targetable by Silver Rose)

- **Aelric:** his older brother **Varran**, Knight of the Sword, fell at the Neraka-border two winters ago. The rose would target this memory with precision — Varran's grief-to-Caen mirror is the tightest the rose has ever found.
- **Thera:** her mother **Adda**, died of winter-sickness when Thera was eight. Thera remembers the last bread her mother baked and the smell of it.
- **Kessa:** her childhood mentor **Vethrenn**, a White Robe who went silent in Ilendra's era — a hundred years before Kessa was born. Kessa inherited Vethrenn's commonplace book and her silver-ink pen.
- **Grom:** his forge-master **Duren Coalbeard**, died in the mine-collapse Grom escaped by chance. Grom carries Duren's hammer-mark tongs.

All four griefs are real. All four can feed the rose.
