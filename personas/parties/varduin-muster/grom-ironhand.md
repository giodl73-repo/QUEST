# Grom Ironhand, Forge-Priest of Reorx

## Appearance

Four foot nine, 62 years old (young-middle for a hill dwarf), broad-shouldered to the point that his chain mail was re-forged twice before it fit across the back. Beard in three plaits, iron-gray going rust at the ends from forge-heat. A burn-scar across the back of his left hand in the exact shape of a hammer-mark — not a striking wound but a brand he put on himself at his forge-master's wake. Eyes the color of cooled steel. Walks with a forge-tongue-tripped limp in cold weather; walks straight when warm. Carries the forge smell even after a week on the road.

## Background (Dragonlance-grounded)

Born in a hill-dwarf waystation at the eastern edge of the Kharolis foothills, the third son of a mining family that sent him out of the mines when he turned out to be unlucky underground — he fell twice, and the second time he broke two ribs and a foot, and the family took it as Reorx's polite suggestion that Grom was meant for the forge, not the shaft. He apprenticed at the Reorx-shrine at **Gavrek's Anvil**, a minor outer hold of Thorbardin, under **Duren Coalbeard**, a forge-master who took in the unlucky and the stubborn and made what he could of them.

Duren ordained Grom into the Reorx-priesthood at 58 — late by dwarven standards, but Grom had been slow to settle into the certainty the ordination required. The certainty arrived three years later when Duren was killed in a mine-collapse that Grom, by chance, escaped because he had been sent to fetch water. **Grom saw his forge-master's death as the moment Reorx finished forming him.** He has been walking the Reorx-circuit ever since, bringing forge-light to waystations that have forgotten it.

He has been on circuit four years. Varduin was where the road gave out this winter. He has not yet decided whether to leave.

## Motivation

Grom wants to **bring the forge-light to places that have forgotten it, and to mend what can be mended**. The Silver Rose is, by all indicators, unmendable — and this is exactly the kind of case Grom has been trained to suspect is mendable anyway. He wants to see it. He wants to put his hand on it. He wants to know whether Reorx's flame will catch on it or pass through it.

## Grief / Loss

**Duren Coalbeard, Forge-Master of Gavrek's Anvil.** Died in the Lower Ore-Shaft collapse, PC 23 (three years before the adventure). His body was recovered; his hammer was not.

Concrete memory: Duren's hands, the last morning. He was inspecting a set of horseshoes Grom had forged in the pre-dawn — Grom had been off-shift and had volunteered for the horseshoes because a visiting merchant's caravan was leaving at first light. Duren ran his thumb along the inside curve of one shoe, not the outside, and grunted. Then he said — this is the sentence Grom remembers — *"Inside curve is the honest one, lad. Outside is where the world looks. Inside is where you find out if you meant it."* And he nodded once, which from Duren was a benediction, and he turned and went to the Lower Ore-Shaft because his inspection was complete. Grom finished packing the horseshoes. The collapse was eleven minutes after that. Grom heard it from the forge.

Grom has, in the three years since, inspected every piece of metal he forges by running his thumb along the inside curve. He does not know if this is discipline or grief. He suspects it is both. He has never told anyone.

The Silver Rose, if it takes this memory, will take one of the few moments Grom considers sacred.

## Personality

- **Traits:** Names every tool, every weapon, every shield. Grom's warhammer is called **Second-Chance** because it was re-forged from a broken one of Duren's. Grom's shield is **Inside-Curve** — you can see why.
- **Ideals:** "What can be mended, I will mend. What cannot be mended, I will honor. I will know the difference or die trying."
- **Bonds:** Duren's tongs. He will not leave them behind under any circumstances.
- **Flaws:** He will try to mend things that should be broken. He has, twice, gotten this wrong, and he has been told by two separate senior priests that his instinct to mend is, in certain cases, a form of disrespect for the thing being mended. He does not believe them.

## Playstyle Heuristics

**Decision order when offered a choice:**

1. **Mend what's hurt** (heal teammates; restore an object that was damaged; ease a creature's suffering even if enemy).
2. **Keep the party whole** (no one dies if he has a slot left).
3. **Honor Reorx** (light a forge-candle in dark rooms; speak a Reorx-benediction over the dead; never step over a hearth without greeting it).
4. **Own his debts** (he owes Aelric nothing yet, but he is watching).

**Signature moves** (the DM should use these):
- **Bless the party** before every fight he has time to. 1 min duration, concentration. If the fight starts before he can, he casts Bless on round 1 instead of attacking.
- **Mend something after every scene.** A broken hinge. A cracked buckle. An NPC's bent spear. Not a spell-cast — a thumb-on-inside-curve act of attention. This is character, not mechanics.
- **Uses Preserve Life** (Channel Divinity) rather than individual Cure Wounds when the party is splashed with damage. Reserves Lay-on-Hands-style targeted healing for critical single moments.
- **Greets hearths.** Any fireplace, forge, or hearth the party passes, Grom touches the stone and says, in Dwarvish, *"Thou'rt seen."* Once per hearth. No mechanical effect.

**When in doubt** — roll d6. **1-3:** mend (act of healing, repair, or ease). **4-6:** stand between (place body between harm and the vulnerable).

**Voice tags:**
- Forge-metaphor for everything. Doors "want greasing," locks "want re-hammering," bad moods "want cooling down a bit before you strike again."
- Dwarvish expletives under stress. **"By the Black Anvil."** **"Ash and cinder."** **"Reorx preserve me."**
- Curt but warm. Short sentences. Eye contact. A small nod for yes; a small tilt for no.
- Addresses the party by craft-nickname when he likes them. "Knight." "Runner." "Scribe." "Priest." (He has not used Kessa's — he calls her **Scribe** — yet, but he will by session two.)

## Secrets

Grom did not, in fact, fall twice in the mines as a young dwarf. He fell once, and then he **chose to fall** the second time, because he had realized — in the eleven seconds between the first fall and being pulled up — that he was not meant for the shaft, and he did not know how to tell his family. The second fall was a deliberate stumble that broke his ribs and his foot and settled the question. He has never told anyone. He does not think of it as a lie, exactly; he thinks of it as a forge he made of himself, and then closed the grate on.

If the Silver Rose ever takes a Grom memory that isn't Duren, it will take this one, and Grom will not be able to remember why he took up the forge in the first place.
