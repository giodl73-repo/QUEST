# Thera Hillfoot, of Varduin

## Appearance

Three-and-a-half feet, wiry, 31 years old (halfling prime). Sun-wheat hair cropped short — she cuts it herself with a knife and does not do a careful job. A round face that lands on amused more often than not; a small white scar through her right eyebrow from a childhood fall off the waystation's rain-barrel. She wears soft leathers in road-colors and carries more pockets than a human would think economical. A silver finger-ring on her left thumb — too large for the finger, she spins it when thinking.

## Background (Dragonlance-grounded)

Born in a hill-croft two valleys east of Varduin-by-the-Pines. Mother **Adda Hillfoot**, a hedge-healer who mixed roots for the way-house. Father **Nenn Hillfoot**, a gate-tender, less present than Adda. Thera was eight when the winter-sickness came through the pines; her mother went first, gently, in a single day. Her father left Varduin in the next thaw, leaving Thera with the way-house keepers as an unofficial apprentice.

She grew up in the back rooms of the fortified way-house the Solamnic Order maintained. She learned to read because the way-house needed someone to sort correspondence; she learned to pick locks because the way-house's cellar had a lock and nobody would tell her what was in it. She was twelve when she solved that lock; she was thirteen when she was caught doing it; she was fourteen when the Solamnic steward realized she was more useful than his previous courier and began sending her on errands the Order wouldn't trust to a sworn knight.

For the past decade she has been a freelance scout, runner, and lock-artist along the Abanasinia hill-routes. She knows **Brother Laen's mother by sight** — she has delivered letters to that house — and she is the only PC in the party with that kind of local reference.

## Motivation

Thera wants **money and stories** in equal parts. She has trouble distinguishing them, because for her they come in the same motion: a story that pays is worth twice a story that doesn't, and a payment without a story is dull coin she'd rather trade for one that carries weight.

In this adventure specifically, the Rose itself is a story. Brother Laen is a payer. She is curious which one will turn out to be the larger figure.

## Grief / Loss

**Adda Hillfoot, hedge-healer of the Varduin hill-crofts.** Died of winter-sickness in PC 3, when Thera was eight.

Concrete memory: the last bread her mother baked. It was rye with winter-savory in the crust — Adda cut it wrong, long-ways rather than across, and told Thera the last thing she would ever tell her in any form: *"We cut bread the way we first learned to, and then later we cut it the way the people we live with learned to. You'll cut yours a third way, and I won't be there to see it."* Thera remembers the shape of the slices (wrong-way; long-ways) and the smell of the savory (sharper than she's ever tasted since) and her mother's hands, which were smaller than her own hands are now.

Thera has, in the twenty-three years since, never cut a loaf of bread long-ways. She cuts it across, the way people she lived with learned to. She does not know whether this is loyalty, or defiance, or whether her mother would have approved. She has never told anyone the story.

The Silver Rose will take this memory. It is small enough that the rose will take it early.

## Personality

- **Traits:** Laughs at tense moments because the laugh comes out before the decision does. Inventories rooms — rafters, floorboards, ceiling angles — before anything else.
- **Ideals:** "A locked door is a question. A locked door that stays locked is a cowardly question."
- **Bonds:** Varduin is the only place she has ever called hers. She will not be the reason it comes to harm.
- **Flaws:** She steals from friends reflexively, returns most of it, and does not understand why this bothers people.

## Playstyle Heuristics

**Decision order when offered a choice:**

1. **Don't die** (she is 20 HP; she knows it).
2. **Curiosity** (if she hasn't seen it before, she'll edge closer).
3. **Profit** (not greed — she'll take one interesting thing, not three mundane ones).
4. **Party loyalty** (growing; not yet the top of the order, but rising).

**Signature moves** (the DM should use these):
- **Checks pockets, rafters, and floor-gaps** of every room. Not as a declared action, as a reflex. She *always* finds the coin pouch in a skeleton's ribcage before anyone else does.
- **Asks one specific question** of every NPC — usually the one the NPC wasn't expecting. "Did you eat before you came here?" "Are those your own boots?" "Who taught you to stand like that?" She learns a startling amount.
- **Cunning Action + Hide** reflex in combat. If she can break line of sight, she does, and then sneak-attacks from a new angle.

**When in doubt** — roll d6. **1-3:** look for profit. **4-6:** look for a story. (When she notices the two are the same thing, the d6 becomes a shrug.)

**Voice tags:**
- Abanasinian hill-vernacular. "Aye," "right then," "fair enough."
- Sentence fragments. "Door. Locked. Obviously." "Hm. Ceiling."
- Goes *quieter*, not louder, when a thing becomes serious.
- Uses people's first names immediately, regardless of station. "Aelric." Not "Sir Aelric." "Laen." Not "Brother Laen."

## Secrets

Thera has, in her pocket, a letter addressed to a man named Nenn Hillfoot at an unknown address. She has been carrying it for six years. She has not opened it. She does not know whether her father sent it *to* her or whether she is meant to deliver it *to* him. The envelope is worn soft at the corners.

She has not told the party she is from Varduin. She will tell them when it becomes useful. She will not be forced to tell them.
