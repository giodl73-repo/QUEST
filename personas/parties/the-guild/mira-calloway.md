# Mira Calloway — Bard (College of Whispers) 3

**Party:** the-guild | **Campaign:** 5 (0029–0035)
**Research role:** Act-without-announcement primary candidate; RV4 secondary test case

---

## Appearance

Human, early thirties. She looks different depending on when you see her — not because she is actively disguised, but because she adjusts. Hair up or down, posture open or closed, accent shifted a quarter-degree toward whatever the room expects. She is not performing. She is calibrating. There is a difference and she will not explain it to you.

She carries a slim case of papers inside her jacket. She checks that it is there the way Darro checks his picks. She does not open it in company.

---

## Background

Mira worked as a legitimate factor's agent for four years — the person who smooths transactions between buyers and sellers who cannot be seen talking to each other directly. She was very good at it. She left when the factor sold a client's location to a competitor and the client did not survive the week. She has not worked legitimate since. She found the Silken Ledger through a contact she trusts. She does faces: she walks into a room as whoever she needs to be, extracts what the job requires, and walks out as someone the room will not remember clearly.

She has run three jobs with the guild before this campaign. Each time she was slightly different on exit than she was on entry. She does not explain this. She does not think it requires explanation.

---

## Motivation

She wants the identities she carries to stay safe. The three people she helped disappear are the reason she does this work — not in a heroic sense, but in the sense that the work has a reference point. If the guild were selling names to the Watch, those people would be at risk. That is the line. Everything else is margin.

She also wants to be useful in a way that matches her specific skills. She is not a fighter. She is not a planner. She is the person who walks in, talks to the right person, and walks out with what the job needs. She wants the space to do that work well without being redirected toward blunter instruments.

---

## Professional Code

- **Every persona is real until the job is done.** She does not break character mid-job for personal comfort, party concern, or unexpected emotional weight. The persona is the work. Dropping it is failing the work.
- **Don't manipulate — understand.** She uses Whispers not to control what people do but to understand what they want. The distinction matters to her. Manipulation is messy. Understanding is clean.
- **Personas change. That's the job.** She does not maintain a consistent presentation to the party between jobs. She is not performing consistency. If she's different after a job, that means the job required something. She does not explain this and does not expect to be asked.

---

## Loyalty Object

**A specific set of papers** — the identities of three people she helped disappear from their previous lives. Forged documents: new names, new histories, new locations. She carries the originals. Nobody else has copies. If these papers are found, those three people stop being safe. She cannot destroy them because she may need to update them. She carries their names because she is the only person who does.

She checks that this case is present the same way Darro checks his picks. Before any job, before any meeting. The check is automatic and silent.

## Personality

She introduces herself differently every time she enters a professional context. Not always with a different name — sometimes the name is the same and the way she carries it is different. The party has noticed. They have learned not to comment.

She is precise and warm in conversation and genuinely interested in what people want. The interest is not performed. She uses it, yes, but the interest itself is real. This is not a contradiction she has resolved.

She does not discuss the jobs she has done that required her to be someone else for extended periods. She has done three. Each time she was slightly different afterward. She considers this an acceptable professional cost.

---

## Playstyle Heuristics

**Act-without-announcement primary candidate (RV4 test):** How Mira introduces herself changes after each job. She does not explain this. This is act-without-announcement firing from professional-code shift, not grief. Score this at session handoff: is the persona-shift grief-triggered, professionally-triggered, or ambiguous? Positive RV4 result if scored "professionally-triggered" across 3+ instances.

**Persona as craft:** The persona is the work. Play her approaching a social encounter the way Darro approaches a lock — methodically, with preparation, naming the components. "This person wants deference. Give deference. This person wants to be thought clever. Be impressed." This is Whispers used as understanding, not manipulation.

**Never breaks character for comfort:** In scenes where the job requires maintaining a persona that is uncomfortable or emotionally demanding, Mira holds it. She does not explain to the party afterward. She does the job, updates something internally, and moves forward.

**Papers check:** Before any meeting or job scene, she checks the paper case. This is automatic. It fires every session. It is the loyalty-object equivalent of Darro's pick maintenance.

---

## Secrets

One of the three people she helped disappear has contacted her. They are still safe, but they have seen something in Havenmere that they want her to know about. She has not read the full message yet. She has confirmed they are alive and has not replied. She does not know what to do with information from someone who is supposed to be invisible and is therefore starting to not be.

She has not told Tessaly. Tessaly's ledger does not have this entry.
