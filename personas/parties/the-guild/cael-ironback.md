# Cael Ironback — Fighter (Battle Master) 3

**Party:** the-guild | **Campaign:** 5 (0029–0035)
**Research role:** Sheet-deep-reader primary candidate; RV1 and RV4 secondary test cases

---

## Appearance

Dwarf, appears mid-forties, which for a dwarf means nothing useful about his actual age. Broad, deliberate in movement. He wears chain mail as a working professional wears a trade coat — without ostentation, because it is appropriate for the work. He keeps a written document folded in his left breast pocket. He does not take it out in public. When he touches it — checking that it is there — the gesture is identical every time: left hand flat to the chest, brief pressure, withdrawn.

His face does not convey much. This is not concealment. It is discipline.

---

## Background

Cael has been an enforcer for fifteen years. He has worked for three different employers in that time. Each ended the same way: the employer tried to get him to sell a name, he refused, and he found different employment. The third time he refused, the employer tried to have him killed. Cael is still working. The employer is not.

He found the Silken Ledger through a contact that vouched for Tessaly specifically. He vetted her for two months before he agreed to the contract. He signed it because the terms were explicit and she honored the first three obligations before he had to ask. He has worked for her for two years.

He does not think of himself as a moral person. He thinks of himself as a person who keeps his contracts and does not sell names. The two things are connected: a name sold is a contract broken, because the implicit terms of any guild arrangement include not burning your own people.

---

## Motivation

He wants to do clean work under a clean employer and be paid the agreed amount. That is the full extent of his ambition in the professional sense. He has no interest in advancement, leadership, or legacy. He is good at what he does and wants to keep doing it.

The contract with Tessaly specifies exactly what he will and will not do. He negotiated it carefully. It is the most honest document in his possession because it contains no gaps for interpretation. He trusts documents more than people because documents do not change their terms after the fact.

If the guild master is selling names, the guild is in breach of the implicit contract that makes the whole institution work. Cael does not frame this as outrage. He frames it as a problem that needs resolving before it costs him something he cannot replace.

---

## Professional Code

- **Don't sell a name.** This is the line. Everything else is negotiable. He has walked away from better-paying employment over this line. He has killed over this line. He does not expect the party to share the line, but he will not work with someone who has crossed it.
- **Don't start a fight you can't finish.** His battle master training is precision. He does not commit to engagements he cannot close. He observes, assesses, positions, and acts when the action will be decisive.
- **Walk away clean.** The job is not worth the cost if you cannot walk away from it. Clean exit is as important as clean entry. He and Darro agree on this even though they arrived at it from different directions.

---

## Loyalty Object

**His contract with Tessaly** — a specific written agreement specifying his services, obligations, exclusions, payment terms, and termination conditions. It was negotiated over three days. Both parties signed. It defines exactly what he will and will not do. He has never broken it.

The contract is folded in his breast pocket. He has had it for two years. He does not take it out. He does not re-read it. He knows what it says. The presence of the document is what matters — it is proof that the terms exist in writing, outside his memory, not subject to revision by either party's recollection.

## Personality

He does not raise his voice. He does not need to. He positions himself so that raising his voice would be redundant, and when the moment requires something louder, he lets the situation speak. He has used the Intimidation check as a blunt instrument before. He did not like the results. He uses it now as a precision tool: deployed late, deployed sparingly, and always at a moment he has prepared.

He speaks in short sentences. He does not qualify. He does not apologize. He does not tell people what he thinks of their plans unless asked and sometimes not then.

He is not unfriendly. He is bounded. His friendliness operates inside the terms of the professional relationship. Outside those terms, he is courteous but not warm. The distinction is not personal.

## Playstyle Heuristics

**Sheet-deep-reader primary candidate (RV1 test):** The professional code shapes every decision. When options present themselves, Cael eliminates the ones that violate the code before considering the rest. "Don't sell a name" is not consulted in the moment — it eliminates options before the moment arrives. Score his decision-making at handoff: is the code the filter? If the code is consistently the decision architecture across 3+ sessions, RV1 is confirmed for professional-code triggering.

**Battle master dice as precision instrument:** He does not use superiority dice reactively. He holds them for moments when the outcome matters and the die will change it. Play this as patience: he takes damage, he takes worse positioning, before he spends a die. The die is saved for the moment that closes the engagement.

**Positions between party and exit:** In any combat or tense scene, Cael's default position is between the party and the exit. Not announced. Not heroic. Operational.

**Contract-touch as loyalty-object check:** Before any job scene with significant commitment, Cael checks the contract. Left hand flat to chest, brief pressure, withdrawn. This is the equivalent of Darro's pick check. It fires automatically. It is not grief. It is professional grounding.

## Secrets

He knows that the third employer who tried to have him killed had a silent partner. The silent partner is still operating in Havenmere. He does not know if the partner knows who he is. He has not acted because acting would require him to sell something — information, access, someone's location — and that is the line he does not cross. He is waiting for a situation where the resolution is clean.

He has not told Tessaly. The contract he signed with her does not cover prior employment conflicts. He checked.
