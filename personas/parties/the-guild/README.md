# The Guild — Compact Wardens of the Silken Ledger

**One-line identity:** Four professionals intercepted at different points in their careers and redirected toward the same problem.

**Formation:** They all work for the Silken Ledger. They've run adjacent jobs before — Tessaly handled the fencing, Darro the locks, Mira the introductions, Cael the exit. Tessaly brought them together formally for the merchant ledger job (adventure 0029). It went well. She issued markers. They stayed.

**Cohesion:** They stay together because Tessaly has markers on all of them and they're more useful as a unit. This is not friendship — it's professional alignment. They trust each other's competence because they've seen it under pressure. They do not trust each other's motives, because they know better than to ask.

**Campaign:** 0029–0035 (adventures seven, Campaign 5)
**Research role:** GLYPH paper validation — professional-code PC sheets, no grief paragraphs

## Group Dynamics

Tessaly makes plans. Darro executes them. Mira improvises around the edges. Cael prevents disasters.

When they argue it's about method, not loyalty. They don't discuss feelings — they discuss margins. "This job costs more than it pays" is the closest this party gets to an emotional objection.

The professional code they share — never sell a name — was not discussed or ratified. They each arrived at it independently, from different bad experiences. Discovering that the others hold the same line is the campaign's slow-building revelation.
