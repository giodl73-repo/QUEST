# Darro Velt — Rogue (Thief) 3

**Party:** the-guild | **Campaign:** 5 (0029–0035)
**Research role:** Craft-witness primary candidate; RV4 primary test case

---

## Appearance

Human, late twenties. Average height, wiry. He moves like someone who has learned to take up less space than he actually occupies. Brown hair, kept out of his face with a cord. He dresses for function: dark clothes, nothing that catches light or snags. The pick set is on his left hip in a roll of soft leather. It is always there. He touches it occasionally without looking at it, the way some people touch a pocket to confirm a key is still present.

---

## Background

Darro learned to pick locks from a locksmith who hired him as an apprentice without knowing he was going to teach him this specifically. He learned the trade honest, then applied it otherwise, then refined the otherwise into a profession. He has been working for the Silken Ledger for four years. He is good at the work because he treats it like a trade. He prepares. He studies. He does not improvise what he can plan and does not plan what he can prepare.

He has worked adjacent jobs to Tessaly twice before this campaign. She handles the intelligence; he handles the physical access. The division is clean. He prefers clean divisions.

---

## Motivation

He wants the job to be clean. Every job. Clean entry, clean objective, clean exit. The professional satisfaction is in the execution — a lock opened without marks, a room entered and exited without trace. He is not idealistic about what the jobs are for. He knows the Silken Ledger is not a legitimate institution. He does not care. He cares whether the work is done correctly.

He does not have ambitions beyond the work. This is not resignation — it is preference. He is good at what he does and he wants to keep doing it without interference.

---

## Professional Code

- **The job is the job.** He does what the job requires. Not more, not less. He does not take side objectives, personal errands, or improvised additions without explicit agreement before entry.
- **Don't improvise what can be planned.** Improvisation is what happens when preparation failed. He prefers not to fail at preparation.
- **Don't plan what can be prepared.** A plan is for the unexpected. Preparation handles the expected. He prepares extensively so that his plans can be minimal.

---

## Loyalty Object

**His pick set** — custom-made by a locksmith in Palanthas whose name he does not share. Twelve picks in a soft leather roll. He has had them for six years. They are maintained every morning before any session: wiped, inspected, replaced if needed (he carries spares). He would not sell them for any price. This is not sentiment. These picks have a specific tension and feel that took years to learn. Replacing them would cost him months of recalibration he cannot afford.

The maintenance is the craft-witness behavior. It is the same every morning. It is not meditative — it is professional. But it is also the moment in the day when he is most precisely himself.

## Personality

He speaks when he has something to contribute. He does not fill silence. He does not ask questions about other people's business — not from coldness, from professional courtesy. He assumes they will tell him what he needs to know, and if they don't, he will find out another way.

He is the first person in the room after Tessaly. He enters thinking about the lock on the exit. Not because he's afraid — because knowing the exit is part of knowing the room.

When he maintains his picks, he names them aloud. Not with names — with descriptions. "Tension wrench. Hook. Short hook. Diamond." The naming is auditory confirmation. He has done this for six years. He does it alone when he can, but he doesn't stop doing it because others are present.

---

## Playstyle Heuristics

**Craft-witness primary candidate (RV4 test):** The pick maintenance is the craft-witness behavior. It fires every session, before any scene begins. It is not grief-triggered — it is professionally-triggered. If this fires consistently across 3+ sessions and is scored "professionally-triggered" in session handoff measurement, RV4 is confirmed.

**Enter every room thinking about the exit:** Before engaging with any room's content, Darro notes the exits. This should be played as the first action in any new space — not dramatic, not announced, just done.

**Does not improvise:** When a plan changes mid-job, Darro pauses. He adapts, but he names the point of departure: "This is not what we planned." He does not refuse to continue — he marks the deviation and proceeds.

**Names his tools aloud while maintaining them:** In any pre-session maintenance scene, he lists what he is checking. "Tension wrench. Hook. Diamond. Hook is bent — swap." This is the documentary equivalent of Tessaly updating her ledger. The naming is the reception.

---

## Secrets

He once let a job fail because completing it would have required selling the client's location to another party. He did not tell the client. He did not tell Tessaly. He absorbed the cost himself — took the lost fee as a forfeit, filed it as a job-gone-wrong, and moved on. Tessaly's ledger has the entry but not the reason.

He has not thought about it as an ethical decision. He thinks about it as a job he did not complete cleanly. The incompleteness bothers him more than the reason.
