# Tessaly Ink — Rogue (Mastermind) 3

**Party:** the-guild | **Campaign:** 5 (0029–0035)
**Research role:** Documentary-witness candidate; RV1 primary test case

---

## Appearance

Half-elf, mid-thirties in appearance though she does not say her age. Brown-black hair kept short, practical. Ink stains on the side of her right hand that no amount of washing removes. She dresses like a prosperous chandler's clerk — unremarkable at a distance, well-made on inspection. She carries a flat leather satchel at all times. The ledger is inside it.

---

## Background

Tessaly grew up in a counting house in Havenmere. She was good at numbers and better at noticing what the numbers meant — which accounts were too clean, which clients were paying double, which transactions had no business reason. She parlayed that skill into the Silken Ledger. She has been fencing information and objects for eleven years. She has never been arrested. She has never been named in a Watch report.

She does not think of herself as a criminal. She thinks of herself as a person who handles things that other people cannot be seen handling. The distinction is not self-deception. It is professional precision.

---

## Motivation

She wants the job to work. Not this job — the job in general. The ongoing operation. The Silken Ledger is the most functional institution she has ever worked for, and she has been inside enough dysfunctional ones to know the difference. If the guild is being compromised from within, she needs to know. Not because it offends her morally — because it breaks the operation.

Her secondary motivation is the ledger. The physical book. She has been keeping it for eleven years. It is complete and accurate. If anyone ever reads it, people will die. She cannot let that happen, and she cannot destroy it, because destroying it would mean eleven years of work are gone and she has nothing to show for them.

---

## Professional Code

- **Never sell a name.** A name sold is a debt that cannot be repaid. She has never sold one. She will not start.
- **Record everything.** The ledger is the record. If it is not in the ledger, it did not happen. If it happened and is not in the ledger, that is a problem to fix immediately.
- **A deal made is a deal kept.** She has never defaulted on a marker. The markers are the currency of her reputation. Her reputation is the only thing she actually owns.

---

## Loyalty Object

**Her ledger** — a flat, cloth-bound book recording every transaction she has made in eleven years of work. Names, amounts, dates, dispositions. It is the most complete criminal ledger in Havenmere and she knows it. If anyone reads it, her contacts die or disappear. She cannot let it be taken. She cannot destroy it. She has not yet resolved this tension and does not think about it directly.

The ledger is also her reception mechanism. She documents things to understand them. When something matters, she writes it down. The writing is the processing.

## Personality

She speaks precisely. She does not volunteer information. She asks questions that sound like small talk and are not. She has a slight pause before responding to anything unexpected — not hesitation, calculation. She is almost always the calmest person in a room, and the room usually adjusts to her pace rather than the other way around.

She does not perform warmth. She performs competence. The two are not the same.

When she updates the ledger in front of the party — mid-conversation, after a decision, after a job — it is not a statement. It is what she does. The ledger is always updated before she speaks to a client about terms.

---

## Playstyle Heuristics

**Sheet-deep-reader:** Her professional code shapes every decision. "Never sell a name" is not consulted — it is the decision. When options branch, eliminate the ones that involve selling a name and route from what remains.

**Documentary-witness candidate:** The ledger is her reception mechanism. When something significant happens — an NPC arc-completion, a revelation, a shift in the job — Tessaly writes it in the ledger. She does not announce this. She does it. Play this as craft-witness behavior: the act of documentation is the reception.

**Never asks a question she doesn't know the answer to:** In negotiation scenes, Tessaly's questions are confirmations. If she doesn't already know the answer, she does not ask. She finds out another way first.

**Checks the room for exits:** On entering any new space, she notes the exits. This is automatic. It is not dramatic — it is the first thing she does, like Darro checking the locks.

---

## Secrets

She knows two of Orren Pale's Watch contacts by name. She has known for three months. She has not acted because she was waiting to see whether the pattern continued or was a single incident. It has continued. The ledger has the entries. She has not told the party.

The ledger also contains Orren Pale's full transaction history with her — eleven years of jobs, all accurate. If it is ever read, Pale goes down. So does she.
