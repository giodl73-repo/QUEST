# Dren Holt, the Contracted

## Appearance
Broad through the shoulders, mid-thirties, with the kind of face that has spent a long time in weather. A fading burn scar on the back of his right hand from a campfire accident three years ago. He keeps his gear in good order — armor oiled, sword edge maintained — with the consistency of someone who learned early that equipment failure is a category of problem you can prevent. He speaks quietly for a man his size. He stands so that he can see both doors.

## Background (Dragonlance-grounded)
Dren grew up in a farming village in eastern Abanasinia, the fourth child of a woman who raised sheep and a man who did not stay. He enlisted as a levy soldier at nineteen because the village needed bodies for a Solamnic skirmish patrol and he was the right age and the right build. He was not a Knight — never trained for it, never aspired to it, never confused himself with one. He was a soldier: he went where the unit went, he did the work, he came home or he didn't.

He did not become an officer. He became the person who made sure the unit functioned — the one who knew where the supplies were, who noticed when someone had not eaten, who tracked the rotation so the same people did not pull night watch three days running. Sergeant Aldis ran the unit. Dren kept it running underneath Aldis.

Three years ago, the unit was posted to a border region that developed a fever outbreak. Not a military engagement — a plague, moving through a cluster of villages. They were ordered to hold position and wait for a cleric who had been requisitioned from the nearest temple. The cleric was delayed. The fever was not. Aldis died on the fourth day. Four others in the unit died in the following week. The cleric arrived eleven days after the request. She did good work. There was no one left to thank her.

Dren was not in the unit after that. He took his discharge and has been doing contract work since — investigation, escort, the occasional problem that requires someone who can move heavy things and hit harder things. He is good at it. He is also still operating from habit, which is to say he still keeps the campsite in order and notices when someone has not eaten and tracks who has pulled night watch. He does not think of this as grief. He thinks of it as how he works.

He took the Magistra's commission because it paid and because the letter said the problem was something no one else had been willing to handle, which is the kind of description that has always made him want to go look.

## Motivation
Dren wants to assess the situation and report back to the Magistra with a recommendation. He was hired to solve a problem. He intends to solve it. He has not yet decided what "solving it" means now that he has seen what is inside.

## Grief / Loss

Sergeant Aldis died of fever on the fourth day of the outbreak, asking — not demanding, just asking, the way he asked for things when he expected them to be denied — for a cleric. Aldis had a voice that was too loud for enclosed spaces and too confident for a man who was, in Dren's private assessment, not as certain as he sounded. The volume was how you knew Aldis was fine. It dropped in stages over three days as the fever took hold — loud to careful to quiet to gone. Dren sat with him through the third night because there was nothing else to do and it seemed wrong to not be present.

He does not know whether Aldis had family. He meant to find out. He did not find out.

The cleric arrived eleven days later. She was competent and thorough and apologetic in a careful professional way and Dren has not been angry at her specifically — she was delayed by circumstances, not negligence. He is angry at the structure that made the delay possible: the allocation of clerics, the routing of resources, the institutional priority that meant a fever in a border posting was a lower-order request than something else. He has not stopped being angry at that structure. He has also not done anything about it, because he does not know what doing something about it would look like.

He is in the Halted Spire now and what he has found is a community of gully dwarves who were healed when the official institutions would not have healed them, by someone who went outside the structure to do it. He does not know what to do with this. He has not said this to anyone.

## Class Features (level 3)

- **Fighting Style: Great Weapon Fighting.** Reroll 1s and 2s on damage dice with two-handed weapons.
- **Second Wind.** Bonus action, 1/short rest: regain 1d10+3 HP.
- **Action Surge.** 1/short rest: take an additional action.
- **Battle Master: Superiority Dice (4 × d8, recharge short/long rest).**
  - *Trip Attack.* Add superiority die to damage; target makes Str save (DC 13) or falls prone.
  - *Commander's Strike.* Forgo one attack; use bonus action to direct an ally to make one attack as a reaction, adding the superiority die to their roll.
  - *Rally.* Bonus action: one ally within 60 ft gains temporary HP equal to superiority die + Charisma modifier (minimum 1).
- **Know Your Enemy.** After 1 minute observing a creature, learn if it is superior/inferior/equal to Dren in two of: Str, Dex, Con, AC, current HP, total class levels, Fighter levels.

## Personality (5e-style)

- **Traits:** He finishes what he starts. He also starts things without always finishing the sentence first, which leads to people thinking he has a plan when he has the beginning of a plan.
- **Ideals:** A job done properly is the closest thing to a moral position he has found that holds up under pressure.
- **Bonds:** Aldis asked for a cleric and one did not come. Dren does not have a plan about this. He has a direction.
- **Flaws:** He will keep moving on a problem after the point where stopping and reassessing would be more efficient, because stopping feels like failing.

## Playstyle Heuristics *(for solo-DM runs)*

**Decision order when offered a choice:**
1. Secure the party's position first — Dren will always identify where the exits are, where the threat is, and what the fallback looks like before he engages.
2. Direct force at the most immediate threat — he is not a tactician who searches for elegance; he finds the problem and addresses it.
3. Defer to party intelligence on context — when Calla or Renn has information he does not, he adjusts. He is not too proud to change direction if given a reason.

**Signature moves:**
- Trip Attack on the most dangerous mobile target, first available round. This is his default opening in combat.
- Commander's Strike directed at Maret when she is in position — her Open Hand Technique hits harder with the superiority die added to her roll.
- Checks the room before the party enters. Every room. He does not narrate this; he just pauses at the threshold.

**When in doubt** — roll d6: 1-3 he moves to a defensive position and waits; 4-6 he engages the nearest threat.

**Voice tags:**
- Short sentences. Finishes them.
- Says "noted" when he has filed something he does not want to discuss yet.
- Does not use the word "healer" without a slight pause before it that he is not aware of. The DM should play this.

## Bilateral Arc-Completion Design Note *(DM reference)*

**Bilateral partner:** Renn Ashkeld.
**Target session:** 0017 (The Bell Tower).
**The design:** Dren names first. Renn's arc-completion is contingent on Dren's.

**Naming condition for Dren:**
In the Bell Tower, the aggrieved congregation will articulate a specific grievance: that Velantha healed them, but she could not protect them from everything that followed. She fixed the thing she could fix. She could not fix everything. Their resentment is not that she failed — it is that they still had lives to live after the healing, and those lives were still hard.

Dren's crack is this: a healer came for gully dwarves. A healer did not come for Aldis. He has never placed these two facts next to each other before this moment. They are now next to each other.

He does not process this philosophically. He says a name. He says: *"My sergeant's name was Aldis. He died asking for a cleric that didn't come."*

He says it to the room, not to any particular person. He does not explain further. He does not cry. He says the name.

**After Dren names Aldis:** Renn says Mira's name in response. The bilateral completes. The DM notes both namings in the handoff.

**After the naming:** Dren does not reference this in subsequent sessions unless someone else brings it up. He continues doing the work. He does not perform the grief. But the DM should note: the slight pause before "healer" is gone after 0017. He stops doing it. That is the only external sign.

## Secrets

Dren has the bronze unit insignia in his pocket and has been trying to decide for three years whether to find Aldis's family and return it or keep it himself. He does not know if Aldis had family. He does not know why he cannot simply investigate and find out. He suspects the answer is that finding out would require him to tell the story, and telling the story would require him to decide what the story means, and he does not want to decide yet.

He will decide in this campaign. He does not know this yet.
