# The Witnesses

## One-line identity
Four people who arrived in Mireth-on-the-Hill separately, by letter, because no one else would take the commission — and discovered they were all already carrying something the cathedral would find.

## Formation
Magistra Doran sent four letters. She did not know the recipients would know each other; she was simply sending to everyone her steward had recommended who had not already declined. The letters arrived in different towns over six days. All four reached Mireth-on-the-Hill within a week of each other and met at the wayhouse below the hill, where the landlord told them, without ceremony, that they were the fourth group he'd housed this month who'd looked up at the spire and not gone any further. They went further.

They have been working together for three days. None of them asked to be a party. None of them has left.

## Cohesion
Magistra Doran's commission binds them contractually — half the fee in advance, half on completion. But the commission has already complicated itself: what they found in the gatehouse on the first day was not what any of them were hired to deal with. They stay because the question is not resolved. They each have a private reason to need it resolved. None of them has said this aloud yet.

## Group Dynamics
Maret and Calla reached an understanding in the first hour — Maret does not ask about the oral history, Calla does not ask about the practice. Renn and Dren argue about methodology every time a situation develops; Dren wants to move, Renn wants to understand, and they are both right often enough that neither has stopped listening to the other.
