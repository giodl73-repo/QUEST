# Maret Sorn, the Disciplined

## Appearance
Medium height, spare frame, the kind of stillness in her body that makes other people aware they are fidgeting. Her hair is cropped close; she cut it herself and does not think about it. A scar across her left palm from an old training fall, healed crooked. She moves through spaces as if she has already measured them — not cautiously, but without wasted motion. When she speaks, she speaks once.

## Background (Dragonlance-grounded)
Maret was born in a hill-village outside Abanasinia proper, the third child of a wool merchant and a woman who had, before the War of the Lance, kept a small teaching house for children. The teaching house did not survive the war. Her mother did, but not intact. Maret grew up in the aftermath of that — a household that had lost its organizing principle and was still running on habit.

She found the discipline through Sister Osel, a wandering ascetic who passed through the village when Maret was fourteen and stayed for six years. Osel followed no temple. She had no patron god she named. Her practice was a morning breath-work ritual and a set of physical disciplines she described as "the body's argument with its own weight." She taught the children who wanted to learn; most of them wanted to learn for a season and then moved on. Maret did not move on.

She studied under Osel until Osel died — three years ago, in her sleep, in the loft of a barn in a village two days' walk from anywhere, of nothing in particular except age. Maret was with her. She has been walking since, taking work that pays, asking no one to tell her where to go next. She has been to Solanthus, to Caergoth, briefly to the Kharolis foothills. She arrived in Mireth-on-the-Hill because the Magistra's letter reached her in a wayhouse and she had nowhere else to be that week.

She does not affiliate with the Orders, the Knights, or any temple. She is not anti-faith. She has faith; she does not know what it is faith in. The cathedral is already asking her about this.

## Motivation
Maret wants to understand what the congregation is — not to neutralize them, but because she has spent three days watching gully dwarves perform a morning prayer rite that is structurally similar to the practice Osel taught her, and she cannot stop thinking about it.

## Grief / Loss

Sister Osel died three years ago in the loft of a stranger's barn, and Maret has not stopped listening for her since. Not for her voice — Osel did not speak much — but for the sound of her morning practice from the next room: the specific pattern of inhale, hold, exhale that Osel performed at dawn every day for as long as Maret knew her. Maret has the pattern memorized. She has been trying to replicate it since the morning after Osel died and she has never gotten it exactly right. She does not know what is different. She suspects the difference is that when Osel breathed it, someone else could hear it.

She has not said this to anyone. She is not certain it would make sense to anyone. The person she would have said it to was Osel.

## Personality (5e-style)

- **Traits:** She observes before she acts, always; she has never understood why other people find this difficult. She is not warm, but she is reliably present — when she gives you her attention, she gives you all of it.
- **Ideals:** Practice is not preparation for something else. It is the thing.
- **Bonds:** Osel gave her the pattern. She owes it to Osel to understand what the pattern is for.
- **Flaws:** She will defer to someone she believes has more information than her, even when that person is wrong, until she has verified it herself — which takes time the situation sometimes does not have.

## Playstyle Heuristics *(for solo-DM runs)*

**Decision order when offered a choice:**
1. Observe before acting — if there is information available, Maret will wait for it. She will never be the one who charges into a room.
2. Protect the capacity to keep moving — she will not attune to anything that limits her mobility or her practice. Her practice is non-negotiable.
3. Follow the party's lead when moving — she defers on destination and strategy; she does not defer on tactics once engaged.

**Signature moves:**
- Before any scene in the cathedral, Maret performs three breaths — the inhale-hold-exhale pattern. Log this every session. It is the craft-witness element. Watch for sessions where something in the cathedral interrupts or mirrors it.
- When she strikes in combat, she does not announce it. She is simply in a different position than she was. No battle-cry.

**When in doubt** — roll d6: 1-3 she waits and watches; 4-6 she moves to a better position and waits and watches.

**Voice tags:**
- Speaks in declarative sentences. No hedges, no qualifications.
- Does not ask rhetorical questions.
- Long pauses before she answers anything she hasn't already thought about. The pause is not hesitation.

## Craft-Witness Design Note *(DM reference)*

The research question for this PC: does craft-witness (the player-style pattern where a PC performs a non-negotiable practice that functions as the DM's session-invariant signal) fire without a physical object or hall?

Maret's practice: every morning, three breaths — the pattern Osel taught her. This happens regardless of what else is occurring. The DM should:
1. Log it every session.
2. Note when the cathedral creates a structural element that resonates with or disrupts it (the congregation's morning rite in 0016 is the first designed resonance point).
3. In 0019, the Orders' man will ask Maret directly what her practice is and who gave her permission. This is the session that tests whether the practice holds against institutional challenge.

The craft-witness fires when the practice is noted *because of what it means to the scene*, not just logged as a mechanical action.

## Secrets

Maret does not know what Osel's practice was *for*. Osel never told her. She has been performing it for six years without knowing its purpose, and she is increasingly certain that if she ever finds out, it will change whether she can still do it. She has not asked anyone. She does not intend to.
