# Renn Ashkeld, the Marked

## Appearance
Late twenties, lean in the way of someone who forgets to eat when he is thinking. Dark circles under his eyes that have been there long enough to look permanent. He keeps his coat buttoned to the collar regardless of weather. His hands are always slightly cold — not noticeably so unless someone shakes his hand, at which point they notice immediately. He carries a small leather-bound book that he writes in often and has never offered to let anyone see.

## Background (Dragonlance-grounded)
Renn grew up near the Neraka border — a region the War of the Lance passed through rather than stopped in, which meant it absorbed the consequences of the war without most of the formal aid. His family ran a small trade operation out of a border village; his father moved things north and south through passes the Knights did not bother to watch. It was a marginal life run by people who had learned to ask no one for permission.

His sister Mira was seven years younger than him. She was the one in the family who had opinions about what things ought to be like — not in the way that got people killed, but in the persistent, cheerful way of someone who had not yet been told enough times that it was not worth it. She died of a fever in the second winter after the war. She was twenty-one. Renn was twenty-eight.

He had made his pact six months earlier.

He found the site by accident — a collapsed Istaran wayhouse in a valley three days' walk from his village, sealed under a landslide until a wet autumn loosened the stones. He went in because he was curious. He found something in the deepest chamber that he has never fully described to anyone: a presence, ancient, indifferent in the way oceans are indifferent, that offered him a transaction. He accepted. He received the abilities of a warlock without a test, without an Order, without any of the formal structures the Conclave of Wizards would have imposed if he had ever told them.

He told no one. The Orders do not know. He has been using the pact's gifts for four years to do work that is useful — investigation, protection, the occasional negotiation where someone needed to feel observed — and has been trying not to think about the fact that he made the pact to save Mira and she died before he had the power to do anything.

He took the Magistra's commission because problem-solving from the outside is what he does now.

## Motivation
Renn wants to understand what the Orders' man in the Sacristy gave Velantha — specifically whether it was the same kind of unsanctioned transaction he made, or something worse. He has not told his party why this interests him.

## Grief / Loss

Mira Ashkeld died of fever in the second winter after the War of the Lance. She was twenty-one. Renn had made a pact with something ancient and unnamed six months before, for power he hoped would let him help people the established institutions had stopped bothering to help. He was too slow. She was already past the point where anything he could do would matter by the time he reached her.

The last morning he saw her conscious, he had made her a cup of broth — something warm, something practical, something he could do. She held it in both hands the way she always held warm things: wrapped tight, thumbs up, as if she was keeping the heat from escaping. Her hands were always cold, even in summer. He remembers the cup. He does not remember what she said. He has tried to remember and he cannot. He can only remember her hands around the cup, and the steam going up, and then her eyes closing in a way that was, at that moment, just sleep.

He got the power in time for everything else. He did not get it in time for her.

## Personality (5e-style)

- **Traits:** He asks clarifying questions when he should move and moves when he should ask clarifying questions; he has never identified which situation he is in until after it is over. He is honest about the limits of his knowledge and dishonest about everything else.
- **Ideals:** Power that arrives too late is still power. You use it anyway.
- **Bonds:** He owes Mira a different use of the pact than what he has made of it so far.
- **Flaws:** He will not tell his party what he is, what he did, or where his abilities come from. He tells himself this is for their protection. He knows it is for his.

## Playstyle Heuristics *(for solo-DM runs)*

**Decision order when offered a choice:**
1. Understand the situation before committing — Renn will always try to gather information via Awakened Mind, Detect Thoughts (if Calla has it running), or direct observation before he decides.
2. Control the encounter before engaging — Hold Person on the most dangerous target; Hex on the one that needs to die. He prefers to neutralize over to kill.
3. Protect the party; do not expose himself unnecessarily — he will not use his pact abilities in a way that would require him to explain them unless the alternative is worse.

**Signature moves:**
- Uses Awakened Mind to communicate with party members in tense situations without speaking aloud — a tactical advantage he has had to explain less than he expected.
- Will attempt to identify the nature of any magical ability or object he encounters. This is compulsive. He will stop what he is doing to examine something that does not fit.

**When in doubt** — roll d6: 1-3 he watches and telegraphs attention via Awakened Mind; 4-6 he acts and telegraphs reasoning via Awakened Mind after.

**Voice tags:**
- Speaks precisely; avoids contractions when he is being careful.
- Asks "what are you keeping?" rather than "what do you know?" — a tell that he is interested.
- Goes quiet when he is using Awakened Mind. His lips don't move but he looks at whoever he is speaking to.

## Bilateral Arc-Completion Design Note *(DM reference)*

**Bilateral partner:** Dren Holt.
**Target session:** 0017 (The Bell Tower).
**The design:** Renn's arc-completion cannot fire until Dren's has. The bilateral structure requires Dren to name Aldis first; Renn names Mira in response.

**Naming condition for Renn:**
In the Bell Tower, when the aggrieved congregation articulates the specific grievance — "she healed us but she could not save us from everything that came after" — Renn recognizes this structure. He made a pact that gave him power and still could not save the person the power was for. He does not say this. He watches Dren.

When Dren says Aldis's name aloud, Renn says: *"My sister's name was Mira. I was also late."*

That is the arc-completion line. It does not need to be longer. The DM should not extend it. Renn names her, places her in the same category as Aldis — "also late" — and stops. He does not explain the pact. He does not say more. This is the first time he has named her to anyone in this way.

**After the naming:** Renn does not reference this exchange again unless Dren brings it up. He continues doing the work. The arc is complete. The DM notes it in the handoff.

## Secrets

Renn made his pact with something he cannot identify and has never named. He does not know if it is a god, a remnant of Istar, something that predates the Cataclysm, or something that has no category in any theology he has encountered. He suspects it is indifferent to his purposes in the way that deep water is indifferent to what drowns in it. He made the transaction anyway. He uses the gifts. He has not investigated the patron beyond determining that it does not appear to want anything from him yet. "Yet" is the word he does not say aloud.

He has told no one about the pact. Not its existence, not its source, not its cost. He does not know the cost yet.
