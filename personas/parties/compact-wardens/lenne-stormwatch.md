# Lenne Stormwatch

**Red Robe Abjuration Wizard, Level 7.** Human, 29. Joined the compact-wardens in C2-S02 at Volenn's Tower, Kharolis foothills.

## Stat block

**AC** 12 (Mage Armor 15) | **HP** 44 | **Speed** 30 ft.
**STR** 8 | **DEX** 14 (+2) | **CON** 13 (+1) | **INT** 19 (+4) | **WIS** 13 (+1) | **CHA** 10

**Saves:** INT +7, WIS +4
**Skills:** Arcana +7, History +7, Investigation +7, Insight +4

**Attacks:** Dagger +5 (1d4+2) | Fire Bolt +7 (2d10)

**Spells prepared (11):** Detect Magic, Identify, Mage Armor, Shield, Counterspell, Misty Step, Dispel Magic, Fireball, Hold Person, Arcane Eye, Banishment

**Arcane Ward (Abjuration School):** When Lenne casts an abjuration spell of 1st level or higher, a magical ward appears that has HP equal to twice her wizard level + INT mod (= 22 HP). The ward absorbs damage for her until depleted; then refreshes when she next casts an abjuration spell of 1st level or higher.

**Projected Ward:** When another creature she can see within 30 ft. takes damage, she can use her reaction to expend the ward's HP to reduce that damage. (This is her primary contribution to party durability — she protects others, not just herself.)

## Appearance

Post-S02 Lenne is the same as NPC Lenne, with one difference she cannot see: she is slightly less tightly wound than she was in the tower. Not relaxed — she does not do relaxed — but less compressed. She still has the ink on her right hand. She keeps a small notebook in her added robe-pocket at all times; she fills about a page per day.

## Grief paragraph

**What she lost:** Volenn died eight months before the triple-moon convergence he had been tracking for forty years. He was the one who was supposed to be there. He had the calendar, the research, the contacts, the forty years of understanding. He died in autumn. The convergence is in spring. He missed it by eight months.

She does not say she grieves him. She says: *"He should have been there."* She says it rarely and means it completely.

**The specific sensory memory:** The sound of his voice correcting her pronunciation of High Istaran, which he did every time she read an artifact inscription aloud for the first two years of her apprenticeship. She still hears it when she reads inscriptions. She says the words correctly now. He is not there to confirm it.

## Background: Volenn's apprentice; now his successor

Six years of apprenticeship, then eight years alone in his tower. She now knows the tower, the Kharolis region, the Red Robe chapter-house in Korrath, the Palanthas supplier network, and approximately eight years of compact-artifact research data. She has three additional artifact-fragment references in Volenn's archive (not shards — other objects he was studying that may connect to the compact's context).

She joined the compact-wardens because Volenn was supposed to be there for the convergence and she is the closest thing to Volenn that still exists. She did not say this out loud. She said: *"How many shards do you have?"*

## PC sheet: decision-order in play

1. **Object first:** She will examine an artifact before she looks at the room around it. This is not inattention — it is methodology. The object is usually more honest than the situation. When this gets her into trouble, it will be because she trusted an artifact over the people holding it.
2. **Volenn's methods:** His systematic research process — document the question before attempting the answer; record failures in order; never test a hypothesis with the primary specimen — is her first-response framework. She will ask "how would Volenn approach this?" before she asks "how do I want to approach this?"
3. **Overreach watch:** Post-compact-context, she is alert to situations where someone (including herself) is claiming more than their share. This is new behavior, post-S02. She named it herself. She is not sure yet how it will manifest.
4. **The fourth heuristic (new):** *"What am I not seeing because I have been alone too long?"* She added this after S02. She has not yet had to use it in the field.
5. **In doubt:** Document. If she cannot write, outline. If she cannot outline, stop speaking until she can. Decisions made before the question is fully articulated are not decisions; they are reactions.

## Research assets (carried forward from Volenn's archive)

- **Volenn's trial journal** (her copy, 327 entries) — systematic vibration-response data for the Clause of Ambition. Now that she knows what it is, the data is recontextualized. She will have notes on 3 other compact-related fragments within 48 hours of joining.
- **Volenn's moon-calendar copy** — she made her own copy before leaving the tower. She is now tracking the convergence daily.
- **Three fragment references** in Volenn's archive (she left them in the tower; she knows their locations and their catalog entries). May be relevant to later adventures.

## Interplay with the party

- **Thessaly:** Two wizards; different schools (Divination vs. Abjuration); different orientations (Thessaly reads people; Lenne reads objects). They will not agree on methodology. They will respect each other's competence. They will argue.
- **Orik:** He named the hall. She gave him the shard. She has not said anything about this since. She is processing what it means that a hall-guard understood her situation in approximately four minutes.
- **Sera:** She held space twice in the tower. Lenne noticed. She did not say anything about it. She is not sure how to respond to someone who cares through attention.
- **Calder:** He is older than the others; he has the manner of someone who has administered last rites many times. She does not know what this means about him yet. She knows what it means about the work — he understands the weight of things that are finished and things that are not.
