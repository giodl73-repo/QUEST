# Calder of the Third Road

**Human Life Domain Cleric of Mishakal, Level 5.** 52.

## Stat block

**AC** 18 (Chain Mail + Shield) | **HP** 48 | **Speed** 30 ft.
**STR** 14 (+2) | **DEX** 10 | **CON** 14 (+2) | **INT** 12 | **WIS** 18 (+4) | **CHA** 14 (+2)

**Saves:** WIS +7, CHA +5
**Skills:** Medicine +10, Insight +7, Religion +4, Persuasion +5

**Attacks:** Mace +5 (1d6+2) | Sacred Flame +7 (2d8 radiant, no cover)

**Channel Divinity (2/short rest):**
- **Preserve Life:** Distribute up to 5× cleric level HP among creatures within 30 ft., up to half their HP max each.
- **Turn Undead:** Undead must succeed WIS DC 15 or flee for 1 minute.

**Disciple of Life (Life Domain):** Healing spells heal for 2 + spell level additional HP.

**Spells prepared (8+4 domain):** Cure Wounds, Healing Word, Bless, Shield of Faith, Spiritual Weapon, Hold Person, Mass Healing Word, Beacon of Hope
**Domain spells:** Bless, Cure Wounds, Lesser Restoration, Spiritual Weapon, Revivify, Beacon of Hope

## Appearance

Calder is a large man gone slightly soft after decades of walking rather than fighting. His robes are Mishakal's blue-and-white, worn through at the elbows and mended three times. His hands are a healer's hands — practiced, steady, always slightly cool. He carries more bandages than is strictly necessary and a flask of purified water he blesses every morning whether he expects to use it or not. His hair is mostly grey. He has the kind of face that people decide to trust and then are not sure why.

## Grief paragraph

**What he lost:** Calder ministered to a village in the Abanasinian river-country during a plague outbreak fourteen years ago. He arrived three days after the plague began; he left eight days later. Forty-one people died in those eight days. Seventeen survived. He administered last rites for all forty-one; he sat with them, he held their hands, he spoke Mishakal's words for each one. He saved seventeen. He has never fully believed that seventeen is enough.

**The specific sensory memory:** The weight of his pack when it's full of remedies he has used. It is lighter then — lighter than when he arrives, lighter than when he leaves. He can feel the difference in his shoulders. After the plague village, he started carrying an empty stone in his pack to keep the weight from feeling like evidence of failure. He still carries it.

## Background: traveling healer, Mishakal's Third Road chapter

The Third Road is Calder's informal designation for the itinerant healers of Mishakal who don't hold a shrine or temple but walk the roads between settlements. He has been on the Third Road for twenty-two years. Archivist Tessamine knew him from a previous commission — she was injured on a field survey and he mended her broken arm and refused payment. She remembered.

## PC sheet: decision-order in play

1. **Triage:** Someone dying? Everything else is secondary until that is addressed. This is not a moral hierarchy; it is a trained reflex. He has been doing this for thirty years.
2. **Weight:** How much will this decision cost to carry? Calder has been carrying his plague village for fourteen years. He knows what it is to make a choice that was right and that still weighs. He is not reckless, but he is also not afraid of cost. He is afraid of not being able to carry it.
3. **The ignored:** He notices who the room has forgotten. The person the conversation has moved away from; the NPC everyone decided doesn't matter; the PC who's been quiet for two scenes. He turns toward them.
4. **Mishakal's requirement:** Do no harm, bear witness, carry those who cannot carry themselves. He will not harm the innocent to save others. He will not lie to protect someone's feelings. He will not abandon someone because it would be easier to.
5. **In doubt:** Administer what you can. You cannot heal everything. That is not a failure; it is the condition. He has said this to dying people. He believes it. Most days.

## Interplay with the compact

The Clause of Defiance's second inscription — "as I wrote above, what any mortal swore, any mortal may unsay" — will matter to Calder when the context arrives: the defiant wizard's objection was to a clause that allowed the Orders to withhold healing during a precursor event. The plague Calder couldn't stop happened during a period when the Orders were not cooperating with healers of the non-Order gods. He does not know this yet. When the Hint 3 delivery happens and Calder (passive Religion 15) reads the defiant wizard's amendment, he will understand, in that moment, what the compact cost before the Cataclysm ended it. That is his arc.
