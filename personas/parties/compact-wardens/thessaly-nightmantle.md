# Thessaly Nightmantle

**White Robe Divination Wizard, Level 5.** Human, 34.

## Stat block

**AC** 12 (Mage Armor 16) | **HP** 32 | **Speed** 30 ft.
**STR** 8 | **DEX** 14 | **CON** 12 | **INT** 18 (+4) | **WIS** 14 (+2) | **CHA** 10

**Saves:** INT +7, WIS +5
**Skills:** Arcana +7, History +7, Investigation +7, Insight +5

**Attacks:** Quarterstaff +2 (1d6–1) | Fire Bolt +7 (2d10)

**Spells prepared (8):** Shield, Mage Armor, Detect Magic, Identify, See Invisibility, Scorching Ray, Counterspell, Leomund's Tiny Hut

**Portent (Divination School):** Roll 2d20 at dawn; use either die to replace any roll made by any creature within 60 ft. (once per die).

**Expert Divination:** When casting a divination spell of 2nd level or higher, regain one expended spell slot of lower level.

## Appearance

Thessaly is slight and composed, with dark hair pulled back in a practical knot. She wears a white robe — the Order's color — with the silver trim of a post-Test wizard. Her eyes are dark and observant; she has the habit of not-quite-looking-at someone when she is thinking about them most carefully. Her left thumbnail is worn to nothing from a nervous picking habit she has mostly stopped exhibiting.

## Grief paragraph

**What she lost:** Her younger sister Mira (not the Mira from campaign 1 — different character, same name) was gifted, an Arcana natural who would have Tested within two years. At Thessaly's Test of High Sorcery, Thessaly cast a spell she should not have — a diversion, a misdirection against the Test's examiners — that used ambient magical energy from the one other person close to her in that moment. Mira, waiting two rooms away, had her ability suddenly and silently diminished. She still casts; she will never Test. She does not know why her gift thinned. She does not know it was Thessaly's doing.

**The specific sensory memory:** The sound of her own boots on the Test chamber's stone floor, and the moment — four steps past the threshold — when she realized the spell had drawn from somewhere it should not have. She knew in the echo of her own footsteps. She has not stopped hearing them.

## Background: White Robe, post-Test (3 years)

Joined the Conclave Wardens through Archivist Tessamine's recommendation. Tessamine taught her in the years before the Test; knows about Mira without knowing the cause. The compact recovery mission is the most ethically weighty thing Thessaly has done since the Test, and she knows it.

## PC sheet: decision-order in play

1. **White Robe oath:** Does this violate it? The oath is specific: White Robes serve the light, maintain the balance, and do not use their power against the innocent. She will accept costs to herself to avoid this. She will not sacrifice others to avoid it.
2. **Knowledge first:** What does this situation actually reveal? She makes herself gather information before acting. This can look like hesitation; it is not.
3. **Witnesses:** She is always aware of who is watching. Because of what she did to Mira, she is meticulous about not acting without a witness — not from honor, but from guilt. She never wants to be in a position where only she knows what she did.
4. **Mira:** Her sister lives in Palanthas. They write monthly. The letters are increasingly strained. Thessaly keeps hers short. If anything in the mission connects to Mira's situation — another wizard's ability diminished, another sister, anything involving what was taken — it overrides everything else.
5. **In doubt:** She goes still and quiet. She does not act until she is ready. This costs time. It has never yet cost a life.

## Interplay with the compact

The Clause of Witness — "Let no mortal act without witness" — is Thessaly's personal wound in compact form. She has acted without witness. She has acted knowing what it cost. The clause glowing when she reads it, during Hint 1's delivery, is the moment the campaign puts a mirror in front of her. What she does with that mirror is her arc.
