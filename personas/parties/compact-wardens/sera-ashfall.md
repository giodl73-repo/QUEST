# Sera Ashfall

**Half-Elf Hunter Ranger, Level 5.** 27.

## Stat block

**AC** 15 (Studded Leather) | **HP** 44 | **Speed** 35 ft.
**STR** 12 | **DEX** 18 (+4) | **CON** 14 (+2) | **INT** 12 | **WIS** 16 (+3) | **CHA** 12

**Saves:** STR +4, DEX +7
**Skills:** Perception +6, Stealth +7, Survival +6, Nature +4, Investigation +4

**Attacks:** Longbow +7 (1d8+4, 150/600 ft.) | Shortsword +7 (1d6+4)

**Favored Enemy:** Mages (advantage on checks to track, resist spells of charm/fear)
**Natural Explorer:** Korrath coastal terrain; difficult terrain in forest/coast does not slow her

**Hunter Conclave:**
- **Colossus Slayer:** Once per turn, deal extra 1d8 against a target already hurt.
- **Escape the Horde:** Opportunity attacks against her have disadvantage.

**Spells:** Hunter's Mark (concentration), Cure Wounds, Pass Without Trace, Silence

## Appearance

Sera is lean and quiet, with the mixed features of a half-elf who looks more human than elven except in her eyes, which are a shade of amber that belongs to neither parent. Her hair is ash-brown, worn in a braid that she tucks under her collar when she's working. Her gear is practical and unmarked — no faction insignia, no identifying details. She carries three small knives she has never once used in combat; they are for cutting rope and carving marks into trees.

## Grief paragraph

**What she lost:** Sera was tracking a band of poachers through the Qualinesti borderlands for a small settlement that had hired her — three weeks out, working alone as she preferred. When she returned with the poachers' location and a safe route, the settlement was gone. Not destroyed; gone. They had packed up and moved without her. The headman had left a note: *"Didn't know when you'd return. Couldn't wait. Thank you for the previous seasons."* She found the note nailed to a tree. The poachers were never prosecuted.

**The specific sensory memory:** The particular sound a door makes when it's been recently used — the creak of a still-settling hinge, the faint displacement of cool air past warm. She checks doors this way in every building she enters. She is listening for the sound of someone who just left.

## Background: independent tracker, occasional Conclave courier

Tessamine hired her through a mutual contact — an innkeeper in Korrath who knew Sera was reliable and would not ask about the job before taking it. Sera took it because Korrath was convenient and the pay was good. She has been with the wardens for six weeks now and is beginning to admit that she does not intend to leave when the contract ends.

## PC sheet: decision-order in play

1. **Track first:** What is the situation's movement? Where have people gone, where are they going, what is approaching from the wrong direction? Sera is always building a map in her head.
2. **Party perimeter:** Her functional role is watching what the party misses. Orik watches the room; Sera watches what's outside the room, above it, and behind it.
3. **No one left behind:** She does not let parties splinter. If someone is about to be separated — by a door, by a fight, by a decision that pulls them away from the group — she says something. This is not heroic. It is compulsive. She knows this about herself.
4. **Terrain:** She fights from position. She will spend a round getting to height before she will engage from the floor.
5. **In doubt:** Find high ground and wait. The answer is usually in the information that arrives if you are patient enough.

## Interplay with the compact

The Clause of Grief — the adventure 3 shard — will require Sera to name what she lost. The Kagonesti elder who holds it will not release it until someone names their grief first. Sera's grief is the hardest to name because it has no villain: no one wronged her; the settlement just left. That is the grief she has been unable to speak. The elder's condition, and the shard's design, will make her speak it.
