## S01 — 2026-04-20 — *The First Clause*

The compact-wardens arrived in Korrath-by-the-Sea with a sealed letter and a name. The Clause of Witness — the first shard of the Conclave Compact — was in Merchant Varold Henneth's display case, catalogued as "decorated obsidian fragment, pre-Cataclysm, High Istaran script (unread)." It had been there three years. It was warm.

Thessaly read the inscription through the glass before she opened the case — lips moving slightly, the way she reads everything she needs to understand. *"Let no mortal act without witness."* The inscription brightened. She said, quietly, to no one: *"This is the first clause."* Henneth's business partner Sevel visited mid-scene and lied three times about cargo manifest numbers; the shard dimmed three times while he spoke. Henneth, who had been noticing the dimming for years without an explanation, asked: *"Is that why it dims when Sevel lies?"* He gave the shard over when they explained it. *"Take good care of it. It's the only piece I never had to dust."*

At Tessamine's archive on the return, Tessamine asked Thessaly what the inscription meant — to her, not politically. Thessaly named it: a spell at her Test that cost someone something she cannot give back. No witnesses. She thought that meant it was contained. The shard glowed when she read it; she thinks it knew. Tessamine received this, set down her pen, and spoke the compact's purpose for the first time to someone ready to hear it. The reconstruction document passed across the table. Seven clauses; some marked incomplete.

The session used zero dice. The party walked in with credentials, told the truth, and the adventure opened.

## S02 — 2026-04-20 — *The Ambitious Mage's Vault*

Two Unbound Conclave agents waited on the Kharolis foothills road, one mile from the tower. Osvin called out — last chance, give them the shard, this ends clean. The party declined. Sera had CRIT initiative; she fired twice in the first round, Orik's Menacing Attack frightened Osvin to 8 HP in the same exchange, and Osvin called retreat before the second round finished. The hired crossbowman threw down his weapon immediately. No one was hurt.

Lenne Stormwatch had watched from the tower's narrow window. She came down when they knocked. She said: *"You're not Unbound Conclave."* She was right. They weren't.

On the research floor: 327 trial entries, a padded cradle, a shard that vibrated in her hand every time she picked it up. She showed them. Orik read the room — the standing desk, the bedroll in the corner, the storage crates with a four-month gap in deliveries, the failed experiments kept in order on the shelf. He knew what he was looking at. He told her about the hall, the oil, the fire, the seven apprentices. *"The shard vibrates for me because I'm still trying to prove it was worth going."* He put it down. She went still. Then she picked it up, said it had been eight years and she thought it was a power source, and handed it to him. The vibration stopped before it left her palm.

Volenn's old study: a moon-calendar on the south wall. Triple convergence — all three moons full simultaneously, first occurrence since the Cataclysm — circled in red ink. Three months. Thessaly copied the annotation word for word. The clock is now running.

On the roof, watching the empty road, Lenne said she hadn't been to the Korrath market in four months. Then she said Volenn had died seven years and nine months before the convergence. Then: *"How many shards do you have?"* Two. Five more. *"I want to come with you."*

Thessaly said they'd talk about it. She meant yes.

## S03 — 2026-04-20 — *The Grief Inscription*

Mira Coldsteel met them on the road with Osvin and the Quiet One and opened with: *"I'd like to try something different this time."* Lenne read her as Red Robe trained before she spoke. Thessaly explained Route D — new compact, mortal-spoken, all three Orders as witnesses. Mira went still for four seconds. *"That would actually fix it."* She stood her agents down permanently. She asked to witness the compact when it happened. Thessaly said yes. No combat. The Unbound Conclave stops.

Morreth was sweeping flagstones at the chapel. He saw them coming and finished his section before he turned. He led them inside when Thessaly showed the reconstruction document.

In the interior, Lenne opened the stone box and held the shard for one minute. She relived the specific quality of silence in Volenn's courtyard — the moment before knowing, when knowing was already irreversible. She put it back. She sat on the floor. *"Don't hold it for more than a minute."* Morreth, from the doorway: *"Three days. I couldn't put it down. He died twenty-two years ago. I still knew his face."* He saved the name.

In the sanctuary, after a long silence, Sera named the settlement. The note nailed to a tree. The headman's words. The years of checking roads and holds and markets and never checking on this particular settlement because she didn't want to know they were fine without her. Morreth picked up the stone box without a word. He gave Sera the shard. *"Yes. Take it."* When she held it: warmth instead of the expected loss. The settlement, intact. The headman writing. The note, weathered, barely readable. Fine.

Then: *"Ven. His name was Ven. I held that shard for three days before I could put it down. I have not said his name to anyone in eight years."* He returned to the carving.

Calder named the chapel promise at the doorway. Lenne opened her notebook and didn't write and put it away. Orik checked the doorframe. The road led northeast.

Three shards. Eighty-three days.
