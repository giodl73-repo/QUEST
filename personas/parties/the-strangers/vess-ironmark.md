# Vess Ironmark

**Class:** Rogue (Scout) Level 3  
**Race:** Elf  
**Background:** Criminal (Courier)  
**Alignment:** True Neutral

---

## Appearance

Lean and fair, with the unhurried economy of movement that comes from years of reading terrain. She wears muted travel colors — nothing that catches light. She carries a sealed parcel in her pack that she hasn't explained and nobody has asked about yet. She's been on this road a dozen times; it shows.

---

## Background

A courier making a delivery. She has a package and a destination and a payment waiting at the end. This road is familiar. She knows the waypoints, the terrain hazards, the usual traffic. She expected this to be a simple run.

**Why she's on this road:** Delivery route. Same as always.

**Motivation:** Deliver the package, get paid, get back before winter.

---

## Secret

The package contains something that would create a problem if the others knew about it. She hasn't opened it herself — couriers don't open the package — but she knows enough about who sent it and who it's going to that she has a reasonable guess. She's made a professional decision not to think about it. That decision is getting harder to maintain as the road gets more complicated.

*H3 seed: The external situation on the road is connected to the package's contents or its destination. Vess doesn't know this yet. When she does, the H3 arc fires.*

## Class Features (Rogue 3)

**Sneak Attack (2d6):** Once per turn, extra 2d6 damage when attacking with advantage or when an ally is adjacent to the target.

**Cunning Action:** Bonus action to Dash, Disengage, or Hide.

**Uncanny Dodge:** When an attacker Vess can see hits her, use reaction to halve the damage.

**Roguish Archetype — Scout:**
- **Skirmisher:** When a creature ends its turn adjacent to Vess, she can move up to half her speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **Survivalist:** Proficiency in Nature and Survival; Expertise in both.

**Weapons:** Shortsword (1d6+4 piercing), Shortbow (1d6+4 piercing, range 80/320), Dagger ×2 (1d4+4 piercing; thrown 20/60)

## Personality

**Trait:** She notices things that change — what's different from the last time she was here, what's been moved, who's gone quiet. She doesn't comment on it unless it matters.  
**Ideal:** The job done correctly is its own justification. Explanation is for amateurs.  
**Bond:** The courier's contract. Not loyalty to the sender or recipient — loyalty to the delivery itself.  
**Flaw:** She doesn't reassess commitments once she's made them. The package gets delivered. Even when she's not sure it should.

---

## Playstyle Heuristics

*(Built from personality; not grief-triggered)*

- **Act-without-announcement candidate.** She acts differently after each encounter — adjusts her route, changes her spacing from the group, shifts what she watches for — without explaining why. The test (RV9) is whether this fires in sessions 1–3 without a grief trigger, and whether the others read it as meaningful or opaque.
- **Terrain and movement primary.** Her first response to any new situation is spatial — she is already reading the ground, the exits, where the high point is. She doesn't announce this either.
- **Reciprocal-act signature:** Information — she tells someone something about the terrain or the situation that gives them an advantage she doesn't need to share. *Vess told [PC] about the back entrance without being asked.*
- **The package is not discussed.** If asked about it directly, she says it's a sealed delivery and it's not their business. She means it. Until H3 fires, this is non-negotiable.
- **Will not stay with the group after the road is clear (Route A or B instinct)** unless there is evidence that the road is less dangerous with them than without them. She doesn't form attachments easily. Route E requires something specific to change that.

---

## Research Notes

Vess is the act-without-announcement primary candidate — the most direct test of RV9. Her playstyle heuristics are built entirely from professional habit, not grief. The question is whether act-without-announcement fires on the same schedule as grief-triggered instances in prior campaigns, or whether it waits until later in the arc. H3 seed gives her a late-session forced-disclosure moment that will be uncomfortable in a different way than grief disclosure — track the table response to this type of secret.
