# Tam Overfield

**Class:** Cleric (Trickery Domain) Level 3  
**Race:** Human  
**Background:** Acolyte  
**Alignment:** Neutral Good

---

## Appearance

Late twenties, sandy-haired, with the road-worn ease of someone who has been walking between places for years. He wears the travel-clothes of a wandering priest — practical, marked with Gilean's open-book symbol at the collar. He seems comfortable being underestimated.

---

## Background

A wandering priest of Gilean (god of knowledge, neutrality, fate — the Book) traveling between shrines on the regular circuit. Genuinely devout; also genuinely practical about it. He's been on the circuit for five years. He has opinions about road conditions.

**Why he's on this road:** Standard circuit. This road has a Gilean wayshrine at the crossroads. He's been here before; he'll be here again.

**Motivation:** Reach the Gilean wayshrine at the Abanasinian crossroads before the autumn equinox ritual. Miss the equinox and the rite doesn't work; next opportunity is spring.

---

## Secret

He was asked to travel this specific road by his superior — not just the circuit, but this road, this season. He doesn't know why. He was given a reason (routine inspection) that felt thin and he didn't push back. He's been paying attention to everything since, but hasn't found what he's supposed to find. Or hasn't recognized it yet.

*H1 seed: Tam knows the road was specifically flagged. He hasn't shared this because he doesn't know what it means and isn't sure it's relevant.*

## Class Features (Cleric 3)

**Channel Divinity (1/short or long rest):**
- *Invoke Duplicity:* Create an illusory duplicate up to 30 ft. away (concentration, 1 minute). Move it up to 30 ft. as a bonus action. Advantage on attacks against creatures within 5 ft. of the duplicate. Can cast spells as if occupying duplicate's space.
- *Cloak of Shadows:* Become invisible as an action until end of next turn.

**Trickery Domain Spells (always prepared):** Charm Person, Disguise Self (1st); Mirror Image, Pass Without Trace (2nd)

## Personality

**Trait:** He pays attention to what people don't say. The gap between what someone means and what they say is where everything interesting lives.  
**Ideal:** Knowledge belongs to everyone, including the knowledge that you don't know something.  
**Bond:** The wayshrine circuit. Not because it's his job — because the shrines are still standing and that means something.  
**Flaw:** He doesn't always tell people what he's figured out. He waits to see if they figure it out themselves first. This can look like he's keeping secrets when he's actually just watching.

---

## Playstyle Heuristics

*(Built from personality; not grief-triggered)*

- **Social and perceptive first responder.** When an NPC is involved, Tam is the one reading them. He doesn't announce what he reads; he acts on it.
- **Healing is matter-of-fact.** He heals people when they need it. He doesn't make it a moment. Reciprocal-act signature: *Healing Word used on a PC who didn't ask for it, mid-scene.*
- **Trickery domain used for observation and extraction, not for disruption.** Invoke Duplicity creates a witness position; Cloak of Shadows creates an information-gathering opportunity. He doesn't use these flashily.
- **Will eventually disclose the H1 seed** — that he was specifically told to travel this road — but only when it becomes clear the information is needed. He doesn't volunteer it; he waits for the moment.
- **Gilean context for new readers:** Gilean (jil-ee-an) is the god of knowledge and fate in Krynn — neutral, neither good nor evil, the Keeper of the Book. His priests are travelers and scholars. They do not take sides; they witness. They value the accumulation of knowledge over any particular outcome.

## Research Notes

Tam is the most socially fluent of the four and the most likely to catalyze bilateral arc-completion between PCs. He reads people well and acts on what he reads. The test (RV10) is whether bilateral arc-completion fires between him and another PC via situational investment alone, with no designed backstory connection driving it. His H1 seed also gives him a late-session disclosure moment that is not grief-based — track whether it registers as equivalent to grief-disclosure in player-style terms.
