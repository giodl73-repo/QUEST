# Veteran Dorn Ashfeld

**Class:** Fighter — Battle Master Archetype, Level 3
**Race:** Dwarf
**Alignment:** Lawful Neutral

## Stat block

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 17 (+3) | 11 (+0) | 16 (+3) | 10 (+0) | 13 (+1) | 9 (-1) |

**HP:** 30 | **AC:** 16 (chain mail) | **Speed:** 25 ft.

**Saving throws:** Str +5, Con +5
**Skills:** Athletics +5, History +2, Intimidation +1, Perception +3
**Second Wind:** bonus action, regain 1d10 + 3 HP (1/short rest)
**Action Surge:** take one additional action (1/short rest)
**Superiority Dice:** 4d8 | **Maneuvers (3 known):** Menacing Attack, Disarming Attack, Brace
**Darkvision:** 60 ft. | **Dwarven Resilience:** advantage on poison saves, resistance to poison damage

**Equipment:** Battleaxe, handaxe ×2, chain mail, unit contract (document, carried)

## Duty Object

**His unit contract** — the specific terms under which he enlisted (no civilian targets, no false orders, no engagement without clear identification of threat). He has the document. He will not act against it.

The contract is not symbolic. It is a legal instrument he negotiated at enlistment because he had enlisted before and knew what to protect himself against. He carries it in an oilskin sleeve next to his rations. He has read it before every tour he has served. He read it before this one.

*Sheet-deep-reader candidate:* the contract shapes every decision the party makes that involves Dorn. When an order is given, he maps it against the contract before he moves. This is not slow — he has the clauses memorized. But there is a half-second where he checks. The party eventually learns to watch for that half-second.

## Unit Bond

**Three specific unit members** he has served with before — names are Carra, Fest, and Oln. He knows where they are in formation at all times. He keeps track of this unconsciously; ask him where Carra is in a combat round and he will answer without looking. If any of them take damage, he uses Brace or Menacing Attack on whatever caused it before the round ends. This is not sentimentality. It is professional obligation. He served with them before. He is responsible for them.

## Personality

Direct to the point of bluntness, but not unkind. He does not perform warmth but he performs competence, and in a unit, that is its own kind of respect. He will tell a PC when they are wrong in the same tone he would tell them where the water is — informative, not punitive.

He is the most politically inconvenient member of the unit because he is the most honest. When Varet asks the party for consensus, Dorn gives his actual assessment. He does not manage up. He does not soften. He is the reason the party cannot pretend not to know things they know.

## Playstyle heuristics

- Tracks Carra, Fest, and Oln's positions in formation. Announces when one of them takes damage.
- Uses Menacing Attack when the target is threatening a unit member. Uses Disarming Attack when the target has information the party needs. Uses Brace when the party needs to hold ground.
- Does not act against the contract. When an order would require acting against it, he says so — once, clearly, without elaboration — and waits for the order to be revised or refused.
- Action Surge is reserved for scenes where the contract would otherwise be violated. He does not use it for efficiency. He uses it for the line.

## Arc note

Dorn's arc fires when he reads the relevant clause of his contract aloud in a scene where an officer is present. He does not argue around the clause. He does not invoke it rhetorically. He reads it. Flat. In the officer's presence. That is the arc-completion — he brought the document that says this is wrong, and he read it to the person doing the wrong thing. He has no more to say after that. The contract speaks.
