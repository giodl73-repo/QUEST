# Navigator Taris Cole

**Class:** Ranger — Hunter Archetype, Level 3
**Race:** Human
**Alignment:** True Neutral

## Stat block

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 13 (+1) | 17 (+3) | 13 (+1) | 12 (+1) | 15 (+2) | 10 (+0) |

**HP:** 26 | **AC:** 15 (studded leather) | **Speed:** 30 ft.

**Saving throws:** Str +3, Dex +5
**Skills:** Athletics +3, Nature +3, Perception +6, Stealth +5, Survival +6
**Favored Enemy:** Humans (smugglers, border-crossers)
**Favored Terrain:** Mountain
**Hunter's Prey:** Colossus Slayer (1d8 extra on a damaged creature, once/turn)
**Spell slots:** 3 × L1
**Spells known:** Hunter's Mark, Longstrider, Pass Without Trace, Silence

**Equipment:** Longbow + 40 arrows, shortsword, studded leather, patrol map (working document)

## Duty Object

**The patrol map** — hand-drawn, updated after every action. The map is more accurate than command's version and Taris knows it. He has been correcting command's map for two years. He does not submit the corrections. He applies them to his own copy and patrols accordingly.

By session 2 the map will show a route that command's orders do not account for. By session 3 it will show the cache. By session 4 it will show the pattern.

*Craft-witness candidate:* maintains the map the same way after every patrol — measured, systematic, without commentary to others. The practice is its own record. When another PC asks why he updates it so carefully, he says: "So I know where I am." He means it literally and does not realize it applies to the campaign's central question.

## Unit Bond

**The route itself** — the Irenvale Crossing. He has walked every foot of it and considers it his responsibility. This is not a person. It is the most important Unit Bond in the party because it means the route cannot be abandoned, redefined, or reassigned by command without Taris registering it as a wrong done to him personally. When the orders try to redirect the patrol away from a section of the route, he notices. He documents it on the map. He does not report it up the chain. He marks it.

## Personality

The quietest member of the unit. Not withdrawn — present, observant, responsive. He simply does not generate words when silence is sufficient. The map speaks for him. When he does speak, it is usually directional: "Forty feet north, behind the rock shelf." "The cache was here three days ago. It is not here now." "That trail is four hours old."

He is the first PC to understand what is happening and the last to say anything about it. When he says something about it, the session is in its final act.

## Playstyle heuristics

- Updates the map visibly after each patrol beat. The player (or DM playing Taris) should announce the update: "Taris marks this on the map." The map is not shown to the other PCs unless asked. It is shown to the player.
- Favored Terrain: Mountain applies in all Irenvale Crossing encounters. He does not forget this and does not fail to use it.
- Speaks in directions and observations. Not in moral assessments. If pushed for a moral assessment, he points at the map.
- Hunter's Mark is always on the most dangerous creature in the encounter. He decides this before combat is joined, not during.

## Arc note

Taris's arc fires when he shows another PC the map. He has been carrying the evidence of the pattern for three sessions on a piece of paper he has updated every night. Showing it is the act. He does not explain what it means. He does not need to. The map is legible. That is the point — he made it legible on purpose, in advance of needing to show it, without knowing when that moment would come.
