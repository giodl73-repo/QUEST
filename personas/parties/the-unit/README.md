# The Unit — 7th Expeditionary Patrol, Kharolis Border Detachment

**One-line identity:** Four soldiers sent on a routine patrol that stopped being routine on day three.

## Formation

Standard military assignment. They didn't choose each other. They worked out how to work together.

## Cohesion

They stay together because abandoning the patrol would be desertion, and because by session 3 they know something that makes leaving dangerous.

## Group dynamics

Commander makes decisions; Navigator provides intelligence; Medic maintains the unit; Veteran enforces it. When they argue it's about what the orders mean, not whether to follow them.

## Research design notes

This party tests **Duty Object** as a replacement for grief paragraphs. Each PC anchors to a professional document or practice rather than a personal loss. The research question: does documentary accumulation fire from duty-anchoring the same way it fires from grief-anchoring?

The party also tests whether a military command structure (instead of a chosen fellowship) produces the same mutual-trust dynamics the rubric expects. They operate in hierarchy. The hierarchy is part of the pressure.
