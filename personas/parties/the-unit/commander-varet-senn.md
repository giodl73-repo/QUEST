# Commander Varet Senn

**Class:** Paladin — Oath of the Crown, Level 3
**Race:** Human
**Alignment:** Lawful Good

## Stat block

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 (+2) | 10 (+0) | 14 (+2) | 13 (+1) | 15 (+2) | 16 (+3) |

**HP:** 27 | **AC:** 18 (chain mail + shield) | **Speed:** 30 ft.

**Saving throws:** Wis +4, Cha +5
**Skills:** Athletics +4, History +3, Insight +4, Persuasion +5
**Lay on Hands:** 15 HP/day pool
**Divine Smite:** expend spell slot on hit for 2d8 radiant (L1) or 3d8 (L2)
**Spell slots:** 4 × L1, 2 × L2
**Spells prepared (6):** Bless, Command, Cure Wounds, Protection from Evil and Good, Aid, Lesser Restoration

**Oath of the Crown features:**
- *Sacred Weapon:* action to imbue weapon with +Cha to attack rolls for 1 min
- *Turn the Tide:* reaction to grant 1d6 + Cha temp HP to nearby creature at 0 HP
- *Channel Divinity (2/rest)*

## Duty Object

**The field journal** — she documents every order received and every action taken. Has done so for 9 years. The journal is not a record of feelings; it is a record of decisions.

The journal has a structure: date, order received, action taken, outcome, discrepancy (if any). Discrepancy entries are sparse. By session 4 they will not be sparse. The journal is the campaign's Route E delivery mechanism — if Varet's arc fires, she presents it herself.

*Documentary-witness candidate:* the field journal IS her reception mechanism. Does it accumulate atmospheric content across sessions without being designed for it?

## Unit Bond

**Sergeant Arren Vole** — her NCO, 12 years of service, the person she trusts to tell her when she's wrong. He is the unit's internal check. If Arren says something is wrong, Varet hears it as an official concern, not a personal complaint. His loyalty to the unit — not to command, not to Varet specifically — is the thing she relies on.

## Personality

Professional to a fault. Not cold — she notices everything, including the things she does not put in the journal. She puts facts in the journal. Facts are what she can defend. The discrepancy column is the one place she is fully honest.

She does not tell the party how she feels about the orders. She tells them what the orders say and what the evidence says. She expects them to put those two things together themselves.

## Playstyle heuristics

- Documents in real time. If the session produces an event, there will be a journal entry before the scene closes. The player (or DM playing Varet) should read journal entries aloud when relevant.
- Follows orders until the discrepancy column has more entries than the outcome column. When that ratio inverts, the arc fires.
- Does not seek moral permission. Seeks evidence.
- Lay on Hands is used when needed, not rationed. She does not calculate the HP pool against future sessions. She calculates it against the person in front of her.

## Arc note

**Route E deliverable:** The field journal, presented by Varet herself. She does not speak a speech. She opens the journal to the discrepancy column and reads it aloud. That is the finale. The party gave her the vocabulary to act by filling in what she could not observe directly. When she reads it back in the tribunal, it is the party's evidence in her voice, under her authority, on her nine-year record.

The arc fires when Varet writes a discrepancy entry about her own commanding officer. That is the moment. Everything before it is accumulation.
