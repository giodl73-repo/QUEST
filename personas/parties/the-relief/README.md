# The Relief

## One-line identity

Four people intercepted by separate Rose Council couriers and redirected to a fortress that doesn't want them.

## Formation

None of them volunteered. A Rose Council authority — operating from Palanthas, three weeks' travel from The Needle — sent individual orders to each: a Knight who owed a posting, a healer who owed a favor, a ranger who knew the terrain, and a former Red Robe whom the Council had contracted for his arcane utility. They met at the last waystation before The Needle, four days before the siege tightened, and have been traveling together long enough to know each other's names and not much else. The Warden at Thorngate was not consulted.

## Cohesion

They stay together because leaving would require a reason, and none of them has one yet. Davan is bound by orders. Sava is bound by obligation she doesn't fully understand. Talis took a fee. Caelith has nowhere else to be. These are not inspiring reasons. They are honest ones. What develops beyond them is the campaign's work.

## Group Dynamics

Davan and Sava default to each other as the party's practical center — he handles the fighting, she handles the aftermath, and they have learned to stay out of each other's way while doing it. Talis gives information once and does not repeat it; Caelith gives information with context attached and does not apologize for the length. When the party argues, it is usually about whether to act or wait, and it is usually Davan (act) versus Caelith (wait), with Sava deciding and Talis already gone.
