# Personas

Five design-canon voices who critique our adventures. Selected for substance over performance — designers, writers, and module authors, not actual-play celebrities.

## Roster

| File | Lens |
|---|---|
| `gygax.md` | Old-school discipline, resource attrition, treasure-as-XP, player skill over character build |
| `jaquays.md` | Non-linear dungeon topology, loops/verticality/shortcuts ("Jaquaysing the dungeon") |
| `schick.md` | *White Plume Mountain* author — signature-treasure rooms, restraint on railroading |
| `moldvay.md` | *B/X D&D*, *B4: The Lost City* — procedural clarity, brutal on table-readiness |
| `patrick-stuart.md` | *Deep Carbon Observatory*, *Veins of the Earth* — literary weirdness, treasure-as-haunting |

## File Format

Each persona file has:

1. **Biography snippet** — who they are, what they wrote, why their voice matters here.
2. **Design values** — what they prize, what they reject.
3. **Rubric weights** — their multipliers for each of the 8 axes in `rubric.md` (sum to 8.0; unweighted = 1.0 on each).
4. **Critique style** — prose voice, vocabulary, length guidelines.
5. **Red flags** — things that will draw their wrath.
6. **Greenlights** — things that will draw their respect.

## Ethical note

The three historical personas (Gygax d. 2008, Moldvay d. 2007, Jaquays d. 2024) are dramatized from their published writing. Reviews are in-voice critique in the spirit of their design philosophy — not impersonation for attribution. We do not put real opinions in their mouths on matters they didn't publish on.

Schick and Stuart are living designers; same principle applies — critique is in the spirit of their published work.

## Rubric

See `rubric.md` for the shared 8-axis scoring rubric.

## Reviews

Persona reviews are written to `adventures/NNNN-<slug>/reviews/<persona>-YYYY-MM-DD.md`. A `SUMMARY.md` in the same directory aggregates weighted scores.
