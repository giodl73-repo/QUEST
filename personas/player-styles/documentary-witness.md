# Player Style — documentary-witness

## Definition

A PC whose primary mode of atmospheric reception is **external documentation**: writing, annotation, cataloging, and archiving rather than internal monologue, behavioral change, or voiced response. The documentation IS the reception. The PC files what they observe; the accumulation of filed observations constitutes their emotional and intellectual engagement with the campaign's material.

## What it looks like in play

- The PC writes in a notebook, annotates a map, catalogs evidence, or makes records in lieu of speaking or acting on what they receive
- The documentation accumulates across sessions without being surfaced to other PCs
- A single session's entry may be brief and opaque; the pattern only becomes visible across multiple sessions
- The final entry in the pattern is often the thing that cannot be filed — the observation that exceeds the PC's current vocabulary or framework, archived as a failed filing rather than denied

## Triggers

This style is active when:
- A PC responds to an atmospheric beat by documenting rather than reacting
- The documentation is present in the session log (written in the PC's notes, charted, inscribed) but not announced to the party
- The PC's relationship to their own records becomes characterologically visible (consulting them, adding to them, failing to add to them)
- A session ends with an unanswered notation that prior sessions' notations converge upon

## How session-runner handles it

- When a PC with this style encounters an atmospheric beat: narrate the documentation act rather than an internal response. *"Caelith writes two lines. He does not tell anyone what they say."*
- Track what the PC has documented across sessions in the handoff: *"Caelith's notebook: S1 grey-blue annotations (pre-planned siege positions); S3 Pren's phrase ('she came back for something unfinished'); S5 Helin's line; S6 'the annotations converge.'"*
- The final entry fires when the accumulated documentation produces either: (a) a single synthesizing line that names the pattern, or (b) a failed entry — the observation that cannot be categorized, archived as such

## Arc structure

Three stages (analogous to verbal-documentation-arc but expressed through writing rather than speech):

1. **Accumulation** — entries made across sessions without surfacing; the PC files what they observe
2. **Convergence** — a session in which the accumulated entries resolve into a pattern, often visible only to the reader of the log (not to the PC, who may not yet see it)
3. **Limit** — an entry that cannot be made; the framework encounters its edge; the failure is documented rather than denied. *"Unable to categorize."* This is the arc's completion.

## Cross-applicability

**Amplifies:** campaigns with layered information that rewards sustained attention (C4's grey-blue annotations across 6 sessions; C1's Vethrenn margin notes); any PC with Investigation or Arcana as primary skills.

**Complements:** sheet-deep-reader (both require the PC's character-sheet content to be deep and specific); craft-witness (both involve a private practice maintained regardless of audience).

**Undermines:** sessions where all information is immediate and in-the-moment; campaigns without cross-session information threads; parties where every PC processes atmospherically through action rather than observation.
