# Player Style — Verbal Documentation-Arc

## Definition

A PC whose documentation practice is **oral** (recitation, memory, oral history) encounters content that exceeds, breaks, or reveals gaps in their current vocabulary. The arc progresses through three stages across sessions:

1. **Cannot categorize** — the PC observes something they cannot place in any tradition they know. The observation is held but not spoken.
2. **Identifies the gap** — the PC recognizes what is absent: not just "I don't know what this is" but "I know what this tradition is missing." Vocabulary for the gap develops before vocabulary for the thing itself.
3. **Names the thing** — the PC speaks the held observation aloud, to someone who can receive it. The naming may change the thing in the speaking (oral traditions are altered by transmission). This is the arc's completion.

## What It Looks Like

The PC goes still when encountering the content. They observe without writing, without recording, without categorizing. They hold the observation across scenes and sessions. Their verbal practice is tested not by what they can hold but by what they cannot.

The arc does not resolve through a single session. It develops. Each session adds a stage or deepens an existing stage.

## Source Instances (Calla Fosse, C3)

**Stage 1 activations (C3-S1):**
- Scene 2.1: Insight fumble (6 vs DC 12) — the congregation's shrine arrangements break her categorical framework entirely. She can describe; she cannot name. *(framework-failure arc-activation — I-C3-S1-01, adopted v1.7)*
- Scene 5: Second encounter with shrine arrangements (R5, Gatehouse). Same failure, deeper: she now sees a developmental arc in the practice without being able to name the tradition.

**Stage 1 → Stage 2 transition (C3-S2):**
- Scene 2.3: She recognizes the developmental arc across shrine locations (gatehouse → nave). The silence has a shape now; she can see where it came from.
- Scene 2.4: Religion exactly meets DC 13 — she recognizes the imperfect rite's wrong sequence. First success: she identifies a gap between correct and imperfect forms. She gives the correct sequence: *"The verse comes third. The sharing comes second."* She is now in Stage 2.
- Scene 2.7: Veth names his own gap (*"I don't know if I am doing it right."*) — she receives this. Her arc and his meet at the same place.

**Stage 3 (future):** Calla speaks her held account of Velantha's true story. Designed entry-point: adventure 0020 (the Crypt), when Velantha speaks directly to her. Stage 3 is the PC-authored finale deliverable.

## Distinction from Other Styles

**vs. sheet-deep-reader:** Sheet-deep-reader fires when any PC acts from their character sheet's depth. Verbal-documentation-arc is specific to oral-practice PCs encountering the limits of their tradition.

**vs. documentation-arc (written):** The written documentation-arc was observed in C2 (Lenne, with her chronicle practice). The verbal variant differs in one critical way: verbal traditions are altered in the recitation. What Calla holds cannot be reproduced exactly when spoken. The transmission is the loss, not a failure of memory.

**vs. craft-witness:** Craft-witness fires when a PC's devotional practice resonates with an observed practice. Verbal-documentation-arc fires when the practice encounters its limits.

## DM Discipline

- **Design one documentation-suspension beat per session** for the PC with this style — one scene where the content exceeds their current vocabulary.
- **Do not force stage transitions.** Stage 2 emerges when the PC has gathered enough observations to identify a gap. Stage 3 emerges when the right conditions for naming are present.
- **Track the stages in the handoff.** Note which stage the PC is in at the end of each session. The transition from stage to stage should be observable in the session logs across the campaign.
- **The PC-authored finale deliverable is Stage 3.** When designing the campaign spine's PC-authored deliverable for a verbal-documentation-arc PC, the deliverable is the spoken account they have been building. It is not a document; it is a recitation. And it changes in the speaking.

## Scoring Implications

Each documentation-suspension beat (any stage) counts for:
- **Atmospheric Landing** — the moment the PC holds the unnameable
- **Character Agency** — the arc progresses from PC behavior, not module scripting

Stage transitions count additionally for **Surprise** (the arc developing visibly across sessions is unpredicted by any single session's design).

## Cluster Threshold

- 2 activations per session: the style is active.
- 5 activations across 2 sessions: style ratified (this threshold).
