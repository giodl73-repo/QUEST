# Player Style — act-without-announcement

## Definition

A PC acts differently in a later scene or session as a direct result of a prior naming event — without scene structure, dice, or declaration. The behavior is the completion; it does not require narration or confirmation from the PC or DM. The naming happened; the act follows. Anyone watching who knows the PC's history understands the connection; no one announces it.

## What it looks like in play

- A PC who named a loss in session N does something in session N+1 that echoes it — without explanation
- The act is minimal: cutting bread long-ways, scanning a perimeter before sitting, placing a token beside tools rather than in a pocket
- The connection to the prior naming is legible to the log reader but unspoken in the fiction
- The PC does not look up. Does not comment. Continues.

## Triggers

This style is active when:
- A PC named something (a grief, a loss, a longing, a professional commitment) in a prior scene
- In a subsequent scene, the PC's behavior reflects that naming — and the reflection is visible in the action log without being announced
- The behavior change is observable and traceable to the prior naming, not to tactical or narrative necessity

## How session-runner handles it

- When a PC with this style acts in a way that echoes a prior naming: write the act, not the connection. *"Thera cuts the bread long-ways. She is aware of doing it. She does not explain."* The reader knows; Thera doesn't announce.
- Do not have other PCs comment on it. Do not have the DM note it in narration. It lands silently or it doesn't land.
- Track in handoffs: *"Thera cut bread long-ways (road home from S07). Adda-grief complete. She acts from the named longing without announcement."*

## Arc structure

This style does not have a staged arc like verbal-documentation-arc. It is a recurring behavioral mode that activates after naming events. Key structure:

- **Naming event** (any session): PC names the thing — grief, longing, professional commitment, failure. This is the setup.
- **Act without announcement** (subsequent session): PC acts from the named thing without re-naming it. This is the payoff.
- The cycle can repeat: each naming event can produce a subsequent act. A PC with multiple naming events across a campaign will produce multiple acts-without-announcement.

## Cross-applicability

**Amplifies:** campaigns where PCs have specific named griefs (grief-themed party archetypes); sessions with atmospheric landing opportunities; any session following a Route D/E finale where a PC named something significant.

**Complements:** sheet-deep-reader (both operate from the PC sheet's grief paragraph, one through action, one through recognition); craft-witness (both involve non-announced behavioral patterns); bilateral-arc-completion (the second beat of a bilateral arc often fires as an act-without-announcement).

**Undermines:** parties where all PCs process atmospherically through speech; sessions with no prior naming events to draw from; first sessions (the naming must precede the act — no cross-session connection yet available).

## Amendment history

- **v1.5 ratification** (2026-04-20): Cross-session behavioral consequence without announcement — a PC acting differently in a later scene or session as a result of a prior naming event, without scene structure, dice, or declaration. Constituent innovations: I-S07-02 (Thera's bread), I-C2-S01-02 (Sera S01), I-C2-S02-03 (Sera S02, third instance).
- **v1.12 intra-session variant** (2026-04-22): The intra-session form — when a PC receives a named moment and expresses it through action/placement without announcement in the same session — is recognized as a valid variant. Constituent innovations: I-C4-S2-01 (Sava's token), I-C4-S3-01 (Davan's naming).
