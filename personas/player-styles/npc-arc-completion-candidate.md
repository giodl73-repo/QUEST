# NPC Arc Completion (candidate)

## Definition (provisional)

An NPC whose character arc reaches completion through an unscripted action that emerges from their character logic, not from DM-scripted dialogue. The completion is recognized as "right" because it is consistent with everything established about the NPC and because it provides what the session needed without being written in advance.

## The two instances

**S03 (Mira Vaenshold):** After the Wrath-Coin destruction, Mira asked Thera for a corner of her notebook, wrote "My mother's name was Seralen. I am grateful." and gave it to Grom. The module did not script this. Mira's arc — 30 years of refusing to say Seralen's name aloud — completed through a written note, in ink, handed to the forge-priest who witnessed the end of the refusal. The DM authored this from Mira's character logic.

**S05 (Wena Laen):** During the joint meeting, Wena offered to witness Tiran without the Circlet — to be present when Tiran told someone who loves her what Edric saw. The module included this as a possibility "if the party navigates well." In play, it emerged from Wena's character logic (novice trained to sit with the dying; has been Tiran's guest for 3 months; knows what witnessing means to her Order) without DM needing to consciously play the offer. It was the right move at the right moment.

## Common features

- Unscripted by module (either not in dialogue or in module as conditional "if the party navigates well")
- Consistent with established NPC character
- Completes a session-long or adventure-long arc
- Provides what the session needed emotionally without being designed to do so
- Usually involves the NPC doing something small (writing a note; making an offer) rather than a grand gesture

## Triggers / signals

- An NPC has been fully characterized across multiple scenes
- The party has created space for the NPC to act (through successful facilitation or simply through not interrupting)
- The session's emotional question has been surfaced (Mira: can the 30-year refusal end? Wena: is there a path that doesn't require the Circlet?)
- The DM recognizes the character-logic conclusion and lets it land

## Why it matters

This is not a DM writing a "good scene." This is the DM so thoroughly inhabiting an NPC that the character completes their own arc at the moment the session requires it. It is the highest form of NPC-play: character-logic > script. When it fires, it is usually the session's most remembered moment.
