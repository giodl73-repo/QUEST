# The Craft-Witness

## Definition

A PC whose character sheet specifies a **daily craft-ritual or attention-practice** (forge-priest's inside-curve greeting; silversmith's hand-in-water; scholar's pen-blessing; hunter's pre-dawn listening) and who, by virtue of that practice, receives atmospheric flags other PCs miss. **The craft-practice becomes a reception-antenna.**

This is not a caster-class effect. It is a *ritual-practice* effect. Wisdom modifier is not the relevant variable; the named, specific, daily practice is.

## The seven instances (S01-S03)

All seven are Grom Ironhand (forge-priest of Reorx, hill dwarf, Playstyle Heuristic "greet hearths: *'Thou'rt seen'*"):

1. **S01 Scene 2 — Grom hears the tomb's voice-fragment** while Kessa (the diviner wizard, the expected listener) does not. Failed Wisdom save DC 10 on Grom, pass on Kessa. The *who heard it* surprised the module.
2. **S01 Scene 6 — Grom names the rose's mechanic via forge-metaphor.** *"This is not a device. This is a starvation. It eats who made it."* Kessa's scholarly cataloging had not reached this reading. Craft-language produced theology.
3. **S02 Scene 1 — Grom receives the weight of Mensa's un-sent bread** though Kessa found the scrap. Finder and receiver diverged; craft-ritual PC was the receiver.
4. **S02 Scene 7 — Grom reads all three cells** in the Cell-Block: the chalk woman, the 63 tally-marks, the old scrubbed blood-stain. Says aloud: *"They've held people here for years. Not raiders — people."* Craft-receptivity extends to reading rooms as histories.
5. **S03 Scene 1 — Grom greets the Crystalmir arch** by tracing the half-bloomed rose on the inside-curve. *"Thou'rt seen,"* in Dwarvish. His fourth hearth-greeting of the day was delivered to a Vaenshold carving he had never seen before; the arch's stone recognized his gesture.
6. **S03 Scene 4 — Grom's silent benediction to Mira.** Insight 23 read Mira's 30-year grief-shape as Seralen-refusal. Without prompt, Grom stood, walked to Mira, said four words: *"Seralen was loved."* Mira closed her eyes for a long moment; when she opened them, she nodded once. Craft-witness delivered an NPC-completion chain the module did not script.
7. **S03 Scene 6 — Grom's craft-reading of the Mirror.** *"This wood knows what it is holding."* Then, on seeing the unconverged polish-lines: *"We will honor this. At the forge. At dawn. Reorx takes back what his children could not finish."* The destruction-argument came from craft-witness reading; the Mirror was named *unmendable* by a priest whose heuristic-1 is "mend first." Craft overrode heuristic.

## Triggers / signals

- A PC performs their named craft-ritual (off-camera or on) — thumbing the inside-curve, touching a hearth, blessing a forge-tool, etc.
- In the scenes following that ritual, the PC catches an atmospheric beat other PCs (including wisdom-casters) missed.
- The PC's reception is traceable to their practice, not their stat-block.
- When pressed, the PC can *name* the connection: *"The dwarf-work told me."* *"The hearth knew first."* *"I listen at forges."*

## Cross-applicability

**Amplified by:**
- Explicit ritual-practice on the PC sheet ("thumb on inside-curve," "hand-in-water at dawn," "pen-blessing before reading").
- Heritage-craft backgrounds (Reorx-priesthood, hedge-silversmith lineage, hunter-tradition).
- PC Playstyle Heuristics that include a signature move tied to the craft.

**Neutral (does not preclude):**
- Wisdom score. Grom (Wis 16) is high, but the pattern is traceable to practice, not stat.

**Undermined by:**
- PCs whose craft is listed as background-only, without daily practice specified.
- Sessions where the PC's ritual is never performed (on-camera or off).
- DMs who do not prompt the ritual to fire (though in S01 and S02, Grom's rituals fire *without* prompts — the sheet carries it).

## Play implications

When a DM designs an adventure for a party with a Craft-Witness:

- **Provide atmospheric beats the craft can catch.** The module should have *something to listen for* that the craft-ritual specifically tunes. A voice-fragment, a hearth-memory, a forge-mark, a room's history.
- **Let the ritual's language name the mechanic.** Do not have the module pre-name mechanics; let the craft-PC name them in craft-language. "Starvation that eats who made it" is better writing than "curse."
- **The Craft-Witness may be the party's *moral* centerpoint, not the *tactical* one.** In S02, Grom counter-argued the attunement. The sheet's craft-ritual ("mend first, keep party whole") produced ethics, not combat.
- **Do not require the Craft-Witness to cast spells for their reception.** Their reception is *character*, not *class feature*.

## Sub-type watch (for future cluster-growth)

All four original instances are Grom. Future instances may produce sub-types:

- **Forge-priests** (Grom-pattern): craft-ritual is hearth-greeting; reception is spatial + historical.
- **Herbalists / hedge-healers**: craft-ritual might be pulse-reading or root-sorting; reception might be biological + time-based.
- **Scribes / illuminators**: craft-ritual is pen-blessing or ink-consecration; reception might be textual + attribution-based.

These are predictions. The workshop will know when S03+ produces non-Grom instances.

## Historical note

Like `sheet-deep-reader`, this is a first-ever promoted style (both ratified post-S03). The pattern is Grom-specific across S01-S03 (all 7 instances), but the *principle* — craft-ritual as reception-antenna — generalizes. The DM/designer should write future PCs with explicit craft-rituals when they want to produce Craft-Witnesses.

**S03 reinforcement specifically** showed craft-witness extending *beyond* Grom's original reception pattern: Grom not only *received* atmospheric weight (Mira's grief-shape, the Mirror's unmendability) but *delivered* craft-language as decision-input for the rest of the party. The argument for destruction was craft-witness reasoning; the three other PCs accepted it without rolling counter-checks. **Craft-witness became a party-level moral anchor at S03** — not just a reception pattern.

Chronicle's analog: the **Ceremonial-Genealogical voice register** (Oral Tradition family) — an emergent register where formal invocation practices become the channel for historical reception. Similar discovery-by-clustering pattern.
