# Player Style — NPC Arc-Completion

## Definition

An NPC delivers their arc-completion moment by **character logic** rather than DM scripting. The conditions in the NPC's `npcs/<slug>.md` Arc-Completion section are met by party action, and the NPC fires their arc because the conditions are satisfied — not because the DM decided it was time.

The arc-completion is designed before play and triggered by play. The DM does not choose the moment; the party creates it.

## What It Looks Like

- The party asks an NPC the specific question, makes the specific offer, or takes the specific action the arc requires.
- The NPC responds with their designed arc-completion line or act — the exact thing they were capable of saying or doing that closes their arc.
- The moment is recognizable because it is *smaller* than expected: not a speech, not a monologue, but one line, one act, one gesture.
- The DM does not announce that the arc fired. It simply fires.

## Source Instances

**Mig (C3-S1):** Condition — party opened the inner door cooperatively. Act — *"She steps through, and does not look back."* No line. The act was the completion.

**Veth (C3-S2):** Condition — party asked *"what are you trying to understand?"* Act — *"I don't know if I am doing it right. I do it anyway."* Said once. Then back to work.

**Gorret (C3-S2):** Condition — Dren gave an honest answer to *"why do you need to go up?"* Act — *"At least you say what you are."* Said once. Then stepped aside.

## DM Discipline

- **Design the arc-completion before play.** The line or act must be in the NPC's file before the session begins. Improvised arc-completions do not count.
- **Name the exact condition.** Not "if the party earns Gorret's trust" — but "if a party member gives an honest answer to Gorret's specific question." The condition must be specific enough that the DM knows with certainty when it has been met.
- **Log it when it fires.** The session log should note: "[NPC]'s arc-completion fired — [scene reference] — what they said or did."
- **Do not extend the moment.** The arc-completion line is the completion. The NPC does not explain it, expand on it, or provide further exposition. The DM moves on.

## Scoring Implications

When NPC-arc-completion fires by character logic (not DM scripting), credit goes to:
- **Atmospheric Landing** — the moment lands because it was designed, not forced
- **Module Fidelity** — the NPC's arc section was written and honored
- **Character Agency** — the party's behavior triggered the condition

Firing by DM scripting (the DM decides "now is the time") does not count for scoring purposes and does not produce the same quality of landing.

## Cluster Threshold

- 1 instance per session: strong session.
- 2+ instances per session: exceptional session (only twice documented in the workshop).
- 3 instances across 2 sessions: style ratified (this threshold).

## What This Style Measures

This style confirms that the npc-architect pipeline is working. When NPCs have designed arc-completions, they fire by character logic. When they don't, the DM has to improvise the moment, and improvised arc-completions are weaker.

**The style is evidence of pipeline discipline, not improvisation.**
