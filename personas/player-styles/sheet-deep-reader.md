# The Sheet-Deep Reader

## Definition

A PC whose character sheet is read *as a live source of agency* throughout session-play. The PC (or DM-playing-PC under Option A) surfaces backgrounds, prior-adventure hooks, and institutional training as current-scene decision inputs **without being prompted by the DM**.

The sheet is not reference material. The sheet is *doing agency*.

## The five original instances

1. **Kessa recognized the adventure** from Vethrenn's margin-note at the moment Brother Laen described the Silver Rose (S01 Scene 1). Her PC sheet's "backstory: mentor went silent" paragraph became the session's private parallel arc — without the module scripting anything.
2. **Thera knew VANOR was wrong** because she grew up in a Solamnic way-house (S01 Scene 3). Her PC sheet's "origin: Varduin" fact produced Solamnic-family-name expertise the module's DCs alone could not have surfaced.
3. **Kessa's private investigation** ran parallel to the party's shared arc across S01. The sheet carried the through-line.
4. **Aelric read the duty-roster as dual intelligence + grief** (S02 Scene 5). Solamnic-officer training + Varran-grief combined. Sheet doing work.
5. **Kessa declined the Mirror by articulating blank-risk from her own grief-paragraph** (S03 Scene 6). She named Vethrenn-never-met, articulated the Blank-Mirror Trap risk, and used her silver-ink pen signature move to record without touching. Her decision-order's heuristic-3 ("Don't violate the Test") over-rode heuristic-1 ("Understand it") — a cross-heuristic read that the sheet's structure itself justified.

## Triggers / signals

- The PC makes an in-fiction recognition from sheet-content the DM did not prompt.
- The PC's agency is *carried* by the sheet rather than *called up* by the module.
- The PC delivers a private-arc-beat in a shared-arc scene.
- The PC's background-knowledge surfaces as an in-character observation, not a metagame statement.

## Cross-applicability

**Amplified by:**
- PC sheets with detailed grief-paragraphs (named dead, concrete memory).
- PC sheets with local-origin notes (born in X; trained at Y).
- PC sheets with prior-adventure hooks (mentor went silent; family has a rumor).
- Institutional-training backgrounds (Solamnic knight; Wizard of High Sorcery; Reorx-priest).

**Undermined by:**
- Generic backgrounds ("wanted adventure").
- PCs whose sheets are mostly combat-statted with minimal roleplay hooks.
- Sessions where the DM never pauses long enough for sheet-content to activate.

## Play implications

When a DM designs an adventure for a party with Sheet-Deep Readers:

- **Plant connections to PC sheets deliberately.** The module's module-fidelity score benefits when PC-sheet content can *activate* inside the adventure.
- **Provide sensory hooks the sheets can catch.** A name, a family-crest, a passage from a book, a vocabulary from an order — things the specific PC's sheet primes them to notice.
- **Do not crowd out the sheet-moment.** If the scene is busy, the sheet-moment may miss. Let scenes breathe.
- **Reward the sheet-read with consequence, not exposition.** When Kessa recognized the rose from Vethrenn's note, her private arc *shifted*. That is the reward. Not the DM saying *"and then you learn..."*

## Sub-type watch (for future cluster-growth)

Potential sub-types emerging from S01-S03 data; watching for cluster-promotion:

- **Local-origin readers** (Thera-pattern): PCs whose geographic origin produces setting-knowledge.
- **Prior-hook readers** (Kessa-pattern): PCs whose backstory contains an undelivered promise the current adventure fulfills.
- **Institutional readers** (Aelric-pattern): PCs whose order/faith/training produces dual-reads of intel + grief.
- **Doctrinal-silence readers** *(NEW post-S03, 1 instance)*: PCs whose primary heuristic is over-ridden by their secondary heuristic under existential artifact risk, in a way the sheet's explicit structure justifies. Kessa S03 Scene 6 — she did not abandon "Understand it"; she *re-read her own heuristic-stack* and found "Don't violate the Test" higher-weighted against a Blank-Mirror outcome. Watch for second instance in S04+ before formal sub-type promotion.

These are not sub-types yet; they are categories to watch.
